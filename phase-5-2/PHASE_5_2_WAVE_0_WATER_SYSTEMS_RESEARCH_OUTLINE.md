# Phase 5.2 Wave 0 Water Systems — Research Outline

**Purpose**: Operational research outline for writing the four Water Systems domains as publication-ready community resilience guides. Phase 5.2 Wave 0 is the first wave of original content production — the goal is working text, not further strategy documents. This outline organizes the research questions, key sources, expert contact types, and timeline for completing all four domains to publication standard.

**Relationship to existing work**: The content outlines (PHASE_5.2_WATER_SYSTEMS_CONTENT_OUTLINES.md) define the chapter structure at section level. The sourcing checklist (PHASE_5.2_WATER_CONTENT_SOURCING_CHECKLIST.md) defines data acquisition. This document bridges them to active writing: it converts section outlines into specific research questions that each section must answer, and maps those questions to sources and expert contacts. It is used by the writer, not the contributor manager.

**Format note**: All four domains follow the established Phase 5.1 format standard: YAML frontmatter, numbered step procedures, decision tables, worked examples, and a numbered sources section. The sample section (water-systems-domain-sample-section.md) is the canonical formatting reference.

## Section 1: Domain Structure and Content Goals

### 1.1 Four-Domain Architecture

Water Systems content divides into four standalone domains, each designed to be usable without the others:

| Domain | Core Question | Primary Reader | Content Type |
|--------|---------------|----------------|--------------|
| 1: Water Assessment & Testing | How do I know if this water is safe? | Anyone in an emergency or uncertain water situation | Decision trees, test procedures, interpretation tables |
| 2: Rainwater Harvesting & Storage | How do I collect and store my own water? | Homeowners, rural residents, permaculture designers | Sizing guides, installation procedures, legal maps |
| 3: Water Treatment & Purification | How do I make unsafe water safe? | General users; specific focus on low-resource contexts | Step-by-step treatment procedures, chemical quantity tables |
| 4: Wastewater & Greywater Systems | What do I do with water after use? | Rural residents, off-grid households, small communities | System selection, installation procedures, maintenance guides |

### 1.2 Wave 0 Priority Ordering

Research effort should be weighted in this order for Wave 0, based on urgency and source availability:

1. **Domain 3: Water Treatment & Purification** — highest life-safety relevance; sources (CDC, WHO, SODIS) are fully mapped and authoritative; most searchable by readers in crisis
2. **Domain 1: Water Assessment & Testing** — prerequisite knowledge for all treatment decisions; sources well-mapped; commercial kit information requires brand-level research
3. **Domain 2: Rainwater Harvesting & Storage** — well-sourced; calculation-heavy but methodologically straightforward; legal landscape research is time-intensive but bounded
4. **Domain 4: Wastewater & Greywater Systems** — lower immediate life-safety urgency; more complex regulatory landscape; some sections require professional knowledge verification

## Section 2: Research Questions by Domain

### 2.1 Domain 1: Water Assessment & Testing (15 Research Questions)

1. What are the WHO-recommended turbidity thresholds for each treatment pathway, and how do field-measurable proxies (Secchi disk, white paper test) correlate to NTU values?
2. What coliform test kits (brand, cost, time-to-result, sensitivity) are available without internet ordering in most US rural counties, and what are their documented false-positive and false-negative rates?
3. What is the complete test sequence for a well that has just been flooded — which parameters to test first, second, and third, and why?
4. What does a positive E. coli test definitively indicate, and what does it not indicate? (Specifically: does E. coli presence prove other pathogens, or only indicate fecal contamination?)
5. At what turbidity level does chlorination become unreliable, and what is the evidence base for this threshold?
6. What agricultural-runoff chemical contaminants (nitrates, atrazine, glyphosate) survive boiling or filtration, and how does a field user know when to seek lab testing versus treating in the field?
7. What is the minimum testing frequency for a shallow well used as a primary household water source — and what triggers indicate more frequent testing is warranted?
8. What natural indicator plants or site observations can indicate likely contamination before testing (proximity to CAFO, specific vegetation indicators, land use history)?
9. What is the documented accuracy of DIY pH testing using red cabbage juice, and at what pH range does the error become significant for treatment decisions?
10. How do you safely collect a water sample for field testing — specifically, what containers, handling, and timing errors will give false results?
11. What is the field procedure for detecting nitrate contamination without a commercial kit, and what are the accuracy limits of improvised methods?
12. When is a visual assessment definitive ("this water cannot be used"), and when does visual clarity falsely suggest safety?
13. What documentation format should a household water monitoring log use for integration with shared community records?
14. What are the most common field testing errors (inadequate contact time, contaminated sample vessel, expired reagent, incorrect color comparison) and how does a user verify their own test integrity?
15. When should a reader stop field testing and seek professional water quality lab services — and how do they find a certified lab without internet access?

### 2.2 Domain 2: Rainwater Harvesting & Storage (16 Research Questions)

16. What is the complete yield calculation for different climate types — temperate, semi-arid, tropical — with worked examples at three catchment sizes (500, 1,500, 3,000 sq ft)?
17. What roof materials produce the least contamination in harvested water, and what is the documented contamination difference between metal, asphalt shingle, and clay tile?
18. What first-flush volume is required to achieve acceptable potable-use water quality from each roof type — and is there a safe simplification that works across types?
19. What are the regulatory requirements in each US state for rainwater harvesting — specifically, which states restrict potable use, which require permits, and which have the most favorable rules?
20. What storage tank types have the longest lifespan-to-cost ratio in US contexts, and what are the documented failure modes for IBC tote repurposing at 5 and 10 years?
21. What is the complete DIY concrete cistern construction procedure (materials, mix ratios, curing time, waterproofing) for a 2,000-gallon above-ground tank?
22. What are the minimum specifications for a gravity-fed distribution system from a 2,500-gallon elevated tank — pipe diameter, head pressure, flow rate at 5 feet, 10 feet, and 20 feet elevation differential?
23. How does algae bloom form in stored rainwater, and what combination of tank color, coverage, and tank material best prevents it without chemical treatment?
24. What tank size is needed to bridge a 60-day summer dry period for a household of four using rainwater for garden irrigation only?
25. What are the standard sizes and costs for commercially available polyethylene tanks in the 500, 1,000, 2,500, 5,000, and 10,000-gallon ranges, and what foundation requirements does each impose?
26. What maintenance failures most commonly cause rainwater system contamination (specifically: which failures result in E. coli detectable at the tap)?
27. How do freeze protection requirements differ by climate zone, and what is the minimum-cost procedure for winterizing a gravity-fed system in Zone 5 (Midwest)?
28. What is the documented ROI for rainwater harvesting systems in high-cost-of-water US markets vs. low-cost markets — at what water rate does the system achieve positive ROI in under 10 years?
29. What are the water balance implications of serving livestock (per-animal daily requirements by species) from a rainwater system, and how does this change tank sizing?
30. How does a first-flush diverter need to be redesigned for tropical climates with high-intensity events, vs. the standard design for temperate climates?
31. What are the safety requirements for potable-use rainwater systems in California — the most restrictive major state — and what does compliance look like in practice?

### 2.3 Domain 3: Water Treatment & Purification (14 Research Questions)

32. What is the exact CDC and WHO recommended boiling time at sea level, and what are the altitude-adjusted times (with the specific altitudes that trigger each adjustment)?
33. What is the precise sodium hypochlorite dose per liter of water for different starting turbidities, and what is the standard contact time before consumption is safe?
34. How does the effectiveness of chlorination change with water temperature — specifically, does cold water (below 15°C) require a longer contact time, and by how much?
35. What pathogens does boiling fail to kill or inactivate — specifically, are there any waterborne pathogens for which boiling is insufficient at the recommended 1-minute rolling boil?
36. What is the complete field procedure for SODIS — specific bottle requirements, turbidity threshold for eligibility, exact exposure times at different UV index levels, and indicators that treatment is complete?
37. What are the documented performance specifications for a slow sand biosand filter: E. coli removal rate, turbidity removal, required hydraulic residence time, and failure conditions?
38. What household chemicals are acceptable substitutes for water treatment when standard sodium hypochlorite bleach is unavailable — and what are the exact dosing conversions for calcium hypochlorite (pool shock) at different concentrations?
39. At what point is water definitively untreatable by household methods — what contaminant types or concentrations require professional intervention regardless of available treatment?
40. What is the multi-stage treatment sequence for worst-case surface water (high turbidity, suspected fecal, suspected agricultural chemical), and what does each stage remove?
41. What are the documented performance specifications for commonly available ceramic water filters — specifically, what is the flow rate, what pore size, and what is the maintenance schedule that preserves performance?
42. How does activated charcoal (made from local materials) compare to commercial granular activated carbon for organic contaminant removal — and what is the documented procedure for making and activating charcoal in a field setting?
43. What are the contraindications and dosing errors most likely to cause harm when using iodine tablets — specifically, what populations should not use iodine treatment for extended periods?
44. When treated water is stored for more than 24 hours, what is the documented recontamination risk and what storage conditions minimize it?
45. What is the field procedure for testing whether chlorination was successful (confirming 0.2–0.5 mg/L residual chlorine) without a test kit?

### 2.4 Domain 4: Wastewater & Greywater Systems (12 Research Questions)

46. What is the legal status of greywater diversion for landscape irrigation in each of the 10 most populous US states, and what are the permit or notification requirements?
47. What contaminants does typical household greywater contain (soap, food particles, fecal indicator bacteria — from what sources, at what levels), and what does this mean for safe reuse applications?
48. What is the minimum design specification for a gravity-fed greywater landscape irrigation system: components, flow rates, distribution options, and safety requirements to prevent ponding?
49. What is the standard septic tank pump-out interval, and what factors (household size, garbage disposal use, age of tank) shorten this interval significantly?
50. What are the documented signs of septic system failure in order of severity — which signs indicate days-to-failure vs. which indicate immediate overflow risk?
51. What is the step-by-step procedure for inspecting a septic system lid and inlet/outlet baffles, and what should a homeowner look for to assess remaining service life?
52. What are the documented performance specifications for a constructed wetland cell treating household greywater: surface area per daily gallons, vegetation selection, substrate requirements?
53. What is the permit process for a composting toilet in a jurisdiction that requires septic system approval — specifically, can a composting toilet satisfy the blackwater treatment requirement?
54. What are the material differences between a legal greywater laundry-to-landscape system (no permit in most states) and systems that require permits — at what point does system complexity trigger regulatory review?
55. What household water use reductions are achievable through low-flow fixture replacement alone, and how does this reduce greywater system sizing requirements?
56. What are the documented failure modes for DIY constructed wetland systems — specifically, what causes clogging, and what is the recovery procedure?
57. When is a septic system definitively at end-of-life and requires replacement rather than repair — what inspection finding or failure mode is the definitive trigger?

## Section 3: Key Sources (47 Preliminary Sources)

### 3.1 Primary Government Sources (Public Domain; Fully Usable)

**[S1] CDC Water Treatment Guidance**
- URL: https://www.cdc.gov/healthywater/drinking/index.html
- What it covers: Boiling times by elevation, chlorination dose tables, SODIS procedure, emergency storage guidance
- Priority use: Domains 3 and 1; all chemical quantity values must be cross-checked against this source before publication

**[S2] CDC Emergency Water Supply Creation Guide**
- URL: https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/about/how-to-create-and-store-an-emergency-water-supply.html
- What it covers: Household water storage volumes, container selection, storage duration, safe water handling
- Priority use: Domain 1 (emergency protocols section) and Domain 3 (storage after treatment)

**[S3] EPA National Primary Drinking Water Regulations (NPDWR)**
- URL: https://www.epa.gov/ground-water-and-drinking-water/national-primary-drinking-water-regulations
- What it covers: Maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) for 90+ regulated contaminants; treatment technique requirements
- Note: 2024 update added PFAS MCLs (PFOA/PFOS at 4.0 ng/L, effective June 2024) — include in chemical contaminants section
- Priority use: Domain 1 (testing interpretation: what levels trigger action)

**[S4] EPA Emergency Response for Drinking Water Utilities**
- URL: https://www.epa.gov/waterutilityresponse
- What it covers: Utility-level emergency response; less relevant to household procedures but useful for contextualizing when community systems fail
- Priority use: Cross-domain context section (integration with community water systems)

**[S5] FEMA / Ready.gov Water Guidance**
- URL: https://www.ready.gov/water
- What it covers: 1 gallon/person/day baseline recommendation; 2-week self-sufficiency target; storage container guidance
- Note: FEMA's 2-week preparation target (updated from 72-hour original guidance) is significant for sizing calculations
- Priority use: Domain 2 (storage sizing section); Domain 3 (emergency storage after treatment)

**[S6] USDA Rural Development Water / Well Guides**
- URL: https://www.rd.usda.gov and https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/wps/portal/nrcs/detail/national/water/
- What it covers: Well drilling methods, pump selection criteria, yield estimation, well casing requirements
- Priority use: Domain 1 (groundwater source assessment) and Domain 2 (alternate sources section)

**[S7] EPA Rainwater Harvesting: Conservation, Credit, Codes, and Costs**
- URL: https://www.epa.gov/water
- What it covers: State regulatory summary, stormwater credit programs, system cost data
- Priority use: Domain 2 (legal landscape section)

**[S8] USDA NRCS Rainwater Harvesting Technical Note**
- URL: https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/
- What it covers: Sizing methodology, catchment efficiency factors, design standards
- Priority use: Domain 2 (yield calculation section)

**[S9] Texas A&M AgriLife Extension Rainwater Harvesting for Texas**
- URL: https://aggie-horticulture.tamu.edu/
- What it covers: State-specific sizing guide with worked examples; catchment efficiency tables by roof type
- Priority use: Domain 2 (worked examples section; Texas is the most favorable large-population state for rainwater harvesting)

**[S10] EPA On-Site Wastewater Treatment Systems**
- URL: https://www.epa.gov/septic

- Priority use: Domain 4 (septic sections)

## Section 6: Integration Points with Other Phase 5.2 Domains

### 6.1 Food Security Domain
- Domain 2 (rainwater) provides irrigation water; food security domain defines crop water requirements
- Domain 3 (treatment) covers water for food preparation; food safety domain covers cooking and food storage
- Integration note: Water for food production (irrigation) does not require potable-quality treatment; water for cooking does — this distinction should be explicit in both domains

### 6.2 Healthcare Domain
- Domain 3 (treatment) references medication-water interactions (iodine contraindications for thyroid patients)

- Integration note: Medical reference module (Phase 5.2 Priority 1) uses the same source citation format; Water domain should use identical citation structure for cross-referencing

### 6.3 Energy Domain
- Domain 2 (storage) includes pumping energy requirements; energy domain covers solar-powered pumping options
- UV disinfection (powered by electricity) is one treatment option in Domain 3; energy domain specifies when reliable power is available
- Integration note: All water treatment and distribution procedures should include a "power-available" and "power-unavailable" pathway

- Cross-domain integration note: The Kansas LEMA water governance model (documented in systems-resilience research outline S33) is relevant to community-scale water sharing — should be referenced in the community governance domain, with a pointer from Domain 2
