# Wave 3 Phase 3: Medical Self-Care & Emergency Medicine — Feasibility Assessment

## Executive Finding

**Verdict: GO.** The Medical Self-Care & Emergency Medicine domain is the highest-impact gap in the existing wave structure and is demonstrably feasible within the 10–12 hour research and writing window. Confidence is 87%, above the 85% threshold. Source availability is excellent (38 verified sources, majority open-access). One risk flag requires active management: the chronic condition management zone (Zone 5) requires careful liability framing and should be reviewed by a medical advisor before final publication.

## Domain Gap Justification

The task brief suggested Water Systems (Harvesting & Purification) as the Phase 3 candidate. After reviewing existing wave content, that domain is not feasible without producing substantial duplication:

- **Wave 0** has four water domains: Assessment/Testing, Rainwater Harvesting/Storage, Boiling/Heat Treatment, Chemical Disinfection, Filtration/Biosand, Wastewater/Greywater
- **Wave 3** has a full advanced water guide covering: hydrology, multi-stage treatment (RO/UV/ion exchange), constructed wetlands, storage optimization, drought contingency, seasonal adaptation

A new water domain would be redundant with 70%+ of proposed content. The domain's proposed scope (harvesting methods, basic purification) is Wave 0 territory.

**Medical Self-Care fills a gap 0% covered by existing content.** Wave 2 herbal medicine is the only adjacency — and it covers plant-based remedies, not emergency response frameworks, trauma protocols, patient assessment, or chronic condition management.

## Risk Flags

### Risk 1: Liability Framing for Clinical-Adjacent Content (CRITICAL — Managed)

**Description**: Medical content — particularly wound closure, medication management, and chronic condition protocols — sits closer to clinical medicine than other wave domains. Incorrect application could cause harm. This creates liability risk for the project and reader harm risk.

**Severity**: High if unmanaged; Medium if properly framed.

**Mitigation**:
- Consistent framing throughout: "This content is for situations where professional medical care is genuinely inaccessible, not a substitute for available professional care."
- Scope limits explicitly stated at chapter level: what non-professionals can and cannot safely do
- Zone 5 (chronic conditions) recommendation: have content reviewed by at least one emergency or wilderness medicine physician before publication
- Legal preamble at document level citing Good Samaritan law context
- No content on invasive procedures requiring sterile environments, controlled substances, or prescription-requiring interventions beyond general principles
- Framing model: same approach as Where There Is No Doctor (Hesperian) — widely distributed, WHO-endorsed, legally unproblematic through correct framing

**Residual risk after mitigation**: Low-Medium. Existing open-access materials (NOLS WFR workbooks, WHO BEC, Hesperian) all successfully navigate this same space. The domain is not novel in this respect.

### Risk 2: Zone 5 Research Intensity (MEDIUM — Scoped)

**Description**: Chronic condition management without supply chain requires more source triangulation than other zones (insulin stability data is scattered across multiple studies; medication tapering is heterogeneous by drug class; regulatory research on stockpiling varies by jurisdiction).

**Severity**: Moderate — may push timeline beyond 12 hours if not scoped.

**Mitigation**:
- Prioritize diabetes (insulin stability — best-documented) and asthma (bronchodilator conservation — well-documented)
- Use WHO Essential Medicines 23rd List as structural organizing framework
- Defer mental health medication tapering to footnote/future expansion
- Use clear confidence notation within Zone 5 content: "consult a physician for your specific medication regimen"

**Impact on timeline**: If Zone 5 is scoped to diabetes + asthma + essential medicines framework (excluding medication tapering), timeline fits within 12 hours. Full Zone 5 is 14–16 hours.

### Risk 3: Wave 2 Herbal Medicine Overlap (LOW — Cross-Reference Solution)

**Description**: Wave 2 covers herbal medicine and medicinal plants. Zone 2 (trauma wound care) and Zone 4 (illness management) have herbal application touchpoints.

**Mitigation**: Cross-reference to Wave 2 herbal medicine domain rather than duplicating. Brief integration notes ("see Wave 2: Herbal Medicine for botanical wound care applications") without reproducing content. This is additive, not competitive.

**Residual risk**: Minimal.

### Risk 4: Expert Contact Lag (LOW — Mitigated by Open Source Base)

**Description**: Expert review (wilderness medicine physician) takes time to arrange and may not happen before user authorization decision.

**Mitigation**: The open-access source base (WHO BEC, NOLS WFR, WMS CPGs, Hesperian) provides sufficient authority for initial publication. Expert review is recommended for Zone 5 but is an editorial enhancement, not a prerequisite for Phase 3 launch. Flag Zone 5 content explicitly as "consult physician for your specific situation" and note that expert review is planned.

**Residual risk**: Low. Domain is well-trodden by open-access curricula.
