## Zone 1: Emergency Response Frameworks & Patient Assessment

### Authoritative Curricula and Manuals

**S1. WHO-ICRC Basic Emergency Care: Participant Workbook**
- URL: https://www.who.int/publications-detail-redirect/basic-emergency-care-approach-to-the-acutely-ill-and-injured
- PDF direct: https://hlh.who.int/docs/librariesprovider4/hlh-documents/who-icrc-basic-emergency-care.pdf
- Access: Open access, CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 IGO
- Annotation: WHO/ICRC/IFEM co-produced training curriculum for frontline providers with limited resources. Covers primary survey, ABCDE approach, triage, and specific condition management. Directly applicable to resource-limited settings; high content alignment with Zone 1 scope. **High confidence — primary source.**

**S2. NOLS Wilderness Medicine Core Curriculum**
- URL: https://www.nols.edu/wilderness-medicine/core-curriculum/
- Access: Curriculum overview public; full workbook available through NOLS courses; portions in open-source WFA participant workbooks
- Annotation: Gold-standard wilderness medicine curriculum covering Patient Assessment System, evacuation decision-making, and all major injury/illness categories. Developed by industry experts, field-tested over decades. Foundation for most US wilderness first aid programs. **High confidence — definitive curriculum source.**

**S3. Wilderness First Responder (WFR) Participant Workbook (2021 edition)**
- URL: https://wildsafe.org/wp-content/uploads/2021-WFA-Participant-Workbook.pdf
- Access: Freely available PDF
- Annotation: Full participant workbook for WFA/WFR level training. Contains patient assessment system, evacuation criteria, environmental medicine, trauma protocols. Directly quotable for procedures and decision trees. **High confidence — verified accessible.**

**S4. American Red Cross Wilderness and Remote First Aid Curriculum**
- URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/cpr/wilderness-sports
- Course fact sheet: https://www.redcross.org/content/dam/redcross/training-services/course-fact-sheets/Wilderness-and-Remote-First-Aid-Instructor-Course-Fact-Sheet.pdf
- Access: Curriculum outline and fact sheets public
- Annotation: ARC WRFA is based on BSA WFA doctrine; nationally recognized; covers patient assessment, trauma, environmental emergencies, medical conditions. Good secondary source to triangulate NOLS protocols. **High confidence — authoritative secondary curriculum.**

**S5. START Triage Protocol (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment)**
- URL: https://chemm.hhs.gov/startadult.htm (CHEMM/HHS)
- Access: Public domain (US government)
- Annotation: DHHS-hosted explanation of START triage for mass casualty events. Lays out the 30/2/can-do algorithm. Applicable to Zone 1 multi-casualty section. **High confidence — official government source.**

## Zone 2: Trauma Care

**S6. Stop the Bleed Program Materials — American College of Surgeons**
- URL: https://www.stopthebleed.org/get-trained/
- Academic basis: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9611563/
- Access: Program materials public; academic framework open access PMC
- Annotation: ACS national program; 5M+ people trained; covers direct pressure, wound packing, tourniquet application. PMC article documents framework for public access trauma programs. Directly provides Zone 2 bleeding control curriculum. **High confidence — primary source for hemorrhage control.**

**S7. Wilderness First Responder (WFR) Curriculum — survivalmed.org**
- URL: https://survivalmed.org/wp-content/uploads/Wilderness-First-Responder-Curriculum.pdf
- Access: Freely available PDF
- Annotation: Comprehensive WFR curriculum including fractures, dislocations, splinting skills, burns, wound management, head injury. Covers improvised materials use. Key source for Zone 2 musculoskeletal and wound sections. **High confidence — detailed procedural content.**

**S8. PHTLS: Prehospital Trauma Life Support (NAEMT)**
- Publisher: NAEMT/Elsevier; 10th ed. 2023
- ISBN: 978-1284280708
- Access: Purchase required; widely held in libraries; key protocols cited in open literature
- Annotation: Standard reference for prehospital trauma care worldwide. Spinal immobilization protocols, hemorrhage control hierarchy, burns assessment all sourced here. Not fully open access — cite principles and reference; supplement with open sources. **High confidence — authoritative professional reference.**

**S9. Tactical Emergency Casualty Care (TECC) — NAEMT**
- URL: https://www.naemt.org/education/naemt-education/tecc
- Access: Curriculum overview public; training materials through NAEMT
- Annotation: Civilian adaptation of TCCC; three-phase model (Direct Threat, Indirect Threat, Evacuation Care). Most current civilian trauma framework bridging military TCCC and urban emergency medicine. Relevant for wound packing, tourniquet protocols. **High confidence — current civilian standard.**

**S10. Herbal First Aid Wound Care — 7Song**
- URL: https://7song.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Herbal-First-Aid-Wound-Care.pdf
- Access: Freely available PDF
- Annotation: Herbalist/paramedic combines wound physiology with plant-based wound support. Covers healing stages, herb selection (calendula, yarrow, comfrey, plantain), poultice vs. salve applications. Links to Wave 2 herbal medicine domain. Good secondary resource for botanical wound care integration. **Moderate confidence — practitioner-authored, not peer-reviewed, but aligns with PMC article PMC3495394.**

**S11. Wound care with traditional, complementary and alternative medicine — PMC**
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3495394/
- Access: Open access (PubMed Central)
- Annotation: Peer-reviewed review of evidence for traditional wound care approaches including plant-based treatments. Provides academic grounding for herbal wound care integration. **High confidence — peer-reviewed.**

## Zone 3: Environmental Emergencies

**S12. WMS Clinical Practice Guidelines: Hypothermia (2019 Update)**
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1016/j.wem.2019.10.002
- PDF accessible: https://www.wildmedcenter.com/uploads/5/9/8/2/5982510/wms_hypothermia_2019.pdf
- Access: PDF freely accessible via wildmedcenter.com; journal article may require login
- Annotation: Expert panel evidence-based guidelines for out-of-hospital evaluation and treatment of accidental hypothermia. Covers staging, field rewarming methods, after-drop, evacuation criteria. Definitive source for Zone 3 hypothermia content. **High confidence — peer-reviewed WMS guidelines.**

**S13. WMS Clinical Practice Guidelines: Frostbite Prevention and Treatment (2024 Update)**
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10806032231222359
- PDF direct: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/10806032231222359
- Access: Open access PDF (2024)
- Annotation: Most current WMS frostbite guidelines. Addresses tissue oxygenation, field rewarming when re-freeze risk exists, thrombolytics (hospital setting), field care limits. Essential for Zone 3 frostbite section — particularly the re-freeze contraindication question (Research Question 8). **High confidence — open-access peer-reviewed.**

**S14. WMS Clinical Practice Guidelines: Acute Altitude Illness (2024 Update)**
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1016/j.wem.2023.05.013
- Access: Journal; abstract freely accessible
- Annotation: Current AMS/HACE/HAPE prevention, diagnosis, and treatment guidelines. Less relevant for Zone 5 Midwest context (low altitude) but important for completeness and any mountain-adjacent readers. Include as reference with Zone 3 applicability note. **High confidence — peer-reviewed, note regional relevance limitation.**

**S15. ORS Formulation — WHO Oral Rehydration Therapy**
- URL: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/diarrhoeal-disease (WHO Factsheet)
- Also: WHO ORS technical bulletin
- Access: Open access
- Annotation: WHO standard ORS formula: 1 liter water + 6 teaspoons sugar + 0.5 teaspoon salt. Improvised preparation from food-storage ingredients directly applicable to Zone 3 dehydration and Zone 4 GI illness management. **High confidence — WHO primary source.**

**S16. Lightning Strike Resuscitation Paradox — Wilderness Medical Society**
- URL: Published in Wilderness & Environmental Medicine journal (WMS)
- Reference: Cooper MA, Andrews CJ (2019) Lightning Injuries. In: Auerbach's Wilderness Medicine 7th ed
- Access: Chapter reference; key principles in open literature
- Annotation: Establishes the key counter-intuitive principle: lightning victims who appear dead should be resuscitated first (they are most likely to survive). Important for Zone 3 lightning section accuracy. **High confidence — established WMS position.**

## Zone 4: Illness Assessment and Management

**S17. Merck Manual Consumer Edition — Free Online**
- URL: https://www.merckmanuals.com/home
- Access: Free online, comprehensive
- Annotation: Gold-standard consumer medical reference. Covers diagnosis, symptoms, treatment for all major conditions at 8th-grade reading level. Primary reference for Zone 4 illness identification and management content. Appendicitis, UTI, respiratory illness, fever all well-covered. **High confidence — authoritative free consumer reference, 1899 origin.**

**S18. Where There Is No Doctor — David Werner / Hesperian Health Guides**
- URL: https://archive.org/details/wherethereisnodo0000wern (Internet Archive, free)
- Direct PDF: https://ia601902.us.archive.org/24/items/WhereThereIsNoDoctor-English-DavidWerner/14.DavidWerner-WhereThereIsNoDoctor.pdf
- Access: Free PDF, Internet Archive; Hesperian Open Copyright policy
- Annotation: WHO-recognized most widely-used healthcare manual in the world; translated into 100+ languages; designed for community health workers without professional training. Direct content overlap with all Zone 4 topics. Practical, illustrated, non-clinical framing ideal for target audience. **High confidence — definitive lay-provider reference.**

**S19. Where Women Have No Doctor — Hesperian Health Guides**
- URL: https://en.hesperian.org/hhg/Where_Women_Have_No_Doctor (online chapters)
- Store: https://store.hesperian.org/products/where-women-have-no-doctor
- Access: Chapters online; Open Copyright policy; chapters downloadable
- Annotation: Hesperian guide addressing women's health in resource-limited settings. Key source for childbirth, obstetric emergencies, and gender-specific health content. Open Copyright compatible with CC-BY-4.0 project requirements. **High confidence — complementary Hesperian resource.**

**S20. Addressing Dental Emergencies — American Dental Association**
- URL: https://www.ada.org/resources/community-initiatives/action-for-dental-health/emergency-department-referrals
- Also: JADA emergency dental protocols
- Access: Public
- Annotation: ADA guidance on dental emergencies including avulsed tooth protocols, abscess recognition, referral criteria. Key for Zone 4 dental section — provides authoritative basis for field protocols. **High confidence — authoritative professional organization.**

**S21. Emergency Dental Kit for Disasters — Primal Survivor**
- URL: https://www.primalsurvivor.net/preppers-emergency-dental-kit-checklist/
- Access: Free
- Annotation: Practitioner-reviewed summary of emergency dental supplies and protocols. Covers temporary crown cement, dental mirror, pain management options. Good practical resource for material recommendations; cross-verify key claims with ADA sources. **Moderate confidence — practitioner site, not peer-reviewed; use as supplement.**

**S22. COVID-19 Self-Care Dental Pain Management — PMC**
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9921035/
- Access: Open access
- Annotation: Cross-sectional study on self-care methods for acute dental pain when professional care unavailable. Validates some improvised approaches; useful for Zone 4 dental section evidence base. **Moderate-high confidence — peer-reviewed, specific context but generalizable principles.**

## Zone 5: Chronic Condition Management

**S23. WHO Model List of Essential Medicines — 23rd List (2023)**
- URL: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/WHO-MHP-HPS-EML-2023.02
- IRIS PDF: https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/371090/WHO-MHP-HPS-EML-2023.02-eng.pdf
- Access: Open access, free PDF
- Annotation: 430+ medicines for primary care; framework for strategic household stockpiling recommendations. Essential for Zone 5 essential medicines discussion. Covers antibiotics (amoxicillin, doxycycline, metronidazole), analgesics, antihypertensives, insulin, bronchodilators. **High confidence — WHO definitive reference.**

**S24. Insulin Stability at Room Temperature — Peer-Reviewed Evidence**
- Primary: Vimalavathini R, Gitanjali B (2009). "Effect of temperature on the potency & pharmacological action of insulin." Indian J Med Res. 130(2):166-9. PMID: 19797822
- Also: Woods RJ et al (2015). "The stability of insulin in human serum." PMC4632805
- Access: PubMed accessible; full text varies
- Annotation: Documents insulin stability beyond stated manufacturer limits. Most formulations retain 80%+ potency for 28-90+ days at room temperature (varies by type). Critical for Zone 5 diabetes management — most critical life-sustaining medication. **High confidence — peer-reviewed primary literature; key research question 14.**

**S25. Medication Storage Guidelines — FDA**
- URL: https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-information-consumers/where-and-how-store-your-medicines
- Access: Free, public domain
- Annotation: FDA guidance on proper medication storage conditions. Temperature, humidity, light requirements for common drug categories. Essential for Zone 5 medical supply management section. **High confidence — primary regulatory source.**

**S26. Managing Chronic Conditions in Resource-Constrained Settings — FHI 360**
- URL: https://www.fhi360.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Managing-Chronic-Conditions-Optimizing-Health-Throughout-Life.pdf
- Access: Free PDF
- Annotation: Evidence-based framework for chronic disease management in settings with limited resources. Covers diabetes, hypertension, respiratory conditions. 2024 publication — current. Useful for Zone 5 non-pharmacologic management protocols. **High confidence — major development organization, evidence-based.**

**S27. ReliOn Insulin (Walmart) — Product and Access Information**
- Reference: Multiple news/consumer coverage; no single authoritative URL
- Key source: American Diabetes Association coverage of OTC insulin access
- Annotation: Documents affordable OTC insulin access at Walmart; NPH insulin as fallback for Type 1 and Type 2 diabetics. Important practical recommendation for Zone 5 supply disruption planning. **Moderate confidence — widely documented but verify current availability and pricing at publication.**

## Zone 6: Psychological First Aid

**S28. SAMHSA Psychological First Aid Field Operations Guide (2nd ed.)**
- URL: https://www.samhsa.gov/resource/dbhis/psychological-first-aid-field-operations-guide-second-ed
- Access: Free download from SAMHSA
- Annotation: Primary US government resource for PFA. 8-action framework; lay-provider adaptable; multilingual. Available in Spanish, Chinese, Japanese, Norwegian. Direct source for Zone 6 PFA framework content. **High confidence — federal agency primary source.**

**S29. WHO Psychological First Aid: Guide for Field Workers**
- URL: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241548205
- Access: Free PDF via WHO
- Annotation: WHO companion to SAMHSA PFA; international framing; covers Look, Listen, Link framework. Complementary to SAMHSA guide; international context helpful. **High confidence — WHO primary source.**

**S30. Development of PFA Guidelines for Disaster Survivors — PMC**
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8535812/
- Access: Open access
- Annotation: Peer-reviewed examination of PFA evidence base and guideline development. Addresses Research Question 17 (evidence base for 8-core-action framework). **High confidence — peer-reviewed.**

**S31. Community Resilience Research — SAMHSA DTAC**
- URL: https://www.samhsa.gov/sites/default/files/dtac-disaster-behavioral-health-interventions-inventory.pdf
- Access: Free PDF
- Annotation: SAMHSA inventory of disaster behavioral health interventions; covers community resilience factors, caregiver fatigue, substance use escalation. Useful for Zone 6 community resilience section. **High confidence — federal agency resource.**

**S32. Grief and Bereavement Models — Bonanno et al.**
- Reference: Bonanno GA (2004). "Loss, trauma, and human resilience." American Psychologist. 59(1):20-28
- Access: PubMed PMID 14736317; widely cited; full text accessible via university libraries
- Annotation: Documents the resilience trajectory (majority of people) vs. chronic grief and recovery trajectories. Challenges older stage-based grief models. Key for Research Question 20 (grief models evidence base). **High confidence — highly cited peer-reviewed.**
