## Three Outcome Scenarios

**Trigger**: 
- Overall open rate ≥15% (baseline: Phase 1 averaged 12-14%)
- Reply count ≥3 across all domains combined
- Gist views ≥50 unique (or ≥20% of contacts + assumed non-openers)
- Hostile/unsubscribe <5% of replies

**Interpretation**: Constituencies receptive, high signal strength, ready for Phase 2 expansion

**Automatic Routing Path**:
1. **Activate Domains 51 + 59 immediately** (full researcher allocation)
   - Domain 59: Senate Finance CTC angle, 3-send express path (AFL-CIO, CBPP, NWLC)
   - Domain 51: CA Fair Elections Act angle, 2-send Tier 1 (CLC, Issue One)
   - Both: 2-3 day stagger to separate outreach windows
   - Tier 2 follow-up (post-Day-14): conditional on STRONG replies to Tier 1

2. **Reallocation decision**: 
   - Domain 49-50 (Environmental Justice + LGBTQ+): OK to proceed July 1-15 research (was contingent on Phase 1 strength)
   - Domain 48 (Criminal Justice): OK to proceed July 1 (secondary priority, but unblocked by engagement)
   - Domain 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal): defer to August per original plan (not impacted by Phase 1)

3. **Resource allocation**:
   - 40 researcher-hours June 18-25 (Domain 59 express + 51 Tier 1)
   - 80 researcher-hours July 1-15 (49-50 parallel + 48 independent path)
   - 50 researcher-hours July 20-31 (57 pre-production)
   - Total June-July capacity: 170 hours (achievable)

4. **Communication**:
   - Day 8 (June 18 EOD): Send Phase 2 activation email to all Domain 59/51 Tier 1 contacts with immediate timeline
   - Day 14 (June 24): First engagement check-in (replies received? ready for Tier 2?)
   - Day 21 (July 1): Phase 2 formal kickoff memo

5. **Success metrics**:
   - Day 14 (June 24): Domain 59 Tier 1 50%+ committed; Domain 51 Tier 1 40%+ committed
   - Day 30 (July 7): First draft Phase 2 Domain 59 completed
   - Day 42 (July 19): First draft Phase 2 Domain 51 completed
   - Day 60 (August 6): Domain 49-50 parallel research complete

**Confidence**: 92% (Phase 1 engagement tracking proved predictive; Tier structure validated)

### SCENARIO B: MODERATE Engagement

**Trigger**:
- Overall open rate 8-14% (below STRONG, above baseline expectation)
- Reply count 1-2 across all domains
- Gist views 20-49 unique
- Hostile <5% of replies

**Interpretation**: Mixed signal. Some constituencies engaged, others distant. Selective activation recommended.

**Automatic Routing Path**:

**Option B1 — Selective Activation (Recommended)**:
1. **Activate Domain 59 ONLY** (highest-urgency Senate Finance window)
   - Execute 3-send express path (AFL-CIO, CBPP, NWLC) immediately
   - 2-week research sprint (June 18-30)
   - Defer Domains 51/48 to July (post-July-1 activation decision)

2. **Defer other domains**:
   - Domain 51 (CA Fair Elections): Defer to July 1-20 research (July 1 deadline has 14-day buffer)
   - Domain 48 (Criminal Justice): Defer to July 5-25 (independent timeline)
   - Domain 49-50 (EJ+LGBTQ+): Defer to July 1+ (original plan)
   - Domain 57 (Multilateral): Defer to August (no urgency)

3. **Resource allocation** (conservative):
   - 30 researcher-hours June 18-30 (Domain 59 express only)
   - 40 researcher-hours July 1-15 (51 + 48 + 49-50 staggered)
   - 30 researcher-hours July 20-31 (57 pre-production)
   - Total: 100 hours (lower intensity, reduces risk)

4. **Communication**:
   - Day 8 (June 18 EOD): Send Domain 59 express activation to 3 contacts + brief explanation of selective path
   - Day 14 (June 24): Re-engagement survey with Phase 2 candidates (are they ready to commit now?)
   - Day 30 (July 7): Based on re-engagement, activate remaining domains with adjusted timeline

5. **Success metrics**:
   - Day 14 (June 24): Domain 59 Tier 1 60%+ committed to express path
   - Day 28 (July 5): Domain 59 first draft complete
   - Day 45 (July 22): Domain 51 first draft complete (if re-engagement STRONG)
   - Day 60 (August 6): Remaining domains on track

**Option B2 — Conservative Hold**:
If reply count = 0 and only modest opens (8-10%):
1. Do not activate any Phase 2 domains immediately
2. Run diagnostic email June 20-21 asking for feedback on barriers to engagement
3. Activate Phase 2 based on diagnostic results (expected July 5-10)
4. This adds 1-2 week delay but prevents wasted researcher time on unresponsive paths

**Recommendation**: Option B1 (selective activation) is preferred unless diagnostic email shows consistent "too busy" or "not interested" feedback.

**Confidence**: 85% (moderate scenarios have higher variance; re-engagement survey crucial for path clarity)

### SCENARIO C: WEAK Engagement

**Trigger**:
- Overall open rate <8%
- Reply count = 0 (no responses from any contact)
- Gist views <20 unique
- Or: Hostile/unsubscribe >10% of delivery

**Interpretation**: Constituencies unresponsive. Phase 1 did not register. Diagnostic required before Phase 2.

**Automatic Routing Path**:

1. **Do NOT activate any Phase 2 domains immediately** 
   - Risk: investing researcher hours on uninterested constituencies = wasted capacity

2. **Run diagnostic investigation** (June 18-22):
   - Send 1 follow-up email to 3-5 key contacts per domain asking: "Barriers to engagement? Timing wrong? Content not compelling? Other constraints?"
   - Expected response window: 24-48 hours
   - Goal: Identify if problem is timing (everyone busy), messaging (wrong angle), or genuine disinterest

3. **Decision branch based on diagnostic** (by June 23):
   - **Path C1 — Timing mismatch**: "We're interested but swamped June; can we defer to July?"
     → Activate Phase 2 July 1+ with adjusted timeline (same domains, later start)
   - **Path C2 — Messaging mismatch**: "Content angle doesn't match our priorities"
     → Reframe Phase 2 research with user guidance (new angle, different constituencies)
   - **Path C3 — Genuine disinterest**: "Not a priority for our org"
     → Shift to alternative Phase 2 candidates (Domains 47, 52, 53 backup list)
   - **Path C4 — Contact list problem**: "These people don't work here / email wrong"
     → Rebuild contact list with corrected emails, retry Phase 1 Wave 2 with new contacts

4. **Resource allocation** (minimal immediate):
   - 5-10 researcher-hours (diagnostic emails + analysis) June 18-23
   - 40-60 researcher-hours (revised Phase 2 based on diagnostic findings) June 25-July 31
   - Total: 50-70 hours (delayed but preserved)

5. **Communication**:
   - Day 1 (June 18): Analysis diagnosis: "Phase 1 engagement metric shows WEAK signal. Running diagnostic before Phase 2 activation."
   - Day 5 (June 22): Diagnostic emails sent to key contacts
   - Day 8 (June 25): Diagnostic results reported + Phase 2 path recommendation
   - Day 15 (July 2): Phase 2 activation with revised approach (pending diagnostic findings)

6. **Success metrics** (revised):
   - Day 5 diagnostic response rate >40% (validates list/timing)
   - Day 30 (July 7): Phase 2 path clarified and agreed with user
   - Day 60 (August 6): Phase 2 research on new path 50%+ complete

**Confidence**: 78% (weak scenarios have highest uncertainty; diagnostic adds 1-week lead time but provides clarity)

## Contingency Routing (Edge Cases)

### If Wave 1-2 execution DELAYED (not sent by June 17 midnight)
- Delay Day 7 checkpoint to June 18-19 (wait 1 day for full Wave 1-2 to execute)
- Collect engagement metrics June 18-19
- Checkpoint analysis June 19-20
- Phase 2 activation June 20+ (same paths, 1-2 day delay)
- No impact to July deadlines (still 10+ days buffer)

### If specific domain has STRONG replies but others WEAK
- Activate high-engagement domain immediately (don't wait for group consensus)
- Example: Domain 59 gets 5 replies but Domain 51 gets 0 → Start Domain 59 June 18, hold Domain 51 for diagnostic
- Allows parallel research on ready domains while diagnosing others

### If user wants to override Day 7 decision
- User can post to INBOX.md: "Proceed with Phase 2 regardless of engagement metrics"
- Orchestrator will activate all staged domains immediately
- Risk: researcher time on unresponsive constituencies (user accepts this risk)
