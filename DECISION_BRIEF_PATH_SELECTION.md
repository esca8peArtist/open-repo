# Decision Brief: Resistance-Research Phase 1 Distribution Path
**Date**: May 15, 2026 | **Deadline**: TODAY (May 15) | **Execution window**: May 15-17 (if approved)

---

## Executive Summary

**Three execution paths for Phase 1 democratic-renewal proposal distribution.**

All paths use the same 40-domain research framework, templates, and contact lists. The difference is in **scope and timing**:

| Path | Scope | Timeline | Outcome | Effort |
|------|-------|----------|---------|--------|
| **Path A** | 40 domains immediately | May 15-17 (Batch 1) → June 15+ (Tier 2) | Comprehensive, slower movement adoption | 3-4 hours |
| **Path A+37 Hybrid** | 39 domains now + Domain 37 timed to election groups | May 15-17 (Batch 1) → June 15 (add Domain 37) | Maximum impact, captures election urgency | 3-4 hours (Wave 1) + 1 hr (Domain 37 timing) |
| **Path B** | Continue Phase 2 updates before distribution | June 1+ | Most authoritative, slower launch, risks Nov 3 election timing | 2-3 weeks prep |

---

## Path Details

### Path A: Full Immediate Distribution
**Proposal**: Send all 40 domains to all contact tiers (Batch 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3) starting May 15.

**What you do**:
1. **May 15 (3-4 hours)**: Batch 1 send — 25 highest-leverage contacts (senators, think tanks, law schools)
2. **May 17-18**: Batch 2 — academic/media/legal audiences
3. **May 21+**: Batch 3 — state networks, faith coalitions, civil rights orgs
4. **June 15+**: Tier 2 (broader adoption, monthly cadence)

**Pros**:
- Maximum timeliness for November 3 midterm election cycle
- Captures May urgency window (government capacity still high pre-summer)
- All 40 domains out before summer focus shifts
- General audiences get full 40-domain framework

**Cons**:
- Election protection organizations get Domain 37 too early (no election-specific framing)
- Domain 37 (federal interference) doesn't have specialized messaging for its highest-value audience
- Harder to track which messaging resonates with which audiences

**Best for**: Immediate, comprehensive dissemination to broad audiences

---

### Path A+37 Hybrid (RECOMMENDED)
**Proposal**: Send 39 domains to Batch 1 now (May 15), then send Domain 37 separately with election-specific messaging to election protection organizations (June 10-15).

**What you do**:
1. **May 15 (3-4 hours)**: Batch 1 send — all 39 domains (excluding Domain 37) to 25 contacts
   - Templates already filled for all 39
   - Domain 37 simply omitted from multi-domain package
2. **May 17-18**: Batch 2 send (academic/media, 39 domains)
3. **May 21+**: Batch 3 send (state networks, etc., 39 domains)
4. **June 10-15 (1 hour)**: Domain 37 + election-specific brief to 12 election protection orgs
   - Specialized subject line and messaging
   - Emphasizes federal interference during 2026 midterms (11 appointees, ICE-at-polls threat, mail voting ban EO)
   - Positions as 2026-specific actionable intelligence for election monitoring groups

**Pros**:
- **Captures two distinct messaging strategies**: Democracy-renewal framework for broad audiences, election-specific threat assessment for specialized audiences
- **Higher resonance with election protection community** (they care about November 2026, not general democratic renewal)
- **Still immediate for general audiences** (May 15-21)
- **Measurable outcome differentiation** (track adoption by audience type)
- **Preserves strategic sequencing** (Domain 37 gets its own launch moment with maximum context)

**Cons**:
- Requires two execution phases (May 15-21, June 10-15)
- One additional send wave (1 hour on June 10-15)

**Best for**: Strategic sequencing across different audience types; maximum impact where it matters most (election protection)

---

### Path B: Continue Phase 2 Updates Before Distribution
**Proposal**: Continue Phase 2 research expansion (Domains 57-60, 90-110 additional hours) before distribution. Target June 1 distribution with 44+ domain framework.

**What you do**:
1. **May 16 - May 31**: Phase 1 remains dormant; focus resources on Phase 2 research
2. **June 1-14**: Complete Phase 2 research (estimate 80-90 hours for 3-4 domains)
3. **June 15+**: Distribution with 44+ domain framework instead of 40

**Pros**:
- More authoritative, comprehensive proposal
- Time for peer review integration
- Higher likelihood of academic/institutional adoption

**Cons**:
- **Delays all distribution by 3+ weeks** (loses May urgency window)
- Election protection organizations get information in mid-June (6 months before election, less timely)
- Harder to track which parts of framework resonate (all delivered together in larger package)
- **Risks November 3 window for election-specific content** (Domain 37 framing depends on 2026 specific threats)
- Uses 90-110 research hours that could go toward Phase 2 production roadmap execution

**Best for**: Organizations prioritizing completeness over timeliness

## Recommendation

**Path A+37 Hybrid** provides:
- ✅ **Immediate distribution** (May 15) to capture current political moment
- ✅ **Election-specific positioning** (June 10-15 to election organizations)
- ✅ **Measurable audience segmentation** (track outcomes by audience type)
- ✅ **Preserved strategic reserve** (can launch Domain 37 at optimal moment)
- ✅ **Minimal additional effort** (1 extra hour on June 10-15)

**This captures both:**
1. **Democratic renewal argument** for broad audience (law schools, think tanks, labor unions)
2. **Election protection urgency** for specialized audience (election monitoring orgs, voting rights groups)

**No strategic value to Path B** given that:
- Phase 2 expansion (Domains 57-60) is already outlined and sequenced for June 16+ production
- Delaying distribution sacrifices May momentum and election cycle urgency
- Phase 1 + Phase 2 parallel execution is already planned (Phase 1 distribution doesn't block Phase 2 research)
