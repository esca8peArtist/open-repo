# Phase 5 Wave 2 Recruitment Communication Templates
## Community-Scale Domains — Five Ready-to-Send Email Templates

### How to Use These Templates

1. **Copy the template** (entire text block)
2. **Replace bracketed fields** `[LIKE_THIS]` with author-specific information
3. **Customize the hook** with domain-specific expertise or referral details
4. **Send immediately** — all templates are designed for 2–3 minute personalization before send
5. **Log each send** in the author tracking table (see Section 7)

**Key principle**: Lead with impact + credibility; make the ask clear and specific; provide context without overwhelming; make accepting easy.

**Timing expectations**:
- **Cold outreach**: June 14 08:00–12:00 UTC (independent candidates, no Wave 1 connection)
- **Warm introductions**: June 14 14:30–18:00 UTC (Wave 1 authors, referrals, known connections) — sent immediately post-matching
- **Follow-ups**: June 15 21:00 UTC (48h after initial contact, no response)
- **Acceptances**: June 16–17 (confirming participation, domain assignment, onboarding)
- **Declines**: June 16–17 (respectful no-thank-you, door open for future)

## Section 6: Personalization Guide

For each template, customize these fields to match the specific candidate:

### For Template 1 (Cold Outreach)

- `[Author Name]`: Full name or first name if you have informal relationship
- `[SPECIFIC WORK]`: Reference their published work, organization, or expertise area
  - Examples: "10+ years of food sovereignty work with La Via Campesina" / "research on mesh networks in rural communities" / "community mediation training and practice"
- `[Domain Name]`: The domain they're being invited to contribute to
  - Governance & Decision-Making
  - Food Systems & Supply Chain
  - Information Infrastructure
  - Security & Defense
  - Scaling Pathways & Thresholds

### For Template 2 (Warm Introduction)

- `[Referrer Name]`: Who referred this candidate? E.g., "Alex Chen" or "the Food Sovereignty Network community"
- `[SPECIFIC_QUALITY]`: What did the referrer say? E.g., "ability to make complex research accessible" / "deep roots in the cooperative movement"
- `[Domain Name]`: The assigned domain
- `[Domain description]`: 1–2 sentence description of the domain focus
- `[THEORETICAL_GROUNDING]`: The theory/framework — e.g., "commons governance principles" / "distributed systems design" / "nonviolent community defense"
- `[LIVED_EXPERIENCE]`: Where they have field experience — e.g., "running a food cooperative" / "facilitating community mesh networks" / "training in restorative justice circles"
- `[ORGANIZATION/ROLE]`: Where they currently work or volunteer

### For Template 3 (Follow-Up)

- `[Author Name]`: First name or full name
- `[Date + time]`: When you sent the original message (e.g., "Thursday, June 14 at 10:00 UTC")
- `[DATE]`: Deadline for response (e.g., "June 17" or "end of day Friday")

### For Template 4 (Acceptance Confirmation)

- `[Author Name]`: First name or full name
- `[Domain Name]`: The assigned domain
- `[1–2 sentence domain problem statement]`: From the Phase 5.1 publication. Examples:
  - **Governance**: "How communities transition from whole-group assembly to delegate councils as they scale past 100 people, and how to maintain accountability at scale."
  - **Food Systems**: "Coordinating distributed food production and supply chains for 50–500 person communities without price signals or centralized planning."
  - **Information Infrastructure**: "Building community communication networks that function without centralized internet, and maintaining information integrity during infrastructure failures."
  - **Security**: "Organizing community safety without professional law enforcement — from conflict de-escalation to inter-community coordination during disruption."
  - **Scaling**: "How communities successfully navigate growth transitions (Dunbar threshold, assembly limits, federation thresholds) and redesign governance before crisis."
- `[DATE]`: When first draft is due (e.g., "July 15")
- `[Your phone]` and `[Your email]`: Your contact info for emergencies

### For Template 5 (Decline)

- `[Author Name]`: First name or full name
- `[Organization]`: Your organization name

## Section 9: Domain-Specific Hooks (Personalization Examples)

Use these examples to craft domain-specific hooks for cold outreach:

### Governance & Decision-Making

**Hook for academics**: "Your research on [Ostrom / polycentric governance / commons institutions] directly applies to the governance transitions communities face at 100–500 person scale."

**Hook for practitioners**: "Your work training sociocracy facilitators has shown exactly what works in real communities — we need that practitioner perspective in our Wave 2 governance guide."

**Hook for organizers**: "You've built delegate councils from scratch — we want to document that experience for communities trying the same transition."

### Food Systems & Supply Chain

**Hook for researchers**: "Your research on food sovereignty and cooperative supply chains is the foundation we're building on — we want you to help translate it for practitioners."

**Hook for farmers/coordinators**: "Running a food cooperative has given you lived experience in supply chain coordination without prices or centralized planning — that's exactly what Wave 2 needs."

**Hook for academics**: "Your publications on food system resilience are foundational; we're writing the practitioner guide. Will you help us translate the research?"

### Information Infrastructure

**Hook for technologists**: "You've built community mesh networks in low-resource contexts — we want to document what actually works for communities trying to build communication resilience."

**Hook for researchers**: "Your work on distributed information systems and digital commons is the framework we're using. We want your input on the practitioner translation."

**Hook for organizers**: "You've organized community network infrastructure from the ground up — that hands-on experience is what makes our guide credible."

### Security & Defense

**Hook for researchers/practitioners**: "Your work on nonviolent community defense and conflict resolution is the foundation for how we're reframing 'security' away from military models toward community safety infrastructure."

**Hook for organizers**: "You've built community defense structures that actually work — we want to document your approach for other communities building the same capacity."

**Hook for mediators**: "You facilitate conflict resolution in communities. We're creating a guide on how communities handle security and defense without professional law enforcement — your expertise is essential."

### Scaling Pathways & Thresholds

**Hook for researchers**: "Your research on cooperative scaling and organizational thresholds is the framework for our guide. We want you to help translate it for community practitioners."

**Hook for practitioners**: "You've scaled a cooperative from 25 people to 200+. We want to document the governance transitions you navigated so other communities can plan for growth."

**Hook for organizers**: "You've lived through multiple governance redesigns as your community grew — that's the real-world knowledge that makes our scaling guide credible."

## Section 10: Crisis Response Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Candidate Accepts, Then Asks for Scope Reduction

**Situation**: Candidate confirms interest but reveals they only have 1–2 hrs/week, not 2–3 hrs/week.

**Response**:
1. Offer scope reduction: 2,500–3,500 words instead of 4,000–5,000
2. Increase checkpoint frequency: T+2, T+5, T+10 instead of T+3, T+7, T+14
3. Pair with peer reviewer for weekly feedback
4. Confirm: "We can make this work with this adjusted scope. Are you good with 2,500 words + weekly feedback?" 

### Scenario 2: Wave 1 Author Declines Wave 2

**Situation**: Wave 1 completer you were counting on declines Wave 2 invitation.

**Response**:
1. Ask why (workload, burnout, competing priorities?)
2. If workload: offer consulting role instead (1 hr/week review, $500 flat fee)
3. If burnout: respect the boundary, thank them, ask if they'd be interested in Wave 3 later
4. Immediately activate backup candidate (next-ranked Tier A/B in that domain)

### Scenario 3: Candidate Withdraws Post-Matching, Pre-Kickoff

**Situation**: June 20, Jordan Williams withdraws from Information Infrastructure (family emergency).

**Response**:
1. Acknowledge, express support, thank them for the notice
2. Within 24h, contact next-ranked Info Infra candidate (Tier A or B)
3. Offer expedited onboarding call (June 21, 30 min catch-up)
4. Confirm: "Can you launch first draft on our original July 15 schedule with accelerated onboarding?"
