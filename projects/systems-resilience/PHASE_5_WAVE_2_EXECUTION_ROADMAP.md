## Section 1: Sequencing Comparison — Two Paths to Phase 5 Completion

The **critical scheduling decision** is not whether to produce all four documents, but in what order. The choice determines: (a) when Phase 5 is complete, (b) resource load distribution, (c) quality risk profile, (d) opportunity for parallel execution.

### Path A: Psychological-First Sequential Production

**Execution timeline**: July 16 – October 15 (13 weeks)

**Rationale**: Psychological support is the foundational layer for the other three documents. Community resilience begins with individual mental health literacy. A household that collapses under stress from psychological factors defeats all other preparations. Psychological support infrastructure is the load-bearing foundation for conflict resolution (de-escalation) and veterinary care (decision-making under stress).

**Production sequence**:

| Phase | Document | Duration | Start | End | Resource |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 2a | Psychological Support Infrastructure | 4.5 weeks | Jul 16 | Aug 12 | 1 agent |
| Wave 2b | Conflict Resolution Deep Dive | 4 weeks | Aug 1 | Sept 1 | 1 agent (overlap with 2a final revision) |
| Wave 2c | Veterinary Care in Crisis Contexts | 4.5 weeks | Aug 15 | Sept 15 | 1 agent |
| Wave 2d | Community Coordination Framework | 4.5 weeks | Sept 15 | Oct 15 | 1 agent |

**Timeline visualization**:
```
Jul 16 ─────────── Aug 12    Psych Support (writing + review)
        Aug 1 ─────── Sept 1     Conflict Resolution (parallel final week)
              Aug 15 ─────── Sept 15  Veterinary Care
                        Sept 15 ──────── Oct 15  Community Framework
```

**Resource requirements**:
- Single agent throughout (no parallel capacity needed)
- Wave 2a: 40–50 hours (Psych Support drafting + review)
- Wave 2b: 35–45 hours (Conflict Resolution drafting + review, with Psych overlap for cross-reference integration)
- Wave 2c: 45–55 hours (Veterinary Care drafting + review, now with Psych Supply Cache section for medication/depression interaction notes)
- Wave 2d: 35–45 hours (Community Framework drafting + review, with all three Tier 2 documents complete as source material)

**Quality implications**:
- **Strength**: Each document is complete before the next begins. Tier 3 (Community Framework) has all prerequisite documents fully drafted and can extract community-scale extensions with confidence.
- **Strength**: Psychological Support first establishes the mental health context that the other three documents reference. Conflict Resolution and Veterinary Care can both cite Psychological Support's evidence base.
- **Weakness**: Total elapsed time (13 weeks) misses the September 1 target by 6 weeks. Wave 2 completes October 15.
- **Risk**: If any single document stalls (source verification bottleneck, SME review delay), the entire schedule slips by the duration of that stall.

**Recommendation**: Use **only if** September 1 completion is not critical and single-agent resource constraint is firm. Produces the highest quality Tier 3 document because all prerequisites are complete.

**Execution timeline**: July 16 – September 10 (8 weeks)

Drafting them simultaneously and coordinating at the de-escalation/facilitation overlap point is more efficient than sequencing them. Once both are in first draft (Aug 10), Veterinary Care and Community Framework can run in parallel. Veterinary Care completes while Community Framework is finishing, so the Community Framework has access to a complete Vet Care document.

## Section 2: Veterinary Care Production Critical Path

Veterinary Care is the most technically demanding Wave 2 document and the one most likely to experience bottlenecks. Here is the production workflow from research completion to live publication.

### Step 1: Research Verification and Source Staging (5–7 days)

**Activities**:
- Verify all 28 staged sources load and are current (USDA/CFSPH URLs active, publication dates correct)
- Flag any sources that have moved or require updates (typical: 1–2 sources per domain)
- Search for Zone 5–specific sources: query "Zone 5 livestock disease" + "goat dystocia ISU" + "Banamine OTC status 2026" to fill any remaining gaps
- Create a working source document with all URLs and quick-reference summaries

**Bottleneck risk**: **Banamine OTC status verification** — this medication was transitioning from OTC to prescription-only in 2024–2025. Must confirm current status before drafting Section 5 (Supply Cache). If status has changed, the document must reflect that change. Time cost if unclear: 30–45 minutes additional research.

**Responsible party**: Author or research support
**Completion deadline**: 7 days into production window

---

### Step 2: Section Outlining and Source Mapping (3–4 days)

**Activities**:
- Read PHASE_5_WAVE_2_EXECUTION_PACKAGE.md Section 3 (Per-Domain Veterinary Care outline)
- Create a 5-section outline matching the planning document:
  1. Zone 5 Livestock Profile and Disease Calendar
  2. Preventive Protocols — Vaccination and Biosecurity
  3. First Response — Injury, Illness, and Obstetric Emergency
  4. Zoonotic Disease Recognition
  5. Household Veterinary Supply Cache and Knowledge System
- For each section, list which sources map to it. Identify any section with <2 Primary sources and flag for additional targeted search.
- Confirm the outline matches the execution package (modifications allowed only if source review contradicts planning, with justification logged).

**Expected output**: Detailed outline with source-to-section mapping and any identified gaps (usually 0–2 gaps per document at this stage).

**Responsible party**: Author
**Completion deadline**: 4 days into production window

### Step 3: Bottleneck Mitigation Research (2–3 days, concurrent with Step 2)

Three bottlenecks were identified in the execution package:

**Bottleneck A — Obstetric Emergency Protocols (Section 3)**:
- Sources are scale-mismatched: MDPI roadmap is cattle-focused, Texas Real Food guide is goat-focused, AVMA guidelines are veterinary-focused.
- Mitigation: Perform a targeted search for "dystocia management goats ISU Extension" and "goat dystocia complications zone 5." Goal: find one additional ISU Extension publication that bridges cattle and goat protocols.
- Time cost: 45 minutes
- Defer synthesis until Step 3 (first draft) — the sources are sufficient, they just require careful synthesis.

**Bottleneck B — OTC Medication Status (Section 5)**:
- Banamine (flunixin meglumine) transitioned from OTC to prescription-only in some formulations in 2024–2025.
- Mitigation: Search "flunixin meglumine OTC injectable livestock 2026" + "Banamine prescription requirement 2026." Call a farm supply store (Tractor Supply, Rural King) in your region and ask explicitly: "Do you currently sell Banamine without a prescription?" Document the answer.
- Time cost: 30 minutes
- If Banamine is now prescription-only, Section 5 must state this clearly and explain the veterinary relationship importance.

**Bottleneck C — Emergency Slaughter Protocols (Section 3)**:
- The document must include humane slaughter guidance for animals suffering from untreatable conditions.
- Mitigation: Verify AVMA Euthanasia Guidelines 2020 are accessible. Confirm USDA FSIS "Home Processing" guidelines exist and address meat safety from sick animals. Confirm that the guidance is clear and does not venture into prescription-only territory.
- Time cost: 30 minutes
- These are established guidance documents — the bottleneck is not source availability but framing responsibility correctly.

**Responsible party**: Author with research support for Banamine status
**Completion deadline**: 7–10 days into production window (concurrent with Step 2)

### Step 4: First Draft Writing (20–25 days)

**Activities**:
- Write each section in full, following the outline from Step 2.
- Use inline citation placeholders (e.g., `[CITE: USDA APHIS Sheep/Goat Disease Portal]`) — do not resolve to numbered citations yet.
- Target word counts per section:
  - Section 1: 1,100–1,250 words
  - Section 2: 1,100–1,250 words
  - Section 3: 1,250–1,400 words (most complex)
  - Section 4: 1,000–1,150 words
  - Section 5: 1,050–1,200 words
  - Introduction + "Most Important Finding": 400–500 words
  - **Total target**: 6,500–7,500 words

**Writing order** (most to least research-intensive):
1. Section 3 (First Response) — requires synthesis of obstetric protocols, triage framework, emergency slaughter
2. Section 4 (Zoonotic Disease) — requires medical accuracy, cross-reference with individual/05-healthcare.md
3. Section 1 (Disease Calendar) — straightforward but Zone 5–specific compilation
4. Section 2 (Vaccination/Biosecurity) — well-sourced, clear from planning document
5. Section 5 (Supply Cache) — largely list-based, lowest prose burden

**Quality gates during drafting**:
- Every empirical claim has at least one source citation (placeholder is fine, but every claim has a placeholder).
- Zone 5–specific framing is present in each section (not just Section 1).
- Interdependencies are cited: cross-references to individual/06-agriculture.md (livestock basics), individual/05-healthcare.md (zoonotic response), household coordination guide (stress decision-making context).
- No free-standing safety claims: emergency procedures cite established sources (AVMA, USDA, CFSPH, extension).

**Responsible party**: Author
**Completion deadline**: 25 days into production window

### Step 5: Citation Resolution and Source Verification (4–5 days)

**Activities**:
- Replace all inline citation placeholders with numbered citations `[1]`, `[2]`, etc.
- Create a numbered source list at the end of the document (format: `[1] USDA APHIS, "Title," date, https://url.`).
- Verify every URL loads and the content matches the citation (broken links are immediate rejections).
- Confirm citation count meets target: **28–35 citations total**, with minimum **6 Primary sources from government agencies** (USDA, CFSPH, CDC, extension).
- If citation count is below 28, add missing sources. Identify gaps: if Section 3 has only 2 citations and 1,300 words, likely needs 1–2 more. If Section 5 (supply cache) has 8 listings and no citations, that is expected (supply cache is list-based).

**Typical workflow**:
- Count current citations across all sections (if below 28, note which sections are under-cited)
- For under-cited sections, identify the 1–3 most important claims without citations
- Find appropriate sources and add them
- Verify final citation list against the source staging in PHASE_5_WAVE_2_EXECUTION_PACKAGE.md (should use sources from the staged list, not novel sources unless the staged list has a gap)

**Responsible party**: Author with research support
**Completion deadline**: 30 days into production window

**Activities**:

  - Does it cross-reference individual/05-healthcare.md for zoonotic response and infection management?
  - Does it reference the household coordination guide for stress decision-making context?
  - Does it set up Community Framework expectations (does Section 3 mention a community veterinary cooperative, or does that forward-reference belong in Community Framework)?

- Read the "Most Important Finding" paragraph. Does it lead with the most substantive insight, or is it just a summary of contents? (Good MIF: "The rural vet shortage is structural, not temporary, which reframes household veterinary literacy from luxury to load-bearing infrastructure." Bad MIF: "This document covers Zone 5 livestock disease management.")
- Flag any sections that feel incomplete or under-sourced for SME review.

**Responsible party**: Author with project lead review
**Completion deadline**: 33 days into production window

  - **SME 2 — Veterinary perspective**: Large-animal veterinarian or extension agent (can be remote/phone consultation). - **Priority 2** (should change): Unclear protocols, missing Zone 5–specific context, under-sourced claims
- **Priority 3** (consider): Suggestions for restructuring, style improvements, example additions

**Responsible party**: Author with SME outreach
**Completion deadline**: 38–40 days into production window

**Conflict Resolution Deep Dive**:
- **Individual scale**: Knowledge individual (NVC speaker, de-escalation facilitator)
- **Household scale**: Conflict resolution protocols, NVC as household facilitation language, escalation thresholds, restorative circles at household scale
- **Zone 5 specificity**: Rural household conflict (land boundaries, labor disputes, resource sharing) handled before external mediation is possible
- **Forward to Community**: Inter-household mediation panel, escalation from household to community facilitators

**Community Coordination Framework** (Tier 3):

## Section 10: Contingency Decision Trees

Production rarely runs exactly as planned. When roadblocks occur, here is the decision framework:

### Contingency 1: Key SME Becomes Unavailable Mid-Review

**Trigger**: SME confirms review availability, then becomes unavailable when draft arrives.

**Decision point**: Can the review proceed with 1 SME instead of 2–3?

**If yes** (recommended if you have at least 1 SME still available):
- Proceed with 1 SME feedback
- Flag the document as "single-SME reviewed" in the YAML frontmatter
- Plan post-publication errata collection for the missing SME perspectives

**If no** (you have 0 SMEs available):
- Escalate to project lead
- Delay publication 1–2 weeks to recruit replacement SME
- Note: this is why SME recruitment happens by June 20 — to avoid this contingency in-production

**Timeline impact**: +5–10 days if replacement SME must be recruited and scheduled.

---

### Contingency 2: Emergent Research Gap During Production

**Trigger**: During drafting, you discover a source that contradicts the planning outline, or a section requires research that wasn't in the staged sources.

**Decision point**: How significant is the gap?

**If small gap** (<2 days research to fill):
- Author executes targeted search immediately
- Continue drafting other sections while research is proceeding
- Incorporate new findings into revision pass

**If moderate gap** (2–5 days research):
- Pause drafting that section
- Execute research with research support
- Flag the section for extended review in SME review stage
- Adjust timeline: +3–5 days

**If large gap** (>5 days research, major outline change):
- Escalate to project lead
- Decision: (a) Defer the problematic section to Wave 3 revision pass, or (b) Extend the production timeline by the research duration
- Adjust timeline: +5–14 days depending on decision

---

### Contingency 3: Citation Count Falls Short of Minimum

**Trigger**: During citation resolution, total citations are <25 (minimum threshold).

**Decision point**: Where are citations missing?

**If 1–2 sections are under-cited** (e.g., supply cache section has no citations because it's list-based):
- Accept the distribution: some sections are citation-light by design
- Verify that all empirical claims have citations, even if citation density is lower in certain sections

**If multiple sections are under-cited**:
- Add sources to the 3–5 most under-cited sections (identify the 1–2 most important claims per section without citations)
- Execute targeted searches to find appropriate sources
- Add sources to reach minimum threshold of 25 total

**If minimum cannot be reached even with targeted searches**:
- Escalate to project lead
- Decision: Accept the document with <25 citations (flag as "citation-light" in frontmatter), or defer publication pending additional sourcing
- This is rare if research staging was thorough, but possible for domains with limited published literature

---

### Contingency 4: Production Stalls (Author Unavailability)

**Trigger**: Primary author becomes unavailable mid-production (illness, family emergency, competing priority).

**Decision point**: How much time remains until deadline?

**If >20 days remain**:
- Transition to backup agent (who should have been identified in Gate 2)
- Brief backup agent on progress to date
- Backup agent continues from current point
- Timeline impact: +2–3 days for handoff and context absorption

**If 10–20 days remain**:
- Escalate to project lead
- Decision: (a) Find backup agent and proceed with handoff, (b) Defer the document to Wave 3, or (c) Reduce scope (smaller document, fewer sections)
- Timeline impact: +5–10 days (for handoff) or +30–45 days (if deferral)

**If <10 days remain**:
- Document is moved to Wave 3 revision pass
- Remaining Wave 2 documents continue on schedule
- This is why backup agents should be identified pre-production

---

### Contingency 5: Publication Rate Becomes Unsustainable

**Trigger**: You realize mid-production that managing four documents simultaneously is overwhelming, or SME feedback indicates lower-than-expected quality across multiple drafts.

**Decision point**: Is the wave-2 model unsustainable?

**If feedback is manageable with extended revision**:

- Allow timeline slip of +5–10 days if revision is substantial

**If feedback indicates fundamental issues**:
- Delay publication of weakest document by 2–4 weeks
