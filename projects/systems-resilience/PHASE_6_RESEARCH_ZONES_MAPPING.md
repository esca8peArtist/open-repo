# Phase 6 Research Zones Mapping
## Domains B–F: Economic Foundations through Implementation Feasibility

> **Lead finding**: Phase 3 established governance as the load-bearing wall; Phase 6 domains B–F are the structural systems that sit on that wall. Domain B (Economic Foundations) is the most foundational of the five — without working exchange systems, Domains C and D have nothing to allocate or adjudicate. Domain F (Implementation Feasibility) is the last to be researched but must be designed in parallel, because every other domain's research will accumulate infeasible assumptions if no one is tracking the practical bottleneck layer. Recommended sequencing: B first, C and D in parallel, E after C, F running as a continuous audit thread across all.

---

**Phase 3 integration**: Builds directly on Phase 3 Domain 1 (Community Coordination Infrastructure) supply chain and mutual aid findings; extends Phase 3 timebanking research (hOurworld, Bed-Stuy Strong) into a full economic architecture
**Critical dependency**: Domain B provides the resource allocation substrate that Domains C (governance) and D (judicial independence) operate on. B must be substantially complete before C and D can finalize their resource-allocation and dispute frameworks.
