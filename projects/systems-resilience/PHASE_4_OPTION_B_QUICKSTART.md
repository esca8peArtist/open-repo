# Phase 4 Option B Quick-Start: 14-Day Ramp Strategy

> **Trigger**: User selects Option B on May 31. All 66,442 words publish June 15 in a single unified release.
> **Phase 4 task**: Run governance activation (Days 1-4) in parallel with Phase 5 editorial integration (Days 5-14), so that by June 15 the governance architecture is baked into every Wave 3 section.
> **Outcome**: June 15 release is a fully integrated governance + implementation system, not a document dump.

---

## Strategic Logic

Option B's advantage is coherence: all 66,442 words publish as an integrated system, with cross-references between household-scale and community-scale, between governance and security, between immediate June actions and September crunch. Phase 4's job is to make that coherence real — not just assert it — by testing the governance structure against every Phase 5 Wave 3 scenario before June 15.

The 14-day window has two parallel tracks:
- **Track 1 (Days 1-4)**: Same governance activation as Option A (workshop, household structure, Zone 5 audit)
- **Track 2 (Days 5-14)**: Integration work unique to Option B (authority mapping, timing dependency map, stress testing)

Both tracks must complete by June 14.

### Days 5-14: Integration Work (June 5-14)

**Day 5 (June 5): Domain Reading and Decision Point Extraction**

Each domain coordinator reads their assigned Phase 5 document(s) and extracts decision points — moments in the document where governance authority is invoked or assumed. This is the core integration task.

Decision points to flag:
- "Who decides X?" moments where the Phase 5 document assumes someone has authority
- Thresholds that are undefined ("if food is low" — how low?)
- Authority conflicts between two coordinators
- Escalation gaps (a scenario not covered by the governance structure)

Coordinators work independently. 2-3 hours per coordinator. Flag using this format:

```
DECISION POINT
Document: [Phase 5 filename]
Page/section: [section name]
The document assumes: [e.g., "someone has authority to restrict water consumption"]
Our governance covers this: YES / NO / UNCLEAR
Gap: [if NO or UNCLEAR, what's missing?]
Proposed resolution: [coordinator's suggestion]
```

Deliverable by Day 5 evening: Each coordinator submits 3-5 decision points from their domain.

---

**Day 6 (June 6): Authority Matrix Construction**

Orchestrator / project lead aggregates all decision points into a single authority matrix. Format:

```
GOVERNANCE AUTHORITY MATRIX — OPTION B INTEGRATION
===================================================

| Domain | Decision | Coordinator Authority | Escalation Threshold | Escalation Method |
|--------|----------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Food | Daily distribution | Food coordinator | Consumption rate changes >20% | Household meeting 24h |
| Food | Rationing level | Food + Governance jointly | Ration <3,000 cal/day | Full assembly vote |
| Water | Emergency reserve allocation | Water coordinator | Winter backup failure | Assembly within 24h |
| Energy | Load-shedding | Energy coordinator | Generation <30% | Household meeting |
| Health | Crisis intervention | Health coordinator | Suicide risk identified | Override privacy; report to governance |
| [continue for all domains] |
```

Deliverable by Day 6 evening: Authority matrix draft (all 7 domains covered).

---

**Day 7 (June 7): Authority Matrix Review + Gap Identification**

Full coordinator team reviews authority matrix together (2-hour assembly).

Tasks:
1. Review each row. Is the authority clear? Is the escalation threshold specific and measurable?
2. Identify conflicts: two coordinators claiming authority for the same decision
3. Identify gaps: a decision point with no named authority
4. Identify ambiguities: threshold is vague ("food is running low")

Deliverable by Day 7 evening: Authority matrix annotated with conflicts, gaps, and ambiguities flagged.

---

**Day 8 (June 8): Charter Amendment Pass**

Governance holds amendment session (2-3 hours) to resolve all flagged issues from Day 7.

Protocol for each flagged item:
1. Present the gap to the assembly
2. Propose a resolution (coordinator has prepared this in advance)
3. Vote to adopt or modify the resolution
4. Record the amendment in the charter

Maximum 10 amendments in one session. If more than 10, prioritize life-safety decisions (food rationing, heating allocation, medical authority) and defer lower-priority items to a June 12 follow-up session.

Deliverable by Day 8 evening: Amended charter signed by all household coordinators. Authority matrix finalized.

---

**Day 9 (June 9): Timing Dependency Map**

Create a timeline showing which Phase 4 governance decisions must be made before June 15 to make Wave 3 comprehensible to communities. This prevents "governance decided too late" failures.

Format:

```
JUNE 2026 GOVERNANCE DEPENDENCY TIMELINE
=========================================

June 1 — Charter adopted
June 8 — Authority matrix finalized, charter amended

JUNE 9 DECISIONS NEEDED (must be made before June 15):
  → Food coordinator authority for emergency procurement confirmed: YES/NO?
  → Psychological support coordinator privacy override authority confirmed: YES/NO?
  → Winter heating protocol pre-decided and recorded: YES/NO?

JUNE 12 DECISIONS NEEDED:
  → Energy backup procurement authorized: YES/NO?
  → Water winterization responsibility assigned with deadline: YES/NO?
  → Information infrastructure update schedule confirmed: YES/NO?

JUNE 14 (final check before publication):
  → Does every decision point in Phase 5 documents have a named authority in our charter?
  → Are all thresholds specific and measurable?
  → Have all households signed the amended charter?
```

Deliverable by Day 9 evening: Timing dependency map complete. Any remaining decisions scheduled for Day 10-12.

---

**Day 10 (June 10): Zone 5 Timing Stress Test**

Work through Zone 5-specific scenarios and verify governance can handle each one correctly before June 15 publication.

**Scenario 1 — Spring planting delay**: "It's June 10. The community planting schedule has slipped 2 weeks due to a cold snap. Food coordinator estimates 20% yield reduction for summer crops. Does governance have authority and procedure to activate rationing contingency? Test your charter against this scenario."

**Scenario 2 — Solar cliff**: "It's November 15. Solar production has dropped 75% as predicted. Three households have backup propane but two do not. Energy coordinator needs to make an emergency recommendation. Does governance have authority to mandate energy sharing? What is the escalation path?"

**Scenario 3 — Medical authority conflict**: "A community member shows signs of severe depression. Health coordinator wants to involve them in a care plan, but privacy norms are unclear. Does health coordinator have authority to discuss this with governance? What triggers disclosure?"

Run each scenario through the charter and authority matrix. Document: passes / fails / needs amendment. Fix any failures before Day 13.

Deliverable by Day 10 evening: All three scenarios resolved; any remaining charter gaps identified.

---

**Day 11-12 (June 11-12): Cross-Reference Integration**

This is the Option B-specific editorial task: add governance cross-references into Phase 5 documents so that communities reading the June 15 release know exactly who has authority for each decision point in each document.

Process:
1. For each Phase 5 Wave 3 document (7 documents: food preservation, water systems, livestock care, seed saving, healthcare offline, fuel production, educational governance): identify all decision points already flagged in Day 5
2. Add a one-paragraph "Governance Integration Note" at the top of each document section that contains a decision point: "This section assumes [coordinator] has authority for [decision]. Your community governance charter should specify this in [section of charter]. If not, add it before implementing this guidance."
3. Update the master table of contents to include a "Governance integration" index showing which Phase 5 sections invoke which governance authorities

Time estimate: 4-6 hours across Days 11-12.

Deliverable by Day 12 evening: All 12 Phase 5 documents have governance cross-references. Master TOC updated.
