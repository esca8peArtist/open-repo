# Parallel Execution Feasibility — Phase 6 Impact by Publication Option

> Lead finding: Phase 6 research can start June 1 under all three publication options. Publication
> timing does not gate Phase 6 because the Phase 6 framework, domain outlines, and selection
> matrix are already committed. The meaningful differences between options are (1) Orchestrator
> bandwidth available for Phase 6 review during June, (2) the date by which Wave 2 author
> onboarding competes with Phase 6 setup, and (3) whether the Option C rolling schedule creates
> sustained contention through July that delays Phase 6 Domains E/F/H second-wave launch.

---

## 1. The Core Question: Can Phase 6 Start June 5 Under Option A?

**Yes. Phase 6 research starts June 1, not June 5.**

The Phase 6 groundwork committed May 26–27 includes:
- `phase-6-candidate-community-economic-resilience.md` — Domain A full research outline + source list
- `phase-6-candidate-skills-development.md` — Domain C full research outline + source list
- `phase-6-candidate-governance-scaling.md` — Domain D full research outline + source list
- `phase-6-selection-matrix.md` — 10-domain comparison, decision-ready
- `phase-6-sequencing-recommendation.md` — Week 1–3 launch order with dependency map

All three Week 1 domains (A, C, D) are scoped, sourced, and ready for research agent activation.
The only remaining gate is the user's domain selection decision, due May 31. Once received, the
research agent can be briefed June 1 and begin source library compilation immediately.

**The Wave 1+2 publication editorial work (June 1–5 under Option A) uses the Orchestrator, not
the research agent.** These are different roles. They compete only for Orchestrator coordination
bandwidth (setup decisions, handoffs), not for writing capacity.

## 2. Option A: Phase 6 Start June 5 — Feasibility Analysis

### Resource burn June 5–30 under Option A

Under Option A, the Wave 1+2 editorial peak completes by June 5. After that, the Orchestrator
transitions to monitoring mode (Wave 1+2 reader feedback, passive). This creates a real Phase 6
activation window starting June 5–6.

| Phase 6 activity | Window | Hours | Owner | Conflict with Option A tracks? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Domain A/C/D research agent briefing | June 1–3 | 2–3 hrs | Orchestrator | LOW — batched into June 1 decision session |
| Domain A/C/D source library build | June 1–5 | 6–9 hrs/domain | Research agent | NONE — research agent independent of editorial |
| Domain A/C/D first-draft production | June 3–14 | 15–20 hrs/domain | Research agent | NONE |
| Orchestrator first-draft review (Domains A, C, D) | June 10–15 | 8–12 hrs | Orchestrator | LOW — Option A editorial peak is over by June 5 |
| Domain A/C/D integration edits | June 15–25 | 10–15 hrs | Orchestrator | LOW-MEDIUM — overlaps Wave 3 editorial (June 15–27), different document sets |
| Domains E/F/H second-wave activation decision | June 20–25 | 2–3 hrs (briefing) | Orchestrator | LOW |
| Domains E/F/H first-draft production | June 25–August 15 | 15–20 hrs/domain | Research agent | NONE (steady state) |

**Total Orchestrator Phase 6 hours June 1–30 (Option A)**: 22–33 hours
**Total research agent Phase 6 hours June 1–30 (Option A)**: 63–87 hours (3 domains, parallel)

### Can Wave 2 author onboarding + Phase 6 research happen simultaneously (June 1–9)?

Under Option A, both activate in the same window:
- Author onboarding sprint: June 1–9, Orchestrator load ~12–15 hours
- Phase 6 research agent briefing: June 1–3, Orchestrator load ~2–3 hours

Combined Orchestrator demand June 1–9: 14–18 hours. This is within a 9-day window (roughly
2 hrs/day), compatible with concurrent execution. The author onboarding is the higher-priority
track because it has a harder June 10 start dependency. Phase 6 briefing is lower-stakes — if
it slips to June 3–5, domains A/C/D can still complete first drafts by June 14.

**Verdict: Yes. Both can proceed simultaneously June 1–9. Explicit stagger: author onboarding
gets Orchestrator priority June 1–2; Phase 6 briefing completes June 2–4.**

## 3. Option B: Phase 6 Start Date and July Impact

### What does Option B do to Phase 6 start?

Under Option B, the Orchestrator is absorbed in June 1–14 editorial integration (10–15 hours of
cross-reference work across 12 documents, master TOC, Wave 3 consistency review). This creates
moderate Phase 6 briefing contention.

Phase 6 research agent briefing can still happen June 1 — the research agent is independent.
However, Orchestrator Phase 6 setup decisions (confirm domain selection, resolve any scoping
questions) will be batched into June 2–4 at earliest, because June 1 is consumed by the Option
B editorial kickoff.

**Net effect on Phase 6 under Option B**: 2–3 day delay in Orchestrator setup decisions. Domain
A/C/D first drafts still complete June 7–14 (research agent is not waiting on Orchestrator during
production). Orchestrator first-draft review deferred to June 14–18 (vs. June 10–15 under Option A).

### What does the June 15 publication do to July 10 Wave 2 completion?

Under Option B:
- Author onboarding starts June 8–10 (not June 1; Orchestrator in editorial integration June 1–7)
- Full author production starts June 20 (not June 10)
- Draft delivery: July 20–22 (vs. July 10 under Option A)
- If July 10 is a hard deadline for Wave 2 draft completion, Option B misses it by 10 days

**Does this create a June/July resource crunch?** Yes, in a specific way. Under Option B:
- July 1–20: Author production active (requires Orchestrator feedback cycles)
- July 1–15: Phase 6 Domains A/C/D integration work continues (if not resolved in June)
- July 10–20: Phase 6 Domains E/F/H activation decision window
- July 15–20: Option B post-publication feedback monitoring (reader response to the 66K unified release)

This stacks four concurrent Orchestrator demands in July 1–20 (author feedback + Phase 6 integration
+ Phase 6 second-wave activation + publication monitoring). Under Option A, the author draft delivery
is July 10 and the post-June-5 publication feedback is already absorbed by July.

**Option B July crunch assessment**: REAL but manageable. The crunch is 15–20 days (July 1–20)
with approximately 25–35 hours of Orchestrator demand. This is above the sustainable weekly rate
(8–10 Orchestrator hrs/week) but concentrated rather than sustained.

## 4. Option C: Three Publication Windows and Phase 6 Start Date

### Phase 6 start under Option C

Option C does not delay Phase 6 start — domain A/C/D research can begin June 1 regardless.
However, the rolling publication schedule (Weeks 1–6, May 30–July 4) creates sustained Orchestrator
demand that competes with Phase 6 review and integration across the entire period.

Weekly Option C Orchestrator demand (per cycle):
- Editorial pass + announcement prep: 4–6 hours
- Feedback monitoring + integration decision: 2–3 hours
- Per-week total: 6–9 hours sustained for 5–6 weeks

This 6–9 hours/week is the floor, not the ceiling. In weeks where reader feedback is substantial
or a revision is needed, the demand rises to 10–14 hours. This leaves minimal Orchestrator
headroom for Phase 6 first-draft review cycles, which require 3–4 hours per domain per review.

**Phase 6 Domain A/C/D first-draft review under Option C**: Must compete with ongoing weekly
publication cycles. Best-case stagger: Phase 6 domain reviews scheduled in the 2–3 day gaps between
weekly publication events. This is operationally possible but increases cognitive load and context-
switching costs.

**Phase 6 Domains E/F/H second-wave activation under Option C**: Cannot start until Option C's
rolling schedule either completes (July 4+) or the user accepts that Phase 6 second-wave review
is interleaved with publication cycles. The realistic Phase 6 second-wave start shifts to July
10–15 vs. June 20–25 under Option A.

**Phase 6 completion under Option C**: September 20–October 1 (vs. August 30–September 5 under
Options A or B). The 3-week delay is attributable entirely to the 6-week Option C tail consuming
Orchestrator bandwidth that would otherwise accelerate Phase 6 integration work.

## 6. The Media Pickup Lag Question

The task prompt raises a specific question: if Option A publishes Wave 1+2 June 5, must Phase 6
wait 1–2 weeks for media pickup and feedback integration before starting? Or can it start June 5?

**Answer: Phase 6 does not wait for media pickup. It starts June 1.**

The rationale: Phase 6 research is independent content (Community Economic Resilience, Skills
Development, Governance Scaling). It does not depend on Wave 1+2 reader feedback to scope its
domains — the Phase 6 candidate outlines are already complete and sourced. If Wave 1+2 reader
feedback reveals a gap (e.g., "conflict resolution needs more on inter-community disputes"), that
gap would be documented and incorporated as a Phase 6 addendum or Domain revision in the integration
document, not as a blocker to Phase 6 initial domain production.

The only scenario where Wave 1+2 feedback could affect Phase 6 start is if a major structural
error in the Wave 1+2 corpus required the Orchestrator to redirect from Phase 6 to remediation work.
This risk is LOW — Wave 1+2 has undergone multiple revision cycles and is described as fully polished
in the production decision documentation.

**Media pickup lag (1–2 weeks post-June 5)**: No effect on Phase 6 research start. Potential
light effect on whether feedback is incorporated into the Wave 3 editorial pass (June 15–27).
If pickup is slow and substantive feedback arrives after June 25, Wave 3 cannot incorporate it.
This is an acceptable outcome — Wave 3 documents were written from primary sources and are not
dependent on Wave 1+2 reader feedback for their core validity.
