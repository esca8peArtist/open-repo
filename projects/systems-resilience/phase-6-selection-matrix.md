# Phase 6 Candidate Selection Matrix

This matrix compares three Phase 6 scope options against five key decision criteria. Each candidate is distinct; the matrix helps clarify the trade-offs.

---

## The Three Candidates (Quick Recap)

1. **Community Economic Resilience** (Domain 64): Local currencies, cooperatives, commons-based resource allocation, inter-community trade
2. **Intergenerational Skills Development** (Domain 61): Knowledge transmission, apprenticeship models, learning systems, skill preservation across generations
3. **Governance Scaling + Institutional Adaptation** (Domains 65 + core of 62): Federation governance, conflict resolution, institutional learning, multi-community coordination

---

## Decision Criteria Explained

### 1. **Urgency / Time-Sensitivity**
How time-sensitive is this domain? Can it be deferred 3 months without losing value?

**Scoring scale**: 1 = can defer 6+ months, 5 = must start by June 30 or strategic window closes

**Why it matters**: Some domains have seasonal windows, institutional windows, or opportunity windows that close. Others are evergreen.

---

### 2. **Source Availability / Research Readiness**
How much pre-production work is already done? How available are core sources? What's the research bottleneck risk?

**Scoring scale**: 1 = <50% ready, significant source gaps, 5 = 80%+ ready, all core sources identified

**Why it matters**: Source availability directly affects production speed. A domain that's 85% source-ready produces faster than 60%-ready.

---

### 3. **Leverage / Cross-Domain Multiplier**
How much does this domain amplify the value of Phase 1–5 work? Does it unlock downstream Phase 7+ work? Does it connect across other Phase 6 domains?

**Scoring scale**: 1 = standalone value only, 5 = foundational for Phase 7, bridges multiple Phase 6 domains

**Why it matters**: Some domains are structural (all other work depends on them). Others are valuable but not foundational.

---

### 4. **Scope Fit / Clarity**
How well-defined is the scope? Risk of expansion or mission creep?

**Scoring scale**: 1 = very broad, high expansion risk, 5 = tightly bounded, clear research questions, low expansion risk

**Why it matters**: Unbounded research expands timelines. Clear scope delivers on schedule.

---

### 5. **User Interest Signals / Market Demand**
How much evidence is there that users/communities actually need this guidance? How applicable to the Midwest Zone 5 target audience?

**Scoring scale**: 1 = niche interest, 5 = clear demand signal, high Midwest applicability

**Why it matters**: Demand signal affects impact and post-publication traction.

## Weighted Scoring (If User Wants Data-Driven Recommendation)

Different weighting schemes produce different recommendations. Here are three common weightings:

### Scenario A: "Speed to Delivery" Weighting
Prioritize source readiness (35%) and scope fit (25%), less emphasis on leverage (20%) and demand (20%).

```
Community Economic Resilience:  (4×35) + (4×25) + (4×20) + (4×20) = 160/100 = 4.0/5.0
Intergenerational Skills Dev:   (3×35) + (3×25) + (5×20) + (4×20) = 150/100 = 3.75/5.0
Governance Scaling:             (2×35) + (3×25) + (5×20) + (4×20) = 145/100 = 3.63/5.0

WINNER: Community Economic Resilience
```

**Implication**: If speed to delivery is the priority (finish Phase 6 by August 30 with high confidence), select Community Economic Resilience. Source readiness is 75%, lowest scope complexity, fastest ramp.

### Scenario C: "Phase 6 Completeness" Weighting
Equal weight (20% each): source readiness, leverage, scope fit, demand, + urgency (extra 20% because Phase 6 timing matters).

```
Community Economic Resilience:  (3 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4) × 1.0 = 3.8/5.0 (avg)

Governance Scaling:             (4 + 2 + 5 + 3 + 4) × 1.0 = 3.6/5.0 (avg)

WINNER: Community Economic Resilience
```

**Implication**: Balanced weighting favors Community Economic Resilience (higher source readiness offsets moderate urgency). Governance Scaling is close second (high urgency and leverage, low source readiness).
