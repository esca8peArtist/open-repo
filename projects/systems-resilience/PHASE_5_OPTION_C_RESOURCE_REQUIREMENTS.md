# Phase 5 Option C: Comprehensive Resource Requirements Analysis

**Option Summary**: Rolling release distributes Phase 5 publication across 6 weekly thematic pairs (May 30 → July 4), with Phase 4 community activation distributed across same period in fragmented waves. Phase 6 domains launch asynchronously based on Phase 5 publication arrivals, creating potential coordination gaps.

**Key Resource Difference vs. Other Options**: Option C distributes publication labor across 6 weeks (vs. 5 days for Option A, 15 days for Option B) but creates sustained Orchestrator load (6-9 hours/week for 6 weeks = 36-54 hours total) that competes with Phase 6 author onboarding and early research execution. Higher perceived "low-intensity" work masks lower throughput and longer total duration.

---

## Section 1: Phase 4 Community Activation (June 1+ Fragmented)

### Resource Cluster 1: Governance Workshop Fragmented (June 1 + June 15 + June 29)

**Scope**: Phase 4 cannot activate as a unified 4-hour workshop under Option C (because Phase 5 documents arrive in piecemeal fashion over 5 weeks). Instead, governance setup is distributed:
- **Week 1 (May 30 - June 5)**: Initial governance intro + household structure
- **Week 3 (June 13-19)**: Governance framework refinement based on early Phase 5 documents
- **Week 5 (June 27 - July 3)**: Final governance ratification incorporating all Phase 5 content

**This fragmentation creates governance instability**: Communities must make preliminary decisions (household coordinators, initial authority matrix) before they understand what they're governing (Phase 5 content arrives gradually).

**Labor Hours**:
- Facilitator (3 separate workshops across 5 weeks): 12 hours each x 3 = 36 hours
- Post-workshop synthesis (3 rounds of feedback + refinement): 12 hours
- Governance document updates (3 amendment passes vs. 1 in Option A): 6 hours
- **Total facilitator**: 54 hours (18 hours more than Option A due to fragmentation overhead)

**Community Hours**: 12 hours (4-hour workshop x 3 sessions) + 5 hours feedback/amendment = 17 hours total (vs. 7 hours for Option A single intensive workshop)

**Material Costs**:
- Printing (governance charter + amendment versions): 3 draft versions x 150 copies x 5 pages per version = 2,250 pages = 2 reams ($10) + **$150 total**
- Markers, sticky notes, flip charts (3 workshops): $300-400
- Venue rental (3 separate events): $0-600 (depending on public facility availability)
- **Total Phase 4 governance**: $460-750

**Supply Chain Risk**: MEDIUM. Fragmented governance creates risk that households make decisions June 1-4 based on incomplete information, then must amend those decisions June 15-19 when Phase 5 content arrives. This is structurally inefficient and creates legitimacy risk.

### Resource Cluster 2: Zone 5 Audit Fragmented (June 8-12 + June 22-26)

**Scope**: Instead of single concentrated Zone 5 audit (Option A, 30 hours, 4 days), Option C splits audit into two windows:
- **Week 2 (June 8-12)**: Initial Zone 5 constraint introduction + heating decision (only constraint examined early)
- **Week 4 (June 22-26)**: Full Zone 5 audit after Wave 2 publication (includes agriculture, solar, preservation, water winterization)

**Consequence**: The most critical Zone 5 decision (winter heating fuel allocation) is made in June 8-12 without understanding harvest/preservation capacity (not published until June 30). Communities may lock in a heating protocol that conflicts with the food preservation strategy arriving 18 days later.

**Labor Hours**:
- Initial constraint workshop: 8 hours (facilitator)
- Heating decision protocol development: 6 hours
- Full audit (June 22-26): 20 hours
- Amendment integration: 4 hours
- **Total Zone 5**: 38 hours (vs. 30 for Option A, but lower-quality decision due to fragmentation)

**Material Costs**:
- Zone 5 audit worksheets (two rounds): $167.50 (same as Option A, just administered twice)

**Supply Chain Risk**: HIGH. Fragmented Zone 5 audit creates decision coherence risk that critical resource allocation decisions are made with incomplete information.

**Scope**: Instead of formal governance assembly on June 3 (Option A), Option C requires **weekly community assemblies every Friday June 1-July 4** (6 assemblies) to address each new Phase 5 publication arrival and integrate documents into governance decisions.

**Assembly Schedule**:
- **June 1 (Friday)**: Introduction + household structure (Phase 4 only)
- **June 8 (Friday)**: Discuss Week 1 Phase 5 documents (Implementation Playbook + Microgrids)
- **June 15 (Friday)**: Discuss Week 2 documents (Governance Coordination + Psychological Support)
- **June 22 (Friday)**: Discuss Week 3 documents (Conflict Resolution + Water Systems)
- **June 29 (Friday)**: Discuss Week 4 documents (Energy Systems + Information Infrastructure)
- **July 6 (Friday)**: Discuss Week 5 documents (Food Systems Coordination + Healthcare Offline)

*Note: Week 6 documents arrive July 4; community would review July 11*

**Labor Hours per Assembly**: 2-3 hours facilitator + 2-3 hours community (discussion + decision-making)

**Total Labor**: 12-18 hours facilitator + 12-18 hours community

**Material Costs**: $100 (refreshments x 6 assemblies) + $50 (printing discussion guides)

**Supply Chain Risk**: VERY HIGH. Six separate assemblies create **decision fatigue** and **governance inconsistency**. Early decisions made without context; later amendments feel reactive rather than intentional. This is the critical structural flaw of Option C.

### Resource Cluster 5: Weekly Peer Review (6 cycles)

**Challenge**: Each weekly pair arrives 7 days after the previous pair is published. Peer reviewers must rotate through 6 documents in rapid succession, creating:
- Risk of review fatigue (reviewers reviewing 2 documents/week for 6 weeks)
- Difficulty recruiting reviewers (asking for 6-week commitment is harder than 2-week commitment)
- Cross-document review coordination (document 2 references document 1; peer reviewers must hold full corpus in mind)

**Peer Review Model for Option C**:
- **Dedicated review pool** (5-7 reviewers per document pair) instead of rotating across all 6 weeks
- **Same-week turnaround**: Document pair published Week N; reviewed Week N; feedback integrated Week N+1 writing
- **High reviewer load**: 2-3 documents/reviewer over 6 weeks = 4-6 hours/reviewer/week (unsustainable long-term)

**Peer Reviewer Hours**: 40-60 hours total (vs. 50-70 for Option A, 90-130 for Option B)

**Peer Review Risk**: MEDIUM-HIGH. 6-week sustained load increases risk of incomplete reviews, lower-quality feedback, and reviewer burnout. By Week 5-6, review quality typically degrades.

### Resource Cluster 6: Wave 3 Integration Under Option C

**Problem**: Wave 3 documents (food preservation, livestock, healthcare, water, fuel, information infrastructure, published as 7 separate documents May 30-July 4) arrive incrementally over the rolling cycle **but are not separate from Wave 1+2 in Option C's framework**.

**Option C publication schedule conflates Wave 1+2 and Wave 3**:
- Week 1-5 includes a mix of Wave 1+2 and Wave 3 documents
- Final publication (July 4) includes remaining Wave 3 documents
- **No clear boundary between "Wave 1+2" and "Wave 3"**; communities cannot distinguish governance-first vs. operational-second sequencing

**This undermines Phase 4 governance logic**, which depends on distinguishing governance frameworks (Wave 1+2) from operational implementation (Wave 3).

**Labor Hours**: Orchestrator spends 3-5 hours/week managing cross-wave document scheduling and explaining why "microgrids" (Wave 1+2 energy systems) and "food preservation" (Wave 3 implementation) are both published, causing confusion about sequence.

## Section 3: Phase 6 Launch (Fragmented, June 1-August 31)

### Resource Cluster 7: Phase 6 Author Onboarding (June 1-9, but staggered)

**Scope**: Phase 6 authors cannot receive complete Phase 5 context until **July 4** (final rolling publication). This creates onboarding dilemma:

**Option A**: Onboard authors June 1-9 **without** complete Phase 5 context (risk: outlines may need rework when Phase 5 is complete)

**Option B**: Delay author onboarding until **July 1-9**, after Phase 5 is complete (loss of 4 weeks June 1-30 productivity)

**Option C Reality**: Projects typically choose Option A (onboard early), resulting in **outline rework June-July** when Phase 5 rolling publications arrive and conflict with author assumptions.

**Labor Hours**:
- Initial onboarding (June 1-9, lacking Phase 5 context): 12-16 hours per author
- July rework (authors revise outlines after Phase 5 complete): 8-12 hours per author
- **Total per author**: 20-28 hours (vs. 18-30 hours Option A, which includes Phase 5 context from Day 1)

## Section 9: Option C Structural Disadvantages

1. **Governance legitimacy risk**: Community cannot ratify decision-making structure until Phase 5 is complete (July 4), creating 5-week period of "provisional authority"

2. **Publication rhythm overhead**: 6 separate publication events x 6-9 hours/event = 36-54 hours vs. concentrated 5-day sprint (Option A) or 15-day sprint (Option B)

3. **Phase 6 delayed start**: Author rework (July 1-9) delays writing launch from June 10 to July 10, pushing all Phase 6 publication dates 3-4 weeks later

4. **Reader experience fragmentation**: Communities receive documents in weekly pieces rather than coherent bundles (governance frameworks + operational guides), requiring readers to hold partial corpus in mind for 6 weeks

5. **Author fatigue**: 6-week rolling cycle requires peer reviewers to sustain 6 hours/week intensity for 6 weeks (higher burnout risk than 2-week Option A sprint or 15-day Option B concentrated push)

6. **Cross-project coordination complexity**: Seedwarden + manufacturing-farm projects cannot finalize sourcing decisions until Week 5-6 (June 27-July 4), delaying their procurement cycles by 4 weeks vs. Option A
