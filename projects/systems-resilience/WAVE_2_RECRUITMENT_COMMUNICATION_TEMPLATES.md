# Wave 2 Recruitment Communication Templates
## Phase 6 Author Recruitment — Five Email Templates

> **How to use**: Copy template, replace bracketed fields [LIKE_THIS], customize with author-specific details, and send. All templates are designed for 2–3 minute personalization before send.

> **Key principle**: Lead with impact + credibility; make the ask clear and specific; provide context without overwhelming; make accepting easy.

---

## Template 1: Cold Outreach — Initial Contact

**Purpose**: First contact with potential Wave 2 authors who may not know about the project

**Send to**: Tier A/B candidates with no prior involvement; 3–4 sentence hook

**Subject line**: `Systems Resilience Phase 6 Wave 2: [Domain Name] Author Opportunity`

---

```
Subject: Systems Resilience Phase 6 Wave 2: [Domain Name] Author Opportunity

Hi [Author Name],

I'm reaching out because of your [SPECIFIC WORK/BACKGROUND: e.g., "10+ years of cooperative development work" / "research on ecosystem restoration in the Midwest"]. 

We're recruiting authors for Phase 6 Wave 2 of Systems Resilience, a multi-author guide to community-scale resilience in Zone 5 Midwest context. We're looking for someone to develop a research-grounded, practitioner-focused guide on [Domain Name] for communities preparing for infrastructure challenges.

The scope: 8 hours/week, July 16 – September 15 (9 weeks). $65/hour + completion bonus. All source materials provided; your role is synthesis + practitioner translation.

If this interests you, would you have 20 minutes for a brief call in the next week? No obligation — just a chance to explore fit.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Organization]
[Phone] | [Email]

P.S. — Wave 2 documentation is robust; if you've never led a research synthesis project, we provide templates and weekly check-in support.
```

No obligation either way. Just let me know.

Best,
[Your Name]

**Send to**: Tier C candidates or those who don't meet domain minimums; keep door open for future waves

**Subject line**: `About your Phase 6 Wave 2 interest — alternative opportunity`

---

```
Subject: About your Phase 6 Wave 2 interest — alternative opportunity

Hi [Author Name],

Thank you for your interest in Phase 6 Wave 2! After reviewing your intake form and discussing timeline constraints, we've been thinking about how best to use your expertise given how Phase 6 Wave 2 is structured.

The writing intensity is high, and most domains have specific credential requirements (e.g., domain-specific hands-on experience). While your [STRENGTH: "deep commitment to [DOMAIN]" / "excellent communication skills"] is genuinely valuable, the fit for this particular wave is not optimal.

**That said** — we don't want to lose you. We have [OPTION A OR B]:

**Option A: Community Editor role**
If Domain [X] matches your interest, we'd like to invite you to be a community editor/reviewer for that domain's draft. Your role: Read the author's draft and provide feedback on [SPECIFIC: accessibility to immigrant justice communities / applicability to urban gardeners / etc.]. 2–3 hours/week, June–August. $40/hour.

This is valuable work — author feedback shapes the final guide — and it's a great way to build writing/editing credentials for Wave 3 if you're interested.

**Option B: Wave 3 candidate**
We're gearing up for Phase 6 Wave 3 expansion (September–January), which will have lower intensity (4–6 hrs/week) and more flexible domain assignments. Would you be interested in joining that round instead?

**Which appeals to you?**
- Option A (community editor for Domain ___) → I'll send you details
- Option B (Wave 3 candidate) → We'll add you to the recruitment list
- Neither right now, but stay in touch → No problem; we'll reconnect next year

Reply with your preference, and I'll follow up accordingly.

Thanks again for considering this. Your work is important, and I hope we can collaborate in one of these ways.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title] | [Organization]
[Phone] | [Email]

---

P.S. — If you're interested in strengthening your writing skills for future authorship, I have recommendations for online practitioner writing workshops. Happy to share if helpful.
```

## Customization Checklist

Before sending ANY template, verify:

- [ ] **Author name correct** (spell it right — first impression counts)
- [ ] **Specific reference to their work** (not generic template language; at least one sentence about why THEM)
- [ ] **Domain name filled in** (if applicable; make sure it's the right domain)
- [ ] **Dates accurate** (intake deadline, call window, offer deadline match current plan)
- [ ] **Contact info correct** (your phone + email; correct project title)
- [ ] **Tone matches relationship** (warm intro for referrals, professional but friendly for cold outreach)
- [ ] **Call to action crystal clear** ("What we need from you by what date")
- [ ] **Proofread** (no typos; no author name typos especially)
- [ ] **Attach required docs** (intake form, link to calendar, brief, etc. as applicable)

## Tone Principles

All templates follow these principles:

1. **Respect**: Acknowledge this is a big commitment; make declining easy
2. **Clarity**: Crystal-clear what you're asking for and when
3. **Warmth**: Personal touch; reference specific work/expertise; not a form letter
4. **Authority**: Convey that you know what you're doing; project is real + funded
5. **Support**: Emphasize that "we'll support you"; you won't be left alone
6. **Flexibility**: Signal that some constraints (August, timezone, hours) can be negotiated
7. **Low pressure**: Make it safe to say "no" or "let me think about it"
