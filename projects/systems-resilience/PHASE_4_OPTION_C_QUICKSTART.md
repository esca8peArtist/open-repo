# Phase 4 Option C Quick-Start: 6-Week Rolling Coordination Plan

> **Trigger**: User selects Option C on May 31. Weekly thematic releases begin May 30 (Week 1 is already due — prep immediately if selected).
> **Phase 4 task**: Governance evolves each week alongside new Phase 5 content. Phase 4 is not a 3-day event; it is a 6-week iterative governance activation that strengthens with each release.
> **Outcome**: By July 4 (Week 6), governance is not just theorized — it has been tested and refined against every Phase 5 domain in real time.
> **Resource requirement**: 5-7 hours per week for 6 consecutive weeks. Coordinator must be available all 6 weeks.

---

## Strategic Logic

Option C publishes in thematic clusters, weekly, so communities receive content in digestible portions (15-20K words/week vs. 43K+ in one release). Phase 4's job in this model is different from Options A and B: governance does not fully activate on Day 1. Instead, it grows incrementally — minimal skeleton at Week 1, expanded authority at Week 2, fully operational by Week 4.

This is appropriate for communities that are still forming trust or governance consensus. The iterative model means governance is road-tested in real time. A decision that fails in Week 2 can be amended in Week 3 without having collapsed the whole structure.

The trade-off: 6 weeks of sustained coordination overhead. If the coordinator goes offline at Week 3, the model breaks. Confirm coordinator availability for the full 6 weeks before selecting Option C.

## Week-by-Week Templates

---

### Week 1 (May 30 - June 5): Community Playbook + Microgrids

**Thursday May 30 — Release day (6 PM UTC):**
- Community reads: Implementation Playbook (skim) + Microgrids section (key decisions)
- Come to Thursday assembly with 1 question or concern

**Thursday June 5 — Assembly agenda (2 hours):**

1. (20 min) **Playbook overview**: "Playbook gives us a 4-week implementation roadmap. Week 1-2: governance + household structure. Week 3-4: domain-specific execution. Are we adopting this roadmap? Vote."
2. (20 min) **Microgrids decision**: "Zone 5 requires solar + backup fuel. Solar-only fails in winter. Do we authorize energy coordinator to plan backup procurement? Vote."
3. (15 min) **Authority matrix — Week 1 rows**: Add to the authority matrix: Implementation coordinator (daily authority for playbook execution); Energy/Microgrids coordinator (daily authority; escalates if capital purchase needed).
4. (10 min) **Week 2 preview**: "June 6 brings Psychological Support + Conflict Resolution — the hardest governance decisions. Come with thoughts on: who leads mental health support? What authority does that person have?"
5. (10 min) **Logistics**: Next assembly June 12, 7-9 PM. Microgrids coordinator schedules inspection of solar system June 7-8.

**Week 1 audience routing:**
- Communities organizing for the first time: focus on playbook (governance structure is the primary deliverable)
- Communities with existing governance: focus on microgrids (energy decision is the immediate actionable)
- Communities in Zone 5 winter prep: add to microgrids agenda: "We need backup heat source confirmed by July 1."

**Week 1 success metrics:**
- [ ] Governance skeleton posted and visible
- [ ] Playbook adopted as coordination structure
- [ ] Microgrids coordinator named and authority confirmed
- [ ] Week 2 preview distributed

### Week 2 (June 6 - June 12): Psychological Support + Conflict Resolution

**Thursday June 6 — Release day:**
- Community reads: Psychological Support Guide + Conflict Resolution Framework
- These are the most governance-intensive documents in Phase 5 — every section involves authority decisions

**Thursday June 12 — Assembly agenda (2 hours):**

1. (25 min) **Psychological support authority**: "Health coordinator needs authority to: [a] check in with community members weekly, [b] escalate if someone shows crisis signs, [c] override privacy norms in emergency. Do we grant this authority? Where are the limits?" Vote and record.
2. (25 min) **Conflict resolution protocol**: "When two community members conflict, what's the procedure? Coordinator mediation first; assembly escalation if unresolved in 48 hours. Do we adopt this? Who mediates?" Vote and assign mediator.
3. (20 min) **Authority matrix — Week 2 rows**: Add: Psychological Support coordinator; Conflict Resolution mediator (both with thresholds and escalation paths).
4. (15 min) **Week 1 retrospective**: "What worked in Week 1 governance? What needs amending?" (Max 2 amendments; present next week for vote.)
5. (10 min) **Week 3 preview**: "June 13 brings Veterinary Care + Livestock Care. Who is responsible for community animals? Does anyone have vet experience?"

**Week 2 success metrics:**
- [ ] Psychological support authority defined and recorded
- [ ] Conflict resolution procedure adopted
- [ ] Authority matrix rows 3-4 complete
- [ ] Week 1 amendments logged (to vote on Week 3)

### Week 4 (June 20 - June 26): Food Preservation + Seed Saving

**Thursday June 20 — Release day:**
- This is the highest-urgency Zone 5 release. August-October preservation window requires June decisions.

**Thursday June 26 — Assembly agenda (2 hours):**

1. (30 min) **Rationing protocol adoption**: "If stores fall below 45 days, food coordinator activates Level 1 rationing (no stockpiling, redistribution of surpluses). If below 30 days, Level 2 (assigned daily rations). If below 15 days, emergency assembly. Do we adopt these thresholds?" This is the most important governance vote in 6 weeks. Take the time to debate and amend as needed.
2. (20 min) **Preservation priority**: "With limited canning and storage capacity, what preserves first: grains, beans, root vegetables, fruit? Food coordinator has authority to decide within these priorities. Vote to adopt priority list."
3. (20 min) **Seed saving governance**: "Seed saving requires multi-year planning. Seed coordinator (or food coordinator) has authority to: designate seed-saving plots, allocate isolation distances, maintain seed library. Vote to assign."
4. (15 min) **June 15 Zone 5 planting check**: "Is June planting complete or nearly complete? If delayed by more than 2 weeks, call emergency food coordination session this week."
5. (10 min) **Week 5 preview**: "June 27 releases Water Systems + Healthcare Offline. Come prepared to discuss winterization authority for water systems."

**Week 4 success metrics:**
- [ ] Rationing protocol adopted with specific thresholds
- [ ] Preservation priority list adopted
- [ ] Seed saving governance assigned
- [ ] Zone 5 planting status verified

### Week 6 (July 4 - July 10): Fuel Production + Educational Governance

**Thursday July 4 — Release day:**
- This is the final release. Governance should now be fully operational.

**Thursday July 10 — Assembly agenda (2 hours, final rolling assembly):**

1. (20 min) **Fuel production authority**: "Fuel coordinator manages: procurement decisions, storage safety, emergency rationing. Vote to adopt fuel coordinator authority and assign."

6. (10 min) **Celebration**: "We built governance. It is tested. It is real."

## What Breaks Option C (Watch For)

**Coordinator absence**: If the facilitator misses a week without a substitute, the assembly fails and the rolling model loses momentum. Identify a backup facilitator by Week 1.

**Amendment overload**: If each week generates 5+ amendments, governance becomes unstable. Limit to 2 amendments per week, batched and voted on the following Thursday.

**Declining attendance**: If attendance drops after Week 3, the later assemblies (food rationing, water authority) lack legitimacy. Address attendance actively Week 2-3 if early signs appear.

**Zone 5 agricultural urgency**: Week 4 (June 20) is the preservation planning deadline. If this assembly produces weak governance decisions on food rationing, the community enters August without authority to manage food scarcity. Week 4 is the most important assembly in the 6 weeks.
