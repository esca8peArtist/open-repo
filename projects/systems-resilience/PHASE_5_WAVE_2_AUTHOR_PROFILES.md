# Phase 5 Wave 2 — Second Writer Candidate Profiles

> **Context**: These profiles are only relevant if the user selects Option B (Partial Parallel) or Option C (Full Parallel) in `PHASE_5_WAVE_2_DECISION_FRAMEWORK.md`. Option A (Serial) requires no second writer.
>
> **Domain assignment logic**: The document requiring the highest credential specificity is Psychological Support (licensed therapist background preferred for PFA clinical framing and suicide warning signs accuracy). The document with the lowest credential threshold is Conflict Resolution (community mediation training + facilitation experience is sufficient). Veterinary Care requires RVT or small-animal DVM background — this is the most specialized requirement and is best retained by the primary writer who built the veterinary care research base.
>
> **Recommended assignment**: Second writer owns Conflict Resolution. Primary writer retains Psychological Support (requires licensed therapist framing) and Veterinary Care (requires RVT/DVM background for triage protocol credibility). Tier 3 synthesizes from all three, best handled by the primary writer.

---

## How to Use These Profiles

Each profile below reflects the credential profile, experience type, and rate range appropriate for each candidate type. These are role archetypes with sourcing notes — not pre-identified individuals. The hiring process requires identifying specific candidates through the channels listed under each profile.

**Minimum viable qualification for any candidate**:
- Has written long-form (5,000+ word) research-grounded content for a non-academic audience
- Can work from a staged source list and citation protocol (provided in PHASE_5_WAVE_2_EXECUTION_PACKAGE.md)
- Available for 3.5 weeks of focused writing (July 16 – August 10)
- Comfortable with the Zone 5 Midwest homestead/resilience context

## Candidate 1: Conflict Resolution Specialist (Recommended Second Writer)

### Role Fit

Conflict Resolution is the Wave 2 document with the lowest specialized credential threshold. The content is grounded in practitioner literature (Rosenberg's NVC, Fisher and Ury's Getting to Yes, restorative justice circles) rather than clinical evidence — a community mediator with facilitation experience can write this credibly without a graduate-level social work or psychology credential. This is the lowest-cost and lowest-risk assignment for a second writer.

### Credential Profile Required

- Minimum: Completed NVC training (CNVC-certified or equivalent) plus active community mediation practice (2+ years, case-based)
- Preferred: Certified mediator through a state court ADR panel or community mediation center; experience facilitating restorative circles or harm processes; familiarity with intentional community governance contexts
- Not required: Advanced degree in social work, psychology, or law

### Where to Find Candidates

- National Association for Community Mediation (NAFCM) mediator directory: https://www.nafcm.org/find-community-mediation
- Resolution Services Institute (RSI) practitioner network: https://www.aboutrsi.org
- Center for Nonviolent Communication (CNVC) certified trainers list: https://www.cnvc.org/find-trainer
- Foundation for Intentional Community (FIC) network — practitioners with intentional community facilitation experience: https://www.ic.org
- Upwork or Reedsy — filter for "community mediation" or "conflict resolution" + "long-form writing"

### Availability Window

July 16 – August 10 (3.5 weeks, approximately 35–45 hours total for a 6,000–7,000 word document including research verification and integration pass).

### Rate Range

- Practitioner-writer with both credentials: $40–55/hour
- At 40 hours: $1,600–2,200
- At 45 hours: $1,800–2,475

### Fit Assessment

**Strong fit indicators**:
- Has published or contributed to community mediation guides, training materials, or practitioner handbooks
- Has written for non-legal, non-academic audiences (homesteaders, intentional communities, mutual aid networks)
- Understands the distinction between escalation thresholds (when facilitation is not the right tool) — this is the most risk-prone section and requires practitioner judgment, not just content synthesis
- Familiar with restorative circle facilitation, not just NVC theory

**Weak fit indicators** (screen out):
- Only formal dispute resolution experience (litigation support, HR mediation) — the document is for lay practitioners, not professional mediators
- No long-form writing experience — the 6,000-word target requires sustained argument structure, not just practitioner notes
- No familiarity with rural or community-scale conflict contexts — the Zone 5 Midwest homestead context is specific

**Interview question to assess fit**: "Describe a restorative circle process you have facilitated, and where you drew the line between a conflict that could be resolved through process and one that required a membership or structural decision." The answer reveals whether the candidate has the practitioner judgment that Section 5 (Escalation Thresholds) requires.

## Candidate 2: Psychological Support Specialist (Alternative Second Writer — Higher Cost, Higher Value)

### Role Fit

Psychological Support is the Wave 2 document with the highest credential requirement. The PFA evidence caveat — accurately representing the limited RCT evidence base while making the framework practically usable — requires someone who understands clinical framing well enough to navigate the line between evidence-informed and evidence-based. The suicide warning signs section (Zone 5 November–March elevated risk, direct inquiry protocol) requires clinical accuracy; a lay writer constructing this section from research alone risks clinical misstep. This candidate is the appropriate second writer if the primary writer's workload makes it necessary to offload Psychological Support rather than Conflict Resolution.

### Credential Profile Required

- Minimum: Active licensed therapist or counselor (LCSW, LPC, LMFT, or equivalent) with disaster mental health or community mental health experience
- Preferred: Licensed professional with experience delivering Psychological First Aid (PFA) training or applying PFA frameworks in community/field settings; familiarity with SAMHSA disaster behavioral health frameworks
- Strongly preferred: Rural mental health experience or Midwest practice context — the Zone 5 isolation and seasonal amplifier material requires practitioner grounding in rural mental health, not urban clinic norms

### Where to Find Candidates

- Psychology Today therapist directory — filter by "trauma," "community mental health," "rural" specializations: https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/therapists
- SAMHSA Disaster Technical Assistance Center (DTAC) — disaster mental health practitioners: https://www.samhsa.gov/dtac
- National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) — disaster recovery volunteer network
- American Counseling Association — rural mental health section practitioners: https://www.counseling.org
- Upwork or Contra — search "licensed therapist" + "mental health writing" + "crisis intervention"
- Rural Health Information Hub — rural mental health practitioner directories: https://www.ruralhealthinfo.org/topics/mental-health

### Availability Window

July 16 – August 10 (3.5 weeks, approximately 40–50 hours for a 7,000–8,000 word document including research verification, PFA clinical framing review, and integration pass).

### Rate Range

- Licensed therapist with disaster mental health experience: $60–75/hour
- At 45 hours: $2,700–3,375
- At 50 hours: $3,000–3,750

### Fit Assessment

**Strong fit indicators**:
- Has delivered PFA training or used PFA frameworks in field conditions (disaster response, community crisis, school settings)
- Has written for non-clinical audiences — therapists who have only written for professional journals often produce inaccessible prose; the document needs lay-accessible language
- Understands the distinction between PFA (structured lay approach) and clinical intervention — the document must be clear about what lay practitioners can do and when clinical referral is needed
- Rural mental health practice experience, or documented understanding of seasonal affective disorder management in Northern climates without pharmaceutical access

**Weak fit indicators** (screen out):
- No disaster or community mental health experience — private practice therapists without this background may not understand PFA's scope or limitations
- No long-form writing experience outside clinical documentation
- Unfamiliar with SAMHSA frameworks or PFA field guide — the document is anchored to these sources; a writer who has not read them cannot verify accuracy

**Interview question to assess fit**: "How would you frame the PFA evidence base for a reader who might dismiss it as unproven? What is your honest assessment of what lay practitioners can and cannot accomplish with PFA training?" The answer tests both clinical honesty and communication skill — both are required for the document's evidence caveat to land correctly.

## Candidate 3: Community Organization and Governance Specialist (Optional — For Option C Only)

### Role Fit

If Option C (Full Parallel) is selected, a third writer is needed to handle the Community Implementation Playbook (Tier 3) — or to assist with Conflict Resolution while the second writer handles Psychological Support. This candidate profile covers that third slot. Tier 3 is a synthesis document, not a research-intensive document — its labor is integration of the other Phase 5 Tier 2 documents and the Phase 3 corpus, structured by the Ostrom commons governance framework. The credential requirement is community organizing or collective governance experience, not a clinical credential.

**Critical note**: Tier 3 should not be assigned to a third writer unless the primary writer has explicitly reviewed and approved this arrangement. The dependency cascade risk — Tier 3 Section 4 cannot be written until Veterinary Care, Psychological Support, and Conflict Resolution are in first draft — is highest under Option C, and a third writer unfamiliar with the prior documents compounds this risk.

### Credential Profile Required

- Minimum: 3+ years experience in community organizing, collective governance, or cooperative development; familiarity with commons governance models (Ostrom, sociocracy, or equivalent)
- Preferred: Direct experience building or facilitating inter-organizational coordination structures; knowledge of the Foundation for Intentional Community (FIC) network or similar multi-household collective contexts
- Not required: Academic credential in political science or governance — practitioner experience is more valuable than theoretical background for this document

### Where to Find Candidates

- Foundation for Intentional Community (FIC) network — community founders and governance practitioners: https://www.ic.org
- Sociocracy For All — certified sociocracy facilitators with collective governance experience: https://www.sociocracyforall.org
- P2P Foundation — commons governance practitioners and researchers: https://p2pfoundation.net
- Commons Transition — practitioners working on commons governance at community scale: https://commonstransition.org
- Cooperative development organizations — National Center for Employee Ownership, USFWC for worker cooperative experience

### Availability Window

August 10 – September 10 (4.5 weeks under Option B; concurrent with other documents under Option C). Approximately 35–45 hours for a 7,500–9,000 word synthesis document.

### Rate Range

- Community governance practitioner-writer: $45–60/hour
- At 40 hours: $1,800–2,400
- At 45 hours: $2,025–2,700

### Fit Assessment

**Strong fit indicators**:
- Has direct experience with delegate selection, inter-group coordination, or federated governance structures at the 50–150 person scale
- Familiar with Ostrom's eight design principles for commons governance — can apply them to specific resource types (water systems, food storage, seed libraries) rather than just summarizing them abstractly
- Has written operational guides rather than academic analysis — the 18-month federation timeline must be usable, not theoretical
- Understands the Zone 5 Midwest agricultural context — the commons being governed are water systems, grain storage, and livestock equipment, not urban shared spaces

**Weak fit indicators** (screen out):
- Only academic governance background without community organizing experience
- No experience with rural or agricultural community contexts
- Unfamiliar with Phase 3 concepts (the document synthesizes five Phase 3 domain documents; the writer must read and understand them before drafting begins)
