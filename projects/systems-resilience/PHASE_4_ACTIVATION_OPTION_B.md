### Purpose

Take the governance structure (created June 1-4) and test it against the full Phase 5 corpus (Waves 1-3) to ensure internal consistency. Identify gaps, timing conflicts, and dependencies that might create governance failure modes if not surfaced before June 15 publication.

### Integration Task 1: Governance Authority Mapping (2 days)

**Input**: Phase 3 governance document + Phase 4 charter created June 1-4 + Phase 5 Wave 1-3 documents (food, healthcare, livestock, water, etc.)

**Process** (June 5-6):

For each of the 7 domains in Phase 5 (microgrids, veterinary care, psychological support, conflict resolution, community implementation, food, water/healthcare/other in Wave 3):

**Task per domain (2-3 hours):**
1. **Read Phase 5 document** for that domain (30-45 min)
2. **Identify all decision points** requiring governance authority:
   - Example from food: "If spring planting yields are low, who decides rationing level: coordinator, delegation, full assembly?"
   - Example from water: "If winter backup water system fails, who has emergency authority to allocate emergency reserves?"
   - Example from psychological support: "If someone shows signs of suicide risk, who initiates crisis intervention and are they authorized to override privacy?"
3. **Map each decision to your governance structure:**
   - Is this a coordinator authority? (e.g., food coordinator decides daily rationing)
   - Is this a household escalation? (e.g., household meeting if rationing >20%)
   - Is this a community escalation? (e.g., community assembly if rationing threatens survival)
4. **Identify conflicts**:
   - Conflicting authorities? (two people trying to make same decision)
   - Unclear thresholds? ("low yields" — how low? 10%? 50%?)
   - Missing escalation? (no protocol for [scenario])
5. **Record on authority matrix** (see template below)

**Deliverable by June 6 evening**: Authority matrix filled for all 7 domains. Example:

### Integration Task 3: Household-Community Escalation Stress Test (2 days)

**Input**: 10-15 realistic crisis scenarios + your governance + your household structure

**Process** (June 9-10):

Walk through realistic scenarios and verify governance can escalate correctly. This is the "murder board" test.

**Scenario 1: Food Shortage (Spring)**
- Situation: Planting germination rate is 60% (low). Coordinator projects 30% yield loss.
- Question: Who decides if rationing happens? How fast? What's the decision process?
- Test: Walk through your governance charter. Does it answer this? If "community assembly votes," how fast can you assemble? (If decision takes 2 weeks but food situation is urgent, governance is broken.)
- Outcome: Record "governance answer" and "risk assessment" (Is this fast enough? Legitimate enough?)

**Scenario 2: Psychological Crisis (Winter)**
- Situation: A household member (identity: elder, vulnerable to seasonal depression) is showing signs of suicidal ideation. Psychological support coordinator wants to escalate to community health coordinator (involves privacy violation).
- Question: Does your governance authorize this? What's the threshold? Who decides?
- Test: Walk through charter. Does it say what psychological support coordinator can do? Does it say when privacy can be violated?
- Outcome: Record answer and flag if unclear.

**Scenario 3: Governance Failure (Leadership Incapacity)**
- Situation: Governance facilitator is incapacitated (illness, injury, mental health crisis). No one else is trained in facilitation.
- Question: Does your governance work without that person? What's the fallback?
- Test: Who runs the next meeting? Who breaks tie votes? Does governance pause?
- Outcome: Record answer. (Communities usually discover they have a single point of failure here.)

**Scenario 4: Household Conflict (Resource Fairness)**
- Situation: One household's water coordinator is rationing more strictly than others. Other households feel punished.
- Question: Does your governance have a fairness review mechanism? Who arbitrates?
- Test: Walk through your escalation process. Is it clear? Can it be done in <1 week?
- Outcome: Record answer.

**Scenario 5: Scaling Trigger (Population Growth)**
- Situation: Three neighboring families ask to join the community. Population goes from 100 to 115. Your governance threshold for restructuring was 150.
- Question: Does governance adapt before you hit critical mass? Or does it fracture at 120, 140, 165?
- Test: What's the governance's response to the join request? Assembly vote? Delegation to membership committee?
- Outcome: Record answer and note whether you want to lower the threshold to 120-130 (proactive) or keep it (conservative).

**Deliverable by June 10 evening**: Stress test report (3-5 pages max). Each scenario has: "Governance answer," "Risk assessment," "Amendment if any." Identify 1-3 highest-risk scenarios.

### Integration Task 4: Charter Amendment Pass (2 days)

**Input**: Authority matrix + timing dependency map + stress test report

**Process** (June 11-12):

Make 3-5 amendments to the governance charter created June 1-4. These amendments are informed by what you learned when you mapped governance against all of Phase 5.

**Example amendments:**
1. **Food rationing authority**: "If spring yield projection <80%, food + governance coordinators jointly activate Level 1 rationing protocol (1,800 cal/day/person). This happens by [decision date], not by assembly vote, to preserve speed."
2. **Psychological support escalation**: "Psychological support coordinator may escalate to health coordinator + household liaison if suicide risk is assessed; privacy is overridden automatically (not permission-based)."
3. **Governance incapacity**: "If facilitator is incapacitated, liaison from largest household runs meeting. Decisions are consensus only (no votes) until facilitator returns."
4. **Fairness review**: "Household coordinates meet monthly (separate from community assembly) to compare resource allocation rates. Disparities >20% trigger governance review."
5. **Scaling threshold adjustment**: "If population grows to 120+, community assembly meets weekly (instead of monthly) and discusses restructuring options (delegates council vs. nested circles vs. new sub-assembly)."

**Process**: Community governance assembly meets June 11 evening. Facilitator presents amendments. Discussion (30-45 min). Vote to adopt. Re-sign amended charter by June 12 midnight.
