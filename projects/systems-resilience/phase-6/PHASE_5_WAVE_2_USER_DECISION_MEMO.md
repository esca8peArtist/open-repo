# Phase 5 Wave 2: User Decision Memo

> **Decision required by June 1, 2026.**
> This memo answers: which of three launch sequences maximizes impact for June 1–August 31 Phase 6 execution? It draws on the full Wave 2 production state audit (PHASE_5_WAVE_2_PUBLICATION_CHECKLIST.md), the execution sequencing analysis (PHASE_5_WAVE_2_PHASE_6_EXECUTION_SEQUENCING.md), and the Phase 6 research already complete.

---

## The Most Important Thing to Know

Wave 2 is not fully complete. Four household-scale documents (veterinary care, psychological support, conflict resolution, community implementation playbook) are at 35% preliminary draft — they are research-staged, outlined, and citation-ready, but require 100–130 additional writing hours to reach publication quality. The microgrids research (two documents, ~14,849 words) is production-ready today.

Phase 6 pre-research, by contrast, is nearly complete. Farm equipment repair (44,000+ words, 78 sources across three documents) and Meshtastic mesh networking (15,600+ words, 71 sources across two documents) have already been researched in depth. Phase 6 is not waiting on research — it is waiting on the June 1 sequencing decision to begin editorial integration and community deployment.

This memo is therefore less about "what to do" and more about "in what order, using what resources, against what timeline."

---

## Part 1: What Is Actually Being Decided

You are choosing between three paths for the June 1–August 31 window:

**Path A (Psychological-first)**: Execute the four household-scale documents in serial order, beginning with psychological support and conflict resolution. Phase 6 editorial integration runs in parallel during the second half.

**Path B (Parallel execution)**: Assign the four household-scale documents to a small writing team (two people), running psychological support and veterinary care simultaneously, then conflict resolution and community playbook. Phase 6 begins simultaneously with the second wave.

**Path C (Microgrid-first)**: Publish the production-ready microgrids research immediately, then address the household-scale documents starting with the highest-priority gap (veterinary care). Phase 6 editorial integration overlaps with the end of the household writing sprint.

The decision also determines whether a second writer is needed (required for Path B only), and whether Phase 6 publication can reach a September 1 or October 1 target date.

## Part 2: Critical Path Analysis

### Binding Dependency

The community implementation playbook (the Tier 3 synthesis document) cannot be finalized until the other three household-scale documents reach approximately 70% draft. This is the binding constraint in every path. Until veterinary care, psychological support, and conflict resolution are each at ~5,000+ words, the community playbook cannot extract the community-scale extensions it synthesizes.

Implication: the community playbook is always last. Paths A, B, and C differ in how fast they get the first three to 70%.

### Domain Dependencies on Each Other

- Psychological support and conflict resolution share a de-escalation methodology layer. Writing them consecutively (within 2–3 weeks of each other) produces better integration of their shared content than writing them months apart.
- Veterinary care is structurally independent from psychological support and conflict resolution. It can be written at any point without waiting on the other two.
- The microgrids research has no dependencies on the household-scale documents. It is already independent and publication-ready.

### Phase 6 Dependencies

Phase 6 editorial integration (combining the farm equipment and Meshtastic research into a unified deployment guide) requires no new primary research — the research is done. It requires approximately 20–30 hours of editorial work: cross-referencing, gap-filling the 6 [RESEARCH NEEDED] markers in the farm equipment documents, and creating a unified introduction and appendices. This work can begin the moment the June 1 decision is made, regardless of which path is selected for the household-scale documents.

## Part 3: The Three Options

### Option A: Psychological-First (Serial)

**Sequence**: Psychological Support → Conflict Resolution → Veterinary Care → Community Playbook → Phase 6 Integration

**Timeline**:
- June 1–20: Psychological Support (35% → 100%), ~25 hours
- June 20–July 10: Conflict Resolution (35% → 100%), ~22 hours
- July 10–31: Veterinary Care (35% → 100%), ~22 hours
- Aug 1–31: Community Playbook (35% → 100%), ~30 hours
- Aug 1–31 (parallel): Phase 6 integration and editorial, ~25 hours

**Total**: ~124 hours writing + ~25 hours Phase 6 = ~149 hours, June 1–Aug 31

**Resources needed**: Single writer. No coordination overhead. Phase 6 integration can run as a second track beginning August 1.

**What it maximizes**: Document quality. Writing psychological support and conflict resolution consecutively produces the best integration of their shared de-escalation methodology. Single-writer focus means no cross-person inconsistency.

**What it delays**: Veterinary care is not available until late July. Communities in Zone 5 with livestock will lack the veterinary guide through the peak summer livestock health season (heat stress, parasite load peaks in July–August). This is a meaningful practical cost for the target audience.

**Completion date**: Community Playbook complete ~August 31. Phase 6 integration complete ~September 15 (slight overrun if August has holiday gaps).

**Risk level**: Low. No second writer coordination. Writing schedule is achievable with consistent daily effort.

### Option B: Parallel Execution

**Sequence**: Psychological Support + Veterinary Care simultaneously (two writers) → Conflict Resolution + Community Playbook sequentially → Phase 6 Integration

**Timeline**:
- June 1–July 1: Psychological Support AND Veterinary Care in parallel (two writers), ~25 hours each
- July 1–20: Conflict Resolution (~22 hours, primary writer)
- July 20–Aug 20: Community Playbook (~30 hours, primary writer) — can begin once Conflict Resolution reaches 70% and other two are complete
- June 15–July 15 (parallel): Phase 6 integration begins while primary writer works on Conflict Resolution, ~25 hours

**Total**: ~102 hours primary writer + ~25 hours second writer + ~25 hours Phase 6 = ~152 hours total, June 1–Aug 20

**Resources needed**: Two writers. Second writer takes veterinary care; primary writer takes psychological support. Coordination overhead: ~3–5 hours (onboarding, style alignment, editorial integration pass). Second writer cost: $40–75/hour for a freelance specialist with RVT or small-animal DVM background.

**What it maximizes**: Speed. Phase 6 reaches completion by August 20 rather than September 15. Veterinary care is publication-ready by July 1 — before the peak Zone 5 summer livestock health window closes.

**What it risks**: Second writer onboarding introduces quality variance. The veterinary care guide requires accurate clinical framing — an underqualified second writer produces a document that requires heavy editorial revision, potentially consuming the time saved. The PHASE_5_WAVE_2_AUTHOR_PROFILES.md notes the veterinary care document has the most specialized credential requirement (RVT or small-animal DVM background).

**Completion date**: All Wave 2 + Phase 6 complete by August 20.

**Risk level**: Medium. Contingent on finding a qualified second writer in June.

**Timeline**:

- June 1–25: Veterinary Care (35% → 100%), ~22 hours
- June 25–July 15: Psychological Support (~25 hours)

**What it changes**: Psychological support and conflict resolution are delayed one month versus Option A. The community playbook completion slips to September 5, roughly one week past the August 31 target.

**Completion date**: Community Playbook September 5. Phase 6 integration August 15 (Phase 6 completes before Wave 2 does).

**Risk level**: Low. Single writer. No second-writer coordination. Slight overrun past August 31 on the community playbook only.

## Part 5: Recommendation

**Option C (Microgrid-first) is recommended.**

The reasoning in order of weight:

1. **Immediate publication captures momentum.** The Wave 1 documents (14,600 words) and microgrids research (14,849 words) are production-ready today. Publishing them on June 1 demonstrates project progress, creates an anchor for the community audience, and provides distributable content while the household-scale writing sprint proceeds. Under Options A and B, these documents sit idle until August.

2. **Veterinary care is the highest-value document for Zone 5 summer timing.** Zone 5 livestock health peaks in summer (heat stress protocols, parasite management, fly control, dehydration). A July 1 publication date reaches the audience at the moment of highest practical need. Delaying veterinary care to late July (Option A) means the most time-sensitive practical guidance misses its most useful window.

3. **Phase 6 editorial integration completes earliest under Option C.** Phase 6 research is done. Moving to editorial integration by July 15 means Phase 6 is publication-ready by August 15 — two weeks before the project's August 31 target. Under Option A, Phase 6 cannot begin until August and overruns September 15.

4. **Option B's second writer benefit is real but requires finding a qualified RVT/DVM writer in June.** If a strong candidate is available, Option B is strictly better than Option C on every timeline dimension. But the qualification bar is high and the sourcing timeline is short. If the second writer search extends past June 15, Option B becomes Option C with extra overhead.

**The single tradeoff in Option C** is that psychological support and conflict resolution are pushed to the second half of the sprint (July–August) rather than the first half. For the project's audience, this is less consequential than the veterinary timing miss: mental health and conflict resolution guidance is less seasonally sensitive than livestock care.
