# Phase 6 Domain B: Community Economic Resilience
## Success Criteria — Final Document Pass/Fail Definitions

**Purpose**: This document defines what "complete" means for the Domain B research deliverable. It is used at the June 30 final checkpoint to verify the document meets production standards before it is accepted as complete. It is also used by the research agent at the Day 20 and Day 25 checkpoints to assess whether the document is on track.

---

## 1. Structural Completeness — Pass/Fail

These are binary: pass or fail. No partial credit.

**1.1 Section completeness**
- PASS: All 10 sections present with substantive content (no [INCOMPLETE], [TBD], or [placeholder] markers anywhere in the document)
- FAIL: Any section contains placeholder text or is absent

**1.2 Implementation templates**
- PASS: Both templates (community local currency design guide; commons management charter) are present and fillable without additional research by a Zone 5 community organizer with no economics background
- FAIL: Either template is absent, incomplete, or requires additional research to use

**1.3 Case studies**
- PASS: All four case studies present (WIR, Argentina trueque, Vermont timebanking, Zone 5 cooperative) using the standardized format: context → economic mechanism → governance structure → outcomes → failure modes → transferable design principles
- FAIL: Any case study is absent or does not follow the standardized format

**1.4 Cross-domain bridges**
- PASS: All five cross-domain bridges are explicitly documented with specific document cross-references: (1) Phase 3 governance, (2) Phase 5 community playbook, (3) Phase 5 conflict resolution framework, (4) Phase 6 Domain A community platform architecture, (5) Phase 6 Domain C skills development (if activated)
- FAIL: Any bridge is absent or references the wrong document

## 2. Quantitative Thresholds — Measured at Final Review

### 2.1 Word Count Target
- **Target**: 7,000–8,000 words (total document, excluding frontmatter)
- **Acceptable range**: 6,300–8,800 words (±10%)
- **Fail below**: Under 6,300 words indicates insufficient research depth; specific sections must be identified and expanded before acceptance
- **Fail above**: Over 8,800 words indicates scope drift; the agent must identify which sections expanded beyond their targets and trim before acceptance
- **Measurement**: Count words in the body of the document (all 10 sections + introductory paragraph); exclude YAML frontmatter, section headers at the document level, and the bibliography

### 2.2 Citation Density
- **Target**: 40–60 sources cited inline (in-text citations or footnotes)
- **Minimum acceptable**: 40 unique sources cited
- **Maximum guideline**: 60 sources (above 60 suggests citation padding — verify each citation is load-bearing)
- **Measurement**: Count unique in-text citations; do not count bibliography entries that are not cited in the text

### 2.3 Evidence Quality Distribution
- **Target**: >70% of cited sources rated Strong or Moderate; <30% rated Weak
- **Acceptable minimum**: >65% Strong or Moderate
- **Fail below**: If Weak sources exceed 35%, the agent must identify which sections rely primarily on weak evidence and either upgrade the sources or explicitly label the weakness
- **Measurement**: Using the evidence quality ratings in domain-b-economic-resilience-sources.md; count Strong + Moderate citations vs. Weak; new sources added during research must be rated using the same criteria (Strong = peer-reviewed + government; Moderate = government evaluation + well-documented practitioner case study; Weak = blog + news report + unverified claim)

## 3. Content Quality — Substantive Pass/Fail

These criteria evaluate whether the document answers its core research questions. They require judgment, not just measurement.

### 3.1 Exchange Mechanism Section (Section 2)
- **Pass**: WIR case study complete with specific quantitative evidence (CHF transaction volume with year, member count, countercyclicality evidence with data); Bristol Pound failure case complete (specific failure mechanism, not just "it didn't work"); currency design taxonomy covering backed/mutual credit/time-based with tradeoffs documented
- **Fail**: WIR or Bristol Pound case missing specifics; design taxonomy absent or vague; quantitative evidence not cited

### 3.2 Commons Management Section (Section 3)
- **Pass**: All eight Ostrom design principles named and explained with a community-scale application example; at least one Zone 5 commons documented; allocation protocol for resource scarcity described with specific mechanism (rationing, priority queue, or other)
- **Fail**: Fewer than eight principles; no Zone 5 example; no scarcity allocation protocol

### 3.3 Cooperative Enterprise Section (Section 4)
- **Pass**: Three cooperative models documented (worker cooperative, agricultural cooperative, mutual aid) with governance structure, not just description; Mondragon three-body governance structure described with COVID-era performance; Zone 5 cooperative tradition documented with at least 3 specific Zone 5 cooperative institutions named
- **Fail**: Fewer than three models; governance structure described as philosophy not mechanics; no Zone 5 specifics

### 3.4 Implementation Usability Test (Sections 10 + Templates)
- **Pass**: A reviewer (simulating a Zone 5 community organizer with no economics background) can read the currency design guide and make a go/no-go decision on local currency adoption; can read the commons management charter and know what to fill in for their specific community; does not need to look up any terms or concepts
- **Fail**: Templates contain unexplained economics jargon; templates require prior knowledge of economics to use; templates are missing required fields

## 4. Zone 5 Integration Requirements

### 4.1 Geographic Coverage
- **Pass**: All 5 Zone 5 states (Illinois, Michigan, Iowa, Indiana, Wisconsin) represented in Section 9 with at least one specific institutional example per state
- **Fail**: Any Zone 5 state absent from Section 9

### 4.2 Zone 5 Case Studies per Section
- **Pass**: Each of the 5 major sections (Sections 2, 3, 4, 6, 9) contains at least 2 Zone 5-specific references — not repeated uses of the same 2 cases, but 2 distinct Zone 5 examples
- **Partial credit threshold**: 3 of 5 sections with 2 Zone 5 references = marginal pass; document must be flagged for revision in the under-represented sections
- **Fail**: 2 or fewer of the 5 major sections have 2 Zone 5 references

### 4.3 Seasonal Economy Addressed
- **Pass**: Zone 5 seasonal agricultural economy (income concentration in fall; input expenditure in spring) is explicitly addressed in at least Section 9 (Zone 5 implementation) and in both implementation templates
- **Fail**: Seasonal economy absent from both locations

## 5. Source Verification Requirements

These are non-negotiable quality standards.

**5.1 URL verification**
- Every URL cited in the document must be verified accessible at the time of the final citation audit (June 30)
- If a URL has become inaccessible since the source was initially compiled, document the alternative access path (library, archive, DOI redirect) or replace with an accessible equivalent source
- Document inaccessible URLs with the date of inaccessibility check; do not silently remove them

**5.2 No ghost citations**
- Every factual claim that requires a citation has one
- A ghost citation is a claim presented as fact without a source: "Most community currencies fail within five years" requires a source; "WIR has operated since 1934" requires a source; "Zone 5 has more agricultural cooperatives per capita than any other region" requires a source
- If a claim cannot be sourced, it must be framed as inference or removed

**5.3 Publication year attribution**
- Every source must have a publication year cited inline or in the bibliography
- The difference between a 1993 Ostrom paper and a 2025 mutual credit paper matters — readers must be able to assess recency
- If the publication year is unknown, note this explicitly: "(date unknown)"

**5.4 Evidence quality labeling for weak sources**
- Weak sources (practitioner blogs, news reports, unverified claims) must be labeled as such in the text
- Acceptable framing: "practitioner documentation suggests..." or "news coverage at the time reported..." or "based on community reports (not peer-reviewed)..."
- Unacceptable framing: treating a Resilience.org blog post as equivalent evidence to a peer-reviewed JEBO paper

## 6. Scope Compliance — Final Audit

**6.1 Macroeconomic policy absence**
- PASS: Document does not address Federal Reserve policy, national monetary policy, inflation mechanics at national scale, or national fiscal policy as primary topics; brief contextual mentions (e.g., "conventional banking failed in Argentina in 2001") are acceptable
- FAIL: Any paragraph that could be removed and placed in a national macroeconomics document without revision

**6.2 Cryptocurrency absence**
- PASS: Cryptocurrency and blockchain appear only as a brief footnote or technical aside noting their existence; they are not analyzed, recommended, or evaluated as primary community economic tools
- FAIL: Any section analyzing cryptocurrency as a community resilience tool

**6.3 Phase 5 food production non-duplication**
- PASS: Food production techniques appear only as context for the supply chain and commons sections; document references Phase 5 Wave 3 documents for food production details without re-documenting them
- FAIL: Any section explaining how to grow, preserve, or process food in the same level of detail as Phase 5 Wave 3

**6.4 Scale calibration**
- PASS: Every design principle and implementation recommendation is calibrated to 20–500 person community scale before the section is complete; examples involving Mondragon (70,000 workers) or Land O'Lakes ($14B revenue) are explicitly translated to 200-person scale before use
- FAIL: Recommendations that are only applicable at enterprise or national scale without community-scale translation

## 7. Summary Scoring — Final Acceptance Criteria

The document is accepted as production-complete when it meets all of the following:

| Criterion | Minimum Threshold | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Structural completeness (all sections, templates, case studies, bridges) | 100% (no partial credit) | 100% |
| Word count | 6,300–8,800 words | 7,000–8,000 words |
| Inline citations | 40 unique sources minimum | 45–55 sources |
| Evidence quality (Strong + Moderate %) | >65% | >70% |
| Zone 5 state coverage (Section 9) | All 5 states represented | All 5 states with 2+ institutional examples each |
| Zone 5 case study density | 3 of 5 major sections have 2 Zone 5 references | All 5 major sections |
| URL verification | All cited URLs verified accessible or alternative access documented | 100% |
| Ghost citations | Zero | Zero |
| Scope compliance (macro, crypto, food production) | Zero violations | Zero violations |
| Implementation usability (templates reviewable by non-economist) | Pass | Pass |

**Marginal pass**: A document that meets all minimum thresholds but not all targets. A marginal pass triggers a specific revision request targeting the gap areas before final acceptance.

**Fail**: A document that fails any minimum threshold. Failure triggers targeted revision with a specific completion date.

**The production-complete standard**: The document is ready to be published as part of the systems-resilience corpus. A Zone 5 community organizer can read it without an economics background, understand what tools are available to them, and use the two implementation templates to begin building community economic infrastructure without additional research.

## Comparison to Phase 3–5 Domain Standards

Domain B is calibrated to match the production quality of Phases 3–5 research domains. For comparison:

- **Phase 5 Wave 2 domains** (community implementation playbook, psychological support guide, veterinary care guide): 6,500–8,500 words; 35–55 citations; Zone 5 specificity throughout; practitioner-usable implementation templates
- **Phase 5 Wave 3 domains** (food preservation, water systems): 5,500–7,500 words; 30–50 citations; heavy Zone 5 specificity; step-by-step implementation guidance

Domain B targets are at the upper end of this range (7,000–8,000 words; 40–60 citations) because the economic resilience domain integrates heterodox economics, anthropological evidence, cooperative governance, and Zone 5 practitioner data — a higher synthesis burden than single-discipline domains.
