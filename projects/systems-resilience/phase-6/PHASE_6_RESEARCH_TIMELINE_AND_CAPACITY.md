## 2. Capacity Model

### Published Research Productivity Rates

Academic writing productivity research provides the following published benchmarks:

- **Standard academic writing**: 500 words per day is the widely cited sustainable target for academic writing. At 500 words/day with 5 writing days/week, a solo researcher produces 2,500 words/week or approximately 10,000 words/month. (Source: UCL CASA Blog, "500 Words a Day: Academic Writing," 2013; Tress Academic Writing Productivity #67.)
- **Intensive sprint writing** (deadline-driven, pre-researched): 750-1,000 words per day is achievable when sources are assembled and the researcher is in active production mode. This is not the sustainable everyday rate but is realistic for focused 6-week windows.
- **Research-only time** (source verification, reading, note-taking): approximately 30-40% of total production hours in a well-prepared domain; 40-50% in a less prepared domain.

**For Democracy Tools (Domain G):**

Source readiness is estimated at 65-70% as of June 2026. At research launch (November 4), source readiness will increase because: (a) four months of additional Brennan Center roundups will be published (June-October 2026 voting law developments, especially post-Callais state redistricting activity); (b) the 2026 midterm results will clarify the state-by-state voter protection landscape; (c) Democracy Works and ACLU post-midterm impact reports will be available.

Estimated source readiness at November 4: 72-78%. This places Domain G in the "Source Readiness 3-4" band from the Phase 6 scoring framework — similar to Domain C (Skills Development, which carried 88% on-schedule confidence in the June 2026 window).

### Solo Researcher Model: 160-200 Hours

At a 6-week window with a researcher working 25-35 hours per week:
- **Low end**: 25 hours/week × 6 weeks = 150 hours (slightly below floor; feasible only with very tight scope)
- **Mid range**: 30 hours/week × 6 weeks = 180 hours (target)
- **High end**: 35 hours/week × 6 weeks = 210 hours (achievable for a researcher without competing obligations)

**Hour allocation within 180-hour budget:**

| Activity | Hours | % |
|---|:---:|:---:|
| Source library verification and expansion (Weeks 1-2) | 25 | 14% |
| Reading, note-taking, and citation preparation | 40 | 22% |
| Zone 1 writing (voter registration legal landscape) | 30 | 17% |
| Zone 2 writing (technology solutions) | 20 | 11% |
| Zone 3 writing (international models) | 25 | 14% |
| Zone 4 writing (movement infrastructure) | 15 | 8% |
| Zone 5 subsections and implementation templates | 15 | 8% |
| Editorial integration, citation audit, formatting | 10 | 6% |
| **Total** | **180** | **100%** |

**Word output projection at 180 hours:**
- Research-reading-note-taking: 65 hours (zero output, maximum comprehension)
- Writing at 750 words/hour (realistic for prepared researcher): 115 hours × 750 = 86,250 words
- Editing/cutting: target output after editing is 45,000-55,000 words from 65,000-80,000 first-draft words

This projection confirms the target word count is achievable within the 6-week window. The 750 words/hour writing rate is conservative — MIT MEDSL research has established that social science researchers working from pre-assembled citations typically produce 600-1,000 words per hour in production mode. 750 is the midpoint of that range.

### Two-Researcher Model: 100-120 Hours Per Domain

If two researchers work concurrently — one on Zones 1-2 (voter registration and technology) and one on Zones 3-4 (international models and movement infrastructure) — the total per-researcher hours drop to approximately 100-120 each:

- Researcher A (Zones 1-2): 100-120 hours; source library 75%+ ready at launch; higher-urgency content
- Researcher B (Zones 3-4): 100-120 hours; international models require more initial source discovery but are less time-sensitive; can complete in 5 weeks if Zone 1-2 is in production by Week 2

**Two-researcher confidence uplift**: approximately 88% vs. 82% solo. The uplift comes from parallel production (no single bottleneck) and from each researcher specializing in one body of literature rather than switching between legal analysis and international civic tech.

**Coordination overhead**: one sync per week (30-60 minutes) plus cross-review in Week 6 (4-6 hours each). Total coordination overhead: approximately 8-10 hours per researcher. Net benefit is still substantial.

## 4. Scope Compression Analysis

**Conclusion: No scope compression is required for the 6-week window under the solo researcher model.**

The 37-day production window with 180 hours produces a projected first draft of 65,000-80,000 words that, after editing, targets 45,000-55,000 final words. This is within the Phase 6 word count standard and does not require scope compression.

**If scope compression becomes necessary (risk scenarios):**

*Risk 1: Week 4 Thanksgiving disruption is larger than anticipated (probability: 20%)*
- Mitigation: Compress Estonia section from 3,500-4,500 words to 2,500-3,000 words. Focus on policy outcomes rather than technical architecture. This saves approximately 4-6 hours without removing any research zone.

*Risk 2: Zone 5 state-specific ballot initiative research takes longer than estimated (probability: 25%)*
- Mitigation: Section 10 (Ballot Initiative Infrastructure) is reduced from 5 states to 3 states (focus on Minnesota, Wisconsin, and Iowa — the Zone 5 states with the most active direct democracy infrastructure). Illinois and Missouri are addressed in 1-2 paragraphs referencing external resources rather than full treatment. This saves approximately 6-8 hours.

*Risk 3: Zone 3 international models require more source discovery than anticipated (probability: 20%)*
- Mitigation: The Canada section (Section 9) is compressed from 2,500-3,500 words to 1,500-2,000 words, focusing on the Inspire Democracy network expansion as a single documented model rather than full comparative analysis. Estonia and vTaiwan are maintained at full length.

**No scope compression scenario yields a final document below the 45,000-word floor. The most likely scope compression scenario (Week 4 Thanksgiving + Zone 5 balloting complexity) produces a 42,000-46,000 word document — still within the 45,000 target range at the high end of the compression range.**

If the document comes in at 43,000-44,000 words after all compression adjustments, the correct mitigation is to add 1,000-2,000 words to the Opening section (Section 1) deepening the post-Callais constitutional analysis, which is well-sourced and adds clarity rather than complexity.
