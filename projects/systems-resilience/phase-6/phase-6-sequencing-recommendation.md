## Section 2: Two-Domain Production Timelines

### Pairing A: Economic Resilience + Governance Scaling

**Recommended pairing for maximum Phase 6 strategic value**
**Confidence: 85% both complete by August 23, 2026**

This pairing produces the strongest conceptual coherence: economic structures shape what governance models are feasible, and governance structures determine what economic models communities can sustain. The two domains are tightly coupled and benefit from shared conceptual framing.

**Why this pairing works:** The Economic domain (75–80% source ready) completes its source library by June 7–8 and begins full production June 8–10. During those first two weeks of Economic production, the Governance author conducts extended source library research (their bottleneck). By the time Governance production begins around June 15–22, the Economic author can provide context on economic-governance interdependencies. Economic domain completes around July 13, freeing attention to review the Governance draft.

**Resource requirement:** Two simultaneous agents; one assigned each domain. Orchestrator checkpoint at June 21 (cross-domain alignment) and July 5 (integration points confirmed).

**Integration deliverable:** A 3,000–5,000 word integration section co-authored by both agents explaining how economic structures and governance architecture co-evolve. This is written August 10–15 after both primary documents are complete.

**Timeline summary:**

### Pairing C: Governance Scaling + Skills Development

**For maximum long-term leverage**
**Confidence: 82% both complete by August 30, 2026**

This is the highest-leverage pairing but the highest-risk combination: both domains have moderate-to-high scope expansion risk, and Governance has the lowest source readiness. This pairing should only be selected if both agents have relevant domain expertise (political science + education backgrounds respectively) and if the orchestrator can provide active weekly scope oversight.

The benefit: governance and skills transmission are deeply interdependent. Educational governance (what to teach, who decides, how to validate) is a direct subset of community governance design. A community that cannot make governance decisions at scale cannot implement a coherent skill transmission system. Producing both simultaneously creates natural conceptual cross-pollination.

**Resource requirement:** Two agents with strong domain backgrounds; orchestrator weekly scope check on both. Integration requires a joint section on educational governance (2,000–3,000 words) after both primary documents draft.

## Section 3: Three-Domain Option

Producing all three domains simultaneously in the June–August window is feasible but requires three simultaneous agents and aggressive scope management. The overall confidence drops to approximately 65–70% that all three complete by August 30.

**Recommended alternative to three-domain concurrent:** Select Economic + one other candidate for June–August (Phase 6a). Launch the third candidate in a Phase 6b cycle with a September 15 target. This produces 90%+ confidence on Phase 6a and 92%+ confidence on Phase 6b. Phase 6 publishes as a two-volume set — economically and structurally stronger than a rushed three-domain concurrent effort.

**If three-domain concurrent is required:**
- Economic domain completes first (~August 2)
- Skills domain completes second (~August 15)
- Governance domain completes last (~August 30, tight)
- Cross-review and integration: September 1–5
- Distribution package: September 10

Production sequencing: start Governance source library June 1 before Economic production begins, to offset its longer lead time. Economic and Skills can begin production June 8–10 in parallel with Governance source work.

## Section 4: Critical Path Dependencies

### Is Phase 6 blocked by Phase 5 publication timing?

No. Phase 6 research is fully independent of Phase 5 Wave 1 publication. The Phase 6 domains do not wait for Phase 5 to be published — they reference Phase 5 documents as research material (which are already complete), not as prerequisite publications.

Phase 5 Wave 1 publication (production-ready as of May 27) is a separate user decision with a separate timeline. It does not affect when Phase 6 can begin.

### What actually gates June 1 launch?

1. **Anya's domain selection** (required by May 31 EOD): this is the only true gate. Without a domain selection, no agent can be briefed and no production can start.
2. **Agent assignment** (required by June 1–2): a research agent briefed on the selected domain. The kickoff template provides everything the agent needs.
3. **Source library access** (required by June 5–8): this happens in the first week and is not a gate to starting — it is the first week's primary task.

### Dependency between Phase 6 domains (if multiple selected)

If Economic + Governance are selected together: Governance author benefits from seeing Economic's inter-community trade section before drafting the federation governance section. Recommend a June 22 sync to share the Economic draft's inter-community trade framing with the Governance author. Not blocking, but improves quality.

If Skills + Governance are selected together: governance institutional design shapes what skill transmission governance looks like. Recommend sharing Governance federation section draft with Skills author by July 13.

If all three are selected: Economic domain's cooperative and exchange infrastructure framing should be shared with both Governance and Skills authors by July 13 (when Economic domain completes).
