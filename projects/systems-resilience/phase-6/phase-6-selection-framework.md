*Does starting June 2026 versus deferring to September meaningfully change the value delivered?*

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|

| 2 | Low urgency — deferral costs something but not much |

### Writing Standards

**Tone**: write for a community organizer or practitioner who wants to do something with this information, not an academic audience.

**Specific, not conceptual:**
- Wrong: "Communities should consider resilient economic models."
- Right: "A 100-household community in Zone 5 can establish a functional food cooperative in 6–12 months for $3,000–$8,000 in founding capital, using the USDA Rural Cooperative Development Grant for technical assistance."

**Evidence-based**: support every factual claim with at least one citation. When evidence is limited, say so directly ("practitioner evidence suggests..." or "no peer-reviewed data exists; the following case studies indicate..."). Never speculate without labeling it as extrapolation.

**Section structure:**
1. Open with a concrete problem (1–2 paragraphs — state specifically what fails when this domain is not addressed)
2. Present research findings (bulk of section, 1,500–3,500 words — what does the literature show?)
3. Translate to Zone 5 (300–500 words — how do findings apply in Midwest rural and suburban contexts?)
4. Provide actionable guidance (300–500 words — what should a community do first? what are the failure modes?)

**Citation format**: numbered inline — [1], [2], [3]. Full bibliography at end. Aim for 60–70% academic sources, 20–30% case studies and reports, 10–15% primary documents.
