# Phase 6 Domain B: Community Economic Resilience
## Annotated Source Library — 55 Sources

**Evidence quality definitions**:
- **Strong**: Peer-reviewed academic publication, government statistical report, or multi-year longitudinal study with clear methodology
- **Moderate**: Government evaluation, NGO research report, peer-reviewed publication with methodological limitations, or well-documented practitioner case study
- **Weak**: Practitioner blog, news report, single-source claim, or historical document without modern verification; useful as illustrative evidence but not load-bearing

## Section 1: The Exchange Problem — Why Production Without Distribution Fails

**1.1** Graeber, David. *Debt: The First 5,000 Years*. Melville House, 2011.
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debt:_The_First_5,000_Years (entry; book widely available via library)
- Relevance: Anthropological evidence that debt, not barter, is the historically prior form of exchange; dismantles the "natural barter economy" assumption; provides the conceptual foundation for why communities need formal exchange systems, not informal barter
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (academic; interdisciplinary; widely cited but not quantitative)

**1.2** Rapid Transition Alliance. "The 'instant' local economy — how communities saved themselves when mainstream economics failed in Argentina."
- URL: https://rapidtransition.org/stories/autonomous-local-action-how-communities-saved-themselves-when-mainstream-economics-failed-in-argentina/
- Relevance: Primary documentation of Argentina 2001 trueque barter network; covers scale (2.5–7M participants), governance structure, and social outcomes; examines both success phase and collapse
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (practitioner/journalist; based on documented sources)

**1.3** P2P Foundation. "Argentine Social Money Movement."
- URL: https://wiki.p2pfoundation.net/Argentine_Social_Money_Movement
- Relevance: Synthesizes academic and practitioner documentation on the trueque network; governance failure analysis (counterfeiting, over-issuance) is central to understanding why the network collapsed
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (synthesis document; references primary academic sources)

**1.4** ResearchGate. "Argentina's Barter Network: New Currency for New Times?"
- URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228021614_Argentina's_Barter_Network_New_Currency_for_New_Times
- Relevance: Academic analysis of the trueque network's economic function, scale, and governance; peer-reviewed; documents the counterfeit crisis that collapsed the network
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed academic)

**1.5** Academia.edu. "Mastering the crisis? Sustaining governance in the Argentine Redes de Trueque."
- URL: https://www.academia.edu/2343381/Mastering_the_crisis_Sustaining_governance_in_the_Argentine_Redes_de_Trueque
- Relevance: Governance failure analysis of the trueque; directly addresses how economic systems without governance authority collapse; most useful source for the failure modes argument in Section 1
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed; governance focus)

**1.6** Centro de Estudios Puertorriqueños (Hunter College, CUNY). Reports on Puerto Rico post-Hurricane Maria informal exchange.
- URL: https://centropr.hunter.cuny.edu
- Relevance: Documents informal economic exchange during and after Hurricane Maria (2017); centros de acopio (collection centers) as proto-exchange nodes; mutual aid as economic survival mechanism
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (university research center; primary documentation)

## Section 2: What Money Actually Does — Community Currency Design

**2.1** WIR Bank. Annual Reports and Academic Analyses.
- URL: https://www.wir.ch (primary); Monneta: https://monneta.org/en/wir-bank-switzerland/
- Relevance: Primary documentation of the world's longest-operating mutual credit system; 90-year track record; 50,000 SME members; CHF 1.43B+ annual transaction volume; countercyclical design validated by multiple academic studies
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (primary institutional documentation + peer-reviewed validation)

**2.2** Stodder, James. "Reciprocal Exchange Networks: Implications for Macroeconomic Stability." *International Electronic Journal of Rural and Remote Health Research*, Education, Practice, and Policy (multiple publications, 2000–2009).
- URL: https://base.socioeco.org/docs/wir_and_the_swiss_national_economy.pdf (WIR and Swiss National Economy report)
- Relevance: Primary academic documentation of WIR's countercyclical function; proves that WIR transaction volume increases during Swiss economic downturns — the most important empirical finding for community currency design
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed; quantitative)

**2.3** ScienceDirect. "For better or for worse: How Mutual Credit Systems bolster resilience of small firms." *Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization* 230 (2025).
- URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S016726812400475X
- Relevance: 2025 peer-reviewed evidence that mutual credit systems provide resilience to small firms during economic stress — the most current quantitative evidence for mutual credit design at community scale
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed; 2025)

**2.4** arXiv. "Imitation and Heterogeneity Shape the Resilience of Community Currency Networks" (2025).
- URL: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2507.02678
- Relevance: 2025 computational modeling of community currency network resilience; provides design principles for which network topologies are most resilient under stress
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed preprint; quantitative modeling)

**2.5** Bristol Pound Evaluation Report (2020). HM Government / University of the West of England.
- URL: https://bristolpound.org (archived documentation)
- Relevance: Government-commissioned evaluation of what worked and what failed in the Bristol Pound (UK, 2012–2021); documents why convertibility to national currency creates drainage risk; most useful failure case study for Section 2
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (government evaluation; independent review)

**2.6** Lietaer, Bernard. *The Future of Money: Creating New Wealth, Work and a Wiser World*. Century, 2001.
- URL: (widely available via library)
- Relevance: Design taxonomy for complementary currencies; covers full range of currency types from WIR to timebanking to LETS; provides the classification framework for Section 2
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (academic; not peer-reviewed in traditional sense but widely cited)

**2.7** Seyfang, Gill. "Sustainable Consumption, the New Economics and Community Currencies: A Critical Evaluation." *Regional Studies* 40(7): 781–791, 2006.
- URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/09607490600868083
- Relevance: Critical evaluation of community currency outcomes across multiple case studies; documents gap between theoretical claims and measured social outcomes; important for calibrating expectations
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed; critical)

**2.8** Schumacher Center for a New Economics. "Switzerland" — WIR and Community Currency Directory.
- URL: https://centerforneweconomics.org/apply/local-currencies-program/localcurrenciesdirectory/europe/switzerland/
- Relevance: Practitioner documentation of WIR; provides comparative context with Berkshire region (BerkShares) complementary currency program in rural New England — Zone 5 analog
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (practitioner; reputable organization)

**2.9** Resilience.org. "Beyond Community Currencies: Strengthening Your Local Economy" (2025).
- URL: https://www.resilience.org/stories/2025-03-28/beyond-community-currencies-strengthening-your-local-economy/
- Relevance: Current practitioner synthesis on the full ecosystem of local economic strengthening beyond currency alone; bridges currency design to broader local economy strategy
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (practitioner; 2025)

**2.10** Frontiers in Blockchain. "Community Currencies as Crisis Response: Results From a Randomized Control Trial in Kenya."
- URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/blockchain/articles/10.3389/fbloc.2021.739751/full
- Relevance: Randomized controlled trial (strongest possible evidence design) showing community currency effectiveness for crisis response in Kenya; demonstrates food security and transaction volume outcomes; the methodological gold standard for community currency effectiveness evidence
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (RCT; peer-reviewed)

## Section 3: Commons Management

**3.1** Ostrom, Elinor. *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press, 1990.
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governing_the_Commons (entry; book widely held)
- Relevance: Foundational; the eight design principles for successful commons governance are the primary analytical framework for Section 3 and all community resource allocation in this domain
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (Nobel Prize-winning research; foundational)

**3.2** Ostrom, Elinor. "Design Principles in Long-Enduring Irrigation Institutions." *American Political Science Review* 87(3): 677–678, 1993.
- URL: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2938806 (JSTOR)
- Relevance: Ostrom's own application of the design principles to irrigation systems — one of the strongest long-duration (centuries) commons governance case studies; irrigation system governance maps directly onto community food water commons
- Evidence quality: **Strong** (peer-reviewed; empirical)

- Relevance: Practitioner-accessible introduction to commons governance; bridges Ostrom's academic framework to community implementation; shorter and more accessible than Governing the Commons
- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (academic practitioner; not peer-reviewed)

**3.4** The Farmers Land Trust. "How the Farmland Commons integrates all Eight of Elinor Ostrom's Principles for a Successful Commons."

- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (practitioner; verifiable)

**3.5** LSE Review of Books. "Elinor Ostrom's work on Governing The Commons: An Appreciation."

- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (academic assessment; not peer-reviewed)

**3.6** Resilience.org. "Tools for growing the commons" (2025).
- URL: https://www.resilience.org/stories/2025-12-17/tools-for-growing-the-commons/

- Evidence quality: **Moderate** (practitioner; 2025)
