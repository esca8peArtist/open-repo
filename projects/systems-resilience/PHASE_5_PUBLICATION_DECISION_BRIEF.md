# Phase 5 Publication Decision Brief

Decision brief prepared May 27, 2026 — User decision needed by May 31

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 5 is complete: 66,442 words, 423 citations, 12 documents, zero placeholders. The only remaining question is how to release it. The choice between staged and unified distribution affects when real communities get usable content, how much editorial work is required in a tight window, and whether there is a feedback window before the final documents are locked. This decision gates Phase 6 launch scheduling.

---

## Option A: Phased Distribution — Wave 1+2 June 5, Wave 3 June 30

**Timeline**
- June 1-5: Final editorial pass + Wave 1+2 published (43,621 words across 5 documents)
- June 5-25: Reader feedback window; light Wave 3 cross-reference pass
- June 28-30: Wave 3 published (22,821 words across 7 documents)

**Advantages**
1. Communities get high-leverage content (energy infrastructure, veterinary care, psychological support, conflict resolution, implementation playbook) at least 10 days sooner than any other option.
2. A 3-4 week feedback window between Wave 1+2 publication and Wave 3 finalization allows real practitioner signal to inform the operational documents.
3. Wave 1+2 is independently coherent — the arc from infrastructure to human systems to governance is complete without Wave 3.
4. Lower execution risk: two clean publication events with a 25-day gap, no tight editorial deadline.

**Disadvantages**
1. Two-volume structure creates mild discoverability friction; readers who find Wave 1+2 must separately locate Wave 3.
2. Wave 3 may be perceived as a lesser supplement rather than a co-equal part of the corpus if the June 30 release is not actively promoted.
3. Requires a second distribution event with its own announcement and logistics.

**Decision matrix score: 30/40**

---

## Option B: Unified Distribution — Full 66,442-word Release June 15

**Timeline**
- June 1-14: Editorial integration of all 12 documents (master TOC, cross-references, frontmatter standardization) — estimated 10-15 hours
- June 15: Complete corpus published in a single release

**Advantages**
1. Single release, single URL, single announcement — the strongest artifact for outreach to networks and organizations that want one authoritative reference.
2. Master table of contents and integrated cross-references are in place before any reader encounters the documents.
3. "The complete 66,000-word systems resilience guide" is a more compelling distribution artifact than a staged release.
4. One distribution event to coordinate instead of two.

**Disadvantages**
1. No content reaches any reader until June 15 — a 10-day delay relative to Option A, with no benefit to communities waiting to begin planning.
2. Requires 10-15 hours of editorial integration work in the June 1-14 window; any delay pushes publication into July.
3. No feedback window before Wave 3 is locked — if practitioners identify gaps after June 15, correction is post-publication.

**Decision matrix score: 24/40**

---

## Recommendation

The decision framework scores Option A at 30/40 and Option B at 24/40. Option A is recommended because:

- The Wave 1+2 content is the content organized community groups use first (organization, energy, human systems, governance). Wave 3 is the operational supplement communities need once they are functional. Option A's publication sequence matches the natural reading sequence.
- Wave 1+2 has already been through multiple revision cycles. No additional editorial work is required before June 5.
- The feedback window between June 5 and June 30 is genuinely useful: practitioner signal can be incorporated into Wave 3 before it publishes.
- Option B's primary advantage (unified artifact) can be partially recovered by including a "Volume 2 coming June 30" note in the Wave 1+2 release and promoting Wave 3 actively on June 30.

Option B is the right choice if unified corpus coherence matters more than speed for your intended distribution channels.

## Success Metrics

**If Option A:**
- Wave 1+2 reaches intended audience within 2 weeks of June 5 publication

**If Option B:**

- Master TOC covers all 12 documents with clear navigation
- No Wave 3 quality issues identified post-publication that a feedback window would have caught
