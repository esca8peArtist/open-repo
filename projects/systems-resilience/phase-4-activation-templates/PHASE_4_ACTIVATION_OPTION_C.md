# Phase 4 Activation Playbook — Option C

> **Trigger**: User selects Option C on May 31 at the May 31 CHECKIN.
> **First action**: June 1 morning — governance synthesis workshop Day 1 begins; Week 1 module pair releases Thursday June 5 (or as scheduled).
> **Completion target**: Each week's Phase 5 module publishes on schedule; Phase 4 governance evolves in parallel, operational by end of Week 2.

---

## Execution Logic

Option C publishes Phase 5 in six thematic clusters over 6–8 weeks, one cluster per week. Phase 4 does not run as a single activation sprint. Instead, Phase 4 work is organized **per module**: for each Phase 5 publication module, there is a corresponding Phase 4 task that runs in the same week.

The Phase 4 per-module task has three components:
1. **Identify which Phase 4 content must be ready** before this week's Phase 5 module can be implemented
2. **Determine what individual-scale deepening** the module's content requires
3. **Confirm Zone 5 constraints** that affect how this module is implemented in climate zone 5

The coordination structure is simple: Thursday evening assembly, same time every week. Phase 5 module releases Thursday. Assembly meets Thursday evening. Governance decisions made Thursday night with benefit of having just read the week's content. Authority matrix updated. Next week preview distributed.

**Resource constraint**: Distributed across 8 weeks (June 1 – late July). No week requires more than 4–6 hours of concentrated work. Weeks 7–8 consolidate everything into final governance documentation.

**The critical dependency**: Governance must be operational by the end of Week 2 (June 12) — not Week 6. This is the Zone 5 constraint. Heating season decisions (fuel procurement, heating rationing protocol) must be made before October. A community that reaches Week 6 governance decisions in July still has time. A community that does not have governance operational until Week 6 does not.

### Reading the Fail-Safe Column

"Can hold 1 week?" asks: if Phase 4 work is 1 week behind, which modules can absorb the slip without downstream damage?

- **No** modules are those where a slip propagates: Week 1 delay delays all subsequent weeks; Week 4 delay tightens the preservation equipment window; Week 6 delay risks the fuel procurement deadline.
- **Yes** modules can absorb a 1-week slip if the stated "but not past" limit is respected.

If Phase 4 work is 2 or more weeks behind, all modules are at risk. The correct response to a 2-week slip is to convene an emergency half-day catch-up session before the next Thursday assembly, not to attempt to absorb the slip in normal weekly cadence.

## Fail-Safe: Which Modules Hold When Phase 4 Slips

### Scenario 1: Phase 4 is 1 week behind entering Week 3

Phase 4 charter adoption (scheduled Week 2) did not happen. Charter is still a draft.

**Impact**: Week 3 (Veterinary Care + Livestock Care) releases without adopted governance. Veterinary coordinator has been nominated but not formally authorized. Communities can read the content but cannot make binding governance decisions.

**Response**: Week 3 assembly has two items instead of the normal one: (1) Emergency charter adoption vote (15 minutes — governance has been waiting one week, most issues are resolved), (2) Veterinary governance decisions (normal Week 3 agenda). Week 3 assembly runs 2.5 hours instead of 2 hours. Charter adoption must happen at Week 3 assembly — it cannot slip past this point.

**What must not hold**: The Week 4 assembly needs governance operational to make the food rationing threshold decision. A Week 4 assembly without adopted governance produces advisory decisions, not binding policy. The preservation equipment audit and August harvest window cannot wait for governance to catch up.

### Scenario 2: Phase 4 is 1 week behind entering Week 4

Phase 4 is behind on Zone 5 adaptation guide distribution and food coordinator has not been named entering Week 4.

**Impact**: Week 4 (Food Preservation + Seed Saving) releases without a food coordinator named. The rationing threshold decision is made collectively without a named lead.

**Response**: Name an interim food coordinator at the start of Week 4 assembly (5 minutes). Make the rationing threshold decision. Formally ratify the food coordinator role the following week. The preservation equipment audit can begin without formal coordinator appointment — the most technically knowledgeable household member can lead it informally.

**What must not hold**: Preservation equipment audit cannot hold past June 26. Equipment procurement lead time is 6 weeks. If the audit is not complete by June 26 and equipment gaps are identified, procurement must begin by July 3. A July 3 procurement order for canning equipment arrives by mid-August — barely inside the preservation window.

### Scenario 3: Phase 4 is 2+ weeks behind entering Week 5

Two or more weeks of accumulated slip. Charter may or may not be adopted. Multiple coordinator roles unfilled.

**This is the critical slip scenario.** Two weeks behind in a 6-week rolling schedule means Phase 4 has been operating without a catch-up protocol. The response is not another week of normal cadence.

**Response**: Convene an emergency half-day catch-up session (3–4 hours, scheduled for the Saturday or Sunday before Week 5's Thursday release). Agenda: (1) Adopt the charter if not yet adopted (30 minutes), (2) Fill all unfilled coordinator roles (30 minutes), (3) Make the two or three most critical governance decisions that slipped (food rationing, livestock model, irrigation backup) in compressed form (30 minutes each), (4) Reschedule the Phase 5 Week 5 release to the following Thursday if Phase 4 catch-up is not complete before Thursday.

If catch-up session cannot be scheduled before Week 5 Thursday: delay Week 5 release one week (July 10 instead of July 3). Notify community. Run catch-up session between now and July 10. The Zone 5 September 15 winterization deadline still has adequate lead time from a July 10 Week 5 decision.

## Success Criteria

Each week's module publication proceeds on schedule despite Phase 4 dependencies. The coordination structure runs for 6–8 weeks without governance whiplash (no more than 2 amendments to existing charter sections per week; decisions voted at one assembly are not relitigated at the next).

The Option C Phase 4 activation is complete when: (1) all six Phase 5 clusters have published, (2) the final governance charter has been written capturing 6 weeks of evolution (Week 7 deliverable), (3) all Zone 5 critical decisions have been made before their deadlines, and (4) the community has confirmed winter preparedness (heating backup, water winterization, fuel stockpiled) before September 15.

**Option C is the right choice** when the community wants to build governance iteratively, with each week's Phase 4 governance decision made with the benefit of having just read the relevant Phase 5 content. The rolling format produces governance that has been stress-tested on all six domains before the final charter is written. The trade-off is 8 weeks of sustained coordination effort — this only works if Thursday assembly attendance is consistent.
