## Domain-by-Domain Deep Assessment

### Domain 64: Community Economic Resilience

**Current state of existing research**

Substantial Phase 1-5 groundwork exists. Phase 3 documented decision-making structures for resource allocation. Phase 5 Wave 2 community implementation playbook explicitly identified resource distribution fairness as an unresolved question. Phase 5 Wave 3 food and water systems document what communities produce; none of it answers how they exchange it. The Phase 6 candidate one-pager (`PHASE_6_CANDIDATE_COMMUNITY_ECONOMIC_RESILIENCE.md`) is complete with scope definition, source library audit, production hours estimate, and June 1 readiness assessment. Agent brief (`PHASE_6_COMMUNITY_ECONOMIC_AGENT_BRIEF.md` in this directory) is copy-paste ready.

**Confidence level**: High. Foundational sources (Ostrom, Graeber, WIR Bank documentation, cooperative movement literature) are stable, freely accessible, and directly applicable. The 2025 Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization study on mutual credit resilience is the most recent addition to the source library.

**Risk factors**:
1. Timebanking quantitative effectiveness data is thin — qualitative case studies substitute but reduce rigor in that subsection. Document gap explicitly; it does not block production.
2. Regional economic simulation models for 200-2,000 person communities are scattered across MIT SUST model and grey literature. Aggregation is feasible in Week 1 but adds 4-6 hours.
3. Midwest-specific agricultural cooperative performance data requires aggregation from the Cooperative Development Foundation. Not a bottleneck — requires patience, not specialized access.

**June 1-30 execution timeline**:
- June 1-7: Source library finalization, author onboarding, research outline
- June 8-15: Complementary currency section + commons governance foundations (5,000-7,000 words)
- June 15-22: Cooperative enterprise models + capital formation (7,000-9,000 words)
- June 22-30: Inter-community trade and federation models (7,000-9,000 words)

### Domain 61: Intergenerational Skills Development

**Current state of existing research**

Phase 5 Wave 3 educational governance document (2,147 words) addresses community schooling structure. That document is the direct ancestor of this domain — it covers school governance; this domain covers adult skill transmission, apprenticeship, and the operational competency layer. Phase 6 candidate one-pager is complete. The key observation is that all Phase 1-5 procedures assume someone knows how to do them — this domain systematizes how that knowledge survives personnel loss.

**Confidence level**: High on apprenticeship and situated learning theory (Lave/Wenger is among the most-cited educational frameworks; empirical foundation is solid). High on Midwest applicability (4-H extension data from USDA land-grant universities is extensive and directly accessible). Medium on learning curve data for specific trades in low-resource settings (practitioner reports available; peer-reviewed data thin).

**Risk factors**:
1. Scope expansion is the primary risk. Educational research literature is deep; without a hard boundary at kickoff (12-15 critical skills, not comprehensive educational system design), the domain expands to 80,000+ words and misses the August 23 target. The agent brief must enforce this boundary explicitly.
2. Learning curve data gap requires a June 8-12 research sprint (email authors of practitioner reports; use FEMA CERT and Red Cross curricula as validated proxies). Adds approximately 5-8 hours to the source verification phase.
3. Author background matters more here than in the other two domains. Without education or adult learning background, add 20-30 hours to the timeline. This affects author selection at June 1 kickoff.

**June 1-30 execution timeline**:
- June 1-7: Source verification, learning curve gap research sprint initiated
- June 8-12: Learning curve data sprint (FEMA/Red Cross proxies + practitioner report emails)
- June 10-22: Critical skills inventory + apprenticeship models (8,000-10,000 words)
- June 22-30: Documentation strategies + intergenerational teaching dynamics (7,000-9,000 words)

### Domain 65: Governance Scaling

**Current state of existing research**

Phase 3 governance documents (`phase-3/01-governance-decision-making.md`, `phase-3/05-scaling-pathways-and-thresholds.md`) are the primary ancestors. They address governance at 20-50 person scale. Phase 5 Wave 2 conflict resolution framework documents acute conflict response. Neither addresses what breaks at 100-500 persons, how federations of communities coordinate, or how institutions learn from governance failures. Regional governance pre-research is embedded in `regional-governance-federation-framework.md`. Phase 6 candidate one-pager is complete.

**Confidence level**: High on foundational sources (Ostrom polycentric governance, Indigenous governance literature, Swiss cantonal system, Christchurch recovery governance). Medium on governance failure case studies in resource-constrained settings — failures are systematically underreported in the literature relative to successes. Low on optimal federation size empirically — this is a genuine research frontier with no rigorous guidance.

**Risk factors**:
1. Source bottleneck is real and the longest of the three domains. Governance failure case studies in resource-constrained settings require dedicated aggregation (kibbutz governance reform studies, Venezuelan parroquia breakdowns, disaster management retrospectives). This adds 6+ hours to Week 1 and delays production start to June 15.
2. Scope expansion risk is high. Governance and institutional change literature is vast. Without weekly scope audits (weeks 3, 6, 9) and a hard ceiling at 150-500 person federation scale, the domain expands into comparative political systems or constitutional law, neither of which serves Zone 5 communities.
3. Author expertise is strongly recommended (political science or public administration). Without it, add 2-3 weeks to the timeline and reduce confidence from 78% to ~65%.
4. Paywall exposure is the highest of three candidates (~30% of desired governance sources). Budget for interlibrary loan or open-access alternative identification.

**June 1-30 execution timeline**:
- June 1-10: Extended source library work (governance failure case study aggregation)
- June 10-15: Outline finalized + production scope confirmed
- June 15-22: Governance failure modes section + federation model research (6,000-8,000 words)
- June 22-30: Consensus at scale + inter-community conflict resolution (6,000-8,000 words)

## Sequencing Analysis

### Can all three run in parallel?

Do not attempt all three in a single 12-week window starting June 1. Combined on-time confidence drops to approximately 65% due to: (a) Governance scope expansion risk amplified by parallel execution, (b) author coordination overhead increasing non-linearly at three domains, (c) no buffer for source bottleneck resolution in Governance.

### Single domain

Select Economic Resilience. Completes August 9 with 95% confidence. Produces the most immediately actionable outputs. Unlocks the most Phase 7 templates. No coordination overhead.

### Two domains

**Recommended: Economic + Skills.** Lowest overhead (no required syncs), independent tracks, 90% confidence for both by August 23. The learning curve gap in Skills resolves independently of Economic production.

**Alternative: Economic + Governance.** More interdependency (July 13 sync required), 90% confidence for Economic by August 9, ~82% confidence for Governance by August 23 (Economic framing reduces Governance scope expansion risk). Higher strategic value — these two domains together provide the complete community decision-making architecture. Choose this pairing if the governance scaling gap is your acute concern.

### Three domains

**Recommended: Economic + Skills June 1, Governance September 1.**
- Phase 6a (June 1-August 23): Economic Resilience + Skills Development in parallel — 90% confidence
- Phase 6b (September 1-October 15): Governance Scaling standalone — 82-85% confidence with ruthless scope management
- All three complete by October 15 at higher quality than a simultaneous June 1 three-domain launch

## User Decision Checklist

Answer these five questions. They route to a domain recommendation.

**Question 1**: What is the acute gap Phase 5 left open for your community?
- [ ] How do community members compensate each other when cash fails? → Economic Resilience is the primary domain
- [ ] How does the community ensure critical skills survive personnel loss? → Skills Development is the primary domain
- [ ] How does governance function when the community grows past 50 people? → Governance Scaling is the primary domain

**Question 2**: How important is fastest delivery (August 9 vs. August 23 vs. August 30)?
- [ ] August 9 matters → Economic Resilience only, or Economic as the leading domain
- [ ] August 23 is acceptable → Any two-domain pairing works
- [ ] October 15 is acceptable → All three domains on the recommended sequencing

**Question 3**: Is a specialist author available for June 1?
- [ ] Yes, education/adult learning background → Skills Development ramp is accelerated; go Economic + Skills
- [ ] Yes, political science/public administration background → Governance Scaling is viable; go Economic + Governance
- [ ] No, general researcher → Economic Resilience only, or Economic + Skills (Skills recovers from non-specialist with 2 extra weeks)

**Question 4**: How much coordination overhead is acceptable?
- [ ] Zero — run independently → Economic only, or Economic + Skills (no required syncs)
- [ ] One sync acceptable → Economic + Governance (July 13 sync)
- [ ] Two syncs acceptable → Governance + Skills (June 29 + July 27)

**Question 5**: Is Governance Scaling actually urgent for your community?
- [ ] Yes — governance deadlock or federation planning is happening now → Include Governance in Phase 6a (accept 78% confidence, add political science author)
- [ ] No — governance is stable at current scale → Defer Governance to Phase 6b September; use June-August for Economic + Skills at 90% confidence
