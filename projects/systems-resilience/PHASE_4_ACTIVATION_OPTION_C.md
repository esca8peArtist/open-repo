## Why This Sequence (Iterative Logic)

Option C publishes in thematic clusters, starting **May 30 (one day before decision!)** and rolling through July 4. This model suits communities that want to:
- **Learn by doing**: Governance is tested and refined weekly as new Phase 5 domains are published
- **Reduce overhead**: No long integration phase; Phase 4 evolves alongside Phase 5
- **Build trust gradually**: Governance decisions are made in small increments with real feedback loops
- **Scalability testing**: Early clusters (May 30: Playbook + Microgrids) are "safe" to test governance; later clusters (June 27: Psychology + Conflict) are higher-stakes and benefit from weekly governance refinement

The trade-off: Governance is less cohesive at launch but more robust and legitimate by mid-July (because it's been stress-tested on real Phase 5 scenarios).

### Key Principles for Weeks 2-6

1. **Decisions are made WITH information, not before it.** (Contrast to Options A/B where governance is pre-made.) Communities read Phase 5 content Thursday evening, decide Thursday night. No "decide in a vacuum" risk.

2. **Governance evolves iteratively.** Assembly amends governance every Thursday based on what Phase 5 taught them. By July 4, governance is not a document that was written once; it's a living operating system that's been stress-tested on real Phase 5 scenarios.

3. **Feedback loop is tight.** If Week 2's decision (conflict resolution authority) breaks in Week 3 (vet care crisis), the community notices and fixes it by Week 3 assembly. Contrast to Options A/B where you don't know governance is broken until August.

4. **Each week's Phase 4 work is contained.** Week 2 focuses on psychology + conflict (authority mapping for mental health + disputes). Week 3 focuses on vet + livestock (authority mapping for animal health). No 14-day "integration" phase; integration is ongoing and weekly.

## Why This Model Works (And Why It's Risky)

**Strengths**:
- **Legitimate**: Governance decisions are made with knowledge of the Phase 5 domains they affect. No surprises on July 4.
- **Adaptive**: If Week 2's psychology decision breaks Week 3's veterinary decision, it's fixed within 7 days.
- **Engaging**: Communities don't feel like governance is being imposed; they're building it collaboratively with Phase 5 content.
- **Stress-testing**: By mid-June, governance has been tested on 3-4 real scenarios. By July 4, it's been tested on all 6 domains.

**Risks**:
- **Slow initial activation**: Community is learning governance and Phase 5 simultaneously. Risk of decision paralysis ("we don't understand Phase 5 content well enough to decide yet").
- **Incomplete authority mapping at launch**: If Phase 5 Week 1 content requires decisions that aren't addressed until Week 2-3, communities might move forward with incomplete authority.
- **Amendment fatigue**: If governance is amended every week, communities can experience "governance whiplash" (too many changes, no stability). Mitigate by limiting to 2-3 amendments per week max.

**Mitigation**:
- Facilitator is highly skilled (can speed reading + decide-making in 2-hour window)
- Phase 5 themes are sequenced to be easier early (playbook + microgrids = safe), harder later (conflict + psychology = high-stakes, but now governance is mature enough)
- "Backward references": Each week's Phase 5 includes a "connections to earlier weeks" section, so governance can stay integrated even when content is rolling
