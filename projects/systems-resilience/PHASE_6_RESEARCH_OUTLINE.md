## Phase 6 Scope: Institutional Permanence & Intergenerational Continuity

Phase 5 delivered community-scale implementation frameworks for crisis response. Phase 6 addresses the long-term question: How do communities sustain resilience infrastructure across generations and maintain institutional capacity during non-crisis periods?

**Scope**: Cross-border infrastructure, diplomatic frameworks, UN Sendai Framework implementation, bilateral/multilateral agreements

**Key Research Areas**:

**Scope**: Indigenous knowledge systems, traditional ecological knowledge preservation, elder-mentee frameworks, cultural transmission during crisis recovery

**Key Research Areas**:
- Intergenerational knowledge transmission in Indigenous communities (kinship structures, rituals, oral traditions)
- Traditional ecological knowledge (TEK) as foundation for adaptive management
- Elder-led mentorship frameworks and knowledge preservation during displacement
- Language preservation and cultural identity as resilience factor
- Institutional structures that anchor knowledge across generations

### Domain 62: Cross-Sector Infrastructure Interdependencies & Systemic Resilience

**Scope**: Critical infrastructure mapping, cascade failure prevention, system-of-systems coordination, interdependency analysis frameworks

**Key Research Areas**:
- Critical Entities Resilience Directive implementation (energy, transport, banking, water, food, health, digital, space)
- Interdependency mapping: power grids ↔ water systems ↔ food systems ↔ healthcare ↔ digital infrastructure
- Cascade failure mechanisms and early-warning systems
- Distributed resilience planning: node criticality analysis, redundancy strategies
- Post-crisis infrastructure prioritization (water → food → energy → healthcare sequence)

**Unique Contribution**: How to design infrastructure networks that don't amplify cascading failures, with redundancy built for critical nodes

**Estimated Effort**: 50–60 hours
**Cross-references**: Phase 5 Wave 1 (infrastructure), Domain 44 (technology networks), Domain 26 (accountability)
**Movement Leverage**: Critical infrastructure protection agencies (CISA, Europol), grid operators, water utilities, telecommunications, academic infrastructure resilience research
**Timeline**: June 5–July 25

### Domain 63: Ecosystem Restoration & Regenerative Agriculture as Resilience Infrastructure

**Scope**: Long-term soil restoration, biodiversity recovery, climate adaptation, food system decentralization through regenerative practices

**Key Research Areas**:
- Regenerative agriculture principles: soil health, carbon sequestration, water retention, biodiversity
- Ecosystem service restoration: pollination, water filtration, nutrient cycling, climate regulation
- Measurement frameworks: soil metrics, biodiversity indicators, carbon accounting, MRV capacity
- Land-use transitions: from degraded → regenerative agricultural systems within 5–10 year cycles
- Role of farmers as ecosystem stewards: economic incentives, market structures, certification standards
- Regional food system resilience: polyculture, seed preservation networks, perennial crop adoption

**Unique Contribution**: How to rebuild soil and ecosystem capacity (the foundation of food resilience) using proven regenerative practices, with measurement frameworks for restoration progress

**Estimated Effort**: 45–55 hours
**Cross-references**: Phase 5 Wave 1 (food systems, environment), Domain 31 (healthcare/food nexus)
**Movement Leverage**: Regenerative Organic Alliance, Soil Conservation Commission networks, permaculture organizations, farmer cooperatives, climate-smart agriculture initiatives
**Timeline**: June 15–August 1

**Key Sources**:
- [Regenerative Agriculture: Restoring Ecosystems' Resilience (Agricultural Reviews)](https://arccjournals.com/journal/agricultural-reviews/R-2779)
- [Rooted in Resilience - CSA News](https://www.sciencesocieties.org/publications/csa-news/2025/august/rooted-in-resilience-regenerative-agriculture-and-the-future-of)

### Domain 64: Economic Resilience & Circular Economy Structures

**Scope**: Resource security, economic diversification, alternative economic models (cooperatives, mutual aid, local economic systems), material loop closure

**Key Research Areas**:
- Circular economy frameworks: closing material loops, reducing extraction dependency, rebuilding supply chain resilience
- Alternative economic models: worker cooperatives, credit unions, timebanking systems, local exchange networks
- Resource sovereignty: local sourcing, distributed manufacturing, supply chain mapping
- Post-crisis economic transitions: from extraction → regeneration, from linear → circular
- Financing mechanisms: community development finance, social enterprises, impact investing for resilience
- Measurement frameworks: circular economy indicators, economic resilience metrics, local resilience indicators

**Unique Contribution**: How to restructure local economies away from brittle, extractive supply chains toward regenerative, circular systems that function during crises and maintain livelihood security

**Estimated Effort**: 45–55 hours
**Cross-references**: Phase 4 (parallel institutions, power mapping), Phase 5 (community coordination)
**Movement Leverage**: Circular economy networks (Ellen MacArthur Foundation), cooperative development organizations (CCEC, ICA), local economy movements, alternative finance networks
**Timeline**: June 10–August 5

**Key Sources**:
- [Circular Economy Economic Imperative (ISEP Global 2026)](https://www.isepglobal.org/resources/blogs/2026/april/circularity-is-an-economic-imperative-not-just-an-environmental-one/)

### Domain 65: Institutional Learning & Long-Term Governance Sustainability

**Scope**: Post-crisis institutional reform, adaptive management frameworks, governance structures that survive leadership transitions, institutional memory systems

**Key Research Areas**:
- Institutional learning cycles: feedback loops, after-action review protocols, embedded adaptation
- Governance reform pathways: from centralized → distributed, from reactive → anticipatory
- Policy continuity mechanisms: institutional structures that outlast political transitions, leadership succession planning
- Discourse institutionalism: How institutional language and legitimate decision-making processes enable long-term coherence
- Institutional resilience case studies: successful governance adaptations, institutional collapse patterns
- Monitoring & adaptation frameworks: Early warning systems, institutional health metrics

**Unique Contribution**: How to design governance institutions that learn, adapt, and survive leadership changes without losing institutional capacity

**Estimated Effort**: 40–50 hours
**Cross-references**: Phase 3 (decision-making), Phase 4 (institutional accountability), Phase 5 Wave 1 (governance)
**Movement Leverage**: Governance research centers, institutional design networks, civil service reform advocates, accountability organizations
**Timeline**: June 20–August 10

**Key Sources**:
