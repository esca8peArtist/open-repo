# Phase 6 Candidate: Intergenerational Skills Development

**One-sentence definition**: How communities preserve, transmit, and scale critical practical knowledge across generations when formal educational institutions, apprenticeship systems, and professional certification are unavailable.

**Domain ID**: Domain 61 (Intergenerational Skills Development)

---

## Research Scope

This domain answers three bounded questions:

**1. Which knowledge and skills are actually critical for community resilience, and what are the learning time requirements?**
Priority skill mapping: medical response, water system maintenance, food preservation, energy system operation, structural repair, materials science basics. Learning time curves: how long does it take an adult to learn well-drilling, manage a solar microgrid, or perform emergency surgery without formal training? Knowledge prerequisites — what foundational knowledge must come first? Case studies: Puerto Rico hurricane recovery, Cuba post-Soviet skill transmission, Christchurch earthquake community rebuild.

**2. How do communities transmit complex technical skills without schools or credentialing institutions?**
Apprenticeship models: one-on-one, small group, peer teaching, learning by doing. Documentation strategies: how to write procedures, create diagrams, preserve knowledge for offline use. Testing and validation: how to certify competence without professional boards. Cultural transmission: embedding skills in narratives, rituals, and social structures. Cross-generational teaching: what fails when 60-year-olds teach teenagers, and what works.

**3. What institutions and governance structures support skill transmission at community scale?**
Learning cooperatives and skill-sharing networks. Knowledge management: libraries, oral history, apprenticeship registries. Resource provision: tools, materials, and time allocation for skill building. Quality assurance: how communities maintain standards when external certification is unavailable. Midwest case studies: 4-H clubs, extension services, apprenticeship networks.

**Out of scope**: Full K-12 curriculum design, formal higher education policy, online learning platforms as primary delivery mechanisms (relevant only as backup documentation method in crisis contexts).

## Unique Contribution

**What this adds that Phases 1-5 do not contain:**

Phase 1-5 documents assume that communities have — or can acquire — the skills to execute documented procedures. That assumption is almost universally false for communities in the early months of crisis. A community whose water system operator dies, whose solar technician ages out, or whose single medical responder is injured loses its entire technical infrastructure unless knowledge was systematically transmitted.

This domain addresses the failure mode that kills communities in the second year: not resource depletion, but skill depletion. The entire Phase 1-5 corpus becomes inert documentation without this domain.

**Multi-generational framing**: This is the only Phase 6 candidate explicitly addressing 10-20 year timescales. The other candidates address current community coordination. Skills Development asks: how does the community's operational knowledge survive one generational turnover?

**Market specificity**: Midwest 4-H infrastructure and Extension Service tradition provide direct implementation anchors. Homeschooling interest post-COVID has created visible demand for structured alternative educational models. The research translates directly to existing Midwest community networks.
