## How May 21 Synthesis Outcome Gates Phase 4

The May 21 synthesis outcome determines:
1. **Whether Phase 4 should proceed at all** (STRONG/MODERATE → yes; WEAK → contingency only; TOO_EARLY → defer)
2. **When Phase 4 should launch** (STRONG → immediate June 1; MODERATE → June 1 or later per user decision)
3. **Which Phase 4 topic(s) to prioritize** (research intensity + timeline tradeoffs)

### Synthesis Outcome Mapping

#### **Synthesis = STRONG** (May 21 outcome)
*Definition: Wave 1 achieved 3+ measurable movement responses OR 2+ institutional responses with 50%+ approval signals*

**Phase 4 Context**: STRONG synthesis indicates successful Phase 1 execution. Resistance-research domains now have documented institutional interest and potential adoption pathways. Systems-resilience Phase 4 should launch in parallel to support institutions implementing governance/food/information infrastructure.

**Phase 4 Decision**: **Proceed immediately with Phase 4 Option A (Full), prioritizing Technology Repair + Governance Scaling**

**Rationale**:
- Institutions implementing Domain 37 (Executive Interference) defense need communication infrastructure (Phase 4's information systems)
- Institutions implementing Domain 56/58 (Civil Service / Tribal Sovereignty) need governance frameworks (Phase 4's governance scaling)
- 2+ institutions actively building need equipment reliability documentation (Phase 4's technology repair)

**Sequencing**:
- June 1: Start Technology Repair research (15–21 hours, June 1–30)
- June 15: Start Governance Scaling research (12–17 hours, June 15–July 15)

**Budget**: 55–79 hours over 4 months; parallel execution with resistance-research Phase 2 scaling

#### **Synthesis = MODERATE** (May 21 outcome)
*Definition: Wave 1 achieved 2+ movement responses OR 1 institutional response with 70%+ approval signals*

**Phase 4 Context**: MODERATE synthesis indicates selective Phase 1 adoption. Some institutions are interested, but not a broad institutional wave. Systems-resilience Phase 4 should launch selectively, focusing on highest-value topics that support existing institutional interest.

**Phase 4 Decision**: **Proceed with Phase 4 Option C (Agricultural Intensification + Technology Repair), deferred 2 weeks**

**Rationale**:
- Not all institutions need governance scaling immediately; focus on practical topics (food, equipment)
- 2-week deferral allows user to confirm which institutions are most engaged (May 21–June 5)
- If additional institutions join June 5–15, upgrade to Option A
- Technology Repair + Agricultural Intensification form the minimum viable Phase 4

**Sequencing**:
- June 15: Start Technology Repair research (15–21 hours, June 15–July 15)
- July 1: Start Agricultural Intensification research (15–22 hours, July 1–Aug 15)
- Aug 15: Both documents production-ready for selective institutional distribution

**Budget**: 30–43 hours over 2 months; can upgrade to Option A if demand increases

#### **Synthesis = WEAK** (May 21 outcome)
*Definition: Wave 1 achieved 1+ movement responses OR 0 institutional responses but no negative signals*

**Phase 4 Context**: WEAK synthesis indicates limited immediate Phase 1 adoption. Institutions are not requesting additional content. Systems-resilience Phase 4 should defer OR focus on Option B (Technology Repair only) as preparatory work for future demand.

**Phase 4 Decision**: **Defer Phase 4, OR proceed with Phase 4 Option B (Technology Repair minimal, 1 month) as contingency**

**Rationale**:
- If synthesis is WEAK, Phase 1 adoption is not generating immediate Phase 4 demand
- Deferring Phase 4 allows focus on resistance-research Phase 2 expansion (Domains 57–59)
- Option B is 15–21 hours (minimal commitment) and provides immediate-use content if demand suddenly increases
- If user wants to proceed, Technology Repair is highest-value, lowest-dependency topic

**Sequencing (if choosing Option B)**:
- July 1: Start Technology Repair research (15–21 hours, July 1–Aug 1)
- Aug 1: Technology Repair documents production-ready
- Aug 15: Evaluate Phase 1 adoption signals; decide whether to continue Phase 4 or defer fully

**Sequencing (if deferring entirely)**:
- May 21–June 1: Focus shifts to resistance-research Phase 2 domain sequencing (Domains 56, 57, 58, 59)
- Phase 4 re-evaluated in September 2026 based on Phase 1 institutional adoption signals

**Budget**: 15–21 hours (Option B only); or 0 hours (full defer)

---

#### **Synthesis = TOO_EARLY** (May 21 outcome)
*Definition: Wave 1 signal log insufficient for classification; user decision required*

**Phase 4 Context**: TOO_EARLY synthesis means Wave 1 data collection window was too short (fewer than 3 domains provided signals). Resistance-research Phase 1 decision is deferred. By extension, systems-resilience Phase 4 should be fully deferred until May 31 synthesis + user decision.

**Phase 4 Decision**: **Full defer until May 31**

**Rationale**:
- TOO_EARLY classification means Phase 1 institutional interest is unclear
- Phase 4 launch depends on understanding Phase 1 adoption (which determines user priorities)
- May 31 provides 10 days of additional signal collection (May 21–31) before re-evaluating

**Sequencing**:
- May 21–31: Extended signal collection (user fills updated log, adds May 24–31 data)
- May 31: Synthesis re-run with full 10-day window → STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK outcome
- June 1: Phase 4 decision made based on May 31 re-synthesis

**Budget**: 0 hours until May 31; full Phase 4 decision deferred
