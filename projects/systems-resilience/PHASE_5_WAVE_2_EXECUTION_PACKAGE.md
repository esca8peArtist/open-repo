## Most Important Finding

Wave 2 source research is substantially complete. The four domains do not have symmetric research burdens: Veterinary Care has the clearest source base (USDA, AVMA, CFSPH documentation is well-developed and recent), Psychological Support has the richest but most contested evidence base (PFA systematic reviews through 2024 confirm effectiveness uncertainty, which must be accurately represented in the document), Conflict Resolution has strong practitioner literature and moderate RCT evidence, and the Tier 3 Community Framework is a synthesis document that depends on the other three more than it depends on external research. The Tier 3 document will require the most integration work, not the most research hours.

The critical sequencing constraint is confirmed: **Tier 3 cannot begin until Veterinary Care, Psychological Support, and Conflict Resolution are all in first-draft form.** The dependency is not incidental — Section 4 of the Tier 3 document (Scaling Phase 5 Tier 2 to Community) can only be written when the household-scale documents are complete enough to extract their community-scale extensions.

### Domain 2: Psychological Support Infrastructure

**Research status**: Rich but contested. The PFA evidence base is actively debated: the 2023 PMC 10624106 systematic review confirmed that while PFA is widely adopted, RCT evidence is limited. A 2024 integrative review in *Psychological Trauma* (Sage) documented implementation variability. Both findings must be represented in the document's evidence caveat — this is not a reason to exclude PFA, but a reason to frame it as an evidence-informed (not evidence-based) approach. Collective trauma recovery literature is more consistent.

### Domain 3: Conflict Resolution Deep Dive

**Research status**: Strong practitioner literature, moderate RCT evidence. NVC has a 2024 PMC scoping review (PMC 10916228) confirming effectiveness in interpersonal conflict reduction, though primarily in healthcare settings. Restorative justice has a 2025 meta-analysis (Fulham et al., Sage) confirming moderate recidivism reduction. The intersection of NVC + restorative circles for community settings remains under-studied, which is accurately what the document claims.

## Section 2: Research and Writing Templates

### Evidence Checklist (Per Section)

Before drafting any section, confirm:

- [ ] Primary source is dated within 5 years where temporal currency matters (USDA shortage data, PMC clinical reviews). Exception: foundational texts (Ostrom 1990, Fisher/Ury 1981, Rosenberg NVC) are cited as-is with original dates.
- [ ] Each empirical claim has at least one PMC or government agency citation. No free-standing claims.
- [ ] Contested evidence is labeled as contested. For PFA: explicitly note limited RCT evidence. For NVC: note healthcare-setting limitation of the 2024 scoping review. For RJ: note that recidivism data comes from criminal justice settings, not community settings.
- [ ] Zone 5-specific framing is present in at least Section 1 (the regional risk profile section) of each document.
- [ ] Interdependencies are cited in the text. Each document cross-references the two documents it depends on and the one document it feeds forward to.
- [ ] Supply cache / implementation section uses only OTC-available or freely accessible resources. Prescription-only items are labeled as requiring veterinary relationship. Clinical-only interventions (medication for depression, professional therapy) are labeled as such.

### Citation Protocol

**Inline format**: `[#]` where # increments from 1. Place at end of the sentence containing the claim, before the period when practical.

**Footnote format** (document bottom, numbered list):
```
[1] USDA NIFA, "Veterinary Services Shortage Situations Map," accessed 2026. https://www.nifa.usda.gov/vmlrp-map
[2] AVMA, "Addressing Rural Veterinary Shortages," 2025. https://www.avma.org/advocacy/addressing-rural-veterinary-shortages-must-animal-and-public-health
```

**Internal document cross-reference format**:
```
(→ Phase 3: Governance Document, Section 2)
```

**Evidence qualifier tags** (use inline before citation when needed):
- `[evidence: strong]` — multiple RCTs or systematic reviews agree
- `[evidence: contested]` — systematic reviews present, findings inconsistent
- `[evidence: practitioner consensus]` — widely adopted, limited RCT evidence
- `[evidence: case study only]` — no systematic review; cite the best available case study

### Outline-to-Draft Process (5 Steps)

**Step 1 — Section scaffold (30 min)**
Write the section headings and one-sentence summary of what each section argues. Do not write prose yet. Confirm the scaffold matches the PHASE_5_WAVE_2_PLANNING.md outline for that document. Adjust only if a source discovered in staging contradicts the planned structure. Log any structural changes.

**Step 2 — Source mapping (45 min)**
For each section in the scaffold, list which sources from Section 1 of this document map to it. Identify gaps (sections with fewer than 2 Primary sources). If a gap exists, run one targeted search before proceeding. Do not proceed to Step 3 with any section that has zero Primary sources.

**Step 3 — First draft (2–3 hours)**
Write each section in full. Write the Most Important Finding paragraph last (after all sections are drafted — it should reflect what actually emerged, not what was planned). Citation numbers are placeholders at this stage; resolve in Step 4.

Draft length targets:
- Veterinary Care: 1,100–1,250 words per section × 5 sections + intro = 6,500–7,500 words
- Psychological Support: 1,100–1,350 words per section × 6 sections + intro = 7,000–8,000 words
- Conflict Resolution: 1,000–1,175 words per section × 6 sections + intro = 6,000–7,000 words
- Tier 3: 1,250–1,500 words per section × 6 sections + intro = 7,500–9,000 words

**Step 4 — Citation resolution (45 min)**
Resolve all citation placeholders. Verify URLs load. Confirm citation format matches the protocol above. Count total citations — if below the minimum for the document (28 for Vet Care, 30 for Psych Support, 25 for Conflict Resolution, 35 for Tier 3), identify and add missing sources.

**Step 5 — Integration pass (30 min)**
Read the completed draft against the PHASE_5_WAVE_2_PLANNING.md outline for that document. Confirm: (a) all key sections from the planning document are present or explicitly de-scoped, (b) the Most Important Finding leads with the most substantive insight, not a summary of contents, (c) Zone 5 regional specificity is present throughout, not just in the first section, (d) interdependencies section is complete. Update YAML frontmatter word_count and citation_count.
