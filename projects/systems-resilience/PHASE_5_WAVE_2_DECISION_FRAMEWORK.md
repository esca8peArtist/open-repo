## Decision 1: Wave 2 Sequencing — Vet-First or Psychological-First?

### The Recommendation

**Psychological Support first** is the recommendation from the Wave 2 Planning document, and preliminary drafting has confirmed the reasoning. However, this decision belongs to the user. The considerations below are intended to support that decision, not to make it.

### Option A: Psychological-First (Recommended in Planning Document)

**Sequence**: Psychological Support → Conflict Resolution → Veterinary Care → Tier 3

**Rationale**:
1. Highest-impact gap: mental health is the dominant health burden in extended disruption (SAMHSA), and no existing document in the Phase 1-4 corpus addresses it. The veterinary gap is also real but already partially addressed through Seedwarden's husbandry content and the individual agriculture document.
2. Documents 2 and 3 (Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution) are structurally coupled — they share a de-escalation layer. Writing them consecutively produces a stronger pair than writing them with Veterinary Care between them.
3. Zone 5 winter amplifier: the Psych Support document's most critical content (winter depression, caregiver burnout, grief infrastructure) is directly relevant to the November–March period. If execution begins in July, the document would be production-ready before the Zone 5 winter window.
4. No specialized equipment knowledge required: Psych Support and Conflict Resolution can be researched and written with existing knowledge base; they do not require verification of rapidly-changing regulatory information (unlike Veterinary Care's OTC medication status).

**Risk**: The food security argument for Veterinary Care first is real. Livestock loss in a Zone 5 extended disruption is a potentially catastrophic food security event. If the user's primary concern is agricultural resilience, this risk matters.

### Option B: Veterinary-First

**Sequence**: Veterinary Care → Psychological Support → Conflict Resolution → Tier 3

**Rationale**:
1. Food security priority: livestock death from a preventable disease event is an irreversible loss. Psychological distress is recoverable; livestock is not.
2. Regulatory research window: the OTC medication status questions (Banamine, LA-200) need to be verified before the document is production-ready. Doing this research earlier in the Wave 2 window is better than deferring it.
3. Independence: Veterinary Care can be produced independently of Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution. If only one session is available before July, Veterinary Care is the cleanest standalone.

**Risk**: By separating Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution across a Veterinary Care session in the middle, the de-escalation content shared between them requires additional integration work. This is manageable but adds friction.

**Trade-off matrix**:

| Criterion | Psychological-First | Veterinary-First |
|---|---|---|
| Gap severity (existing corpus) | Higher (no psych coverage) | Medium (partial coverage via Seedwarden) |
| Zone 5 winter relevance | Direct (Nov–Mar amplifier) | Indirect (disease calendar is seasonal) |
| Research independence | Yes — Psych/Conflict pair produces cleaner output | Yes — Vet is fully standalone |
| Regulatory time-sensitivity | Low | High (OTC medication status needs verification) |
| Food security argument | Indirect | Direct |

**Recommendation held**: Psychological-first. The key insight is that Psych + Conflict together produce more than the sum of their parts because of the shared de-escalation layer. This paired production benefit is lost if Veterinary Care is placed between them.

## Decision 2: Parallel vs. Serial Execution

### The Three Options

This decision is the most consequential for completion timing. The options are reproduced from the Execution Package with the preliminary draft context added.

**Option A: Serial (Sequential)**

One document completed before the next begins.

- Elapsed time: ~13 weeks (July 16 – October 15)
- Agent requirement: 1 agent throughout
- Cross-document risk: lowest
- Phase 5 completion: October 15 (misses any September target)

**When to choose**: User or execution context constrains to one active session at a time. Quality priority over timeline priority.

**Option B: Parallel Aggressive**

Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution run simultaneously as parallel agents. Veterinary Care begins as they near completion. Tier 3 begins when Psych + Conflict are in first-draft form.

- Elapsed time: ~8 weeks (July 16 – September 10)
- Agent requirement: 2 simultaneous agents for 3.5 weeks (July 16 – August 10)
- Cross-document risk: moderate (the de-escalation layer is shared between Psych and Conflict; requires coordination)
- Phase 5 completion: September 10

**The coordination mechanism for parallel Psych + Conflict**: The de-escalation overlap (PFA's de-escalation actions from the Psych Support guide and NVC facilitation skills from the Conflict Resolution guide) should be drafted once and incorporated by reference in both documents. Designate which document owns the canonical text; the other cross-references it.

**When to choose**: 2 agents available July 16–August 10; September completion matters.

**Option C: Parallel Conservative**

Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution run simultaneously. Veterinary Care and Tier 3 run serially after both complete.

- Elapsed time: ~12.5 weeks (July 16 – October 10)
- Agent requirement: 2 agents for 3.5 weeks only
- Cross-document risk: low-moderate
- Phase 5 completion: October 10 (close to Serial option but gains the Psych/Conflict coupling benefit)

**When to choose**: 2 agents available for the initial parallel window; October completion acceptable; quality of the Psych/Conflict pair is a priority.

### Decision Matrix

| Factor | Option A (Serial) | Option B (Parallel Aggressive) | Option C (Parallel Conservative) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Completion date | October 15 | September 10 | October 10 |
| Agent requirement | 1 throughout | 2 for 3.5 weeks | 2 for 3.5 weeks |
| Psych/Conflict pair quality | Good (sequential but separate) | Requires coordination overhead | Highest (parallel with coordination) |
| Tier 3 prerequisites met | Yes (all complete before Tier 3) | Mostly (Vet Care in-process when Tier 3 begins) | Yes (all complete before Tier 3) |
| Overall risk | Lowest | Moderate | Low |

**Recommendation**: Option B if 2 agents are available and September completion matters. Option C if quality of prerequisites for Tier 3 is the priority. Option A if agent constraints are binding.

## Decision 3: Tier 3 Scope

### Full vs. Abbreviated

**Full scope** (default, 7,500–9,000 words, 35–45 citations):
- Develops all six sections of the Community Implementation Playbook in full
- Includes the 18-month federation timeline with detailed milestone checklists
- Includes the complete Ostrom application with Zone 5 resource governance examples
- Includes the delegate selection and communication protocol in full
- Is the definitive capstone of the Phase 5 corpus
- Estimated: 4–5 hours synthesis + 30–35 hours drafting

**Abbreviated scope** (~4,000 words, 20–25 citations):
- Maps Phase 5 household prerequisites onto Phase 3 community activation requirements (the activation matrix from Section 5)
- Provides the federation timeline in summary form (milestone markers, not detailed checklists)
- Cross-references rather than fully developing the Ostrom commons governance application
- Serves as a bridge document rather than a standalone capstone
- Estimated: 2–3 hours synthesis + 12–15 hours drafting

**Trade-offs**:
- The activation matrix (Section 5) is the most load-bearing section in the Tier 3 document. Even the abbreviated version should develop this fully.
- The Ostrom commons governance section (Section 2) is the most practically useful section for users building actual community governance. Abbreviating it loses significant practical value.
- The 18-month timeline (Section 6) is the most actionable section. Abbreviating it to milestone markers still provides decision-making utility.

**Recommendation**: Full scope. The Tier 3 document is the capstone of the entire Phase 5 corpus — it integrates four Tier 2 documents and five Phase 3 documents. Abbreviating it produces a cross-reference document rather than a usable operational guide. The additional session time is well-spent at this stage of the corpus.

**Exception**: If execution timeline is constrained and October completion is required with Option A (Serial), the Tier 3 abbreviated scope allows compressing the last session from 4–5 hours to 2–3 hours. This is the only legitimate reason to choose abbreviated scope.

## Decision 4: Farm Equipment Repair — Phase 5 or Deferred?

### The Gap

Phase 4 Synthesis Framework Gap 4 identified farm equipment repair (Zone 5 diesel tractor, small engine, and solar system failure modes) as a meaningful gap. It was not included in Wave 2 because: (a) it requires different research expertise than the four Wave 2 documents; (b) the gap is less acute than the four Wave 2 documents in most extended disruption scenarios; (c) adding it to Wave 2 creates a fifth parallel track or extends the serial timeline.

### Arguments for Including in Phase 5

- Zone 5 agricultural dependence on tractor function is high: without a working tractor, planting and harvest capacity drops dramatically
- Equipment repair skills decay quickly without reference material; a gap in this domain is practically costly
- The gap has no existing coverage in any document in the current corpus

### Arguments for Deferring to a Future Phase

- The research domain is fundamentally different from the four Wave 2 documents — it requires hands-on equipment knowledge, manufacturer documentation, and parts availability research that does not overlap with the human-centered domains of Wave 2
- Adding it extends Wave 2 from four documents to five without a natural coupling
- A standalone equipment repair guide could be commissioned as a Phase 6 document or as a standalone Tier 2 supplement without disrupting Wave 2 momentum

**Recommendation**: Defer to Phase 6 or standalone supplement. Add to the Phase 5 completion documentation as an explicitly acknowledged gap with a forward-reference to future production. Do not add to Wave 2.

**Exception**: If the user prioritizes agricultural food security above psychological resilience infrastructure, the equipment repair document could replace the Tier 3 scope decision (run equipment repair in Wave 2b instead of full Tier 3 scope). This is a non-default option.
