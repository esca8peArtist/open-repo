# Phase 5 Wave 2 Decision Framework

> **Purpose**: Structured decision package for June 1 direction-setting. Three decisions required: execution sequencing, Tier 3 scope, and whether a second writer is needed. Once made, July 16 execution begins immediately from the 35% preliminary drafts already in place.
>
> **Wave 2 scope**: Four documents — Veterinary Care Guide (6,500–7,500 words), Psychological Support Guide (7,000–8,000 words), Conflict Resolution Framework (6,000–7,000 words), Community Implementation Playbook / Tier 3 (7,500–9,000 words). Total: ~27,000–31,500 words across four documents.
>
> **Wave 1 baseline**: Complete. ~14,800 words, 59 citations, production-ready. Includes Tier 1 (Individual Education and Pedagogy) and Tier 2 (Household Coordination Infrastructure Guide).

---

## Lead Finding

The execution sequencing decision is the only truly time-sensitive choice. All three execution options (A, B, C) produce the same total production volume — approximately 160–180 hours — but differ in elapsed calendar time and whether a second writer is required. If September completion is a real constraint, Option B (Partial Parallel) with a second writer is the only path that achieves it. If October completion is acceptable, Option A (Serial) requires no second writer, no coordination overhead, and produces the highest per-document quality by allowing focused expertise on each topic without cross-document interference.

The Tier 3 scope decision is the second most consequential choice. The Community Implementation Playbook is the capstone document that activates the entire Phase 5 corpus. Abbreviating it saves roughly 15–20 hours of drafting but produces a cross-reference document rather than a usable operational guide. Full scope is recommended unless a timeline hard constraint forces the tradeoff.

## Part 1: Execution Options Analysis

### The Three Options

All cost estimates below assume the existing primary writer (current rate, approximately $25–35/hour equivalent for research-and-draft production) as the baseline. Second writer costs assume a freelance specialist at $40–75/hour depending on credential level.

---

### Option A: Serial (One Topic at a Time)

**Sequence**: Psychological Support → Conflict Resolution → Veterinary Care → Tier 3 Community Framework

**Why this order**: Psychological Support and Conflict Resolution are structurally coupled — they share a de-escalation layer — and produce stronger output when written consecutively. Veterinary Care is more independent and contains time-sensitive regulatory verification (OTC medication status). Tier 3 is a synthesis document that structurally cannot begin until the other three are in first-draft form.

**Timeline**: July 16 – October 15 (13 weeks)

| Document | Start | End | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Psychological Support | July 16 | Aug 1 | 4.5 weeks |
| Conflict Resolution | Aug 1 | Sept 1 | 4.5 weeks |
| Veterinary Care | Sept 1 | Sept 15 | ~3 weeks (research pre-staged) |
| Tier 3 Playbook (full scope) | Sept 15 | Oct 15 | 4.5 weeks |

**Hours**: ~160–180 total (single writer throughout)

**Cost**:
- Primary writer only: $0–2,000 depending on writer compensation model
- No second writer required
- No coordination overhead

**Quality profile**: Highest per-document focus. Each topic receives undivided research and writing attention. The Psych/Conflict pair benefits most from consecutive execution — the facilitator skills and PFA de-escalation layer that span both documents can be drafted once and integrated without cross-session inconsistency. Veterinary Care's regulatory verification bottlenecks (OTC medication status, goat dystocia protocol sourcing) can be resolved cleanly without timeline pressure.

**Tradeoffs**:
- Veterinary Care is not production-ready until late September. Zone 5 readers who need the livestock health guidance will not have it until then.
- October 15 completion misses any September target by six weeks.
- No author coordination risk.

**When to choose**: Agent constraints limit parallel capability; quality-over-timeline priority; October completion acceptable.

---

### Option B: Partial Parallel (Two Topics Concurrently)

**Sequence**: (Psychological Support + Conflict Resolution simultaneously) → Veterinary Care → Tier 3

**Why parallel for the Psych/Conflict pair**: These two documents share a de-escalation layer — PFA's de-escalation actions (Psychological Support) and NVC facilitation skills (Conflict Resolution) overlap significantly enough that parallel development with a shared canonical de-escalation text is more efficient than serial development. The shared text is drafted once, owned by the Psychological Support document, and cross-referenced in Conflict Resolution.

**Timeline**: July 16 – September 10 (8 weeks)

| Document | Start | End | Duration | Agent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Psychological Support | July 16 | Aug 10 | 3.5 weeks | Agent 1 |
| Conflict Resolution | July 16 | Aug 10 | 3.5 weeks | Agent 2 |
| Veterinary Care | Aug 1 | Sept 1 | 4.5 weeks | Agent 1 (continuing) |
| Tier 3 Playbook (full scope) | Aug 10 | Sept 10 | 4.5 weeks | Agent 2 (continuing) |

**Hours**: ~160–180 total, compressed to 8 elapsed weeks

**Cost**:
- Primary writer: $0–1,500
- Second writer (for the 3.5-week parallel window): $2,000–3,500 depending on credential level and domain assignment
  - If second writer handles Conflict Resolution: generalist community mediation background sufficient; lower rate ($40–50/hr)
  - If second writer handles Psychological Support: licensed therapist or counselor background preferred; higher rate ($60–75/hr)
- Coordination overhead: 4–6 hours for de-escalation layer alignment, shared terminology, and integration pass

**Quality profile**: Good. The Psych/Conflict pair benefits from parallel development if the coordination mechanism is enforced (designate Psychological Support as owner of the canonical de-escalation text before drafts begin; Conflict Resolution cross-references rather than redevelops it). Veterinary Care runs cleanly as a standalone after the parallel window. Tier 3 begins Aug 10 when Psych and Conflict are in first draft — note that Veterinary Care will still be in progress at that point, so Tier 3's Section 4.1 (Community Veterinary Cooperative) must be flagged as a pending integration task and completed after Veterinary Care's first draft is available.

**Tradeoffs**:
- Requires author vetting and hiring before July 16 (approximately 4–6 weeks lead time for a qualified second writer; hiring decision needed by June 1).
- Coordination overhead is real but manageable with the shared de-escalation layer protocol.
- September 10 completion is achievable — the only option that meets a September target.
- Tier 3 is written with Veterinary Care in-process, creating one integration task deferred to the final pass.

**When to choose**: Second writer is available or can be hired; September completion is a real constraint; Psych/Conflict parallel coupling benefit is a priority.

---

### Option C: Full Parallel (All Four Topics Simultaneously)

**Sequence**: All four documents in parallel across one 8-week window, using 2–3 specialized writers.

**Timeline**: June–July (8-week parallel window, earlier start possible)

**Hours**: ~160–180 total, distributed across 2–3 writers

**Cost**:
- Primary writer (Veterinary Care, owns the most specialized content): $0–1,000
- Second writer (Psychological Support, licensed therapist background): $3,500–5,500
- Third writer (Conflict Resolution + Community Framework, or Conflict Resolution only with primary handling Tier 3): $2,500–4,000
- Total writer cost: $6,000–10,500
- Coordination overhead: 8–12 hours (terminology alignment, cross-reference protocol, integration passes)

**Quality profile**: Varies significantly by author capability. Veterinary Care requires RVT or small-animal DVM background to handle diagnostic triage protocols credibly — the 35% preliminary draft exists but the triage decision trees and obstetric emergency protocols need practitioner review. Psychological Support requires licensed therapist or counselor background to navigate the PFA evidence caveat and the suicide warning signs section accurately. Conflict Resolution and Community Framework can be handled by a writer with community organizing or restorative justice background at lower credential (and lower cost) level.

The critical quality risk: if wrong authors are selected, the document may pass internal review but fail practitioner scrutiny — particularly in the Veterinary Care triage section and the Psychological Support clinical framing. Hiring and vetting overhead is 4–6 weeks minimum for qualified candidates.

**Tier 3 dependency cascade risk (high)**: Tier 3 Section 4 (Scaling Phase 5 Tier 2 to Community) requires the other three documents in draft form before it can be written accurately. Under full parallel execution, all four documents develop simultaneously, which means Tier 3's most load-bearing section is written without its source material. This is the primary quality risk of Option C.

**Tradeoffs**:
- Fastest possible timeline (June–July completion if started immediately).
- Highest cost ($6,000–10,500 in writer fees).
- Highest coordination burden.
- Highest quality risk (wrong author selection; Tier 3 dependency cascade).
- Requires immediate hiring decisions, not June 1 decisions.

**When to choose**: Completion urgency is the overriding constraint; budget permits $6,000–10,000 in writer fees; author vetting resources are available immediately.

## Part 2: Decision Matrix — 9 Combinations

The table below scores each combination of execution option and Tier 3 scope on three dimensions:
- **Cost**: 1 (lowest) to 3 (highest)
- **Timeline**: 1 (longest elapsed) to 3 (shortest elapsed)
- **Quality**: 1 (lowest risk) to 3 (highest risk — note inverted: 3 = highest quality risk, not highest quality)

| | Tier 3: Minimal (Wave 2 only, Tier 3 deferred) | Tier 3: Standard (Wave 2 + abbreviated playbook ~4,000 words) | Tier 3: Comprehensive (Wave 2 + full playbook 7,500–9,000 words) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Option A: Serial** | Cost: 1, Time: 1, Quality: 1 | Cost: 1, Time: 1, Quality: 1 | Cost: 1, Time: 1, Quality: 1 |
| **Option B: Partial Parallel** | Cost: 2, Time: 3, Quality: 2 | Cost: 2, Time: 2, Quality: 2 | Cost: 2, Time: 2, Quality: 2 |
| **Option C: Full Parallel** | Cost: 3, Time: 3, Quality: 3 | Cost: 3, Time: 3, Quality: 3 | Cost: 3, Time: 3, Quality: 3 |

**Reading the matrix**: Option A + Comprehensive Tier 3 is the lowest cost, lowest timeline efficiency, and highest quality combination. Option C + any Tier 3 scope is the highest cost, fastest timeline, and highest quality risk. Option B + Comprehensive Tier 3 is the recommended combination — it meets a September target, requires only one additional writer for 3.5 weeks, and produces the full capstone document without the Tier 3 dependency cascade risk of Option C.

**Recommended combination**: Option B (Partial Parallel) + Tier 3 Comprehensive. This is the only combination that meets a September completion target while maintaining Tier 3's full operational value and containing quality risk through the coordinated de-escalation layer protocol.

## Part 3: Tier 3 Scope Framework

The Community Implementation Playbook (Tier 3) is the capstone document of the entire Phase 5 corpus. It connects the household-scale Tier 2 documents to the Phase 3 community-scale corpus, answering the question no existing document addresses: how do internally coherent households federate into a functioning 50–150 person community?

### Option A (Minimal): Wave 2 Complete Only — Tier 3 Deferred Indefinitely

**Scope**: Veterinary Care, Psychological Support, and Conflict Resolution complete. No Tier 3 Community Playbook.

**What this produces**: Three standalone Tier 2 household documents. The Phase 3 community-scale corpus remains effectively unconnected to Phase 5 household work — the activation bridge does not exist.

**Cost/Timeline**: Saves approximately 35–50 drafting hours (4.5-week session eliminated). Phase 5 "complete" by early September under any parallel option, or by September 15 under Option A serial.

**Quality impact**: Significant. Phase 3 governance, food systems, security, and scaling documents assume household-level prerequisites that only Phase 5 builds. Without the Tier 3 bridge, practitioners building toward community coordination have no integration guidance. The corpus ends at household scale.

**When appropriate**: Budget or timeline constraint is absolute; the three Tier 2 documents are the operational priority; Tier 3 can be produced as a standalone supplement in a later phase.

### Option B (Standard): Wave 2 + Abbreviated Playbook (~4,000 words)

**Scope**: All four Wave 2 documents complete. Tier 3 produces the activation matrix (mapping Phase 5 household prerequisites to Phase 3 activation requirements), the 18-month federation timeline in summary form, and an Ostrom commons governance overview with cross-references rather than full development.

**Cost/Timeline**: Saves approximately 15–20 drafting hours relative to full scope. Reduces Tier 3 from a 4.5-week session to approximately 3 weeks. Phase 5 completion advances by 2 weeks under any option.

**Quality impact**: Moderate. The activation matrix is the most load-bearing section and is fully developed even in the abbreviated version. The federation mechanics, delegate selection protocol, and full Ostrom application are abbreviated to summary form with cross-references. Practitioners can use the abbreviated document for orientation but will need additional guidance for implementation.

**When appropriate**: Timeline compression is needed within the Tier 3 session; the three Tier 2 documents are the primary deliverables; Tier 3 is a bridge document rather than a standalone operational guide.

### Option C (Comprehensive): Wave 2 + Full Playbook (7,500–9,000 words)

**Scope**: All four Wave 2 documents complete. Tier 3 is the full capstone: federation problem and models analysis (Section 1), complete Ostrom application with Zone 5 examples (Section 2), delegate selection and communication protocol (Section 3), full community-scale extensions of all three Tier 2 documents (Section 4), Phase 3 activation matrix (Section 5), and detailed 18-month implementation timeline with milestone checklists (Section 6).

**Cost/Timeline**: Full 4.5-week session. October 15 completion under Option A; September 10 under Option B.

**Quality impact**: Highest. This is the definitive capstone of the Phase 5 corpus. The Ostrom commons governance application with Zone 5 resource governance examples, the full delegate selection protocol, and the 18-month implementation timeline are the most practically useful sections — abbreviating any of them reduces the document from an operational guide to a reference document.

**Recommendation**: Option C (Comprehensive). The production cost difference between Standard and Comprehensive is approximately 15–20 hours. The operational value difference is substantial — the Ostrom application and 18-month timeline are the sections most likely to be used by practitioners building actual community governance. Abbreviating the capstone document of a multi-phase corpus to save 15 hours is not a favorable tradeoff.
