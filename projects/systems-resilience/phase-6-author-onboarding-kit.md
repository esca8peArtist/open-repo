## 2. Your Contribution Scope — Domain A

**What you are writing**: The 38,200 to 48,200 new words that, combined with the existing 6,800-word pre-research chapter, produce the full Domain A document. You are not starting from zero. The pre-research chapter (`phase-6/01-community-economic-resilience.md`) handles economic failure scenarios, cooperative basics, credit unions, mutual aid, and complementary currencies. Read it before writing anything.

**The three research questions your work must answer**:

1. What economic architectures sustain exchange when the dollar fails? (local currencies with documented designs, mutual credit protocols, barter coordination at 50 to 500 person scale, moving beyond the double-coincidence problem)
2. How do communities allocate capital and coordinate production without market price signals? (cooperative formation, commons governance applying Ostrom's eight design principles, community land trusts, the economic logic of specialization under alternative exchange systems)
3. What economic bridges prevent community isolation? (inter-community trade protocols, comparative advantage at regional scale, Zone 5 regional food hub models, inter-community dispute resolution for economic agreements)

**Sections you are responsible for** (the pre-research chapter covers Sections 1 and part of 2):

- Section 3: Policy barriers and reform pathways — cooperative formation law, USDA Rural Cooperative Development Grant mechanics, credit union chartering, state-level enabling legislation
- Section 4: International case studies with Zone 5 transferability — WIR Bank (depth treatment), Mondragon (governance evolution not just worker ownership), Emilia-Romagna (regional cooperative density effect), Argentina Red de Trueque (scale-up under acute crisis conditions)
- Section 5: Midwest-specific implementation — Zone 5 cooperative inventory, Iowa and Minnesota food hub data, agricultural cooperative governance models applicable to broader community resource management
- Section 6: Inter-community economic architecture — this is the gap not covered in any Phase 1 to 5 document, and it is the highest-priority new research in Domain A
- Section 7: Implementation roadmap — minimum viable exchange network (no external capital, no legislation), phase-two (USDA grant access), phase-three (dense cooperative economy)

**Out of scope**: National monetary policy. Federal Reserve dynamics. Corporate finance. Ancient monetary history (anything before 1900 unless directly analogous). International development economics beyond the four case study regions. Do not let the literature pull you into macroeconomics — every sentence should answer "what does a Zone 5 community organizer do with this?"

## 3. Format and Style Guide

**Model document**: Read `phase-5-wave-2-community-implementation-playbook.md` before writing your first paragraph. That document sets the tone, section structure, depth, and citation density we are targeting. It is the closest stylistic match to what Domain A should produce.

**Section length**: 6,000 to 9,000 words per section. Subdivide with H3 headers every 800 to 1,200 words. Avoid sections longer than 10,000 words without subdivision.

**Citations**: Inline Markdown footnote format — `[1]` in text, numbered reference list at section end. Every factual claim has a citation. Every quoted statistic has a citation with the specific data source, not just the publication. No citation padding — cite sources because you are relying on them, not to inflate the count.

**Tone markers to maintain**:

- Practitioner voice, not academic voice. "Here is how to do this" not "research suggests that."
- Zone 5 specificity throughout. Do not write a general cooperative formation guide and add a Zone 5 paragraph at the end. Build Zone 5 into every section.
- Honest uncertainty. If the evidence is weak (timebanking quantitative data, for example), say so explicitly. Practitioners trust documents that acknowledge limits.
- Active present tense for current institutions. "Mondragon operates 260 cooperatives" not "Mondragon was founded in 1956 and has grown."

**Headers**: Title Case for H2, sentence case for H3 and below. Match the heading structure in the model document.

**Length calibration**: The existing pre-research chapter is 6,800 words. A typical section in Phase 5 Wave 2 runs 8,000 to 10,000 words. You are producing five to six sections at that depth. If a section is running short (under 5,000 words), you have either missed a subsection or merged two topics that should be separate. Check the scope questions above.
