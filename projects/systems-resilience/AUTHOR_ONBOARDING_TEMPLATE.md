## Template 2: Confirmation Email (After Agreement Signed)

**Subject**: Phase 6 Research — Welcome + June 1 Onboarding Materials

Hi [NAME],

Welcome to the project. Signed agreement and first milestone payment ($[AMOUNT]) confirmation are attached. Here is everything you need to begin.

**Your production window**: June 10 – August 9, 2026  
**Onboarding week**: June 1–9 (reading, source verification, research outline)

**Files I am sharing with you now** (in the project folder I am adding you to):

1. **Model document**: `phase-5-wave-2-community-implementation-playbook.md` — read this first. It is the closest stylistic match to what you are producing. Tone, section structure, citation density, Zone 5 specificity — this is the target.
2. **Domain candidate file**: `[RELEVANT DOMAIN CANDIDATE FILENAME]` — scope definition, the three research questions your document must answer, hard out-of-scope boundaries, and source readiness notes.
3. **Author onboarding kit**: `phase-6-author-onboarding-kit.md` — project history, your specific contribution scope, format and style guide, success criteria, and support available.
4. **Pre-research chapter**: `[RELEVANT PRE-RESEARCH FILENAME]` — existing research you are building on, not replacing. Read before writing.
5. **Source library**: [FOLDER LINK] — 30–40 pre-staged sources with annotation: what each source covers, which sections it primarily supports, and whether it is open access or may need an alternative.

**First week checklist**:

By June 4: Confirm you have read the model document and the domain candidate file. Send me a 2–3 sentence summary of the three research questions in your own words. This confirms we are aligned on scope before production begins.

By June 7: Complete source library verification (see the task description in the source library folder). Flag any inaccessible sources with proposed alternatives.

By June 9: Submit your section-by-section research outline (10–12 sections, 2–3 sentences scope per section, primary sources assigned to each, word count estimate per section, Zone 5 implication note per section).

**Checkpoint schedule** (non-negotiable dates):
- June 29: Sections 1–4 submitted for review (10,000–15,000 words, 60+ citations)
- July 13: Sections 1–6 submitted (25,000–30,000 words, all case studies underway)
- July 27: Full draft submitted (40,000–45,000 words, all sections, 2 implementation templates)
- August 9: Final production-ready document delivered

**Communication**:
- Weekly status email by Friday 17:00 [YOUR TIME ZONE]: current word count, any blocking issues, any scope questions. I will respond by end of week.
- If something is blocking you — a source you cannot access, a scope question, uncertainty about a section — flag it in the Friday note, not the week before the checkpoint. Early flags are easy to resolve; late flags can affect the timeline.

**My response time**: I will review and respond to draft submissions within 48 hours. Feedback is structured as: (a) factual corrections, (b) citation needs, (c) Zone 5 specificity notes, (d) scope observations. I will not restructure your sections or rewrite your prose — the editorial role is to catch errors and scope drift, not to take over your document.

Looking forward to working with you.

[YOUR NAME]

## First-Week Checklist (Author Self-Tracking)

Use this to track your own onboarding progress. Mark each item complete before moving to production.

### June 1–2: Administrative
- [ ] Signed agreement returned to Orchestrator
- [ ] Confirmed first milestone payment received or in process
- [ ] Accepted project file folder access invitation
- [ ] Confirmed Slack/email communication channel with Orchestrator

### June 1–4: Orientation Reading
- [ ] Read model document: `phase-5-wave-2-community-implementation-playbook.md` — note tone, section structure, citation format
- [ ] Read domain candidate file — mark the three research questions; note the out-of-scope boundaries
- [ ] Read author onboarding kit — confirm your assigned sections
- [ ] Read pre-research chapter — note what is already established and what sections you are responsible for building new

### June 4: Scope Alignment Confirmation
- [ ] Send Orchestrator a 2–3 sentence summary of the three research questions in your own words
- [ ] Note one potential scope expansion risk you identified in the domain candidate file (shows you read the out-of-scope boundaries)

### June 3–7: Source Library Verification
- [ ] Open source library folder
- [ ] Work through each source: confirm URL is accessible; note open-access vs. paywall
- [ ] For each paywall source: identify whether the abstract is sufficient or full text is needed
- [ ] For each inaccessible source: propose 1–2 alternatives on the same topic
- [ ] Submit verified source list to Orchestrator by June 7 (Google Sheet or markdown table)

### June 5–9: Research Outline
- [ ] Draft 10–12 section outline
- [ ] Each section: 2–3 sentence scope description + primary sources assigned + word count estimate + Zone 5 implication note + out-of-scope note
- [ ] Confirm minimum 2 implementation templates are planned (note which sections they appear in)
- [ ] Submit research outline to Orchestrator by June 9

### June 9: Orientation Complete Check
- [ ] Scope alignment confirmed (summary sent and acknowledged)
- [ ] Source list verified and submitted
- [ ] Research outline submitted
- [ ] Ready to begin production writing June 10
