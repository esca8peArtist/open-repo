# Phase 6 Candidate: Governance Scaling + Institutional Adaptation

**One-sentence definition**: How governance structures that work at one scale (household, village) are extended to coordinate multiple communities, and how those decision-making institutions learn from failures to prevent repeated failure modes.

**Domain ID**: Domain 65 (Governance Scaling + Institutional Adaptation)

---

## Research Scope

This domain answers three bounded questions:

**1. What governance structures allow multiple communities to coordinate decisions without a central authority?**
Federation models: how communities maintain autonomy while coordinating on shared challenges (energy, water, supply). Consensus and deliberation protocols that work at 500+ person scale. Inter-community conflict resolution and dispute mechanisms. Information flow and communication cascades across federations. Case studies: Swiss cantons, Iroquois Confederacy governance, UNDRR regional coordination, disaster recovery governance in Haiti and Christchurch.

**2. How do institutions learn from failures and adapt governance to prevent the same failures from recurring?**
Crisis learning cycles: post-crisis review, institutional change, implementation, re-testing. Adaptive management: how to change procedures when conditions change (drought, new technology, new population). Institutional memory: how communities document lessons and transfer them to new leaders. Feedback loops and early warning systems. Failure mode analysis for governance — what governance structures fail under what conditions?

**3. What incentive structures and accountability mechanisms keep scaled governance honest?**
Checks and balances across federation layers. Transparency and information access in distributed governance. Resource allocation mechanisms that prevent predation. Term limits, rotation, and leadership accountability. Midwest case studies: county governance, agricultural cooperative federations, regional water authorities, soil-water conservation district cooperatives.

**Out of scope**: National and international governance theory, comparative political systems at macro scale, constitutional law as practiced by nation-states. Hard boundary: 150-500 person federation scale only.

## Unique Contribution

**What this adds that Phases 1-5 do not contain:**

Phase 3 documented governance *structure* at community scale. Phase 5 Wave 2 documented conflict resolution and psychological support. Neither addresses what happens when a governance structure designed for 25 people encounters 150 people, or when founding members die and new members don't share institutional memory.

This domain fills two specific gaps:

First, the **scaling gap**: every governance structure has a failure mode at some scale. The Phase 3 structures work for the scale Phase 3 describes. This domain maps the failure modes that emerge as communities grow, and provides the constitutional tools to design structures that survive growth.

Second, the **learning gap**: most resilience work documents what to do. This domain documents how to change what you do when it doesn't work. Institutional reflexion — the community equivalent of the SuperClaude ReflexionPattern — is what distinguishes communities that improve from communities that repeat failures.

**Market specificity**: The Midwest has deep experience with cooperative governance (agricultural cooperatives, rural water authorities, soil conservation districts). Regional water authority coordination is a current active topic in Wisconsin and Iowa. The research translates directly to governance questions these communities are already wrestling with.
