## Personalization Notes for All Templates

### What to Research About Each Candidate Before Sending

1. **Their recent work/publications**: Reference a specific project, publication, or initiative they've led
2. **Their stated values**: If they've spoken publicly about economic justice, commons, mutual aid — reference that
3. **Their network relevance**: If they're connected to other contacts you have or organizations you respect — mention that connection
4. **Their geographic/cultural context**: Zone 5 reference if they work in that region; adapt for their local context if not

### How to Personalize Each Template

**Academic version**:
- Replace [SPECIFIC RESEARCH AREA] with their actual research focus (from their website, recent papers)
- Reference 1-2 of their recent publications if you've read them
- Example: "Your 2024 work on 'Nested Institutions in Agricultural Commons' directly parallels Ostrom's design principles we're synthesizing"

**Cooperative version**:
- Research their specific role (board member, development specialist, regional coordinator)
- Reference their known cooperative or regional network
- Reference specific cooperative model they're known for (credit unions, buying clubs, agricultural co-ops, worker co-ops)
- Example: "Your 20+ years with [Cooperative Name] — particularly the capital-raising work you led in [Region] — is exactly the expertise we need"

**Mutual aid version**:
- Reference specific initiative they've led or been known for
- Mention specific crisis response if applicable (COVID mutual aid networks, hurricane response, etc.)
- Reference their community geography if relevant
- Example: "Your work building the [City] mutual aid network during 2020–2021 demonstrated exactly the economic coordination mechanisms we're documenting"

### Email Personalization Checklist

- [ ] Use their first name (not "Dear Sir/Madam" or "To Whom It May Concern")
- [ ] Reference something specific about their work (publication, project, initiative)
- [ ] Explain why *their specific* background fits *this specific* project
- [ ] Mention a specific source document or theoretical framework they might recognize
- [ ] Make the timeline clear and achievable
- [ ] Provide clear next steps and decision timeline
- [ ] Include your contact information (email minimum, phone optional)
- [ ] Keep tone professional but warm — not overly formal or corporate
