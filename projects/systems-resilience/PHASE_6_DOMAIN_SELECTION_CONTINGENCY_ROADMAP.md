## Success Criteria (Universal — All Combinations)

### Document Quality Standards (From Phase 3/5 Precedent)
- Word count per domain: 8,000–10,000 words minimum per Phase 6 document (final documents 45,000–60,000 words)
- Source density: 40–55 sources per domain; no section with fewer than 3 sources
- Practitioner accessibility: Zone 5 community member can read and act on content without academic background
- Citation completeness: all citations include full reference + URL where available
- Integration sections: each domain document includes explicit integration points with other Phase 6 domains
- No placeholders: final documents contain no [RESEARCH NEEDED] markers; all gaps are addressed or explicitly bounded

### Timeline Quality Gates
- June 1–9: Author outreach sent and initial responses received
- June 9–12: Author(s) confirmed; onboarding packages delivered
- June 15: Production begun (outline or first section drafted)
- July 15: All domains in at least 30% draft
- August 1: All domains in at least 70% draft
- August 15: First complete drafts ready for editorial review
- August 23–31: Final documents complete (date varies by combination)

### Contingency Triggers
- If author not confirmed by June 14: escalate to secondary sourcing channel or split the domain between two part-time authors
- If first section not drafted by June 22: production timeline is at risk; reassess scope and simplify if needed
- If 30% draft not complete by July 22: combination is 1–2 weeks behind; consider scope reduction on highest-complexity domain

The integration point between governance (B) and mechanization/repair (D) is the commons tool library: who can borrow the tractor? Who decides when it needs maintenance? Who enforces equipment care standards? The commons tool governance design is:

1. Tool registry: document of all community equipment with maintenance history, authorized operators, and borrowing protocol
2. Maintenance responsibility: clear assignment of who performs what maintenance; tied to borrowing privileges
3. Failure accountability: when equipment is damaged through operator error, what is the remedy? (This is a conflict resolution question — B's Section 3 directly applies)
4. Replacement decision: when equipment reaches end of life, what is the governance process for deciding to replace vs. repair vs. acquire different equipment?

Document this integration in both B and D at the appropriate sections. The B author writes it from the governance-protocol perspective; the D author writes it from the practical maintenance-management perspective. Cross-reference explicitly.
