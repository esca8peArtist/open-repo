# Phase 6 Alternate Combination Scoring
## All 8 Primary 3-Domain Combinations

> **Lead finding**: No single combination dominates all six scoring dimensions. The currently-staged A+C+D combination scores highest on author availability and implementation confidence. The A+B+E combination scores highest on strategic coherence and cross-domain interdependency. The user's primary decision variable should be: what is the most important gap in the Phase 1–5 corpus that Phase 6 should fill? Answer that question, then read the combination that fits.

---

## Scoring Framework

### Six Dimensions Defined

Each combination is scored 1–5 on six dimensions. **5 is always the better score.**

| Dimension | Score 5 means | Score 1 means |
|---|---|---|
| **Implementation Complexity** | Combination can launch June 1 with minimal pre-production work | Requires 10+ day source sprint across multiple domains before production |
| **Resource Requirements** | Low orchestrator hours, low author hours, low coordination overhead | High across all three; sustained orchestrator demand; complex author coordination |
| **Author Availability** | All three domain profiles are easy to staff from known networks | One or more domain profiles requires rare credential combination; long sourcing time |
| **Strategic Impact** | Combination fills Phase 1–5's most critical gaps; highest Phase 7 enablement | Fills gaps that are less urgent; delays most critical content |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Single-domain failure does not cascade; combination has built-in redundancy | Single-domain slip ripples across all three; high interdependency risk |
| **Interdependencies** | Domains reinforce each other without requiring synchronized production | Domains require tight coordination milestones; production serialization forced |

**Economic Resilience + Skills Development + Mechanization/Tool Repair**

### What this combination covers

The exchange layer (A), the skill transmission layer (C), and the physical repair capability layer (D). Together they address: how communities exchange when markets fail, how they maintain competence across time, and how they keep their tools working.

### 6-Dimension Scorecard

**Best fit for**: Users who want fastest delivery with highest confidence; users whose primary community challenge is practical (repair, skills, exchange) rather than structural (governance); users with tight author budgets.

## Head-to-Head Analysis on Key Trade-offs

### If delivery speed is the priority

**Choose A + D + E**: Highest August 23 confidence (91%) because A, D, and E all have above-70% source readiness and no governance scope expansion risk. All three can begin production by June 10. This combination trades governance and skills transmission content for maximum delivery speed.

**Second choice: A + C + D** (88%): The reference baseline; one of the two highest-confidence combinations; adds skills transmission at the cost of ecological content.

### If strategic impact is the priority

**Choose A + B + C**: Fills the three foundational social infrastructure gaps (exchange, governance, transmission). Strategic coherence is highest. Accept 84% delivery confidence and 10–14 day author sourcing challenge for Domain B.

**Close second: A + B + E** (83%): Stronger ecological integration; Zone 5 commons governance coherence; slightly lower confidence than A+B+C due to E's data gaps.

### If Zone 5 ecological coherence is the priority

**Choose A + B + E**: The only combination where all three domains directly address the Zone 5 commons land stewardship challenge — governance of land (B), restoration of land (E), and exchange of what the land produces (A).

**Acceptable alternative: A + C + E**: Same ecological commitment without governance risk; accepts B deferral to Phase 6b.

### If author budget is constrained

**Choose A + D + E**: Lowest total author cost; all three domains have mid-range rates; D (mechanical practitioner) typically charges lower than governance or education specialists.

**Avoid: B + E + F**: Highest sourcing difficulty; B and F both require rarer credential combinations; author costs will be above average.

### If the user already has a confirmed author with governance expertise

**Choose A + B + C or A + B + D**: Domain B is the sourcing bottleneck in all B-containing combinations. If the user already has a confirmed governance author, the main argument against B-combinations disappears. A+B+C with confirmed governance author rises to 90%+ confidence and becomes the strategic recommendation.
