# Phase 4 Option A Quick-Start: 3-Day Kickoff Plan

> **Trigger**: User selects Option A on May 31. Phase 4 activates June 1 evening, concurrent with Wave 1+2 publication.
> **Outcome**: Governance synthesis, household structure, and Zone 5 adaptation all operational by June 4 evening.
> **Zero setup lag**: Each task below is self-contained. No further planning needed.

## Why 3 Days

Option A publishes 43,621 words (Wave 1+2) on June 1: microgrids, veterinary care, psychological support, conflict resolution, community implementation playbook. Communities receive these documents and need an immediate activation scaffold — governance first, then household structure, then Zone 5 reality-check. Three days covers the core infrastructure. ## Day 1 (June 1 Evening — Publication Day): Governance Synthesis Workshop

**Time required**: 4-5 hours (facilitator + participants)

**Task**: Run a 4-hour governance synthesis workshop with the community using the outline below. If community is >100 persons, run two parallel groups, then a 30-minute synthesis.

**Workshop agenda (4 hours):**

**Segment 1 — Current State (45 min)**: Facilitator asks: "How do we actually make decisions now?" Document current decision method on a large sheet (informal consensus, loudest voice, rotating informal leader). Identify 2-3 decisions that felt broken and why.

**Segment 2 — Governance Principles (60 min)**: Present three governance models using Phase 3 governance document (read Section 1 before workshop):
- Direct assembly (all decisions together, works below 150 persons)
- Delegate council (elected representatives, monthly full assembly, works 150-500 persons)
- Nested circles (working groups with liaisons, horizontal scaling)

Break into sub-groups of 15-20. Each group role-plays a resource allocation scenario using their assigned model. Report back: most communities select delegate council.

**Segment 3 — Charter Skeleton (60 min)**: Fill in one-page governance charter (template below). Assign coordinator roles for each domain. Specify: who has daily authority, what thresholds trigger escalation, how information flows to governance.

**Segment 4 — Household Structure (45 min)**: Each household (8-25 persons) designates coordinators: water, food, health, energy, governance liaison. Each household signs a one-page household charter (template in PHASE_4_ACTIVATION_OPTION_A.md, Component 2).

**Segment 5 — Commitment (15 min)**: Read draft governance charter aloud. Open for three amendments maximum. Commit: "This governance is in effect starting June 2."

**Charter template (copy into COMMUNITY_GOVERNANCE_CHARTER.md):**

```
COMMUNITY GOVERNANCE CHARTER — [Community Name] — Adopted [Date]
================================================================

Decision-making method: [Direct Assembly / Delegate Council / Nested Circles]
Quorum: [# of people required for valid decisions]
Delegate selection: [Elected / Rotated / Volunteer — specify frequency]
Escalation: [Who arbitrates disagreements?]
Emergency authority: [Name] — immediate decisions during crisis; debriefs at next meeting
Assembly schedule: [Day, time, frequency]
Information infrastructure: [How does governance receive accurate data? Who updates inventory?]
Scaling threshold: [At what population size does governance restructure?]

Domain Coordinators + Authority:
  Water: [Name] — daily authority; escalates if storage <3 days
  Food: [Name] — daily authority; escalates if stores <10 days supply
  Health: [Name] — daily authority; escalates if emergency medical needed
  Energy: [Name] — daily authority; escalates if generation <30% capacity
  Security: [Name] — daily authority; escalates to assembly for major threats
  Governance: [Name/Facilitator] — runs meetings; escalates if legitimacy challenged

Signed: [All adult members]
```

**Day 1 deliverable**: Governance charter drafted and signed. Household coordinators named.

## Day 2 (June 2): Zone 5 Adaptation Guide

**Time required**: 2-3 hours (coordinator team)

**Task**: Apply Zone 5 reality to every governance decision made on Day 1. The governance structure created June 1 is correct in principle but may have critical gaps for Midwest winter survival.

**Zone 5 audit checklist (work through each item with coordinator team):**

1. **Winter heating allocation**: Has the community pre-decided what happens if heating fuel reaches 30-day supply? If not, decide now, in writing, before October. Specifically: what temperature floor, what space consolidation protocol, what procurement emergency authority. This decision is genuinely difficult and must be made when there is no crisis pressure.

2. **Agricultural calendar alignment**: Is June planting already finalized? The Zone 5 growing window is May 15 to September 15 (4 months). Every week of delayed June planting is lost harvest. The food coordinator must confirm planting schedule is locked.

3. **Solar backup requirement**: Any household with solar-only energy must identify a backup heat source (propane, wood, kerosene) and authorize the capital purchase by July. Zone 5 December-February solar production is approximately 20% of summer rating. Solar-only systems create an energy cliff in November.

4. **Preservation capacity**: Confirm by June 2 that canning equipment, root cellar capacity, or dehydrator capacity exists for the full harvest. If not, emergency procurement must happen in July — September is too late.

5. **Water winterization plan**: Who is responsible for winterizing all outdoor water infrastructure before October 1? What is the specific procedure? Assign name and date.

**Zone 5 implementation calendar (post and share):**

| Month | Non-Negotiable Action | Responsible Coordinator |
|---|---|---|
| June | Planting finalized; preservation equipment confirmed; winter heating decision made | Food + Energy |
| July | Solar backup installed or ordered; fuel delivery scheduled | Energy |
| August | Preservation in progress; root cellar stocked; winterization plan reviewed | Food + Water |
| September | Fuel stockpiled (120 days minimum); water infrastructure winterized; governance fully operational | All |
| October | Heating operational; food stores complete; governance enters quarterly-review mode | All |

**Day 2 deliverable**: Zone 5 audit complete. Calendar posted publicly. Every gap has a named owner and deadline.

## Day 3 (June 3-4): Governance Test and First Real Decision

**Time required**: 2 hours (full governance assembly)

**Task**: Convene the first real governance assembly. Make a real decision using the charter adopted on Day 1.

**Agenda for first governance assembly:**

1. **Ratify charter** (15 min): Read the June 1 charter aloud. Vote to formally adopt. Record in governance log.

2. **First real decision — Zone 5 heating** (45 min): "If heating fuel reaches 30-day supply, the following protocol activates: [fill in]. Vote to adopt." This is the hardest governance decision and the most important to make pre-crisis. Making it in assembly on Day 3 proves that governance works.

3. **Information infrastructure spec** (30 min): "What information does governance need to make decisions each week?" Expected answer: resource inventory (food, fuel, medical), population status, equipment status, external conditions. Assign someone to maintain and update this inventory weekly.

4. **June schedule** (15 min): Set governance assembly schedule for all of June. Confirm who facilitates each meeting.

5. **Phase 5 integration** (15 min): "Wave 3 documents (food preservation, water systems, healthcare offline, fuel production, educational governance) arrive by June 30. Our governance will need to make decisions based on these documents. Each coordinator should read their domain document the week it arrives and bring a decision proposal to the next assembly."

**Day 3 deliverable**: Charter formally ratified. First real governance decision recorded. Information infrastructure specification assigned. June governance calendar confirmed.
