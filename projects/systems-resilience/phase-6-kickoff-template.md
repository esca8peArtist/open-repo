2. **Present research findings** (bulk of section, 1,500–3,000 words)

   - What are the key design principles or mechanisms?

3. **Translate to Zone 5 context** (300–500 words, REQUIRED)

   - What Midwest examples already exist?

4. **Provide actionable guidance** (300–500 words, REQUIRED)

## Part 5: Writing Standards (5 minutes)

### Tone and Voice

- **Specific, not conceptual** (see README.md core principles)
  - NOT: "Communities should consider resilient economic models"
  - BUT: "A 200-person community can sustain itself with 3 foundational economic structures: a local currency for day-to-day exchange, a commons resource pool for shared assets, and a cooperative enterprise for specialized production."

- **Evidence-based, not prescriptive**
  - Support claims with citations to research, case studies, or data
  - When limited evidence exists, say so explicitly
  - Never speculate without acknowledging it as speculation

- **Practical and actionable**
  - Every section should answer "what should a community actually do?"
  - Include examples, templates, and step-by-step procedures
  - Reference Phase 1–5 work where relevant (e.g., "See Phase 3 governance structure for decision-making framework")

### Citation Standards

- **Minimum**: 120 citations, maximum 180
- **Format**: [Author Year] inline citations, full bibliography at end
- **Diversity**: Mix of academic sources (60–70%), case studies (20–30%), and primary documents (10–15%)
- **Quality check**: Every claim of fact (not opinion) must have at least one citation

Example:
> "Complementary currencies have been used successfully in resource-constrained contexts for 30+ years. The WIR mutual credit system in Switzerland has maintained 170,000+ members and €3+ billion in annual transaction volume since 1934, demonstrating that alternative currencies can sustain at significant scale [WIR 2023]. In modern contexts, the Bristol Pounds (2009–2022) and Berkshire Dollars demonstrated both adoption and eventual consolidation, showing both success and limits of local currency models [Greco 2010; Seyfang 2015]."

### Cross-Referencing Standards

- **Internal**: When you reference Phase 1–5 concepts, use explicit cross-references (e.g., "See Phase 3: Community Scale governance model (page XX)")
- **Within domain**: Number your sections and subsections for easy reference
- **To other Phase 6 domains** (if concurrent): Note connection points without waiting for final text (e.g., "This economic model depends on governance structure (to be detailed in Domain 65)")
