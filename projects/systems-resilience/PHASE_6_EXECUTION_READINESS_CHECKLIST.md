# Phase 6 Execution Readiness Checklist
## Domains B–F: Per-Domain Assessment

> **Lead finding**: Domains B (Economic Foundations) and C (Political Governance) are the most execution-ready with the deepest source libraries and clearest researcher profiles. Domain D (Judicial Independence) has a significant practitioner gap in transformative justice that requires interview-based research. Domain E (Multilateral Coordination) is the most dependent on B and C being substantially complete before it can proceed fully. Domain F (Implementation Feasibility) should run as a parallel audit thread from the start, not a sequential final step.

> **Integration with Phase 5**: Phase 5 Wave 2 produced microgrid, water, food, healthcare, veterinary care, and conflict resolution research. Phase 6 domains B–F build the social, economic, and governance architecture that governs access to and management of those Phase 5 physical systems. Domain F is the explicit integration point.

## Domain B: Economic Foundations

### Researcher Requirements

**Primary researcher profile**:
- Background: community economics, solidarity economy, or cooperative development — one of the three
- Skill level: Practitioner-researcher hybrid; PhD-level economics background preferred but not required if practitioner depth is strong
- Essential knowledge: Ostrom commons governance principles; cooperative economic models (Mondragon-level familiarity); complementary currency design; food sovereignty frameworks
- Facilitation experience: Moderate — will need to conduct 3–5 practitioner interviews with cooperative directors and community currency administrators
- Writing: Must produce practitioner-accessible long-form content (8,000–10,000 words for non-academic audience); ability to synthesize theory and case studies without academic jargon

**Secondary researcher (optional for parallel zone execution)**:
- Background: local food systems or agricultural economics
- Focus: B-3 (Food Sovereignty) and B-5 (Commons Resource Pooling) zones specifically

- [ ] Phase 5 economic content reviewed: PHASE_5_WAVE_2_MICROGRIDS_RESEARCH.md and food systems research for integration points

### Data Dependencies

**Must be complete before B can start**:
- Phase 5 Wave 2 and Wave 3 physical infrastructure research reviewed (food systems, water, microgrids) — required for B-3 and B-5 integration
- No Phase 6 domain dependencies (B is foundational)

**What depends on B being complete**:

### Success Criteria

Production-ready Domain B research output meets these conditions:

1. **Complementary currency design guide** (B-1): Documents at minimum 3 implemented community currency systems with governance structures, failure modes, and design decisions; produces a community-usable design checklist
2. **Timebanking implementation protocol** (B-2): Provides a step-by-step implementation guide for a community of 100–500 persons, including governance, trust mechanisms, and skill valuation
3. **Food sovereignty economic model** (B-3): Documents minimum acreage requirements per person (Zone 5) and economic structures for food commons governance
4. **Cooperative enterprise guide** (B-4): Compares at least 3 cooperative models with documented success under economic stress; provides governance design decision tree
5. **Commons resource pooling framework** (B-5): Documents tool library, land trust, and seed commons governance designs with Ostrom principle mapping

7. **Community wealth building framework** (B-7): Documents local multiplier effects and import substitution strategies with Zone 5 applicability assessment
8. **Cross-domain integration** (all zones): Each zone explicitly documents integration points with Domains C, D, and E

**Quality bar**: A community organizer with no economics background should be able to read Domain B and understand which economic model is appropriate for their community, what governance it requires, and how to begin implementation. The research is not complete until this test is met.

## Domain C: Political Governance

### Researcher Requirements

**Primary researcher profile**:
- Background: political science, public administration, community organizing, or organizational design — one of the four
- Skill level: PhD-level or equivalent practitioner depth; must have direct experience with horizontal governance (not just studied it)
- Essential knowledge: Ostrom design principles; deliberative democracy facilitation; sociocracy or similar consent-based governance; case studies of governance failure
- Facilitation experience: High — must be able to run governance workshops and facilitate conflict about governance design; has personally facilitated multi-party decisions
- Writing: Must produce practitioner-accessible governance guides that working communities can implement without a political science degree

**Red flags (screen out)**:
- Corporate governance background without community governance grounding
- Academic-only without direct facilitation experience
- Advocate for a single governance model (sociocracy, consensus, etc.) without comparative analysis capacity

### Pre-Start Checklist

- [ ] Domain B (Economic Foundations) substantially drafted — required for C-4 resource allocation governance
- [ ] Phase 3 governance findings reviewed in full (phase-3-community-infrastructure-research-outline.md, Domain 2)
- [ ] PHASE_6_DOMAINS_B_E_F_RESEARCH_OUTLINES.md Domain B (Institutional Governance) section reviewed for overlap and gaps
- [ ] Source library sprint: Rojava Information Center current documentation verified accessible
- [ ] Source library sprint: Sociocracy For All current training materials verified
- [ ] Source library sprint: FIC failure case documentation — direct contact with FIC to request unpublished governance failure cases
- [ ] Participedia case database searched for Zone 5 Midwest governance cases

### Data Dependencies

**Must be complete before C can start**:
- Domain B substantially drafted (C-4 resource allocation governance requires economic context from B-1 through B-6)
- Phase 3 Domain 2 governance research reviewed and gaps documented

**What depends on C being complete**:
- Domain D (Judicial Independence) — governance accountability structures (C-3) required before D-1 and D-6 can be fully designed
- Domain E (Multilateral Coordination) — delegate and representative structures (C-2) required before E-1 (federation design) can proceed
- Domain F (Implementation Feasibility) — governance failure analysis (C-5) required for F-5 risk mapping

### Success Criteria

1. **Participatory democracy guide** (C-1): Documents at minimum 4 decision-making models (consensus, sociocracy, liquid democracy, sortition) with scale ranges, strengths, failure modes, and implementation steps
2. **Scale transition guide** (C-2): Provides explicit protocols for governance transitions at 25, 50, 150, and 500 person thresholds; includes delegate council design templates
3. **Accountability system design** (C-3): Documents at minimum 3 recall/accountability mechanisms with evidence of effectiveness; provides implementation checklist
4. **Resource allocation governance** (C-4): Integrates with B-domain economic findings to provide decision-making protocols for monetary and non-monetary resource allocation
5. **Governance failure taxonomy** (C-5): Documents at minimum 5 failure modes with case study evidence and prevention protocols
6. **Emergency authority framework** (C-6): Provides time-limited authority design with automatic sunset mechanisms and renewal protocols

**Quality bar**: A community with no political science background should be able to read Domain C, choose a governance model appropriate for their size and context, and implement basic accountability structures within 30 days.

### Contingency Routing

**If governance failure case documentation is inaccessible**:
- FIC published documentation is sparse; primary interviews with 4–6 FIC-network community practitioners are required
- If interviews cannot be arranged: Use academic analyses of kibbutz governance evolution and Twin Oaks long-duration documentation as proxies; flag as preliminary

**If Zone C-5 (Governance Failure Analysis) exceeds timeline**:
- C-5 is the most important secondary zone; do not defer it
- Defer C-6 (Emergency Authority) to Phase 7 if timeline forces a choice
- Rationale: Failure analysis prevention is more valuable for early-phase communities than emergency authority design

**If Rojava documentation is inaccessible**:
- Substitute: Zapatista inter-community governance documentation (30+ year record; better documented than Rojava in English)
- Secondary substitute: Mondragon social council documentation (parallel governance body structure)

## Domain D: Judicial Independence

### Researcher Requirements

**Primary researcher profile**:
- Background: restorative justice, community mediation, conflict transformation, or legal anthropology — one of the four
- Skill level: Practitioner-researcher with direct facilitation experience; PhD desirable but practitioner depth with published work is equally acceptable
- Essential knowledge: Restorative justice theory and practice (circle keeping, victim-offender mediation); indigenous peacemaking frameworks; transformative justice principles; mediation practice
- Facilitation experience: High — must have personally facilitated conflict resolution processes; no classroom-only background
- Special requirement: Must be able to treat transformative justice (D-4: serious harms) with appropriate gravity; experience with survivor-centered accountability preferred

**Critical screening question**: "Can you describe a transformative justice process you facilitated or closely supported for a serious harm?" A practitioner without a real answer to this should not write D-4.

### Pre-Start Checklist

- [ ] Domain C (Political Governance) substantially drafted — specifically C-3 (accountability structures) required before D-1 and D-6
- [ ] Navajo Nation Peacemaking Court documentation reviewed
- [ ] NARF Indigenous Peacemaking Initiative current programming confirmed
- [ ] Generation FIVE and Creative Interventions documentation reviewed (D-4)
- [ ] NAFCM community mediation center directory reviewed for Zone 5 contacts
- [ ] Restorative justice outcome evidence base reviewed: CSG Justice Center, Sentencing Project, OJJDP documentation
- [ ] Survivor advocate consultation arranged for D-4 (minimum 1 consultation before drafting)

### Data Dependencies

**Must be complete before D can start**:
- Domain C (C-3 accountability structures) substantially drafted
- Domain B (B-5 commons resource pooling) substantially drafted (for D-5 property disputes)

**What depends on D being complete**:
- Domain E (Multilateral Coordination) — E-4 (inter-community conflict resolution) requires D framework
- Domain F (Implementation Feasibility) — D-4 and D-6 have implementation risk that must be assessed in F-5

### Success Criteria

1. **Restorative justice implementation guide** (D-1): Provides circle-keeping protocols, facilitator training requirements, and case type triage (what cases are appropriate for restorative circles)
2. **Indigenous peacemaking adaptation guide** (D-2): Documents peacemaking principles applicable in non-indigenous community contexts with appropriate cultural attribution; provides implementation protocols for communities willing to adopt these frameworks with permission
3. **Community mediation protocol** (D-3): Provides mediator training pathway, case intake process, and outcome documentation framework
4. **Serious harm protocol** (D-4): Documents transformative justice processes for violence, abuse, and serious harm; includes survivor-centered accountability frameworks; does not minimize harm or rush accountability
5. **Property and resource dispute protocols** (D-5): Provides commons-specific dispute resolution protocols with integration into Ostrom-based commons governance
6. **Non-coercive enforcement framework** (D-6): Documents social sanction systems, ostracism protocols, and the limits of non-coercive enforcement; includes honest assessment of when non-coercive enforcement fails

**Quality bar**: A community mediator with no restorative justice training should be able to read Domain D and know: (a) what process to use for each type of conflict, (b) who needs to be trained in what, (c) when to escalate from restorative to transformative justice processes.

### Contingency Routing

**If indigenous peacemaking researchers decline participation**:

- Substitute: document restorative justice models that incorporate indigenous principles (New Zealand family group conferencing; Canadian Gladue sentencing principles) rather than specific indigenous practices
- Navajo Nation Peacemaking documentation is public and can be cited with appropriate attribution

**If Zone D-6 (Enforcement Without Coercion) exceeds timeline**:
- D-6 is the zone with the most significant source gap; can be deferred to Phase 7
- Produce a gap documentation memo instead: what is known, what the failure modes are, what additional research is required

## Cross-Domain Decision Trees

### Decision Tree 1: Which Domain to Start First

```
Is the community currently facing economic instability? 
  YES → Start Domain B immediately
  NO  → Does the community have a functional governance structure?
         YES → Start Domain C to formalize and strengthen it
         NO  → Start Domain B (economics) and Domain C (governance) in parallel
                 if two researchers are available; 
                 if only one, start Domain B first
```
