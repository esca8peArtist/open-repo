  - Implementation Playbook, Microgrids, Governance Coordination, Psychological Support, Conflict Resolution, Water Systems, Energy Systems, Information Infrastructure, Food Systems Coordination, Healthcare Offline
- **June 30**: Wave 3 (22,821 words, 2 documents)  
  - Food Preservation & Storage, Livestock Management, Small Medical Emergency Kits

**Why This Works:**
1. **Immediate activation** (June 1): Phase 4 governance workshop happens the same evening Wave 1+2 is published. Communities get decision-making frameworks and psychological support in the same window they form their operational structure.
2. **Sequenced knowledge**: Wave 1+2 covers governance, psychology, and coordination systems. Wave 3 covers operational execution (food, livestock, healthcare). Communities learn governance first, then apply it to operational domains.
3. **Feedback incorporation**: 25-day gap (June 5-30) between waves allows incorporating early community feedback into Wave 3 content.
4. **Publishing burden**: Distributes publication work across two gates (June 5 + June 30) rather than concentrating 66K words into a single release event.
5. **Phase 4 integration**: Domain coordinators designated June 1-2 receive Wave 3 content June 30 as operational guidance for their assigned domains.

**Tradeoff:**
- Communities receive documents in two batches, creating a two-phase activation rather than unified launch. Minor coherence cost; significant operational benefit.

  - Week 1: Implementation Playbook + Microgrids
  - Week 2: Governance Coordination + Psychological Support

- Allows "early reader" feedback on each weekly pair before subsequent weeks publish

2. **Document coherence**: Readers must piece together the full narrative across 6 weeks, rather than receiving integrated documents.
3. **Distribution fragmentation**: 6 separate publication events (vs. 1-2) create higher administrative load.
4. **Community fatigue**: Weekly releases create "release addiction" rather than enabling communities to activate at their own pace.

**Confidence**: 60% (due to the Phase 4 governance coherence problem).
