## 1. Overview

Design a complete 6-day-per-week Push/Pull/Legs (PPL) workout program that:
- Builds **strength as the primary goal**
- Develops **muscular endurance as a secondary goal**
- Progresses toward advanced **calisthenics skills** (planche, L-sit, muscle-up)
- Incorporates **mobility work and kinetic chain strengthening**
- Draws from **Judd Lienhard's MASS Method** philosophy (Movement, Athleticism, Strength, Speed)
- Fits within a **hard 75-minute session window**
- Is **gym-based** (full commercial equipment access, no rings, TRX available)

## 4. Session Structure

### Heavy Day (D1, D2, D3 — Push1, Pull1, Legs1)
| Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-Up + Chain Activation | 10 min | Dynamic mobility, kinetic chain prep, Lienhard-style movement prep |
| Block 2 — Strength | 35 min | 2 heavy compound lifts + calisthenics skill block |
| Block 3 — Accessories | 20 min | 2–3 accessory movements supporting compounds and skills |
| **No HIIT block** | — | Omitted on heavy days to fit 75-min window |

**Heavy day intensity:** Main compounds RPE 8–9; accessories RPE 7–8; skill work at max quality

### Volume Day (D4, D5, D6 — Push2, Pull2, Legs2)
| Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-Up + Chain Activation | 8 min | Shorter but still includes activation work |

| Block 3 — Accessories | 12 min | 2 targeted accessory movements |
| Block 4 — HIIT Circuit | 13 min | 3–4 rounds metabolic burnout circuit |

**Volume day intensity:** Main compounds RPE 7–8; accessories RPE 7; circuit RPE 9–10

## 5. Push / Pull / Legs Split

### Push Days → Planche Progressions
- Primary movers: chest, shoulders, triceps
- Calisthenics skill: planche (scapular protraction/depression, straight-arm pushing strength)
- Chain focus: anterior shoulder chain, serratus anterior, wrist extensors

### Pull Days → Muscle-Up Progressions
- Primary movers: lats, mid-back, biceps, rear delts
- Calisthenics skill: muscle-up (explosive pull, bar transition, straight bar dip)
- Chain focus: posterior chain of upper body; scapular retraction + depression
- TRX: use for transition drills (simulates ring angles)

### Leg Days → L-Sit Progressions
- Primary movers: quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves
- Calisthenics skill: L-sit (hip flexor + quad compression strength, tricep support)
- Chain focus: posterior chain (glutes, hamstrings), hip flexor mobility, ankle mobility
- Note: L-sit skill work goes BEFORE heavy squats so hip flexors are fresh

## 6. Calisthenics Skill Progressions

### Planche Progression (starting from: knee planche lean)
**Stage 0 (current):** Knee planche lean
**Stage 1:** Full planche lean (feet on floor, body angled forward, arms straight) — target: 30s hold
**Stage 2:** Pseudo planche push-ups (hands at hips, lean forward, controlled) — target: 3×10
**Stage 3:** Tuck planche hold (knees to chest, feet off floor) — target: 10s hold
**Stage 4:** Advanced tuck planche (back parallel to floor) — target: 10s hold
**Stage 5:** Straddle planche → Full planche

**Supporting exercises:** Scapular protraction push-ups, wrist conditioning, RTO support holds (rings if ever available)

**Progression rule:** Master current stage for 2 consecutive sessions before advancing. Minimum hold/rep standard above.

### L-Sit Progression (starting from: single-leg L-sit)
**Stage 0 (current):** Single-leg L-sit (alternating)
**Stage 1:** Full L-sit on floor or parallettes — target: 5s hold
**Stage 2:** Full L-sit — target: 10s hold
**Stage 3:** Full L-sit — target: 20s hold
**Stage 4:** Full L-sit — target: 30s hold
**Stage 5:** L-sit to V-sit transition

**Supporting exercises:** Seated pike compression, hanging leg raises, hip flexor strengthening, parallel bar support holds (tricep lockout)

**Key:** L-sit goes BEFORE heavy leg compounds (hip flexors need to be fresh)

### Muscle-Up Progression (starting from: 20+ pull-ups, cannot clear the bar; no rings; TRX available)
**The problem:** Transition — user has the pulling strength but cannot get over the bar

**Stage 1:** Chest-to-bar pull-ups (explosive, pull as high as possible) — target: 5×5 clean
**Stage 2:** Straight bar dips (critical: the "getting over" motion is a dip at the top) — target: 3×10
**Stage 3:** TRX muscle-up drill (set straps low, simulate pull-to-dip transition in partial ROM)
**Stage 4:** Jump-assisted bar muscle-up negatives (jump to support position, lower slowly) — target: 5 controlled negatives
**Stage 5:** Kipping bar muscle-up (learn the timing/rhythm of the hip snap + pull)
**Stage 6:** Strict bar muscle-up

**Progression rule:** Stages 1 and 2 simultaneously. Do not attempt Stage 5 until Stage 4 is solid (5 controlled negatives).

## 7. Kinetic Chain & Mobility Requirements

### Warm-Up Activation (Block 1 — appropriate for all sessions)
- Banded pull-aparts (pronated + supinated) — shoulder health
- Banded hip flexor activation — hip mobility
- Thoracic rotations (seated or in quadruped)
- Shoulder CARs (controlled articular rotations)
- Bear crawl (8–10m) — full-body chain activation
- Hip CARs

### Training Block Chain Exercises (Block 2 or 3 — strength-level load)
- Jefferson curl (light-to-moderate — posterior chain + spinal mobility)
- Nordic curl (posterior chain — hamstrings)
- Loaded carries (farmer's, suitcase, overhead)
- Kettlebell swings (hip hinge chain)
- Pallof press (anti-rotation / lateral chain)
- Reverse hypers (posterior chain)

### Wrist Prehabilitation (MANDATORY on all Push days)
Push day warm-ups must include: wrist circles (2 min), wrist flexor/extensor stretch (30s each), reverse wrist curls or rice bucket work (2×15). Non-negotiable with planche in the program.

## 8. Intensity & Progression Rules

### Heavy vs. Volume Day
- **Heavy days (D1/D2/D3):** Lower rep ranges (3–6), higher load, RPE 8–9 on main compounds
- **Volume days (D4/D5/D6):** Moderate rep ranges (8–12), moderate load, RPE 7–8 on compounds + HIIT burnout

### Progressive Overload Rule
- Add weight when all prescribed reps are completed at clean form (not just "done")
- Increment: 2.5 kg for upper body, 5 kg for lower body
- If a set fails (miss reps), keep same weight next session

### Deload Protocol (Addresses RF-1 — added by reviewer)
- Every 4th week: reduce Block 2 to 2–3 sets (down from 4–5), keep same weight, skip Block 4
- Continue calisthenics skill work at 50% volume during deload (skill quality maintained)
- This creates a 4-week mesocycle: 3 weeks load → 1 week deload

### Rest Periods
- Main compounds: 2.5–3 min
- Accessories: 60–90s
- Skill holds: 90s–2 min between sets
- Circuit: 20s between exercises within round, 60s between rounds

## 10. Constraints and Exclusions

- No machine-only or isolation-only sessions
- No static stretching as warm-up (dynamic only)
- No behind-the-neck pressing
- No steady-state cardio in circuit
- Dragon flags: classified as primary skill movement (not an accessory) — do not stack with heavy spinal-loading leg work
- Session must fit in 75 minutes hard — no exceptions
