# Workout Program Proposals — v2

**Project:** Workout PPL Redesign
**Version:** 2.0
**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Status:** Awaiting User Selection

## Changes from v1
- Warm-ups replaced with light versions of the day's actual exercises (no CARs or bear crawls)
- All 3 proposals now fully follow Judd Lienhard's MASS Method structure: **Prep → Build → Condition**
- Supersets used throughout accessory blocks to save time and increase density
- 3 compound movements per session (up from 2) — trim on warm-up and accessories made room
- HIIT circuits have a more athletic/MASS Method character

---

## Key for All Proposals

- **RPE** = Rate of Perceived Exertion (1–10); 10 = max effort
- **Superset** = perform A, immediately perform B, then rest — counts as one round
- **Heavy days (D1, D2, D3):** No HIIT block
- **Volume days (D4, D5, D6):** HIIT circuit at the end
- **L-sit always FIRST on leg days** — hip flexors must be fresh
- **Wrist prep always on push days** — mandatory before any planche work
- **MASS Method session structure every day:**
  1. **PREP** — Light movement, warm-up with exercise variants of the day
  2. **BUILD** — Power opener → Compounds → Skill
  3. **CONDITION** — Superset accessories (all days) + HIIT circuit (volume days only)

# PROPOSAL 1: "MASS METHOD — BARBELL POWER"

**Philosophy:** MASS Method structure with heavy barbell compounds as the primary strength tool. Every session opens with a power/explosive movement (PAP — primes the CNS for heavier lifts). Warm-ups are light versions of the day's exercises. Supersets keep the accessories dense and efficient. Most directly barbell-focused of the three.

**Best for:** You want the clearest strength progression on the big lifts while building skills and conditioning.

---

## DAY 1 — PUSH HEAVY | 75 min, no circuit

### PREP — 8 min (light exercise warm-up)
1. Push-ups (bodyweight) — 2×12 *(warm-up the push pattern)*
2. Light dumbbell press (50–60% of working weight) — 1×12 *(rehearsal of the movement)*
3. Band pull-aparts — 2×15
4. Wrist circles + reverse wrist curls (light) — 2 min *(planche prehab — always)*

### BUILD — 37 min

**Power Opener:**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plyo Push-ups | 3×5 | 90s | Max explosion off the floor. Full reset between reps. PAP primer. |

**Compounds:**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barbell Bench Press | 4×4-5 | 2.5–3 min | RPE 8–9. Primary strength driver. Add 2.5kg when all reps are clean. |
| Weighted Dips | 3×5-6 | 2 min | RPE 8. Belt or dumbbell. Full ROM — chest dip, slight lean forward. |
| Military Press (strict barbell) | 3×6-8 | 90s | RPE 7–8. Overhead strength feeds directly into planche lean. |

**Skill Block:**
| Exercise | Sets × Duration/Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Planche lean (full, feet on floor) | 3×20-25s | 90s | Arms straight, shift weight forward over wrists. Protract scapula. |
| Pseudo planche push-ups | 3×6-8 | 90s | Hands at hip level. Lean forward. Controlled eccentric. |

### CONDITION — 12 min (supersets only — no circuit on heavy day)
**Superset A** — 3 rounds, 60s rest after each round:
- A1: Y-Raise (prone incline bench) — 12-15 reps
- A2: Barbell Skull Crushers — 10-12 reps

**Superset B** — 3 rounds, 60s rest after each round:
- B1: Cable Lateral Raise — 12-15 reps each side
- B2: Wrist Curl + Reverse Wrist Curl — 15 reps each *(wrist strength for planche)*

**Session time: ~72 min**

# PROPOSAL 2: "MASS METHOD — CALISTHENICS FORWARD"

**Philosophy:** MASS Method structure where weighted pull-ups and weighted dips are the primary push and pull compounds. Barbells support them, not the other way around. Skill progressions get more volume because the whole program points toward planche, L-sit, and muscle-up as the pinnacle. Conditioning circuits use athletic bodyweight and kettlebell movements. Most skill-progressive of the three.

**Best for:** You want the fastest path to your muscle-up and planche, and you're comfortable making weighted bodyweight your primary strength tool.

---

# PROPOSAL 3: "MASS METHOD — FULL ATHLETIC"

**Philosophy:** The most faithful to Judd Lienhard's MASS Method. Every session has a distinct athletic identity — speed, rotational power, lateral movement, and loaded carries are woven into the structure. Warm-ups are light exercise versions of the day but add a Lienhard-style athletic prep element (e.g., a light explosive movement or rotational drill before the heavy work). The conditioning circuits are the most athletic of the three — multi-plane, explosive, chain-integrated. This plan trains you to be an athlete who lifts, not a lifter who does some conditioning.

**Best for:** You resonate with Lienhard's "always be an athlete" mindset and want your training to feel dynamic and athletic, not just gym-mechanical.

**Key MASS Method additions unique to this proposal:**
- Rotational band press/pull appears weekly (anti-rotation + rotational chain)
- Loaded carries appear weekly (suitcase, farmer's, overhead)
- Speed/lateral conditioning in every HIIT circuit
- Power opener every single session including volume days

---

# CALISTHENICS PROGRESSION GUIDE (All Proposals)

## 8–12 Week Timeline

### Planche (Baseline: knee planche lean)

| Weeks | Target | Advance When |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Full planche lean (feet on floor) — 20s | 20s × 3 sets for 2 sessions in a row |
| 3–4 | Planche lean 30s + pseudo planche push-ups 3×5 | 30s hold + 5 clean push-up reps |
| 5–6 | Pseudo planche push-ups 3×10 | 10 clean reps all 3 sets |
| 7–8 | First tuck planche hold — 5s | Feet off floor, any hold |
| 9–10 | Tuck planche 10s | 10s × 3 sets |
| 11–12 | Tuck planche 15s + begin advanced tuck | 15s consistently |

### L-Sit (Baseline: single-leg L-sit)

| Weeks | Target | Advance When |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Single-leg L-sit 12s each | 12s × 3 sets for 2 sessions in a row |
| 3–4 | First full L-sit — 3s | Both legs off floor |
| 5–6 | Full L-sit 5–8s | 5s × 3 sets |
| 7–8 | Full L-sit 10s | 10s consistently |
| 9–10 | Full L-sit 15–20s | 15s × 3 sets |
| 11–12 | Full L-sit 20–30s + V-sit leans | 20s+ |

### Muscle-Up (Baseline: 20+ pull-ups, can't clear bar, TRX available, no rings)

| Weeks | Target | Advance When |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Chest-to-bar pull-ups + straight bar dips (simultaneously) | 5 chest-to-bar + 10 straight bar dips, clean form |
| 3–4 | Bar consistently at lower sternum + straight bar dips 15 reps | Sternum contact each rep |
| 5–6 | Jump-to-support negatives — 5 reps × 3s descent | All 5 controlled |
| 7–8 | Negatives 5s descent + first kipping attempts | Some kipping reps landing |
| 9–10 | Kipping bar muscle-up — 1 clean rep | Consistent kipping rep |
| 11–12 | Kipping bar muscle-up — 3+ reps + begin slow-lower from top | 3 reps + 1 controlled negative |

## Deload Protocol (Week 4, 8, 12)

- **Block 2:** Reduce to 2–3 sets. Keep same weight — do NOT drop load.
- **Accessories/Supersets:** Drop to 2 rounds only.
- **HIIT:** Skip entirely on deload weeks.
- **Skill work:** 50% volume (fewer sets, same quality). Do not skip.
- Sessions should feel almost easy. That's the point.
