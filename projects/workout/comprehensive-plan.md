## Philosophy

Every plan in this document targets the same north star: **maximum strength growth** while genuinely developing athleticism, mobility, and calisthenics competence — not as afterthoughts, but as integrated training qualities.

**Core principles across all tiers:**

1. **Progressive overload is king.** Every plan includes a clear mechanism for making exercises harder over time — whether that's adding weight, adding reps, advancing to a harder variation, or increasing band resistance.
2. **Compound movements first.** Multi-joint exercises that load the most muscle mass get priority placement when you're freshest.
3. **Mobility is trained, not stretched.** Active mobility under load (CARs, loaded stretches, full-ROM movements) beats passive stretching for both flexibility and injury prevention.
4. **Calisthenics progressions are strength training.** A planche lean is not "bodyweight stuff" — it's a heavy press. Progressions are treated with the same periodization rigor as barbell work.
5. **Athletic qualities emerge from intent.** Speed, power, and coordination come from performing movements explosively and with purpose — not from doing "agility drills."

# PART 2 — PROGRAMMING TEMPLATES BY FREQUENCY

## 3-Day Plan (Full Body × 3)

**Best for:** Beginners, those with limited time, or recovery-constrained schedules.
**Split:** Full body every session, 48+ hours between sessions.
**Session length:** 60–75 minutes.

| Day | Focus | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (Mon) | Full Body — Push emphasis | Horizontal push compound, squat pattern, pull accessory, core |
| Day 2 (Wed) | Full Body — Pull emphasis | Vertical pull compound, hinge pattern, push accessory, core |
| Day 3 (Fri) | Full Body — Legs emphasis | Bilateral leg compound, push + pull accessories, athletic circuit |

**Structure per session:**
1. Warm-up + mobility (8 min)
2. Power opener — 1 explosive movement (5 min)
3. Primary compound (12 min)
4. Secondary compound — different plane (10 min)
5. Calisthenics skill block (8 min)
6. Superset accessories (10 min)
7. Conditioning finisher — Day 3 only (7 min)

---

## 4-Day Plan (Upper/Lower × 2)

**Best for:** Intermediate lifters wanting more volume than 3-day but not ready for 6.
**Split:** Upper → Lower → Rest → Upper → Lower → Rest → Rest.
**Session length:** 65–75 minutes.

| Day | Focus | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (Mon) | Upper Heavy | Heavy push + pull compounds, calisthenics skill |
| Day 2 (Tue) | Lower Heavy | Heavy squat/hinge, L-sit skill, athletic power |
| Day 3 (Thu) | Upper Volume | Moderate push + pull, accessories, HIIT circuit |
| Day 4 (Fri) | Lower Volume | Moderate legs, accessories, conditioning circuit |

**Structure per session:**
1. Warm-up + mobility (8 min)
2. Power opener (5 min)
3. Compound A (12 min)
4. Compound B (10 min)
5. Calisthenics skill block (8 min)
6. Superset accessories (12 min)
7. Conditioning circuit — volume days only (10 min)

# PART 4 — PROGRESSION SYSTEMS

## Bodyweight / Calisthenics Progression Rules

Since you can't add weight easily to bodyweight movements, progression comes from **harder variations** and **volume/tempo manipulation**.

### Push Progression Ladder
```
Push-up → Diamond push-up → Decline push-up → Archer push-up →
Pseudo planche push-up → One-arm push-up (assisted → full)
```

### Pull Progression Ladder
```
Australian row → Pull-up (assisted → full) → Chin-up →
Chest-to-bar pull-up → Archer pull-up → Muscle-up
```

### Squat Progression Ladder
```
Bodyweight squat → Bulgarian split squat → Skater squat →
Shrimp squat → Pistol squat (assisted → full)
```

### Hinge Progression Ladder
```
Glute bridge → Single-leg glute bridge → Band-assisted Nordic curl →
Nordic curl (partial ROM → full) → Nordic curl holds
```

### Advancement Rule
- **Complete all prescribed reps at clean form for 2 consecutive sessions** before advancing to the next variation.
- If a variation is too hard, add a "bridge" step: assisted version, partial ROM, or eccentric-only.

### Volume Progression (When Variation Can't Increase)
- Week 1: 3×8
- Week 2: 3×10
- Week 3: 4×10
- Week 4: Deload (2×8)
- Week 5: Advance variation, reset to 3×6–8

### Tempo Manipulation
When you hit a plateau and can't advance variation yet:
- Add a **3-second eccentric** (lowering phase)
- Add a **2-second pause** at the bottom
- Both dramatically increase time under tension without changing variation

## Band Resistance Progression

Most band sets come in 4–5 levels. Progression:

1. **Light band** → master exercise form (15+ clean reps)
2. **Medium band** → working sets (10–12 reps, last 2 hard)
3. **Heavy band** → primary strength work (6–10 reps, RPE 8–9)
4. **Extra-heavy band** → max strength (5–8 reps, RPE 9)
5. **Double band** → stack two bands for further overload

### Band-Specific Tips
- Band resistance is **not linear** — it's hardest at full stretch. Exercises feel very different from free weights.
- For pressing movements, this means the lockout is hardest (similar to chains on a barbell).
- For pulling movements, ensure full stretch at the start position — slack bands waste the exercise.
- Replace bands when they show cracks, tears, or discoloration. A broken band mid-exercise is dangerous.

---

## Barbell / Weighted Progression

### Linear Progression (Beginners / Early Intermediates)
- Add weight every session when all prescribed reps are completed with clean form.
- Upper body: +2.5 kg per session
- Lower body: +5 kg per session
- If you fail to complete all reps, keep the same weight next session.

### Double Progression (Intermediates)
- Work within a rep range (e.g., 4×6–8).
- Start at the bottom (4×6). Add reps each session until you hit the top (4×8).
- Then add weight and reset to 4×6.

### Deload Protocol (All Tiers)
- **Every 4th week**: Reduce volume by 40% (drop 1–2 sets per exercise, keep weight same).
- Continue calisthenics skill work at 50% volume.
- Deload week creates the 4-week mesocycle: 3 weeks loading → 1 week recovery.

# PART 5 — MOBILITY AND WARM-UP PROTOCOLS

## Universal Warm-Up (All Tiers, All Days) — 5 min minimum

This is the base. Add day-specific elements on top.

1. **Joint circles** (1 min): Neck, shoulders, elbows, wrists, hips, knees, ankles — 5 circles each direction.
2. **Hip CARs** (1 min): 3 full-range circles each direction, each leg.
3. **Shoulder CARs** (1 min): 3 full-range circles each direction, each arm.
4. **Light movement** (2 min): Whatever matches the day — push-ups for push days, squats for leg days, etc.

## Day-Specific Additions

### Push Days — Add:
- Wrist circles + wrist flexor/extensor stretch — 2 min (MANDATORY with planche work)
- Reverse wrist curls — 2×15 (light)
- Scapular push-ups — 2×8
- Band pull-apart — 2×15 (shoulder balance)

### Pull Days — Add:
- Scapular pull-ups — 2×8 (if bar available)
- Band pull-apart — 2×15
- Light row or pulldown — 1×12

### Leg Days — Add:
- Banded lateral walk — 2×10 each
- Bodyweight squat (slow, full depth) — 2×10
- Single-leg glute bridge — 2×8 each
- Cossack squat (bodyweight) — 2×5 each

## Active Mobility Exercises (Integrate Into Training)

These aren't "stretches" — they're mobility under load. Work them into warm-ups or accessory blocks:

| Exercise | What It Opens | When To Use |
|---|---|---|
| Jefferson curl (light/BW) | Posterior chain, spinal mobility | Pull days, leg days |
| Cossack squat | Adductors, ankle mobility, hip ROM | Leg days |
| Shoulder CARs | Full shoulder ROM | Every day |
| Hip CARs | Full hip ROM | Every day |
| Thoracic rotation (quadruped) | T-spine rotation | Push and pull days |
| Deep squat hold (30s–60s) | Ankle, hip, thoracic mobility | Leg day warm-up |
| Wrist conditioning | Wrist extension for planche | Push days (mandatory) |
| Pigeon pose (loaded, 30s holds) | Hip external rotation | Leg days |

## Notes

- **All plans are compatible.** You can run Plan A on travel weeks, Plan B at home, and Plan C at the gym — the movement patterns and progressions carry over.
- **Calisthenics skills progress across all tiers.** You can practice planche leans at home, banded planche work with bands, and parallette planche at the gym. They all build the same strength.

- **Sleep and nutrition are not optional.** No plan compensates for poor recovery. Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep and 1.6–2.2 g protein per kg bodyweight per day.
