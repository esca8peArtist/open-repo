# Nutrition 03 — Meal Planning Templates

**How to use these plans:**
1. Identify your goal (hypertrophy, strength, or conditioning)
2. Choose your budget tier (standard or budget)
3. Run the 7-day plan for 4 weeks minimum before assessing
4. Adjust calories up or down based on bodyweight trend (see Nutrition 01 for targets)
5. Swap individual foods freely — what matters is hitting the macros, not the exact foods

**Reference athlete:** 82 kg (180 lb), 4 days/week training, male. Female athletes reduce calories by 10–15% and reduce protein by 0.2 g/kg from targets (still hit minimums).

**Vegan adaptations** are listed after each plan. All plans can be made vegetarian by swapping meat for eggs, dairy, legumes, and tofu.

### Days 3–7 Pattern

Rather than listing every day in full (which adds bulk without new information), follow this template system:

**Training days (Days 1, 3, 5, 7 in a Monday/Wednesday/Friday/Sunday example):**
- Meals 1–3 as shown in Day 1 (anchor breakfast, pre-workout, post-workout)
- Dinner rotates protein source: beef Monday, salmon Wednesday, chicken Friday, eggs/legumes Sunday
- Keep carb total at 380–420 g; protein 160–180 g

**Rest days (Days 2, 4, 6):**
- Follow Day 2 structure: reduce carbs to 160–200 g, increase fats, maintain protein
- Rotate protein sources for dietary variety

### Budget Hypertrophy Option (~$40–45/week, $5.50–6.50/day)

Swap these:
- Salmon → Canned sardines ($1.20/can) or eggs (add 2 more per day)
- Almonds/walnuts → Peanut butter ($0.20/serving vs $0.50 for nuts)
- Greek yogurt → Regular plain yogurt (lower protein, cheaper)
- Whey protein → Whole milk (add 500 ml/day for extra protein + calories)
- Sports drink → Diluted orange juice + pinch of salt (comparable carbs + electrolytes)
- Fresh salmon → Frozen tilapia ($3/500 g vs $9 for salmon)

Macros remain equivalent. Cost drops to approximately $5.50–6.50/day.

### Vegetarian/Vegan Adaptation (Hypertrophy Plan)

**Replace all meat with:**
- Tofu (firm): 100 g = 8 g protein; use 250–300 g per meal for adequate protein
- Tempeh: 100 g = 19 g protein (best whole-food plant protein for athletes)
- Lentils: 200 g cooked = 18 g protein; pair with rice for complete amino acid profile
- Black beans: 200 g cooked = 15 g protein
- Edamame: 150 g = 18 g protein

**Protein supplement adjustment (vegan):** Replace whey with pea+rice protein blend (look for brands with 25–27 g protein per serving; leucine content should be 2.5+ g per serving). Add 10–15% more total daily protein vs. omnivore target.

**B12 supplementation is non-negotiable for vegans:** 250–500 mcg methylcobalamin daily.

**Sample vegan hypertrophy day:**
- Breakfast: Oats + soy milk + peanut butter + banana (490 cal, 20 g protein, 72 g carbs)
- Pre-workout: Tempeh stir-fry (150 g) + white rice (150 g cooked) (430 cal, 37 g protein, 46 g carbs)
- Post-workout: Pea+rice protein shake + banana + sports drink (350 cal, 28 g protein, 58 g carbs)
- Lunch: Tofu scramble (250 g) + sweet potato (200 g) + spinach (460 cal, 22 g protein, 44 g carbs)
- Dinner: Red lentil pasta (150 g cooked) + lentils (100 g) + tomato sauce (480 cal, 31 g protein, 70 g carbs)
- Sleep snack: Soy yogurt (250 g) + pumpkin seeds (20 g) (290 cal, 16 g protein, 20 g carbs)
- **Total: 2,500 cal | 154 g protein | 310 g carbs | 65 g fat**
- *Increase portions of tempeh/lentils to reach 170 g protein target*

## Quick-Prep Meal Strategies (Under 30 Minutes Weekly Prep)

### Batch-Cooking System (Sunday 45 minutes, saves time all week)

**Rice:** Cook 1 kg dry white rice at once in a large pot. Portion into 150 g servings in containers. Refrigerate — lasts 5 days. Reheat with splash of water.

**Protein:** Bake 1 kg chicken breast in oven (200°C, 25 min). Store in fridge, slice and serve cold or reheated. Alternatively: hard-boil 12 eggs at once (12 min).

**Sweet potato:** Microwave 4–6 medium sweet potatoes (7 min each or all together in batches). Portion and refrigerate.

**Result:** Every meal is 5 minutes to assemble. Batch prep eliminates the daily decision about food and keeps compliance high.

### Fastest Individual Meals (Under 5 Minutes)

| Situation | Meal | Macros (approx) |
|---|---|---|
| No time for breakfast | Protein shake + banana + OJ | 25 g P, 50 g C, minimal fat |
| Missed pre-workout meal | Greek yogurt + honey + banana | 18 g P, 55 g C, 5 g fat |
| Post-workout (travelling) | Protein bar + sports drink | 20–25 g P, 50–60 g C |
| No cooking at dinner | Canned tuna + rice cakes + avocado | 35 g P, 30 g C, 15 g fat |
| Late-night protein | Cottage cheese (no prep) | 20–25 g P, 7 g C |

### Freezer-Friendly Batch Meals

**Beef and rice bowls:** Brown 1 kg ground beef, season, divide into 200 g portions with 200 g cooked rice. Freeze in ziplock bags. Microwave 4 minutes from frozen.

**Egg muffins:** 12 eggs + diced vegetables in a muffin tin, baked at 180°C for 20 min. Store 5 days. 2 per serving = 13 g protein, 0 g carbs.

**Overnight oats:** 5 minutes prep night before. 80 g oats + 300 ml milk + protein powder (25 g) + banana slices. Refrigerate overnight. No morning cooking.
