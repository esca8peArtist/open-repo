**Project:** Workout PPL Redesign
**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Awaiting User Selection

---

## Key for All Proposals

- **RPE** = Rate of Perceived Exertion (1–10 scale; 10 = max effort)
- **Rest** = time between sets
- **D1/D4** = Day 1 (heavy) / Day 4 (volume) of same split
- All sessions: **75-minute hard cap**
- Heavy days (D1, D2, D3): No HIIT block
- Volume days (D4, D5, D6): HIIT circuit at the end
- L-sit skill work always BEFORE heavy compounds on leg days (hip flexors need to be fresh)
- Wrist warm-up is mandatory every push day before any planche work

# PROPOSAL 1: "IRON FOUNDATIONS"

**Philosophy:** Barbell strength is the backbone. The big compound lifts form the structural core of each session. Calisthenics skills are dedicated blocks treated with the same seriousness as the main lifts. Judd Lienhard-style chain activation in every warm-up. This is the plan for someone who wants to get seriously strong on the barbell while layering in world-class body control skills.

**Best for:** If you want the most direct path to raw strength gains and a clear, predictable progression structure.

**Exercises from your current plan preserved:** Jefferson curl, Pendlay row, RDL, Nordic curl, box jump, front squat, back squat, hack squat, incline bench, skull crushers, Y-raise, lateral raises, Arnold press, T-bar row, weighted chin-ups, cossack squat, spider curls

## DAY 1 — PUSH HEAVY (75 min, no circuit)

### Warm-Up — 10 min
1. Wrist circles + wrist flexor/extensor stretch — 2 min *(mandatory planche prehab)*
2. Reverse wrist curls (light) — 2×15
3. Band pull-aparts (pronated) — 2×20
4. Scapular push-ups (protract/retract) — 2×10
5. Shoulder CARs — 5 reps each arm
6. Bear crawl — 2×10m

### Block 2 — Strength (35 min)
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barbell Bench Press | 4×4-5 | 2.5–3 min | RPE 8–9. Add 2.5kg when all reps clean |
| Weighted Dips | 3×5-6 | 2 min | RPE 8. Belt or dumbbell between legs |
| **PLANCHE SKILL** | | | |
| → Planche lean | 3×20s | 90s | Arms straight, shift bodyweight forward over wrists. Squeeze everything. |
| → Pseudo planche push-ups | 3×5-8 | 90s | Hands at hip level, elbows back, big lean. Quality over quantity. |

### Block 3 — Accessories (20 min)
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Y-Raise (prone on incline bench) | 3×12-15 | 60s | Thumbs up, slow and controlled — shoulder health |
| Cable Lateral Raise | 3×12-15 each | 60s | Clean arc, no shrugging |
| Barbell Skull Crushers | 3×10-12 | 60s | Slow eccentric |

**Session time estimate: ~68 min**

# PROPOSAL 2: "CALISTHENICS HYBRID"

**Philosophy:** Weighted bodyweight movements form the primary push and pull. Barbells are supporting players, not the stars of the show. Skill work is more heavily integrated — you're building toward planche, L-sit, and muscle-up first, and the weight room serves those goals. Pull-ups and dips come before bench and row on heavy days. This program develops the best calisthenics athlete that also happens to be strong in the weight room.

**Best for:** If you want the fastest path to your muscle-up and planche, and you're comfortable with weighted bodyweight as the primary training tool.

**Key difference from Proposal 1:** Weighted dips and pull-ups are the PRIMARY push and pull movements (not accessories). Barbell work supports them.

# PROPOSAL 3: "MASS METHOD ATHLETIC"

**Philosophy:** Directly inspired by Judd Lienhard's MASS Method (Movement, Athleticism, Strength, Speed). Every session has an explosive/power component that comes BEFORE the strength work (Post Activation Potentiation — the power work primes your CNS for heavier lifts). Chain mobility is woven throughout, not just in the warm-up. The goal is to become a complete athlete — strong, powerful, mobile, and skilled. The conditioning circuits have an athletic character (not just "more reps" but explosive and multi-plane).

**Best for:** If you want to train like an athlete — not just a lifter — and want the most Lienhard-influenced approach.

**Key difference:** Explosive power work comes FIRST in Block 2, before the heavy compounds. This is the PAP (Post Activation Potentiation) approach — power primes strength. Every session feels different from a movement quality standpoint.

# DELOAD WEEK PROTOCOL (Week 4, 8, 12 and onwards)

Every 4th week:
- **Block 2:** Reduce to 2–3 sets (same weight, don't drop load)
- **Block 3:** Reduce to 2 sets
- **Block 4 (HIIT):** Skip entirely on deload weeks
- **Skill work:** Continue at 50% volume (fewer sets, same quality — do not skip entirely)
- **Feel:** Sessions should feel almost too easy. That's the point.
