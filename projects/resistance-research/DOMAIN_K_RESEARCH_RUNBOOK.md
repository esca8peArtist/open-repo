## THE 6-WEEK EXECUTION TIMELINE

### Week 1 (November 4-10): Legislative Inventory Assembly and Election Results Integration

**Primary objective:** Update the legislative baseline from June 2026 to November 2026. Identify which reform vehicles survived the 119th Congress in any form (committee hearings, floor votes, Republican co-sponsors) and confirm the 120th Congress legislative landscape given election results.

**Specific research tasks:**

1. Congress.gov status check — all six reform vehicles:
   - S.1814 / H.R.3513 (SCERT Act): Any committee movement? Any Republican co-sponsors?
   - H.R.1074 (term limits): Bipartisan co-sponsor count; any Republican expressions of interest?
   - SHADOW Act / SCCOTUS Act / Raskin package: Any committee hearings?
   - JUDGES Act (H.R.1702): Did the bipartisan vehicle advance? Any floor vote?
   - Goldman-Booker Ethics and Investigations Act: Status in 119th Congress
   - Manchin-Welch constitutional amendment: Any additional co-sponsors since December 2024?

2. Election results integration:
   - Did Democrats retake the Senate? House? Both? Neither?
   - Identify incoming Judiciary Committee membership and chair/ranking member assignments
   - Which reform champions retained their seats? Which reform opponents lost?
   - Activate the appropriate electoral scenario from DOMAIN_K_RESEARCH_ZONES_MAPPING.md Section 4 (Senate takeover / House takeover / split control / Republican retention scenarios)

3. Shadow docket statistics update:
   - Update Brennan Center shadow docket tracker through October 31, 2026
   - How many total emergency applications did Trump administration file in 2025-2026?
   - Has the grant rate changed? Has the Court voluntarily added any explanation requirements?

**Hour estimate:** 8-10 hours. Heavy primary source work (Congress.gov, SCOTUSblog, election results tracking). No deep analysis required in Week 1 — this is data gathering and status update.

**Key source:** Congress.gov for all bill status; Brennan Center shadow docket tracker for statistics; FiveThirtyEight or AP for definitive election results.

**Week 1 checkpoint:** By November 10, you should know: (a) which electoral scenario applies, (b) which reform vehicles are ready for 120th Congress reintroduction, (c) updated shadow docket statistics.

## RISK MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Expert Contact Unresponsive

**Most likely unresponsive contact:** International contacts (V-Dem, IDI, Verfassungsblog) and high-demand academics (Chemerinsky).

**Mitigation:**
- All Tier 1 contacts are organizational, not individual — organizations respond more reliably than individual academics
- Begin Tier 2 academic outreach October–November (not November–December) to allow 2-3 follow-up cycles
- For V-Dem and IDI: the data verification asks are small and specific ("did judicial independence indicators drive the downgrade?" / "was the selection law ruling issued?") — small specific asks have higher response rates than general engagement requests
- Contingency contact list in DOMAIN_K_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md has fallbacks for all high-priority contacts

**If V-Dem is unresponsive:** Substitute Freedom House 2026 Democracy Report (covers the US downgrade with its own metrics); or Carnegie Endowment POMED index; both cover the same deterioration.

**If IDI is unresponsive:** Substitute Haaretz English edition legal coverage; or Ofer Grosskopf (Tel Aviv University Law) who has written on Israeli judicial reform in English-accessible venues.

**If Chemerinsky is unresponsive:** Substitute Laurence Tribe (Harvard Law, emeritus) for the "most credible progressive constitutional law voice" function; or Jonathan Gould (Berkeley Law) who focuses on election law and constitutional structure.

### Risk 2: Source Paywalled or URL Defunct

**Most likely paywalled sources:** Cambridge "Ascent of the Canadian Judicial Council" (Source 39); individual law review articles not in HLR or VLR open-access archives.

**Mitigation:**
- All 22 anchor sources in DOMAIN_K_SOURCE_MASTER_DATABASE.md are free or accessible through standard institutional databases
- For paywalled academic sources: HeinOnline, JSTOR, and institutional library access cover all law review sources identified in the database
- UK JCIO Annual Report direct link may change — navigate via judicialconduct.gov.uk to locate current annual report
- Canadian Judicial Council reports navigate via cjc-ccm.ca for current annual report
- Congress.gov URLs are the most stable government source URLs; verify bill status but URL structure is consistent

**If a specific source is inaccessible:** The Phase 3 research brief does not need to cite every source in the database — the anchor sources are sufficient. Most paywalled sources can be substituted with Brennan Center or Fix the Court secondary analyses that cite the same underlying scholarship.

### Risk 3: Trump v. Slaughter Not Yet Decided by November 4

**Scenario:** The Court delays the ruling past the expected June-July 2026 window, and it remains pending on November 4, 2026.

**Mitigation:**
- The June 2026 preliminary research (PHASE_3_DOMAIN_K_PRELIMINARY_RESEARCH.md Section 5.1) already drafted the "expected ruling" framing: "If decided as signaled by six conservative justices at December 8, 2025 oral arguments..."
- If still pending November 4: use the "pending ruling" framing as a monitoring trigger — "Trump v. Slaughter's expected ruling on removal power will directly bear on the case for judicial reform by demonstrating whether an unreformed Court is concentrating executive power"
- Do not delay Domain K research past Week 2 waiting for the ruling — proceed with the pending-ruling framing and update the brief if the ruling issues before December 13

### Risk 4: Election Outcome Requires Major Brief Restructuring

**Scenario:** Republican retention of both chambers means the January 2027 legislative window is closed entirely.

**Mitigation:**
- The Domain K brief is valuable under any electoral outcome; the advocacy strategy shifts, not the research value
- Republican retention scenario: redirect framing to (a) coalition-building infrastructure work for 2028, (b) state-level judicial reform pipeline (47 states already have fixed terms), (c) ACS/law school distribution for long-term movement infrastructure
- The ethics reform polling (78-83% support) and term limits polling (74% support) remain valid organizing arguments regardless of electoral outcome — they establish public mandate for reform even when legislative pathway is closed
- The Tier 1 distribution to Fix the Court, Brennan Center, and ACS is valuable under any scenario (they do long-term organizing work regardless of which party controls Congress)

### Risk 5: Phase 2 Carryover Domains Create Competing Week 1-2 Demand

**Scenario:** Domains 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal, hold until August 10), Domain 59 Wave 2 follow-up, or Domain 54 (Youth Civic Power, August 1 integration deadline) generate competing Week 1 obligations.

**Mitigation:**
- All Phase 2 distribution windows should be complete or in monitoring-only status by October 31 — this is the primary pre-Phase 3 capacity protection action
- If Phase 2 monitoring obligations require 3-5 hours in Week 1, reduce Week 1 Domain K research to 5-7 hours and carry the deficit into Week 2 (the analytics-heavy week can absorb 2-3 hours of carryover)
- Do not let Phase 2 monitoring extend past Week 2 — set hard stop for Phase 2 active work November 17
