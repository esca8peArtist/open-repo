## Timing Windows

### Window A: Pre-*Callais* Follow-Up (May 20 – June 1, 2026)

**Status**: Active now.

The *Callais* ruling issued April 29, 2026. The first 30 days post-ruling are the window when voting rights organizations are actively publishing analyses and seeking practitioner resources. Domain 58's post-*Callais* section (Section 6 of the canonical document) provides:

- The Montana SB 490 May 11 injunction as the first post-*Callais* proof-of-concept state constitutional challenge
- Three alternative doctrinal frameworks (state constitutional equal protection, APA challenges to election administration rules, VRA Section 11(b) voter intimidation provision)
- Nevada SB 421 as the positive state legislative model

**Distribution action**: Send the post-*Callais* section (Section 6) as a standalone practitioner memo to Campaign Legal Center, NARF, Brennan Center, and NAACP LDF. Label it "Post-*Callais* Doctrinal Alternatives for Tribal Voting Rights — Practitioner Analysis." This is the fastest way to get the document into practitioner use before the full July 15 distribution window.

**Go/no-go gate**: If *Trump v. Barbara* issues before June 1, abort this window and go directly to Window B.

### Window B: Pre-*Turtle Mountain* Conference (May 25 – 28, 2026)

**Status**: Imminent. *Turtle Mountain Band of Chippewa Indians v. Howe* (No. 25-253) was redistributed for SCOTUS conference on May 14, 2026. Conference decision is expected before the end of May 2026.

**Distribution action**: If cert is granted, send Domain 58 immediately to Campaign Legal Center (active litigant), Brennan Center (amicus filer), NARF (monitoring), and NAACP LDF (coalition partner). Frame: "Cert granted in *Turtle Mountain*. Here is the full democratic infrastructure analysis of what private Section 2 enforcement means for tribal voting rights." This is a same-day distribution trigger — the cert grant makes Domain 58 immediately timely and practitioner-relevant.

**If cert is denied**: The Eighth Circuit rule eliminating private Section 2 enforcement now controls in North Dakota, South Dakota, Minnesota, Iowa, Nebraska, Missouri, and Arkansas — the states with some of the largest concentrations of tribal voters. Document the implications in Section 6 of the canonical file and distribute to the same organizations with the frame: "SCOTUS declined to resolve the Section 2 enforcement gap. Here is what tribal voting rights organizations in the Eighth Circuit face and what alternatives are available."

**Go/no-go gate**: Cert decision is the trigger. Either outcome creates a distribution opportunity; only a "relisted without action" outcome delays.

### Window C: *Trump v. Barbara* Ruling (June – July 2026)

**Status**: Expected late June/early July 2026. This is the hard deadline and the highest-priority distribution trigger.

**If any version of Sauer's "not birthright citizens" argument survives in the opinion (dicta or holding)**:

- [ ] Distribute Domain 58 to NCAI, NARF, and tribal legal offices within 72 hours
- [ ] Subject line: "*Trump v. Barbara* ruling — Indian Citizenship Act reaffirmation legislation needed now"
- [ ] Lead with Section 8.3 (Indian Citizenship Act Reaffirmation) and Section 9.1 Scenario A (rapid-response protocol)
- [ ] Contact Senate Indian Affairs Committee and House Natural Resources Indian Affairs Subcommittee directly with pre-drafted legislative text
- [ ] Activate full secondary contacts list simultaneously
- [ ] Do not wait for July 15 baseline date

**If the Court broadly rejects the EO (clean Citizenship Clause reaffirmation)**:

- [ ] Distribute Domain 58 to full contact list within 72 hours
- [ ] Subject line: "After *Trump v. Barbara*: What tribal nations won at SCOTUS — and why the assault on tribal democratic capacity continues"
- [ ] Lead with the ruling as validation of the Indian Citizenship Act's force, then pivot to the three ongoing tracks (BIA/IHS defunding, *Callais* voting rights erosion, appropriations crisis)
- [ ] This is the better hook for mainstream democratic reform audiences — use it to bring non-tribal organizations into the distribution network

**If the ruling addresses only the injunction scope (procedural outcome)**:

- [ ] Update Section 3 of the canonical document with the procedural posture
- [ ] Proceed on July 15 baseline schedule
- [ ] Monitor for merits-stage briefing and set a calendar reminder for the next distribution trigger

**Go/no-go gate**: Ruling release is the trigger. No action needed until it issues; preparation work (pre-drafted emails, legislative text, rapid-response memo) should be complete before the end of June.

**Sovereignty and Franchise Cluster** (for tribal legal organizations and voting rights litigators):
- Domain 58 + Domain 49 (*Callais* VRA Redistricting Emergency) + Domain 1 (Voting Rights and Elections)
- Sequence: *Callais* created the crisis (Domain 49) → voting suppression architecture (Domain 1) → tribal-specific constitutional dimension and available remedies (Domain 58)
