Hi Rebecca / 5calls team,

I'm a constituent organizer and I've been tracking the ICE detention expansion closely. I saw you already have scripts on ICE raids, mass detention, and the warehouse camps — great coverage. I want to suggest three issues that aren't yet on your platform and offer research to support them if it would help your team:

**1. Restore Detention Oversight (OIDO + CRCL)**
The Office of the Immigration Detention Ombudsman and DHS Civil Rights and Civil Liberties office were both placed on administrative leave in March 2025, effectively abolished. Both were partially restored after legal pressure, but are not at full capacity. Meanwhile: 32 people died in ICE custody in 2025 (deadliest year in 20 years), inspection reports are down 36% while the detained population is up 78%, and 94% of independently inspected facilities were found non-compliant. There's a clear constituent ask: restore OIDO and CRCL to full operational status and fund independent inspections. This pairs well with Senator Ossoff's January 2026 investigation (206 documented cases of medical neglect).

**2. Investigate Deaths in ICE Custody**
At least 32 deaths in 2025, 12+ through mid-March 2026, and one confirmed homicide (Geraldo Lunas Campos, Fort Bliss, medical examiner ruling). The ACLU/PHR found 95% of deaths from 2017–2021 were preventable. There's a concrete congressional ask: GAO investigation into ICE custody deaths and mandatory reporting reform. Senator Ossoff has the report; constituents can push their members to co-sponsor an investigation.

**3. Stop DOGE Surveillance of Immigrants (IRS/SSA Data Sharing)**
ICE has been granted access to IRS and Social Security Administration data through DOGE over objections from senior agency officials. Palantir received a $30M contract to build ImmigrationOS, an AI targeting system. The system has wrongly flagged US citizens. Courts have issued partial injunctions (42,695 documented violations). The constituent ask: support legislation blocking IRS and SSA data from being used for civil immigration enforcement.

I've done primary source research on all three and can send supporting documentation if it would help your team write the scripts faster. Happy to format it however is most useful.
