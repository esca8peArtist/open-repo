## Section 8: November Zone Output Quality Standards

Zone outputs are not acceptable if they contain any of the following:

- [TODO], [VERIFY], [ADD CITE], or equivalent placeholder language
- Secondary source summaries where the primary source (bill text, court opinion, CRS report) is available and accessible
- Unattributed characterizations of constitutional debates (must name the scholarly or advocacy position: "Heritage Foundation argues..." / "Fix the Court's position is...")
- Executive summary claims that cannot be traced to a specific zone section (the Zone K-4 executive summary will be written later — do not write a summary in Weeks 1–4 that outruns the underlying analysis)
- Claims about international comparative systems that are not verified against the primary source for that jurisdiction (Bundesverfassungsgericht structure must be cited from the German Basic Law or official court documents, not from secondary American legal commentary)

**Plain-language test for Zone K-2**: Have a non-legal-specialist read the Zone K-2 conclusion paragraph. If they cannot determine (a) whether statutory term limits are constitutional, (b) what the main objection is, and (c) what Fix the Court believes, the conclusion is not ready.
