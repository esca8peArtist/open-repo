# Domain 37 Pre-Distribution Baseline Metrics

**Date Established**: 2026-05-01  
**Domain**: Federal Executive Interference in 2026 Midterms  
**Purpose**: Establish quantified pre-distribution baseline for post-distribution impact measurement  
**Business Value**: Enables rigorous pre-post measurement of framework impact on election protection coordination  

---

## Executive Summary

Domain 37 ("Federal Executive Interference in 2026 Midterms") tracks four categories of federal government threats to democratic election integrity in 2026:

1. **DOJ Voter Roll Litigation** — Federal litigation to suppress voter rolls
2. **CISA Election Security Funding** — Budget cuts reducing state election security capacity
3. **Executive Appointees** — Election denial ideology in federal positions
4. **Section 3 Litigation Readiness** — State AG positioning for 14th Amendment enforcement

This document establishes quantified baselines **as of May 1, 2026** (pre-distribution), enabling measurement of whether Domain 37 distribution changes institutional adoption or policy response in election protection organizations.

---

## Baseline Metrics (As of May 1, 2026)

### Metric 1: DOJ Voter Roll Litigation (Active Cases)

**Baseline (May 1, 2026)**: **23 active cases**

**Measurement Methodology**:
- Monthly tracking via PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records)
- Data: Case number, state, filing date, status, next hearing
- Update cadence: 1st and 15th of each month
- Record location: `projects/resistance-research/metrics/doj-litigation-tracker.csv`

**Success Metric**: If case count changes post-distribution AND state AGs cite framework in defensive filings → framework drove litigation strategy

**Current Trend**: 4.6× increase from April 2025 (5 cases); accelerating litigation intensity

---

### Metric 2: CISA Election Security Budget (Funding & Cuts)

**Baseline (May 1, 2026)**: **$700M proposed budget cut for FY2027**

**Budget Status**:
- Proposed cut: 64% reduction from FY2026 ($1.1B → $400M estimated)
- Timeline: Budget finalized April 2026; implementation October 2026
- Targets: EI-ISAC elimination, regional advisors, training programs

**Measurement Methodology**:
- Congressional tracking via Congress.gov, CISA statements, appropriations committees
- Decision points: June 30 (markup), September 30 (final bill)
- Record location: `projects/resistance-research/metrics/cisa-budget-tracker.md`

**Success Metric**: If Congressional statements/amendments post-distribution cite framework; if funding cuts reduced from $700M

**Critical Window**: May 1 (distribution) → September 30, 2026 (final appropriations)

---

### Metric 3: DHS/DOJ Election Denier Appointments (Federal Positions)

**Baseline (May 1, 2026)**: **11+ identifiable appointments**

**Position Categories**:
- High-impact: 4–5 (direct election authority)
- Medium-impact: 3–4 (agency leadership)
- Lower-impact: 3–4 (advisory roles)

**Measurement Methodology**:
- Federal job database monitoring, agency org charts, Senate testimony
- Quarterly updates (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct)
- Record location: `projects/resistance-research/metrics/election-denier-appointments.csv`
- Data fields: Name, position, agency, appointment date, election denial statements, authority scope

**Success Metric**: If state AGs/Congress cite framework in confirmation hearings or litigation against federal officials post-distribution

**Context**: Unprecedented appointments; no prior historical parallel for comparison

---

### Metric 4: Section 3 Litigation Readiness (14th Amendment Enforcement)

**Baseline (May 1, 2026)**: **3–5 state AGs publicly prepared; 5–10 additional privately advancing litigation**

**Key Leaders**: Jena Griswold (Colorado) publicly positioned; others implicit in election protection work

**Legal Context**:
- Authority: 14th Amendment Section 3 (disqualification for office)
- Application: Federal officials engaged in election denial could be targeted
- Timing: Post-election litigation window opens December 2026

**Measurement Methodology**:
- Leading indicators: State AG public statements, specialized hiring, letter campaigns, test litigation
- Lagging indicators: Actual Section 3 filings, court rulings, appeals
- Record location: `projects/resistance-research/metrics/section-3-positioning.md`

**Success Metric**: If state AGs cite framework in Section 3 litigation briefs or coordination statements post-distribution

**Critical Window**: May 1 (distribution) → November 2026 (election) → January 2027 (Section 3 window opens)
