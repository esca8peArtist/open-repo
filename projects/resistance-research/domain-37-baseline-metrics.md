This document fixes seven quantified baselines as of May 5, 2026 — before Domain 37 enters distribution. ## Metric 2: CISA Election Security Budget — FY2026 Enacted vs. FY2027 Proposed

**Baseline (May 5, 2026): CISA FY2026 election security earmark $39.6M; FY2027 proposal eliminates the program entirely as part of a $707M gross / $360M net CISA budget cut**

### Program-Level Budget Status

| Program | FY2026 Status | FY2027 Proposed |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| EI-ISAC (Election Infrastructure ISAC) | CISA terminated cooperative agreement with Center for Internet Security; $39.6M FY2026 earmark nominally preserved but underlying program defunded | Eliminated |
| MS-ISAC (Multi-State ISAC) | CISA cut $10M in funding (announced March 2025); still operating in paid capacity but projected to lose ~2/3 of state/local members | Eliminated |

| CISA total appropriation | FY2026: ~$2.7B (down ~$135M/4.6% from prior year) | FY2027 proposal: net reduction of ~$360M from FY2026; 867 positions eliminated |

### Congressional Resistance Signal

The FY2027 CISA budget requires Congressional approval. In the prior year's appropriations fight, proposed cuts of $490M were narrowed to $130–300M through Congressional resistance, including from Republican committee members. Rep. Andrew Garbarino (R-NY) has stated on the record that "Congress has a responsibility to ensure the agency has the resources it needs to succeed." The FY2027 proposal is more aggressive; the appropriations trajectory remains the key measurement window.

## Metric 4: Section 3 (14th Amendment) Litigation Readiness

**Baseline (May 5, 2026): Active Section 3 enforcement doctrine is post-Anderson constrained to Congressional action or federal judicial proceedings; no current active Section 3 disqualification proceeding against any 2026 election-administration official; CREW maintains the most developed factual record**

### Legal Landscape

- **Trump v. Anderson (March 4, 2024)**: Supreme Court unanimous ruling — states lack authority to enforce Section 3 for federal offices; Congress must provide enforcement mechanism under Section 5. This eliminates state-administrative disqualification of federal officeholders but does not eliminate: (a) congressional enforcement legislation; (b) federal court enforcement once factual record is established through proper federal proceeding; (c) application to state-level officials.

- **Current federal enforcement statute**: None. No Section 5 enforcement statute enacted in 119th Congress as of May 2026.

- **CREW factual record**: Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington maintains the most developed factual documentation of January 6 participation by individuals who now hold federal positions — the predicate for any Section 3 proceeding.

- **Colorado context**: Jena Griswold (current Colorado Secretary of State, running for AG) pursued Section 3 ballot challenge in 2023-24 cycle; she is publicly positioned on election protection and qualified for the 2026 primary ballot at the March 28 state assembly (41.78% of assembly vote). As AG, she would have additional authority to file federal Section 3 enforcement proceedings.

- **State AG positioning**: No state AG has publicly filed a Section 3 proceeding targeting current federal election-administration officials as of May 5, 2026. At least 23 state AGs are in active election protection litigation (the mail ballot EO coalition). Their public record-building on federal election interference creates the factual foundation for subsequent Section 3 proceedings.

**Sources**: [Lawfare, Section 3 analysis](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/section-3-disqualification-answers-and-many-more-questions), HIGH confidence; [CREW, Section 3 enforcement record](https://www.citizensforethics.org/reports-investigations/crew-reports/past-14th-amendment-disqualifications/), HIGH confidence; [Colorado Newsline, Griswold AG campaign](https://coloradonewsline.com/briefs/secretary-of-state-jena-griswold-will-run-for-colorado-attorney-general-in-2026/), HIGH confidence; Domain 37 source document, Section IV.B, HIGH confidence.

**Measurement Protocol (Post-Distribution)**
- Track CREW filings, state AG public statements, and Congressional Section 5 bill introductions
- Measure pre-election: whether any AG files declaratory action establishing factual record of specific official's engagement in 2020 reversal efforts (the "factual record" pathway identified in Domain 37)
- Measure post-election: number of Section 3 disqualification proceedings filed against officials who interfered with 2026 results
- Indicator that framework contributed: Section 3 briefs or Congressional testimony citing the "post-Anderson enforcement architecture" framing developed in Domain 37
