## SECTION 4: Domain 58 (Tribal Sovereignty) — Rapid-Response Check

Three developments to track, all confirmed via live search May 26, 2026:

### Development 1: Turtle Mountain Band v. Howe — GVR Issued May 18

**Status**: GVR (Grant, Vacate, Remand) confirmed issued by SCOTUS on **May 18, 2026**.

The Supreme Court vacated the 8th Circuit's May 14, 2025 ruling that had eliminated private plaintiffs' right of action to enforce VRA Section 2. The Court remanded to the 8th Circuit for further consideration in light of *Louisiana v. Callais* (April 29, 2026).

**Significance**: The 8th Circuit's original ruling had been the most aggressive anti-VRA ruling in the post-*Callais* environment — it removed private enforcement entirely. The GVR is a partial reprieve: the 8th Circuit must now reconsider whether Section 2 has a private right of action under the intent standard *Callais* requires. The tribal plaintiffs (Spirit Lake Tribe, Turtle Mountain Band) who won at the district court level (November 2023) remain in active litigation.

**What to add to Domain 58**: Update Section on VRA enforcement mechanisms to note that the 8th Circuit's private right of action elimination was vacated as of May 18. The *Callais* impact on tribal VRA claims is now remanded for further development — the situation is slightly better than it appeared post-*Callais* but still highly uncertain.

**Sources**: NARF (narf.org/tmc-voting-rights/), Brennan Center (brennancenter.org/our-work/court-cases/turtle-mountain-band-chippewa-indians-v-howe), SCOTUSblog

### Development 2: Montana SB 490 — Injunction Confirmed May 11

**Status**: Injunction granted **May 11, 2026** by Montana First Judicial District Court, Lewis and Clark County.

Montana Senate Bill 490 (2025 legislative session) would have eliminated eight hours of Election Day voter registration opportunity. The court enjoined the law, finding:
- The state presented no "competent evidence demonstrating that SB 490 reduces administrative burdens or improves election efficiency"
- No "evidence of voter fraud associated with Election Day Registration"
- The law would "disproportionately impact certain groups of voters, including Native American voters and young voters"

Tribal intervenors in the case: Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes, Blackfeet Nation, Fort Belknap Indian Community, Northern Cheyenne Tribe, and Western Native Voice.

Montana's existing same-day voter registration rules remain in place through the injunction.

**NARF connection**: This case connects directly to NARF's Northern Cheyenne Tribe v. Montana litigation (challenging Montana's failure to designate tribal enrollment offices as NVRA voter registration agencies). The SB 490 injunction reinforces the same theme: Montana courts are enforcing baseline registration access against legislative attacks.

**What to add to Domain 58**: Add Montana SB 490 injunction (May 11) to Pathway 1 as a successful defensive action — demonstrates that state-level tribal voting rights litigation is active and achieving results even in the hostile post-*Callais* environment.

**Sources**: Daily Montanan (dailymontanan.com/2026/05/11/montana-district-court-blocks-law-restricting-voter-registration-hours/), Montana Free Press, NARF (narf.org/cases/mt-electoral-process-2025/)
