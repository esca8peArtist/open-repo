## Contingency Decision Trees

### Decision Tree 1: Congressional Bill Status Changes (Week 1-2)

```
NEW BILL OR MAJOR AMENDMENT IDENTIFIED?
│
├─ YES: Bill significantly advances judicial reform agenda
│   ├─ Is the bill central to Domain K narrative? (Term limits, ethics, shadow docket)
│   │   ├─ YES → Allocate 2-3 hours to integrate into Section 1 (Legislative Inventory)
│   │   │    Update Tier 1 contact engagement triggers
│   │   │    Proceed to Week 2 as scheduled
│   │   │
│   │   └─ NO → Add as footnote or update note
│   │        No schedule impact
│   │
│   └─ Is the bill likely to be reintroduced in 120th Congress (post-midterms)?
│       ├─ YES → Include in research; validate with Tier 1 contacts
│       └─ NO → Situate historically; no prioritization needed
│
└─ NO: No new major bills
   └─ Proceed to Week 2 on schedule
```

### Decision Tree 2: Litigation Ruling Arrives During Research Window (Week 1-4)

```
SUPREME COURT RULING ON DOMAIN-RELEVANT CASE?
│ (Trump v. Slaughter, Trump v. Cook, or related removal power case)
│
├─ YES: Ruling addresses executive power / judicial independence
│   ├─ Does ruling strengthen executive power over independent agencies / courts?
│   │   ├─ YES → Integrate into Domain K Section 2.4 (removal power case) 
│   │   │    and Domain H Section 1 (constitutional vulnerabilities)
│   │   │    Allocate 2-4 hours; consult Tier 1 contacts on implications
│   │   │
│   │   └─ NO (or neutral) → Add as update note; minimal revision needed
│   │
│   └─ Does ruling affect statutory term limits constitutionality analysis?
│       ├─ YES → Revise Domain K Section 2 (constitutional feasibility); 
│       │    2-3 hours; re-validate with Fix the Court (Roth)
│       └─ NO → Proceed with scheduled timeline
│
└─ NO: No ruling or ruling on unrelated topic
   └─ Proceed to scheduled timeline
```

### Decision Tree 3: Coalition Feedback Suggests Major Revision (Week 3)

```
TIER 1 PRACTITIONER FEEDBACK IDENTIFIES SIGNIFICANT CONCERN?
│
├─ CONCERN TYPE 1: Legislative Feasibility (e.g., "statutory approach won't pass SCOTUS")
│   ├─ Is concern backed by legal authority we haven't addressed?
│   │   ├─ YES → Allocate 2-3 hours to strengthen Section 2 (constitutional feasibility)
│   │   │    Add legal authority / ABA analysis; revise by December 13
│   │   │    Proceed with Gist launch on schedule
│   │   │
│   │   └─ NO (i.e., concern is speculative) → Document in cover letter to recipients
│   │        Proceed with research as-is; let academic community weigh in
│   │
│   └─ DECISION: Delay Gist launch? 
│       ├─ YES (if revision >3 hours) → Revise to Dec 18; alert distribution list
│       └─ NO (if revision <3 hours) → Revise in Week 6; maintain Dec 15 Gist date
│
├─ CONCERN TYPE 2: Coalition Activation (e.g., "my org isn't ready to co-launch")
│   └─ Allocation: 0 hours to research; adjust distribution timeline (Week 4-5)
│      Proceed with research as-is
│
└─ CONCERN TYPE 3: Missing Research Zone or Topic
   └─ Is the gap material to legislative strategy or policy analysis?
      ├─ YES → Allocate 3-5 hours; delay Gist to Dec 18-20
      └─ NO → Document as "future research" appendix; proceed with Dec 15 Gist
```

### Decision Tree 4: Post-Midterm Election Scenario Analysis (Late October – Early November)

**Used to adjust Phase 3 research framing if election produces unexpected outcome:**

```
2026 MIDTERM ELECTION OUTCOME KNOWN (by Nov 1-2)?
│
├─ SCENARIO A: Democrats gain House + Senate majorities
│   └─ ADJUSTMENT: Domain K research confirms legislative window is LIVE
│      • Emphasize Tier 1 reform pathways (ethics, shadow docket, judgeships)
│      • Tier 2 reforms (term limits) secondary but viable with majorities
│      • No research revision needed; adjust distribution tone/urgency
│      • Allocate 1-2 hours for cover letter/briefing framing adjustment
│
├─ SCENARIO B: Democrats gain House OR Senate (not both)
│   └─ ADJUSTMENT: Legislative window is PARTIAL; emphasize achievable reforms
│      • Prioritize ethics enforcement (lowest-controversy, highest-support reforms)
│      • Shadow docket transparency via House (if Dems have House) or stall (if Senate)
│      • No research revision; adjust distribution framing to single-chamber strategy
│      • Allocate 1-2 hours for framing adjustment
│
└─ SCENARIO C: Republicans retain both chambers
   └─ ADJUSTMENT: Legislative window CLOSED for 2027; shift to movement building
      • Reframe research as "agenda for next Democratic majority"
      • Emphasize international precedent and state-level pathways
      • Extend distribution timeline to 2027-2028 (post-2028 election positioning)
      • Allocate 2-3 hours for strategic repositioning
      • Consider delaying full distribution until January 2027 (post-election clarity)
```
