## Most Important Finding

Adding all 3 new candidates (M, N, O) to Phase 3 without deferring any existing domains would push Phase 3 total hours from 169-208 to 229-283 hours — roughly 30-36% above the original plan. This is not feasible without deferring 2-3 existing domains to Q2 2027. The critical path (Domains H, K, 49, 37a) is inviolable and cannot absorb additional load. Candidate M (Direct Democracy) must be treated as a Phase 2 acceleration, not a Phase 3 addition — it needs to ship before the November 2026 ballot measures, which is before Phase 3 formally launches.

## Scenario Analysis

### Scenario 1: Add Candidate M Only (Phase 2 Acceleration)

**Candidate M** has a September 2026 deadline — it belongs in Phase 2, not Phase 3. It does not consume Phase 3 capacity.

**Capacity impact on Phase 2**: 20-25 additional hours in the July-September 2026 window, when Phase 2 is already running Domains 49, 50, 31x, and 37a prep. This adds stress to the Phase 2 close but does not affect Phase 3 at all.

**Recommendation**: Treat Candidate M as Phase 2 domain, not Phase 3 expansion. It requires a Phase 2 acceleration decision (separate from this analysis). If Candidate M slips past October 31, 2026, its post-election analysis (what happened to the November supermajority ballot measures?) carries into Phase 3 as a 6-8 hour update, not a full domain — functionally parallel to the Domain 51 and Domain 56 post-election updates.

**Phase 3 capacity change**: 0 hours if Candidate M executes as Phase 2. +6-8 hours in December 2026 if post-election update is needed.

---

### Scenario 2: Add Candidate N (Whistleblower Protection)

**Candidate N** has a January 3, 2027 Congressional seating deadline for the legislative push component. It pairs naturally with Domain 56 (civil service restoration) because:
- Same distribution contacts: AFGE, NTEU, Government Accountability Project
- Same legislative window: 120th Congress first-90-days
- Same analytical frame: executive branch accountability infrastructure

**Hour addition**: 18-22 hours. If Domain N is bundled with Domain 56 (which runs November 15 – December 20), the combined Domain 56 + Domain N package becomes 36-44 hours in the November-December window.

**Which domain defers to accommodate Domain N?**

Domain I (International Standards) is the deferral candidate. Domain I was already the lowest-urgency domain in the original scorecard (6/10 urgency) and is explicitly deferrable to Q2 2027 per Item 96. The February 28, 2027 Freedom House submission deadline was identified as Domain I's primary hook, but Freedom House's window extends to March in practice.

**Deferral**: Domain I moves from January 10 – February 15, 2027 to March 1 – April 15, 2027.

**Cascade effect**: Domain L (Movement Infrastructure, starts March 14) slides to May 2027. Domain J (Cryptographic Democracy, February-March) is unaffected because it draws from a different source library and can run concurrently with Domain N in January-February.

**Phase 3 capacity impact**:
- November-December hours: +18-22 (add N) → 60-66 hours combined for this window
- January-March rate: unchanged (Domain I deferred, Domain N complete by January 3)
- Freedom House submission: missed February window, catches March (consequence: low)
- Phase 3 mid-cycle review: shifts from March 31 to April 30 (Domain L starts May)

**Verdict**: Scenario 2 is feasible without major disruption. Domain N bundles with Domain 56, Domain I defers 6 weeks, Freedom House gets a March submission instead of February.

### Scenario 3: Add Candidates N and O

**Candidate O** (Government Procurement) adds 22-28 hours in February-March 2027. At Phase 2 pace, this is nearly a full domain worth of new work in an already compressed window.

**Deferral required**: To add both N and O without exceeding capacity, two existing domains must defer:
1. **Domain I** defers to Q2 2027 (as in Scenario 2): saves 28-35 hours in January-February
2. **Domain L** defers to Q3 2027 (from March 2027 to June 2027): saves 20-25 hours in March

**Net effect**: Domains I and L defer; Candidates N and O replace them in the January-March window. The Phase 3 mid-cycle review moves from March 31, 2027 to June 30, 2027 — a full quarter slip.

**Phase 3 capacity with Scenario 3**:

| Period | Hours (original 9-domain) | Hours (add N+O, defer I+L) | Delta |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------------------|-------|
| Nov-Dec 2026 | 82-100 hrs | 100-122 hrs (+N) | +18-22 |
| Jan-Feb 2027 | 62-73 hrs | 54-65 hrs (-I+O) | -8 to -22 |
| March 2027 | 30-35 hrs | 22-28 hrs (-L) | -8 to -7 |
| **Total Nov-March** | **174-208** | **176-215** | **+2-7** |

The total Phase 3 hours increase only modestly because N and O are shorter than I and L combined. The real cost is the mid-cycle review slip (March → June 2027) and the Freedom House submission miss.

**Contingency cascade rule**: If Candidate O's FAR comment period slips past March 31, 2027, the domain defers to Q2 2027 entirely. This does not cascade further — Domain J and Domain N are complete by February-March, and the remaining capacity in April-June can absorb Candidate O alongside Domain I.

### Scenario 4: Add All Three New Candidates (M, N, O)

**Candidate M** executes as Phase 2 (no Phase 3 capacity impact). **Candidates N and O** execute as in Scenario 3. Net Phase 3 impact = Scenario 3.

This is the recommended expansion path if the user confirms all three new candidates are high enough priority. It does not require deferring any load-bearing Phase 3 domains (H, K, 37a, 49 are all inviolable). It defers the two lowest-urgency domains (I and L) by one quarter.

## Resource Reallocation Rules

**Rule 1 — Critical path protection**: If any of Domains H, K, 37a, or 49 runs over estimate by 10+ hours, immediately review whether Domain N or Domain O can absorb the slip by starting 2 weeks later. Do not defer critical path domains to accommodate new candidates.

**Rule 2 — Bundling efficiency**: Domain N and Domain 56 share 80% of their distribution contacts (AFGE, NTEU, GAP, government oversight committee staff). Produce them in the same research window (November 15 – December 20) and distribute as a combined package. Estimated bundling efficiency: 15-20% reduction in combined distribution overhead.

**Rule 3 — Candidate M early trigger**: If Candidate M is not research-complete by September 15, 2026, truncate scope to a 1,500-word rapid analysis covering the four November ballot measure states (Missouri, North Dakota, South Dakota, Utah) only. Ship by October 1. Drop the full comparative analysis to a post-election update in December 2026 alongside Domain 51 and Domain 56 updates.

**Rule 4 — Domain I Freedom House fallback**: If Domain I is deferred to Q2 2027 (Scenarios 2-4), submit a 1,500-word supplementary brief to Freedom House's US country researchers by February 28, 2027, drawing from the V-Dem March 2026 US downgrade data and the *Callais/Slaughter* SCOTUS rulings. This maintains the Freedom House relationship even without a full domain submission.
