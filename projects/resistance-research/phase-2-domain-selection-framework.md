# Phase 2 Domain Selection Framework

**April 29, 2026**

This framework converts Phase 1 distribution results into Phase 2 research decisions. It is the inverse of Phase 1 planning: where Phase 1 asked "what is the most complete and accurate diagnostic possible," Phase 2 asks "what did the distributed framework fail to provide that institutional audiences needed?" The gap-finding is systematic, not impressionistic.

Phase 1 launches with 34–35 domains covering the full spectrum of U.S. democratic erosion. This framework assumes Phase 1 distributes under Path A, Path A+37, or Path B — all three paths generate the same Phase 2 input data. What differs is timing: the Phase 2 decision window opens 4–6 weeks after Phase 1's first outreach wave.

---

## Part I: Phase 1 Data Collection Window (Weeks 1–6)

### What to Track

The goal is not volume metrics — impressions, total emails opened, subreddit upvotes. Those measure reach; Phase 2 domain selection requires relevance signals. The following five metrics distinguish a domain that should be deepened from one that landed adequately.

**1. Citation frequency by domain.** When a Tier 1 or Tier 2 contact responds, cites the framework, or publishes something that references it, note which specific domains they mention. A domain cited repeatedly by different contact types is either (a) landing as the most useful content, or (b) landing as the most incomplete — both are Phase 2 signals. Use the Overton Impact Tracking dashboard for external citations if the framework reaches academic or grey literature; use manual email log tagging for direct contacts.

**2. Institutional adoption rate by sector.** Adoption is a behavioral signal, not a sentiment signal. It registers when an organization moves from receiving the framework to using it: a bill drafter excerpts Domain 1 language into legislation; a law school clinic assigns Domain 6 as course reading; a state AG office requests the implementation toolkit. The measurement-and-iteration-framework.md already defines sector-specific adoption signals for five sectors (state legislators, appellate litigators, career executive agency staff, law schools, civil rights coalitions). Log adoption events in those five columns and track which sectors are moving fastest.

**3. Question frequency by domain.** Every inbound question from a contact — whether via email reply, Google Form, or Reddit comment — is a signal that something is missing. A question like "does Domain 16 address what happens to green card holders in ICE detention?" is an explicit gap report. Aggregate inbound questions by domain tag within the first three weeks. By Week 4, pattern frequency will surface the two or three domains generating the most "but what about..." follow-ups.

**4. Topic clustering in feedback.** Feedback rarely arrives in domain-aligned packages. A voting rights organization may respond to the election framework (Domain 37) but ask about deportation procedures — a Domain 16 question embedded in an election context. A fiscal policy NGO may engage with Domain 5 (Fiscal Reform) but ask about the military contracting pipeline — a Domain 8 / Domain 19 intersection. Cluster feedback thematically rather than by domain number. Clusters that cross two or more existing domains are the highest-value Phase 2 candidates: they identify intersections the framework covers separately but has not synthesized.

**5. Domain skip rate.** Equally important is what gets ignored. If Tier 1 think tanks consistently respond to Domains 1, 6, 23, and 29 but never mention Domains 10, 12, or 18, those skipped domains either (a) do not match the contacts' institutional focus areas, (b) are already covered well enough by existing research, or (c) are not politically salient in the current moment. Track silence as a data point. It tells you what not to deepen in Phase 2.

### Feedback Collection Infrastructure

**Primary channel: Google Form.** Every outreach email, Substack post, and Reddit distribution includes a link to a single-page feedback form with the following fields:
- Which domain(s) did you find most useful? (checkbox, domains listed)
- Which domain(s) did you wish had gone deeper? (open text)
- What question arose that the framework did not answer? (open text)
- What is your institutional role? (dropdown: academic / advocacy org / legislative staff / legal practitioner / journalist / other)
- Would you like to be notified when Phase 2 research is complete? (yes/no, email)

Keep the form to five questions. Longer forms generate near-zero completion rates in institutional outreach contexts. The VoterVoice 2024 Advocacy Benchmark Report documents that non-profit sector action rates peak at 14.2% and drop sharply with form length — a five-question form in this audience should expect 8–12% response among direct outreach contacts.

**Secondary channel: Email reply tagging.** Not every contact will complete the form. Replies to personalized outreach emails often contain richer qualitative signal than form responses. Designate a single email address for Phase 2 feedback and respond to all substantive replies with a short acknowledgment. Tag every reply with: (a) domain references mentioned, (b) institutional sector of sender, (c) gap or question identified (if any), (d) adoption signal level (received / engaged / adopted / cited). A simple spreadsheet suffices; this is not a CRM problem until adoption scale exceeds 50 active institutional contacts.

**Tertiary channel: Social monitoring.** Reddit mentions are trackable through post comment threads directly. Twitter/X mentions require keyword alerts on the proposal title, URL, and primary domain names. This channel generates the lowest signal-to-noise ratio but occasionally surfaces organic adoption by contacts who were not directly targeted — the highest-quality indicator that the framework has achieved self-sustaining credibility in a community.

## Part III: Gap Analysis Triggers (Weeks 4–6, Ongoing)

### Trigger Rules

Three trigger rules convert feedback signals into actionable Phase 2 research candidates. These are the core of the decision framework — clear, binary conditions that eliminate ambiguity about when a domain moves from "candidate" to "researched."

**Trigger A — Repetition across contacts.** When three or more contacts from different institutional types ask about the same missing topic within the same six-week window, that topic is a confirmed Phase 2 research candidate. The three-contact threshold prevents individual feedback from driving research decisions while ensuring genuine institutional demand is recognized. Example: if a voting rights org, a law school clinic, and a state legislative staffer all ask about the operational mechanics of CISA dismantlement and what state-level alternatives exist, Domain E (Election Administration Seizure) moves to Tier 1.

**Trigger B — Cross-sector spillover.** When a contact from Sector X asks about a domain that belongs in Sector Y's primary cluster, it indicates that an intersection between domains has not been made explicit enough in the framework. Example: if a fiscal policy NGO asks about the military contracting pipeline in the context of Domain 5 (Fiscal Reform) — a national security / defense budget question that sits in Domain 8 and Domain 19 territory — a synthesis document connecting fiscal authority and defense spending becomes a Phase 2 candidate. Cross-sector spillover is the highest-value gap signal because it identifies structural gaps the original domain architecture missed, not just depth gaps within a covered area.

**Trigger C — Hard-deadline domains.** Some Phase 2 candidates have external deadlines that make them time-sensitive regardless of feedback volume. These bypass the six-week collection window and proceed to research if they are not resolved before the deadline:
- Domain E (Election Administration Seizure): The 2026 midterms are November 3, 2026. For this domain to be actionable for election protection organizations, research must complete by July 2026. Trigger condition: Election protection organizations show any adoption signal.
- Domain G (Tribal Sovereignty): The *Trump v. Barbara* SCOTUS ruling on birthright citizenship is expected late June / early July 2026. Once issued, the ruling either confirms or changes the analytical framing of this domain significantly. Research should begin immediately after the ruling, not after a full six-week Phase 1 feedback window.
- Domain 21/25 updates (FISA Section 702 outcome): Already tracked in surveillance-tracking.md. A post-lapse or post-reauthorization update (300–500 words) triggers on the outcome itself — no feedback required.

## Part IV: Phase 2 Domain Prioritization (Week 6 Decision, Implement Weeks 7–10)

### Tier Structure

The tiered structure prevents scope creep while ensuring the highest-demand domains receive full research attention. Tier assignments are made at Week 6 based on the data collected in Parts I–III.

**Tier 1 — Highest demand (3–4 domains): 2,000–4,000 words each.** These are domains where feedback triggers have fired and institutional adoption signals confirm that a research investment will meet confirmed demand. They are researched first, in Weeks 7–10, and integrated into the proposal for a Phase 2 distribution round.

Pre-launch Tier 1 candidates (subject to confirmation by Phase 1 data):
- **Domain E: Election Administration Seizure** — acute external deadline, high sourcing ease, fills the operational gap between Domain 1's structural electoral reform focus and the active CISA dismantlement / federal voter roll crisis. Research trigger: any voting rights or election protection adoption signal.
- **Domain F: Civil Society Suppression** — OBBBA Section 112209 (enacted), Florida HB 1471 (effective July 1, 2026), IRS/FBI task force operational. High urgency; fills the gap between Domain 3 (democratic participation) and Domain 7 (rights protection) by documenting nonprofit infrastructure as democracy infrastructure. Research trigger: three contacts from advocacy organizations or legal practitioners ask about the nonprofit designation mechanism.
- **Domain B: Healthcare Access Implementation (OBBBA Medicaid Crisis)** — if Domain 31 depth proves insufficient for civil rights or health-sector contacts, an expanded Domain 31b addressing work requirement advocacy window (June 2026 HHS guidance comment period) becomes high-priority. Research trigger: health sector or civil rights sector contacts ask about OBBBA implementation specifics not covered in Domain 31.

**Tier 2 — Planned updates (2–3 domains): 1,000–1,500 words each.** These are lighter refreshes to existing domains, driven by specific legal or legislative developments. They are produced in parallel with Tier 1 research in Weeks 10–11.

- **Domain 1 update (SAVE Act follow-up + 2026 voting rights landscape refresh)**: If voting rights organizations adopt strongly, a May or June 2026 refresh of Domain 1 incorporating SAVE Act Senate failure analysis, the March 2026 election executive order status, and the D.C. district court injunction outcome is warranted. Approximately 800–1,200 words of new content integrated into existing Domain 1.
- **Domain 21/25 update (FISA Section 702 post-deadline, surveillance implications)**: The April 30 deadline has passed; outcome integration is a light update to surveillance-tracking.md (300–500 words). Triggered by confirmed outcome, not feedback volume.
- **Domain 25 update (War Powers constraints per Iran/NATO/Taiwan developments)**: If foreign policy organizations or national security contacts adopt the framework, a Domain 19f / Domain 25 update incorporating post-Iran-war developments, NATO credibility degradation, and Taiwan security window analysis from the Phase 2 expansion roadmap is warranted. Already research-complete in phase-2-expansion-roadmap.md — integration is the task.
- **Domain 37 follow-up (Post-2026 midterm election analysis)**: Conditional on Domain 37 Path A+37 Phase 2 targeting showing institutional adoption. A December 2026 post-election update to Domain 37 documenting what actually occurred versus what the framework predicted becomes valuable for credibility and for 2028 preparation.

**Tier 3 — Conditional domains: Research begins only if specific institutional partnerships form.**

These domains have high analytical value but are lower-priority absent confirmed demand from specific institutional partners who would use them.

- **Domain G: Tribal Sovereignty and Indigenous Democratic Exclusion** — trigger condition: NARF, NCAI, or any tribal government legal team engages with the framework, or *Trump v. Barbara* ruling issues with a holding that changes the citizenship analysis.
- **State Legislative Autocratization deepening (EXPLORATION_QUEUE Candidate 2)**: trigger condition: three or more state legislative staff contacts engage with the framework and request additional depth on state-level gerrymandering, preemption packages, or single-party supermajority entrenchment.
- **Congressional Power-of-the-Purse Restoration (EXPLORATION_QUEUE Candidate 3)**: trigger condition: fiscal policy organizations (EPI, CAP fiscal team, CBO-adjacent academic contacts) request the institutional side of the impoundment and budget authority question.

## Part VII: Success Metrics for Phase 2

Phase 2 is not measured by whether it produced more research. It is measured by whether the additional research increased institutional utility and adoption beyond Phase 1's baseline.

**Adoption velocity per new domain.** Phase 1 will establish baseline adoption rates per domain within its first 90 days. Phase 2 Tier 1 domains should show 20–30% higher adoption rate in their target sectors compared to the Phase 1 baseline for the same sector. A domain researched in response to confirmed demand from election protection organizations should be adopted by election protection organizations at higher rates than the Phase 1 election-relevant domains reached them. If it does not, the feedback-to-research pipeline had a signal problem, not a research problem.

**Institutional partnership formation.** At least one formal institutional partnership — an NGO, think tank, or law school clinic that formally incorporates the framework into ongoing work — within 90 days of Phase 2 distribution. "Formally incorporates" means: the organization cites the framework in a published document or publicly describes it as part of their analytical infrastructure. This is the threshold between a research product that was read and a research product that is being used.

**Citation velocity.** Phase 2 domains should appear in papers, policy briefs, or litigation documents within 2–3 months of publication. The measurement-and-iteration-framework.md tracks citations via Overton and Google Scholar alerts. Citation within 90 days is achievable for domains with strong sourcing and high institutional urgency. Election administration (Domain E) and civil society suppression (Domain F) are the most likely to generate fast citation because they address active litigation and live legislation — not merely reform proposals.

**Feedback quality improvement.** Phase 2 feedback should be more specific than Phase 1 feedback. If Phase 1 generates "this is very useful" responses and Phase 2 generates "we're using Section 4 of Domain E to brief our state AG coalition on the CISA gutting" responses, the feedback loop is working correctly. Qualitative signal specificity is itself a success metric.

The following decision tree operationalizes Parts I–IV into a single-pass decision instrument for use at Week 6.

    NO  → Domain E moves to Tier 3 (conditional). Continue.

[2] Have 3+ contacts from different sectors asked about nonprofit/IRS/terrorism designation?
    YES → Domain F (Civil Society Suppression) is Tier 1. Begin Week 7.
    NO  → Domain F stays Candidate inventory. Continue.

[3] Are civil rights or health sector contacts asking about OBBBA specifics not in Domain 31?
    YES → Domain 31b (OBBBA Medicaid depth addendum) is Tier 2. Begin Week 10.

    YES (issued June/July 2026) → Domain G (Tribal Sovereignty) activates immediately. Tier 1 or 2 depending on ruling scope.
    NO  → Domain G remains conditional.

[5] Are 3+ state legislative staff contacts engaging with the framework?
    YES → EXPLORATION_QUEUE Candidate 2 (State Autocratization) is Tier 2.
    NO  → Defer.

[6] Are fiscal policy organizations (EPI, CAP, CBO-adjacent academics) adopting?
    YES → EXPLORATION_QUEUE Candidate 3 (Power-of-the-Purse) is Tier 2.
    NO  → Defer.

        Log decision in WORKLOG.md. Begin Week 7 research.

## Stakeholder Communication Note

This framework works only if institutional contacts understand what kind of feedback is most useful. Outreach emails should include a brief orientation for recipients: "If you find a gap in the framework — a topic you needed that wasn't addressed, or an angle that needs more depth for your institutional context — the feedback form takes three minutes and directly shapes what we research next."

That sentence does two things. It reduces friction on feedback submission by framing it as an act of influence, not an act of evaluation. And it tells institutional contacts that their specific gaps, not general praise or criticism, are what drive the next research cycle. The clearer that is, the more specific the feedback will be, and the more accurately Phase 2 research will target genuine institutional demand.

The goal is not to produce more research. It is to produce the exact research that the organizations most likely to use the framework are currently unable to find anywhere else.
