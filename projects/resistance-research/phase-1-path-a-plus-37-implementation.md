## Section 2: Messaging Strategy — How A+Domain37 Differs from Path A

### The Fundamental Difference

Path A's message: "Complete 35-domain diagnostic framework for democratic recovery."

Path A+Domain37's message: "Comprehensive framework covering all major policy domains of democratic governance, anchored by an integrated election-security package — domestic and foreign interference together — that addresses the 2026 midterm threat directly."

The distinction matters because it changes what makes the framework distinctive. Path A's completeness argument (all 35 domains simultaneously) is a breadth claim — this is comprehensive. Path A+Domain37's argument is a depth-plus-timing claim: the election security domains are uniquely developed, internally integrated, and time-sensitive in a way that creates immediate institutional utility beyond what any other single research document provides.

### The New A+Domain37 Positioning With Two Domain 37 Documents

The addition of the foreign interference Domain 37 strengthens the A+Domain37 positioning in a specific way: it completes the election security package. The existing Domain 37 (domestic federal interference) documents the mechanisms by which the current administration is manipulating election infrastructure. The new Domain 37 (foreign interference) documents the mechanisms by which foreign adversaries — operating through the same institutional gaps created by CISA's dismantlement, the FBI task force's disbandment, and the DNI threat assessment's suppression — are positioned to exploit those gaps in 2026.

Together, the two documents constitute an integrated analytical product that no other source provides: a simultaneous, cross-referenced analysis of domestic executive interference and foreign adversary exploitation of the vulnerabilities that interference creates. The domestic interference domain is the supply side: who is dismantling the defense architecture and why. The foreign interference domain is the demand side: who benefits from those gaps and what they are doing to exploit them. The combination is analytically more powerful than either document alone.

For distribution purposes, both Domain 37 documents are packaged together for election-protection audiences as "The 2026 Election Security Briefing: Domestic and Foreign Interference Mechanisms." For general framework audiences, both documents are included in the 37-domain corpus with a framing note: "The election security section of the framework is particularly developed because the 2026 midterms are the most immediate institutional pressure point — it covers both the domestic executive interference mechanisms and the foreign adversary operations that exploit those mechanisms."

## Section 3: Success Metrics

### Metric Philosophy

The measurement framework in `phase-1-distribution-infrastructure.md` establishes five components of adoption measurement. This section adapts those components to the specific A+Domain37 path, with metrics designed to answer the practical question: is Phase 1 working well enough to justify Phase 2 activation?

Institutional adoption works on long cycles. A Tier 1 contact who receives the framework on Day 1 may cite it in a published policy brief 60-90 days later. What is measurable in the first 30 days is engagement signal — not citation — and what that signal predicts is eventual adoption probability. The metrics below are calibrated to the engagement signal level, not the adoption endpoint.

### Tier 1 Metrics (Days 1-28)

**Response rate**: Target 30% response rate from Tier 1 contacts within 14 days of initial send. "Response" counts substantive acknowledgment (referencing specific content in the framework), forwarding to a colleague, or a request for more material. Automated or generic acknowledgments do not count.

**Substantive engagement rate**: Of those who respond, target 50% with substantive engagement — they identify a specific domain or argument that is relevant to their current work. A response of "thank you for sharing" without content specificity is not counted as substantive.

**Warm introduction rate**: Target 3+ warm introductions (a Tier 1 contact introducing the researcher to a colleague who should see the research) by Day 28. Warm introductions are the highest-value Phase 1 signal because they indicate the research is being treated as network-worthy, not merely acknowledged.

**Domain 37 specific**: For election-protection-adjacent Tier 1 contacts (Klobuchar, Democracy Docket, Protect Democracy, States United, ACLU Voting Rights), target 40% response rate and at least 2 contacts who express interest in the advocacy window framing. The May 30 DOJ consent decree window is the first advocacy deadline — any contact that expresses interest before May 30 should receive the May 30 framing update email referenced in DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md.

### Tier 2 Metrics (Days 28-56)

**Academic engagement**: Target 5+ academic researchers who engage substantively (writing back with a citation, a course assignment inquiry, or a seminar invitation). Target 1+ researcher who indicates they are considering a paper, chapter, or review that would reference the framework.

**Media**: Target 2+ journalists or editors who express interest in covering the framework or adapting it into a piece. Target 1+ confirmed publication or pending submission by Day 56.

**Law review**: Target 2+ law review editorial teams that have received and acknowledged the submission pitch by Day 56. Target 1 confirmed submission in process.

### Tier 3 Metrics (Days 56-84)

**Organizational engagement**: Target 15+ Tier 3 organizations that have responded to outreach with a substantive engagement signal.

**Coalition signals**: Target 3+ organizations that express interest in formally endorsing or publicly citing the research in their communications.

**State integration signals**: Target 2+ state-level organizations (LWV chapter, state ACLU affiliate, state AG office) that indicate they are using or planning to use Domain 37 material in their 2026 election protection work.

### Phase 2 Entry Gate

Phase 2 (grassroots amplification and coalition building) activates when:
- At least 5 Tier 1 contacts have responded substantively (not rate — absolute count)
- At least 1 media mention or law review citation confirmed
- At least 3 coalition partnership conversations active with Tier 3 organizations

If Phase 1A does not generate 5 substantive Tier 1 responses by Day 28, diagnose before proceeding: are the subject lines generating opens? Is the domain-specific personalization insufficient? Is the framework's framing creating credibility barriers that more targeted positioning would resolve? The `execution/objection-responses.md` file contains 12 objection frameworks that can inform a messaging adjustment.

The plan is executable with approximately 90 minutes of user time to activate (email verification, template personalization for Batch 1A, publishing strategy confirmation) and 20-30 minutes per week of user time to maintain (logging responses, reviewing CHECKIN.md flags, sending follow-ups and new batches per the timeline).

If the user has a lower time budget in any given week, the timeline can be compressed at the front or expanded at the back without losing the most critical windows: the May 30 advocacy window requires Batch 1A election-protection contacts to be sent by May 15; the August 7 NVRA quiet period window requires Phase B election-protection targeted outreach to be complete by July 20. Within those hard deadlines, the timeline is flexible.
