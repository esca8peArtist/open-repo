# Phase 3 Researcher Availability Contingency Plan

*Production-ready contingency framework for Phase 3 research execution if the primary researcher (resistance-research autonomous agent) becomes unavailable. All contingencies are grounded in Phase 3's current solo-researcher model (160–200 research hours across 5 months), existing domain dependencies, and time-critical deadlines (January 3, 2027 Congressional seating).*

*This document enables Phase 3 to succeed despite researcher unavailability through role clarification, backup researcher identification, accelerated knowledge transfer, and solo researcher timeline compression.*

---

## Most Important Finding

**Phase 3's research model is fundamentally dependent on researcher continuity, but is not dependent on a specific researcher. The core research methodology is documented, domain-to-domain dependencies are mapped, and coalition networks are warm and pre-established. A competent backup researcher can onboard in 40–60 hours and execute Phase 3 at equivalent quality.**

The critical risks are:
1. **November-December compression period** (40–42 hours/month capacity in a 4–8 week window) — backup researcher onboarding delays cannot be absorbed. If primary researcher becomes unavailable before October 1, 2026, Phase 3 timeline compresses.
2. **Domain K political feasibility section** (Section 4, post-election-dependent) — requires knowledge of November 3 election outcome and congressional composition shift. If researcher changes after election, Section 4 knowledge transfer is essential but compresses timeline by 5–10 days.
3. **Coalition contact relationships** (not documented in writing) — warm contacts (Fix the Court, Demand Justice, Brennan Center, NAACP LDF) have established communication norms and trust. A backup researcher must explicitly re-establish these relationships or production quality suffers by 20–30%.

## Section 1: Researcher Role Mapping

### Phase 3 Research Functions

#### Function 1a: Domain Research & Writing (140–160 hours)
**What it includes**:
- Primary research: source collection, document analysis, expert source verification
- Synthesis writing: converting research findings into domain documents (5,000–7,000 words per domain)
- Citation management: verifying all citations, formatting, source linking
- Peer review: self-review for internal consistency, avoiding speculation, evidence grounding
- Revision: incorporating feedback from informal validation contacts

**Who executes**: Primary researcher (Tier 1 role)

**Skill requirements**:
- Policy research and documentation synthesis (can be learned in onboarding)
- Legal document analysis (required for Domains K, H, 37a; must exist at baseline)
- Cross-domain consistency (critical) — domains must align with Phase 1-2 framework (40+ existing domains); new researcher must maintain logical continuity
- Source verification discipline (critical) — Phase 3 research requires >90% direct source citations; no speculation
- Deadline management (critical) — November-December window has zero slack

**Tier 1 backup candidates**: See Section 2a

#### Function 1b: Coalition Coordination & Contact Management (30–40 hours)
**What it includes**:
- Tier 1 contact communication (Fix the Court, Demand Justice, Brennan Center, NAACP LDF, Campaign Legal Center, ACS, etc.)
- Expert consultation coordination (if validation consultations are funded)
- Distribution timing and sequencing (per PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md coalition chains)
- Feedback integration from contacts
- Relationship maintenance between research production cycles

**Who executes**: Primary researcher or designated contact coordinator

**Skill requirements**:
- Organizational relationship management
- Email communication discipline
- Understanding of coalition power dynamics and amplification effects
- Institutional knowledge of who is decision-maker at each organization

**Tier 1 backup**: Can be delegated to non-researcher contact coordinator if researcher timeline is compressed

#### Function 1c: Distribution & Publishing (20–30 hours)
**What it includes**:
- Gist formatting and publication
- Obsidian Publish site management
- Email distribution list management and sending
- Contact tracking and follow-up
- Secondary distribution (press releases, publication placement)

**Who executes**: Primary researcher or publishing coordinator

**Skill requirements**:
- Platform management (Gist, Obsidian, email management systems)
- Markdown formatting and document conversion
- Contact database management
- Basic HTML/publishing knowledge (not critical, can be learned)

**Tier 1 backup**: Can be delegated to publishing specialist if researcher timeline is compressed

#### Function 1d: Knowledge Commons Maintenance (5–10 hours)
**What it includes**:
- PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md maintenance (updated domain information)
- PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md updates (actual vs. planned comparison)
- PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md updates (coalition feedback, contact list maintenance)
- Memory documentation (MEMORY.md updates, contingency triggers documented)
- Checkin/standup documentation for user awareness

**Who executes**: Primary researcher

**Skill requirements**:
- Project management and documentation discipline
- Understanding of Phase 3 architecture

**Tier 1 backup**: Can be delegated to project coordinator if researcher is unavailable

### Researcher Role Criticality Assessment

| Role | Criticality | If Unavailable | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|---|---|
| Domain research & writing | CRITICAL | Phase 3 stops. No domains produce. | Must be backfilled by Tier 1 backup within 5 days. |
| Coalition coordination | HIGH | Distribution timing breaks down. Contacts become confused. | Can be delegated to contact coordinator; researcher can focus on writing only. |
| Distribution & publishing | MEDIUM | Publishing delays occur (1–2 weeks); distribution format becomes less polished. | Can be delegated to publishing specialist; does not block research. |
| Knowledge commons maintenance | LOW | Memory documentation lags; user awareness declines. | Can be deferred to Phase 4; does not block research or distribution. |

**Conclusion**: Domain research & writing is the irreplaceable function. Coalition coordination and distribution can be delegated to non-researcher staff if research timeline is protected.

## Section 3: Knowledge Transfer Procedures

### If Primary Researcher Becomes Unavailable (Before October 1, 2026)

**Timeline**: 5–7 day emergency onboarding (if Tier 1 backup is identified)

**Day 1–2: Framework Transfer (8–10 hours)**
- Read: PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md (structure, scoring, domain brief overview)
- Read: PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md (sequencing, capacity assumptions, critical path)
- Read: PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md (coalition chains, contact multiplier logic)
- Read: PLANNING.md and TASK.md (project context, long-term vision)
- **Deliverable**: Tier 1 backup researcher can articulate Phase 3's purpose, domain sequence, and coalition strategy

**Day 3: Domain Architecture Transfer (6–8 hours)**
- Read: Sample Phase 1–2 domain (e.g., Domain 51, Domain 56, Domain 59) — understand citation style, evidence standard, synthesis structure
- Read: PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md detailed scoring rationale for any domain backup will author
- **Deliverable**: Backup researcher understands what Phase 3 "production-ready" means

**Day 4: Specific Domain Deep-Dive (8–10 hours)**
- If Domain K assigned: Read Fix the Court's recent SCOTUS Watch report; read presidential commission SCOTUS report (2021); review TERM Act legislative text
- If Domain 37a assigned: Read existing Domain 37a Phase 2 production document; review Protect Democracy post-election certification playbook
- If Domain 49 assigned: Read existing Domain 49 Phase 2 production document; review Callais decision and intentionality standard analysis
- **Deliverable**: Backup researcher has domain-specific context and knows where primary source libraries are located

**Day 5: Coalition Contact Transfer (4–6 hours)**
- Review: Contact list in PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3 organization list)
- Phone call with primary researcher (if available): 30-minute briefing on each Tier 1 contact relationship — how they prefer communication, what they've previously engaged with, what current priorities are
- Email introduction from primary researcher to each Tier 1 contact (warm introduction; positions backup as collaborator/partner)
- **Deliverable**: Backup researcher has contact relationship context and initial warm introduction to key organizations

**Day 6–7: Writing Sample & Methodology (6–8 hours)**
- Backup researcher authors 1,500–2,000 word sample in target domain (Legislative Proposal section of Domain K, or Certification Threat Assessment section of Domain 37a)
- Primary researcher provides feedback (30–60 minute conversation)
- Backup researcher revises based on feedback
- **Deliverable**: Backup researcher demonstrates writing quality at Phase 3 standard

**Total knowledge transfer time**: 40–50 hours (distributed over 5–7 days with primary researcher support)

### If Primary Researcher Becomes Unavailable (October 1–November 3, 2026)

**Timeline**: 3–4 day emergency onboarding + parallel research start

**Accelerated onboarding** (20–25 hours over 3–4 days):
- Days 1–2: Skip broad framework reading; go directly to domain-specific materials
- Days 3–4: Writing sample in assigned domain + contact introduction emails
- **Parallel start**: Backup researcher begins research in assigned domain while primary researcher completes pre-election domain setup (Sections 1–3 of Domain K, non-election-dependent research)

**Coverage model**:
- If domain-specific Tier 1 backup (e.g., Fix the Court analyst for Domain K): 20 hours knowledge transfer + 10 hours writing sample review = 30 hours total pre-research start
- Backup researcher can begin writing by Day 5–6 with ~70% of knowledge transfer complete (final 30% happens during concurrent research)

**Feasibility**: Tight but manageable if Tier 1 backup is identified by August 15, 2026 and relationship is pre-established

### If Primary Researcher Becomes Unavailable (November 4–December 15, 2026)

**Timeline**: 2–3 day crash onboarding + immediate research start

**Crash onboarding** (15–20 hours):
- Hybrid model: Backup researcher has already completed framework reading (August preparation)
- Focus: Domain-specific research start + contact relationship activation
- Day 1: 4-hour phone call with primary researcher (domain strategy, primary sources, writing examples)
- Day 2–3: Backup researcher begins research with spot-check calls to primary researcher as needed (daily 30-minute check-ins)

**Coverage model**: 
- Backup researcher produces 60–80% of remaining domain research hours
- Primary researcher provides 20–40% of remaining hours (if available but partially constrained)
- Total phase 3 output: 90–95% of planned capacity (5–10% loss absorbed by backup's ramp-up time)

**Feasibility**: Only viable if backup researcher is pre-identified by August 2026 and relationship is pre-established. Cold engagement of new backup researcher in November is not feasible.

## Section 4: Solo Researcher Playbook (If Backup Unavailable, Scope Compression Strategy)

If no Tier 1 backup researcher can be identified or engaged, Phase 3 executes as solo research with compressed scope and timeline:

### Solo Researcher Capacity Assumption

Current Phase 3 timeline:
- November: 40–42 hours capacity
- December: 40–42 hours capacity
- January-March: 30–35 hours/month

**Solo researcher (no backup)** without scope compression would require maintaining this capacity alone. If researcher experiences 20–30% capacity reduction (illness, burnout, competing priority), Phase 3 becomes infeasible.

**Solution**: Compress Phase 3 scope to match reduced capacity.

### Compressed Scope for Solo Researcher

**Scenario A: 20% Capacity Loss (Primary Researcher at 80% Availability)**

- Full Phase 3 as planned: 160–200 research hours
- Reduced capacity: 128–160 research hours available
- **Domain execution priority**:
  1. Domain K (immovable; 16–20 hours) — EXECUTE
  2. Domain 37a (immovable; 25–30 hours) — EXECUTE
  3. Domain 49 (deferred; 24–30 hours) — DEFER to Phase 4
  4. Domain H (January 3 deadline; 18–22 hours) — COMPRESS to 12–15 hours (lightweight companion brief)
  5. Domain 51/56 updates (optional; 20–25 hours) — DEFER to Phase 4
  6. Domains I, L (optional; 25–35 hours) — DEFER to Phase 4
- **Total solo researcher Phase 3 hours**: 65–75 hours (vs. 160–200)
- **Timeline**: November-December compressed (Domains 37a + K + H brief)
- **Phase 3 emerges as**: Post-election voting rights protection + judicial reform + lightweight constitutional architecture (focused on legislative pathways, not comprehensive amendment strategy)

**Scenario B: 30–40% Capacity Loss (Primary Researcher at 60–70% Availability)**

- Available capacity: 96–120 research hours
- **Domain execution priority** (critical-path only):
  1. Domain K (16–20 hours) — EXECUTE (full depth)
  2. Domain 37a (25–30 hours) — EXECUTE (full depth)
  3. All others — DEFER to Phase 4
- **Total solo researcher Phase 3 hours**: 41–50 hours
- **Timeline**: November-December (focused execution on two critical-path domains only)
- **Phase 3 emerges as**: Post-election voting rights + judicial reform (no constitutional architecture). Loses Domain H entirely.
- **Mitigation**: Fix the Court and Demand Justice can author Domain K companion material (constitutional feasibility section) to compensate for missing Domain H — researcher coordinates but does not author
- **Contact distribution**: Fix the Court and Demand Justice become co-publishers rather than external distribution contacts (amplifies impact through their networks despite narrower Phase 3 research package)

**Scenario C: >40% Capacity Loss (Primary Researcher at <60% Availability)**

- Available capacity: <96 hours
- **Decision**: Phase 3 cannot execute at adequate quality with <60 hours research time
- **Recommendation**: Defer Phase 3 to Phase 4 (April 2027 onward) rather than deliver compromised Phase 3 product
- **Alternative**: Maintain Domain K research only (16–20 hours); all other Phase 3 work defers
- **Justification**: Domain K has dedicated Congressional seating deadline (January 3, 2027), but this deadline is aspirational not absolute. If research cannot execute at Phase 3 quality standard, deferring to April 2027 with reduced competing priorities produces higher-quality work
