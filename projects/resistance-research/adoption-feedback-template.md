# Adoption Feedback Templates

**May 6, 2026**

Four templates for operationalizing the feedback loop documented in `post-phase-1-institutional-adaptation-roadmap.md`:

1. Email survey template (async, send to substantive Phase 1 responders at Week 3–4)
2. Institutional interview script (20-minute structured call for highest-leverage contacts)
3. Feedback triage worksheet (classify and route incoming signals)
4. Monthly synthesis template (aggregate across sectors, drive Phase 2 decisions)

---

## Template 1: Email Survey (Async, 4 Questions)

**When to send**: Week 3–4 after a contact's substantive reply to Phase 1 outreach. Do not send to contacts who have not engaged substantively. Do not send as a mass email.

**Subject line options**:
- "Quick check-in on the framework — 3 questions"
- "Following up — how is the framework landing in your work?"
- "A few questions on the Democratic Renewal Framework"

**Body**:

---

Hi [Name],

Thank you again for engaging with the Democratic Renewal Framework — your response to our initial outreach was genuinely useful.

I have three short questions that would help us refine the framework for Phase 2 and ensure the analysis is actually useful for the work you are doing. This should take about five minutes.

**1. What have you used or shared from the framework?**

[Name the domain(s) or section(s) you have used, referenced, or forwarded to colleagues. Even "I read Domain X carefully and used it as background for my thinking on Y" is useful information.]

**2. What did you modify, adapt, or find yourself wishing was different?**

[No adaptation or modification means the framework fit your context directly — that is worth noting. If you added state-specific precedents, simplified the analysis for a different audience, or found a section analytically incomplete, please describe what you changed and why.]

**3. What is most missing for your specific context?**

[The framework covers 35 domains. Phase 2 will add 5–10 new domains or deepen existing ones. What analysis would be most immediately useful for the work you are doing — whether that means a new domain, a deeper cut into an existing domain, or a sector-specific application brief?]

**4. Has the framework reached anyone beyond your office or organization through you?**

[Secondary distribution is the most powerful diffusion mechanism. If you forwarded the framework to a colleague, shared it in a meeting, or included it in a publication or memo, please describe where it went — even informally. This helps us understand how the analysis is spreading.]

You can reply to this email directly. A few sentences per question is sufficient.

Thank you for the work you are doing.

[Signature]

---

**Send protocol**:
- Personalize the first sentence to reference the specific domain or point they raised in their original reply
- For contacts in active litigation contexts (AG offices, Democracy Docket, civil rights litigators): add "We understand you are in an active litigation context — please only share what is publicly available or what you would share with any outside researcher."
- Do not follow up more than once if no response

## Template 2: Institutional Interview Script (20-Minute Structured Call)

**When to use**: For the 5–10 highest-leverage contacts who offered a call, or whom you initiate a call with at Month 3 and Month 6 check-ins. Highest-leverage contacts are those producing publicly verifiable outputs: court filings, published reports, legislation, curriculum materials.

**Before the call**:
- Review the contact's recent public outputs (filings, publications, press releases) for evidence of framework language or domain analysis
- Identify which specific domain sections are most relevant to their current work
- Prepare one or two domain-specific follow-up questions based on their context

**Opening (2 minutes)**:

"Thanks for making time. I have a few structured questions — I am trying to understand how the framework is actually being used in practice, so Phase 2 can build on what is working and fill what is missing. Nothing here is on the record; I am gathering operational feedback, not research data."

---

**Section A: Current Use (6 minutes)**

1. "Which parts of the framework have you found most directly useful for your current work?"

   — Note: listen for domain-specific references, not general praise. "The voting rights analysis" is less informative than "Domain 1's SAVE Act error rate section." Probe if general: "Can you point to a specific section or argument?"

2. "Have you used any of the framework's analysis in a document, filing, brief, or publication — or shared it internally in a meeting or memo?"

   — This is the adoption threshold question. A yes here is a Level 2 adoption event (reference). Follow up: "Which domain sections, and in what context?"

3. "Has the framework reached anyone beyond your organization through you — colleagues, peer institutions, other networks?"

   — Secondary distribution probe. If yes, ask: "Where did it go and in what form — did you forward the original, create a derivative document, or reference it verbally?"

---

**Section B: Modifications and Gaps (8 minutes)**

4. "What did you find yourself modifying or adapting for your specific context?"

   — Note: adaptations are expected and positive. Listen for structural modifications (they changed the analytical structure) vs. contextual modifications (they added state-specific facts). The former is more informative for framework revision.

5. "Were there sections you found analytically incomplete, factually outdated, or formatted in a way that created friction for your institutional context?"

   — This is the content gap question. Be specific in follow-up: "What would make it more useful — more evidence, different framing, sector-specific application notes, statutory language?"

6. "What is most missing from the framework's current scope for your work — either a new topic area, a deeper cut into an existing domain, or a different kind of analytical product (model legislation, litigation brief template, training packet)?"

   — This is the Phase 2 demand signal question. Listen for: domain-specific requests (Phase 2 research candidate), format requests (brief, template, training tool — different production problem than new domain), or scope requests (deeper coverage of an existing domain).

---

**Section C: Phase 2 and Continued Engagement (4 minutes)**

7. "What Phase 2 development would make you most likely to continue engaging with the framework — and to share it with your network?"

   — This is the continued engagement question. Listen for: specific domain or format requests, or structural suggestions (a standing update mechanism, a sector-specific newsletter, a practitioners' working group).

8. "Is there a colleague, organization, or network you think should receive the framework but has not?"

   — Secondary distribution probe. Even if they cannot introduce you, knowing where they think the framework should go informs Phase 2 outreach prioritization.

**Closing**: "This is very useful. I will incorporate this into Phase 2 planning. Is it okay to follow up with you when Phase 2 materials are ready — particularly if we develop analysis you told us you needed?"

---

**Post-call documentation**: Within 24 hours, complete the Triage Worksheet (Template 3) based on call notes. Log: (A) Adoption level reached (1–4), (B) domain sections cited, (C) modifications made, (D) gaps identified, (E) Phase 2 demand signals, (F) secondary distribution confirmed destinations.
