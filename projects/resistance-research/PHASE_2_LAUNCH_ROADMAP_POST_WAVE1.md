### Per-Constituency Engagement Definitions

What "engagement" means is not the same across constituencies. Use these definitions to assess sector-specific performance independently of the overall rate.

**Law Schools (Goodman at Just Security/NYU Law, Chenoweth at Harvard Kennedy School)**

Engagement for law school contacts means:
- Score 3+: A reply that references a specific domain by number, asks a methodological question, or offers a substantive framing critique. "This is interesting, I'll pass it to [clinic director]" = Score 4 (referral).
- Score 1: "Thank you, I'll take a look." No follow-up content.
- Score 0: No reply within 21 days, or a declination.

Law schools respond to: methodological rigor, citability, clinic applicability (does this give a constitutional law or election law clinic something to work with?), and potential for co-publication or testimony use. A reply from Goodman asking whether a domain could be formatted as a Just Security submission is the highest-value signal from this sector. Chenoweth's frame is comparative/theoretical — a response citing her civil resistance methodology research is strong.

**Immigration Legal Aid (Elias at Democracy Docket/Elias Law Group)**

Engagement for immigration legal contacts means:
- Score 5: References a specific pending case, asks to adapt a domain analysis for a brief, or asks for a version focused on the Callais cascade or NVRA/Medicaid voter registration nexus.
- Score 4: Asks how to use the framework operationally in litigation or advocacy.
- Score 1: "Got it" or similar acknowledgment with no substantive content.
- Score 0: No reply.

Immigration legal aid responds to: litigation utility. Elias Law Group and Democracy Docket are active docket organizations — they need analysis they can use in briefs, public filings, or coalition communications, not general academic framing. The test is whether the contact asks anything that signals they are thinking about specific application. Any reply from Elias mentioning a case, a docket number, or a specific legal theory is a score 4–5.

**Unions (AFL-CIO, SEIU, CWA — not Batch 1, but Tier 1 Wave 1 targets for Week 2+)**

Engagement for union contacts means:
- Score 4–5: Asks about integration into member education materials, internal policy briefings, or legislative testimony. Refers the research to a specific department (legislative, education, research).
- Score 3: Asks a specific policy question about Domain 17 (labor organizing), Domain 59 (economic precarity), or Domain 31 (OBBBA Medicaid/worker coverage).
- Score 1: Acknowledges receipt, no further signal.

Union contacts respond to: worker-facing policy utility. The strongest engagement signal from a union is a request to adapt research for internal education or for legislative testimony support. Unions work on shorter timelines than law schools — if they respond, they often respond within 72 hours, and silence at 10 days is meaningful non-response.

**Think Tanks (Brookings, Roosevelt Institute, EPI — Tier 1 Wave 1 targets)**

Engagement for think tank contacts means:
- Score 5: Researcher mentions integrating findings into a current report, policy brief, or congressional testimony.
- Score 4: Asks about collaboration or requests domain-specific extract for internal circulation.
- Score 3: Substantive question or framing critique — signals the researcher read seriously.
- Score 1: "Interesting work, I'll share with colleagues" without named colleagues.

Think tanks respond to: novelty, policy leverage, and timing alignment with their own publication pipelines. A Brookings or Roosevelt researcher who asks a substantive question about Domain 56 (Civil Service) or Domain 59 (Economic Precarity) is signaling that the domain fits their current research focus. That is the highest-leverage signal — it means the analysis will be cited in work that reaches congressional staff.

## SECTION 2: Phase 2 Domain Prioritization Per Outcome

### Scenario A: STRONG Outcome (>40% engagement)

**Lead insight**: Batch 1 constituencies are substantively engaged. The credibility anchors have validated the framework. Phase 2 can launch at full intensity immediately. The primary question is sequencing — which domains reach which audiences at maximum policy-window alignment.

**Domain 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal) and Domain 59 (Economic Precarity) — parallel tracks, June 15 start, dual completion by August 10.**

With strong Phase 1 validation, the full Phase 2 research budget (90–110 hours) activates on schedule. Domain 59 and Domain 57 run in parallel from June 15 — Domain 59 enters its research phase (Sections 1–3: income-participation gap, scarcity framework, housing instability cascade) while Domain 57 enters pre-production (confirming all 21 sources live, acquiring Ikenberry's "Liberal Leviathan," completing the constitutional asymmetry analysis in Section 2). The parallel execution is viable because the two domains have zero substantive interdependency — a researcher or orchestrator can work on one domain in the morning and the other in the afternoon without context collision.

The resource allocation for a Strong outcome: allocate approximately 55–65 hours to Domain 59 and 45–51 hours to Domain 57, for a combined 90–110 hour range. Domain 59 carries the higher research load because Section 5 (the OBBBA multiplicative compounding analysis) is the most data-intensive single synthesis task in Phase 2. The compounding argument — that simultaneous OBBBA cuts to Medicaid, SNAP, housing assistance, childcare subsidies, and student loan protections produce a multiplicative rather than additive reduction in civic participation capacity for 80 million Americans — cannot be assembled without sustained 8–12 hour synthesis blocks. Build those blocks into Week 7 (July 6–12) as protected time.

**Domains 38–40 — distribution-only under a Strong outcome, no additional research required.**

Domains 38 (AI Regulatory Capture), 39 (Healthcare Access as Democratic Infrastructure), and 40 (Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Manipulation) have production-ready outlines. Under a Strong outcome, the priority is distributing these to the constituencies that validated the framework — not completing additional production work that delays Domain 57/59. Domain 39 is the exception: the June 1 HHS interim final rule on OBBBA Medicaid work requirements creates a one-time distribution window that must be captured regardless of outcome. Target Domain 39 pre-distribution to healthcare advocacy and voting rights contacts by June 1 using the existing outline as the distribution document. Full production follows in Q3.

**Domain 56 (Civil Service) and Domain 58 (Tribal Sovereignty) — concurrent with Phase 1 monitoring, Weeks 1–4.**

Both are light-lift completions (8–12 hours each) that can proceed alongside Phase 1 monitoring without displacing Domain 57/59 research bandwidth. Domain 56 distributes by May 28 (H.R. 492 legislative window). Domain 58 distributes by June 15 (pre-Trump v. Barbara ruling). Neither competes with the June 15 Domain 57/59 research launch.

**Tier 2 pre-contact activates Week 5 (June 15–21)** for the Strong outcome. The strong Phase 1 validation is the most important Tier 2 trigger — it means you have positive data to lead with ("strong initial response from law schools and policy organizations"), which is the most persuasive framing for immigration legal aid, faith organizations, and labor contacts who respond to social proof.

### Scenario B: MODERATE Outcome (25–40% engagement)

**Lead insight**: The framework is landing but not igniting. At least one sector is engaged; others are watching. The sequencing question shifts: which domain reaches the most receptive audience fastest, and which Phase 1 constituency needs additional Phase 2 material to convert from acknowledgment to integration?

**Domain 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal) — primary track, June 10 start, August 10 completion.**

Domain 57 is the primary domain under a Moderate outcome for a specific strategic reason: its primary audience (international law organizations, Senate Foreign Relations Committee staff, climate advocacy networks affected by UNFCCC/IPCC withdrawal, democracy promotion organizations like NDI and IRI) is largely separate from the Phase 1 Batch 1 constituency. This means Domain 57 expands the research's reach to a new audience regardless of Phase 1 performance — it does not depend on Phase 1 momentum to find its audience. Under a Moderate outcome, this independence is the decisive advantage. Start June 10; target August 10 completion to capture the full six-week UNGA 81 pre-distribution window (August 10 → September 22).

**Domain 59 (Economic Precarity) — secondary track, July 15 target.**

Under a Moderate outcome, Domain 59 begins research no later than July 1 (not June 15 as in the Strong scenario). This one-week slip absorbs zero buffer against the August 1 distribution target for voting rights and economic justice organizations — they need Domain 59's pre-midterm analysis by August 1, and a July 15 completion allows two weeks of peer review before distribution. Do not push Domain 59 research start past July 1 regardless of Phase 1 signals. The November 3 midterm deadline does not respond to Phase 1 performance.

**Resource allocation under a Moderate outcome**: 45–51 hours to Domain 57 (primary); 55–65 hours to Domain 59 (secondary, begins July 1); total 90–110 hours across June–August. The Moderate outcome's sequencing difference from Strong is a two-week delay in Domain 59 research start, not a reduction in total scope.

**Domains 38–40**: Under a Moderate outcome, Domain 39 retains its June 1 pre-distribution priority (HHS rule deadline does not move). Domains 38 and 40 are deferred to the production queue following Domain 57 completion (after August 10), unless a specific constituency signal from the Phase 1 engagement data indicates that an AI governance or electoral security audience is strongly interested.

**Tier 2 pre-contact activates Week 6 (June 22–28)** for the Moderate outcome. One additional week of Phase 1 monitoring data clarifies which constituencies to prioritize for Tier 2 messaging, and the Domain 56 + 58 distribution responses (Weeks 2–4) provide additional engagement signal to inform Tier 2 angles.

### Scenario C: WEAK Outcome (<25% engagement)

**Lead insight**: The framework is not cutting through — or the wrong contacts received it. The critical diagnosis question is whether the problem is messaging/framing, contact list selection, or technical delivery failure. This diagnosis drives everything. Domains 38–40 become the immediate research priority because they are faster to produce and have the most time-sensitive external deadlines; Domains 57/59 are deferred to August–September.

**Domains 38–40 — immediate priority, June 1 to July 15.**

The Weak outcome accelerates Domains 38–40 because they solve the specific problem a weak Phase 1 response creates: a need to re-engage constituencies through different entry points with more policy-proximate research. Domain 39 (Healthcare as Democratic Infrastructure) has the June 1 HHS deadline and the clearest immediate policy hook. Domain 38 (AI Regulatory Capture) has the August 2 EU AI Act Article 50 deadline — a media-visible enforcement event that creates its own attention. Domain 40 (Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Manipulation) has the hardest November 3 election constraint and should reach election protection organizations by July 15 at the latest.

Under a Weak outcome, produce Domains 39, 38, and 40 in that order, in the period June 1–July 15. Total production estimate: 10–14 hours for Domain 39 (most research-ready), 10–14 hours for Domain 38, 10–14 hours for Domain 40. Total 30–42 hours. This is achievable in six weeks with concurrent Phase 1 remediation activity.

**The Week 5–6 pivot decision**: At Week 5 (June 15), assess whether the Phase 1 remediation has recovered to Moderate or Strong levels. If yes, shift back to the Moderate scenario sequencing — Domain 57 begins, Domain 59 enters the production queue. If the Phase 1 situation is still Weak at Week 6 (June 22), complete Domains 38–40 as fast-turn research items and defer Domains 57/59 to August, accepting reduced UNGA 81 lead time for Domain 57 (3 weeks instead of 6) and reduced peer review time for Domain 59.

**Domains 57 and 59 — deferred to August under a sustained Weak outcome.**

If Phase 1 does not recover to Moderate levels by Week 6, Domain 57 research begins August 1. This produces a September 1 completion — three weeks before UNGA 81 High-Level Week rather than the planned six weeks. Domain 57's September 22 UNGA window is the hard constraint; distributing with three weeks of lead time instead of six weeks is suboptimal but not fatal for the Senate Foreign Relations Committee audience, which has ongoing staff capacity. Domain 59 research begins July 15, targeting an August 31 completion with no peer review buffer. The August 31 Domain 59 completion still reaches voting rights and economic justice organizations before mid-September, which is sufficient for organizations with October advocacy deadlines.

**Tier 2 pre-contact holds until Week 6+ under a Weak outcome.** Do not activate Tier 2 outreach while Phase 1 remediation is underway. Tier 2 outreach without Phase 1 validation data is cold outreach — it loses the social proof advantage. Activate Tier 2 only after the Phase 1 engagement signal has recovered enough to provide a credible "strong initial engagement from [sector]" framing.

### Scenario D: SPLIT Outcome (Strong in one or two constituencies, Weak in others)

**Lead insight**: Sector-specific engagement divergence is the most information-rich outcome. It tells you which framing is working and which is not before Tier 2 launches.

A Split outcome is defined as: one or two constituencies at Strong levels (>40% engagement within the sector) while one or more constituencies are at Weak levels (<25% within the sector). This is the most likely real-world result given the four-constituency mix and the different response cycle timing.

**How to read a Split outcome**: If law schools are Strong and think tanks are Weak, the academic framing is working but the policy-shop framing needs revision before Tier 2 law firm or congressional staff outreach. If immigration legal aid is Strong but unions are Weak, the litigation-utility angle is landing but the worker-power angle needs adjustment. Use the engagement signals from the strong sector to revise the messaging for the weak sector before Tier 2.

**Domain sequencing for a Split outcome**: Follow the STRONG scenario for domains that serve the performing constituencies, and the MODERATE or WEAK scenario for domains that serve the underperforming ones. Example: If law schools are Strong and unions are Weak, launch Domain 57 (constitutional law angle — strong law school resonance) on the Strong timeline (June 15) and delay Domain 59 (economic precarity/worker power angle — union resonance) to the Moderate timeline (July 1 start).
