## MESSAGING FRAMEWORKS

### Tier 1 Framework: Implementation Feasibility

Tier 1 contacts are evaluating whether this proposal is actionable. They have policy expertise and will quickly dismiss anything that is not grounded, specific, and cognizant of political constraints. They are not inspired by vision — they are convinced by evidence and mechanism.

**What they care about:**
- Which reforms can be enacted at state level without federal legislation
- What has been tried elsewhere and what the outcomes were
- Whether the constitutional analysis holds up to peer scrutiny
- Who else has endorsed or engaged the framework
- Whether the proposal acknowledges tradeoffs and political constraints

**Messaging frame for Tier 1:**
> "This is a 35-domain diagnostic framework grounded in 160+ movement case studies and comparative constitutional law from 40+ functioning democracies. It is designed to be modular — individual domains can be advanced independently without requiring the full package. The judicial independence domain includes a specific three-track response to the CASA universal injunction ruling. The war powers domain includes four Venezuela-specific proposals. Each domain includes what the reform would require legislatively, what can be done through executive action, and what litigation pathways exist."

**Subject line variants for Tier 1 outreach:**
- For legislators: "Domain [X] research — [specific current bill/hearing] — free for organizational use"
- For think tanks: "Comparative constitutional analysis framework — [specific domain] — Creative Commons"
- For law schools: "35-domain structural reform research — clinic and seminar applications"

---

### Tier 2 Framework: Methodology and Research Quality

Tier 2 contacts (academics, journalists, legal commentators) are evaluating whether this is credible, well-sourced research. They will cite it only if they can defend it to their own editors, peers, and critics.

**What they care about:**
- Quality and verifiability of sources
- Whether comparative evidence is correctly interpreted
- Whether the proposal acknowledges limits and counterarguments
- Whether it is original work or a synthesis of existing policy advocacy
- Intellectual honesty about what is uncertain vs. established

**Messaging frame for Tier 2:**
> "The proposal documents the evidentiary record on 35 domains using primary sources (court decisions, legislative text, comparative constitutional law studies). It is not partisan advocacy — it is structured as a diagnostic and prescription framework, citing both success cases and failure cases. The Chenoweth 3.5% threshold, the Varieties of Democracy index, V-Dem data, and academic democratic backsliding literature are used throughout. The research notes are separately documented and available."

**Subject line variants for Tier 2 outreach:**
- For journalists: "Research framework on democratic structural reform — primary sources and case studies available"
- For academics: "35-domain democratic reform framework — peer review and citation welcome"
- For law reviews: "Symposium/online forum proposal — comparative constitutional analysis of U.S. democratic institutions"

---

### Tier 3 Framework: Political Impact and Coalition Value

Tier 3 contacts (unions, advocacy coalitions, state networks) are evaluating whether the proposal helps their members, advances their legislative agenda, or gives them a credible framework to cite in public campaigns.

**What they care about:**
- Does this make their members' lives better in specific, concrete ways?
- Can they excerpt it and adapt it without having to read 200 pages?
- Does it validate what they are already fighting for?
- Can they use it in coalition with other organizations?
- Does it give them ammunition for specific current fights?

**Messaging frame for Tier 3:**
> "Domain 17 documents what collective bargaining reform looks like in Germany, Denmark, and Canada — and what the fiscal ROI of those systems is. Domain 11 shows what universal healthcare coverage costs vs. what the U.S. currently spends ($12,318 per capita vs. $3,000–$6,000 in peer countries). Domain 14 documents that Norway's 20% recidivism rate vs. the U.S. 76% rate represents $120+ billion in annual savings. These are not aspirational numbers — they are documented outcomes from countries that adopted these reforms."

**Subject line variants for Tier 3 outreach:**
- For unions: "Labor reform research — what Germany/Denmark/Canada achieved and how — free to use and adapt"
- For advocacy coalitions: "35-domain reform framework — yours to excerpt, adapt, distribute"
- For state networks: "State-level democracy reform case studies — what has worked, what can be done without Congress"

## NETWORK AMPLIFICATION: Bridge Nodes and Cascade Pathways

Bridge nodes are contacts whose network position causes information to flow across structural gaps between communities that would not otherwise exchange research. A well-placed bridge node can cascade the proposal across three or four otherwise-disconnected networks.

---

### Bridge Node 1: ACS Network → Legal Academia → Hill Staff

**Path**: American Constitution Society (Phil Brest, president) → 200+ law school chapters → law school faculty → law review publications → congressional staff reading

### Saturation Prevention

**Do not:**
- Contact all Tier 1 contacts simultaneously (creates impression of mass outreach; proposal will be dismissed as advocacy spam)
- Contact media contacts before institutional citations exist
- Send the full 200-page document as an initial outreach (use domain-specific one-pagers)
- Post publicly on social media before Phase 2 institutional activation (premature public release undermines the "peer-reviewed" credibility signal)

**Do:**
- Send domain-specific materials that match each contact's known work
- Ask for feedback, not endorsement
- Keep a contact log — who received what, when, what the response was
- Follow up once, with new information or a relevant current event, not with a repeat of the initial ask
- Allow each tier's contacts to discover the proposal through the tier above them when possible (organic discovery is more credible than cold outreach)
