# Phase 3 Political Crisis Routing Framework

*Production-ready decision framework for navigating major political events that occur during Phase 3 execution (November 2026 – March 2027). All routing decisions are designed to execute in under 30 minutes. This document translates unplanned crises into Phase 3 opportunities or defensive responses, with specific domain acceleration triggers, coalition re-coordination steps, and publication timing adjustments.*

*This document is a decision flowchart: start at Section 1 (Is this a Phase 3 blocker or opportunity?), follow your path, and execute the corresponding action checklist.*

---

## Most Important Finding

**Major political events between November 2026 and January 2027 generally accelerate Phase 3's impact rather than block it.** The Phase 3 domains (Constitutional Architecture, Federal Judiciary Restructuring, Post-Election Certification Protection, Redistricting Litigation, Civil Service Restoration) are the analytical framework for understanding governance failures that crises expose. A political crisis is Phase 3's proof of concept.

The critical distinction is between:
- **Phase 3 Opportunities**: Crises that validate Phase 3 analysis and create immediate distribution opportunity (e.g., SCOTUS power grab, mass Schedule Policy/Career implementation, election certification chaos)
- **Phase 3 Blockers**: Crises that consume Phase 3 resources and divert researcher attention away from planned domains (e.g., foreign military conflict, humanitarian crisis that demands immediate research response)
- **Phase 3 Accelerators**: Crises that compel completion of Phase 3 domains earlier than planned (e.g., major SCOTUS ruling affecting Domain K, post-election Section 3 enforcement threats)

## Section 1: Crisis Routing Decision Tree (30-Second Assessment)

Use this decision tree to route any major political event to the appropriate Phase 3 response within 30 seconds:

```
POLITICAL CRISIS OCCURS
        ↓
Is the crisis directly related to a Phase 3 domain 
(judicial power, executive capture, post-election threats, 
civil service, constitutional vulnerabilities)?
        ├─ YES → Go to Section 2 (Phase 3 Opportunity Routing)
        └─ NO → Go to Section 3 (Phase 3 Blocker Assessment)

PHASE 3 OPPORTUNITY ROUTING (Section 2)
Does the crisis demand immediate (within 1-2 weeks) 
research response?
        ├─ YES → Go to Section 2a (Research Acceleration)
        └─ NO → Go to Section 2b (Distribution Timing Opportunity)

PHASE 3 BLOCKER ASSESSMENT (Section 3)
Does the crisis require >20 hours of research 
diversion in the next 4 weeks?
        ├─ YES → Go to Section 3a (Scope Compression Decision)
        └─ NO → Go to Section 3b (Monitor and Continue)
```

## Section 2: Phase 3 Opportunity Routing

### Section 2a: Research Acceleration Trigger

**Trigger events that demand immediate Phase 3 research response (within 1–2 weeks)**:

#### Crisis Type 1: Supreme Court Ruling Affecting Judicial Independence
**Examples**: 
- *Trump v. Slaughter* (expected June 2026, pending at Phase 3 planning): SCOTUS eliminates for-cause removal protections for independent agencies
- *Trump v. [Constitutional Question]* (hypothetical 2026–2027): SCOTUS expands executive removal power or contracts congressional authority

**Why it's Phase 3 Opportunity**: Domain K (Federal Judiciary Restructuring) directly addresses independent agency removal power, MSPB jurisdiction, FEC independence, and structural reform. A major ruling on removal power transforms Domain K from analysis into real-time commentary on unfolding governance failure.

**Immediate response** (within 48 hours):
- [ ] Read the decision fully (release opinion, any concurrences/dissents)
- [ ] Alert Tier 1 contacts (Fix the Court, Demand Justice, Brennan Center, ACS) by email: "Major ruling on [topic] will impact Phase 3 Domain K research; may accelerate publication timeline"
- [ ] Assess Domain K research status: Is Section 4 (political feasibility per chamber composition) already drafted? If yes, revision is minimal. If no, assess whether ruling changes the fundamental analysis.
- [ ] If Phase 3 Domain K research is pre-positioned (in draft), publish accelerated version within 1 week incorporating the ruling

**Execution decision** (24-hour decision window):
- **If Domain K research is 70%+ complete**: Accelerate completion and publish by [next Friday]. The ruling amplifies Domain K's relevance; publish while ruling is in news cycle.
- **If Domain K research is <70% complete**: Assess whether to accelerate completion (add 8–12 research hours to compress timeline) or stick to planned timeline and incorporate ruling into existing draft as a "footnote section."
- **Recommendation**: If Phase 3 launch is November 4 and ruling occurs before October 1, accelerate completion. If ruling occurs after November 4, incorporate into normal Phase 3 timeline.

**Domain reordering decision**:
- If removal power ruling occurs pre-election (before November 3), this may promote Domain K higher in Phase 3 priority order — publish Domain K in December even if Domain H is not yet complete. The judicial independence frame becomes urgent.
- If removal power ruling occurs post-election (after November 3), stick to planned sequence (Domain K → Domain H with 10–14 day gap).

**Contact reactivation**:
- Email Fix the Court, Demand Justice, ACS within 48 hours: "Based on [ruling], we're accelerating Domain K publication to provide research foundation for legislative response."
- Offer brief (30-minute) call with Tier 1 contacts to align on publication timing and messaging
- **Goal**: Tier 1 contacts integrate Domain K into their immediate post-ruling advocacy campaigns

#### Crisis Type 2: Post-Election Section 3 Implementation Threat
**Examples**:
- November 4–8, 2026: State-level enforcement of Trump-era executive order interpretation activating Section 3 removal mechanisms against specific candidates or officials
- Immediate litigation filed challenging Section 3 enforcement or invoking 14th Amendment restoration
- Congress attempts to suspend Section 3 through legislative action

**Why it's Phase 3 Opportunity**: Domain 37a (Post-Election Section 3 Recovery) directly addresses certification defense and Section 3 enforcement. Active Section 3 threat transforms Domain 37a from analysis into urgent legislative/legal response document.

**Immediate response** (within 48 hours):
- [ ] If Section 3 threat involves specific congressional races: Contact Democracy Docket (Marc Elias) directly — alert that Phase 3 Domain 37a addresses exactly this scenario
- [ ] If litigation is being filed: Contact Protect Democracy, States United Democracy Center — offer Phase 3 research as legal brief supporting legislation
- [ ] Assess Domain 37a research status and publication readiness
- [ ] If Domain 37a is research-complete (pre-positioned): Publish emergency distribution within 3 days to Democracy Docket, Protect Democracy, election protection contacts

**Execution decision** (24-hour decision window):
- **If Domain 37a is fully research-complete before November 4**: This is the scenario PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md anticipated. Publish emergency distribution by November 7 using template from Section 3a below.
- **If Domain 37a is pre-positioned but requires post-election application layer**: Use actual Section 3 threat as the real-world case study. Complete application layer within 3 days of threat materialization.
- **If Domain 37a is not yet researched (pre-election research was deferred)**: Begin emergency research immediately (25–30 hours compressed into 3–4 days if necessary). This is the "emergency carryover" scenario described in PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md, Section 4.

**Distribution acceleration**:
- Tier 1 distribution (Democracy Docket, Protect Democracy, States United) within 72 hours of threat
- Tier 2 distribution (Common Cause, LWV, state secretaries of state) within 5 days
- Goal: Domain 37a becomes legal brief in active litigation defense

#### Crisis Type 3: Mass Schedule Policy/Career Implementation Begins
**Example**: 
- November 4–15, 2026: Federal agencies begin implementing Schedule Policy/Career (Schedule F) reclassifications at scale; MSPB or litigation challenges are filed

**Why it's Phase 3 Opportunity**: Domain 56 (Post-Election Civil Service Update) addresses Schedule F implementation and MSPB litigation. Mass implementation transforms Domain 56 from analysis into urgent political advocacy document.

**Immediate response** (within 48 hours):
- [ ] Confirm implementation status (news reporting, AFGE statements, OPM announcements)
- [ ] Alert AFGE, NTEU, Partnership for Public Service, GAP within 24 hours
- [ ] Assess Domain 56 research status
- [ ] If implementation data is available, begin Domain 56 research immediately (this becomes urgent post-election research, advancing timeline by 2–3 weeks)

**Execution decision** (24-hour decision window):
- **If implementation occurs November 4–15**: Compress Domain 56 research into 2–3 weeks (8–12 hours instead of planned December timeline)
- **If implementation occurs later (December+)**: Stick to planned timeline; incorporate emerging implementation data into December post-election update

**Contact coordination**:
- AFGE and Partnership for Public Service become immediate distribution partners
- Position Domain 56 as "implementation impact analysis" rather than predictive analysis
- AFGE legislative affairs can integrate Domain 56 into congressional testimony/briefing materials

### Section 2b: Distribution Timing Opportunity (No Research Acceleration)

**Examples**:
- Senate Judiciary Committee holds hearing on SCOTUS term limits (Domain K opportunity)
- House Oversight Committee holds hearing on civil service (Domain 56 opportunity)

**Response** (within 48 hours, no research changes needed):
- [ ] Identify which Phase 3 domain the hearing/action relates to
- [ ] Check if that domain research is complete
  - **If complete**: Accelerate publication by 1–2 weeks and coordinate distribution with the hearing/action timing
  - **If not complete**: Hold publication; do not pre-position domain in front of hearing until research is finalized
- [ ] Email Tier 1 contacts associated with that domain:
  ```
  Subject: Phase 3 Research Timing Opportunity — [Domain] Publication to Align with [Hearing/Action]

  ```
- [ ] Adjust Phase 3 timeline if contact confirms distribution opportunity

**Decision**:

#### Opportunity Type 2: Major News Development Validating Phase 3 Analysis

**Examples**:

**Response** (within 48 hours, no research changes needed):
- [ ] Identify which Phase 3 domain the news validates
- [ ] If domain research is complete, consider publishing to leverage news moment
- [ ] If domain is already in distribution, send follow-up email to contacts: "The [news development] validates the [analysis section] of Domain [X]. Additional context attached."
- [ ] This is secondary distribution through existing networks, not a new research effort

**Decision**: 
- **If news directly validates a Phase 3 domain currently in distribution**: Send one follow-up email to Tier 1 contacts
- **If news occurs before domain publication**: Incorporate news as case study into domain (add 2–3 hours research)
- **If news is tangential to Phase 3 domains**: File for reference but don't change Phase 3 timeline

## Section 3: Phase 3 Blocker Assessment

### Section 3a: Scope Compression Decision (If Crisis Requires Research Diversion)

**Trigger**: A major crisis occurs that demands immediate (within 1–2 weeks) research response that is NOT related to Phase 3 domains, consuming >20 hours of research capacity in November-December

**Examples**:
- Major humanitarian crisis overseas (refugee crisis, famine) requiring research response
- US foreign military involvement (military escalation, regional conflict) requiring strategic analysis
- Economic crisis (financial collapse, recession) requiring economic policy analysis
- Major scandal unrelated to Phase 3 domains

**Assessment** (24-hour decision window):

1. **Is this research demand mandatory?** (User decision, not agent decision)
   - Is this a higher priority than Phase 3 by organizational standards?
   - Would deferring this research until Phase 4 (April+) be acceptable?
   - **If mandatory**: Proceed to step 2
   - **If deferrable**: Do not compress Phase 3; continue normal timeline

2. **How many research hours does this crisis demand?**
   - <10 hours over 1 month: Absorb as background research; no Phase 3 compression needed
   - 10–20 hours over 2–4 weeks: Compress Phase 3 scope (Section 4 of PHASE_3_FUNDING_CONTINGENCY_PLAN.md applies)
   - >20 hours: Major scope reduction required; escalate to user decision

3. **Which Phase 3 domains are affected?**
   - If crisis is unrelated to Phase 3 domains: Can compress Phase 3 scope without affecting critical-path domains (K, 37a)
   - If crisis is related but secondary to Phase 3 (e.g., new election security threat that extends Domain 37a): Integrate crisis research into existing domain
   - **Recommendation**: If crisis research is <20 hours and unrelated to Phase 3 domains, compress lower-priority Phase 3 domains (I, L, 51-update, 56-update) and protect critical-path (K, 37a)

**Execution** (if scope compression is chosen):

Refer to **Section 4 of PHASE_3_FUNDING_CONTINGENCY_PLAN.md** (Scope Reduction Decision Tree):
- Critical-path domains (K, 37a) remain immovable
- Lower-priority domains (I, L, 51-update, 56-update) are deferred
- Domain H can be compressed to 12–15 hour "brief" version instead of 18–22 hour full domain

**Timeline adjustment**:
- [ ] Update PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md with deferred domains
- [ ] Email Tier 1 contacts: "Due to emerging priority research, Phase 3 is focusing on judicial reform and post-election protection (Domains K and 37a). Remaining domains defer to Phase 4 (April+). Publication timeline unchanged for Domain K (December 20) and 37a (November 15)."
- [ ] Reallocate researcher hours from deferred domains to crisis research demand

### Section 3b: Monitor and Continue (No Phase 3 Changes)

**Trigger**: A major crisis occurs that does NOT demand immediate Phase 3 research diversion (<20 hours over 1 month)

**Response**:
- [ ] Monitor the crisis; file relevant articles/analysis for potential Phase 4 reference
- [ ] Do NOT change Phase 3 timeline
- [ ] If the crisis develops into a Phase 3 opportunity (Section 2), re-route to opportunity response

**Example scenarios**:
- Foreign policy crisis that doesn't demand immediate research response: Monitor, file, continue Phase 3
- Scandal in an unrelated agency: Monitor, file, continue Phase 3
- State-level legislative action not directly related to redistricting/voting rights/judicial reform: Monitor, file, continue Phase 3

## Section 6: Dependency Re-Routing (Parallel Domain Execution Strategy)

If a crisis makes one Phase 3 domain urgent, the following re-routing preserves other domains:

### Scenario 1: Domain K Becomes Urgent (Judicial Independence Crisis)

**Normal sequence**: Domain K (December) → Domain H (January)
**Urgent sequence**: Domain K (November acceleration) → Domain H (December, one month earlier)

**Impact**:
- Domain K acceleration: Add 8–12 hours, compress into November (4 weeks becomes 2–3 weeks)
- Domain H moved earlier: Pushes publication from January 10 to December 10
- Domain H can run parallel with Domain K finish (Week 3–4 November: Domain K completion + Domain H research launch)
- **Total impact**: No net loss to Phase 3; both domains complete earlier; distribution window shifts forward 3–4 weeks

### Scenario 2: Domain 37a Becomes Urgent (Post-Election Crisis)

**Normal sequence**: Domain 37a (November) → Domain K (December)
**Urgent sequence**: Domain 37a (November acceleration, 1–2 weeks earlier) → Domain K (November continuation)

**Impact**:
- Domain 37a acceleration: If research-complete, publish November 7–10 (within 48–72 hours of crisis)
- Domain K research continues November-December as planned
- Domain 37a emergency distribution (Week 1–2) does not block Domain K research (Week 3–4 and beyond)
- **Total impact**: No net loss to Phase 3; Domain 37a publishes 1–2 weeks earlier; Domain K unaffected

### Scenario 3: Domain 56 Becomes Urgent (Civil Service Implementation)

**Normal sequence**: Domain K (December) + Domain 56 post-election update (December) parallel
**Urgent sequence**: Domain 56 accelerated (November) → Domain K (December) → Domain 56 follow-up (January)

**Impact**:
- Domain 56 emergency research: 8–12 hours compressed into early November (November 4–15)
- Domain K research unaffected (December timeline)
- Domain 56 follow-up brief: January publication of implementation update (adds 6–8 hours to January timeline)
- **Total impact**: Shifts 8–12 hours from December to November and adds 6–8 hours to January; no net loss, but increases January workload

## Section 7: Coalition Re-Coordination Protocol (If Crisis Affects Distribution)

```
SUBJECT: Phase 3 Domain [X] Publication Acceleration — Coordination Request

Hi [Name],

Following [crisis/development], we're accelerating publication of Phase 3 Domain [X] to provide 
research foundation for [specific response: litigation / legislation / advocacy].

Current plan: Publish [date], distribution to [organizations]. Domain [X] directly supports 
[specific effort] through [specific research section].

Three questions:
1) Would Domain [X] publication by [date] be useful for your work?
2) Are there other contacts you'd like us to coordinate with on distribution timing?
3) If Domain [X] becomes part of your advocacy campaign, should we adjust the framing or 
   add any specific sections?

Recommend 15-minute call if you have feedback on timing or framing.

Best,
[Contact]
```

**Contact response options**:
- **Yes, accelerate publication**: Confirm publication date and distribution list with contact
- **Yes, but adjust framing**: Incorporate contact feedback into domain before publication
- **No, stick to normal timeline**: Confirm normal publication date and move forward
