### 2. Domain 56 (Civil Service Politicization): Litigation Confirms No Injunction — MSPB Jurisdiction Strip Operational

**What happened**: This domain received thin coverage in prior passes. A May 20 search confirms the following as the current operational status:

**Schedule Policy/Career (Schedule F revival) — litigation posture**: Four lawsuits are pending in federal district court seeking to block Schedule Policy/Career. No court has granted a preliminary injunction blocking implementation as of May 20. The rule took effect March 8, 2026 (30 days after February 6 publication). The administration has successfully cleared the runway: no judicial check is currently in place.

**MSPB jurisdiction strip — operational**: The Merit Systems Protection Board announced in a February 23, 2026 Federal Register notice that it will not hear appeals challenging the conversion of positions into Schedule Policy/Career. The change became effective March 9 — the same day Schedule Policy/Career took effect. Employees converted to Schedule Policy/Career have no administrative appeal right at the MSPB. Their recourse is direct to federal circuit courts — a much higher threshold.

**NTEU v. OPM (FOIA case)**: On April 29, 2026, the court entered a joint stipulation: the government will produce the administrative record by June 8, 2026, and the parties will file a joint status report by June 22, 2026 on next steps. Cross-motions for summary judgment are upcoming. No injunction pending. The FOIA case is seeking records on which positions are being reclassified — the government has withheld this information.

**Second amended complaint (AFGE/AFSCME coalition)**: On March 4, 2026, the coalition expanded their complaint to target the entire framework: the underlying EO, the OPM final rule, and the reclassification effort itself. This is the most comprehensive pending challenge but remains without injunctive relief.

**MSPB immigration judges ruling**: On March 20, 2026, the two-Republican-member MSPB upheld the Trump administration's authority to fire immigration judges on Article II grounds, relinquishing jurisdiction. This ruling extends well beyond immigration judges: it establishes that the MSPB will defer to Article II arguments on civil service removal cases, making MSPB review of Schedule Policy/Career terminations doubly unavailable (both by rule and by precedent).

**H.R. 492 / S. 134 (Saving the Civil Service Act)**: Both bills remain in committee with no floor action as of May 20. H.R. 492 was referred to the House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform; S. 134 was referred to the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs. Neither has moved in 2026.

**Phase 2 integration for Domain 56**: The prior Domain 56 research was built around the threat of Schedule Policy/Career. The May 20 confirmation is that the threat has actualized — the rule is live, the MSPB appeal pathway is closed, no injunction is in place, and the legislative backstop is dead in committee. Domain 56's framing should shift from "threatened" to "partially implemented — contested in litigation with no current injunctive protection." The NTEU/AFGE litigation timeline (June 8 administrative record production, June 22 joint status report) establishes the next Phase 2 monitoring gate for this domain.

**Urgency**: HIGH for domain framing — Domain 56 distribution materials must reflect enacted/operational status, not threatened status.
