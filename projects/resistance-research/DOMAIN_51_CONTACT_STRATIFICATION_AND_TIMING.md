## Section 5: Engagement Pattern Expectations by Organization Type

### National Policy Organizations (CLC, Issue One)
**Typical response timeline**: 2–5 business days for research-relevant organizations
**Response type distribution** (estimated from comparable outreach patterns):
- 40%: No response
- 25%: Auto-acknowledge or out-of-office reply
- 25%: Brief acknowledgment from researcher or communications staff
- 10%: Substantive engagement (request for follow-up, citation of research in their own materials, or request to speak with the author)

**What counts as a response**: Any reply — including auto-acknowledgments that include a named contact — is a signal. An out-of-office reply with a name and direct email is an upgrade opportunity (follow up with that named contact after June 16).

### State Campaign Organizations (Common Cause CA, LWV CA, Clean Money Action Fund)
**Typical response timeline**: 3–7 business days; often faster for highly relevant content
**Response type distribution** (estimated):
- 70%: No response (campaign mode reduces external research engagement)
- 15%: Auto-acknowledgment or form reply
- 10%: Brief acknowledgment or forward to a specific campaign contact
- 5%: Substantive engagement (active use of research in campaign materials or direct request for conversation)

**What counts as a win**: A California campaign contact forwarding the Gist URL to another campaign staffer counts as a strong signal even without a direct reply — if Gist analytics show multiple new views from California-geolocated IPs in the 48 hours after June 11, that is a meaningful engagement signal.
