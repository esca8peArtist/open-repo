## Part 1: Tier 2/Tier 3 Staggering Workflow

### Tier Definitions

**Tier 1**: Primary high-alignment institutional contacts. Organizations whose mandate directly overlaps with the domain's core argument. Send in the initial distribution window. Response rate target: 40%+ within T+7.

**Tier 2**: Secondary contacts within the same constituency networks. Lower organizational seniority or narrower mandate overlap, but high quality. Activate if Tier 1 response rate falls below 40% by T+7, OR as a follow-on amplification wave if Tier 1 response rate exceeds 40% (using Tier 1 engagement as social proof).

**Tier 3**: Academic, grassroots, and earned-media contacts. Long response cycle (10–21 days). Activate only after Tier 1/2 signal is confirmed. These contacts convert to network multipliers rather than direct adoption agents.

## Part 2: Tracking Template Specification

### Spreadsheet Structure

Use a single spreadsheet with one tab per domain (Domain 51, Domain 37, Domain 42 Phase 2b, Domain 54). Columns are identical across all four tabs.

**Required Columns**

| Column | Contents | Format | Auto-Calculation |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------------|
| A: Contact Name | First Last | Text | — |
| B: Organization | Full org name | Text | — |
| C: Email Address | Verified email | Text | — |
| D: Tier | 1, 2, or 3 | Number | — |
| E: Send Date | Date email sent | Date (YYYY-MM-DD) | — |
| F: Delivery Status | Delivered / Bounced / Unknown | Text | — |
| G: Reply Date | Date of first reply (if any) | Date (YYYY-MM-DD) | — |
| H: Reply Content Summary | 1-2 sentence summary | Text | — |
| I: Engagement Score | 1–5 (see scoring rubric) | Number | — |
| J: Days to Reply | G minus E | Auto: =IF(G2="","",G2-E2) | — |
| K: Adoption Signal | Yes / No / Pending | Text | — |
| L: Tier 3 Promotion | Yes / No / Not eligible | Text | — |
| M: Follow-up Required | Yes / No / Scheduled | Text | — |
| N: Follow-up Date | Scheduled follow-up date | Date (YYYY-MM-DD) | — |
| O: Notes | Free text | Text | — |

**Summary Row Formulas (Row 2 of a Summary tab)**

```
Total contacts:         =COUNTA(SheetName!A2:A100)
Tier 1 contacts:        =COUNTIF(SheetName!D2:D100,1)
Sent (Tier 1):          =COUNTIF(SheetName!F2:F100,"Delivered")
Replied (any):          =COUNTA(SheetName!G2:G100)
Tier 1 response rate:   =COUNTIFS(SheetName!D2:D100,1,SheetName!G2:G100,"<>")/COUNTIFS(SheetName!D2:D100,1,SheetName!F2:F100,"Delivered")
Score 3+ replies:       =COUNTIF(SheetName!I2:I100,">=3")
Score 4+ replies:       =COUNTIF(SheetName!I2:I100,">=4")
Avg days to reply:      =AVERAGEIF(SheetName!J2:J100,"<>")
Tier 2 trigger met:     =IF((Tier 1 response rate)<0.4,"YES - ACTIVATE TIER 2","No - monitor")
Tier 3 promotions:      =COUNTIF(SheetName!L2:L100,"Yes")
```

**Engagement Escalation Logic (conditional formatting)**

Apply red fill to column I if score = 1; yellow if score = 2; green if score = 3+.
Apply red fill to column J (Days to Reply) if value > 10 and column G is empty.
Apply orange fill to column F if value = "Bounced" — bounced contacts require immediate re-verification.

## Part 3: Contact Engagement Scoring (5-Point Scale)

This scale is identical to the QRP (Quality Reply Points) scale used in Phase 1 and Phase 2a contact management. Scores are additive across contacts but assigned per-contact, not per-organization.

| Score | Definition | Signal Type | Examples |
|-------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **1** | Acknowledged receipt, no substantive engagement | Weak signal | "Thank you, I'll review this." / Auto-reply / Out-of-office with no follow-up |
| **2** | Substantive acknowledgment — mentioned a specific finding or expressed interest, no commitment | Moderate passive signal | "This is relevant to our work on [issue]." / Request for a one-pager / forwarded to colleague |
| **3** | Active engagement — specific organizational action implied or stated | Strong signal | Request for call or briefing / Commitment to share with team / Specific question about implementation |
| **4** | Institutional adoption signal — domain integrated into organizational work product | Very strong signal | Referenced in organizational report or testimony / Cited in briefing materials / Shared with board or membership network |
| **5** | Transformative signal — direct amplification or formal partnership | Maximum signal | Published citation of domain / Formal collaboration offer / Co-authored policy brief / Congressional testimony referencing domain |

**Score Assignment Rules**:
- Assign score at time of reply, not retrospectively
- If no reply after T+14: default score = 0 (do not assign 1 unless at least acknowledgment of receipt received)
- Score can be upgraded (e.g., from 2 to 4 if organization later cites the domain) but never downgraded
- Academic contacts: standard 7-day response cycle applies. Score 2 from an academic contact within 7 days is equivalent to Score 3 from an advocacy organization (academic norms are slower)

### Adoption Signal Detection

An "adoption signal" (column K: Yes) is triggered by any of the following behaviors, regardless of engagement score:

1. Contact's organization uses vocabulary from the domain document in subsequent public communications (vocabulary marker test — see Domain 37 baseline metrics Section 8)
2. Contact requests additional research materials from related domains
3. Contact introduces a second colleague or organization to the framework
4. Contact cites the Gist URL in public writing, social media, or internal communications
5. Contact organization uses the domain's reform proposals in advocacy materials or testimony

Mark column K "Yes" as soon as any adoption signal is observed. This contact is eligible for Tier 3 promotion regardless of engagement score.

### Tier 3 Promotion Criteria

A contact is eligible for Tier 3 promotion (inclusion in subsequent domain distributions and deeper engagement) when any two of the following are true:

1. Engagement score is 3 or higher
2. Adoption signal detected (column K = Yes)
3. Contact has forwarded materials to at least one new contact (network multiplication)
4. Contact has requested a follow-up conversation
5. Contact's organization has a standing mandate that directly covers 2+ Phase 2b domains

Mark column L "Yes" when promotion criteria are met. Tier 3 contacts receive all future domain distributions automatically, without individual re-verification steps.

## Part 4: Feedback Triage Protocol

When a reply arrives, categorize it within 2 hours using one of five reply types. The category determines the response workflow.

### Reply Type 1: Implementation

**Definition**: Contact is asking how to use the domain research in their operational work — litigation, advocacy, congressional testimony, member education, organizational planning.

**Signals**: "How should we use this in our [specific work]?" / "Can you explain how [finding] applies to [their context]?" / "Our team is working on [related project] — could we discuss?"

**Response workflow**:
1. Within 24 hours: Acknowledge and offer a 20-minute call or written Q&A
2. Within 48 hours: Prepare domain-specific implementation brief (2–3 pages max) if needed
3. Flag in tracking spreadsheet as Score 3+ (or Score 4 if implementation intent is clear)
4. If call is agreed: schedule within 5 business days; use call to identify whether they need related domains

**Contact this triggers**: Note which domain the implementation question relates to — if they're asking about Domain 54 but also need Domain 42, this is a cross-domain distribution opportunity. Add related domain to follow-up materials.

---

### Reply Type 2: Critique

**Definition**: Contact disagrees with a finding, questions a source, or suggests the argument is incomplete or flawed.

**Signals**: "I don't think [finding] is accurate." / "You're missing [counterpoint]." / "The framing here doesn't account for [consideration]."

**Response workflow**:
1. Within 24 hours: Acknowledge the critique without defensiveness — "Thank you for this — I want to understand your concern specifically."
2. Within 72 hours: Verify the challenged finding against primary sources. If the critique is correct: update the domain Gist and acknowledge the correction to the contact.
3. If the critique is incorrect or involves a different framing preference: respond with the source citation and explain the analytical choice
4. Do NOT argue — acknowledge, verify, respond once
5. Flag in tracking: Score 2 minimum (engagement is substantive even if critical); upgrade to Score 3 if critique leads to follow-up exchange

**What not to do**: Do not revise the domain document in response to a single critique without verifying the challenger's claim. Do not share updated versions with other contacts before verifying.

---

### Reply Type 3: Request

**Definition**: Contact is requesting something specific — the full framework document, a one-pager, a presentation, a call, introduction to another contact, or permission to share.

**Signals**: "Can you send the full framework?" / "Do you have a summary version?" / "Can I share this with my board?" / "Would you be willing to speak with our team?"

**Response workflow**:
1. Within 24 hours: Fulfill the request if it is straightforward (full framework: provide GitHub Gist index; permission to share: always grant; one-pager: provide if one exists or create a 1-page abstract)
2. Sharing permission: always say yes. There is no distribution restriction on the framework. Explicitly grant permission: "Yes, please share freely — this research is CC Attribution 4.0."
3. Presentation request: if capacity allows, schedule within 2 weeks. If capacity is constrained, offer a 30-minute call instead and provide a slide deck template.
4. Flag in tracking: Score 3 minimum for any specific request. Score 4 if the request implies institutional review (board presentation, staff briefing).

---

### Reply Type 4: Adoption

**Definition**: Contact confirms they are integrating the domain research into their organization's work product — explicitly and without prompting.

**Signals**: "We're including this in our [report / testimony / campaign materials]." / "I cited your Gist in our [document]." / "We're sharing this with our [member network / board / coalition]." / Public citation found independently.

**Response workflow**:
1. Within 24 hours: Acknowledge and express gratitude. Do not ask for attribution requirements (already CC Attribution 4.0).
2. Offer related domains: "Since you found Domain 54 useful, you may also want Domain 42 — they share the disenfranchisement analysis angle."
3. Request permission to note their adoption in future outreach (as social proof for Batch 2/3 contacts): "With your permission, I may mention that [Organization] has found this research useful in briefings to other organizations."
4. Flag in tracking: Score 4 minimum. Mark column K (Adoption Signal) = Yes. Mark column L (Tier 3 Promotion) = Yes.
5. Note their specific use case in column O — this language becomes social proof framing for subsequent domain distributions.

---

### Reply Type 5: Question

**Definition**: Contact has a factual or analytical question that is not an implementation question and not a critique.

**Signals**: "What methodology did you use for [finding]?" / "Can you explain why [framing choice]?" / "Where does this source say [specific claim]?"

**Response workflow**:
1. Within 48 hours: Answer the question directly using the domain's source list and section references.
2. If the question suggests the contact has read the document carefully: upgrade to Score 3
3. If the question is about methodology: this is a research interest signal — consider whether the contact would benefit from related academic sources beyond the Gist
4. Flag in tracking: Score 2 minimum; Score 3 if question demonstrates substantive engagement with the argument

**General <20% escalation checklist** (apply to any domain hitting this threshold):
- [ ] Confirm delivery: did the send land in inbox (self-test from same account)?
- [ ] Review subject line: is it specific to the organization's mandate?
- [ ] Review opening sentence: does it reference their specific work, not generic framing?
- [ ] Verify contact email is current (not outdated, not a general webform address)
- [ ] If delivery confirmed and contact current: wait until T+14 before treating as non-engagement

### Scenario 2: Zero engagement from specific high-priority contact after T+10

**High-priority contacts** (those marked "Critical" in Tier 1 above):
- Campaign Legal Center (Brendan Fischer) — Domain 51
- The Sentencing Project (Marc Mauer) — Domain 54

If either of these critical contacts has not replied by T+10:

**Escalation path**:
1. Re-verify email address independently (check organization website, not prior records)
2. If email is confirmed correct: send a brief follow-up (one paragraph, different subject line: "Following up — [Organization] and [Domain-specific hook]")
3. Follow-up send is a one-time action. Do not send more than 2 total emails (initial + one follow-up) to any contact.
4. If follow-up produces no reply by T+14: mark column M (Follow-up Required) = No further action; escalate to Tier 2 contacts only.

### Scenario 3: A contact replies with a specific ask that is beyond single-agent capacity

Examples: Request for a 45-minute briefing, request to co-author a document, request for access to raw data or unpublished sections.

**Response protocol**:
1. Acknowledge the request within 24 hours
2. Flag in CHECKIN.md under "User Decision Required" with specific ask and contact organization
3. Do not commit to briefings, co-authorship, or data sharing without user approval
4. Default response: "I'll confirm our capacity within 2–3 days and come back to you with a specific proposal."

### Scenario 4: Fast-track for next domain (Tier 3 promotion triggered)

When a contact meets Tier 3 promotion criteria (Part 3 of this document), the following actions are automatic:

1. Add to "Tier 3 fast-track" list in tracking spreadsheet
2. Include in next domain's Tier 1 distribution (not Tier 2 — these contacts have demonstrated adoption readiness)
3. Use their organization's engagement as social proof in the next domain's opening paragraph (with permission confirmed in prior engagement)
4. No re-verification required — use same email address from prior send unless bounced

**Fast-track sequence example**: If Campaign Legal Center (Domain 51) produces a Score 4 reply and is Tier 3 promoted, it becomes an automatic Tier 1 contact for Domain 37 distribution — receiving Domain 37 on June 2 even though it was not originally in Domain 37's Tier 1 list. This is the "social proof relay" mechanism: earlier domain engagement validates the framework for later domain sends.
