### T+Days 18-21: Follow-Up Cadence (Batch 1 and 2)

**Rule**: Follow up once, 7-10 days after initial send. Use new information, not a repeat of the ask.

**Good follow-up structure**:
"I'm following up on the email I sent [date] about the 35-domain democratic reform framework. Since then, [new development — a court ruling, a legislative action, a citation by a peer in their field]. I think [specific domain] is more relevant than ever to [their specific work]. If you had a chance to look at the materials, I'd welcome any response. If not, no pressure — the documents remain available at [link], CC 4.0."

**Bad follow-up**: Repeating the original email. Asking if they "had a chance to read it." Pressuring for a response.

**After one follow-up**: Do not pursue further. The contact has the materials. They will use them if they find them useful.

## SATURATION PREVENTION RULES

1. **Do not contact all Tier 1 simultaneously** — the batching structure exists precisely to prevent this. Simultaneous mass outreach signals advocacy spam, not research.

2. **Do not contact media before institutional citations exist** — premature media outreach that yields a story before the research is institutionally credentialed undermines the "peer-reviewed" signal.

3. **Do not post publicly before Batch 1 is sent** — public social media posts before the institutional outreach wave means contacts receive the research as mass distribution, not targeted engagement.

4. **Do not repeat the ask** — one follow-up, with new information, then stop. Persistence past one follow-up communicates that the research is not finding organic traction.

5. **Track everything** — a contact log is not optional. Who received what, when, what they responded, what the outcome was. This log is the foundation for Phase 2 targeting.
