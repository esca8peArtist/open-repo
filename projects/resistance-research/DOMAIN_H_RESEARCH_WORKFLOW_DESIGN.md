## Lead Finding

**Domain H research is substantially complete as of June 2026**, which changes the resource math for this workflow in an important way: the 12-week sprint is primarily an editorial and distribution execution plan, not a from-scratch research plan. The DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md production document (7,500 words, 90+ citations, four zones fully researched) was produced in the June 6, 2026 research sprint. The actual hours required in November–January are 30–45 hours of editorial, post-election updating, and distribution work.

**For planning purposes, the from-scratch baseline** — which governs how to scope this workflow if Domain H had not been pre-produced — is: four zones at 25–30 hours each equals 100–120 research hours, plus 60–80 hours of editorial, checkpoint, and distribution work, totaling 160–200 hours across 12 weeks on the solo path. This from-scratch baseline is documented in DOMAIN_H_SOLO_VS_TEAM_RESOURCE_MODEL.md as the "baseline scenario" from which both the solo and team paths derive. The 30–45 hour actual is the "existing research" adjusted figure.

The January 3, 2027 hard deadline is the immovable anchor for both the from-scratch baseline and the actual adjusted plan. Every workflow element sequences backward from it.

## 12-Week Sprint Calendar: November 4, 2026 – January 31, 2027

### Phase A: Post-Election Editorial Production (Weeks 1–6: Nov 4 – Dec 15)

**Week 1 — November 4–11: Post-Election Triage and Content Audit**

*Primary obligation: Domain 37a post-election deployment (separate from Domain H, but creates the context for Domain H's amendment strategy section)*

Domain H work this week (2–3 hours):
- Read the existing DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md in full — identify which arguments gain or lose force based on November 3 election results
- Determine which of the five Zone 3 amendments are now more vs. less politically viable given the 120th Congress composition
- Draft a one-page "electoral outcome overlay" memo: if Democrats capture the House, Senate, or both, how does Domain H's amendment strategy sequencing change?

Checkpoint (end of week 1): Electoral outcome overlay memo complete. Domain H's four zones confirmed as production-ready from June 2026. List of specific additions required based on election results.

**Week 2 — November 11–18: Electoral Outcome Application Layer**

Domain H work this week (4–5 hours):
- Write the electoral outcome-specific framing for Zone 3 (Amendment Strategy), Section 3.1. The existing text covers the generic January 2027 window argument; add the scenario-specific analysis: which of the five priority amendments are realistically advanceable in the 120th Congress given actual chamber composition?
- Update Zone 4 (Movement Infrastructure) Section 4.1 to include Democracy 2025's post-election litigation performance: how many of the 405 pre-election victories held under post-election pressure? Did any Phase 2 carryover cases produce SCOTUS outcomes that alter Domain H's constitutional vulnerability analysis?
- Verify sources in Zone 2 (International Precedent) that have time-sensitive components — specifically, the Venice Commission's December 2025 Checklist update (CDL-AD(2025)002) and any post-June 2026 German Federal Constitutional Court developments.

Checkpoint (end of week 2): Electoral outcome application layer complete. Zone 3 Section 3.1 revised. Zone 4 Section 4.1 updated. Source verification complete.

**Week 3 — November 18–25: Domain K Integration Review**

Domain K distributes December 12–20. Domain H trails by 10–14 days (January 10 distribution target). This week's Domain H task is reviewing the final Domain K production document to ensure the two documents form a coherent sequential argument rather than a disjointed pair.

Domain H work this week (3–4 hours):
- Review Domain K's Zone 1 (Legislative Inventory) against Domain H's Zone 3 (Amendment Strategy). Confirm that Domain H's Zone 3 explicitly identifies which of Domain K's legislative proposals are constitutionally achievable by statute and which require constitutional amendment. The Zone 3 statutory reclamation section in DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md already covers this, but must reference Domain K's specific bills (H.R. 1074, SCERT Act, SHADOW Act) by name so readers arriving from Domain K find immediate continuity.
- Review Domain K's Zone 3 (International Precedent) against Domain H's Zone 2. Identify any comparative material that appears in both documents and ensure the accounts are consistent and non-duplicative. The German FCC analysis appears in both zones; Domain K's version is legislatively focused (term structure, appointment process), Domain H's is constitutionally focused (Basic Law protection, eternity clauses, December 2024 amendment). This distinction must be explicit in both documents.
- Draft the two-paragraph bridge text for each document: at the end of Domain K, a paragraph pointing toward Domain H as the constitutional architecture layer; at the start of Domain H, a paragraph confirming its relationship to Domain K.

Checkpoint (end of week 3): Cross-document integration review complete. Bridge text drafted for both documents. No content contradictions identified.

**Week 4 — November 25 – December 2: Gist Creation**

Domain H work this week (3–4 hours):
- Create the Domain H GitHub Gist. Formatting follows the established project standard (Markdown, organized by zone, executive summary at top, sources at bottom).
- The Gist header must specify the hard distribution deadline (January 3, 2027 Congress seating) and the relationship to Domain K.
- Specific Gist formatting decisions: Zone 2 (International Precedent) should be formatted as a comparative table (country, judicial tenure structure, appointment process, relevant US reform analog) for maximum scannability. The Combined Brief version (Domain K + Domain H summary, 6–8 pages) is a separate formatting task — see Week 5.

Checkpoint (end of week 4): Domain H Gist draft complete. Gist URL created. Visual formatting reviewed.

**Week 5 — December 2–9: Combined Brief Production**

The highest-leverage distribution format for the January 2027 congressional window is the "Constitutional Reform Briefing Package" (PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md, Brief 2): a 6–8 page synthesis of Domain K (judiciary restructuring) + Domain H (constitutional architecture) formatted for Capitol Hill staff.

Domain H work this week (4–5 hours):
- Produce the Combined Brief. Format: executive summary (1 paragraph), Domain K synthesis (2 pages — specific bills, constitutional feasibility, ethics-first sequencing), Domain H synthesis (2 pages — top three amendment priorities, amendment window analysis, international comparators), contact/distribution information (1 page). Total: 6 pages.
- The Combined Brief's Domain H section must foreground the five priority amendments in Zone 3 and the January 2027 window argument in Section 3.1. Congressional staff need a single paragraph per amendment, not the full academic analysis.
- Proofread both the Combined Brief and the main Domain H production document. Verify that all Congressional bill numbers are current for the 120th Congress (H.R. 1074 and its Senate companion will require re-introduction in January 2027 — note this in the text).

Checkpoint (end of week 5): Combined Brief complete and ready for distribution. Domain H production document final edit complete.

**Week 6 — December 9–15: Distribution List Finalization and Pre-Send Verification**

Domain H work this week (2–3 hours):
- Finalize the Tier 1 distribution list for Domain H. Using the contact list from PHASE_3_DOMAINS_H_K_37A_COALITION_MAPPING.md Section 1: American Constitution Society (programs staff), National Constitution Center (Jeffrey Rosen), Protect Democracy (Ian Bassin), incoming House and Senate Judiciary Committee members, constitutional law faculty (Kim Lane Scheppele, Yaniv Roznai, Erwin Chemerinsky, Heather Gerken, Jack Balkin).
- Verify email addresses against the most recent confirmation data.
- Confirm that Domain K has distributed successfully to Fix the Court, Demand Justice, and ACS by December 20 — this is the dependency that unlocks Domain H's January 10 send. If Domain K has distributed successfully, proceed to Week 7 on schedule. If Domain K distribution is delayed, extend Week 6 by one week and shift Domain H distribution from January 10 to January 14 (four-day slip, still within the committee assignment window).

Checkpoint (end of week 6): Distribution list finalized. Domain K distribution status confirmed. Go/no-go for January 10 Domain H send.

### Phase B: Distribution and Coalition Activation (Weeks 7–12: Dec 15 – Jan 31)

**Week 7 — December 15–22: Domain K Distribution (Not Domain H work, but monitoring week)**

Domain H researcher's obligation this week: confirm Domain K has reached Fix the Court, Demand Justice, and ACS. Monitor for any substantive Domain K responses that should be incorporated into Domain H's framing before the January 10 send. If a Tier 1 Domain K contact identifies a gap or objection, assess whether Domain H can address it.

Domain H work this week (1–2 hours): Monitor and triage only.

**Week 8 — December 22–29: Holiday Hold**

No new research sessions scheduled. This is the annual holiday period during which Tier 1 contacts are largely unavailable. Use any available time to:
- Finalize the email outreach templates for Domain H's January 10 distribution (personalized subject lines for each Tier 1 recipient, noting their specific connection to the material)
- Confirm that the ACS programs staff contact has been reached by Domain K and is aware a related document is coming in January

Checkpoint (end of week 8): Outreach templates complete. Confirmation that key contacts are expecting the follow-up document.

**Week 9 — January 3–10: Congress Seating and Domain H Tier 1 Distribution**

January 3: 120th Congress seated. The advocacy window is now open.

Domain H work this week (2–3 hours):
- Send Domain H Tier 1 distribution on January 10 (or as close to January 3 as the committee assignment process will make the timing productive — if committee assignments are moving faster than expected, move the distribution to January 7).
- The Combined Brief (Domain K + Domain H) goes simultaneously to Senate Judiciary Committee staff contacts (Whitehouse and Durbin offices) and incoming House Judiciary Committee staff (Raskin's office if Democrats control the House).
- The standalone Domain H document goes to ACS, National Constitution Center (Rosen), Protect Democracy (Bassin), and constitutional law faculty contacts.
- Log every send in the send log (follow existing domain send log format).

Checkpoint (end of week 9): All Tier 1 sends confirmed. Send log complete.

**Week 10 — January 10–17: Committee Assignments Window — Response Monitoring**

January 17: Committee assignments expected to finalize. After January 17, the "blank slate" window for incoming members closes.

Domain H work this week (1–2 hours):
- Monitor for Tier 1 responses. Any substantive engagement within the first seven days signals Level 2 adoption (contact has read and engaged, not just received).
- Send Tier 2 follow-up emails to contacts who have not responded: Alliance for Justice, Constitutional Accountability Center, FairVote (Zone 3 RCV/12th Amendment connection), Common Cause Defend Our Constitution (Zone 1 Article V threat).
- Document any responses in the Coalition Activation Log.

Checkpoint (end of week 10): Response monitoring complete. Tier 2 sends complete. Committee assignments finalized — record which Judiciary Committee members have received Domain H and Domain K.

**Week 11 — January 17–24: Tier 3 Distribution and Legal Community Outreach**

Domain H work this week (2–3 hours):
- Law school clinic distribution: distribute Domain H to NYU Constitutional Litigation Clinic, Stanford Supreme Court Litigation Clinic, Yale Law Supreme Court Advocacy Clinic, Columbia Center for Constitutional Governance, Georgetown ICAP. Use the law school distribution contact list from Zone 4 Section 4.2.
- Submit Zone 2 (International Precedent) as a conference paper abstract to any constitutional law conference with a 2027 spring call for papers. The IACL-AIDC annual congress proceedings are the primary venue; Yaniv Roznai's comparative network is the secondary channel.
- Flag the WashU Law Model Constitutional Convention follow-up: was a second Model Convention convened for 2027? If so, Domain H is a natural distribution target for the faculty and student participants.

Checkpoint (end of week 11): Law school distribution complete. Conference abstract submitted if applicable. Any new expert contacts from law school outreach logged.

**Week 12 — January 24–31: Phase 3 Domain H Sprint Close**

Domain H work this week (1–2 hours):
- Compile a one-page Domain H sprint summary: sends completed, Tier 1 responses received, Level 2+ adoption documented, key engagement signals, open follow-up threads.
- Write a brief assessment of whether Domain H's Zone 3 amendment strategy arguments are being incorporated into advocacy organization communications. Any organization that cites Domain H's sequencing argument or uses its five-amendment framework in their own communications has reached Level 3 adoption — this is the target signal.
- Identify any Domain H arguments that should be revised based on post-election developments or post-distribution feedback before Domain I begins in February.

Final Checkpoint (end of week 12): Sprint summary complete. Level 2+ adoption logged. Domain H production and distribution closed.

**Week 12 Total Hours: Domain H sprint complete. Estimated total: 30–45 hours across 12 weeks.**

## Zone-by-Zone Research Structure

The four zones are production-complete from the June 2026 research sprint. The workflow's editorial hours are distributed unevenly across zones based on which zones require post-election updating versus which are durable:

### Zone 1: Constitutional Vulnerabilities (Structural Fragility Points)

Sections: 1.1 Emergency Powers/Weimar Parallel; 1.2 Judicial Capture Post-Dobbs/Loper Bright; 1.3 State Constitutional Degradation; 1.4 Amendment Process Weaponization.

Research status: Complete. All four sections are fully researched with source verification.

Post-election update requirement: Section 1.2 requires updating based on Trump v. Slaughter and Trump v. Cook outcomes (expected June–July 2026 but not confirmed as of this writing). If these removal power cases eliminated Humphrey's Executor, Section 1.2 gains significant empirical force and should add a paragraph documenting the ruling's structural effect. Section 1.4 requires updating with post-November 3 Article V convention count: if any new state resolutions passed or were rescinded in 2026 state legislative sessions, the 28/34 count needs adjustment.

Hours for Zone 1 editorial work: 2–3 hours.
Checkpoint: Zone 1 source verification and post-election update complete by end of Week 2.
Dependencies: Removal power case outcome (Trump v. Slaughter); post-election Article V convention state count.

### Zone 2: International Precedent (Resilience Mechanisms Transferable to the US Context)

Sections: 2.1 Germany (December 2024 Constitutional Amendment); 2.2 Canada (Mandatory Retirement and Notwithstanding Clause); 2.3 South Africa (30-Year Institutional Resilience); 2.4 Israel (2023–2024 Judicial Reform Crisis); 2.5 Venice Commission (Updated 2025 Checklist).

Research status: Complete. All five sections fully researched with verified sources.

Post-election update requirement: None for the core analysis. Zone 2's international precedent is not time-sensitive in the same way as domestic legal developments. However, two monitoring items:
- Has Germany's Federal Constitutional Court issued any significant rulings since December 2024 that test the new constitutional protection?
- Has the Venice Commission issued any follow-up to its December 2025 checklist specifically addressing the US?
If either produces new material, add a brief update paragraph. Estimated: 1–2 hours of monitoring.

Hours for Zone 2 editorial work: 1–2 hours.
Checkpoint: Zone 2 monitoring complete by end of Week 3.
Dependencies: None blocking. Zone 2 is the most stable section.

### Zone 3: Constitutional Amendment Strategy

Sections: 3.1 Amendment Sequencing and the January 2027 Window; 3.2 Priority Amendment Architecture (Five Targets); 3.3 Statutory Reclamation (Post-Loper Bright Landscape).

Research status: Complete. This is the most time-sensitive zone — its argument depends most heavily on the electoral outcome and 120th Congress composition.

Post-election update requirement: High. Section 3.1 must be fully rewritten to incorporate November 3 results. The five-point argument for the January 2027 window (as written in June 2026) assumed generic "midterm election" framing. Post-election, it must specify: which of the five priority amendments are advanceable given actual chamber composition? If Democrats recapture neither chamber, the statutory reclamation strategy (Section 3.3) becomes the primary active track, and the amendment track becomes a longer-horizon organizing agenda. This rewrite is the single most substantial editorial task in the 12-week sprint.

Section 3.2 (Five Priority Amendments) requires updating the National Popular Vote status: Virginia joined April 13, 2026 (Governor Spanberger), bringing the count to 222/270 electoral votes. The six target states for compact completion need post-election update: did any target state legislature move closer to or further from compact enactment in 2026?

Hours for Zone 3 editorial work: 4–5 hours.
Checkpoint: Zone 3 electoral outcome application layer complete by end of Week 2. Section 3.2 National Popular Vote update complete by end of Week 2.
Dependencies: November 3 election results (Zone 3 cannot be finalized before results are known).

### Zone 4: Movement Infrastructure (Existing and Needed Assets for Constitutional Defense)

Sections: 4.1 Democracy 2025 Litigation Infrastructure; 4.2 Law School Constitutional Defense Clinics; 4.3 State Constitutional Justice Networks; 4.4 International Alliance Dimension.

Research status: Complete. Zone 4 is the most durable section — the organizational landscape changes slowly.

Post-election update requirement: Low to moderate. Section 4.1 (Democracy 2025) should be updated with post-election litigation performance data: did the 516 tracked cases grow? Did any cases produce landmark post-election rulings? The 405 victory figure (as of the June 2026 research sprint) needs post-election confirmation. Section 4.3 (State Constitutional Justice Networks) should note the Michigan constitutional convention outcome: did Michigan voters authorize a state constitutional convention in 2026? If yes, this becomes a significant Zone 4 development.

Hours for Zone 4 editorial work: 2–3 hours.
Checkpoint: Zone 4 post-election update complete by end of Week 2.
Dependencies: Michigan 2026 convention vote outcome; post-election Democracy 2025 litigation count.

**Total research/editorial hours across all four zones: 9–13 hours.** This is below the Phase 3 scorecard's initial 18–22 hour estimate because the June 2026 research sprint completed the foundational research; the 12-week sprint is editorial and distribution work.

## Blocker Escalation Procedures

**Blocker Type 1: Source Unavailable**

Definition: A URL cited in the production document returns a 404, or a document is behind a paywall that prevents access.

Procedure:
1. Check the Internet Archive Wayback Machine for a cached version. Add the archive URL as the citation.
2. If the source is a law review article, check the author's SSRN page or academia.edu profile for an open-access version.
3. If the source is a government document, check the agency's .gov site directly — URL structures change but documents persist.
4. If none of the above works within one hour, remove the citation and replace with an alternative source that makes the same point. Flag in the checkpoint template under "Blocker Log."

Resolution timeline: 1 hour maximum per source. Do not hold the production document for a single inaccessible source.

**Blocker Type 2: Author Contact Fails**

Definition: A Tier 1 expert contact (Scheppele, Roznai, Chemerinsky, Gerken, Balkin) does not respond to an outreach email within 14 days.

Procedure:
1. Week 1 follow-up: resend via secondary contact channel (co-author, institutional communications office, LinkedIn message).
2. If no response after three weeks: proceed without that expert's endorsement. Do not delay distribution waiting for endorsement.
3. Document the attempted contact in the outreach log. A non-response is not a rejection — it is an opportunity for a second outreach during Domain I distribution (February 2027).

Resolution timeline: 3 weeks maximum wait for expert engagement. Distribution proceeds on the January 10 schedule regardless.

**Blocker Type 3: Timeline Slip Triggers**

Definition: A Phase 2 carryover obligation (Domain G, Domain 31x) consumes more than 10 hours in November and delays Domain H production work.

Procedure:
1. Assess whether Domain H's editorial work can be compressed. The most compressible elements are the Gist formatting (can use a simpler template), the Combined Brief (can be shortened to 4 pages if necessary), and the Tier 2 distribution (can defer to January 17–24 window).
2. The immovable minimum: the Tier 1 distribution of Domain H (to ACS, NCC, Protect Democracy, Judiciary Committee staff) must occur no later than January 14, 2027. This is 11 days before committee assignments expected to finalize and is the minimum to reach incoming members before their schedules lock.
3. If the January 14 target cannot be met due to Phase 2 slip: produce the "emergency minimum" version of Domain H — a 2,000-word document covering the five priority amendments and the January 2027 window argument. This is the Scenario C deliverable from PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md.

**Blocker Type 4: Domain K Distribution Slip**

Definition: Domain K does not distribute by December 20, which removes the 10-14 day Domain K lead that Domain H's sequencing depends on.

Procedure:
1. If Domain K is delayed to December 27: Domain H can still distribute January 10. The gap shrinks from 10–14 days to 5–7 days, which is a constraint but not a failure — legal advocacy organizations check email in early January and both documents will be visible to contacts who did not read Domain K until returning from holiday.
2. If Domain K is delayed past January 3: distribute Domain H and Domain K simultaneously on January 10 as a combined package. Loss: the "escalating specificity" mechanism (contacts receiving K first, then H) does not operate. But the January 3–17 committee window is still reached.
3. Do not hold Domain H past January 14 waiting for Domain K, under any circumstances.

## Contingency Workflow: Phase 2 Slippage Scenarios

### If Phase 2 Closes September 30 (One-Month Slip from August 31)

Domain H impact: Minimal. The September 30 close is before the November 4 Phase 3 launch. Domain H's 12-week sprint begins November 4 on schedule.

Zone adjustments: None required.

Which zones shift to October work: None — Domain H work begins November 4 as planned. No Zone shifts to October.

Which must complete by November 30 for January 3 deadline: Zones 1 and 3 (most time-sensitive for post-election updating) must be editorially complete by November 30. Zone 2 and Zone 4 editorial work can extend to December 7.

### If Phase 2 Closes October 31 (Two-Month Slip)

Domain H impact: Minor. Domain H work begins November 4 on schedule but November capacity is split with Domain 37a emergency production. Approximately 10–15 hours of November capacity is consumed by Domain 37a, leaving 15–20 hours for Domain H editorial work in November. This is sufficient given the 9–13 hour total Domain H editorial requirement.

Zone adjustments:
- Zone 3 editorial work compresses into Week 2 only (rather than Weeks 2–3). This is achievable because the electoral outcome overlay is the primary Zone 3 task.
- Zone 1 source verification moves to December 1 (one-week slip).

Which zones shift to December: Zone 4 editorial work and Gist creation shift from Weeks 4–5 to Weeks 5–6. Combined Brief production shifts to Week 6. This compresses the pre-send verification to one week rather than two, which is acceptable.

Must complete by November 30: Zone 3 electoral outcome application layer (this gates the Combined Brief and cannot slip further without affecting the January 10 distribution).

### If Phase 2 Does Not Close by October 31 (Catastrophic Slip — Scenario C)

Domain H impact: Significant. November capacity is consumed entirely by Domain 37a emergency production and Phase 2 carryover audit. Domain H work cannot begin until November 22 at the earliest.

Emergency minimum version: A 2,000-word Domain H brief covering:
- The five priority amendments with a single paragraph each
- The January 2027 window argument (one paragraph)
- The German December 2024 constitutional amendment as the primary international comparator
- The Democracy 2025 litigation infrastructure as movement foundation

This emergency minimum is distributable by January 3 at 8–10 hours of production work in late November–December. It is not the full 7,500-word production document but is sufficient to brief incoming legislative staff.

Zone deferral under Scenario C:
- Zone 2 (International Precedent): defer to Domain I's February 2027 window. The comparative constitutional analysis will reach the legal community through Domain I's February 2027 distribution.
- Zone 4 (Movement Infrastructure): defer detailed coalition mapping to PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md, which can be shared directly with organizational contacts who need it.
- Zones 1 and 3: must be in the January 3 distribution even in emergency minimum form.

Critical: even under Scenario C, the January 14 distribution deadline cannot slip. Congressional committee assignment windows close January 17. Missing January 17 means Domain H arrives when Judiciary Committee staff are no longer in "what should we prioritize?" mode but "what bills are in front of us?" mode.
