## Lead Finding

The December 1, 2026 hard deadline for Domain H and Domain K research writing — derived by working backward from the January 3, 2027 Congress seating with a minimum three-week editorial, formatting, and distribution lead — creates a critical path constraint that is six weeks earlier than the Phase 3 scorecard's nominal November 4 start date implies. Research for H and K must begin no later than November 18, 2026 to reach December 1 completion targets at Phase 2's average research pace. This means any Phase 2 slip past October 31, 2026 compresses H and K research into an inadequate window. The October 31 Phase 2 exit gate is not a buffer — it is the H/K research start date.

## Part 1: Phase 2 to Phase 3 Integration — Which Domains Feed H/K/37a

### Domain 51 (Campaign Finance Reform) → Domain K (Judiciary Restructuring)

Domain 51 documents the dark money architecture that funds anti-judicial-reform campaigns. The connection is direct: the same corporate and donor network that opposed campaign finance disclosure requirements (documented in Domain 51's analysis of the $2B+ 2026 cycle dark money) has been the primary funder of the campaigns to maintain the current SCOTUS composition and block judicial reform legislation.

For Domain K, Domain 51 provides the adversarial political economy context: judicial reform bills (SCERT Act, TERM Act, SHADOW Act) face opposition funded by the same dark money networks whose interests the current Court serves. When Domain K's legislative viability analysis assesses the Senate filibuster calculus for the SCERT Act, the Domain 51 donor mapping provides the specific names of the corporate coalitions lobbying against it. Combined, the two domains constitute the structural argument: dark money captures courts, captured courts enable more dark money, requiring structural reform of both simultaneously.

Distribution implication: the combined Domain 51 post-election update + Domain K package (the "constitutional reform briefing package" identified in PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md) reaches the Campaign Legal Center and OpenSecrets contacts who worked on Domain 51 with an argument they are uniquely positioned to amplify — that judicial reform and campaign finance reform address the same underlying capture mechanism.

### Domain 59 (Civil Service Protection and Economic Democracy) → Domain K (Judiciary Ethics)

Domain 59 documented the mechanism through which Schedule Policy/Career (Schedule F revival) politicizes executive branch decision-making. The connection to Domain K is the judicial ethics enforcement gap: SCOTUS operates under a voluntary self-policing code while every other federal judge is governed by the Code of Conduct for United States Judges — enforced by the judicial councils of each circuit. Schedule Policy/Career's logic — replacing independent career officials with politically aligned appointees — is the executive branch analog of the SCOTUS ethics gap. Both represent the same structural failure: professional independence norms enforced only by voluntary compliance and social pressure rather than structural mechanism.

For Domain K, Domain 59's civil service research provides the legislative precedent for how Congress has historically mandated professional independence standards through statutory requirement (the Civil Service Reform Act of 1978, the Inspectors General Act of 1978) — and the path by which those standards were undermined. The SCERT Act's enforcement mechanism (appellate judge panels reviewing ethics complaints) draws directly on the civil service model of external review by peers rather than self-policing. Domain 59's research on the MSPB restoration legislative strategy is the closest existing template for how Congress can reimpose structural independence requirements on an institution that has successfully resisted voluntary compliance.

Distribution implication: AFGE and NTEU contacts (Domain 56/59 Tier 1 recipients) are natural allies for Domain K's ethics enforcement argument precisely because the civil service independence and judicial independence failures are structurally identical. A Domain K briefing that explicitly connects SCERT Act enforcement mechanisms to the civil service independence model they know will land with labor organizations that have not previously engaged on judicial reform.

### Domain 48 (Healthcare-Medicaid-OBBBA Intersection) → Domain 37a (Certification Vulnerability)

Domain 48 documents the OBBBA Medicaid work requirements and their administrative implementation timeline (January 1, 2027 effective date for several provisions). The connection to Domain 37a is less direct but operationally significant: the same administrative law capacity constraints that affect Schedule Policy/Career implementation also affect election administration. States managing Medicaid eligibility recertification under OBBBA work requirements are simultaneously managing 2026 election certification. State agency capacity is finite. The OBBBA Medicaid administrative burden, landing on state agencies in October-December 2026, creates the precise capacity constraint in state health and human services agencies that election officials must compete with for IT systems, data management, and administrative personnel.

For Domain 37a, Domain 48's research on OBBBA implementation timelines provides the state-capacity context for why certification resistance scenarios are more likely in states already under maximum administrative load. States managing contested Medicaid work requirement implementation challenges in November-December 2026 are also the states most likely to face certification disputes — Arizona, Georgia, Wisconsin, and Nevada are the overlap cases. A post-election certification analysis that ignores state administrative capacity constraints will miss this dimension of contestation risk.

Distribution implication: CBPP and NWLC contacts (Domain 48 Tier 1 recipients) can be reached with a Domain 37a briefing that explains the state-capacity connection. These organizations have the state-level policy infrastructure to warn state officials about administrative overload before certification season.

## Part 2: Research Hour Budget for H/K/37a

### Existing Estimates and Rationale

The Phase 3 scorecard established the following estimates before this Item 110 deepening:

| Domain | Scorecard Estimate | Hours Type |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| Domain H | 18-22 hours | Largely new-from-scratch; substantial source base in existing Domain H research report |
| Domain K | 16-20 hours | Largely new-from-scratch; Zone 1-4 complete in Domain K research report |
| Domain 37a | 25-30 hours | Split: 10-15 hours pre-election legal framework + 10-15 hours post-election application |
| **H/K/37a total** | **59-72 hours** | |

The task brief's guidance targets 40-60 hours combined for H/K/37a, "higher due to Jan 3 hard deadline." The existing scorecard estimates exceed this range at the high end. The reconciliation:

The Domain K and Domain H research reports are production-ready from June 2026 sprint work. Both documents exist as full research productions (Domain K: 12,700+ lines across four zones; Domain H: ~7,500 words with Zone 1-4 complete). The hours required for Phase 3 distribution are not full research hours but editorial, formatting, and distribution production hours — the gap between the existing research sprint product and the final distribution-ready document.

**Revised Phase 3 production hours for H and K**: 8-12 hours each (editorial completion, Gist creation, combined brief production, coalition sequencing coordination). Domain H and K research are substantially complete; the production gap is the post-election application layer that adds electoral outcome-specific framing (different analysis depending on whether Democrats recapture one or both chambers) and the distribution packaging.

**Domain 37a remains at 25-30 hours** because it has a genuine pre-election/post-election research split that cannot be compressed: the legal framework layer (10-15 hours, completable by October 31) and the state-specific post-election application layer (10-15 hours, begins November 4 based on results). The legal framework layer is the more research-intensive component; the application layer is primarily analytical mapping of results onto the pre-built legal framework.

**Revised H/K/37a combined estimate: 43-54 hours** — within the 40-60 hour target range established by the task brief, and representing a more accurate assessment of Phase 3 production work given the existing research completions.

**Comparison to other Phase 3 domains**:
- Domain I (International Standards): 28-35 hours — highest per-domain research hours in Phase 3, genuinely new from scratch
- Domain L (Movement Infrastructure): 20-25 hours — new from scratch, survey-dependent
- Domain 49 (Callais post-election): 24-30 hours — results-dependent, needs full research production
- Domains 51, 56 updates: 18-22 hours combined — structured updates to existing documents

H/K/37a's elevated allocation relative to the per-domain Phase 2 average (28.5 hours) is justified by the January 3 hard deadline, but the June 2026 research sprints that produced Domain H and K reports have already consumed a substantial portion of the research hours. Phase 3 allocates the remaining editorial and distribution production work.

## Part 3: Critical Path Timeline — Working Backward from January 3, 2027

### The Immovable Dates

**January 3, 2027**: 120th Congress seated. Committee assignments begin January 4-6 (leadership) and January 9-17 (full committee assignments). After January 17, the "blank slate" window for shaping incoming member reform priorities closes. This is the hard anchor.

**January 10, 2027 (derived)**: Domain H must be in Tier 1 distribution by this date to land in Judiciary Committee offices during the committee assignment deliberation period, when incoming members are most receptive to structural reform framing.

**December 20, 2026 (derived)**: Domain K must be in Tier 1 distribution. Domain H trails Domain K by 10-14 days (sequencing constraint from PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md). December 20 gives 10 days before January 1 holiday slowdown for Fix the Court, Demand Justice, and ACS to process Domain K before Domain H arrives January 10.

**December 1, 2026 (derived)**: Hard deadline for H and K research writing to be complete. Working backward from December 20 distribution: allow 2 weeks for editorial production, Gist creation, and distribution list preparation. Research writing must finish by December 1.

**November 18, 2026 (derived)**: Domain K research start date. Domain K requires 8-12 Phase 3 production hours at the research completion stage. Working backward from December 1 completion: at 4-6 hours per research session (Phase 2 pace), two research sessions are required. Two sessions between November 18 and December 1 = achievable with one session per week, consistent with Phase 3 capacity planning.

**November 4-14, 2026 (immovable)**: Domain 37a post-election application layer. The 10-day window after election results is the operational deployment window for certification defense analysis. This is not a research production window — it is a rapid-analysis deployment. The pre-election legal framework layer (completable by October 31) is what makes the 10-day window achievable.

**October 31, 2026 (derived)**: Phase 2 exit gate and Domain 37a legal framework completion. Domain 37a pre-election research (FCEA mechanics, Section 3 standing post-*Anderson*, TRO pre-certification strategy) must complete by October 31 for the November 4-14 deployment window to be feasible. This is the single most important Phase 3 capacity protection date in the entire calendar.

### Why H/K Research Must Finish Earlier Than Other Phase 3 Domains

Every other Phase 3 domain has a later effective hard deadline:

- Domain 49 (Callais redistricting): December 31 redistricting litigation filing deadline. Research begins November 4 (results-dependent) and must complete December 20. Later than H/K.
- Domain 51 post-election update: FEC post-general reports due December 3; full analysis can complete December 20. Later than H/K.
- Domain I (International Standards): Freedom House submission window is February 28, 2027 (preferred) with practical extension to March. Later than H/K.
- Domain L (Movement Infrastructure): March 2027 mid-cycle review. Significantly later than H/K.

Only Domain 37a shares the immovable November 4-14 urgency with H/K, but for a different reason: Domain 37a is operational deployment, not research production. H/K are the only domains in Phase 3 where research writing must be complete in the December 1, 2026 window.

This earlier deadline justifies the elevated research hour allocation for H/K within Phase 3: not because the research is more voluminous, but because the compressed November-December window requires dedicating the research capacity that would otherwise be spread across four months into a six-week sprint.

## Part 4: Contingency Cascade — What Defers to Q2 2027 if Phase 2 Slips

### The Answer: Nothing in H/K/37a Defers

The task brief correctly identifies that H/K/37a cannot defer to Q2 2027 under any Phase 2 slippage scenario. The argument:

**Domain H** defers = missing the January 3-17 committee assignment window. After committee assignments finalize, incoming Judiciary Committee members shift from "what structural reforms should we prioritize?" to "what specific bills are in front of our committee?" A January 3 distribution that arrives on January 20 is not late distribution to the same audience — it is a different, less receptive audience doing different, more constrained work. The advocacy value of Domain H in Q2 2027 is substantially lower than in the January window.

**Domain K** defers = missing the first-90-days reform agenda formation period. Fix the Court, Demand Justice, and ACS are running their highest-intensity campaigns in the January-March 2027 window if any Democratic legislative majority exists. If Domain K research reaches them in May 2027 rather than December 2026, they have already finalized their legislative strategy without the benefit of Domain K's legislative scoring and constitutional feasibility analysis. They may not be wrong in their approach, but they will not have used Domain K's analysis to ground it.

**Domain 37a** defers = losing all operational value. After December 1, 2026, the post-election contestation window has either resolved or hardened into litigation. Domain 37a's analysis converts from real-time decision support (which organizations need in November) to historical documentation (which is useful but not urgent). There is no Q2 2027 audience for post-election certification analysis at the urgency level that makes Domain 37a a Tier 1 priority.

### What the Cascade Actually Means

If Phase 2 slips past October 31, 2026, the cascade is not "defer H/K/37a to Q2 2027." The cascade is:

**Phase 2 slips to November 3**: Domain 37a's pre-election legal framework layer is compressed from a planned October completion to a rushed November 4-7 emergency production. Domain K and Domain H start dates are unaffected (November 18 is after election day regardless). Net impact: Domain 37a quality degrades; H/K unaffected.

**Phase 2 slips to November 15**: Domain 37a's pre-election legal framework layer is lost entirely; the post-election application layer must incorporate legal research simultaneously with empirical analysis, compressing a planned two-stage process into a single rushed production. Domain K and Domain H start dates move from November 18 to November 22 (one week slip), moving the December 1 completion target to December 8 and the December 20 distribution target to December 27. Domain H's January 10 target holds but with one week of margin eliminated. Net impact: Domain 37a substantially degraded; H/K marginally compressed.

**Phase 2 slips past November 15**: Under the Phase 3 timeline's Scenario C, Domain H and K are produced as "emergency minimum documents" — 1,500-2,000 words each covering the most time-critical content (specific legislative proposals for K, constitutional amendment priorities for H). These are not full production documents but sufficient to brief incoming legislative staff before January 3. Domain 37a is produced as a rapid-response document under a compressed 15-hour budget rather than the planned 25-30 hours.

In no scenario does the slip produce a Q2 2027 deferral of H/K/37a. The deferral under catastrophic Phase 2 slip is quality reduction, not timeline shift.
