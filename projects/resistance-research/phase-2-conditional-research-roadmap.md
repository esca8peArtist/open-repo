# Phase 2 Conditional Research Roadmap

**Internal planning document — May 19, 2026 (pre-synthesis)**

This document provides **outcome-conditional execution instructions** for Phase 2 research, based on Wave 1 synthesis results. May 21 synthesis will classify Wave 1 outcome into one of four branches; this document pre-stages the Phase 2 path for each branch, eliminating decision paralysis in the May 22-31 window.

**Core decision:** Wave 1 outcome → Phase 2 research sequencing OR Wave 1 extension → Phase 2 deferred

---

## Executive Summary: Four Outcome Branches

The May 21 19:00 UTC synthesis will classify Wave 1 response data into one of four branches:

| Outcome | Definition | Evidence | Phase 2 Trigger | Phase 2 Start |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **STRONG** | >5 institutional replies + >20 individual contacts engaging | >5 org responses to Batch 1; pattern of replies across >2 sectors (law, policy, civil rights) | Phase 2 research immediate launch | June 1, 2026 |
| **MODERATE** | 2-4 institutional replies + >10 individual engagement signals | 2-4 org replies; individual reply rate >15% | Wave 1 extension through June 1; Phase 2 staging | June 15, 2026 |
| **WEAK** | <2 institutional replies + <10 individual signals | 0-1 org reply; individual reply rate <10% | Wave 1 analysis + regroup | July 1, 2026 |
| **TOO_EARLY** | Inconclusive (email delivery <100%, no window closed yet) | Synthesis runs before May 21 10:30 UTC user check | Continue Wave 1 through June 7 | June 15, 2026 |

**User decision tree**: If synthesis outcome lands on branch boundary (e.g., 4 institutional replies, unclear engagement patterns), user decision determines assignment; pre-drafted decision framework in Section 5 below.

## Branch 3: WEAK Outcome (<2 institutional + <10 individual replies)

**Probability estimate**: 20% (possible if messaging ineffective or distribution channels not optimal)

**Phase 2 path**: Wave 1 analysis + regroup; Phase 2 deferred to July 1 or later

### 3.1 Wave 1 Analysis Framework

A WEAK outcome suggests either: (a) distribution channels ineffective, (b) messaging not resonant, or (c) timing premature. Before Phase 2 launches, analyze Wave 1 failure modes:

**May 22-26: Post-mortem analysis**
- Email delivery verification: Did Batch 1 emails reach inboxes? (check bounce rates, spam folder issues)
- Reply rate by sector: Where are the replies coming from? (law schools vs. think tanks vs. civil rights?)
- Message engagement: Which email subject lines got opens? (Google Analytics on Gist links)
- Contact-level analysis: Did certain contact subcategories reply more? (law schools >think tanks, etc.?)
- Timing analysis: Did replies cluster on certain days? (day-of-week, time-of-day patterns)

**May 26-30: Strategy adjustment workshop**
- User + orchestrator review analysis together
- Decision points:
  - **Problem 1: Delivery/technical**: Fix spam filter issues, resend Batch 1 to bounce addresses, proceed to Batch 2 immediately
  - **Problem 2: Messaging**: Revise subject lines, personalization, call-to-action based on engagement patterns; stage Batch 2 variant messaging
  - **Problem 3: Timing**: If May timing is suboptimal, plan June 15 relaunch with summer-specific angles
  - **Problem 4: Channel**: If institutional channel weak, pivot to individual/academic channel for Batch 2

### 3.2 Alternative Distribution Channels

If primary channel ineffective, stage alternative channels for June-July:

1. **Academic channel**: University press releases, faculty email lists, law review societies
2. **Policy blog channel**: Medium, Substack, policy blog syndication networks
3. **Congressional channel**: Direct outreach to House/Senate committee staff (bypass think tank intermediaries)
4. **Labor/union channel**: AFL-CIO, SEIU, UFW email lists + newsletters
5. **Nonprofit channel**: Foundation Program Officer network, National Council of Nonprofits distribution

Each channel has different lead time, reach, and adoption profile.

### 3.3 Phase 2 Deferred (July 1 start)

If Wave 1 analysis suggests systematic channel/messaging issue, defer Phase 2 research to July 1 to allow time for:
- June 1-15: Implementation of messaging/channel fixes
- June 15: Batch 2 soft launch with revised strategy
- June 20-30: Batch 2 response monitoring
- July 1: Phase 2 research launch (based on revised Wave 1 success)

## Section 5: Boundary Case Decision Tree — Ambiguous Outcomes

If synthesis outcome lands on branch boundary (e.g., 5 institutional replies but only 12 individual signals; 4 institutional but 22 individual), use this decision tree:

```
Institutional replies = 5, Individual signals = 12?
→ Move to STRONG track (threshold met for institutional)

Institutional replies = 4, Individual signals = 18?
→ User decision: MODERATE or STRONG?
   Ask: Are the 4 replies from diverse sectors?
   - Yes (law + civil rights + policy) → STRONG (4 is threshold, plus diversity matters)
   - No (all law schools, for example) → MODERATE (quantity counts, but sector concentration matters)

Institutional replies = 2, Individual signals = 15?
→ MODERATE (institutional threshold not met, but individual engagement strong)

Institutional replies = 0, Individual signals = 25?
→ TOO_EARLY or channel-pivot decision?
   Ask: Are replies still arriving?
   - Yes (still getting emails daily) → TOO_EARLY, extend to June 7
   - No (plateau reached) → WEAK + alternative channel pivot (June 15)

Institutional replies = 3, Individual = 8, but >50% from single organization network?
→ User decision: Are they 3 independent orgs or 3 people from same org?
   - Independent → MODERATE
   - Clustered → WEAK + channel issue diagnosis
```
