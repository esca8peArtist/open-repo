## Part 1: Senate Finance CTC Window Verification

### What Is Actually Happening

**OBBBA CTC Provisions (signed July 4, 2025)**: The One Big Beautiful Bill Act permanently increased the maximum CTC from $2,000 to $2,200 per child while retaining the income phase-in restrictions that leave the lowest-income families excluded. CBPP has documented that 17-19 million children are excluded from the full credit. The American Family Act (Democratic alternative) would eliminate all refundability restrictions — the structural change that Domain 59 supports on democratic participation grounds.

- A second reconciliation bill focused on DHS/ICE funding passed both chambers in May-June 2026. This bill does not contain CTC provisions.
- The relevant advocacy window is the floor and amendment campaign around any 2026 tax vehicle — which is ongoing — not a specific noticed markup.

**What This Means for the Emails**: The existing email templates reference "Senate Finance Committee CTC markup window" and "June 30 markup deadline." This framing is accurate in the sense that advocates are treating the June-July period as a critical window for building the record for CTC refundability, but it may overstate the precision of the legislative calendar. The recommendation is to modify the framing slightly: replace "markup deadline: June 30" with "active Senate CTC advocacy window through summer 2026" before sending. This preserves the urgency (which is real) without overstating the precision of the deadline (which is not a hard procedural date).

**Confidence on Window Timing**:

- MomsRising and AFL-CIO are active in Senate lobbying on economic security. High confidence that a democratic participation frame is novel and useful to their advocacy.

**Passage Likelihood by June 30**: Near-zero for a standalone CTC expansion bill by June 30. The second reconciliation bill has passed without CTC changes. The advocacy leverage is not passage by June 30 — it is building the organizational record and Senate Finance member awareness ahead of any future tax vehicle (end of year continuing resolution, potential omnibus, or 2027 standalone).
