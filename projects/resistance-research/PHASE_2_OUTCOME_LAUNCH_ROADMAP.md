### 1.2 Law Schools

**Wave 1 contacts in this constituency**: Ryan Goodman (Just Security/NYU Law School), Erica Chenoweth (Harvard Kennedy School).

**Response cycle norms**: 5–10 days from send for initial reply; up to 21 days for law school contacts who are traveling or in late-semester review periods. Silence at 72 hours (May 21 gate) is within sector norm — do not classify law school performance as Weak at 72h.

**Threshold definitions**:

| Classification | % Threshold | What it means at 2 contacts |
|---|---|---|
| Strong | >30% reply rate with Score 3+ | At least 1 of 2 contacts replies substantively within 10 days with domain-specific content, methodological question, or referral to a clinic director or colleague |
| Expected | 20–30% | 0–1 reply within the 72h window; 1 of 2 replies within 10 days at Score 1–2 |
| Weak | <15% | Zero substantive replies (Score 3+) from either contact by Day 14; OR both contacts explicitly decline |

**What Strong looks like**: Goodman asks whether a domain could be reformatted as a Just Security symposium piece; Chenoweth offers a methodological critique of the comparative civil resistance framework; either contact names a clinic director or faculty colleague to receive the materials directly. Score 4 from one contact with no reply from the other = STRONG for this constituency.

**What Weak looks like**: Both contacts acknowledge receipt with Score 1 and do not follow up; or zero replies from either contact by Day 14 with confirmed delivery. One acknowledgment-only reply (Score 1) and one non-response by Day 14 classifies as Expected, not Weak — the non-response at Day 14 for law school contacts is not meaningful.

**Phase 2 signal implications**: Law school Strong engagement unlocks Domain 57 (constitutional asymmetry analysis — the most intellectually demanding domain and the one most likely to attract constitutional law clinic use) and validates the comparative methodology in the historical case study domains. Think tank outreach in Tier 2 relies on law school social proof more than any other sector. A Strong law school signal accelerates think tank outreach by 1–2 weeks.

**Good signal threshold for Tier 2 sequencing**: >30% (1 of 2 substantive replies within 10 days). Good signal enables the social proof framing in Tier 2 law school emails; below this threshold, use the utility-forward framing instead.

### 2.2 Per-Constituency Reading of the Matrix

**Law schools (Strong D57/D59 acceleration)**: Law school constituencies are most activated by Domain 57 (constitutional asymmetry in treaty withdrawal — directly applicable to constitutional law clinics) and Domain 59 (income-participation gap, civic exclusion compounding — applicable to election law and public policy clinics). Under Strong, both launch June 15, giving law school Tier 2 contacts 6 weeks of production-in-progress access before August 1 distribution. Under Moderate, D57 launches June 10 and law school Tier 2 outreach activates Week 6 with D57 partial draft available. Under Weak, neither domain is available for law school engagement until August — Tier 2 law school outreach should pivot to D39 (health law clinic applicability: OBBBA Medicaid work requirements + NVRA nexus) and D56 (civil service law clinic applicability: Schedule F constitutional design analysis).

**Immigration legal aid (D39 always, D57 Section 3 on track)**: Domain 39 is the time-critical immigration legal aid domain regardless of outcome — the HHS June 1 interim final rule creates a distribution window that immigration legal organizations must have analysis for by June 1. Domain 57 Section 3 (ICC sanctions chilling effect on US civil society cooperation) is the analytically novel immigration legal hook — this analysis does not exist elsewhere. Under Strong, both D39 pre-distribution and D57 Section 3 are available by the time Tier 2 immigration legal outreach activates (Week 5). Under Weak, only D39 is available by Week 7 Tier 2 activation — lead with D39 and offer D57 Section 3 as coming-soon.

**Unions (D59 primary, D39 secondary)**: Domain 59 is the most directly applicable union domain — the OBBBA compounding argument and gig worker voting rights proposal give union legislative staff concrete tools. Domain 39's rural hospital closure voter turnout data applies to union-heavy rural manufacturing communities. Under Strong, D59 research launches June 15 — union Tier 2 outreach in Week 5–6 can offer early access to production outlines. Under Moderate, D59 research launches July 1 — union Tier 2 outreach in Week 6 leads with D39 utility and offers D59 as a forthcoming resource. Under Weak, D59 does not start until July 15, but D39 fast-turn production (June 1–15) is available as the union hook — Medicaid cuts' impact on healthcare worker employment and SNAP cuts' impact on worker economic security are union issues regardless of the democratic design framing.

**Think tanks (D57 primary, D59 secondary for analytical novelty)**: Think tanks respond to analytical novelty and research-in-progress access. D57's multilateral withdrawal reframing (not a foreign policy event — a domestic accountability infrastructure removal) and D59's compounding argument (five simultaneous OBBBA cuts produce multiplicative, not additive, civic participation reduction) are the two arguments that do not exist in current policy literature. Under Strong, offering think tanks access to D57/D59 working outlines from June 15 is the primary pitch. Under Moderate, this offer is available from Week 6 with D57 outline only. Under Weak, neither is available until August — think tank Tier 2 outreach should be deferred to Week 7+ and anchor to D38 (AI regulatory capture analysis is directly in Brookings/AEI think tank terrain) or D40 (electoral manipulation analysis aligns with CFR and democracy think tank concerns).

### 2.3 Pivot Decision at Week 5 (June 15–21)

Regardless of initial classification, reassess at June 15. If Phase 1 has recovered (Weak → Moderate; Moderate → Strong indicators), shift to the higher-outcome domain sequence from Week 5 forward. The domains that have not yet launched (D57, D59, D38) have sufficient lead time to shift upward without missing deadlines. If classification is unchanged or has deteriorated, continue on the current track. Do not shift sequencing downward (e.g., from Moderate to Weak) without confirming that the lower-tier domain fast-turn production is actually necessary — downshifting prematurely sacrifices D57/D59 lead time for no operational gain.
