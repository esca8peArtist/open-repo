### II.2 — Domain 42: Section 591 FY2027 Appropriations Rider — CRITICAL DEADLINE UPDATE

**Development**: The May 13, 2026 House Appropriations Committee markup of the FY2027 Commerce-Justice-Science (CJS) bill contains Section 591 — a rider that would bar the use of any appropriated funds to reschedule marijuana or remove it from the controlled substances schedules. If enacted as written, Section 591 would freeze DEA implementation of the April 23 medical track rescheduling, block the June 29-July 15 ALJ hearing from producing any rescheduling outcome, and retroactively undermine the dual-track framework before it stabilizes.

**Critical context**: The May 13 markup is in six days from today (May 7). This is a materially new development since Domain 42 was written on May 7. Historical precedent: the same language appeared in the FY2026 CJS bill but was stripped in conference. However, the current political environment (the administration simultaneously reschedules and faces congressional resistance) makes Section 591's survival path more uncertain.

**DEA-1362 May 28 deadline status confirmed**: The participation notice deadline remains May 28, 2026 (email) or May 20, 2026 (mail). The hearing remains scheduled June 29-July 15. Section 591 does not affect the hearing participation deadline, but it creates a risk that the hearing outcome would have no legal effect if the rider becomes law.

**Update needed**: Add a note to Domain 42 Section 5 (or wherever legislative threats are discussed) documenting Section 591, the May 13 markup date, historical precedent for stripping the rider, and the dual-track legal uncertainty it creates.
