# Phase 3 Movement Leverage Architecture

*Authored: June 6, 2026. Cross-reference: PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md for scoring rationale; PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md for quarterly sequencing.*

---

## Design Principle

The 40+ domain framework was built as a diagnostic tool. Phase 3's job is to convert diagnostic depth into durable organizational adoption. Movement leverage architecture is the map of which Phase 3 domains activate which existing networks, and in what sequence those activations compound each other.

The key insight from Phase 2: domains do not activate independently. A single domain reaching a single Tier 1 contact is a signal. A domain cluster reaching three Tier 1 contacts in the same coalition ecosystem is an activation event — it enables those contacts to coordinate on the shared analytical foundation, which is the lever for Level 3-4 adoption (framework integrated into organizational materials, framework generative of derivative advocacy).

Phase 3 movement leverage architecture is therefore not a contact list. It is a sequencing logic for which domains to deploy to which networks in which order to create compounding activation.

All already in the contact network. Domain 37a is the post-election follow-up to Domain 37 distribution — it does not require new contact discovery, only new content.

**Cross-amplification with Phase 2**: Domain 37a + Domain 49 (Callais redistricting) together constitute the complete 2026 election interference landscape — pre-election federal interference (Domain 37) + redistricting-based voter dilution (Domain 49) + post-election certification threats (Domain 37a). These three should be distributed as a single cluster to election protection organizations.

### Domain K (Federal Judiciary Restructuring and Supreme Court Reform)
**Amplifies**: Domain 6 (Judicial Independence), Domain 35 (Supreme Court 2026 Term Preview), Domain 29 (Prosecutorial Weaponization and DOJ Capture)

**What it adds**: Domain 6 documents the erosion; Domain 35 documents the 2026 term's specific threats. Domain K provides the structural repair blueprint — SCOTUS term limits, Court expansion, appellate restructuring, FISA Court reform, shadow docket elimination. **Cross-amplification**: Domain K + Domain 6 + Domain 35 constitute the complete judicial independence cluster. For Senate Judiciary Committee staff, distributing Domain K should be preceded by a short "Domain 6 reminder" hook to refresh the diagnostic frame before presenting the reform proposals.

### Domain H (Constitutional Resilience Architecture and Amendment Strategy)
**Amplifies**: Domain 39 (Constitutional Architecture and Article V Convention Threat), Domain 26 (Government Accountability and Institutional Resilience), Domain 34 (Congressional Power of the Purse)

**What it adds**: Domain 39 covers the defensive argument — blocking a runaway Article V convention. Domain H adds the affirmative argument: what constitutional amendments would durably prevent the institutional erosions documented across the 40-domain framework, and what is the realistic pathway to ratification? This is the framework's most forward-looking structural argument, targeted at the constitutional reform horizon (2027-2032).

**Coalition re-activation**: The American Constitution Society (already in Phase 1-2 contact network), the National Constitution Center, law school constitutional law clinics (Harvard, NYU, Yale, Columbia, Michigan, Georgetown). The ACS subscriber list of 12,000+ legal professionals is the primary secondary distribution network.

**New coalition activation**: Domain H reaches the Article V Convention project advocates (who are defensive about a convention but receptive to specific amendment proposals as an alternative) and the scholars working on structural constitutional reform (who have not historically engaged with democracy-movement research frameworks). This is a new audience segment with its own distribution infrastructure: the National Academy of Public Administration, the Constitution Accountability Center, and the Andrew Goodman Foundation's young voter / constitutional education programs.

**Cross-amplification**: Domain H + Domain K together constitute the constitutional repair package — the specific amendments that would fix what Domain K documents as fixable through legislation, and the structural architecture changes that legislation alone cannot achieve. For ACS distribution, Domain K and Domain H should be released within two weeks of each other to create a comprehensive constitutional reform moment in the legal advocacy community.

**Amplifies**: Domain 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal and US Commitment Collapse), Domain 20 (Corruption and Institutional Accountability), Domain 6 (Judicial Independence), Domain 43 (Epistemic Infrastructure and Disinformation Crisis)

**What it adds**: Domain 57 documents what the US withdrew from. Domain I is the framework's comparative evidence base, answering the practitioner question "what would actually work?" with documented international precedent.

**Coalition re-activation**: Phase 3 strategic roadmap lists Senate Foreign Relations Committee staff as Domain I targets. Specifically, staff contacts established through Domain 57 distribution (August 2026 UNGA prep outreach to CICC and HRW) are the bridge to Senate Foreign Relations. **New coalition activation**: The Transatlantic Democracy Working Group (GMF), the National Democratic Institute, the International Republican Institute (for cross-partisan credibility), and the Council on Foreign Relations democracy research staff are new contacts enabled by Domain I's comparative policy framing. These organizations have their own substantial distribution networks (NDI reaches democracy practitioners in 75+ countries; CFR publications reach State Dept, congressional staff, and foreign policy think tanks).

**Freedom House / V-Dem integration**: Domain I provides the supplementary documentation for Freedom House's US country researchers (annual report submission window: January-February 2027) and V-Dem's country expert network. A single successful integration of Domain I findings into Freedom House's "Freedom in the World 2027" report converts the framework into an internationally cited primary source — a qualitative step change in the framework's institutional credibility.

**Cross-amplification**: Domain I + Domain 57 constitute the complete international argument: domain 57 shows what was lost (multilateral withdrawal); Domain I shows what peer democracies built and maintained. For CICC and HRW contacts, distributing them together maximizes the "comparative argument to US policy" framing.

## Part 2: Coalition Chain Maps

Coalition chains are sequences of domain deployments that compound their activation effect by reaching the same coalition network at multiple points in time, with each deployment building on the organizational trust established by the previous one.

## Part 4: Contingency Cascade — Phase 2 Slippage Impact on Phase 3

If Phase 2 closes September 30 instead of August 31, the following cascade applies:

### Domains that become load-bearing for Phase 4 if incomplete at Phase 3 close:

**Domain K (judiciary)** is Phase 4 load-bearing because: Phase 4's institutional playbooks for the legal/judicial community (planned for 2028) require Domain K as their structural reform foundation. Domain K needs to be a citable, circulating document before Phase 4's playbook work begins.

**Domain H (constitutional architecture)** is Phase 4 load-bearing because: Phase 4's 5-year vision includes Domain H contributing analysis to at least one active constitutional reform campaign. A campaign organized in 2027-2028 needs Domain H to be in circulation before its organizational planning begins.

**Domain I (international standards)** is Phase 4 load-bearing because: Phase 4's Freedom House / V-Dem integration (the international credibility step-change) depends on Domain I being a published, citable document at the time of annual report compilation. Missing the February 2027 Freedom House submission window means waiting until February 2028.

**Domain J (cryptographic democracy)** is Phase 4 load-bearing because: state pilot program legislative sessions open in January each year. Missing 2027 means waiting for 2028. For a domain whose primary value proposition is enabling state-level digital voting pilots, a one-year slip is a material loss.

**Domain L (movement infrastructure)** is Phase 4 load-bearing because: it is the empirical foundation for Phase 4's Tier 3+ distribution architecture (targeting 500+ organizations). Without the survey, Phase 4 has no systematic map of the organizational landscape it is trying to reach.

### Cascade priority under Phase 2 slippage:

If Phase 2 slips to Sept 30, Phase 3 loses approximately 30 days of launch window. The cascade priority is:

1. **Do not defer Domain 37a** — November 4 window is absolute
2. **Do not defer Domain K** — January 3 Congressional seating window is absolute
3. **Domain H can slip 2-4 weeks** without losing the constitutional reform network window (Feb-March 2027 is still sufficient)
4. **Domain I can slip 2-3 weeks** without missing the Freedom House submission window; missing that window is consequential but not a Phase 3 failure
5. **Domain G can slip to December** if Phase 2 slips — the midterm press freedom documentation value is reduced but the domain's long-run distribution value is unaffected
6. **Candidate 6 and Candidate 8 can slip to January** without losing their primary windows (election security legislation window and Congressional session window both extend through February 2027)
7. **Domain J can slip to March** without losing all 2027 state pilot program legislative sessions (some sessions run through April)
8. **Domain L must complete before Phase 3 mid-cycle review (March 2027)** — if slippage pushes Domain L past March, the review is delayed and Phase 4 planning is set back by a full quarter
