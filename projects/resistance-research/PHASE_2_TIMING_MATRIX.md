## Constraint Analysis & Bottleneck Prevention

### Can Domains 56 + 39 Distribute in Parallel?

**Short answer**: YES. No conflict.

**Detailed analysis**:

| Dimension | Domain 56 | Domain 39 | Conflict? | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Timing** | May 28 send (single day) | May 28–June 3 sends (staggered, 5 contacts) | NO | Stagger Domain 39 across 3 tiers; Day 1 is May 28 internal milestone (no public sends) |
| **Contact overlap** | 4 organizations (Volcker, DF, CREW, Gov Exec) | 5 organizations (Georgetown, NHeLP, Brennan, IRG, Black Mamas) | NO | Zero overlap; completely separate constituencies |
| **Resource (person-hours)** | 2 hours (30 min × 4 sends) | 2 hours (Tier 1); 1 hour (Tier 2); 0.5 hour (Tier 3) = 3.5 hours | NO | Total ~5.5 hours across May 28–June 3 is within normal capacity |
| **Constituency bandwidth** | Civil service reform (PfPS, Volcker, NAPA, AFGE, NTEU, PEER) | Healthcare/voting rights (health orgs, voting rights orgs) | NO | Completely different constituencies; no institutional conflicts |
| **Gist URLs** | Domain 56 Gist (1 URL) | Domain 39 Gist (1 URL) | NO | Two separate Gists; no technical conflict |
| **External triggers** | H.R. 492 June 1 advocacy window | HHS OBBBA June 1 rule | **Aligned timing** | Both have June 1 deadline; parallel execution is optimal |

**Conclusion**: Domains 56 and 39 can and should execute in parallel. The staggering of Domain 39 Tier 1 (May 30) vs. Domain 56 (May 28) provides breathing room for response monitoring, but there is zero operational conflict.
