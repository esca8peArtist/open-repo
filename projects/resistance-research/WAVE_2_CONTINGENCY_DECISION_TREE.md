## Checkpoint 1: Day 3 Assessment
### June 4, 2026

**What to measure:** How many of Domain 39's 5 Tier A contacts have replied to the June 1 email?

Replies count as: substantive email response, web form acknowledgment, phone call, social media mention, or public citation. A bounce-back auto-reply does not count.

---

### Branch 1A: Day 3 Response = 0 contacts

**Signal:** No Tier A contacts have replied within 3 days of the June 1 send.

**Assessment:** This is within the normal range — Tier A contacts at policy organizations often take 5–14 days to respond to cold outreach. Do not interpret as failure at Day 3.

**Wave 2 adjustment:** None. Proceed with default WAVE_2_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md.

**Domain 38 action:** Continue as planned — research content review June 5–10, Gist creation June 11–14, Tier A send June 15–20.

**Domain 40 action:** Continue as planned — Gist creation June 21–30, Tier A send July 15–20.

**Monitoring flag:** Set a calendar reminder for Day 7 (June 8) assessment. If Day 7 also shows 0 responses, escalate to Branch 2A protocol.

---

### Branch 1B: Day 3 Response = 1 contact

**Signal:** One Tier A contact has replied within 3 days.

**Assessment:** Within the expected range. A single quick response often indicates the document reached someone who was immediately engaged — good signal, not yet statistically meaningful.

**Wave 2 adjustment:** None. Proceed with default timeline.

**Action:** Respond to the engaged contact within 24 hours. If the response indicates interest in a briefing call or collaboration, prioritize scheduling that before the Domain 38 Tier A send (so you have a first success story to reference in Domain 38 outreach).

---

### Branch 1C: Day 3 Response = 2–5 contacts (healthy)

**Signal:** 2–5 Tier A contacts have replied within 3 days — this indicates substantive early engagement.

**Assessment:** This is the "healthy" baseline — better than expected for Day 3, but not exceptional. Domain 39 is working as intended.

**Wave 2 adjustment:** None. The default timeline is appropriate for a healthy response environment.

**Domain 38 action:** Proceed as planned. Domain 38 Tier A send June 15–20.

**Domain 40 action:** Consider whether any Domain 39 respondents have indicated interest in electoral/surveillance issues — if so, note them as potential Domain 40 warm contacts for the July 15 send (not new organizations, but existing Domain 39 respondents who expressed interest in Domain 40 themes).

**Action on engagement:** Respond to each engaged contact within 24 hours. If a Domain 39 respondent mentions AI or election protection as adjacent concerns, note their name for Domain 38/40 follow-up outreach in June/July.

---

### Branch 1D: Day 3 Response = more than 5 contacts

**Signal:** More than 5 of Domain 39's 5 Tier A contacts have replied within 3 days (this would require one or more contacts sharing the document and generating secondary inquiries from outside the original send list, or very rapid engagement from multiple contacts simultaneously).

**Assessment:** Exceptional early response. Domain 39 is generating organic distribution beyond the original 5 contacts.

**Wave 2 adjustment — escalation:**

1. **Domain 38 Tier B scope expansion:** Activate the full 25–30 contact Tier B list immediately (begin June 4–7 rather than waiting for June 21). The strong Domain 39 engagement signals that the broader AI governance audience is receptive.

2. **Domain 40 timing acceleration:** Consider whether to move Domain 40 Tier A send to June 15 (simultaneously with Domain 38 Tier A) rather than July 15. This is only appropriate if the Domain 39 response has identified organizations with active interest in electoral surveillance. See Branch 4D below for the full escalation protocol.

3. **Joint action coordination:** If 2+ Domain 39 respondents indicate they are interested in collaboration or joint distribution, facilitate an introduction between the responding organizations. This is the coalition-building goal — Domain 39 as the bridge document that connects healthcare and voting rights advocates.

- Current approach: "[Organization-specific hook] — for your [specific work area]"

**Do not cancel Wave 2.** A zero Domain 39 response at Day 7 tells you about Domain 39's specific contacts and framing — it does not predict Domain 38 or 40 response rates. AI governance professionals and election protection organizations are distinct audiences.

## Special Case: Strong Healthcare Coalition Interest (Day 7+ Assessment)

**Trigger:** Domain 39 responses on or before Day 7 indicate that two or more healthcare or disability rights organizations are interested in connecting their work to voting rights organizations.

**This is a distinct signal from volume.** The trigger here is not the number of responses but the content: do any respondents explicitly mention AI, election protection, or voting rights as connected to their healthcare work?

**If yes:**

- Flag these respondents as potential bridge contacts for both Domain 38 (AI governance) and Domain 40 (electoral manipulation)
- Move Domain 40 Tier A send to July 1 rather than July 15 — the healthcare-to-elections coalition signal means there is a warmer introduction context that will only be available in the July 1–15 window before the coalition energy dissipates
- In Domain 40 Tier A emails, reference the healthcare coalition: "Organizations working on Medicaid and voter registration connected this week — the companion surveillance analysis documents why their convergence matters for November 3"

This gives Domain 40 a narrative hook ("emerging coalition") rather than a cold-outreach context.

## Red Flag Protocol: Dual Weak Response

**Trigger:** Both Domain 38 and Domain 40 produce fewer than 1 Tier A response each by Day 14 of each respective send.

Domain 38 Day 14 = June 29 (14 days after June 15 send)
Domain 40 Day 14 = July 29 (14 days after July 15 send)

**Assessment:** If both domains produce <1 Tier A response each by their respective Day 14, the cold email outreach channel is not working for this audience combination.

**Actions:**

1. **Do not send more emails.** Additional cold email to the same audience segment is unlikely to produce different results. Stop Tier B and Tier C email sends until a channel assessment is complete.

2. **Shift to social and community channels.** Move budget from email to Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Substack distribution (Tier D). The August 2 EU enforcement date is the natural hook for organic social engagement — time all Tier D distribution to that date.

3. **Seek warm introductions.** Use the litigation tracker network, the existing Domain 42/56 contacts, and the Phase 1 distribution contacts to identify someone who has an existing relationship with at least one Tier A contact in Domain 38 or 40. A single warm introduction is worth 50 cold emails.

4. **Reconsider distribution framing.** Conduct a 30-minute analysis of whether the Gist document's opening section is compelling for a first-time reader who receives it as a cold email attachment. The leading finding must create immediate recognition of relevance. If it does not, revise the first 200 words of each domain document before any further distribution.

5. **Do not cancel.** The November 3 deadline is real. Weak cold-email response does not eliminate the need to get Domain 40's analysis in front of election protection organizations — it means the delivery channel needs to change. Proceed with social distribution regardless of email outcomes.
