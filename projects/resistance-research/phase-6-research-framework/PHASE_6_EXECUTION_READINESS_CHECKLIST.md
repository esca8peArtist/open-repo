# Phase 6 Execution Readiness Checklist

*Created: June 23, 2026*
*Launch target: November 15, 2026*
*Baseline: Phase 3 complete (Domain K / Domain H). This checklist assumes a new researcher can pick it up with zero prior briefing from the Phase 3 team.*

## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Each domain section specifies: (1) researcher expertise required to execute research independently, (2) data dependencies that must be satisfied before that domain's research starts, (3) success criteria for declaring a domain research-complete, and (4) contingency routing if the primary researcher is unavailable or a dependency cannot be satisfied. The critical path and timeline section (Section 6) aggregates all domains.

A researcher using this document should be able to answer "am I ready to start Domain X?" from Section 2–6 alone, without reading any other Phase 6 document.

## SECTION 2: DOMAIN B — ECONOMIC FOUNDATIONS

### 2.1 Researcher Expertise Required

**Primary researcher**: PhD in economics or political economy OR 10+ years in fiscal/economic policy research at CBO, EPI, Brookings, Peterson Institute, or equivalent. Must be able to: (a) evaluate distributional analyses (ITEP, TPC) critically, (b) locate and interpret IMF/OECD primary data, (c) connect economic structure variables to democratic health indicators (V-Dem integration required), (d) read PNAS-level empirical social science.

**If primary unavailable**: Economic journalist with 5+ years covering fiscal policy + 3+ years covering democratic politics (separate beats OK if same person), with peer-review literacy sufficient to evaluate methodology of PNAS Rau-Stokes paper. Must be supplemented by a PhD-level reviewer for the academic sources in B-1 and B-3.

**What will not substitute**: A purely macro economist without political science training will miss the democracy-inequality causal mechanism. A political scientist without fiscal policy quantitative literacy will misread CBO projections and distributional tables.

### 2.3 Success Criteria

### 6.1 Researcher Expertise Required

Must be able to: (a) read and apply transition economics literature (ScienceDirect sequencing papers), (b) apply V-Dem data to ROI analysis, (c) conduct scenario-based contingency analysis with specificity (not just "things could go wrong"), (d) synthesize inputs from Domains B–E without losing domain-specific accuracy.

**CRITICAL NOTE**: Domain F is the synthesis domain. It cannot be fully executed until Domains B–E are at least 70% complete. The recommended execution order is B/C/D/E in parallel (or as close to parallel as researchers allow), then F sequentially.

### 7.2 Recommended Execution Sequence

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–3): Parallel execution of Domains B, C, D, E**
- Domain D should start first within this phase because (a) it has the most Phase 3 overlap (enabling faster ramping), (b) Zone D-1 (courts as independent actors) provides analytical vocabulary used in Domains C-2, E-3, and F-5.
- Domain B and Domain C can begin simultaneously with Domain D.
- Domain E begins no later than Week 2 of Phase 1 to ensure E-1 (treaty architecture) does not bottleneck Domain F Zone F-2 (sequencing).

**Deconfliction checkpoint after Week 1**: All four domain researchers meet to confirm they are not duplicating material (specific risk: Domain C Zone C-2 and Domain D Zone D-2 both touch executive authority and constitutional interpretation; confirm scope boundaries).

**Phase 2 (Week 4): Domain F begins**
- Domain F Zone F-1 and Zone F-3 can begin once Domains B and C are 70% complete.
- Domain F Zone F-2 requires Domain D to be 80% complete.
- Domain F Zone F-5 requires all other F zones complete.

**Phase 3 (Week 5–launch): Review, integration notes, and confidence rating assignment**
- All domains complete first drafts.
- Cross-domain integration notes written (see "Integration notes" in each domain section of PHASE_6_RESEARCH_ZONES_MAPPING.md).
- Confidence ratings assigned per zone.
- Pre-launch verification: check all URLs against source index for link rot (some [Free] URLs will have moved between June 2026 and November 2026).

### 7.3 Critical Path

The critical path runs through:

1. **Trump v. Slaughter ruling** (expected June 30, 2026) → Domain C Zone C-2 and Domain D Zone D-1 cannot be finalized without this ruling. If the ruling is late or produces an unexpected holding, C-2 and D-1 require revision. Estimated revision: 4–6 hours per zone.

2. **Kansas merit selection amendment** (August 4, 2026) → Domain D Zone D-3 cannot include this result before August 4. Domain F Zone F-5 contingency analysis changes based on result. Estimated incorporation: 3–4 hours.

3. **WTO MC14 outcomes** (March 2026 — already past as of June 2026) → Domain E Zone E-3 and Domain B Zone B-6 depend on MC14 results. If results are inconclusive (high probability), research must characterize the impasse rather than deliverables. This is a known known: MC14 was scheduled and its outcomes (or lack thereof) are available now.

4. **Domains B–E 70% completion** → Domain F cannot begin productively until B–E are substantially complete. This is the most common single-point failure in multi-domain synthesis research. Protect the Week 4 Domain F start date by maintaining B–E progress throughout Weeks 1–3.

5. **V-Dem Democracy Report 2026** (released March 2026 — already available) → This is the primary quantitative anchor for multiple zones across all domains. Confirm the full PDF is downloaded and the US-specific indicator tables are extracted before any domain research begins.

### 7.4 Parallelization Opportunities

- Domain B (all zones) with Domain E (all zones): no overlapping sources, no conceptual dependency
- Domain C Zones 1–3 with Domain D Zones 1–3: different institutional focus (legislature/executive vs. courts)
- Domain C Zones 4–6 with Domain D Zones 4–5: electoral/party systems are independent from appellate structure

**Must be coordinated (overlapping territory):**
- Domain C Zone C-2 (executive authority) AND Domain D Zone D-2 (constitutional interpretation): both draw on Trump v. United States and Loper Bright; coordinate so they don't produce contradictory analyses of the same cases
