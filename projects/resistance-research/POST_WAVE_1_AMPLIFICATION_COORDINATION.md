## SECTION 5: Contingency Escalation

### 5.1 Reply Surge — High Volume Management (>80% Reply Rate)

**Scenario**: Four or five Batch 1 contacts reply Score 3+ within 48 hours. At 5 contacts, this is manageable in volume but requires a specific triage approach to prevent Batch 2 execution from being displaced by Batch 1 conversation.

**Protocol**:

1. **Do not delay Batch 2 execution to manage Batch 1 replies**. Reply to Batch 1 contacts within 24 hours of each reply — prioritize Elias and any contact who has asked a specific question or made a referral. But do not let Batch 1 correspondence consume the May 20–21 execution window.

2. **If 2+ Batch 1 contacts request calls**: schedule for May 22–24, after Batch 2 launches. Do not sacrifice the Batch 2 execution window for synchronous conversation.

3. **Triage by score**: Score 1 acknowledgments can wait 72 hours for a response. Score 3+ clarification questions need a 24-hour reply. Score 4+ referrals need same-day follow-up — reach the referred contact while momentum is live.

4. **The only scenario requiring a Batch 2 delay**: A Batch 1 contact raises a fundamental methodological or factual challenge (Score 3+ critique) that, if valid, affects the specific claims appearing verbatim in Batch 2 emails. In that case: hold Batch 2 for 24 hours, assess whether the challenge is addressable with existing evidence, then either (a) proceed unchanged, or (b) revise the relevant claim in Batch 2 emails before sending. Do not revise the entire campaign — revise only the specific claim that was challenged.

5. **Flag in CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input"** if two or more contacts raise substantive questions requiring research you have not yet completed.

### 5.2 Negative Sentiment — Replies Questioning Premises

**Scenario**: One or more Batch 1 contacts reply with Score 3+ critiques questioning framework premises. Most likely critique points by contact, based on their published work and organizational function:

- Goodman (Just Security): methodological critique of the 3.5% participation threshold — is it applicable to US conditions?
- Weiser (Brennan Center): pushback on the SAVE Act false positive rate extrapolation — are the four-state data points representative?
- Chenoweth (HKS): challenge to the reversibility argument — Poland vs. Hungary as comparators
- Bassin (Protect Democracy): "the litigation theory in Domain 29 conflicts with current standing doctrine"
- Elias (Democracy Docket): "the Callais framing mischaracterizes the April 29 ruling's actual holding"

**Fallback strategy for premise challenges**:

1. **Respond privately, not publicly**. Acknowledge the specific challenge and turn it toward collaboration: "You're right to push on this — how would you characterize the reversibility comparison?" A critique resolved in correspondence is a Score 4 signal in practice.

2. **Assess whether the critique affects Batch 2 sends**. If Elias questions the Callais framing and that framing appears verbatim in Batch 2 emails to similar litigation-focused contacts, revise it before sending. If the critique is about a domain section that Batch 2 emails reference only generally, proceed.

3. **Bipartisan re-emphasis for institutionalist pushback**: If critique comes from an institutionalist or proceduralist frame (Bassin questioning litigation theory, Weiser questioning empirical extrapolation), pivot relevant Batch 2 emails to emphasize the rule-of-law and institutional legitimacy arguments over the democratic reform arguments. Domain 2 (executive power constraints) and Domain 34 (impoundment) have the strongest bipartisan framing and are the lowest methodological risk.

4. **Sub-domain re-focus if a specific domain draws critique**: If Domain 37's SAVE Act methodology is challenged, shift Batch 2 away from leading with Domain 37 for contacts with similar institutional profiles. Lead instead with Domain 1 (gerrymandering, efficiency gap — more empirically settled) or Domain 56 (Schedule F — historical/institutional framing, less contested). Preserve the distribution campaign while the contested domain is refined.

5. **Language for critique reply**: "Thank you for engaging at this level of detail — your pushback on [specific point] is exactly the stress-testing I'm seeking before broader distribution. Would you be willing to share your preferred characterization of [specific claim]? I would rather revise the framing to your standards than distribute an analysis you have already identified as methodologically unsound."
