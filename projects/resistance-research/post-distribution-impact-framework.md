## I. Institutional Adoption Pathways — Sector Analysis

The resistance-research framework will diffuse through institutional networks differently depending on sector capabilities, constraints, and incentive structures. This section maps expected adoption patterns to enable targeted measurement.

### A. State Attorneys General (SAGs) — Highest-Leverage Institutional Anchor

**Adoption Pathway**:
- **Entry vector**: Litigation networks (National Association of Attorneys General, State AG litigation associations, peer referrals)
- **Activation timeline**: 2-4 weeks (existing NAAAG relationships, litigation coordination infrastructure)
- **Decision nodes**: (1) Domain 6 (Judicial Reform) litigation viability assessment, (2) Domain 34 (Fiscal Authority) standing determination, (3) Coalition formation with peer AGs
- **Expected metrics**:
  - Adoption: 8-12 state AGs (40% of litigation coalition = 20 AGs estimated, 40-60% adoption rate)
  - Adaptation pattern: Focus on Domains 6, 13, 34 (core institutional power)
  - Diffusion mechanism: Formal legal memos + litigation strategy integration
  - Timeline to impact: 60-120 days (amicus brief preparation, docket filing)

**Measurement approach**:
- **Citation tracking**: PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) — search filings mentioning framework or specific domain reasoning
- **Network tracking**: State AG coordinate-sharing networks (NAAAG conference attendance, email list participation)
- **Quantitative signal**: (1) Amicus briefs referencing framework, (2) AG coalition letters citing proposal sections, (3) State litigation strategy documents incorporating recommendations
- **Dashboard KPIs**:
  - Unique AGs adopting framework: count by quarter
  - Litigation filings referencing framework: PACER search monthly
  - Coalition formation: Track formal AG coordination + joint statements
  - Success threshold: ≥8 AGs citing framework in litigation documents by Day 120

### B. Law Schools & Legal Clinics — Content Anchors for Lasting Reach

**Adoption Pathway**:
- **Entry vector**: Law review networks, clinics (environmental, civil rights, government accountability), faculty networks
- **Activation timeline**: 4-8 weeks (course preparation, syllabus integration)
- **Decision nodes**: (1) Law review article acceptance (Domain 6, 13, 34 as articles), (2) Clinic case integration, (3) Curriculum committee approval

**Expected metrics**:
- Adoption: 25-35 law schools (≈8-12% of top 100, concentrated in T14 + public interest schools)
- Adaptation pattern: Deep integration into core courses (Constitutional Law, Admin Law, Civil Rights)
- Citation pattern: Academic citations = quick signal of legitimacy; policy citations = institutional integration

**Measurement approach**:
- **Academic citation tracking**: Google Scholar alerts for framework title, key domain concepts
- **Law review publication**: Monitor submissions to top journals (Harv Law Rev, Yale LJ, Colum L Rev, Mich L Rev)
- **Clinic case tracking**: Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests to environmental/civil rights clinic networks
- **Curriculum tracking**: Annual law school survey / phone interviews with faculty
- **Dashboard KPIs**:
  - Law review article acceptances: count by quarter
  - Schools integrating into curriculum: annual survey
  - Clinic cases leveraging framework: tracked via FOIA, annual reporting
  - Success threshold: ≥5 law review articles published, ≥15 schools integrating into courses by Day 240

### C. Think Tanks & Policy Institutes — Rapid Legitimacy Multipliers

**Adoption Pathway**:
- **Entry vector**: Policy networks (Center for American Progress, Aspen Institute, CFR, Urban Institute, Brookings), ideological silos (progressive via ACLU, conservative via Cato/Reason)
- **Activation timeline**: 2-6 weeks (executive director approval, publication plan)
- **Decision nodes**: (1) Report publication, (2) Conference presentation slots, (3) Media strategy integration

**Expected metrics**:
- Adoption: 8-15 think tanks (concentrated in top 30 by influence)
- Adaptation pattern: Selective domain adoption based on institutional focus (e.g., Cato emphasizes Domains 11/15 libertarian angles, CAP emphasizes Domains 6/13/34)
- Diffusion speed: Fastest of all sectors (2-week report turnaround possible)

**Measurement approach**:
- **Report publication tracking**: Monitor think tank policy/research pages for framework citations
- **Conference circuit**: Track speaker bios, presentation slides at major conferences (Aspen Ideas, ABA Annual Meeting, Law & Economics symposia)
- **Media placement**: Count policy op-eds citing framework (LexisNexis policy database)
- **Network partnerships**: Formal collaboration agreements or co-published reports
- **Dashboard KPIs**:
  - Think tank publications citing framework: monthly count by institute
  - Conference presentations: quarterly count
  - Op-ed/media placements: monthly
  - Success threshold: ≥8 think tank publications, ≥20 conference presentations by Day 180

### D. Advocacy Organizations & Issue Networks — Grassroots Amplification

**Adoption Pathway**:
- **Entry vector**: Civil rights (ACLU, NAACP, Lawyers' Committee), government accountability (CREW, Project On Government Oversight), environmental (NRDC, EarthJustice), labor (AFL-CIO), voting rights (Common Cause, Protect Democracy)
- **Activation timeline**: 4-12 weeks (organizational alignment, leadership approval, member communication)
- **Decision nodes**: (1) Framework compatibility with organizational mission, (2) Resource commitment (research, lobbying, grassroots), (3) Coalition building with peer organizations

**Expected metrics**:
- Adoption: 15-25 national organizations (concentrated in voting rights, government accountability, civil rights)
- Adaptation pattern: Organizational "forking" (ACLU emphasizes Domains 1, 6, 11; Protect Democracy emphasizes Domains 19, 37; EarthJustice emphasizes Domains 21, 31)
- Diffusion mechanism: Member briefings, advocacy alerts, legislative testimony

**Measurement approach**:
- **Member communication tracking**: Subscribe to organization email lists, monitor newsletter references
- **Legislative testimony database**: Monitor congressional testimony databases (Congress.gov)
- **Grassroots campaign tracking**: Social media mentions, petition campaigns, member mobilization metrics
- **Litigation docket tracking**: Cases filed by advocacy organizations (Pacer search)
- **Dashboard KPIs**:
  - Advocacy organizations adopting framework: count by mission area
  - Grassroots campaign mentions: quarterly social media/newsletter scan
  - Legislative testimony citations: annual count
  - Coalition formation: Track formal advocacy alliances
  - Success threshold: ≥15 organizations integrating into campaigns, ≥25 legislative testimonies citing framework by Day 365

### E. Media & Academic Researchers — Legitimacy & Longevity

**Adoption Pathway**:
- **Entry vector**: Journalists covering government accountability, political analysis, constitutional law (ProPublica, Washington Monthly, The Atlantic, NPR, academic researchers in political science, law, economics)

**Measurement approach**:

- **Academic citation tracking**: Google Scholar, SSRN (for working papers)
- **Journalist contacts**: Direct relationship tracking (emails, calls, interviews)
- **Podcast/multimedia**: Identify major policy podcasts citing framework
- **Dashboard KPIs**:
  - Media mentions (news articles): monthly count
  - Academic citations: quarterly count
  - Podcast appearances: quarterly count
  - Success threshold: ≥50 news articles, ≥30 academic citations by Day 365

## II. Impact Metrics Framework — Data Sources & Collection

### A. Citation Tracking Stack

**Primary tools**:
1. **Google Scholar** (free, academic focus)
   - Alerts: Framework title, key phrase combinations
   - Limitations: Lag time (1-2 months), no quantitative filtering
   - Monthly manual review of new citations

2. **LexisNexis / Westlaw** (paid, legal focus)
   - Law review articles + litigation databases
   - Better for state AG tracking (PACER backup)
   - Cost: ~$500/month (institutional access if applicable)

3. **PACER** (free, litigation focus)
   - Federal court filings search by keyword
   - Search: "(democratic renewal OR institutional resilience OR Domains 1-37) AND (proposal OR framework)"
   - Quarterly manual review (monthly if high-volume adoption detected)

4. **Media databases**:
   - Google News alerts (free)
   - ProPublica (free)
   - Nexis Uni (paid, $500+/month) for comprehensive news + legal + policy

**Collection workflow**:
- Weekly: Google Scholar + Google News alerts (automated email)
- Biweekly: Manual review of alert results + quantification
- Monthly: PACER search (target: litigation citations)
- Quarterly: LexisNexis search (law review comprehensive)

### B. Adoption Survey Framework

**Institutional surveys** (quarterly):
- 5-minute survey to random sample of SAGs, law schools, think tanks, advocacy organizations
- Questions: (1) Framework awareness (yes/no), (2) Current usage (not using / citing / integrated / dependent), (3) Adaptation level (adopted as-is / significantly modified), (4) Impact felt (no impact / starting to see impact / documented impact), (5) Likelihood of continued use (1-5 scale)
- Target: 30-40 respondents per quarter (mix of sectors)
- Cost: ~$1,500-$2,500/quarter (survey platform + analysis)

**Incentives**: Small gift card or charitable donation in respondent's name to increase response rate (typical 25-30% for policy surveys).

### C. Diffusion Network Tracking

**Node categories**:
- Individual adopters (researchers, lawyers, advocates)
- Organizational adopters (law firms, nonprofits, government agencies)
- Network hubs (conferences, coalition meetings, professional associations)

**Tracking mechanism**:
- Manual relationship mapping: Maintain spreadsheet of "known adopters" + their introductions/referrals
- Quarterly relationship audits: Contact sample adopters to identify secondary spreads
- Network analysis: Use free tools (Palladio, Kumu.io) to visualize adoption network structure
- Pattern identification: Which organizations/individuals drive most adoptions?

**Success pattern indicators**:
- Adoption cascades: Person A cites framework → mentions to Person B → Person B independently adopts (indicates organic diffusion, not forced)
- Sector clustering: Geographic or ideological clusters of adoptions (indicates network concentration)
- Cross-sector bridges: Adoption spreading from one sector to another via key connectors

## III. Success Patterns & Adaptation Taxonomy

### A. Domain-Level Adaptation Patterns

**Hypothesis**: Different domains will be adopted/adapted differently based on institutional alignment.

**Expected patterns**:

| Domain | Likeliest Adopters | Adaptation Pattern | Success Metric |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Domains 1, 6, 13 | Law schools, SAGs | Cited in litigation | Litigation filings |
| Domain 25, 37 | Advocacy, media | Used in campaigns | Media mentions, legislation |
| Domains 19, 28 | Think tanks, academics | Analyzed/critiqued | Academic citations |
| Domains 23, 31 | Sector-specific orgs | Adapted for sector | Organizational adaptations |
| Domains 27-29 | Policy experts | Detailed analysis | Policy briefs |

**Adaptation modes** (mapped to institutional capacity):
1. **Citation-only**: Mention in bibliography or footnote (easiest adoption, lowest commitment)
2. **Case study integration**: Use framework as analytical lens for specific case
3. **Organizational integration**: Formal adoption into organizational strategy/messaging
4. **Implementation**: Framework principles guide actual policy/advocacy work
5. **Remixing**: Significant modification to match organizational mission/constraints

**Dashboard tracking**:
- Pie chart of adoption modes by quarter (citation-only : case study : integrated : implementation : remixed)
- Trend over time: Do early adopters stay at citation-only, or deepen to implementation?
- Sector breakdown: Which sectors move to implementation fastest?

### B. Failure Patterns & Misadaptation Signals

**Risk zones** (misinterpretation patterns to detect early):

1. **Politicization beyond design intent**
   - Signal: Framework cited primarily as Democratic talking point (vs. institutional reform)
   - Detector: Count mentions in partisan context vs. nonpartisan policy context
   - Recovery: Issue clarifying brief reemphasizing institutional vs. partisan framing

2. **Domain cherry-picking without systems view**
   - Signal: Only Domains 1, 19, 37 cited (crisis domains), Domains 6-13 (structural reform) ignored
   - Detector: Monthly citation pattern analysis (if >70% citations to <30% of domains)
   - Recovery: Targeted outreach highlighting systems integration value

3. **Oversimplification / Textbook reductions**
   - Signal: Framework reduced to 5-point summary (vs. nuanced structural analysis)
   - Detector: Compare academic citations to source length (if mostly citing secondary summaries)
   - Recovery: Original proposal availability + invitation to deeper engagement

4. **Organizational co-optation / Scope creep**
   - Signal: Organization claims framework as their own, modifies without acknowledgment
   - Detector: Detect via relationship audits + network tracking
   - Recovery: Public attribution + collaborative relationship building (avoid antagonism)

## IV. Dashboard Architecture & KPI Specification

### A. Real-Time Dashboard Components

**Component 1: Adoption Heatmap** (updated weekly)
- X-axis: Sectors (SAGs, law schools, think tanks, advocacy, media/academic)
- Y-axis: Time (weeks since distribution launch)
- Color intensity: Cumulative adoption count per sector
- Value: Visual trend identification (which sectors adopt fastest?)

**Component 2: Citation Velocity** (updated weekly)
- Line graph: Cumulative citation count over time
- Three lines: Total citations, policy/litigation citations, academic citations
- Target line: Extrapolated trajectory needed to hit success threshold by Day 365
- Value: Early indication of under/over-performing diffusion

**Component 3: Adaptation Mode Distribution** (updated monthly)
- Pie chart: Citation-only vs. Case study vs. Integrated vs. Implementation vs. Remixed
- Trend line: % moving to Implementation over time
- Value: Indicator of adoption depth (citations = shallow, implementation = deep)

**Component 4: Domain Popularity Index** (updated monthly)
- Bar chart: Citation count per domain (Domains 1-37 ranked by citations)
- Highlight: Which domains driving adoption? Which under-cited?
- Value: Identify adoption imbalances, target outreach accordingly

**Component 5: Sector Growth Trajectory** (updated monthly)
- Line chart: Cumulative organizational adoptions per sector
- Benchmark lines: Target adoption for each sector by quarter
- Value: Identify sectors under-performing, redirect outreach effort

**Component 6: Key Incidents & Anomalies** (updated as discovered)
- Log: Significant adoption milestones (e.g., "Framework cited in Senate testimony"), misadaptations detected, partnership opportunities
- Value: Narrative context for quantitative metrics

## V. Failure Mode Detection & Course Correction

### A. Early Warning Indicators

**Indicator 1: Adoption Plateau by Week 8**
- Signal: Citations/week flatten (e.g., 20/week for 4 weeks, then 5/week)
- Likely cause: Initial adopters exhausted, organic diffusion insufficient
- Course correction: (1) Targeted outreach to underperforming sectors, (2) High-profile endorsement campaign, (3) "Derivative work" initiative (solicit remixes/adaptations)

**Indicator 2: Sector Imbalance by Week 12**
- Signal: ≥60% of citations from single sector (e.g., law schools dominate, SAGs minimal)
- Likely cause: Diffusion network skewed toward initial referral pathway
- Course correction: (1) Cross-sector outreach sprint, (2) Identify "bridges" between sectors, (3) Targeted amplification in lagging sectors

**Indicator 3: Domain Concentration Bias by Week 16**
- Signal: ≥80% citations to <40% of domains (e.g., Domains 1, 19, 37 cited 80% of time)
- Likely cause: Crisis domains (immediate threats) cited, structural reform domains (long-term solutions) ignored
- Course correction: (1) Highlight systems integration value, (2) Release targeted briefs on under-cited domains, (3) Outreach emphasizing systems approach

**Indicator 4: Stalled Adaptation Depth by Week 20**
- Signal: ≥70% of adoptions remain at "citation-only" or "case study" modes, <5% in "implementation"
- Likely cause: Framework seen as academic/theoretical rather than actionable
- Course correction: (1) Create implementation playbooks for top domains, (2) Highlight real-world case studies, (3) Training webinars for institutional adoption

### B. Recovery Protocols

**Protocol A: High-Profile Amplification**
- Action: Secure major media placement (op-ed in NYT, WSJ, or major policy outlet)
- Target: Reach policy/media elites who influence downstream adoption
- Timeline: Execute within 2 weeks of detection
- Expected impact: 20-30% temporary boost in citation velocity

**Protocol B: Derivative Work Initiative**
- Action: Invite researchers/institutions to create "branded" versions adapted to specific sectors
- Examples: "Framework for State AGs", "Framework for Law Schools", "Framework for Labor Organizations"
- Timeline: Launch by Week 24
- Expected impact: Moves adoption from "citation" to "integrated" modes

**Protocol C: Cross-Sector Bridge Building**
- Action: Identify institutions that span multiple sectors (e.g., large law firms + advocacy arms)
- Strategy: Activate bridges to introduce framework to adjacent sectors
- Timeline: Ongoing, prioritize underperforming sectors
- Expected impact: Sector imbalance correction
