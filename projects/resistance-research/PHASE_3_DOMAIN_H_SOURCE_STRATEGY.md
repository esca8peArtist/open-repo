## LEAD FINDING

Domain H research is production-complete as of June 6, 2026 (7,500 words, 90+ citations, four zones). This source strategy is NOT a from-scratch plan. It is a pre-sprint infrastructure document that performs three specific functions the existing files do not: (1) consolidates 50+ named expert contacts into a single outreach-ready database with email addresses, activation timing, and hit-rate projections; (2) specifies the access strategy for primary legal sources (PACER, Google Scholar, institutional repositories) the research sprint will need; (3) maps the 50+ law review articles and SCOTUS opinions that constitute the literature base, cross-referencing where Phase 2 research already covered them.

The key planning decision this document formalizes: expert contact outreach begins July 15, 2026, not November 2026. Beginning outreach 4+ months early — while Phase 2 distribution is still running — de-risks the November 4 launch by ensuring interview slots are secured before expert availability locks up for the fall semester and the election-season advocacy calendar. Phase 2 research already mapped the landscape. The work now is logistics, not discovery.

## PART 1: EXPERT CONTACT MAP — 50+ CONTACTS

### Contact Architecture Overview

The 50+ contacts are organized into three tiers. Tier designations reflect the role each contact plays in the research process — this is distinct from the distribution tiers in DOMAIN_H_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md (which covers post-November 4 advocacy distribution). Here, Tier 1 = primary interview subjects; Tier 2 = secondary sources and accuracy verifiers; Tier 3 = institutional liaisons for coalition coordination.

Outreach hit rate projection by tier, based on Phase 2 actuals (Batch 1: 23% response rate from cold outreach; Batch 1 warm contacts: 71% response rate):
- Tier 1 (institutional warm contacts): 60-75% expected response rate, 2-3 week response time
- Tier 2 (academic cold outreach with specific ask): 20-30% expected response rate, 3-4 week response time
- Tier 3 (organizational institutional outreach): 40-55% expected response rate, 1-2 week response time

Total target interviews: 25-30. At 20-30% average across all tiers, 100 outreach emails to 50+ contacts should yield 25-30 confirmed interviews by Week 8 (August 22).

Access protocol:

2. Search case names in PACER Case Locator (free search function)
3. Download opinions, amicus briefs, and district court records as needed
4. For cases already decided: SCOTUSblog (free) and Justia (free) provide full text of SCOTUS opinions without PACER

Specific PACER research tasks for pre-sprint:
- Trump v. Slaughter: Download the January 2026 oral argument transcript and all amicus briefs (30+ filed); the amicus briefs are the primary scholarly intervention points and often contain the most analytically useful constitutional theory

### Google Scholar — Free law review access

Google Scholar (scholar.google.com, filter: Articles, Legal Documents) provides free full-text access to most law review articles published before 2023 and many through 2025. Search protocol:
1. Author + title keyword for known articles
2. Zone-specific search: "constitutional resilience" + "United States" + 2024-2026 returns new scholarship not in the current 110-source database
3. Cited-by function: search citing articles for Huq & Ginsburg (2018) and Roznai (2017) to identify the most current scholarship building on these foundational sources

### Institutional Repositories (HeinOnline, JSTOR, SSRN)

HeinOnline access: Available free through most public law library systems (law.indiana.edu, law.uchicago.edu — alumni access programs). Contains the full law review archive back to first issue. Essential for the 50+ law review article literature scan.

SSRN (ssrn.com): Free preprint access to most contemporary constitutional law scholarship. All five of the must-read books (Huq-Ginsburg, Strauss, Tushnet, Levinson, Balkin) have SSRN-hosted preprint chapters.

HathiTrust Digital Library: Free access to pre-2000 books including the foundational constitutional theory texts.
