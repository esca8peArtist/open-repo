## Indivisible Chapters

### Indivisible Houston (Citywide Hub)
- **Website**: indivisiblehouston.org
- **ActionNetwork**: actionnetwork.org/groups/indivisible-houston
- **Instagram**: @indivisible_houston | **Facebook**: facebook.com/indivisiblehouston
- **Covers**: All of Harris County — TX-2, 7, 8, 9, 10, 18, 22, 29, 36
- **Status**: Highly active. Organized "Hands Off! Houston Fights Back" (April 2025) and "NO KINGS Houston." Running blockwalks, phonebanks, town halls, and candidate endorsements (Indivisible Houston Slate).
- **Best entry point**: ActionNetwork signup; contact via website form

### Indivisible TX-02 (Kingwood / North Houston)
- **Website**: indivisibletx02.com
- **Covers**: TX-02 — Kingwood, northeast Harris County. Dan Crenshaw's district.
- **Leader**: Cindi Hendrickson
- **Status**: Active. Protest at The Woodlands Country Club (April 2025), voter registration drives, advocacy training.

### West Houston Indivisible
- **Mobilize**: mobilize.us/indivisible/event/798379
- **Covers**: West Houston / Katy corridor
- **Status**: Formative/growing — weekly Zoom meetings while building local presence

### Indivisible Fort Bend County / No Surrender Coalition
- **Website**: nosurrendercoalition.org
- **Mobilize**: mobilize.us/indivisiblefortbendcounty
- **Covers**: Fort Bend County — Sugar Land, Missouri City, Richmond, Rosenberg, Stafford
- **Meets**: 2nd Wednesday monthly, rotating locations
- **Status**: Active. "NO KINGS FORT BEND COUNTY" event, Emergency Town Hall on redistricting, Labor Day march.
- **Note**: Fort Bend has a large South Asian and Latino immigrant population — high relevance for know-your-rights distribution

## Immigration Legal Services (Know-Your-Rights Distribution Partners)

### Houston Immigration Legal Services Collaborative (HILSC)
- **Website**: houstonimmigration.org
- **Hotline**: 833-468-4664 (M–F 9am–5pm)
- **Active KYR page**: houstonimmigration.org/know-your-rights-feb-2025/
- **What they are**: Umbrella of 50+ legal service providers; runs the Immigrant Resource Hotline
- **Why they matter**: Already have KYR infrastructure — our guide could complement or be co-branded with theirs

### RAICES Texas – Houston Office
- **Address**: 1900 St. James Pl #450, Houston TX 77056
- **Phone**: (833) 372-4237
- **Website**: raicestexas.org
- **What they are**: Largest immigration legal services agency in Texas; removal defense, asylum, DACA

### Galveston-Houston Immigrant Representation Project (GHIRP)
- **Website**: ghirp.org
- **What they are**: Free citizenship services and Know Your Rights presentations; covers Galveston and Harris Counties
- **High distribution potential**: Already runs KYR presentations — direct partner opportunity

### Justice for All Immigrants
- **Phone**: (713) 454-6470
- **Website**: justiceforallimmigrants.org
- **What they are**: Asylum, DACA, TPS, deportation defense, Know Your Rights

### City of Houston Office of New Americans
- **Website**: houstontx.gov/na
- **What they are**: Citizenship, language access, referrals — city-funded, accessible entry point

## Advocacy Coalitions (Priority Partners)

### Houston Leads ⭐ (Highest Priority)
- **Website**: houston-leads.org
- **Facebook**: HoustonLeads713
- **What they are**: Coalition of 15+ orgs committed to immigrant protection in Houston area
- **Core members**: ACLU of Texas, HILSC, ILRC, Mi Familia Vota, SEIU Texas, Tahirih Justice Center, United We Dream
- **Recent wins**: Secured $2M+ from Harris County Commissioners Court for immigrant legal services
- **Why they matter**: Single most important coalition for immigration-specific material distribution — 15+ orgs already coordinating, existing hotline infrastructure

### Texas Organizing Project (TOP)
- **Website**: organizetexas.org
- **What they are**: Statewide, Houston-anchored Black and Latino community organizing, electoral and immigration work
- **Note**: Hired an Immigration Justice Organizer for Harris County in 2025

### Houston DSA – Immigration Justice Campaign
- **Website**: houstondsa.org/campaigns/immigration-justice-campaign
- **What they are**: Running city council pressure campaign against Houston-ICE cooperation; joined Houston Leads coalition

### Mi Familia Vota
- **Website**: mifamiliaenaccion.org/texas
- **What they are**: Voter registration, citizenship drives, Know Your Rights sessions for Latino community; HILSC partner

### Workers Defense Project
- **What they are**: Community organizing for low-wage immigrant construction workers — high-risk population for ICE encounters

## Strategic Priority Order for Outreach

1. **Indivisible Houston** — largest, most organized, covers all of Harris County. Start here with call scripts and KYR guide.
2. **Houston Leads** — 15+ immigration orgs already coordinating. Single email reaches the whole coalition.
3. **HILSC** — already has KYR infrastructure; our guide complements and could be co-branded.
4. **Indivisible Fort Bend County** — independently active, high immigrant population, ready to use materials.
5. **GHIRP** — already runs KYR presentations; our guide is directly usable in their existing programming.
