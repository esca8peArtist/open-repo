## Most Important Finding

**Three domains have truly immovable external deadlines that no slip scenario can recover from: Domain 49 redistricting advocacy (filing windows already ongoing, June-July 2026), Domain 58 Trump v. Barbara ruling trigger (late June/early July, uncontrollable), and Domain 54 youth civic organization integration (August 1, after which early-vote periods open and organizational bandwidth locks in). Every other Phase 2 domain has a hard anchor date that damages but does not eliminate advocacy value if missed by up to 8 weeks.**

The August 31 overall Phase 2 completion deadline is not itself a single external event — it is a sequencing constraint imposed by Phase 3's January 3, 2027 Congress seating window. Specifically: Domains H (Constitutional Architecture) and K (Judicial Reform) must be research-complete and in Tier 1 distribution before January 3, 2027. Working backward at Phase 2 actuals pace (28.5 hours/domain average, minimum 4 weeks per domain for production + peer review), Phase 2 must close by October 31, 2026 at the absolute latest for Phase 3 Domains H and K to hit January 3. August 31 is the planned close; October 31 is the hard backstop.

## Per-Domain Deadline Audit

### Domain 48 — Criminal Justice, Civic Exclusion Architecture, and Democratic Participation Crisis

**Research status (June 10, 2026)**: Production-ready per PHASE_2_BATCH_2_ACTIVATION_ROADMAP.md. Route A confirmed. Distribution execution window: June 16–20 per DOMAIN_48_TIMING_AND_RESOURCE_COORDINATION.md.

**Primary external window — Virginia Voting Rights Restoration Amendment (HJR 2 / SJ 248)**:

The Virginia General Assembly placed the constitutional amendment on the November 3, 2026 ballot (House 65–33 January 14; Senate 21–18 January 16). The amendment would restore voting rights immediately upon release from incarceration, replacing the current life-disenfranchisement-until-governor-restores rule. The Virginia Right to Vote Coalition is building ballot campaign infrastructure now. Coalition organizational integration deadline: **July 15, 2026**. This is the date by which voter education materials for the campaign must lock in their research resources.

King v. O'Bannon parallel track: U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia issued a permanent injunction January 22, 2026, effective May 1, 2026, protecting non-common-law-felony offenders from disenfranchisement under the 1870 Readmission Act. The injunction is in effect as of June 10 and pending Fourth Circuit review. Domain 48 provides the legal architecture analysis supporting this litigation track regardless of ballot measure outcome.

**Secondary external window — Alabama SB 24**: Effective October 1, 2026. Voter education and outreach window for Alabama contacts: September 2026.

**Is the July 15 deadline immovable?** The ballot campaign materials lock date is a coalition decision, not a statutory requirement. It could shift to July 30 or August 1 if the Virginia Right to Vote Coalition announces an extension. However, DOMAIN_48_TIMING_AND_RESOURCE_COORDINATION.md treats July 15 as the planning anchor because no extension can be assumed in advance. If July 15 slips, the fallback is: distribute to litigation organizations (Brennan Center, NAACP LDF) by July 31 and to voter education organizations by August 15 — still usable for ballot campaign advocacy before early-vote opens in September.

**Prerequisite domain relationships**: Domain 48 does not require any other Phase 2 domain to complete first. It cross-references Domains 22, 29, and 42 but those are Phase 1 complete documents. No blocking dependency.

**Minimum completion date to avoid Phase 3 cascade**: Domain 48 distribution by August 15 does not affect Phase 3 (Domain H or K). It affects only the Virginia ballot campaign and pre-midterm criminal justice advocacy ecosystem. Phase 3 is not dependent on Domain 48 completion.

### Domain 49 — Louisiana v. Callais / VRA Redistricting Cascade

**Research status (June 10, 2026)**: Research complete at 6,071 words (May 13, 2026). Expansion to 8,000+ words, contact list, and templates recommended per PHASE_2_BATCH_2_ACTIVATION_ROADMAP.md. Distribution already initiated.

**Primary external window — redistricting filing deadlines**: The advocacy window for Domain 49 is not a single date but an ongoing cascade. Louisiana Governor Landry signed the new congressional map approximately June 1, 2026, eliminating one majority-Black district. Tennessee, Alabama, South Carolina redistricting proceeding under Callais standard. Primary filing windows for 2026 elections are closing in June–July 2026 in most affected states. After maps finalize and filing windows close, Domain 49 transitions from "emergency litigation prevention" framing to "ongoing litigation support + voter education" framing — still valuable but with reduced advocacy leverage.

The analysis cannot substitute for actual litigation — but organizations drafting emergency briefs need the constitutional framework (Arlington Heights intentional discrimination theory, Section 3 bail-in remedy) before briefs are filed, not after.

**Key external organizations with hard integration timelines**:
- Democracy Docket: daily litigation operations; integration turnaround 24–48 hours
- NAACP LDF: Callais is their case; briefing active regardless of external research
- Campaign Legal Center (Blair Bowie, Restore Your Vote): redistricting and disenfranchisement intersection
- MALDEF: 2026 cycle Latino voting rights litigation
- Brennan Center redistricting team (Michael Li, Senior Counsel)

**Prerequisite domain relationships**: Domain 49 does not require any other Phase 2 domain to complete first. The existing research file in `domains/domain-49-callais-vra-redistricting-emergency.md` is the base document; expansion work is additive.

**Minimum completion date to avoid Phase 3 cascade**: Domain 49 expanded distribution by July 1 covers the primary litigation window. Domain 49 also has a secondary Phase 3 obligation: post-election Callais redistricting cascade analysis (results-dependent, November 4 start, December 20 completion target per PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md). That Phase 3 work is the analytical update, not a re-production of the Phase 2 document. Phase 2 completion does not gate Phase 3 for Domain 49 specifically.

## Critical-Path Analysis: Which Domains MUST Complete by August 31 to Avoid Phase 3 Cascade

The August 31 completion date is a planning target, not a hard external deadline. The hard external deadline is Phase 3's January 3, 2027 Congress seating window. The question is: what is the minimum Phase 2 completion date that still allows Phase 3 Domains H and K to reach Tier 1 distribution before January 3?

### Minimum Phase 3 Backward Date Calculation

Working backward from January 3, 2027:
- Domain K (Judiciary Reform): 16–20 hours research + 2 weeks production + 2 weeks distribution staging = approximately 6 weeks from research start to Tier 1 distribution
- Domain H (Constitutional Architecture): 18–22 hours research + 2 weeks production + 1 week distribution staging = approximately 5–6 weeks from research start to Tier 1 distribution
- Both must be in distribution before January 3 → research must start by November 18, 2026 at the latest

Phase 3 research cannot start until Phase 2 carryover obligations are cleared. Per PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md, Phase 3 Week 1 (November 4–11) includes Phase 2 carryover audit. If Phase 2 still has active research obligations on November 4, those obligations consume November research capacity before Domain K or H can begin.

**Minimum Phase 2 completion date to protect January 3 window**: October 31, 2026.

**Domain G (Press Freedom)** is the single Phase 2 obligation with the most direct Phase 3 blocking effect. Per PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md Scenario B note: "Domain G (press freedom) must close before Phase 3 can formally announce the knowledge commons initiative." Domain G is not in the seven Phase 2 domains listed in the task brief, but it is a Phase 2 carryover risk that blocks the Phase 3 announcement email if not complete.

### Cascade Failure Scenario

If Phase 2 does not complete by October 31:
- Phase 3 Week 1 (November 4–11) is consumed by carryover obligations
- Domain K research cannot start until mid-November at the earliest
- Domain K requires 6 weeks to reach Tier 1 distribution → distribution target shifts to late December 2026
- Domain H trails Domain K by 1–2 weeks → January distribution at best
- January 3 Congress seating window closes before Domain H is distributed
- Committee assignments finalize January 17 — the one-time blank-slate window for shaping incoming member priorities is lost for Domains H and K

This cascade is the primary justification for the August 31 Phase 2 completion target. The cascade can be partially mitigated under Scenario C (emergency minimum document protocol: 1,500–2,000 words each for H and K before January 3) but emergency minimum documents have materially lower advocacy leverage than full production documents.
