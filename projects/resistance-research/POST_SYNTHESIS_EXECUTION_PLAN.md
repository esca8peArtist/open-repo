## Section 3: Trump v. Barbara Decision Tree

Independent of synthesis outcome. Ruling expected late June to early July 2026.

### If ruling issues BEFORE June 15 (pre-distribution prep window)

This is the medium-probability scenario if SCOTUS accelerates its June opinion schedule.

**Autonomous tasks (Hours 0–4 after ruling)**:
1. Check SCOTUSblog scotusblog.com/cases/trump-v-barbara for first-read summary
2. Identify scenario: constitutional holding (broad Wong Kim Ark reaffirmation), statutory holding (avoids constitutional question), or any EO-uphold / adverse tribal language
3. Open DOMAIN_58_DISTRIBUTION_BRIDGE.md Window 1 — apply the matching scenario protocol
4. Add 2–3 sentence ruling alert at the top of `domains/domain-58-tribal-sovereignty.md` (ruling date, holding summary, tribal implication in one sentence)
5. Commit the updated canonical file to master

**Hours 4–8**: Send Tier 1 distribution emails (NARF, NCAI, Native News Online, Indian Country Today, Indianz.com, Four Directions, Western Native Voice) using Playbook 3 template from DOMAIN_58_DISTRIBUTION_BRIDGE.md

---

### If ruling issues AFTER June 15 (standard two-wave sequence)

This is the expected scenario.

**Pre-ruling (June 15)**: Domain 58 Wave 1 distribution proceeds with forward-looking framing. Organizations receive the document before the ruling, with Section 9 (rapid-response protocol) clearly flagged in cover email.

**Upon ruling**: Execute the Hour 0–24 protocol from TRUMP_V_BARBARA_CASE_RESEARCH.md Section 6. Update Domain 58 Section 3 (Trump v. Barbara citizenship threat) and Section 9 (actionable intelligence) with the holding. Send update notification to all Wave 1 contacts. Commit updated canonical file to master.

**Ruling outcome sub-branches**:

- **Constitutional holding (broad Wong Kim Ark reaffirmation)**: Best-case outcome. Distribution frame: constitutional anchor for tribal citizenship is settled for a generation. Section 9 update: note the constitutional holding and what it forecloses for future statutory maneuvers. Send Tier 2 contacts (Brennan Center, Campaign Legal Center, NAACP LDF, ACLU Voting Rights) within 72 hours.

- **Statutory holding (avoids constitutional question)**: Favorable but leaves vulnerability. Distribution frame: Congress must now pass Indian Citizenship Act reaffirmation amendment to close the gap. Add one-paragraph Indian Citizenship Act reaffirmation request to Domain 58 cover emails sent to Senate Indian Affairs Committee. Send ASU Indian Legal Program (Robert Miller) as part of Tier 2 academic distribution.

- **Adverse ruling / EO upheld in part**: Highest-urgency scenario. Activate NCAI emergency resolution framework; coordinate with NARF on tribal client documentation burden; issue call for Senate Indian Affairs Committee emergency hearing. Domain 58 becomes a crisis-response document. Use Montana SB 490 (May 11, 2026 injunction) as the "here is what works now" anchor frame in all distribution.
