## Domain Descriptions (Reference for Versions A and B)

Use these descriptions verbatim in Version A (reviewer) or Version B (institutional partner). Each description is approximately 100 words.

**Domain 56: Civil Service Politicization and Nonpartisan Governance Architecture**
6,267 words — 45 citations — Available now; distribute May 26 – June 7

Schedule Policy/Career reclassification is not an employment dispute. It is the conversion of the merit-based civil service — a democratic infrastructure system designed in 1883 to end patronage corruption — into a loyalty instrument. Domain 56 documents five causal pathways through which civil service capture converts into democratic governance failure: partisan employee screening destroying institutional memory, MSPB appeal removal eliminating information-disclosure functions, enforcement agency hollowing stripping NLRB, EPA, and DOJ Civil Rights Division capacity, and whistleblower protection dismantlement. The international precedent documentation (Hungary: 66 political appointments per year post-2010 versus 49 prior; Poland's elite replacement strategy) provides the predictive model for US trajectory at scale.

**Distribution target**: June 2026, aligned with H.R. 492 / Saving the Civil Service Act legislative window.

---

**Domain 57: Multilateral Withdrawal and US Commitment Collapse**
9,201 words — 51 citations — Available August 10, 2026

The January 7, 2026 withdrawal from 66 international organizations is not foreign policy adjustment. It is the removal of domestic accountability infrastructure. International institutions serve three domestic accountability functions that disappear upon withdrawal: electoral benchmarking (OSCE/ODIHR and International IDEA data that domestic civil society uses to document suppression), treaty compliance obligations enforceable in US courts, and reputational cost imposition from peer democracies. Domain 57 documents five causal pathways through which multilateral withdrawal converts foreign policy choices into domestic democratic dismantlement — regulatory capture acceleration, climate migration pressure, resource conflict escalation, institutional decay, and ally democratic fragmentation.

**Distribution target**: August 10, 2026, six weeks before UNGA 81 High-Level Week (September 22–28, 2026).

---

**Domain 58: Tribal Sovereignty as Democratic Infrastructure**
11,388 words — 90 citations — Available June 15, 2026

The 574 federally recognized tribal nations are sovereign governments, not a minority population seeking civil rights protections. The 2025–2026 assault on tribal program infrastructure — 25+ BIA office closures, 13% BIA workforce reduction, $8.05B IHS funding against a $63B documented need, Trump v. Barbara citizenship threat (argued April 1, 2026; ruling expected late June/early July), and post-Callais voting rights erosion affecting 4.7 million eligible-but-unregistered Native voters — is the dismantling of governmental capacity in 574 functioning democracies. Domain 58 documents seven causal pathways, provides a post-Callais litigation analysis, and includes a pre-built rapid-response protocol for the Trump v. Barbara ruling.

**Distribution target**: June 15, 2026 (pre-ruling); updated rapid-response distribution within 24 hours of ruling.

---

**Domain G: Press Freedom and the Information Ecosystem as Democratic Infrastructure**
8,695 words — 50 citations — Available June 15, 2026 (simultaneous with Domain 58, or June 22 staggered)

The United States ranked 64th in RSF's 2026 World Press Freedom Index — down 7 places from 2025 — because the journalism production infrastructure has been systematically dismantled through five simultaneous attack vectors: FCC license investigations used as punitive tools against critical coverage (five documented cases since January 2025), DOJ journalist subpoenas normalizing source exposure (WSJ reporter case documented by CPJ), CPB dissolution and USAGM gutting removing public broadcasting capacity, local news collapse leaving 222 counties — 28 million Americans — with no local news source, and media consolidation concentrating 70% of broadcast television in three companies. Domain G provides the supply-side analysis that complements Domain 43's demand-side epistemic infrastructure work: together these two documents constitute the full information ecosystem argument. The Snyder-Stromberg (2010) causal evidence anchors the democratic theory: newspaper circulation loss reduces congressional accountability directly, not through diffuse mechanisms.

**Distribution target**: June 15, 2026 (CPJ and RCFP as primary peer review and distribution contacts; PRESS Act reintroduction legislative window). Canonical file: `domains/domain-G-press-freedom-epistemic-infrastructure.md`.

---

**Domain 59: Economic Precarity and the Civic Participation Crisis**
8,450 words — 49 citations — Available June 1, 2026

The 42-percentage-point income-participation gap documented in 2024 (42% non-voting rate for households under $50,000 versus 16% for households over $100,000) is not the result of individual apathy. It is the structural output of time poverty — the systematic deprivation of the temporal, cognitive, and material resources required to participate in democratic life. Domain 59 provides causal evidence (Dallas Fed wp2517r1: mortgage relief causally increases voter turnout; Slee/Desmond 2023: eviction reduces turnout by 8–9 points; PNAS 2024: residential mobility persistently depresses voting) for five mechanisms through which economic precarity converts into civic exclusion. The OBBBA compounding analysis documents five simultaneous cuts that fall on overlapping populations with multiplicative — not additive — civic participation effects.

**Distribution target**: June 1, 2026, aligned with OBBBA implementation scrutiny and CTC/RTC Senate Finance advocacy window.
