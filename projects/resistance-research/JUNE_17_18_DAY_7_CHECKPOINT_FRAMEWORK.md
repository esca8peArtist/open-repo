## Section 3: Tier 2 Activation Decision Framework

This section documents the Day 7 decision tree for activating Wave 2 / Tier 2 contacts. Apply separately for each domain.

### Decision Rule: Signal Thresholds

**STRONG signal** (30%+ reply rate, or any Score 4-5 reply from a Tier A contact):
- Definition: At least 2 replies from the 5-7 contacts reached in Wave 1, OR 1 reply that includes a specific engagement action (request for conversation, citation of research in their own materials, forwarding to a colleague, or integration into an active campaign document)
- Action: Activate Tier 2 contacts immediately. Do not wait for Day 14. The signal indicates the research is landing with the right people.
- Domain 51 Tier 2: Montana Plan I-194 campaign, Michigan Clean Elections Coalition, New Mexico Common Cause, Issue One Advisory Board, OpenSecrets
- Domain 59 Tier 2: Economic Policy Institute (epi.org), National Housing Law Project (nhlp.org), Demos (demos.org), National Partnership for Women and Families (npwf.org), Center for American Progress (americanprogress.org), SEIU (seiu.org)
- Domain 48 Tier 2: NAACP Legal Defense Fund (info@naacpldf.org), Fines and Fees Justice Center (info@finesandfeesjusticecenter.org), ACLU of Virginia (acluva@acluva.org)

**MODERATE signal** (10-29% reply rate, or 1 substantive but not action-oriented reply):
- Definition: 1 reply from Wave 1 contacts — any reply that goes beyond a form acknowledgment or auto-response
- Action: Activate the 2 highest-leverage Tier 2 contacts per domain only. For Domain 51: Issue One Advisory Board follow-up + OpenSecrets. For Domain 59: EPI + NHLP. For Domain 48: NAACP LDF.
- Hold remaining Tier 2 contacts until Day 14 (June 24-25) when signal may strengthen.

**WEAK signal** (<10% reply rate — 0 substantive replies, only auto-responses or silence):
- Definition: Zero substantive replies from all contacts reached in Wave 1. May include auto-acknowledgments, OOO replies, or silence.
- Action: Do NOT activate Tier 2 contacts. Run delivery diagnostic first.
  - Delivery diagnostic: Send a 1-sentence test email to yourself from the same account, check if it reaches inbox (not spam); verify Gist URL still loads; verify at least one contact email address is still current via their organization website.
  - If delivery confirmed: Assess message framing. Consider whether the email was sent to a general inbox vs. a named decision-maker. The fix for weak signal is direct personalization, not volume expansion.
  - If delivery problem found: Fix delivery before any Tier 2 sends.
- Exception: If Domain 48 sends are <72 hours old at the time of this checkpoint, a WEAK designation is too early — check again June 20.

**BOUNCE/OOO signal** (any hard bounce or out-of-office reply with a named contact):
- A hard bounce with a known contact's address is a reachability problem, not a content problem. Re-verify the address and resend within 48 hours.
- An OOO that includes a named alternate contact is an upgrade opportunity. Follow up with the named contact after the OOO window closes (usually 1-2 weeks). Log the alternate in `DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md`.
