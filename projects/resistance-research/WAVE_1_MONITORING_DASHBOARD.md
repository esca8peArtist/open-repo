## SECTION 4: Tier 2 Pre-Identification

Tier 2 targets are contacts who have demonstrated enough engagement depth to warrant a second, more substantive send — with additional domain context, a follow-up conversation offer, or a coalition framing request.

**Tier 2 Wave 1 activation**: Week 6 (approximately June 22), if adoption assessment May 25–28 shows strong signals. Do not activate Tier 2 before assessment.

### Tier 2 Identification Criteria

A contact qualifies as a Tier 2 candidate if they meet at least two of the following three criteria:

**Criterion 1 — Integration signal**: Did they mention using the research in their work? Signals: referenced a specific domain, asked about a case application, mentioned a brief or testimony, asked for a domain-specific variant or extract.

**Criterion 2 — Engagement depth**: Reply quality score 3 or higher. Score 3 = clarification question or substantive critique. Score 4 = implementation ask or referral. Score 5 = confirmed integration in work product.

**Criterion 3 — Network multiplier**: Did they connect the sender to another person or organization? Any reply that mentions a colleague, a network, a coalition, or says "you should talk to [name]" qualifies. These are the most valuable signals for cascade adoption.

## SECTION 5: Early Warning Signals

These are conditions to watch for — some expected, some requiring immediate action.

### Bounces

**Hard bounce** (permanent address failure):
- Detection: bounce notification in inbox, usually within 1–2 hours of send
- Severity: HIGH — do not resend to the same address
- Action: Re-verify address on institutional website that day; check PHASE_1_WAVE1_EXECUTION_PREP.md for alternate addresses; resend to alternate address within 24 hours; log in DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md

**Soft bounce** (temporary failure — full inbox, server down):
- Detection: bounce notification with message "temporarily unable to deliver"
- Severity: MEDIUM — retry in 24 hours
- Action: Wait 24 hours, attempt resend once. If second soft bounce occurs, treat as hard bounce and re-verify

**Bounce rate threshold**: >10% (more than 1 bounce in 5 Batch 1 sends) = HIGH alert. Pause further sends until delivery is diagnosed. See PHASE_1_CONTINGENCY_STRATEGY.md Trigger 1.

---

### Declines (Opt-outs)

**Detection**: A reply that explicitly says "please remove me from future outreach" or "not interested / not relevant"

**Action**:
1. Immediately note in tracking sheet — mark as Score 0, Status: Declined
2. Do NOT send any follow-up to this contact or this organization
3. Record the reason if stated — this is research data, not a failure signal
4. Analyze pattern: if 2 or more declines share a reason (e.g., "too political," "not our focus area"), this indicates a framing problem — flag in CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input"

**Pattern analysis question**: Are declines coming from a specific sector? If law schools decline at a higher rate than mutual aid orgs, the academic framing may need adjustment before Tier 2 sends. Document this in Section 6 daily reflection.

### Non-Response Thresholds

| Elapsed time | Status | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| 0–24h | Normal — no action | None |
| 24–48h | Expected — no action | None |
| 48–72h | Expected — monitor | Check Bitly for any Gist clicks as proxy for opens |
| 72h–7d | Within sector norms for most contacts | None — wait for sector SLA (Section 2 table) |
| 7–10 days | Watch zone | Re-check delivery (no bounce notification = probably delivered); prepare follow-up draft |
| 10–14 days | Decision threshold | One follow-up email optional — not required for law school contacts who may have 21-day cycle |
| 14+ days | Unlikely engagement | Log as non-response; keep on record for Wave 3 or domain-specific follow-up |

**Key distinction**: Non-response is not the same as decline. Do not interpret silence as rejection. Academic contacts at law schools routinely have 10–21 day response cycles. The absence of a reply by Day 3 is expected and tells you nothing about engagement likelihood.

### Sector-Specific Anomalies

Watch for these patterns — each requires a specific diagnostic response:

**Anomaly 1 — Law schools respond faster than policy orgs**: Unexpected but positive. A law school contact who replies within 48h is signaling high salience. Log the domain they engaged with — this tells you which framing is resonating with academic readers.

**Anomaly 2 — Policy orgs slower than expected (>7 days)**: Could indicate a framing problem or a staff bandwidth issue (policy orgs often have 2–3 person research teams). Check if there is a major news cycle absorbing their attention before concluding framing failed.

**Anomaly 3 — Mutual aid orgs ask for something specific (domain extract, plain-language version)**: This is a Tier 2 signal, not a complaint. Mutual aid networks work with communities that cannot use a 35-domain academic document directly. An ask for a plain-language version = genuine interest. Log as Score 4 and create the extract within 48h if feasible.

**Anomaly 4 — Multiple contacts from same org respond independently**: Two people at the same organization (e.g., two Brennan Center staff) both engage. This is a cascade signal — the material is circulating internally. Mark both as Tier 2 candidates and track whether the org makes an institutional-level adoption decision.

**Anomaly 5 — Zero engagement signals across all sectors by Day 5**: If Bitly shows zero clicks, zero replies, and no bounce notifications, the most likely cause is a spam filter issue. Run the delivery self-test (send to yourself from same account). If self-test lands in spam, address is flagged. See PHASE_1_CONTINGENCY_STRATEGY.md Section 2, Trigger 1 for diagnosis and resolution.
