# Messaging Templates — Phase 1 Distribution

**April 28, 2026 — Session 565**

Nine core templates (three per tier, four for Tier 3 due to sector variance) plus path-specific subject line overlays. These consolidate and extend the Session 527-553 template infrastructure with cleaner decision logic. The existing `execution/outreach-email-templates.md` contains the full A/B/C variant language for each type — these templates provide the framing architecture and personalization parameters.

## PERSONALIZATION PARAMETERS REFERENCE

Before sending any template, complete these parameters:

**Universal parameters (required for all)**
- `[FIRST_NAME]` — recipient's first name
- `[THEIR_RECENT_WORK]` — title of a specific recent paper, testimony, article, or public statement by this person; look up before writing
- `[THEIR_SPECIFIC_FOCUS]` — one sentence on the recipient's current research or advocacy focus
- `[MOST_RELEVANT_DOMAIN]` — the one or two proposal domains most relevant to their work
- `[DOMAIN_DESCRIPTION]` — one sentence on what that domain covers
- `[YOUR_NAME]` — sender's name
- `[DOCUMENT_LINK]` — working URL for the relevant document
- `[EXEC_SUMMARY_LINK]` — working URL for the 2-page executive summary
- `[FULL_FRAMEWORK_LINK]` — working URL for the full 35-domain document

**Personalization escalation rule**: The first sentence must reference something the recipient has published or said within the last 12 months. If you cannot identify a specific recent item, do not send the email — find the item first. Generic openers result in immediate archiving.

## TIER 1 TEMPLATES: Senators, Think Tank Leaders, Law School Clinic Directors

**Framing principle**: Tier 1 contacts evaluate policy for institutional feasibility. They are not inspired by vision. They are convinced by evidence, mechanism, and specificity. They receive unsolicited research constantly. The differentiator is precision: cite the exact domain that matches their known bill, hearing, or litigation activity. Ask for feedback, not endorsement. Make the contact clearly aware that the research is modular — they can use one domain without committing to the full framework.

**Decision criteria for Tier 1 contacts**:
- Which reforms can be enacted at state level without federal legislation?
- What has been tried elsewhere and what were the outcomes?
- Does the constitutional analysis survive peer scrutiny?
- Who else has engaged with this framework?
- Does it acknowledge political constraints honestly?

## TIER 2 TEMPLATES: Academics, Media, Legal Commentators

**Framing principle**: Tier 2 contacts evaluate methodology and credibility. They cite the framework only if they can defend it to their editors, peers, and critics. They will scrutinize sourcing, acknowledge limits, and look for counterarguments. The ask is not endorsement — it is engagement with the research as research. Methodological honesty ("I'm uncertain whether this holds up here") is a credibility signal to academics. Generic confidence is a red flag.

**Decision criteria for Tier 2 contacts**:
- Are sources verifiable and correctly interpreted?
- Is the comparative evidence accurately applied?
- Does the framework acknowledge limits and counterarguments?
- Is this original analysis or a synthesis of existing work?
- Where exactly does the analysis break down?
