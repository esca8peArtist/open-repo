**Section 2: Historical Foundation — The Pendleton Design (500 words)**

- [ ] 1978: Civil Service Reform Act — MSPB, OSC, OPM as the implementation infrastructure

- [ ] The distinction: political appointees (legitimate) vs. career service capture (illegitimate)

**Section 3: Pathway 1 — Schedule Policy/Career (800 words)**

- [ ] What protections are removed: Chapter 75, Chapter 43, Section 2302, MSPB jurisdiction
- [ ] The "policy-influencing" framing and its scope creep problem

- [ ] Loper Bright argument: courts now interpret this language independently; strong APA challenge
- [ ] Current litigation: PEER v. Trump (N.D. Cal.); NTEU v. Trump (D.D.C.); PEER v. OPM (D. Md.)
- [ ] Cite CRS LSB11412; Federal Register; Democracy Forward

**Section 4: Pathway 2 — DOGE Workforce Reduction (600 words)**

- [ ] Cite Federal News Network January 2026; Fortune March 2026; CBPP

**Section 5: Pathway 3 — MSPB / OSC / OPM Hollowing (500 words)**

**Section 7: Pathway 5 — Whistleblower Protection (500 words)**

- [ ] OSC enforcement jurisdiction reduced
- [ ] The behavioral mechanism: at-will status → infinite cost of whistleblowing → chilling
- [ ] GAP's explicit documentation that Schedule P/C targets whistleblower infrastructure
- [ ] Cite GAP; National Whistleblower Center; MSPB Q&A

**Section 8: International Precedents (700 words)**
- [ ] Hungary (case study 1): Orbán's civil service capture 2010-2014
  - 66 political appointments/year vs. 49/year pre-Orbán
  - "Elite replacement" strategy across judiciary, civil service, media simultaneously
  - Result: classified as "electoral autocracy" by Freedom House, V-Dem, and EIU
  - CSIS 2025: "The Hard Road Back to Democracy" — why reversal is structurally difficult
- [ ] Poland (case study 2): PiS "elite replacement" 2015-2019
  - Sweeping personnel changes across civil service, judiciary, public media
  - Journal of Democracy 2026: "Democracy After Illiberalism: A Warning from Poland"
  - Tusk government (2024-present): CER documents difficulties of reversal
  - Key lesson: civil service politicization is durable — firing loyalists hired under PiS proved legally and practically constrained
- [ ] Germany (comparative model): Berufsbeamtentum
  - Constitutionally embedded civil service independence in Basic Law (1949)
  - Explicitly designed as counter to 1933 Nazi civil service capture
  - Only by guaranteeing internal and external independence can civil servants insist on rule of law even where politically disliked
  - The reform model: constitutionalization, not just statutory protection

**Section 9: The Voter Disenfranchisement Connection (500 words)**
- [ ] Four mechanisms: voting rights enforcement collapse; election security infrastructure; Census politicization; contractor workforce politicization
- [ ] DOJ Voting Section at 6 attorneys cannot enforce VRA Section 2 cases
- [ ] CISA and EAC career staffs support state election administration — DOGE reduces capacity
- [ ] Census Bureau: career statisticians vulnerable to Schedule P/C; undercount risk
- [ ] Civic participation argument: government services administered on political loyalty basis reduce democratic participation by low-income, minority, and opposition-aligned communities

**Section 10: Reform Architecture (600 words)**
- [ ] Statutory: H.R. 492 / S. 134 (Saving the Civil Service Act); S. 591 (Restore Merit Act)
- [ ] Legislative calendar: 119th Congress committee stage; 2027 post-midterm window if Democrats gain House
- [ ] Appropriations: riders prohibiting Schedule P/C implementation funding
- [ ] Litigation: APA challenge (strongest current vehicle); Loper Bright as legal lever
- [ ] Constitutional: Humphrey's Executor / independent agency argument
- [ ] Long-term: independent Civil Service Commission (pre-1978 model strengthened); possible constitutional amendment to embed merit principle
- [ ] The midterm frame: 300,000+ terminated federal workers as a politically mobilized constituency; civil service reform as a 2026 campaign issue
- [ ] Cite H.R. 492; S. 134; S. 591; CRS LSB11412 (congressional options section)
