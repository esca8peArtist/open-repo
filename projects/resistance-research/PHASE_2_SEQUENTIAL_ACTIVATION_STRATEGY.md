## Section 2: Four Sequence Path Analysis

### Path A — Immediate Parallel (Domains 51 + 48 in June–July, then 49–50)

**Timeline**: Domain 51 and Domain 48 distribute in parallel June–July. Domains 49 and 50 follow July–August. Domain 57 fixes to August 10. Total completion: late August.

**Sequencing logic**: Activates the two most structurally complete domains immediately, treating their coalition networks as independent tracks with no bandwidth conflict. The argument for this path is speed: both domains have hard July and August deadlines, and parallel distribution doubles the advocacy footprint in the June–July window.

**Coalition leverage assessment**: Strong independently. Domain 51 reaches democracy and campaign finance organizations entirely unreached in Phase 1 (Common Cause, CLC, Issue One). Domain 48 reaches M4BL Policy Table, Worth Rises, and Brennan Center Justice Program — also unreached in Phase 1. True parallel activation means both networks receive research simultaneously, creating a July window where democracy and criminal justice advocates work simultaneously with Phase 2 materials. However, user execution bandwidth in the June 4–18 window is approximately 6–8 hours. Domain 48's distribution templates require distinct personalization (M4BL framing differs from CLC framing), adding marginal preparation time pressure.

**Critical weakness**: Violates the documented research dependency — Domain 51 documents the dark money architecture (private prison expansion lobbying, ALEC model legislation) that directly funds opposition to Domain 48's restoration proposals. Distributing them in parallel means Domain 48 recipients receive the civic exclusion research without the funding mechanism explanation. Criminal justice organizations receiving Domain 48 benefit from already having Domain 51 in their reading when Domain 48 Section 8 references dark money as the reform-blocking mechanism. The dependency is not a hard block — both domains are independently self-contained — but the sequencing value is real.

**Resource utilization**: Peak user demand June 9–18 (Domain 51 Wave 1 + Domain 48 prep simultaneously). This compresses two distinct distribution preparations into the same two-week window.

**Path A viability rating: HIGH** — technically feasible, optimizes for speed, carries modest coalition sequencing cost. Best fallback option if user confirms elevated June execution capacity.

### Path B — Urgency-Sequenced (Recommended)

**Timeline**: Domain 59 distributing now (June 3–4). Domain 51 distributing June 9–12. Domain 48 distribution prep July 1, sends July 10–20. Domains 49 and 50 preparation July 15–August 1, sends August 1–30. Domain 57 August 10. Domain 58 rapid-response on ruling.

**Sequencing logic**: Sequences by external deadline urgency rather than research production date. Domain 59 leads because the Senate Finance CTC markup closes June 30. Domain 51 follows immediately because the CA Fair Elections Act ballot campaign has launched with a July 1 PROJECTS.md hard deadline for California contacts. Domain 48 follows in July because its primary advocacy hooks have a summer ramp-up timeline (Virginia ballot measure peaks in September–October). Domains 49 and 50 run in parallel in July–August because both have hard August 1 deadlines targeting entirely different coalition networks with no execution conflict.

**Coalition leverage assessment**: Maximum for every domain. Domain 59 reaches CBPP, ITEP, and NWLC precisely when they are testifying on CTC provisions in Senate Finance reconciliation markup — research becomes active advocacy ammunition, not background reading. Domain 51 reaches CA ballot campaign organizations with 3–4 months before November messaging lock-in (the messaging development window). Domain 48 reaches M4BL Policy Table with 3 months before the Virginia ballot measure campaign intensifies. Domains 49 and 50 landing simultaneously in July–August means climate justice and LGBTQ+ rights organizations each have their primary research materials before early-vote periods open.

**Research dependency compliance**: Domain 51 (June 9–12) precedes Domain 48 (July 10–20) by six weeks, within the 4–6 week recommended gap. Criminal justice organizations receiving Domain 48 can immediately cross-reference Domain 51 when Domain 48 Section 8 notes dark money funds private prison expansion and bail bond lobbying opposing restoration reform. This sequencing creates cross-referencing capability that Path A loses.

**Resource utilization**: Load is well-distributed. June 3–12: Domain 59 and 51 sends (~4.5 hours). July 1–20: Domain 48 Gist and Tier 1 sends (~2.5 hours). July 15–August 1: Domain 49 and 50 preparation (~3–4 hours). August 1–30: Domains 49, 50, and 57 sends (~4 hours). Total: approximately 14–15 hours across three months.

**Path B viability rating: HIGHEST** — recommended path. Sequences to external deadlines, maximizes coalition leverage timing, respects dark money to criminal justice research dependency, distributes user execution load across accessible windows.

### Path C — Seasonal-Gated (51 → 48 → 57 → Domains 49+50 Deferred to August)

**Timeline**: Domain 51 distributes June. Domain 48 distributes July. Domain 57 distributes August 10. Domains 49 and 50 defer to August–September.

**Sequencing logic**: Prioritizes clean sequential execution over coalition leverage timing. By deferring Domains 49 and 50 to August–September, avoids the July parallel track that requires managing two research-distribution pipelines simultaneously. The seasonal logic is that August (post-51 and 48 distribution, pre-UNGA) creates a reinforcing distribution "season" where multiple Phase 2 domains arrive in overlapping advocacy community windows.

**Critical weakness**: Domain 50's August 1 ballot measure integration deadline is genuinely hard. Lambda Legal and AT4E need research for ballot campaign messaging infrastructure, not general advocacy. If Domain 50 lands in late August rather than early August, it misses the messaging development window entirely. Election-season advocacy organizations typically lock messaging frameworks 90 days before election day (approximately August 4 for November 3 midterms). A late August distribution may arrive after their architecture is set. Path C's August timing satisfies the general advocacy window but misses the campaign infrastructure window.

**Resource utilization**: Lower peak demand in July (no parallel track). Higher demand in August as deferred domains stack — Domains 49, 50, and 57 all distributing in a two-week window may be harder to execute cleanly than the July parallel track Path B requires.

**Path C viability rating: MODERATE** — technically feasible but sacrifices Domain 50's most time-sensitive advocacy window. Not recommended over Path B unless user execution capacity is substantially constrained. Should be the fallback to Path B, not Path A.
