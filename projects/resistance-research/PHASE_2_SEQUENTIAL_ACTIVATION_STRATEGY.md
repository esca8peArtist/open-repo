## Section 2: Three Execution Path Analysis

Three credible execution paths exist for the June–December 2026 window. They differ not in which domains are distributed but in the sequencing logic and coalition leverage timing.

### Path A — Immediate Parallel (Domains 51 + 48 in June–July, then 49–50)

**Timeline**: Domains 51 and 48 distribute in parallel June–July. Domains 49 and 50 follow July–August. Domain 57 fixes to August 10. Total completion: late August.

**Sequencing logic**: Activates the two most structurally complete domains immediately, treating their coalition networks (democracy advocates + criminal justice / civil rights) as independent tracks with no bandwidth conflict. The argument for this path is speed: both domains have hard July and August deadlines, and parallel distribution doubles the advocacy footprint in the June–July window.

**Coalition leverage**: Strong for both tracks independently. Domain 51 reaches campaign finance and democracy organizations entirely unreached in Phase 1 (Common Cause, CLC, Issue One). Domain 48 reaches M4BL Policy Table, Worth Rises, and Brennan Center Justice Program — also unreached in Phase 1. The parallel activation means both networks receive research simultaneously, creating a July window where democracy and criminal justice advocates are simultaneously working with Phase 2 materials. The risk is that the user has approximately 6–8 hours of execution bandwidth in the June 4–18 window (per BATCH_2_RESOURCE_ALLOCATION_MATRIX.md), making true parallel execution tight. Domain 48's distribution templates require slightly more personalization (M4BL framing is distinct from CLC framing), adding marginal preparation time.

**Research depth trade-offs**: None. Both domains are production-complete. The parallel path does not compromise document quality.

**Resource utilization**: Peak user demand June 9–18 (Domain 51 Wave 1) and July 1–15 (Domain 48 Gist creation and Tier 1 sends). Agent demand minimal (1–2 hours combined for any coalition follow-up research).

**Weakness**: The research dependency identified in the runbooks is violated — Domain 51 documents the dark money architecture that funds private prison expansion and ALEC model legislation opposing Domain 48's restoration proposals. Distributing them in parallel means Domain 48 recipients receive the civic exclusion research without the funding mechanism explanation. This is suboptimal but not a blocking flaw; both domains are independently self-contained.

**Path A viability rating: HIGH** — technically feasible, optimizes for speed, carries modest coalition sequencing cost.

**Timeline**: Domain 59 distributing now (June 3–4). Domain 51 distributing June 9–12. Domain 48 distribution prep July 1, sends July 10–20. Domains 49 and 50 in parallel July 15–August 1 (preparation) and August 1–30 (distribution). Domain 57 August 10. Domain 58 rapid-response on ruling confirmation.

**Sequencing logic**: This is the path recommended by the Phase 2 Candidates Readiness Assessment and confirmed by the Phase 2 Decision Memo. It sequences by external deadline urgency rather than research production date, ensuring every domain reaches its coalition partners before the most time-sensitive advocacy window closes. Domain 59 leads because the Senate Finance CTC markup closes June 30. Domain 51 follows immediately because the CA Fair Elections Act ballot campaign has launched and July 1 is the PROJECTS.md hard deadline for California contacts. Domain 48 follows in July because its primary advocacy hooks (Virginia November ballot measure, pre-midterm window) have a summer ramp-up rather than an immediate-close window. Domains 49 and 50 run in parallel in July–August because both have hard August 1 deadlines (Domain 50 ballot measure integration; Domain 49 D.C. Circuit briefing window) and target entirely different coalition networks with no execution conflict.

**Coalition leverage**: Maximum. Domain 59 economic precarity research reaches CBPP, ITEP, and NWLC exactly when they are testifying on CTC provisions in Senate Finance reconciliation markup — the research becomes active advocacy ammunition, not background reading. Domain 51 reaches CA ballot campaign organizations with 3–4 months before November messaging lock-in. Domain 48 reaches M4BL Policy Table with 3 months before the Virginia ballot measure campaign intensifies. Domain 49's Climate Justice Alliance contact reaches 100 frontline organizations in a single outreach — and the post-Callais redistricting cascade makes the environmental justice / democratic participation argument even sharper than when the domain was written, because the same communities losing EJ procedural rights are now losing redistricting protection.

**Research depth trade-offs**: Minimal. Domain 48 benefits from a July start because Domain 51's dark money architecture section is in active distribution before Domain 48 sends reach their targets. Criminal justice organizations receiving Domain 48 in mid-July can immediately cross-reference Domain 51 (sent to CLC in June) when Domain 48 Section 8 notes that dark money funds private prison expansion and bail bond lobbying opposing restoration reform. This sequencing creates cross-referencing capability that Path A loses.

**Resource utilization**: User execution load is well-distributed. June 3–12: Domain 59 and 51 sends (combined ~4.5 hours per BATCH_2_RESOURCE_ALLOCATION_MATRIX.md). July 1–20: Domain 48 Gist and Tier 1 sends (~2.5 hours). July 15–August 1: Domain 49 and 50 preparation (~3–4 hours). August 1–30: Domain 49, 50, and 57 sends (~4 hours). Total: approximately 14–15 hours of user execution work across three months, consistent with the 6–8 hours per window constraint.

**Path B viability rating: HIGHEST** — recommended path. Sequences to external deadlines, maximizes coalition leverage timing, respects dark money → criminal justice research dependency, distributes user execution load across accessible windows.

### Path C — Seasonal-Gated (51 → 48 → 57 → 49+50 Deferred to August)

**Timeline**: Domain 51 distributes June. Domain 48 distributes July. Domain 57 distributes August 10. Domains 49 and 50 defer to August–September (deferred per ballot deadline logic, not urgency logic).

**Sequencing logic**: This path prioritizes clean sequential execution over coalition leverage timing. By deferring Domains 49 and 50 to August–September, it avoids the July parallel track that requires managing two research-distribution pipelines simultaneously. The seasonal logic is that August (post-Domain 51 and 48 distribution, pre-UNGA) creates a distribution "season" when all remaining domains land together, potentially creating a reinforcing effect where multiple Phase 2 domains arrive in the same advocacy community window.

**Coalition leverage**: Weaker than Path B. Domain 50's hard August 1 ballot measure integration deadline is genuinely hard — Lambda Legal and AT4E need research for ballot campaign messaging infrastructure, not just advocacy. If Domain 50 lands in late August rather than early August, it misses the messaging development window. The domain's own production documents note "August 1 deadline for ballot campaign integration" as distinct from general pre-election advocacy. Path C's August timing may satisfy the general advocacy window but misses the campaign infrastructure window.

**Research depth trade-offs**: For Domains 49 and 50, a late August delivery creates a compressed distribution-to-campaign-integration timeline. Election-season advocacy organizations typically lock messaging frameworks 90 days before election day (approximately August 4 for November 3 midterms). A late August distribution to ballot campaign contacts may arrive after their messaging architecture is set.

**Resource utilization**: Lower peak demand in July (no parallel track). Higher demand in August as all deferred domains stack. The August load (Domains 49, 50, and 57 all distributing in a two-week window) may actually be harder to execute cleanly than the July parallel track Path B requires.

**Path C viability rating: MODERATE** — technically feasible but sacrifices Domain 50's most time-sensitive advocacy window without a compensating benefit. Not recommended over Path B unless user execution capacity is substantially lower than the 6–8 hours per window estimate.

## Section 4: Go/No-Go Decision Framework

### 4.1 Phase 1 Engagement Tiers

The go/no-go framework depends on Phase 1 engagement data measured by the PHASE_1_IMPACT_EVALUATION_FRAMEWORK.md checkpoints. The June 15 T+14 checkpoint is the first meaningful assessment point (Wave 1 sends began May 18–28; June 15 is approximately Day 18–28 depending on wave, covering the Day 14–30 window). The following thresholds directly govern Phase 2 activation.

**STRONG Engagement (greater than 20% open rate, greater than 50 coalition responses by June 15)**

Activate Domains 51 and 59 immediately (both already executing). Accelerate Domain 48 preparation to begin June 15 rather than July 1. Authorize Domain 49 and 50 parallel track confirmation for July 1 activation. Upgrade Domain 57 preparation to include an expanded contact list (add Senate Foreign Relations Committee staff, CFR, and Carnegie Endowment contacts not in the base list). Use confirmed Phase 1 engagement as social proof in all Phase 2 outreach: "this framework has been engaged by [named Tier 1 organizations]." The STRONG engagement signal doubles the expected response rate in Phase 2 distribution because Phase 2 contacts are more risk-averse than Phase 1 pioneers — social proof from named peers materially increases opening probability.

**MODERATE Engagement (10–20% open rate, 20–50 coalition responses by June 15)**

Maintain current sequencing (Path B) without acceleration. Domain 51 and 59 continue executing. Domain 48 preparation begins July 1 as planned. Domain 49 and 50 parallel track activates July 1. Do not add speculative contacts to Phase 2 distribution lists — focus on the highest-confidence contacts identified in the runbooks. If Phase 1 response data is available at the constituency level, deprioritize Phase 2 domains whose target constituencies showed weak Phase 1 engagement and front-load domains reaching constituencies with strong Phase 1 engagement. Specific: if civil rights organizations showed strong Phase 1 engagement, accelerate Domain 48. If labor showed strong engagement, accelerate Domain 59 Tier 2 (EPI, SEIU are Tier 2 contacts). If law schools showed strong engagement, amplify Domain 51's academic distribution.

**WEAK Engagement (less than 10% open rate, fewer than 20 coalition responses by June 15)**

Do not halt Phase 2 distributions already executing (Domains 59 and 51 are time-gated to Senate Finance and CA ballot windows — those windows do not move with engagement data). Conduct root-cause analysis on Phase 1 weak signal before activating new Phase 2 domains (Domain 48 preparation would start later than July 1). Two root cause categories:

*Infrastructure gap*: Were emails delivered? (Check bounce rates, spam filter rates via Gmail label data.) Was the Gist accessible? (Check Bitly click data — if clicks are near-zero, the Gist link may have been filtered.) Infrastructure gaps have mechanical fixes and do not indicate weak content quality.

*Messaging gap*: Were emails opened but not clicked? (A low Bitly click rate with confirmed delivery indicates the email content did not motivate click-through.) If this is the pattern, revise Phase 2 email openers before Domain 48 sends. The runbooks recommend leading with the finding most relevant to each specific organization's current work — if Phase 1 emails led with the full framework overview rather than the most operationally relevant domain, that is a correctable framing error.

Root-cause analysis at June 15 should produce a determination between these two categories. If infrastructure gap: fix and re-send without Phase 2 delay. If messaging gap: revise Domain 48 and Domain 49 email templates before July sends, but do not delay the sends beyond July 15.

## Section 5: Fallback Sequencing

### 5.1 If the June Window Closes Without Domain 59 Distribution

**Scenario**: Senate Finance CTC markup closes without Domain 59 reaching CBPP, ITEP, NWLC, MomsRising, or AFL-CIO.

**Impact**: The primary legislative urgency hook (CTC markup window) closes. The domain is not invalidated — the economic precarity / civic participation argument remains accurate and the November midterm voter mobilization window is still open through September 2026.

**Fallback**: Shift Domain 59 framing from "CTC markup testimony support" to "pre-midterm voter engagement analysis." The domain's five causal pathways (economic precarity → reduced civic participation) are directly relevant to voter mobilization organizations planning fall 2026 campaigns. A July distribution to the original contact list with a revised opener focusing on the midterm voter turnout argument recovers approximately 70% of the original distribution value. CBPP and ITEP remain valid targets — their CTC analysis work continues beyond the markup window. AFL-CIO and MomsRising are even more relevant in the pre-election framing.

**Timing**: July CTC round (reconciliation bill expected floor debate July–August). If Senate Finance does not complete reconciliation markup in June, a second markup window may open in July. Monitor Senate Finance Committee schedule.
