### Revision Loop Architecture

Three revision categories apply to all optional peer review cycles:

1. **Empirical overclaiming**: Causal claims stated stronger than underlying evidence allows. Catch during peer review. Fix by hedging claim language to match source confidence levels. Highest risk in Domain 59 Section 5 (the multiplicative OBBBA compounding argument requires precise quantification — "five simultaneous cuts" becomes less defensible if stated as multiplicative rather than additive without explicit modeling).

3. **URL rot**: Citations live during research but dead before publication. Catch during final citation verification pass (browser-tested, not memory-confirmed). Fix with Internet Archive or substitute source.
