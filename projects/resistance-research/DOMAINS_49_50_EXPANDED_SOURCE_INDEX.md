# PART I: DOMAIN 49 — ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE AND DEMOCRATIC PARTICIPATION

## Domain 49 Overview and Research Questions

**Core thesis**: Environmental justice communities are democratic participants who have been systematically stripped of the legal and procedural tools through which they exercised civic power. EPA's 2025–2026 infrastructure elimination is not deregulation — it is the coordinated removal of democratic participation infrastructure from the communities most dependent on it.

**Primary research questions**:
1. What specific legal and procedural tools did frontline communities use to contest environmental decisions?
2. How does the EPA's EJ infrastructure elimination constitute civic exclusion, not merely environmental deregulation?
3. What are the five causal pathways from environmental burden to democratic disengagement?
4. What does the international environmental democracy standard (Aarhus Convention) require, and how far does the US fall short?
5. What statutory reforms are necessary to prevent future executive elimination of environmental participation rights?
