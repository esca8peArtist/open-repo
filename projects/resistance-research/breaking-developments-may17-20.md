## Scope 2: Court Decisions (District and Appellate — Domains 1/37/57/58)

### SCOTUS GVR: *Turtle Mountain v. Howe* — Private Right of Action Unresolved (May 18)

**This is the single most significant new development in the May 17–20 window.**

The Supreme Court issued a GVR (grant, vacate, remand) order in *Turtle Mountain Band of Chippewa Indians v. Howe* (No. 25-253) on Monday May 18, 2026. Simultaneously, the Court GVR'd a companion voting rights case, *Board of Election Commissioners v. NAACP* (Mississippi). Both were remanded to their respective circuits "for further consideration in light of *Louisiana v. Callais*."

The prior monitoring posture — "cert pending, no action" — was superseded. The GVR is materially different from a straight denial or a cert grant with briefing schedule. The Eighth Circuit must now reconsider its prior ruling (that Section 2 of the VRA does not create a private right of action for tribes) in light of *Callais*. Justice Ketanji Brown Jackson dissented, arguing that *Callais* did not address the private enforceability question at all, and that the GVR muddies rather than resolves the issue. Justices Kagan and Sotomayor did not join Jackson's dissent.

**Domain 58 impact**: EMERGENCY UPDATE REQUIRED before May 21 synthesis. Domain 58's litigation section on *Turtle Mountain v. Howe* must be rewritten from "cert pending, no action" to: "GVR issued May 18, 2026 — remanded to 8th Circuit for *Callais* reconsideration; private right of action unresolved at SCOTUS; Jackson dissent flags that *Callais* did not address the private enforceability question; Kagan/Sotomayor silence is ambiguous; July 24, 2025 stay remains in effect pending further proceedings." The domain's crisis framing was built around the binary cert/deny outcome; the GVR extends uncertainty rather than resolving it and is a worse outcome than denial (which would have let the case proceed with the prior circuit ruling in place).

**Domain 1 impact**: HIGH. The Mississippi NAACP GVR (companion) adds a second circuit (Fifth) now reviewing VRA enforcement rights post-*Callais*. This is the multi-circuit erosion mechanism in action: SCOTUS is not resolving enforcement questions but returning them to circuits already predisposed against VRA plaintiffs after *Callais*. Add to Domain 1's VRA enforcement section as "SCOTUS GVR cascade — two circuits simultaneously reviewing enforcement rights under post-*Callais* lens."
