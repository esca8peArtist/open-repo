# Houston Volunteer Organizations — Near 570 Katy Freeway
## Personal Reference | May 2026

**Reference point**: Topgolf at 570 Katy Freeway, Houston TX 77024 (I-10 at Washington Ave corridor, roughly at I-10/Shepherd).

**Priority filter**: Organizations with ongoing recurring volunteer opportunities (weekly or monthly regular shifts), not one-time events. Distance threshold: approximately 15-minute drive under normal conditions.

**Focus areas covered**: Social justice, civic engagement, legal aid, at-risk youth, community organizing, housing/homelessness, food insecurity, immigration.

---

## Tier 1 — Within 5 miles / Under 10 minutes

---

### 1. West Houston Assistance Ministries (WHAM)

**Mission**: Provides emergency financial assistance, food, clothing, and referral services to individuals and families in crisis in the west Houston area. Longest-standing emergency services nonprofit in the immediate area.

**Address**: 10501 Meadowglen Lane, Houston, TX 77042
**Distance**: Approximately 4 miles / 8–10 minutes west on I-10 to Gessner area

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Friday Drive-Through Food Distribution (7:30 am – 12:00 pm) — bagging produce, client intake, loading trunks, light cleanup. Serves 150+ families per week.
- Resale shop sorting and sales (ongoing, weekday shifts)
- ESL instruction for adult clients
- General office and program support

**Signup/application process**: Visit whamministries.org/volunteer. Contact Patrice Stpe at patrice.stpe@whamministries.org for group or recurring individual volunteer scheduling.

**Website/contact**: whamministries.org | (713) 780-2727

**Flexibility notes**: The Friday food distribution is the clearest recurring weekly slot — show up every week, same time. ESL instruction is an ongoing commitment (semester-based). Resale shop shifts are flexible and schedulable on Volunteer Houston.

---

### 2. Houston Volunteer Lawyers (HVL)

**Mission**: Largest provider of pro bono civil legal services in Harris County. Provides free legal advice and representation to low-income Houstonians — family law, landlord-tenant, consumer debt, immigration, benefits, and more.

**Address**: 1000 Louisiana Street, Suite 3600, Houston, TX 77002
**Distance**: Approximately 5 miles / 10–12 minutes east on I-10 to downtown core

**Volunteer roles available** (attorney-only for advice; non-attorney roles also exist):
- Wednesday evening phone clinic — attorneys provide advice over phone (recurring every week)
- Friday afternoon phone clinic — recurring weekly
- Veterans Clinic at DeBakey VA — every Friday afternoon, roughly 50 veterans seen per week
- Spanish-language clinic — first Thursday of each month, 6–8 pm
- Semi-monthly Saturday Legal Advice Clinics (8:30 am – 1:00 pm, walk-in)
- Non-attorney volunteers: client intake screening, document translation, administrative support

**Signup/application process**: probono@hvlp.org | Sign up at makejusticehappen.org/get-involved. Malpractice insurance provided for all attorney volunteers.

**Website/contact**: makejusticehappen.org | legalhelphouston.org | (832) 779-4030

**Flexibility notes**: The phone clinics are highly flexible — attorneys sign up for individual Wednesday or Friday slots. Saturday clinics are about twice/month. Non-attorney roles (intake, translation) may require a more structured application — contact directly.

## Tier 2 — Within 5–10 miles / 10–15 minutes

---

### 3. The Beacon

**Mission**: Downtown day center providing essential services (meals, hygiene, laundry, case management, legal aid) to people experiencing homelessness in Houston. One of the primary daily-access shelters in the city.

**Address**: 1212 Prairie Street, Houston, TX 77002
**Distance**: Approximately 7 miles / 12–15 minutes east (downtown, near Discovery Green)

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Day Center — Essential Services: client check-in/intake (2-hour shifts), meal prep and service, laundry operations (3-hour 15-minute shifts)
- Pro Bono Attorneys: civil legal aid through Beacon Law program (requires active Texas bar)
- Brigid's Hope Mentors: one-on-one mentoring with women in transitional housing (12-month commitment, bi-weekly check-ins)

**Shift times**: Tuesday–Saturday
- First shift: 7:15 am – 10:00 am (21 slots)
- Second shift: 10:00 am – 1:00 pm (20 slots)

**Signup/application process**: New volunteers complete application at volgistics.com/appform/180020273. Allow 72 hours for processing. Registered volunteers self-schedule through existing account.

**Website/contact**: beaconhomeless.org/volunteer-with-us | (713) 220-9737 (Day Center Coordinator)

**Flexibility notes**: Highly flexible recurring model — you pick your own shifts and can self-remove if unavailable. Ideal for someone who wants to show up 2x/month on a consistent schedule. The mentoring program is a deeper 12-month commitment on its own track.

### 4. Healthcare for the Homeless — Houston

**Mission**: Provides integrated primary care, behavioral health, dental, and specialty services to people experiencing homelessness in Harris County. Project Access connects patients to specialist care.

**Address (volunteer site)**: Caroline Street Clinic, 1934 Caroline Street, Houston, TX 77002
**Distance**: Approximately 7 miles / 12–14 minutes east (Midtown/Museum District border)

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Project Access Support volunteer — no medical or clinical training required. Full guidance provided. Assists with care coordination, patient navigation, and clinic support functions.
- Licensed clinicians (MDs, RNs, etc.) — credentialing process required; separate track

**Shift times**: 
- Mondays: 8:00 am – 12:00 pm
- Wednesdays: 8:00 am – 12:00 pm

**Signup/application process**: Complete Volunteer Registration Form at homeless-healthcare.org/volunteer. Slots are limited — organization reaches out when a matching shift opens. Submit early.

**Website/contact**: homeless-healthcare.org | (713) 286-6113

**Flexibility notes**: Recurring weekly schedule (same day each week once placed). Waitlist likely — apply now, especially for Monday/Wednesday slots. Not suitable for irregular one-off volunteering; this is a recurring committed placement.

### 5. Lone Star Legal Aid — Houston Office

**Mission**: Fourth-largest legal services organization in the US. Provides free civil legal help to low-income residents across 72 counties in East Texas, with the Houston office serving Harris County. Areas include family law, housing, benefits, consumer, immigration, and disaster recovery.

**Address**: 1415 Fannin Street, Houston, TX 77002
**Distance**: Approximately 6 miles / 10–13 minutes east (downtown/midtown)

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Pro bono attorney representation — trial, appellate, transactional, ADR
- Non-attorney: law students, paralegals, community members for document support and intake assistance
- Disaster Legal Services (surges during hurricane season)

**Schedule**: Monday–Friday 9 am – 5 pm; project-based and clinic-based formats

**Signup/application process**: Visit lonestarlegal.org/volunteer or call (713) 652-0077. Malpractice insurance provided for attorney volunteers.

**Website/contact**: lonestarlegal.org | Local: (713) 652-0077 | Toll-free: (800) 733-8394

**Flexibility notes**: Attorney volunteers can take on project-based work (take a case, resolve it, done) or sign up for recurring clinic shifts. Non-attorneys should contact directly to discuss what support roles are available at a given time.

### 6. Texas Organizing Project (TOP) — Houston

**Mission**: Year-round community organizing in Black and Latino Houston communities, connected to an electoral PAC. Runs relational door-knocking, base-building, legislative advocacy, and independent electoral campaigns 365 days a year. Most consequential structural power-building organization in the Houston area.

**Address**: Houston office (contact via organizetexas.org for current location — offices have been in the Third Ward / Midtown corridor)
**Distance**: Approximately 6–8 miles / 12–15 minutes east

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Door-to-door canvassing (weekends and some evenings) — precinct-based, organized by staff
- Phone banking and text banking for legislative and electoral campaigns
- Volunteer voter registration drives
- Leadership training and civic education events

**Signup/application process**: organizetexas.org/take-action/volunteer/ or contact via website form at organizetexas.org/about-top/contact-us/. Staff will follow up with orientation and first action scheduling.

**Website/contact**: organizetexas.org | Contact: grants@organizetexas.org (general) or via website form

**Flexibility notes**: Not a traditional shift-based model. Volunteers join an active organizing team, attend trainings, and show up for actions and canvasses. Recurring involvement is the expected model; one-off participation is less useful to them. Best entry: sign up online, attend a new volunteer orientation, get placed on a canvassing team.

### 7. Big Brothers Big Sisters — Houston (Community-Based Program)

**Mission**: Youth mentoring program matching adult volunteers ("Bigs") with children ages 6–18 ("Littles") from single-parent or under-resourced households. One of the most evidence-backed at-risk youth interventions available to individual volunteers.

**Address**: Main Houston enrollment office at 1003 Washington Avenue, Houston, TX 77007
**Distance**: Approximately 2 miles / 5–7 minutes east (Washington Corridor, very close to Topgolf)

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Community-Based Big: meet your Little 2–4 times per month for shared activities (you set the schedule). Minimum one-year commitment.
- Mentor2.0: high-school-focused program with weekly online contact plus monthly in-person school meetings
- School/Site-Based: meet during school hours at a partner school — more structured, lower time demand than Community-Based

**Signup/application process**: Submit application at bbbstx.org/be-a-big-houston. Six-step process: application, virtual/in-person interview with Enrollment Specialist, two-hour Pre-Match Training, matching, introduction meeting, ongoing support from Match Support Specialist.

**Website/contact**: bbbstx.org/houston | bbbstx.org/be-a-big-houston

**Flexibility notes**: This is a long-term relational commitment, not a drop-in role. Community-Based is the most flexible format (you and your Little decide timing). The Washington Ave office is the closest major organization to the Topgolf reference point.

### 8. Houston Food Bank — Portwall Warehouse + North Branch

**Mission**: Largest food bank in the United States by volume. Distributes food through a network of 1,500+ partner pantries across 18 counties. Warehousing, packing, and mobile distribution operations run on volunteer labor.

**Addresses**:
- Main warehouse: 535 Portwall Street, Houston, TX 77029 (East End, near 610 East Loop)
- North Branch: 146 Knobcrest Drive, Houston, TX 77060 (near Beltway 8 North / I-45)

**Distance**:
- Portwall: Approximately 11 miles / 14–18 minutes east via I-10 or I-69
- North Branch: Approximately 20 miles / 20–25 minutes — likely outside target radius

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Food sorting and inspection
- Repackaging dry goods into family-sized bags
- Building home delivery kits and disaster boxes (North Branch specializes in these)
- Mobile food distribution support
- Emergency Food Pantry stocking

**Shift times** (Portwall main warehouse):
- Mon–Sun: 8:00 am – 12:00 pm
- Mon–Sat: 1:00 pm – 4:00 pm
- Tue–Fri: 6:00 pm – 9:00 pm

**Signup/application process**: Register an account and self-schedule shifts at houstonfoodbank.civicore.com. Check in at the Welcome Desk prior to each shift. Volunteer hotline: (713) 547-8604.

**Website/contact**: houstonfoodbank.org/ways-to-give/give-time/ | (713) 223-3700

**Flexibility notes**: Maximum flexibility — no ongoing commitment required. Sign up for individual shifts as your schedule allows. The most accessible recurring volunteer experience on this list if you want to drop in week-to-week without a formal relationship. Portwall is within the 15-minute target; North Branch is not.

### 9. Habitat for Humanity Northwest Harris County

**Mission**: Builds and repairs homes for low-income families in Northwest Harris County through community volunteer labor. Partner families earn equity and complete sweat equity hours alongside volunteers.

**Address**: 13350 Jones Road, Houston, TX 77070
**Distance**: Approximately 18 miles / 18–22 minutes northwest via I-10 W and TX-249 — on the edge of the 15-minute radius but reachable at off-peak times

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Home construction crew (Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays — 2–4 days/month; some construction experience helpful but not required)
- Deconstruction crew (weekday flexible shifts; commitment to volunteer regularly required)
- ReStore — currently closed to new volunteers as of 2026

**Signup/application process**: Contact Genavieve Matthews at gmatthews@habitatnwhc.org for construction crew. Deconstruction inquiries: deconstruction@thehabitatrestore.org. All volunteers complete a volunteer waiver before first day.

**Website/contact**: habitatnwhc.org | habitatnwhc.org/ways-to-help/longterm-volunteer-opportunities.html

**Flexibility notes**: The construction crew is explicitly a recurring long-term commitment (2–4 days/month ongoing). Deconstruction is more schedule-flexible but also expects regular ongoing participation. This is the best housing-construction volunteer option in the northwest corridor.

**Address**: 2575 Reed Road, Houston, TX 77051 (South Loop / Hobby area)
**Distance**: Approximately 15 miles / 15–20 minutes south via I-59 / I-69 South

- Devotional leading / chapel (faith-oriented role)
- Mentoring residents in transitional housing programs
- Sorting and distributing donated goods

**Website/contact**: sohmission.org | (713) 228-2421

### 11. RAICES Texas — Houston Area

**Mission**: Largest immigration legal services organization in Texas. Provides removal defense, asylum, DACA, family reunification services, and immigrant rights advocacy.

**Address**: 1900 St. James Place, Suite 450, Houston, TX 77056 (Galleria/Uptown area)
**Distance**: Approximately 6 miles / 10–13 minutes west on I-10 to Loop 610 area

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Language Services Provider (translation/interpretation — remote-eligible): document translation, live meeting interpretation, court testimony interpretation. Requires orientation, background check, training. Useful for Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, and other languages.
- Pro Se Clinic Volunteer (in-person and remote): legal professionals and law students assist individuals filing DACA, asylum, and naturalization applications. Contact: partnerships@raicestexas.org
- Phonebank/Textbank Advocate (remote): contact elected officials on migrant justice campaigns. Fully remote, very low barrier.
- Subject Matter Expert: professionals leading workshops or curriculum on immigration and social justice topics.

**Signup/application process**: Complete volunteer application at raicestexas.org/volunteerapp (via BetterImpact platform). Orientation held at start of each month (virtual). Pro se and fundraiser roles have direct contact emails.

**Website/contact**: raicestexas.org | (833) 372-4237

**Flexibility notes**: Most roles are remote-eligible, making them highly flexible. In-person Pro Se Clinic has the clearest recurring structure. Monthly orientation is the entry gate — apply early to catch the next cycle.

### 12. Indivisible Houston

**Mission**: Grassroots civic action network advocating for democratic accountability through public pressure on elected officials, voter registration, candidate endorsement, and resistance to anti-democratic policies. West Houston Indivisible sub-chapter covers the Katy Freeway corridor.

**Address**: No permanent physical office — operates through member-hosted events, Zoom, and shared spaces. West Houston Indivisible meets via weekly Zoom (check mobilize.us for in-person events in the corridor).

**Distance**: Events organized throughout Harris County, including west Houston / Memorial corridor — some within 5 miles of the reference point.

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Block walking (door-to-door canvassing) in specific precincts
- Phone banking for active campaigns
- Voter registration drives
- Town hall and advocacy event organizing and staffing
- Social media, data, and communications support

**Signup/application process**: Sign up via actionnetwork.org/groups/indivisible-houston or indivisiblehouston.org. West Houston Indivisible: check mobilize.us/indivisible for local events. Once signed up, you receive action alerts and event invitations.

**Website/contact**: indivisiblehouston.org | actionnetwork.org/groups/indivisible-houston | Facebook: facebook.com/indivisiblehouston

**Flexibility notes**: Highest flexibility of any organization on this list — show up when you can, do what fits your capacity. No formal commitment required. The West Houston sub-chapter makes geographic proximity realistic. Most consistent recurring activity is phone banking (can be done from home).

### 13. Texas Civil Rights Project — Houston

**Mission**: Litigation and advocacy organization focused on voting rights, police accountability, immigration rights, and prisoner/detainee rights across Texas. Houston office handles local litigation and outreach.

**Address**: Houston office location — contact txcivilrights.org for current address. National Pro Bono opportunities listed through probono.net and volunteermatch.org.

**Distance**: Varies by office; prior locations have been in the Midtown / downtown corridor — approximately 6–10 miles / 12–15 minutes

**Volunteer roles available**:
- Pro bono attorney: legal research, brief drafting, legislative/policy advocacy, litigation support, mentoring volunteer lawyers
- Non-attorney: community outreach, know-your-rights education, translation, event support
- Law students: case research, writing

**Signup/application process**: volunteermatch.org/search/org460877.jsp or txcivilrights.org/join-our-team. Contact through VolunteerMatch or directly via their site.

**Website/contact**: txcivilrights.org | VolunteerMatch listing: volunteermatch.org/search/org460877.jsp

**Flexibility notes**: Project-based model — you take on an assignment, complete it, and take another. Does not require weekly recurring commitment. Good fit for someone who wants substantive advocacy work on a flexible timeline.

## Entry-Point Recommendations

**If you want something you can walk into this weekend with zero setup**: Houston Food Bank Portwall. Register online, show up Saturday morning. Done.

**If you want recurring work with real weekly rhythm**: WHAM Friday distribution or The Beacon morning shifts. Both have self-scheduling systems and recurring slot structures.

**If you want legal aid work**: Houston Volunteer Lawyers phone clinics (attorneys) or Lone Star Legal Aid intake support. HVL is most accessible and has the clearest recurring weekly structure (Wednesday evenings, Friday afternoons).

**If you want the highest political impact per hour**: Texas Organizing Project door-knocking during an active campaign period. Requires more orientation investment upfront but feeds directly into the structural power-building work.

**If you want at-risk youth mentoring**: Big Brothers Big Sisters is the most evidence-backed option and the closest geographically (Washington Ave, 2 miles away). Requires 12-month commitment — do not start unless you can follow through.

**If you want immigration work**: RAICES is the most directly aligned with resistance-research themes. Remote language services is lowest barrier; Pro Se Clinic is highest impact.
