## FREE LEGAL REPRESENTATION

### Harris County Immigrant Legal Services Fund (ILSF)
**The most important resource for detained Harris County residents.**
- Provides FREE legal representation for Harris County residents detained in any Houston-area ICE facility
- Also covers children previously detained
- Income-eligible (below 80% area median family income)
- Intake via hotline: **1-833-468-4664**
- Website: houstonimmigration.org/ilsf
- **Note**: Texas AG sued to shut this down — appeals court rejected that challenge (Feb 2026). Program continues.

### GHIRP — Galveston-Houston Immigrant Representation Project
- Non-profit legal org exclusively focused on immigration; holistic model includes social services
- Removal proceedings, bond hearings, asylum, family reunification
- **Phone**: (713) 561-3059 | Toll-free: 1-866-301-3901
- **Email**: info@ghirp.org
- **Houston office**: 6001 Savoy Drive, Suite 400, Houston TX 77036
- **Website**: ghirp.org

### Justice for All Immigrants
- Affordable legal services for low-income families; part of ILSF network
- **Phone**: (832) 779-4030
- **Address**: 2220 Broadway Street, Houston TX 77012
- **Website**: justiceforallimmigrants.org

### RAICES Texas — Houston Office
- Removal defense, asylum, DACA, bond assistance

- **Phone**: 1-800-408-5161

### BakerRipley — Immigration & Citizenship Services

## CHURCH SANCTUARY NETWORK

Houston-area churches have activated sanctuary programs. The sensitive locations policy was rescinded January 20, 2025 — ICE can now legally act at churches. Some congregations are posting "judicial warrant required" signage.

- **To find Houston sanctuary congregations**: Contact FIEL (713-364-3435) or HILSC hotline (1-833-468-4664)
- **National UCC guidance**: ucc.org/immigration-enforcement-action-what-churches-need-to-know

## CRITICAL LOCAL CONTEXT

> **HPD cooperates with ICE.** Unlike sanctuary cities, Houston Police Department actively contacts ICE for administrative warrants with a 30-minute response window. This is being challenged by FIEL, TOP, and HILSC but remains current policy. Families need to know: any law enforcement contact in Houston carries ICE referral risk.

> **DV survivors are at heightened risk.** Tahirih has documented HPD contacting ICE after domestic violence 911 calls. Undocumented survivors should contact Tahirih (713-496-0100) before or immediately after any law enforcement contact.

> **Harris County ILSF survived a legal challenge.** Texas AG tried to shut down the county's free legal representation program — appeals court rejected it February 2026. The program continues and is fully funded.
