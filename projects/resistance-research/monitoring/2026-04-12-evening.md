## Priority Flag: White House Ballroom — Remand Details Clarified

The morning pass logged the April 12 remand ruling. Evening research adds important procedural detail:

The DC Circuit's 2-1 order (issued April 11, published April 12) does NOT simply give Judge Leon more time. It remands with a specific instruction: Leon must clarify whether and how his injunction interferes with the administration's underground security infrastructure — bomb shelters, military facilities, medical facilities. The administration argued these systems are "inseparable" from above-ground ballroom construction, which directly contradicts earlier filings claiming security work was independent. The panel flagged this inconsistency explicitly.

**What this means operationally**: The Trump administration now has until April 17 to seek emergency Supreme Court intervention if Leon's revised ruling still blocks construction. Given the administration's stated plan to invoke SCOTUS, the April 17 window is a hard clock. Watch for a SCOTUS emergency application to the Chief Justice (or to Kavanaugh, who handles the DC Circuit) if Leon holds firm.

## Mail Voting EO Litigation — No New Ruling, But January Ruling Context Clarified

The March 31, 2026 EO (EO 14399) cases remain at the complaint/early-motion stage. No injunction ruling has issued as of April 12.

However, a separate ruling from January 10, 2026 (Judge John Chun, W.D. Wash., *Washington and Oregon v. Trump*) permanently blocked the earlier March 2025 anti-voting EO (EO 14248) on three grounds:
- Proof-of-citizenship requirement on federal voter registration forms — blocked
- Withholding federal election funding to coerce state compliance — blocked
- National ballot-receipt deadline overriding state postmark rules — blocked

This January ruling is distinct from and precedent for the March 31 EO litigation. The 23-state lawsuit and the Lawyers' Committee/NAACP/Common Cause/Black Voters Matter 15th Amendment suit (both filed early April, District of Massachusetts) are proceeding without a ruling yet. Democracy Docket is the primary tracker: [case page](https://www.democracydocket.com/cases/massachusetts-trump-election-integrity-executive-order-challenge/).

A separate development from April 9: A federal judge in Massachusetts dismissed a DOJ lawsuit seeking statewide voter data from Massachusetts, citing lack of basis. This is in the voter-data access track, not the mail-voting EO track, but is part of the same election-integrity enforcement pattern.

## Immigration Enforcement — Pattern Shift Noted

ICE enforcement data for early April 2026 shows a notable directional shift:

- After pulling out of Minnesota following court losses and political pressure, ICE book-ins have dropped below 1,000/day (from a peak of ~1,400/day during the Minnesota surge)
- The composition of arrests is shifting: roughly 65% of recent book-ins have criminal convictions or pending charges, up from earlier in the administration when non-criminal arrests spiked 2,450%
- This shift is consistent with political retreat after the Hussen v. Noem finding that agents detained people "solely on the basis of their race" in Minnesota
- NYC data shows ICE arrests are increasing but deportations from the NYC office are declining — suggesting detention bottlenecks

**Significance**: The enforcement pullback from Minnesota appears to have produced the composition shift. This is a rare case of litigation-backed political pressure visibly changing enforcement pattern — at least temporarily.
