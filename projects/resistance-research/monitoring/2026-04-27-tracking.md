## THREAD 1: Abrego Garcia — April 28 Pre-Hearing Intelligence

### What the Public Record Showed Going Into April 28

The case entered the April 28 hearing in a sealed posture. Both parties filed motions under seal on April 23; Judge Xinis issued a stay of all discovery "with the agreement of the parties" until April 30 at 5:00 p.m. The reason is sealed. The government's April 23 criminal-investigation timeline disclosure (through the unsealed CBS/December 2025 court records) adds the following to the evidentiary record available to Xinis:

- DOJ official Aakash Singh contacted the Middle District of Tennessee on April 27, 2025 — the day before SCOTUS issued its original "facilitate return" order — about opening a criminal case against Abrego Garcia.
- Singh declared the prosecution a "top priority" on April 30, 2025.
- U.S. Attorney McGuire noted by May 15, 2025 that "the DAG and PDAG would like Garcia charged sooner rather than later."
- Indictment followed May 21, 2025.

This timeline is direct evidence that DOJ began the criminal prosecution in direct temporal response to the civil case proceedings — the core basis for the vindictive-prosecution claim before Judge Crenshaw. If Crenshaw dismisses the criminal case before or concurrent with the April 28 hearing, it eliminates the government's "criminal proceedings require U.S. presence" rationale for the Liberia removal demand. Judge Xinis has access to this record.

**Enforcement posture for the hearing**: The DC Circuit's April 15 Rao ruling (Boasberg contempt termination) is the administration's primary legal defense if Xinis attempts contempt. The administration will argue any order Xinis issues must satisfy the "unmistakably clear and specific" specificity standard. The Erez Reuveni whistleblower complaint — documenting DOJ's deliberate decisions to resist court orders in multiple deportation proceedings — is Abrego Garcia's attorneys' best evidence for a specificity-proof contempt order, because it documents intent and pattern rather than ambiguous noncompliance.

**Status**: April 28 hearing outcome — to be logged in `monitoring/2026-04-28-results.md` as it occurs.

## THREAD 4: Eyes Up / ICE Sightings App — Accurate Status

### What the Injunction Actually Does

This thread corrects an error in `mayday-2026-action-guide.md` Section 4 (Resources). The current text states the apps "are now restored under court protection." That statement is inaccurate. The full picture:

**What the N.D. Illinois injunction (Judge Alonso, April 17-23, 2026) actually does:**
- Blocks the Trump administration from pressuring Apple and Facebook to remove ICE-monitoring content in the future
- Provides the app developers legal cover to negotiate with Apple/Facebook for restoration
- Does NOT compel Apple or Facebook to restore the apps — platform restoration is each company's editorial prerogative
- Does NOT automatically reinstate the apps to the App Store or on Facebook

**Actual status as of April 27:**
- Eyes Up: removed from Apple App Store in October 2025; injunction does not require Apple to restore it; Apple has not announced restoration; the company now has legal cover to restore without government retaliation, but has not done so publicly
- ICE Sightings - Chicagoland (Facebook group): disabled October 14, 2025; same situation — Facebook has legal cover to restore, but no announcement of restoration confirmed
- The court has not yet issued the final terms of the injunction; terms were described as "forthcoming" as of April 20

**What this means for May Day participants:**
The apps cannot be reliably directed to as active safety resources on May 1. Participants who have already downloaded Eyes Up before October 2025 may retain access to prior versions depending on their device state. New downloads are not reliably available. The ICE Sightings - Chicagoland Facebook group cannot be accessed in its original form.

**Alternative community monitoring tools that are confirmed operational:**
- Alianza Americas rapid-response network: [alianzaamericas.org](https://alianzaamericas.org)
- United We Dream rapid-response and ICE sightings network: [unitedwedream.org/take-action/ice-raid-response/](https://unitedwedream.org/take-action/ice-raid-response/)
- City-specific rapid-response hotlines (listed in Action Guide Section 4)
- No Kings Coalition "Eyes on ICE" training community (200,000+ trained as of April 2026)
