## Pre-Call Context (as of April 26)

### What This Call Is

The April 29 Mass Call is the final national coordination checkpoint before May Day 2026. It is the primary channel through which the May Day Strong coalition — which coordinates across AFL-CIO state federations, Indivisible, NEA, NNU, People's Action, and hundreds of local groups — will synchronize on logistics, safety, and any last-minute coalition announcements.

**Why this call matters more than prior coordination calls**: The scale of the May Day action has grown significantly since earlier coordination calls. With 922+ confirmed events and 1 million+ union members mobilized through the NJ AFL-CIO alone, the call is the last structured moment before the action where the coalition can shift guidance or respond to developments from the April 28 Xinis hearing.

### What to Watch For Specifically

**Highest-priority watch item: AFL-CIO national endorsement.** The national AFL-CIO federation has not as of April 26 issued a formal May Day strike call or formal endorsement. If that endorsement comes, it would likely arrive at this call given the timing. An AFL-CIO national call would add potentially millions of union members to the labor action component and would be the single most significant pre-May Day development.

**Second priority: Safety guidance updates.** Anything resulting from the April 28 Xinis hearing (contempt order, escalation, DOJ emergency appeal) could affect organizer safety guidance. Watch for:
- Any guidance about the ICE enforcement posture on May 1 in specific cities
- Updated information on Eyes Up and ICE Sightings app restoration
- Guidance on what to do if approached by ICE at a protest

**Third priority: Last-minute logistics changes.** In cities with permitted marches, route changes can come late. NYC, Chicago, LA, Seattle, and Portland OR are the highest-attendance events; any logistics change in those cities should be recorded.
