# May 17-18 Breaking Developments Integration

## Executive Summary

Two developments since the last domain update cycle are material enough to require integration before Wave 1 distribution: (1) the Supreme Court's April 29 ruling in *Louisiana v. Callais* (6-3) effectively gutting Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act, with immediate downstream gerrymanders already enacted in Florida, Louisiana election suspensions, and Alabama primary delays — this is the single most significant voting-rights rollback since *Shelby County v. Holder* (2013) and must be reflected in Domain 1 and Domain 37; and (2) the Montana First Judicial District Court's May 11 injunction blocking SB 490 (Election Day registration restriction targeting Native voters) updates Domain 58 with a positive development that partially offsets the VRA gutting. Domains 57 and 37 have significant ongoing developments (Hungary ICC withdrawal effective June 2, DOJ voter roll litigation with 5 dismissals and ongoing appeals) that are not strictly "breaking" May 17-18 news but require confirmation that existing domain content reflects the current state.

## Domain 37 — Federal Executive Interference in 2026 Midterms

### Findings

- **DOJ voter roll litigation — 30 states sued, 5 dismissals, ongoing appeals.** As of mid-May 2026, the DOJ has sued 30 states and D.C. demanding unredacted voter registration files including SSNs and driver's license numbers. Federal district courts in California (Jan 2026), Michigan, Oregon, Massachusetts (Apr 9, 2026), Rhode Island, and Arizona (Apr 28, 2026) have dismissed DOJ lawsuits. The DOJ has appealed. A federal judge ruled the administration "may no longer be trusted" in this litigation. Common Cause and ACLU filed suit April 21, 2026 to block creation of a national voter database. Source: [Brennan Center tracker](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/tracker-justice-department-requests-voter-information); [ACLU press release](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/voting-rights-groups-sue-doj-to-block-national-voter-surveil-and-purge-database); [Arizona dismissal, Votebeat](https://www.votebeat.org/arizona/2026/04/28/department-justice-trump-adrian-fontes-unredacted-voter-rolls-lawsuit-dismissed-brnovich-ruling/); [Democracy Docket — judge trust ruling](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/federal-judge-rules-doj-can-no-longer-be-trusted-in-voter-roll-crusade/)

- **DOJ emergency motions explicitly threaten midterm legitimacy.** In the California, Michigan, and Oregon proceedings, DOJ filed emergency motions arguing that without the voter data, it "cannot ensure a fair election in 2026." Direct quote from court filings: "Absent a final Court determination on this matter there is no other process to ensure a fair election in 2026." This is the administration pre-seeding a legitimacy-challenge narrative for November. Source: [Stateline](https://stateline.org/2026/03/13/in-bid-for-voter-data-trumps-doj-lays-groundwork-to-question-midterm-results/)

- **CISA election security gutted.** Since February 2026, CISA has cut more than a third of its workforce and halted most programs working on elections — red teams, incident response units, and regional election security advisors. The FY2027 Trump budget proposes eliminating CISA's election security program entirely. Senate Intelligence Committee Vice Chairman Mark Warner formally demanded answers from DHS in May 2026. Source: [Nextgov/FCW — FY27 budget](https://www.nextgov.com/cybersecurity/2026/04/trump-proposes-cutting-cisa-election-security-program-fy27-budget/412672/); [Defense One — senator warning](https://www.defenseone.com/threats/2026/05/cisa-election-security-vulnerable/413410/); [Brennan Center — federal undermining report](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/how-federal-government-undermining-election-security)

- **Election denier candidates: 53+ running for statewide office in 24 states.** States United Democracy Center's ElectionDeniers.org tracking for 2026 identifies 53+ election deniers running for governor, secretary of state, or attorney general in 24 states, including Arizona, Georgia, and Michigan. Federal-level: Heather Honey (Election Integrity Network) placed at DHS; Kari Lake appointed senior adviser to U.S. Agency for Global Media. Source: [States United press release](https://statesunited.org/press-release-2026-election-deniers/); [NPR — candidates running](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/04/nx-s1-5803149/election-denial-2026-candidates); [Common Dreams](https://www.commondreams.org/news/election-deniers-2026-midterms)

- **ICE at polling places — active threat, not yet materialized.** Acting ICE chief stated "no reason" for agents at polling places; DHS officially denied deployment plans. However: Steve Bannon publicly cited ICE airport operations as "perfect training for fall 2026"; DHS Secretary nominee Mullin refused to rule it out at confirmation hearings; blue-state legislatures in 8+ states are moving preemptive bans (already illegal under federal law). Nevada and Arizona bills are active as of May 2026. Source: [Democracy Docket — ICE chief admission](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/ice-chief-federal-immmigration-agents-polling-places-2026-midterms/); [Stateline — blue states push](https://stateline.org/2026/03/05/blue-states-push-to-ban-ice-at-the-polls-amid-federal-voter-intimidation-fears/); [Brennan Center — legality analysis](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/sending-ice-polling-places-illegal); [Nevada Current — May 6](https://nevadacurrent.com/2026/05/06/ice-at-polling-places-is-against-federal-and-state-law-lombardo-should-say-so/)

## Domain 1 — Voting Rights

### Findings

- **SCOTUS *Louisiana v. Callais* (April 29, 2026) — Section 2 VRA effectively gutted.** In a 6-3 decision (Alito, majority; Kagan, dissent joined by Sotomayor and Jackson), the Court struck down Louisiana's second majority-Black congressional district and rewrote the Section 2 standard. Challengers must now show "evidence [that] supports a strong inference that the State intentionally drew its districts to afford minority voters less opportunity because of their race" — an intent-based standard that replaces the prior results test. Justice Thomas concurred separately, calling for full Section 2 elimination. The Campaign Legal Center states the ruling "eviscerated Section 2... the greatest achievement of the Civil Rights Movement." The Court simultaneously ordered the decision to take immediate effect, refusing to stay it pending appeals. Sources: [SCOTUSblog case page](https://www.scotusblog.com/cases/louisiana-v-callais-2/); [SCOTUS opinion PDF](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/25pdf/24-109_21o3.pdf); [Campaign Legal Center](https://campaignlegal.org/update/us-supreme-court-has-eviscerated-voting-rights-act-whats-next); [Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/louisiana-v-callais); [Al Jazeera explainer](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/30/has-the-us-supreme-court-weakened-the-voting-rights-act-and-how)

- **Immediate downstream gerrymanders activated.** Within one hour of the *Callais* ruling, the Florida House voted 83-28 to pass a DeSantis-backed redistricting map designed to add four Republican House seats; the Senate passed it 21-17; DeSantis signed May 4. Analysts project the map could net Republicans 24 of 28 Florida congressional districts. Legal challenges filed by voting rights groups in Florida state court, arguing violation of the Florida Constitution's anti-partisan-gerrymandering provision. Sources: [Democracy Docket — Florida gerrymander](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/an-hour-after-scotus-guts-voting-rights-act-florida-house-passes-gop-gerrymander/); [NPR — Florida map](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/29/nx-s1-5804703/florida-redistricting-voting-map-republicans-house-seats); [Votebeat — redistricting landscape](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/05/04/florida-redistricting-supreme-court-louisiana-callais-gerrymander-2026-election/)

- **Louisiana House elections (May 16) suspended; Alabama primaries delayed to August.** Gov. Jeff Landry suspended Louisiana House elections originally scheduled for May 16 following the ruling. Alabama pushed back primaries in affected districts to August. These are among the first concrete electoral consequences of *Callais*. Source: [Stateline — election chaos](https://stateline.org/2026/05/04/supreme-court-voting-rights-ruling-set-to-reshape-local-power-from-statehouses-to-school-boards/); [NBC News — redistricting criticism](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/supreme-court/supreme-court-criticism-redistricting-law-election-2026-voting-rights-rcna344734)

- **Ruling applies to state legislative and local maps.** The *Callais* decision is not limited to congressional districts — it governs all redistricting under Section 2, including state legislative chambers, county commissions, and school boards. The Virginia Mercury (May 11) reported Virginia legislative leaders warning minority representation could erode at all levels. Source: [Virginia Mercury](https://virginiamercury.com/2026/05/11/after-scotus-voting-rights-ruling-virginia-leaders-warn-minority-representation-could-erode/)

- **Arkanas, Missouri, Mississippi, South Carolina identified as next gerrymander targets.** Multiple analysts and voting rights organizations have flagged these states as likely to pass new maps now that the VRA's Section 2 barrier has been substantially lowered. No bills yet passed as of May 17, but legislative activity is expected before the November filing deadlines. Source: [NBC News — what the ruling means for 2026](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2026-election/supreme-courts-ruling-race-redistricting-means-2026-elections-rcna342712)

### Integration Notes

This is a major update for Domain 1. If existing Domain 1 content was drafted before April 29, 2026, the *Callais* ruling must be added as a primary development — it is the most significant VRA rollback since *Shelby County* (2013) and directly affects the 2026 midterm map landscape. Specific integration points: (a) any section on Section 2 litigation should reflect the new intent-based standard; (b) any discussion of redistricting remedies should note that majority-minority districts are now legally vulnerable; (c) the Florida, Louisiana, and Alabama cascades are concrete evidence of immediate harm. Urgency flag: Florida voting rights groups are likely to seek emergency injunctions — this case could be in front of SCOTUS again before November 2026.

## Domain 57 — Multilateral Withdrawal

### Findings

- **Hungary ICC withdrawal effective June 2, 2026.** Hungary notified the UN Secretary-General on June 2, 2025; withdrawal takes effect June 2, 2026 under Article 127 of the Rome Statute. Hungary becomes the first EU member state to exit the Rome Statute. The withdrawal was directly motivated by ICC arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu and other Israeli officials, issued after Netanyahu's state visit to Budapest in early April 2025 where Orbán hosted him despite the warrant. The Assembly of States Parties presidency expressed formal regret. Source: [ICC press release](https://www.icc-cpi.int/news/president-assembly-states-parties-regrets-withdrawal-rome-statute-hungary); [Coalition for the ICC](https://coalitionfortheicc.org/news/hungary-withdraws-icc-rome-statute); [ASIL](https://www.asil.org/ILIB/hungary-withdraws-international-criminal-court); [JURIST](https://www.jurist.org/news/2025/06/hungary-condemned-over-withdrawal-from-icc/)

- **Sahel states (Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger) announced withdrawal September 22, 2025 but have not formally notified the UN.** The Alliance of Sahel States declared they were leaving the ICC "with immediate effect" on September 22, 2025, but have not submitted formal notification to the UN Treaty Office as of early 2026. Withdrawal only becomes legally effective one year after formal notification. Until notification is submitted and the year elapses, the AES states remain legally bound to cooperate with the ICC. Source: [TRIAL International](https://trialinternational.org/latest-post/sahel-states-icc-withdrawal-a-step-back-for-victims-and-justice/); [The Africa Report — delayed formalization](https://www.theafricareport.com/404763/bluffing-or-just-delayed-mali-burkina-faso-and-niger-have-yet-to-formalise-their-withdrawal-from-the-icc/); [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/09/24/sahel-countries-icc-withdrawal-endangers-civilians)

- **US sanctions on ICC: two rounds since June 2025.** ICC Chief Prosecutor Karim Khan was sanctioned under Trump's February 6, 2025 executive order (OFAC SDN list). Additional sanctions were imposed on two more ICC judges over Israel-related cases. Khan stepped aside in early 2025 pending a sexual misconduct investigation. The ICC faces "intense political pressure, including two rounds of U.S. sanctions since June 2025." Source: [Stimson Center — UN accountability analysis](https://www.stimson.org/2026/the-united-nations-was-built-to-deliver-accountability-can-it-still-do-so/); [Times of Israel — two more judges](https://www.timesofisrael.com/us-slaps-sanctions-on-two-more-icc-judges-over-cases-against-israel/); [White House EO](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/02/imposing-sanctions-on-the-international-criminal-court/)

- **Duterte ICC case active in May 2026.** The East Asia Forum (May 1, 2026) reported on the Duterte ICC case and the broader question of whether justice can be achieved when powerful states refuse to cooperate. Philippines rejoined the ICC in 2023; the Duterte case is in pretrial. Source: [East Asia Forum](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/01/dutertes-icc-case-questions-whether-justice-can-be-done/)

- **No new UN withdrawal announcements May 17-18.** No evidence of additional UN agency withdrawals or new ICC departure notifications in the May 17-18 window specifically. The Hungary effective date (June 2) is 16 days away — the most time-sensitive development in this domain.

### Integration Notes

Domain 57 should note: (a) Hungary's withdrawal is now imminent (June 2) rather than announced — this is a status change from when the domain was likely drafted; (b) the Sahel states' formalization gap is a nuance worth capturing — the announced withdrawal may effectively be a political gesture that doesn't translate to legal exit; (c) the two-round US sanctions pattern is documented and should be reflected if not already present. No urgent integration is required for Wave 1 timing, but the Hungary effective date is a hook for near-term news value.

## Domain 58 — Tribal Sovereignty

### Findings

- **Montana SB 490 enjoined May 11, 2026 — win for Native voters.** Judge Adam Larsen of the Montana First Judicial District Court granted a preliminary injunction against SB 490, which would have eliminated Election Day voter registration after noon — the precise window when Native American voters in rural tribal communities disproportionately register. Plaintiffs include Western Native Voice, Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes, Blackfeet Nation, Fort Belknap Indian Community, and Northern Cheyenne Tribe. The court found the state "has not presented competent evidence demonstrating that SB 490 reduces administrative burdens or improves election efficiency." Source: [Democracy Docket](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/montana-court-blocks-republican-backed-attack-on-election-day-registration/); [NARF](https://narf.org/montana-voting-2025/); [Daily Montanan](https://dailymontanan.com/2026/05/11/montana-district-court-blocks-law-restricting-voter-registration-hours-allows-voter-id-law/); [Montana Free Press](https://montanafreepress.org/2026/05/11/judge-blocks-election-day-voter-registration-changes/)

- **Montana SB 276 (voter ID expansion) upheld in same ruling.** The same May 11 ruling declined to block SB 276, which expanded photo ID requirements but also added tribal photo ID cards and military IDs to the accepted list. The court found no evidence yet that eligible voters had been denied the right to vote under SB 276. This is a mixed outcome: the restriction was blocked, but the ID expansion (with its accompanying risk of suppression for voters without compliant IDs) stands. Source: [Daily Montanan](https://dailymontanan.com/2026/05/11/montana-district-court-blocks-law-restricting-voter-registration-hours-allows-voter-id-law/)

- **BIA workforce reduced 13% overall; regional offices down 22-29%.** A GAO report (January 2026, GAO-26-108673) documented: BIA lost 13% of workforce since January 2025; Office of the Assistant Secretary for Indian Affairs lost 27%; Pacific Regional Office down 29%; Southern Plains down 26%; Alaska Regional Office down 22%. National Congress of American Indians president Mark Macarro stated the reorganization was conducted "without consultation with tribal nations." Source: [Alaska Beacon](https://alaskabeacon.com/2026/03/31/bureau-of-indian-affairs-could-face-reorganization-deeper-staff-cuts/); [Native News Online](https://nativenewsonline.net/sovereignty/bureau-of-indian-affairs-could-face-reorganization-deeper-staff-cuts/); [GAO report](https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-26-108673/index.html)

- **Trump FY2027 budget proposes deeper BIA cuts.** The FY2027 budget submission proposes additional deep cuts to Native American programs. Tribal business news and legal advisors are tracking this. Source: [Tribal Business News](https://tribalbusinessnews.com/sections/policy-and-law/15123-trump-budget-proposes-deep-cuts-to-native-american-programs); [Skenandore Wilson legal analysis](https://www.skenandorewilson.com/post/president-s-fy-2026-budget-proposes-deep-cuts-to-indian-affairs-programs)

- **Montana voter ID: tribal ID cards now accepted.** SB 276's inclusion of tribal photo ID cards as accepted identification is a partial protection for Native voters in the ID expansion — but the law still increases the documentary burden relative to prior rules and the injunction against SB 490 may face appeal.

- **No new NARF-filed cases or BIA actions specifically dated May 17-18.** The most recent breaking development in this domain is the May 11 SB 490 injunction. No new tribal sovereignty court rulings or federal BIA actions were identified in the May 17-18 window specifically.

### Integration Notes

Domain 58 should integrate: (a) the SB 490 injunction as a current positive development — it directly validates the domain's analysis of voter suppression targeting Native voters; (b) the SB 276 mixed ruling — the partial uphold of voter ID expansion is a note of caution alongside the SB 490 win; (c) the BIA workforce data from GAO-26-108673 is now the authoritative source for the 13%/27% figures if the domain previously relied on estimates; (d) if Domain 58 mentions Montana SB 490 as a "pending threat," update to "enjoined as of May 11, 2026, subject to potential state appeal." The NARF case page (Northern Cheyenne Tribe v. Montana) is the canonical tracking source.
