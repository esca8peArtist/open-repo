## Domain 1 — Voting Rights

### Findings

- **SCOTUS *Louisiana v. Callais* (April 29, 2026) — Section 2 VRA effectively gutted.** In a 6-3 decision (Alito, majority; Kagan, dissent joined by Sotomayor and Jackson), the Court struck down Louisiana's second majority-Black congressional district and rewrote the Section 2 standard. Challengers must now show "evidence [that] supports a strong inference that the State intentionally drew its districts to afford minority voters less opportunity because of their race" — an intent-based standard that replaces the prior results test. Justice Thomas concurred separately, calling for full Section 2 elimination. The Campaign Legal Center states the ruling "eviscerated Section 2... the greatest achievement of the Civil Rights Movement." The Court simultaneously ordered the decision to take immediate effect, refusing to stay it pending appeals. Sources: [SCOTUSblog case page](https://www.scotusblog.com/cases/louisiana-v-callais-2/); [SCOTUS opinion PDF](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/25pdf/24-109_21o3.pdf); [Campaign Legal Center](https://campaignlegal.org/update/us-supreme-court-has-eviscerated-voting-rights-act-whats-next); [Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/louisiana-v-callais); [Al Jazeera explainer](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/30/has-the-us-supreme-court-weakened-the-voting-rights-act-and-how)

- **Immediate downstream gerrymanders activated.** Within one hour of the *Callais* ruling, the Florida House voted 83-28 to pass a DeSantis-backed redistricting map designed to add four Republican House seats; the Senate passed it 21-17; DeSantis signed May 4. Analysts project the map could net Republicans 24 of 28 Florida congressional districts. Legal challenges filed by voting rights groups in Florida state court, arguing violation of the Florida Constitution's anti-partisan-gerrymandering provision. Sources: [Democracy Docket — Florida gerrymander](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/an-hour-after-scotus-guts-voting-rights-act-florida-house-passes-gop-gerrymander/); [NPR — Florida map](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/29/nx-s1-5804703/florida-redistricting-voting-map-republicans-house-seats); [Votebeat — redistricting landscape](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/05/04/florida-redistricting-supreme-court-louisiana-callais-gerrymander-2026-election/)

- **Louisiana House elections (May 16) suspended; Alabama primaries delayed to August.** Gov. Jeff Landry suspended Louisiana House elections originally scheduled for May 16 following the ruling. Alabama pushed back primaries in affected districts to August. These are among the first concrete electoral consequences of *Callais*. Source: [Stateline — election chaos](https://stateline.org/2026/05/04/supreme-court-voting-rights-ruling-set-to-reshape-local-power-from-statehouses-to-school-boards/); [NBC News — redistricting criticism](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/supreme-court/supreme-court-criticism-redistricting-law-election-2026-voting-rights-rcna344734)

- **Ruling applies to state legislative and local maps.** The *Callais* decision is not limited to congressional districts — it governs all redistricting under Section 2, including state legislative chambers, county commissions, and school boards. The Virginia Mercury (May 11) reported Virginia legislative leaders warning minority representation could erode at all levels. Source: [Virginia Mercury](https://virginiamercury.com/2026/05/11/after-scotus-voting-rights-ruling-virginia-leaders-warn-minority-representation-could-erode/)

- **Arkanas, Missouri, Mississippi, South Carolina identified as next gerrymander targets.** Multiple analysts and voting rights organizations have flagged these states as likely to pass new maps now that the VRA's Section 2 barrier has been substantially lowered. No bills yet passed as of May 17, but legislative activity is expected before the November filing deadlines. Source: [NBC News — what the ruling means for 2026](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2026-election/supreme-courts-ruling-race-redistricting-means-2026-elections-rcna342712)

### Integration Notes

This is a major update for Domain 1. If existing Domain 1 content was drafted before April 29, 2026, the *Callais* ruling must be added as a primary development — it is the most significant VRA rollback since *Shelby County* (2013) and directly affects the 2026 midterm map landscape. Specific integration points: (a) any section on Section 2 litigation should reflect the new intent-based standard; (b) any discussion of redistricting remedies should note that majority-minority districts are now legally vulnerable; (c) the Florida, Louisiana, and Alabama cascades are concrete evidence of immediate harm. Urgency flag: Florida voting rights groups are likely to seek emergency injunctions — this case could be in front of SCOTUS again before November 2026.

## Domain 57 — Multilateral Withdrawal

Hungary becomes the first EU member state to exit the Rome Statute. The withdrawal was directly motivated by ICC arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu and other Israeli officials, issued after Netanyahu's state visit to Budapest in early April 2025 where Orbán hosted him despite the warrant. The Assembly of States Parties presidency expressed formal regret. Source: [ICC press release](https://www.icc-cpi.int/news/president-assembly-states-parties-regrets-withdrawal-rome-statute-hungary); [Coalition for the ICC](https://coalitionfortheicc.org/news/hungary-withdraws-icc-rome-statute); [ASIL](https://www.asil.org/ILIB/hungary-withdraws-international-criminal-court); [JURIST](https://www.jurist.org/news/2025/06/hungary-condemned-over-withdrawal-from-icc/)

Withdrawal only becomes legally effective one year after formal notification. Until notification is submitted and the year elapses, the AES states remain legally bound to cooperate with the ICC. Source: [TRIAL International](https://trialinternational.org/latest-post/sahel-states-icc-withdrawal-a-step-back-for-victims-and-justice/); [The Africa Report — delayed formalization](https://www.theafricareport.com/404763/bluffing-or-just-delayed-mali-burkina-faso-and-niger-have-yet-to-formalise-their-withdrawal-from-the-icc/); [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/09/24/sahel-countries-icc-withdrawal-endangers-civilians)

Additional sanctions were imposed on two more ICC judges over Israel-related cases. Khan stepped aside in early 2025 pending a sexual misconduct investigation. The ICC faces "intense political pressure, including two rounds of U.S. sanctions since June 2025." ## Domain 58 — Tribal Sovereignty

### Findings

- **Montana SB 490 enjoined May 11, 2026 — win for Native voters.** Judge Adam Larsen of the Montana First Judicial District Court granted a preliminary injunction against SB 490, which would have eliminated Election Day voter registration after noon — the precise window when Native American voters in rural tribal communities disproportionately register. Plaintiffs include Western Native Voice, Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes, Blackfeet Nation, Fort Belknap Indian Community, and Northern Cheyenne Tribe. The court found the state "has not presented competent evidence demonstrating that SB 490 reduces administrative burdens or improves election efficiency." Source: [Democracy Docket](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/montana-court-blocks-republican-backed-attack-on-election-day-registration/); [NARF](https://narf.org/montana-voting-2025/); [Daily Montanan](https://dailymontanan.com/2026/05/11/montana-district-court-blocks-law-restricting-voter-registration-hours-allows-voter-id-law/); [Montana Free Press](https://montanafreepress.org/2026/05/11/judge-blocks-election-day-voter-registration-changes/)

- **Montana SB 276 (voter ID expansion) upheld in same ruling.** The same May 11 ruling declined to block SB 276, which expanded photo ID requirements but also added tribal photo ID cards and military IDs to the accepted list. The court found no evidence yet that eligible voters had been denied the right to vote under SB 276. This is a mixed outcome: the restriction was blocked, but the ID expansion (with its accompanying risk of suppression for voters without compliant IDs) stands. Source: [Daily Montanan](https://dailymontanan.com/2026/05/11/montana-district-court-blocks-law-restricting-voter-registration-hours-allows-voter-id-law/)

- **BIA workforce reduced 13% overall; regional offices down 22-29%.** A GAO report (January 2026, GAO-26-108673) documented: BIA lost 13% of workforce since January 2025; Office of the Assistant Secretary for Indian Affairs lost 27%; Pacific Regional Office down 29%; Southern Plains down 26%; Alaska Regional Office down 22%. National Congress of American Indians president Mark Macarro stated the reorganization was conducted "without consultation with tribal nations." Source: [Alaska Beacon](https://alaskabeacon.com/2026/03/31/bureau-of-indian-affairs-could-face-reorganization-deeper-staff-cuts/); [Native News Online](https://nativenewsonline.net/sovereignty/bureau-of-indian-affairs-could-face-reorganization-deeper-staff-cuts/); [GAO report](https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-26-108673/index.html)

- **Trump FY2027 budget proposes deeper BIA cuts.** The FY2027 budget submission proposes additional deep cuts to Native American programs. Tribal business news and legal advisors are tracking this. Source: [Tribal Business News](https://tribalbusinessnews.com/sections/policy-and-law/15123-trump-budget-proposes-deep-cuts-to-native-american-programs); [Skenandore Wilson legal analysis](https://www.skenandorewilson.com/post/president-s-fy-2026-budget-proposes-deep-cuts-to-indian-affairs-programs)

- **Montana voter ID: tribal ID cards now accepted.** SB 276's inclusion of tribal photo ID cards as accepted identification is a partial protection for Native voters in the ID expansion — but the law still increases the documentary burden relative to prior rules and the injunction against SB 490 may face appeal.

- **No new NARF-filed cases or BIA actions specifically dated May 17-18.** The most recent breaking development in this domain is the May 11 SB 490 injunction. No new tribal sovereignty court rulings or federal BIA actions were identified in the May 17-18 window specifically.

### Integration Notes

Domain 58 should integrate: (a) the SB 490 injunction as a current positive development — it directly validates the domain's analysis of voter suppression targeting Native voters; (b) the SB 276 mixed ruling — the partial uphold of voter ID expansion is a note of caution alongside the SB 490 win; (c) the BIA workforce data from GAO-26-108673 is now the authoritative source for the 13%/27% figures if the domain previously relied on estimates; (d) if Domain 58 mentions Montana SB 490 as a "pending threat," update to "enjoined as of May 11, 2026, subject to potential state appeal." The NARF case page (Northern Cheyenne Tribe v. Montana) is the canonical tracking source.
