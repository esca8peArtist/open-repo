# Trump v. Barbara Rapid-Response Protocol

*Decision tree for Domain 58 distribution upon Supreme Court ruling in No. 25-365.*

---

## How to Use This Document

When the ruling issues, read the opinion's holding section and any tribal-specific language, then follow the branch that matches the outcome. All three branches have pre-staged distribution materials in the Domain 58 production files. This protocol specifies timing, framing, and sequencing only — content is already written.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Ruling Type

Check two questions immediately upon ruling:

**Q1**: Did the Court uphold EO 14160 in any form?
- If yes, go to Branch C (Adverse).
- If no, continue to Q2.

**Q2**: Does the majority opinion include any language characterizing tribal Indians as a "historically excluded" class, validating the statutory-citizenship theory, or citing *Elk v. Wilkins* favorably?
- If yes to either part of Q2, treat as Branch B-Hybrid (described in Branch B below).
- If no, go to Branch A (Favorable) or Branch B (Narrow), depending on how broadly the Court addressed the tribal analogy.

---

## Branch A: Broad Reaffirmation

**Trigger condition**: Court rejects EO 14160 and explicitly repudiates the administration's use of *Elk v. Wilkins* as a historical analogy, or affirmatively holds that tribal citizenship is constitutionally grounded (not merely statutory).

**Probability**: Low-moderate. Court-watchers expect a narrow majority that avoids the tribal question where possible.

**Framing message**: "The Court reaffirmed that tribal citizenship is settled constitutional law. Here is why that victory is necessary but not sufficient — and why the three remaining fronts of the tribal sovereignty assault still require immediate action."

**Day 1 actions (same-day)**:
1. Distribute Domain 58 executive summary to Tier 1 contacts (NARF, NCAI, Campaign Legal Center, Native Vote Alliance).
2. Issue press release version to Native News Online and Indian Country Today with the ruling as the hook.
3. Distribute "Sovereignty and Franchise" cluster — Domain 58 + Domain 49 (*Callais*) + Domain 1 — to NAVRC membership.

**Day 2–3 actions**:
4. Contact Senate Indian Affairs Committee: the ruling validates the constitutional foundation for NAVRA; Congress should act now.
5. Distribute Domain 58 to mainstream democratic reform distribution list (Brennan Center, Lawyers' Committee, ACLU VRP, Open Society democracy funders).
6. Brief the legal clinic network (ASU, University of Montana, UNM, UND, University of Arizona) for academic amplification.

**Legislative activation**: Use the ruling as the trigger for the NAVRA legislative push. Frame to Senate Indian Affairs Committee as: "The Court protected the floor. Now Congress must build on it."

## Branch B: Narrow Rejection (Most Likely)

**Trigger condition**: Court rejects EO 14160 primarily on *Wong Kim Ark* grounds, without extensively addressing the tribal analogy — or addresses it inconclusively, neither fully validating nor fully repudiating the statutory-citizenship theory.

**Probability**: High. This is the expected outcome based on oral argument analysis and bench alignment.

**B-Hybrid note**: If the opinion rejects the EO but includes tribal-Indian dicta that could be weaponized by future litigants (e.g., language calling tribal members a "historically excluded" class), treat it as follows: immediately flag the dicta to NARF and NCAI for legal assessment. Ask them to evaluate whether the language creates a litigation risk. Do not publicly characterize the ruling as adverse without legal review — but do not distribute it as a clean win either. Issue a holding statement within 48 hours and follow up with NARF's assessment within 72 hours.

**Framing message**: "The Court preserved the constitutional floor — but the floor is still under attack through three other channels. Here is the full scope of the ongoing assault and what advocacy looks like now."

**Day 1–2 actions (within 48 hours)**:
1. Distribute Domain 58 to Tier 1 contacts with the ruling as the hook.
2. Issue a factual summary of the ruling's holding and what it does and does not address tribally.
3. Pivot the narrative to the *Callais* cascade and the NAVRA legislative gap: "The citizenship threat has been parried for now. The redistricting and voter registration threats have not."

**Day 3–7 actions**:
4. Distribute full Domain 58 to Tier 2 contacts (Brennan Center, ACLU VRP, Lawyers' Committee, NAACP LDF).
5. Proceed to July 15 baseline distribution schedule.
6. Activate August appropriations advocacy window (Senate Indian Affairs, Interior Appropriations Subcommittee).

## Branch C: Adverse Ruling

**Trigger condition**: Court upholds EO 14160 in any form, or issues an opinion with language validating any part of Sauer's theory that tribal Indians are not birthright citizens under the Fourteenth Amendment.

**Probability**: Low. Oral argument analysis indicates the government is unlikely to win more than two conservative votes (Gorsuch and possibly Thomas) for a position that validates the tribal exclusion theory. A 5-4 adverse ruling would require Barrett and Kavanaugh joining — which oral argument did not suggest.

**Framing message**: "Emergency. The ruling creates an immediate documentation threat to tribal voter franchise. Here is the legal architecture for response."

**Within 24 hours**:
1. Distribute Domain 58 emergency summary to NARF, NCAI, and all tribal legal offices.
2. Contact Senate Indian Affairs Committee with a statutory reaffirmation ask: an Indian Citizenship Act reaffirmation amendment that explicitly grounds tribal citizenship in the Fourteenth Amendment, not merely in statute.
3. Contact Senate Judiciary Committee: the ruling's constitutional implications extend beyond immigration and warrant immediate legislative response.
4. Alert Democracy Docket and NARF litigation teams to monitor state officials who may attempt to impose documentation requirements on tribal voter registration using the ruling as authority.

**Within 72 hours**:
5. Distribute to full distribution list — do not wait for the July 15 schedule.
6. Prepare the "three-front assault" framing: the ruling is a defeat on one front; *Callais* and DOGE defunding represent the other two, and all three require simultaneous response.
7. Contact Native Vote Alliance for emergency 2026 midterm voter registration guidance: what documentation will suffice in which states; what litigation tools remain available; what state constitutional alternatives exist.

**Legislative target**: Indian Citizenship Act Reaffirmation Act — a one-page bill explicitly holding that citizenship under the Indian Citizenship Act of 1924 is constitutionally grounded in the Fourteenth Amendment. Sponsor targets: Senate Indian Affairs Committee Chair + Ranking Member; House Natural Resources Indian Affairs Subcommittee.

## Key Background Facts for Rapid-Response Messaging

- **Case number**: No. 25-365
- **Argued**: April 1, 2026
- **The tribal issue**: Solicitor General Sauer stated at oral argument that children of tribal Indians are "not birthright citizens" under the Fourteenth Amendment — grounding tribal citizenship in the 1924 statute rather than the Constitution
- **What this threatens**: If tribal citizenship is statutory, not constitutional, it can be legislatively repealed or challenged in court; tribal members whose parents cannot document their own citizenship status face documentation burdens for voter registration
- **Historical context**: The Indian Citizenship Act of 1924 was Congress's response to *Elk v. Wilkins* (1884) — the same case Sauer cited to justify ending birthright citizenship for undocumented immigrants
- **Concurrent context**: *Callais* (April 29, 2026) eliminated VRA Section 2 discriminatory-effects standard; *Turtle Mountain v. Howe* GVR'd May 18, 2026; Montana SB 490 enjoined May 11, 2026 — three active fronts beyond *Trump v. Barbara*
- **4.7 million**: Eligible-but-unregistered Native voters — the registration gap that closes Senate races in Montana, North Dakota, South Dakota, Arizona, and Oklahoma
