## Tier 1 Messaging Hooks (Ready-to-Deploy)

These are the three core Tier 1 messages pre-drafted for rapid-response deployment. Select based on ruling outcome.

### Hook 1 — For Broad Reaffirmation (Branch A)

"The Supreme Court just protected the constitutional foundation of Native American citizenship — and exposed exactly how much work remains. The ruling in Trump v. Barbara closed one front of a three-front assault on tribal democratic capacity. The other two fronts — the *Callais* destruction of VRA Section 2 protection for tribal redistricting, and the DOGE defunding of the tribal governmental infrastructure through which 574 sovereign nations deliver services and hold elections — are still fully operational. Here is the full scope of what was won, what wasn't, and what needs to happen in the next 90 days."

**Why this works**: Converts a legal victory into continued urgency. Avoids triumphalism that would reduce engagement. Positions Domain 58's comprehensive analysis as the authoritative follow-up to the ruling's news cycle.

### Hook 2 — For Narrow Rejection (Branch B, Most Likely)

"The Supreme Court preserved the constitutional floor on birthright citizenship — but did not address the specific threat to tribal sovereignty embedded in the government's argument. Here is what the ruling means, what it doesn't resolve, and why three other fronts of the assault on tribal democratic capacity are still fully operational: the *Callais* redistricting erosion, DOGE's dismantling of BIA and IHS infrastructure, and the legislative void where the Native American Voting Rights Act should be."

**Why this works**: Provides genuine analytical value in a news environment where most coverage will focus only on the birthright citizenship headline. Reaches tribal advocacy organizations that need a sophisticated read of what the ruling does and does not accomplish.

### Hook 3 — For Adverse Outcome or Adverse Dicta (Branch C or B-Hybrid)

"The Supreme Court's ruling in Trump v. Barbara [upheld part of the citizenship restriction / included language suggesting tribal citizenship may be statutory rather than constitutional]. This is not primarily an immigration ruling. It is an attack on the legal foundation of Native American civic participation. Here is the legal architecture for immediate response — the Indian Citizenship Act Reaffirmation Act, the state constitutional alternatives to federal VRA enforcement, and the litigation pathways that remain available."

**Why this works**: Reframes the ruling from an immigration story to a tribal sovereignty and democratic participation story. Provides specific legislative action ask. Reaches both tribal legal organizations and mainstream democratic reform audiences.
