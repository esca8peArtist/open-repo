## Part I: Domain 49 — Environmental Justice and Democratic Participation

### Core Thesis (Do Not Lose This)

Environmental justice communities are democratic participants who have been systematically stripped of the legal and procedural tools through which they exercised civic power. The EPA's 2025–2026 infrastructure elimination is not deregulation — it is the coordinated removal of democratic participation infrastructure from the communities most dependent on it.

### Domain 49: Four Research Zones

---

#### Zone 1: Statutory Amendment Pathways
**Focus**: Marin Audubon v. FAA + Montana Callais convergence on same statutory conclusion

The central legal argument of Domain 49's reform architecture: executive restoration of CEQ NEPA regulations is legally insufficient because the D.C. Circuit held (Marin Audubon, November 2024, 2-1) that CEQ lacks statutory authority to issue binding NEPA regulations. A future favorable administration cannot restore participation rights through regulation alone. Statutory NEPA amendment is the only adequate remedy. Montana v. Callais (VRA, May 2026) reinforces this across the redistricting dimension: the same frontier communities that lost NEPA procedural standing are now positioned to claim VRA Section 2 redistricting protections as an electoral replacement mechanism — but only if the post-Callais statutory posture is exploited proactively.

**Source count**: 28 sources (Themes A, D, J from expanded index; N-series NEPA-ICE sources)

Primary sources in this zone:
- A1-A6 (Federal Register documents, Marin Audubon opinion, EOs 14173/14158)
- D1-D13 (legal analysis: Marin Audubon, Endangerment Finding, NEPA rescission — all major law firm analyses)
- J1-J3 (SPEED Act threat, SCOTUS 2025 NEPA ruling, ELI analysis)
- N4-N5 (Seven County SCOTUS — deference squared; CRS analysis)

**What Zone 1 must establish**:
1. Marin Audubon foreclosed CEQ regulatory pathway (not permanently, but currently)
2. The CEQ rescission and Seven County together removed both procedural requirement and judicial check
3. The Callais redistricting consequence: which frontline communities can now claim VRA protection
4. Statutory reform tier (NEPA amendment, IRA EJ entrenchment, Title VI codification) is the only reliable path
5. SPEED Act as the legislative threat that would permanently close the statutory reform pathway

---

#### Zone 2: VRA Redistricting Cascade
**Focus**: 2026 post-Callais opportunities for frontline community electoral power

The argument: EJ communities are disproportionately communities of color concentrated geographically. VRA Section 2 has historically been the primary federal tool through which those communities secured political representation capable of contesting environmental permit decisions at the legislative level. Louisiana v. Callais (May 2026) opens a redistricting window specifically applicable to EJ communities — if the post-Callais statutory posture is analyzed promptly and coalition resources are directed toward it before the 2026 midterm redistricting cycle closes.

**Source count**: 22 sources (Themes C, E/civil participation overlap, H state-level responses)

Primary sources in this zone:
- C1-C12 (civic participation nexus — the vicious cycle mechanism and its five causal pathways)
- H1-H9 (state-level EJ law responses — the redistricting-adjacent state legislative landscape)
- I1-I4 (reform architecture — cumulative impact bills as state alternative to federal VRA dependency)
- C3-C4 (2026 Civic Information Index, Appalachian/Black South civic health gaps)

**What Zone 2 must establish**:
1. The UCS vicious cycle (Pathway 1): pollution burden → chronic illness → depressed turnout → political disengagement → further environmental harm
2. Callais redistricting analysis: which specific EJ geographies are redistricting candidates
3. The electoral mechanism argument: VRA redistricting is not a substitute for NEPA participation rights, but is the only remaining federal mechanism for EJ community political power
4. State EJ law landscape: eight states with cumulative impact bills; the 42-state gap as the redistricting relevance map
5. Appalachian and Black South civic health data (2026 Civic Information Index) as geographic anchor

## Part II: Domain 50 — LGBTQ+ Rights and Democratic Participation

### Core Thesis (Do Not Lose This)

The attack on LGBTQ+ rights in 2026 is a legal infrastructure project, not a culture war. ADF deploys a coordinated model-legislation-plus-litigation-plus-judicial-appointment strategy that attacks democratic institutions simultaneously across multiple issue areas. Trans voters face a stacking suppression crisis — ID mismatch (210,800), EO 14168 document freeze, SAVE Act — at the same moment six anti-trans ballot measures target their legal existence. The ballot initiative channel is being weaponized as a voting suppression mechanism against a specific minority group.

### Domain 50: Four Research Zones

---

#### Zone 1: Trans Voter Suppression Mechanisms
**Focus**: SAVE Act, executive orders, state legislation — the compounding suppression stack

The empirical core of Domain 50's democratic participation argument. The Williams Institute 210,800 figure is the anchor; EO 14168's monthly growth of that figure is the escalation mechanism; the SAVE Act's passport-based registration requirement is the third suppression layer. The SCOTUS passport stay (June 2026) removed the federal court protection that had briefly restored one ID pathway. By November 2026, trans voters in anti-trans ballot measure states face documented triple-layer suppression in the same cycle where their existence is on the ballot.

**Source count**: 28 sources (Theme E trans voter suppression; I5 SCOTUS passport stay; E13 Roll Call deliberate strategy documentation)

Primary sources in this zone:
- E1-E14 (Williams Institute data, EO 14168 impact, SAVE Act analysis — full suppression documentation)
- I5 (SCOTUS passport stay — updated suppression stack status)
- E12, E13, E14 (Roll Call deliberate bundling; 19th News anti-trans SAVE Act additions; Padilla blocking effort)

**What Zone 1 must establish**:
1. The 210,800 baseline (Williams Institute, 2024) — must cite original publication, not secondhand
2. EO 14168 growing the figure monthly (passport freeze, Social Security freeze, DACA document freeze)
3. SAVE Act: current Senate status as of July 1 (Padilla blocking effort; route through Section 6.2 of runbook)
4. SCOTUS passport stay removes the only federal court restoration pathway (as of June 2026)
5. The suppression-suppression paradox: highest documented voting barrier in the cycle with six anti-trans ballot measures on the ballot in the same cycle

## Part III: Cross-Domain Integration Points

The following integration points are the structural connections between Domains 49 and 50 that enable paired distribution, prevent redundant research, and produce the synthesis narrative for December 2026.

---

### Integration Point 1: Health-to-Civic Suppression as Shared Mechanism

Domain 49 Pathway 1 (pollution burden → chronic illness → depressed voter turnout) and Domain 50 Pillar 6 (healthcare denial → mental health crisis → reduced civic capacity) run the same causal logic in opposite directions through the same affected populations. Healthcare access is a prerequisite for democratic participation; both domains document its targeted removal.

**Where this appears**: Domain 49 Zone 3 (community case studies, UCS vicious cycle); Domain 50 Zone 1 (suppression mechanisms, healthcare denial civic consequences).
**Sources**: C1 (UCS vicious cycle), F4 (PMC 12841032 mental health study), K2 (Earthjustice queer liberation/chronic illness data), K5 (CAP LGBTQ+/environmental exposure chain).

---

### Integration Point 2: Information Access as Democratic Infrastructure

Domain 49: EJSCREEN removal is an information access attack (Aarhus Convention Pillar 1 — access to information). Domain 50: EO 14168 document freeze is an identity information attack (trans people cannot access identity documents reflecting who they are). The same administration targeted both community data tools and community identity documentation.

**Where this appears**: Domain 49 Zone 3 (EJSCREEN removal, CEJST); Domain 50 Zone 1 (EO 14168 document freeze, SCOTUS passport stay).
**Sources**: B2 (EJSCREEN removal), B4 (CEJST removal), E2 (EO 14168 impact), I5 (SCOTUS passport stay), G4 (Aarhus Pillar 1).

---

### Integration Point 3: EO 14173 as the Shared Legal Mechanism

EO 14173 ("Ending Illegal Discrimination") is the administrative authority used to close OEJECR (Domain 49, Source A4) AND the authority challenged in SFAF v. Trump as unconstitutional when applied to LGBTQ+ nonprofits (Domain 50, Source M4). If the SFAF preliminary injunction survives appeal, environmental law organizations have a new legal theory for challenging OEJECR's closure under the same EO authority.

**Where this appears**: Domain 49 Zone 1 (statutory amendment pathways, administrative law theory); Domain 50 Zone 4 (coalition activation, SFAF v. Trump).
**Sources**: A4 (EO 14173 Domain 49 use), M4 (SFAF v. Trump Domain 50 injunction).
**Distribution note**: This is the most actionable legal theory connecting the two domains — flag for lambda Legal outreach.

---

### Integration Point 4: Appalachian Geography as Dual-Exposure Convergence Zone

The 2026 Civic Information Index documents Appalachia as the single geography where: (a) voter registration consistently fails to translate to voting participation (Domain 49 vicious cycle); (b) frontline EJ communities face no state EJ law protection in most Appalachian states (Domain 49 state-tier gap); and (c) LGBTQ+ individuals in conservative states face acute legal targeting with lowest state constitutional protection (Domain 50). The Nonprofit Quarterly queer ecological framework for EJ in Appalachia documents organizations working at this exact intersection.

**Where this appears**: Domain 49 Zone 2 (redistricting cascade, civic health data); Domain 50 Zone 3 (post-Skrmetti state court alternatives, Montana as counterexample).
**Sources**: C4 (2026 Civic Information Index), C11 (Appalachian Citizens' Law Center), C12 (NPQ queer ecological framework for Appalachia), I6 (Kalarchik Montana state court victory).

---

### Integration Point 5: Missouri as the Full-Convergence State

Missouri is the single state where all three attacks converge in November 2026: EPA EJ participation rights loss (Domain 49 — Missouri is in the 24-state Massachusetts v. EPA coalition); trans voter suppression (Domain 50 — EO 14168 document freeze applies nationally); and anti-trans ballot measure bundled with abortion rights reversal (Domain 50, Zone 2 — Missouri Amendment 3). The Missouri chemical corridor (Domain 49) overlaps geographically with communities targeted by Amendment 3.

**Where this appears**: Domain 49 Zone 3 (litigation — Massachusetts v. EPA); Domain 50 Zone 2 (ballot measures — Amendment 3); Domain 50 Zone 4 (coalition — Missouri abortion coalition cross-distribution).
**Sources**: E2 (Massachusetts v. EPA state coalition), B8-B10 (Missouri Amendment 3 analysis and campaign launch).
