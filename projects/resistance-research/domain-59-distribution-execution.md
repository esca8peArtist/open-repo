1. They have longer review cycles before responding
2. Their buy-in is credibility infrastructure for the advocacy organizations

Advocacy organizations (MomsRising, AFL-CIO) are sent second because:
1. They act faster and may reach out to Senate Finance offices within 48-72 hours
2. The June 30 window gives 27 days from June 3 — sufficient time for substantive advocacy
3. MomsRising's direct action infrastructure can mobilize member testimony on short notice

**Do not send on Friday afternoon, Monday morning, or the week before July 4.** Best windows are Tuesday-Thursday 09:00-14:00 UTC (04:00-09:00 EST), when DC policy staff email attention is highest before Hill meetings and committee work begins.

## 7. Context Notes

**Why CBPP/ITEP/NWLC/MomsRising/AFL-CIO**: These five organizations are identified in Domain 59 Section 3.1 and Section 6.2 as the coalition with "highest immediate leverage" during the CTC Senate Finance markup window:
- CBPP: research infrastructure for CTC/EITC analysis (Sharon Parrott, economic security division)
- ITEP: primary CTC analysis capacity (Steve Wamhoff, policy director)
- NWLC: childcare and women's economic security legal and policy advocacy
- MomsRising: 5 million-member direct advocacy network
- AFL-CIO: labor-family economic nexus and endorsed Living Wage for All Act + CTC expansion

**Why June 30 deadline matters**: Senate Finance Committee CTC markup window is the single most time-sensitive policy leverage point in the 2026 calendar for economic precarity advocacy. Amendments to make CTC fully refundable — the structural change that would reach the poorest fifth of families currently receiving $0 average benefit — must be introduced during markup, not after. The 28 days from June 2 to June 30 is sufficient time for organizations with existing Senate Finance relationships to incorporate the democratic design framing into testimony and amendment advocacy.

**Domain 59 relationship to Domain 39**: Domain 39 (Healthcare Access as Democratic Infrastructure) documented that Medicaid enrollment increases voter turnout via the Oregon Health Insurance Experiment (randomized evidence, +7% turnout). Domain 59 provides the economic-side mechanism: income stability reduces the financial scarcity cognitive burden that depresses participation, producing the same participation effect through the material pathway. The two domains together support a comprehensive "democratic infrastructure" argument spanning healthcare, income, housing, and childcare.
