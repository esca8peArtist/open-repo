# Domain 48 — Timing and Resource Coordination
## Parallel vs. Sequential Analysis, Virginia Ballot Timeline, and Domain 51 Conflict Matrix

*Prepared June 5, 2026. This document answers three questions: (1) Should Domain 48 execute in parallel with Domain 51 (June 9–12) or sequentially (June 16+)? (2) What is the Virginia ballot measure timeline relative to the Domain 51 execution window? (3) Are there organizational contact conflicts between Domain 51 and Domain 48 that require staggered sends?*

---

## Section 1: Primary Recommendation

**Domain 48 should execute sequentially after Domain 51 — June 16–20, 2026.**

This is not because parallel execution is impossible. The contact lists do not overlap in ways that would cause interference. The recommendation is sequential because:

1. Domain 51 executes June 9–12 and requires the orchestrator's send-window attention on those dates. Adding Domain 48 sends on the same days creates execution risk — the probability of a missed field fill, an unfilled Gist URL placeholder, or a logged-but-unsent email increases when two domain sends are running simultaneously with different contacts, templates, and tracking requirements.

2. Domain 48's Gist does not yet exist as of June 5. Creating it during Domain 51's send window (June 9–12) while also executing Domain 51 sends adds infrastructure load. The Gist creation can be done June 13–15 with no advocacy urgency penalty.

3. The Virginia coalition integration deadline is July 15 — 35 days from the start of the recommended Domain 48 window (June 16). This is sufficient lead time. Parallel execution would compress the deadline by 7 days, gaining minimal benefit while accepting execution risk.

**If parallel execution is necessary** (e.g., an unforeseen acceleration of the Virginia campaign timeline): see Section 4 for the parallel execution protocol.

## Section 2: Virginia Ballot Measure Timeline

### Current Status (June 5, 2026)

**Virginia Voting Rights Restoration Amendment (HJR 2 / SJ 248)**

The Virginia General Assembly placed the constitutional amendment on the November 3, 2026 ballot on January 14–16, 2026 (House passed 65–33 on January 14; Senate affirmed 21–18 on January 16). If passed by voters, the amendment would provide that persons convicted of felonies are entitled to vote immediately following their release from incarceration.

This represents a fundamental change from current Virginia law, which makes Virginia the only U.S. state or territory where anyone convicted of any felony loses their right to vote for life unless personally restored by the governor.

**King v. O'Bannon injunction** (parallel track):
The U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia issued a permanent injunction on January 22, 2026, effective May 1, 2026, holding that Virginia's disenfranchisement of non-common-law-felony offenders violates the 1870 Virginia Readmission Act. As of June 5, 2026, the injunction is in effect and Virginia election officials are prevented from disenfranchising otherwise-eligible voters for conviction of any offense that was not a felony at common law in 1870. Virginia sought interlocutory Fourth Circuit review. The injunction remains effective pending any stay application or appeal outcome.

The ballot measure and the King v. O'Bannon injunction are parallel tracks: the injunction provides interim protection for a subset of formerly disenfranchised voters through the Readmission Act theory; the ballot measure would provide constitutional protection for all formerly incarcerated voters after release.

## Section 4: Parallel Execution Protocol (Use Only If Accelerated Timeline Required)

If the Virginia campaign timeline accelerates and Domain 48 must begin before June 16, use this protocol:

**Parallel execution window**: Domain 48 sends on June 11–12 while Domain 51 sends to California contacts on June 11.

**Feasibility**: YES — feasible because:
- June 11 Domain 51 contacts: Common Cause CA (ca@commoncause.org), LWV CA (lwvc@lwvc.org), Clean Money Action Fund (info@cleanmoney.org)
- June 11 Domain 48 contacts (if parallel): Brennan Center (web form) and Worth Rises (info@worthrises.org)
- Zero organizational overlap on June 11

**Parallel execution risk factors**:
1. Domain 48 Gist must be created by June 10 (not June 13–15 as in the sequential plan)
2. Two domain send logs must be updated simultaneously — higher tracking error risk
3. If either send bounces or has a form error on June 11, troubleshooting for two domains simultaneously compresses response time

**Risk mitigation for parallel execution**:
- Create Domain 48 Gist on June 8 (one day before Domain 51 Wave 1 starts)
- Execute Domain 48 sends in the afternoon (13:00–15:00 local) after Domain 51 morning sends (09:00–11:00 local) are complete and logged
- Use separate browser tabs for each domain's send log to avoid cross-domain entry errors

**If parallel execution is chosen**: Update CHECKIN.md on June 8 with the decision and revised schedule. Update DOMAIN_48_GIST_CREATION_STEPS.md to note the accelerated creation deadline.
