## 1. Strategy

**Phase 1a (Day 0)**: Distribute the 34-domain framework (all domains except Domain 37) to general Tier 1-3 audiences via Batch 1 email (5 contacts), Reddit (4–5 subreddits), and Substack publication. This captures the April launch window — FISA passage, SAVE Act defeat, SPLC prosecutorial weaponization, May Day aftermath — while establishing institutional credibility before Domain 37 reaches its highest-value audience.

**Phase 1b (Day 1–3)**: Send Domain 37 (Federal Executive Interference in the 2026 Midterms) to 12 election protection organizations via targeted outreach. Phase 1b occurs once Phase 1a Batch 1 emails are confirmed sent (same-day or next-day), not after waiting for open/response signals. The "stagger" is strategic framing, not a multi-week delay.

## 2. Rationale

### Why Sequence Domain 37 Separately

Domain 37 is the most time-sensitive and highest-stakes domain in the framework. Its advocacy windows are tied to specific 2026 fixed dates:

- **May 30**: DOJ consent decree finalization — first legal challenge window
- **June 30**: Emergency EO routing window
- **August 7**: NVRA 90-day quiet period begins — systematic voter purge injunction deadline
- **September 2026**: Pre-election litigation positioning
- **October 2026**: Poll observer deployment

Election protection organizations — Democracy Docket, Brennan Center Democracy Program, ACLU Voting Rights Project, Protect Democracy, States United Democracy Center, Common Cause, Lawyers' Committee — receive research in a different institutional context than general academic or think-tank contacts. They are in active litigation, they have docket-specific needs, and they need to assess immediately whether a document is actionable or abstractly interesting. A targeted send that leads with Domain 37's connection to their specific current docket converts faster than a general 35-domain corpus that requires self-identification of the relevant section.

### Why Day 1–3, Not Week 2–3

The Phase 2 timeline in `DISTRIBUTION_PATH_ANALYSIS.md` modeled a 14-day gap to allow Batch 1 feedback to compound Phase 2 credibility. This execution plan tightens the gap to Day 1–3 because:

1. The NVRA quiet period (August 7) and DOJ consent decree clock (May 30) create real urgency.
2. Phase 1a establishes the framework's existence and credibility by the time the election org send lands — even a 24-hour window between sends allows the two outreaches to feel sequenced rather than simultaneous.
3. The framing for election protection contacts ("I'm distributing the general framework to policy and academic contacts; given your organization's specific work I wanted to send Domain 37 directly") is credible after one day, not only after 14 days.

If the user wants the longer 14-day gap to monitor Phase 1a feedback before sending Phase 1b, that is a valid refinement — but it is not required. Either timing works.
