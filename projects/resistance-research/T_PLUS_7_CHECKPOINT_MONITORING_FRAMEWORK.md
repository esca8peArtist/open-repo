## Section 2: Signal Scoring Rubric

*Apply this rubric to every reply before filling the status tables in Section 3. The score determines routing.*

### Signal Classification

**STRONG signal** — Score 4 or 5. Any of the following:

- A reply that includes a specific engagement action: request for a call or meeting, request to forward to a colleague, citation of the research in a document they are working on, or integration into an active campaign communication
- A reply from a named decision-maker (not a general inbox) that engages substantively with the argument (asks a follow-up question, pushes back on a specific claim, or proposes a connection to their current work)
- A reply that includes a forwarding offer ("I'm sending this to X who works on Y") — this is STRONG even if the original contact is not personally acting on it

**MODERATE signal** — Score 3. Any of the following:

- A reply that acknowledges receipt and expresses interest without specifying an action ("This is very relevant to our work, thank you")
- A reply from a general inbox routing to a named team member ("I've forwarded this to our research team")
- A Gist click from a verified organizational IP (documentable from Gist view tracking)
- A reply that requests the full document or additional context (indicates engagement without commitment)

**WEAK signal** — Score 1-2. Any of the following:

- Auto-acknowledgment with no individual name or content engagement
- Form response with no substantive reference to the research
- No reply after 7+ calendar days with delivery confirmed

**BOUNCE / OOO** — Not a signal. Action required:

- Hard bounce: re-verify address via organization website and resend within 48 hours
- OOO with named alternate: log the alternate contact in the distribution execution log; follow up after OOO window closes (typically 1-2 weeks). A named alternate is an upgrade opportunity.
- OOO without alternate: re-check in 5-7 days; adjust T+7 date if contact is not reachable until after the deadline

### Scoring Shorthand for Status Tables

Use these codes in the "Signal" column of the status tables:

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| S | STRONG (Score 4-5) |
| M | MODERATE (Score 3) |
| W | WEAK (Score 1-2) |
| B | BOUNCE (re-verify address) |
| OOO | Out of office (log alternate if named) |
| -- | No reply yet (check again tomorrow) |
