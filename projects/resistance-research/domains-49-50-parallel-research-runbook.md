## Section 1: Parallel Execution Model

### Why These Two Domains Run in Parallel

The production logic for running Domains 49 and 50 simultaneously:
- Different organizational networks: Climate Justice Alliance vs. AT4E/Lambda Legal — no contact list overlap, no double-send risk
- Different source types: EPA regulatory documents vs. SCOTUS dockets and ADF model legislation
- Compatible session scheduling: each domain requires concentrated research sessions, but source compilation for Domain 50 (live trackers, litigation dockets) can proceed while waiting for Domain 49 sources (Federal Register documents, Earthjustice PACER filings)
- August 1 effective deadline for both: parallel execution is the only path to meeting both deadlines with July 1 start

### Execution Model Options

**Option A — Two-Researcher Model (recommended)**
- Researcher 1 owns Domain 49 (Environmental Justice): July 1–August 1 complete
- Researcher 2 owns Domain 50 (LGBTQ+ Rights): July 1–August 1 complete
- Peer review crossover: Researcher 1 reviews Domain 50 Sections 3 and 5 (ballot measure argument) against Domain 49's participation rights framing; Researcher 2 reviews Domain 49's reform architecture section
- Total calendar time: 4 weeks, two researchers, 45–55 hours each

**Option B — Single-Researcher Sequential-Within-Parallel**
- Week 1 (July 1–7): Domain 49 source compilation + outline; Domain 50 source compilation
- Week 2 (July 8–14): Domain 49 Sections 1–4 draft; Domain 50 outline + Sections 1–3
- Week 3 (July 15–21): Domain 49 Sections 5–8 + first complete draft; Domain 50 Sections 4–7
- Week 4 (July 22–31): Domain 49 peer review + final; Domain 50 Sections 8–9 + first complete draft + peer review + final
- Risk: Domain 50 deadline is tighter under this model; if Week 3 Domain 49 runs long, Domain 50 completion slides past August 1

**Option C — Domain 50 First (if capacity is limited)**
If only one researcher is available and scheduling forces a choice:
- July 1–15: Domain 50 complete (harder August 1 deadline)
- July 15–31: Domain 49 complete (EPA comment period may extend into August)
- Reasoning: Four anti-trans ballot measures have a fixed campaign integration deadline; the EPA Endangerment Finding comment period may be extended or contested with a later deadline window

**Contingency rule**: If Option B Week 3 shows Domain 50 completion is at risk of slipping past August 1, immediately switch to Option C sequencing (abandon parallel execution, focus on Domain 50 first).

## Section 2: Domain 49 — Environmental Justice Research Execution

### Domain 49 Core Framing (Do Not Lose This)

Domain 49 is NOT about climate policy or environmental rollbacks. Those are covered in Domain 15 and the environmental rollbacks tracker. Domain 49's unique analytical contribution is:

**The question Domain 49 answers**: Which democratic tools did frontline communities use to contest environmental decisions that threatened their health? How were those tools disabled?

The frame is community participation rights — the legal and procedural infrastructure through which affected communities could challenge decisions, and its systematic dismantling. The normative claim is not "clean air is a right" — it is "voice in decisions about your own survival is a democratic right." This is the constitutional democracy argument that separates Domain 49 from every existing environmental advocacy document.

## Section 3: Domain 50 — LGBTQ+ Rights Research Execution

### Domain 50 Core Framing (Do Not Lose This)

Domain 50 is NOT a rights brief or an LGBTQ+ advocacy document. It is a democratic design analysis of how the ADF legal infrastructure operates simultaneously against multiple democratic institutions. The unique contribution:

**The question Domain 50 answers**: Why are LGBTQ+ rights under systematic attack, and what does that attack share with the attack on ballot initiatives (Domain 33), reproductive rights (Domain 44), and judicial independence (Domain 6)?

The answer is the ADF legal infrastructure — a coordinated model-legislation-plus-litigation-plus-judicial-appointment strategy that uses LGBTQ+ rights as one of its primary targets precisely because it generates maximum political mobilization. The democratic design insight: this is not a culture war; it is a legal infrastructure project using the same tools, funders, and personnel across multiple targets.

The anti-countermajoritarian ballot measure argument is the most legally actionable framing: Romer v. Evans (1996) established that ballot initiatives cannot be used to entrench majority prejudice against a targeted minority. Four 2026 anti-trans ballot measures will generate Romer successor challenges. Domain 50 provides the constitutional theory brief that connects specific ballot measure litigation to the broader democratic erosion framework.
