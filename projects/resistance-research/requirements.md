# Government Requirements Specification

**Version**: 1.0
**Domain**: Constitutional and institutional design — United States, with universal principles
**Methodology**: Evidence-based political science research; structured as a software requirements document
**Status**: Research-based recommendations; not legally binding

---

## Document Purpose

This document specifies what a well-designed government must do, be, and protect. Each requirement is stated precisely, with measurable success criteria. Requirements are prioritized and categorized. This format enables tracking, gap analysis between current and ideal, and phased implementation planning.

**Priority Levels**:
- **Critical**: Without this, the system cannot be considered a just democracy
- **High**: Significantly improves democratic function; strongly supported by evidence
- **Medium**: Important improvements; evidence supports but complexity is higher

## FOUND — Foundational Principles

### FOUND-001: Consent of the Governed
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** Government authority shall derive from and be continuously accountable to the consent of the governed, expressed through free, fair, and regular elections with meaningful competition.
**Rationale:** Foundational to social contract theory (Locke, Rousseau); without democratic accountability, no other right is secure.
**Success Criteria:** Free and fair elections held on regular schedule; no incumbent can unilaterally cancel, delay, or fundamentally alter elections; independent international observers confirm electoral integrity.
**Priority:** Critical

### FOUND-002: Rule of Law
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** No person or institution shall be above the law; all persons and institutions, including the highest government officials, shall be subject to the same laws enforced impartially by an independent judiciary.
**Rationale:** Rule of law is the foundational distinction between constitutional government and arbitrary power; without it, all other protections are theoretical.
**Success Criteria:** Government officials prosecuted for criminal conduct at rates comparable to civilians for equivalent conduct; court orders are consistently complied with; no evidence of selective prosecution for political purposes; CPI score above 80/100.
**Priority:** Critical

### FOUND-003: Separation of Powers
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** Legislative, executive, and judicial powers shall be held by separate institutions with independent authority and genuine capacity to check each other.
**Rationale:** Montesquieu's foundational insight, validated by 20th century evidence: concentrated power produces tyranny.
**Success Criteria:** Each branch retains independent authority to act on its core functions; no single party or individual controls all three branches without effective opposition; legislative oversight of executive is regular and consequential.
**Priority:** Critical

### FOUND-004: Protection of Rights as Non-Negotiable
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** Fundamental individual rights shall be constitutionally protected from violation by majority vote; some rights shall be permanently beyond democratic revision.
**Rationale:** Tocqueville's "tyranny of the majority" — democracy without rights protection permits majorities to oppress minorities.
**Success Criteria:** Core civil liberties (speech, assembly, religion, due process) protected from legislative override; meaningful judicial enforcement mechanism exists and is used.
**Priority:** Critical

### FOUND-005: Government Exists to Serve Citizens
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** Government institutions, in their design, operation, and evaluation, shall be oriented toward improving citizen welfare and human flourishing rather than perpetuating the power of those who hold office.
**Rationale:** Teleological grounding: government is a means to citizen welfare, not an end in itself.
**Success Criteria:** Public satisfaction with government institutions above 50%; government services accessible to all income levels; measurable improvement in HDI, SPI, and other welfare indicators.
**Priority:** High

### FOUND-006: Equal Political Dignity
**Category:** Foundational Principles
**Requirement:** Every adult citizen shall have equal standing in the political process; no person's voice shall count for more than another's on the basis of race, gender, wealth, religion, or any other characteristic.
**Rationale:** Political equality is a precondition for legitimate democratic governance; one person, one vote must be real, not merely formal.
**Success Criteria:** No wealth-based differential in access to candidates or officials; voting processes require no higher burden from any demographic group; representation in elected bodies is not systematically skewed by race or class at statistically significant levels.
**Priority:** Critical

## STRUCT — Structural Requirements

### STRUCT-001: Independent Judicial Review
**Category:** Structural
**Requirement:** An independent court system shall have the authority and capacity to review government actions for constitutionality and shall issue enforceable rulings.
**Rationale:** Without judicial review, rights protections exist only at legislative mercy; separation of powers requires an independent arbiter.
**Success Criteria:** Courts regularly rule against the executive branch and such rulings are complied with; judicial appointments are not subject to pure partisan control; court decisions are implemented without obstruction.
**Priority:** Critical

### STRUCT-002: Democratic Electoral System
**Category:** Structural
**Requirement:** The electoral system shall translate citizen preferences into representation with sufficient accuracy that no large group of citizens is systematically excluded from representation, and that the composition of legislative bodies reflects the distribution of political preferences in the electorate.
**Rationale:** Representative democracy fails if the electoral system systematically distorts representation.
**Success Criteria:** The difference between the share of votes received by any major party and its share of legislative seats shall not exceed 10 percentage points; voter turnout in national elections exceeds 70%; no demographic group participates at less than 70% of the overall rate.
**Priority:** Critical

### STRUCT-003: Peaceful Transfer of Power
**Category:** Structural
**Requirement:** Mechanisms for peaceful transfer of power between governments following elections shall be institutional, automatic, and robust against refusal by incumbents.
**Rationale:** Peaceful transfer of power is the defining characteristic of democratic vs. authoritarian governance; failure is catastrophic.
**Success Criteria:** Every election since 1800 has resulted in peaceful transfer; no incumbent has successfully refused to leave office; automatic mechanisms (legal succession, independent verification of results) function without dependence on incumbent cooperation.
**Priority:** Critical

### STRUCT-004: Legislative Supremacy in Lawmaking
**Category:** Structural
**Requirement:** Laws shall be made by the democratically elected legislature and shall not be created, altered, or suspended by executive decree except within strict statutory limits with automatic expiration and legislative override.
**Rationale:** Executive lawmaking is the mechanism through which democracies most commonly slide into autocracy.
**Success Criteria:** No major policy change implemented solely through executive order without statutory authorization; executive orders can be overridden by simple legislative majority; no class of law exempt from judicial review.
**Priority:** High

### STRUCT-005: Constitutional Amendment Process
**Category:** Structural
**Requirement:** The constitution shall be amendable through a deliberate, supermajority process that requires broad consensus but is not so difficult as to prevent necessary adaptation.
**Rationale:** Constitutions that cannot be amended become obsolete; constitutions that can be amended too easily provide no stability.
**Success Criteria:** Constitutional amendment requires sustained engagement of multiple institutions; no single party can unilaterally amend the constitution; amendments occur when sufficiently broad consensus exists; constitution has been updated within the past generation to address contemporary rights and governance issues.
**Priority:** High

## RIGHTS — Rights and Liberties

### RIGHTS-001: Freedom of Expression
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** All persons shall have the right to express opinions on political and social matters without government prosecution or retaliation, subject only to narrowly drawn restrictions for direct incitement to imminent violence.
**Rationale:** Mill's argument: free expression is required for truth to emerge; Locke's argument: political speech is the primary mechanism of accountability.
**Success Criteria:** No criminal prosecution of journalists or citizens for political speech; no government content moderation of political platforms; V-Dem freedom of expression score above 0.9.
**Priority:** Critical

### RIGHTS-002: Freedom of the Press
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** Independent journalism shall be free from government interference, licensing, or control; journalists shall not be subject to criminal prosecution for reporting on matters of public concern.
**Rationale:** Press is the primary external accountability mechanism for government; press freedom is the first target of autocratizing governments.
**Success Criteria:** RSF Press Freedom Index rank in top 20; no journalist imprisoned for reporting; no government ownership of major media outlets without editorial independence safeguards; shield law protects source confidentiality.
**Priority:** Critical

### RIGHTS-003: Freedom of Religion and From Established Religion
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** Government shall neither establish nor preferentially support any religion; no law shall be justified solely by religious doctrine; all persons shall be free to practice or not practice religion without government interference.
**Rationale:** Locke's Letter Concerning Toleration; Madison's Memorial and Remonstrance; historical evidence that state religion produces oppression.
**Success Criteria:** No government funding of religious institutions for religious activities; no laws enforceable only through appeal to religious doctrine; evidence of equal treatment across religious and non-religious citizens.
**Priority:** Critical

### RIGHTS-004: Due Process and Equal Protection
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law; all persons shall receive equal protection of the law regardless of race, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, religion, disability, or economic status.
**Rationale:** Foundational legal rights without which all other rights are vulnerable.
**Success Criteria:** No documented pattern of systematic discrimination in law enforcement, prosecution, or sentencing against protected groups; administrative hearings include meaningful process; right to legal counsel ensured in criminal proceedings.
**Priority:** Critical

### RIGHTS-005: Right to Privacy
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** All persons shall have the right to privacy in their homes, communications, and personal information; government surveillance shall require judicial authorization based on individualized suspicion; commercial data collection shall be regulated to protect personal autonomy.
**Rationale:** Privacy is the precondition for autonomy and the exercise of all other rights; digital age requires explicit privacy rights beyond traditional Fourth Amendment protections.
**Success Criteria:** Comprehensive data protection legislation comparable to EU GDPR; warrant requirement enforced for all digital surveillance; no mass surveillance programs collecting data on citizens without individualized suspicion; right to access, correct, and delete personal data held by government.
**Priority:** High

### RIGHTS-006: Right to Vote
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** Every adult citizen shall have the right to vote in elections for the legislative and executive bodies that govern them; this right shall not be conditioned on wealth, literacy, criminal history after sentence completion, or administrative barriers.
**Rationale:** Political equality requires universal suffrage; criminal disenfranchisement perpetuates punishment beyond sentence; administrative barriers function as de facto poll taxes.
**Success Criteria:** All citizens 18+ eligible to vote; automatic restoration of voting rights upon completion of sentence (or during incarceration, as in most democracies); no ID requirement without free, universally accessible ID; voter turnout above 70% nationally.
**Priority:** Critical

### RIGHTS-007: Right to Counsel
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** Every person charged with a criminal offense shall have the right to competent legal representation; public defenders shall have caseloads that permit meaningful representation; civil legal aid shall be available for significant civil matters.
**Rationale:** Justice without access is no justice; right to counsel is meaningless if counsel is overwhelmed.
**Success Criteria:** Public defender caseloads within NLADA standards (no more than 150 felony or 400 misdemeanor cases per year); civil legal aid available for housing, family, and benefits matters for those below 200% of poverty line; no correlation between quality of counsel and economic status.
**Priority:** High

### RIGHTS-008: Economic and Social Rights
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** All citizens shall have access to healthcare, education, and housing sufficient to enable meaningful participation in social and political life; these shall be recognized as rights, not merely as policy preferences.
**Rationale:** Rawlsian: rights without the material conditions to exercise them are hollow; all post-WWII democratic constitutions include social and economic rights; empirical evidence from Nordic countries shows these rights are achievable.
**Success Criteria:** Universal access to healthcare with no financial barrier at point of service; free public education from pre-K through high school with adequate funding; no person rendered homeless by inability to pay; legal recognition as rights with enforcement mechanisms.
**Priority:** High

### RIGHTS-009: Environmental Rights
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** All persons shall have the right to a safe, clean, and healthy environment; government shall have an obligation to protect environmental conditions necessary for human health and future generations.
**Rationale:** Recognized in many modern constitutions (Portugal, Ecuador, Colombia); climate change and pollution represent systematic threats to fundamental rights.
**Success Criteria:** Constitutional or statutory recognition of environmental rights with enforcement; air and water quality meets WHO standards nationally; government has enforceable obligation to reduce greenhouse gas emissions on science-based timeline.
**Priority:** High

### RIGHTS-010: Bodily Autonomy
**Category:** Rights and Liberties
**Requirement:** All persons shall have the right to make decisions about their own bodies, medical treatment, and reproduction without government compulsion.
**Rationale:** Mill's harm principle: self-regarding conduct is the domain of individual liberty, not government authority.
**Success Criteria:** No criminal prosecution for personal medical decisions; access to reproductive healthcare, including contraception and abortion, not subject to criminal prohibition; no forced medical procedures.
**Priority:** High

## ELECT — Electoral System

### ELECT-001: Ranked Choice Voting
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** All single-winner elections (executive offices, single-member districts) shall use ranked-choice voting (instant-runoff voting).
**Rationale:** RCV eliminates the spoiler effect, allows voters to express genuine preferences, produces majority winners, and reduces incentive for negative campaigning. Evidence from Australia (since 1918), Ireland, and 50+ U.S. jurisdictions.
**Success Criteria:** No election won by plurality less than 45% without runoff; third-party candidates can compete without being spoilers; post-election surveys show 80%+ of voters feel their vote mattered.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-002: Proportional Representation
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** Multi-member legislative bodies shall use proportional or mixed-member proportional representation systems that produce legislative seat shares reflecting vote shares within a tolerance of 10 percentage points.
**Rationale:** Winner-take-all elections in multi-member contests systematically underrepresent minorities and produce disproportionate results; PR produces more representative legislatures and higher turnout.
**Success Criteria:** No party wins majority of seats with less than 45% of votes; women's representation in legislature above 40%; racial minority representation proportional to population share; voter turnout above 70%.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-003: Independent Redistricting
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** District boundaries for all legislative elections shall be drawn by independent, nonpartisan commissions with no party controlling the process, using criteria that prioritize equal population, geographic compactness, community of interest, and minority representation — in that order.
**Rationale:** Legislative self-redistricting creates systematic self-serving bias; independent redistricting produces more competitive elections.
**Success Criteria:** Commission selection process prevents partisan capture; no sitting legislator may serve on or have appointment power over commission; competitive districts (margin of victory less than 10%) constitute at least 25% of total; race or party affiliation of voters is not used as a primary criterion.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-004: Automatic Voter Registration
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** All eligible citizens shall be automatically registered to vote upon first interaction with any government agency (DMV, social services, healthcare enrollment, etc.); the burden of registration shall rest with the government, not the citizen.
**Rationale:** U.S. is nearly unique among democracies in placing registration burden on citizens; AVR consistently increases registration rates and produces more representative electorates.
**Success Criteria:** Over 95% of eligible citizens are registered; registration rates among historically underregistered groups (young, minority, low-income) within 10% of overall rate; no documented cases of fraudulent registration due to AVR.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-005: Universal Ballot Access
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** Voting shall be accessible to all eligible citizens without undue burden; polling places, hours, and modalities shall accommodate the full diversity of the electorate.
**Rationale:** Voting barriers are the primary mechanism of voter suppression; accessibility determines whether formal voting rights are practical rights.
**Success Criteria:** No polling location more than 1 mile from any eligible voter; early voting available for at least 14 days before election day; absentee/mail voting available to all eligible voters without excuse requirement; election day or weekend voting; no ID requirement without universally free and accessible government ID.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-006: Vote by Mail Availability
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** All eligible voters shall have the option to vote by mail without requiring cause or justification; mail ballots shall be sent proactively to all registered voters.
**Rationale:** Vote by mail dramatically reduces barriers for disabled, elderly, rural, and working voters; Oregon has used all-mail elections since 2000 with consistently high turnout and no evidence of increased fraud.
**Success Criteria:** Mail voting available to all registered voters in all jurisdictions; turnout in mail-voting jurisdictions significantly above national average; no documented fraud attributable to mail voting at rate above 0.001% of ballots.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-007: Felon Reenfranchisement
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** Voting rights shall be automatically restored upon completion of criminal sentence; no permanent disenfranchisement shall be imposed as a consequence of any criminal conviction.
**Rationale:** The U.S. disenfranchises approximately 5.2 million citizens with past convictions; most democracies restore rights upon release or allow voting during incarceration; disenfranchisement disproportionately affects racial minorities.
**Success Criteria:** Voting rights restored to all citizens upon release from incarceration; disenfranchisement rates do not exceed 1% of eligible population; no racial disparity in disenfranchisement rates.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-008: Campaign Finance Transparency
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** All political contributions and expenditures above $100 shall be publicly disclosed within 48 hours of receipt or expenditure; no "dark money" (contributions concealed from public) shall be permitted in any election.
**Rationale:** Voters have a right to know who is funding political campaigns; dark money enables corruption by concealing conflicts of interest.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of contributions above $100 disclosed in real-time searchable database; no dark money organizations operating without donor disclosure; enforcement actions taken for violations within 60 days.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-009: Public Campaign Financing
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** A public matching fund program shall amplify small donations from citizens (within the represented constituency) at a ratio of at least 6:1, making small-donor fundraising financially viable for candidates.
**Rationale:** Reliance on large donors creates conflicts of interest and access disparities; small-donor matching maintains candidate-constituent connection while reducing dependence on wealthy donors.
**Success Criteria:** Candidates participating in matching program can run viable campaigns without relying on donations above $250; candidate pool is more demographically diverse than before program; participating candidates spend less time on fundraising.
**Priority:** High

### ELECT-010: Secure and Verified Elections
**Category:** Electoral System
**Requirement:** All elections shall use systems that produce a voter-verifiable paper audit trail; post-election audits shall be mandatory and conducted by entities independent of any candidate or party.
**Rationale:** Election integrity requires both security and verifiability; voters must be able to confirm their vote was counted as cast.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of jurisdictions use paper ballot or paper audit trail; risk-limiting audits conducted after every election; audit results publicly published; no jurisdictions using electronic systems without paper backup.
**Priority:** Critical

## LEGIS — Legislative

### LEGIS-001: Majority Rule with Minority Protection
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** The legislature shall be able to pass legislation supported by a majority of its members; procedural rules shall not permit a minority to indefinitely block majority-supported legislation.
**Rationale:** Minority obstruction of majority will is anti-democratic; the appropriate protection for minorities is rights, not procedural vetoes.
**Success Criteria:** No legislation with majority legislative support can be blocked indefinitely by procedural means; minority rights are protected through constitutional rights provisions, not legislative procedure.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-002: Legislative Transparency
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** All legislative proceedings — committee hearings, markups, amendments, and floor votes — shall be recorded and published within 24 hours; no "secret votes" shall be permitted; all bill text shall be publicly available at least 72 hours before floor votes.
**Rationale:** Democratic accountability requires that citizens can see how their representatives vote; "emergency" provisions for rapid legislation shall not apply to normal legislative business.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of roll call votes published within 24 hours with full record; no legislation voted on without at least 72 hours of public text availability except in declared national emergencies with sunset provisions; CSPAN-equivalent coverage of all public proceedings.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-003: Conflict of Interest Disclosure
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** All legislators shall publicly disclose all financial interests annually; legislators shall recuse themselves from votes directly affecting their financial interests; stock trading in sectors under legislative jurisdiction shall be prohibited.
**Rationale:** Financial conflicts of interest corrupt legislative judgment; voters have the right to evaluate whether representatives have conflicts.
**Success Criteria:** Annual financial disclosure published in machine-readable format within 30 days of deadline; no legislator votes on legislation directly benefiting their disclosed investments without recusal; STOCK Act violations result in financial penalties.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-004: Lobbying Disclosure
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** All persons paid to influence legislative or regulatory action shall register as lobbyists; all contacts between registered lobbyists and legislators or staff shall be disclosed within 5 business days; expenditure reports shall be filed quarterly.
**Rationale:** Lobbying is legitimate advocacy; secret lobbying is a corruption mechanism.
**Success Criteria:** All registered lobbying contacts in public searchable database; quarterly lobbying expenditure reports published; no unregistered lobbying activities; cooling-off period violations enforced.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-005: Revolving Door Restrictions
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** Former legislators shall be prohibited from lobbying their former chamber or colleagues for a period of no less than 5 years after leaving office; former legislative staff shall be prohibited from lobbying the office they served for 2 years.
**Rationale:** The revolving door between legislative and lobbying work creates systematic conflicts of interest and corrupts legislative judgment both prospectively and retrospectively.
**Success Criteria:** No former legislator registered as lobbyist within 5 years of leaving office; enforcement actions taken for violations; compliance rate above 95%.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-006: Independent Redistricting
**Category:** Legislative
(See ELECT-003 — this applies specifically to legislative districts)
**Requirement:** Legislative districts shall be drawn by a process independent of the legislature being elected in those districts.
**Success Criteria:** Legislators have no direct control over commission composition, process, or outcome.
**Priority:** High

### LEGIS-007: Oversight Authority
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** The legislature shall have and regularly exercise meaningful oversight authority over the executive branch, including through subpoena power, contempt authority, and access to executive documents and personnel.
**Rationale:** Legislative oversight is the primary constitutional check on executive power; without effective oversight, separation of powers is nominal.
**Success Criteria:** Regular oversight hearings in all major policy areas; subpoenas enforced within 30 days; executive agencies respond to oversight requests within statutory deadlines; contempt referrals result in enforcement actions.
**Priority:** Critical

### LEGIS-008: Appropriations Authority
**Category:** Legislative
**Requirement:** The legislature shall retain exclusive authority to appropriate public funds; the executive shall not reprogram or impound appropriated funds without specific statutory authorization; no funds shall be spent without legislative appropriation.
**Rationale:** The power of the purse is the legislature's primary check on the executive; executive impoundment or reprogramming evades this check.
**Success Criteria:** No executive impoundment of appropriated funds; all reprogramming requires congressional notification with objection rights; budget process completed on statutory schedule.
**Priority:** Critical

## EXEC — Executive

### EXEC-001: Executive Power Limits
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** Executive authority shall derive from statute; executive orders shall not create binding law without statutory authorization; no executive action shall override enacted statute.
**Rationale:** Unlimited executive discretion is indistinguishable from autocracy; executive power must be grounded in legislative authorization.
**Success Criteria:** Major executive orders cite specific statutory authority; courts regularly review and when necessary invalidate executive actions that exceed statutory authority; no class of executive action deemed "unreviewable."
**Priority:** Critical

### EXEC-002: Emergency Powers Constraints
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** All executive emergency declarations shall expire automatically after 30 days unless reauthorized by majority vote of the legislature; the legislature shall have the power to terminate any emergency declaration by majority vote; emergency powers shall not include the power to suspend elections, abrogate rights, or make permanent regulatory changes.
**Rationale:** Emergency powers are the most common mechanism through which democracies slide into authoritarianism; automatic expiration prevents emergency rule from becoming permanent.
**Success Criteria:** No emergency declaration exceeding 30 days without legislative reauthorization; legislative override mechanism functional; courts review emergency declarations for constitutionality; no election delayed or canceled due to emergency declaration.
**Priority:** High

### EXEC-003: Civil Service Independence
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** The professional civil service shall be merit-based; appointment, promotion, and removal shall be based on competence and performance, not political affiliation; senior civil servants shall be protected from removal without cause.
**Rationale:** A merit-based civil service is essential for effective, impartial government delivery; political patronage produces incompetence and corruption.
**Success Criteria:** Civil service positions at GS-15 and below filled through competitive merit-based process; no political affiliation used as hiring or firing criterion; civil servants who report misconduct are protected from retaliation; public satisfaction with government service delivery above 60%.
**Priority:** High

### EXEC-004: Inspector General Independence
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** Inspectors General shall be independent within their agencies; they shall not be removed without cause; their budgets shall not be reduced as retaliation for findings; their reports shall be automatically transmitted to the legislature.
**Rationale:** IGs are the primary inside-government accountability mechanism; their effectiveness depends entirely on independence from the actors they oversee.
**Success Criteria:** No IG removed without documented cause within 30-day congressional notification; no IG budget reduced following a significant critical finding; IG referrals to DOJ tracked publicly; IG reports published within 30 days of completion.
**Priority:** High

### EXEC-005: Pardon Power Limits
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** The executive pardon power shall not extend to the executive's own criminal liability, to crimes in which the executive was a victim or participant, or to crimes committed against the government itself; pardons shall require a written public statement of reasons.
**Rationale:** Self-pardon violates the basic principle that no one is judge in their own case; pardons should not be a mechanism for the executive to immunize themselves or close political allies.
**Success Criteria:** No self-pardon; written public rationale for all pardons; judicial review available for pardons that violate constitutional limits.
**Priority:** High

### EXEC-006: Emoluments Enforcement
**Category:** Executive
**Requirement:** The President shall not receive any financial benefit from foreign governments or from any domestic or foreign entity doing business with or regulated by the federal government; personal and family business interests shall be placed in blind trusts during service.
**Rationale:** Financial conflicts of interest in the executive are a direct corruption risk and undermine public trust.
**Success Criteria:** All executive financial interests disclosed and placed in independently managed blind trust; independent monitoring mechanism; financial penalty and potential removal for violations.
**Priority:** High

## JUD — Judicial

### JUD-001: Judicial Independence
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** Judges shall have sufficient independence from political pressure to make decisions without fear of retaliation; independence mechanisms shall include secure tenure, salary protection, and removal only through formal process for cause.
**Rationale:** Courts can only function as constitutional checks if they are insulated from political pressure; the evidence consistently shows that elections and short terms compromise judicial independence.
**Success Criteria:** No judge removed for an unpopular but legally sound decision; no evidence that judicial decisions correlate with electoral/appointment pressures on the judge making the decision; judiciary consistently scores above 0.8 on independence metrics.
**Priority:** Critical

### JUD-002: Merit-Based Judicial Selection
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** All federal and state judicial appointments shall be screened by an independent judicial nominating commission that evaluates candidates on qualification, temperament, and ethics, and submits a short list to the appointing authority.
**Rationale:** Pure partisan appointment produces highly politicized courts; nominating commissions constrain but do not eliminate political considerations; pure elections create electoral dependence; commission model is the best available compromise.
**Success Criteria:** Appointing authority restricted to candidates on commission's list; commission composition includes majority non-political members; commission process is transparent and public; no candidate advanced who has not met qualification standards.
**Priority:** High

### JUD-003: Supreme Court Term Limits
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** Justices on the highest court shall serve fixed terms of 18 years, staggered so that one vacancy occurs every two years; each president shall appoint one justice in the first and third years of their term; justices completing terms shall serve in senior capacity on lower courts.
**Rationale:** Lifetime tenure creates enormous stakes in each vacancy, incentivizes strategic retirement, and can leave aging courts out of touch; 18-year terms maintain independence while regularizing appointments.
**Success Criteria:** Each presidential term produces exactly 2 Supreme Court appointments; no justice serves beyond 18 years in active capacity; no vacancy determined by luck of timing; Senate cannot block appointment for more than 6 months.
**Priority:** High

### JUD-004: Judicial Ethics Enforcement
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** All federal judges, including Supreme Court justices, shall be subject to a binding code of ethics enforced by an independent ethics body; violations shall result in formal sanctions, including recusal requirements.
**Rationale:** The current situation where Supreme Court justices have weaker ethics rules than lower court judges is untenable; unethical conduct by judges destroys public trust.
**Success Criteria:** Code of ethics applies to all federal judges equally; independent enforcement body with authority to investigate and sanction; all gifts and financial benefits above $100 publicly disclosed; all travel provided by outside parties disclosed.
**Priority:** High

### JUD-005: Court Size Stability
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** The size of the Supreme Court shall be fixed by statute at a number specified with supermajority legislative approval required to change; court-packing shall be prohibited as a form of political manipulation.
**Rationale:** Court-packing to achieve political results destroys judicial independence; stability of court size is essential for legitimacy.
**Success Criteria:** Supreme Court size unchanged without 2/3 majority in both chambers; no president adds justices for strategic political purposes; court composition reflects a range of judicial philosophies.
**Priority:** High

### JUD-006: Access to Justice
**Category:** Judicial
**Requirement:** The justice system shall be financially accessible to all persons regardless of income; filing fees shall be proportional to income; legal aid shall be funded sufficiently to serve all persons below 200% of the poverty line in civil matters.
**Rationale:** A justice system accessible only to the wealthy is not a justice system; access to justice is a prerequisite for equal protection.
**Success Criteria:** No civil matter unresolved for financial inability to access courts; civil legal aid funded at ratio of 1 attorney per 5,000 eligible persons; filing fees income-scaled; public defender caseloads within national standards.
**Priority:** High

## INDEP — Independent Institutions

### INDEP-001: Independent Electoral Administration
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** Election administration shall be managed by an independent, nonpartisan commission with commissioners who are selected through a bipartisan or merit-based process, serve fixed terms, and can be removed only for cause.
**Rationale:** Partisan control of election administration creates inherent conflicts of interest; the party in power should not control the rules of its own reelection.
**Success Criteria:** No partisan official serves as primary election administrator without bipartisan oversight; commission decisions require supermajority; no election official removed within 2 years of an election they are administering; commission budget insulated from political retaliation.
**Priority:** Critical

### INDEP-002: Independent Anti-Corruption Agency
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** A dedicated, independent anti-corruption prosecutorial body shall exist with authority to investigate all branches of government, dedicated budget, and protection from political interference; its head shall serve a fixed term and be removable only for cause.
**Rationale:** The U.S. lacks a dedicated anti-corruption prosecutor; DOJ operates under executive control and cannot effectively investigate executive branch corruption.
**Success Criteria:** Agency investigates all three branches without political interference; prosecution rates for government corruption comparable to international best practices; agency head cannot be removed without cause; budget cannot be reduced more than general government reduction.
**Priority:** High

### INDEP-003: Independent Central Bank
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** Monetary policy shall be set by an independent central bank with statutory mandates for price stability and maximum employment; the executive shall not direct monetary policy or remove central bank officials for policy disagreements.
**Rationale:** Independent central banking has a strong international evidence base for producing lower inflation and more stable economic conditions.
**Success Criteria:** No president removes Federal Reserve chair for policy disagreements; inflation remains within target range in ordinary conditions; Fed reports publicly to Congress without political filter.
**Priority:** High

### INDEP-004: Government Accountability Office
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** An independent legislative audit agency (equivalent to current GAO) shall have authority and resources to audit all government programs; reports shall be mandatory, public, and agencies shall be required to formally respond.
**Rationale:** Independent audit is the primary mechanism for assessing government efficiency and effectiveness.
**Success Criteria:** GAO audits all major federal programs on 5-year rotating basis; agencies respond formally to all GAO recommendations within 180 days; all GAO reports published and searchable; GAO has access to classified programs with appropriate clearances.
**Priority:** High

### INDEP-005: Free and Independent Press
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** Government shall take active measures to support independent journalism including: federal shield law protecting sources, anti-SLAPP protection, antitrust oversight of media consolidation, and public broadcasting with governance structures insulating editorial decisions from political control.
**Rationale:** A free press is a structural component of democracy, not merely a civil liberty; concentration and political capture of media are documented precursors to democratic backsliding.
**Success Criteria:** Local news deserts are addressed through support mechanisms; no concentration of media ownership that allows single political perspective to dominate more than 30% of local markets nationally; public broadcaster independent from political appointment; federal shield law in effect.
**Priority:** High

### INDEP-006: Congressional Budget Office Independence
**Category:** Independent Institutions
**Requirement:** The CBO shall remain independent, nonpartisan, and adequately funded; CBO cost estimates shall be mandatory for all major legislation; CBO director shall serve a fixed term and be removable only for cause.
**Rationale:** Non-partisan fiscal analysis is essential for informed legislative decision-making.
**Success Criteria:** CBO estimates published for all major legislation before floor vote; no CBO director removed for producing politically inconvenient analysis; CBO methodology transparent and peer-reviewed.
**Priority:** High

## ECON — Economic Justice

### ECON-001: Progressive Taxation
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** The tax system shall be structured so that effective tax rates are progressive — individuals with higher incomes pay a higher percentage of income in taxes; tax avoidance through offshore mechanisms shall be actively prevented.
**Rationale:** Progressive taxation reduces inequality while funding public goods; evidence shows high marginal rates do not significantly harm growth; extreme wealth concentration undermines democratic equality.
**Success Criteria:** Effective tax rates are higher at each income decile; no mechanism for legally sheltering income from taxation through offshore structures; wealth concentration does not exceed Gini coefficient of .40; billionaire population does not represent more than 10% of national wealth.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-002: Universal Healthcare
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** All residents shall have access to essential healthcare services without out-of-pocket cost at point of service; no one shall be denied healthcare for inability to pay or insurance status.
**Rationale:** The U.S. spends 17% of GDP on healthcare — significantly more than any other country — while achieving worse outcomes and leaving millions uninsured; evidence from all other rich democracies shows universal coverage is achievable at lower cost.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of residents covered for essential health services; no medical bankruptcy; life expectancy at or above OECD average; healthcare spending below 12% of GDP while covering all residents; no correlation between income and preventive care utilization.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-003: Universal Public Education
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** All children shall have access to free, high-quality public education from pre-kindergarten through grade 12; funding shall not be dependent on local property tax alone; per-pupil expenditures shall not vary by more than 20% between the richest and poorest districts.
**Rationale:** Education is both an economic investment and a democratic requirement; funding tied to local property wealth perpetuates inequality; evidence from Heckman shows early childhood investment has 7-12% annual return.
**Success Criteria:** Universal pre-K available for all 3-4 year olds; per-pupil spending variance below 20% between richest and poorest 10% of districts; PISA scores in top 15 OECD countries; no correlation between zip code and educational outcome at high school level.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-004: Labor Rights
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** All workers shall have the right to organize, collectively bargain, and strike; the legal framework shall make exercising these rights practically possible, not merely formally legal; labor law enforcement shall be adequately funded.
**Rationale:** Labor rights are the primary mechanism for workers to participate in economic governance; union density decline correlates with inequality increase; collective bargaining is standard in all high-functioning democracies.
**Success Criteria:** NLRA violations result in meaningful penalties; card check or easier union certification mechanisms; sectoral bargaining available as option; union density above 20%; collective bargaining coverage above 40%; wage share of GDP above 55%.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-005: Anti-Monopoly Policy
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** Competition policy shall be actively enforced to prevent monopolization or oligopolization of markets; market concentration shall be monitored and anticompetitive conduct prosecuted; mergers that would substantially lessen competition shall be blocked.
**Rationale:** Economic concentration reduces innovation, increases prices, reduces wages, and converts economic power into political power; U.S. has experienced dramatic consolidation across most industries since 1980.
**Success Criteria:** Market concentration (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index) declining or stable in major sectors; antitrust enforcement actions at levels comparable to 1960s-1980s; no single firm controlling more than 40% of market in essential sectors without regulatory justification; tech platforms treated as essential infrastructure with access requirements.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-006: Social Safety Net
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** Government shall provide an adequate social safety net that prevents poverty-level deprivation among all residents; programs shall include unemployment insurance, disability support, housing assistance, and nutritional support with benefits sufficient to meet basic needs.
**Rationale:** Rawlsian difference principle: inequalities are only just if they benefit the least advantaged; empirical evidence from Nordic and European welfare states shows comprehensive safety nets are affordable and produce positive social outcomes.
**Success Criteria:** Poverty rate (below 50% of median income) below 5%; no resident unable to meet basic nutritional needs; no family rendered unhoused by inability to pay rent; unemployment insurance replacement rate above 60% for the first 6 months; disability support available without 2-year waiting period.
**Priority:** High

### ECON-007: Beneficial Ownership Transparency
**Category:** Economic Justice
**Requirement:** All legal entities operating in or with the United States shall disclose the real human beings who ultimately own or control them; this information shall be in a public, searchable registry updated in real time.
**Rationale:** Anonymous shell companies are the primary mechanism for money laundering, tax evasion, and corruption; beneficial ownership transparency is the foundational anti-corruption reform.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of legal entities above a minimal size registered with beneficial ownership disclosed; information publicly searchable; penalties for false disclosure are meaningful (criminal liability); compliance rate above 95%.
**Priority:** High

## PART — Democratic Participation

### PART-001: Citizen Assemblies
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** Government shall establish mechanisms for convening citizen assemblies of randomly selected citizens to deliberate on complex constitutional and policy questions; assemblies shall have formal advisory status and their recommendations shall require formal legislative response within 12 months.
**Rationale:** Ireland's experience shows citizen assemblies can resolve questions partisan institutions cannot; deliberation improves policy quality; random selection produces more representative bodies than elections.
**Success Criteria:** At least one major policy area reviewed by citizen assembly every 5 years; assembly composition reflects demographic diversity of population; formal government response required within 12 months; evidence of increased civic engagement among participants.
**Priority:** Medium

### PART-002: Participatory Budgeting
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** A meaningful portion of discretionary capital budgets at city and county level shall be allocated through participatory budgeting processes that enable residents to directly determine spending priorities.
**Rationale:** Porto Alegre evidence shows participatory budgeting increases civic engagement, improves resource targeting, and rebuilds trust in government; NYC's program has shown positive results.
**Success Criteria:** Participatory budgeting available in all jurisdictions above 50,000 population; at least 5% of capital budget subject to participatory process; participation rates represent full demographic diversity of jurisdiction; evidence of improved resource allocation to underserved areas.
**Priority:** Medium

### PART-003: Civic Education
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** Public education shall include substantive civic education covering how government works, constitutional rights and responsibilities, historical failures of democracy, and how to evaluate political information.
**Rationale:** Democratic self-governance requires citizens who understand the system; civic knowledge correlates with participation, resilience against misinformation, and institutional support.
**Success Criteria:** All students graduate having passed a meaningful civic knowledge assessment; civic education includes local, state, and federal government; curriculum includes historical examples of democratic failure and recovery; assessments go beyond recitation to include analysis.
**Priority:** High

### PART-004: Deliberative Democracy Infrastructure
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** Government shall fund and facilitate deliberative democracy infrastructure including public forums, community meeting spaces, and digital platforms that enable informed public deliberation, not merely the expression of uninformed preferences.
**Rationale:** Aggregate preferences without deliberation reflect media influence and misinformation as much as genuine values; deliberative infrastructure improves the quality of democratic inputs.
**Success Criteria:** Public deliberative forums available in all jurisdictions on major policy questions; deliberative process precedes major local decisions; evidence that participants update views based on information exchange.
**Priority:** Medium

### PART-005: Initiative and Referendum
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** Citizens shall have the right to initiate legislation through direct petition process with a threshold of signatures (1-5% of registered voters); initiated legislation shall go to referendum; referendums shall require majority of registered voters (not just those voting) to amend the constitution.
**Rationale:** Switzerland's evidence shows direct democracy increases civic engagement and improves policy quality over time; U.S. state experience shows risks of simple majority constitutional amendment; signature threshold and voter majority requirement prevent misuse.
**Success Criteria:** Initiative process available at federal and all state levels; petition threshold achievable without unlimited spending; constitutional amendment by referendum requires 60% of votes cast; simple majority required for statutory initiatives; courts review for constitutional rights compliance before and after vote.
**Priority:** Medium

### PART-006: Anti-Suppression Enforcement
**Category:** Democratic Participation
**Requirement:** Active voter suppression — including polling place closures, voter purges without proper notice, intimidation at polling places, or disinformation about voting procedures — shall be treated as federal crimes with mandatory criminal enforcement.
**Rationale:** Voter suppression systematically targets minority voters; current civil remedies are slow and inadequate; criminal enforcement with meaningful penalties is required.
**Success Criteria:** Federal prosecution of documented voter suppression within 90 days of documented activity; no voter purges within 90 days of an election; election disinformation hotlines staffed and responsive; polling place closures require federal preclearance in jurisdictions with history of suppression.
**Priority:** High

## ACCT — Accountability and Transparency

### ACCT-001: Freedom of Information
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** All persons shall have the right to access government records within 10 business days; requests shall be presumed to favor disclosure; exemptions shall be narrowly drawn and independently reviewed; no fee may exceed $25 for ordinary requests.
**Rationale:** Public access to government information is the foundational mechanism of democratic accountability; the current FOIA system is chronically backlogged and over-used to deny disclosure.
**Success Criteria:** Median FOIA response time under 10 business days; denial rate below 10% (compared to 25%+ current); appeals to independent ombudsman resolved within 60 days; automatic disclosure of all frequently-requested documents online.
**Priority:** High

### ACCT-002: Whistleblower Protections
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** Any person who discloses government waste, fraud, abuse, or illegal activity shall be protected from retaliation, and the burden of proof in retaliation cases shall rest on the employer; financial rewards shall be available for disclosures leading to recoveries.
**Rationale:** Whistleblowers are the single most cost-effective source of government accountability information; False Claims Act evidence shows qui tam provisions produce massive recoveries.
**Success Criteria:** Retaliation complaints resolved within 180 days; burden of proof on employer; financial rewards in 15-30% range of any recovery; national security disclosure pathway through Congress protected; no criminal prosecution for disclosing information about illegal activity.
**Priority:** High

### ACCT-003: Financial Disclosure Requirements
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** All senior government officials — including president, vice president, members of Congress, senior executive officials at GS-15 and above, and federal judges — shall file detailed annual financial disclosures covering all income, assets, liabilities, and business interests, published in searchable online format within 30 days of filing.
**Rationale:** Public officials make decisions that affect their financial interests; disclosure enables voters and ethics officials to identify conflicts.
**Success Criteria:** 100% compliance with filing; all filings in machine-readable format online; significant violations (underreporting above $10,000) automatically referred for investigation; disclosure includes spouse and dependent financial interests.
**Priority:** High

### ACCT-004: Revolving Door Restrictions
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** Former senior officials shall be prohibited from lobbying their former agency for 5 years after leaving government; former members of Congress and senior congressional staff shall be prohibited from lobbying their former chamber for 5 years; lifetime prohibition on lobbying on specific matters handled while in government.
**Rationale:** Revolving door creates systematic conflicts of interest; current 1-2 year cooling off periods are routinely circumvented.
**Success Criteria:** No former senior official registered as lobbyist for their former agency within 5 years; violations result in criminal prosecution; compliance rate above 95% (with monitoring).
**Priority:** High

### ACCT-005: Open Data and Open Contracting
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** All government spending data shall be published in real time in machine-readable format; all government contracts shall be publicly disclosed including the identity of contractors, amounts, deliverables, and performance assessments; no-bid contracts above $100,000 shall require public justification.
**Rationale:** Open contracting reduces corruption and enables civil society oversight; secret government spending is the primary mechanism for waste and fraud.
**Success Criteria:** 100% of federal spending data available in real-time searchable database; all contracts above $25,000 publicly disclosed; contractor performance ratings public; no-bid contracts above $100,000 require justified exception within 48 hours.
**Priority:** High

### ACCT-006: Inspector General Protections
**Category:** Accountability and Transparency
**Requirement:** Inspectors General shall not be removed without documented cause and 30-day advance notice to Congress; their budgets shall not be reduced as retaliation for critical findings; their reports shall be automatically transmitted to and published by Congress.
**Rationale:** IGs are among the most cost-effective accountability mechanisms; their effectiveness depends on independence from the officials they oversee.
**Success Criteria:** No IG removed without documented cause; IG report findings publicly published within 30 days; DOJ tracks IG referrals publicly; no budget reduction in year following significant critical finding.
**Priority:** High

## FED — Federalism and Local Government

### FED-001: Baseline Rights Floor
**Category:** Federalism and Local Government
**Requirement:** The national government shall establish minimum rights standards applicable in all states and localities; states and localities may exceed but may not fall below these standards; the courts shall enforce the floor against state and local violations.
**Rationale:** Federalism without rights floors permits local majorities to oppress local minorities; the Civil War and Civil Rights era demonstrate this risk; U.S. history shows states cannot be fully trusted to protect minority rights without federal enforcement.
**Success Criteria:** Federal rights floor enforced against all state and local violations; private right of action available for floor violations; states may exceed floor (higher minimum wages, stronger privacy rights, broader healthcare coverage).
**Priority:** Critical

### FED-002: Local Democracy
**Category:** Federalism and Local Government
**Requirement:** Local governments shall have meaningful autonomy over local decisions; states shall not preempt local decisions on local matters (minimum wage, non-discrimination, environmental standards) except where state-level coordination is genuinely required.
**Rationale:** Local government is the most accessible level of democratic participation; state preemption of progressive local policies has become a systematic tool for overriding local democratic choices.
**Success Criteria:** No state preemption of local minimum wages above state minimum; no state preemption of local non-discrimination protections; local government funding not used as political weapon against political opponents.
**Priority:** High

### FED-003: Federal Fiscal Equalization
**Category:** Federalism and Local Government
**Requirement:** The federal government shall maintain fiscal equalization mechanisms that ensure all states and localities can provide comparable minimum levels of public services; equalization shall not be conditioned on political alignment.
**Rationale:** Large fiscal disparities between states produce large disparities in opportunity; federal equalization is how Canada, Germany, and Australia maintain national minimum standards.
**Success Criteria:** Per-student education funding variance between states reduced by federal equalization; healthcare coverage standards enforced nationally through federal funding requirements; fiscal capacity (per-capita tax revenue) differences between states not exceed 3:1 ratio after federal transfers.
**Priority:** Medium

### FED-004: State Policy Innovation
**Category:** Federalism and Local Government
**Requirement:** States shall retain authority to experiment with policy innovations in areas within their constitutional jurisdiction; the federal government shall monitor state experiments and facilitate diffusion of successful innovations.
**Rationale:** Justice Brandeis's "laboratories of democracy" insight: states can test policies before national adoption; Medicaid, welfare reform, marriage equality, cannabis legalization all originated at state level.
**Success Criteria:** Federal government actively tracks and evaluates state policy experiments; successful innovations explicitly considered for national adoption; no federal preemption of state experiments that do not violate rights floor.
**Priority:** Medium

## RESIL — Democratic Resilience

### RESIL-001: Autocratization Early Warning
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** The government shall maintain and publicize an annual assessment of democratic health using objective metrics (V-Dem, Freedom House, EIU); decline in democratic indicators shall trigger formal legislative review.
**Rationale:** V-Dem evidence shows early warning is possible and enables preventive action; self-monitoring creates accountability for democratic health.
**Success Criteria:** Annual democratic health report published by independent commission; triggers defined in statute (e.g., 5% decline on three or more indicators) require mandatory legislative debate; civil society organizations have standing to challenge non-compliance.
**Priority:** Medium

### RESIL-002: Anti-Disinformation Infrastructure
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** Government shall not produce political disinformation; government communications shall be truthful and based on best available evidence; government shall support media literacy education and invest in public interest journalism.
**Rationale:** Government disinformation is the most effective tool for autocratization; V-Dem identifies it as a top autocratization indicator; public media literacy is a defense against both government and private disinformation.
**Success Criteria:** No documented government disinformation campaign; independent fact-checking bodies supported but not controlled by government; media literacy integrated into public school curriculum; public interest journalism funding at levels comparable to international peers.
**Priority:** High

### RESIL-003: Civil Society Protection
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** Civil society organizations — nonprofits, advocacy groups, labor unions, religious institutions, professional associations — shall be free from government harassment, politicized prosecution, or discriminatory taxation; their operations shall be free from government surveillance absent warrant.
**Rationale:** Civil society is the primary counterweight to state power; its suppression is an early step in autocratization.
**Success Criteria:** No documented pattern of targeted prosecution or IRS audit of organizations based on political orientation; civil society organizations' communications not subject to warrantless surveillance; funding from foreign governments required to be disclosed but not prohibited per se.
**Priority:** High

### RESIL-004: Electoral Integrity Protection
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** All federal officials shall have a statutory duty to not interfere with federal elections; any attempt by a federal official to overturn or refuse to certify a legitimate election result shall be a federal crime; the military shall have explicit authority to refuse unlawful orders to interfere with elections.
**Rationale:** The most catastrophic failure mode of democracy is an executive that refuses to accept electoral defeat; statutory protections are needed beyond conventional norms.
**Success Criteria:** Statutory prohibition on interference with election results by government officials; criminal referral for violations within 30 days; electoral count reform eliminates ambiguity in counting presidential votes; military doctrine explicitly prohibits election interference.
**Priority:** Critical

### RESIL-005: Constitutional Literacy
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** Democratic education shall ensure that all citizens have baseline literacy in: how elections work, what rights they have and how to exercise them, how to evaluate political information, and what historical democratic failures looked like and how they unfolded.
**Rationale:** An uninformed electorate is vulnerable to manipulation and cannot effectively exercise democratic self-governance; civic literacy correlates with institutional support and democratic participation.
**Success Criteria:** National civics assessment shows 80%+ of high school graduates can identify major constitutional rights; 80%+ can describe how their local, state, and federal representatives are elected; 70%+ can identify characteristics of democratic backsliding.
**Priority:** High

### RESIL-006: International Democratic Integration
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** The United States shall maintain active membership in international democratic institutions (UN, NATO democratic standards, Freedom House monitoring, OECD governance frameworks); shall subject itself to international human rights monitoring; shall actively support democratic development globally.
**Rationale:** International integration embeds democratic commitments in external relationships; international monitoring provides accountability; historical evidence shows democracies that participate in democratic alliances are more resilient.
**Success Criteria:** Active participation in all major international monitoring mechanisms; U.S. submits to Universal Periodic Review and implements recommendations; bilateral relationships explicitly conditioned on democratic standards for non-treaty allies; foreign policy promotes rather than undermines democratic governance.
**Priority:** High

### RESIL-007: Succession and Continuity
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** Clear, automatic, and tamper-resistant mechanisms for succession and continuity of government shall exist for all elected offices; no gap in constitutional authority shall be possible; emergency succession shall not grant additional powers not held by the original officeholder.
**Rationale:** Succession crises create openings for power grabs; clarity eliminates ambiguity that can be exploited.
**Success Criteria:** Succession order defined for all elected offices including simultaneous vacancies; succession is automatic and does not require any single official's cooperation; successor's powers are identical to predecessor's; succession cannot be manipulated by the outgoing official.
**Priority:** Critical

### RESIL-008: Norm Codification
**Category:** Democratic Resilience
**Requirement:** Democratic norms that have been violated in the recent past shall be codified in statute with enforcement mechanisms; conventions that protect democracy shall not remain dependent on voluntary compliance.
**Rationale:** Experience shows that conventions without statutory backing are vulnerable to actors willing to violate them; codification converts voluntary norms into enforceable law.
**Success Criteria:** All major democratic norms that were violated in 2015-2025 period codified in statute; enforcement mechanism specified; violations result in automatic consequences, not merely political controversy.
**Priority:** High
