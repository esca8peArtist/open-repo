# Phase 3 Playbook: Religious Coalitions

**Audience**: Interfaith Alliance leadership, Interfaith America program directors, denominational advocacy offices (United Methodist, Evangelical Lutheran, Episcopal, United Church of Christ, Catholic diocesan social action offices, Muslim, Hindu, Jewish, Sikh, Buddhist coalition representatives), Black church network organizers

---

## Executive Summary

Religious institutions represent the largest organized civic infrastructure in the United States: 380,000 congregations, 70+ million weekly attendees, physical presence in communities that have lost every other civic institution. The faith community's leverage in May 2026 is specific and time-bounded. The Interfaith Alliance and Protect Democracy United launched the second phase of the #LoveNotICE campaign immediately ahead of the midterms — a direct response to the ICE/CBP reconciliation markup and its 287(g) expansion. Interfaith America's 2026 Vote is Sacred Fellowship is assembling faith and civic leaders specifically to develop 2026 midterm election strategies. Faiths United to Save Democracy's Poll Chaplains program, currently in 9 states, needs to expand to 20+ before November. The administration's Religious Liberty Commission (challenged by the Interfaith Alliance in April 2026) represents an attempt to co-opt the religious liberty frame for an exclusionary subset of faith communities — the correct counter is not to abandon the frame but to contest it with the full range of America's faith traditions. The single most important action in the next 30 days: expand the Poll Chaplains training pipeline from 9 states to 20 and coordinate with the AG coalition's pre-election TRO templates for the scenarios where ICE proximity to polling sites creates voter intimidation.

## Strategic Opportunities

**Opportunity 1: #LoveNotICE Phase 2 as midterm voter protection infrastructure**

The Interfaith Alliance's #LoveNotICE Phase 2, launched in direct response to the ICE/CBP reconciliation markup, has 18,000 trained volunteers with a 100,000 activation target. The activation target is the right number; the challenge is converting training into coordinated deployment. The specific deployment: identify the 50 congressional districts with the highest documented ICE enforcement activity within 1 mile of the 2022 polling sites in those districts. In those districts, deploy #LoveNotICE trained volunteers as Poll Chaplain-equivalent presences at the highest-risk polling sites on November 3, 2026. Coordinate with the AG coalition's TRO templates: if ICE enforcement occurs within documented proximity to a polling site, #LoveNotICE volunteers are the witnesses whose documentation triggers the TRO filing.

**Opportunity 2: Poll Chaplains expansion from 9 to 20+ states (deadline: August 1)**

Faiths United to Save Democracy needs to expand from 9 states to 20+ before November. The training pipeline (Skinner Leadership Institute / Georgetown Center for Faith and Justice) exists. The expansion requires: (1) identifying lead denominational partners in 11 additional states (the targets are competitive states where voting intimidation risk is documented: Arizona, Georgia, Nevada, Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, Michigan, North Carolina, Virginia, New Hampshire, Colorado, and Minnesota); (2) securing training venue space at existing theological seminaries and divinity schools in each state; (3) coordinating with state AGs to ensure Poll Chaplains' documentation capacity integrates with pre-staged TRO templates. Target: signed commitments from lead denominational partners in 11 states by June 30; training completed by August 1.

**Opportunity 3: Catholic Health Association as Medicaid cut institutional opposition**

The OBBBA reconciliation bill includes Medicaid cuts that directly threaten Catholic hospital systems' operating margins. Catholic Health Association (CHA) represents 600+ hospitals and 1,600 long-term care facilities that operate with Medicaid as a primary payer for low-income patients. CHA's institutional authority on healthcare access is the most persuasive voice available to Catholic Republican senators: CHA is not a partisan organization and cannot be dismissed as Democratic advocacy. Target: CHA public statement opposing the Medicaid provisions in OBBBA, specifically quantifying the number of Catholic hospitals in each Republican senator's state whose viability depends on Medicaid reimbursement rates. This is the same rural constituency leverage model used for the PRESS Act — Catholic hospitals are often the only hospital in rural Republican districts.

**Opportunity 4: Contest the Religious Liberty Commission framing**

The Trump administration's Religious Liberty Commission (challenged by Interfaith Alliance, April 2026) attempts to define religious liberty as a protection for a specific subset of conservative religious practices, excluding the majority of American faith traditions. The counter-frame is not secular — it is interfaith. The Establishment Clause protects all faith traditions equally, not just those politically aligned with the administration. The Interfaith Alliance should coordinate a multi-denominational statement (Jewish, Muslim, Hindu, Buddhist, mainline Protestant, and reform Catholic signatories) asserting that the Religious Liberty Commission speaks for a political faction, not the American faith community. Published in advance of any administration action that invokes the Commission's authority.

## Implementation Pathway

**Weeks 1-2 (May 18-31): #LoveNotICE deployment mapping and CHA activation**

Interfaith Alliance staff: pull the documented ICE enforcement geographic data from the ACLU warrantless arrest tracker and the litigation tracker. Identify the 50 congressional districts with the highest enforcement activity near documented polling sites. Brief #LoveNotICE regional coordinators on the district-specific deployment plan.

Simultaneously: Interfaith Alliance government relations staff request a meeting with Catholic Health Association's government affairs director. The ask: CHA public statement on Medicaid before the June 1 Senate reconciliation vote, specifically quantifying hospital vulnerability by senator's state. This is not a partisan ask — it is CHA's institutional mission statement about what the Medicaid cuts mean for patients.

**Weeks 3-8 (June 1-30): Poll Chaplains expansion and multi-denominational Religious Liberty Commission response**

Skinner Leadership Institute and Georgetown Center for Faith and Justice launch the expansion training calendar. Confirmed seminary venues in at least 8 of the 11 target states by June 30. Denominational lead sign-offs: each target state has a named denominational lead (Episcopal, UMC, ELCA, or Black church network lead appropriate to the state's denominational landscape) who owns the training coordination.

Multi-denominational Religious Liberty Commission response: Interfaith Alliance drafts the joint statement; JCPA, ISNA, ELCA Advocacy, UCC Justice and Witness, and NETWORK are the core signatories. Target publication in at least two national newspapers and distribution through all denominational channels before any administration action invoking the Commission.

**Weeks 9-16 (July-August): Pre-election deployment coordination**

Poll Chaplain training complete. Deployment coordination meeting: Interfaith Alliance, Faiths United to Save Democracy, state AG offices, and Democracy Docket — four-party coordination session to ensure that Poll Chaplain documentation protocols integrate with TRO filing procedures. The question each party should be able to answer: if a Poll Chaplain documents ICE enforcement within 500 feet of a polling site at 8:00 a.m. on November 3, what is the exact protocol for getting that documentation to the AG office that files the TRO by 9:00 a.m.?

Black church GOTV: August is the last window before the election to integrate Black church GOTV infrastructure with the broader democratic renewal frame. AME, NBC, and NBCA denominational networks should receive the "Vote is Sacred" Fellowship materials and electoral accountability frame by September 1. Sunday before Election Day (November 1): coordinated Souls to the Polls operations in every competitive state.

**Months 4-12: Community anchor and constitutional amendment infrastructure**

Faith institutions as anchor institutions for community health and housing initiatives (Domain 11, Domain 14) that operate independently of federal Medicaid and housing funding threats. Catholic Charities, Episcopal Charities, Jewish Federations, and ISNA's social services networks are the largest private social service infrastructure in the country. Coordinating these networks as a community resilience layer — operating alongside and independent of federal programs — is the Wave 2 function.

Constitutional amendment ratification: faith community networks are the organizational infrastructure for state ratification campaigns. The denominational networks in 34 states represent exactly the kind of broad, trusted-messenger, non-partisan-appearing infrastructure that constitutional amendment campaigns need. Begin mapping denominational chapter networks to state ratification infrastructure by January 2027.

## Risks and Contingencies

**Risk 1: Internal coalition fractures on reproductive rights, LGBTQ protections, immigration enforcement.** The faith coalition spans traditions with genuinely divergent views on these issues. Mitigation: organize the coalition around domains of shared agreement (voting rights, healthcare access, anti-deportation, rule of law) and do not require unanimity on domains that will fracture the coalition. The Catholic-evangelical wing can oppose deportation without supporting reproductive rights; the liberal mainline Protestant wing can support reproductive rights without being required to share the Catholic institutional platform. Domain-specific coalition composition is not coalition weakness; it is coalition management.

**Risk 2: Administration Religious Liberty Commission co-optation.** The Commission attempts to define legitimate religious advocacy as its own subset. Mitigation: the interfaith multi-denominational response (Jewish, Muslim, Hindu, Buddhist, mainline Protestant) makes the co-optation visible by demonstrating how narrow the Commission's claimed constituency is.

**Risk 3: Poll Chaplain legal exposure.** Trained volunteers documenting ICE enforcement near polling sites may face confrontation from enforcement agents or political operatives. Mitigation: RCFP's legal defense protocols apply to Poll Chaplains as observers; pre-establish legal defense relationships before deployment. The legal authority for Poll Chaplains as election observers is well-established in state election codes; confirm the specific legal authority in each state before training.

**Risk 4: Capacity fragmentation across 380,000 congregations.** The coordination infrastructure for this many institutions does not exist at the granular level. Mitigation: work through denominational networks (AME, ELCA, UCC, USCCB) rather than individual congregations. The denominational networks are the organizational unit; individual congregations are reached through the denominational channel.
