# Phase 1 Distribution — Pre-Launch Baseline Metrics

**Established: May 5, 2026 — before Phase 1 distribution**

This document fixes five quantified baselines at Day 0. Each metric includes a current count or state description, the measurement method used to establish it, and the re-measurement protocol that will be applied at Day 30, Month 3, Month 6, and Month 12. These baselines are the denominator against which any claimed Phase 1 impact will be calculated. Nothing here can be revised retroactively; this document locks the pre-launch state.

- **Month 12**: Full re-run plus law review publication search. Target: 10-30 citations consistent with early adopter phase of S-curve.

### 3.2 — Attribution Significance

The normal churn rate is the critical denominator. A framework-attributable litigation development would require:
- Filing of new coordinated cases using the framework's analytical structure (domain taxonomy vocabulary in briefs)
- Amicus briefs citing framework evidence in pending cases
- New multi-state coordination that uses the framework's comparative international precedents

**These signals are distinguishable from background churn.** Background churn (cases filed based on pre-existing legal theories) is expected to continue at 8-12 cases/week regardless of framework distribution. Framework-attributable additions would be cases using framework-specific vocabulary and evidence structures.

## Metric 5: Contingency Failure Metrics — Phase 1 "Failure to Launch" Thresholds

**Purpose**: Define, before distribution, what constitutes insufficient Phase 1 performance requiring diagnosis and course-correction. Failure thresholds must be set ex ante to prevent post-hoc rationalization.

### 5.1 — Absolute Failure Thresholds (Triggers Immediate Diagnosis)

If any of the following are true as of Day 30, Phase 1 distribution has failed to launch and requires immediate tactical review:

| Threshold | Definition | Trigger Action |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Email open rate | Less than 2% of recipients confirmed to have opened initial outreach email | Diagnose: subject line, sender domain reputation, list quality; test re-send with modified subject |
| Reply rate | 0 substantive replies (any tier) from first distribution wave | Diagnose: email deliverability; confirm list accuracy; consider phone follow-up to 5 highest-priority contacts |
| Institutional early adopters | Fewer than 2 confirmed engaged contacts (reply, request for materials, confirmed routing) by Day 30 | Escalate: shift from cold email to warm introduction strategy via bridge contacts |
| Framework citations | 0 citations in any Tier A source by Day 60 | Diagnose: whether outreach reached publication-level contacts; consider direct pitch to Just Security or Lawfare editors |
| Domain-specific engagement | Zero engagement with any single domain across all contacts | Diagnose: whether domain framing is relevant to recipient context; consider re-leading with highest-urgency domains (37, 29, or 4) |

### 5.2 — Underperformance Thresholds (Triggers Tactical Adjustment, Not Abandonment)

If any of the following are true at Day 30 or Month 3, Phase 1 is underperforming expectations but has not failed:

| Metric | Underperformance Signal | Expected Target | Tactical Response |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Open rate | 2-8% | 12-18% | A/B test subject lines; segment list by domain relevance |
| Engaged contacts | 2-5 | 10-15 by Day 30 | Activate warm introduction network; shift to phone/LinkedIn follow-up |
| Think tank citations | 0 by Month 3 | 1-2 | Direct author pitch to Brennan Center or Protect Democracy staff |
| Law school engagement | 0 by Month 3 | 2-4 faculty confirmed | Shift from cold email to conference/event context |
| AG office engagement | 0-1 by Day 30 | 2-4 | Engage through NAAG-connected intermediaries; route through state Dem party channels |

### 5.3 — Success Confirmation Thresholds (Day 30)

Phase 1 has achieved minimum viable launch if, by Day 30, **all three of the following are true**:

1. At least **10 Tier 1-2 contacts** have confirmed receipt or replied (from a target pool of 103)
2. At least **2 domain documents** have been confirmed requested or forwarded by recipients
3. At least **1 Tier A or B source** has expressed editorial interest OR 1 AG/law school contact has confirmed routing to staff

**If all three conditions are met, Phase 1 launch is confirmed viable and Month 2-3 measurement proceeds.**

### 5.4 — Structural Failure vs. Content Failure Distinction

Phase 1 failure can result from two distinct causes, and the diagnosis determines the response:

**Structural failure** (distribution problem, not content problem): Email does not reach inboxes; contact list is inaccurate; sender reputation is low; subject line triggers spam filters. Signals: very low open rate, delivery failures, no engagement from any contacts. Response: fix deliverability, rebuild contact list from scratch, shift to platform (Substack) delivery.

**Content failure** (content problem, not distribution problem): Emails are opened but do not generate engagement; contacts explicitly decline; feedback indicates content does not match audience need. Signals: adequate open rate but zero reply rate; explicit "not useful for our work" feedback. Response: domain re-framing, shorter executive summary, direct domain one-pagers to domain-specific audiences rather than full 35-domain framework.

**The two failures require opposite responses and must not be confused.**
