## Lead Finding

Three Phase 3 domains — H (Constitutional Resilience), K (Judiciary Restructuring), and 37a (Post-Election Certification) — are load-bearing for the January 3, 2027 hard deadline in ways that distinguish them from every other Phase 3 domain. Domain K requires distribution by December 20, 2026 to allow incoming Judiciary Committee offices 14 days of review before the January 3 seating. Domain H must trail Domain K by no more than 10-14 days, targeting January 10. Domain 37a's most operationally urgent layer — the post-certification contestation analysis — must reach States United Democracy Center, Protect Democracy, and Democracy Docket within 10 days of November 3, 2026 election results. None of these can defer to Q2 2027 without losing the primary advocacy window entirely.

The coalition mapping below is organized as three sections of approximately 1,000-1,200 words each, with specific expert contacts, distribution channels, and 2026-2027 litigation and legislative windows for each domain.

Contact: Reichman University faculty directory. Roznai is the leading international scholar on constitutional amendment limitations and unamendability doctrine — the theoretical foundation for Domain H's Article V analysis and its treatment of Germany's Basic Law Article 79(3) eternity clauses. His 2026 comparative work on Israel's judicial overhaul and democratic backsliding from political violence creates a live empirical test case for Domain H's arguments. He has been cited in IDEA blog posts (idea.int), which provides a warm routing channel through International IDEA contacts.

A Chemerinsky endorsement of Domain H's constitutional architecture framework would carry more mainstream legal credibility than any other single contact.

### 2026-2027 Litigation and Legislative Windows

**November 4, 2026**: Post-midterm window opens. Which constitutional vulnerabilities were exploited or exposed during the 2026 election cycle? Which states elected reformist attorneys general who could serve as Article V convention blocking coalitions? The domain gains empirical grounding from the results.

**January 3-17, 2027**: 120th Congress seating to committee assignments. This is the "blank slate" window for incoming Judiciary Committee members. Domain H must be in Tier 1 distribution before January 10 to land before committee assignments harden.

**January-February 2027**: Freedom House and V-Dem annual documentation submission windows. Domain H's comparative constitutional design material supplements these surveys' US country assessments.

**2026-2027 Litigation Unlocking Constitutional Reform Arguments**:

*Trump v. Callais* (post-April 29, 2026 SCOTUS decision): The *Callais* ruling's narrowing of Section 2 VRA has already triggered litigation in Arizona, Wisconsin, Louisiana, Alabama, and Tennessee. In Arizona specifically, GOP leaders signaled they would challenge the independent redistricting commission's maps under *Callais* standards. Arizona and Wisconsin post-*Callais* state VRA litigation, expected to produce circuit-level rulings in 2026-2027, will generate the empirical redistricting outcomes that strengthen Domain H's argument that statutory voting rights protections require constitutional buttressing. The *Callais* cascade's nine-state state-VRA implications (states with their own VRA laws) are a live test of whether state constitutional protections can substitute for weakened federal protections.

*Lucy v. Skrmetti* (Middle District of Tennessee, June 2026): A new challenge under H.B. 1704 (signed April 21, 2026) filed in June 2026 — confirming that the constitutional litigation generated by *Skrmetti*'s June 2025 SCOTUS ruling continues at the district and circuit level. The Sixth Circuit applications of *Skrmetti* in 2026-2027 will test whether the sex-discrimination equal protection framework survives in the lower courts after SCOTUS foreclosed the statutory path. These cases provide the empirical hook for Domain H's Section 1.2 argument about judicial capture as the precondition for broader erosion.

*Post-Wilcox removal power cases* (Trump v. Slaughter, Trump v. Cook, expected June-July 2026): The removal power rulings, by eliminating or substantially narrowing *Humphrey's Executor* (1935), create the structural condition that Domain H identifies as the most consequential judicial-capture mechanism of the current term. If the Court eliminates independent agency protection, Domain H's constitutional fix argument gains immediate urgency: the erosion is no longer prospective but accomplished.

**Article V Convention Defensive Window**: The Convention of States Action coalition holds 28 of 34 required state resolutions. The next state legislative sessions (January-June 2027 in most states) are the next opportunity for either side to change the count. Domain H's Article V threat assessment must be in circulation before those sessions open.

### 2026-2027 Legislative Windows

**Live Bills in 119th Congress (current vehicles for 120th Congress re-introduction)**:
- H.R. 1074 — Supreme Court Term Limits and Regular Appointments Act (Hank Johnson, D-GA): 18-year term/biennial appointment schedule. Will require re-introduction in 120th Congress if not passed by January 3, 2027.
- Supreme Court Biennial Appointments and Term Limits Act (Whitehouse/Booker/Blumenthal/Padilla): Senate companion. Committee status: held by Republican Judiciary majority. Immediate re-introduction vehicle for a Democratic-controlled Judiciary Committee.
- SCERT Act (Whitehouse/Johnson): Ethics enforcement mechanism. Advanced out of committee in 118th Congress; blocked on Senate floor. Highest probability of passage with any Democratic Senate majority due to 83% public support for mandatory recusal provisions.
- SHADOW Act (Raskin): Shadow docket written opinions requirement. No Republican co-sponsors in 119th Congress; potential bipartisan vehicle if combined with judgeships expansion.
- Federal Judgeships Act: Bipartisan 63-district-court-judgeship expansion bill (September 2025 introduction). Only Domain K reform with realistic bipartisan passage under any configuration of the 120th Congress.
- Manchin-Welch Constitutional Amendment (December 2024): Article V amendment approach to 18-year term limits. Requires supermajority; 10-15 year horizon, but introduces political record pressure on members.

**FISA Court Reform**: The June 12-15, 2026 deadline for disclosure of a March 17, 2026 FISC opinion under the Wyden declassification deal is the immediate Domain K monitoring trigger. If the disclosure deadline passes without compliance, it creates an immediate legislative hook for S.3696 (FISA Accountability and Extension Act) in the 2026 advocacy window before the midterms.

**Key Legislative Sequencing for 120th Congress (January 3, 2027 forward)**:
- January 3-17: Committee assignment period. Domain K must be in distribution before assignments finalize.
- January-March 2027: First 90 days of reform window. Ethics-first coalition strategy: SCERT Act and Federal Judgeships Act are the highest-probability bills to move first, using their bipartisan polling support to establish reform credibility before the more contested term limits legislation.
- March-June 2027: If ethics legislation passes or moves, statutory term limits legislation (H.R. 1074 analog) becomes the next legislative priority.

### Predictable Post-Certification Litigation Triggers

**Scenario 1 — House control contested in 5 or fewer seats**: Federal Contested Elections Act (FCEA) mechanics become immediately operative. The FCEA (2 U.S.C. §§ 381-396) provides a statutory procedure for House members to challenge election outcomes after certification. Domain 37a's pre-positioned legal analysis of FCEA procedural requirements — notice within 30 days of certification, grounds, evidence standards — is the research that Protect Democracy and Lawyers' Committee need within 10 days of election results.

**Scenario 2 — Section 3 enforcement action against a winning candidate**: *Trump v. Anderson* (2024) foreclosed the self-executing state enforcement path for Section 3 disqualification, but left open congressional action (Section 5) and federal criminal prosecution. If a candidate who won a House or Senate seat is subject to a Section 3 challenge, the litigation architecture — which Domain 37a was designed to map — activates immediately. The most likely trigger is a candidate with documented involvement in the January 6, 2021 events who wins a competitive 2026 seat.

**Scenario 3 — Post-election redistricting impacts on certified results**: The *Callais* redistricting cascade (nine states with pending mid-decade redistricting challenges) will produce some states where election results occur under maps that are simultaneously being litigated. Any post-election contest in an affected district (Alabama's CD-7 area, Louisiana's CD-2 analog, Tennessee, Wisconsin) will intersect Domain 37a's certification architecture with Domain 49's redistricting cascade analysis.

**Scenario 4 — Certification refusal by a county official**: The most operationally dangerous scenario and the primary focus of States United Democracy Center's 2026 monitoring work. If any of the 12 election denier candidates running for secretary of state in 2026 win, their first certification decision (in states with 2026 SoS elections, the new SoS takes office before or concurrent with the November election depending on state law) could trigger a certification refusal. Domain 37a's Emergency County-Level Response Protocol — TRO filing strategy, jurisdiction of federal vs. state courts, ECRA's nondiscretion standard — is the operational resource for this scenario.

**Scenario 5 — RNC convention challenges and rules disputes**: The Republican National Committee's 2028 presidential convention rules are determined at the 2026 RNC winter meeting (January 2027). Domain 37a's institutional scope extends to the party rules architecture that governs how delegate certifications translate into nomination outcomes — a layer of certification vulnerability separate from government-administered election certification.
