## Category 1: Trade Decoupling — IEEPA Constitutional Crisis and WTO Collapse

These sources document the core trade pathway: how IEEPA tariff escalation bypassed congressional authority, produced a Supreme Court ruling that rebalanced executive-congressional trade power, and how the parallel WTO non-compliance removes the multilateral constraint on executive trade action.

---

**T1. *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump*, No. 24-1287 (Supreme Court, February 20, 2026)**
URL: https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/25pdf/24-1287_4gcj.pdf
Author/Institution: US Supreme Court
Date: February 20, 2026
Annotation: The Supreme Court's 6-3 decision holding that IEEPA does not authorize the president to impose tariffs. This is the most constitutionally significant trade ruling in decades — it establishes that the executive's use of emergency economic power to impose sweeping tariffs exceeded statutory authority. Required for the democratic accountability argument in Section 2: the ruling confirms that IEEPA tariff authority is not plenary executive power, that congressional authorization is required, and that the Youngstown framework applies to executive trade actions. Priority reading for production; confirms the Section 122 replacement context.

---

Author/Institution: Steptoe & Johnson LLP (international trade law firm)
Date: February 2026
Annotation: Practitioner analysis of the *Learning Resources* decision's immediate operational implications. Documents the cascade of legal and practical consequences: CBP implementing the termination of IEEPA tariffs, refund processing orders, and the Section 122 replacement tariff structure. Essential for understanding the post-ruling trade policy landscape — particularly the 150-day Section 122 clock (expires July 24, 2026). Cross-reference with Norton Rose Fulbright and Skadden analyses for comprehensive practitioner perspective.
