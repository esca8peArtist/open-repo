## Scoring Key

Five dimensions, 1-5 each, 25 points maximum.

| Code | Dimension | What it measures |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **A** | Adoption Likelihood | How readily does this sector's research culture integrate external analytical documents? |
| **I** | Movement Infrastructure Depth | Does this sector have federation structures with staff capacity to distribute and adopt? |
| **L** | Cross-Domain Leverage | Does this domain broker connections between multiple Phase 1 and Phase 2 domains? |
| **U** | Timing Urgency | Is there a hard external deadline within 90 days that increases this domain's value? |
| **C** | Content Availability | Is there sufficient existing literature and sourcing to produce a strong domain efficiently by June 2026? |

### Rank 1 — Reproductive Freedom as Democratic Architecture

**Score: A:5 I:4 L:5 C:5 U:5 = 24/25** | Production: 8-12 hours | Wave: June 2026

**Democratic Design Gap.** No Phase 1 domain analyzes reproductive freedom as a civic design problem. Domain 33 (ballot initiative suppression) touches the mechanism by which successful reproductive rights ballot measures are being overridden or pre-empted, and Domain 31 (Medicaid crisis) covers the Medicaid-reproductive care intersection. But neither provides the structural argument: restrictions on reproductive autonomy function as civic exclusion mechanisms that suppress voter registration, reduce economic independence, and disenfranchise reproductive-age women who are the most decisive turnout variable in competitive 2026 midterms. This argument does not exist in any current advocacy document from any of the named organizations.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** Three certified 2026 ballot measures: Missouri Amendment 3 (legislatively referred — would repeal 2024 protective amendment and impose near-total ban with limited exceptions); Nevada Question 6 (requires second passage to constitutionally entrench reproductive rights, active campaign underway); Virginia Right to Reproductive Freedom Amendment (would constitutionalize reproductive freedom, with a "compelling state interest" exception threshold). Active signature collection in Idaho and Nebraska. The Fifth Circuit's May 1, 2026 nationwide mifepristone restriction order (temporarily stayed by SCOTUS through May 11, 2026) and the Comstock Act enforcement threat are live — not hypothetical.

**Movement Infrastructure.** Exceptional and entirely absent from Phase 1. Reproductive Freedom for All is the coordinating hub for all three active ballot campaigns. Center for Reproductive Rights has active litigation in five states. SisterSong Women of Color Reproductive Justice Collective (200+ member organizations) connects reproductive justice to environmental, immigration, and disability justice sectors simultaneously — making it the Phase 2 distribution bridge into multiple other domains at once. NARAL, National Network of Abortion Funds, and Planned Parenthood Action Fund (51 state affiliates) provide a national-to-local distribution structure.

**Timeline Constraint.** CRITICAL. Ballot campaigns are in active qualification or campaign phases now. Mifepristone legal status is live. Any Comstock Act enforcement action by DOJ triggers immediate 72-hour rapid briefing protocol regardless of standard sequencing.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. Guttmacher Institute state law data, KFF reproductive rights dashboard, Center for Reproductive Rights litigation tracker, IWPR economic suppression data ($11K-$28K annual earnings impact documented by multiple studies), Sommer et al. peer-reviewed confirmation of post-Dobbs voter registration surge (35% women vs. 9% men). International comparative evidence from Ireland 2018 and France 2024 constitutional model is well-documented. Research can proceed immediately.

**Unique Contribution.** The democratic-design argument — reproductive restrictions as civic exclusion architecture with quantified voter registration, economic, and participation impacts — is analytically distinct from all existing reproductive rights advocacy material, which frames the issue in terms of bodily autonomy and health. This framing connects reproductive justice organizations to the framework's voting rights (Domain 1) and ballot initiative suppression (Domain 33) analysis in ways that existing advocacy literature does not.

### Rank 2 — Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Manipulation

**Score: A:5 I:4 L:5 C:5 U:5 = 24/25** | Production: 12-15 hours | Wave: June 2026

**Democratic Design Gap.** Phase 1's Domain 25 (FISA/Surveillance) covers foreign intelligence collection architecture. Domain 36 covers AI governance in government decision-making. Neither analyzes the convergence of commercial surveillance infrastructure (4,000+ data broker companies selling psychographic profiles), government warrant-bypass via commercial data purchase, and AI-generated electoral manipulation into a unified electoral integrity threat. The gap is the convergence analysis — no existing document synthesizes all three streams.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** The June 12, 2026 FISA Section 702 renewal deadline (45-day extension passed April 30) is the specific legislative window. The central unresolved dispute in Senate negotiations: whether to close the commercial data broker loophole through which intelligence agencies purchase data about Americans' political activity without warrants. Senator Wyden has documented FBI searches of 19,000 donors to a congressional campaign using FISA Section 702 procedures. The NRSC Talarico deepfake (March 11, 2026 — first confirmed national party committee synthetic candidate video) is the 2026 electoral manipulation anchor. PNAS February 2026 study: AI-generated suppression ads reduce turnout 1.86% overall, 14.2% for non-white voters in minority-majority battleground counties.

**Movement Infrastructure.** Cross-partisan: Brennan Center, ACLU, EFF, EPIC, CDT on the left; Cato Institute and Reason Foundation on the libertarian-right; Senators Rand Paul and Ron Wyden provide the floor coalition. The bipartisan structure means this domain can reach constituencies unreachable by any other Phase 2 domain. State AGs who filed challenges to Trump surveillance executive orders provide an additional distribution tier.

**Timeline Constraint.** CRITICAL. June 12 FISA deadline is a hard external event. Research must complete by June 8 to enable Senate floor distribution. After June 12, this domain's urgency drops significantly regardless of its analytical importance.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. Brennan Center Section 702 2026 resource page is the comprehensive legal anchor. Wyden-Lee-Davidson-Lofgren bill text provides the legislative vehicle. PNAS February 2026 voter suppression study provides the empirical foundation. EU comparison (GDPR Article 9 prohibition on political opinion data processing without consent; EU Regulation 2024/900 banning political microtargeting in elections — which caused Meta to exit the EU political ad market) is well-documented. Palantir-ICE documentation through ACLU, EFF, and NPR is accessible.

**Unique Contribution.** First domain in the framework to analyze how commercial surveillance and government surveillance converge as a single electoral manipulation threat. Builds on Domain 25 (FISA) and Domain 36 (AI governance) but reaches a different conclusion: the democratic design failure is not in either stream independently, but in their convergence — a mechanism through which aspiring autocrats can surveil political opponents using data that private-sector companies collect without constitutional constraints.

### Rank 3 — Criminal Justice, Abolitionism, and the Civic Exclusion Architecture

**Score: A:4 I:5 L:5 C:5 U:4 = 23/25** | Production: 12-18 hours | Wave: July 2026

**Democratic Design Gap.** Domain 29 covers prosecutorial weaponization and DOJ capture — how the justice system is weaponized by the executive. Domain 48 (in the existing pipeline) covers the structural downstream consequence: how criminal justice contact, conviction, and collateral consequences systematically remove people from the civic body. The gap is the distinction between the instrument (prosecutorial abuse) and the architecture (civic exclusion by design). The civic exclusion frame — 4.4 million disenfranchised, 1 in 13 Black adults unable to vote, jury exclusion, 40,000+ federal job categories barring felony records, housing discrimination, and debt burden — is analytically distinct from any existing Phase 1 domain.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** Virginia's Right to Vote Amendment (HJ 2 / SJ 2) will appear on the November 2026 ballot — if adopted, it would restore voting rights to 230,000+ Virginians with felony convictions, eliminating a lifetime voting ban. A federal judge in January 2026 ruled Virginia's existing lifetime ban violates federal law. Automatic restoration bills are active in Massachusetts, Washington, and New Hampshire state legislatures. SCOTUS OT2025-2026 includes two disenfranchisement-adjacent cases. M4BL's People's Assembly Project (launched April 2026) is building governing infrastructure in California, Illinois, and North Carolina — providing an immediate distribution vehicle.

**Movement Infrastructure.** Highest leverage of any single domain. M4BL Policy Table (100+ organizations) connects criminal justice, reproductive justice, environmental justice, economic democracy, and housing simultaneously. A distribution relationship with M4BL reaches a coalition network that dwarfs Phase 1's entire contact list. Additional infrastructure: Critical Resistance, Brennan Center Justice Program, Sentencing Project, Worth Rises, Prison Policy Initiative, NAACP LDF (existing Phase 1 contact through Domain 42 drug policy distribution).

**Timeline Constraint.** HIGH. Virginia ballot measure in active campaign phase. State legislative restoration bills in current sessions. SCOTUS cases timed to spring/summer 2026 decisions.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. Sentencing Project annual disenfranchisement survey, Brennan Center "Democracy and Justice" series, Worth Rises debt burden data, Prison Policy Initiative civic exclusion research — all are production-ready primary sources requiring no additional access.

**Unique Contribution.** No Phase 2 domain except this one connects the criminal justice system to the democratic design framework through the civic exclusion argument rather than through rights or prosecutorial abuse. This framing gives M4BL's policy ecosystem a tool that is analytically distinct from its existing abolition and decarceration literature — and that connects it explicitly to Phase 1's voting rights (Domain 1), prosecutorial (Domain 29), and electoral interference (Domain 37) analysis.

### Rank 4 — Environmental Justice and Community Participation Rights

**Score: A:4 I:5 L:5 C:5 U:4 = 23/25** | Production: 12-18 hours | Wave: July 2026

**Democratic Design Gap.** Phase 1's `environmental-rollbacks-tracker.md` documents the events. No standalone domain analyzes environmental justice as a democratic participation question — specifically, the right of frontline communities to meaningful procedural voice in decisions imposing pollution burdens, and the destruction of the institutional infrastructure that made that voice possible.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** NEPA's implementing regulations were removed from the Code of Federal Regulations on January 8, 2026, eliminating the primary participation mechanism available to communities without formal political power. All ten EPA environmental justice regional offices were closed; $400 million in EJ grants were canceled and are now in federal litigation (Earthjustice, SELC, Public Rights Project coalition lawsuit). The EPA Endangerment Finding revocation is in active D.C. Circuit proceedings. A coalition of 18 state AGs has filed opposing the endangerment finding revocation. Earthjustice's climate protection repeal lawsuit is in active D.C. Circuit briefing.

**Movement Infrastructure.** Climate Justice Alliance (100+ frontline organizations across 7 regions) is the fastest single distribution channel into frontline community organizing networks. The NRDC-Sierra Club legal team (existing Phase 1 distribution adjacent through environmental rollbacks tracker) provides the institutional bridge. WE ACT for Environmental Justice, Deep South Center for Environmental Justice, and Indigenous Environmental Network provide regional depth.

**Timeline Constraint.** HIGH. EPA Endangerment Finding comment period active through summer 2026. D.C. Circuit briefing schedule creates advocacy windows in June-August.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. EPA formal EJ office termination orders, Earthjustice complaint documentation, Climate Justice Alliance reports, Robert Bullard's environmental justice research archive — all accessible. International comparisons (EU Aarhus Convention participation rights, UK Environmental Assessment frameworks) are well-documented.

**Unique Contribution.** Reframes the environmental rollbacks narrative as a democratic participation rights narrative — the EPA's actions eliminate not just environmental protections but the specific procedural tools through which political outsiders exercise democratic voice. This is analytically distinct from climate policy advocacy and gives environmental justice organizations a constitutional democracy argument that is not currently in their litigation or advocacy framing.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** The Fifth Circuit's August 2025 ruling that NLRB removal protections are likely unconstitutional stands despite the SpaceX case's February 2026 jurisdictional dismissal (NLRB relinquished jurisdiction after the National Mediation Board ruled SpaceX is covered by the Railway Labor Act). SpaceX, Amazon, Trader Joe's, and Starbucks have all raised the constitutional challenge in pending proceedings. The AFL-CIO filed a Supreme Court intervention motion in November 2025 in the SpaceX case; the constitutional question will eventually reach SCOTUS through one of the remaining circuit cases.

**Movement Infrastructure.** Unmatched in scale. AFL-CIO's 12.5 million members represent a member-education and research-distribution infrastructure that is larger than any other movement sector. EPI publication automatically triggers distribution into the AFL-CIO's legislative and research infrastructure. SEIU, CWA, and Labor Notes provide sector-specific depth.

**Timeline Constraint.** HIGH. Third and Ninth Circuit cases proceeding toward merits decisions in 2026. Organizing window before any ruling is finite — a SCOTUS decision upholding the Fifth Circuit would immediately impair NLRB's functioning as an organizing protection institution.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** Medium-high. The Fifth Circuit ruling is documented; the Third and Ninth Circuit proceedings are active. The labor-democracy nexus literature (unionization and voter turnout correlation; EPI research on union density and democratic participation) requires some synthesis work. Estimated 12-18 hours reflects this.

**Unique Contribution.** The Humphrey's Executor constitutional framing — the same removal-power mechanism that destroyed CFPB independence is now attacking the NLRB — is already documented in Phase 1 (Domain 6). This domain is the explicit labor-sector application of Domain 6's constitutional analysis, with the AFL-CIO's 12.5 million members as the distribution constituency.

**Score: A:4 I:4 L:4 C:5 U:4 = 21/25** | Production: 8-12 hours | Wave: July 2026

**Democratic Design Gap.** No Phase 1 domain analyzes campaign finance as the structural mechanism connecting Citizens United to every sector-specific corporate capture throughout the framework. **Current Structural Vulnerability.** Dark money expenditures: $1.9 billion in 2024, nearly double the 2020 record. Republican filibuster is the blocking mechanism — not lack of majority support. Four state dark money disclosure ballot measures active for November 2026 (providing direct campaign toolkit integration opportunity). **Movement Infrastructure.** Campaign Legal Center, Common Cause (existing one-degree-of-separation from Phase 1 network), Brennan Center Democracy Program, Issue One (200+ former members of Congress — cross-partisan reach), End Citizens United / Let America Vote.

**Timeline Constraint.** HIGH. DISCLOSE Act Senate markup window in late summer 2026. Four state ballot measure campaigns with active August-September campaign periods.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. OpenSecrets 2024 dark money database, Brennan Center reports, FEC enforcement record, Citizens United decision and subsequent circuit development, DISCLOSE Act legislative text — all production-ready.

**Unique Contribution.** The meta-analysis contribution: this is the domain that explains how all the sector-specific corporate captures throughout Phase 1 operate through a single legal architecture. It is not a campaign finance advocacy document — it is a structural analysis of how dark money is the connective tissue of every other democratic erosion documented in the framework.

### Rank 8 — Medicaid-Democracy Nexus (OBBBA-NVRA Intersection)

**Score: A:4 I:4 L:5 C:5 U:5 = 23/25 (ranked 8 due to research overlap with existing Domain 50)** | Production: 10-14 hours | Wave: June 2026

**Note on ranking.** This domain scores 23/25 but is ranked 8 because Domain 50 (existing: `domain-50-healthcare-democracy-nexus-obbba-nvra.md`) covers significant overlapping ground. The remaining gap is the enforcement mechanism: the specific statutory theory under which state AGs can file NVRA enforcement actions against state Medicaid agencies that reduce voter registration offers during OBBBA implementation — converting a healthcare advocacy argument into a constitutional democracy argument with a specific cause of action.

**Current Structural Vulnerability.** HHS interim final rule on OBBBA work requirements issued June 1, 2026. State outreach to affected beneficiaries required June 30-August 31, 2026. January 1, 2027 implementation deadline. CBO projects 15 million people losing coverage by 2034; Institute for Responsive Government documents 30-40 million adult citizens interact with NVRA-covered programs annually, including Medicaid.

**Timeline Constraint.** CRITICAL. June 1 HHS rule is the immediate anchor document. January 1, 2027 is the advocacy close. NVRA enforcement actions need to be filed before implementation to have maximum procedural effect.

**Evidence Base Maturity.** High. June 1 HHS rule (to be anchored immediately upon publication), CBPP and KFF OBBBA analyses, Institute for Responsive Government NVRA-Medicaid research, DOJ Civil Rights Division enforcement history — production-ready.

**Unique Contribution.** The NVRA causal chain: enrollment capacity cuts are legally equivalent to voter registration infrastructure cuts under the National Voter Registration Act. This argument gives state AGs a specific statutory enforcement theory that does not require winning an abstract rights argument — it requires only proving that Medicaid agencies are failing to comply with NVRA's specific voter registration offer requirements. This is the domain that makes Domain 31's healthcare analysis legally actionable for the voting rights litigation community.
