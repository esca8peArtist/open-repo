## 1. Unique Angle (200 words)

Domain 39's defining contribution is a reframing that no existing healthcare advocacy document has achieved: healthcare access treated as civic infrastructure rather than social welfare. The distinction matters operationally because it determines which audiences engage.

Election protection organizations, voting rights advocates, and democratic reform coalitions do not currently receive healthcare policy as relevant to their work. Domain 31 — the framework's existing Medicaid/OBBBA crisis document — provides coverage loss numbers, work requirement mechanisms, and NVRA Section 7 enforcement theory. What it does not provide is the democratic-design argument: the five empirically grounded causal pathways through which healthcare deprivation produces civic exclusion. Domain 39 provides those pathways.

The strongest anchor is methodologically rare: a randomized controlled trial (the Oregon Health Insurance Experiment) establishing that Medicaid coverage causes a 7% increase in voter turnout. Not correlation — causation, with random assignment ruling out selection effects. Combine this with the American Political Science Review's 2025 study linking rural hospital closures to measured 3.8 percentage-point turnout declines, and you have peer-reviewed causal evidence, at scale, connecting healthcare infrastructure to democratic participation.

This is the only document in the 40-domain framework that makes that empirical bridge explicit across five independent pathways simultaneously — and does so in time for the June 1 HHS comment deadline.

## 2. Key Themes and Evidence (300 words)

**Pathway 1: Rural hospital closures reduce voter turnout.** APSR 2025 (10.5 million rural residents, 2016-2020 elections): individuals whose nearest hospital closed before an election were measurably less likely to vote than unaffected counterparts, with effects strongest for older and lower-income residents. Effects decayed within 12 months — consistent with economic and social capital disruption, not permanent deregistration. Eighteen rural hospitals closed or converted in 2025 alone. Over 700 remain at closure risk; one-third of rural healthcare facilities nationally face risk according to University of North Carolina analysis. The OBBBA's Medicaid revenue cuts directly threaten rural hospital viability — rural hospitals serve disproportionately Medicaid-dependent populations.

**Pathway 2: Medical debt as civic exclusion.** Americans carry at least $220 billion in medical debt. The mechanism: medical emergency produces debt; debt produces credit damage; credit damage produces residential instability; residential instability produces voter registration loss. The CFPB's January 2025 rule removing medical debt from credit reports was vacated in July 2025 by a Trump-joined plaintiffs' motion. OBBBA's 11.8 million projected coverage losses (CBO) expand the uninsured pool that accumulates debt.

**Pathway 3: Medicaid offices as NVRA voter registration infrastructure.** The NVRA requires public assistance agencies to offer voter registration at every in-person enrollment contact. Traditional NVRA implementation at Medicaid offices produces approximately 1.6% registration rates. States with Medicaid Automatic Voter Registration (AVR) reach 85%+ of eligible unregistered contacts. OBBBA coverage losses reduce contact volume; 6-month recertification doubles administrative burden without staffing increases; work requirement compliance infrastructure crowds out NVRA capacity.

**Pathway 4: Maternal mortality as democratic harm.** Black women die in childbirth at 3-4x the rate of white women (CDC 2023: 50.3 per 100,000 live births). Communities experiencing concentrated maternal mortality are simultaneously communities with the highest structural voting barriers. OBBBA accelerates rural obstetric unit closures.

**Pathway 5: Disability disenfranchisement.** Three mechanisms: guardianship strips voting rights for 1.3 million Americans without individualized capacity assessment; FY2026 budget proposes zeroing the PAVA program (the primary legal infrastructure for disabled voter access); OBBBA work requirement exemptions are inadequate for episodic conditions.

**June 1 urgency.** HHS interim final rule on OBBBA work requirement implementation is due June 1, 2026. This is the comment window. Organizations with NVRA enforcement standing should audit the rule within 30 days of release.
