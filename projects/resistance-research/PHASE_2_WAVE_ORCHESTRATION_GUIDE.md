## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Zero replies after 48 hours

Normal. National policy organizations (CLC, Issue One) take 3-7 business days. California campaign organizations take 3-10 days during campaign mode. Do not send a follow-up before Day 14. The campaign finance organizations you are contacting are in the business of reading external research — they have processes for it. Silence at Day 2 means nothing.

### Risk 2: Gist URL returns 404

Recreate the Gist. This takes 10 minutes. Source document: projects/resistance-research/domain-51-campaign-finance-dark-money.md. Template for format: GIST_TEMPLATE_DOMAIN_56.md. The new URL will need to replace the URL in all unsent templates before sending.

### Risk 3: California Fair Elections Act removed from ballot before you send

Check Ballotpedia before Wave 2. If removed: modify email subject lines from "California Fair Elections Act campaign" to "California campaign finance reform analysis." The body templates remain valid. Full decision tree in DOMAIN_51_CONTINGENCY_SB_299_FALLBACK.md, Section 6.

### Risk 4: Email bounces

If an email bounces, try the backup address:
- CLC: info@campaignlegal.org (general inbox)
- Common Cause CA: ca@commoncause.org (general inbox, will route to Kemp)
- All other contacts have single verified addresses; if they bounce, log it and note in WORKLOG.md

### Risk 5: Low engagement at T+7 (fewer than 2 STRONG signals)

This is a WEAK signal outcome. The T7_CHECKPOINT_DECISION_AUTOMATION.md document routes you through the Phase 2 decision. WEAK outcome means hold Phase 2 and escalate to user for a contingency decision. Options include stakeholder substitution (secondary-tier contacts), framing revision (single-domain pitch vs. full framework), or hold pending Day 30 assessment. None of these are blocking issues — they are re-calibrations.
