# Phase 3 Resource Reallocation Framework
## Budget Scenarios A/B/C | Timeline Compression | Parallel-Track Trade-offs

**Purpose:** When Phase 3 resources (time, budget, researcher capacity) are not at baseline, this document tells you exactly what to do with them. Three budget scenarios (A = conservative, B = nominal, C = aggressive) with specific dollar figures. Timeline compression analysis showing exactly how much faster work goes with additional budget. External researcher pricing model with sourcing, vetting, and onboarding timeline. Parallel-track trade-off analysis showing which research streams can compress vs. which are critical path.

**Key constraint:** Phase 3 research is time-bounded (January 3, 2027 Congressional seating), not dollar-bounded. The central resource being reallocated is researcher time, not cash. Cash reallocation either buys more researcher time (external consultants) or improves the quality of researcher time (data access, distribution infrastructure). Budget scenarios A, B, C are calibrated around this constraint.

## EXTERNAL RESEARCHER PRICING MODEL

### Market Context

External research consultants for constitutional law and policy research come from three markets: academic (law school researchers), practitioner (policy organization staff as consultants), and freelance (independent researchers from platforms). These markets have different pricing, vetting requirements, and onboarding costs.

### Academic Researchers (Law School)

**Profile:** JD/PhD candidates or postdoctoral fellows in constitutional law, political science, or public policy programs at top-tier law schools.

**Sourcing:** Contact law school career services at Yale Law, Harvard Law, Georgetown Law, or Berkeley Law with a research assistant posting. Alternatively, reach directly through law school faculty research coordinators.

**Pricing:**
- JD candidate (2L/3L with research experience): $45–75/hour
- SJD or JSD candidate (advanced law PhD): $60–90/hour
- Law school research assistant (summer or semesterly): $20–35/hour (if managed through law school program)
- Postdoctoral fellow in constitutional law: $80–100/hour (limited availability)

**Vetting requirements:** Request 2 writing samples (policy brief format preferred; law review article is acceptable but signals different format capabilities). 1-hour trial task (verify 20 citations in a provided document; present findings). Request one faculty reference.

**Onboarding time:** 20–30 hours for a researcher who does NOT have Phase 3 context (reads PHASE_3_PARALLEL_RESEARCH_RUNWAY.md, PHASE_3_DOMAIN_K_PRELIMINARY_RESEARCH.md, sample Phase 2 domain document). First week of output typically at 75–80% of a fully onboarded researcher's quality.

**Total cost for Domain K specialist (20–30 hours research + 20-hour onboarding):** 40–50 hours × $60–75/hr = $2,400–3,750.

**Optimal use:** Source verification (Congress.gov bill status, co-sponsor counts, committee assignments), international case study research (Israel HCJ cases, Germany Basic Law amendment tracking), Gist formatting and copyediting, research gap filling.

**NOT optimal for:** Primary analytical framework decisions, electoral feasibility assessment, expert contact outreach (relationship management requires continuity), final Gist writing.

### Practitioner Consultants (Policy Organization Alumni)

**Profile:** Former staff at Fix the Court, Brennan Center, Demand Justice, Campaign Legal Center, Protect Democracy who are now independent or have capacity for consulting.

**Sourcing:** LinkedIn search for "former [Fix the Court / Brennan Center / Demand Justice] [research director / policy analyst / attorney]" who are now freelancing or at firms. Alternatively, ask a Tier 1 contact (Gabe Roth, Josh Orton) for referrals.

**Pricing:**
- Former policy organization research director: $100–150/hour
- Former staff attorney: $120–175/hour
- Former communications director: $75–120/hour (for Gist writing and distribution coordination)

**Vetting requirements:** Prior work samples from organization (request publicly available reports they authored). 1-hour consultation call to assess Phase 3 domain familiarity. No trial task needed if sourced through Tier 1 referral.

**Onboarding time:** 8–15 hours. Practitioner consultants understand the policy and advocacy landscape; they need only to be oriented to Phase 3's specific methodology and existing research (not to the broader policy context).

**Total cost for Domain K specialist with practitioner background (20–30 hours research + 10-hour onboarding):** 30–40 hours × $125–150/hr = $3,750–6,000.

**Optimal use:** Zone 4 (legislative political feasibility) in Domain K — practitioners have the contact network and recent Hill experience to assess bill viability. Expert contact outreach strategy. Distribution channel strategy. Review and validate primary researcher's analytical conclusions.

**NOT optimal for:** Mechanical source verification (overqualified; not cost-effective at $125/hr for URL checking).

### Freelance Research Platforms

**Profile:** Researchers on Catalant, Expert360, or Upwork with policy research backgrounds.

**Sourcing:** Catalant (catalant.com) — specializes in connecting organizations with former management consultants and expert-in-residence researchers. Expert360 (expert360.com) — similar marketplace. Upwork — lower price point but less vetted quality.

**Pricing:**
- Catalant senior policy researcher: $80–130/hour
- Expert360 policy consultant: $75–120/hour
- Upwork policy researcher: $30–65/hour (higher variance in quality)

**Vetting requirements:** Full vetting required regardless of platform. Request 2 writing samples in policy brief format. 2-hour trial task (research a specific question from Domain K and produce a 500-word brief with citations). This adds 2 hours × platform rate to onboarding cost.

**Onboarding time:** 25–40 hours. Freelance researchers lack both academic context and practitioner context; full Phase 3 orientation required.

**Total cost for Domain K verification work (10–15 hours + 30-hour onboarding):** 40–45 hours × $50–80/hr = $2,000–3,600.

**Optimal use:** Mechanical verification tasks (URL checking, co-sponsor count updates, citation formatting) where quality variance is acceptable. Not for analytical or writing work where Phase 3's specific standard must be maintained.

**NOT optimal for:** Anything requiring Phase 3 methodology context before Week 3 of work.

## PARALLEL-TRACK TRADE-OFF ANALYSIS

### Critical Path vs. Compressible Tracks

The December research plan in PHASE_3_PARALLEL_RESEARCH_RUNWAY.md has five concurrent tracks. Not all tracks are equal. This section maps which tracks can be compressed or deferred without threatening January 3, and which cannot.

**Critical Path Tracks — Cannot Compress or Defer:**

| Track | Domain | Why Non-Deferrable | Hours Required | Deadline |
|-------|--------|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| A | Domain K: final production, Gist, distribution | January 3 congressional seating window. Domain K is the primary judicial reform research package. Missing December 20 closes the "blank slate" congressional window. | 8–12 | December 20 |
| B (partial) | Domain H: post-election Zone 3 update | Zone 3 feasibility scoring is directly tied to 120th Congress composition. Cannot be updated after January 3 with the same immediacy. | 4–6 | December 20 (for Zone 3 only) |

**Combined Brief:** The 6–8 page K+H combined brief is technically compressible (can defer to January 10) but is the highest-leverage single document for congressional staff who will not read 7,500 words. Deferring it to January 10 is acceptable but weakens the January 3 distribution.

**Compressible Tracks (Can Defer to January–March 2027 Without Material Harm):**

| Track | Domain | Why Deferrable | Estimated Deferral Impact | Deferred Deadline |
|-------|--------|----------------|--------------------------|------------------|
| B (full Domain H Gist) | Domain H: full Gist + Tier 1 distribution | January 10 is the Domain H hard deadline, not December 20. Domain H Tier 1 distribution (Scheppele, Roznai, Balkin, Chemerinsky, Protect Democracy, NCC) does not lose its value between December 20 and January 10. | Low (2-week delay in expert outreach) | January 10, 2027 |
| C | Domain 49: Callais redistricting post-election | Litigation deadlines begin January 1 but filing preparation has a rolling window. Domain 49 distribution to NAACP LDF and redistricting litigants is valuable January–February 2027. | Low | February 28, 2027 |
| D | Domain 51: dark money 2026 cycle update | Dark money analysis is valuable for spring 2027 budget and grant-making cycle. No hard January deadline. | Low | March 31, 2027 |
| E | Domain 56: Schedule F post-election update | Civil service reform analysis gains nothing from December timing vs. January timing. No January 3 connection. | Low | March 31, 2027 |

### Compression Decision Tree

**At November 25 assessment — choose which tracks to compress:**

```
Is Domain K research >80% complete?
├── YES → proceed with full Track A timeline (Dec 2 Tier 1 distribution target)
└── NO → compress Track A: defer Tier 1 distribution to Dec 9–16; see PB-T1

Is Domain H post-election Zone 3 update complete?
├── YES → proceed with full combined brief production in Week 6 (Dec 9–16)
└── NO → defer full Domain H Gist to Jan 10; produce combined brief only in Week 7

Is December capacity ≥40 hours confirmed?
├── YES → execute all five tracks (A, B, C, D, E)
└── NO → execute tracks A and partial B only; defer C, D, E to January

Is researcher availability at 100% for December?
├── YES → standard execution per PHASE_3_PARALLEL_RESEARCH_RUNWAY.md
└── NO → activate Resource Reallocation (Scenario B or C as appropriate); OR compress to Tracks A + B only
```

- Removing Domain H Zone 3 post-election update: Zone 3 feasibility scoring is the most time-sensitive Domain H contribution; the whole document exists to provide this
- Reducing Domain K source verification: the 67 source citations must all be verified before Tier 1 distribution; credibility is lost if sources are outdated

### Shift Scenario 3: From Expert Consultation to External Researcher Time

**When to use:** Primary researcher is at 80% capacity (illness recovery, competing obligations) and needs production time freed up more than it needs expert validation.

**From:** $3,000 in expert consultation budget (three consultations at $500–1,000 each)

**To:** $3,000 in external researcher time (verification and source-checking consultant at $100/hr = 30 hours)

**Calculation:**
- 30 hours of consultant verification work at $100/hr = $3,000
- Equivalent to: complete Domain H 90+ citation URL verification pass (12 hours), Domain K full bill status update for 12 bills (4 hours), Domain H Gist formatting and copyedit (6 hours), email distribution preparation (8 hours)
- Total primary researcher time freed: 30 hours — equivalent to nearly one full month at standard Phase 2 pace (28.5 hours/month average)

**Trade-off accepted:** Expert validation consultations eliminated; primary researcher self-validates against published sources rather than consulting with domain practitioners. Quality impact: moderate for Domain K Zone 4 (political feasibility) where practitioner input has the most value. Mitigated by: (a) Fix the Court's published SCOTUS Watch annual report provides the same feasibility data without a consultation; (b) informal in-kind feedback from Tier 1 contacts who receive the distribution email.

## CRITICAL PATH PROTECTION: WHAT MONEY CANNOT BUY

Regardless of budget scenario, the following Phase 3 assets cannot be purchased or replaced:

1. **Coalition relationship continuity.** The warm relationships with Fix the Court, Brennan Center, Demand Justice, ACS built in Phase 2 are non-transferable to an external researcher. If the external researcher makes distribution contact, it is a cold outreach, not a warm contact activation. External researchers can produce research; only the primary researcher or user can activate Phase 2 coalition relationships for Phase 3 distribution.

2. **Cross-domain consistency.** 40+ Phase 1–2 domain documents form the analytical framework that Domain K and Domain H must be consistent with. An external researcher without this context will produce high-quality research that is inconsistent with the existing framework. The primary researcher's internalization of the full Phase 1–2 methodology is a knowledge asset that cannot be contracted.

3. **Expert contact relationship state.** Which experts have been contacted, what they responded to, how they like to be communicated with — this is relationship memory, not research knowledge. A brief can transfer the contact list; it cannot transfer the trust built through sustained Phase 2 outreach.

**Implication for resource reallocation:** Budget resources are most effective when used to free primary researcher time for critical-path research and coalition activation — not to replace primary researcher involvement in those tasks. The optimal resource reallocation always involves primary researcher retaining the analytical lead and coalition activation lead, while external resources absorb the mechanical and time-consuming but skill-portable tasks (source verification, formatting, data compilation).
