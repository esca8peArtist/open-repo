### 1.1 Core Argument (One Paragraph — Memorize This Before Writing)

The United States withdrew from 66 international organizations in a single January 7, 2026 presidential memorandum — the largest single multilateral withdrawal event in any democracy's recorded history. This is not primarily a foreign policy event. It is an architectural attack on three categories of domestic democratic infrastructure: accountability benchmarking mechanisms that civil society, journalists, and opposition politicians use to hold the US government to international democratic standards; treaty compliance obligations that courts and Congress can enforce against executive overreach; and the reputational constraint architecture that deters the most extreme domestic norm violations. The ICC sanctions (ongoing through 2026) illustrate the domestic stakes most sharply: US persons providing "material support" to ICC investigations now face up to 20 years in federal prison, severing American civil society's legal capacity to invoke international accountability mechanisms. Domain 57's unique contribution is the reframe: international accountability institutions are domestic democratic infrastructure, not foreign policy tools. Reforming the constitutional asymmetry that lets a president exit Senate-ratified treaties unilaterally — while requiring Senate supermajority consent to enter — is democratic design work.

### 2.1 Core Argument (One Paragraph — Memorize This Before Writing)

Democratic participation is not a free activity. The United States' 2024 income-participation gap — 42% non-voting among those earning under $50,000 versus 16% among those earning over $100,000 — is not an accident of preference or enthusiasm. It is the predictable output of a political economy that depletes time, cognitive bandwidth, residential stability, and material resources in the populations most affected by policy decisions. Three peer-reviewed studies establish the causal structure: the Dallas Fed's HARP quasi-experiment shows mortgage relief directly increased 2012 voter turnout; Slee and Desmond (Politics and Society, 2023) shows neighborhood eviction rates causally reduce turnout; PNAS 2024 shows induced residential mobility persistently depresses voting among low-income Black and Hispanic adults for two decades. Domain 59's central novel contribution is naming the OBBBA's simultaneous Medicaid, SNAP, housing assistance, childcare, and student loan cuts as a coordinated reduction in civic participation capacity for 80 million Americans — a compounding effect that is multiplicative, not additive, and that functions as democratic design failure, not merely poverty policy failure.

### 3.4 Bias Checking Procedure

Before submitting either domain document for peer review, the researcher must run the following bias audit. Flag any item that raises concern; do not resolve it alone — bring it to the peer reviewer.

**Step 1: Source composition audit**
- [ ] Are the primary causal claims supported by peer-reviewed studies, not advocacy organization reports?
- [ ] Do advocacy organization sources appear in reform and coalition sections, not in evidentiary sections making causal claims?
- [ ] Are all quantitative claims traced to the primary data source (Census Bureau, BLS, CBO), not a secondary summary?
- [ ] Are there sources that contradict the domain's argument? If yes, are they acknowledged and addressed?

**Step 2: Claim accuracy audit**
- [ ] Does any section describe a finding as "proven" or "established" when the underlying study uses "suggestive," "consistent with," or "associated with" language?
- [ ] Are all causal claims (Domain 57: withdrawal removes domestic accountability; Domain 59: precarity suppresses civic participation) based on causal studies (experiments, quasi-experiments, instrumental variable designs)? Correlational studies may support the overall picture but should not carry the causal claim.
- [ ] For Domain 57: is the constitutional law analysis accurate? Does it say "Congress has authority to constrain withdrawal" (accurate) or "presidential withdrawal is unconstitutional" (overclaimed)?
- [ ] For Domain 59: is the Dallas Fed wp2517 finding accurately characterized? (The study shows relief increases turnout, not that precarity decreases it — the direction of the causal inference matters.)

**Step 3: Cross-validation**
- [ ] For each major quantitative claim, identify at least one alternative source that produces a similar finding. If only one source exists, note that in the domain document.
- [ ] For the Domain 59 OBBBA compounding claim specifically: if quantitative support for the multiplicative (vs. additive) effect is unavailable, the claim must be made as a qualitative argument with explicit acknowledgment that quantitative confirmation is not yet available — do not present a qualitative compounding argument as empirically demonstrated.
