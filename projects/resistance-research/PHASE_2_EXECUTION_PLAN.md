## 2. Research Execution Sequencing

### Priority Ordering and Rationale

The sequencing below is not flexible for the first three items. Calendar-forced deadlines govern.

**Priority 1 — Domain 58 (Tribal Sovereignty)**: Complete immediately, May 20–June 14. Only 8–12 hours of completion work on an existing base draft. The deadline logic is not publication but preparation: organizations need this domain's sovereignty-as-democratic-infrastructure framing six weeks before the *Trump v. Barbara* SCOTUS ruling (expected late June/early July 2026) to respond coherently. Once the ruling lands, every advocacy organization will be in reactive mode and external analysis competes with their own rapid-response materials. Target distribution: June 15.

**Priority 2 — Domain 56 (Civil Service Politicization)**: Distribute immediately upon Phase 1 user review. This is a verification-and-distribution task, not a research task. The domain document is production-ready. The H.R. 492 legislative window closes June 30, 2026. Congressional staff need 2–3 weeks minimum to absorb external analysis before floor activity. Every week of delay is non-recoverable lead time. Distribution target: May 28.

**Priority 3 — Domain 59 (Economic Precarity)**: Full research begins June 16. This is the framework's hardest research task. Section 5 — documenting how OBBBA's five concurrent enacted cuts (Medicaid, SNAP, housing, childcare, student loan protections) produce a multiplicative reduction in civic participation capacity for 80 million Americans — requires approximately 12–14 hours of synthesis work alone before writing begins. Do not start Domain 59 before June 16. Starting earlier creates a collision with Phase 1 monitoring overhead (Weeks 1–4, 4–8 hours/week), which will fragment the sustained synthesis blocks that Section 5 requires. The OBBBA was enacted July 4, 2025 — this domain documents enacted law with confirmed implementation timelines, which strengthens rather than relaxes the research burden.

**Priority 4 — Domain 57 (Multilateral Withdrawal)**: Pre-production begins June 16 in parallel with Domain 59 research. Full writing begins July 1. Section 2 (constitutional law: Article II silence on withdrawal; 2024 NDAA Section 1250A NATO protection; Curtiss-Wright doctrine; CRS R44761 and R48868) and Section 3 (domestic accountability reframe via Ikenberry's "Liberal Leviathan") are the most technically demanding components and require library or JSTOR access secured before Week 6. The January 7, 2026 Presidential Memorandum withdrawing the United States from 66 international organizations is the empirical anchor. No existing document makes the connection between multilateral withdrawal and domestic democratic accountability infrastructure explicitly — that synthesis is Domain 57's unique contribution.

**Priority 5 — Domains 38, 39, 40, G**: These are already production-ready documents awaiting distribution sequencing. They do not require additional research. Integrate into Tier 2 distribution waves per Section 3 below.

### Parallel Research Windows

Phase 1 distribution and Phase 2 research can run concurrently from Day 1. They do not share resources in a way that creates true conflict — the user executes distribution, the orchestrator executes research. The sequencing constraint is orchestrator capacity for monitoring.

**Weeks 1–4 (May 18–June 14)**: Phase 1 monitoring requires 4–8 hours/week of orchestrator attention (compiling Gist view rates, response rates, secondary distribution signals, Gate 1/2/3 assessment). During this period, only light Phase 2 tasks are appropriate: Domain 56 distribution (4 hours), Domain 58 completion (8–12 hours spread across 4 weeks), and Domain 37 Phase B distribution.

**Week 5 onward (June 15+)**: Phase 1 monitoring drops to 2–3 hours/week (monthly cadence). Full Phase 2 research capacity available. Domain 59 and Domain 57 intensive work begins.

### Resource Constraints and Pacing

Domain 59 at 59–65 hours over 4.5 weeks (June 16–July 15) averages 13–14 hours/week — achievable but requires consistent pace. The Section 5 OBBBA compounding argument and the Section 2 scarcity framework (Mullainathan/Shafir; Dallas Fed wp2517 causal findings) are prerequisite to all other sections and must complete in Week 1 of research. If they slip, the writing phase compresses.

Domain 57 at 45–51 hours over 6 weeks (July 1–August 10) averages 8–9 hours/week — the more comfortable pace. The binding constraint is library access for Ikenberry (Princeton UP, 2011). If open-access alternatives are not located before July 1, the Section 3 synthesis stalls.

### Dependency Order

Domain 59 requires reading Domains 31 (Healthcare Access / OBBBA), 47 (Housing Security), and 50 (Healthcare-Democracy Nexus / NVRA) before production. These cross-reference boundaries must be precise or Section 5 will duplicate existing documents. This read-through is 4 hours of pre-production, not research.

Domain 57 requires reading Domains 23 (Trade Policy) and 28 (War Powers) before production. The constitutional executive power analysis in both domains establishes the framework context within which Domain 57's Article II withdrawal analysis sits.

Domain G (Press Freedom) targets June 15 completion and requires no cross-domain reading prerequisites.

### Domain Prioritization Decision Gates

**If Tier 1 adoption signals are weak (Gate 2 or Gate 3)**: Deprioritize Tier 2 organizational outreach expansion. Do not add new contacts to the distribution list while Phase 1 framing is unresolved. Use orchestrator capacity for Phase 1 remediation (subject line testing, messaging revision) and lower-cost Phase 2 tasks (D56 distribution, D58 completion). D57 and D59 intensive research defers to July 1.

**If SCOTUS fires *Trump v. Barbara* before Domain 58 distributes (expected late June/July)**: Domain 58 becomes emergency Tier 1 regardless of Phase 1 adoption state. Distribute within 72 hours of ruling to NARF and NCAI. All other queue positions pause for 48 hours.

**If *Trump v. Barbara* rules against Indian Citizenship Act**: Domain 58 citizenship crisis section is the primary analytical anchor. Distribute immediately with a targeted cover note citing the ruling directly.

**If Phase 1 adoption is strong but Domain 59 falls behind schedule**: Do not cut Section 5 (OBBBA multiplicative compounding argument). This is the domain's unique contribution. ### Monthly Checkpoints

**June 15**: Gate 3 feedback aggregation — assign Track B domain priorities based on Phase 1 signals. What success looks like: D56 distributed and 2+ Tier 2 responses received; D58 production-complete; D59 pre-production done and research begun; Domain G peer-reviewed and distribution-ready.

### Failure Conditions

**Trigger pause**: Zero substantive Phase 1 replies after 21 days (by June 8). Before pausing, confirm delivery — check bounce rates, spam filter blocks, Gist URL accessibility. If delivery confirmed and engagement is zero, pause Phase 2 Track B. D56 and D58 proceed regardless (independent deadlines).

**Trigger pivot**: Two rounds of subject line and framing testing (10 contacts each) fail to produce engagement. Pivot to new coalition tracks (tribal rights, civil service reform, press freedom) rather than re-attempting the same Tier 1 contacts with different framing.

**Trigger scope reduction**: If by August 1, 2026, neither D57 nor D59 has a complete draft, deprioritize D57 and allocate all remaining capacity to D59. D57 targets UNGA 82 (2027) or the November 3 midterm civic exclusion frame as the distribution hook. The domestic constitutional asymmetry argument is not calendar-constrained in the way the UNGA hook is.

## 6. Risk Mitigation

### Primary Risk: User Availability

The largest single risk to Phase 2 is user bandwidth for distribution actions. The orchestrator can research, draft, and stage — but cannot send emails, create Gist URLs, or make GO/NO-GO decisions. The plan requires three specific user actions in the May 25–June 15 window: (1) Phase 1 adoption assessment (May 28); (2) Domain 56 Gist creation and distribution launch (May 28); (3) Domain 58 Gist creation upon completion (June 14). If any of these three actions slips beyond their window, the associated distribution leverage is lost.

**Contingency**: The orchestrator will stage all distribution materials to the maximum extent possible — personalized drafts, Gist-ready content, pre-filled send checklists — so user action is limited to: fill two fields (name, contact info), open Gist, click send.

### Primary Risk: Phase 1 Adoption Below Threshold

If Phase 1 produces weak signals (Gate 3), committing 100+ orchestrator hours to D57/D59 research while Phase 1 framing is unresolved is a poor allocation. See Section 5 for gate thresholds. The key mitigation is that D56 and D58 have independent deadlines (H.R. 492 window; *Trump v. Barbara* ruling) that justify completion regardless of Phase 1 outcomes.

### Primary Risk: D57/D59 Scope Creep

Both domains have clearly bounded "out of scope" definitions in their production specifications. Domain 57 does not cover foreign policy consequences as such — it covers domestic accountability mechanisms. Domain 59 does not cover poverty policy as such — it covers democratic participation effects. If either domain begins pulling in out-of-scope material, the word count will exceed 10,000 and the synthesis will lose its distinctive contribution. The quality gate in `DOMAINS_57_59_PRODUCTION_ROADMAP.md` Section 4 is the enforcement mechanism — run the self-check validation before any section enters writing phase.

### Monitoring Checkpoints

Weekly WORKLOG entries document research hours, domain progress, and any blocking events. Monthly synthesis (June 15, July 15, August 15) produces a one-paragraph status update in CHECKIN.md under "Phase 2 Status" for the user's review. Any urgent development (SCOTUS ruling, legislative hard trigger, Phase 1 adoption collapse) escalates to CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input" within 24 hours.
