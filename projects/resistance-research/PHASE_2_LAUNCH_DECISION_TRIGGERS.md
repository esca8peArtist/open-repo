# Phase 2 Launch Decision Triggers

**Decision point**: May 25 Week 1 checkpoint (primary), or earlier if STRONG signals arrive before May 25.  
**User action required**: Select one of three scenario pathways and approve Phase 2 launch. Orchestrator does not begin Phase 2 research or Tier 2 outreach without explicit user approval.  
**Time to decide**: Allow 30–45 minutes at the May 25 checkpoint to review Week 1 data and select a scenario.

## SCENARIO 1: STRONG PHASE 1 RESPONSE (Composite CRS >40)

### Activation Condition

Activate this scenario when the composite constituency response score from WAVE_1_CONTINGENCY_DECISION_TREE.md Part 2 is 40 or higher, OR when Score 4+ signals are received from two or more high-weight Tier 1 contacts before May 25.

Early activation before May 25 requires user approval but is permitted.

### What Strong Response Means for Phase 2

A strong Phase 1 response means the proposal has achieved initial credibility validation across key constituencies. This creates two Phase 2 leverage effects:

**Social proof leverage**: Tier 2 contacts can be told, honestly, that Tier 1 contacts (academics, policy organizations, legal advocates) have engaged with the research. This dramatically increases Tier 2 open and reply rates. Contacts who receive a cold outreach email framing the proposal as "work that has been reviewed and engaged by [credible contact names/organizations]" respond at substantially higher rates than contacts receiving the same proposal without social proof.

**Research momentum**: Strong engagement from legal academics and policy organizations creates citation demand — they want to cite or reference the research. This means Phase 2 research should be published (as Gist documents) on a schedule that allows Tier 1 contacts to begin using Phase 2 domains before they finalize their own work. If Goodman is working on a Just Security article, and Domain 57 is relevant, he needs Domain 57 research available before his piece is finished. This creates a Phase 2 research sprint obligation.

### Phase 2 Domain Sequence (STRONG)

All domain sequences assume research sprint format: one research session per domain, 3,500–5,000 words, Gist-published immediately upon completion.

**Week 1 (May 25 – June 1)**:
- Domain 57: Multilateral withdrawal and institutional positioning — begin immediately. This has the longest lead time before the August 10 distribution deadline; starting May 25 gives 10 weeks, which allows for one revision pass.
- Domain 59: Economic precarity and democratic erosion — begin in parallel with Domain 57. This domain has the broadest general-audience relevance and will increase Tier 2 response rates by demonstrating policy breadth.

**Week 2 (June 1–8)**:
- Domain 39: Healthcare access as democratic infrastructure — research must begin no later than June 1 to support the HHS interim rule framing window (rule effective June 1, state-level advocacy window June–August).
- Domain 38: AI regulatory capture and governance — high-relevance to August 2 EU AI Act enforcement. Begin June 1 or sooner.

**Week 3 (June 8–15)**:
- Domain 40: Surveillance capitalism and electoral manipulation — begin after Domain 38 to maintain AI-governance thematic coherence for contacts in this space.
- Domain 56 supplement: Civil service depoliticization updates — check whether any post-distribution developments have occurred; if so, update the Gist.

**Week 4 (June 15–22)**:
- Domains 41–43 (queued) — begin based on actual Tier 1 engagement signals. If immigration legal aid contacts have been particularly engaged, advance Domain 42 (DEA briefing) supplement. If labor union contacts have been active, advance a labor-specific supplemental domain.

**Tier 2 Outreach (STRONG)**:
- First Tier 2 batch: May 26–28, 2026 (1 week after Phase 1 send)
- Second Tier 2 batch: June 8–10, 2026 (once Phase 2 Domains 57 and 59 are available)
- Third Tier 2 batch: June 22–24, 2026 (aligned with Domain 38/40 completion)
- Tier 2 framing note: Lead the outreach email with the Phase 1 engagement signal. Example: "This research framework has been reviewed by [institutional contacts] following initial distribution in May. We are extending outreach to [Tier 2 constituency] because [specific relevance to their work]."

### User Approval Gate (STRONG)

Before executing the STRONG path, confirm the following with the user:

- [ ] Phase 2 domain sequence (Domain 57 first, Domain 59 second) approved
- [ ] Tier 2 pre-contact timing (May 26–28) approved
- [ ] Social proof framing for Tier 2 (which specific Tier 1 contacts / organizations to name) approved
- [ ] Research sprint pace (2 domains per week) confirmed feasible
- [ ] Domain 39 June 1 deadline prioritization acknowledged (even in STRONG path, Domain 39 must not slip past June 1 start)

## SCENARIO 2: MODERATE PHASE 1 RESPONSE (Composite CRS 25–40)

### Activation Condition

Activate this scenario when the composite constituency response score falls between 25 and 39 at the May 25 Week 1 checkpoint.

Also activate this scenario if the composite CRS is technically above 40 but the response distribution is heavily concentrated in one constituency (e.g., only immigration legal aid responded substantively — see WAVE_1_CONTINGENCY_DECISION_TREE.md Scenario B). A composite score inflated by a single high-weight constituency without cross-constituency validation is a MODERATE signal, not STRONG.

### What Moderate Response Means for Phase 2

A moderate response means the proposal has achieved partial validation — enough to proceed, but not enough to warrant maximum velocity. The key strategic implication is that Phase 2 must be selective rather than comprehensive.

In the MODERATE path, Phase 2 research focuses on the two fastest-leverage policy windows (electoral security and healthcare) because these have the nearest external deadlines and the broadest Tier 2 constituency relevance. The higher-complexity domains (Domain 57 multilateral, Domain 38 AI governance) are delayed by approximately 2 weeks relative to the STRONG path to allow time for additional Tier 1 follow-up to convert potential adopters.

The MODERATE path also includes a 2-week follow-up email to all non-responding Tier 1 contacts from the highest-weight constituencies (law schools and think tanks). This follow-up is a single, brief email referencing one specific policy event that occurred after the initial send — it is not a repeat of the original email, and it should not re-send the full proposal. Its purpose is to re-enter the contact's inbox with a news hook that gives them a reason to re-engage.

### Phase 2 Domain Sequence (MODERATE)

**Week 1 (May 25 – June 1)**:
- Domain 37 deep supplement: Electoral security — there is already an existing Domain 37 research base. This week's task is a targeted update to the Gist incorporating any post-distribution developments (DOJ voter data demands, CISA disruption confirmed as of May 17–18 scan). This is a 1–2 hour task, not a full research sprint.
- Domain 39: Healthcare access — begin full research sprint. Must be available before June 15 for state-level healthcare advocacy contacts to use the HHS framing window.

**Week 2 (June 1–8)**:
- Follow-up emails to non-responding Tier 1 contacts (law schools and think tanks only — these are the highest-weight constituencies). Single follow-up email, 3–4 paragraphs, news-hook framing.
- Domain 57: Begin research — 10-week runway to August 10 deadline starting June 1 is adequate if maintained.

**Week 3 (June 8–15)**:
- Domain 38: AI regulatory capture — begin after Domain 57 foundation is laid. Timing still compatible with August 2 EU AI Act enforcement deadline.
- Domain 59: Economic precarity — begin, lower urgency than Domain 57 and Domain 39, but broadens Phase 2 reach.

**Week 4 (June 15–22)**:
- Domain 40: Surveillance capitalism — begin
- Tier 2 second batch outreach (June 15–17), assuming positive Tier 1 follow-up response signals by this date.

**Tier 2 Outreach (MODERATE)**:
- First Tier 2 batch: June 1–3, 2026 (2 weeks after Phase 1 send, giving time for follow-up signal from Tier 1)
- Second Tier 2 batch: June 15–17, 2026 (aligned with Domain 57 and Domain 39 completion)
- Third Tier 2 batch: June 29 – July 1, 2026 (aligned with Domain 38/59 completion)
- Tier 2 framing note: Lead with policy window urgency in the MODERATE path, not social proof. Example: "With the HHS interim Medicaid rule now in effect, this research framework directly addresses the state-level implementation gap that advocates face through August 2026."

### Per-Constituency Decision Rules (MODERATE)

In the MODERATE path, which constituency's response drives which Phase 2 element:

**Law school response** drives academic adoption likelihood. If law schools responded at ≥25% CRS (even in a MODERATE overall result), prioritize domain completeness and citability in Phase 2 research. Format Phase 2 domains with explicit footnote-style citation structures that academics can directly reference. If law schools are at <25% CRS, prioritize policy-relevance framing over academic-citation formatting.

**Think tank / policy org response** drives Phase 2 legislative window integration. If policy orgs responded at ≥25% CRS, lead Phase 2 with domain-specific legislative window framing (which subcommittee, which bill, which regulatory comment period is relevant to each domain). If policy orgs are at <25% CRS, the legislative window framing may be too narrow — broaden the policy relevance framing in Phase 2 documents to be less Washington-insider-specific.

**Immigration legal aid response** drives Domain 42 prioritization. If immigration legal aid contacts responded at ≥25% CRS, advance Domain 42 to the Phase 2 Week 1 queue alongside Domain 37. If <25% CRS, keep Domain 42 in Week 3–4 queue.

**Union response** drives labor-sector Tier 2 timing. If union contacts responded at ≥20% CRS, the union-specific Tier 2 outreach can proceed with the first Tier 2 batch. If <20% CRS, develop a union-specific one-page summary document before Tier 2 union outreach — the standard email template is not translating for this constituency.

## SCENARIO 3: WEAK PHASE 1 RESPONSE (Composite CRS <25)

### Activation Condition

Activate this scenario when the composite constituency response score is below 25 at the May 25 Week 1 checkpoint, OR when the following conditions are all simultaneously true at the May 21 72-hour checkpoint: zero substantive replies, Gist total delta below 5, no OOO returns pending. A Gist total delta of zero with zero replies and zero OOO messages is the strongest early indicator of delivery failure, not engagement failure.

### What Weak Response Means for Phase 2

A weak response is diagnostic data. It does not mean the proposal is without value. It means that either (a) the delivery mechanism failed (emails did not reach inboxes), (b) the messaging did not create sufficient urgency or relevance in the subject line and opening paragraph to motivate a reply, or (c) the contact list contained incorrect addresses or contacts who have moved organizations.

The WEAK path prioritizes diagnosis over continuation. Launching Phase 2 research or Tier 2 outreach before understanding why Phase 1 underperformed is a resource waste. Tier 2 contacts who receive a proposal that has already produced weak Phase 1 results — and that has not been re-examined for messaging issues — are likely to produce similarly weak results.

### Phase 1 Post-Mortem Protocol (complete before June 1)

Execute this diagnostic process in the week of May 25–June 1.

**Step 1: Delivery audit (May 25–26)**

For every contact who did not reply and for whom no Gist delta is attributable:
- Send a brief delivery test: forward a short email to the same address asking if they received the initial message (subject: "Following up on [date] email re: democratic renewal research"). A high rate of OOO or bounce on this test email confirms delivery failure.
- Alternatively, use a test account at the same domain (if you have one) to check whether emails from your domain are landing in spam.

**Step 2: Messaging audit (May 26–27)**

Read your five Batch 1 email templates against the following criteria:
- Does the subject line reference something the recipient is demonstrably working on, or is it generic?
- Does the opening paragraph arrive at the specific relevance to this contact within 2 sentences?
- Is the proposal framed as a request (please review) or an offer (here is something you can use immediately)?
- Is the Gist link prominent, or buried below 3 paragraphs of context?
- Does the email exceed 300 words? (Longer emails are disproportionately archived without reading by policy org contacts)

**Step 3: Contact quality audit (May 27–28)**

Verify current contact information for the highest-weight contacts who did not respond:
- Check their current institutional affiliations (have they moved organizations?)
- Check for recent publications or statements that would indicate active work in the proposal's domain space (if they just published on a related topic, a subject-line reference to that publication is the highest-relevance opener)
- Check whether the organization has a general submissions email that might be a more reliable path than a direct personal address

**Step 4: Revised messaging and contact list (May 28 – June 1)**

Based on Steps 1–3, produce:
- Revised email templates with stronger subject lines and shorter, higher-specificity opening paragraphs
- Updated contact list with corrected addresses and new alternate contacts identified in Step 3
- Decision on whether to re-send to the same contacts (appropriate if delivery failure is confirmed) or shift to alternate contacts at the same organizations

**Phase 2 launch decision (June 1–8)**:
Phase 2 does not begin until:
- Post-mortem Steps 1–4 complete
- At least one response (any type) is received from the revised messaging test (send to 3–5 high-priority contacts with revised templates by June 1, evaluate within 72 hours)
- User approves Phase 2 launch at June 8 decision gate

### Phase 2 Domain Sequence (WEAK)

The WEAK scenario does not have a pre-specified domain sequence because the post-mortem determines which constituency was most mismatched in Phase 1. The domain sequence is built from the post-mortem output.

However, two constraints apply regardless of post-mortem findings:

1. Domain 39 (healthcare) research should begin no later than June 8 given the June 1 HHS rule framing window. Even if the post-mortem extends Phase 2 launch, Domain 39 should proceed as the first Phase 2 research deliverable because it has the most immediate external deadline.

2. Domain 57 (multilateral withdrawal) research should begin no later than June 15 to preserve the August 10 distribution deadline. If Phase 2 launch slips past June 15, the August 10 deadline may require a dedicated sprint to compress the research timeline.

**Target Phase 2 launch date (WEAK)**: June 8, conditional on post-mortem completion and messaging validation.

**Tier 2 Outreach (WEAK)**:
- No Tier 2 outreach before post-mortem completion
- Tier 2 first batch: June 8–15, IF messaging revision produces positive signals from revised Tier 1 test
- Tier 2 framing: Do not attempt to use weak Phase 1 response as social proof. Frame Tier 2 outreach purely on policy window urgency.

### User Approval Gate (WEAK)

The WEAK path requires the most user engagement of the three scenarios because the decisions are less pre-specified.

Before executing any action in the WEAK path, confirm:

- [ ] Post-mortem protocol (Steps 1–4) approved — user should read post-mortem output before June 1 Phase 2 decision
- [ ] Decision on re-contacting same Tier 1 contacts vs. shifting to alternate contacts approved
- [ ] Revised messaging review: user reads revised email templates before they are sent to any contact (these must be explicitly approved, not just drafted)
- [ ] June 8 Phase 2 launch gate confirmed: user approves Phase 2 commencement date after reviewing post-mortem
- [ ] Domain 39 June 8 sprint start confirmed regardless of other decisions

## CROSS-SCENARIO RULES (APPLY IN ALL THREE SCENARIOS)

### Rule 1: OOO contacts are not scored absent until their return date

If a contact sent an OOO autoresponse indicating a return date after May 25, do not include them in the May 25 constituency response score calculation. Instead, log their return date and follow up 1 business day after their stated return. These contacts are in a pending state, not a non-response state.

### Rule 2: A single Score 5 adoption signal overrides the scenario classification

If any Tier 1 contact produces a Score 5 adoption signal (public citation, formal collaboration offer, briefing invitation) before May 25, activate the STRONG path immediately regardless of the composite CRS. A public endorsement from a credentialed institution changes the strategic situation faster than any aggregate response rate metric.

### Rule 3: Domain 39 is the one non-negotiable Phase 2 deliverable

Regardless of which scenario applies, Domain 39 (healthcare access as democratic infrastructure) research must begin no later than June 8 because the June 1 HHS Medicaid rule creates an immediate and time-bounded framing window for healthcare advocacy contacts. This is the single Phase 2 domain that has a hard external deadline within reach.

### Rule 4: User approves before Phase 2 execution begins

The orchestrator does not commission Phase 2 research, draft Tier 2 outreach emails, or contact any Tier 2 organization without explicit user approval of the selected scenario path. The approval gate is mandatory.

**Approval format**: User states one of: "Proceed with STRONG path" / "Proceed with MODERATE path" / "Proceed with WEAK path, I have read the post-mortem" — and optionally notes any modifications to the domain sequence or Tier 2 timing.
