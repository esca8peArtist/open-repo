# Phase 3 Contingency Routing — Scope Adjustment Playbook

*This document handles the scenarios where Wave 1 execution (June 10-13) reveals that Phase 3 domain priorities should shift from the pre-Wave estimates. It is a decision tree, not a research document. Each scenario has one routing decision and one action.*

---

## Most Important Finding

Three scenarios would require immediate Phase 3 re-routing rather than the default sequencing in PHASE_3_RESEARCH_EXECUTION_PLAN.md:

1. Wave 1 signals show Domain 51 (campaign finance) is urgently needed in a form that addresses SCOTUS campaign finance reform more than FEC enforcement — this routes Phase 3 toward Domain K (Judiciary) sooner than planned.
2. Wave 1 signals show zero engagement from all contacts — this routes to a format pivot rather than additional domain research.
3. Wave 1 signals show CLC or Issue One want to collaborate on distribution — this routes to partnership infrastructure setup rather than solo researcher continuation.

## Contingency B: Domain Priority Downgrade (Low Signals for Domain 51)

### Trigger

Wave 1 Day 7 checkpoint (June 17) shows:
- No replies from CLC, Issue One, Common Cause CA, LWV CA, or Clean Money Action Fund
- Gist view count below 20 across all five contacts combined (suggesting zero engagement beyond delivery)
- All 5 emails bounced or went to spam (contact delivery failure)

### What It Means

The Domain 51 campaign finance framing did not reach the intended audiences, or the audiences are not engaging with externally shared research in this format. This is a distribution methodology problem, not a domain content problem — the research quality is independent of the engagement signal.

### Routing Decision: Pivot to Direct Network Approach

If Wave 1 produces zero engagement across all five contacts:

1. **Do not add more contacts to the same distribution format** — adding more of the same list to the same email approach does not fix a format problem
2. **Consult PHASE_1_IMPACT_EVALUATION_ROUTING.md** — specifically the "high-clicks/no-replies" contingency and the format pivot guidance
3. **Delay Domain K and Domain H distribution preparation** — do not invest Phase 3 resources in a distribution format that Wave 1 has empirically shown does not work
4. **Initiate format investigation** (one session, June 17-24): test whether Domain 51 as a Substack post reaches the same contacts through passive discovery rather than direct email

**Phase 3 implication**: If the direct email format fails, Phase 3 domains (H, K) should be distributed through the Obsidian Publish site (target June 2026 per strategic roadmap) and Substack rather than direct institutional outreach. The domain research remains valid — the distribution vehicle changes.

## Contingency C: Partnership Opportunity

### Trigger

Wave 1 produces a reply from CLC, Issue One, or a CA contact that:
- Offers to co-publish or co-distribute the research
- Asks whether the author would be available for a call or meeting
- Invites the framework to be presented at an event or submitted to a publication

### What It Means

An organizational partnership has materialized. This is the highest-value Wave 1 outcome. It fundamentally changes Phase 3's distribution architecture — instead of one researcher distributing to organizations, an organization with its own network amplifies the framework.

### Routing Decision

1. **Accept the call / meeting** — reply within 24 hours proposing a 30-minute introductory call
2. **Do not commit to specific deliverables** before understanding what the organization actually wants
3. **Flag immediately in CHECKIN.md** as "Needs Your Input" — this is a user decision, not an autonomous decision
4. **Pause Phase 3 domain research** until the partnership conversation clarifies what format the partner needs (their template, their publication, their research brief format) — producing Domain K in the current format before knowing the partner's needs may waste research hours

**Phase 3 implication**: If partnership materializes, Phase 3 research should adapt to the partner's format and publication norms. The domain research content is the asset; the format is negotiable.

## Contingency D: Domain 51 Urgency Frame Success Varies by Audience

### Trigger

Wave 1 produces:
- Strong engagement from CLC or Issue One (legal/national organizations)
- No engagement from CA campaign contacts (Common Cause CA, LWV CA, Clean Money Action Fund)
- Or vice versa: CA contacts engage, national contacts do not

### What It Means

The two audience types (legal/policy organizations vs. active ballot campaign organizations) respond to different urgency frames. Legal organizations care about the FEC collapse and constitutional theory. Ballot campaign organizations care about the California-specific framing.

### Routing Decision

**If legal organizations engage and CA contacts do not**: The Wave 2 send templates (Emails 1-3) should be revised before the June 12-13 send to lead with the ballot campaign-specific frame. Move the FEC enforcement content from the email body to a "background context" position. Lead with the specific California Fair Elections Act advocacy mechanics.

**If CA contacts engage and legal organizations do not**: For Phase 3, Domain H and Domain K should be designed for ballot campaign organizations and direct advocacy audiences, not just scholarly and legal audiences. The distribution list for those domains should prioritize state-level democracy campaign organizations over national legal reform organizations.

**Action**: Flag the engagement asymmetry in WORKLOG.md. Do not change Phase 3 domain research — change the distribution targeting list in PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md to reflect which audience tier is actually engaging.

### Routing Decision

3. **Document the integration signal** in `DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md` as a Level 3 adoption event.

## Contingency F: Callais VRA Cascade Creates Redistricting Emergency Window

### Trigger

The post-Callais (April 29, 2026) redistricting cascade (Tennessee, Louisiana, other southern states eliminating majority-minority districts) creates an urgent distribution window for Domain 49 before the 2026 election filing deadlines.

### What It Means

Domain 49 has a hard advocacy window tied to redistricting filing deadlines in affected states — typically 90-180 days before the November 2026 election. That window is likely June-August 2026. If Domain 49 is not research-complete and distributed before August, it misses the cycle.

### Routing Decision

**Immediate action**: Prioritize Domain 49 Callais update (WAVE_1_NEWS_INTEGRATION.md Patch 2) for completion in June 2026. Create a Domain 49 Gist. Flag in WORKLOG.md for user distribution to redistricting and VRA organizations.

**Phase 3 implication**: Domain 49 is a Phase 2 deliverable that requires post-Callais update. If it is completed and distributed in summer 2026, it becomes a Phase 2 success metric and reduces Phase 3 carryover obligations.

**Distribution targets for Domain 49**: NAACP LDF (primary redistricting litigator), Campaign Legal Center (redistricting enforcement), Brennan Center (redistricting monitoring), state redistricting advocacy organizations in Tennessee, Louisiana, Alabama, and Florida.
