# Phase 1 Pre-Distribution Baseline Metrics

**Established**: May 5, 2026
**Distribution launch window**: May 5–12, 2026 (Path A / A+37) or June 2026 (Path B)
**Purpose**: Lock quantified zero-points before any framework materials enter circulation. These baselines make post-distribution attribution possible — without them, any observed changes are uninterpretable.

The five sections below each address a distinct evidence question: (1) does the framework's specific vocabulary already exist in the literature? (2) how aware are Tier 1 targets before we contact them? (3) what is the current rate of policy change in each domain, against which we can measure acceleration? (4) how often does the relevant framing appear in media today? (5) what would constitute failure-to-launch, and what are the pivots?

Unprompted awareness is effectively 0.

### Industry Baseline: Email Open Rates for Policy Outreach

The relevant benchmark for cold outreach to policy and academic professionals is the nonprofit/academic sector, not general business-to-business email. Current benchmarks as of 2025-2026:

- **Nonprofit/academic open rate**: 45–53% (Hunter.io 2025 data; nonprofit sector outperforms all other sectors by a substantial margin due to mission-driven messaging and less saturated inboxes)
- **Cold policy outreach reply rate (general)**: 3.4–4.5% average across all industries (Hunter.io 2025; Backlinko 2025)
- **Warm introduction conversion multiplier**: Warm introductions convert at 3-5x the rate of cold outreach (GrowLeads 2026 data)
- **Highly personalized, domain-specific outreach**: 8–16% reply rate achievable when email references the recipient's specific recent work and makes a specific, low-friction request (Sopro.io 2026 benchmark for consulting/advisory outreach)

**Adjusted sector baseline for this framework's outreach**:

The Batch 1 contacts represent a specific profile: senior scholars and organization directors, each with an active docket or research program that directly intersects with framework content, each contacted with a personalized email referencing their specific recent work. This profile is significantly above the generic nonprofit cold outreach scenario. Realistic baseline expectations:

| Contact Tier | Cold Open Rate Baseline | Cold Reply Rate Baseline | Adjusted (Personalized) Reply Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 (5 named senior leaders) | 55–65% | 8–15% | 15–25% |
| Batch 2 (7 institutional directors, partial personalization) | 45–55% | 5–10% | 10–18% |
| Batch 3-5 (15+ contacts, lower personalization) | 35–45% | 3–8% | 5–12% |
| State AG staff (direct cold contact, no warm intro) | 30–40% | 2–5% | 5–10% |

**Attribution note**: The baseline rates above are the pre-distribution zero-point for engagement measurement. Responses that exceed these baselines — in rate, depth of engagement, or speed of follow-on action — constitute evidence of framework pull. A 20% reply rate in Batch 1 is not unusual for personalized outreach; a 40% reply rate with multiple domain-specific requests constitutes meaningful signal.

### Measurement Protocol by Sector

Track the following fields in the contact log for every outreach event:

- **Email sent date** and **batch number**
- **Open date** (if using tracking) and **first response date**
- **Response type**: acknowledgment / substantive feedback / referral offer / forwarding offer / call request / no response
- **Domain specificity**: did the response reference a specific domain, or was it generic?
- **Inbound ask**: did the recipient request additional materials, a call, or clarification?
- **Referral generated**: did this contact provide a new warm introduction?

Track results by sector monthly: AGs, academics, think tanks, civil rights organizations, media contacts, legislative staff. Sector-level variation in response rate will indicate where the framework is resonating and where messaging needs adjustment.

## Section 3: Policy Outcome Baseline

**Core question**: What is the current rate of policy change in each domain, and at what velocity, so that any acceleration after distribution can be measured as an attributable delta?

### Methodology: "Policy Momentum" Metric

For each domain, the policy momentum baseline is defined as the count of the following events per month in the 60 days preceding distribution (March–April 2026): (a) bills filed in Congress or any state legislature directly addressing the domain, (b) federal court decisions materially affecting the domain, (c) state AG formal actions (lawsuits, coalition letters, consent decrees) addressing the domain, and (d) executive orders or agency rules affecting the domain.

This four-category count establishes the pre-distribution velocity against which post-distribution acceleration is measured.

Landry (April 29, 2026 — gutted VRA Section 2); mail voting EO challenge filed by 24-state AG coalition; CISA FY27 budget proposal zeroing election security program (April 7, 2026).

## Section 5: Failure Mode Definition

**Core question**: What thresholds constitute failure-to-launch, and what are the indicators that warrant an immediate pivot?

### Failure Mode Taxonomy

There are three distinct failure modes, each with a different diagnostic and a different fix.

**Failure Mode 1: Delivery Failure**

Definition: Framework materials are not reaching intended recipients. Email open rates below threshold, Gist views near zero, no responses of any kind.

Diagnostic thresholds:
- Batch 1 email open rate below 20% after 72 hours (should be 45–65% for this audience; below 20% indicates deliverability problem — emails going to spam, bounce, or wrong address)
- Gist views below 50 combined across all 6 documents after 14 days (should be 300–500 if even a fraction of Batch 1-2 contacts opened and clicked)
- Zero responses of any kind from Batch 1 after 7 days (should expect 2–4 responses in this window)

Diagnostic action: Verify email deliverability (test from same address to a known-working institutional address). Check Gist URLs are correct in all templates. Verify Batch 1 contact emails via LinkedIn or institutional directory.

Fix: Re-send via alternate channel (LinkedIn InMail, institutional contact form, warm introduction from a mutual connection). Move to warm-introduction-only outreach for Batch 2.

**Failure Mode 2: Engagement Failure**

Definition: Materials are reaching recipients (emails opened, Gists viewed) but not generating substantive engagement. High open rate, low reply rate; or responses that are generic acknowledgment without any domain-specific engagement.

Diagnostic thresholds:
- Open rate above 35% but reply rate below 3% after 30 days (the email is being read but not generating a response — content or ask is not compelling)
- Gist views above 200 but zero downstream sharing signals (views without interaction — the document is being read but not circulated)
- Batch 1 responses are all generic ("thank you for sharing") with no domain-specific engagement after 30 days

Diagnostic action: Review the email ask. Is the request too vague, too large, or not matched to the recipient's current operational needs? Is the document too long for the initial ask (the executive summary is the right entry point, not the 813 KB full proposal)?

Fix: Tighten the ask to a single domain-specific question. Follow up with a one-paragraph summary of the domain most relevant to the recipient's current docket. Shift from "I am sharing this framework" to "I want to know if this specific analysis [on your current case/research] is useful."

**Failure Mode 3: Adoption Failure**

Definition: Materials are reaching recipients, engagement is occurring (replies, requests for more), but no institutional uptake is observable after 90 days. The framework is read and appreciated by individuals but is not being incorporated into organizational work.

Diagnostic thresholds:
- Batch 1-3 response rate above 10% with positive engagement, but zero institutional citations, zero requests for domain-specific deep-dives, and zero evidence of organizational use after 90 days
- Individual contacts express interest but note "this isn't something my organization focuses on right now"
- No organic citations in media or policy literature after 90 days

Diagnostic action: Assess whether the framework is perceived as academic (interesting but not operational) rather than actionable (directly usable in current work). The key pivot is moving from framework framing to operational tool framing — "this gives you the legal argument structure for your pending brief" rather than "this is a comprehensive analysis."

Fix: Create domain-specific one-pagers (2–3 pages each) for the top 5 highest-relevance domains, formatted as policy memos rather than proposal chapters. Re-initiate contact with the framing "I've created a brief for [your specific docket/research area] based on the framework — would this be useful for [specific upcoming deadline]?"

### Contingency Metrics (Early Pivot Indicators)

If by Day 30, the following pattern is observed, initiate Phase 2 adjustment before the full 90-day assessment window:

| Early Indicator | Threshold | Pivot Action |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 reply rate | Below 5% (expected: 15–25%) | Switch to warm introduction strategy for Batch 2; hold Batch 3 |
| Domain pull concentration | 3 or fewer domains generating all engagement | Double down on high-pull domains; deprioritize low-pull domains in Batch 2-3 messaging |
| Geographic/sector clustering | All responses from one sector, zero from others | Develop sector-specific versions of the executive summary for underperforming sectors |
| Gist view duration | Short median session (under 3 min) | The document is too long; create a standalone 5-page summary for Batch 2 outreach |
| State AG silence | Zero AG responses after direct outreach to 5 AG offices | Move to bridge-node strategy — seed Brennan Center and Democracy Docket first; use their engagement as social proof for AG outreach |

**The critical 45-day window**: If by Day 45 there are zero domain-specific adoptions (no AG brief citing the framework, no think tank publication using the vocabulary, no academic requesting permission to cite), initiate the domain-specific memo pivot and re-engage Batch 1 contacts with a specific operational ask rather than a general framework distribution. Days 45–90 remain the primary adoption window for institutional users; a pivot at Day 45 does not constitute failure.
