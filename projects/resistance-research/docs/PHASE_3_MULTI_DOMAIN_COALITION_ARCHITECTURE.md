# Phase 3 Multi-Domain Coalition Architecture

*Authored: June 10, 2026. Item 89 deliverable.*
*This document extends PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md (Item 93) by mapping coalition activation pathways for the 3 expansion domains (G, 50-Update, N) and identifying cross-domain synergies that the Item 93 5-chain analysis did not cover. It adds 4 new coalition chains (Chains 6-9) and documents the contact overlap and sequencing rules for the full 10-domain Phase 3 framework.*

## Lead Finding

**The highest-leverage new addition from Item 89's expansion domains is Domain N (State AG Multistate Coalition Architecture), not Domain G or Domain 50-Update.**

Domain N does something no other Phase 3 domain does: it converts research distribution into legal action by delivering analysis directly to organizations that have the legal authority to file cases, issue opinions, and negotiate settlements. When Fix the Court or Demand Justice receives Domain K, they use it for advocacy communications. When California AG Bonta's policy staff receives Domain N, they use it to inform litigation strategy decisions worth hundreds of millions of dollars in state enforcement capacity. The leverage multiplier is qualitatively different.

**The specific highest-leverage synergy in the expanded architecture is Domain N + Domain K + Domain H delivered as a combined brief to AG offices with AG coalition coordination infrastructure in place.** AG offices are currently running parallel tracks to the federal judiciary reform movement -- state courts, state constitutional protections, and AG enforcement actions are the backup system for every federal judicial reform that SCOTUS invalidates. Domain K's federal judiciary restructuring argument and Domain N's state AG coalition architecture together constitute the complete "what happens if SCOTUS blocks federal reform" contingency -- answering the question that every judicial reform advocate is privately asking: "What do we do if the Court strikes down the TERM Act?"

**Domain 50-Update's highest-leverage synergy is with Domain 59 (Phase 2-complete) and Domain L.** The Medicaid-civic participation chain (Domain 59 documented the mechanism; Domain 50-Update documents the OBBBA collapse of that mechanism) is the Phase 3 economic democracy argument in its most urgent form. If 4.9-10.1 million people lose Medicaid coverage in 2028 under high-end projections, the economic precarity depression of civic participation documented in Domain 59 will measurably worsen in exactly the communities that Phase 3 is trying to reach. Domain L's organizational capacity survey, completed in March 2027, should explicitly assess which labor and health advocacy organizations are positioned to convert this data into legislative action.

## Coalition Chain 6: Press Freedom and Democratic Information Architecture (January - March 2027)

**Sequence**: Domain G (Press Freedom) -> Domain 43 (Phase 2-complete: Epistemic Infrastructure) -> Domain 36 (Phase 2-complete: AI Governance) -> Domain I (International Standards)

**Why this chain?** Domain G documents the structural attack on press freedom as a democratic institution. Domain 43 (Epistemic Infrastructure and Deepfakes) documents the information ecosystem collapse: AI-generated political advertising, synthetic candidate videos, algorithmic content moderation, and coordinated inauthentic behavior as attacks on democratic deliberation. Domain 36 (AI Governance) documents the regulatory capture failure: the same corporate actors that created the surveillance capitalism-electoral manipulation ecosystem (Domain 40) are now controlling the AI governance process. Domain I's international comparison closes the chain with the question "what have peer democracies done about this?" -- Sweden's Freedom of the Press Act, Finland's constitutional media protection, and the Nordic public service broadcaster models are the most directly relevant comparative evidence.

### How the Domains Amplify Each Other

**Domain G -> Domain 43**: Domain G documents the state mechanisms (EO 14290, journalist arrests, DOD press restrictions) attacking legacy press freedom. Domain 43 documents the private mechanisms (deepfakes, synthetic media, algorithmic suppression) attacking the broader information ecosystem. Together they constitute the complete information architecture threat: state suppression of legacy media + corporate manipulation of digital media. Organizations working on each dimension separately (press freedom organizations work on Domain G problems; digital rights organizations work on Domain 43 problems) have not produced an integrated analysis. The chain provides that integration.

**Domain 43 -> Domain 36**: Domain 43 identifies AI-generated political advertising as a new dark money vector (documented in Domain 51) and deepfake political media as a voter suppression tool. Domain 36 documents the AI governance regulatory capture that prevents effective enforcement. For policymakers designing AI regulation, the chain from deepfake production (43) to regulatory failure (36) is the argument for why existing tools are insufficient and specific new regulatory architecture is required.

**Domain 36 -> Domain I**: Domain I's international comparison section should include the European Union's AI Act (effective August 2024 for foundation models, full enforcement by 2026) as the primary peer democracy comparison for AI-generated political media regulation. The EU's "systemic risk" assessment framework for large AI platforms -- requiring risk assessments, incident reporting, and algorithmic accountability measures for platforms with 45+ million EU users -- is the regulatory model that the Domain 36 advocacy argument needs to make its US legislative proposal concrete.

### Coalition Contacts Activated

**Tier 1 (Domain G primary)**:
- Knight First Amendment Institute (Jameel Jaffer): primary constitutional litigator on press freedom; bridge to First Amendment academic and litigation community
- Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press (Bruce Brown): files amicus briefs in AP access and all press freedom cases; primary distribution target for Domain G
- Committee to Protect Journalists: cpj.org -- publishes annual Attacks on the Press report; secondary distribution channel to international press freedom network

**Tier 1 (Domain 43/36 primary)**:
- Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF): eff.org -- primary digital rights organization; covers domain 36's AI governance and domain 43's synthetic media problems simultaneously
- Stanford Internet Observatory: cyber.fsi.stanford.edu -- publishes election integrity research on synthetic media and platform manipulation; primary data source for Domain 43 post-election update
- Center for AI Safety (CAIS): safe.ai -- policy-focused AI safety organization; bridge to AI governance regulatory advocacy community

**New contacts unlocked by Chain 6**:
- Senate Commerce Committee staff (jurisdiction over FCC, communications law, AI governance): Domain G's press freedom analysis + Domain 36's AI governance regulatory capture analysis provides a complete brief for the incoming committee staff setting their 2027 agenda
- Knight Foundation: knightfoundation.org -- major funder of both press freedom and digital information ecosystem research; a Knight Foundation grant for Phase 3 follow-on research would provide institutional credibility and funding for Phase 4
- European AI regulators (informal contacts through Domain I): the EU AI Act enforcement network includes NGOs and civil society organizations with deep expertise in algorithmic accountability that would be valuable for Domain 36 updates

**Contact Multiplier Effect**: EFF's 500,000+ member list and media relationships reach both the policy community and the general technology-engaged public. Stanford Internet Observatory's election integrity reporting reaches a wide media audience during election cycles. The Chain 6 contact multiplier, at full activation, produces estimated secondary reach of 600,000-700,000 readers across digital rights, press freedom, and AI governance audiences.

### Sequencing

Domain G must distribute before Domain 43 update. Domain 43 update draws on Domain G's press freedom structural analysis as the state-mechanism anchor for the information architecture argument. Domain 36 follow-up can distribute simultaneously with Domain 43 update -- they address different regulatory dimensions without a strong sequential dependency.

Timeline:
- Domain G: January-February 2027 distribution (Tier 1 press freedom contacts)
- Domain 43 post-election update: February-March 2027 (Stanford IO election integrity report available December 2026; provides the empirical foundation)
- Domain 36 follow-up: March 2027 (AI governance regulatory update with EU AI Act enforcement data)

## Coalition Chain 7: Healthcare Democracy and Civil Society Capacity (November 2026 - March 2027)

**Sequence**: Domain 50-Update (OBBBA Medicaid) -> Domain 59 (Economic Precarity -- Phase 2 complete) -> Domain 31-update (OBBBA Medicaid/tariff collision -- Phase 2 scope) -> Domain L (Movement Infrastructure)

**Why this chain?** This is Phase 3's most direct connection to the frontline organizing constituency: the workers, caregivers, patients, and community members who are the subjects of the economic precarity documented in Domain 59. Domain 50-Update brings that analysis current: OBBBA's Medicaid work requirements will lose coverage for 4.9-10.1 million people in 2028, and the people losing coverage are overwhelmingly in the working-class and lower-income communities that Domain 59 identified as experiencing the highest civic participation depression. Domain 31 (healthcare tariff collision -- Phase 2 scope) documents the pharmaceutical tariff-supply chain disruption compounding the Medicaid coverage loss. Domain L synthesizes which organizations are positioned to convert this combined economic-healthcare-democratic exclusion data into legislative action.

### How the Domains Amplify Each Other

**Domain 50-Update -> Domain 59**: Domain 50-Update provides the structural implementation data for the OBBBA Medicaid collapse. Domain 59 provided the democratic design argument for why that collapse matters -- the economic precarity-civic participation depression mechanism. Together: Domain 50-Update is the "what is happening" document; Domain 59 is the "why this is a democracy crisis, not just a healthcare crisis" document. Organizations that received Domain 59 in Phase 2 (CBPP, AFL-CIO, NWLC, MomsRising, Senate Finance Committee staff) are now the primary audience for Domain 50-Update because they already accepted the democratic design argument and can immediately apply the OBBBA implementation data to their advocacy.

**Domain 59 -> Domain 31-update**: Domain 59's economic precarity mechanism is compounded by Domain 31's pharmaceutical tariff and supply chain analysis: the communities experiencing the highest Medicaid coverage losses under OBBBA are also experiencing the highest medication cost increases from Section 232 pharmaceutical tariffs. The compounding argument -- OBBBA removes Medicaid coverage; pharmaceutical tariffs make out-of-pocket medication unaffordable; the result is a healthcare access collapse concentrated in working-class swing districts -- is the spring 2027 budget argument for CTC expansion and Medicaid protection in FY2028 reconciliation.

**Domain 31-update -> Domain L**: The organizational capacity question for the spring 2027 budget fight is: which organizations can run a sustained legislative campaign connecting healthcare access, economic precarity, and democratic participation in the FY2028 reconciliation process? Domain L's organizational capacity survey maps exactly this -- which labor, health advocacy, and antipoverty organizations are positioned for a sustained campaign vs. which mobilized for the 2026 election and are now in organizational fatigue recovery.

- Families USA: Phase 2 warm contact; Frederick Isasi (Executive Director); national consumer health advocacy
- AFL-CIO: Phase 2 warm contact (through Domain 59); health policy as a labor issue; federal employees (AFGE, NTEU) are both workers and administrators of Medicaid work requirement compliance

## Coalition Chain 8: State-Level Democratic Protection Architecture (January - April 2027)

**Sequence**: Domain N (State AG Coalition) -> Domain H (Constitutional Architecture) -> Domain 33-update (State Legislative Autocratization) -> Domain 44-update (Reproductive Freedom as Democratic Architecture -- if Phase 2-complete)

**Why this chain?** This is Phase 3's state-level constitutional protection chain. Domain N maps the AG coalition as the most active legal resistance infrastructure in the country. Domain H's constitutional amendment strategy requires state-level champions for both Article V congressional pathway lobbying (Senators respond to their state AGs and governors) and the eventual 38-state ratification campaign. Domain 33-update provides the post-election assessment of state legislative autocratization: which ballot initiative suppression toolkit measures succeeded in 2026, and what is the 2027-2028 reform pathway at the state level? Domain 44-update (if Phase 2-complete) connects the ballot initiative outcomes on reproductive freedom to the broader democratic design argument.

### How the Domains Amplify Each Other

**Domain N -> Domain H**: Domain N documents the AG coalition's constitutional standing architecture and identifies which coalition members are best positioned for Article V amendment advocacy (the AGs of large states whose senators are needed for 38-state ratification). Domain H's amendment strategy, when distributed to AG offices through the DAGA channel, reaches the officials who can convert constitutional reform advocacy into direct executive action -- filing the amicus briefs, issuing the constitutional opinions, and lobbying their state legislators that the amendment strategy requires.

**Domain H -> Domain 33-update**: Domain H's reform pathway analysis (which requires state-level cooperation) connects directly to Domain 33-update's ballot initiative outcomes. If the California Fair Elections Act passed in November 2026, California becomes an Article V Article of Convention blocking state (no convention needed in California) and a ratification state for targeted amendments. The Domain 33-update tracks this state-by-state reform viability data.

**Domain 33-update -> Domain 44-update**: Domain 33's ballot initiative suppression toolkit directly targeted reproductive freedom ballot measures. The post-election results -- which state reproductive freedom measures passed or failed, which were challenged under the new Callais intentionality standard -- are the empirical test of Domain 33's thesis. Domain 44 (Reproductive Freedom as Democratic Architecture, if Phase 2-complete) provides the democratic design framing: reproductive restrictions function as voter suppression by excluding women and families from civic participation. The 2026 ballot measure results are Domain 44's most important post-election empirical data.

## Failure Mode Analysis (Extended)

Extending PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md's failure mode analysis with Item 89's additional risks:

**Failure Mode 6 -- Domain N too late for AG litigation planning cycle**: If Domain N distributes after March 31, the spring 2027 AG litigation planning cycle for many state AG offices has already set their agenda. AG offices typically set annual litigation priorities by April. Distribution after April misses the highest-leverage planning window; the domain retains value but is more likely to influence 2028 priorities than 2027 actions. Mitigation: Domain N Tier 1 distribution to DAGA and lead AG offices by March 15.

**Failure Mode 7 -- Domain 50-Update misses the lame-duck window**: If Domain 50-Update does not distribute by December 15, the lame-duck session amendment window (November 4 - January 3) cannot be activated. The domain retains post-January 1 implementation value but the prevention advocacy argument is lost. Mitigation: Domain 50-Update research must begin November 15 and complete by December 10; distribution by December 15 is a hard deadline, not a soft target.

**Failure Mode 8 -- Domain G produces a press freedom distribution without the AI governance integration**: If Domain G distributes without the Domain 43/36 chain framing, it lands as a traditional First Amendment document rather than an information architecture document. Press freedom organizations are already well-served by traditional First Amendment documents; what they lack is the AI-generated political media / algorithmic suppression integration. The Chain 6 value is specifically in the cross-domain framing. Mitigation: Domain G production should explicitly include a section on AI-generated political advertising as a press freedom issue (the FEC's disclosure gap for AI-generated ads connects directly to the dark money argument in Domain 51 and the AI governance capture argument in Domain 36).

**Failure Mode 9 -- Contact faturation at Brennan Center, Campaign Legal Center, and Protect Democracy**: These three organizations appear in 4 chains each. If Phase 3 sends them separate documents for each chain activation event, they will stop reading them. Mitigation: for these three organizations specifically, use only combined briefs in Phase 3. Each organization should receive at most 3 combined briefs across all of Phase 3: (a) the 2026 Election Analysis Package (December 2026); (b) the Constitutional Reform Briefing Package (January 2027); (c) the Civil Service / Economic Democracy package (March 2027).
