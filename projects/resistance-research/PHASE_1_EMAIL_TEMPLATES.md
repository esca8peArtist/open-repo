## Subject Line Psychology Reminder

These five subject lines are calibrated to the three principles from `PHASE1_EXECUTION_MATERIALS/TIER1_EMAIL_TEMPLATES_FINAL.md`:

**Specificity**: Each subject line contains something that only applies to this contact's domain — "SPLC analysis" for Bassin, "Watson v. RNC" for Elias, "3.5% threshold" for Chenoweth. Generic phrases like "democratic reform research" are avoided.

**Urgency without alarm**: References to specific cases with expected decision timelines (Watson v. RNC, June–July 2026) and the NVRA quiet period (August 7) are real, documented constraints — not manufactured urgency.

**Curiosity**: "OLC memo analysis + statutory gap" implies specific information the reader does not have. "Judicial threat documentation" implies data the reader wants to see. These constructions create a knowledge gap the subject line alone cannot fill.
