## SOLO RESEARCHER vs. 2-RESEARCHER PARALLEL TRACKS

### Solo Researcher Model

**Capacity:** 40-42 hours/month, sequentially. Cannot truly parallelize — must choose which track to advance in each session.

**Critical constraint:** Domain K distribution (December 20) and Domain H distribution (January 10) overlap in a way that requires Domain K to be production-complete before Domain H's Gist and combined brief can be finalized (because the combined brief requires both domains complete). A solo researcher working sequentially should finish Domain K by November 25, then pivot exclusively to Domain H from November 25 through December 20.

**Recommended sequencing for solo:**

| Period | Primary Focus | Secondary Focus |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| Nov 4-11 | Phase 2 carryover audit + Trump v. Slaughter integration | Election results documentation |
| Nov 11-25 | Domain K completion (electoral frame + remaining sections) | Domain 49 research (parallel, different sessions) |
| Nov 25 – Dec 5 | Domain K Gist + Tier 1 distribution | Domain H Zone 3 post-election update |
| Dec 5-16 | Domain H remaining work (Zones 1-4 updates, integration) | Domain 51 update (parallel, different sessions) |
| Dec 16-20 | Domain H Gist + Combined Brief + Tier 1 distribution prep | — |
| Dec 20+ | January roadmap (Domain I launch, congressional window) | — |

**Risk in solo model:** If Domain 37a requires emergency production (25-30h in November), Tracks B and C compress severely. Solo mitigation: have the Domain 37a legal framework pre-staged (see Scenario A in PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md) so the emergency production is limited to post-election application (10-12h).

---

### 2-Researcher Parallel Model

**Capacity:** 80-84 hours/month combined (each researcher at 40-42 hours).

**Division of labor:** True parallelization. Researcher A owns Domain K from start to Tier 1 distribution; Researcher B owns Domain H from start to Tier 1 distribution. The only synchronization points are the Trump v. Slaughter integration (both domains need the same factual update) and the Combined Brief (both researchers contribute).

**Recommended division for 2-researcher:**

| Period | Researcher A (Domain K) | Researcher B (Domain H) |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Nov 4-11 | Trump v. Slaughter K-integration + electoral frame | Trump v. Slaughter H-integration + election results documentation |
| Nov 11-18 | Domain K remaining research + Gist begin | Domain H Zone 3 post-election update |
| Nov 18-25 | Domain K final production + source verification | Domain H Zones 1-2 monitoring + Zone 4 update |
| Nov 25 – Dec 2 | Domain K Tier 1 distribution | Domain H Zone 4 completion + combined brief outline |
| Dec 2-9 | Domain K Tier 2 distribution + combined brief A-contribution | Domain H final production + Gist begin |
| Dec 9-16 | Combined brief production (lead) | Domain H Gist final + combined brief B-contribution |
| Dec 16-20 | Domain H Tier 1 send coordination | Domain H Tier 1 distribution |

**2-researcher advantage:** The combined brief can be produced in Week 6 (December 9-16) instead of being squeezed into Week 7. Domain H Tier 1 distribution moves to December 18-20, giving incoming congressional staff 14 days of review before January 3. The solo model barely achieves December 20; the 2-researcher model achieves it with buffer.

**Synchronization protocol:** The only mandatory synchronization points are:
1. Week 1: Trump v. Slaughter integration (same facts, each researcher writes their domain's section independently)
2. Week 6: Combined Brief (synchronization meeting required — agree on structure, then divide writing sections)
3. Week 7: Consistency review of Gist and distribution packages (confirm bill numbers, international precedent descriptions, and expert contact list are consistent across both documents)

## CONTINGENCY SCENARIOS

### Scenario A: Trump v. Slaughter overrules Humphrey's Executor (highest-probability outcome)
**Impact:** 2-4 additional hours in both domains.
**Domain K response:** Add Zone 1.3 section on independent agency structural collapse; update legislative advocacy framing — SCERT Act alone is no longer sufficient; a statute restoring Humphrey's Executor-era removal protection joins the legislative agenda.
**Domain H response:** Add new Section 1.5 on regulatory independence collapse as constitutional amendment priority; update Zone 3 Statutory Reclamation section.
**Timing:** The ruling is expected June 30, 2026 — 4 months before the November 4 Phase 3 launch. Both documents should incorporate it in pre-November preparation, not in November itself. If the ruling has not been incorporated by October 31, treat as Week 1 priority.
**Does NOT change December 20 deadline:** 2-4 hours is within capacity buffer.

### Scenario B: Domain 37a was NOT pre-staged (two-month slip)
**Impact:** 15-25 additional hours in November, crowding out Domain K and H work.
**Response:** Domain K shifts to December 2-20 window exclusively (compress from 5 weeks to 3 weeks). Domain H shifts to December 16 – January 10 window (compress from 7 weeks to 4 weeks).
**Risk:** Domain H Tier 1 distribution may slip from January 10 to January 17. This is still within the "blank slate window" before committee assignments harden (January 17-20). Acceptable slippage — not ideal, but within tolerance.
**Solo mitigation:** If solo, prioritize Domain K over Domain H in the compressed window; Domain H combined brief can be a simplified 4-page version rather than 6-8 pages.

### Scenario C: Illness or capacity shortfall (researcher unavailable 1-2 weeks)
**Impact:** 40-80 hours lost from the 7-week window.
**Response for 1-week loss:** Defer Domain 51 and 56 updates to January 2027. These are Track C/D parallel tracks that don't gate Domain K or H.
**Response for 2-week loss:** Defer Domain 49 (redistricting analysis) to January 2027. The December 31 soft deadline for Domain 49 becomes a January 10 Tier 1 distribution instead of a December 25 distribution. This compresses the redistricting litigation analysis timeline but does not eliminate it.
**Domain K and H remain non-deferrable** even under a 2-week loss — their January 3 hard deadline is fixed.
**Emergency minimum (Scenario C Extreme):** If less than 15 hours are available in November-December, produce emergency abbreviated documents: 1,500-2,000 words each on the most time-critical content (specific legislative proposals for K, constitutional amendment priorities for H), distributed to Tier 1 contacts before January 3. These are not full production documents but are sufficient to give incoming staff the structural reform proposals before committees form.

### Scenario D: Major unexpected development (new SCOTUS ruling, constitutional crisis)
**Trigger:** A major SCOTUS ruling or executive action between October 31 and December 20 that directly affects Domain K or H analysis.
**Response protocol:**
1. Assess impact within 24 hours of ruling: does it change the analytical frame (major) or require a citation update (minor)?
2. Minor (citation update): queue for production during next scheduled research session; do not interrupt current track.
3. Major (new analytical section required): interrupt current track; produce a 500-1,000 word "Rapid Update" document flagged in CHECKIN.md; decide within 48 hours whether to integrate into the main production document or distribute as a standalone update.
**Example:** If a new SCOTUS ruling between November 4 and December 20 eliminates merit selection for federal judges (no current case approaching this, but illustrative), Domain K would require a Zone 1.4 addition and Domain H Zone 2's nominating commission section would require significant expansion. In this scenario: allocate 4-6 hours immediately, integrate the analysis, and add to the Tier 1 distribution package as a "Phase 3 Update Note."

### Scenario E: 2-researcher model, researcher handoff needed mid-sprint
**Trigger:** One researcher must hand off their domain to the other partway through the 7-week sprint.
**Pre-conditions for a clean handoff:**
- The handing-off researcher completes a HANDOFF_NOTES.md document covering: current status, next 3 tasks, open questions, source verification status, and contact activation status.
- The receiving researcher schedules a 1-hour synchronization call or async review before resuming work.
- The combined brief synchronization (Week 6) is the hardest handoff point; avoid mid-sprint handoffs in Weeks 5-7 if possible.
