## Section 3: Recommendation Synthesis

### Recommended Scenario: B (Balanced Path)

**Why B over A**: Scenario A leaves three high-value research windows unused. Domain 51's DISCLOSE Act Senate markup may not recur in the same form before the midterms. Domain 50's August 1 ballot measure window is permanent — once the early-vote period opens in those four states, the campaign integration window closes. Scenario A is safe but it represents the lowest research-to-impact ratio of any option.

**Why B over C**: Scenario C's quality risk is real. The July 4 holiday week is a documented constraint — Week 5 in Scenario C has approximately 30–50% reduced turnaround capacity, and it sits exactly where both D49 and D50 are mid-draft. Domain 50's August 1 deadline is not recoverable if it slips. Scenario B starts D49/D50 from July 1 (same runbook date) instead of June 16, giving the parallel execution a cleaner start with four full weeks.

**Why B over D to start**: D is B with an October add-on. The decision between B and D should be made after the August distributions complete — if August went well (any engagement from ASIL, Lambda Legal, or Climate Justice Alliance), activating D54 in October is straightforward. If August was low-engagement, use October to assess whether the outreach model needs revision before another domain distribution. Making that call now, before August signals are visible, is premature.

**Scenario B capacity requirement**: approximately 55–65 orchestrator hours across June–August, with 2-agent parallel execution in July. Prior 3-session project cycles have been documented at approximately 30–40 hours per domain. Domain 51 (3 sessions, 10–12 hours) is well within that range. The July D49/D50 parallel block (20–24 hours combined) is the largest single execution commitment.

### What This Scenario Costs

Scenario B defers Domain 54's standalone distribution until at least October. It also defers Domain 57's June distribution (choosing the August 10 UNGA path). These are calculated tradeoffs, not failures:
- Domain 57 in August is stronger than Domain 57 in June. The UNGA hook is the better anchor.
- Domain 54 distribution in October serves the Virginia ballot measure campaign better than a June send with no civic hook.

## Section 5: Risk Register

### Risk 1 — Domain 50 August 1 Hard Deadline

**Description**: Domain 50 has the hardest deadline in the Phase 2 plan. Four anti-trans ballot measure campaigns (confirmed state list pending verification) need to integrate the Romer v. Evans constitutional framing before early-vote periods open. Once campaign advocacy teams are locked into their messaging, adding new research is difficult.

**Trigger**: If Domain 50 first draft is not ready for peer review by July 21, the deadline is at risk.

**Mitigation**: The parallel runbook has an automatic contingency built in. If the Week 3 end-of-gate check shows Domain 50 behind, it switches to Domain 50 exclusively (Option C in the runbook) and pauses Domain 49 work. Domain 49 can tolerate a 1–2 week slip; Domain 50 cannot.

**Residual risk after mitigation**: Low if July 1 activation is confirmed. Medium if activation slips to July 8 or later.

### Risk 2 — July 4–8 Holiday Week (Scenario C specific)

**Description**: The July 4 US holiday reduces orchestrator turnaround by approximately 30–50% in any task that falls across that week. In Scenario C, Weeks 5–6 (July 1–12) are the heaviest execution weeks — D49/D50 are mid-draft, D51 is being distributed. A 50% capacity reduction in Week 5 compresses D50's completion by 3–5 days, directly threatening the August 1 deadline.

**Mitigation**: Scenario B eliminates this risk by not scheduling any new research starts before July 1. The July 4 holiday falls at the very start of D49/D50 source compilation in Scenario B — a relatively light-load phase — rather than mid-draft.

**Residual risk**: This risk exists only in Scenario C. It is the primary reason Scenario B is recommended over C.

### Risk 3 — D49/D50 Quality Degradation from Parallel Execution

**Description**: Both Domain 49 and Domain 50 have August deadlines and both require 12–14 research hours. Running them truly in parallel (two researchers simultaneously) is designed into the runbook, but it requires either two separate agents or careful task interleaving within a single-agent model. If quality checks are skipped to meet deadlines, both documents could fall below the 40+ citation threshold or miss the analytical distinctiveness requirement.

**Mitigation**: The parallel runbook has explicit quality gates at end of Week 1, Week 3, and Week 4. The Week 3 gate (both first drafts ready for peer review by July 21) is the one that matters. If either domain fails the Week 3 gate, the protocol is: stop Domain 49 work, complete Domain 50, then return to Domain 49 with remaining time. The asymmetry is intentional — Domain 50's deadline is harder.

**Residual risk**: Low if Week 3 gate check happens on schedule. Medium if the gate check is skipped under time pressure.
