### Patch 1 — Domain 59: OBBBA Urgency Frame Replacement

**Domain**: Domain 59 (Economic Precarity and the Civic Participation Crisis)
**Affected files**: `domain-59-send-templates.md`, `domains/domain-59-economic-precarity-and-civic-participation.md`
**Severity**: CRITICAL — sends MUST NOT go out with current framing

**Problem**: The current Domain 59 research document and all send templates are framed around the "Senate Finance Committee markup window" closing June 30, 2026. The actual legislative history:

- The OBBBA passed the House 218-214 (July 3, 2025) and Senate 51-50 with VP Vance tiebreaker (July 1, 2025)
- President Trump signed the bill July 4, 2025
- The OBBBA's CTC provisions: maximum credit raised to $2,200 per child (permanent); refundable portion $1,700 maximum; SSN requirement for both child and taxpayer claiming credit; income phase-out thresholds unchanged at $200,000/$400,000

**What the OBBBA CTC provisions actually mean for Domain 59's core argument**:

The OBBBA's CTC design confirms and deepens Domain 59's structural argument, not weakens it:
- The new SSN requirement for the taxpayer explicitly excludes mixed-status households — disproportionately lowest-income families — from the credit
- The income phase-out thresholds remain unchanged, meaning the families ITEP documented as receiving "$0 average benefit" (poorest fifth) still receive no benefit despite the headline credit increase
- The refundability cap at $1,700 means fully refundable CTC reform (which Domain 59 argues is necessary to close the cognitive-bandwidth/civic-participation gap) was rejected
- The enacted law creates a new opportunity frame: OBBBA passed but left the structural problem intact, making the democratic participation argument for full refundability more urgent, not less

**Required patch to send templates**: Replace the "Senate Finance markup" urgency frame with the OBBBA-enacted frame:

OLD FRAMING (do not use):
> "Comments to the Committee are due by June 30. The full refundability argument... becomes considerably more compelling when paired with the documented democratic participation effects."

NEW FRAMING (use this):
> "The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (enacted July 4, 2025) increased the per-child credit to $2,200 but left the structural refundability gap intact — the poorest fifth of families still receive little or no benefit due to unchanged income-phase-out thresholds and the new SSN-for-taxpayer requirement that explicitly excludes mixed-status households. The democratic participation gap Domain 59 documents — the 42-percentage-point income-based turnout gap (2024) — is caused by the same financial scarcity that the OBBBA's CTC design fails to address. The advocacy window is the OBBBA implementation period (2025-2027 tax years) and the 2026 midterm cycle, where the argument for full refundability can be framed as a democratic correction to the enacted law."

**Action required**: Before distributing Domain 59 to CBPP, ITEP, NWLC, MomsRising, or AFL-CIO, update the send templates. This is a 15-minute edit. The urgency frame still exists; the hook has shifted from "pending markup" to "OBBBA implementation failure."

**Domain research document update needed**: Section on CTC Senate Finance markup should be replaced with OBBBA enactment analysis. Estimated 30 minutes of research to document OBBBA's specific CTC provisions and their democratic participation implications. Not required before Wave 1 (Domain 51 is Wave 1), but should be complete before Domain 59 sends (already scheduled for June 2 — those sends, if not yet executed, should be held pending this patch).

- Struck down Louisiana's congressional map creating a second majority-Black district

This is an update, not a rebuild — the domain's analytical framework remains valid; the ruling outcome is the missing empirical fact.
