# Quick-Reference Rebuttal Matrix

**April 29, 2026 — Session 628**

**How to use this table:** Identify the objection category (column 1), find the common phrasing (column 2), pull the one-sentence rebuttal (column 4), and cite the source (column 5). For full sourced rebuttals with 3-5 alternatives, see `objection-handling-framework.md`. For oral talking points with 30-second versions, see `talking-points.md`.

**Design principle:** The "Key Rebuttal" column contains the single sharpest empirical point — not a rhetorical frame. Lead with the number, then give the mechanism. Do not lead with values.

## Left/Right Skeptic Routing

**Left-of-center skeptics (incrementalism objection):**
Primary objections: 5.1, 5.3, 3.1. Key argument: The proposal's research base is 160 movement case studies; every reform included has been enacted by a functioning democracy with documented outcomes; incrementalism has a 40-year track record of failure on structural questions. Key data point: Gilens-Page 2014 — zero correlation between median voter preferences and policy outcomes after three decades of incremental effort.

**Right-of-center skeptics (government expansion objection):**
Primary objections: 1.4, 4.1, 2.3. Key argument: The proposal's revenue architecture closes documented evasion rather than adding new tax burdens on middle-income earners; the labor reforms update 1959 law for the 2026 economy, not expand a new bureaucracy; the fiscal design includes strong ROI mechanisms (IRS enforcement: $5-12 per $1 spent). Key data point: Milton Friedman called the land value tax "the least bad tax"; Alaska Permanent Fund (1982-present) is a conservative state's citizen dividend.
