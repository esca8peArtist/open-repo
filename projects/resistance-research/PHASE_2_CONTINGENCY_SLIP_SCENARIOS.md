# Phase 2 Contingency Slip Scenarios

*Produced June 10, 2026 — Item 107 contingency planning deliverable.*
*This document analyzes three slip scenarios against domain sequencing, Phase 3 impact, and minimum completion dates. Companion documents: PHASE_2_HARD_DEADLINE_DEPENDENCY_MAP.md and PHASE_2_RESOURCE_CONTENTION_TRIGGERS.md.*

---

## Most Important Finding

**A 2-week slip (July 15) costs Domain 48's Virginia ballot campaign window but keeps all other Phase 2 deadlines viable and preserves Phase 3 compatibility. A 4-week slip (July 30) forfeits Domain 48's Virginia window and narrows Domain 50's anti-trans ballot measure window to near-minimum, but does not cascade into Phase 3. An 8-week slip (September 15) forfeits Domain 48's Virginia window, narrows Domain 50 and 54 to emergency-minimum adoption, and forces Phase 3 Domains H and K onto a compressed 10-week production schedule with January 3 Congress seating still achievable — but requiring dedicated sessions from November 4 onward with no Phase 2 carryover capacity available.**

The minimum Phase 2 completion date that keeps Phase 3 H+K on track for January 3, 2027 without emergency compression is **October 31, 2026**. If Phase 2 completes by September 15 (the 8-week slip scenario), Phase 3 Domain H and K have 11 weeks to reach Tier 1 distribution before January 3 — tight but achievable under normal production pace.

## Scenario 1: 2-Week Slip — July 15 Baseline → August 1 Effective Completion

### Definition

Phase 2 distribution waves that were targeted for completion by June 30 are delayed by 2 weeks. Effective completion date: July 15. This shifts downstream distribution windows as follows:

| Domain | Original Target | Slipped Target | Deadline Consequence |
|--------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Domain 48 | June 16–20 Wave 1; July 15 Virginia coalition | July 1–5 Wave 1; July 29 Virginia coalition window | Virginia coalition integration July 15 MISSED — research arrives 14 days late |
| Domain 54 | July 15 Gist creation; July 17–25 sends | July 29 Gist creation; July 31–August 7 sends | August 1 organizational integration deadline — NARROW MISS; research arrives same week |
| Domain 50 | July 1 Gist + Lambda Legal send | July 15 Gist + Lambda Legal send | September 1 pre-early-vote window preserved with 47 days margin |
| Domain 59 | August 1 distribution anchor | August 15 distribution | September advocacy cycles: 30-day margin compressed to 15 days |

### Domain Sequencing Under Scenario 1

**Domains that pause under 2-week slip**: None. The 2-week slip does not trigger any domain pause — it shifts all distribution windows by 14 days.

**Domains that accelerate**: Domain 54 should be treated as a priority given the August 1 organizational integration deadline. Under the 2-week slip, Domain 54 must execute within days of Gist creation (July 29 Gist → July 31 send → August 1 organizational integration). This is achievable but requires zero delay between Gist creation and distribution.

**Domains that shift to Phase 3 Q4**: None under Scenario 1. All seven Phase 2 domains remain in Phase 2 execution with shifted windows.

**Domain 48 assessment**: The Virginia coalition integration deadline (July 15) is the primary casualty of Scenario 1. Research arrives July 1–5 — two weeks after the July 15 deadline. This is actually not a miss: the 2-week slip puts Domain 48 Wave 1 on July 1–5, and the Virginia coalition integration deadline is July 15. That is a 10-day margin. This scenario actually works if the slip is exactly 2 weeks and Domain 48 Wave 1 executes July 1–5 as the first post-slip action.

**Correction to initial analysis**: Under Scenario 1 (2-week slip), Domain 48's June 16–20 target shifts to July 1–5. The Virginia coalition deadline is July 15. This leaves 10 days between Wave 1 send and Virginia integration deadline — adequate for organizational integration. Scenario 1 does NOT forfeit the Virginia window. It only forfeits the Virginia window if the slip exceeds 3 weeks (i.e., Domain 48 Wave 1 after July 5).

### Phase 3 Impact Under Scenario 1

No Phase 3 impact. The 2-week slip does not consume Phase 3 production capacity. Phase 3 Domain K research begins November 18, Domain H research begins December 2 — both per original schedule. January 3 Congress seating window preserved with full margin.

**Phase 3 Domains H+K status**: Green. No adjustment required.

### Resource Reallocation Under Scenario 1

| Resource | Reallocation Action |
|----------|---------------------|
| Agent research sessions | No reallocation. Existing sequence shifts 2 weeks. No additional sessions required. |
| User execution time | Domain 48 Wave 1 compresses against Virginia deadline — prioritize July 1–5 execution above Domain 50 Gist creation. Stage Domain 50 Gist creation for July 8 (one week after Domain 48 Wave 1). |
| Domain 57 | No change. August 10 send date unaffected. |
| Domain 58 | No change. Ruling-triggered; independent. |

## Scenario 2: 4-Week Slip — July 15 Baseline → August 15 Effective Completion

### Definition

Phase 2 distribution waves delayed by 4 weeks from June 30 baseline. Effective completion date: July 30–August 1. This shifts downstream distribution windows as follows:

| Domain | Original Target | Slipped Target | Deadline Consequence |
|--------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Domain 48 | June 16–20 Wave 1; July 15 Virginia coalition | July 14–18 Wave 1; August 12 Virginia coalition window | Virginia coalition integration July 15 FORFEITED — research arrives same day or 1 day after |
| Domain 54 | July 15 Gist; July 17–25 sends | August 12 Gist; August 14–22 sends | August 1 organizational integration FORFEITED — research arrives 2 weeks late |
| Domain 50 | July 1 Gist + Lambda Legal | July 29 Gist + Lambda Legal | September 1 pre-early-vote window: 33 days margin remaining — viable but narrow |
| Domain 59 | August 1 distribution | August 29 distribution | September advocacy cycles: 3-day margin — near miss; September 1 is the operational boundary |

### Domain Sequencing Under Scenario 2

**Domains that pause**: Domain 57 (August 10 send target) — if the slip causes orchestrator capacity to concentrate on accelerating Domains 48 and 54, Domain 57 pre-distribution preparation (contact re-verification, UNGA framing paragraph update) must complete by August 8 regardless. Domain 57 itself does not "pause" — it is already on hold by design until August 8.

**Domains that accelerate**: Domain 50 becomes the most time-sensitive active domain under Scenario 2. Its September 1 pre-early-vote window is achievable (33-day margin) but requires Gist creation July 29 with Lambda Legal send July 30 — no buffer. User must execute these two actions on schedule.

**Domains that shift to Phase 3 Q4**: Domain 59 second distribution wave. Under Scenario 2, Domain 59's August 29 distribution puts the pre-midterm advocacy cycle reach at near-zero margin. If September 1 is the operational boundary for advocacy cycle usefulness, an August 29 distribution is technically inside it but with 3 days for organizations to route internally — insufficient for organizational adoption. The August 29 send is worth executing for background documentation value, but pre-midterm campaign integration is not achievable. The active pre-midterm advocacy use of Domain 59 shifts to Phase 3 Q4 (December 2026 / January 2027 economic democracy brief targeting Budget Committee).

**Domain 48 assessment**: Virginia coalition integration is forfeited under Scenario 2. The July 15 deadline is missed when Domain 48 Wave 1 executes July 14–18. However, the domain retains significant value: the King v. O'Bannon litigation track (Fourth Circuit pending review) continues regardless; the Alabama SB 24 October 1 window (September voter education) is fully preserved; and post-ballot-measure-outcome analysis is still possible. Domain 48 shifts from "primary ballot campaign resource" to "litigation support + secondary voter education resource."

**Domain 54 assessment**: August 1 organizational integration is forfeited. Research arrives August 14–22, which is after early-vote windows begin opening in some states. However, NextGen America, Campus Vote Project, and CIRCLE can still use the research for: (1) late-stage GOTV planning through September; (2) November 3 election-day operations; (3) post-election analysis for 2028 cycle planning. The August 1 window is the maximum leverage point, but distribution in mid-August is not worthless.

### Phase 3 Impact Under Scenario 2

**Minor compression, no cascade**. Phase 2 completes August 15 under Scenario 2 (distribution waves complete, not necessarily research complete for Domain 59). Phase 3 Domain K research begins November 4 (election day) per PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md Scenario A (one-month slip from August 31). Phase 3 Domains H and K remain on schedule for January 3 distribution.

One risk: if Domain 59 full research production is not complete by October 31 (the backstop date), it creates Phase 3 research carryover into November. Under Scenario 2, Domain 59's production timeline should be confirmed against October 31 before August 15.

**Phase 3 Domains H+K status**: Yellow — monitor Domain 59 completion against October 31 backstop. All other Phase 3 dependencies Green.

| Resource | Reallocation Action |
|----------|---------------------|

## Scenario 3: 8-Week Slip — July 30 Baseline → September 15 Effective Completion

### Definition

Phase 2 distribution waves delayed by 8 weeks from June 30 baseline. Effective completion date: September 15. This is the most severe pre-Phase 3 slip scenario that still preserves Phase 3 January 3 compatibility.

| Domain | Original Target | Slipped Target | Deadline Consequence |
|--------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Domain 48 | June 16–20 Wave 1 | August 25–29 Wave 1 | Virginia July 15 FORFEITED; Alabama September 1+ voter ed preserved marginally |
| Domain 54 | July 15 Gist; July 17–25 sends | September 10 Gist; September 12–20 sends | August 1 organizational integration FORFEITED; September distribution = campaign infrastructure only |
| Domain 50 | July 1 Gist + Lambda Legal | August 26 Gist + Lambda Legal | September 1 pre-early-vote window FORFEITED in most states; research = background documentation for active campaigns |
| Domain 59 | August 1 distribution | September 27 distribution | Pre-midterm advocacy cycle FORFEITED; research distributes during UNGA/fall advocacy overlap — useful but not campaign-cycle integrated |
| Domain 57 | August 10 distribution | August 10 distribution (HOLD — already scheduled) | August 10 deadline NOT AFFECTED — Domain 57 is already a hold order independent of slip |

### Domain Sequencing Under Scenario 3

**Domains that pause**: Domain 54 and Domain 50 effective advocacy windows are forfeited but distribution should still execute. Organizations receive the research as background documentation for active campaigns, ongoing litigation, and 2028 cycle planning.

**Domains that accelerate**: Domain 57 does not accelerate (the August 10 date is fixed regardless of slip). The only genuine acceleration candidate under Scenario 3 is Domain 58 — but it cannot be accelerated because it is ruling-triggered.

**Domains that shift to Phase 3 Q4**: 
- Domain 59 second wave: September 27 distribution positions it for post-midterm economic democracy brief. The Phase 3 economic democracy brief (Domain 59 + Domain 51 + Domain 34) targets March 15, 2027 Budget Committee contacts — September 27 distribution gives organizations 5+ months to integrate the research before that brief. This is the primary residual value of a September 27 Domain 59 distribution.
- Domain 50: September 1+ distribution has residual value for Lambda Legal ballot challenge litigation (Romer v. Evans theory is not time-bounded by early-vote opening) and for 2028 LGBTQ+ rights strategic planning. Not a Phase 3 shift per se — Domain 50 remains a Phase 2 deliverable executed late.
- Domain 54: October distribution (if further delayed) can still reach organizations planning 2028 youth voter registration infrastructure.

**What cannot defer to Phase 3 under any scenario**: Domains 49, 58. Domain 49 redistricting analysis is already executing; the litigation support value is not time-recoverable. Domain 58 is ruling-triggered and executes when the ruling drops regardless of slip.

**Phase 3 PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md Scenario A analysis (one-month slip from August 31 = September 30 Phase 2 close) maps closely to Scenario 3's September 15 effective completion.**

Under Scenario A in PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md:

**Phase 3 Domains H+K status under Scenario 3**: Green with one condition — Phase 2 must not have unresolved carryover obligations on November 4. Specifically, Domain G (Press Freedom), Domain 37a (Post-Election Section 3), and Domain 31x (Healthcare Tariff Collision) must be confirmed complete by October 31 or their carryover blocks Phase 3 Domain K start.

**The minimum Phase 2 completion date for Phase 3 H+K to hit January 3**: October 31, 2026. September 15 effective completion under Scenario 3 leaves the October 31 backstop fully intact with 46 days of buffer.

### Do Domains H+K Still Hit January 3 If Phase 2 Completes September 15?

Yes, with the following conditions:
1. No Phase 2 carryover obligations on November 4 (Domain G, 37a, 31x confirmed complete)
2. Domain K research starts November 18 (7 weeks to January 3 distribution — adequate for 16–20 hour domain)
3. Domain H research starts December 2 (4 weeks to January 3 distribution — tight; requires concentrated research sessions)
4. Distribution staging complete by December 26 for January 3 send

The 4-week window for Domain H (December 2 to January 3) is the binding constraint. Phase 2 actuals show 6–8 hours per week of research output. At 8 hours/week over 4 weeks, Domain H has 32 production hours available — adequate for the 18–22 hour total estimate, with buffer. However, December also carries Domain K distribution obligations (December 12–20 Tier 1 send per PHASE_3_EXECUTION_TIMELINE.md) and data consolidation work for Domain 49 final analysis and Domain 51 update. December's 42–48 hour target is the high-load month; it should be planned explicitly against November completion.
