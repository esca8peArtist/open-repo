# Domain M: Phase 2 Ballot Acceleration Case

**Bottom line up front**: YES, Domain M warrants Phase 2 acceleration. The November 3, 2026 ballot measures in Missouri, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Utah are real, certified, and on track for a vote. Opposition campaign organizations are active NOW. The July–August 2026 distribution window aligns precisely with the peak advocacy period for the four simultaneous supermajority measures. A 500-word brief to BISC and Common Cause distributed by July 15 would reach the campaign organizations actively running "No" campaigns in the window when voter contact and coalition framing decisions are still being made. Waiting for Phase 3 (January 2027) means distributing a post-mortem after the votes have been taken.

---

## (a) Ballot Deadline Date

**Decision date: November 3, 2026.** Four states have certified ballot measures targeting the citizen initiative process:

**Missouri Amendment 4** — Requires citizen-initiated constitutional amendments to achieve majority approval in every single congressional district in addition to a statewide majority. Because Missouri has 8 congressional districts drawn by a Republican legislature, this requirement is structurally impossible for any measure with progressive support concentrated in St. Louis and Kansas City. Under this rule, every Missouri ballot initiative since 2020 would have failed — including marijuana legalization, minimum wage increases, paid sick leave, Medicaid expansion, and the abortion ban reversal. The Missourians for Fair Governance campaign is running opposition. A parallel protective measure ("Respect Missouri Voters") sought 170,000 signatures to appear on the same ballot — its qualification status determines whether voters face one measure (the supermajority restriction) or two competing measures simultaneously.

**North Dakota Constitutional Measure 2** — Raises the approval threshold for all constitutional amendments (citizen-initiated and legislature-referred alike) from a simple majority to 60%. Currently certified for November 3, 2026. Under this rule, North Dakota's 2018 Medicaid expansion (which passed at 53%) would have failed.

**South Dakota** — A legislature-referred constitutional amendment requiring 60% approval for citizen-initiated constitutional amendments. Certified for November 3, 2026.

**Utah** — A legislature-referred constitutional amendment requiring 60% approval for citizen-initiated measures making tax-related changes. This is narrower than the Missouri/North Dakota/South Dakota measures but targets the most common mechanism through which Utah voters have reversed tax cuts enacted by the legislature.

The signature submission window for any 2026 citizen-initiated counter-measure has largely closed (most states' deadlines fell February–June 2026). The advocacy window that remains is the voter persuasion and coalition framing window: July through October 2026.

## (b) What Phase 2 Messaging Would Help

Domain M Phase 2 messaging serves three functions that existing Phase 2 domains do not cover:

**Frame the structural argument, not just the individual measures.** The four measures are not isolated state-by-state debates — they are a coordinated attack on direct democracy as the bypass mechanism for gerrymandered legislatures. Campaign organizations in Missouri know they are running against Amendment 4, but they may not have a research synthesis framing the 100+ bills in 15+ states as part of the same authoritarian infrastructure playbook. That synthesis is Domain M's contribution. BISC's March 2026 report provides data; a Phase 2 research brief would provide the integrated constitutional argument that campaign staff and coalition partners can deploy in voter communication, media outreach, and coalition fundraising.

**Equip Tier 1 contacts with the legislative bypass argument.** The most important argument against the supermajority measures is not procedural ("it's harder to pass amendments") but structural: in states with gerrymandered legislatures and voter suppression measures documented across Domains 37, 49, and 51, the citizen initiative is the only remaining democratic bypass mechanism. When legislatures are captured and elections are compromised, direct democracy is the check. The supermajority measures eliminate it. This argument connects Domain M to existing research in the framework and is the framing most persuasive to organizations working at the intersection of voting rights and democracy reform.

**Provide the historical record.** Missouri, North Dakota, and South Dakota voters have used citizen initiatives in the last decade to expand Medicaid, legalize marijuana, raise minimum wages, restore voting rights, and pass campaign finance restrictions — in states where those measures would never have passed through the legislature. The research brief should document this record explicitly. The argument is empirical: what direct democracy has actually delivered in these states is what the supermajority measures are designed to prevent.

**Assessment: Feasible with a compressed 20-25 hour research sprint in July.** The distribution window is July 1 – August 15, 2026, based on three anchors:

1. **Opposition campaign decision cycles**: Campaign organizations make voter communication and coalition framing decisions 90–120 days before the election. The distribution does not require building a new contact set from zero — it uses existing relationships with a new research brief attached.

3. **Research compression**: Domain M was assessed at 20–25 hours in the June 2026 Phase 3 domain candidate scorecard (PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATES_UPDATE_JUNE_2026.md).
