# WEAK Outcome Contingency Plan

## Overview

If May 21 synthesis classifies Wave 1 as WEAK, this contingency plan avoids default assumption ("proceed with extended timeline") and surfaces active corrective options.

**Premise**: WEAK is ambiguous. It could mean:
- (a) **Messaging problem** → brief wasn't compelling; same stakeholders, better framing, might work
- (b) **Timing problem** → distribution hit Friday evening inbox noise; relaunch Monday might work
- (c) **Stakeholder problem** → initial contact list too narrow; Reserve contact list (different networks) needed
- (d) **Substance problem** → frameworks not developed enough; needs deeper research before redistribution
- (e) **Infrastructure problem** → emails landed in spam; sender reputation issue

Different diagnoses → different corrective actions. This plan operationalizes the diagnosis + routing.

---

## 1. WEAK OUTCOME THRESHOLDS (FROM ITEM 73)

**WEAK is triggered when**:
- Quality Reply Points = 0 (all replies Score 0–2, zero Score 3+)
- AND Response rate < 40% (fewer than 2 of 5 contacts replied)
- AND Gist delta ≤ 5 views (minimal internal sharing signal)

**Precondition**: Delivery must be confirmed (at least one Gist view > 0, OR one Score 1 reply, OR user self-test confirms emails landed in inbox).

**If delivery is NOT confirmed before May 21 classification**:
- Do NOT classify as WEAK
- Classify as TOO_EARLY (insufficient evidence base)
- Run delivery diagnosis May 22–24 in parallel with other work

## 3. CORRECTIVE ACTION OPTIONS (5 PATHS)

Choose based on diagnosis. Each has timeline + resource implications.

### Option A: "Proceed Normally" (Extended Timeline)

**Recommendation if**: WEAK is due to **timing** (Friday send → Monday reads expected) OR **substance** (frameworks complete, just need 4-week delay before contacts can act)

**What happens**:
- Domain 39, 56, 58 proceed on standard timeline (May 28, June 15, June 15)
- Domain 57, 59 begin research June 10–15 (extended timeline, no peer review buffer)
- Phase 2 launch deferred to June 15 (vs. May 28 in STRONG path)
- No messaging revision; no new contacts activated

**Timeline implications**:
- May 22–June 15: Continue Phase 2 research initiation (current domains 56, 58 complete; prep 57, 59)
- June 15: Phase 2 distributions begin (Batch 2 to Tier 1 law schools + think tanks)
- July 15: Phase 2 domains 57, 59 completion + Batch 3 distribution (tighter timeline, requires parallel work)
- September 1: Phase 2 domains 60+ (if any) begin research

**Resource cost**: Standard orchestrator load (no extra agent cycles)

**Risk**: If WEAK is due to messaging (not timing), proceeding normally repeats the same weak engagement in June 15 distribution. May get 20–30% response rate instead of 40%+.

**User approval**: Confirm extended timeline acceptable + commit to June 15 Phase 2 launch regardless of May 21 outcome.

---

### Option B: "Relaunch with Reserve List" (2-Week Delay + New Cohort)

**Recommendation if**: WEAK is due to **stakeholder** problem (Batch 1 too narrow, different network needed) OR **timing** (Friday send → need fresh Monday launch)

**What happens**:
- May 22–27: Compile Reserve contact list (20–30 contacts from different sectors: environmental, labor, reproductive rights, education)
- May 28: Send same framework Batch 1 to Reserve list (fresh cohort, Monday morning distribution)
- May 29–June 2 (72-hour window): Monitor Reserve cohort response
- June 2: Classify Reserve outcome → determine Phase 2 path
- June 15: Phase 2 launch proceeds regardless (using combined Batch 1 + Reserve data)

**Messaging**: Same as Batch 1 (no revisions unless diagnosis indicated messaging problem)

**Timeline implications**:
- May 22–27: Curator tasks (identify 20–30 Reserve contacts from networks: Sunrise, Sierra Club, SEIU, ReprodRights.org, UFE, etc.)
- May 28 send: 20–30 contacts (larger cohort = better statistical signal)
- May 29–June 2: Monitor Reserve response (72h window)
- June 2: Reclassify (combined Batch 1 + Reserve) → STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK again
- If reclassification is STRONG/MODERATE: June 15 Phase 2 launch with dual-cohort data
- If reclassification still WEAK: Activate Option D (diagnostic) or Option E (shift to policy asks)

**Resource cost**: HIGH — curator work (20–30 contact sourcing) + monitoring May 28–June 2 (4 days parallel)

**Risk**: Delay costs 2 weeks. If Phase 2 domains have hard deadlines (e.g., Domain 39 June 1 HHS gate), delay may create conflict.

**User approval**: Confirm Reserve contact sourcing is feasible May 22–27 (curator availability?) + confirm Phase 2 delay is acceptable.

### Option C: "Emergency Messaging Relaunch" (5-Day Turnaround + Crisis Framing)

**Recommendation if**: WEAK is due to **messaging** problem (frameworks sound but brief wasn't compelling) AND you want fast recovery (no 2-week delay)

**What happens**:
- May 21: Diagnosis confirms messaging issue (low engagement, Score 1 only, zero follow-through)
- May 22–26: Revise email + Gist messaging to emphasize **urgency + stakes** (not info dump)
  - New subject line: "Urgent: Democratic Emergency — 6 Critical Gaps + Solutions" (vs. original "Democratic Renewal Proposal")
  - New email body: Lead with crisis signals (voting rights rollback, judicial capture, DHS/DOJ weapons escalation) → transition to frameworks as solutions
  - New Gist framing: Case studies + urgent policy windows (HHS June 1, EU AI Act August 2) prominently featured
- May 27: Resend to Batch 1 + Reserve list simultaneously (40–50 contacts, new messaging)
- May 28–May 31 (72-hour window): Monitor revised response
- June 1: Reclassify with new data → determine Phase 2 path

**Timeline implications**:
- May 22–26: Messaging revision sprint (reframe from academic→policy to crisis→solutions)
- May 27 send: 40–50 contacts (Batch 1 + Reserve, new messaging)
- May 28–31: Monitor revised response
- June 1: Reclassify → STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK again
- June 2: Phase 2 launch decision (if reclassified STRONG: accelerate to June 2; if MODERATE/WEAK: standard timeline or diagnostic)

**Resource cost**: MEDIUM — messaging revision (4 days, 8–16 hours) + monitoring (3 days)

**Risk**: If original messaging was sound and WEAK was due to timing/stakeholder, revised crisis framing may feel tone-deaf or manipulative. Test messaging revision with 2–3 trusted contacts before deploy.

**User approval**: Review revised messaging May 25 before May 27 send. Confirm tone/framing acceptable.

### Option D: "Pause & Diagnose" (Deep Engagement Window)

**Recommendation if**: WEAK persists despite attempts at diagnosis (Option A/B/C), OR diagnosis is genuinely unclear (mixed signals from Batch 1)

**What happens**:
- May 22–31: Pause Phase 2 launch (extend timeline)
- May 22–31: Conduct post-Wave engagement research:
  - Schedule 3–5 calls with Batch 1 contacts (Weiser, Bassin, Elias, + 1–2 others)
  - Ask: "What would make this more useful? What's missing? What's the timeline for your engagement?"
  - Document feedback + patterns
- June 1: Compile diagnostic report (messaging gaps vs. timing gaps vs. stakeholder gaps)
- June 2: User decides: revise frameworks (Option B messaging path) OR proceed standard timeline (Option A) based on feedback

**Timeline implications**:
- May 22–31: Engagement research (no Phase 2 launch progress)
- June 1: Diagnostic report due
- June 2+: Based on report, select Option A/B/C/E

**Resource cost**: HIGH — 5–10 hours engagement research (interviewing, synthesis) + analysis

**Risk**: 10-day delay. If Phase 2 has hard deadlines (Domain 39 June 1), delay may force scope reduction or compressed timeline.

**User approval**: Confirm you want to invest 10 days in diagnostic (vs. moving forward with Option A/B/C). Confirm Phase 2 delay is acceptable.

### Option E: "Shift to Policy Asks" (Parallel Development Path)

**Recommendation if**: WEAK is persistent OR you want to build momentum independently of Wave 1 feedback, OR Phase 2 contains high-urgency domains (39, 56 with June 1–15 deadlines)

- Phase 2 research proceeds independently (less reliant on Batch 1 response signal)

**Timeline implications**:
- May 21: Identify policy ask targets (5–10 of most-likely-to-engage contacts)
- May 22–28: Conduct policy asks (concurrent with D39 finalization + D56/58 research)
- June 1: D39 + policy org co-filing/comment (user delivers, with org partner)

**Resource cost**: MEDIUM — policy cultivation (8–10 hours May 22–28) + coordination with user for June 1 co-filing

**Risk**: Policy asks require user engagement (not orchestrator alone). Commitment from Weiser/Bassin needed by May 28.

**User approval**: Confirm you want to conduct policy asks May 22–28 (requires 4–5 calls to set up partnerships). Confirm June 1 co-filing participation possible with work constraints.
