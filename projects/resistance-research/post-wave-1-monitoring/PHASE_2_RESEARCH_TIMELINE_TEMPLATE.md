## General Peer Review Protocol

Use this protocol for all Phase 2 domain peer reviews, regardless of path.

### Reviewer selection & outreach

- **Academic reviewers** (2-3): Select from prior Phase 1 reviewers or university contacts with domain expertise. Request 3-5 hours commitment per domain.
- **Practitioner reviewers** (1-2, PATH C only): Select from Tier 2 organizational contacts with policy window urgency (e.g., CICC for D57, EFF for D38). Request 2-3 hours.
- **Outreach timeline**: Recruit reviewers 1-2 weeks before submission (May 28 for June 10 submissions, June 20 for July 1 submissions).

### Review brief template

When sending draft to reviewers, include:

```
# Peer Review Brief — Domain [XX]

**Domain**: [Title]  
**Length**: [word count]  
**Sources**: [count] citations  
**Deadline for feedback**: [date, 12:00 UTC]  

## What I need help with:
1. Factual accuracy check: Do all empirical claims match source citations?
2. Causal pathway logic: Are the five mechanisms compelling and evidence-based?
3. Regional specificity: Is the Midwest Zone 5 analysis accurate for this context?
4. Movement leverage: Did I identify the highest-impact organizations/contacts?
5. Policy window: Are the June/July/August deadlines real and actionable?

## Time estimate:
- Quick read: 45 min
- Detailed annotation: 2.5 hours
- Deep review (ideal): 3-4 hours

Please focus on [PRIORITY 1], then [PRIORITY 2] if time permits.

Thank you,
[Your name]
```

### Feedback synthesis

After receiving peer review feedback:
- Create a review-feedback folder in `phase-2-research/[domain]/peer-review/`
- Log reviewer name, date received, key findings (3-5 bullet points), revision recommendations
- For each recommendation, mark: **ACCEPT** (revise draft), **CONDITIONAL** (needs verification before accepting), or **DEFER** (out of scope for this domain)
- Track revision time per recommendation (most take 15-60 minutes; major structural revisions take 2-4 hours)
