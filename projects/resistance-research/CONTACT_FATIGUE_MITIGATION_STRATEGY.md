## Section 5: Contact Fatigue Scoring Model

Contact fatigue is operationalized here as the perceived burden on a contact of receiving multiple emails from the same sender-domain within a defined time window. The model uses four variables:

**Variable A — Email frequency score**: Number of emails from sender-domain within a rolling 4-week window.
- 1 email: Score 0 (no fatigue)
- 2 emails, same staff: Score 3 (moderate — notable)
- 2 emails, different staff: Score 1 (minimal — different inbox)
- 3+ emails, any configuration: Score 5+ (high fatigue risk)

**Variable B — Content relatedness score**: Degree to which email content overlaps with a prior email.
- Fully distinct domains (campaign finance vs. criminal justice): Score 0
- Related but distinct (two campaign finance subdomains): Score 2
- Near-identical content: Score 4

**Variable C — Time spacing score**: Days between emails to same organization.
- 0–2 days: Score 3 (high recency; perceived as bulk)
- 3–6 days: Score 2 (moderate; notable)
- 7–13 days: Score 1 (low; within professional norm)
- 14+ days: Score 0 (below perception threshold)

**Variable D — Response status score**: Reduces fatigue perception if the contact has already replied.
- Contact replied and conversation is warm: Score -2 (reduces fatigue)
- Contact has not replied: Score 0 (neutral)

**Fatigue score formula**: A + B + C + D = Total Fatigue Score

**Interpretation**:
- 0–2: No action needed
- 3–5: Monitor; ensure strong subject-line differentiation
- 6–8: Restructure send timing or content framing
- 9+: Do not send without user review

## Section 6: Wave 1 → Wave 2 Sequencing Recommendation

**Recommended minimum gap: 7 days** from last Domain 51 send (June 12) to first Domain 48 send (June 16). The existing plan provides exactly this gap: 4 days between the June 12 clean-up day and the June 16 Domain 48 Wave 1 opens.

**Rationale**:
- 4-day gap is within the professional email follow-up norm (7-day norm for policy organizations — see DOMAIN_51_CONTACT_STRATIFICATION_AND_TIMING.md Section 5)
- At 4 days, no CLC contact has had time to generate a substantive reply to Domain 51; the Domain 48 send arrives before any conversation has been established
- However, since Domain 48's CLC contact is Blair Bowie (a different person than Erin Chlopak), this is not a concern at the individual level — the 4-day gap is only relevant if CLC institutional awareness of dual-domain outreach becomes a factor

**If Wave 1 STRONG signal detected early** (e.g., CLC replies within 48 hours, June 11): Can the Wave 2 gap be compressed?

### Contingency: STRONG Signal — Compress Wave 2 Timing?

If the Day 7 checkpoint comes back STRONG (composite score 8–10), the DOMAIN_51_JUNE_16_DECISION_LOGIC.md calls for immediate Domain 48 activation (June 16–20). This is already the plan. There is no need to compress further — June 16 is 4 days after June 12, which is within acceptable range.

However, if a STRONG signal is detected much earlier — say, CLC replies substantively on June 10 and Issue One replies June 11 — the following compressed timeline is defensible:

**Compressed STRONG path** (if 2+ substantive replies received by June 11):
- June 12: Execute Domain 48 pre-flight (contact verification, template review)
- June 13 (Saturday): Skip — maintain professional weekend boundary
- June 14 (Sunday): Skip
- June 15 (Monday): Begin Domain 48 Wave 1 sends — Sentencing Project first

This compresses the gap from 4 days to 3 days (June 12 → June 15). Fatigue risk remains low because Domain 48 contacts are entirely distinct from Domain 51 contacts. CLC is the only shared organization, and the 6-day spacing (June 9 CLC → June 15 CLC Tier B send) is within the 7-day professional norm.

**Recommendation**: Do not compress below 3 days from last Domain 51 send to first Domain 48 send. The June 16 date in the existing plan already provides 4 days; use it unless STRONG signal creates an urgent operational opportunity.

## Section 7: Wave 2 → Wave 3 Sequencing Recommendation

Domain 57 (Wave 3) targets a completely separate contact pool (international affairs organizations, think tanks). There is zero contact overlap with Waves 1 or 2. The fatigue model scores all Wave 3 contacts at 0 regardless of Wave 1/2 timing.

**However**, Wave 3's optimal timing is tied to the pre-UNGA window (August 10 target) per PHASE_2_BATCH_2_ACTIVATION_ROADMAP.md. The STRONG-path compressed timeline places Domain 57 sends at June 20–22, which is well before August 10. Two options:

**Option A — STRONG path accelerated (June 20–22)**: Send Domain 57 immediately following Domain 48. Fatigue risk is zero. The tradeoff is losing the UNGA framing leverage (August–September multilateralism discussion). Appropriate only if there is an urgent current-events hook (e.g., NATO formal withdrawal initiated) that makes June timing valuable.

**Option B — Hold Domain 57 for August anchor**: Even in the STRONG scenario, defer Domain 57 to August 8–12 to capture UNGA framing. Domain 48 completes June 16–20; Domain 57 begins August 8. The 7-week gap to Wave 3 is logistically comfortable.

**Recommendation**: Use Option B unless a current-events trigger activates Domain 57 urgency before August. The UNGA framing is worth the wait. Wave fatigue is not a factor, but strategic timing is.
