### 1.2 Outcome Definitions — Four Tiers

These definitions apply at the 72-hour window close (May 21 10:30 UTC) and again at the 7-day gate (May 25). The thresholds account for the 5-contact sample size — at this scale, 1 engagement equals 20%, which is "Moderate" not "Weak."

**Outcome A — Very Strong (>50% engagement, equivalent to 3+ of 5 contacts at Score 3+)**

At least 3 of the 5 Batch 1 contacts have replied with Score 3 or higher (substantive question citing a specific domain, implementation ask, referral signal, or integration signal). Alternatively: 2 contacts at Score 3+, with at least 1 at Score 4+ (implementation ask or named referral). Alternatively: any single Score 5 contact (public citation, formal collaboration offer, brief filing reference) is an immediate Very Strong override regardless of aggregate numbers.

What this looks like in practice:
- Weiser asks for a one-page SAVE Act brief for state AG contacts AND Elias asks for the Domain 37 redistricting analysis for an active case
- Chenoweth replies with a methodological question AND Bassin forwards to his state AG network AND any positive Gist delta
- Any contact references the framework in a published brief, article, or court filing

Phase 2 signal: This constituency mix is producing fast-cycle operational engagement. The most time-sensitive Phase 2 domains should activate immediately.

**Outcome B — Strong (35–50% engagement, equivalent to 2 of 5 contacts at Score 3+, no Score 4+)**

Two contacts have replied with substantive questions or domain-specific engagement (Score 3), but neither has asked for implementation materials or named a referral. Gist delta is positive (>5 views) but not exceptional. Law school contacts may still be in their 5–10 day response window.

What this looks like in practice:
- Bassin replies with a clarification question about Domain 29 sourcing
- Weiser replies with a comment about the SAVE Act analysis but no implementation ask
- One Gist URL shows 8–12 views above baseline

Phase 2 signal: Engagement is real but not yet operational. Phase 2 should proceed on standard timeline, with domains sequenced by proximity to the engaging constituencies' active work.

**Outcome C — Moderate (20–35% engagement, equivalent to 1 of 5 contacts at Score 3+, or Gist delta >10 with zero replies)**

One contact has replied substantively, or no contacts have replied but Gist activity is above baseline (10+ views) indicating delivery and read-but-not-replied behavior. Law school contacts' silence is explicitly not penalized — their response window extends to Day 10 (May 28).

What this looks like in practice:
- One reply from Bassin or Weiser with a domain-specific question
- Zero replies but 15+ Gist views, indicating emails were opened and materials accessed
- One OOO from a law school contact (not penalized — pending, not silent)

Phase 2 signal: Research is reaching its audience but not yet converting to operational engagement. Phase 2 proceeds in conservative mode — domains with hard external deadlines take priority over domains requiring institutional momentum to activate.

**Outcome D — Weak (<20% engagement, equivalent to 0 of 5 contacts at Score 3+, Gist delta ≤5)**

Zero substantive replies. Gist views are at or near baseline (no evidence of opening/reading). No OOO responses. Delivery diagnosis is required before classifying as content failure — see PHASE_1_POST_WAVE1_CONTINGENCY.md Section 2 decision tree. If delivery is confirmed (test email lands in inbox, not spam) and engagement is still zero, this is a messaging/framing failure requiring post-mortem before new sends.

What this looks like in practice:
- Zero replies from all 5 contacts
- Gist dashboard shows 0–5 additional views since May 18 baseline
- No bounce notifications (which would route to Variant A1)

Phase 2 signal: Do not proceed to Phase 2 domain research until the delivery/messaging question is resolved. Phase 2 domain sequencing shifts to discovery-mode domains — those that can reach audiences through SSRN, Substack, and academic discovery channels independently of email momentum.

## Section 2: Per-Constituency Impact Scoring Matrix

The question this matrix answers: if you have limited Phase 2 research bandwidth (realistically 2–3 domain sprints before the August advocacy windows close), which constituency's engagement translates into the highest leverage for Phase 2 distribution?

Score is 1–5 on three dimensions: Phase 2 research amplification (does this constituency propagate new research quickly?), policy window relevance (do their active advocacy windows align with Phase 2 domain deadlines?), and institutional adoption speed (how fast do they convert research into filings, reports, briefs, or distribution?).

**Law Schools (Goodman/Chenoweth)**

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Phase 2 research amplification | 4/5 | Law review citations, clinic use, student research pipelines; Goodman's Just Security publication is faster than academic journals; Chenoweth's Ash Center connects to international democracy research networks |
| Policy window relevance | 3/5 | Law school windows are the slowest — citation to published use takes 6–18 months in standard academic channel; but Just Security publishes within weeks |
| Institutional adoption speed | 3/5 | Slower than litigation orgs; faster than standard academic publishing; clinic adoption for Domain 56 (civil service) and 58 (tribal sovereignty) can be fast if faculty engage directly |

Law school engagement is the credibility anchor that enables think tank and media amplification of the same research.

## Section 6: Messaging Strategy Adjustments by Outcome

If the contingency path is activated (Outcome C or D), the messaging changes are specific and apply to Batch 2 and Phase 2 distribution sends, not to the research itself.

### 6.1 If Outcome C (Moderate) — Shift from Framework-First to Deadline-First

The original Batch 1 emails pitched the full framework with two domain leads per contact. Under Outcome C, the single responding contact has validated that the research reaches its audience — but the conversion rate is low. The diagnosis is likely framing, not content.

**Key adjustment**: Lead every Batch 2 email with a specific external deadline, not the framework. The June CTC window, the Aug 2 EU AI Act enforcement date, and the Aug 10 UNGA deadline are all real urgency anchors that stand independently of institutional engagement history.

Example shift for Domain 59 (Economic Precarity) — Batch 2 to union contacts:

Original framing: "I'm writing to share a 35-domain democratic renewal research framework that may be relevant to your work on..."

Revised framing: "The June 15 Child Tax Credit cliff deadline is 28 days away. Our Domain 59 analysis documents how the OBBBA SNAP/Medicaid cuts stack with the CTC expiration to produce a compounding participation barrier affecting 22 million union-household families. Specific finding: households losing both SNAP and Medicaid enrollment lose approximately 340 hours per year of time currently spent on benefits administration — time that is directly subtracted from civic participation capacity. One-page brief available."

**Why this works under Moderate**: Union contacts do not need institutional momentum from law schools or think tanks to act on OBBBA research. The deadline-urgency framing bypasses the credibility-chain dependency that makes framework-first pitching slower.

### 6.2 If Outcome D (Weak) — Shift from Email-Push to Discovery-Pull

Under Outcome D, email-push distribution has temporarily failed. The adjustment is not to email harder — it is to make the research discoverable through channels that do not require an inbox.

Three specific channel shifts:

**SSRN submission** (May 22): Submit the executive summary and framework introduction as a working paper. Title it for search discoverability: "Democratic Backsliding and Institutional Resilience: A 35-Domain Empirical Framework for U.S. Democracy Crisis Analysis, 2024–2026." SSRN indexes in Google Scholar within 48–72 hours. Academic researchers searching for "democratic backsliding 2026" or "Schedule F civil service" will find it without receiving an email.

**Substack publication** (May 25–June 1): Publish one domain-specific long essay per week, beginning with Domain 59 (OBBBA cuts and democratic exclusion). Lead each essay with the most counterintuitive finding — the one that generates organic sharing. For Domain 59: "A family that loses Medicaid enrollment under the OBBBA loses an average of 340 hours per year to benefits reapplication processes — hours that come directly from civic participation time." The Substack infrastructure is already built (published/substack-posts/).

**Reddit/academic forums** (May 22–23): Post data-forward threads to r/PoliticalScience and r/democracy with the specific empirical findings, not advocacy conclusions. "Analysis: 35-domain framework finds X pattern in constitutional norm erosion — methodology critique welcome." Academic audiences engage with methodology challenges; this creates engagement even from skeptics.

Under all three channels, the email outreach continues in parallel — A2 retargets to Batch 1 (May 21), A/B test to 10 contacts (May 22–23) — but it is no longer the only distribution pathway. If SSRN produces citations before Batch 2 launches, those citations become the social proof for Batch 2 emails.

### 6.3 If Outcome A or B — Strengthen the Social Proof Chain Immediately

Under Very Strong or Strong outcomes, the most important messaging adjustment is moving from "research pitch" to "engagement continuation" within 24 hours of receiving a substantive reply.

The window for continuing an engaged contact's momentum is short: 24–48 hours. A response that arrives May 20 and is followed up May 25 loses 60–70% of its conversion potential. A response followed up within 24 hours with a narrowly targeted brief converts at significantly higher rates.

**Immediate follow-up protocol** (Score 3+ reply received):
- Within 24 hours: send a 3-paragraph reply
- Paragraph 1: Acknowledge the specific point they raised — not generic thanks, but a direct engagement with the domain they cited
- Paragraph 2: Offer the one piece of material most relevant to what they said — a one-page brief, a specific section of the Gist, or a cross-reference to another domain
- Paragraph 3: One specific question that invites a second reply (not an open-ended "what do you think" — a yes/no or short-answer question about their specific use case)
- Do not send all 8 Gists again. Do not re-pitch the framework. The relationship has opened — maintain it with specificity.
