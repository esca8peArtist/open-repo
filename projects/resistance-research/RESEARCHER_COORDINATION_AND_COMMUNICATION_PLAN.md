# Researcher Coordination and Communication Plan
## Phase 3 Research Sprint: November 4 – December 20, 2026

**Purpose:** Establish communication norms, decision-making authority, escalation thresholds, and feedback loops for the Phase 3 research sprint. This plan enables asynchronous daily work with lightweight weekly synchronization, clear escalation pathways, and full researcher autonomy over substantive research choices within defined parameters.

**Scope:** Coordination between user (project lead) and lead researcher (primary author of Domains K and H). If a secondary researcher or backup researcher is activated mid-sprint, the same protocol applies with role clarifications.

**Philosophy:** Researchers have full autonomy over research methodology, source selection, expert consultation, and analytical framing within confidence thresholds. User role is to unblock access/budget obstacles, interpret expert feedback when conflicting, and make final publication format decisions. Weekly syncs are for decision verification (catching contradictions), not micro-management of research progress.

## COMMUNICATION CHANNELS & NORMS

### Primary Channel: Slack (#phase-3-domain-k, #phase-3-domain-h)

**Slack channel purpose:** Lightweight, asynchronous daily updates and quick questions. Ideal for:
- "I found this source and it looks relevant; adding to bibliography"
- "Expert contact X responded; key feedback is [2-sentence summary]"
- "Running into access blocker on [Source Y]; trying Wayback Machine workaround"
- Quick wins: "Domain K Zone 1 draft completed"
- Questions: "Is [analytical choice] consistent with Phase 1-2 framing?"

**Slack communication norms:**
- Researcher posts daily brief update: 1-3 bullets covering what was completed, what blockers exist, what's next (5 min/day to write)
- User does not require response to daily updates; async check-in is sufficient
- If researcher asks a question in Slack, user responses within 24 hours acceptable
- Use **emoji reactions** for source validation: ✅ = verified/high confidence, ⚠️ = access issue/needs workaround, 🔴 = source unreliable/do not use
- Thread replies to keep channel organized: do not reply to a daily update as separate message; use thread to keep conversation together

**Escalation in Slack:** If researcher encounters issue requiring immediate user action (Severity 2-3 per contingency matrix), post with @[User] mention and "ESCALATION:" prefix:
- "ESCALATION: @user — 4 Domain K legislative sources became inaccessible (Congress.gov API change?). Need access verification within 2 hours."
- "ESCALATION: @user — Tier 1 contact (Fix the Court) unresponsive as of Day 4. Activating PB-E1. Identifying secondary contact."

**Example daily update (5 min to write):**
```
**Nov 5 Day 1 Update — Domain K**
- ✅ COMPLETED: Zone 1 legislative inventory (updated 2026 bills from Congress.gov)
- ✅ COMPLETED: 10 of 22 Domain K anchor sources verified accessible
- ⚠️ BLOCKER: JSTOR access behind institutional login; requesting library activation
- 🔴 SOURCE: "Fix the Court 2025 Year in Review" (K-1-C) — URL offline; activating Wayback Machine fallback
- NEXT: Complete source verification (Items 4), begin Zone 2 case law research, schedule Brennan Center coordination call
- CONFIDENCE: On track for Week 1 completion
```

### Secondary Channel: Weekly Sync Call (Tuesday 14:00 UTC, 1 hour)

**Sync purpose:** Weekly decision checkpoint + blocker escalation + next week planning. Only 1-2 decisions will typically require discussion; most syncs are brief (30-45 min).

**Sync cadence:** Every Tuesday, 14:00 UTC, Nov 4 – Dec 20 (8 weeks total, 8 calls)
- Call 1: Tuesday, Nov 5 (post-launch check-in)
- Call 2: Tuesday, Nov 12
- Call 3: Tuesday, Nov 19
- ...
- Call 8: Tuesday, Dec 17 (final call before Dec 20 distribution deadline)

**Sync agenda (standard format, 60 minutes):**

1. **Progress Update** (15 minutes)
   - Researcher walks through: which zones completed since last sync, which sources accessed, which experts contacted
   - What preliminary findings have emerged?
   - Are findings aligning with PHASE_3_DOMAIN_K_SOURCES_AND_CONTACTS.md and DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md framing?
   - Researcher shares: estimated completion date for current zone(s)

2. **Blockers & Escalations** (15 minutes)
   - Access issues: "Source X is inaccessible; tried Y workaround; next step is Z"
   - Expert non-response: "Tier 2 contact Y hasn't replied to outreach; has been 5 days"
   - Source quality problems: "Found conflicting data between Source A and Source B; need guidance on which to trust"
   - Budget/resource issues: "Need $X for [expense]; requesting approval"
   - Research direction disagreements: "I want to prioritize amendment strategy (Zone 3) but we may need to compress for timeline"
   - **User action items emerge from this section**

3. **Decision Log Review** (15 minutes)
   - Researcher presents: all research decisions made since last sync (per decision log template)
   - Example decision: "Prioritized 22 anchor sources over 110-source broad set due to Nov 1 pre-staging of Trump v. Slaughter analysis (saves 8 hours)"
   - User reviews: Are decisions consistent with Phase 1-2 framework? Do any decisions create conflicts with other domains?
   - If conflicting decisions: user and researcher align on single approach before proceeding
   - User provides: guidance on decisions that may affect Tier 1 contact messaging later

4. **Next Week Preview** (10 minutes)
   - Researcher previews: which zones will be completed next week, which experts will be contacted, what preliminary analysis to complete
   - User confirms: any upcoming news events, expert panel discussions, or external factors that should influence research focus?
   - Researcher requests: any specific sources or expert input user wants for next week?

5. **Administrative** (5 minutes)
   - Confirm next Tuesday sync time
   - Clarify: any Slack messages that need follow-up?
   - If backup researcher was activated this week: 15 minutes of handoff discussion (push other agenda items to 30 min each)

**Sync communication:** Zoom or phone call preferred; Slack thread unacceptable (too hard to capture decisions). Share meeting notes in #phase-3-domain-k after call: 5-bullet summary of decisions made + user action items + deadline confirmations.

**Skip conditions:** Weekly sync may be deferred to async (Slack thread) if:
- No blockers have emerged since last sync (researcher posts "No blockers; on track" in Slack, user agrees to defer call)
- Researcher requests deferral due to on-schedule progress and no decisions pending (acceptable once per month max)
- Critical news event or expert outreach makes sync urgent (move from Tuesday to earlier in week)

## DECISION-MAKING AUTHORITY

### Researcher Autonomy: Full Authority Over These Decisions

Researchers may make the following decisions **without user approval**, communicating decisions in weekly sync or Slack as FYI:

1. **Source prioritization:** Rank sources within each zone by confidence score, decide to use 22 anchor sources vs. 110-source broad set, deprioritize low-confidence sources
   - Constraint: Must remain above 90% direct-source citation rate (no speculation)
   - Communication: Document decision and rationale in decision log

2. **Expert contact prioritization:** Decide which experts to contact first, which to skip if timeline compresses, which Tier 3 media contacts to defer to post-distribution
   - Constraint: Must contact all Tier 1 coalition anchors (Fix the Court, Brennan Center, etc.) before Dec 20
   - Communication: Share expert contact log weekly with user (email summary)

3. **Research zone sequencing:** Reorder research zones if new information emerges (e.g., if Trump v. Slaughter ruling comes out, prioritize that zone update)
   - Constraint: All zones must be completed by deadline per PHASE_3_PARALLEL_RESEARCH_RUNWAY.md
   - Communication: Notify user in Slack when reordering research zones

4. **Analytical framing & methodology:** Choose analysis frameworks (e.g., which experts to cite for constitutional rot analysis, which Germany case study elements to emphasize), decide on evidence presentation, choose synthesis writing approach
   - Constraint: Framing must be grounded in >90% direct sources, must align with Phase 1-2 domain logic, must not contradict NIST AI RMF or other normative anchors
   - Communication: Discuss framing in weekly sync if novel (e.g., new comparative case study), otherwise document in decision log

5. **Timeline compression choices (within bounds):** If researcher falls behind by <1 week, decide to compress research zones, defer lower-confidence sources, reduce expert consultation rounds
   - Constraint: Cannot compress beyond pre-authorized PB-T1, PB-T2, PB-T5 procedures; Severity 2+ timeline overruns require user escalation
   - Communication: Notify user immediately in Slack if compression is needed; discuss in next sync

### Researcher Autonomy: Limited Authority (User Consultation Required)

These decisions require **async consultation with user before proceeding**:

1. **Budget increases beyond pre-approved amount:** Researcher requests additional funding for expert compensation, travel, or database access
   - Process: Email user with cost + justification; user responds within 24h with approval or alternative

2. **Scope changes affecting Tier 1 messaging:** Researcher wants to emphasize a new analytical angle that would change how research is positioned to Tier 1 contacts (e.g., "I want to make the international precedent section the centerpiece instead of the US constitutional vulnerability section")
   - Process: Raise in weekly sync; user approves if consistent with Phase 1-2 logic, or proposes alternative framing

3. **Expert contact changes:** Researcher wants to deprioritize a Tier 1 contact (not contact them at all) or significantly change engagement timing
   - Process: Raise in Slack or email with rationale; user confirms it's acceptable per PHASE_3_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md engagement triggers

4. **Domain compression beyond pre-authorized playbooks:** Researcher determines that full completion of both Domain K and Domain H is impossible within timeline and proposes compressing scope beyond PB-T3 or PB-T4 procedures
   - Process: Escalate immediately to user per Severity 2+ escalation protocol; user makes final scope decision

### User Authority: Final Decision Over These

User (project lead) has final authority over:

1. **Publication format:** Will Domain K be published as individual Gist, law journal, both? Will Domain H be published to constitutional scholars, policy channels, both? Will combined brief be distributed to all Tier 1 contacts or split delivery?
   - Research completion is prerequisite (researcher cannot make publication format choice that requires research not yet done), but user makes final format call

2. **Tier 1 contact interpretation:** If Tier 1 contact provides conflicting feedback (e.g., Fix the Court says "emphasize legislative solutions" but Demand Justice says "emphasize movement litigation"), user decides which guidance to prioritize
   - Researcher frames the conflict clearly in weekly sync; user resolves

3. **Escalation decisions (Severity 2+):** If researcher activates a Severity 2+ contingency (PB-R2, PB-D2, PB-T1, etc.), user reviews activation and approves next steps
   - Researcher executes steps 1-3 autonomously, then notifies user; user approves step 4+ or redirects

4. **Go/no-go for publication:** User makes final call on whether research is publication-ready on Dec 12-14 (for Domain K) and Dec 16-20 (for Domain H). Researcher proposes "ready to send," user confirms or requests final revisions

## ESCALATION PROTOCOL & RESPONSE TIMES

### Severity 1 Escalations: Autonomous (No User Notification Required)

**Examples:** Source access workaround (Wayback Machine), single expert non-response (pivot to secondary contact), publication platform hiccup (use GitHub fallback), minor scheduling change

**Researcher action:** Solve independently, document in decision log, mention in weekly sync as FYI. User reads about it at sync and confirms it was handled well.

**Response time:** N/A (no user response needed)

---

### Severity 2 Escalations: Execute Then Notify (24-Hour Notification)

**Examples:** Primary researcher extended absence (activate backup researcher), 3+ sources inaccessible (initiate fallback protocol), Domain K behind schedule (activate PB-T1 scope compress), Tier 1 contact unresponsive for 5+ days (pivot to secondary contact)

**Researcher action:** 
1. Confirm trigger detection (yes, this is really happening; not a false alarm)
2. Execute steps 1-3 of relevant playbook (PB-R2, PB-D2, PB-T1, etc.)
3. Post in Slack: "ESCALATION: [Playbook code] activated due to [trigger]. Actions taken: [1, 2, 3]. Next step pending user review."
4. Within 24 hours, send formal email to user with: trigger confirmation, steps executed, timeline impact assessment, user action items needed for steps 4+

**User response time:** Within 24 hours, user reviews escalation email and either:
- Approves next steps of playbook; researcher continues
- Proposes alternative approach; researcher pivots
- Requests rollback (only in rare cases where escalation was triggered incorrectly); researcher reverses actions

**If no user response within 24h:** Researcher may proceed with step 4 of playbook autonomously (escalation protocols assume researcher knows the solution; user approval is for confirmation, not decision-making). Researcher posts Slack update: "No user response to [Date] escalation; proceeding with step 4 per [Playbook]. Will report outcomes in weekly sync."

---

### Severity 3 Escalations: Immediate User Notification (Within 1 Hour)

**Examples:** Primary researcher full exit (cannot backfill; Phase 3 at risk), both Domain K and H behind schedule simultaneously (December 20 deadline threatened), 5+ Domain K sources permanently inaccessible, expert feedback contradicts entire analytical frame

**Researcher action:**
1. Confirm trigger detection
2. Post immediate Slack alert: "@user ESCALATION SEVERITY 3: [Scenario]. Do not proceed without user approval. Standing by for instructions."
3. Do NOT execute playbook steps until user responds
4. If user does not respond within 1 hour, call user by phone (if phone number provided) or escalate to backup user contact

**User response time:** Within 1 hour, user:
- Approves playbook execution; researcher proceeds
- Requests emergency action meeting to discuss alternative approach
- Makes judgment call on scope compression or timeline adjustment

**If user is unreachable for >2 hours:** Researcher may escalate to backup contact (identified in PHASE_3_RESEARCH_TEAM_READINESS_CHECKLIST.md Item 16) or execute most conservative Severity 3 response (e.g., pause research, await contact)

## DECISION LOG PROTOCOL

### Decision Log Template (Weekly Entry)

**Document location:** `projects/resistance-research/phase-3-domain-k/DOMAIN_K_DECISION_LOG.md` (and equivalent for Domain H)

**Entry format (one entry per research decision):**
```markdown
## Week [#] Decision Log

**Decision [#]: [Title]**
- Date: [Date, time zone UTC]
- Category: [Source prioritization | Expert contact timing | Zone sequencing | Scope compression | Analytical framing | Other]
- Decision: [Specific choice, 1-2 sentences]
  - Example: "Prioritized 22 anchor sources over 110-source broad set due to time constraint."
- Rationale: [Why this choice? Constraints, expert input, Phase 1-2 alignment, etc.]
  - Example: "Trump v. Slaughter integration delayed research start. 22 anchor sources provide 95% of analytical coverage; remaining 88 sources are supplementary. 8-hour savings allows completion of Domain H Zone 3 post-election updating."
- Expert input: [Did an expert influence this? Who, and what did they recommend?]
  - Example: "Gabe Roth (Fix the Court) recommended prioritizing legislative proposals with voting whip count; deprioritizing shadow docket (lower leverage in 120th Congress)."
- Confidence level: [1-5 scale, where 5 = high confidence, 1 = uncertain]
  - Example: "5/5 — Phase 1-2 domain methodology supports source confidence-based prioritization."
- Reversible?: [Yes/No — can we revisit this decision later in research?]
  - Example: "Yes. If Domain H completes early, can expand Domain K source review to full 110-source set for additional research depth."
- Cascading impacts: [Does this decision affect other domains or dependencies?]
  - Example: "None — Domain H research is independent of Domain K source selection."

---

**Decision [#+1]: [Next decision, if applicable]**
...
```

**Decision log review in weekly sync:** Researcher presents 3-5 key decisions made since last sync. User confirms:
- Are decisions consistent with Phase 1-2 framework? (Yes/No/needs clarification)
- Do any decisions create contradictions if a second researcher is on Domain H track? (No/needs alignment)
- Should any decision be reversed or modified based on new information?

**Decision log copy:** At end of research sprint, copy final decision log to CHECKIN.md memory system for future sessions (enables rapid researcher onboarding if backup researcher is needed later).

## CONFLICT RESOLUTION: WHEN RESEARCHERS DISAGREE

**Scenario:** Phase 3 is executed with lead researcher on Domain K track and secondary researcher on Domain H track (parallel 2-researcher model). Both researchers propose conflicting decisions (e.g., both recommend different expert prioritization schemes, or different publication timing).

**Resolution protocol:**
1. **Identify conflict** (weekly sync): Lead researcher and secondary researcher both present their positions clearly in sync call
2. **Document positions** (in decision log): Record both positions and rationale, mark as "PENDING RESOLUTION"
3. **User decision** (within 24h of sync): User reviews both positions in email exchange with researchers, then decides single path forward
4. **Communicate decision** (via email + Slack): User posts decision in Slack with brief explanation of reasoning; both researchers confirm understanding

**Escalation:** If researchers cannot agree on framing before user can weigh in, and deadline is within 48 hours, researcher with narrower domain (e.g., Domain K only) defers to researcher with broader domain (Domain H, which includes constitutional integration). This respects principle that broader analytical frames take precedence for consistency.

**Avoid:** Do not compromise by splitting the difference. User decides single coherent approach to publication, messaging, and analytical frame.

## COMMUNICATION NORMS: WHAT NOT TO DO

**Do NOT:**
- Use email for daily progress updates (use Slack for that)
- Use Slack for large document feedback (attach PDF or share Google Doc link)
- Expect researcher to respond to messages outside 9:00-17:00 UTC business hours Monday-Friday
- Post lengthy strategic discussions in Slack threads (move to email or sync call)
- Assume researcher read a Slack message without explicit confirmation ("Got it" emoji or response)
- Schedule impromptu calls without 24h notice (researchers need focus time for research work)
- Use Slack DMs instead of public channel (keep communication transparent in #phase-3-domain-k)

**Do DO:**
- Use Slack for quick questions and daily updates
- Use email for formal escalations and records
- Use weekly sync for strategic decisions and direction alignment
- Give researcher 24h notice for meetings
- Confirm all major decisions in writing (email or decision log)
- Assume researcher is offline if no response within 24h (they may be in deep research work)

## RESEARCHER AUTONOMY BOUNDARIES

Researchers operate with full autonomy **within these hard constraints:**

1. **January 3, 2027 congressional seating deadline is immovable.** All contingency responses prioritize Dec 20 distribution target over research depth.

2. **Tier 1 contact distribution is required.** All research must reach Fix the Court, Brennan Center, Demand Justice, ACS, and Protect Democracy (Domain H) by Dec 20. If research is late, compress scope rather than miss distribution.

3. **>90% direct source citation rate is required.** No speculation. All analytical claims must be grounded in cited primary or secondary sources.

4. **Consistency with Phase 1-2 domains is required.** Domain K research must align with existing 40+ domain framework logic. Domain H must integrate with constitutional reform analysis from prior domains.

5. **Expert contact engagement triggers are required.** Tier 1 contacts must be engaged per PHASE_3_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md activation windows. Researcher cannot unilaterally skip contact without user approval.

6. **Weekly decision log documentation is required.** Enables recovery if researcher changes mid-sprint.

**Within these constraints, researchers have 100% authority over research methodology, source selection, analytical framing, and timeline management.**

## EXAMPLE: DECISION-MAKING IN PRACTICE

**Scenario: Tuesday Nov 12 Weekly Sync, Domain K Track**

**Researcher opens call:** "Zone 2 (case law) is complete, but I found an issue. I was planning to emphasize Trump v. United States (2024) as the framing case for removal power analysis. But Gabe Roth [Fix the Court] sent me feedback on Nov 10: he says case law emphasis is less useful to legislators than legislative proposal emphasis. He wants us to lead with H.R.1074 [SHADOW Act] rather than case law. This changes my analytical frame for Zone 2-3."

**User response:** "Interesting. Let me ask: does de-emphasizing Trump v. United States break the Phase 1-2 framework? Did Phase 1 establish that case law was foundational?"

**Researcher:** "I checked. Phase 1 did establish case law as foundational, but it was establishing the *problems* (judicial capture, etc.). For *solutions* (legislative reform), Roth's point is that legislators care more about bill specifics than about case law grounding. He's right that we should lead with H.R.1074, but we still need to cite Trump v. United States as the constitutional basis for the bill."

**User:** "OK. So instead of leading Zone 2 with 'Trump v. United States established removal power', we lead with 'H.R.1074 responds to removal power problems documented in Trump v. United States'? You're reframing, not deprioritizing case law?"

**Researcher:** "Exactly. Case law remains in every zone, but the narrative flow changes from 'case law → problems → solutions' to 'proposed solution → problem (case law) → why solution is constitutionally grounded'."

**User:** "That's actually consistent with Phase 1-2 advocacy logic. Audiences want to know solutions first, then evidence. Go ahead and reframe. Document it in your decision log as 'Reframed Zone 2-3 narrative order per Gabe Roth input; maintained case law substantive content; changed presentation order only.' Confidence level?"

**Researcher:** "5/5. Roth's feedback is spot-on for advocacy audience."

**User:** "Great. Does this cascading to Domain H at all?"

**Researcher:** "No. Domain H is constitutional architecture, not legislative solutions. Domain H will keep the case law → problems → constitutional design framing."

**User:** "Perfect. Document it, move forward. Anything else this week?"

[Conversation continues with next blocker/decision...]

**After call:** Researcher updates decision log and Slack:
```
## Week 2 Decision Log

**Decision: Reframe Domain K narrative order (Zone 2-3)**
- Date: Nov 12, 15:15 UTC
- Category: Analytical framing
- Decision: Lead Zone 2-3 with proposed legislative solutions (H.R.1074 emphasis), then ground in case law evidence (Trump v. United States), rather than leading with case law
- Rationale: Gabe Roth (Fix the Court) feedback: legislative audiences prioritize solution specifics over constitutional grounding. Reframing maintains case law substantive content but improves advocacy narrative flow.
- Expert input: Gabe Roth (Fix the Court, Nov 10 email)
- Confidence level: 5/5 — advocacy methodology supports solution-first framing
- Reversible?: No. Narrative structure affects all downstream zones.
- Cascading impacts: None — Domain H maintains case law → constitutional design framing (different audience)
```

**User receives decision log entry in weekly sync notes and confirms in Slack:** "✅ Reframe approved and documented. Move forward with Zone 3."

## SUMMARY: WORKING TOGETHER

**Researcher brings:** Deep subject matter expertise, source knowledge, analytical rigor, expert network relationships, capacity to work independently for weeks without day-to-day supervision

**User brings:** Phase 1-2 framework context, Tier 1 contact relationships, publication decision authority, budget/resource authority, conflict resolution when different tracks propose contradictory approaches

**Weekly sync brings:** Decision verification (catching conflicts before they cascade), blocker escalation (when researcher needs user to unblock), strategic alignment (confirming new information doesn't change overall direction)

**Async Slack brings:** Lightweight daily transparency (user knows researcher is making progress), quick questions (researcher can ask clarifications without waiting for sync), documentation (decision log becomes automatically recorded)

**Result:** Researchers operate with full autonomy and velocity (40-48 hours/month research without bureaucratic overhead), while user maintains oversight and can intervene when needed (Severity 2-3 escalations).

**Success metric:** Phase 3 launches on Nov 4, both Domains K and H reach Tier 1 distribution by Dec 20, combined brief lands in congressional inboxes before Jan 3 Congressional seating, all research maintains >90% source citation and Phase 1-2 consistency. Researchers feel empowered to make substantive choices; user feels confident that research quality and timeliness are on track.
