## Case Background

**Cases**: Little v. Hecox, No. 24-38 (Idaho HB 500 — transgender sports ban) + West Virginia v. BPJ (consolidated) — argued January 13, 2026.

**What the Court is deciding**: Whether state laws prohibiting transgender girls and women from competing on female athletic teams in public schools violate the Constitution's equal protection clause under the Fourteenth Amendment, and/or Title IX's sex discrimination prohibitions.

**Lower court posture before SCOTUS**:
- Ninth Circuit: affirmed district court's injunction blocking Idaho's HB 500; found Hecox likely to succeed on equal protection claim
- Fourth Circuit: blocked West Virginia's ban in BPJ case; found violations of both Title IX and equal protection
- SCOTUS granted certiorari before judgment in multiple circuits, consolidating the cases

**Oral argument read (January 13, 2026)**: Court observers and SCOTUSblog analysis project that most of the Court will uphold the state sports bans, likely on Title IX grounds or narrow equal protection grounds that avoid a broad ruling on trans rights generally. The outcome most expected is an AFFIRM (upholding the bans). A narrow REVERSE on procedural grounds is possible but unlikely. DISMISS is unlikely given the Court took the cases on the merits.

**Key open questions regardless of outcome**:
- Whether the ruling extends beyond sports to other gender-segregated contexts (bathrooms, locker rooms, housing)
- Whether the ruling is grounded in Title IX (statutory — overrideable by Congress) vs. equal protection (constitutional — more durable)
- Whether any language in the opinion validates or rejects the broader Skrmetti framework (United States v. Skrmetti, argued earlier in the term)

## Path C: DISMISS — No Decision (Dismissed as Improvidently Granted or Remanded)

**Unlikely.** The Court granted cert on specific questions and will almost certainly decide them.

**Action**: Monitoring-only. No rapid-response distribution.

**What DISMISS means**: The Court either dismisses as improvidently granted (DIG) — meaning they decide not to decide — or remands for further proceedings on procedural grounds. In either case, the existing injunctions blocking the state bans likely remain in place temporarily, and the legal question returns to lower courts.

**What to do**:

```
[ ] Post "Little v. Hecox — DISMISS" to INBOX.md
[ ] Wait 48 hours for legal analysis from SCOTUSblog, ACLU, and Lambda Legal
    on what the DISMISS means for the lower-court injunctions
[ ] Reassess Domain 50 and 54 activation timing based on whether injunctions
    remain in place
[ ] No sends to external organizations until legal picture is clear
[ ] Check CHECKIN.md — orchestrator will flag if further action is needed
```

**No Discord notification needed** for DISMISS until the picture clears.

## Path D: PARTIAL — Fractured Decision

A fractured SCOTUS majority (no single opinion commanding 5 votes) would produce a PARTIAL outcome where the practical result is clear (bans upheld or struck) but the legal rationale is contested.

**Action**: Default to AFFIRM sequence if bans are upheld in any meaningful way. Default to REVERSE sequence if injunctions remain in place.

**How to classify**:

```
IF bans upheld in 5+ states as a practical result:
   → Treat as AFFIRM; execute AFFIRM sequence

IF injunctions remain in place and bans unenforceable:
   → Treat as REVERSE; execute REVERSE sequence

IF unclear (e.g., state-by-state variation, some bans allowed, some struck):
   → Post to INBOX.md: "Little v. Hecox — PARTIAL [describe]"
   → Orchestrator will flag in CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input"
   → Hold all sends until user reviews and selects AFFIRM or REVERSE path
```

## Why This Protocol Matters

The Hecox/BPJ ruling is the most anticipated LGBTQ+ rights decision since Bostock v. Clayton County (2020). Whatever the outcome, it lands in the final 2-3 weeks before the close of the SCOTUS term, in the middle of Wave 2 distribution activity for Domains 51/48/59. The risk without this protocol: the ruling lands, the user needs 4-6 hours to re-analyze, write templates, verify contacts, and decide on a sequence — by which time the 24-48 hour rapid-response window where organizations are most receptive has passed.

With this protocol: ruling lands → user reads 2-sentence outcome → routes to Path A/B/C/D → begins sends within 2 hours. The planning is already done.
