# Phase 1 Institutional Outreach Prioritization
## Domain 42 / May 28 DEA Participation Deadline
### 21 Days Remaining as of May 7, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The May 28 participation deadline for DEA Docket No. DEA-1362 is a narrow, hard-close window. Organizations that do not submit written notice of desired participation by May 28 cannot testify at the June 29–July 15 hearing on marijuana rescheduling. This is not a soft advocacy window — it is a federal administrative record deadline that cannot be extended after the fact. The goal of this outreach sequence is to ensure the highest-leverage institutions in the Domain 42 stakeholder universe are in the administrative record by May 28, and that they arrive there carrying the democratic exclusion framing that distinguishes this hearing from a conventional drug policy debate.

There is an additional urgency layer that tightens the timeline from 21 days to 6 days for the first wave: the House Appropriations Committee's full committee markup of the FY2027 Commerce, Justice, Science spending bill is scheduled for May 13. That bill's Section 591 rider — advanced by the subcommittee on April 30 by an 8-6 party-line vote — would bar any appropriated funds from being used to reschedule marijuana. If that language survives full committee and is eventually enacted, it could freeze DEA implementation and retroactively destabilize the dual-track framework the April 23 order created. Organizations that have already filed participation notices before May 13 are on the administrative record regardless of the Section 591 outcome. Organizations that wait are taking on appropriations risk they cannot control. The Day 0 send on Gist launch day and the Day 1 Category A wave are therefore the two most time-sensitive actions in this document.

### What "Success" Actually Looks Like After May 28

The May 28 deadline is not the end of the window — it is the beginning of the hearing campaign. After May 28, measure:

- How many organizations that filed used democratic exclusion framing (not conventional drug policy framing) in their participation notices
- Whether any Tier 1 or Tier 2 organization cites Domain 42 or the Gist in their testimony or written evidence
- Whether any filing organization has agreed to a background briefing with press — this converts one participation notice into press coverage of the democratic accountability argument
- Whether the framing from Domain 42 appears in coverage before the June 29 hearing opens

If the hearing produces a democratic exclusion argument in the administrative record, Domain 42's outreach has succeeded at the substantive level even if only 1–2 organizations actually file. The argument needs to be in the record; it does not need to be attributed.

## Section 4: Contingency Recovery Paths — If Launch Delays Past May 10

The critical variable is how many days between launch and May 28. Each delay scenario reduces available lead time for institutional contacts and increases the probability of missing the participation window for organizations with longer internal approval chains.

### Scenario A: Launch Delays to May 10–11 (3–4 Day Delay)

**Impact**: Low. Organizations contacted on May 10–11 have 17–18 days before the deadline. All Tier 1 and Tier 2 contacts can still act within their normal lead times. The Section 591 pre-markup urgency framing is lost (markup is May 13 — organizations contacted May 11 have only 2 days to file before May 13), but the post-markup framing from DOMAIN_42_DISTRIBUTION_RISK_MITIGATION.md Scenario B is available if the markup proceeds.

**Adjustments**:
- Collapse Day 0 and Day 1 into a single send — no staging, just send all Tier 1 emails as fast as possible on May 10 or 11
- Move Day 3 (Category B) send to May 13–14 — still 14+ days of lead time for all Tier 2 contacts
- Move Day 7 (AG and press) to May 17–18 — still 10–11 days of lead time, which is sufficient for press contacts; AG public statement timeline is tight but feasible
- No contacts drop from the outreach plan

**No minimum viable path reduction required.** Full outreach plan remains executable.

### Scenario B: Launch Delays to May 13–14 (6–7 Day Delay)

**Impact**: Moderate. The May 13 appropriations markup has already occurred by the time any email is sent. The Section 591 urgency framing must be updated with the post-markup language (DOMAIN_42_DISTRIBUTION_RISK_MITIGATION.md Scenario B, Adjustment 1) before sending. NAACP LDF and Weill Cornell (Meinhofer) — both with 10–14 day lead times — are now at serious risk of missing the May 28 deadline. They should still receive outreach but the participation ask should be softened to "written evidence submission" rather than "participation notice."

**Adjustments**:
- Update all email templates to use post-markup Section 591 language before sending
- Collapse Category A and Category B into a single three-day window (May 13–15): send DPA, LEAP, NORML, MPP, ACLU, Sentencing Project, SSDP on May 13; send Brennan Center, PPI, Moritz DEPC on May 14–15; send Mason Marks and Meinhofer on May 14 (both require longest processing time)
- Move AG and press to May 17–19 — 9–11 days of lead time; AG public statement is feasible but an actual AG filing notice is unlikely in this window
- Drop Weill Cornell (Meinhofer) from the participation notice ask; reframe as "please connect your May 1, 2026 study to hearing implications in a public statement or press interview" — lower bar than an APA filing
- Keep NAACP LDF on the list but frame as "written evidence submission to the administrative record" (which can be submitted post-hearing) rather than as a pre-deadline participation notice

**Minimum viable path for May 13 launch**: DPA (media@drugpolicy.org) + LEAP (diane@lawenforcementaction.org) + Mason Marks. Three emails in 8 minutes. These three are the highest-impact filings and should be sent before anything else on May 13.

### Scenario C: Launch Delays to May 17–18 (10–11 Day Delay)

**Impact**: High. Organizations with 10+ day lead times — NAACP LDF, Weill Cornell, DAGA, and state AGs — cannot realistically act on a May 28 deadline from a May 17 contact. The Tier 3 contacts (AGs, press) should be dropped from the participation ask entirely and reframed as post-hearing engagement targets. Tier 2 academic contacts (Meinhofer, Moritz DEPC) should be reframed as expert commentators for the June 29 hearing, not pre-deadline participation filers.

**What survives the compressed timeline**:
- All Tier 1 Category A contacts (DPA, LEAP, NORML, MPP, ACLU, Sentencing Project) — they can file in 3–5 days and have 10–11 days of lead time
- SSDP — chapter mobilization with 10 days of lead time can still generate 5–10+ chapter filings if SSDP national activates chapters immediately
- Brennan Center and PPI — both can file in 7–10 days, so 10–11 days is sufficient but tight
- Mason Marks — academic decision to engage has a 7–10 day timeline; 10–11 days is sufficient

**Adjustments**:
- Send all Tier 1 contacts on May 17 (collapse into single wave)
- Send Tier 2 contacts (Brennan Center, PPI, Mason Marks) on May 18–19
- Drop NAACP LDF, Meinhofer, AGs, and DAGA from the participation deadline ask; add them to a June 1 post-deadline contact list for hearing engagement (written record comments, press briefing support, post-hearing APA challenge support)
- Send press contacts (WeedPress, Democracy Docket) on May 19 — frame as "organizations are filing in the next 9 days, here is the story"

**Minimum viable path for May 17 launch**: DPA + LEAP + ACLU + Brennan Center. Four organizations covering the policy reform, law enforcement credibility, civil liberties, and democracy framing vectors. If only four organizations file with high-quality democratic exclusion framing, the administrative record is meaningfully influenced.

### Scenario D: Launch Delays Past May 21 (More Than 14 Days Delay)

**Impact**: Severe. This is the "Scenario C" in DOMAIN_42_DISTRIBUTION_RISK_MITIGATION.md — the outreach is so compressed that most institutional contacts cannot act. The minimum viable path shrinks to the three contacts that can file in 2–3 days: DPA, LEAP, and NORML. These three have the organizational infrastructure to file a participation notice within 48 hours of a well-framed request, because they file in every major DEA proceeding as a matter of institutional practice.

**Recovery path**: Send DPA, LEAP, and NORML on the day of launch, by whatever means available. If email is unavailable, call directly:
- DPA: 212-613-8020
- LEAP: Email is the only public contact; call the general LEAP number from their website
- NORML: 202-483-5500

**Post-May 28 pivot**: Redirect all outreach energy to the June 29 hearing engagement track — written comments to the administrative record (typically open 30 days post-hearing), media support for organizations that did file, and op-ed support using Domain 42's framing for organizations willing to publish under their own name.

## Section 5: Pre-Drafted Participation Notice Language

For use in the Day 14 and Day 18–20 follow-up waves. These can be sent as-is or adapted by the receiving organization. The format matches the Federal Register notice requirements: name, interest, oral or written evidence preference.

**For Drug Policy Alliance:**
"The Drug Policy Alliance respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. Drug Policy Alliance is a national advocacy organization whose work addresses drug policy reform, racial equity in drug law enforcement, and the democratic accountability of federal drug scheduling. Drug Policy Alliance wishes to present oral and written evidence regarding the structural design of the DEA scheduling process, its deviation from the statutory intent of the Controlled Substances Act, and its disproportionate impact on communities of color."

**For Law Enforcement Action Partnership:**
"Law Enforcement Action Partnership respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. Law Enforcement Action Partnership is a nonprofit organization of law enforcement professionals — police officers, judges, prosecutors, and other criminal justice practitioners — who support evidence-based drug policy reform. Law Enforcement Action Partnership wishes to present oral evidence regarding the institutional design of the DEA scheduling process as viewed from the perspective of law enforcement professionals who work directly with the consequences of federal drug scheduling on communities."

**For ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project:**
"The American Civil Liberties Union respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. The ACLU is a national civil liberties organization whose work on criminal law reform addresses the structural relationship between drug enforcement, mass incarceration, and democratic participation. The ACLU wishes to present oral and written evidence regarding the felony disenfranchisement feedback loop that drug enforcement produces — specifically, the mechanism by which drug enforcement generates felony convictions at racially disparate rates, removing the communities most affected by enforcement from the electorate."

**For Brennan Center for Justice:**
"The Brennan Center for Justice at NYU Law respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. The Brennan Center is a nonpartisan law and policy institute whose democracy program addresses voting rights, felony disenfranchisement, and the structural conditions that affect democratic participation. The Brennan Center wishes to present written evidence regarding the democratic participation implications of the current federal drug enforcement framework, including the relationship between drug conviction rates, felony disenfranchisement, and political representation in enforcement-intensive communities."
