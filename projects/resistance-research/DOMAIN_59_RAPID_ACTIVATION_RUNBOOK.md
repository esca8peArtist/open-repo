### Zone 1 — Causal Evidence Architecture (2-3 hours)
*Research question: What is the current state of quasi-experimental evidence for the financial-precarity-to-civic-participation causal pathway?*

The production research document establishes four anchor claims. Zone 1 deepens the causal evidence with updated 2026 data and extends to the OBBBA's compounding effects.

**Primary causal studies**:
1. [Liu, McCartney, Ramcharan, et al. — Dallas Fed Working Paper 2517 (revised March 2026)](https://www.dallasfed.org/~/media/documents/research/papers/2025/wp2517r1.pdf) — primary causal anchor; mortgage relief → voter turnout
2. [Slee and Desmond — Eviction and Voter Turnout (Politics and Society, 2023)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/00323292211050716) — eviction causally reduces turnout; NLIHC summary: https://nlihc.org/resource/evictions-associated-lower-voter-turnout
3. [Kuk, Hajnal, Lajevardi — Residential Mobility (PNAS, 2024)](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2306287121) — open access via PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11098094/ — Moving to Opportunity natural experiment; induced residential mobility depresses voting 20 years
4. [Rastogi and Laurison — Income Inequality in US Presidential Voting (Sociological Science, 2025)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/23780231251338441) — precinct-level; income-participation gap widens under high-turnout conditions
5. [Uslaner and Brown — Inequality, Trust, and Civic Engagement (Russell Sage)](https://www.russellsage.org/sites/default/files/u4/Uslaner%20and%20Brown.pdf) — inequality → trust collapse → civic disengagement

**Behavioral/psychological framework**:

7. [Scarcity — APA summary](https://www.apa.org/monitor/2014/02/scarcity)

9. [Scholars Strategy Network — Working-Class Political Engagement](https://scholars.org/contribution/strengthening-political-engagement-among)
