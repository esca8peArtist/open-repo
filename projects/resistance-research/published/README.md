## Research Foundation

- **[U.S. Democracy Crisis Analysis 2026](../us-democracy-crisis-analysis-2026.md)** — Crisis metrics (57/100 Century Foundation Democracy Meter, Bright Line Watch "mixed regime" classification), institutional failures documented domain by domain, international comparison, and DOGE database disclosure analysis. The diagnostic that grounds the full proposal.

- **[Resistance Movements Meta-Analysis](../resistance-master-report.md)** — 160+ movement case studies (1917-2024), success factors, the 3.5% participation threshold (Chenoweth), the role of organizational depth versus mobilization peaks (Tufekci), security force loyalty as the single most determinative variable, and the transition gap problem. The theory of change foundation.

- **[Case Studies — Labor and Civil Rights](../case-studies-labor-civil-rights.md)** — SCLC/SNCC organizational infrastructure, Wagner Act to Taft-Hartley arc, labor movement decline and democratic participation correlation.

- **[Case Studies — Pro-Democracy Movements](../case-studies-prodemocracy.md)** — Poland 2023, Hungary 2010-2023, Czechoslovakia Civic Forum, South Africa ANC, Philippines 1986, Taiwan democratization, South Korea 1987, Ukraine Orange Revolution and Euromaidan.

- **[Case Studies — Modern, Digital, and Populist Movements](../case-studies-modern-digital-populist.md)** — Arab Spring, Occupy, Black Lives Matter, Sunrise Movement, No Kings (2025), digital organizing cost curves, and Tufekci's tactical freeze analysis.

- **[Case Studies — Anticolonial and Independence Movements](../case-studies-anticolonial-independence.md)** — Ghana, India, Algeria, Kenya, Vietnam, and the nonviolent discipline evidence base.

- **[ICE Detention Comprehensive Report](../ice-detention-comprehensive-report.md)** — Detention system scale, conditions, algorithmic decision-making (ICM/FALCON/ImmigrationOS), civil rights litigation, and alternatives evidence. Foundation for Domain 16 (Immigration and Citizenship).

- **[Algorithmic Decision-Making in Immigration](../algorithmic-decision-making-immigration.md)** — NIST facial recognition bias data, ISAP electronic monitoring, EU AI Act comparison, Gonzalez v. ICE and related litigation, and AI impact assessment requirements.

- **[Remote Electronic Voting Research](../remote-electronic-voting-research.md)** — Cryptographic voting systems maturity spectrum (production-ready through unsolved), ElectionGuard E2E-V deployments, three-layer verification model (paper + E2E-V + risk-limiting audits), post-quantum migration path, and governance prerequisites. Foundation for Domain 1 (Electoral Reform) cryptographic analysis.

- **[Palantir Threat Model](../surveillance-tracking.md)** — Technical capabilities, government contracts, data integration architecture, and civil liberties implications. Foundation for Domain 21 (Data Privacy and Digital Surveillance).

## How to Use These Documents

**For general understanding:** Start with the 2-page Executive Summary (`democratic-renewal-executive-summary.md`), then read the full Proposal (`democratic-renewal-proposal.md`) beginning with Sections 1.1-1.3 (crisis and theory of change) and the cross-domain synthesis (Section 5.4).

**For policy work:** Use specific domain sections of the full Proposal plus the relevant supporting trackers. Each domain includes: the structural problem, proposed reforms, evidence and precedents, international benchmarks with fiscal estimates, and implementation pathway.

**For litigation strategy:** Litigation Tracker 2026 for active cases and their strategic status. Domain 6 (Judicial Independence), Domain 7 (Rights Protection), Domain 14 (Criminal Justice), and Domain 16 (Immigration) for the legal reform framework. ICE Detention and Algorithmic Decision-Making reports for immigration litigation specifically.

**For organizing and activation:** Resistance Meta-Analysis and case studies for theory of change. Trackers for current documented abuses to share with constituents. Executive Summary for recruiting conversations. Full Proposal for coalition-building with policy-engaged organizations.

**For public advocacy:** 2-page Executive Summary for general audiences. Specific tracker updates for social media (a documented fact about consent decree defiance or a journalist assault is more shareable than a policy argument). Distribution Guide for platform-specific messaging.

**For institutional adoption:** Contact legal aid organizations, policy centers, and academic law review editors with domain-specific sections that match their current focus. Reference the existing legislative vehicles (Redistricting Reform Act of 2025, American Opportunity Accounts Act, PRO Act, DISCLOSE Act) to connect the proposal to active legislative work. The Distribution Guide has a full institutional outreach section.

**For academic research:** Every claim in the proposal is sourced to primary research or government data. The fiscal analysis in Section 5.5 of the full proposal provides the most comprehensive international comparative evidence. The four case study files cover the 160+ movement dataset underlying the theory of change.
