## Section 5: Faith Coalitions (15 contacts)

### Sector Logic

Faith organizations have not appeared in prior Tier 2 planning documents. They are included here because: (1) faith coalitions are significant amplifiers in immigration policy and economic justice advocacy; (2) several domains connect directly to faith community concerns — D39 (healthcare access for low-income congregants), D59 (economic precarity as moral failing, not individual failure), D42 (criminal disenfranchisement of faith community members), and D57 (international human rights accountability mechanisms that faith-based international development organizations rely on); (3) faith coalitions frequently operate in communities underserved by academic and policy outreach. The framing for faith contacts emphasizes justice and democratic participation, not structural constitutional analysis.

**Note on messaging**: Faith contacts require a distinctly different framing from the other four sectors. The democratic design language that works for law school clinics will not resonate with faith communities. The framing should emphasize: (a) the moral and ethical stakes of civic exclusion; (b) the concrete impact on congregants and communities the faith organization serves; (c) the actionable response — what the faith community can do with the analysis. Do not lead with legal or constitutional framework.
