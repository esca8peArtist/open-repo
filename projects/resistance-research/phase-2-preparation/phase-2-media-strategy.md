# Phase 2 Media Strategy

*Resistance Research Agent — May 18, 2026*

**Purpose**: This document identifies the specific journalists, podcasts, and publications most likely to engage with the framework's domains, and provides the pitch logic for each outlet. It is designed to be read alongside phase-2-tier-2-organizational-expansion.md: Tier 2 institutional outreach builds practitioner adoption; media outreach builds ambient legitimacy and secondary amplification. The two tracks reinforce each other — practitioner engagement provides social proof for media pitches; media coverage provides credibility for practitioner outreach.

**Timing note**: Media outreach should not precede practitioner outreach. A journalist who covers the framework before institutional contacts have received it creates a problem: contacts who first hear about the work through a news article rather than direct outreach lose the sense of being a primary audience. Sequence: Tier 2 first wave sends → 7-day response window → media outreach begins. Exception: if a Tier 1 or Tier 2 contact independently mentions the framework to a journalist, respond to that journalist immediately — do not hold back under the sequencing protocol.

## Section 1: National Political Reporters (22 contacts)

### Pitch Logic

National political reporters covering democratic institutions, legal affairs, and government accountability are the primary target. The pitch is not "here is a 35-domain research framework" — that is not a story. The pitch is domain-specific: "here is original analysis of [specific mechanism] that is not available anywhere else." Each journalist contact is paired with the specific domain argument most relevant to their beat.

**Standard pitch structure** (adapt per journalist):
- One paragraph: what the specific finding is and why it matters now
- One paragraph: what makes this analysis different from what is already being covered (the novel contribution)
- One sentence: invitation to review the full domain document or the framework overview
- No attachments on first contact — link to Gist only

## Section 5: Contingency — Counterargument Preparation

Wave 1 could generate pushback. Three forms of pushback are most likely, in order of probability.

### 5.1 Methodological Challenge (Most Likely)

**Form**: "This is an independent researcher without institutional affiliation. How do we know the methodology is sound?"

**Pre-positioned response**:
The framework's credibility argument is not institutional affiliation — it is evidentiary foundation. Each domain cites primary sources: legislative text, court records, government reports, peer-reviewed academic studies, primary data. The methodology challenge is best addressed by pointing to the specific citations in the relevant domain, not by defending the author's credentials. Example: "The SAVE Act error rate analysis in Domain 37 draws on the ACLU's documented legal challenge record and the CBO cost score — both primary sources available to any researcher." If an academic contact challenges the methodology on specific grounds, the response is to engage the specific challenge with the primary sources. Do not defend the framework in the abstract.

**Additional pre-position**: The framework is CC 4.0. It explicitly invites methodological scrutiny. Any critique that strengthens the analysis is welcome. If a credentialed academic makes a methodological correction, incorporate it in the domain and thank the corrector in the document's attribution. A correction from a Tier 1 or Tier 2 contact is more valuable than silence.

### 5.2 Political Framing Challenge (Moderate Probability)

**Form**: "This is partisan advocacy dressed up as research."

**Pre-positioned response**:
Each domain's reform proposals are specifically designed to avoid partisan framing. Domain 57 proposes a bipartisan statutory mechanism (Treaty Withdrawal Notification Act — modeled on the 2024 NDAA Section 1250A which passed with broad bipartisan support). Domain 56 proposes civil service protections that have historically had Republican support (the original Hatch Act was bipartisan; the 1978 Civil Service Reform Act was a Carter administration project). Domain 42's DEA ALJ independence reform is an administrative law argument, not a drug legalization argument. The framework documents mechanisms, not political conclusions. The structural analysis identifies what is happening; the reform proposals identify how the same structural problem was addressed in prior periods by non-partisan or bipartisan mechanisms. If a specific domain is challenged as partisan, point to the bipartisan legislative model in its reform proposals section.

### 5.3 Scope Challenge (Lower Probability)

**Form**: "This 35-domain framework is too ambitious to be credible. Real research focuses on one question."

**Pre-positioned response**:
The scope is deliberate and methodologically justified: the democratic design failures documented in these domains are causally interconnected, not independent. Domain 37 (electoral interference) and Domain 56 (civil service politicization) share the same mechanism (executive capture of enforcement capacity). Domain 39 (healthcare) and Domain 59 (economic precarity) share the same pathway (benefit reduction → economic insecurity → reduced civic participation capacity). The scope is not a weakness — it is the finding: the erosion is systematic, not episodic. However, for any contact who finds the scope overwhelming, the response is to offer a single domain-specific document that can be evaluated on its own terms. The domain files are designed to stand alone. "You don't have to engage the full framework. Here is just Domain 57 — evaluate this argument on its own merits."

## Section 6: Media Sequencing and Timing Rules

1. **No media outreach before Tier 2 first wave.** Institutional contacts who first hear about the framework through a news article have a different relationship to the work than those who received direct outreach. Sequence: Tier 2 first wave → 7-day response window → media outreach.

2. **Just Security and Lawfare outreach is conditional on Wave 1 signal.** If Goodman (Just Security) responds at Score 3+, follow up directly with a domain-specific pitch rather than using the cold pitch template. Lawfare outreach is independent of Wave 1 but should wait until D57 is in production (at least an outline to share).

3. **Under WEAK: media outreach focuses only on external-deadline news hooks.** D39 June 1 HHS rule, D38 August 2 EU AI Act, D40 November 3 midterm. These are independently newsworthy regardless of Phase 1 momentum. Pitch these three as standalone news hooks, not as part of the framework.

4. **Score 5 signal coordination**: If any Batch 1 or Tier 2 contact cites the framework in a public document, coordinate media outreach within 48 hours. The citation is the news hook. Identify two journalists from Section 1 who cover the citing organization's domain and pitch that same day.

5. **Social media**: Existing Substack and Reddit distribution channels (documented in distribution-substack-drafts.md and distribution-reddit-templates.md) should be calibrated to follow media outreach, not precede it. A Substack post that appears the same week as a media mention has compound reach. A Substack post with no media context has no echo.
