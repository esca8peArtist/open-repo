## Section 4: Messaging Adaptation Strategy (Using Actual Phase 1 Data)

The single most powerful shift in Tier 2 messaging is replacing generic language ("leading researchers have engaged") with specific, accurate references to what actually happened. The table below maps Phase 1 data to Tier 2 social proof language.

### Translation Table: Phase 1 Signal to Tier 2 Messaging Language

| Phase 1 Signal | Accurate Tier 2 Language | What NOT to Say |
|---|---|---|
| Elias replies Score 4 with docket reference | "Election law practitioners at Democracy Docket have engaged with the Domain 37 analysis specifically for active litigation work" | "Leading election lawyers have endorsed the framework" |
| Weiser replies Score 3, asks for brief formatting | "Brennan Center researchers have requested domain-specific briefs formatted for state AG distribution" | "The Brennan Center has adopted our research" |
| Bassin replies Score 3, asks for implementation summary | "Protect Democracy has requested an implementation summary for their state-level network" | "Constitutional law organizations are using this research" |
| Goodman replies Score 4, proposes Just Security symposium | "Just Security editorial team is evaluating Domain 57 for symposium consideration" | "Major legal publications have picked up our research" |
| Chenoweth replies Score 3 with methodological critique | "The comparative civil resistance methodology has received academic review from Harvard Kennedy School" | "Harvard has validated our methodology" |
| Score 1 (acknowledgment only) from any contact | DO NOT USE as social proof — Score 1 is acknowledgment, not engagement | Never use Score 1 to claim "engagement" |
| No reply from a contact | DO NOT reference in Tier 2 messaging | Never imply engagement where there was none |
| D39 pre-distribution produces Score 2+ reply | "Healthcare policy organizations are engaging with the OBBBA Medicaid analysis ahead of the June 1 HHS rule implementation" | — |

### Social Proof Calibration Rules

1. **Use organization-level descriptions, not names**, unless the contact has explicitly given consent to be named. "A senior researcher at the Brennan Center" is safer than "Wendy Weiser."

2. **Match the social proof to the constituency being pitched.** Legal practitioner social proof (Elias) is most persuasive to other legal practitioners. Academic social proof (Chenoweth) is most persuasive to other academics. Do not use practitioner social proof with academics or academic social proof with practitioners.

3. **Quantify what you can.** "Five initial contacts at policy organizations and law schools" is more credible than "policy organizations." At the point of Tier 2 activation, the specific count is accurate (5 Batch 1 contacts received the work); what varies is the engagement quality.

4. **Acknowledge the stage honestly.** Under MODERATE: "We are in early distribution to policy practitioners and law schools." This is accurate. Under WEAK: do not reference Wave 1 social proof at all — the social proof must come from D39 or D38 distribution signals.

## Section 5: Which Constituencies Matter Most for Phase 1-to-Phase 2 Momentum

If signals are mixed, not all constituency performance is equally important for Phase 2 momentum. Priority order:

**1. Immigration legal aid (Elias) — highest leverage for Phase 2 speed**. A Score 3+ signal from Elias creates an immediate, usable practitioner hook for the most operationally urgent domains (D39, D57 Section 3). It also provides the fastest social proof for the Tier 2 immigration legal network (RAICES, NILC, CLINIC), which has the densest contact network across all Tier 2 sectors.

**2. Think tanks (Weiser + Bassin) — highest leverage for Phase 2 breadth**. Think tank engagement validates the analytical credibility of the framework for the broadest downstream audience: think tank publications are read by Congressional staff, state legislators, press, and academic researchers simultaneously. A Weiser or Bassin Score 3+ signal is the most credible social proof available for Tier 2 outreach.

**3. Law schools (Goodman + Chenoweth) — highest leverage for Phase 2 depth**. Law school engagement, particularly from a Just Security symposium or Harvard Kennedy School methodological validation, gives the academic community a credibility anchor that the policy community does not provide. Law school engagement is most important for D57 (constitutional asymmetry analysis needs constitutional law scholar validation to withstand peer scrutiny).

**4. Unions (no Batch 1 contact) — Phase 2 momentum depends entirely on Tier 2 engagement**. Union engagement is exclusively a Tier 2 signal. D39 and D59 pre-distribution are the anchors. Union Tier 2 outreach does not benefit from Wave 1 social proof as directly as the other sectors — it benefits from D39 HHS June 1 time urgency and D59 legislative utility.

**5. Faith organizations (no Batch 1 contact) — Phase 2 momentum is slowest**. Faith coalition outreach is lowest urgency and has the longest response cycle. Activate faith contacts in Wave 3 of Tier 2 (Sends 36–91) with established social proof from earlier waves.

## Section 6: Decision Rules for Phase 2 Domain Prioritization Under Mixed Signals

These rules operationalize the mixed-signal scenarios for domain sequencing decisions.

**Rule 1 (Domain 39 is non-negotiable)**: D39 pre-distribution executes by June 1 regardless of any Phase 1 signal. This rule exists because D39's HHS interim final rule creates an independent audience activation window that cannot be recovered after June 1.

**Rule 2 (D57 research begins regardless of think tank signal if law school is Strong)**: If any law school contact (Goodman or Chenoweth) produces Score 3+ by Day 10, D57 research begins June 15 even if think tank contacts are Weak. Law school strong + think tank weak = D57 leads, D59 follows July 1.

**Rule 3 (D59 is never the first research launch under Weak)**: D59 requires 45–65 research hours and a peer review buffer. Under Weak, D38–D40 fast-turn production generates engagement data before the longer sprint begins. D59 is not "skipped" — it launches July 15 at latest — but it does not replace fast-turn production as the first response to weak Phase 1 signals.

**Rule 4 (D38 is the fallback domain under sustained Weak)**: D38 (AI Regulatory Capture and Governance) has an external deadline (EU AI Act August 2 enforcement date), a credible analytical hook for think tanks (Brookings, CATO both cover AI governance), and a manageable production window (10–14 hours). If Phase 1 signal remains Weak through June 15, D38 is the primary domain that generates the engagement signal needed to anchor Tier 2 outreach.

**Rule 5 (Do not abandon Phase 1 contacts before Day 21)**: The 72-hour gate is a preliminary classification, not a final judgment. Law school contacts operate on 5–10 day cycles; think tank contacts on 2–5 day cycles; academic contacts can take up to 21 days during teaching periods. Do not conclude that a contact has rejected the framework until Day 21 with confirmed delivery. Maintain the follow-up calendar per PHASE_2_CONTINGENCY_PLAYBOOK.md Branch 1 protocols.
