## I. Urgency Scoring — May 18 Status

### Domain 56: Civil Service Politicization — Score 8/10

**Hard deadline**: June 1–30 (litigation window + Partnership for Public Service / AFGE briefing May 19–23)

The Schedule Policy/Career rule has been in effect since March 9, 2026. The advocacy window now is not legislative passage of S. 134 (which has no scheduled markup and faces a Republican-controlled Senate) — it is litigation support and coalition building as the first appellate decisions on Schedule P/C challenges are expected in June. The Partnership for Public Service briefing window (May 19–23) is the immediate advocacy entry point — within 24 hours of this matrix.

**Key urgency fact**: Domain 56 is the only Phase 2 domain already at full production-ready status (6,800 words, 47 citations). Every day it isn't in distribution is a day the June litigation window is unused.

### Domain 57: Multilateral Withdrawal — Score 6/10 (up from 5/10 on May 15)

**Hard deadline**: August 10 (six weeks before UNGA 81 High-Level Week, September 22–28)

Three developments since May 15 raise this score:

1. **Hungary ICC withdrawal effective June 3** (16 days): The one-year notice period under Rome Statute Article 127 terminates June 3. Hungary becomes the first EU member state to formally exit the ICC. This creates a concrete advocacy moment — Peter Magyar's Tisza Party won the April 12 election and has pledged to reverse the withdrawal, but the formal exit occurs before reversal is possible.

2. **5,000 US troops withdrawn from Germany; Italy and Spain threatened**: Trump announced Germany withdrawal May 2 and stated "a lot further" reductions are planned, specifically naming Italy and Spain. European NATO allies are actively preparing contingency planning. This converts Domain 57's core narrative from "rhetorical posture" to "physical implementation" — the Section 2.4 argument (multilateral withdrawal as domestic democratic dismantlement) is now empirically reinforced.

3. **Bloomberg "zombies" framing mainstreamed** (May 6–7): The mainstream policy press has adopted the argument that UN, WTO, NATO, ICC, and NPT are "functionally incapacitated." Domain 57's argument is now in the elite discourse; distribution before UNGA 81 capitalizes on this framing while it remains active.

**Remaining constraint**: Domain 57 requires 40–50 hours of original production (the most technically demanding work in Phase 2) and cannot begin in parallel with Domain 58 production without a second researcher. Begin no earlier than Domain 58 completion (June 10 target).

### Domain 58: Tribal Sovereignty — Score 10/10

**Hard deadline**: Late June / early July (Trump v. Barbara ruling)

The urgency is unchanged from May 15 — and still the highest in the queue. Two SCOTUS cases remain in active pending status as of May 18:

- **Trump v. Barbara** (birthright citizenship): Argued April 1, 2026. Majority appeared likely to side against Trump per SCOTUSblog. Ruling expected June–July 2026. If cert is granted, the case goes to October 2026 term — and Domain 58's VRA Section 1983 analysis needs to be in distribution before argument.

**What's confirmed since May 15**: The May 18 domain currency scan found no new rulings, no new BIA/IHS budget actions, and no Domain 58 integration needed. Domain 58 is clean and production-ready from its existing 5,200-word draft + complete outline.

**Production advantage**: The existing draft (domains/domain-38-tribal-sovereignty-indigenous-democratic-design.md, ~5,200 words, 34 citations) reduces production to approximately 25–35 hours rather than the 40–60 hours required for Domains 57 or 59 from scratch.

### Domain 59: Economic Precarity — Score 5/10

The urgency argument is real but does not create a near-term forcing function. The OBBBA (One Big Beautiful Bill Act) was enacted July 4, 2026 per the domain-updates tracking (House 215-214 May 22, Senate 51-50 July 1). **Implication for timing**: The enactment resolves the May 15 "data gap" concern — no need to wait for Senate disposition. Domain 59 production can proceed with full knowledge of what passed. However, the production estimate (50–60 hours) and the September distribution target still mean there is no benefit to beginning before Domain 58 is complete.

**Key data**: 591,850 bankruptcy filings in the 12 months ending March 2026 (up 11.9%); Harvard IOP Spring 2026 Youth Poll showing 45% of 18–29-year-olds struggling to make ends meet; now confirmed $863B Medicaid and $295B SNAP cuts in enacted law.

## III. Synergy Assessment — Which Domain Reinforces Phase 1 During Wave 1?

The first two to four weeks of adoption (May 18 – June 14) are when Phase 1 recipients are deciding whether to cite, engage, or share the framework. A Phase 2 communication during weeks 2–4 that connects directly to a Phase 1 argument will convert "this is interesting" into "this is something I'm using."

**Domain 56 wins the synergy test for breadth.** Twelve of the 40 Phase 1 domains address institutional integrity, separation of powers, or democratic accountability. The civil service argument — that merit-system protections are democratic infrastructure, not employment benefits — is the administrative-layer equivalent of the judicial and legislative erosion documented throughout Phase 1. Law school contacts primed by Phase 1 (the primary distribution target for law-school outreach) already understand administrative law; Domain 56 extends the conversation into OPM/MSPB territory that law schools are actively litigating. A June 1 follow-up email to Phase 1 recipients introducing Domain 56 as a companion document costs zero production time and reaches the entire existing network.

**Domain 58 wins the synergy test for targeted reinforcement.** Voting rights organizations and constitutional law faculties who received Phase 1 with Domain 49 (Callais) are the most receptive immediate audience. The Montana SB 490 injunction (May 11) and the pending Trump v. Barbara ruling give an immediate topical anchor: "a Montana court just blocked a voter suppression law targeting Native American voters — Domain 58 provides the full framework for this legal fight." This message is immediately actionable for Brennan Center, Democracy Docket, and NARF contacts.

**Domain 57 reinforces a smaller but high-value subset**: foreign policy and international law contacts, who are likely a smaller portion of Phase 1's distribution network but tend to be amplifiers (congressional staff, think tank fellows, journalists covering foreign policy). The Hungary June 3 withdrawal effective date and the Germany troop withdrawal give Domain 57 an immediately topical peg within the Phase 1 wave 1 window.

**Domain 59 broadens rather than reinforces**: economic justice and labor union constituencies are underrepresented in Phase 1's academic/legal distribution. Domain 59 is the right Phase 2 investment for audience expansion — but that expansion is not time-sensitive relative to the SCOTUS and litigation deadlines driving Domains 56 and 58.

## IV. Risk Assessment

### Domain 56 — Risk: Low

**Upside**: Zero production time. June 1 Partnership for Public Service briefing window is within 14 days. AFGE's Second Amended Complaint (already documented in the Civil Service Unions playbook) gives an immediate litigation peg. Federal employee unions represent the largest single untapped Tier 2 network in the Phase 1 distribution structure.

**Risk**: Audiences may associate civil service protection with union politics rather than democratic infrastructure. The bipartisan frame (S. 134 has 2 Republican cosponsors, H.R. 492 has 78 cosponsors total) is real but thin — careful framing is required.

**Data gap**: No scheduled committee markup on S. 134. The June 1–30 window is driven by litigation milestones and advocacy pressure, not a floor vote. Manage recipient expectations accordingly.

### Domain 57 — Risk: Medium

**Upside**: The novel thesis (multilateral withdrawal as domestic democratic dismantlement) is genuinely original and is now validated by mainstream discourse (Bloomberg "zombies" framing). UNGA 81 is a high-visibility diplomatic window that creates a strong distribution anchor.

**Risk**: 40–50 hours of production for the most technically demanding synthesis in the Phase 2 queue. Production cannot start in parallel with Domain 58 without a second researcher. If Domain 58 production slips past June 10, the August 10 deadline for Domain 57 becomes tight. The post-Trump v. Barbara ruling (expected before July) may require updating Domain 57's constitutional analysis in Section 2 if the Court addresses birthright citizenship and executive foreign affairs power simultaneously.

**Data gap**: Hungary's new government (Tisza Party, won April 12) intends to reverse the ICC withdrawal — but the formal exit occurs June 3. The reversal process under Article 127.3 takes additional time. Domain 57 should document both the formal exit and the reversal pathway without overstating the certainty of reversal.

### Domain 58 — Risk: Medium-High (deadline risk only)

**Upside**: The Trump v. Barbara ruling, when it issues, will be the single highest-profile SCOTUS decision affecting Native American rights since the citizenship era. A Domain 58 document in distribution before the ruling positions the research as an anticipatory briefing — more valuable than a post-ruling explainer. The existing 5,200-word draft and complete outline mean production begins from an advanced position.

**Risk**: The deadline risk is real. If production begins May 20 and targets June 10 completion (21 days, ~25–35 hours), the timeline is tight but achievable. The risk is that the ruling issues during production — late June is the typical SCOTUS end-of-term, and there is no certainty the ruling will not come in the first two weeks of June. If the ruling issues before Domain 58 is in distribution, the document becomes a post-hoc analysis rather than an anticipatory one. That reduces but does not eliminate its value.

**Data gap**: Turtle Mountain v. Howe cert timeline is uncertain. If SCOTUS grants cert at any conference through June, it goes to October 2026 term — which means the Domain 58 VRA Section 1983 argument needs to be in distribution before argument. Monitor SCOTUS orders lists every sitting Monday.

### Domain 59 — Risk: Low (timeline risk only)

**Upside**: Broadest raw constituency (80M+ SNAP/Medicaid affected). Faith community and labor union audiences are underserved by Phase 1. OBBBA enactment (July 4, per the may17-18 post-wave scan) provides a concrete legislative anchor — $863B Medicaid and $295B SNAP cuts are now enacted law, not projections.

**Risk**: 50–60 hours of production. Five causal pathways across behavioral economics, housing, labor, and healthcare — the most complex synthesis in the Phase 2 queue. The September 2026 distribution target provides adequate lead time, so the risk is not deadline pressure but production quality if rushed. No cost to delaying Domain 59 start until after Domain 58 is complete and Domain 57 is in research.

**Data gap**: The RTC/CTC June-August 2026 advocacy window (outlined in Domain 59) proceeds independently of the domain's production timeline. If the RTC/CTC vote occurs in June-July, the domain cannot capitalize on that window unless production begins immediately — which would require parallel execution with Domains 56/58 that current bandwidth does not support.

## VI. Concurrent vs. Sequential Execution Decision

The only domains that compete for the same researcher time are those with overlapping production windows. Domain 56 distribution is orthogonal to all production work — it requires only user approval, not research time. The sequential bottleneck is:

- **Domain 58 production** (May 20 – June 10): Requires 25–35 hours of research/writing
- **Domain 57 production** (June 10 – August 10): Requires 40–50 hours; cannot run parallel with Domain 58 at single-researcher bandwidth

If two researchers are available, Domain 57 research phase (sources, constitutional law review) can begin June 1 while Domain 58 is in writing phase. This would allow Domain 57 writing to begin July 1 and hit the August 10 deadline comfortably. The Domain 57 outline explicitly notes: "Two-researcher parallel execution feasible; single-researcher sequential execution requires Domain 59 completion by July 15 at latest."

Domain 59 production (July 15 – August 31) would overlap with Domain 57 writing (July–August). If single-researcher, run Domain 57 writing to completion (August 10 distribution) before starting Domain 59 production.
