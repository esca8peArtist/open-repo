# Domain H Solo vs. Team Resource Model
## Researcher Path Analysis, Skill Requirements, Knowledge Transfer, and Trade-Off Recommendation

*Authored: June 16, 2026 — Item 113 (Phase 3 Workflow Design)*
*Cross-references: DOMAIN_H_RESEARCH_WORKFLOW_DESIGN.md, DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md, PHASE_3_H_K_37A_RESEARCH_SEQUENCING_FRAMEWORK.md*

## Lead Finding

**The solo researcher path is strongly recommended for Domain H.** The primary reasons are narrative continuity and argument coherence: Domain H's core contribution is the integration of comparative constitutional design (Zone 2), structural vulnerability analysis (Zone 1), amendment strategy (Zone 3), and movement infrastructure (Zone 4) into a single argument about what constitutional resilience requires and how to build it. Splitting this across two researchers risks argument fragmentation at precisely the zones where the integration matters most — Zone 3 (Amendment Strategy) depends on Zone 1 (Vulnerability Mapping) and Zone 2 (International Precedent) being read and understood deeply by the researcher writing Zone 3. A team model can compress timeline but at real cost to argument quality.

That said, the team path becomes necessary if Phase 2 slips past November 1 and the January 3 deadline must be met with full research quality rather than the emergency minimum version. In that scenario, a two-researcher parallel production is the only way to deliver complete Domain H production before January 10.

The resource model below specifies both paths, their skill requirements, and the hand-off procedures that minimize knowledge transfer costs in the team path.

## Baseline Hours: From-Scratch Reference Model

Before specifying the solo and team paths, the from-scratch baseline establishes what Domain H would require if no prior research existed. This baseline is the reference point for understanding how Phase 2 research completion has already consumed hours.

**From-scratch zone hours** (25–30 hours per zone, per Phase 2 actuals and scorecard estimate):

| Zone | From-Scratch Hours | Research Type |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| Zone 1: Constitutional Vulnerabilities | 25–30h | New primary research — emergency powers doctrine, comparative authoritarian case studies, state constitutional degradation analysis |
| Zone 2: International Precedent | 25–30h | New primary research — German Basic Law sources, South African CCR analysis, Venice Commission, Israel judicial reform |
| Zone 3: Amendment Strategy | 25–30h | New primary research — amendment legislative history, coalition mapping, NPV compact state count, Article V convention analysis |
| Zone 4: Movement Infrastructure | 20–25h | New primary research — Democracy 2025 organizational survey, law school clinic network, international alliance dimension |
| **Zone total** | **95–115h** | |
| Editorial, checkpoint, and distribution work | 65–85h | Gist creation, combined brief, distribution list, weekly checkpoints, response monitoring |
| **Solo path total (from scratch)** | **160–200h** | |

The 160–200 hour from-scratch solo total spans the 12-week sprint at approximately 13–17 hours per week — at the high end of Phase 2's capacity model (Phase 2 ran at 25 hours per month average). This compression is the reason Domain H was prioritized for pre-production in the June 2026 research sprint.

**Actual hours (existing research)**: Because Zones 1–4 are research-complete, the Phase 3 sprint requires only editorial completion, post-election updating, and distribution. Actual hours: 30–45 total. The delta between 160–200 and 30–45 represents the research work already banked in the June 2026 sprint.

The solo and team path specifications below use the **actual hours** (30–45) as the operative planning figure, with the from-scratch baseline noted for reference in any future domain that lacks pre-production.

## Part 1: Solo Researcher Path

### Total Hours: 30–45 hours across 12 weeks (Nov 4 – Jan 31) [Actual]; 160–200h [From-Scratch Baseline]

**Revised estimate rationale**: The PHASE_3_H_K_37A_RESEARCH_SEQUENCING_FRAMEWORK.md revised the Phase 3 Domain H production hours from the scorecard's 18–22 hour estimate to 8–14 hours for editorial and production work, given that the June 2026 research sprint completed the foundational research. The 30–45 hour total here includes that 8–14 hours plus:

- Electoral outcome overlay and Zone 3 rewrite: 4–6 hours
- Domain K integration review and bridge text: 3–4 hours
- Gist creation and formatting: 2–3 hours
- Combined Brief (Domain K + Domain H) production: 4–5 hours
- Pre-send verification and distribution list management: 2–3 hours
- Response monitoring and coalition activation logging (Weeks 9–12): 5–8 hours
- Source verification and minor updates to Zones 1, 2, 4: 3–5 hours
- Weekly checkpoint documentation: 2–3 hours

This 30–45 hour range reflects the sprint as primarily an editorial and distribution execution task, not a primary research task.

### Zone Sequence (Sequential: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4)

The solo researcher must read and work the zones in this order, even though the underlying research is complete in all four. The sequential discipline ensures Zone 3's amendment strategy arguments are shaped by Zone 1's vulnerability analysis and Zone 2's international evidence before the editorial work in Zone 3 begins. Skipping to Zone 3 without first working Zone 1 and Zone 2 risks producing an amendment strategy that is not properly grounded in the structural vulnerabilities it is meant to address.

**Weeks 1–2 (Zone 1 and 2 editorial review)**: The researcher reads Zones 1 and 2 in full, verifies all sources, adds post-election updates, and flags any arguments that need strengthening based on current legal developments. This reading is not passive — it is active editorial work that will inform what to emphasize in Zone 3.

**Weeks 2–3 (Zone 3 editorial production)**: With Zones 1 and 2 fresh, the researcher writes the electoral outcome overlay for Zone 3, revises Section 3.1 (Amendment Sequencing and the January 2027 Window) to reflect post-November 3 chamber composition, and confirms the five priority amendments' current legislative status.

**Weeks 3–4 (Zone 4 and Domain K integration)**: Zone 4 is the most self-contained zone — its movement infrastructure survey is relatively independent of the other zones. This is also the week for the Domain K integration review, which requires the researcher to hold both documents in mind simultaneously.

**Weeks 4–5 (Gist and Combined Brief production)**: These are formatting and editorial synthesis tasks that require the researcher to have worked through all four zones. Do not attempt the Combined Brief before completing the Zone 1–4 editorial work.

### Skill Requirements for Solo Researcher

The solo researcher requires cross-zone competency. Domain H is not a specialist document — it moves between constitutional law (Zones 1 and 3), comparative constitutional design (Zone 2), and organizational and movement analysis (Zone 4). The range is wide.

**Minimum required competencies**:

- Constitutional law literacy at the level of following primary case citations (INS v. Chadha, Trump v. United States, Loper Bright, Humphrey's Executor) without requiring explanation. The researcher does not need to be a lawyer but must be able to read and evaluate constitutional scholarship.
- Comparative governance familiarity: the researcher must be able to assess the German Basic Law discussion, the South African Constitutional Court analysis, and the Venice Commission checklist without treating it as entirely foreign territory. Domain H's international sections are written for a policy-literate audience, not a specialist one, so this bar is achievable by a non-lawyer with strong policy research skills.
- Amendment strategy and advocacy sophistication: Zone 3's amendment strategy requires the researcher to think about legislative coalitions, supermajority threshold arithmetic, and the difference between organizing-as-strategy and legislation-as-strategy. This is advocacy-strategy thinking, not legal analysis.
- Movement and organization analysis (Zone 4): familiarity with legal advocacy organizations (Democracy 2025, ACS, Brennan Center, Fix the Court) and their advocacy models is necessary to assess Zone 4's movement infrastructure arguments.

**Skills not required for solo researcher**: Deep constitutional law expertise is not required. Kim Lane Scheppele and Yaniv Roznai are the scholarly authorities cited in Zone 2 — the researcher does not need to independently develop their analytical frameworks, only to assess whether the existing text correctly represents their arguments.

### Solo Path Risk Profile

The primary risk in the solo path is Zone 3 rewrite capacity in weeks 2–3. The Zone 3 electoral outcome application layer requires significant original writing (not just editing), and weeks 2–3 overlap with the most intense Domain 37a monitoring period. If Domain 37a's post-election application layer consumes more than 15 hours in Weeks 1–2, Zone 3 rewrite may slip to Week 3–4. This is manageable if the researcher extends the Zone 3 editing deadline to December 7 and compresses the Gist creation and Combined Brief from Week 4–5 to Week 5–6. The contingency schedule in DOMAIN_H_RESEARCH_WORKFLOW_DESIGN.md accommodates this one-week compression.

## Part 2: Team Path (2 Researchers)

### Total Effective Hours: 35–50 hours across 8 weeks (Nov 4 – Dec 31)

### Why the Team Path Compresses Timeline But Not Total Hours

Two researchers working parallel zones does not reduce the total research hours required — the editorial and production tasks are the same regardless of who does them. What the team path achieves is timeline compression: instead of 12 weeks of work, 8 weeks suffice because Researcher A handles Zones 1 and 2 while Researcher B handles Zones 3 and 4 in parallel during weeks 1–4.

This means Domain H's Gist, Combined Brief, and Tier 1 distribution can reach contacts by December 20 — two to three weeks earlier than the solo path's January 10 target. In the team scenario, Domain H could distribute simultaneously with Domain K rather than trailing by 10–14 days.

However: distributing H and K simultaneously eliminates the "escalating specificity" activation mechanism that PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md identifies as Domain H's highest-leverage distribution format. Contacts receive both documents at once rather than K first (building legislative specificity) and H two weeks later (providing constitutional architecture). The sequential format converts contacts from passive readers to active anticipators of the second document. The team path's timeline advantage comes at this strategic cost.

**When the team path is worth it**: Only if Phase 2 slips past November 1 and the solo researcher cannot complete Domain H by January 10 with acceptable quality. In that scenario, the team path's ability to produce Domain H by December 20 preserves the January 3 Congress seating deadline even if the sequential K→H distribution strategy is sacrificed.

### Zone Division: Zone 1 || Zone 2 (Parallel, Weeks 1–4) and Zone 3 || Zone 4 (Parallel, Weeks 1–4)

**Researcher A — Zones 1 and 2 (Constitutional Vulnerabilities and International Precedent)**

Zone 1 (Constitutional Vulnerabilities) and Zone 2 (International Precedent) are the most research-intensive zones for post-election updating. Zone 1 requires monitoring Trump v. Slaughter outcomes, tracking Article V convention state count updates, and verifying that the Weimar/Hungary/Turkey comparative arguments hold against any recent scholarship. Zone 2 requires monitoring German FCC developments post-December 2024 amendment and Venice Commission follow-ups.

Researcher A's weeks 1–4 work:
- Weeks 1–2: Zone 1 full source verification, post-election update (removal power case outcomes, Article V count), and any additions from current legal developments (15–18 hours)
- Weeks 3–4: Zone 2 full source verification, post-election monitoring (German FCC developments, Venice Commission follow-up), and comparative table creation for the Gist (8–10 hours)

Researcher A total: 23–28 hours in weeks 1–4.

**Researcher B — Zones 3 and 4 (Amendment Strategy and Movement Infrastructure)**

Zone 3 (Amendment Strategy) is the most rewrite-intensive zone. Zone 4 (Movement Infrastructure) is the most rapidly evolving zone — it requires post-election organizational updates and Domain K integration work.

Researcher B's weeks 1–4 work:
- Weeks 1–2: Zone 4 post-election update (Democracy 2025 litigation count, Michigan convention outcome, ACLU State Supreme Court Initiative post-election cases), and Zone 4 formatting for Gist (8–10 hours)
- Weeks 3–4: Zone 3 electoral outcome application layer (full Section 3.1 rewrite), Zone 3 five-amendment status update, and Domain K integration review (10–14 hours)

Researcher B total: 18–24 hours in weeks 1–4.

### Integration Sprint (Weeks 5–6): One Researcher Synthesizes

Weeks 5–6 must be completed by one researcher — the one with stronger constitutional law background — to ensure the document reads as a unified argument rather than a compiled set of zone reports.

Integration sprint tasks (10–14 hours):
- Read the complete document as produced by both researchers and identify any argumentative gaps between zones
- Write the bridge text between Zone 2 and Zone 3: the transition from international precedent to US amendment strategy is the document's most important argumentative hinge. It must not read as a topic change.
- Write the bridge text between Zone 1 and Zone 4: the constitutional vulnerabilities analysis (Zone 1) must explicitly connect to the litigation and movement infrastructure (Zone 4) that is actively contesting those vulnerabilities.
- Produce the Gist and Combined Brief

Integration sprint total: 10–14 hours.

### Skill Requirements by Zone in Team Path

**Researcher A (Zones 1 and 2) requires**:
- Constitutional law literacy (stronger requirement than Researcher B — Zone 1 requires understanding separation-of-powers doctrine, IEEPA mechanics, and the administrative law trifecta at a level sufficient to evaluate whether the constitutional vulnerability arguments are correctly stated)
- Comparative constitutional law familiarity (Zone 2 requires navigating German Basic Law text, South African Constitutional Court precedent, and Venice Commission standards)
- Source verification and legal citation skills

**Researcher B (Zones 3 and 4) requires**:
- Advocacy strategy and legislative analysis skills (Zone 3 requires assessing amendment coalition requirements, supermajority arithmetic, and the difference between organizing-as-amendment-advocacy and legislation-as-amendment-advocacy)
- Organizational landscape familiarity (Zone 4 requires knowing the legal advocacy organizations well enough to assess their capacity claims — Democracy 2025's 650+ organizations, ACS's 12,000+ legal professionals, Fix the Court's 50,000-subscriber list are all claims that require some organizational knowledge to assess)
- Less constitutional law depth than Researcher A

**Integration researcher requires**: Both skill sets, with constitutional law literacy being more critical for the zone-to-zone bridge text.

### Knowledge Transfer Procedures for Mid-Sprint Researcher Switch

If either researcher must be replaced during the sprint (personal capacity, illness, organizational change), the following hand-off checklist minimizes knowledge transfer cost:

**Hand-off documentation package**:

1. Zone completion memo (1 page): Which sections are complete, which are in draft, which have unresolved source issues. What arguments the outgoing researcher was planning to make in incomplete sections.
2. Source list transfer: The full bibliography for the zone(s) in progress, with annotations indicating confidence level (verified, unverified, paywall) for each source. Do not hand off an unannotated source list — the incoming researcher cannot distinguish between sources that have been checked and sources that have not.
3. Argument continuity note (1 paragraph per incomplete section): The outgoing researcher's intended argument for each section not yet drafted. The incoming researcher is not bound by this intention, but having it prevents the incoming researcher from accidentally contradicting a structural argument choice made in an earlier section.
4. Integration flag log: Any places where the outgoing researcher flagged a connection to another zone's argument that needs to be maintained in the final document. This is the most critical continuity item — zone-to-zone argument connections are the hardest things for an incoming researcher to reconstruct without explicit documentation.

**Hand-off timing constraint**: A researcher switch in Weeks 1–4 (zone research and editing phase) costs approximately 3–4 hours of incoming researcher ramp-up time. A researcher switch in Weeks 5–6 (integration sprint) costs approximately 6–8 hours because the incoming researcher must read the full document in context. A researcher switch after Week 6 (distribution and response monitoring) costs approximately 2 hours. Never switch researchers during Week 5–6 integration sprint unless unavoidable.

## Part 3: Trade-Off Recommendation

### Recommendation: Solo Researcher, With Team Trigger Condition

**Default choice: Solo researcher.** The argument for this choice:

Domain H's central analytical move — integrating constitutional vulnerability analysis (Zone 1) with comparative international precedent (Zone 2) into an amendment strategy (Zone 3) grounded in existing movement infrastructure (Zone 4) — requires the researcher writing Zone 3 to have read and deeply internalized Zones 1 and 2. This is not knowledge that transfers efficiently through a hand-off documentation package. The comparative constitutional design argument about Germany's eternity clauses, Israel's judicial reform crisis, and South Africa's 30-year institutional resilience must be second-nature to the researcher who is writing the amendment strategy section's argument that American constitutional repair requires this level of institutional thinking. A researcher who received a one-page Zone 2 summary from Researcher A before writing Zone 3 will produce weaker argument continuity than a solo researcher who spent hours working through Zone 2 herself.

Additionally, the team path's main advantage — timeline compression — is only valuable if Phase 2 is slipping and the January 10 deadline is at risk. Under the primary scenario (Phase 2 closes on schedule), the solo path's January 10 distribution target is achievable with available capacity, and the strategic advantage of the sequential K→H distribution (10–14 days apart) is preserved.

**Tighter narrative in the solo path also reduces editing time**. A two-researcher document requires an integration sprint to produce argumentative coherence; a solo document does not, because the researcher building Zone 3 already knows what she concluded in Zones 1 and 2. This means the solo path's effective editorial hours are 30–45 hours rather than the team path's 35–50 hours despite the latter distributing more broadly.

**Team trigger condition**: Activate the team path if Phase 2 has not closed by November 1, 2026 AND the solo researcher's November capacity assessment indicates that Domain 37a emergency production plus Phase 2 carryover audit will consume more than 20 hours in Weeks 1–2 of November. In that specific capacity-constraint scenario, the two-researcher parallel model is the only path to full Domain H production before the January 10 deadline.

### Summary Trade-Off Table

| Dimension | Solo Path | Team Path |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Narrative continuity | High — single researcher holds argument coherence | Medium — requires integration sprint to harmonize |
| Total hours | 30–45 | 35–50 |
| Timeline (distribution) | January 10 (with contingency to January 14) | December 20 (simultaneous with Domain K) |
| Sequential K→H activation | Preserved — 10–14 day gap | Lost — simultaneous distribution |
| Skill matching | One researcher needs full cross-zone range | Can specialize — legal vs. advocacy skills separated |
| Phase 2 slip resilience | Vulnerable if slip exceeds 3 weeks | Resilient up to 5-week slip |
| Recommendation | Default | Phase 2 slip contingency only |
