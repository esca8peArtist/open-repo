## Section 1: Role Definitions

Phase 3 requires 5 contractor roles per major domain (K and H). Not all roles need to be filled by separate individuals — the core researcher role is the critical hire; the others are supporting. The 5-role structure below applies to the full 6-domain scope. For Domains 57 and 54, the same role structure applies with domain-specific content.

### Role 1: Primary Researcher (2 per domain)

**What this role does**: Executes research zone production (Zone K-1 through K-4 for Domain K; Domain H extensions A, B, C). Produces 10,000–20,000 words over 7–10 weeks. This is the highest-stakes hire — all other roles are support.

**Required credentials**:
- PhD candidate (final year) or PhD recipient within 4 years
- Minimum 2 peer-reviewed publications or 1 publication + 2 policy reports (5,000+ words each) in last 3 years
- Demonstrated familiarity with constitutional law, judicial politics, or democratic backsliding literature
- Available 10–15 hours/week, November 4 – January 31, 2027

**Rate**: $35–55/hour (advanced PhD candidate: $35–40; recent PhD: $45–50; postdoctoral: $50–55)

**Upwork search terms**: "constitutional law researcher," "political science PhD writer," "legal research academic," "democratic governance policy writer"

### Role 2: Constitutional Law Expert (1 per domain)

**What this role does**: Fact-checks constitutional arguments, verifies primary source citations against bill texts and court opinions, flags arguments that overreach the primary source record. Engaged in Weeks 3–4 (Domain K) and Week 9 (Domain H) for embedded expert review. Expected time: 6–10 hours total per domain.

**Required credentials**:
- JD + 3+ years practice in constitutional law, OR
- PhD in public law or political science with 2+ publications in constitutional law venues
- Has navigated SCOTUS opinions, CRS reports, and bill text analysis professionally

**Rate**: $60–90/hour (short engagement; expert rate appropriate)

**Upwork search terms**: "constitutional law attorney," "SCOTUS legal analysis," "federal courts expert," "constitutional scholar freelance"

### Role 3: Legal Sourcing Specialist (1 per domain)

**What this role does**: Builds and maintains the source database — verifies URLs, retrieves paywalled articles, tracks down primary sources, monitors Congress.gov for bill status updates on a weekly basis. Not a research writer; a research infrastructure role. Expected time: 4–6 hours over the 13-week window.

**Required credentials**:
- Library science degree or equivalent experience with legal research databases
- Demonstrated comfort with Congress.gov, PACER, Westlaw/LexisNexis, JSTOR, and SCOTUSblog
- Can produce a source accessibility report within 24 hours of request

**Rate**: $25–35/hour

**Upwork search terms**: "legal research librarian," "PACER database researcher," "congressional bill tracker," "primary source verification"

### Role 4: Editorial Reviewer (1 per domain)

**What this role does**: Final copy edit — grammar, citation format consistency, internal cross-reference accuracy, plain-language readability of executive summaries. Engages in Weeks 6–7 (Domain K final edit) and Week 11–12 (Domain H final edit). Expected time: 6–8 hours per domain.

**Required credentials**:
- 3+ years editing policy documents, legal briefs, academic manuscripts, or advocacy research
- Can produce a tracked-changes edit of a 15,000-word document within 3 business days
- Plain-language editing experience strongly preferred (can simplify technical language without distorting meaning)

**Rate**: $30–50/hour

**Upwork search terms**: "policy document editor," "academic manuscript editor," "legal brief editor," "advocacy research proofreader"

## Section 3: Contractor Screening Rubric

Use this rubric at two gates: (1) Profile screen before phone call; (2) Interview evaluation before offer.

### Gate 1: Profile Screen (Go/No-Go Before Phone Call)

Score each criterion Pass/Fail. All three must pass to advance.

| Criterion | Pass | Fail |
|-----------|------|------|
| Academic credentials | PhD candidate or recent PhD (last 4 years) in qualifying field; OR JD + 3 years constitutional practice (expert role only) | JD without research publications; MA/MSc only; no advanced degree |
| Publication record | 2+ peer-reviewed publications or 1 publication + 2 policy reports in last 3 years | Only unpublished dissertation work; only conference papers without external review; no writing sample available |
| Timeline availability | Explicitly states availability for Nov 4 – Jan 31 window; no competing hard deadlines (defense, manuscript, teaching above 50%) | Says "should be available" without confirmation; has dissertation defense, book manuscript, or journal revision due in window; teaching load above 50% of normal |
| Writing sample quality | Sample leads with findings; primary source citations accurate (spot-check 3); readable by non-specialist; clear actionable conclusion | Sample is primarily methodological or theoretical; citation inaccuracies; prose requires legal background to comprehend |

### Gate 2: Interview Evaluation (Scored 1–5 per Dimension)

Score each dimension 1–5. Combined score of 14+ required to advance to offer. Score of 10–13: advance only if no better candidates in pool. Score below 10: do not offer.

| Dimension | 5 (Excellent) | 3 (Acceptable) | 1 (Disqualifying) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Timeline stress test: "Describe your most compressed writing project — deadline, wordcount, outcome" | 5,000+ words in under 2 weeks; external accountability; delivered on time | 5,000+ words in 3–4 weeks; internal deadline; delivered late but recovered | Only self-directed projects; no external delivery history; never missed a deadline but never had one |
| Domain orientation: "What are the main constitutional objections to statutory term limits for SCOTUS?" | Cites Article III "good Behaviour" debate; Fix the Court statutory analysis; knows SCERT Act or H.R. 1074 | Knows the general debate; cannot cite specific bills or organizations | Cannot describe the constitutional debate at all |
| Collaboration style: "How do you prefer to receive feedback on drafts?" | Describes iteration positively; has integrated editorial feedback into published work; gives specific example | Accepts feedback but describes preference for "minimal changes" or end-product feedback only | Frames feedback as judgment on their work; prior conflict with editor or client over revisions |
| Advocacy fluency: "Have you written research for a policy or advocacy audience?" | Has written for Brennan Center, ACS, Democracy Docket, or equivalent; knows how academic arguments translate to legislative framing | Has written policy reports but without advocacy organization context | Only academic publications; no prior work translating to advocacy or legislative framing |

## Section 7: Contingency Protocols — If Hiring Falls Below 70% by October 10

**70% threshold**: 4 of 5 core role categories have at least one committed candidate. Below 4: contingency activates.

### Contingency A: Expand Primary Researcher Eligibility Criteria

If no qualified primary researchers have emerged from Upwork by October 8, expand criteria in the following order:

1. Accept candidates whose "publication record" is one peer-reviewed article plus one published thesis chapter under external committee review (rather than external publication). Risk: slightly lower proven writing-to-deadline track record. Mitigation: weight interview stress test higher.
2. Accept JD candidates who have 2+ externally published legal analysis pieces (law review articles, substantial ACS Issue Briefs, Brennan Center reports) even without PhD. Risk: research synthesis skills may be more practice-oriented than analytical. Mitigation: request writing sample of analytical synthesis work, not brief or argument work.
3. Accept researchers outside US time zones if they commit to at least 3 overlapping Eastern business hours per day for the engagement. Risk: check-in logistics. Mitigation: move weekly check-in to asynchronous Slack/email format.

### Contingency B: Reduce to Minimum Viable Configuration

If Domain H primary researcher is not secured by October 15 but Domain K is secured:

- Launch November 4 with Domain K primary researcher only
- Coordinator executes Domain H extension personally (reduces Domain H output from 4,000–6,000 words of extension to approximately 2,500–3,000 words — meets minimum viable target but reduces depth of Extension A international case studies)
- Continue Domain H researcher search through October 22; if secured by October 22, onboard November 18 on staggered schedule

### Contingency C: Activate Non-Upwork Recruitment Channels

If Upwork yields fewer than 3 qualified candidates by October 8, activate the following channels simultaneously:
