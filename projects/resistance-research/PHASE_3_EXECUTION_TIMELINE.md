# Phase 3 Execution Timeline — Nov 2026 through March 2027

*Authored: June 6, 2026. Operational scope: November 4, 2026 – March 31, 2027 (primary research window).*
*This document is the quarterly implementation plan. For domain scoring rationale see PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md. For coalition amplification logic see PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md.*

---

## Most Important Finding

**The January 3, 2027 Congressional seating is the immovable deadline that organizes the entire Phase 3 execution calendar.** Every domain in the legislative advocacy cluster — Domain H (Constitutional Architecture) and Domain K (Judiciary Restructuring) — must be research-complete, Gist-published, and in Tier 1 distribution before that date. The seven-week window from November 4 (election day) to January 3 (Congress seating) is Phase 3's highest-leverage period and its most compressed capacity challenge.

Three dates anchor the timeline:

- **November 4, 2026**: Midterm election day. Triggers post-election research window. Domain 37a (Post-Election Section 3) deploys within 10 days if certification challenges materialize. Domain 49 (Callais redistricting post-election analysis) research begins immediately using certified results.
- **January 3, 2027**: 120th Congress seated. Domain H and Domain K must be in distribution. Committee assignments harden January 10-17; after January 17, the "blank slate" window for shaping incoming member priorities closes.
- **December 31, 2026**: Last day to publish post-election redistricting analysis (Domain 49) before litigation filing deadlines begin. After December 31, the analysis converts from active advocacy to historical documentation.

## Operating Assumptions

**Capacity model**: Phase 2 ran at approximately 25 hours/month across 8 months. Phase 3 requires 40-42 hours/month in November-December (compressed window, four concurrent domains, election-dependent timelines) and approximately 30-35 hours/month January-March (legislative window pace, three sequential domains plus parallel update passes). This is the task brief's stated constraint: Phase 3 is more compressed than Phase 2 per month despite lower total domain count.

**Phase 2 carryover risk**: If Domains G (press freedom), 37a (post-election certification), or 31x (healthcare tariff collision) are not research-complete at Phase 3 launch, they become Phase 3's first-week obligations before new research can begin. This is the single most consequential Phase 2-to-Phase 3 risk factor. The single most important Phase 3 capacity protection action is completing Domain 37a before October 31, 2026.

**Electoral outcome dependency**: The coalition deployment sequence and distribution targeting shift significantly based on November 3 results. The timeline below is designed to remain valid under all three outcome scenarios (Democratic House recapture, Republican retention of both chambers, split control). Domain-specific adjustments per scenario are noted at each decision gate.

**Research hour calibration**: Phase 2 actuals (28.5 hrs/domain average) serve as the baseline. Phase 3 domains that are structured updates to existing production documents (37a, 49, 51 post-election, 56 post-election) are estimated at 18-30 hours — below the Phase 2 average. Genuinely new domains (H, K, I, L) are estimated at 16-35 hours depending on scope — at or above Phase 2 average. All estimates in the scorecard table in PHASE_3_DOMAIN_CANDIDATE_SCORECARD.md.
