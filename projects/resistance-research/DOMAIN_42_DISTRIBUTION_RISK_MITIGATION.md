## Section 1: Execution Risk Scenarios

### Scenario A — Category A Send Delayed to May 9-10

**Trigger**: User does not act on May 8 but acts within 48 hours.

**Impact**: Low. Organizations contacted on May 9-10 have 18-19 days before the May 28 participation deadline. That is sufficient time for policy directors to review Domain 42, draft a participation notice (2-3 sentences required per the Federal Register notice), and submit to nprm@dea.gov. The May 20 mail transit cutoff is still clearable for any org that decides to mail rather than email.

**What changes**: The Section 591 urgency argument in the CHECKIN notes weakens slightly — the May 13 House Appropriations markup is only 3-4 days after a May 9 send. Still worth referencing in the email: "The appropriations markup on May 13 adds urgency — organizations filing before May 13 establish standing regardless of the appropriations outcome."

**No contingency action required.** Proceed with Category A send as soon as user is available. Nothing else changes.

### Scenario B — Category A Send Delayed to May 13 or Later

**Trigger**: Execution does not happen before the May 13 appropriations markup.

**Impact**: Moderate. The Section 591 urgency hook in the email loses its forward-looking framing — the markup will have already passed (or failed) by the time organizations receive the email. Two adjustments:

**Adjustment 1 — Update the Section 591 reference in all Category A/B emails.**

Replace the sentence: "Organizations filing before May 13 establish standing regardless of the appropriations outcome."

With this post-markup language:
- If Section 591 survived markup: "Section 591 survived the May 13 House Appropriations markup. Its enactment risk in conference is historically low (the same language appeared in the FY2026 CJS bill and was stripped), but it creates uncertainty. Organizations already in the administrative record are positioned to contest a Section 591 freeze through APA challenge — organizations that have not filed participation notices lose that standing."
- If Section 591 was stripped: "Section 591 was stripped at markup. The path to the June 29-July 15 hearing is now clearer. The participation deadline remains May 28."

**Adjustment 2 — Shorten the split-wave timeline if needed.**

If send window compresses to May 13-17 for all three categories, collapse Category A and B into a single three-day window (May 13-15) and move Category C (AGs, press) to May 16-19. The May 28 deadline is still met with 9-15 days of notice to each organization.

**Mail submission is at serious risk.** The May 20 cutoff for postal transit (to reach DEA's Springfield VA address before May 28) cannot be met if Category A send happens May 13+. All outreach should direct organizations to the email submission path: nprm@dea.gov, Docket No. DEA-1362, subject line "DEA-1362 Participation Notice — [Organization Name]." Organizations that prefer mail should be told the email path is equally valid per the Federal Register notice.

**Split-wave fallback for time-limited execution**: If the user has limited time on May 13+ and cannot send all 7 Category A emails, prioritize these three:
1. Drug Policy Alliance (media@drugpolicy.org) — highest institutional leverage, most likely to file independently
2. Law Enforcement Action Partnership (diane@lawenforcementaction.org) — strongest credibility with DEA ALJ process, likely to be designated as hearing participants
3. Mason Marks / administrative law faculty (see Category C admin law contacts) — academic credibility for APA structural argument; likely to submit formal written evidence

These three are the highest-probability participants in the DEA hearing and have the widest network effect (DPA and LEAP will inform their partner organizations; Mason Marks' engagement will attract other law faculty).

### Scenario C — Entire Domain 42 Track Postponed Past May 21

**Trigger**: No Category A, B, or C emails sent by May 21 (one week before the deadline).

**Impact**: High. Organizations that have not been contacted by May 21 are unlikely to act on a 7-day window — most institutional policy teams require 5-7 business days to draft and submit a participation notice. The participation rate for the June 29-July 15 hearing would be lower than it could have been.

**What does not fail permanently**: The Gist (https://gist.github.com/esca8peArtist/98dc61a3294a612482b37bd90f5c94ab) remains live and public. The Domain 42 document remains complete. The hearing participation infrastructure (nprm@dea.gov, DEA-1362) remains open until May 28.

**Restart trigger conditions for a compressed final push**:

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| User available May 21-24 | Send Category A only (7 emails, 15 min). Reference 7-day deadline. Omit Section 591 (irrelevant post-markup). |
| User available May 22-25 | Send DPA + LEAP + Mason Marks only (3 emails, 5 min). This is the minimum viable distribution. |
| User available May 25+ | Do not send participation-notice outreach. Send the Gist URL to 2-3 contacts as a "for your awareness" document with no ask attached. Domain 42 remains usable as background for hearing coverage and post-hearing analysis. |

**Post-May 28 engagement options** (the hearing does not end when the participation window closes):

The DEA-1362 hearing runs June 29 through July 15. Organizations that did not file participation notices by May 28 cannot testify — but they can:
- Submit written comments to the administrative record after the hearing (the comment window typically stays open 30 days post-hearing)
- Brief reporters covering the hearing using Domain 42 as background
- Engage with testimony prepared by organizations that did file participation notices
- Prepare for post-hearing APA challenge if DEA rules contrary to the evidence

Contact organizations with this reframing if the participation window closes without outreach: "You missed the May 28 participation deadline. Here is what you can still do in the June 29-July 15 window: [list above]."

## Section 3: Post-Send Monitoring (Days 1-21, May 8 through May 28)

### 3.1 Expected Response Windows

| Response Type | Expected Timing | What It Looks Like |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Auto-reply | Within minutes | Generic "Thank you for contacting [org]" |
| Initial staff acknowledgment | 2-5 business days | "Thank you for flagging this — we are reviewing" |
| Substantive engagement | 5-10 days | Detailed response, questions about Domain 42, request for additional materials |
| Participation notice filing confirmation | 7-21 days | "We have submitted our participation notice to DEA-1362" |
| No response | — | Triggers follow-up on May 15 (see Section 3.3) |

**Expected response rate for cold institutional outreach of this type**: 20-35% will reply within 10 days. 5-15% will confirm participation notice filing. The goal is not universal response — it is that organizations who were going to file anyway do so with the democratic exclusion framing, and that 1-3 organizations who would not have filed without the prompt do file as a result of the outreach.

---

### 3.2 Indicators of Successful Outreach

Track these signals as indicators that Domain 42 is having impact, even absent direct responses:

**Strong indicators (confirm outreach is working)**:
- Organization files a participation notice with DEA-1362 referencing democratic accountability or regulatory capture framing (not just drug policy reform framing)
- Organization cites Domain 42 or the Gist in a public communication, newsletter, or press statement
- Organization tweets, posts, or emails about the May 28 deadline with the democratic exclusion framing
- Staff member from a target organization asks to be connected to the framework author for background conversation
- Secondary contact from a non-targeted organization, received because a Category A contact forwarded the email

**Moderate indicators (outreach reached the audience)**:
- Reply acknowledging the deadline without committing to file
- Request to share the email with a colleague or partner organization
- Auto-reply indicating staff is at a conference or advocacy event (suggests the org is active and engaged in the policy space)

**Weak or ambiguous indicators**:
- Generic contact form acknowledgment
- No response (absence of response is not evidence of non-impact; many organizations file without confirming with the sender)

A participation filing rate of 60%+ among the Category A/B targets represents meaningful mobilization of the reform coalition. Below 60% means the outreach reached but did not convert.

**If below threshold by May 28**: Document the outcome accurately. Note in the tracking log:
- Which organizations replied but did not file
- Which did not reply at all
- What reason, if given, for non-participation

**Post-May 28 engagement for non-filers**: Organizations that did not file participation notices can still engage with the hearing through:
- Written comments to the administrative record (typically open 30+ days post-hearing)
- Media support for organizations that did testify
- Amicus briefs if any party pursues APA litigation post-hearing

Send a brief June 1 email to non-filers: "The participation deadline has passed, but the DEA-1362 hearing runs June 29 through July 15.
