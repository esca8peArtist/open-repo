These are the actions that actually change structural conditions. They require sustained commitment but have the highest long-term leverage.

School boards are currently the front line of authoritarian capture — curriculum control, library censorship, and DEI elimination are implemented at this level. They are also among the most winnable races with the lowest voter turnout.

**Actions:**
- Attend school board meetings — they are public and the public comment period matters
- Know who is running in the next cycle and support candidates aligned with democratic values
- Organize your parent/community network around specific school board issues

### 3. Develop a Civic Conversation Practice
The research is clear: one-on-one conversations across difference are the most effective tool for political persuasion. This means deliberately having real conversations — not arguments, not debates — with people in your life who hold different views.

**The method (Deep Canvassing, evidence-based):**
- Lead with genuine curiosity, not argument
- Ask about personal experience, not political positions
- Share your own story, not statistics
- Find genuine common ground before addressing differences
- Plant seeds, don't demand conversion

## Part VI: The Personal Sustainability Plan

Burnout is among the most common causes of movement failure at the individual level. Based on the research:

### The Sustainability Rules

**1. Protect your capacity.** You cannot sustain what you do not maintain. Sleep, relationships, joy, and rest are not indulgences — they are the infrastructure of sustained commitment. The activists who last decades are those who treat themselves as a resource to be preserved, not consumed.

**2. Community over content.** The loneliest form of activism is doom-scrolling and posting. The most sustainable form is showing up consistently to a community of people doing real things together. Prioritize relationships.

**3. Celebrate wins.** The authoritarian strategy of flooding the zone produces a subjective experience of continuous losing even when wins are happening. Consciously mark and celebrate: the court case that was won, the candidate that was elected, the organization that was built, the community member who registered to vote. Wins are real.

**4. The 40% rule.** Never commit more than 40% of your capacity to any single cause or organization. This protects against capture by any single group's internal dynamics and maintains your ability to respond to new developments.

**5. Diversify your impact.** Some weeks you make calls. Some weeks you write. Some weeks you show up. Some weeks you rest. Consistency over years matters more than intensity in any given week.

**6. Know why you're doing this.** Movements grounded in love — for community, for future generations, for the values you hold — are more sustainable than those grounded in fear or anger. Fear and anger are real and valid. They are not, by themselves, sufficient fuel for the long work.
