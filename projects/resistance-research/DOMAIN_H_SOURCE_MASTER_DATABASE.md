# Domain H Source Master Database
## Constitutional Resilience Architecture — Phase 3 Research Infrastructure

**Purpose:** Single authoritative source reference for Domain H research. Organizes 110+ sources by research zone, with reliability confidence scores (1-10), access notes, relevance tags, and brief annotations. Identifies 20 anchor sources — the cross-cluster, high-reliability bridges most production-ready for November 4 research.

**How to read confidence scores:**
- 9-10: Official primary source (statute text, court opinion, government statistics, official institutional publication). Near-certain reliability, minimal verification needed.
- 7-8: Established secondary source (peer-reviewed law review, major think tank, recognized investigative journalism with documented primary sources). High reliability; verify specific claims before citation.
- 5-6: Useful secondary source (expert blog post, advocacy organization analysis). Reliable framing; cross-check specific factual claims.
- Below 5: Background only — do not cite without independent verification.

**Source count:** 110 sources across 4 research zones + 2 supplementary categories. 20 anchor sources marked with ★.

**Deduplication note:** Domain H and Domain K share German FCC structure, Canada mandatory retirement, South Africa 12-year terms, and SCOTUS term limits as argument overlaps. Domain K owns the legislative feasibility analysis; Domain H owns the constitutional architecture framing. Sources shared between domains are marked with [K-bridge] where Domain K owns depth and Domain H cross-references.
