## Section 2: Unified Measurement Infrastructure

### 2.1 Core Measurement Architecture

**Eight-tab Google Sheets workbook** (from PHASE_1_IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_INFRASTRUCTURE.md):

1. **Master Contact Log** — All 25 Tier 1 contacts with engagement tracking (reply status, adoption level, sector SLA, follow-up action)
2. **Gist View Log** — Bitly click tracking + Gist fork monitoring + view velocity
3. **Email Reply Log** — Gmail label automation + reply categorization + secondary contact identification
4. **Substack Metrics** — Substack analytics aggregation (open rates, read time, shares by section)
5. **Citation Log** — Institutional forks, media mentions, GitHub citations
6. **Contingency Trigger Log** — Threshold breaches (Tier 1 signals < 10% reply rate, sector silence >21 days)
7. **Sector SLA Tracker** — Law school / think tank / civil rights org / D42 specific deadlines and reply targets
8. **D42 DEA Tracking** (Domain 42 only) — DEA-1362 docket participation, commitment-to-file status, filed notice detection

### 2.2 Daily/Weekly/Monthly Views

**Daily monitoring** (5 minutes, 7am UTC):
- Filter Master Contact Log to show rows updated in last 24 hours
- Scan for new replies, bounces, or actions due today
- Update Contingency Trigger Log if any threshold has breached
- Distribute Discord briefing (see Section 3)

**Weekly synthesis** (15 minutes, Sunday 18:00 UTC):
- Aggregate reply rates by sector (calculate C18-C22 formulas in KPI Summary tab)
- Identify patterns: which sectors are engaging, which are silent
- Update adoption level scores (0-4 scale) for contacts with engagement signals
- Run Tier 2 decision gate checks (are we on track to hit target thresholds?)
- Archive replicated contacts to secondary contact log (for June Tier 2 planning)

**Monthly decision gate** (30 minutes, 23:59 UTC on June 30 / July 20 / July 30):
- Aggregate all KPI Summary metrics (overall reply rate, sector breakdowns, adoption levels, media pickup, citation events)
- Compare against path-specific success criteria (Section 1)
- Count how many metrics hit target
- Determine GO / CONDITIONAL / NO-GO for Tier 2 launch
- Update DISTRIBUTION_PATH_DECISION_FRAMEWORK.md with actual results and Tier 2 recommendation

## Section 4: Success Metrics by Distribution Channel

### 4.1 Email Channel Metrics

**Setup**: Bitly link tracking + Gmail label automation (from PHASE_1_IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_INFRASTRUCTURE.md Section 1)

**KPIs**:
- Reply rate (% of recipients sending substantive reply)
- Reply timing (days to first response)
- Bounce rate (% of invalid email addresses)
- Reply category (interested / forwarded / declined)
- Secondary contact identification (replies naming other contacts to engage)

**Data collection**: Daily from Gmail labels + Bitly dashboard
**Reporting**: Updated in Master Contact Log + Contingency Trigger Log

### 4.2 Gist/GitHub Channel Metrics

**Setup**: GitHub API monitoring + Bitly link tracking (from PHASE_1_IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_INFRASTRUCTURE.md Section 2)

**KPIs**:
- Gist views (by day, by week)
- Gist forks (institutional copies = Level 3 adoption signal)
- Gist stars (informal bookmarking)
- Gist comments (inline engagement)
- Fork repository affiliation (did fork come from `.gov`, `.edu`, nonprofit domain?)

**Data collection**: Manual (GitHub API daily check) + Bitly (automatic)
**Reporting**: Updated in Gist View Log + Citation Log

### 4.3 Substack Channel Metrics

**Setup**: Substack analytics dashboard (native)

**KPIs**:
- Email open rate (%)
- Read time (minutes per reader)
- Click-through rate (% clicking embedded Gist links)
- Share rate (% forwarding to others)
- Topic engagement (which sections/domains drive clicks)

**Data collection**: Manual (Substack dashboard daily check)
**Reporting**: Aggregated in Substack Metrics tab + KPI Summary

### 4.4 Media / Citation Metrics

**Setup**: Google Alerts + Google Scholar Alerts + Congress.gov keyword monitoring (from PHASE_1_IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_INFRASTRUCTURE.md Section 3)

**KPIs**:
- Media articles mentioning proposal (count, outlet tier, narrative frame)
- Academic citations (papers referencing proposal or frameworks)
- Congressional references (staff memos, briefing materials)
- Policy influencer mentions (think tank, advocacy org social media)
- Vocabulary adoption (use of "democratic renewal" or domain-specific frames in external writing)

**Data collection**: Automated (Google Alerts) + Manual (CourtListener, Congress.gov, LegiScan)
**Reporting**: Aggregated in Citation Log + KPI Summary

## Section 8: Risk Mitigation & Contingency Protocols

### 8.1 If Overall Reply Rate is Very Low (<10% by Day 14)

**Diagnosis**: Messaging misalignment or institutional distribution list quality issue

**Actions**:
1. Verify email addresses in Master Contact Log against institutional directories (LinkedIn, university websites)
2. Re-validate institutional email domain (many universities have changed mail servers)
3. Audit email body with 2-3 non-recipient domain experts (is value prop clear?)
4. Consider email delivery issue: test sending to personal Gmail to rule out institutional mail filtering

**Path-specific contingencies**:
- **Path A**: Pivot from email to direct outreach (phone calls to department chairs, in-person talks)
- **Path A+37**: If overall low but election orgs engaged, reposition messaging to emphasize election protection angle
- **Path B**: Audit Phase 2 outline quality with academic experts; if low quality, delay Tier 2 and complete Phase 2 research

### 8.2 If Media Pickup is Absent (0 articles by Day 30)

**Diagnosis**: Messaging framing not resonating with journalists; competition from other news events

**Actions**:
1. Identify 3-5 target journalists covering democracy/governance themes
2. Develop a journalist-specific pitch emphasizing timeliness (election protection, infrastructure threats)
3. Consider thought leadership (op-ed placement in major outlet)
4. Pitch to democracy-focused outlets explicitly (Axios Pro, Democracy Digest, 2026midterms.org)

**Path-specific contingencies**:
- **Path A+37**: Emphasize election protection angle ("Federal Deception in 2026 Midterms")
- **Path B**: Emphasize research quality ("Comprehensive Policy Research on All 40 Democratic Systems")

## Appendix: Template Formulas (Quick Reference)

**Copy these into Google Sheets KPI Summary tab** (exact cell locations from PHASE_1_IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_INFRASTRUCTURE.md Section 5.1):

```
=== OVERALL METRICS ===
B2: Total contacts sent
=COUNTA(Contacts!A2:A200)-1

B3: Total replies received
=COUNTIF(Contacts!K2:K200,"Y")

B4: Reply rate (%)
=IF(B2=0,"N/A",TEXT(B3/B2,"0.0%"))

B5-B8: Adoption levels (1+, 2+, 3+, 4)
=COUNTIF(Contacts!S2:S200,">=1")  [etc for 2, 3, 4]

B9: Avg days to first reply
=IFERROR(AVERAGEIF(Contacts!K2:K200,"Y",Contacts!X2:X200),"No replies yet")

=== SECTOR BREAKDOWN ===
C18: Think Tank reply rate
=IFERROR(COUNTIFS(Contacts!B2:B200,"Think Tank",Contacts!K2:K200,"Y")/COUNTIF(Contacts!B2:B200,"Think Tank"),"0%")

[Repeat for C19–C22 with sector names: Civil Rights Org, Law School, State AG, Drug Policy Org]

=== ALERT CELLS ===
D35: Day 7 reply rate alert
=IF(AND(TODAY()-MIN(Contacts!H2:H200)>=7,B4<0.10),"ALERT: <10% reply rate at Day 7","OK")

[Repeat D36–D38 for bounce rate, sector silence (Think Tanks), sector silence (D42)]
```
