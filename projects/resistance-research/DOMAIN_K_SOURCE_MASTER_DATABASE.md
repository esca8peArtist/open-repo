# Domain K Source Master Database
## Federal Judiciary Restructuring — Phase 3 Research Infrastructure

**Purpose:** Single authoritative source reference for Domain K research. Organizes 110+ sources by research zone, with reliability confidence scores (1-10), access notes, and relevance tags. Identifies 20 "anchor sources" — the cross-cluster, high-reliability bridges most production-ready for November research.

**How to read confidence scores:**
- 9-10: Official primary source (bill text, court opinion, government statistics, institutional annual report). Near-certain reliability, minimal verification needed.
- 7-8: Established secondary source (peer-reviewed law review, major think tank, recognized investigative journalism with documented primary sources). High reliability; verify specific claims before citation.
- 5-6: Useful secondary source (opinion journalism, advocacy organization analysis, blog post by recognized expert). Reliable framing; cross-check specific factual claims.
- Below 5: Background only — do not cite without independent verification.

**Source count:** 110 sources across 4 research zones + 2 supplementary categories. 22 anchor sources marked with ★.

## SOURCE CONFLICT NOTES

**Potential coordinated bias flags:**
- Fix the Court and Brennan Center cross-cite each other extensively on term limits and ethics statistics. Both lean toward statutory reform viability. Their analysis on what Congress can do by statute should be cross-checked against the more skeptical view in the 2021 Presidential Commission's hedged conclusions and against conservative legal scholarship (see AEI's work on Humphrey's Executor as the closest analogy to constitutional limitations on SCERT Act enforcement).
- The ProPublica Thomas/Alito reporting is investigative journalism, not legal scholarship. Its factual claims about undisclosed gifts are well-documented with primary sources; its framing of the legal significance is advocacy-adjacent. Use the factual documentation, not the framing conclusions, in Phase 3 research.
- V-Dem's 2026 US downgrade is methodologically rigorous but note that their "liberal democracy" vs. "electoral democracy" distinction is a contested scholarly category. The downgrade is real; the specific threshold at which a country crosses categories involves the index's own weighting decisions.
