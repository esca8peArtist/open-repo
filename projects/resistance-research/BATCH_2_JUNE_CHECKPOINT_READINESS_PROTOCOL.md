## Critical Finding Up Front

The June 8 checkpoint is a measurement event, not a decision gate for most Batch 2 domains. Domain 51 executes June 9–12 regardless of June 8 Domain 39 signal. Domain 49 execution (if approved) is also independent of Domain 39 signal — the redistricting cascade is a separate pipeline with its own external deadlines. The June 8 checkpoint's primary function for Batch 2 is to determine whether Scenario A's Day 7 acceleration trigger for Domains 50–51 is met — and to provide early validation that the distribution methodology is working before the higher-stakes July domains execute.

The June 15 checkpoint is the more consequential decision gate: it determines whether Domain 48 activates in late June, whether Domain 50 Gist creation moves up from July 1, and whether the orchestrator can absorb Batch 2 expansion in the June 20–July 10 window if stockbot Lever B passes.

## June 8 Checkpoint: Domain 39 Day 7 Assessment

### What the Orchestrator Collects

**Time required**: 30 minutes total

| Data Category | Source | How to Capture |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| Reply count from Domain 39 contacts | Email inbox | Count replies from the 5 Tier 1 contacts (Georgetown CCF, NHeLP, Black Mamas Matter Alliance, Brennan Center, IRG) |
| Reply quality score (1–4) | Email inbox | Apply scoring rubric (below) to each reply |

| Gist unique viewer estimate | GitHub Gist traffic (not always available) | If GitHub Pro: check traffic tab. If not: total views ÷ 2 as rough unique estimate |
| Any cross-forwarding signals | Email inbox, social mentions | Note if any contact cc'd a colleague or forwarded the email |
| Bounce/delivery failures | Email sent folder | Check for any bounce-back messages |

**Reply quality scoring rubric**:
- Score 1: Auto-reply or generic acknowledgment ("We received your message")
- Score 2: Personal acknowledgment, no substantive engagement ("Thank you for sharing")
- Score 3: Substantive engagement — references specific finding or asks follow-up question
- Score 4: Action intent — requests meeting, co-distribution, specific additional analysis, or indicates they will use the research

## Standing Rules for June Checkpoints

1. **June 8 does not gate Domain 51.** Domain 51 Wave 1 sends (Campaign Legal Center, Issue One) execute June 9 regardless of any June 8 signal assessment. The July 1 CA ballot deadline is the governing constraint.

2. **June 8 does not gate Domain 49 (if already approved).** Domain 49 execution is gated by the redistricting cascade's external timeline, not by Domain 39 engagement. If approved, it runs on its own schedule.

3. **June 15 stockbot PASS → Domain 48 defers only.** All active Batch 2 domains continue on their schedules. Only Domain 48's optional June activation (which has no hard deadline) defers to July 1.

4. **Domain 50 Gist moves to June 20 only if Domain 39 OR Domain 49 is Strong at June 8.** Both thresholds independently authorize acceleration. One Strong signal is sufficient.

5. **Domain 57 scope is fixed until August.** No June or July engagement signal modifies Domain 57 scope or timing. The UNGA window governs Domain 57 independently.

6. **SAVE Act override supersedes all checkpoint logic.** If the SAVE Act passes the Senate, immediately execute Domain 50 distribution regardless of checkpoint gate status, scheduled timing, or competing project work. This is the highest priority override in Batch 2.
