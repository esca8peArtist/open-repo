The strategic implication of STRONG is acceleration, not expansion. The research content is not changed by a STRONG outcome. What changes is the timeline compression for Phase 2 domain production (D57 and D59 launch June 15, parallel, instead of July 1 sequential), and the availability of social proof language for Tier 2 outreach (which activates Week 5, June 15–21, rather than Week 6 or 7).

D58 distributes June 15 on the existing schedule. D39 pre-distribution hits June 1 as a non-negotiable across all paths. A reply from Elias mentioning a specific Callais-cascade redistricting brief, for example, would make Domain 37 election interference materials the immediate priority, not D57.

**Decision point: Path continuation vs. escalation.** STRONG triggers one mandatory user gate before Phase 2 pre-production begins. This gate is non-negotiable because STRONG path commits 90–110 hours of Phase 2 research capacity across June–August. Do not begin D57/D59 pre-production (source confirmation, outline drafting, Ikenberry acquisition) until this gate clears. Do not treat silence at the gate as confirmation.

### MODERATE (QRP >= 1 from any Score 3+, or Gist delta > 10 with zero email replies)

MODERATE at 72 hours is the highest-probability outcome for this contact cohort structurally. It means at least one practitioner found the research worth a substantive question or referral signal — that is the threshold that distinguishes MODERATE from the null result, and it is a meaningful threshold given the cohort's composition (two law school contacts expected to be silent at Day 3 regardless of content quality).

A Gist-delta-driven MODERATE (Gist views > 10 but zero email replies) carries different strategic weight than a reply-driven MODERATE. ### WEAK (QRP = 0, Gist delta <= 5, delivery confirmed via self-test)

**What the signal means strategically.** WEAK requires immediate diagnosis triage before any strategic interpretation is warranted. The delivery self-test (send a test email from the same account to your own address; check whether it lands in inbox or spam) must run before WEAK is confirmed. If the test email lands in spam, the classification is DELIVERY PROBLEM, not WEAK — and that is a categorically different situation. Content revision in response to a delivery problem is the most common and most costly mistake in this scenario. Do not revise the research framing, the subject line, or the contact selection before confirming delivery is working.

If delivery is confirmed, WEAK at 72 hours means one of three things: (a) the messaging framing is not connecting with the specific contacts selected (content problem), (b) the contact selection for Batch 1 was suboptimal for the current policy moment (contact problem), or (c) the research is reaching contacts who are in a response trough due to external factors (conference travel, end-of-semester, major litigation filing weeks). The monitoring window through May 25 often resolves (c) — post-synthesis replies arriving between Day 3 and Day 7 can upgrade WEAK to MODERATE. Do not commit to full WEAK remediation protocols before the May 25 gate closes.

**Phase 2 domains to prioritize.** WEAK defers D57 and D59. Instead, Domains 38 (AI Regulatory Capture), 39 (Healthcare Access), and 40 (Surveillance Capitalism / Electoral Manipulation) become the primary Phase 2 production targets because they are faster-turn (10–14 hours each vs. 45–65 hours for D57/D59) and their external deadlines are path-independent. D39 June 1 pre-distribution is non-negotiable across all paths. D38 targets June 30 (six weeks before the August 2 EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement date). D40 targets July 15 (needs to reach election protection organizations before August for the November 3 midterm cycle). D57 defers to August 1 research start, targeting September 1 completion (three-week UNGA lead time instead of six weeks). D56 May 28 and D58 June 15 are path-independent and continue.

**Decision point.** WEAK is the only outcome that requires an explicit user decision before Phase 2 research begins. The user decision required is: is this a delivery problem or a content problem? These are different interventions. A delivery problem means fixing sender reputation, re-verifying contact emails, and re-sending to the same contacts with no content changes. A content problem means auditing messaging (subject lines, framing, ask complexity) before re-engaging the same contacts or expanding to Batch 2. Post to CHECKIN.md explicitly: "Delivery confirmed. User decision needed: is this a contact selection issue, messaging framing issue, or neither (structural silence — await May 25 gate)?"

### Fallback Protocols

**Unexpected state 1: Two replies, one Score 3, one contradictory Score 1.** Score 3 generates 1 QRP. Score 1 generates 0 QRP. Total QRP=1. Classification=MODERATE. Contradictory signals do not cancel.

**Unexpected state 2: Ambiguous reply (neither clearly Score 3 nor Score 1).** Score conservatively. "Happy to hear more" without domain specificity = Score 2 (0 QRP). MODERATE classification from conservatively-scored ambiguous reply is more defensible than TOO EARLY.

**Unexpected state 3: Forwardee replies (contact forwarded to colleague, colleague replies).** Original contact gets Score 4 (forwarded to named colleague). Forwardee reply scored independently. If Score 3+, add their QRP. Forwardee is now warm contact.

**Unexpected state 4: Synthesis framework documents conflict.** MAY_21_SYNTHESIS_EXECUTION_FRAMEWORK.md is authoritative. Framework takes precedence over any execution checklist conflicts.

### Signal Log Validation

**Validation 1: Contact count.** Signal log must have ≥5 rows (one per Batch 1 contact) even if 4 are Score 0.

**Validation 2: Score & QRP consistency.** Verify QRP matches score using scoring guide (Score 0–2=0 QRP, Score 3=1 QRP, Score 4=2 QRP, Score 5=STRONG OVERRIDE).

**Validation 3: Gist delta is net, not cumulative.** Delta = increase since H+0 (May 18 ~08:00 UTC), not total view count.

**Validation 4: OOO and bounce entries logged.** If OOO autoresponder or hard bounce arrived, log in signal log. Adjusted send count denominator = 5 minus hard bounces minus OOOs.
