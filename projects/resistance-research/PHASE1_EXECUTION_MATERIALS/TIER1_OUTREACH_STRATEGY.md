## Sequencing Logic

Phase 1 is not a blast campaign. It is a credibility-building sequence designed so that each contact's engagement strengthens the next. The core principle from the policy-influencer-mapping analysis: **do not contact mass distribution channels until at least one scholarly or institutional citation exists.** Tier 1 creates that citation. Tier 2 and 3 amplify it.

The 25 Tier 1 contacts are divided into three waves:

**Wave 1 (Days 1-5): Credibility anchors — two think tanks, two Senate staff contacts, two law organization contacts**
These contacts have the fastest credibility-production timeline. A Just Security publication can appear within days of submission. A Brennan Center staff review creates a credibility signal even without a public product. Whitehouse and Klobuchar staff contacts connect the framework directly to active committee work. These contacts are the ones that make every subsequent contact more likely to respond.

**Wave 2 (Days 6-14): Think tank depth + law school clinic directors**
Once Wave 1 contacts have received and (ideally) acknowledged the framework, Wave 2 reaches the institutional intermediaries who convert research into policy pipelines — CAP, Brookings, EPI, and the clinic directors at Harvard, Columbia, NYU, and Georgetown who route proposals into legal casework and student notes.

**Wave 3 (Days 15-21): Civil society national leaders**
ACLU, NAACP LDF, Lawyers' Committee, Common Cause, Democracy Docket — these contacts need to see that the framework has institutional traction before they invest staff time. The Wave 1 and 2 contacts give them that signal. This sequencing also ensures that civil society leaders receive the framework after their colleagues in the think tank world have had time to form impressions.
