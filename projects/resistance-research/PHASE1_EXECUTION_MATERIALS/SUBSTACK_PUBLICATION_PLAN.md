## 8-Week Content Calendar

**Guiding principle**: Three to four posts per week is the optimal Substack cadence for an actively read newsletter in the policy space. Fewer and subscriber engagement drops; more and the publication feels like noise. The 8-week calendar below schedules 3 posts per week in Weeks 1-4 (launch intensity) and 2 posts per week in Weeks 5-8 (consolidation phase). Total: approximately 20 posts.

The existing 7 drafts in `distribution-substack-drafts.md` cover Posts 1 (Launch Overview), 2 (Electoral Reform), 3 (Accountability and Oversight), 4 (Trackers), 5 (Higher Education), 6 (Venezuela War Powers), 7 (DOJ Capture/SPLC). New drafts needed are indicated below with [DRAFT NEEDED].

## Cross-Platform Amplification Strategy

**Reddit threading** (coordinate with REDDIT_OUTREACH_THREADS.md):
- Post each Substack essay to the relevant subreddit within 24-48 hours of Substack publication
- Reddit post is not a copy-paste of the Substack post — it is a shorter version (400-600 words) with the Substack post linked as "full analysis"
- Use the Reddit post to generate discussion; link engaged Reddit commenters back to Substack for subscription

**Twitter/X threading:**
- Each Substack post generates one Twitter/X thread (6-10 tweets)
- Thread structure: Hook tweet (most arresting stat or finding) → 4-6 evidence tweets → close tweet with Substack link
- Do not thread all posts — select the three highest-impact posts per week for threading (usually the data-heavy posts)
- Tag relevant contacts when their work is cited (e.g., "@EricaChenoweth your 3.5% threshold research is the analytical foundation of...") — this generates organic retweets from the cited researchers

**LinkedIn:**
- Cross-post the Series 3 activation posts to LinkedIn (policy and legal professional audience)
- LinkedIn format is slightly more formal than Substack; add "This research is CC 4.0 — free for organizational use" prominently
- Target LinkedIn audiences: lawyers, policy analysts, think tank staff, congressional staff, law school faculty

**Bluesky:**
- Growing policy community; more hospitable than Twitter/X for long-form policy discussion
- Post Substack links with a 300-character hook
- Engage with the existing democratic reform community on Bluesky (Heather Cox Richardson, Steven Levitsky, Erica Chenoweth, and others have Bluesky presence)

**Email newsletter cross-promotion:**
- If you have relationships with newsletter operators in overlapping policy spaces, request a mention in exchange for a reciprocal mention when they publish something relevant to the framework
- The Tier 1 outreach contacts who have active newsletters (Richardson, Elias/Democracy Docket, Just Security daily newsletter) are the highest-value cross-promotion targets

## Substack Analytics Dashboard

Monitor these metrics weekly:
- **Open rate**: Target 30%+ for the active subscriber base (35%+ is strong for a policy newsletter; 25% is the minimum acceptable rate before adjusting subject lines)
- **Subscriber growth**: Target 50+ new subscribers per week during the 8-week launch window
- **Referral sources**: Check the "Sources" tab in Substack Analytics to identify which platforms are driving subscriptions (Reddit, Twitter/X, direct links, other Substack publications)
- **Top posts by open rate and by click rate**: These are different — high open rate means the subject line worked; high click rate means the content drove readers to the full proposal
- **Comment volume and quality**: A single substantive comment from a Tier 1 contact (e.g., "Professor [X] at [Institution] responded in the comments") is more valuable than 100 anonymous comments
- **Unsubscribe rate**: Normal churn is 1-3% per month; above 5% per month indicates the content cadence is too high or not relevant to the audience

Update the tracking dashboard in DISTRIBUTION_TRACKING_DASHBOARD_SETUP.md weekly.
