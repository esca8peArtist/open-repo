## 2. Phase 3 Existence — Confirmed Yes

Phase 3 exists and was formally scoped in `phase-3-strategic-roadmap.md` (published May 18, 2026). The formal launch date is **January 2027**.

### The Phase 3 Thesis

Phase 1 tested whether the framework could reach Tier 1 institutional audiences. Phase 2 tested whether that signal converts to organizational adoption. Phase 3 converts distribution into infrastructure: a **public knowledge commons** — version-controlled, community-cited, accessible to movement networks, citable in court filings and academic work, adaptable by practitioners.

### Five New Research Domains (Phase 3 Primary Work)

Entirely new domains addressing structural gaps Phase 1–2 research identified but did not fill:

**Domain H — Constitutional Resilience Architecture and Amendment Strategy** (Q1 2027, 2–3 sessions, 6,000–8,000 words)

What constitutional amendments, conventions, or litigation strategies could durably lock in the reforms this framework advocates? Core sources: Levinson "Our Undemocratic Constitution," National Constitution Center's Constitution Drafting Project, Germany's Basic Law eternity clauses, South Africa's Constitutional Principles.

Primary distribution: law schools, American Constitution Society, National Constitution Center.

**Domain I — International Democratic Standards and Comparative Accountability Architecture** (Q2 2027, 2–3 sessions, 7,000–9,000 words)

How do Germany, Canada, the Nordic states, the UK, and New Zealand structurally protect democratic institutions against executive capture? Core sources: V-Dem Institute, OECD "Drivers of Trust in Public Institutions," IDEA Global State of Democracy Report 2026, Germany's Basic Law Article 21.

Primary distribution: State Department oversight staff, Council on Foreign Relations, Transatlantic Democracy Working Group.

**Domain J — Cryptographic Democracy and Digital Voting Infrastructure** (Q3 2027, 3 sessions, 6,000–7,000 words)

What does secure, verifiable, accessible digital voting look like? Empirical record on remote voting security? How do end-to-end verifiable (E2E-V) systems work? Political economy of their adoption? Core sources: Stanford Internet Observatory, Colorado's risk-limiting audit framework, Swiss cantonal digital voting analysis, CISA 2025 election security guidance.

Primary distribution: election administration scholars, Cybersecurity Coalition, state secretaries of state.

**Domain K — Federal Judiciary Restructuring and Supreme Court Reform** (Q4 2027, 2 sessions, 5,000–7,000 words)

What specific legislative and constitutional proposals exist to restore judicial independence — Supreme Court term limits, Court expansion, appellate court restructuring, FISA Court reform, shadow docket elimination? Political feasibility analysis for each? Timed to post-2026-midterm legislative window.

Primary distribution: Fix the Court, Law and Democracy practitioners, Congressional judiciary committee staff.

**Domain L — Movement Infrastructure and Organizational Capacity Survey** (Q1 2028, 2 sessions, 5,000–6,000 words)

What organizational capacity exists in the US democratic resistance movement? What coordination gaps exist? Which organizational models have successfully scaled grassroots capacity while maintaining strategic coherence? Core sources: Chenoweth and Stephan "Why Civil Resistance Works," Bridgespan Group philanthropic infrastructure, Democracy Fund practitioner survey, Indivisible organizing model documentation.

## 6. Adoption Measurement System (Three Stages)

**Stage 1 (Jan–Jun 2027)**: Baseline measurement. Level 2+ contact count confirmed, designated point-of-contact for each, 30-day post-distribution check-in protocol active.

**Stage 2 (Jul 2027–Jun 2028)**: Integration signals. Which organizations are integrating the framework into operational work? Published reports citing analyses, legislative testimony, litigation filings (strongest signal), academic syllabi, coalition communications.

**Stage 3 (Jul 2028–Dec 2030)**: Outcome tracking. Is the framework influencing policy and legal outcomes? VRA revival legislation, Supreme Court term limits bill, NVRA-Medicaid litigation, CTC expansion, US re-entry into multilateral institutions.

## 7. User Role vs. Orchestrator Role in Phase 3

**User role (irreplaceable):**
Every distribution send. Pattern: orchestrator produces research and prepares distribution packages; user fills [YOUR_NAME] / [YOUR_CONTACT_INFO], performs final verification, and executes the send. Phase 3 intensifies this at Tier 3 scale (500+ organizations).

Phase 3 user decisions: Tier 3 channel endorser selection, Obsidian Publish configuration and domain registration, quarterly feedback sweep review, Phase 3 mid-cycle gate review (June 2028).

**Orchestrator role:**
Research production (Domains H–L, 86–117 hours total, 20–45 sessions over 24 months). Distribution package preparation. Adoption measurement tracking. Infrastructure (tracker maintenance, SCOTUS monitoring, international precedent updates). Synthesis documents and quarterly feedback integration.

## 9. Key Uncertainties

**High confidence:** Phase 3 research queue is well-scoped. Tier 3 distribution architecture is designed. Infrastructure items are technically feasible.

**Medium confidence:** Adoption measurement depends on Batch 1–3 contact responses to check-in protocols. Historical engagement rates suggest 20–40% response.

**Genuine uncertainty:** The 2026 midterm outcome is the most consequential external variable. Democratic majority dramatically changes which domains are actionable and expands distribution audience. Republican trifecta shifts emphasis toward movement infrastructure and defensive resilience.

**Planning gap identified:** The Phase 3 strategic roadmap does not specify what happens if the Obsidian Publish site is not activated by June 2026 soft launch deadline. This is now four weeks away and no activation checklist exists. If public knowledge commons is not live before Tier 2 distribution peaks (Aug–Oct 2026), adoption quality degrades.
