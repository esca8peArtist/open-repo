## DOMAIN 50 — LGBTQ+ Rights: The ADF Infrastructure and Democratic Participation Crisis

### Status Update: 8 States, 12 Ballot Measures

**The most significant change from June 1 scaffolding**: Ballotpedia's full 2026 tracking shows 8 states with 12 ballot measures touching sex, gender, and LGBTQ+ issues — not 6 measures in 4 states. The full picture:

**Anti-trans (restrict): 5 measures** — Colorado (2), Maine (1), Missouri Amendment 3 (1), Washington (1)
**Pro-equality (protect/expand): 7 measures** — Vermont, Ohio (2), Oregon (1), Missouri (1), Virginia (1)

The pro-equality measures are equally analytically significant: they represent states using ballot initiatives to restore or establish protections that courts and legislatures have provided under attack. This is the Romer argument in reverse motion: if anti-trans measures unconstitutionally remove minority protections, pro-equality measures are the democratic correction mechanism. Domain 50's Section 5 should address both vectors.

**Ohio AG Dave Yost's resignation** (effective June 7, 2026) to join ADF as VP of Strategic Research is concrete confirmation of the state AG pipeline documented in the domain's ADF infrastructure analysis.
