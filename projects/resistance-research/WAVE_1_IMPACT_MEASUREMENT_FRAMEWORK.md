# Wave 1 Impact Measurement Framework

**Version 1.0 — June 6, 2026**

**Lead finding**: The Day 7 checkpoint on June 16 is the highest-leverage decision point in this distribution cycle. Signal patterns at Day 7 — reply timing from CLC and Issue One, Bitly click velocity on the Domain 51 Gist, California ballot campaign organization engagement — predict the 30-day engagement trajectory with high accuracy and directly determine whether Phase 2 batch activation (Domains 48, 57) proceeds in June or shifts to a contingency path. This framework arms the June 16 checkpoint with specific per-organization-type thresholds, a composite signal score, and three pre-built Phase 2 routing decisions.

**This document is written for the June 16–18 reader, not the June 6 drafter.** Assume Wave 1 sends (June 9–12) have completed. You are now 7 days out and running the first major checkpoint.

---

## 1. What "Success" Means for Wave 1

Wave 1 has three distinct success definitions operating at different time horizons. Do not conflate them.

**Minimum viable success (Day 7, June 16–18)**: Domain 51 materials have been received, opened, and engaged with by at least one of the four Tier 1 contacts. Evidence: at least 1 Bitly click on the Domain 51 Gist link, OR at least 1 reply at any score level (including OOO). This confirms delivery and clears the infrastructure diagnostic threshold. Without this minimum signal, run the delivery diagnostic before drawing any content or engagement conclusions.

**Distribution success (Day 30, July 9)**: At least 2 of 5 Wave 1 contacts have replied substantively (Score 3+), at least 1 cross-organizational reference has been detected (a contact naming a colleague at another organization, or an unsolicited email from a non-Tier-1 contact), and at least 1 adoption signal has been recorded (a contact indicating they will use Domain 51 materials in specific organizational work). The July 1 California Fair Elections Act hard deadline makes Day 30 signal particularly meaningful — any Domain 51 engagement before July 1 carries a ballot campaign urgency multiplier that post-July-1 engagement will not have.

**Movement-scale impact (Day 60, August 8)**: At least 2 organizations have integrated Domain 51 materials into operational practice (policy brief, litigation toolkit, campaign communications, testimony preparation), and the dark money architecture analysis has reached at least one audience beyond the initial 5 contacts through organizational amplification. Evidence: public citation, PACER filing, policy document citing framework vocabulary, or confirmed forwarding chain extending beyond Wave 1 recipients.

The Day 7 checkpoint is the diagnostic gate. Day 30 is the engagement confirmation. Day 60 is the impact validation. Run all three; do not collapse them.

## 2. Per-Organization-Type Success Definitions

Wave 1 contacts span four distinct organization types with different engagement timelines, adoption patterns, and success indicators. Do not apply uniform thresholds across types.

### Type A: National Democracy Reform / Campaign Finance Advocacy (Campaign Legal Center, Issue One)

**Who they are**: Policy organizations with staff mandated to engage with external campaign finance research. Both have legislative relationships and publish regularly. CLC litigates FEC enforcement cases. Issue One operates the bipartisan ReFormers Caucus.

**Engagement timeline**: 3–7 days for initial reply. These organizations have faster reply cycles than law schools or think tanks because campaign finance reform is their operational core, not a secondary research interest.

**Day 7 minimum threshold**: At least 1 reply at any score level from CLC or Issue One. A Score 1 (acknowledgment only) still confirms delivery and shows the email reached the right person. Zero contact from both Tier A organizations at Day 7 is the primary failure indicator for Wave 1 — not content quality, but delivery or relevance gap.

**Day 7 strong threshold**: Reply from at least 1 Tier A contact at Score 3+ (substantive engagement: a domain-specific question, reference to their current enforcement or legislative work, request for additional materials). CLC citing the "200+ day FEC enforcement shutdown" angle or Issue One citing the "AI PAC proliferation following Fairshake template" angle is the most specific strong-signal pattern to look for — these were the primary hooks in the send templates.

**Day 30 strong threshold**: Both CLC and Issue One have replied at Score 2+. At least one has indicated operational intent: a reference to how Domain 51 connects to a current filing, publication, or legislative brief. A CLC reply referencing Chevron-era FEC deference implications or an Issue One reply mentioning the ReFormers Caucus research arm using the materials constitutes a strong adoption signal for this organization type.

**Adoption signal definition for Type A**: Any of — (a) explicit statement that Domain 51 materials will be incorporated into a filing, brief, or publication; (b) request for a formatted extract (e.g., "can we cite Section 4 in our FEC comment?"); (c) forward to a named colleague at a congressional office, FEC, or DISCLOSE Act working group; (d) citation in a publicly visible CLC or Issue One publication within 60 days.

**Network multiplier target**: CLC or Issue One naming a congressional staff contact, FEC commissioner staffer, or Senate Rules Committee contact constitutes a network multiplier event. Score at 3x weight of a standard reply.

### Type B: California Ballot Campaign Organizations (Common Cause California, League of Women Voters CA, Clean Money Action Fund)

**Who they are**: State-level campaign organizations running the "Californians for Fair Elections" coalition. In June 2026, they are in the active signature gathering or early mobilization phase of the ballot campaign, which means their bandwidth is split between campaign execution and external engagement.

**Engagement timeline**: 5–14 days for substantive reply. California campaign organizations respond more slowly than national policy organizations during active campaign windows — they are running logistics, not processing external research. A Day 7 silence from California contacts is expected and does not trigger a failure flag.

**Day 7 minimum threshold**: At least 1 Bitly click on the Domain 51 Gist from the California send window (June 11–12). Even a click without a reply confirms the email was opened and the link was followed, which is meaningful given bandwidth constraints. Zero clicks and zero replies from all three California contacts at Day 7 warrants a delivery diagnostic but is not a content failure signal.

**Day 14 minimum threshold**: At least 1 reply from any California contact at Score 1+. Common Cause CA is the highest-likelihood responder. LWV CA and Clean Money Action Fund have longer decision cycles given their operational roles.

**Day 30 strong threshold**: At least 2 of 3 California contacts have replied. At least 1 has indicated campaign-specific utility: a reference to the ballot campaign messaging development window, a request for materials formatted for voter education, or a citation of the AI PAC or Citizens United architecture for their campaign communications. This is the highest-value signal for the ballot campaign urgency multiplier — any reply before July 1 that references the campaign specifically indicates Domain 51 has reached the campaign architecture development window.

**Adoption signal definition for Type B**: Any of — (a) explicit statement that Domain 51 research will be used in campaign communications, voter education, or media outreach; (b) request for a simplified voter-facing version or executive summary; (c) reference to sharing Domain 51 materials at a campaign staff or coalition meeting; (d) any mention of the research in a Californians for Fair Elections public document or communication before October 2026.

**July 1 urgency multiplier**: Any Type B engagement signal received before July 1, 2026, is weighted at 1.5x relative to post-July-1 signals because the ballot campaign messaging development window closes around that date. A July 5 reply that would score MODERATE post-framework may represent a genuine ballot campaign integration signal even without explicit confirmation — campaign organizations often act before they reply.

## 3. Measurement Methodology

All measurement methods are calibrated for 15-minute-per-week operational overhead. Three primary channels.

### 3.1 Bitly Click Tracking (Primary Passive Signal)

**What to measure**: Click counts on the Domain 51 Gist short link (bit.ly/domain51-campaign-finance or the equivalent link created at send time — check DISTRIBUTION_GIST_URLS.md for the exact back-half). Record daily click totals for June 9–16 (7-day window) from the Bitly dashboard.

**Data access**: Log in to bitly.com/a/dashboard. Select the Domain 51 link. Open the "Clicks" tab. The daily breakdown shows clicks by date — record each day individually.

**Spike detection**: A single-day spike of 5+ clicks on the Domain 51 link is a flag event. Cross-reference the spike date against the Wave 1 send schedule:
- Spike June 9–11: Corresponds to CLC / Issue One opens (Wave 1, national contacts)
- Spike June 11–13: Corresponds to California contacts opens (Wave 2)
- Spike after June 13 with no corresponding send: Organic amplification signal — a contact forwarded the link. Flag as network multiplier event.

**Velocity pattern**: Compare click velocity in Days 1–3 versus Days 4–7. A front-loaded pattern (most clicks in Days 1–3, decay in Days 4–7) indicates the initial read-through happened but forwarding has not occurred. A flat or back-loaded pattern (sustained or rising clicks in Days 4–7) indicates the link is being shared within organizations or forwarded to colleagues.

**Weekly time required**: 3 minutes. Log in, record daily totals, calculate 7-day sum, note spike events.

**Reply rate tracking**: Check Gmail label structure for replies from Wave 1 contacts. The relevant contacts are:

**Reply scoring**: Use the 5-level scoring system from DOMAIN_51_JUNE_16_CHECKPOINT_METRICS_TEMPLATE.md:
- Score 1: OOO only (confirms delivery; no substantive signal)
- Score 2: Form acknowledgment ("Thanks, we'll review" — confirms receipt, weak engagement)
- Score 3: Substantive engagement (domain-specific question, request for more info, reference to current work)
- Score 4: Forward or network multiplier (names a colleague, forwards to a coalition partner, references sharing with a team)
- Score 5: Explicit adoption signal ("We will cite/use/integrate this in [specific project]")

For Phase 2 decision purposes, only Score 3+ replies count toward the engagement threshold. Score 1–2 replies confirm delivery but do not constitute engagement signals for Phase 2 activation.

**Content keyword monitoring**: When reviewing replies, note any specific content references. The highest-value keywords to watch for: "FEC enforcement," "DISCLOSE Act markup," "AI PAC," "Fairshake," "Citizens United," "Fair Elections Act," "SB-42," "ballot campaign," "Californians for Fair Elections." These confirm the contact engaged with specific substantive content, not just the framework overview.

**Weekly time required**: 5 minutes.

## 4. Day 7 Checkpoint: Decision Gate Thresholds

The Day 7 checkpoint runs June 16–18 (7 days after June 9 Wave 1 send). Execute WAVE_1_DATA_COLLECTION_TEMPLATE.md first (15 minutes), then apply these thresholds.

### Composite Signal Score (from DOMAIN_51_JUNE_16_CHECKPOINT_METRICS_TEMPLATE.md Section 6)

The composite score combines four weighted metrics (scale 0–10):

| Metric | Weight | Strong Value | Moderate Value | Weak Value |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------------|------------|
| Email open rate (%) | ×2 | ≥75% | 50–74% | <50% |
| Bitly clicks / expected (5) | ×2 | ≥5 clicks | 3–4 clicks | 1–2 clicks |
| Reply rate Score 3+ (%) | ×2 | ≥50% | 25–49% | <25% |
| Adoption signals (count) | ×1 | ≥2 | 1 | 0 |

**STRONG**: Composite score 8–10. At least 2 of 4 criteria in strong range. Confirmed engagement from at least 1 Tier A contact (CLC or Issue One) at Score 3+.

**MODERATE**: Composite score 5–7. At least 2 of 4 criteria in moderate or better range. At least 1 reply from any Wave 1 contact at Score 2+. Bitly clicks confirm delivery to multiple contacts.

**WEAK**: Composite score 3–4. One or more metrics below minimum threshold. Zero Score 3+ replies at Day 7 with confirmed delivery. Requires root-cause diagnosis before Phase 2 activation.

**FAILURE**: Composite score below 3. Zero clicks and zero replies with confirmed delivery. Requires immediate contingency protocol — delivery diagnosis first, content diagnosis second.

---

### Per-Organization-Type Day 7 Status

Beyond the composite score, assign a status to each organization type:

**Type A (CLC, Issue One)**: PASS if at least 1 reply at any score, or Bitly click spike correlated to June 9–10 send. MONITOR if zero reply and zero spike but no bounce detected. FAIL if hard bounce on either contact.

**Type B (California contacts)**: PASS if at least 1 click on Bitly link during June 11–13 send window. MONITOR if no click signal yet (California contacts have a 14-day effective window — Day 7 silence from California is within norms). FAIL only if hard bounce from all three California contacts.

**Override conditions**: Any Score 5 signal (explicit adoption statement) from any contact at any point in the monitoring window immediately classifies Wave 1 as STRONG regardless of other metrics. Document in CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input" and activate STRONG Phase 2 path same day.

## 5. Day 30 and Day 60 Checkpoints

### Day 30 Checkpoint: July 9

**Date context**: July 9 is 8 days after the July 1 California Fair Elections Act hard deadline. By this point, ballot campaign organizations will have either used Domain 51 materials in their campaign infrastructure or their window for doing so will have closed for the ballot campaign framing (though litigation and advocacy framing remains open).

**Strong threshold (triggers full Phase 2 activation)**:
- Overall reply rate from Wave 1 contacts: at or above 50% (3 of 5 or more have replied at any score)
- At least 2 of 5 contacts have replied at Score 3+
- At least 1 cross-organizational reference detected (a Wave 1 contact naming another organization, or an unsolicited email from a non-Wave-1 contact)
- At least 1 confirmed adoption signal

**Moderate threshold (triggers selective Phase 2 activation)**:
- Overall reply rate: 30–49%
- At least 1 contact at Score 3+
- No confirmed adoption signals but engagement trajectory rising (Bitly click velocity holding in Week 3–4)

**Weak threshold (triggers contingency review)**:
- Overall reply rate below 20%
- Zero Score 3+ replies
- No cross-organizational references
- Bitly click velocity at zero in Weeks 3–4 (no organic engagement remaining)

**Day 30 decision output**: Routes directly to WAVE_1_PHASE_2_SEQUENCING_DECISION_MATRIX.md Section 2 for 30-day-signal-based Phase 2 decisions.

---

### Day 60 Checkpoint: August 8

**Target**: At least 2 organizations have integrated Domain 51 into operational practice. Evidence methods: direct contact log review, web search for domain vocabulary in published documents, PACER search for any FEC filings citing the dark money architecture analysis, partner survey (3-question maximum, delivered August 6–8 to Wave 1 contacts who replied at Score 2+).

**Movement-scale threshold**: The "100 people exposed" threshold from the Phase 1 framework applies here via the organizational amplification multiplier. A single CLC publication citing Domain 51 analysis reaches an estimated 1,000–5,000 readers (CLC publication readership). A Common Cause CA ballot campaign communication incorporating Domain 51 vocabulary reaches the full campaign coalition membership. One confirmed organizational use event at Day 60 likely exceeds the 100-person threshold by an order of magnitude — track the organization type and estimated reach, not just the count.

## 6. Contingency Activation

### STRONG Outcome (Composite ≥8)

Within 24 hours of the Day 7 STRONG determination:
1. Update CHECKIN.md: "Wave 1 Day 7: STRONG (score ___/10). Phase 2 parallel batch authorized."
2. Proceed to WAVE_1_PHASE_2_SEQUENCING_DECISION_MATRIX.md Section 1 (STRONG path — Domain 48 + 57 parallel activation)
3. Use any confirmed Score 3+ replies as social proof in Domain 48 and 57 outreach: "This analysis has been engaged by [organization type], confirming practitioner relevance to the campaign finance / criminal justice funding pipeline"
4. Accelerate Domain 48 Gist creation to June 16–20 if it has not already begun

### MODERATE Outcome (Composite 5–7)

Within 24 hours of the Day 7 MODERATE determination:
1. Update CHECKIN.md: "Wave 1 Day 7: MODERATE (score ___/10). Phase 2 sequential activation."
2. Proceed to WAVE_1_PHASE_2_SEQUENCING_DECISION_MATRIX.md Section 2 (MODERATE path — Domain 48 first, Domain 57 contingent)
3. Continue Wave 1 monitoring through Day 30. If trajectory improves to STRONG by Day 14, upgrade Phase 2 path at that point
4. Send optional one-sentence follow-up to Common Cause CA only (highest-leverage California contact) on June 25 if no reply received: "Following up on the research I shared June 11 — the July 1 messaging window is approaching and I wanted to make sure it reached the right person."

### WEAK Outcome (Composite 3–4)

Within 24 hours of the Day 7 WEAK determination:
1. Run infrastructure diagnostic: email bounce check, Gist URL verification, Bitly link test
2. If infrastructure is clear (delivery confirmed, Gist accessible): content/framing diagnostic. Review Wave 1 email templates against contacts' current public work — is the framing mismatch with what they are actively engaged in right now?
3. Do not halt Domain 48 preparation — its July 10–20 send window is independent of Domain 51 Day 7 signal
4. Domain 57 (fixed August 10 send) is not affected by Domain 51 WEAK signal
5. Escalate to CHECKIN.md "Needs Your Input" with contingency options per WAVE_1_PHASE_2_SEQUENCING_DECISION_MATRIX.md Section 3

### Failure Recovery

If the composite score is below 3 with confirmed delivery:
1. Stakeholder substitution: Replace 20–30% of Phase 2 initial contacts with the next tier down — state campaign finance directors rather than national directors, regional ACLU chapters rather than national offices
2. Framing revision: Shift from framework overview to single operational hook — the FEC enforcement shutdown angle for litigation organizations, the AI PAC proliferation angle for media and think tank contacts, the ballot campaign urgency angle for California advocacy organizations
3. Channel shift: If direct email is not producing engagement, submit a shortened version of Domain 51's FEC analysis to Just Security or Lawfare, or deposit it in the proceedings of a relevant conference (APSA, law school symposium on campaign finance reform)
