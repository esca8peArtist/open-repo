## Section 8: Policy Window Pre-Emption Rules

A major court decision, legislative action, or executive action creates an immediate distribution window that overrides the sequenced timeline. Apply these rules regardless of current synthesis classification:

**Trump v. Barbara ruling (any date it issues)**:
- Override: D58 distributes within 5 days of ruling, regardless of current Phase 2 position.
- Do not delay D58 because D57 or D59 research is underway.
- The ruling window is time-bounded: 5–10 days of peak coverage and organizational urgency. Outside that window, the document is still valuable but the urgency hook dissipates.

**SCOTUS ruling on Schedule F extension (if issued before September)**:
- Override: D56 distribution gets an update with the ruling context added to the cover page.
- Do not delay D56 distribution — add a bolded ruling alert at the top of the Gist and send immediately.

**OBBBA amendment or modification (any legislative action)**:
- Override: Update D39 and D59 Gist covers to reflect the amendment. Distribute immediately if the amendment tightens or loosens Medicaid/SNAP/housing provisions in ways the current analysis predicts.

**Pre-emption rule**: When a policy window pre-empts the sequenced timeline, flag in CHECKIN.md and adjust the subsequent domain sequence by one week. Do not abandon research in progress — add the urgent distribution, then return to the sequence.
