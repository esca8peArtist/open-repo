## Per-Contact Ask Customization Rules

1. **Never send a generic ask.** Every email should close with a question that (a) the contact can actually answer based on their specific expertise, and (b) provides useful feedback on whether the analysis is accurate and applicable. Generic asks ("let me know what you think") get no response.

2. **Match ask to domain section, not domain title.** "I'd be interested in your reaction to Domain 57" is not a specific ask. "Does the characterization of the sole organ doctrine in Section 2 accurately describe the current state of the political question doctrine's application to treaty exit?" is a specific ask.

3. **Social proof ask calibration**: Under STRONG, the ask can include a coordination offer ("can we explore how this analysis might be adapted for your advocacy materials?"). Under MODERATE, the ask is narrower ("does this add anything useful to your current work?"). Under WEAK, the ask is purely analytical ("is this accurate?").

4. **Research-in-progress offer**: Available only under STRONG (both D57 and D59 outlines), MODERATE (D57 outline only, if available), or not at all under WEAK. Do not offer research access you cannot deliver.

5. **Deadline anchors**: Always note the relevant policy window deadline in the ask. D56: H.R. 492 June 1–30 window. D57: UNGA 81 September 22–28. D58: ruling timing. D39: HHS June 1 rule. D59: November 3 midterms. The deadline makes the ask time-sensitive rather than speculative.
