# Decision Tree Templates
## Democratic Renewal Proposal — Phase 1 Outreach Navigation

**Created**: May 7, 2026 (Session 870)
**Status**: Production-ready
**Purpose**: Actionable flowcharts for Phase 1 outreach teams. Determines when to continue engagement, when to escalate, when to pivot domains, and when to move on.

---

## How to Use These Trees

Each tree covers a specific decision point in the outreach lifecycle. Start with Tree 1 for every new contact. Move to Trees 2–4 as conversations develop. Trees are designed for email/Slack context — each decision node should take under 30 seconds to evaluate.

**Critical rule**: Do not run more than two decision trees simultaneously on one contact. Pick the most relevant tree for the current stage.

## TREE 4: PATTERN ANALYSIS AND AUDIENCE SCREENING
*Use when: Standard rebuttals are not moving the conversation; need to diagnose what's actually happening*

```
WHAT OBJECTION PATTERN IS PRESENT?
        |
        +── Pattern A: "IMPOSSIBLE" + "COSTS TOO MUCH" (two objections together)
        |       Diagnosis: Elite capture concern — speaker understands reforms disrupt
        |                  existing power arrangements; looking for exits
        |       Response: Shift from rebuttal to stakeholder mapping
        |                 Ask: "What reform in this space would your org prioritize?"
        |                 The answer reveals alignment opportunities or non-target status
        |       Decision: If they name a meaningful reform → continue engagement on that domain
        |                 If they name only status-quo-preserving measures → screen out
        |
        +── Pattern B: "LEGAL BARRIERS" + "INCREMENTAL PREFERENCE"
        |       Diagnosis: Career/institutional risk of association
        |                  Common with law faculty, think tank researchers, government staff
        |                  who must maintain relationships with current administration
        |       Response: Frame as research/analysis, not endorsement
        |                 "We'd value your scholarly critique of the legal architecture"
        |                 Offer attribution-optional review of domain-specific analysis
        |       Decision: If they engage on research terms → continue at research level
        |                 If they decline even that → likely non-target; screen out
        |
        +── Pattern C: "INTERNATIONAL IMPLICATIONS" + "CURRENT STABILITY"
        |       Diagnosis: Organizational alignment with status quo
        |                  Often signals institution with existing relationships to
        |                  current power structures
        |       Response: Do not escalate persuasion resources
        |                 Ask: "Are there colleagues at your organization analyzing
        |                 democratic resilience questions independently?"
        |                 Identify internal allies before full deprioritization
        |       Decision: If internal allies identified → route engagement to them
        |                 If not → screen out; log org as low-priority in this cycle
        |
        +── Pattern D: "WORKS IN DENMARK, NOT HERE" + "TOO MANY MOVING PARTS"
        |       Diagnosis: US exceptionalism + insufficient confidence in mechanism
        |                  Speaker is persuadable but needs narrower on-ramp
        |       Response: Narrow to single domain most relevant to their work
        |                 "Let's focus specifically on Domain [X]. Does the evidence hold?"
        |                 Convert from system-level resistance to domain-level engagement
        |       Decision: If they engage on the single domain → build from there
        |                 If they resist even narrow engagement → likely genuinely unconvinced
        |
        +── Pattern E: SINGLE SHARP TECHNICAL QUESTION (no follow-up pressure)
        |       Diagnosis: Genuine intellectual due diligence — no hidden agenda
        |       Response: Answer directly and fully
        |                 This is the highest-value engagement pattern
        |       Decision: Always continue; offer domain deep-dive and escalation
        |
        +── Pattern F: REPEATED REFRAMING WITHOUT ENGAGING EVIDENCE
                Diagnosis: Not a persuasion target in this cycle
                Response: One final clear statement; do not argue further
                          "I've shared the evidence. Happy to revisit when [X condition]."
                Decision: Screen out; log for future cycle; redirect time to higher-value targets
```

## TREE 5: RESOURCE ALLOCATION
*Use when: Managing multiple active conversations simultaneously; need to prioritize investment*

```
ACTIVE CONTACT LIST — PRIORITIZATION TRIAGE
        |
        v
For each contact, evaluate:

TIER 1 (invest fully — multiple follow-ups, document preparation, escalation ready):
    ✓ Institutional decision-making authority (can endorse, testify, draft legislation)
    ✓ Pattern E (genuine technical engagement)
    ✓ Demonstrated domain alignment (their existing work overlaps proposal domain)
    ✓ Multiplier potential (their endorsement reaches 10+ secondary contacts)
    → Action: Assign primary outreach owner; weekly follow-up cadence; offer briefing

TIER 2 (invest moderately — 2 follow-ups, standard materials):
    ✓ Staff-level engagement (can brief up to decision-maker)
    ✓ Research-level engagement (faculty, analysts — can produce supporting work)
    ✓ Pattern D (persuadable, needs narrower on-ramp)
    → Action: Domain-specific deep-dive; offer to connect to domain expert

TIER 3 (invest minimally — one follow-up, then move on):
    ✓ Pattern B (career risk concern — engage only on research terms)
    ✓ Org type misalignment (their mission doesn't overlap enough domains)
    → Action: Send one domain-specific resource; note for future cycle

SCREEN OUT (do not invest further in this cycle):
    ✗ Pattern A + C (elite capture / status quo alignment)
    ✗ Pattern F (repeated reframing without evidence engagement)
    ✗ VII-b objection ("current stability is fine")
    → Action: Log contact; identify if internal allies exist; revisit in 6+ months
```
