---

### Level 1 — High Probability (>30%), Short Detection Window

**1A. FEC Stow/Woodson confirmed before June 9 (the known risk)**

- Probability: 25-35% (Senate calendar opened, nominations technically pending since February 11)
- Detection lead time: 24-48 hours (Senate floor schedule posted 2-3 days ahead; vote confirmation same-day via NOTUS or fec.gov)
- Response action: Execute Patch 6 from the June 6 dry-run report — update Section 2.1 framing from "FEC cannot act" to "FEC will enforce selectively with 3-2 Republican quorum." 15-minute document update. Distribution proceeds unchanged; framing adjustment is contained to one paragraph. The meta-argument (Citizens United architecture makes dark money disclosure enforcement structurally unenforceable) survives either scenario intact.
- Detection channel: Daily check of notus.org/money and senate.gov/floor between June 6-9.

**1B. FIFA World Cup opening dominates media coverage June 11-12 (new risk)**

- Probability: 85% — confirmed US co-host, games begin June 11 at Los Angeles and New York venues; opening ceremony expected June 10-11
- Detection lead time: Known now
- Impact on distribution: Wave 1 sends on June 11-12 land in inboxes during peak World Cup coverage. Policy org staff are not distracted by sports coverage in the same way as general public, but the overall media noise environment compresses the organic news-cycle tailwind that advocacy emails benefit from. Campaign finance reform is not competing favorably for inbox attention against World Cup-driven political coverage (trade implications, player access issues, etc.)
- Response action: This is one of two primary drivers for the timing recommendation in Section 4. Sending Wave 1 on June 10 (Tuesday), before World Cup saturation peaks, avoids the worst of the media environment competition. See Section 4 for timing analysis.

### Level 2 — Moderate Probability (10-30%), Medium Detection Window

**2A. Senate reconciliation floor action on OBBBA creates campaign finance news peg (new risk)**

- Probability: 20-30% (Senate reconciliation floor debate expected mid-June; campaign finance provisions of the OBBBA have not received extensive attention but could surface)
- Detection lead time: 3-5 days (Senate schedule)
- Impact: If reconciliation debate produces a news cycle specifically about campaign finance provisions (e.g., DISCLOSE Act being stripped, or a dark money rider being added), this is either a tailwind (existing news peg supports Domain 51 relevance) or a risk (if the development materially contradicts Domain 51's framing — e.g., unexpected DISCLOSE Act passage)
- Response action: If a campaign finance-relevant reconciliation development occurs between June 6-9, assess whether it strengthens or complicates Domain 51 framing before sending. Strengthening: send immediately, add a one-sentence PS referencing the development. Complicating (e.g., partial DISCLOSE Act passage): 30-minute framing check against domain document before send.
- Detection channel: Congress.gov reconciliation tracker; NOTUS money coverage.

**2B. Montana I-194 signature gathering fails before June 19 county deadline (new risk)**

- Probability: 15-25% (initiative at signature-gathering stage as of June 6; qualification uncertain per available reporting; 30,121 valid signatures required by June 19)
- Detection lead time: 0-5 days (failure announcement could come any day between June 6-19; county deadline is June 19 — after Wave 1 sends)
- Impact on distribution: If I-194 fails to qualify before June 9, the domain document's Montana section references an active ballot initiative that will not be on the November ballot. Minor accuracy issue, not a material framing problem (the document's Montana argument is about the corporate spending prohibition reform effort, not about ballot qualification status). CLC and Issue One are national organizations unlikely to care about Montana qualification status.
- Response action: No hold on distribution. If I-194 fails before June 9, add a one-line parenthetical in the email body noting Montana's initiative is in qualification phase (not yet confirmed on ballot) rather than referencing it as a confirmed ballot measure. Domain document update is optional — the June 1 update section correctly identifies June 19 as the county deadline without asserting qualification.
- Detection channel: Daily Montanan; Ballotpedia Montana 2026 page.

**2C. Key contact org staff departure or restructuring (new risk)**

- Probability: 10-20% across the five Wave 1 and Wave 2 contacts over a 4-day window (low individually, non-trivial across five orgs)
- Specific risk: info@campaignlegal.org and info@issueone.org are generic intake addresses — staff turnover does not affect deliverability, but if the specific contact or team responsible for research intake has changed since the runbook contact list was last verified, the email reaches the organization but not the right person
- Response action: See Section 3 (coalition activation risk) for full dropout contingency. For this specific risk: use the generic intake addresses as primary and add the most recently confirmed named staff contact from each org as a CC if available. Do not delay distribution for contact re-verification if the email is addressed to info@ — the generic address is the intended channel.

### Level 3 — Low Probability (<10%), Longer Detection Window

**3A. California SB-42 legal challenge filed before November ballot (new risk)**

- Probability: 5-10% (opponents of public campaign financing have challenged similar measures in other states; California FPPC or industry-aligned groups could file an injunction attempt)
- Detection lead time: Would appear in California court filings and CA political press; 5-10 days minimum from filing to any injunction ruling
- Impact: If SB-42 faces a legal challenge before November, the distribution framing ("California Fair Elections Act is on the November ballot") remains technically accurate but gains additional urgency. This is a tailwind, not a headwind — a legal challenge to SB-42 increases the advocacy relevance of Domain 51.
- Response action: No action required unless an injunction succeeds. A filed challenge: send email with added urgency language. An injunction: assess whether ballot status is materially affected before Wave 2 CA contacts send.

**3B. DISCLOSE Act unexpectedly passes Senate (low probability)**

- Probability: 3-5% (all 47 Democratic senators cosponsor; still requires 60 votes to break filibuster; no Republican support identified)
- Detection lead time: Real-time (floor vote would be known same day)
- Impact: If the DISCLOSE Act somehow passes, Domain 51's "Congress is blocked from acting" framing requires substantial revision. This is the highest-consequence low-probability scenario.
- Response action: Postpone distribution for 48 hours. Reframe domain document to focus on implementation challenges, enforcement adequacy, and state-level reform as the remaining gaps. The Citizens United architecture analysis remains relevant even with DISCLOSE Act disclosure requirements — dark money structure remains, only disclosure rules change.

**3C. New SCOTUS campaign finance ruling issued before June 9 (new risk)**

- Probability: 3-7% (SCOTUS end-of-term period; campaign finance cases can surprise)
- Detection lead time: Same-day (SCOTUS issues opinions Thursdays and Fridays in June, occasionally Mondays)
- Impact: Depends entirely on ruling direction. A ruling expanding Citizens United logic: increases urgency, is a distribution tailwind. A ruling narrowing dark money (unlikely but possible): requires framing check.
- Detection channel: SCOTUSblog.com opinion tracker — check June 7-9 before sending.

## Section 4: June 9 Wave 1 Timing Analysis

The current scheduled send time of June 9 09:00 UTC translates to:

- 05:00 AM Eastern (New York / DC — CLC and Issue One are DC-based)
- 02:00 AM Pacific
- 10:00 AM UK / 11:00 AM Central Europe

No policy organization reads research submissions at 5:00 AM Eastern. The email arrives in the inbox during the night and competes with overnight email accumulation when staff arrive at 09:00 AM Eastern / 15:00 UTC. This is not catastrophically wrong — the email will be present in the inbox — but it is not the highest-probability open-rate window.

**The additional timing constraint**: The FIFA World Cup co-hosted by the US opens June 11 (games begin at US venues from June 11 through July 19). Opening coverage will be intense from June 10-12. Policy staff are not sports-distracted in the same way as general audiences, but the broader media noise environment around June 11-12 compresses organic policy email traction. Sending Wave 1 before World Cup saturation begins has a measurable inbox positioning advantage.

---

### Alternative Timing Window A: Tuesday June 10, 14:00 UTC (10:00 AM ET / 07:00 AM PT)

**Pros:**
- 10:00 AM Eastern is the confirmed peak open-rate window for nonprofit and advocacy email recipients (Tuesday 9-11 AM ET per 2026 email benchmarks)
- Staff at CLC and Issue One have cleared Monday's accumulated email and are in active decision-making mode
- Sends before World Cup opening media saturation (games begin June 11)
- Tuesday is the single best-performing day for nonprofit policy emails — confirmed across multiple 2026 benchmarks
- 14:00 UTC gives a user in US timezones a 09:00 AM ET morning send window with normal working-day lead time on June 10

**Cons:**
- Requires the user to not send on June 9 as currently scheduled — a one-day shift
- Issue One and CLC staff may be in planning meetings on Tuesday morning (common for policy org rhythms)
- June 10 is one day later, compressing the buffer before the June 15 checkpoint assessment

**Recommended weight: STRONG — this is the highest-confidence timing window.**

---

### Alternative Timing Window B: Wednesday June 11, 15:00 UTC (11:00 AM ET / 08:00 AM PT)

**Pros:**
- 11:00 AM ET is within the proven high-open-rate window (9 AM-noon)
- Wednesday is a strong second to Tuesday for policy organization email
- Midweek positioning means staff have the rest of the week to respond before the weekend

**Cons:**
- June 11 is the FIFA World Cup opening day in the US (games at Los Angeles and New York venues); media coverage begins that morning
- World Cup opens with significant US political-media overlap (trade, immigration, venue politics), increasing inbox competition from news digests
- One more day of buffer lost before June 15 checkpoint

**Recommended weight: ACCEPTABLE fallback if June 10 is not feasible. Not preferred over Window A.**

---

### Current Scheduled Window: June 9, 09:00 UTC (05:00 AM ET) — Assessment

This timing should not be used. A 5:00 AM Eastern arrival delivers the email before any policy staff begins their workday, placing it in the overnight accumulation pile rather than in the morning decision window. The email will be read eventually, but inbox position at 5:00 AM ET is among the least favorable possible. A one-day shift to June 10 14:00 UTC (10:00 AM ET) costs nothing and improves positioning materially.

**Recommended timing**: June 10, 14:00 UTC (10:00 AM Eastern). Send CLC and Issue One simultaneously in this window. Move Wave 2 CA contacts to June 12-13 (Thursday-Friday), which still closes the full distribution before the June 15 checkpoint.
