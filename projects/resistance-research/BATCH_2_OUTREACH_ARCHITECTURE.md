## Section 1: Architecture Overview

### 1.1 Batch 2 Strategic Logic

Batch 1 targeted five individuals at the intersection of legal theory, institutional litigation, and civic organizing: Just Security (Goodman), Brennan Center (Weiser), Harvard Kennedy School (Chenoweth), Protect Democracy (Bassin), and Democracy Docket (Elias). These contacts were chosen for their network centrality — each is a bridge node connecting legal, academic, and civic constituencies simultaneously. Batch 1 validated whether the framework resonates at the top of the institutional hierarchy.

Batch 2 targets the next tier of institutional multipliers: organizations that do not merely receive and respond to legal-democratic analysis but operationalize it — state AGs who file it as briefs, law school clinics that use it as teaching material, environmental law organizations that need the democratic-design frame to contextualize ESA litigation in structural terms, and indigenous rights organizations for whom tribal sovereignty is the exact intersection of Domain 58's subject matter.

Batch 2 is a lateral expansion, not a vertical escalation. The goal is not to find bigger names but to find organizations whose operational workflows create natural embedding points for Phase 1 research:

- State AG offices are actively producing legal briefs on democratic structure in 2026; they need constitutional-design arguments, not just case law
- Law school democracy clinics train the next generation of election lawyers; embedding the research in clinic curricula has a 5-10 year multiplier effect
- Environmental law organizations are fighting ESA rollbacks that use exactly the separation-of-powers and administrative-state arguments the Phase 1 research documents
- Tribal organizations are directly implicated by Domain 58 (Trump v. Barbara birthright citizenship) and by the Callais cascade — they have the highest operational urgency of any Batch 2 tier

## Section 2: Contact Tier Prioritization — 50 Organizations

Organizations are scored on three dimensions:

**Movement leverage (ML, 1-5)**: How much does this organization amplify the Phase 1 message? High score = large constituency, media presence, or structural position that turns engagement into reach.

**Reciprocal interest (RI, 1-5)**: How strong is the alignment between this org's current work and the Phase 1 research? High score = the org is actively working on the exact legal and political terrain the research covers.

**Domain alignment (DA, 1-5)**: How directly does this org's work connect to Phase 2 domain targets (D56: civil service, D57: multilateral withdrawal, D58: tribal sovereignty, D59: economic precarity)?

**Priority Score = ML + RI + DA (max 15)**. Orgs are ranked within each tier by priority score.

Measurement: Track in a column-extended version of BATCH_1_CONTACT_LOG.md. Add columns: Tier (T1/T2/T3/T4), Priority Score, Response Score (1-5, same scale as Batch 1), Response Date, Notes.

- Tier 1 (AG Offices): Target 20%. AG offices have institutional gatekeeping; 2 of 10 responding substantively is a strong outcome.
- Tier 2 (Law School Centers): Target 35%. Center directors are the decision-makers and have scholarly incentive to engage with research; 4 of 12 responding is achievable.

Target: 10% of respondents (2-3 organizations) take a concrete partnership step by June 15.

**Metric 3: Tier-to-Phase-2-Domain Alignment (operational)**
