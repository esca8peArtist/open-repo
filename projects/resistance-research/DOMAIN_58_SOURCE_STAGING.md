## Category 6: Litigation — Current Cases to Monitor

Five pending cases with material implications for Domain 58 content and timing.

**CASE 1: *Trump v. Barbara* (No. 25-365)**
- Court: United States Supreme Court
- Issue: Whether EO 14160 (birthright citizenship restriction) violates the Fourteenth Amendment; tribal-specific: Solicitor General's "not birthright citizens" argument
- Status as of May 15, 2026: Argued April 1, 2026; ruling expected late June/early July 2026
- Docket: https://www.scotusblog.com/cases/trump-v-barbara/
- Track at: https://sct.narf.org/ | https://www.theusconstitution.org/litigation/trump-v-barbara/
- Domain 58 impact: Directly affects Pathway 7 (franchise threat); ruling triggers rapid-response protocol in Section 5.1 of existing draft

**CASE 2: *Turtle Mountain Band of Chippewa Indians v. Howe* (No. 25-253)**
- Court: United States Supreme Court (on petition for certiorari from Eighth Circuit)
- Issue: Whether private plaintiffs can sue to enforce VRA Section 2 through Section 1983; Eighth Circuit eliminated private right of action May 2025
- Status as of May 15, 2026: SCOTUS granted stay of Eighth Circuit mandate July 2025; cert petition filed September 2025; redistributed for conference January 2026; cert status unknown as of May 2026
- Docket: https://sct.narf.org/caseindexes/turtle_mountain_v_howe.html | https://www.scotusblog.com/cases/case-files/turtle-mountain-band-of-chippewa-indians-v-howe/
- Track at: https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/court-cases/turtle-mountain-band-chippewa-indians-v-howe
- Domain 58 impact: If cert granted and Section 2 private right of action is eliminated nationally, all Native voter protection litigation based on VRA Section 2 becomes structurally unavailable — post-*Callais* and post-*Turtle Mountain* combination is catastrophic for tribal voting rights

**CASE 3: *Winnemucca Indian Colony v. United States* (No. 25-1170)**
- Court: United States Supreme Court (on petition for certiorari)
- Issue: Breach of federal trust duty to tribal government
- Status as of May 15, 2026: Petition filed April 8, 2026; response was due May 13, 2026
- Docket: https://sct.narf.org/
- Domain 58 impact: Cert grant would give SCOTUS another term to define the scope of federal trust obligations — a direct hook for IHS/BIA defunding litigation

**CASE 4: *Northern Cheyenne Tribe v. Montana* (NARF, 2025)**
- Court: Federal district court (Montana)
- Issue: Montana's failure to designate tribal enrollment offices as NVRA voter registration agencies
- Status as of May 15, 2026: Active litigation; outcome unknown
- Docket: https://narf.org/cases/mt-electoral-process-2025/
- Domain 58 impact: Most directly relevant test of NVRA tribal implementation post-*Callais*; a favorable ruling would provide the primary reform model for NAVRA advocacy

**CASE 5: Post-*Callais* Tribal VRA Challenges (Various)**
- Court: Multiple circuits
- Issue: States with documented patterns of Native American voting access barriers; post-*Callais* intent-based challenges
- Status as of May 15, 2026: Organizations including NARF, Campaign Legal Center, and Brennan Center are developing post-*Callais* doctrinal alternatives; no specific tribal cases yet in litigation
- Track at: https://www.democracydocket.com/ | https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/section-2-voting-rights-act-supreme-court
- Domain 58 impact: The alternative litigation strategies being developed NOW are the reform pathway content for Domain 58's Section 6
