**Specific program for Domain 50**: Lambda Legal's ballot measure and voting rights teams — check lambdalegal.org for current program contacts at execution date.
**Key leadership**: Jennifer C. Pizer, Chief Legal Officer (reported January 2025)
**Address**: 120 Wall Street, 19th Floor, New York, NY 10005
**Phone**: (212) 809-8585
**Website**: lambdalegal.org

**Why Priority 1**: Lambda Legal is the most litigation-intensive LGBTQ+ legal organization in the country. Their $285M fundraising completion means they are now better resourced to act on research than at any prior point. The Colorado ballot measures are in the state that produced Romer v. Evans — Lambda Legal's Romer challenge is the most constitutionally significant ballot measure case this cycle. The August 1 deadline is driven by Lambda Legal's campaign integration timeline.

**Specific ask**: The Romer anti-countermajoritarian argument (Section 1.1 and Section 1.2 Colorado analysis) for ballot measure litigation support; the Kalarchik state constitutional strict scrutiny model (Section 9.1) for state court alternative strategy; the trans voter suppression stack (Section 2) for election protection planning.

**Engagement note**: Lambda Legal's Skrmetti FAQ is cited in Domain 50 as a primary source. This is derivative and supportive work, not cold outreach.

Operates the "Voting While Trans" resource guide — cited in Domain 50 as a primary source for voter ID assistance information. The organization most directly focused on trans civic participation.

**Primary contact**: contact@transequality.org
**Also known as**: AT4E at at4e.org; some resources still at transequality.org
**Website**: at4e.org and transequality.org

**Why Priority 1**: AT4E is the primary organizational target for Domain 50's trans voter suppression analysis. Their "Voting While Trans" guide is the operational counterpart to the Williams Institute data — AT4E provides the practical voter assistance infrastructure that the 210,800 trans adults with ID mismatches depend on. Domain 50's three-layer suppression stack documentation directly supports AT4E's advocacy for election protection resources and SAVE Act opposition.

**Specific ask**: The Williams Institute 210,800 figure update for AT4E's communications materials; the EO 14168 document freeze analysis as evidence that the suppression population is growing; the SAVE Act compound barrier framing for Senate advocacy communications.

**Note on SAVE Act trigger**: If the SAVE Act passes the Senate before August 1, distribute to AT4E IMMEDIATELY using Template A with SAVE Act activated framing — do not wait for the August 1 deadline.
