# Domain 40 — Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Microtargeting
## Phase 2 Research Launch Brief

---

## 1. Domain Title and Unique Research Angle

**Title**: Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Manipulation: How Commercial Data Infrastructure Became Democratic Attack Infrastructure

**Unique angle (2-3 sentences)**: This domain does not argue that data privacy is bad — it documents that voter targeting precision now threatens election integrity in ways that have no federal regulatory response. The electoral manipulation threat in 2026 is not primarily from foreign interference: it is from the systematic integration of commercial surveillance infrastructure (data brokers with 1,600+ data points per US voter, AI generation tools with sub-$50 deepfake costs, geofencing platforms with sub-church-attendance targeting resolution) with political operations, in an environment where the FEC cannot pass binding rules, the FCC lacks jurisdiction over social media, and a December 2025 executive order is eliminating the only functioning accountability layer — 28 state-level AI disclosure laws. The same companies that comply with EU AI Act Article 50 synthetic content marking and EU Regulation 2024/900 political microtargeting prohibitions deploy unregulated manipulation infrastructure in US domestic political contexts.

---

## 2. Core Research Questions

- Who are the major data brokers now active in voter targeting, what data sources feed voter profiles, and what is the operational chain from consumer surveillance to voter behavioral model? Thomson Reuters CLEAR (six ICE contracts, same database as political targeting), LexisNexis Accurint (276 million US residents), L2 (220 million voter profiles), TargetSmart (171 million matched cell numbers) — how does the same infrastructure serve both immigration enforcement and campaign microtargeting?
- What data sources feed voter profiles at the level of detail that enables digital voter suppression? Grocery loyalty data, healthcare-adjacent behavioral signals, financial stress indicators from credit bureau data, geolocation from smartphone app data sold to aggregators, genetics (what is the 2025-2026 evidence on genetic data in political profiling?) — and which of these data categories are prohibited from use in EU political advertising under GDPR Article 9 and EU Regulation 2024/900 while remaining legally accessible for US political use?
- Can voter microtargeting be detected and audited? The January 2026 PNAS study (targeted digital voter suppression ads reduced turnout 1.86%, with non-white voters in minority-majority battleground counties receiving ads at 4x the rate of white voters in non-battleground counties) used a real-time ad exposure tracking app installed by 10,000+ participants — what methodologies exist for independent detection and auditing of voter suppression targeting campaigns, and what legal obligations currently require disclosure?
- What are the international patterns — GDPR enforcement against political data use, Australian scoping reviews on voter data commercialization, OSCE election monitoring standards for digital political advertising — and what do they suggest about the minimum regulatory floor that would address documented harm?

---

## 3. Why This Domain Matters for Democratic Resistance

Domain 40 provides the framework's first systematic analysis of commercial surveillance infrastructure as electoral attack infrastructure. Domain 43 (Epistemic Infrastructure) covers the epistemic harm from deepfakes — the liar's dividend, information environment degradation. Domain 37 (Federal Executive Interference) covers government-side election manipulation. Neither covers the commercial data broker industry as the supply chain that makes targeted voter manipulation possible at scale and at the targeting resolution now documented in peer-reviewed evidence.

The PNAS January 2026 finding changes the evidentiary foundation. Digital voter suppression is no longer theoretical. It has a measured turnout effect (1.86%), a documented racial targeting disparity (4x concentration on non-white voters in minority-majority battleground counties), and an estimated historical electoral impact (4.7 million prevented votes in the 2016 election, extrapolated from the study's exposure data). This is actionable under the Voting Rights Act's discriminatory effect framework. Election protection organizations — which are currently framing 2026 work primarily around voter ID laws, polling place access, and voter roll purges — do not yet have this finding in their operational briefings. Domain 40 makes it available.

The regulatory vacuum is the structural anchor. The FEC has lacked a policymaking quorum since May 2025 — it cannot pass binding guidance on AI-generated political content. The FCC's jurisdiction does not reach social media or digital advertising — the channels through which the NRSC's Talarico deepfake, the Collins-Ossoff deepfake, and the Spanberger deepfake all circulated. The 28-state disclosure patchwork is the only functioning accountability layer, and the December 2025 preemption EO is designed to eliminate it before any federal floor is established. The domain documents the accountability vacuum by design and identifies what structural regulation would actually require.
