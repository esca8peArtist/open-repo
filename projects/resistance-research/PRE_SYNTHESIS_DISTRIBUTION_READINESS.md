## Decision Logic: Why Domains 56 and 39 Are Independent

### Domain 56 — Civil Service Politicization

**Why it ships regardless of synthesis outcome:**

1. **Fixed legislative window**: H.R. 492 / S. 134 (Saving the Civil Service Act) are in committee during the June work period. The summer recess (July-August) pauses committee scheduling. Distribution before recess is the window to influence markup and co-sponsorships. This window is not affected by Phase 1 signal strength.

2. **Target audience is independent**: Partnership for Public Service, AFGE, NTEU, GAP, Volcker Alliance — these organizations' utility logic for Domain 56 is not contingent on Phase 1 momentum. They do not need to know that law school contacts in immigration law found the framework compelling. They need the democratic-design argument for the merit system, which exists independently.

3. **Litigation timeline**: PEER v. Trump (N.D. Cal.) motion to dismiss ruling is pending (heard May 12, 2026; expected Q3 2026). NTEU's D.D.C. facial challenge is active. Early distribution positions organizations to respond to rulings with pre-existing infrastructure.

4. **Multiple prior documents confirm this**: POST_SYNTHESIS_ANALYSIS_FRAMEWORK.md (line 109): "May 28: Domain 56 distribution. Domain 42 DEA participation notice hard deadline." PHASE_2_EXECUTION_ROADMAP.md (line 176): "Domain 56 distributes May 28." Phase 2 Wave 2 Research Activation Timeline: "Domain 56 Distribution Launch (May 28) — Why it is unconditional."

**Classification implication**: Under STRONG, MODERATE, WEAK, or TOO_EARLY, Domain 56 sends May 28. The only variable is whether social proof framing (from Phase 1 engagement) is available for the cover email. Under TOO_EARLY or WEAK, omit social proof references; use utility-only framing (the document's analytical value stands without Phase 1 endorsement context).

### Domain 39 — Healthcare Access as Democratic Infrastructure

**Why it ships regardless of synthesis outcome:**

Advocacy organizations must act before and at rule issuance to influence HHS's discretionary implementation choices. This deadline is fixed by external statute — it does not move based on Phase 1 engagement metrics.

2. **Target audience is independent**: Georgetown CCF, NHeLP, CBPP, KFF, Brennan Center, Black Mamas Matter Alliance, NDRN — these organizations' utility logic for Domain 39 is their mandate (healthcare access, voting rights, disability rights). Domain 39's democratic participation argument strengthens standing arguments and public interest framing for APA challenges — work that must start now.
