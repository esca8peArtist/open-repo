**Sample opening**: "The framework documents criminal justice civic exclusion as a structural democracy problem — not a reform vs. abolition argument, but an empirical analysis of how criminal justice contact removes people from the civic body. ### Template A-2 — Academic & Policy Organizations

**Lead with**: Empirical extension of existing work

**Sample opening**: "Domain 54 extends [specific researcher's] work on [specific empirical claim] into a democratic participation framing that I believe has actionable use for state AG restoration litigation. The specific extension: [one sentence]. I would welcome feedback on whether the empirical inference holds."

**Suitable for**: Sentencing Project, Vera Institute, Prison Policy Initiative, Brennan Center, academic contacts (Forman, Alexander, Muhammad), FAMM, PolicyLink, Impact Justice

**Sample opening**: "The civic exclusion architecture analysis in Domain 54 is designed to be usable as a brief companion for disenfranchisement restoration litigation and state-level automatic restoration advocacy. I would value your assessment of whether the legal theory as framed supports the specific arguments you are making in [active case or campaign]."

**Suitable for**: NAACP LDF, ACLU National Prison Project, Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights, Equal Justice Initiative

## Key Features for Domain 54 Distribution

**Research document strengths** (document ready as-is):
- Comprehensive coverage of five dimensions of civic exclusion (disenfranchisement, jury exclusion, employment bars, housing exclusion, LFOs)
- Racial disparity data: 1 in 22 Black Americans disenfranchised (3x rate for non-Black Americans)
- Scale: 4 million Americans currently disenfranchised; 20+ million excluded from jury service
- Feedback loop mechanism: communities most affected by disenfranchisement cannot vote on the policies that created it
- LFO evidence: 200,000+ Floridians disenfranchised under Amendment 4 debt condition; $13,607 average court debt
- Virginia development: January 2026 federal court ruling finding automatic disenfranchisement violates 1870 Readmission Act
- Abolitionist framing: "Civil death" — the loss of full democratic membership as system outcome, not incidental consequence

**Contact fit assessment** (why this distribution network):
- M4BL Policy Table: Direct alignment with their 2016 "Vision for Black Lives" platform language on criminal justice exclusion
- Sentencing Project: Authored the disenfranchisement research Domain 54 cites throughout
- Worth Rises: Specialized in LFO dimension — critical for Tier 1 validation
- Brennan Center: Existing Phase 1 contact with electoral justice program
- NAACP LDF: Active litigation on voting rights restoration (Domain 54 connects to their strategic cases)
- Vera Institute: Racial justice infrastructure focus; institutional research capacity
- Critical Resistance: Direct abolitionist mandate; their membership cares deeply about civic exclusion from incarceration
- Academic anchors: Forman (Yale), Alexander (Berkeley), Muhammad (Harvard) are the three most-cited scholars in the civic exclusion/mass incarceration space; their endorsement amplifies credibility across academia and policy
