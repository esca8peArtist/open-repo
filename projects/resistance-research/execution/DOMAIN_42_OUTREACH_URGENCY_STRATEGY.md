## Section 1: May 28 DEA Hearing Context

### What May 28 Is — and Is Not

May 28, 2026 is **not** the hearing date. It is the deadline for interested persons to file a written notice of desired participation in DEA Docket No. DEA-1362 — the formal administrative proceeding that will determine whether marijuana is rescheduled from Schedule I to Schedule III under the Controlled Substances Act. The hearing itself runs June 29 – July 15, 2026, at the DEA Hearing Facility, 700 Army Navy Drive, Arlington, VA 22202. Submission is to nprm@dea.gov (electronic) or 8701 Morrissette Drive, Springfield, VA 22152 (mail, postmark by May 20 to allow transit).

This distinction matters operationally. A participation notice is not a full comment — it is a declaration of intent to participate, which preserves the right to testify, submit written evidence, and cross-examine witnesses in June and July. The cost is near-zero: a one-to-two page document referencing DEA-1362 and stating the organization's grounds for participation. Filing a notice creates no obligation to appear. Not filing forecloses all formal participation rights.

### Why It Matters for Phase 1 Distribution

The May 28 window creates the first external deadline in the 35-domain framework that cannot move. The DEA hearing proceeds regardless of whether reform-aligned organizations participate. Domain 42 (Drug Policy, Regulatory Capture, and Democratic Legitimacy) provides the specific analytical frame — regulatory capture operating in the hearing's structure, the felony disenfranchisement feedback loop, and the 24-state democratic conflict — that will otherwise be absent from the formal record.

The 2024 predecessor proceedings (withdrawn before completion) received 160+ participation notices; DEA selected approximately 25 participants, disproportionately pharmaceutical industry and anti-reform advocates. NORML filed and was excluded. This participation-selection asymmetry is itself evidence of the regulatory capture Domain 42 documents. The Phase 1 distribution goal is to get Domain 42's framing into enough participation notices that exclusion of all of them becomes a documented pattern, not a quiet administrative decision.

### Comment Submission Window

- **Electronic participation notice**: Must be received at nprm@dea.gov by **May 28, 2026**
- **Mail participation notice**: Must be postmarked by **May 20, 2026** (allow postal transit)
- **Practical electronic deadline**: May 24–25 to allow for technical issues and institutional sign-off delays
- **Organizations likely to participate**: Pharmaceutical industry filers (Jazz Pharmaceuticals, Sorrento), anti-legalization coalitions (Smart Approaches to Marijuana), cannabis industry operators (National Cannabis Industry Association), select state AGs. Reform-aligned civil rights organizations are underrepresented in the anticipated participant pool — this is the gap Domain 42 outreach addresses.

## Section 3: Email Sequencing Timeline

All times below assume **May 15** as Day 0 (Phase 1 launch date). If launch occurs on May 14, shift all dates one day earlier. The May 21 hard stop is fixed regardless of launch date.

**Day 0 — May 15 (Phase 1 launch + Domain 42 first wave)**

Domain 42 launches as a **priority sub-batch concurrent with Phase 1 general distribution**, not sequentially. The May 28 deadline cannot wait for the general distribution sequence to reach drug policy contacts organically.

Send Priority 1 contacts in this order, spaced 30–45 minutes apart: DPA (9:00 AM ET) → NORML (9:45 AM ET) → ACLU Criminal Law Reform (10:30 AM ET) → Sentencing Project (11:15 AM ET) → LEAP (2:00 PM ET). Afternoon gap before LEAP allows morning responses from DPA and NORML to inform whether to add a social proof sentence to the LEAP send.

Template: D42-A for DPA, NORML, LEAP; D42-B for ACLU and Sentencing Project. See `domain-42-email-template.md`.

Key framing addition required in all May 15 sends: include the corrected deadline language verbatim — "The Federal Register deadline is May 28, but mail notices must be postmarked by May 20. Electronic filings to nprm@dea.gov should be received no later than May 24–25 to allow for institutional sign-off." Organizations that calendar only "May 28" and attempt a mail send on May 26 will miss the effective deadline.

For DPA and NORML specifically, add the resubmission note: "Participation notices from the 2024 proceedings (withdrawn) are void. A fresh notice referencing DEA-1362 is required."

**Day 1 — May 16 (follow-up trigger check)**

Review open/response tracking. If DPA or NORML has responded positively, add one sentence to all subsequent Day 3 sends: "Drug Policy Alliance and NORML have both reviewed this briefing and are considering DEA-1362 participation." Use only if factually accurate — do not characterize a receipt acknowledgment as a commitment.

If no Priority 1 response by end of May 16: send a single follow-up to DPA and NORML only (not ACLU or Sentencing Project — over-emailing those contacts risks relationship damage). Subject line: "Quick follow-up — May 20 mail deadline for DEA-1362 participation notice."

**Day 3 — May 18 (Priority 2 wave)**

Send to: NAACP LDF, MPP, SSDP, Americans for Safe Access, California AG, Colorado AG. Use template D42-B for NAACP LDF; D42-A for MPP, SSDP, ASA; D42-D for AG offices (compressed ~350-word version — AG staff have limited time for cold outreach).

AG-specific ask: "Is your office planning to file DEA-1362 comments or a participation notice? If so, would Domain 42's federalism analysis (Section 4) be useful as supplemental background?" This converts the ask from "initiate a new filing" to "add a framing argument to an existing one."

Michigan AG and Washington AG: send only if contact currency is confirmed before May 18. If not confirmed, drop — do not send to unverified AG offices.

**Day 7 — May 22 (final reminder wave)**

Send final reminder only to Priority 1 organizations that have not responded with a clear yes/no/maybe and to Priority 2 organizations that responded "maybe" on May 18. This is a one-sentence email: "The electronic filing window closes May 28 — we wanted to make sure you had the submission guide before the deadline." Attach `domain-42-may-28-dea-submission-guide.md` again.

**May 21 — Hard Stop for New Contacts**

After May 21, do not initiate contact with organizations that have not already received the Domain 42 briefing. Organizations reached after May 21 have fewer than 5 business days to route internally, obtain authorization, and file electronically — not a functional window for institutional filers. Resources after May 21 shift to response management and participation tracking, not new outreach.

## Section 4: Success Metrics

### Minimum Viable, Strong, and Stretch Outcomes

| Metric | Minimum | Strong | Stretch |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| Organizations requesting Domain 42 briefing materials (by May 21) | 2 | 4 | 7 |
| Organizations committing to DEA-1362 participation notice (by May 24) | 2 | 4 | 6 |
| Secondary contacts reached through Wave 1 network amplification | 5 | 15 | 30 |
| Participation notices filed by May 26 (2-day buffer before May 28) | 1 | 3 | 5 |

The minimum viable outcome (2 commitments) is DPA and NORML — they will file regardless of Domain 42 outreach. The minimum viable Domain 42-specific contribution is at least 1 counterfactual organization: an org that files containing Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing and would not have done so without the briefing. ACLU or Sentencing Project are the most likely counterfactual filers.

### Measuring Participation

Track through three channels, in parallel:

1. **DEA docket monitoring**: Check the DEA-1362 docket at regulations.gov periodically starting June 1. The DEA publishes a designated participants list approximately one week before the June 29 hearing start; this is the ground-truth record of who was accepted. Organizations that filed and were excluded are as important to document as those selected — exclusion patterns are evidence for Domain 42's regulatory capture argument.

2. **Organizational press releases**: DPA, NORML, ACLU, and MPP all publish press releases when they file significant DEA comments. Set a Google Alert for "DEA-1362" and "DEA rescheduling participation." A press release citing Domain 42 framing (democratic legitimacy, felony disenfranchisement feedback loop, regulatory capture) is the strongest attributable signal.

3. **Email confirmations**: Log all responses to outreach in the tracking spreadsheet (`execution/domain-42-success-tracking.md`). A "yes" response with a description of the participation angle is the clearest attribution signal.

### What "Participation" Means in This Context

There are three tiers, in ascending impact: (a) filing a participation notice — preserves rights, creates a docket record, costs near-zero; (b) filing a participation notice and submitting written testimony incorporating Domain 42 framing — creates a citable document in the formal administrative record; (c) being selected for hearing participation by DEA and testifying or cross-examining witnesses in person June 29 – July 15. Even tier (a) is a win: 15 participation notices containing democratic exclusion language create a hearing record that does not otherwise exist.

## Section 5: Contingency Planning

### If Phase 1 Launch Is Delayed Past May 20

If the path decision (A/A+37/B) is not made and Phase 1 general distribution cannot launch by May 20, Domain 42 proceeds as a **standalone sub-batch independent of the general path decision**. The structure for this is already documented in `domain-42-phase-1-outreach-prioritization.md` Section 4. The sub-batch has no dependency on Gist publication or the general distribution sequence.

Standalone minimum sequence for a May 20 launch: contact only DPA, NORML, and ACLU. Attach the Domain 42 markdown directly (do not wait for Gist URL). The electronic filing deadline of May 28 gives these three organizations 8 calendar days — tight but functional for organizations with DEA filing infrastructure. State AGs and NAACP LDF are non-viable at this compressed timeline; drop them entirely.

### If No Organizations Respond by May 21

Fallback sequence, in priority order:

1. **Direct DEA submission with Domain 42 as the submission**: File a participation notice under the sender's own name or a named project designation, attaching Domain 42 as the written evidence. This costs nothing and creates a docket record regardless of whether organizations co-file.

2. **Media outreach to accelerate coverage**: Contact reporters covering the DEA-1362 proceeding at STAT News, Marijuana Moment, and Politico's cannabis vertical. Press coverage of the "democratic exclusion" angle accelerates organizational awareness faster than direct outreach in a compressed timeline.

3. **Coalition signatory approach**: DPA runs coalition signatory mechanisms for DEA proceedings. If DPA is willing, ask whether Domain 42 can be included as background material for a coalition participation notice that smaller organizations can co-sign, reducing the institutional routing burden.

### If May 28 Deadline Is Extended or Hearing Is Rescheduled

Track through two channels: (a) the DEA's official docket page (dea.gov/marijuana-rescheduling-regulatory-actions) and (b) the Federal Register at federalregister.gov/documents/search?q=DEA-1362. Any extension or rescheduling will be published as a Federal Register notice. Set a daily Google Alert for "DEA-1362" through June 15. If the deadline extends, the compressed sequencing in this document relaxes — but do not plan around an extension that has not been confirmed.

## Section 6: Integration with Phase 1 Distribution Path Decision

### Path A — Immediate Phase 1 Launch (Recommended for May 28 Window)

Path A (immediate 35-domain distribution) enables the May 15–21 outreach window without modification. Domain 42 launches as a concurrent priority sub-batch on Day 0, using the email templates in `domain-42-email-template.md` alongside the general Batch 1 institutional outreach. The Gist URL for the Domain 42 document (once created in Block 1 of PHASE_1_EXECUTION_READINESS.md) serves as the distribution anchor for all outreach.

Path A is strongly preferred. The 3–4.5 hour execution time (per PHASE_1_EXECUTION_READINESS.md) means a May 14 path decision enables a May 15 launch and a full 6-day Priority 1 window before the May 21 hard stop.

### Path A+37 — Hybrid Phase 1 with 37-Domain Expansion (Equally Viable)

Path A+37 adds the Domain 37 election-interference focus to the Phase 1 send but does not delay Domain 42 outreach. The Domain 42 sub-batch is structurally independent of the Domain 37 expansion. An A+37 decision on May 14 yields the same Domain 42 outreach timeline as Path A. The only addition is creating a second Gist for domain-37-federal-executive-interference-2026-midterms.md in Block 1 of the execution checklist, which adds approximately 10 minutes. Path A+37 is equally viable for the May 28 window.

### Path B — Deferred Phase 1 (Incompatible with May 28 Window)

Path B (staged distribution with feedback integration before broader send) introduces a 4-week feedback collection window after Batch 1, which structurally prevents reaching Priority 2 organizations before the May 21 hard stop. Under Path B, the Domain 42 sub-batch must still launch independently on Day 0 — Path B's staged structure cannot apply to the DEA-deadline-constrained sub-batch without forfeiting the May 28 opportunity entirely.

If Path B is selected, the recommendation is to carve Domain 42 outreach out of the Path B staged framework explicitly: launch the 5-organization Priority 1 wave on May 15 regardless, then revert to the Path B staged structure for all other domains. This hybrid is operationally coherent — the Domain 42 sub-batch has no dependencies on the broader distribution sequence.

### Recommendation

Path A or Path A+37 decision by May 14 is strongly preferred. The May 28 participation notice deadline is the highest-leverage initial Phase 1 advocacy opportunity in the framework — it creates entries in a formal federal administrative record that will inform the June 29 – July 15 hearing, which in turn produces a rulemaking outcome with national legal effect. This opportunity does not recur. A Path B decision that defers all outreach until after the feedback window forfeits this window with no equivalent substitute.

If the path decision cannot be made by May 14, authorize the Domain 42 sub-batch as a standalone send independent of the path decision. The sub-batch requires no path authorization — it is a 5-organization priority send using already-verified contacts, already-written templates, and an already-created submission guide. The only execution step is sending five emails.
