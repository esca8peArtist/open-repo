## Emergency Contact Procedures — Key Organizations Not Committed by May 20

If any of the following four organizations (the highest-impact targets) has not reached COM or REQ status by **May 20** (8 days before the deadline), execute these emergency procedures.

### Emergency Protocol: Drug Policy Alliance Not Committed by May 20

**DPA is the highest-priority anchor contact.** If DPA has not responded by May 20, something went wrong (email routing failure, incorrect contact, or explicit non-engagement).

**Step 1**: Check DPA's organizational calendar and social media (Twitter/X at @DrugPolicyOrg). If DPA is publicly commenting on DEA-1362, they are already engaged with the hearing — your outreach may not have reached the right person.

**Step 2**: Try secondary contact. The contact list documents `info@drugpolicy.org` as secondary to `press@drugpolicy.org`. If the primary has not responded, try the secondary with a shorter 3-sentence version: "[Name], I sent a briefing on the DEA-1362 hearing democratic exclusion analysis to your communications team on May 8. The deadline is May 28. Can I confirm this reached the right person?"

**Step 3**: Check LinkedIn for DPA federal policy director. Many advocacy organizations have named policy directors reachable through LinkedIn direct message if email routing fails. This is an unorthodox path but appropriate for a critical anchor contact.

**Step 4**: Accept the outcome. If DPA does not respond by May 20, they are either already planning their own participation (common — DPA files in all DEA proceedings) or have made a capacity decision. The NORML + ACLU + Sentencing Project combination can achieve minimum viable success without DPA.

---

### Emergency Protocol: ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project Not Committed by May 20

ACLU's participation is not a foregone conclusion — they do not routinely file in DEA drug scheduling proceedings. An ACLU non-response by May 20 is more likely to mean routing failure than a decision not to file.

**Step 1**: Try the alternate direct-outreach path. ACLU has a public media contact form at aclu.org/en/contact/contact-media. Send a 3-sentence version to the media team: "I've sent a briefing on Domain 42's democratic exclusion analysis of DEA-1362 to the Criminal Law Reform Project. The deadline is May 28. Can you confirm routing to the right team?"

**Step 2**: Check whether the NAACP LDF or Sentencing Project has communicated with ACLU about the hearing. If either organization is in REQ or COM status, ask whether they can introduce the briefing to their ACLU contacts — the civil rights organizational network is dense, and an introduction from Sentencing Project carries more weight than a cold outreach.

**Step 3**: Lower the specific ask. Rather than asking ACLU to file a participation notice, ask whether they would be willing to cite Domain 42 Section 3 in any existing disenfranchisement publications or advocacy materials. This is a much lower bar and may be achievable even without full DEA proceeding engagement.

---

### Emergency Protocol: NAACP Legal Defense Fund Not Committed by May 20

LDF's internal approval process is the longest of any Category B contact. If LDF has not responded by May 20, they likely received the briefing but the internal process has not completed.

**Step 1**: Send a very short follow-up (2 sentences) on May 20 to the webform and any staff email: "The DEA-1362 participation notice deadline is May 28. If the LDF has capacity to file even a short written participation notice focused on the disenfranchisement feedback loop, the filing takes under 10 minutes at nprm@dea.gov."

**Step 2**: Accept that LDF's internal process may not complete in time. LDF is a Tier 2 contact for a reason — their value is high but their routing is slow. If LDF does not file by May 28, their value shifts to the post-deadline phase: Domain 42 remains relevant to LDF's voting rights and disenfranchisement work, and the post-May 28 pivot can cultivate LDF as a longer-term distribution partner.

---

### Emergency Protocol: No Drug Policy Organization at COM Status by May 20

If neither DPA nor NORML has reached COM status by May 20, execute the minimum viable success protocol:

1. **Confirm NORML is engaging with the hearing at all**: Check norml.org/news and @NORML on Twitter. NORML is virtually certain to file in DEA-1362 — if their public statements indicate engagement, their non-response to this outreach is a routing failure, not a substantive non-engagement. Note in tracking log that NORML is likely filing independently.

2. **Re-prioritize to BRIEF outcome**: If neither DPA nor NORML reaches COM through this outreach, the outreach may still have succeeded in getting Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing into their filing rationale — even if they did not explicitly confirm this. The domain is CC 4.0; if they received it and found it useful, they can use it without attribution notification.

3. **Evaluate whether LEAP is the fallback anchor**: Law Enforcement Action Partnership (A-4) is sent May 8 (Tier 2 send order, 11:00 AM). LEAP's law enforcement voice is unusually credible in DEA proceedings. If LEAP reaches COM status — a long shot given their organizational capacity — that is a higher-impact filing than a second drug policy advocacy organization.
