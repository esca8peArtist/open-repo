# Phase 2 Outcome-Based Launch Roadmap and Messaging Strategy

*Prepared May 17, 2026. Production-ready for activation May 18 10:00 UTC.*

This document answers a single operational question: **Given what Wave 1 tells you about constituency engagement on May 18–20, what does Phase 2 look like — which domains, in what order, with what messaging, and on what timeline?**

The document is organized around three outcome scenarios (Strong, Moderate, Weak) and is written to be read on May 20–21 when the first Wave 1 signals are in. Read Section 1 to score your outcome. Then read only the sections for that outcome. Do not read all three paths — it will create decision paralysis.

## Section 1: Outcome Definitions by Constituency — Scoring Wave 1

### What "Engagement Rate" Means in This Framework

The Wave 1 engagement scoring system (documented in `WAVE_1_ENGAGEMENT_SCORING_CALCULATOR.csv`) uses a weighted formula:

```
engagement_score = (reply_score × 0.6) + (gist_clicks × 0.2) + (opened_24h × 0.2)
```

Reply types are scored 0–5: Integration signal (5), Implementation question (4), Referral (4), Clarification question (3), Critique (3), Acknowledgment only (1), Decline or no reply (0).

**Constituency engagement rate** for Phase 2 decision-making is defined as the **reply rate** (contacts with substantive replies / total contacts sent) read against sector-specific baselines. The thresholds below are calibrated against the baselines in `phase-1-baseline-metrics.md` (Section 2.2: policy-to-policymaker outreach 30–40% typical; academic-to-academic 25–35%; legal-to-legal 40–50%) and the engagement scoring architecture.

### Law Schools and Academic Constituencies

Wave 1 law school contacts: Ryan Goodman (Just Security / NYU Law), Erica Chenoweth (Harvard Kennedy School).

**Strong signal (first-response rate >35% within 7 days)**
A response from either Goodman or Chenoweth within 7 days of send, where the response goes beyond acknowledgment — specifically: the contact asks a question about a domain, references their own current work in relation to the research, requests a one-pager or expanded domain brief, or indicates they will share with a colleague or course. Goodman's response window from the contingency playbook is 3–5 days; Chenoweth's is 5–10 days. A Goodman reply by Day 5 with substantive content = Strong. A Chenoweth reply by Day 7 with methodological engagement = Strong. An engagement score of 3+ from either contact = Strong.

**Moderate signal (first-response rate 20–35% within 7 days)**
A reply arrives but is limited to acknowledgment ("thanks, I'll read this") or very brief engagement without follow-up content or an ask. Or: one contact responds with depth but the other is silent at Day 7. Engagement score 1–2 from both contacts combined = Moderate.

**Weak signal (first-response rate <20% within 7 days, or no response)**
No substantive reply from either law school contact by Day 7. Goodman silence at Day 7 is the most diagnostic: Just Security publishes daily and Goodman's editorial pace makes a 7-day non-response a reliable non-interest signal, not a timing artifact. Chenoweth silence by Day 10 confirms weak law school engagement. Zero engagement scores = Weak.

**What law school engagement unlocks**: Academic contacts are the framework's citation pipeline — the pathway to law review citations, course syllabi integration, and congressional testimony. Strong law school engagement activates the academic citation track (Google Scholar monitoring, SSRN working paper targets) and justifies heavy investment in Domain 57's constitutional law sections (the most academically demanding research in Phase 2). Weak law school engagement means the academic distribution channel is not working and Phase 2 energy should redirect toward practitioner channels (election protection orgs, litigation support networks).

A Goodman citation at Just Security is worth more for Domain 57's Senate distribution than two additional weeks of production time.

## Section 4: Tier 2 Pre-Contact Timing and Messaging

### Wave-Specific Messaging Tone — Signal-Strength Calibration

**Strong signal (Days 20–25): Collaborative expertise tone**

The first sentence of every Tier 2 email names a specific engagement signal from Wave 1. "In the past two weeks, [name if available, or 'law school and policy organization researchers'] have been engaging with the framework's documentation of [specific finding]. Given your organization's work on [X], I wanted to reach out directly."

This is not name-dropping — it is establishing a distribution context. Think tanks and advocacy organizations receive research constantly. They prioritize material that other credible actors have already vetted. Even a moderate Wave 1 engagement signal (a substantive question from a law school contact) is worth referencing because it signals the material has been subjected to expert review.

Tone: **peer-to-peer**. You are not asking them to review the framework. You are coordinating on overlapping research with a specific deadline.

**Moderate signal (Days 25–30): Problem-specific framing tone**

Do not lead with Wave 1 engagement signals if they are weak. Lead with the specific finding most relevant to the recipient's current work. "Your organization's [current campaign/report/litigation] intersects directly with our documentation of [specific mechanism]. We have 47 citations on this specific point."

Tone: **practitioner-to-practitioner**. You are providing specific documented evidence for a problem they are already working on. The framework context is secondary.

**Weak signal (defer pre-contact to Days 30–35): Evidence-first tone**

Under weak signal, Tier 2 pre-contact becomes even more domain-specific. Do not attempt organizational-level engagement with institutions whose confidence in the framework has not been validated by any external signal. Instead, contact individuals (program directors, research staff) with a single-domain brief (2–3 pages, not the full domain document) and a specific ask: "Would this documentation be useful for your [specific current project]?"

Tone: **specific-utility framing**. Reduce abstraction to zero. Name the specific court case, legislative deadline, or organizational campaign the research addresses.

**Template Outline A: Law Schools and Election Law Clinics**

Messaging angle: Election protection as constitutional accountability infrastructure. Lead with Domain 37's CISA destruction documentation (government-source-verifiable, litigation-ready). The 23 DOJ voter roll cases are the practitioner hook — clinics can use these directly.

Opening line: "I'm reaching out to [clinic name] because Domain 37 of our research framework documents the specific federal interference mechanisms most relevant to election law clinical practice in [state] before November — and the advocacy window for [specific window, e.g., pre-election litigation positioning] opens in [X weeks]."

Body: Three numbered findings from Domain 37 most relevant to their state's competitive 2026 context. Cite the document word count and citation count (8,857 words, 50 citations) as a credibility signal. Include the Gist URL.

Call to action: Two asks. First: if the documentation is incomplete, what have they observed that the public record does not capture? This inverts the research relationship and treats them as contributors. Second: if Domain 37 is useful, who at peer organizations should receive it before the [specific advocacy window] closes? The referral ask is the highest-value CTA because election protection clinics are connected to each other through NAAG channels and clinical practice networks.

Avoid: Framing as "here is research you should know about." Frame as "here is documentation that may be useful for your current work."

**Template Outline B: Union Government Affairs Offices**

Messaging angle: Quantified civic capacity reduction from enacted law. Lead with OBBBA's simultaneous cuts as a compound burden on the union-represented workforce. Avoid democratic theory framing entirely — it sounds academic.

Opening line: "The OBBBA's enacted Medicaid work requirements (effective December 2026) will affect [X million] workers in [sector]. Our research documents the civic participation consequences of this burden — including the causal evidence that economic relief directly increases voter turnout. Given your legislative advocacy on [specific AFL-CIO/SEIU priority], I wanted to share this analysis."

Body: Three data points from Domain 59: (1) the income-participation gap (48% voter turnout in lowest income quintile vs. 86% highest — the starting inequality); (2) Dallas Fed wp2517 causal finding (mortgage payment relief directly increased turnout, concentrated among independents — this is the causal mechanism); (3) OBBBA's specific cuts and their implementation timeline (Medicaid work requirements December 2026; SNAP cuts 2027 — the urgency case). Include the research scope (59–65 hours, 45–50 citations in production) and the Gist URL when Domain 59 is complete.

Call to action: Two asks. First: would a briefing on how to integrate this analysis into union educational materials and legislative testimony be useful? Second: would they be willing to share the Domain 59 analysis with EPI's research team and any relevant AFL-CIO state affiliates? The EPI pathway is the fastest route from a single AFL-CIO contact to an externally cited research brief.

Avoid: The word "democracy" in the subject line. Union government affairs offices receive enormous volumes of democracy-adjacent research. Lead with the economic impact, not the democratic framing.

**Template Outline C: International Law and Think Tank Organizations (Domain 57 pre-distribution)**

Messaging angle: US multilateral withdrawal as domestic accountability dismantlement — a reframe that connects foreign policy to domestic civil society capacity. This is relevant to think tanks with international programming (Carnegie, Brookings Foreign Policy, CSIS) and international law organizations (HRW, Amnesty, Coalition for the ICC).

Opening line: "The January 7, 2026 Presidential Memorandum withdrawing the US from 66 international organizations removed three specific domestic accountability pathways: benchmarking data civil society uses to hold government accountable, treaty compliance obligations that courts and Congress can enforce, and the reputational cost that constrains extreme norm violations. Our Domain 57 research documents this mechanism — and I'm sharing it in advance of UNGA 81 High-Level Week (September 22–28) because your organization's advocacy will benefit from having the constitutional and institutional analysis in advance."

Body: Three specific findings from Domain 57 (based on production outline): (1) the 66-organization catalog with accountability function classification; (2) ICC sanctions criminal exposure for US civil society organizations (up to 20 years for material support to ICC investigations — the sharpest domestic civil society impact); (3) the constitutional asymmetry in treaty exit (Article II silence on withdrawal vs. Senate ratification requirement for entry — the reform pathway). Note the production timeline (Domain 57 targets August 10 completion; sharing this in advance of publication for pre-UNGA positioning).

Call to action: Two asks. First: would they be willing to flag the ICC sanctions section to their litigation team before the next UNGA civil society preparatory meetings? Second: would they be open to a pre-publication review of Domain 57 to ensure the international law characterizations are accurate? This positions the organization as a peer reviewer, not a recipient — and peer reviewers become advocates.

Avoid: Distributing Domain 57 before Section 2 (constitutional law) is research-complete. The ICC sanctions and Article II asymmetry claims require full citation support before they reach organizations that will fact-check them against their own legal analysis.

## Section 6: Tier 2 Pre-Contact Checklist

This checklist governs pre-contact preparation in the 5–7 days before the first Tier 2 email sends (approximately June 3–7, or later under Moderate/Weak outcomes). All items must be checked before any Tier 2 email leaves the drafts folder.

### Pre-Contact Preparation Tasks

**Constituency Pre-Screening**

- [ ] Confirm that no Tier 2 target organization is already in the Wave 1 contact list or previously received any outreach from this project
- [ ] Verify current institutional status of key contacts: check organization website, LinkedIn, and recent press coverage to confirm the named contact is still in the role and the organization is still active in the relevant domain
- [ ] For law school contacts: check current semester's course listings to confirm the faculty member is teaching election law or constitutional law this term (not on sabbatical)
- [ ] For union contacts: confirm current legislative calendar — do not contact union government affairs offices in the 72-hour window before a major labor hearing or legislative vote when their bandwidth is consumed
- [ ] For think tank contacts: check the organization's recent publications (past 30 days) to confirm no publication conflict — if they have just published on the same topic as the domain you're leading with, revise the framing to position your research as complementary, not competing

**Relationship Mapping**

- [ ] Map which Wave 1 contacts (if any positive responses received) have existing relationships with each Tier 2 target organization. Documented connections: Goodman (Just Security) has editorial relationships across Lawfare, Brennan Center publishing networks; Weiser (Brennan Center) has NAAG connections and co-counsel relationships with NAACP LDF, Campaign Legal Center; Bassin (Protect Democracy) has co-litigation relationships with ACLU, Lawyers' Committee
- [ ] Determine whether any Tier 2 organization was forwarded the Wave 1 materials by a Wave 1 contact (check the "referral" signal in WAVE_1_ENGAGEMENT_SCORING_CALCULATOR.csv — a referral signal from any Wave 1 contact means that organization may already be warm and should receive acknowledgment of that context in the cover email)
- [ ] For organizations that have no relationship mapping: confirm the contact name and title from the organization's website on the day of send, not from a list compiled days earlier. Contact roles change and using a stale name erodes credibility

**Customized Outreach Angle Per Organization**

Each Tier 2 email requires a minimum of one organization-specific customization that demonstrates the email was not mass-produced. Before drafting each email, complete the following per-organization research:

- [ ] Identify the organization's most recent published report or public statement (past 60 days). Name it specifically in the email body.
- [ ] Identify one specific finding in the target domain (37, 56, 57, 58, 59) that directly extends or complements the organization's recent work. This is the bridge sentence.
- [ ] Confirm the organization's current primary campaign or advocacy priority. Frame the domain research as supporting that specific priority.
- [ ] Identify the specific external deadline most relevant to the organization's work (legislative window, court deadline, regulatory comment period). Name it in the opening sentence.

**Infrastructure Verification**

- [ ] Confirm all Gist URLs for domains being distributed are live and accessible without login. Spot-check each URL in a fresh browser window on the day before send.
- [ ] Confirm the Gist URL being referenced matches the correct domain document (not a placeholder or draft URL)
- [ ] Confirm sender identity fields are complete in each email draft (your name, contact information) — these fields are the most commonly left blank in execution and undermine credibility
- [ ] If using Bitly tracking links: confirm Bitly analytics dashboard is accessible and all links redirect correctly

**Timing Gate**

- [ ] Do not send Tier 2 pre-contact before Day 20 post-Wave-1 regardless of engagement signals. Organizations need to be able to search for the research independently (via the Gist URL or any early Substack posts) before a personal email arrives. Arriving before any ambient awareness exists reduces conversion rate.
- [ ] Do not send Tier 2 pre-contact after Day 35 without re-evaluating the contact list. After 35 days, any legislative advocacy windows referenced in the outreach may have shortened significantly, and the email framing needs refreshing.
- [ ] Under Strong signal: begin Tier 2 outreach Days 20–25
- [ ] Under Moderate signal: begin Tier 2 outreach Days 25–30
- [ ] Under Weak signal: begin Tier 2 outreach Days 30–35 (after pivot domain engagement confirmed)
