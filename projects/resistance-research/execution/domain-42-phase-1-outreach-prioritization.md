# Domain 42 Phase 1 Outreach Prioritization
## DEA-1362 Hearing — May 28 Participation Notice Deadline

---

## Strategic Context

The DEA's June 29 hearing on marijuana rescheduling (Docket No. DEA-1362) closes its participation notice window on May 28, 2026 — 19 days from today. The operational deadlines are earlier: electronic filing to nprm@dea.gov closes May 24 at 11:59 p.m. ET; mail notices must be postmarked May 20. Organizations that miss these cutoffs have no administrative standing in the formal record the June 29–July 15 hearing generates.

The active outreach window is May 9 through May 18. Organizations contacted after May 18 have fewer than 6 business days to route internally, obtain authorization, and file — not a functional window for institutional filers. The hard stop on new contact is May 18.

**Why the May 28 deadline creates a Phase 1 forcing function**: Domain 42 is the first research output in the 35-domain framework with an external administrative deadline that cannot move. The hearing proceeds whether or not reform organizations participate. The sequencing decisions made in the next 9 days determine whether the democratic exclusion framing enters the formal DEA administrative record at all.

**One clarification on what "participation" means here**: The DEA controls participant selection. In the 2024 predecessor proceedings (withdrawn), 160+ parties filed participation requests; 25 were selected, disproportionately anti-reform. NORML filed and was excluded. Filing a participation notice is therefore not participation — it is the attempt to participate. Organizations should be told explicitly: if the DEA excludes them, that exclusion is itself evidence of regulatory capture operating in real time and belongs in the documentary record of Domain 42's distribution.

**Priority rationale for top contacts:**

DPA and NORML score 9 because they combine all three factors at maximum: both have prior DEA filing history (DPA analyzed all 43,000 public comments in the 2023 rescheduling period; NORML filed a 47-page comment and a participation notice in the 2024 proceedings before those were withdrawn), both have large member and coalition networks, and both carry DEA credibility as established advocacy filers. The outreach goal for these two is not to persuade them to file — they will file regardless — but to get Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing into their participation documents.

ACLU scores 8 because its participation would introduce a dimension the anticipated participant pool will not otherwise include. No other anticipated filer will connect DEA scheduling to voting rights suppression through the Weill Cornell disenfranchisement data (Meinhofer et al., *American Economic Journal: Economic Policy*, May 1, 2026). ACLU's institutional standing before federal agencies strengthens the procedural weight of its participation notice.

LEAP scores 7 on participation likelihood (2) but 3 on DEA credibility: law enforcement voices in DEA proceedings are rare among reform-oriented filers, and the DEA's institutional culture creates deference to law enforcement organizational authority that does not extend to civil rights or drug policy organizations.

State AGs score high on DEA credibility (3) and moderate on amplification (2) but route slowly. California and Colorado are the highest-priority because both have documented cannabis banking coalition activity (SAFER Banking AG letters) and therefore have staff already working in the relevant policy area. The ask for AG offices is lower-barrier: does Domain 42 Section 4 (federalism analysis) supplement a comment your office is already preparing, rather than initiating a new filing from scratch.

## Section 2: Three-Wave Email Sequencing

### Wave 1 — Day 0: Phase 1 Gist Launch Day

**Recipients (5 organizations)**: Drug Policy Alliance, NORML, ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project, The Sentencing Project, Law Enforcement Action Partnership

**When**: The same day the Phase 1 Gist is published (the Gist URL is the distribution anchor for all outreach). If the Gist is not yet available, send with the Domain 42 markdown attached directly — the deadline cannot wait for Gist completion.

**Send order**: DPA (9:00 AM ET), NORML (9:45 AM ET), ACLU (10:30 AM ET), Sentencing Project (11:15 AM ET), LEAP (12:00 PM ET). All Eastern time. Stagger to avoid batch-delivery patterns.

**Template**: D42-A for DPA, NORML, LEAP; D42-B for ACLU and Sentencing Project. See `domain-42-email-template.md`.

**Key framing additions beyond standard templates**:
- Include corrected deadline language verbatim: *"The Federal Register deadline is May 28, but electronic filings must be received by May 24. Mail notices must be postmarked by May 20."*
- For DPA and NORML only, add the resubmission note: *"Participation notices from the 2024 hearing proceedings were voided when those proceedings were withdrawn. Organizations that filed then must submit a fresh notice for DEA-1362."*

**Specific call to action**: "Will you submit comments to the DEA hearing?" with explicit yes/no/maybe framing. Track all three responses — a "maybe" from DPA is a follow-up trigger; a "no" from LEAP means pivot to referral ask.

**Attachment**: Domain 42 markdown (or Gist URL) plus `domain-42-may-28-dea-submission-guide.md` as second file.

### Wave 2 — Days 1–2: Secondary Stakeholders and Civil Rights Organizations

**Recipients (6 organizations)**: NAACP Legal Defense Fund, Marijuana Policy Project, Students for Sensible Drug Policy, Mason Marks (confirm current institutional email), Prison Policy Initiative, Americans for Safe Access (if capacity allows)

**When**: May 10–11. NAACP LDF must go no later than May 10 — their internal routing takes 7–10 business days, and their window to the May 24 electronic deadline is already only 14 days.

**Template**: D42-B for NAACP LDF and Prison Policy Initiative; D42-A for MPP, SSDP, ASA; D42-C for Mason Marks.

**Social proof condition**: If DPA or NORML has responded positively by May 10, add one sentence to each Wave 2 send: *"Drug Policy Alliance and NORML have both reviewed this briefing and are considering DEA-1362 participation."* Use this sentence only if factually accurate. Do not characterize a "we received this" reply as confirmation of filing intent.

**Specific call to action for each**:
- NAACP LDF: Frame entirely around voting rights. Ask: "Does your team have capacity to file a participation notice focused on democratic participation and disenfranchisement?" Yes/no/maybe — log the response.
- Mason Marks: Academic peer framing, not advocacy. Ask: does Domain 42's regulatory capture analysis extend your Yale Law Journal argument correctly? If you plan to testify, would the democratic exclusion framing be useful?
- SSDP and PPI: Primary ask is amplification, not direct filing. Ask them to forward Domain 42 through their chapter network / research channels.

### Wave 3 — Days 3–5: State AGs and Tertiary Contacts

**Recipients (4–5 organizations)**: California AG, Colorado AG, Michigan AG, Washington AG (verify current AG before sending — Bob Ferguson became Governor in January 2026; confirm the current Attorney General and whether the SAFER Banking coalition work has transferred), Brennan Center for Justice (add a single paragraph to their existing Phase 1 send if they are in Batch 1–3; do not double-send)

**When**: May 12–14. California AG first (largest recreational market, clearest 280E harm); Colorado AG second (first legalization state, Amendment 64 2012 55.32%); Michigan AG third; Washington AG only if current contact confirmed.

**Template**: D42-D. Compressed send — AG offices have limited staff time for cold outreach. Template D42-D runs approximately 350 words by design.

**Lower-barrier ask**: Do not ask AG offices to file from scratch. Ask whether an existing DEA-1362 comment being prepared through the SAFER Banking coalition can incorporate Domain 42 Section 4 (federalism analysis). This converts the ask from "start a new proceeding document" to "add a framing argument to an existing one."

**Specific call to action**: "Is your office planning to file DEA-1362 comments or a participation notice? If so, would Domain 42's federalism analysis (Section 4) be useful as background?" Yes/no/maybe tracked.

## Section 4: Contingency — If Phase 1 Distribution Delayed Past May 12

If the path decision (A/A+37/B) has not been made and the user does not authorize Phase 1 general distribution by May 12, the Domain 42 sub-batch proceeds independently. The sub-batch has no dependency on the general path decision.

**Minimum viable sequence for a May 12 start**:

The electronic deadline is May 24 — 12 calendar days from May 12, approximately 8–9 business days. This compresses the window but does not close it for Tier 1 contacts.

| Stage | Adjusted Send Date | Organizations | Impact |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|--------|
| Wave 1 | May 12 | DPA, NORML, ACLU, Sentencing Project | 12 days to electronic deadline — tight but functional for organizations with DEA filing infrastructure |
| Wave 2 | May 13 | NAACP LDF, LEAP, Mason Marks | LDF window compresses to 11 days — absolute minimum for institutional routing |
| Wave 3 | May 14–15 | California AG, Colorado AG only | Michigan and Washington AGs drop — 9–10 days is not a functional routing window |
| Follow-up | May 16–17 | DPA, NORML, ACLU, Sentencing Project only | Single follow-up to Tier 1 anchors |
| Hard stop | May 18 | All outreach | Unchanged regardless of start date |

**Adjusted minimum viable success at May 12 start**: 2 COM organizations (DPA and NORML, who will file regardless) plus at least 1 counterfactual organization (ACLU or Sentencing Project). The Brennan Center bridge send, SSDP amplification ask, and NOBLE supplemental contact are all dropped under the compressed timeline.

**If start slips to May 15 or later**: State AGs are non-viable (drop entirely). NAACP LDF is marginal (send but do not count on conversion). Concentrate entirely on DPA, NORML, ACLU, and Sentencing Project. Minimum viable success becomes: 2 COM organizations, at least 1 counterfactual, with Domain 42 framing appearing in at least 1 participation notice.
