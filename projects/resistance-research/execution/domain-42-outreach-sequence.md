### Social Proof Rules

Reference a prior contact's engagement in subsequent waves only if these conditions are all met:
1. The contact has responded substantively (not just an auto-reply)
2. The reference is factually accurate (do not characterize ambiguous replies as endorsements)
3. The referenced organization has not asked for confidentiality

Permitted language: "DPA has reviewed this briefing and their policy team is considering DEA-1362 participation."
Prohibited language: "DPA supports this effort" or "DPA is filing" unless DPA has said exactly that.

## Section 6: Pre-Drafted Participation Notice Language

Use these in follow-up emails on May 21 and May 25–27. Pre-drafted language dramatically lowers the filing barrier — the difference between "we'll look into it" and "confirm this wording and hit send" is the organizational friction that kills deadline compliance.

**For Drug Policy Alliance:**
"The Drug Policy Alliance respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. Drug Policy Alliance is a national advocacy organization whose work addresses drug policy reform, racial equity in drug law enforcement, and the democratic accountability of federal drug scheduling. Drug Policy Alliance wishes to present oral and written evidence regarding the structural design of the DEA scheduling process, its deviation from the statutory intent of the Controlled Substances Act, and its disproportionate impact on communities of color."

**For Law Enforcement Action Partnership:**
"Law Enforcement Action Partnership respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. Law Enforcement Action Partnership is a nonprofit organization of law enforcement professionals — police officers, judges, prosecutors, and other criminal justice practitioners — who support evidence-based drug policy reform. Law Enforcement Action Partnership wishes to present oral evidence regarding the institutional design of the DEA scheduling process as viewed from the perspective of law enforcement professionals who work directly with the consequences of federal drug scheduling on communities."

**For ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project:**
"The American Civil Liberties Union respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. The ACLU is a national civil liberties organization whose work on criminal law reform addresses the structural relationship between drug enforcement, mass incarceration, and democratic participation. The ACLU wishes to present oral and written evidence regarding the felony disenfranchisement feedback loop that drug enforcement produces — specifically, the mechanism by which drug enforcement generates felony convictions at racially disparate rates, removing the communities most affected by enforcement from the electorate."

**For The Sentencing Project:**
"The Sentencing Project respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. The Sentencing Project is a research and advocacy organization whose work on criminal justice reform includes comprehensive documentation of felony disenfranchisement. The Sentencing Project wishes to present written evidence regarding the relationship between federal drug enforcement patterns, felony conviction rates, voter disenfranchisement, and the political underrepresentation of communities most affected by drug policy."

**For Brennan Center for Justice:**
"The Brennan Center for Justice at NYU Law respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. The Brennan Center is a nonpartisan law and policy institute whose democracy program addresses voting rights, felony disenfranchisement, and the structural conditions that affect democratic participation. The Brennan Center wishes to present written evidence regarding the democratic participation implications of the current federal drug enforcement framework, including the relationship between drug conviction rates, felony disenfranchisement, and political representation in enforcement-intensive communities."

**For NAACP Legal Defense Fund:**
"The NAACP Legal Defense Fund respectfully submits this notice of desired participation in the administrative hearing in Docket No. DEA-1362. LDF is a civil rights legal organization whose work on voting rights and racial justice encompasses the structural relationship between criminal law enforcement, felony disenfranchisement, and the democratic participation of Black communities. LDF wishes to present written evidence regarding racial disparities in drug enforcement and their documented effect on voter disenfranchisement, with particular attention to the New York City data documenting increased felony drug arrest disparities between Black and white residents following partial decriminalization."

## Section 8: Post-May 28 Pivot

The May 28 deadline closes the participation window but not the influence window. After May 28:

1. **Track the DEA participant selection (June 22 notification date)**: Which filed organizations were selected? The selection pattern is the next evidence point for Domain 42's Section 2 regulatory capture argument. Exclusion of civil rights organizations while admitting pharmaceutical companies is itself a data point.

2. **Support participating organizations with framing materials**: Any organization that filed and is selected can receive Domain 42 as background for testimony preparation. The June 29 hearing is the next action window.

3. **Press engagement**: Background briefing conversations with WeedPress, Democracy Docket, and Filter Magazine should shift from "file a participation notice" to "these organizations filed; here is what they argued; the selection outcome will tell us whether that argument entered the record."

4. **Written record comments**: Federal agency administrative records are typically open for written comment submission for 30 days after the hearing closes. Organizations that did not file participation notices can still submit written comments. Domain 42 is appropriate background material for any written submission.
