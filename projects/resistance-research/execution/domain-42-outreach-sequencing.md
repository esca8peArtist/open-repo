# Domain 42 — Phase 1 Institutional Outreach Sequencing Strategy

**Hard deadline (electronic)**: May 24, 2026 — nprm@dea.gov  
**Hard deadline (mail)**: May 20, 2026 — postmarked  
**Outreach window**: May 8–18 (send cutoff), May 8–21 (final reminder cutoff)  
**Days to electronic deadline as of May 7**: 17  

---

## Why Sequencing Is the Critical Variable

The 10-organization contact list is fixed. The templates are ready. The domain is written. The only variable that determines whether multiple organizations commit to filing participation notices before May 24 is sequencing: the order in which organizations are contacted, the timing of social proof signals between waves, and the decision criteria that move each organization from "awareness" to "commitment."

Sequencing matters here because of two structural features of the outreach:

**Feature 1 — Short decision window with institutional friction.** Organizations need 7–10 business days to route an external briefing, obtain internal authorization, and file a participation notice. From May 8 to May 24 is 16 calendar days — approximately 11 business days. Any organization not reached by May 10 has a functionally compressed window. Sequencing determines which organizations are reached in time to act.

**Feature 2 — Social proof is a real commitment accelerant.** Drug policy organizations operate in a small, networked advocacy ecosystem. Drug Policy Alliance and NORML know each other's federal policy staff by name. If DPA's response (positive or pending) reaches NORML before NORML's own decision point, NORML's commitment calculus changes — they are filing alongside an established coalition partner, not as a lone voice. The sequencing strategy exploits this dynamic by deploying social proof signals from Wave 1 responses as credibility anchors in Wave 2 sends.

## The Critical Path: Four Commitments Before May 18

The minimum viable outcome is 3 organizations filing participation notices before May 24. The critical path to that outcome runs through 4 commitment decisions, with specific sequencing logic at each stage.

### Stage 1 — Day 0 (May 8): Anchor Organizations

**Organizations**: Drug Policy Alliance, NORML, ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project, The Sentencing Project, Law Enforcement Action Partnership, SSDP

**Strategic role**: Establish the field. DPA and NORML are near-certain to file in DEA-1362 regardless of this outreach — they have filed in every predecessor proceeding. The Stage 1 objective is not to convince them to file but to get Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing into their filing rationale, and to create a documented response that can be referenced in Stage 2.

**What "commitment" means at Stage 1**: A reply from DPA or NORML that acknowledges the briefing and does not decline. Even a "we received this and will pass it to our policy team" constitutes a soft commitment signal that can be used in Stage 2 language. Explicit commitment ("we are planning to file and will look at this framing") is the target but not required to move forward.

**What to watch for**: A response from the ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project is the highest-value Stage 1 signal. ACLU filing a participation notice on disenfranchisement grounds is not a foregone conclusion — it depends on staff capacity and internal authorization. An ACLU reply requesting the submission guide or asking about the filing procedure is a strong positive signal that should trigger an immediate response with the full submission guide.

### Stage 2 — Day 2 (May 10): Amplification Organizations

**Organizations**: NAACP Legal Defense Fund, Prison Policy Initiative, Marijuana Policy Project, Mason Marks, Ohio State Moritz DEPC

**Strategic role**: Deepen the field and activate social proof. By May 10, Wave 1 sends have had 48 hours. Any positive signals from DPA, NORML, ACLU, or Sentencing Project should be referenced explicitly in Stage 2 sends. The language: "Drug Policy Alliance and NORML have both reviewed this briefing and are considering DEA-1362 participation." Use this sentence only if true. Do not fabricate.

**What "commitment" means at Stage 2**: For NAACP LDF, commitment is a reply indicating their voting rights or democratic participation team is reviewing the briefing. For Mason Marks, commitment is methodological engagement — any reply that addresses the regulatory capture argument's validity indicates he is thinking about hearing participation. For PPI and MPP, commitment is a forwarding signal: "I'm passing this to our federal policy team / executive director."

**Critical path dependency**: The NAACP LDF decision is the highest-impact uncertain outcome. LDF's internal approval process for new federal proceedings takes 7–10 business days. A Stage 2 send on May 10 gives LDF 14 calendar days to the May 24 electronic deadline — just within range. A Stage 2 send delayed to May 12 reduces LDF's window to 12 days. Do not let LDF slip past May 10.

### Stage 3 — Day 6 (May 14): Federalism Organizations

**Organizations**: California AG, Colorado AG, Michigan AG, Washington AG

**Strategic role**: State AG filings are the highest-impact long-shot. AG offices file participation notices that carry sovereign institutional authority — a weight that no advocacy organization can replicate. But AG offices route slowly. The Stage 3 framing does not ask AGs to file from scratch; it asks whether an existing DEA-1362 comment being prepared through the SAFER Banking coalition can incorporate Domain 42's federalism analysis (Section 4). This lowers the activation barrier from "file a new proceeding document" to "add a framing argument to an existing one."

**What "commitment" means at Stage 3**: Any acknowledgment from a policy staffer (not just an autoresponse) is Stage 3 success. An explicit "we will pass this to our cannabis policy team" or "our office is aware of the hearing and may be participating" is the target. Full commitment (confirmed participation notice) should not be expected from AG offices at Stage 3 — the realistic outcome is getting Domain 42 Section 4 into the hands of the policy analyst already working on the hearing.

**Confirm before sending**: Washington AG changed when Bob Ferguson became Governor in January 2026. Verify the current AG and confirm whether the SAFER Banking coalition work has transitioned to the new AG's office. Do not send to a lapsed contact.

### Stage 4 — Day 10–13 (May 18–21): Follow-Up and Final Reminder

**Trigger**: Any Tier 1 organization (DPA, NORML, ACLU, Sentencing Project) that has not responded substantively by May 15 should receive one targeted follow-up on May 15–16.

**Hard stop for new outreach**: May 18. Organizations that have not been contacted by May 18 will not have functional time to file by May 24. Do not send to new contacts after May 18.

**Final reminder wave**: May 21. Three sentences maximum. All non-respondents receive this. The purpose is not to secure a commitment — it is to ensure no organization can say they did not know the deadline was approaching. The administrative record of outreach attempts is part of the Phase 1 documentation regardless of uptake.

## Commitment Thresholds: What Counts and Why

Tracking "commitments" against a vague success metric is operationally useless. These definitions are precise and measurable.

### Tier A — Committed to File (COM)
**Definition**: Explicit written statement that the organization will file a participation notice for DEA-1362 before May 24. The commitment does not need to reference Domain 42 specifically — any statement of intent to file constitutes COM.  
**Action required**: Reply within 24 hours with the submission guide and offer to answer procedural questions. Log date and contact name in tracking log.  
**Why it matters for sequencing**: A COM from DPA or NORML is a social proof signal that can be deployed in all subsequent Stage 2 and Stage 3 sends.

### Tier B — Expressed Interest / Reviewing (REQ)
**Definition**: Organization has requested additional materials (submission guide, specific domain sections, procedural clarification), is routing internally, or has acknowledged the briefing without declining. "Forwarding to our policy team" is REQ.  
**Action required**: Send requested materials immediately. Add to follow-up calendar for Day 7 (May 15). Note that REQ does not guarantee COM — an organization can route internally and determine it lacks capacity within the window.  
**Conversion target**: 60% of REQ contacts should convert to COM if follow-up is prompt. The follow-up should be proactive: "Has your policy team had a chance to review? Happy to walk through the filing procedure — it is straightforward."

### Tier C — Request for Briefing Materials Only (BRIEF)
**Definition**: Organization wants the research but has not indicated filing intent. "This is interesting, can you send the full document?" without any mention of the hearing is BRIEF.  
**Action required**: Send materials with a brief reminder of the May 24 electronic deadline embedded. One additional follow-up on May 18 maximum. Do not over-invest in BRIEF contacts when COM and REQ contacts still need support.  
**Note**: BRIEF contacts have downstream value even without filing — an organization that receives Domain 42 and shares it with their network, or cites it in future work, is a Phase 1 success by a different metric.

### Tier D — Declined (DEC)
**Definition**: Explicit statement that the organization will not participate in DEA-1362 or cannot act within the deadline window.  
**Action required**: Log and close. Do not follow up. One declined contact is not a critical path failure — it informs resource allocation for remaining contacts.

### Minimum viable success
**Threshold**: 3 organizations reach COM status before May 24, at least one of which is DPA or NORML (organizations with prior DEA proceeding experience), and at least one of which introduces an argument dimension absent from the anticipated participant mix (ACLU disenfranchisement framing or Mason Marks regulatory capture testimony).

**Why this threshold**: Three independent organizational participation notices citing democratic exclusion framing create a pattern in the administrative record that cannot be attributed to a single source. One filing is an outlier. Three filings, from organizations with different mandates and different evidentiary bases, constitute a record signal.

**Stretch target**: 5 COM organizations, including at least one state AG and at least one academic contact.

## Contingency Paths: What Shifts If Launch Delays 3–7 Days

The nominal Day 0 is May 8. If Phase 1 launch slips for any reason, sequencing must compress. These are the adjusted timelines.

### Delay Scenario A — Launch May 11 (3-day slip)

**Electronic deadline**: May 24 — 13 calendar days from launch  
**Functional business days**: ~9

| Stage | Adjusted Send Date | Organizations | Impact of Delay |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|----------------|
| Stage 1 | May 11 | DPA, NORML, ACLU, Sentencing Project, LEAP, SSDP | DPA and NORML's window compresses from 16 to 13 days — still functional. ACLU's internal routing is tighter but workable. |
| Stage 2 | May 13 | NAACP LDF, PPI, MPP, Mason Marks, Ohio State | LDF's window compresses to 11 days. This is the critical path constraint — LDF must be contacted by May 13 at the absolute latest. |
| Stage 3 | May 15 | California AG, Colorado AG, Michigan AG, Washington AG | AG offices drop from 14 to 9 days. Stage 3 realistic outcome shifts from "add federalism framing to existing comment" to "awareness only — plant for post-deadline engagement." |
| Follow-up | May 17 | All non-respondents | One day for follow-up per contact maximum. |
| Hard stop | May 18 | — | Does not change. May 18 remains the final send date. |

**Adjusted minimum viable success**: 2 COM organizations (not 3) if launch slips to May 11. The compressed window makes ACLU and LDF commitment less likely. Prioritize DPA, NORML, and Sentencing Project as the must-reach targets.

### Delay Scenario B — Launch May 15 (7-day slip)

**Electronic deadline**: May 24 — 9 calendar days from launch  
**Functional business days**: ~6–7

At a 7-day slip, Stage 3 (state AGs) is no longer viable. AG offices cannot route, authorize, and file in 9 calendar days from cold contact. Drop Stage 3 entirely and concentrate on Stages 1–2.

| Stage | Adjusted Send Date | Organizations | Impact of Delay |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|----------------|
| Stage 1 | May 15 | DPA, NORML, ACLU, Sentencing Project | LEAP and SSDP drop from priority — limited capacity window. DPA and NORML are the must-reaches. |
| Stage 2 | May 16–17 | NAACP LDF, Mason Marks, Sentencing Project (if not already Stage 1) | LDF and Marks have 7–8 days. LDF commitment is unlikely; realistic outcome is BRIEF or REQ. |
| Stage 3 | SKIP | State AGs | Do not attempt. Flag AGs for post-deadline follow-up framing Domain 42 as background for future cannabis banking and federalism work. |
| Follow-up | May 18 | DPA, NORML, ACLU only | Single targeted follow-up to Tier 1 anchors. |
| Hard stop | May 18 | — | Unchanged. |

**Adjusted minimum viable success**: 2 COM organizations — DPA and NORML. Both are near-certain filers regardless. The adjusted goal at 7-day slip is: (1) confirm that Domain 42's framing enters their participation documents, and (2) secure at least one commitment from a civil rights organization (ACLU or Sentencing Project) that would not have filed absent this outreach.

**Non-slip rationale**: This is why May 8 launch is not optional. A 7-day slip converts a 5-organization minimum viable outcome into a 2-organization floor with no state AG engagement and no realistic NAACP LDF window. The launch date is the highest-leverage single variable in the entire sequencing strategy.

## The Social Proof Cascade: How to Accelerate Commitments Between Waves

The 10-organization field is not siloed — these organizations know each other and pay attention to each other's procedural moves. The following social proof cascade applies if conditions are met. All applications require the cited signals to be factually true.

**Signal 1 — DPA or NORML reply (positive or pending)**: Deploy in Stage 2 sends beginning May 10. Add one sentence to the opening paragraph of all Stage 2 templates: "DPA [/NORML] has reviewed this briefing and is considering DEA-1362 participation — I wanted to make sure [Organization] had it before the deadline."

**Signal 2 — ACLU requests submission guide**: This is the highest-value Stage 1 signal because the ACLU's DEA participation is not a foregone conclusion. If ACLU requests materials, note this in the LDF outreach: "The ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project is reviewing this briefing for potential participation in the disenfranchisement dimension." ACLU and LDF share analytical territory but do not compete — the LDF sees an ACLU filing as a coalition signal, not a redundancy.

**Signal 3 — Any Stage 1 COM**: A confirmed filing commitment from any Stage 1 organization should be stated explicitly in all remaining sends. "Drug Policy Alliance has confirmed it will file a participation notice for DEA-1362" shifts the framing from "would you like to file?" to "would you like to join the organizations already filing?" The commitment cost for each subsequent organization decreases as the field fills.

**What not to do**: Do not reference organizations that have not responded, do not characterize ambiguous replies as endorsements, and do not fabricate coalition membership. The social proof cascade only works if it is truthful — a single factual misrepresentation, if discovered, invalidates the entire briefing's credibility with the contacted organization.

## Post-May 24 Pivot: What Happens When the Window Closes

The May 24 electronic deadline is hard. After it passes, the sequencing strategy pivots to two tracks:

**Track 1 — Participation confirmation tracking (May 25 – June 22)**: Monitor the DEA-1362 docket for published participant lists. The DEA notifies designated participants by June 22. Compare the participant list against the contact list. Document which contacted organizations filed and which were selected. This documentation is both a Phase 1 success metric and evidence for Domain 42's regulatory capture argument (if reform organizations that filed are disproportionately excluded from the 25 designated participants, that exclusion is an additional data point for the domain's Section 2).

**Track 2 — Downstream framing distribution (May 25 onward)**: Organizations that received Domain 42 but did not file before May 24 are still potential long-term distribution partners. The post-deadline outreach shifts from "file by May 24" to "Domain 42 is available for your ongoing cannabis policy and democratic participation work." Law school clinics, general Substack audiences, and organizations working on felony disenfranchisement reform at the state level receive the domain without the DEA deadline urgency frame.
