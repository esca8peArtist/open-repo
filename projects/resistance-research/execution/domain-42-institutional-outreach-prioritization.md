## Critical Finding Not in Prior Planning Documents

The May 28 date in all Domain 42 outreach materials is the nominal Federal Register deadline. The operational deadlines are earlier:

- **Electronic filing (nprm@dea.gov): May 24, 2026, 11:59 p.m. ET**
- **Mail filing: postmarked May 20, 2026**

All email templates and the submission guide reference May 28 as the deadline. This should be corrected in all future sends or noted in the pre-send language: *"The Federal Register deadline is May 28, but electronic filings must be received by May 24. Mail your participation notice by May 20 to ensure it is postmarked on time."*

Additionally: the DEA controls participant selection after the notice deadline. In the 2024-2025 predecessor proceedings (since withdrawn), over 160 parties filed participation requests; the DEA selected 25. NORML filed a Notice of Appearance and was not selected. The DEA disproportionately included organizations opposed to reform among the 25 designated participants. The June 22 notification date means that even organizations that file timely notices may not be selected for live testimony. This selection mechanism is itself an instance of the regulatory capture argument Domain 42 makes — and every outreach email should frame it as such. Filing is not participation; it is the attempt to participate. The DEA then decides who the record will hear.

This framing is both more accurate and strategically stronger. Organizations should file not only because they might be selected, but because their exclusion — if it happens — is itself evidence for the democratic design argument. The administrative record of participation attempts is part of the public record regardless of selection.

## Section 1: Contact Priority Matrix — Which 3–5 Organizations Move the Needle by May 24

This matrix prioritizes on three factors, each scored 1–3:

- **DEA Access (A)**: Has the organization previously filed in CSA scheduling proceedings and been selected for participation? Score 3 = prior selection, 2 = prior filing / no selection, 1 = no prior DEA participation.
- **Institutional Routing Speed (R)**: How fast can this organization get an external briefing to the staff member who can authorize filing a participation notice? Score 3 = policy team with direct DEA filing experience, 2 = policy team with adjacent federal proceeding experience, 1 = slower routing (academic, AG office).
- **Impact if Selected (I)**: If this organization IS selected, how much does Domain 42's framing shift the hearing's analytical record? Score 3 = introduces a dimension entirely absent from the anticipated participant mix, 2 = strengthens an underrepresented argument, 1 = reinforces what others will already say.

Total score = A + R + I (max 9).

### Priority Rationale

**Drug Policy Alliance (8)**: DPA filed in the 2023–2024 rescheduling comment period (43,000 total public comments; DPA independently analyzed the full comment corpus). Their staff has direct DEA-1362 docket familiarity. They have 350,000 subscribers and existing relationships with the NORML legal committee and state AG cannabis coalitions. Even if not selected for live testimony, a DPA participation notice that cites Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing enters the record.

**NORML (8)**: NORML's legal committee filed a 47-page comment in the 2023 rescheduling process and filed a participation notice in the 2024 hearing proceedings — and was excluded. Their exclusion is directly relevant: they have standing, experience, and the NORML deputy director has already publicly criticized the DEA's selection pattern as biased toward anti-reform organizations. Domain 42's Section 2 (regulatory capture analysis) is precisely the analytical frame for why NORML's exclusion was not accidental. NORML is probably already planning to file for DEA-1362; the outreach should position Domain 42 as analytical support for an argument NORML may already be preparing. This lowers the activation cost to near zero.

**ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project (8)**: The ACLU's impact score is 3 because the ACLU filing a DEA participation notice framed around democratic participation and disenfranchisement is a dimension the anticipated participant pool almost certainly will not include. The DEA-1362 hearing will be dominated by pharmaceutical companies, cannabis industry operators, and prohibitionist medical groups. An ACLU participation notice focused on the felony disenfranchisement feedback loop (Domain 42 Section 3) and the Weill Cornell data (Meinhofer et al., May 1, 2026) introduces the only argument that connects drug scheduling to voting rights — an argument the DEA has never had to address in a formal administrative record. The ACLU also has standing before federal agencies, which strengthens their participation notice's procedural weight.

**The Sentencing Project (8)**: Their routing speed is 3 because Domain 42 already cites their own data ("Locked Out 2022"). They are not receiving a cold briefing on an unfamiliar argument — they are receiving a document that extends their existing research into a new analytical frame. The activation barrier is lower than for any other contact. A Sentencing Project researcher reading Domain 42 Section 3 will immediately recognize the argument as a synthesis and extension of their published work. The specific ask — does your team have capacity to file a DEA participation notice focused on the democratic participation dimension? — is bounded and concrete.

**Law Enforcement Action Partnership (7, Tier 1B)**: LEAP's score is high on impact (3) because law enforcement voices in DEA proceedings are rare among reform-oriented filers, and the DEA's historical deference to law enforcement institutional authority means a LEAP participation notice citing democratic accountability failure carries different credibility weight than a civil rights org filing the same argument. The routing speed score (2) reflects that LEAP is a smaller organization with more limited policy staff capacity for formal administrative proceedings. Treat LEAP as a high-upside contact that warrants a separate follow-up push if May 8 yields no response by May 12.

**Why NAACP LDF is Tier 2 despite high impact**: LDF's internal approval processes for new federal proceedings are longer than DPA, NORML, ACLU, or Sentencing Project. Domain 42 is not yet in their work queue. Getting from cold briefing to authorized filing within 13–17 days is achievable but tight. Contact them on Day 2 (May 10) rather than Day 0 (May 8), and frame the ask explicitly around their existing voting rights and democratic participation mandate rather than drug policy per se.

**Why state AGs are Tier 2 despite potentially high impact**: AG offices have multi-layer routing: press contact to policy staff to senior deputy to authorization. That routing path is 7–10 business days minimum. Given the May 24 electronic deadline, sending to AG offices on May 14 leaves only 10 calendar days. The California and Colorado AGs are the highest-priority because both have prior cannabis federalism coalition activity (the SAFER Banking AG letters). The Michigan and Washington AGs are valuable but lower priority given the time constraint.

## Section 2: Sequencing Strategy — Should May 8 Be Customized for Drug Policy Orgs?

**Answer**: Yes, but narrowly. The existing Template D42-A is already well-calibrated for drug policy organizations. The only May 8-specific customization that adds material value is a single prefatory sentence acknowledging the new DEA-1362 proceedings: organizations that participated in the 2024 predecessor proceedings must resubmit participation notices for the new docket. DPA and NORML almost certainly know this, but making it explicit in the outreach signals that you are operating from current information, not a stale template.

Suggested addition to the opening paragraph of Template D42-A (May 8 only, Category A contacts who participated in 2024):

> *Note: The August 2024 hearing proceedings were withdrawn and all participation requests from those proceedings are voided. Organizations that filed in 2024 must resubmit a fresh participation notice to DEA-1362.*

This one sentence does three things: (1) it demonstrates operational currency, (2) it lowers the barrier for organizations that already have a participation filing in institutional memory (they just need to adapt an existing document), and (3) it subtly flags that the 2024 proceedings being "withdrawn" is itself relevant to Domain 42's argument about the hearing structure.

**Deadline correction**: All May 8 sends should include the corrected deadline language:

> *The Federal Register deadline is May 28, but electronic filings to nprm@dea.gov must be received by May 24. Mail notices must be postmarked by May 20.*

**Wave 1 send order (May 8)**: DPA first, NORML second, ACLU third, Sentencing Project fourth, LEAP fifth, SSDP sixth. This order reflects the priority matrix. Stagger by 30–45 minutes per send, 9:00–11:00 AM ET (all four Category A/B priority contacts are on Eastern time).

**Wave 2 send order (May 10)**: NAACP LDF, Mason Marks, MPP, Prison Policy Initiative, Ohio State Moritz. By May 10, there is a possible social proof signal to activate: if DPA or NORML has responded positively, add a single sentence to each May 10 send — "DPA and NORML have both reviewed this briefing and are considering DEA-1362 participation" — only if this is true. Do not fabricate prior endorsement.

**Wave 3 send order (May 14)**: California AG, Colorado AG, Michigan AG, Washington AG. Confirm current AG offices before sending. Washington AG changed when Bob Ferguson became Governor; the current AG must be verified.

**Adjusted timing based on deadline correction**: The prior outreach plan set a hard stop at May 21. Given the electronic deadline of May 24, the hard stop should be May 18. Organizations receiving outreach after May 18 have only 6 days to route, authorize, and file electronically by May 24. That is not a functional window for an institutional filer. Set May 18 as the final reminder date, not May 21.

## Section 3: Success Metrics — What Does Impact Look Like by May 28?

Success for Domain 42 has two tiers: hearing record impact (measurable by June 22) and downstream framing impact (measurable through the summer).

### Tier A: Hearing Record Impact (Primary)

These metrics determine whether Domain 42 contributed to the formal DEA administrative record in the June 29 hearing.

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Stretch |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| Organizations that file DEA-1362 participation notices before May 24 | 2 | 4 | 7 |
| Participation notices that explicitly cite the democratic exclusion framing or Domain 42 | 0 | 2 | 4 |
| Organizations selected by DEA as designated participants (June 22 notification) | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| Domain 42 cited in written hearing submissions or testimony | 0 | 1 | 3 |

**Minimum viable outcome**: 2 organizations file participation notices before May 24, at least one of which is DPA or NORML. This is achievable given that both organizations have filed before and both will likely be filing regardless — the question is whether Domain 42's framing enters their participation documents.

**Why the DEA selection outcome (June 22) is not fully within our control**: The DEA demonstrated in 2024 that it selects disproportionately against reform organizations. Domain 42's contribution to the selection outcome is indirect: it can strengthen the quality and analytical distinctiveness of each organization's participation notice, which affects the DEA's selection calculus at the margin. A participation notice that introduces a novel analytical argument (democratic exclusion as the structural context for scheduling decisions) is more difficult to exclude than one that repeats arguments already before the record.

**A secondary metric worth tracking explicitly**: How many organizations file participation notices that would not have filed without the Domain 42 outreach? For DPA and NORML, this is likely zero — they would file regardless. For the ACLU, Sentencing Project, and LEAP, it is nonzero. The counterfactual organizations — the ones whose filings are directly attributable to this outreach — are the Domain 42-specific contribution to the hearing.

## Section 4: Contingency Planning

### Contingency A: Path Decision Has Not Been Made by May 8

**Answer: Wave 1 launches independently on May 8. No dependency on the general Phase 1 distribution path decision.**

The reasoning is unambiguous. Domain 42 sub-batch is structurally separate from Batch 1–3. It does not share contacts, templates, or sequencing logic with the general Phase 1 distribution. The general path decision (A / A+37 / B) determines how the broader 35-domain framework reaches its primary Tier 1 audience (civil society, legal, academic contacts in the democracy and rule-of-law space). That decision does not determine whether drug policy organizations receive Domain 42 before the DEA hearing deadline.

Minimal viable execution requires: (1) an email client, (2) the Domain 42 markdown file as an attachment, (3) the four templates in domain-42-email-template.md, and (4) the pre-send checklist in domain-42-may-28-outreach-plan.md Section 5. No Gist, no Substack, no Tier 1 infrastructure, no Phase 1 decision.

If the user is uncertain about whether to launch before the general path decision is made, the relevant question is: what is the cost of waiting? Waiting until May 12 to send Wave 1 reduces the window for DPA and NORML to file electronic notices to 12 days. Waiting until May 15 reduces it to 9 days. The May 24 electronic deadline is not soft. Launch on May 8.

### Contingency B: NORML or DPA Do Not Respond by May 13

**Trigger**: Five days post-Wave 1 send, no response from DPA (A-1) or NORML (A-3).

**Escalation path**:

1. Send one follow-up to DPA and NORML via a secondary contact. For DPA, the secondary contact is the executive director (Maria McFarland Sánchez-Moreno); for NORML, the secondary contact is the legal committee chair or executive director (Erik Altieri). Both are findable via organizational website staff directories. The follow-up subject line: `Following up — Domain 42 DEA-1362 briefing, [X] days to electronic deadline (May 24)`.
2. If still no response by May 16: activate LEAP and SSDP explicitly as referral nodes. Ask them directly: "If you have a working relationship with DPA or NORML's federal policy staff, I would be grateful for an introduction. The DEA electronic deadline is May 24." LEAP and SSDP are less likely to file participation notices independently but more likely to have personal relationships with the DPA and NORML staff who can authorize filing.
3. Simultaneously: ensure the ACLU and Sentencing Project outreach (Wave 1, sent May 8) has produced at least one substantive response. If the ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project replies with interest, their participation notice may be the most analytically distinctive contribution to the hearing regardless of whether DPA or NORML file.

### Contingency C: DEA Excludes All Reform Organizations from the 25 Designated Participants (June 22 outcome)

**This outcome is consistent with the 2024 precedent and should be anticipated, not treated as failure.**

If Domain 42-briefed organizations file participation notices and are excluded by the DEA, the appropriate response is documentation, not disappointment. The DEA's selection pattern — 160+ requestors, 25 selected, disproportionately anti-reform — is itself evidence that Domain 42's Section 2 (regulatory capture of the scheduling process) describes real operational behavior. An exclusion list that includes DPA, NORML, ACLU, and the Sentencing Project, while selecting pharmaceutical companies and prohibitionist groups, is a usable document for every subsequent argument about the DEA's institutional capture.

Organizations briefed on Domain 42 should be told explicitly: "Filing a participation notice creates a public record of your organization's attempt to participate. If the DEA excludes your organization, that exclusion is itself evidence for the democratic design argument. Document your filing date, your participation notice content, and the DEA's June 22 response. This documentation belongs in the DEA-1362 administrative record."

### Contingency D: Domain 42 Is Folded Into the General Phase 1 Distribution Before May 8

If the user decides to launch Path A or A+37 before the May 8 Wave 1 window and wants to include Domain 42 in that distribution, the following three rules apply:

1. Domain 42's 10 contacts are not in Batch 1–3. They go into a separate track, not merged into the general Tier 1 contact list.
2. The corrected deadline language (May 24 electronic / May 20 mail) must appear verbatim in all Domain 42 sends. The general Phase 1 templates do not contain deadline language. Do not let the general templates absorb Domain 42 contacts without preserving the deadline frame.
3. The May 18 hard stop applies regardless of Phase 1 general cadence.
