# Tier 1 Contact Batches — Phase 1 Execution

**April 27, 2026**

This document breaks the Tier 1 contact list from the policy-influencer-mapping.md into three sequenced batches for Phase 1 outreach. The sequencing logic: Batch 1 establishes credibility anchors before broader outreach begins. Batch 2 builds on any Batch 1 responses. Batch 3 closes out Tier 1 before Tier 2 wave begins. Do not send all batches simultaneously — the contact-receipt-response cycle is the credibility infrastructure that makes each subsequent batch land differently.

---

## Sequencing Logic

The goal is not to reach 25 people quickly. The goal is to reach 25 people in an order that maximizes the probability that each subsequent contact has already heard about the framework through their peer networks before they open your email. This "warm arrival" effect — where a contact has already seen a colleague cite the research — is the single most reliable credibility multiplier available without institutional affiliation.

**Batch 1** (Days 1-3): Contacts positioned at network bridge nodes. Their responses — or their social/academic mentions — create the ambient credibility that Batches 2 and 3 benefit from.

**Batch 2** (Days 8-12): Contacts who operate one step removed from the bridge nodes. By Day 8, Batch 1 contacts have had time to forward, mention, or cite the material. Begin Batch 2 with a reference to any Batch 1 response if one exists ("Erica Chenoweth at Harvard's Nonviolent Action Lab has engaged with the resistance meta-analysis section...").

**Batch 3** (Days 15-21): Remaining Tier 1 contacts. By this point, institutional credibility exists — reference it.

---

## BATCH 1: Network Bridge Nodes (Days 1-3, Week 1)

**Target**: 5 contacts. These are the contacts whose institutional positions sit at crossings between policy networks, academic networks, and legal networks. A response from any one of them creates credibility for every subsequent outreach.

**Batch 1 goal**: At least 2 of 5 respond substantively. At least 1 mentions the research to a colleague.

### Contact B1-1: Ryan Goodman — Just Security / NYU Law

**Why first**: Ryan Goodman is co-editor-in-chief of Just Security, which publishes within days of submission. A single published Just Security piece citing or analyzing the framework gives it national security law credibility immediately. Goodman's readership includes Senate Judiciary and Intelligence Committee staff, national security attorneys, and federal judges. He is also an NYU Law professor — academic credibility stacks with the editorial credibility.

**Primary domains to pitch**: Domain 28 (War Powers, Venezuela Operation), Domain 29 (Prosecutorial Weaponization, SPLC indictment).

**Contact method**: Email is the correct channel for a substantive policy pitch. LinkedIn is a secondary option if email bounces. Do not use social media for first contact — it signals to academics that you are seeking publicity, not scholarly engagement.

**Email address**: Contact via Just Security's editorial submissions (justsecurity.org/submissions) for a publication pitch, or via NYU Law faculty contact (goodman@law.nyu.edu — verify before sending, NYU format is first.last@nyu.edu).

**Best day/time**: Tuesday or Wednesday, 9:00-11:00 AM Eastern. Academics check email most reliably mid-morning on mid-week days. Avoid Friday afternoons (pre-weekend) and Monday mornings (catching-up-on-weekend email).

**Subject line**: "Domain 28/29 War Powers + DOJ Capture Research — Submission for Just Security"

**Email template to use**: Template A (Academic/Think Tank) with the following specific framing: lead with the Venezuela Operation Absolute Resolve legal analysis and the 22-case retaliatory prosecution pattern. Note that Just Security has already published analysis of the SPLC indictment — the research extends that coverage with a systematic framework.

**What to send**: Domain 28 and Domain 29 excerpts (5-7 pages combined), executive summary, and a covering note proposing either (a) a standalone analytical piece for Just Security's website, or (b) co-citation in his ongoing work on DOJ accountability.

**Follow-up cadence**: Wait 7 days for a response. If no response, one follow-up email with a single new piece of information (a relevant court development, a new filing in one of the 22 cases). Do not follow up more than once on the initial outreach. If no response to the follow-up, move on — Goodman is a high-volume contact and non-response is not a rejection.

**What a positive response looks like**: He engages with the legal analysis and asks for the full framework; he offers to cite the research in a forthcoming Just Security piece; he forwards to a co-editor or colleague.

**What to do if he responds**: Reply within 24 hours. Offer the full framework and the research notes. Ask if there is a specific domain section most relevant to his current work. Do not ask for endorsement — the relationship is established by being useful, not by being promoted.

## SUCCESS METRICS

**Week 1 (Batch 1) targets**:
- 5 emails sent (Days 1-3)
- Target: 2 of 5 respond within 7 days
- Minimum acceptable: 1 of 5

**Week 2 (Batch 2) targets**:
- 8 emails sent (Days 8-12)
- Target: 3 of 8 respond within 7 days
- Reference any Batch 1 responses in every Batch 2 email

**Week 3 (Batch 3) targets**:
- 12 emails sent (Days 15-21)
- Target: 5 of 12 respond within 7 days
- Reference Batch 1 and 2 responses in every Batch 3 email

**Overall Tier 1 (Week 4 measurement)**:
- Target response rate: 30% or above (8+ of 25 respond)
- Target forwarding rate: 10% or above (at least 3 contacts share with a named peer)
- Minimum gate for Tier 2 launch: at least 1 Tier 1 contact has forwarded or cited the research to another named individual

**If response rate is below 20% after Week 3**: Review messaging before launching Tier 2. The problem is likely framing, not audience selection. Review the template variants and increase personalization. Do not launch Tier 2 on the same messaging if Tier 1 is underperforming.
