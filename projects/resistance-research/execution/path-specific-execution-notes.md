# Path-Specific Execution Notes — Domain 42 Integration with Phase 1 Paths

**Key principle across all paths**: Domain 42's May 28 DEA deadline is path-independent. Regardless of which path the user selects for the main framework distribution, Domain 42 outreach begins May 8. The DEA hearing participation notice deadline is not negotiable and cannot be deferred to match the main framework rollout schedule.

---

## Path A: Standard Phase 1 Distribution (35 Domains, Immediate)

### What "Path A + Domain 42" Means in Practice

Path A launches the full 35-domain democratic renewal framework immediately. The main Phase 1 distribution targets institutional contacts across five Batch 1–3 tiers (Ryan Goodman, Wendy Weiser, Erica Chenoweth, Ian Bassin, Marc Elias in Batch 1; additional 45+ contacts in Batches 2–3). Domain 42's drug policy sub-batch targets 10 drug policy, civil rights, and administrative law contacts — a completely separate audience.

Under Path A, both tracks run in parallel beginning May 8. They share the user's time and email account but do not share contacts, templates, or subject matter.

### Path A Timeline

| Date | Main Framework Track (Path A) | Domain 42 Sub-Batch Track |
|------|------------------------------|--------------------------|
| May 7 | Gist creation for 6 canonical documents; template URL fill-in | Pre-launch prep (contact verification, tracking spreadsheet, template personalization) |
| May 8 | Batch 1 send: 5 institutional contacts (9:00–11:00 AM) | Wave 1 send: 6 Category A/B drug policy + civil rights contacts (1:00–3:30 PM) |
| May 9–11 | Monitor Batch 1 responses; prepare Batch 2 personalization | Monitor Wave 1 responses; create Domain 42 Gist if not done May 8 |
| May 10–12 | Begin Batch 2 preparation (outreach to 5–7 additional Tier 1–2 contacts) | Wave 2 send: 5 Category B/C contacts (May 12) |
| May 14–17 | Continue Batch 2 sends; monitor and respond | Wave 3 send: 4 state AG contacts (May 14) |
| May 15–21 | Batch 3 preparation; Batch 2 follow-ups | Domain 42 follow-up wave; May 21 final reminder |
| May 22–28 | Ongoing Batch 2–3 outreach and response tracking | Support mode; no new outreach; May 28 deadline monitoring |

### Path A Specific Considerations

**Parallel launch logistics**: May 8 involves sending up to 11 emails: 5 main framework Batch 1 contacts + 6 Domain 42 Wave 1 contacts. Recommendation: block the morning (9:00–11:00 AM) for main framework Batch 1, and block the afternoon (1:00–3:30 PM) for Domain 42 Wave 1. Keep the two tracks in separate folders in your email client to avoid confusion in response tracking.

**Gist creation sequencing**: Path A requires creating 6 canonical Gists before Batch 1 sends. The Domain 42 Gist is a 7th. Total Gist creation time: approximately 70 minutes (7 × 10 minutes each). If time-pressured on the morning of May 8: (1) create the executive summary Gist first — it is referenced in all Batch 1 main framework templates; (2) create the main framework Gist (democratic-renewal-proposal.md) second; (3) use the attachment fallback for Domain 42 Wave 1 emails to avoid the Domain 42 Gist from blocking the afternoon send. Create the Domain 42 Gist on May 9.

**No audience overlap between tracks**: None of the 10 Domain 42 contacts are in the main framework Batch 1–3 contact lists. Drug Policy Alliance, NORML, ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project, Sentencing Project, Prison Policy Initiative, NAACP LDF, LEAP, SSDP, Mason Marks, Ohio State Moritz DEPC — none of these are in `DISTRIBUTION_OUTREACH_CONTACTS.md`, `policy-influencer-mapping.md`, or `execution/tier-1-contact-batches.md`. No duplicate-send risk.

**Template non-overlap**: Domain 42 templates (D42-A through D42-D) are completely distinct from main framework institutional outreach templates. Do not send Domain 42 templates to Batch 1–3 contacts, and do not send main framework templates to Domain 42 contacts. Each track has its own subject line, body, and specific ask.

**Success metrics are separate**: Path A's main framework success metrics (Batch 1 engagement rate, Batch 2 academic and think-tank adoption rate, social media reach) are documented in `execution/success-metrics.md`. Domain 42 success metrics are separate: 3+ COM organizations filing DEA-1362 participation notices. Track these independently.

## Path A+37 Hybrid: Domain 37 Integration with Domain 42

### What "Path A+37 + Domain 42" Means in Practice

Path A+37 runs the full Path A distribution plus an additional Domain 37-specific track targeting election-protection contacts. Domain 37 (Federal Executive Interference in 2026 Midterms) is a distinct research domain with a distinct contact list (`DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md`) and distinct templates (`execution/path-a-domain37-materials.md`). Domain 42 is a third, independent track.

Under Path A+37, three tracks run simultaneously beginning May 8: the main framework distribution, the Domain 37 election-protection track, and the Domain 42 drug policy sub-batch.

### When Domain 37 Integrates vs. When It Is Sent Separately

**Domain 37 and Domain 42 are always sent separately.** They target different organizations, use different templates, and have different urgency timelines. The question of whether Domain 37 "integrates" with the main framework vs. is sent separately applies to the main framework contacts — not to Domain 42. For Domain 42, the answer is always separate.

Specifically:
- **Domain 37 → election-protection organizations**: Sent via `DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md` contacts using `execution/path-a-domain37-materials.md` templates. These contacts are not in the Domain 42 sub-batch.
- **Domain 42 → drug policy / civil rights / academic / state AG contacts**: Sent via `domain-42-contact-list.md` using `execution/domain-42-email-template.md` templates. These contacts are not in Domain 37's contact list.
- **Main framework → institutional contacts**: Sent via `DISTRIBUTION_OUTREACH_CONTACTS.md` and `execution/tier-1-contact-batches.md` using main framework templates.

The three contact lists are completely disjoint. There is no organization that should receive all three.

### Domain 37 Gist Timing vs. Domain 42 Gist Timing

| Gist | Create By | Priority |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Main framework (6 canonical Gists) | May 8 morning, before Batch 1 sends | Highest — blocks Batch 1 |
| Domain 37 | May 9–10, before Domain 37 election contacts | Second — blocks Domain 37 wave |
| Domain 42 | May 8 (or use attachment fallback) — before Wave 1 sends | Second — parallel priority with Domain 37 |

Under Path A+37, the total Gist creation load is 8 Gists (6 canonical + Domain 37 + Domain 42). Recommend: create all 8 on May 7 evening if time permits, or use the attachment fallback for both Domain 37 and Domain 42 on their respective launch days and create Gists on May 9.

### Domain-37b Timing Decision — Does It Affect Domain 42?

The Path A+37 execution checklist notes: "Consider whether to complete domain-37b (state election security, ~1 session) before contacting election-security specialists." This decision is entirely independent of Domain 42. Domain 42's research is complete (6,700 words, 54 citations, May 7, 2026). Domain-37b's completion status has zero impact on the Domain 42 sub-batch. Execute Domain 42 outreach on May 8 regardless of the domain-37b decision.

### Managing Three Simultaneous Tracks

Under Path A+37, the user is managing three outreach tracks with separate contacts, templates, and tracking logs. This is operationally manageable but requires discipline in separating the tracks.

**Recommended folder structure in email client**:
- Folder: "Main Framework Outreach" — Batch 1–3 sends and responses
- Folder: "Domain 37 Outreach" — election-protection contacts
- Folder: "Domain 42 Outreach" — drug policy sub-batch

**Recommended spreadsheet structure**: Three tabs in a single tracking spreadsheet: "Batch 1-3" (main framework), "Domain 37" (election contacts), "Domain 42" (drug policy sub-batch). Each tab uses the same field structure (Name, Institution, Verified Email, Date Sent, Response Status, Notes) for consistency.

**Urgency hierarchy across tracks**: Domain 42's May 28 deadline is the governing constraint. Domain 37's election-cycle urgency is real but longer-term (2026 midterms). Main framework distribution urgency is ongoing without a hard deadline. Prioritize Domain 42 tasks when there is a time conflict.

## Path B: Staged Distribution with Feedback Integration

### What "Path B + Domain 42" Means in Practice

Path B sequences the main framework distribution as a staged rollout: Batch 1 sends → 4-week feedback collection window → Batch 2–4 sends incorporating feedback. The staged approach allows the framework to evolve based on Tier 1 institutional feedback before broader distribution.

Domain 42's May 28 DEA deadline breaks the staged model for this specific sub-batch. Path B does not delay Domain 42. Domain 42 outreach begins May 8 regardless of Path B's staged timeline for the main framework.

### Path B Timeline for Domain 42

| Date | Main Framework (Path B staged) | Domain 42 Sub-Batch (NOT staged) |
|------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| May 8 | Batch 1 send (5 main framework institutional contacts) + feedback tracking setup | Wave 1 send (6 Category A/B contacts) |
| May 8 – June 5 | Feedback collection window (4 weeks) — no new main framework sends | Domain 42 follow-up and response tracking through May 28 |
| May 12 | No new main framework sends | Wave 2 send (5 Category B/C contacts) |
| May 14 | No new main framework sends | Wave 3 send (4 state AG contacts) |
| May 28 | No new main framework sends | Domain 42 deadline — log outcomes |
| June 5+ | Batch 2 sends incorporating Batch 1 feedback | Domain 42 post-deadline pivot (long-term distribution framing) |

**Critical Path B gotcha**: Path B's 4-week feedback collection window applies only to the main democratic renewal framework. It does not apply to Domain 42. If a user following Path B delays Domain 42 outreach to match the staged rollout timeline, they will miss the DEA hearing entirely. Domain 42 sub-batch outreach is mandatory on May 8 regardless of Path B staging decisions.

### Path B Feedback Tracking — Separation from Domain 42 Tracking

Path B requires setting up a feedback tracking mechanism (Airtable, Google Form, or spreadsheet) for collecting structured feedback on the democratic renewal framework from Batch 1 contacts. This is a distinct system from the Domain 42 contact tracking log.

**Do not merge these systems.** They serve different purposes:
- **Path B feedback form**: collects structured qualitative feedback on the democratic renewal framework — content accuracy, framing, gaps, suggestions. Respondents are main framework institutional contacts.
- **Domain 42 tracking log**: tracks commitment status (COM/REQ/BRIEF/DEC) and DEA filing procedural status. Respondents are drug policy sub-batch contacts.

A Sentencing Project researcher might appear in both tracks — receiving the main democratic renewal framework via Path B Batch 1 AND receiving Domain 42 via the drug policy sub-batch. If this happens, track their responses separately in each system. Their feedback on the main framework (Path B) and their DEA filing commitment (Domain 42) are separate data points.

### Path B Post-Deadline Domain 42 Integration

After May 28, Domain 42 can be integrated into the Path B staged distribution for Batches 2–4. The integration framing changes post-deadline:

**Pre-May 28**: "Domain 42 is available for DEA hearing participation — deadline is May 28."
**Post-May 28**: "Domain 42 is a detailed analysis of the democratic exclusion architecture of drug prohibition — relevant for your ongoing cannabis policy, voting rights, and administrative law work."

Path B Batch 2–4 contacts (law school clinics, general Substack audience, second-tier institutional contacts) can receive Domain 42 as part of the main framework distribution after May 28. The domain has long-term analytical value independent of the DEA hearing outcome. In the post-May 28 framing, link to the Domain 42 Gist from the main framework distribution materials — it is a Phase 3 domain supplement to the main framework.

## Common Gotchas Across All Paths

**Gotcha 1 — The May 21 hard stop is path-independent**: Do not send any Domain 42 outreach after May 21, regardless of which path is selected for the main framework. This is not a suggestion — it is the operational limit beyond which organizations cannot act in time. Post-May 21 Domain 42 distribution drops the DEA deadline framing entirely.

**Gotcha 2 — Contact list integrity**: The 10 Domain 42 contacts are documented as "not in `execution/tier-1-contact-batches.md`, `DISTRIBUTION_OUTREACH_CONTACTS.md`, or `policy-influencer-mapping.md`" and must not be added to those lists. This is not a redundancy protection — it is an audience coherence requirement. Drug policy organizations receive a drug-policy-specific outreach, not a generic democratic renewal pitch. Merging the contact lists would require rewriting the outreach framing from scratch and would reduce response rates.

**Gotcha 3 — Gist attachment fallback is always valid**: On any path, if the Domain 42 Gist is not ready when Wave 1 sends go out, attach the markdown file directly. The gist creation guide explicitly documents this option. There is no path under which the Gist URL must exist before Wave 1 sends. Do not delay Wave 1 sends to create the Gist.

**Gotcha 4 — Social proof cascade is factual-only**: The social proof cascade protocol — referencing positive signals from Wave 1 organizations in Wave 2 and 3 sends — applies on all paths. The factual-accuracy guardrail also applies on all paths: reference only confirmed signals, and never characterize ambiguous replies as endorsements. A routing failure or generic acknowledgment is not a positive signal.

**Gotcha 5 — Path decision does not affect May 24 electronic filing safety buffer**: The domain-42-outreach-sequencing.md documents that the safe electronic filing deadline is May 24 (not May 28) to ensure timely delivery. This 4-day buffer is relevant across all paths. When communicating the deadline to organizations, the correct message is "file by May 28" (the official deadline), but internally the outreach is designed around a May 24 target.

## Response Triage Protocol — How to Categorize Feedback from Tier 1 Contacts

Responses from Domain 42 outreach fall into four commitment tiers (COM, REQ, BRIEF, DEC) plus a fifth category (non-response). This section documents specific real-world response patterns and how to categorize and act on each.

### Tier A — Committed to File (COM)

**Definition**: Explicit written statement that the organization will file a participation notice for DEA-1362 before May 28.

**Example responses that qualify as COM**:
- "We're planning to participate in the DEA-1362 hearing and will look at this framing for our submission."
- "I've passed this to our federal policy director, who has confirmed we'll be filing."
- "Yes, we're filing by May 28 — this is helpful background."

**What "explicit" does not require**: COM does not require a specific commitment to cite Domain 42 in the filing. It does not require the organization to confirm they will use Domain 42's framing specifically. Any statement of intent to file DEA-1362 participation notice constitutes COM.

**Response action for COM**: Reply within 24 hours. Thank them. Send the full `execution/domain-42-may-28-dea-submission-guide.md` and the tactical guide (`execution/domain-42-dea-hearing-tactical-guide.md` Part 1 — worked comment templates) unless already sent. Offer to answer procedural questions. Log in tracking spreadsheet as COM with date. Deploy as social proof signal in remaining sends if confirmed before Wave 2/3 go out.

**What COM unlocks**: One confirmed COM from DPA or NORML is a social proof signal for all subsequent sends. One confirmed COM from ACLU is the highest-value civil rights signal — flag it explicitly in remaining civil rights organization sends.

---

### Tier B — Expressed Interest / Reviewing (REQ)

**Definition**: Organization has requested additional materials, is routing internally, or has acknowledged the briefing without declining. "Forwarding to our policy team" constitutes REQ.

**Example responses that qualify as REQ**:
- "Thanks for this — I'm forwarding to our federal policy director."
- "This looks relevant. Can you send the submission guide?"
- "Our team is reviewing this — I'll follow up by the end of the week."
- "We received this. Our executive director will review."

**Response action for REQ**: Send requested materials immediately (submission guide, specific domain sections, procedural clarification — whatever they asked for). Add to follow-up calendar for Day 7 from initial send (or May 15, whichever is later). A REQ with 10+ days to the deadline can be converted to COM with a single targeted follow-up. A REQ with fewer than 5 days to the deadline should receive immediate support rather than waiting for the calendar trigger.

**Conversion target for REQ**: 60% of REQ contacts convert to COM if follow-up is prompt and logistical support is available. The primary conversion blocker for REQ contacts is not motivation — it is procedural uncertainty (how do I actually file?). Proactively send the submission guide with the 3 required elements and the nprm@dea.gov address immediately upon receiving REQ signals.

---

### Tier C — Request for Briefing Materials Only (BRIEF)

**Definition**: Organization wants the research but has not indicated filing intent. "This is interesting" or "can you send the full document?" without any mention of the hearing constitutes BRIEF.

**Example responses that qualify as BRIEF**:
- "This is interesting research. Can I get the full domain document?"
- "I'll take a look at this."
- "Thanks for reaching out. I've forwarded to our research team."

**What distinguishes BRIEF from REQ**: REQ involves routing toward a decision-maker with authority over DEA filings. BRIEF involves routing toward a researcher or program staff member without a specific institutional action in view.

**Response action for BRIEF**: Send materials with a brief reminder of the May 28 deadline embedded: "Happy to send the full document — it is attached / available at [Gist URL]. Just noting the DEA-1362 participation notice deadline is May 28, and the electronic filing is straightforward at nprm@dea.gov." One additional follow-up on May 18 maximum. Do not over-invest in BRIEF contacts when COM and REQ contacts still need support.

**Downstream value of BRIEF**: Organizations that receive Domain 42 and share it with their network, cite it in future work, or use it for non-DEA purposes (state-level felony disenfranchisement advocacy, law review articles, Congressional testimony) are Phase 1 successes by a different metric. Log BRIEF contacts as distributed, even if they do not reach COM.

---

### Tier D — Declined (DEC)

**Definition**: Explicit statement that the organization will not participate in DEA-1362 or cannot act within the deadline window.

**Example responses that qualify as DEC**:
- "We're not planning to participate in the DEA-1362 proceeding."
- "We don't have capacity for this within the May 28 window."
- "This isn't within our current advocacy priorities."

**Response action for DEC**: Log and close. Do not follow up. Do not attempt to re-engage before May 28. A DEC is a considered organizational decision and should be respected. If the organization is interested in the research for non-DEA purposes (BRIEF outcome within a DEC response), note that separately in the tracking log and treat it as a post-deadline distribution opportunity.

**What a DEC is not**: A DEC is not a criticism of Domain 42 or its framing. Drug policy and civil rights organizations have many competing priorities. A capacity-based DEC ("we can't act in time") is particularly common in organizations with small federal advocacy teams. It is also not a permanent decision — the same organization that declines DEA-1362 participation may be a strong post-hearing distribution partner for Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing.

---

### Non-Response (NR) — After Day 7

**Definition**: Contact has not replied within 7 days of the initial send.

**Example of NR**: No reply, no auto-response indicating routing, no bounce notification — complete silence after 7 days.

**Response action for NR**: One targeted follow-up per the May 15 escalation protocol (see coordination calendar). Subject: `[Organization name] — following up, May 28 deadline is [X] days away`. Body: 3 sentences maximum. After one follow-up, if still no response: one final reminder May 21 (all non-respondents), then close.

**NR is the most common outcome**: For a cold outreach campaign to 10 organizations, NR is the most statistically likely outcome for at least 4–5 contacts. This is not failure — it is baseline for cold outreach to institutional contacts. The minimum viable success threshold (3 COM) is achievable with 30% conversion on 10 contacts, which is ambitious for cold outreach but realistic given the credibility of the Domain 42 research, the timeliness of the DEA hearing deadline, and the pre-alignment between the domain's arguments and these organizations' existing advocacy lanes.

**What to do with NR after May 28**: Log as NR in the tracking spreadsheet. Post-deadline, NR contacts receive the Domain 42 post-deadline distribution framing (democratic exclusion architecture analysis for long-term policy work) rather than the DEA deadline urgency frame. Some NR contacts will engage post-deadline when the time pressure is removed.

### Special Case: Methodological Feedback Response (Category C Academic Contacts)

Category C contacts (Mason Marks, Ohio State Moritz DEPC) are reached as methodological peers, not as advocacy targets. Their responses should not be categorized as COM/REQ/BRIEF/DEC using the DEA filing framework — they are a separate contact type with different success metrics.

**Category C success tiers**:

| Response | Category | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Substantive methodological engagement — addresses the regulatory capture argument's validity, cites additional scholarship, or questions the framing | High success | Reply within 24 hours engaging the specific methodological points; offer to share revised analysis incorporating their feedback |
| Indicates they are planning DEA-1362 testimony independently | High success | Note this as a significant Phase 1 signal; ask whether their testimony will engage the democratic exclusion framing or primarily the statutory/administrative law question |
| Requests the full domain document for review | Moderate success | Send immediately; this is equivalent to REQ for the academic track |
| Generic acknowledgment without substantive engagement | Low success (but still Phase 1 contact made) | Log; send one follow-up with the specific questions from Template D42-C (does the regulatory capture argument hold up as an administrative law argument? Is there an APA-based procedural challenge available?) |
| No response | NR — follow-up per standard protocol on Day 7 | |

**What not to do with Category C contacts**: Do not push them toward DEA filing as an explicit goal. If Mason Marks or the Ohio State Moritz DEPC want to file a participation notice, they will make that decision independently. The ask in Template D42-C is methodological feedback — stay in that register.
