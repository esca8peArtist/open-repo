# Distribution Sequencing Plan

**April 27, 2026**

This document maps the full distribution sequence from decision day (T-Day 0) through four weeks of Phase 1 execution. The sequence is identical for all three paths at the structural level — the differences are in which documents are distributed and what messaging frames are used. Those differences are documented in the path-specific materials files. This document covers the operational mechanics: what gets sent when, to whom, in what order, and how feedback informs subsequent actions.

## T-DAY 0: Decision Day

**What happens on decision day**:

### Action 1: Batch 1 Emails Sent (Hours 0-4)

Send all 5 Batch 1 contacts within a 4-hour window on the morning of T-Day 0. Do not stagger beyond 4 hours — you want Batch 1 to land as a cohesive initial wave, and the contacts are not aware of each other's presence in the batch.

Order of sending (strategic, not arbitrary):
1. Ryan Goodman (Just Security) — earliest, because Just Security can publish within days
2. Wendy Weiser (Brennan Center) — second, because Brennan Center response may inform Batch 2
3. Erica Chenoweth (Harvard, Nonviolent Action Lab) — third
4. Ian Bassin (Protect Democracy) — fourth
5. Marc Elias (Democracy Docket) — fifth

Log each email sent with exact time in the contact log.

### Action 2: Social Media Draft Posts Queued (Hours 4-8)

Draft and schedule (do not publish yet) the social media posts:

**Substack** (draft for publication at T+3 days):
- Draft 1: Executive summary (full text, reformatted for Substack). Do not publish until at least one Batch 1 response has been received or 72 hours have elapsed.
- Rationale: If you publish publicly before any institutional contact has engaged, you lose the "first credible reader was an institution" framing. The 72-hour delay allows Batch 1 to potentially respond before public launch.

**Reddit** (draft for publication at T+3 days):
- Draft posts for: r/PoliticalScience (full framework), r/LegalAdvice (litigation tracker), r/USPolitics (executive summary), r/dataisbeautiful (fiscal analysis tables).
- Do not cross-post identically — customize each post for the subreddit community.

**X / Bluesky** (draft for publication at T+2 days):
- Thread draft: Opening hook (most arresting specific finding), 3-4 substantive tweets with specific data points, close with link and CC 4.0 note.
- Note: Social media posting is NOT a substitute for institutional outreach. It is the public-facing complement.

### Action 3: Press Release Draft Ready (Hours 8-12)

Draft the decision-specific press release (for distribution to media Tier 2 contacts in Week 2). The press release is path-specific — see path-a-materials.md, path-a-domain37-materials.md, or path-b-materials.md for the specific framing.

Structure of press release:
1. Headline (path-specific framing — see path materials)
2. Lead paragraph: what the framework is, what it covers, why now
3. Second paragraph: two or three specific findings (the most newsworthy domain-level conclusions)
4. Third paragraph: availability and access (CC 4.0, links)
5. Contact information for media inquiries

The press release is not distributed on T-Day 0 — it is held for T+Week 2 when Tier 2 media outreach begins. It is drafted now so it is ready.

## T+DAY 3: Social Media Launch

Publish the queued Substack post, Reddit posts, and X/Bluesky thread.

**If any Batch 1 response has arrived**: Note it briefly in the Substack post ("Since circulating this among policy and academic contacts in the past 72 hours, I've received feedback from [Brennan Center / Just Security / etc.] that [brief characterization of their response]"). This is only appropriate if the contact has agreed to be named or the response is unambiguously positive.

**If no Batch 1 responses have arrived yet**: Publish without the institutional validation. The research quality stands independently. Do not fabricate or stretch any response characterization.

**Social media goal at T+3**: Establish public visibility so that when Batch 2 contacts receive their emails, they can verify the framework exists and is publicly available.

## T+WEEK 1 (Days 4-10): Response Aggregation and Batch 2

### Response Aggregation Protocol (Days 4-7)

Check email tracking (if enabled) every 24 hours for Batch 1:
- **Open rate check**: How many of 5 emails have been opened? (Email tracking shows this if enabled.) If 0 of 5 have been opened by Day 4, consider whether the subject lines are the problem — the emails may be going to spam.
- **Reply tracking**: Log any replies immediately. Even a brief "thanks, I'll take a look" is a positive signal — the contact is aware of the research.
- **Forward indicators**: Some tracking tools show if the email was forwarded. Note this in the contact log.

**If Ryan Goodman responds with interest in a Just Security piece**: This is the highest-value Batch 1 response. Prioritize this engagement. Offer to submit Domain 28 or Domain 29 as a standalone analytical piece within 48 hours. Adjust Batch 2 messaging to reference "a forthcoming Just Security analysis of the framework."

**If Erica Chenoweth responds**: Reference in all subsequent Batch 2 and 3 emails. Her engagement with the resistance meta-analysis is the academic credibility signal that converts cold outreach into warm outreach.

**If Wendy Weiser / Brennan Center responds**: This is the most valuable institutional response for the legislative pathway. Follow up within 24 hours. The specific ask: feedback on Domains 1 and 6, and — once that relationship is established — an introduction to Senate Judiciary staff.

### Batch 2 Launch (Days 8-10)

Begin sending Batch 2 emails (8 contacts) across Days 8-10. Personalize each email to reference any Batch 1 responses that exist.

**If no Batch 1 responses exist by Day 8**: Still launch Batch 2. The absence of Batch 1 responses does not indicate failure — academic and policy professionals often respond 1-2 weeks after initial contact. Do not reference Batch 1 in Batch 2 emails if no responses exist. Send Batch 2 as the first-wave contact for those individuals.

**Batch 2 send order**:
- Day 8: Michael Waldman (Brennan Center), Phil Brest (ACS), Dick Durbin staff
- Day 9: Sheldon Whitehouse staff, Jack Balkin (Yale), Quinta Jurecic
- Day 10: Zack Beauchamp (Vox), Janai Nelson (NAACP LDF)

**Social media during Week 1**:
- Days 4-7: Respond to all substantive comments on the T+3 social media posts. Do not respond to low-effort comments. Respond with substance to anyone who engages with the research seriously.
- Day 7: Post a brief follow-up (X/Bluesky or Substack note) highlighting the 1-2 most interesting responses or questions raised by the community. This shows the research is being engaged with.

## T+WEEK 2 (Days 11-17): Batch 3, Media Outreach, and First Feedback Loop

### Feedback Loop Processing (Days 11-12)

Before launching Batch 3, process the Batch 1 and 2 feedback received to date:

**Assessment questions**:
1. What specific findings are Batch 1/2 contacts engaging with? (Note the specific domains and specific findings that generated questions or comments — these are signals about what is most resonant.)
2. Are any contacts flagging a specific weakness in the analysis? (If multiple contacts raise the same concern about the same domain, address it before Batch 3 launch.)
3. Has any contact shared the research with a named colleague? (If yes: log the colleague's name and institution; they may already be aware of the research before Batch 3 reaches them.)
4. Has Ryan Goodman or any Batch 2 media contact indicated they are planning to publish something? (If yes: coordinate timing so the Just Security piece or Vox article lands during or after Batch 3, not before — to maximize the "organic discovery" effect.)

**Messaging adjustment protocol**:
- If Batch 1/2 responses consistently engage with Domain X but not Domain Y: shift Batch 3 subject lines to lead with Domain X.
- If Batch 1/2 responses raise a consistent objection: add a brief acknowledgment of that objection in Batch 3 emails. ("I've received feedback from several contacts that the redistricting domain overstates the efficiency gap's portability to multi-member district contexts — I want to flag that in advance so you can read that section critically.")

### Batch 3 Launch (Days 15-17)

Send all 12 Batch 3 contacts across Days 15-17. By this point, there should be 1-5 Batch 1/2 responses to reference.

**Batch 3 send order** (follows tier-1-contact-batches.md):
- Day 15: Damon Hewitt (Lawyers' Committee), Amy Klobuchar staff (Senate Rules)
- Day 16: Virginia Kase Solomón (Common Cause), Anthony Romero (ACLU)

### Media Tier 2 Outreach Begins (Days 14-17)

While Batch 3 is in flight, begin media Tier 2 outreach:
- Day 14: Distribute press release to 3-4 Tier 2 media contacts (Washington Monthly, The Nation, NPR Politics desk)
- Day 15: Follow up on Quinta Jurecic and Zack Beauchamp (if no response to Batch 2)
- Day 16: Heather Cox Richardson — send a brief, specific email with the resistance meta-analysis section (not the full framework; her format is historical contextualization, not policy briefing)

**Heather Cox Richardson specific approach**: Richardson writes daily and does not respond to PR pitches. The correct approach: a brief, specific email from the researcher noting one specific historical parallel that her newsletter would contextualize — e.g., the comparison of the current moment to the period between Reconstruction's end and the Progressive Era. If she responds, offer the full framework. If she does not respond, she is not lost — she may discover the research through the academic network outreach.

### Media Coverage Tracking (Week 2)

Track:

- Reddit: upvotes, comments, cross-posts on each subreddit post
- X/Bluesky: retweets/reshares, quote replies, profile link clicks
- External: any publication or blog that mentions the framework (set up Google Alerts for the framework name and key domain terminology)

**Week 2 minimum viable target**: At least 1 external mention of the framework (blog post, newsletter, social media share from someone not in the contact list) beyond the social media posts themselves.

## T+WEEK 3 (Days 18-24): Tier 1 Completion and Tier 2 Wave Prep

### Tier 1 Completion and Assessment (Days 18-21)

Complete any remaining Batch 3 emails. By Day 21 (three weeks after T-Day 0), all 25 Tier 1 contacts have been reached.

**Tier 1 complete response assessment**:
- Count total responses (target: 8+ of 25, or 30%+)
- Count forwarding instances (target: 3+ of 25, or 12%+)
- Note which contacts showed highest engagement (these become the "inner circle" for ongoing communication)
- Note which contacts did not respond (these may be reached organically through Tier 2 as the framework circulates)

**If Tier 1 response is above 30%**: Proceed to Tier 2 launch immediately.

**If Tier 1 response is 20-29%**: Review messaging before Tier 2. The research may be landing, but the framing may be suboptimal. Hold Tier 2 for 3-5 days and adjust subject lines and opening paragraphs based on feedback from responses received.

**If Tier 1 response is below 20%**: Conduct a diagnostic before launching Tier 2 (see success-metrics.md for the diagnostic protocol).

### Tier 2 Wave Preparation (Days 21-24)

**Tier 2 contact list**: 40-50 contacts across the categories documented in policy-influencer-mapping.md's Tier 2 sections (academic researchers, media, legal journals, university programs). Do not contact all simultaneously.

**Tier 2 Wave 1 preparation** (for launch at T+Day 25):
- Select 15 Tier 2 contacts for Wave 1
- Prioritize those most likely to benefit from Tier 1 momentum (academics in the same institution as responding Tier 1 contacts; media outlets where Tier 1 contacts have relationships; law reviews whose faculty editors are in the Tier 1 contact list)
- Personalize templates for Wave 1 contacts

**Domain 37 targeted outreach prep** (Path A+Domain37 only):
- If Path A+Domain37: begin prep for Phase B election-protection organization outreach (see DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md)
- By Week 3, Phase A should have enough institutional credibility to launch Phase B
- Review the DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md decision-point criteria at Days 18-21

### Tier 3 Prep (Week 4)

**Week 4 Tier 3 prep actions**:

- Prepare domain-specific one-pagers for Tier 3 (labor reform for AFL-CIO/EPI; redistricting for NDRC/FairVote; civil rights for NAACP LDF — though LDF is Tier 1)
- Identify the EPI issue brief pathway — if Heidi Shierholz has engaged, the EPI publication pipeline is open; if not, the AFL-CIO labor union distribution will need to wait for EPI credentialing

### 30-Day Assessment (Day 28-30)

See success-metrics.md for the full 30-day assessment protocol. Key gates:

**Gate 1 — Tier 1 response rate**: Did 30%+ of Tier 1 contacts respond? (Required for Tier 2 full launch.)

**Gate 2 — At least 1 Tier 1 share**: Did at least one Tier 1 contact share the research with a named colleague from a different institution? (This is the network amplification trigger — it means the research is moving through networks, not just sitting in inboxes.)

**Gate 3 — Media pickup by Week 2**: Did any publication or platform mention the framework by Day 14? (This is the external validation signal.)

**If all three gates are met**: Proceed to full Tier 2 + Tier 3 launch. Phase 1 is on track.

**If Gate 2 is not met** (no Tier 1 sharing): The research is being received but not propagating. Review the Batch 1-3 email content for whether the "ask" is clear. It is possible the research needs a more specific forwarding request ("If this seems relevant to a colleague at [specific institution type], I would welcome an introduction").

**If Gate 3 is not met** (no external mention): Not necessarily a problem at Day 14. Some academic and policy research circulates slowly. Assess again at Day 28. If no external mention by Day 28, increase social media activity — the framework needs a public-facing hook that journalists can find.

**Path A**: Everything above executes as written. No Domain 37 hold.

**Path A+Domain37**: Everything above executes as written. Additionally: begin Domain 37 Phase B prep at Week 2, launch Phase B election-protection outreach at Week 3 (see DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md).

This creates a sustained engagement rationale — contacts who subscribe to the Substack or follow social channels see new content every 2-3 weeks.
