# Phase 1 Institutional Outreach Prioritization
## Domain 42: Drug Policy, Regulatory Capture, and Democratic Legitimacy
### DEA-1362 Hearing — May 28, 2026 Participation Notice Deadline

---

## Section 1: Domain 42 Strategic Context

**The core finding**: The DEA's June 29 hearing on marijuana rescheduling (Docket No. DEA-1362) is not primarily a drug policy proceeding. It is a structural case study in regulatory capture — a law enforcement agency adjudicating its own scheduling authority, using a participant selection mechanism that historically excludes reform-oriented voices while disproportionately admitting pharmaceutical and prohibitionist organizations. Domain 42's democratic exclusion analysis is the only sustained argument connecting these three mechanisms simultaneously: DEA regulatory capture of the scheduling process, felony disenfranchisement of the communities most subjected to drug enforcement, and the constitutional federalism failure in 24 states where democratic majorities have enacted cannabis legalization that federal law continues to override.

**Why May 28 matters**: The Federal Register deadline for written participation notices to DEA-1362 is May 28, 2026. The operational electronic deadline is May 24, 2026 (11:59 p.m. ET). Mail notices must be postmarked by May 20. These are hard cutoffs. Organizations that do not file participation notices before these dates have no standing in the administrative record that the June 29–July 15 hearing will generate. No administrative standing means no influence on the DEA's selection of designated participants (notified June 22), no written testimony entered into the formal docket, and no basis for challenging the hearing's procedural design.

**What participation would achieve**: Even if the DEA selects none of the reform-oriented organizations that file — consistent with the 2024 pattern, where 160+ requestors produced only 25 selected participants, disproportionately anti-reform — the filing of participation notices by DPA, NORML, the ACLU, and the Sentencing Project creates a public administrative record. That record documents which organizations sought to participate and were excluded. Under Domain 42's Section 2 analysis, a selection outcome that excludes NORML, ACLU, and civil rights organizations while admitting pharmaceutical industry representatives is itself direct evidence of regulatory capture operating in real time. The record of exclusion is as usable as the record of participation.

**Timeline**: Today is May 9. The mail deadline is 11 days away (May 20). The electronic deadline is 15 days away (May 24). The nominal Federal Register deadline is 19 days away (May 28). Functional outreach — outreach that gives an institutional contact enough time to route internally, get authorization, and file — must reach its targets no later than May 18. Organizations receiving the Domain 42 briefing after May 18 have fewer than 6 business days to route and authorize. That is not a functional window for an institutional filer. The active outreach window is May 9 through May 18. Nine days.

## Section 2: Contact Prioritization Framework

### Scoring Methodology

Four factors determine which organizations receive priority outreach and in what sequence. Each factor is scored 1–3.

**Factor A — Institutional Authority Over Target Organizations**: Does this organization have formal standing or documented institutional leverage over the DEA proceeding? Score 3 = prior filing and selection in CSA scheduling proceedings; 2 = prior filing without selection, or equivalent federal proceeding experience; 1 = no prior DEA engagement.

**Factor B — Existing Relationships With Likely Testifiers**: Does this organization have working relationships with organizations already likely to file for DEA-1362? Score 3 = documented coordination history with DPA, NORML, or state AG cannabis coalitions; 2 = adjacent federal policy network overlap; 1 = no documented relationship with likely testifiers.

**Factor C — Media and Public Amplification Potential**: If this organization files and cites Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing, does it have communication infrastructure to amplify the filing publicly? Score 3 = large membership base or subscriber list (100,000+), active media relationships, prior media coverage of DEA proceedings; 2 = moderate amplification capacity; 1 = limited public communication infrastructure.

**Factor D — Legal and Advocacy Bandwidth**: Does this organization have staff capacity to receive a cold briefing, understand an 8,000-word research document, and authorize a formal administrative filing within the 9-day active window? Score 3 = dedicated policy or legal team with DEA filing experience; 2 = policy team with adjacent federal proceeding experience; 1 = limited bandwidth (academic, state government).

**Total score** = A + B + C + D (max 12).

NORML's deputy director has already publicly criticized the DEA's 2024 participant selection pattern as biased toward anti-reform organizations; Domain 42's Section 2 framing is analytical support for an argument NORML may already be preparing, which reduces activation cost to near zero.

NAACP LDF and state AG offices are scored down on bandwidth despite high institutional authority. Both require multi-layer internal routing that consumes 7–10 business days. Given the active outreach window of May 9–18, organizations with slow routing paths must receive earlier, not later, outreach — and should receive a compressed, directive briefing rather than the full research document.

## Section 3: Email Sequencing Strategy

### Day 0 — May 9 (Initial Briefing Send)

**Recipients**: Drug Policy Alliance, NORML, ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project, The Sentencing Project.

**Specific ask**: File a written participation notice with DEA-1362 before the May 24 electronic deadline (May 28 is the Federal Register date; electronic filing closes May 24 at 11:59 p.m. ET). Domain 42 provides the democratic exclusion framing that makes your participation notice analytically distinct from what pharmaceutical companies and prohibitionist medical groups will file.

**Send order**: DPA first (9:00 AM ET), NORML second (9:45 AM ET), ACLU third (10:30 AM ET), Sentencing Project fourth (11:15 AM ET). All four are Eastern time. Stagger to avoid batch-send delivery patterns.

**Attachments**: Domain 42 markdown document and the submission guide (`domain-42-may-28-dea-submission-guide.md`). Include the corrected deadline language explicitly in the body: *"The Federal Register deadline is May 28, but electronic filings must be received by May 24. Mail notices must be postmarked by May 20."*

**Key framing hook for May 9 sends**: NORML's 2024 exclusion from the withdrawn proceeding is relevant. The resubmission note should appear in DPA and NORML sends: *"Participation requests from the 2024 hearing proceedings were voided when those proceedings were withdrawn. Organizations that filed then must resubmit a fresh notice for DEA-1362."*

### Days 1–2 — May 10–11 (Second Wave)

**Recipients**: Law Enforcement Action Partnership, NAACP Legal Defense Fund, Marijuana Policy Project, Mason Marks (Yale Law / confirm current affiliation before sending).

**Specific ask (LEAP)**: LEAP's law enforcement voice carries unusual credibility in DEA proceedings. A LEAP participation notice framing the DEA hearing as a democratic accountability failure — using Domain 42 Section 3's felony disenfranchisement data — is a contribution the DEA will not easily exclude given its institutional deference to law enforcement organizational authority. Ask explicitly: does LEAP have capacity to file by May 24?

**Specific ask (NAACP LDF)**: Frame entirely around voting rights and democratic participation, not drug policy. Section 3 of Domain 42 is the entry point: the felony disenfranchisement feedback loop connecting drug enforcement to political underrepresentation of the communities most subjected to that enforcement. LDF's mandate covers this directly. Do not lead with scheduling or cannabis policy.

**Specific ask (Mason Marks)**: Methodological peer outreach, not advocacy. Ask whether Domain 42's regulatory capture analysis extends his Yale Law Journal "Separation of Drug Scheduling Powers" argument correctly. If he is planning to testify or file comments, Domain 42's democratic exclusion framing complements his statutory analysis. Confirm current institutional email before sending (he may have moved from Yale Law School).

**Social proof insertion (conditional)**: If DPA or NORML has responded positively by May 10, add one sentence to each May 10–11 send: *"DPA and NORML have both reviewed this briefing and are considering DEA-1362 participation."* Do not include this sentence unless it is factually accurate.

### Days 3–5 — May 12–14 (Secondary Stakeholders and Confirmation Loop)

**Recipients**: California AG, Colorado AG, Brennan Center for Justice (Democracy Program), Americans for Safe Access, Prison Policy Initiative.

**Specific ask (state AGs)**: Does the AG office plan to file hearing comments or a participation notice for DEA-1362? Domain 42 Section 4 provides the federalism framing. California's 280E exposure ($2.24B in estimated annual excess taxes, Whitney Economics) and Colorado's status as the first recreational legalization state (Amendment 64, 2012, 55.32%) are the most concrete harms to document. Send a compressed brief, not the full document — AG policy staff need a 300-word summary and the submission procedure, not a 6,800-word research document.

**Specific ask (Brennan Center)**: If Brennan Center is already receiving Phase 1 general outreach, add a single paragraph to that send rather than a separate email. Their criminal justice and democracy intersection program covers Domain 42 Section 3 directly. Flag the DEA hearing deadline specifically.

**Confirmation loop (May 12–14)**: Check for responses from May 9 sends. If DPA or NORML have not responded by May 13, trigger the follow-up protocol: one follow-up email to each, subject line `[Organization] — following up, Domain 42 DEA-1362 briefing, X days to electronic deadline (May 24)`. Secondary contact for DPA is the Executive Director; for NORML, the legal committee chair or executive director.

**Hard deadline for follow-ups**: Send all remaining follow-up communications by May 14. Do not initiate new organizational contact after May 18.

### Days 14–19 — May 23–28 (Final Logistics and Testimony Support)

**Purpose**: Not new outreach. Support organizations that have committed to filing.

**Activities**:
- Provide testimony drafting support for committed organizations using the three worked templates in `domain-42-dea-hearing-tactical-guide.md`
- Confirm that participation notice language is factually accurate before filing (docket number, submission address, deadline)
- For organizations that have committed to filing: confirm electronic submission to nprm@dea.gov with subject line referencing "Docket No. DEA-1362"
- Document filing dates and confirmation details in WORKLOG.md as they arrive

**Post-May 28 (testimony preparation, June 15 deadline)**: Organizations that filed participation notices by May 28 have until June 15 to file formal written testimony (14 days before the hearing opens June 29). Offer testimony drafting coordination using the institutional coordination protocols in Part 4 of the tactical guide: assign non-overlapping focus areas across participating organizations to prevent testimony duplication.

## Section 5: Success Metrics and Contingency

### Primary Metrics (May 9 – May 24)

| Organizations committing to file DEA-1362 participation notice | 2 | 4 | 7 |

**Minimum viable outcome**: 2 organizations file participation notices before May 24, including at least one of DPA or NORML (who would likely file regardless) and at least one that represents a new analytical dimension — specifically ACLU or Sentencing Project. The ACLU participation notice framing the DEA hearing as a democratic participation and voting rights issue is the single highest-value outcome from this outreach; it introduces the only argument that connects drug scheduling to voter suppression in the formal administrative record.

**Secondary metric**: Track counterfactual filings separately. DPA and NORML filings represent baseline hearing participation that would occur without this outreach. ACLU, Sentencing Project, and LEAP filings are directly attributable to Domain 42 distribution. Report the two categories separately in WORKLOG.md.

### Downstream Metrics (June 22 – July 15)

DEA notifies selected participants June 22. Log which Domain 42-briefed organizations were selected and which were excluded. An exclusion list that includes ACLU, Sentencing Project, and LEAP while selecting pharmaceutical representatives is itself documentable evidence of regulatory capture for subsequent advocacy use.

After June 29: check DEA-1362 docket for written testimony submissions. Verify whether Domain 42 Section 3 ("felony disenfranchisement feedback loop") language appears in any filed testimony. Track via Google Alert on "DEA-1362 democratic exclusion" and "DEA regulatory capture hearing."

### Contingency: If Timeline Slips

**If no response from DPA or NORML by May 13**: Activate secondary contact escalation path — DPA Executive Director, NORML legal committee chair. Simultaneously redirect emphasis to ACLU and Sentencing Project, which are the highest-impact counterfactual organizations. Their filing matters more than a DPA/NORML filing because DPA and NORML would likely file regardless.

**If the DEA cancels or freezes the hearing (Section 591 congressional rider)**: Organizations that have already filed participation notices by May 24 retain administrative standing for any reconstituted proceeding. The filings and any exclusion decisions remain in the public record and are usable for subsequent legislative advocacy. Distribute Domain 42 to the secondary academic and Substack audience post-May 28 for longer-term policy framing — the democratic design argument does not expire with the DEA hearing deadline.

**If no organization commits to filing by May 16**: The May 18 hard stop on outreach is firm. Pivot to written testimony support for any organization that filed independently, using Domain 42 as a citation resource rather than an outreach anchor.
