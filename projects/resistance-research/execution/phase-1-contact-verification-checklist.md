# Phase 1 Contact Verification Checklist

**Purpose**: Ensure all contacts are current and reachable before any email is sent. A bounced email to a stale address, or an email to a contact who has changed roles, creates a negative first impression that cannot be undone.

**Scope by path**:
- Path A: Complete Section 1 (Batch 1) + Section 2 (remaining Tier 1, Batches 2-3) — 2 hours
- Path A+37: Complete Sections 1-3 — 2.5 hours
- Path B: Complete Sections 1-4 — 3-4 hours (split across 2 days)

**Can run in parallel**: Contact verification can run while you are deciding path and creating Gists. You do not need to have made your path decision before starting this checklist.

---

## Verification Standard

For each contact, you are verifying three things:

1. **Position currency**: The contact is still at the listed organization in the listed role
2. **Email currency**: The email address is still valid for that organization
3. **Domain relevance**: The contact's current work still connects to the domain(s) you will pitch

**Do not send without verifying position currency.** Academics change institutions, advocacy leaders move between organizations, and journalists change beats. A well-personalized email to the wrong institutional address — or to someone who has changed roles — signals carelessness.

## Email Validation Note

If you have access to an email verification tool (Hunter.io offers bulk verification of up to 50 emails on a free plan), you can use it to validate email address formats in bulk before sending. This catches format errors (typos, old domain names) without triggering actual sends.

**How to use Hunter.io bulk verify**:
1. Go to hunter.io/email-verifier
2. Export your contact list emails to a CSV (one email per row)
3. Upload to Hunter.io's bulk verifier
4. Download results — any "invalid" or "risky" status means verify manually

This is optional but useful for Tier 2 and Tier 3 sends where you cannot check every contact manually.
