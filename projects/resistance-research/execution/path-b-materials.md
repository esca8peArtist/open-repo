# Path B Distribution Materials

**April 27, 2026**

Path B differs from Path A in what is distributed at launch — not the entire 35-domain framework simultaneously, but an initial core release with additional Phase 2 expansion domains published on a rolling basis (2-3 week intervals). Path B positions the framework as "ongoing research in progress" rather than "complete framework released." This creates sustained engagement momentum but requires a different messaging architecture and a different cadence commitment.

---

## Path B Core Distinction

**The sustained engagement argument**: Policy and academic audiences have limited bandwidth for large single releases. A single 200-page framework published at once may generate a burst of attention followed by silence. A rolling release — one new domain or domain cluster every 2-3 weeks — creates a publication rhythm that academic and media contacts can follow over months. Substack serial publication, social media updates, and new email outreach with each release maintain the research's presence in the policy discourse without requiring all contacts to engage with the full framework at once.

**The trade-off**: Rolling release sacrifices the "comprehensive diagnostic" credibility signal that Path A achieves at launch. If the goal is to land with maximum institutional impact in a short window — especially given the 2026 midterm timeline — Path A delivers that more forcefully. Path B is the right choice if the user intends to continue producing research (Phase 2 domains are actively in development) and wants the distribution to reflect ongoing scholarship rather than a final product.

**What "Phase 2 expansion" means**: See phase-2-expansion-candidates.md and phase-2-expansion-roadmap.md for the domains under development. These are the domains that extend the 35-domain base framework — additional international comparative cases, deeper domain analysis, new domain areas not covered in the base framework. Path B distributes Phase 2 domains as they are completed, creating a 6-12 month publication schedule.

## Path B Messaging Architecture

### Primary Frame: "Ongoing Framework Deepening"

"This is not a document that was written and published. It is a research program. The base 35 domains document the foundational structural analysis. The Phase 2 domains extend that analysis into [new areas] as the research develops. Subscribing to the research means receiving new analysis as it is produced."

This framing does two things: (1) it positions the researcher as a continuing research presence, not a one-time document publisher, and (2) it creates a reason for contacts to maintain engagement over months rather than filing the framework and returning to it only when needed.

### Secondary Frame: "Sustained Engagement for Long-Term Impact"

"Democratic backsliding research without ongoing monitoring is obsolete in months. The framework is designed to be updated — new court decisions, new legislative developments, new international case studies — as the situation evolves. The Phase 2 expansion is part of that ongoing commitment."

Use this frame with academic contacts who evaluate research quality partly by its currency. The Poland 2023-2026 case study in the activation architecture is a recent example — it was added in April 2026 because the Hungarian election happened in April 2026. Path B's rolling release model institutionalizes this kind of timely updating.

### Subscriber-Building Frame

Path B is the distribution path that most benefits from Substack as a primary channel. The serial publication model converts email contacts into subscribers, and subscribers into a durable audience that receives every subsequent release automatically. The specific pitch to Tier 1 contacts: "I'm releasing domains on a rolling schedule — if you subscribe [link], you'll receive each new domain as it's published. That may be more manageable than receiving the full 35-domain framework at once."

## Path B Timeline

### T-Day 0: Core Release Launch

The base 35-domain framework is distributed to Batch 1 contacts (or a curated subset — see Path B batch note below). The Substack post is the executive summary, positioned as "the overview before the serial publication begins."

**Path B batch note**: If Path B is selected specifically to allow more time to refine messaging and build credibility before full distribution, Batch 1 may be limited to 3 contacts rather than 5. This is a judgment call — the contacts most likely to engage seriously with the framework are still the right first contacts regardless of path.

**T-Day 0 Substack post framing** (Path B version):

Title: "A 35-Domain Framework for Democratic Renewal — Beginning the Serial Publication"

Opening: "This newsletter begins a serialized publication of a two-year research project: a 35-domain framework for democratic structural reform, grounded in 160+ movement case studies and comparative constitutional law from 40+ functioning democracies. I'll be publishing one domain per week, with Phase 2 expansion domains added as they are completed. The executive summary is below; the first full domain analysis (Domain 1: Electoral Reform) will follow next week."

**What this accomplishes**: Sets subscriber expectations (weekly publication), signals long-term commitment, and gives casual readers the executive summary as a complete experience while giving serious readers a reason to subscribe for the serial.

### T+Week 1-3: Weeks 1-3 Execute as Path A

The Tier 1 contact batching, sequencing, and outreach described in tier-1-contact-batches.md and distribution-sequence.md applies to Path B without modification. The difference is in what is framed as "in progress" vs. "complete."

**Messaging adjustment for Batch 1-3**: Replace "The complete 35-domain framework is available at [link]" with "The 35-domain framework is available in full at [link], with Phase 2 expansion domains being published on a rolling schedule over the coming months. If you prefer to follow the research as it develops, [Substack link] is the easiest way to stay current."

### T+Weeks 4-6: Phase 2 Domain Release 1

At approximately T+3 weeks (25-28 days after launch), publish the first Phase 2 expansion domain. This is the first release after the initial base framework.

**Phase 2 Release cadence**:
- Release 1 (T+Week 4): First Phase 2 domain (see phase-2-expansion-candidates.md for which domain is first)
- Release 2 (T+Week 6-7): Second Phase 2 domain or domain cluster
- Release 3 (T+Week 9): Third Phase 2 domain — by this point, Tier 2 outreach is active, and each new release is an occasion to re-engage contacts who have not responded

**Per-release outreach protocol**: Each Phase 2 domain release is an occasion for a brief re-engagement email to contacts who have received the base framework but not responded. Subject line: "New domain added to the framework: [Domain Name] — [one-sentence summary of what it covers]." This is a brief note, not a full re-pitch. It gives non-respondents a second touch point without being repetitive.

### T+Weeks 6-10: Tier 2 + Tier 3 Launch

Same as Path A/A+Domain37, with the rolling release as a momentum-maintenance mechanism. Each Phase 2 domain release provides a fresh hook for media and academic outreach.

## Path B Momentum Maintenance

The primary risk of Path B is momentum loss between releases. The base framework launches with a burst, then the next contact with each audience member is 2-3 weeks away. The mechanics for maintaining momentum between releases:

**1. Social media between releases**: Post domain-specific threads on X/Bluesky every 5-7 days, drawing from existing domain content. Each post is a fresh hook into the framework without requiring a new domain release. Format: "One finding from Domain [X]: [specific finding with number]. Here's the primary documentation: [link]."

**2. Substack supplementary posts**: Between full domain releases, publish brief "research notes" — 300-500 words on a specific development that connects to an existing domain. These are not new domains; they are updates that demonstrate ongoing monitoring. Example: "A court filed a ruling yesterday in the SPLC case — here is what it means for Domain 29's prosecutorial accountability analysis."

**3. Tier 2 re-engagement with each release**: As noted above, each Phase 2 release is a re-engagement opportunity for any Tier 1/2 contact who has not responded. Never use the same subject line twice; always attach a new piece of information.

## Path B Target Media Outlets

Same as Path A Priority 1/2/3 with one addition:

**Substack-specific pitch**: Letters from an American (Heather Cox Richardson) and comparable history-focused newsletters are more likely to engage with Path B's serial publication format than with a single large release. The pitch: "I'm serializing a 35-domain framework one domain per week — if any domain aligns with a historical parallel you're contextualizing, the underlying research notes are available."

**Magazine long-form**: Washington Monthly, The Atlantic, and The New Republic are more receptive to a "this researcher is producing ongoing work" pitch than a "here is a complete document" pitch. Path B positions the researcher as a policy research presence, not just a document publisher.

## Path B Academic Journal Targets

Same as Path A, with added value for:

**Serialized academic venue**: Some law reviews publish "ongoing forum" pieces where a researcher contributes multiple shorter analyses over a year. Path B's rolling release model maps naturally to this format. Contact Columbia Law Review's Constitutional Democracy Initiative or Harvard Law Review's online forum about a "series" of domain analyses rather than a single submission.

**Conference presentations**: APSA annual convention (August 2026, Philadelphia) accepts proposals for panels and papers. Path B's rolling release schedule means new Phase 2 domains may be ready in time for APSA submission. A conference paper derived from Phase 2 domains extends the academic credibility in a setting where Tier 2 academic contacts (Levitsky, Chenoweth, Fung, Allen) are physically present.

## Path B Social Channels

**Substack**: The primary channel for Path B. Serial publication at 1 domain per week gives subscribers a regular reason to open the newsletter. Track subscriber count, open rate per issue, and reader-to-referral conversion. At 500 subscribers with a 40% open rate, the newsletter is a credible research publication in its own right.

**Reddit**: Path B allows for more sustained Reddit presence. Post one domain thread per week, timed to coincide with the Substack publication. This cross-platform reinforcement drives traffic from Reddit to Substack.

**X/Bluesky**: Post 2-3 times per week — one post coinciding with each Substack publication, and 1-2 standalone finding posts from existing domain content between releases.

## Path B Notes

- Path B requires a publishing commitment: if you select this path, the rolling release schedule must be maintained. Contacts who subscribe for the serial will disengage if releases are irregular or stop.
- The Phase 2 expansion domains must be confirmed as ready before their scheduled release dates. Do not announce a release date you cannot meet.
- Path B does not preclude Path A distribution of the base 35-domain framework. The base framework is still fully available; Path B simply adds the rolling Phase 2 releases on top.
- If Path B generates sufficient engagement in the first 6 weeks, the user may choose to transition to a hybrid (Path A posture for the base framework, Path B posture for Phase 2 additions). That transition is a decision for the next session review.
