**Domain 37 handling**: Included in the general 35-domain send. All contacts receive it in the full proposal Gist without specialized framing.

**Domain 42 (DEA) coverage**: Included for contacts in Category A-D drug policy sub-batch (10 contacts, `domain-42-contact-list.md`). Batch 1 of Domain 42 sub-batch sends May 8-10 (independently of main Tier 1 sequence). May 28 deadline met.

- Fastest path to institutional reach
- Single coherent send event — no sequencing overhead

**Best for**: Users who have established relationships with Democracy Docket, Campaign Legal Center, or state AG election protection staff, and can contextualize Domain 37 in a follow-up conversation. Users for whom speed is the primary constraint.

**Best for**: Most users. Particularly if election-protection contacts are not already personal relationships and a tailored first impression matters more than a same-day send.

**Domain 37 handling**: Included in Cluster 1 (Voting), distributed to all tiers during their respective wave.

**Domain 42 (DEA) coverage**: The May 28 DEA deadline is met for Tier 1 (sent May 15-21). Tier 2 contacts (drug policy sub-batch) receive Domain 42 May 25 – June 7 — the May 28 deadline applies to them but the outreach timing is very tight (3 days before deadline). **Advantages**:
- Broadest reach (150+ contacts vs. 25-37 for Paths A/A+37)
- Customizable per tier — each tier receives the domain cluster most relevant to their work

- Narrative clarity is lower — no single "launch moment" that concentrates media and institutional attention

**Best for**: Users with a specific strategic reason to delay (pending publication partnership, personal availability, specific contact timing windows not visible in the research record), or users who have already built Tier 1 relationships through other channels and want to use this framework primarily for Tier 2/3 mobilization.
