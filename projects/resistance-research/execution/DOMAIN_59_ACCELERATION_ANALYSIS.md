## Part 1: Senate Finance Committee Window — Actual June 2026 Status

### What the Research Reveals

The premise that a Senate Finance Committee CTC markup is "happening NOW in June 2026" is not supported by the evidence. The actual legislative situation is:

**The OBBBA is already law.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act passed the Senate 51-50 on July 1, 2025, passed the House 218-214 on July 3, 2025, and was signed by President Trump on July 4, 2025. That law's CTC provisions: increased the maximum credit from $2,000 to $2,200 per child for 2025, made the $1,700 refundable portion permanent (inflation-indexed), but crucially did not improve the credit's refundability structure for low-income families. CBPP estimates 19 million children — more than one in four children under 17 — will receive less than the full credit in 2026 or no credit at all because their families earn too little.

**The only confirmed Senate Finance Committee activity in early June 2026** is a June 3 hearing on the FY2027 Budget for the Department of the Treasury, with Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent testifying. No CTC markup is on the calendar. The committee's next formal procedural vehicle for new tax legislation would be a FY2027 reconciliation bill, which Republicans could pursue starting October 1, 2026 (start of FY2027), if they choose to use the reconciliation process again.

**What legislative activity does exist:**
- S.1393 (American Family Act) was introduced in the 119th Congress by Senators Warner, Kaine, and 43 colleagues. It would increase the CTC to $6,360 for newborns, $4,320 for ages 1-6, and $3,600 for ages 6-17, and eliminate low-income refundability restrictions. It is referred to the Senate Finance Committee. Under current Republican majority, no floor vote is expected.
- H.R. 8305 (Working Parents Tax Relief Act of 2026), a Democratic House bill, would increase the EITC by up to 42 percentage points for families with children under age 4. Approximately 10 million adults and 8 million children would benefit. No Senate companion vote scheduled.
- The April 15, 2026 EITC/CTC package was covered by CNBC — confirming active Democratic legislative activity but zero passage prospects in this Congress.

**Key decision-makers:**
- Chairman Mike Crapo (R-ID) — has focused on permanent TCJA extension and IRS modernization; not a CTC expansion champion
- Ranking Member Ron Wyden (D-OR) — a consistent advocate for low-income CTC refundability; is the legislative vehicle for any Democratic CTC push
