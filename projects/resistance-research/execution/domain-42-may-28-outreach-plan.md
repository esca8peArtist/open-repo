## Executive Summary

This plan operationalizes the Domain 42 contact list and email templates for immediate execution on May 8. It is designed to be **path-independent**: the minimal viable execution path (Section 4) requires only an email client and the Domain 42 markdown document, no Gist or Phase 1 infrastructure. The plan tiers the 10 organizations by influence on DEA hearing outcomes, sequences outreach to match each tier's decision window, defines measurable success signals, and specifies contingency triggers for every critical dependency.

**Primary goal**: At least 3 organizations file DEA-1362 participation notices before May 28.  
**Stretch goal**: 2 organizations draft substantive hearing comments (3–5 page position papers) that cite Domain 42.

## Section 1: Prioritization — Which Contacts Move the Needle

Not all 10 organizations are equal. Two factors determine priority: (1) **DEA access** — has the organization previously filed CSA comments or participated in DEA administrative proceedings? (2) **Network force-multiplier** — can a single contact decision activate a coalition or a large advocacy infrastructure?

### Tier 1 — Must Reach by May 15

These four organizations have direct DEA proceeding experience, established internal policy teams capable of filing a participation notice within 10 business days of receiving briefing materials, and the organizational credibility to shift the hearing's analytical framing.

| Org | Why Tier 1 | DEA Access Signal | Template |
|-----|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Drug Policy Alliance (A-1)** | Largest drug policy reform org; has filed CSA comments in every major scheduling proceeding since 2002; staff includes former DEA analysts and congressional aides | DPA filed in the 2023–2024 marijuana rescheduling comment period; staff familiar with DEA-1362 docket | D42-A |
| **NORML (A-3)** | Has filed in every CSA scheduling proceeding since 1972; NORML's legal committee maintains standing before DEA ALJs; highest institutional familiarity with DEA procedural requirements | NORML filed a 47-page comment in the 2023 rescheduling process; likely already preparing DEA-1362 participation | D42-A |
| **ACLU Criminal Law Reform Project (B-1)** | ACLU has standing before federal agencies; Criminal Law Reform Project's mandate covers both enforcement disparities and democratic participation; Domain 42's Section 3 (disenfranchisement feedback loop) is directly within their current litigation and advocacy lane | ACLU filed in the 2023 rescheduling period; can file as part of an existing advocacy coalition | D42-B |
| **The Sentencing Project (B-3)** | Their own data ("Locked Out 2022", "Voting Rights in the Era of Mass Incarceration") is already cited in Domain 42; they have staff capacity to produce written hearing comments in under two weeks; Domain 42 extends their existing analytical framework | The Sentencing Project has participated in federal sentencing and drug policy proceedings; research staff can produce comment documents quickly | D42-B |

**Why Tier 1 must be reached by May 15**: These organizations need approximately 10 business days to route an external briefing through policy staff, obtain organizational sign-off on filing, and prepare a substantive participation notice. May 15 to May 28 is 13 days — the functional minimum. Earlier contact gives time for a follow-up if there is no response.

## Section 3: Contingency Planning

### Contingency A — Phase 1 Path Decision Delays Past May 8

**Trigger**: User has not decided on Path A / A+37 / B by the morning of May 8.

**Minimal viable execution path**: No Gist creation required. All four email templates support direct-attachment delivery — replace the [GIST URL] placeholder with "attached." The Domain 42 markdown file is readable in any text editor or GitHub Gist viewer and is 6,700 words (roughly 27 pages). The submission guide (`domain-42-may-28-dea-submission-guide.md`) can be attached as a second file.

**What this path requires**: An email client, the Domain 42 markdown file, and the four category-specific templates from `domain-42-email-template.md`. No Gist, no platform infrastructure, no Phase 1 path decision. The outreach plan is self-contained.

**Path decision does not gate this campaign**: Domain 42 outreach is a separate sub-batch, not part of Batch 1–3. It does not depend on the Tier 1 contact batches or the general Phase 1 launch. Execute this plan on May 8 regardless of the Phase 1 path decision.

### Contingency B — No Response from Tier 1 Contacts by May 14

**Trigger**: By May 14, zero substantive responses from DPA, NORML, ACLU, and Sentencing Project.

**Escalation path**:
1. Send a second follow-up to DPA and NORML specifically — these are the two organizations most likely to file in a DEA proceeding. Subject line: `Direct ask — will [Org] file a DEA-1362 participation notice? Domain 42 framing available`
2. Identify secondary contacts within each organization: DPA's executive director (Maria McFarland Sánchez-Moreno), NORML's legal committee chair. These are findable via organizational website staff directories. A more direct contact path increases response probability.
3. Activate the SSDP and LEAP contacts as referral nodes: ask them explicitly to forward Domain 42 to their contacts at DPA or NORML if they have existing relationships.
4. Contact the Ohio State Moritz DEPC if not yet sent: their academic voice may provide a bridge contact to the advocacy organizations.

### Contingency C — Domain 42 Is Folded Into Phase 1 General Distribution

**Trigger**: User decides that Domain 42 should be included in the general Phase 1 Batch distribution rather than as a standalone sub-batch.

**Handoff protocol**:
1. Domain 42 sub-batch contacts are **not** in Batch 1–3. They must be appended as a separate track in the distribution master, not merged into the existing contact batches.
2. The Domain 42 templates (D42-A through D42-D) are distinct from the Batch 1–3 templates and must not be replaced by the general templates.
3. The May 28 deadline urgency language must be preserved verbatim in any handoff. The Batch 1–3 templates do not contain deadline language — do not let the general templates absorb the Domain 42 contacts without preserving the deadline frame.
4. Handoff note for any outreach team: "These 10 organizations require category-specific templates and wave sequencing as documented in `domain-42-email-template.md` and `domain-42-contact-list.md`. The May 21 hard stop applies regardless of general Phase 1 cadence."
