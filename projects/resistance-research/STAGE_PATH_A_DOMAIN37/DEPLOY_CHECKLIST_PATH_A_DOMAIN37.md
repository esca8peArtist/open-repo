## Phase B Decision Point: Week 8

Before launching Domain 37 targeted outreach, assess all three conditions:

**Condition 1 (Credibility)**: Have at least 2-3 institutional responses arrived from law schools, think tanks, or civil rights organizations? Even one response citing the research qualifies. This is your credibility signal — election-protection organizations receive political research constantly; prior institutional engagement is the differentiator.

**Condition 2 (Window)**: Are any Domain 37 advocacy windows still open?
- May 30, 2026: DOJ consent decrees finalized — first advocacy window
- June 30, 2026: Emergency EO routing window
- August 7, 2026: Primary election stress-testing — SAVE Act error rates
- September 2026: Pre-election litigation positioning
- October 2026: Poll observer deployment and real-time monitoring

If you are launching Phase A in late May or after, the May 30 window may already be past.
Start Phase B with whichever window is next open and frame outreach accordingly.

**Condition 3 (Timing)**: Has Phase A been live for at least 4 weeks? If yes, proceed.
If no, wait — better to arrive with credibility than to cold-pitch Domain 37.

**If all three conditions are met**: Begin Phase B Tier 1 outreach in Week 9.
**If Condition 1 is not met**: Defer Phase B by 2 weeks; continue building Phase A momentum.
**If all windows are past October 2026**: Domain 37 outreach is still valuable for 2027+ election infrastructure work; reframe as post-election accountability research.
