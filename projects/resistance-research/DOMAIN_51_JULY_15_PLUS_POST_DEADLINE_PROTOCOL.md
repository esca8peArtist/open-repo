## Part 1 — Framing Rules for All July 9+ Sends

### Remove These Phrases Entirely

Do not use any of the following in subject lines or email bodies:

- "July 1 deadline"
- "California Fair Elections Act messaging window" or "messaging integration"
- "California Fair Elections Act advocacy deadline"
- "SB 42 urgency" (SB 42 itself is still active, but not as a deadline hook)
- Any date reference implying a missed deadline

### Use These Phrases Instead

**Legislative window framing** (use in opening sentences):
- "With Congress in active session through August 10..."
- "With the DISCLOSE Act of 2026 (S.3991) pending Senate committee markup..."
- "During the active legislative window for campaign finance reform (through August 10)..."

**State ballot measure framing** (use to establish relevance):
- "Montana I-194 is on the 2026 ballot — the corporate charter theory your organization may be asked to assess..."
- "Hawaii SB 2471 (signed May 15, 2026) is the first implementation of the charter-revocation theory in a post-Citizens United framework..."
- "California SB 42 (Fair Elections Act) is on the November 2026 ballot..."

**FEC enforcement framing** (use for policy organizations):
- "The FEC enforcement quorum has been collapsed for 200+ days — Trump's two nominations remain pending Senate confirmation..."
- "The enforcement gap is ongoing and accelerating into the 2026 election cycle..."

**Academic framing** (use for Hasen, Election Law Journal, Brennan Center scholars):
- "The fall 2026 law review submission cycle begins in August..."
- "The Hawaii/Montana corporate charter theory has not yet received extensive academic treatment..."

## Part 4 — Hard Stop and Post-August Protocol

**Hard stop date**: August 8, 2026. No new sends after this date.

**Rationale**: Congress recesses August 10. Policy organizations' Hill-adjacent staff shift to recess mode. Research submissions sent August 8-31 will sit in unread queues. The effort is wasted and may create a negative first impression if followed up in September.

**If any sends are incomplete by August 8**:
- Stop. Record which sends are outstanding in DOMAIN_51_DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md.
- Resume September 14 (first full week post-Labor Day recess; Congress returns from recess September 11).
- Use the same templates without modification. Add one sentence: "I am following up on research I shared in [month] — with Congress returning from recess, the DISCLOSE Act markup window reopens..."
- Do not acknowledge the delay in the opening. Lead with the research.

**September 14 send priority** (if resuming post-recess):
1. Any outstanding Wave 1-3 contacts that were not reached
2. Academic contacts (Hasen, Election Law Journal) — fall term is peak for law review engagement
3. Do not re-send to contacts who have already received the research

## Part 5 — Signal Monitoring for Late Sends

T+7 checkpoint applies regardless of send date. If you send July 15, T+7 is July 22. If you send July 29, T+7 is August 5.

Because Branch B/C sends are close to the August 10 recess, some T+7 windows will fall during or after the recess. This is expected and does not invalidate the research distribution — it means the response monitoring window extends into September.

**Log all replies in DOMAIN_51_DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md regardless of when they arrive.** A September reply from CLC to a July 15 email is still a STRONG signal.

Signal definitions (unchanged from original):
- STRONG: Named reply from a staff person; substantive content (even one sentence); request for follow-up or additional materials
- MODERATE: Auto-acknowledgment with named contact; out-of-office with a specific staff person's name
- WEAK: Form acknowledgment with no named contact
- NONE: No reply by Day 14 (count from send date)
