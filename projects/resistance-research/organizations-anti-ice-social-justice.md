### 1.1 AMERICAN CIVIL LIBERTIES UNION (ACLU)
**Website:** https://www.aclu.org
**Grassroots arm:** https://peoplepower.aclu.org

**What they do:**
- Filed 230+ legal actions against the Trump administration in 2025 alone; 64% delayed, diluted, or defeated agenda items
- Blocked the executive order attempting to end birthright citizenship under the 14th Amendment
- Won two cases challenging use of the Alien Enemies Act — prevented deportations to El Salvador's CECOT prison
- Sued ICE for records on detention center expansion
- Operates in all 50 states through affiliates
- Advanced 78+ policy proposals in 43 states

**Activity level:** Extremely high. 2,175 staff, 7 million members/activists nationally.

**How to join:**
- **People Power Community Action Teams** — volunteer leadership; phone banks, local actions, deeper organizing. Sign up at act.aclu.org/a/join-our-volunteer-teams
- Take direct action at aclu.org/action (petitions, congressional contact)
- Sign the 2026 People Power Pledge
- Attend monthly People Power Action Calls

**Resources they have:** Massive legal infrastructure, national media platform, 50 state affiliates, strong budget.

**Resources they need:** Volunteer leaders at local level (People Power chapters vary enormously in strength); phone bankers; consistent event turnout.

**Notable recent actions:**
- Alien Enemies Act litigation — ongoing, multiple wins
- Blocked birthright citizenship EO
- Lawsuit blocking deportations to CECOT prison

**Website:** https://raicestexas.org
**Houston office:** 1900 Saint James Place, Ste. 450, Houston TX 77056 | (832) 699-6942 | Tue–Thu 9am–3pm

**Resources they need:** Funding (urgently — federal cuts devastated budget), volunteer attorneys, interpreters.

### What These Organizations Need Most Right Now (April 2026)

2. **Volunteer attorneys** — Every legal aid organization in Houston is overwhelmed. If you have a law license or are a law student, you are urgently needed.

3. **Bilingual volunteers (Spanish especially)** — Interpreters, community ambassadors, hotline operators.

4. **Consistent presence** — Organizations don't need people who sign one petition. They need people at meetings, phone banks, and actions, week after week.

5. **Know Your Rights outreach** — Help distribute materials and host workshops in immigrant communities.

6. **Court observers** — Present at immigration court hearings to document what happens and provide community support to detained individuals.
