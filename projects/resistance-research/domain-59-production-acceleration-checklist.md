## Cross-Domain Bridge Language (Pre-Written)

Paste directly into production at appropriate section close:

**For Section 2.1 (eviction pathway) → Domain 1 bridge:**
"The administrative mechanism driving this pathway — voter registration lapse after residential move — is directly addressed by Automatic Voter Registration, analyzed in depth in Domain 1. In 26 states without AVR, economic precarity automatically produces civic disenfranchisement through a procedural mechanism that has nothing to do with voter preference. The SAVE Act's documentary proof-of-citizenship requirement, also analyzed in Domain 1, falls hardest on the population most affected by housing instability — households that have moved frequently and are least likely to have maintained specific documentation in consistent form."

**For Section 2.2 (medical debt) → Domain 42 bridge:**
"Medical debt is one of several debt-coercion mechanisms producing the civic bandwidth depletion documented in this pathway. Legal financial obligations (LFOs) from criminal justice proceedings — the subject of Domain 42 — operate through the same cognitive bandwidth suppression mechanism documented in Mani et al. (2013). The debt-civic participation suppression pathway is general: it applies with equal force to medical creditors, student loan servicers, and criminal court debt collectors. Domain 42's analysis of LFOs and Domain 59's analysis of medical and student debt are mutually reinforcing applications of the same causal mechanism."

**For Section 3 (Midwest) → Domain 54 bridge:**
"The agricultural dimensions of Zone 5 economic precarity — specifically the 46 percent surge in Chapter 12 farm bankruptcies in 2025 and record USDA farm debt of $624.7 billion — are analyzed in depth in Domain 54. Domain 59 treats agricultural bankruptcy as producing the same civic consequences as urban eviction (geographic displacement, voter registration disruption, social capital erosion) but within communities whose entire institutional infrastructure depends on farm viability. The two analyses should be read together; Domain 54 provides the agricultural economic analysis, Domain 59 provides the civic participation consequence."

**For Section 5 (reform architecture) → Domain 22 bridge:**
"The racial dimension of economic precarity's civic consequences is analyzed in Domain 22. This domain does not duplicate that analysis; it provides the economic mechanism through which the structural racial inequalities Domain 22 documents translate into measurable participation gaps. Any reform architecture in Section 5 that addresses economic precarity will have a disproportionate positive civic participation effect for the Black, Hispanic, and Indigenous households that face the deepest income-wealth gaps. Economic floor reforms are not race-neutral in their civic consequences."
