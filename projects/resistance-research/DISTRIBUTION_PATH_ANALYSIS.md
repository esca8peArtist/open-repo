## What Has Changed Since May 5

Three material developments require updating the May 5 path analysis.

**Section 591 appropriations rider is resolved.** The rider that would have blocked DEA from implementing the rescheduling outcome was dropped from the final FY2027 CJS bill. It advanced through subcommittee 8-6 and was stripped before enactment — the same pattern as FY2026. Do not include Section 591 urgency in any Domain 42 outreach. The DEA June 29 hearing is proceeding on its original schedule. The May 28 participation deadline is unchanged and the sole urgency argument.

**Domain 42 Wave 1 was not sent May 8.** The WORKLOG contains no send confirmation. As of May 9, the wave is one day behind plan. The operational window is still viable (May 9–10), but any additional delay compresses margins — NAACP LDF's 10–14 day routing cycle means May 10 is the effective outside send date for that specific contact.

**Framework count is 47+ domains.** The May 5 analysis references a "35-domain framework." As of May 9, the production framework includes Domains 41b, 42, 43, 44 (two variants), 45 (two variants), 46, and 47, plus Domain 52 (Civil Society Suppression) and others documented in the WORKLOG. The Phase 1 distribution paths were designed around a 35-domain corpus; the expanded count does not change the mechanics of Path A / A+37 / B execution but is relevant to framing the scope of the main distribution send.

## 1. Path A: Immediate 34-Domain Distribution

### What Happens

Path A deploys the complete framework to all channels in a single wave beginning on the day the decision is made. All Batch 1–3 contacts receive the integrated framework simultaneously. Domain 37 goes to every contact embedded in the general framework send. Execution is 3–4 hours on Day 0.

**Note on domain count framing**: The May 5 analysis described this as a "35-domain" send. The framework now contains substantially more domains. Two options: (a) frame the send as "35-domain core framework" and describe the expanded corpus as Phase 2 additions available on request, or (b) update the main Gist and framing to reflect the current 42+ domain count. Either is defensible; the mechanics of Path A are unchanged.

### Timeline to Distribution Completion

Day 0 execution: 3–4 hours (Batch 1 sends). Days 1–14: Batch 2. Days 7–30: Batches 3–4 and Substack/Reddit cadence. Distribution is functionally complete — meaning all Tier 1 contacts have received the framework — within 14–21 days.

### Audience Reach Impact

Full Tier 1 reach in a single integrated send. Academic and policy contacts (Erica Chenoweth, Ryan Goodman, Just Security) receive the complete cross-domain architecture. Domain 37 reaches every Tier 1 contact, including election-protection specialists, simultaneously. The integrated full-framework document is the most compelling presentation of research scope for generalist contacts.

The trade-off: Domain 37 arrives at Democracy Docket and Campaign Legal Center embedded in a large corpus rather than as a targeted briefing keyed to their active dockets. A litigator at Democracy Docket must locate Domain 37 within the full document; there is no standalone Domain 37 subject line. Conversion probability for election-protection specialist engagement is modestly lower than under Path A+37.

### Strategic Urgency Factors

**DEA deadline (May 28)**: Not relevant to Path A execution — Domain 42 sub-batch runs independently. Path A does not change the Domain 42 calculus.

**Election protection (Domain 37)**: The five advocacy windows (May 30 DOJ consent decrees, June 30 EO routing, August 7 NVRA quiet period, September pre-election litigation, October poll observer deployment) are all adequately served by Path A's Day 0 timeline if executed this week. A Path A send today (May 9) provides 94 days before August 7 — a strong position on every window.

**Domain 42 DEA sub-batch**: Must launch today regardless. See Section 5 below.

### When Path A Is Right

If Tier 1 contacts are direct personal relationships — contacts who will internally route Domain 37 to the right litigation team regardless of email framing. If Substack is the primary channel (integrated full-framework framing is an advantage for public posts). If coordination overhead is the binding constraint.

## 2. Path A+37 Hybrid: Phased with Election Protection Urgency

### What Happens

Two sequential waves separated by 1–3 days. Phase 1a distributes the general framework (all domains except Domain 37) to general Tier 1–3 audiences. Phase 1b distributes Domain 37 as a standalone targeted briefing to 12 election protection organizations, with subject lines keyed to their active dockets. Total execution: 4–5 hours across Days 0–3.

### Timeline to Distribution Completion

Phase 1a: Day 0 (3–4 hours). Phase 1b: Days 1–3 (1–2 hours). Full Tier 1 reach within 3–5 days. This is the recommended path; it achieves strong distribution timing on all windows with only minor coordination overhead above Path A.

### Audience Reach Impact

The two-wave design serves different audience types differently and correctly. Academic and policy contacts (Phase 1a) receive the integrated multi-domain framework — appropriate for contacts whose value is citation, synthesis, and intellectual engagement. Election-protection litigators (Phase 1b) receive Domain 37 as a standalone briefing with subject lines keyed to their specific active dockets (Democracy Docket active voter roll cases, Campaign Legal Center SAVE Act litigation, ACLU Voting Rights Section 11(b) enforcement, Protect Democracy EAC capture documentation). This framing materially increases the probability that Domain 37 reaches the right litigation team rather than sitting in a general inbox.

Three contacts (Wendy Weiser, Marc Elias, Ian Bassin) appear on both lists and receive two emails 1–3 days apart. The Phase 1b email explicitly acknowledges the Phase 1a send, positioning it as intentional sequencing rather than an error.

### Strategic Urgency Factors

**DEA deadline (May 28)**: Same as Path A — not affected by main framework distribution path. Domain 42 sub-batch runs independently.

**Election protection (Domain 37)**: This path was designed specifically for the May–August 2026 election protection window. The five advocacy windows are served with maximum effectiveness: Domain 37 arrives at election-protection litigators with targeted framing and 94+ days before the August 7 NVRA quiet period. The Protect The Vote 2026 Poll Worker and Observer Academy runs May 15 through August 31; legal coordination for poll observer deployment must be established before training begins. Domain 37 delivered this week positions organizations for that entire training cycle.

The seven Domain 37 baseline metrics (24 active DOJ voter roll cases, $39.6M CISA election security cut, 81% SAVE Act false positive rate, 32 restrictive voting laws in effect for November 2026, 8 election-denier senior officials confirmed) are the core figures that emergency injunction templates need to cite. Organizations that have integrated these figures before August 7 are positioned to file quickly when systematic removal notices begin.

**Domain 42 DEA sub-batch**: Must launch today regardless. See Section 5.

### When Path A+37 Is Right (Recommended)

Election-protection contacts are not direct personal relationships. You want Domain 37 to reach Democracy Docket, Campaign Legal Center, and the ACLU Voting Rights Project with subject lines keyed to their specific active dockets. The May 30 DOJ consent decree deadline and August 7 NVRA quiet period are high-priority windows. The 1–2 hours of additional coordination overhead is an acceptable cost for materially higher election-specialist conversion rates.

This remains the recommended path. The May 5 recommendation has not changed. The Section 591 resolution (removing one urgency factor from Domain 42 outreach) and the current framework expansion do not affect the Path A+37 rationale.

## 3. Path B: 2–4 Week Phase 2 Expansion Before Distribution

### What Happens

Path B defers all Phase 1 distribution until defined Phase 2 research updates are completed and integrated. Realistic delay range: 14–28 days (domain updates only) to 52–61 days (full three-domain expansion including Intelligence Oversight, Reproductive Rights, and Voting Systems Architecture new domains).

As of May 9, the expansion-domain picture has changed materially from the May 5 analysis. Several domains previously listed as "pending" or "optional" are now complete: Domain 44 (Reproductive Freedom, ~7,400 words, 52 citations), Domain 45 (Labor Rights/NLRB, ~7,600 words, 52 citations), Domain 47 (Housing Security, ~7,200 words, 48 citations), Domain 52 (Civil Society Suppression, ~7,400 words, 47 citations). The WORKLOG documents these as completed in Sessions 929–931. Path B's expansion queue is narrower than the May 5 analysis suggested.

### Timeline to Distribution Completion

| Scenario | Realistic Launch Date | Days Delayed |
|----------|----------------------|-------------|
| Domain updates only (optimistic) | May 19–22, 2026 | 10–13 days |
| Domain updates + integration | May 26–June 3, 2026 | 17–25 days |
| + 1 new domain (Intelligence Oversight) | June 5–12, 2026 | 27–34 days |
| Full Phase 2 expansion | Late June–early July | 45–55 days |

The optimistic 10-day scenario assumes consecutive research sessions — unrealistic given tracker maintenance, monitoring obligations, and Exploration Queue items. Realistic minimum delay: 17–24 days. This pushes distribution into late May or early June.

### Audience Reach Impact

A more complete framework reaches institutional hands. Domains confirmed complete but not yet in the main distribution corpus (44, 45, 47, 52) could be integrated into the send. The DEA deadline and election protection windows are served by a more comprehensive document. Academic contacts who would notice pending-outcome framing in Domain 25 (FISA) or Domain 19f (Iran WPR) receive a cleaner version.

The cost: every day of delay is a day off the institutional adoption cycle before the August 7 quiet period. Distribution on June 3 leaves 65 days for institutional adoption before August 7. Distribution on May 9 leaves 90 days. The 25-day difference is material for organizations that need to draft briefs, coordinate with co-litigants, and build internal consensus before acting.

### Strategic Urgency Factors

**DEA deadline (May 28)**: Path B does not affect Domain 42 sub-batch timing. Domain 42 must launch today regardless of the main framework path. However, if Path B is selected and distribution of the main framework is delayed, the value proposition changes: some of the Tier 1 contacts who might have amplified the Domain 42 research to their networks (e.g., Brennan Center, ACLU) would not have received the full framework before May 28. The Domain 42 sub-batch reaches drug policy specialists directly — but the broader network amplification that could come from Tier 1 contacts seeing the framework and forwarding it to their DEA-adjacent contacts would be delayed.

**Election protection (Domain 37)**: Path B's realistic distribution date of May 22–June 3 still provides 66–76 days before August 7 — adequate for the institutional adoption cycle, but with a compressed margin. The May 30 DOJ consent decree deadline is missed or barely caught under any realistic Path B scenario. If the DOJ consent decree window matters for the distribution strategy, Path B is not viable.

**Domain 42 DEA sub-batch**: Must launch today regardless. See Section 5.

### When Path B Is Right

You have a strategic reason to delay not visible in the research record (pending publication partnership, specific contact timing, personal availability constraints). Your primary audience is Senate Judiciary staffers who will specifically flag FISA or Iran WPR pending-outcome framing. Comprehensiveness matters more than timing for your specific distribution goal.

Path B is not recommended if: the May 30 DOJ consent decree deadline matters; the 6–8 week institutional adoption cycle before August 7 is a priority; spring legislative sessions are still open and ICE-at-polls model legislation could still be introduced.
