## What Is Already True Before the Decision

Before the paths diverge, four facts bound every option equally.

The 35-domain framework is production-ready. Domain files are sourced to primary materials, cross-referenced, and current through May 1–5, 2026. The distribution infrastructure — six canonical Gists live, five Batch 1 email templates ready, Tier 1 contact list position-verified April 29 — requires only the user's path decision to activate.

Domain 37 (Federal Executive Interference in the 2026 Midterms) is the single most time-sensitive domain in the corpus. As of May 5, 2026, the baseline metrics for Domain 37 are fully quantified: 24 active DOJ voter roll cases across 24 states (6 dismissed, 0 wins for DOJ), CISA election security earmark $39.6M in FY2026 and slated for elimination in FY2027, 8 named election-denier senior officials confirmed in federal positions, SAVE Act error rate 81% false positive rate in Missouri (24,000+ citizens flagged nationally), and 32 restrictive voting laws enacted in 2025 with 31 in effect for November 2026. These are the pre-distribution baselines against which any framework impact will eventually be measured.

The institutional adoption cycle for Tier 1 contacts is 6–8 weeks from receipt to first operational use. Content received by May 15 is operationally active by late June. Content received by June 15 is operationally active by early August — one week before the NVRA 90-day quiet period begins August 7.

The November 4, 2026 general election is a fixed endpoint. Every advocacy window calculates backward from that date. Distribution delay is not a neutral choice in this environment.

## 1. Path A: Immediate 35-Domain Distribution

### What Happens

Path A deploys the complete 35-domain framework to all channels in a single wave beginning on Day 0, the day the decision is made. All Batch 1 contacts — Ryan Goodman (Just Security), Wendy Weiser (Brennan Center Democracy Program), Erica Chenoweth (Harvard Kennedy School), Ian Bassin (Protect Democracy), Marc Elias (Democracy Docket) — receive the same email simultaneously. Domain 37 goes to every contact embedded in the general 35-domain framework send.

**Day 0 execution timeline (3–4 hours from decision to Batch 1 sent)**:

- Block 1 (0:00–0:30): Gist URL verification across all 6 canonical Gists. This is mechanical — the Gists are live, the task is confirming URLs are accessible and match DISTRIBUTION_GIST_URLS.md.
- Block 2 (0:30–0:55): Template URL replacement in email templates (PHASE_1_EMAIL_TEMPLATES.md placeholder substitution).
- Block 3 (0:55–1:40): Batch 1 contact verification — confirm that position information for all 5 contacts is still current (positions were verified April 29; 5 days of drift is low risk but warrants a 30-second check per contact).
- Block 4 (1:40–3:10): Email personalization — 6 fields per email across 5 contacts, approximately 15 minutes per contact including institution-specific recent publication citation.
- Block 5 (3:10–3:30): Tracking setup — open a tracking log per DISTRIBUTION_EXECUTION_LOG.md format.
- Block 6 (3:30–4:00): Send Batch 1 (5 emails, 30-minute spacing between sends).

**Days 1–7**: Monitor Batch 1 signals. Stage Reddit posts for T+2. Stage Substack Post 1 for T+3. Prepare Batch 2 (5–7 contacts from the Tier 1 pool).

**Days 7–14**: Batch 2 send. Substack Post 1 published. Reddit posts submitted to 4–5 subreddits (r/law, r/PoliticalDiscussion, r/electionreform, r/democracy, and one specialist subreddit keyed to the highest-engagement signal from Batch 1).

**Days 14–30**: Batches 3–4 per DISTRIBUTION_PHASE_ORDER.md. Monthly iteration cycle begins. First monthly currency check scheduled (June 1–5).

### Speed-to-Impact Advantages

Path A's core advantage is undivided institutional momentum. The first Batch 1 contact who opens the email receives a 35-domain integrated framework, not a preview of 34 domains with a promise that Domain 37 will follow. For generalist Tier 1 contacts — Erica Chenoweth at Harvard Kennedy School, Ryan Goodman at Just Security — the integrated full-framework document is the most compelling presentation of the research's scope. No coordination overhead, no staging decisions, no separate contact list management for Domain 37.

Speed matters in May 2026 for a structural reason: the 6–8 week institutional adoption cycle and the August 7 NVRA quiet period create a calendar squeeze that rewards early delivery. Every day of delay is a day off the end of that margin. Path A's Day 0 launch achieves the maximum possible lead time on every advocacy window: 29 days before May 30, 60 days before June 30, and 10+ weeks before August 7. No other path can improve on these numbers.

The Substack and Reddit channels also benefit from integrated framing. A public post titled "35-Domain Framework on Democratic Erosion" is a stronger content signal than "here is 34 of 35 domains, with a follow-up coming for election protection readers." Single-event launches build better distribution momentum on content platforms.

### Distribution Timeline and Institutional Reach

Under Path A, the framework reaches Tier 1 academic and policy contacts in Days 0–14, Tier 2 state-level and civil society contacts in Days 14–30, and the broader public channel (Substack subscribers, Reddit readers, Gist traffic) beginning Day 3. The DISTRIBUTION_PHASE_ORDER.md structure calls for 4 batches across 30 days, covering approximately 20–25 individual contacts at Tier 1–2, plus the public channel's open-ended reach.

Domain 37's five advocacy windows receive the following lead times under Path A:

| Window | Date | Lead Time (from May 5) |
|--------|------|------------------------|
| DOJ consent decrees | May 30, 2026 | 25 days |
| Emergency EO routing | June 30, 2026 | 56 days |
| NVRA quiet period | August 7, 2026 | 94 days |
| Pre-election litigation | September 2026 | 4+ months |
| Poll observer deployment | October 2026 | 5+ months |

All five windows are served adequately by Path A. The 25-day lead time before May 30 is the tightest but is sufficient for organizations with existing legal infrastructure to begin internal briefing processes.

### Resource Requirements

**User time**: 3–4 hours Day 0 execution. Approximately 10–15 hours over the following 30 days for Batches 2–4 personalization and send (estimated 10–12 additional contacts at 10–12 minutes each). The monthly iteration cycle thereafter requires 2–3 hours per month.

**Orchestrator capacity**: Path A is the lowest-demand path. The 3–4 hour Day 0 block is a one-time commitment; ongoing maintenance (tracker updates, monthly domain currency checks, response processing) fits comfortably within 4–6 hours per week, leaving the remaining 10–14 hours of a 15–20 hour/week capacity window available for other project work, including monitoring, litigation tracker updates, and Exploration Queue items.

**Anya's Pi orchestrator modeling**: Path A frees the orchestrator most rapidly. After a 3–4 hour Day 0 commitment, the distribution enters an autonomous rhythm that requires attention only when response signals arrive or when Batch 2–4 send windows open. No research sessions are blocked. No Phase 3 work is deferred.

### Post-Launch Phase 2 Expansion Pathway

Path B's optional domain research — Intelligence Oversight (Domains 38+), Reproductive Rights and Democratic Coalition, Voting Systems Architecture — does not require Path A to complete before it begins. These are standalone research projects that can be conducted in parallel with the Phase 1 distribution cycle and targeted to appropriate follow-up contacts as they are completed. The measurement framework's rolling update design supports this: each new domain is added to the corpus as it is completed, and contacts who have already received the 35-domain framework receive a targeted follow-up email pointing to the new section.

### Risk Profile

**What Path A gains**: Maximum simplicity. Single wave, no coordination overhead, no staging decisions, no parallel contact lists. Domain 37 reaches every Tier 1 contact on Day 0. Organizations like Protect Democracy and Brennan Center, which work across both general democracy reform and election protection, receive the full 35-domain corpus at once.

**What Path A trades away**: Domain 37's strategic value to election-protection specialists is partly diluted when it arrives embedded in a 35-domain general framework send. A litigation-focused contact at Democracy Docket or Campaign Legal Center must locate Domain 37 within a large document rather than receiving it with a subject line keyed to their specific active docket. The probability that Domain 37 gets routed to the right litigation team is lower when it arrives in a general send than when it arrives standalone.

**Risk level**: Low for general distribution. Moderate for election-protection specialist conversion rates. No strategic risk to the framework's validity or credibility.

**When Path A is the right choice**: If you have direct personal relationships with Democracy Docket, Campaign Legal Center, or state AG election protection staff — contacts who will internally route Domain 37 to the right litigation team regardless of how the email arrives. Or if your primary distribution channel is Substack (general audience framing of the full framework is an advantage for a public post). Or if coordination overhead is the binding constraint and a single send event is worth the conversion rate trade-off.

## 2. Path A+37 Hybrid: Phased with Election Protection Urgency

### What Happens

Path A+37 executes in two sequential waves separated by 1–3 days. Phase 1a distributes the 34-domain general framework to all channels; Phase 1b distributes Domain 37 as a targeted standalone briefing to 12 election protection organizations. The framing rationale is that even a 24-hour gap between sends converts the Domain 37 outreach from a cold pitch into a sequenced follow-up from a researcher whose work is already in distribution.

**Phase 1a — Day 0 (3–4 hours)**: Distribute the 34-domain framework (all domains except Domain 37) to general Tier 1–3 audiences via Batch 1 email (5 contacts), Reddit (staged for T+2), and Substack Post 1 (staged for T+3). Each email includes a [PATH A+37] paragraph block noting that Domain 37 is being distributed separately to election-protection contacts. Execution is identical to Path A Blocks 1–6.

**Phase 1b — Days 1–3 (1–2 hours)**: Domain 37 goes to 12 election protection organizations as a standalone targeted outreach. Phase 1b is triggered immediately when Phase 1a Batch 1 emails are confirmed sent — it does not wait for open or response signals.

- Phase 1b Block 7 (0:00–0:30): Create a Domain 37 standalone Gist. Domain 37 is already in the main proposal Gist, but a focused standalone document reduces navigation friction for recipients — a litigation team receiving a 35-domain corpus link has to locate Domain 37; a Domain 37 standalone link delivers them directly.
- Phase 1b Block 8 (0:30–1:30): Personalize 12 election protection org emails. Per DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md, each email is keyed to the organization's active docket: Democracy Docket receives framing connected to their active voter roll cases; Campaign Legal Center receives framing connecting to their March 2026 Texas SAVE Act litigation; ACLU Voting Rights Project receives framing connecting to Section 11(b) enforcement; Brennan Center Democracy Program connects to their HSGP grant conditionality research; Protect Democracy connects to the EAC capture documentation.
- Phase 1b Block 9 (1:30–1:50): Send Phase 1b emails — Tier 1 nationals first (Wendy Weiser, Marc Elias, Ian Bassin, Kristen Clarke / successor at Lawyers' Committee), Tier 2 state networks after — with 15–20 minute spacing.

### Election Protection Urgency and the May–June 2026 Policy Windows

The case for Path A+37 rests on a structural argument about institutional attention and calendar precision, not simply on election urgency as an abstract value.

The seven baseline metrics established in `domain-37-baseline-metrics.md` (May 5, 2026) define what the pre-distribution landscape looks like. As of May 5: 24 active DOJ voter roll lawsuits with a 0-for-6 district court record against them, meaning DOJ is losing but continuing to file; CISA election security infrastructure dismantled and FY2027 budget proposing elimination of the program entirely; 8 named election-denier senior officials in federal positions with documented connections to the Election Integrity Network; 32 state restrictive voting laws enacted in 2025 with 31 in effect for November 2026; SAVE Act error rate of 81% false positives in Missouri (24,000+ flagged nationally). These numbers represent the operational context in which Domain 37's analysis arrives. The earlier it arrives, the longer election-protection organizations have to integrate these figures into their litigation strategy.

The five advocacy windows are fixed calendar deadlines, not flexible targets:

**May 30 — DOJ consent decrees**: The Oklahoma consent agreement (voluntarily signed, allowing voter roll runs through SAVE without encryption or audit requirements) establishes a template that DOJ may attempt to replicate in the 24 pending states. Organizations need pre-filed challenge strategy templates ready before this date. Path A+37 Phase 1b delivery on Days 1–3 provides approximately 22 days of lead time. Path A provides 25 days (general send, less focused). Both are adequate; Path A+37's targeted framing makes those 22 days more actionable.

**June 30 — Emergency EO routing window**: If the administration issues emergency executive orders routing election security functions outside standard HSGP grant review mechanisms, administrative law challenge windows are time-limited. Organizations receiving Domain 37 in early May have 8 weeks to prepare challenge briefs. Those receiving it in late June have days.

**August 7 — NVRA 90-day quiet period**: Under 52 U.S.C. § 20507(c)(2), systematic voter roll removals are prohibited within 90 days of a federal general election. Counting back 90 days from November 4, 2026 yields August 7. The 6–8 week institutional adoption cycle means Domain 37 must arrive in organizational hands by approximately June 22 to be operationally integrated by August 7. Path A+37 Phase 1b arrival on Days 1–3 of May achieves this with 12+ weeks of margin — the strongest possible position. The SAVE error rate baseline (81% false positive) is the core figure that emergency injunction templates need to cite; organizations that have integrated this figure into pre-filed briefs before August 7 are positioned to move rapidly when systematic removal notices begin.

**The Protect The Vote 2026 Poll Worker and Observer Academy** runs May 15 through August 31. Legal coordination for poll observer deployment must be established before training begins, not during it. Domain 37's poll observer section is directly relevant to this training cycle.

### Domain 37 Timing Rationale for 2026 Midterm Infrastructure

Domain 37 is not primarily a research document about election policy — it is a litigation support tool with a defined use-by horizon. The framework's value to Democracy Docket's litigators, Campaign Legal Center's NVRA specialists, and the ACLU Voting Rights Project's Section 11(b) team is highest in the window between now and the August 7 quiet period deadline. After August 7, the litigation strategy for the November 4 election is largely set; new research arriving in September affects observer deployment but not the structural injunction strategy.

The timing rationale for the 2026 midterm specifically is that the DOJ's 24-state voter roll litigation campaign is the most aggressive federal election interference effort since the Voting Rights Act's preclearance regime was gutted in Shelby County v. Holder (2013). The infrastructure being dismantled — CISA election security capacity, EI-ISAC cooperative agreements, regional election security advisors — took years to build and will take years to rebuild. The decisions being made between now and August 7 about which states pursue emergency injunctions, which voter removals get challenged, and which HSGP grant conditions get contested in administrative proceedings will shape not just November 2026 but the institutional baseline heading into redistricting and the 2028 cycle.

### Sequencing Strategy: Which Institutions Receive What First

The two-wave sequencing reflects a structural insight about audience type.

**Phase 1a recipients (Day 0)**: Law schools, think tanks, civil society researchers, academic institutions. These contacts engage with the 35-domain integrated framework as an intellectual architecture. Their value is citation, synthesis, and long-run institutional credibility. For these contacts, the full framework's cross-domain connections are the point — seeing Domain 37 in context with Domain 1 (voting rights), Domain 33 (state legislative autocratization), and Domain 6 (judicial independence) is more valuable than receiving Domain 37 alone.

**Phase 1b recipients (Days 1–3)**: Organizations with active litigation dockets and fixed advocacy deadlines. Democracy Docket, Brennan Center Democracy Program, ACLU Voting Rights Project, Campaign Legal Center, Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights, Protect Democracy, States United Democracy Center, Common Cause, Let America Vote, State Democracy Defenders Fund, Fair Elections Center, Public Interest Legal Foundation (monitoring for opposition filings). These contacts read 30–50 litigation-relevant documents per week. The probability that Domain 37 reaches the right litigator on the team is higher when it arrives as a standalone briefing with a subject line like "Domain 37 briefing: federal election interference mechanisms and NVRA litigation windows" than when it arrives as the 37th domain in a 35-domain corpus.

Three contacts appear on both lists: Wendy Weiser (Brennan Center — general research and election protection portfolios), Marc Elias (Democracy Docket — general democracy reform and active litigator), Ian Bassin (Protect Democracy — structural democracy reform and election protection). These contacts receive two emails, 1–3 days apart. The Phase 1b email explicitly acknowledges the Phase 1a send: "Given your organization's specific work on [X active litigation], I wanted to follow up with Domain 37 separately." This framing positions the two emails as intentional sequencing rather than a coordination error.

### Resource Requirements vs. Path A

**User time**: 4–5 hours total — 3–4 hours Phase 1a (identical to Path A) plus 1–2 hours Phase 1b across Days 0–3. The additional 1–2 hours over Path A covers: one additional Gist creation (30 minutes), 12 election-org email personalizations keyed to active dockets per DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md (approximately 5 minutes each beyond the template), and the Phase 1b send (20–30 minutes with 15-minute spacing between sends).

**Ongoing**: Identical to Path A — 2–3 hours per month for the monthly iteration cycle. The two-track contact log (general / Domain 37 election orgs) requires a tracking spreadsheet with two tabs, adding approximately 30 minutes of setup over Path A's single-track log.

**Orchestrator capacity**: Marginally higher than Path A — the additional 1–2 hours of Day 1–3 commitment is the full overhead. After Phase 1b sends, the distribution enters the same autonomous rhythm as Path A. No research sessions are blocked. Anya's Pi retains the same 10–14 hours/week capacity for other project work as under Path A.

### Risk Profile

**What Path A+37 gains**: Domain 37 reaches its highest-value institutional audience — election-protection litigators — with targeted framing and full lead time on all five advocacy windows. The general framework reaches academic, policy, and think tank contacts simultaneously. The sequence creates the strongest possible first impression for both audience types.

**What Path A+37 trades away**: Single-wave launch simplicity. Two send events, two contact lists, three contacts receiving two emails, one additional Gist to maintain. The coordination overhead is small and the execution is mechanical — DOMAIN_37_SEQUENCING_PLAN.md pre-specifies every organization's framing — but it is more complex than Path A.

**The asymmetric risk consideration**: If Path A+37 executes suboptimally, the worst case is that three contacts receive two emails within three days (mild, easily addressed with the "targeted follow-up" framing in Phase 1b templates). If Path A is chosen and Domain 37's election-protection value is diluted in the general send, the May 30 window is not lost — but the probability of Domain 37 reaching the right litigation team at Democracy Docket or the ACLU Voting Rights Project before that deadline is lower.

### Completeness Arguments

The genuine case for Path B is not speed — it is the quality of the institutional impression the framework makes when it arrives. A framework that includes the confirmed FISA 702 outcome (not "Senate vote pending"), the confirmed Iran WPR post-May 1 scenario (not "three possible scenarios, select one"), the circuit vacancy wave documented as it is actually unfolding in May, and the SCOTUS cert grants known from the May conference is a more complete document than the current May 5 version.

This distinction matters for a specific subset of Tier 1 contacts: Senate Judiciary Committee staffers who work on the FISA file and will notice the pending outcome framing; FISA-specialized litigators at the ACLU National Security Project who will flag the April 29 House passage of S.1318 as an update gap; appellate court reform scholars who follow the vacancy acceleration closely enough to notice if the Domain 6 circuit section does not include May developments. For these contacts, Path B's "post-outcome integration" is a credibility feature, not merely a completeness preference.

A related argument concerns framing register. The current 35-domain framework reads as a crisis-response document — produced in real time, current through April–May 2026, carrying the urgency of immediate developments. A Path B framework launched in late May or June, with pending outcomes resolved and integrated, would read more as a considered democratic diagnosis than a document produced in the heat of ongoing events. For international democracy monitoring organizations, comparative politics scholars, and Congressional Research Service contacts — audiences for whom methodological rigor and analytical distance signal credibility — this distinction may affect how seriously the framework is treated.

### When Path B Is the Right Choice

Path B is the right choice when one or more of the following conditions holds:

1. Your primary distribution audience is not election-protection litigators but rather scholars, staffers, and institutional researchers for whom completeness and analytical distance are credibility signals.
2. You have a specific anchor opportunity in June — a publication partnership, a conference presentation, an institutional briefing — that makes June timing superior to May timing regardless of framework completeness.
3. Your own availability makes a concentrated May distribution impossible, and a June launch aligns better with your actual capacity.
4. You have a specific strategic reason, not visible in the current research record, to delay — a pending conversation with a key contact that should inform the framework before it distributes.
5. You are willing to accept the compression or loss of the May 30 and August 7 advocacy windows as the price of the completeness advantage.

### Trade-off: Later Launch, More Comprehensive Framework

The trade-off is real in both directions. Path B's completed framework is genuinely more complete. The question is whether the completeness advantage outweighs the advocacy window costs.

**What Path B gains**: A more defensible document for the specific contacts who probe currency gaps. The Iran WPR outcome resolved rather than pending. The FISA final vote documented. The circuit vacancy wave current. The potential for three new domains (Intelligence Oversight, Reproductive Rights, Voting Systems Architecture) to significantly deepen the framework's diagnostic scope. If the full three-domain expansion is completed, the framework grows from 35 to 38+ domains with substantially stronger coverage of intelligence accountability and electoral systems design — both areas where the 35-domain version has acknowledged gaps.

**What Path B trades away**: The May 30 DOJ consent decree window almost certainly closes before Path B's realistic launch date (May 22–29 at minimum, and that assumes domain updates only without the new domain research). At 14-day optimistic, organizations receive the framework on May 19, giving them 11 days before May 30 — borderline. The August 7 NVRA quiet period margin narrows from 10+ weeks (Path A, Path A+37) to 6 weeks (realistic domain updates only) or 4 weeks (with one new domain) or 2 weeks (full Path B with three new domains). Below 6 weeks, the 6–8 week institutional adoption cycle means Domain 37 is unlikely to be operationally integrated before the quiet period begins.

**The iterated distribution alternative**: Path B's completeness advantage can be substantially captured without pre-launch delay. Contacts who receive the 35-domain framework on May 5 can receive targeted follow-up emails when the post-May 1 Iran outcome is confirmed (likely May 8–15) and when the FISA Senate vote is final. A "framework update: two pending outcomes now confirmed" email is a second contact point that reinforces researcher credibility, not an admission of incompleteness. Path B treats completeness as a launch prerequisite; the rolling update model treats it as an ongoing feature.

### Orchestrator Capacity Modeling

The orchestrator (Anya's Pi) has an estimated 15–20 hours/week of research and execution capacity. Distribution work competes with monitoring passes, tracker updates, litigation tracker currency, and Exploration Queue research.

- **Path A**: Distribution consumes 3–4 hours Day 0, then 4–6 hours/week for first month (personalization, response processing, Batch 2–4 sends). Leaves 9–15 hours/week for other work.
- **Path A+37**: Distribution consumes 4–5 hours Days 0–3, then 4–6 hours/week for first month (same as Path A, plus Domain 37 tracking tab). Leaves 9–14 hours/week for other work.
- **Path B (domain updates only)**: Research consumes 12–14 hours across 10–14 days (approximately the full weekly capacity for 1–2 weeks). Distribution then follows Path A timeline. During the research phase, other work is significantly constrained.
- **Path B (+ new domains)**: Research consumes 30–42 additional hours across 30–45 days. Near-total orchestrator capacity for 6–8 weeks. Monitoring, tracker updates, and Exploration Queue work are deferred or minimal.

The capacity modeling points to a clear conclusion for this dimension: Path A and Path A+37 are the only options that leave meaningful orchestrator capacity for ongoing work throughout the distribution period. Path B with domain updates is feasible but tight; Path B with new domains effectively blocks all other research for 6–8 weeks.

## 5. Decision Framework

### Which Variables Matter Most

Three variables govern the choice between paths. They are genuinely independent — a user who prioritizes election protection urgency, speed, and orchestrator capacity will land in the same place on all three; a user who prioritizes completeness and has a strategic delay rationale will consistently prefer Path B. The hard cases are when these variables pull in different directions.

**Variable 1: Election protection urgency**

How much do the May 30, June 30, and August 7 advocacy windows constrain your decision?

If these deadlines feel like real operational constraints — if you want Democracy Docket, the ACLU Voting Rights Project, and Campaign Legal Center to have Domain 37's five-mechanism analysis integrated into their litigation planning before these windows close — then Path B is eliminated or severely constrained by the realistic timeline. The question then becomes Path A versus Path A+37, and the deciding factor is whether you have direct personal relationships with election-protection litigators that substitute for targeted Domain 37 framing.

If these deadlines feel secondary — if your primary impact vector is long-run institutional credibility with think tanks, law schools, and Congressional Research Service contacts rather than immediate election-protection litigation support — then the timing pressure partially relaxes and Path B's completeness argument becomes competitive.

**Variable 2: Completeness versus speed**

Which is more valuable in your institutional context: a 35-domain framework current through May 5, or a 38–41-domain framework available in 4–8 weeks with post-outcome integration?

The honest answer depends on who is actually receiving the framework and how they engage with research. Contacts who use the framework primarily as a citation source and conceptual architecture — academic researchers, policy advocates, think tank staff — are unlikely to probe whether Domain 19f's Iran WPR post-May 1 outcome has been filled. Contacts who are active practitioners in the specific legal fields — FISA litigators, voting rights appellate attorneys, Senate Judiciary Committee counsel — are more likely to notice pending-outcome framing and ask whether the document is current.

**Variable 3: Your realistic bandwidth**

This is the variable most likely to be underweighted. Path B requires 12–42 hours of orchestrator research time before any distribution occurs. That is not an abstract research commitment — it is 1–3 weeks during which no framework content reaches any institution, no litigation tracking updates, no response processing. If the orchestrator is already managing tracker currency, monitoring passes, and Exploration Queue work, a full Path B research phase represents a significant operational shift.

### Reversibility

Can you change paths mid-execution?

- **Path A to Path A+37**: Fully reversible after the fact. If you execute Path A and then decide that Domain 37 deserves standalone targeted outreach, you can create the standalone Gist and send Phase 1b-style emails to the 12 election organizations on Day 3, 5, or 10. The framing shifts slightly ("as a follow-up to the general framework I sent, I wanted to reach out specifically about Domain 37") but remains credible. Reversibility cost: negligible.
- **Path A+37 to Path A**: Not applicable — if you want Path A, don't execute Phase 1b. Phase 1a is identical to Path A.
- **Path A or A+37 to Path B**: Not reversible once Batch 1 sends. You cannot unsend an email. If you send the 35-domain framework to Batch 1 contacts and then decide you want to do Path B research first, you have already distributed the current version. Path B's rationale — arrive with post-outcome integration — is no longer available for Batch 1 contacts.
- **Path B mid-execution**: Reversible at any point before Batch 1 sends. If you begin domain update research and decide after 7 days that the May 30 window compression is unacceptable, you can stop research and launch immediately with whatever updates have been completed. Partial completion is not a problem — any integrated updates simply make the current version slightly more complete.

### Recommended Decision Process

The following sequence is designed to surface the information that matters most for this decision without requiring research the user has not already done.

1. **Establish the election-protection priority**: Are Democracy Docket, Campaign Legal Center, ACLU Voting Rights Project, and similar organizations genuine Tier 1 targets for you — meaning, are you targeting them as institutional users of the research, not just distribution recipients? If yes, proceed to step 2. If no (your primary targets are academic institutions, think tanks, or general public audiences), then Path B's completeness argument is stronger and the May 30 window is less binding.

2. **Check for direct relationships**: Do you have direct personal relationships with anyone at the organizations listed in Phase 1b — Democracy Docket, Campaign Legal Center, ACLU VRA Project? Personal relationships substitute for targeted Domain 37 framing, because you can route Domain 37 through a direct conversation rather than a cold email. If yes, Path A's conversion-rate disadvantage is substantially reduced.

3. **Assess your orchestrator bandwidth for May–June**: Is there a realistic research capacity of 12–14 hours available in the next 10–14 days for domain updates, without crowding out monitoring and tracker work? If yes, domain-updates-only Path B is operationally feasible. If no, Path B creates an unacceptable tradeoff with ongoing work.

4. **Check for a strategic anchor reason to delay**: Do you have a specific publication partnership, institutional briefing, or contact timing consideration that makes June superior to May for distribution? If yes, name it and weigh it against the May 30 window cost. If no, Path B's timing disadvantage has no countervailing strategic benefit.

5. **Apply the decision rule**: If election protection is high-priority and no direct relationships substitute for targeted framing, choose Path A+37. If you have direct election-org relationships and prefer single-wave simplicity, choose Path A. If completeness is the dominant priority and you have a strategic reason to delay that outweighs May 30 window compression, choose Path B.

**The one constraint to hold firmly**: If Path B takes longer than 21 days from decision to launch — which the realistic timeline projects it will in most cases — the May 30 DOJ consent decree advocacy window will be unactionable for election-protection organizations. If that matters to you, Path B is eliminated. If it does not, the choice between Path A and Path B is a genuine tradeoff between two legitimate strategic priorities.
