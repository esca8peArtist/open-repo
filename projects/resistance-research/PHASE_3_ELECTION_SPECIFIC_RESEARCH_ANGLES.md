# Phase 3 Election-Specific Research Angles
## Post-November 3, 2026 Research Windows for Phase 1-2 Domain Updates and New Phase 3 Production

*Authored: June 6, 2026*
*Scope: Domain update strategy for the November 4, 2026 – March 31, 2027 post-election window*

---

## Lead Finding

The November 3, 2026 midterm election creates three distinct research windows, each with different source availability and coalition utility:

**Window 1 (November 4-30, 2026)**: Election results + immediate contestation. Data available: preliminary results, exit polls, election protection incident reports, early FEC 24/48-hour independent expenditure reports. Domains activated: 37a (certification strategy), 49 (Callais redistricting impact), 1 (SAVE Act/voter ID impact assessment).

**Window 2 (December 1-31, 2026)**: Post-election data consolidation. Data available: certified results, initial Brennan Center and CLC post-election analyses, FEC post-general reports (filed December 3 deadline for monthly filers), state voting data releases, youth turnout data from CIRCLE, exit poll crosstabs. Domains activated: 51 (campaign finance teardown), 54 (youth civic power update), 40 (surveillance capitalism electoral impact).

**Window 3 (January 3 – March 31, 2027)**: New Congress + structural reform window. Data available: full FEC cycle data, academic post-election studies, V-Dem 2027 report, organized resistance network capacity surveys. Domains activated: H (constitutional architecture), K (judiciary restructuring), I (international standards), L (movement infrastructure), 56 (civil service outcomes).

## Part I: Phase 1-2 Domains Requiring Post-Election Updates

These are existing production-complete Phase 1 and Phase 2 domains that need post-election data appended — not full rewrites, but structured update sections (500-1,500 words each) with new citations. The update trigger is November 3 election results.

### Domain 1 — Voting Rights and Elections (CRITICAL UPDATE)

**What needs updating**: Domain 1 is the framework's foundational electoral analysis. It documented the pre-election suppression landscape (SAVE Act, 12 restrictive state laws enacted January-May 2026, voter purges in Ohio and Texas, Executive Order 14339 on mail voting). Post-election, Domain 1 needs a Section 1h: "2026 Election Outcomes and Suppression Teardown."

**Specific update content**:
- SAVE Act effect: The Senate failed to advance the SAVE Act (cloture vote failed in March 2026 per SPLC documentation). However, Florida, Mississippi, South Dakota, and Utah enacted state versions signed by Republican governors. Post-election data from these four states should be compared to comparable non-SAVE states for voter registration completion rates, registration rejection rates, and turnout differentials by demographic. This is the first empirical test of state-level SAVE implementation.
- Voter purge assessment: Ohio and Texas conducted purges in violation of NVRA (documented pre-election by Campaign Legal Center). Post-election, determine: how many purged registrants appeared at polls, how many provisional ballots were cast in purge-affected counties, what was the litigation outcome.
- EO 14339 mail ballot impact: The executive order instructing USPS to refuse delivery of certain ballots. Post-election data: how many ballots were affected, in which states, and what was the preliminary legal status of the order by November 3.
- Turnout comparison to 2022 baseline: Overall turnout, turnout by race and age, turnout in states with new restrictive laws vs. states that enacted expansive laws (Virginia enacted 6 expansive laws in 2026, per Brennan Center May 2026 roundup).

**Update priority**: High. Must publish by December 31, 2026 to inform post-election advocacy by Brennan Center, CLC, NAACP LDF, and Protect Democracy.

**Data sources for update**: Brennan Center State Voting Laws tracker (updated post-election); CLC "Purge" analysis post-election; CIRCLE youth turnout data (available December 2026); MIT Election Data + Science Lab (releases state-level data within 30 days of election); Democracy Docket litigation outcomes; state election board certification documents.

### Domain 33 — State Legislative Autocratization (HIGH-VALUE UPDATE)

**What needs updating**: Domain 33 documents the ballot initiative suppression toolkit — state laws blocking or raising thresholds for citizen initiatives, preemption of local ordinances, and gerrymandered state legislative maps. The November 3 ballot measure results are the domain's most important empirical test.

**Specific update content**:
- Ballot measure outcomes: Which reproductive freedom ballot measures passed or failed? Which campaign finance reform measures (California Fair Elections Act, Montana Plan) passed or failed? Which voter access measures survived? The post-election results are the empirical evidence for whether the ballot initiative suppression toolkit is working as designed.
- State legislative map results: Which gerrymandered state legislative maps produced uncompetitive outcomes despite demographic shifts? The Callais ruling (*Louisiana v. Callais*, April 29, 2026) enables more aggressive state legislative gerrymanders under the intentionality standard — were any November 3 results clearly distorted by maps enacted under the new standard?
- Preemption inventory update: Which new state preemption laws enacted in 2026 survived legal challenge through Election Day?

**Update priority**: High. California Fair Elections Act result in particular is the Phase 2 Domain 51 distribution campaign's success metric — the update closes the loop on whether the Phase 2 investment in California contacts produced measurable coalition impact.

**Data sources for update**: Ballotpedia 2026 ballot measures database; OpenSecrets state ballot measure spending; National Conference of State Legislatures redistricting tracker; CLC preemption law inventory.

### Domain 37 — Federal Executive Interference in 2026 Midterms (HIGH-VALUE UPDATE)

**What needs updating**: Domain 37 documents the pre-election interference vectors (CISA budget cuts, election denier appointments, Section 3 litigation posture, mid-decade redistricting). Post-election, the domain needs a "What Actually Happened" section.

**Specific update content**:
- CISA election security assessment: CISA's $39.6M election security cut was confirmed in Phase 2 research. Post-election, what did CISA's reduced capacity mean in practice? Were there documented security incidents the reduced CISA could not respond to?
- Election denier official actions: The 8 named senior election denier officials in federal roles (documented in Domain 37 baseline metrics). What specific actions did they take in the 90 days before November 3?
- Section 3 post-election: Were any disqualification proceedings filed? Were any candidates challenged at the certification stage?
- Mid-decade redistricting impact: Texas, North Carolina, and Missouri mid-decade gerrymanders were projected to net 12-14 additional Republican House seats. Did the post-*Callais* maps deliver that margin?

**Update priority**: High. This update is the primary accountability document for the organizations that received the Domain 37 Phase 2 distribution — they need to know what actually happened.

**Data sources for update**: CISA post-election security report (released within 90 days of election); state Board of Elections certification documents; Domain 37 baseline metrics document (`domains/domain-37-baseline-metrics.md`) — which established seven pre-election baselines for exactly this purpose.

### Domain 54 — Youth Civic Power (TIME-SENSITIVE UPDATE)

**What needs updating**: Domain 54 was completed June 1, 2026 ahead of schedule. It documented six suppression vectors targeting young voters (student ID bans, campus polling closures, proof-of-citizenship requirements, student visa weaponization, financial precarity, civic infrastructure dismantlement). Hard distribution deadline was August 1, 2026. Post-election, the domain needs an update section assessing youth turnout outcomes against the suppression landscape.

**Specific update content**:
- Youth turnout 2026 vs. 2022 comparison: CIRCLE (Center for Information and Research on Civic Learning and Engagement, Tufts University) publishes preliminary youth turnout estimates within one week of the election, final data within 60 days. The 2022 youth turnout was 27% overall; the 2026 comparison against the backdrop of the documented suppression vectors is the domain's core empirical test.
- Indiana 7th Circuit ruling outcome: The court stayed the district court injunction blocking Indiana's student ID ban, affecting 40,000-90,000 students (documented in Domain 54). Was this stay in effect on November 3? How many provisional ballots did affected students cast?
- Florida HB 991 (SAVE America Act state version, signed April 1, 2026): Documented in Domain 54. What was the registration rejection rate in Florida under proof-of-citizenship requirements for college students?
- Student visa chilling effect: Did the 6,000+ visa revocations documented in Domain 54 affect campus civic participation in university communities with large international student populations?

**Update priority**: Medium-High. The update is most valuable if published before the January-February 2027 distribution window for youth civic organizations (NextGen America, Campus Vote Project, CIRCLE, Rock the Vote).

**Data sources for update**: CIRCLE preliminary youth turnout (available by November 10); state Board of Elections 18-29 year-old registration and turnout data; Democracy Docket Indiana student ID case docket; Florida Division of Elections rejection data.

## Part II: Phase 2 New Domains Needing Parallel Post-Election Research

These are Phase 2 domains produced in 2026 that have post-election dimensions requiring active research in the November-December window.

### Domain 40 — Surveillance Capitalism and Electoral Manipulation

**Post-election angle**: The NRSC Talarico deepfake (March 11, 2026 — first national party committee synthetic candidate video, documented in Domain 40) established a precedent. Post-election, the research question is whether the 2026 cycle saw escalation: how many synthetic candidate videos were produced, did any measurably affect outcomes, did any state election law or federal FEC enforcement touch these ads? The PNAS February 2026 study found digital suppression ads reduced turnout 1.86% overall and 14.2% in targeted minority-majority battleground counties. Post-election data can test whether the 2026 cycle saw the predicted targeting pattern.

**Key data sources**: FEC political ad disclosure database; Stanford Internet Observatory 2026 election integrity report (published December-January); Brennan Center deepfake disclosure law tracker.

**Update timeline**: December 2026. FEC post-general reports + Stanford IO analysis available December.

### Domain 50 — Healthcare Access and Democracy Nexus (OBBBA/NVRA)

**Post-election angle**: Domain 50 documents the OBBBA Medicaid work requirements (effective January 1, 2027) and their NVRA implications. Post-election, the research window opens on the implementation status: How many states have filed work requirement compliance plans with HHS? What is the implementation timeline for the January 1, 2027 effective date? Were any states that expanded Medicaid under the OBBBA provisions rewarded or punished in the November 3 results in ways that affect implementation politics?

**Key question for advocacy**: If Democrats recapture the House or Senate, is there a legislative window in the lame-duck session (November 4 – January 3) to amend the OBBBA work requirements before they take effect January 1?

**Key data sources**: KFF Medicaid policy tracker; HHS state waiver database; Georgetown CCF analysis of state implementation plans.

**Update timeline**: November 15 – December 15, 2026 (before January 1 effective date).
