## Section 4: Research Gaps Requiring July-August Fieldwork

The following data cannot be obtained through desk research and will require direct outreach or primary reporting during the July-August execution window:

1. **Campus-by-campus polling location inventory:** Number, hours, and days of campus polling locations in the top 30 college-dense competitive congressional districts for 2026. Campus Vote Project is the primary source; CIRCLE should be contacted for any systematic data.

2. **2025-2026 state law cumulative effect modeling:** The quantified net suppression effect of layered state restrictions (ID + proof-of-citizenship + purge) on youth registrations expected to be blocked requires modeling that goes beyond existing published analyses. This may require original estimation using CIRCLE's methodological framework.

3. **Digital demobilization content scale:** No public inventory exists of AI-generated or algorithmically amplified youth voter demobilization content in the 2025-2026 cycle. Brennan Center's AI voter suppression team and the University of Wisconsin-Madison research group (PNAS study authors) should be contacted.

4. **Felony disenfranchisement by age cohort:** The Sentencing Project's "Locked Out 2024" does not disaggregate by age within the disenfranchised population. Direct contact with Sentencing Project researchers to request age-cohort analysis, or original estimation using incarceration age data.

5. **Organization funding stress (2025 DEI cuts):** Multiple youth civic organizations operate with DEI-framed funding that is under threat from both federal (DOGE) and private sector (corporate DEI rollback) funding environments. An assessment of organizational capacity for 2026 requires direct organizational outreach.
