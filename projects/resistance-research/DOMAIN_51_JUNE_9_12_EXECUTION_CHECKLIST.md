## Section 3: Email Sequencing — 3-Wave Send Plan

### Wave 1 — June 9 (Tier A: National Contacts)
**Send window: 09:00–11:00 local**

**Rationale**: National organizations are sent first for two reasons. First, their decision cycle for engaging external research is longer (days to weeks, not hours) — they need the most lead time before the July 1 hard deadline. Second, a CLC or Issue One acknowledgment or citation provides social proof that strengthens the CA campaign contacts' reception of the research on June 11. Even an email noting "we've also shared this with Campaign Legal Center" in the June 11 body text increases credibility with Common Cause CA staff.

Spacing rationale (09:00 and 10:30, not back-to-back): A 90-minute gap is the professional standard for multi-send days. It prevents bulk-send appearance, gives genuine mental separation between recipient framings, and allows time to log the first send before executing the second.

### Wave 2 — June 11 (Tier B and C: California Contacts)
**Send window: 09:00–10:30 local**

**Rationale**: California campaign contacts are sent mid-week (Wednesday) when campaign staff email attention peaks. Monday morning is consumed by weekly planning. Friday afternoon has lowest engagement. The 09:00–10:30 window captures the pre-lunch attention peak in Pacific Time.

30-minute stagger between Common Cause CA (09:00), LWV CA (09:30), and Clean Money Action Fund (10:00) avoids any appearance of a bulk send and allows each email to be genuinely customized in the pre-send moment.

### Wave 3 — June 15 (Tier C: Secondary Follow-through)
**Condition: Only if no replies received from June 9–11 sends by June 14 EOD**

If all 5 contacts are unresponsive by June 14 EOD, send a single brief follow-up to Common Cause CA only (the highest-leverage California contact). One follow-up, not five. The follow-up should be 2 sentences: "Following up on the research I shared June 11. The July 1 messaging window is approaching and I want to confirm the document reached the right person." Do not resend the full email.

**Do not send follow-up to CLC or Issue One before June 16.** National organizations need 7 full days before a follow-up is appropriate.
