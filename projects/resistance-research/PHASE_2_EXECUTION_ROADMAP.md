## SECTION 3: Phase 1 / Phase 2 Concurrency

Phase 2 research and Phase 1 distribution are fully independent. They can run concurrently from Day 1 of Phase 1. The critical question is orchestrator bandwidth — Phase 1 monitoring consumes 4–8 hours/week in Weeks 1–4.

**Phase 1 monitoring schedule** (what it demands by week):
- Week 1 (May 18–24): Passive monitoring only. Domain 42 Wave 1 adoption signals begin arriving. 4 hours.
- Week 2 (May 25–31): Gate 1/2/3 assessment. Compile view rates, reply rates, secondary distribution data. 5–6 hours.
- Weeks 3–4 (June 1–14): Early-adopter feedback arrives. Highest-value monitoring window — quality of engagement (questions, integration requests, methodology critiques) matters more than raw open rate. 4–6 hours/week.
- Weeks 5+ (June 15 onward): Monthly cadence; monitoring overhead drops to 2–3 hours/week.

**Phase 2 work that fits within Phase 1 monitoring overhead**:
- Domain 56 verification (8–12 hours total, Weeks 1–2): Low-intensity source checking and court status update. Zero conflict with Phase 1 monitoring.
- Domain 58 completion (8–12 hours, Weeks 1–4): Can absorb 3–4 hours/week of production work without competing with Phase 1 load.

**Phase 2 intensive research that should wait until Week 5+**:
- Domains 57 and 59 (50–65 hours each): The Section 5 synthesis work for D59 and the constitutional law analysis for D57 require sustained 8–12 hour blocks without interruption. Beginning before June 15 means Phase 1 monitoring will interrupt synthesis at the worst possible moments.
- Risk of premature start: If Phase 1 adoption signals are weak (Gate 3), beginning intensive Domain 57/59 research in Week 3 wastes 20–40 hours that should be redirected to Phase 1 remediation.

## SECTION 4: Phase 1 Metrics — Go/No-Go Gates for Phase 2

These assessments occur at Week 2 (May 25–28) and Week 4 (June 8–14).

### Gate 1: Strong Adoption — Full Phase 2 Roadmap Proceeds

**Thresholds** (all three required):
- Gist view rate ≥ 40% of Wave 1 contacts have opened the Gist link within 10 days
- Organizational response rate: 2+ of the first 5 contacts have replied with substantive engagement
- Secondary distribution: at least 1 contact has shared the Gist within their network

**What this triggers**: Full Phase 2 scope on the timeline in Section 5. Domain 56 distributes May 28. Domain 58 distributes June 15. Domains 59 and 57 begin research June 16 in parallel.

### Gate 2: Moderate Adoption — Reduced Scope

**Thresholds**: 20–39% Gist view rate; 1 organizational response; no confirmed secondary distribution. Or: strong view rates but no engagement (opens without response — framing lands but does not activate).

**What this triggers**: Reduce scope to must-do only. Domain 56 proceeds (legislative window is hard regardless). Domain 58 proceeds (low production cost, independent deadline). Defer Domains 57 and 59 until Week 4 assessment provides clearer signals. Use Week 3 to deepen Phase 1 distribution — extend to Wave 2 and Wave 3 contacts, refine templates based on what engagement data shows.

**Revised timing**: Domain 57/59 research begins July 1 instead of June 16. Domain 59 completes August 1 (losing peer-review buffer). Domain 57 completes September 1 (losing UNGA pre-distribution window).

### Gate 3: Weak Adoption — Phase 2 Paused

**Thresholds**: Under 20% Gist view rate; zero organizational responses after 10 days; multiple bounce or non-delivery signals.

**What this triggers**: Phase 2 research paused entirely. All orchestrator capacity returns to Phase 1. Diagnose the adoption failure: Is it the wrong contacts (fix in 2–3 days with contact list revision)? Wrong framing (fix in 1–2 weeks with messaging revision)? Wrong distribution format (Gist vs. PDF vs. direct proposal attachment)?

**Re-evaluate Phase 2**: Week 6 (June 15). If Phase 1 has recovered to Gate 2 or Gate 1 levels, resume with reduced-scope protocol.

## SECTION 7: Path Decision Tree — How Phase 2 Changes by Distribution Path

This section answers the question: "I have not yet decided between Path A, A+37, and B — how does that choice affect Phase 2 sequencing?"

The answer is more nuanced than it appears. Phase 2 research sequencing is largely deadline-driven (external events, not internal adoption signals) and is therefore mostly path-independent. What changes is: (1) the audience size for Phase 2 distribution, (2) the urgency of specific Phase 2 domains as complements to Phase 1, and (3) the go/no-go gate interpretation.

### Path A: General Distribution (34 Domains) — Phase 2 Implications

**Phase 1 scope**: Domains 1–36 (excludes Domain 37: Federal Executive Interference). Immediate launch May 15–17.

**What this means for Phase 2**:

Path A distributes to general audiences (law schools, think tanks, civil rights organizations, labor unions) without election-specific framing. This creates a specific Phase 2 implication: election-protection organizations — Common Cause, Brennan Center election protection team, Democracy Docket, Voting Rights Alliance — are not targeted with election-specific analysis in Phase 1.

**Phase 2 adjustment for Path A**:

- Domain 37 becomes a Phase 2 priority. Even though Domain 37 exists as a completed document, under Path A it was not distributed. It should be the first Phase 2 distribution action — not a research item, but a distribution action targeting election protection organizations specifically. This means Domain 37 distribution can begin Week 2 (May 25–31) to capture the June 30 emergency EO routing window and the September pre-election litigation window.
- Domains 56, 58, 59, 57 proceed on the standard Phase 2 timeline with no adjustment.
- Go/no-go gate interpretation for Path A: The Week 2 gate assessment should specifically ask whether election protection organizations are engaging with the framework through secondary discovery (did they find it without targeted outreach?). If not, Domain 37's distribution in Week 2 is the remediation action.

**Phase 2 scope under Path A**: Core 3 high-deadline domains (D56, D59, D57) are sufficient for maximum Year 1 impact. Path A does not generate the expanded election-protection audience base that makes Domain 48 (Criminal Justice) immediately actionable — defer Domain 48 to September and Domain 51 to Year 2 unless Gate 1 adoption signals confirm broad engagement.

**Recommended Phase 2 sequence under Path A**:
1. Domain 37 distribution (election-protection orgs only): May 25–31
2. Domain 56 verification and distribution: May 18–28
3. Domain 58 completion and distribution: May 20 – June 15
4. Domains 59 + 57 research (parallel): June 16 – August 10
