## SECTION 4: Phase 1 Metrics — Go/No-Go Gates for Phase 2

These metrics apply to the Week 2 adoption signal assessment (May 22–28) and the Week 4 early-adopter feedback assessment (June 1–14).

### Gate 1: Strong Adoption — Full Phase 2 Roadmap Proceeds

**Indicators (all three needed)**:
- Gist view rate: 40%+ of Wave 1 contacts have opened the Gist link within 10 days
- Organizational response rate: 2+ of the 5 Wave 1 contacts have replied with substantive engagement (questions, thank-yous, referrals, requests for additional domains)
- Secondary distribution: Evidence that 1+ contact has shared the Gist link within their network (referral traffic to the Gist URL, or an email indicating forwarding)

**What this triggers**: Full Phase 2 scope proceeds on the timeline in Section 5. Domain 56 distributes May 28. Domain 58 distributes June 15. Domain 59 research begins June 16. Domain 57 research begins June 16 in parallel. All four Phase 2 MVP domains are in production or distribution by July 1.

### Gate 2: Moderate Adoption — Reduced Phase 2 Scope

**Indicators**: 20–39% Gist view rate; 1 organizational response; no confirmed secondary distribution. Or: strong view rates but no engagement (opens without response — suggests the framing is landing but not activating).

**What this triggers**: Reduce Phase 2 scope to must-do domains only. Proceed with Domain 56 (legislative window is hard regardless) and Domain 58 (low production cost; *Trump v. Barbara* timing is independent of adoption). Defer Domain 57 and Domain 59 research until Week 4 assessment provides clearer adoption signals. Use the Week 3 window to deepen Phase 1 distribution (extend to Wave 2 and Wave 3 contacts, refine outreach templates based on what engagement data shows) before committing to the Domain 57/59 research hours.

**Revised Phase 2 start**: Domain 57/59 research begins July 1 instead of June 16. This pushes Domain 59 completion to August 1 (losing the peer-review buffer) and Domain 57 completion to September 1 (losing the UNGA pre-distribution window). Domains 57 and 59 still complete in 2026 but at reduced advocacy leverage.

### Gate 3: Weak Adoption — Phase 2 Paused, Phase 1 Doubled Down

**Indicators**: Under 20% Gist view rate; 0 organizational responses after 10 days; or multiple bounce/non-delivery signals suggesting contact list quality problems.

**What this triggers**: Phase 2 research paused entirely. All orchestrator capacity returns to Phase 1: diagnose the adoption failure (wrong contacts? wrong framing? wrong distribution format?), revise outreach materials, attempt a second Wave 1 send to revised contacts. This is the circuit-breaker: research that is not reaching its audience is not serving the mission. Identify whether the problem is distribution mechanics (fixable in 2–3 days), framing (requires 1–2 weeks to revise), or contact quality (requires new outreach list construction — 1–2 weeks).

**When to re-evaluate Phase 2**: Week 6 (June 15). If Phase 1 adoption has recovered to Gate 2 or Gate 1 levels, resume Phase 2 with the reduced scope protocol and revised timelines.
