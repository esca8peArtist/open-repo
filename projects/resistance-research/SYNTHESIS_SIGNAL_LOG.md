### Domain 58 — Trump v. Barbara Status (Domain 58 Distribution Trigger)

**Case**: Trump v. Barbara (No. 25-365), U.S. Supreme Court  
**Subject**: Constitutional challenge to Executive Order 14160, which ends birthright citizenship for children of undocumented immigrants and temporary visa holders  
**Tribal relevance**: The case reaches tribal citizenship directly — EO 14160's narrow reading of "subject to the jurisdiction thereof" in the 14th Amendment echoes the logic that courts previously used to exclude tribal members from automatic citizenship (pre-Indian Citizenship Act 1924). Domain 58's rapid-response protocol addresses this threat.

**Procedural posture as of May 20, 2026**:
- Oral arguments: April 1, 2026 (Trump attended — historically unusual)
- Government's position: The 14th Amendment's citizenship clause was intended narrowly, not to apply to children of non-citizens
- Challengers (ACLU, respondents): Clause is inclusive; "subject to the jurisdiction thereof" has only narrow established exceptions (diplomats, foreign sovereigns, occupying armies)
- **Ruling: PENDING — expected late June / early July 2026, end of Supreme Court term**
- **No ruling has issued as of May 20, 2026**

**SCOTUSblog check**: Ruling not yet issued. Domain 58 rapid-response protocol not yet triggered. First daily monitoring check recommended June 15, 2026 per the Phase 2 activation checklist.

**Synthesis relevance**: The Trump v. Barbara ruling window (late June / early July) gives the Domain 58 distribution a hard external clock. The buffer between now (May 20) and earliest plausible ruling date (late June) is 5–6 weeks — adequate for peer review initiation in June.
