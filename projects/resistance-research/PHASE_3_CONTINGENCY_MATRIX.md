# Phase 3 Contingency Matrix
## November 4 – December 20, 2026 (7-Week Parallel Research Window)

**Purpose:** Single-reference decision document for Phase 3 crisis response. When a failure mode activates during the Nov 4 – Dec 20 window, locate your scenario, read the pre-authorized response, and execute. No deliberation required for Tier 1 responses. Escalation to user required only for Tier 2+ scope changes. Playbook details are in PHASE_3_RAPID_RESPONSE_PLAYBOOKS.md.

**Most important finding:** The January 3, 2027 Congressional seating deadline is structurally immovable. Domains K and H must reach Tier 1 contacts before December 20 for the research to shape incoming 120th Congress priorities. Every contingency response below is evaluated first by whether it protects the December 20 distribution target. Contingencies that threaten December 20 are Severity 3 (critical). Contingencies that compress quality but preserve December 20 are Severity 2 (high). Contingencies that add work but do not threaten December 20 are Severity 1 (standard).

## RISK CATEGORY 1: RESEARCHER AVAILABILITY

### R1 — Primary Researcher Short-Term Absence (≤1 Week)

**Description:** Primary researcher unavailable for up to 7 days due to illness, emergency, or travel during the Nov 4 – Dec 20 window.

**Probability:** Medium. Based on Phase 2 actuals: two capacity disruptions occurred across 8 months of Phase 2 research (one illness week, one competing project spike). At the Phase 3 pace of 40–42 hours/month, a single missed week costs 8–10 research hours.

**Impact Score:** Low. A 7-day absence is within the Phase 3 capacity buffer if it occurs before Week 5. If it occurs in Week 6 (Domain K distribution window, Dec 9–16) or Week 7 (Domain H distribution window, Dec 16–20), impact escalates to Medium.

**Severity:** 1 (standard) if Weeks 1–5; 2 (high) if Weeks 6–7.

**Activation Trigger:** Researcher unavailable Day 1 of any week.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Continue all scheduled tasks not requiring primary researcher decision authority (Gist formatting, source verification, contact email prep).
- Reschedule coalition feedback calls to Day 1 of return (calls can slip 3–5 days without critical-path impact before Week 6).
- Expert contact outreach: do not send new Tier 1 distribution emails during absence; queue and send on return.
- If absence falls in Weeks 6–7: activate Tier 2A backup researcher immediately without waiting for primary researcher return (escalate per PB-R2).

**Does NOT require user notification** unless absence extends beyond 5 days.

### R2 — Primary Researcher Extended Absence (1–3 Weeks)

**Description:** Primary researcher unavailable for 7–21 days during the Nov 4 – Dec 20 window.

**Probability:** Low. Estimated at 10–15% probability for any given 7-week research window (based on Phase 2 frequency of disruptions over 8 months).

**Impact Score:** High. A 14–21 day absence during the 7-week sprint eliminates 30–40% of total available research hours. Domain K has 6–10 hours of estimated remaining work after Week 3; Domain H has 14–19 hours of remaining work after Week 3. An extended absence mid-sprint threatens both December deadlines.

**Severity:** 2 (high).

**Activation Trigger:** Absence confirmed to exceed 7 days, OR short-term absence escalates past Day 5.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Activate Tier 2A backup researcher immediately. Briefing: 2–3 hours. Backup resumes research within 24 hours.
- Primary researcher provides async status memo (2–3 sentences per zone: complete, next, blockers) before Day 2 of absence.
- Backup researcher receives: current research status doc, contact list, expert call scripts, Gist templates, outstanding source verification list.
- Primary researcher remains available for async Q&A (max 2-hour response time) if capacity allows.
- If absence spans the Week 6 distribution window (Dec 9–16): escalate immediately to Tier 3 response (PB-R3); do not risk the Domain K distribution deadline on async coordination.

**Requires user notification:** Yes, within 24 hours of Tier 2A activation.

### R3 — Primary Researcher Full Exit (>3 Weeks or Permanent Transition)

**Description:** Primary researcher unavailable for more than 21 days, or indicates permanent unavailability for Phase 3.

**Probability:** Low. Estimated at 5% for the 7-week window based on Phase 2 continuity (zero full exits in 8 months; one extended-absence scenario).

**Impact Score:** Critical. A full exit mid-sprint requires complete domain ownership transition. Pre-identified Tier 3 researcher must onboard in 8–12 hours and assume full ownership. If exit occurs after Week 4, December 20 deadline is at significant risk.

**Severity:** 3 (critical).

**Activation Trigger:** Primary researcher exit confirmed; absence confirmed >21 days.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Activate Tier 3 replacement researcher immediately (see PHASE_3_RESEARCHER_AVAILABILITY_CONTINGENCY.md Section 6 for contact list).
- Tier 3 onboarding: 8–12 hours (read existing research ~12,700 words Domain K + ~7,500 words Domain H; review master calendar; review expert contact framework; sync with peer reviewer).
- Scope compress if exit occurs after Week 4: Domain K distribution target remains December 20; Domain H target shifts to combined brief only (6–8 pages) for December 20 distribution with full Gist deferred to January 10.
- If exit occurs after Week 6: Domain H full production defers to Phase 4; only combined brief publishes by January 10.

**Requires user notification:** Yes, immediately upon exit confirmation. User decision required on scope compress.

### R4 — Domain 37a Consumes November Capacity (Not Pre-Staged)

**Description:** Domain 37a (Post-Election Certification Protection) was not research-complete before November 4, requiring 15–25 hours of emergency production in November. This is the highest-probability high-impact contingency in Phase 3 — PHASE_3_PARALLEL_RESEARCH_RUNWAY.md identifies it as "the single most consequential Phase 2-to-Phase 3 risk factor."

**Probability:** High. If Domain 37a is not pre-staged before October 31 (which depends on Phase 2 completion), this contingency activates with near-certainty. Estimated probability that 37a is not pre-staged at Phase 3 launch: 40–60% based on Phase 2 completion rates.

**Impact Score:** High. Domain K research window compresses from 5 weeks to 3 weeks (December 2–20). Domain H research window compresses from 7 weeks to 4 weeks (December 16–January 10). Domain H Tier 1 distribution may slip from January 10 to January 17 — still within the "blank slate window" before committee assignments harden (January 17–20), but with zero buffer.

**Severity:** 2 (high).

**Activation Trigger:** Domain 37a is not production-complete at Phase 3 launch (November 4).

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Domain K compresses to December 2–20 window (3 weeks). Sufficient for 8–13 hours remaining Domain K work with 3-week window.
- Domain H compresses to December 16–January 10 window (4 weeks). Sufficient for 14–19 hours remaining Domain H work but with zero buffer.
- Solo researcher priority: Domain K first (December 2–20), then Domain H combined brief (December 16–20), Domain H full Gist in January window.
- 2-researcher model: Researcher A completes Domain 37a in November; Researcher B begins Domain K immediately (no waiting for 37a completion).
- Accept partial success metric: if Domain H Tier 1 distribution slips from January 10 to January 17, this remains within tolerance.

**Does not require user notification** if solo researcher executes compression autonomously. Notify user if 2-researcher model requires activating Researcher B earlier than planned.

### R5 — 2-Researcher Model; Mid-Sprint Handoff Required

**Description:** In a 2-researcher model, one researcher must hand off their domain to the other mid-sprint due to availability changes.

**Probability:** Low. Estimated at 10–20% for any 7-week sprint with two researchers.

**Impact Score:** Medium. A clean handoff costs 8–16 hours (handoff document + synchronization). A poorly executed handoff risks continuity errors in source citations and expert contact relationship state.

**Severity:** 2 (high) if handoff occurs in Weeks 5–7 (combined brief and distribution windows); 1 (standard) if Weeks 1–4.

**Activation Trigger:** One researcher confirms unavailability for 1+ remaining weeks mid-sprint.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Handing-off researcher completes HANDOFF_NOTES.md within 24 hours: current status, next 3 tasks, open questions, source verification status (which of 90+ Domain H citations and 67 Domain K sources are still unverified), and contact activation status (which experts have been contacted, what their response was).
- Receiving researcher schedules 1-hour synchronization review before resuming work.
- Avoid initiating handoff in Weeks 5–7 if any alternative exists. If unavoidable: receiving researcher takes the combined brief (Week 6) as their primary deliverable and deprioritizes the full Domain H Gist.
- Both researchers maintain one synchronization check at Week 6 (combined brief coordination) and Week 7 (consistency review) regardless of handoff.

**Requires user notification:** Yes, within 48 hours of handoff initiation.

## RISK CATEGORY 2: EXPERT CONTACT

### E1 — Tier 1 Contact Unresponsive (Fix the Court, Brennan Center, Demand Justice, ACS)

**Description:** A primary Tier 1 contact does not respond within 7 days of initial outreach. The October–November 2026 pre-launch coordination with Fix the Court (Gabe Roth), Brennan Center (Derek Rosenfeld), Demand Justice (Josh Orton), and ACS (Nancy Rodriguez) is the anchor for Domain K's distribution strategy.

**Probability:** Medium. Based on Phase 2 outreach actuals: approximately 30–40% of Tier 1 cold contacts do not respond to initial outreach. The pre-activated relationships from Phase 2 reduce this risk for known contacts, but new contacts at these organizations have unknown response rates.

**Impact Score:** Medium. A single unresponsive Tier 1 contact does not block distribution; it reduces the reach multiplier. Fix the Court (50,000+ email list) is the highest-value contact; an unreached Fix the Court reduces estimated Domain K distribution reach by approximately 25%.

**Severity:** 1 (standard).

**Activation Trigger:** No response from Tier 1 contact within 7 days of initial outreach.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Day 8: Send one follow-up email to same contact with subject line variation (more specific subject; reference specific Domain K finding relevant to their work).
- Day 14: Pivot to secondary contact at same organization (Gabe Roth's deputy at Fix the Court; Derek Rosenfeld's supervisor or program officer at Brennan Center; legislative director at ACS rather than executive director).
- Secondary contacts are documented in PHASE_3_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md and DOMAIN_K_EXPERT_CONTACT_FRAMEWORK.md — both files have tier-by-tier contact lists with secondary names identified.
- If secondary contact also unresponsive by Day 21: mark as "non-responsive" and proceed to Tier 2 distribution contacts. Do not delay Domain K distribution waiting for Fix the Court or Brennan Center response.

**Does not require user notification.**

### E2 — Multiple Tier 1 Contacts Simultaneously Unresponsive

**Description:** Two or more Tier 1 contacts unresponsive simultaneously during the October–November pre-launch window (the warm activation period before formal distribution).

**Probability:** Low. Phase 2 outreach showed that at least some Tier 1 contacts respond when multiple are contacted; simultaneous silence across 3+ contacts suggests either a timing problem (holiday, major news event dominating attention) or a format problem.

**Impact Score:** High. If Fix the Court, Brennan Center, and Demand Justice are all non-responsive, Domain K loses its primary distribution multiplier (estimated 600,000–700,000 readers through their combined networks per PHASE_3_MOVEMENT_LEVERAGE_ARCHITECTURE.md). Distribution falls back to Tier 2 academic and law school networks with estimated 50,000–100,000 reach.

**Severity:** 2 (high).

**Activation Trigger:** Three or more Tier 1 contacts non-responsive after 14 days of initial outreach.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Timing hypothesis first: if non-response coincides with major election news, congressional vote, or holiday period, hold 7 additional days before concluding format problem.
- Format pivot: consult PHASE_3_CONTINGENCY_ROUTING.md Contingency B (format pivot protocol). Test whether the research reaches the same contacts through passive discovery (Substack post, Obsidian Publish) rather than direct email.
- Activate university-based alternates: constitutional law faculty at Yale Law (Jack Balkin), Harvard Law (Laurence Tribe if available), Georgetown Law, or Berkeley Law Chemerinsky are Tier 2 contacts that can substitute for Tier 1 coalition organizations in terms of credibility signal, if not in terms of distribution reach.
- Contact the National Constitution Center (Jeffrey Rosen) directly as a bipartisan bridge contact that does not require progressive coalition activation — NCC can route Domain H content to audiences unreachable through the primary Tier 1 list.
- Do not delay December 20 distribution waiting for Tier 1 responses. Distribute to all responsive contacts on schedule.

**Requires user notification:** Yes, if format pivot decision is required (changes overall Phase 3 distribution strategy).

### E4 — Expert Provides Conflicting Factual Feedback

**Description:** A Tier 1 or Tier 2 expert contact (e.g., Brennan Center's Alicia Bannon on Domain K legislative feasibility, or Fix the Court's Gabe Roth on bill co-sponsor counts) provides factual feedback that contradicts existing Domain K or H analysis.

**Probability:** Medium. Research briefs at Phase 3 depth routinely encounter expert correction on specific legislative details (bill status changes, co-sponsor counts, feasibility assessments). Domain K's legislative inventory (67 sources, 12+ active bills) is particularly susceptible to status changes between June 2026 staging and November 2026 research completion.

**Impact Score:** Medium. Factual corrections to specific bill co-sponsor counts or committee status are minor (correction in-place, no delay). Conflicting analytical assessments (e.g., expert disputes the feasibility scoring methodology for constitutional amendment pathways) require deliberation.

**Severity:** 1 (standard) for factual corrections; 2 (high) for methodology disputes.

**Activation Trigger:** Expert response contains factual or analytical correction.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Factual corrections (bill numbers, co-sponsor counts, legislative status): correct in-place immediately, update the production document, note correction in the document's revision log. No delay.
- Analytical disputes (feasibility scoring, constitutional interpretation): triangulate with a second expert or second source before accepting the critique. If two experts conflict, document both views in the relevant section with attribution (e.g., "Practitioner X assesses the TERM Act as having low passage probability in the 120th Congress; legal scholar Y argues the constitutional basis is stronger than practitioner estimates suggest"). Do not hold publication waiting for consensus.
- If the dispute is on a central finding (not a peripheral claim): flag in CHECKIN.md under "Needs Your Input." Do not modify central analytical framework without user decision.

**Requires user notification only for central finding disputes.**

## RISK CATEGORY 3: DATA SOURCES

### D1 — Congress.gov Legislative Status Unavailable

**Description:** Congress.gov offline or inaccessible during the November research window, preventing real-time bill status verification for Domain K's 12+ active legislative targets.

**Probability:** Low. Congress.gov experiences occasional downtime (estimated 0.5% uptime disruption annually) but has no extended outages in recent years. The window of concern is the November 4–December 20 active research period.

**Impact Score:** Medium. Domain K's legislative inventory requires bill status verification (committee assignment, co-sponsor count, latest action) against Congress.gov primary source. Alternative mirrors exist but are not as authoritative.

**Severity:** 1 (standard).

**Activation Trigger:** Congress.gov inaccessible for more than 4 hours during scheduled research session.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Primary mirror: GovTrack.us — provides real-time bill tracking with committee assignments, co-sponsor counts, and action history. Not a primary source but functionally equivalent for status verification.
- Secondary mirror: Ballotpedia Legislative Reference (if Ballotpedia access active); provides committee status and vote tracking.
- Tertiary: Fix the Court publishes annual legislative status updates that include co-sponsor counts for all major judicial reform bills — contact Fix the Court directly for current counts if Congress.gov and GovTrack are both down.
- Estimate: +2–4 hours added to source verification if using mirrors. Within Phase 3 capacity buffer.
- If outage extends beyond 24 hours: queue all bill status verification items and complete them on Congress.gov return. Do not delay other research tracks waiting for Congress.gov.

**Does not require user notification.**

### D2 — Primary Source URLs Broken (Domain H 90+ Citations)

**Description:** During the November research update pass, a significant portion of Domain H's 90+ citations return 404 or redirect errors. Domain H's production document (DOMAIN_H_CONSTITUTIONAL_RESILIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md) was completed in prior sessions; URL link rot is expected over the 4–5 months between production and distribution.

**Probability:** Medium. Based on general web link rot rates (estimated 5–10% of URLs become inaccessible per year), Domain H's 90+ citation list would expect 5–9 broken links per year, concentrated in government agency pages and NGO report pages that reorganize frequently. Phase 2 source verification passes have consistently found broken government links.

**Impact Score:** High if broken links affect anchor sources (V-Dem Democracy Index, Germany Basic Law amendment primary text, IEEPA statutory text). Low if broken links affect secondary journalism or blog sources.

**Severity:** 2 (high) for anchor sources; 1 (standard) for secondary sources.

**Activation Trigger:** Source verification pass finds more than 10 broken URLs, or any anchor source URL broken.

**Pre-Authorized Response:**
- Pre-built Wayback Machine archive protocol: for all anchor sources, check archive.org for cached version dated within 6 months of original publication. 90%+ of anchor sources have Wayback Machine snapshots available.
- For government primary sources (IEEPA, 28 U.S.C. citations, CRS reports): use official government mirrors (govinfo.gov for historical statutory text; Congress.gov for CRS products; PACER for court documents).
- For international sources (Germany Bundesgesetzblatt for Basic Law amendment, Israel Knesset Official Gazette): use official institutional archives rather than English-language secondary summaries.
- Time estimate: source verification update pass adds 8–12 hours to the Domain H production schedule if URLs require systematic replacement. This is within the 14–19 hours of remaining Domain H work planned for December.
- Prioritization: verify and replace anchor sources first (V-Dem, Germany Basic Law, Humphrey's Executor / Trump v. Slaughter ruling, Article V convention count). Secondary sources can be noted as "verified as of [date]" with Wayback Machine archive dates.

**Does not require user notification** unless anchor source verification reveals the underlying data has changed (e.g., V-Dem scores revised, Germany Basic Law amendment challenged).

**Impact Score:** High if contested results prevent any determination of 120th Congress composition by November 14. Medium if some state results are delayed but national composition is clear.

**Severity:** 2 (high) for full delay; 1 (standard) for partial delay.

Produce all three frame sections simultaneously, then redact two based on final results. Adds 4–6 hours of parallel drafting.

- If results contested past December 1: proceed with Domain K distribution using a split-scenario brief ("Under either likely 120th Congress composition, these three structural reforms are advanceable..."). This is less targeted but reaches Tier 1 contacts before December 20.

**Requires user notification** if no congressional composition determination is possible by November 20 (requires user decision on split-scenario brief approach).

**Description:** GitHub Gist platform unavailable (outage or access issue) when Domain K or H Gist needs to be created or shared at the December 20 distribution deadline.

**Probability:** Low. GitHub Gist has historically high uptime. The primary risk is account-level access issues (lost credentials, GitHub account suspension) rather than platform outages.

**Impact Score:** Medium. All Phase 2 distribution used Gist as the primary sharing mechanism; Tier 1 contacts are familiar with the format. A Gist failure forces a format pivot that may reduce the professional signal of the distribution package.

**Severity:** 1 (standard).

**Activation Trigger:** Gist platform inaccessible within 48 hours of planned distribution send.
