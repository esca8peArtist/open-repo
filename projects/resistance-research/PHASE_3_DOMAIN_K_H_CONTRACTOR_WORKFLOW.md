# Phase 3 Domain K + H Contractor Workflow

**This document governs the full lifecycle of contractor engagement for Phase 3 research, from pre-hire profile definition through final deliverable handoff. It is adapted from the Seedwarden contractor onboarding framework (6-phase pattern) and applies it to an academic research delivery context. Coordinators running the November 4 launch use this document; hired researchers use PHASE_3_RESEARCHER_ONBOARDING_KIT.md.**

## Phase 0: Hiring — Researcher Profile and Budget

### Researcher Profile

The target researcher is not a generalist academic writer. The role requires a person who can navigate dense primary legal source material (bill texts, court opinions, CRS reports, PACER documents), synthesize it at advocacy-ready quality, and deliver 10,000–20,000-word documents on a defined weekly schedule. The specific disciplinary background required:

**Academic standing (required):**
- PhD candidate in final year (dissertation defended or under committee review), or
- PhD recipient within the last four years

**Eligible disciplines:**
- Political science with constitutional law or judicial politics specialization
- Legal studies / law and society (JD-PhD or pure PhD track)
- Public law, comparative politics, or international relations with democratic governance focus
- History of constitutional development (secondary option if legal expertise is strong)

A JD alone without a research track record is not sufficient. A practicing attorney with at least two substantial published policy reports is a case-by-case exception. The work requires research synthesis skills, not legal practice skills.

**Publication record (required):**
- Minimum two peer-reviewed publications in the last three years, OR
- One peer-reviewed publication plus two substantial policy reports (5,000+ words, externally published)

**Substantive domain knowledge (at least one of the following required):**
- Federal judiciary structure, Supreme Court reform, or judicial ethics
- Constitutional design, amendment theory, or structural democratic reform
- International democratic backsliding and comparative constitutional systems
- US democratic erosion literature (V-Dem, Polity, Freedom House frameworks appear throughout both domains)

**Availability (non-negotiable hard constraint):**
- 10–15 hours per week for 13 consecutive weeks, November 4 – January 31, 2027
- No dissertation defense, book manuscript, or major academic deadline inside this window
- No teaching load above 30% of normal during the research window

**Strongly weighted preferences:**
- Prior consulting or employment relationship with Brennan Center, Fix the Court, ACS, Protect Democracy, Democracy Docket, Constitutional Accountability Center, ACLU, or comparable organization
- Comfortable navigating Congress.gov, CRS reports, SCOTUSblog archives, PACER documents without instruction
- Work history shows delivered writing to external clients or journals on a defined schedule

### Budget Ceiling

| Configuration | Hours | Hourly Rate | Total per Domain | Combined Ceiling |
|---------------|-------|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| Solo researcher (Domain K + H, sequential) | 160–200 hrs total | $35–$55/hr | N/A (single engagement) | $5,600–$11,000 |
| Two-person team (Domain K: Researcher A; Domain H: Researcher B) | 80–100 hrs each | $35–$55/hr | $2,800–$5,500 each | $5,600–$11,000 |

**Budget ceiling applied to this workflow: $5,000–$8,000 per domain; $10,000–$16,000 if hiring a solo researcher for both domains combined across the 8-week effort (November 4 – January 31 = 13 weeks at 10–15 hours/week).**

The 8-week framing cited in the task scope is the research production core (Zones K-1 through K-4 run approximately 7 weeks; Domain H extensions run approximately 5 weeks, with 1 week overlap). Total calendar span including distribution prep is 13 weeks.

Expense reimbursement beyond hourly rate: JSTOR annual plan ($199), PACER fees (up to $30/quarter), per-article fees with prior authorization (up to $50 total). 10% contingency buffer recommended above cited ceiling.

### Configuration Decision Framework

**Solo configuration (one researcher, both domains)**: Appropriate when a strong candidate with demonstrated completion record is identified by September 22 and commits firmly to the full window. Advantages: single researcher builds cross-domain contextual continuity; no coordination overhead; lower management burden. Single point of failure risk.

**Two-person configuration (two researchers, one domain each)**: Appropriate if no firm solo commitment exists by September 25. Researcher A executes Domain K; Researcher B executes Domain H beginning November 18 (staggered to allow sequential onboarding). Higher schedule resilience; parallel zone execution compresses overall timeline. Coordination overhead requires a shared reference document and weekly deconfliction on sources shared across both domains.

**Decision deadline: October 1.** Offers extended October 1 regardless of configuration. No-slip rule: November 4 onboarding has no slack. If all October 1 offers decline, activate immediate second-wave outreach with explicit 8–10 hours/week framing for two-person fallback.

## Phase 1: Sources Aggregation

**Timeline: October 18 – November 10 (pre-onboarding through Week 1)**

This phase covers the researcher's access setup and the coordinator's source currency validation before research begins.

### Coordinator actions (October 18 – November 3, before researcher begins):

**Verify source accessibility (20% spot-check across both domains):**
- DOMAIN_K_SOURCE_MASTER_DATABASE.md — verify all external links active, identify dead links for pre-replacement
- DOMAIN_H_SOURCE_MASTER_DATABASE.md — same
- PHASE_3_DOMAIN_K_SOURCES_AND_CONTACTS.md — verify contact emails still current
- PHASE_3_DOMAIN_H_SOURCE_STRATEGY.md — confirm access pathway for any paywalled sources

**Verify legislative currency (Zone K-1 priority items):**
- Congress.gov: H.R. 1074 (Supreme Court Tenure Establishment Act), S.1814 (SCERT Act), Raskin shadow docket package, Manchin-Welch amendment, H.R. 2361 (cameras bill), Goldman-Booker ethics act, H.J.Res.28 — check status as of October 2026
- Document any bills that have advanced to committee markup, received floor votes, or been withdrawn since June 2026 baseline
- Flag any co-sponsor count changes above 5 new co-sponsors (threshold for argument strength update)

**Verify litigation currency (Domain H priority items):**
- Trump v. Slaughter (FTC removal power): confirm ruling issued, integrate into Domain H Section 1
- Any OT2025-2026 SCOTUS opinions affecting independent agency structural argument
- November 2026 ballot results (Missouri, North Dakota, South Dakota, Utah anti-ballot-initiative measures; Kansas judicial selection) — these post-date November 3 and feed directly into Domain H Extension C

### Researcher actions (October 18 – November 3, pre-onboarding):

- Read PHASE_3_RESEARCHER_ONBOARDING_KIT.md in full
- Confirm JSTOR access (purchase JPASS annual plan if no institutional access)
- Create PACER account (pacer.uscourts.gov — free registration)
- Bookmark and spot-check 5 primary sources from Zone K-1 and 3 primary sources from Domain H existing document
- Submit 3 clarifying questions to coordinator by October 25 (receive written answers by October 28)

### Week 1 researcher actions (November 4–10):

- Zone K-1: verify and update all Domain K bill status (Congress.gov primary source; no secondary summaries)
- Domain H: read full existing production document (domains/domain-H-constitutional-architecture.md) — take notes on gaps, update needs, extension opportunities
- Produce: Legislative inventory validation memo (Zone K-1) and Domain H orientation gap assessment by November 10

## Phase 2: Cross-Source Synthesis

**Timeline: Weeks 2–6 (November 11 – December 14)**

The synthesis phase is the core research production window. It runs in parallel for Domain K zones and sequentially for Domain H extensions.

### Domain K synthesis sequence:

**Zone K-2 (Constitutional Feasibility) — Weeks 2–3:**
- Primary sources: Brennan Center active/senior justice statutory analysis; Fix the Court H.R. 1074 constitutional analysis; 2021 Presidential Commission on the Supreme Court (term limits section, minority views)
- Opposing argument analysis: Article III "good Behaviour" interpretation; constitutional amendment requirement argument (Heritage Foundation; Josh Hammer law review articles)
- Confidence level protocol: where the constitutional debate is genuinely unresolved, state explicitly — "the dominant scholarly view holds..." or "this interpretation is contested — [Name] argues X while Fix the Court argues Y"
- Target: Zone K-2 output, 5,000–6,000 words

**Zone K-3 (International Precedent) — Weeks 3–4:**
- Germany: Bundesverfassungsgericht 12-year non-renewable term; appointment model (Bundestag/Bundesrat joint selection); how this compares to SCERT Act statutory approach
- Canada: mandatory retirement at 75 for Supreme Court justices; appointment process reform
- Australia, UK: comparative structures; identify any 2025–2026 developments
- France: Conseil Constitutionnel non-renewable 9-year terms; Brazil mandatory retirement at 75; 47-state domestic comparison
- Cross-domain integration note: coordinate with Domain H researcher (if 2-person) to avoid duplicating Germany/Ireland comparative analysis — Domain H covers Germany's Article 21 eternity clause and Ireland's Citizens' Assembly; Domain K covers Bundesverfassungsgericht term structure. Clear deconfliction boundary: Domain K covers judicial selection and tenure mechanisms; Domain H covers constitutional design and amendment process models
- Target: Zone K-3 output, 4,000–5,000 words

**Zone K-1/K-2/K-3 integration pass — Week 5:**
- Integrate zone outputs into single continuous Domain K draft
- Resolve any contradictions between Zone K-2 constitutional feasibility argument and Zone K-3 international precedent
- Ensure cross-references to Domain H are explicit in both directions: Domain K's term limits analysis should cross-reference Domain H's amendment process analysis where they connect
- Target: Integrated Domain K draft, 13,000–15,000 words

**Zone K-4 (Coalition Activation) — Week 6:**
- This zone cannot begin until Zones K-1 through K-3 are complete and integrated
- Produce: 500-word executive summary for congressional staff; 1,200-word policy brief for constitutional law scholars and ACS/CAC distribution; 2,000-word section-by-section legislative guide for Fix the Court and Demand Justice
- Target: Zone K-4 complete; Domain K full synthesis production-ready

### Domain H synthesis sequence (runs Weeks 5–10, beginning after Zone K-1 is complete):

**Extension A (International Case Studies deepening) — Weeks 8–9:**
- Germany: deepen constructive vote of no confidence analysis; Article 21 eternity clause as model for US statutory reform; how to integrate into Domain H's existing Section 5 argument (not repeat from phase-3-research-roadmap.md — integrate)
- Ireland: Citizens' Assembly deliberative process as constitutional bypass mechanism; transferability to US state-level constitutional reform processes
- New Zealand: Royal Commission electoral reform pathway (Electoral Commission → referendum → STV → MMP implementation); integrate into Domain H reform architecture section
- Target: Extension A draft, 1,500–2,000 words integrated into Domain H Section 5

**Extension B (Reform Architecture legislative update) — Week 9:**
- NPVIC: update state count from 222 electoral votes (April 2026 baseline); any new state joiners through October 2026?
- No Kings Act: co-sponsor count update
- H.R. 1074: committee activity update
- Trump v. Slaughter: ruling integration — if court eliminated for-cause removal protections for FTC, what does this imply for the Domain H argument about independent agency structural reform?
- Target: 1,000–1,500 words of integrated updates, woven into existing sections rather than appended

**Extension C (Post-June 2026 developments) — Week 10:**
- November 2026 ballot results: Missouri Amendment 4, North Dakota Measure 2, South Dakota supermajority measure, Utah tax initiative measure; Kansas judicial selection August 4 result — integrate into Domain H Section 3 (amendment process weaponization argument)
- OT2025-2026 SCOTUS rulings not yet integrated: full sweep of any rulings affecting Domain H's argument about structural constitutional vulnerability
- Target: 500–1,000 words of integrated updates

## Phase 3: Expert Fact-Checking

**Timeline: Weeks 4 and 11 (embedded, not a standalone phase)**

Expert fact-checking in this workflow is not a separate peer review stage — it is embedded outreach at two specific points where expert input adds the most value.

### Expert contact protocol:

Before contacting any expert, read at least one recent published piece of their work. Cold contact without having read their work is inefficient. Do not deviate from the pre-identified list without coordinator confirmation — some contacts have pre-existing relationships with the project.

**Email template for expert contact:**
> Subject: Research inquiry — [Domain K / Domain H], brief question on [specific topic]
>
> Dear [Name],
>
> I am a researcher on a Phase 3 constitutional law research project focused on [judiciary reform / constitutional resilience architecture]. I read your [specific piece] and have a specific question about [narrow topic that their published work does not fully resolve].
>
> [One-sentence question.]
>
> If you have 5 minutes to respond by email, I am available for a brief call at [two specific times] if that is easier.
>
> Thank you for your time.
> [Name]

Follow-up cadence: one follow-up email if no reply within 5 business days. If no reply after follow-up, mark non-responsive and proceed without that input — the timeline does not allow extended expert chasing.

## Phase 4: Final Editing

**Timeline: Weeks 7 and 11–12**

### Domain K final edit (Week 7, December 16–22):

- Fact-check 5 key citations against primary sources (bill texts from Congress.gov; court opinions from SCOTUSblog or official slip opinion PDFs; polling numbers from originating polling organization)
- Verify all URLs are active (spot-check 10 links minimum)
- Read the Zone K-4 executive summary aloud — if it requires a legal background to understand, it is not ready
- Confirm: no placeholder sections, no [VERIFY THIS] flags, no broken links
- Plain-language test: the executive summary must communicate (a) what the structural problem is, (b) what the three most achievable legislative reforms are, and (c) why the January 2027 congressional window matters — to a reader with no prior knowledge of the project
- Submit Domain K to coordinator by December 22

### Domain H final edit (Weeks 11–12, January 20–31):

- Final cross-domain consistency check: ensure Domain K and H cross-references are accurate; no contradictory claims between the two documents
- Integration pass: verify that Extensions A, B, C read as a unified whole with the existing production document — not as appendices
- Domain H companion distribution note: write a 400-word note summarizing how Domain H connects to Domain K for the Tier 1 distribution email (Senate Judiciary Committee staff, ACS, Brennan Center)
- Final fact-check pass: verify 5 key citations against primary sources
- Submit Domain H to coordinator by January 28; two-page research summary (what was produced, what open questions remain, recommended first distribution targets) due January 31

## Phase 5: Distribution Prep

**Timeline: Weeks 6–7 and 12 (embedded in final research weeks)**

Distribution prep in this workflow means preparing the Gist and distribution email infrastructure — not executing the send (which the user does).

### Domain K distribution prep (Week 6–7):

- Extract executive summary (500 words) as standalone document for congressional staff email attachment
- Format Domain K for GitHub Gist (markdown-ready; cross-references hyperlinked where possible)
- Identify Tier 1 send sequence: Senate Judiciary Committee staff → Fix the Court → ACS → Brennan Center judiciary program → Constitutional Accountability Center
- Draft Tier 1 email template: subject line, 3-sentence lead, Gist link, one-sentence ask (review and share with colleagues who work on judicial reform legislation)
- Log distribution prep completion in PHASE_3_EXECUTION_MASTER_CALENDAR.md

### Domain H distribution prep (Week 11):

- Format Domain H for GitHub Gist (integrated document with extensions; 10,000–12,000 words in final form)
- Draft companion distribution email explaining the Domain K + H linked pair: "two companion documents — one on what Congress can do about the judiciary now; one on the constitutional context in which those reforms operate"
- Confirm Tier 1 send sequence is consistent with Domain K contacts (distribute as paired set, not separately)

## Phase 6: Handoff

**Timeline: January 28–31, 2027**

The handoff is the formal completion gate for the contracted engagement.

### Researcher deliverables due January 31:

1. Domain K final document (15,000–20,000 words; production-ready; no broken links)
2. Domain K Zone K-4 executive summary (500 words; standalone)
3. Domain K Zone K-4 policy brief (1,200 words; standalone)
4. Domain K Zone K-4 legislative guide (2,000 words; for Fix the Court and Demand Justice)
5. Domain H final document (10,000–12,000 words; production-ready; Extensions A, B, C integrated)
6. Domain H companion distribution note (400 words)
7. Two-page research summary: (a) 3 findings not anticipated in preliminary research; (b) 2 open questions research could not resolve and why; (c) recommended first distribution contact for each domain
8. Source access log: JSTOR and PACER expenses for reimbursement with receipts

### Coordinator verification at handoff:

- Both final documents are in production-ready condition (no placeholders, no verification flags, no broken links)
- Zone K-4 executive summary passes plain-language test (non-specialist can read it in 5 minutes and understand the problem, the three reforms, and the timing)
- Domain K and H cross-references are consistent and accurate
- All bill texts are cited from Congress.gov primary source
- All court opinions cited from official court, SCOTUSblog, or slip opinion PDFs
- All statistical claims cited from originating organization or institutional publisher

### Final payment:

Final invoice submitted by researcher January 31. Payment within 10 business days of receipt. No payment contingent on adoption outcomes — payment is for research production.

## Contingency Triggers

These conditions require immediate escalation to the coordinator — do not proceed to the next week without resolution:

| Week | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Week 1 | Zone K-1 cannot verify a key bill (H.R. 1074, SCERT Act, Raskin package) — significant changes from June 2026 baseline that alter the Domain K argument | Email coordinator immediately with specific change and argument implications |
| Week 2 | Total output at end of Week 2 under 4,000 words (Zone K-1 + K-2 Part 1 combined) | Email coordinator by Day 7 with revised timeline and scope proposal |
| Week 5 | Integration reveals significant contradiction between Zone K-2 constitutional feasibility and Zone K-3 international precedent | Email coordinator with specific contradiction; do not paper over with hedged language |
| Week 8 | Domain H Extension A research reveals significant post-June 2026 development in Germany, Ireland, or New Zealand that changes the Domain H argument | Email coordinator immediately; discuss whether to update argument or note development |
| Any week | Schedule disruption reducing availability below 8 hours/week for two or more consecutive weeks | Email coordinator within 24 hours of disruption; do not wait until end of affected week |
