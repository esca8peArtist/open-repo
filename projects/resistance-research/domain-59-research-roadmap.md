## Executive Summary

Domain 59 is the most analytically demanding Phase 2 domain because it requires synthesizing three distinct scholarly literatures — political participation research, behavioral economics, and labor economics — into a single causal argument. The outline is complete. Section 1 (the evidence narrative) is fully drafted with current 2025–2026 data. Forty-seven sources are annotated and staged. Seven causal pathways are mapped with their empirical anchors identified.

The production bottleneck is the synthesis of the compounding OBBBA analysis (Section 3): demonstrating that the five simultaneous cuts to civic capacity are multiplicative rather than merely additive requires careful empirical construction. The Dallas Fed WP2517 causal anchor (financial relief → turnout) is confirmed and is the strongest existing evidence linking financial conditions to electoral behavior. The Slee/Desmond and PNAS 2024 housing studies provide the second causal pillar. Mullainathan/Shafir's scarcity framework, confirmed by a 2024 meta-analysis, provides the theoretical mechanism.

This roadmap specifies: (1) what research remains to be done by section, (2) who the interview targets are and the contact sequence, (3) the advocacy window calendar and its implications for production sequencing, and (4) success metrics with measurable definitions.

### PHASE 3: Synthesis Writing (July 14–20)
*10–12 hours*

This is the most analytically demanding phase. The compounding argument — why OBBBA's five simultaneous cuts are multiplicative, not additive — requires careful construction that neither overstates the mechanism (which would invite valid empirical challenge) nor understates it (which would reduce the policy impact of the argument).

**The compounding argument protocol**:
1. Establish the theoretical mechanism (Mullainathan/Shafir tunneling) — this is confirmed and citable.
2. Establish empirically that the affected population faces multiple simultaneous stressors (Urban Institute, CBPP distributional data).
3. Make the multiplicative claim carefully: each additional stressor compounds the others through the bandwidth mechanism because bandwidth is finite and multiple simultaneous stressors do not share it additively. Cite the scarcity literature's findings on multiple scarcity domains.
4. Do not claim a specific multiplier without an empirical source for that multiplier. "5–7x" is too precise without data; "compounding, not additive" is defensible. Adjust language accordingly.

**Synthesis tasks**:
- Write executive summary from completed sections (400–500 words)
- Write "What This Domain Adds" section (Section 7) from outline (500–600 words)
- Ensure all cross-domain bridges are explicit and bidirectional (i.e., each bridge names both the upstream and downstream domain)
- Write the distribution strategy section (Section 8) — the democratic design reframe and its three new advocacy pathways
