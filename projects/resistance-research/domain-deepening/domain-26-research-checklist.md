# Domain 26 Research Checklist

**Use**: When user approves Domain 26, work through this checklist systematically before beginning the domain document. Tier 1 items are required. Tier 2 items strengthen the analysis. Tier 3 items add comparative depth.

---

## Tier 1: Must-Have Sources (Block on these — do not write without them)

### Legal/Statutory Framework

- [ ] WHPA (Women's Health Protection Act) full text — most recent version introduced in Congress
  - Priority questions: exact provisions, current Senate cosponsor count, procedural posture
  - Source: congress.gov
  
- [ ] Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization (2022) — Thomas concurrence specifically
  - Priority questions: exact language of invitation to reconsider Griswold/Lawrence/Obergefell; majority's "deeply rooted" analytical test
  - Source: Supreme Court slip opinion, already well-documented in archive

- [ ] EMTALA April 2026 rescission — what was rescinded and what is the clinical record
  - Priority questions: exact text of Biden EMTALA guidance; exact text of rescission; documented clinical cases of denied care
  - Source: HHS press releases, CMS rulemaking record; ACOG clinical reports

- [ ] United States v. Skrmetti — Supreme Court decision on gender-affirming care bans
  - Priority question: WHAT DID THE COURT DECIDE? (This was argued in late 2024, decision expected in 2025 — the outcome fundamentally shapes 26c)
  - Source: Supreme Court opinion; SCOTUSblog

- [ ] Right to Contraception Act — text and legislative status
  - Source: congress.gov; Senate cosponsors

### Epidemiological Data

- [ ] CDC maternal mortality data — most current available (2021 cited in archive; check for 2022/2023)
  - Priority figures: overall rate, Black maternal mortality rate, comparison to peer nations
  - Source: CDC National Vital Statistics System

- [ ] State abortion ban impact on maternal mortality — documented clinical mechanism
  - Priority questions: what specific conditions are causing deaths; which states have documented cases
  - Source: ACOG, NEJM, BMJ; state Maternal Mortality Review Committee reports

- [ ] Gender-affirming care outcomes evidence
  - Priority: peer-reviewed systematic reviews on mental health outcomes; suicidality data for denied care vs. received care
  - Source: WPATH Standards of Care (8th Edition, 2022); Pediatrics; JAMA; American Academy of Pediatrics

### Political/Movement Context

- [ ] State ballot initiative full record post-Dobbs (2022-2026)
  - Priority: every state, result, margin, and outcome
  - Source: Ballotpedia; Center for Reproductive Rights tracker

- [ ] Republican state legislation to restrict citizen initiatives post-Dobbs
  - Priority: which states, what mechanisms, how many passed
  - Source: NCSL; Brennan Center; state legislative databases

## Tier 2: Should-Have Sources (Important but researchable during writing)

### International Benchmarks

- [ ] France 2024 constitutional amendment — full text of amended Article 34; official government rationale; political coalition; whether practical access changed post-amendment
  - Source: Journal officiel de la République française; NPR/HRW already in archive; Verfassungsblog

- [ ] Ireland referendum — final Citizens' Assembly report (2016-2018); referendum result breakdown by demographics; Health (Regulation of Termination of Pregnancy) Act 2018 provisions
  - Source: Citizens' Assembly official report; Electoral Commission

- [ ] Argentina 2020 legalization — the December 2020 law text; coalition that passed it; comparison to 2018 failure (same bill, different government)
  - Source: Argentine legislature; Reuters/AP; academic analyses

- [ ] Canada post-Morgentaler landscape — current clinical access data; why no federal law was passed; outcomes comparison
  - Source: Abortion Rights Coalition of Canada; Health Canada data

### Surveillance and Data

- [ ] My Health My Data Act (Washington State, 2023) — full text; enforcement record; comparable federal proposals
  - Source: Washington State legislature; EPIC; CDT

- [ ] Documented cases of commercially purchased data used in abortion prosecutions
  - Priority: any confirmed cases as of April 2026
  - Source: EFF; ACLU; journalism (Wired, The Markup)

- [ ] FTC authority over health app data — current FTC rulemaking status
  - Source: FTC press releases; Federal Register

### Maternal Health

- [ ] Momnibus Act — current legislative status (re-introduced 2023); specific provisions; cost estimates
  - Source: congress.gov; Black Mamas Matter Alliance; National Birth Equity Collaborative

- [ ] State Medicaid postpartum coverage extension status
  - Priority: how many states have adopted the 12-month option; which have not
  - Source: KFF Medicaid tracker; CMS

- [ ] Doula program effectiveness evidence
  - Source: Cochrane Review on continuous support during childbirth; Medicaid doula coverage pilot results

## Framing Notes for When Writing Begins

1. **Use "reproductive justice" framing, not just "pro-choice"**: The reproductive justice framework (developed by SisterSong in 1994) encompasses the right to have children AND not have children AND parent in supportive conditions. This is broader and more accurate, and centers the experiences of Black women and women of color who are disproportionately affected.

2. **Lead with the structural problem, not the political controversy**: The domain document should open with the maternal mortality statistic (32.9/100k, highest in wealthy world) rather than the abortion debate framing. The structural problem is medical and democratic — it is not primarily a culture war issue.

3. **The Dobbs doctrinal threat is the democratic dimension**: What makes this a democratic renewal issue (not merely a healthcare issue) is that Dobbs eliminated a right held for 49 years using analytical logic that can eliminate other rights. This connects to Domain 6 (judicial accountability) and the overall proposal's argument about rights entrenchment.

4. **Ballot initiative success is the political proof**: 11/11 ballot initiative wins since Dobbs is the clearest evidence that reproductive rights has majority public support. The Republican legislature response (restricting citizen initiatives) is a direct democracy attack — frame it that way.

5. **Black maternal mortality is a racial justice emergency, not a footnote**: At 69.9/100k, Black maternal mortality is not a disparity to be noted in passing. It belongs in Section 26d as a primary focus alongside the overall rate, with its own evidence and its own reform mechanisms.
