## Quality Assessment Key

- **Tier 1 (Primary)**: Government documents, peer-reviewed law review or political science articles, official ICC/Council of Europe/UN documentation. Cite directly; verify URL before production.
- **Tier 2 (Secondary)**: Expert analysis in law review fora (not peer-reviewed but editorially controlled by top law schools), think tank publications from Carnegie/Brookings/CFR/Just Security/Lawfare, civil society organization reports with documented methodology.
- **Tier 3 (Supporting)**: News reporting from outlets with documented editorial standards (NYT, Guardian, Reuters, Al Jazeera); useful for factual updating but not primary analytical citation.
- **Gap**: Sources identified as necessary but not yet confirmed accessible; require researcher verification.

## Zone 1: Legislative Inventory — Primary Sources

**1. CRS R48868 — Separation of Powers and NATO Withdrawal (February 27, 2026)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48868 | PDF: https://www.congress.gov/crs_external_products/R/PDF/R48868/R48868.3.pdf
- Tier 1. The current definitive analysis of treaty withdrawal constitutional authority specific to NATO and NDAA Section 1250A. Must be read in full before Zone 2 writing. Addresses: (a) the constitutional basis for the NDAA restriction; (b) the *Youngstown* framework analysis if the president defies it; (c) the political question doctrine post-*Zivotofsky*. Cannot be adequately summarized from secondary sources.
- Quality: Authoritative congressional research; authored by congressional legal specialists; no known methodological limitations.

**2. CRS R44761 — Withdrawal from International Agreements: Legal Framework (congressional research background)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R44761
- Tier 1. The background constitutional law document on treaty withdrawal authority generally. Companion to R48868. Addresses the full constitutional debate including the *Curtiss-Wright* plenary executive foreign affairs power doctrine.

**3. NDAA Section 1250A (2024) — Full Text**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/2670 (Section 1250A within the FY2024 NDAA)
- Tier 1. The first statutory constraint on presidential treaty withdrawal from a specific treaty. The model legislation for Phase 3 Zone 1 analysis of extending protection to other treaties.

**4. S.2174 — NATO Act (119th Congress, introduced by Sen. Lee)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/2174/text
- Tier 1. Full bill text. Note: This is the libertarian withdrawal-facilitation version — it requires the president to give formal NATO withdrawal notice. Analytically important as evidence that Congress can regulate treaty relationships procedurally in either direction, establishing the precedent that Zone 1 pro-multilateral legislation would use.

**5. H.R.6508 — NATO Act (119th Congress, introduced by Rep. Massie)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/6508/all-info
- Tier 1. House companion to S.2174. Same analytical function.

**6. H.R.2010 — NATO Edge Act (119th Congress, introduced by Rep. Panetta)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/2010/text
- Tier 1. The affirmative constraint model — prohibits presidential NATO withdrawal. The legislative architecture Phase 3 legislative inventory documents.

**7. S.669 — DEFUND Act of 2025 (119th Congress)**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/669/text
- Tier 1. The adversary legislative vehicle — UN withdrawal facilitation. Map its sponsors and the co-sponsorship overlap with the NATO-withdrawal facilitation bills.

**8. CRS R48504 — U.S. Withdrawal from the Paris Agreement: Process and Potential Effects**
- URL: https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48504
- Tier 1. Useful precedent document for the executive agreement withdrawal (Paris Agreement as executive agreement, not Senate-ratified treaty) versus Senate-ratified treaty distinction.

**9. White House Presidential Memorandum — January 7, 2026 (66-Organization Withdrawal)**
- URL: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/01/withdrawing-the-united-states-from-international-organizations-conventions-and-treaties-that-are-contrary-to-the-interests-of-the-united-states/

**10. URAA Section 125 — WTO Five-Year Review Mechanism**

**11. Lawfare — "What Congress Has Done—and What It Still Needs to Do—to Protect NATO"**

## Zone 2: Constitutional Feasibility — Primary Sources

**13. Harold Hongju Koh — "Presidential Power to Terminate International Agreements"**
- URL: https://yalelawjournal.org/forum/presidential-power-to-terminate-international-agreements
- Tier 1 (Yale Law Journal Forum). The mirror principle argument. Not peer-reviewed in the traditional sense (law review fora operate on editorial rather than peer review), but Yale LJ editorial standard is equivalent. Must be read in full.
- Quality: Koh is the former Legal Adviser of the State Department (2009–2013) and the Sterling Professor of International Law at Yale; the analysis reflects deep operational and scholarly expertise.

**14. Harold Hongju Koh — "Presidential Power to Exit Treaties: Reflecting on the Mirror Principle"**
- URL: https://scholarship.law.gwu.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=3023&context=faculty_publications
- Tier 1. The GWU law review elaboration of the mirror principle. Companion to the Yale LJ Forum piece.

**15. Jean Galbraith — "Rejoining Treaties"**
- URL: https://scholarship.law.upenn.edu/faculty_scholarship/2182/
- Tier 1. The entry-exit symmetry argument from the re-accession direction: if a president can exit, can the next president re-enter without Senate consent? This question bears directly on Phase 3's reversibility claims.

**16. Oona A. Hathaway and Jean Galbraith — Just Security Symposium on Koh's National Security Constitution**
- URL: https://www.justsecurity.org/104281/symposium-harold-hongju-koh-national-security-constitution/
- Tier 2. The symposium exchange is the best available current-events scholarly engagement with Koh's treaty withdrawal theory.

**17. Oona Hathaway — "Withdrawal from International Agreements: Toward a 'Tailored Out'"**
- URL: https://www.justsecurity.org/104496/withdrawal-international-agreements-tailored-out/
- Tier 2. Hathaway's proposal for a context-sensitive framework that builds withdrawal flexibility into treaty design rather than requiring uniform Senate consent for all exits. Important counterpoint to Koh's stricter mirror principle.

**18. Smith v. Trump — ACLU Case Page and Preliminary Injunction Order**
- URL: https://www.aclu.org/cases/smith-v-trump
- Tier 1. Primary litigation document. The preliminary injunction order (July 18, 2025) is the operative legal document establishing that EO 14203 likely violates the First Amendment for speech-based services to the ICC.
- Quality: Direct primary source from federal district court. Retrieve the full order text from PACER or the ACLU case page.
- Gap: Confirm whether the government has filed a First Circuit appeal and whether the district court has issued any subsequent orders narrowing or expanding the injunction scope.

**19. *Goldwater v. Carter*, 444 U.S. 996 (1979)**
- URL: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/444/996/
- Tier 1. The primary precedent on treaty withdrawal justiciability. Splintered plurality; no majority opinion. Read in conjunction with CRS R48868's analysis of which justiciability doctrine controls post-*Zivotofsky*.

**20. *Zivotofsky v. Clinton*, 566 U.S. 189 (2012)**
- URL: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/566/189/
- Tier 1. Narrowed political question doctrine to exclude cases where Congress has legislated — the key case for Phase 3 Claim A (NDAA Section 1250A creates justiciable statutory conflict).

**21. *Zivotofsky v. Kerry*, 576 U.S. 1 (2015)**
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zivotofsky_v._Kerry (wikipedia for quick access; cite the official Supreme Court opinion in production)
- Tier 1. Further narrowed political question doctrine; first case striking down a federal statute as a separation-of-powers foreign affairs infringement. Established that courts will reach the merits of foreign affairs statutory conflicts.

**22. *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump* — February 20, 2026 (major questions/IEEPA tariff challenge)**
- URL: https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/learning-resources-v-trump
- Tier 2. Established that IEEPA tariffs on major questions grounds can be challenged successfully. The Phase 3 parallel argument that defunding 66 organizations without specific congressional authorization fails the major questions test requires engaging this case as precedent.
- Gap: Confirm exact citation and whether the ruling is currently on appeal.

**23. OHCHR — "USA: UN Expert Demands Withdrawal of Sanctions Against ICC Judges and Prosecutors" (January 2026)**
- URL: https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2026/01/usa-un-expert-demands-withdrawal-sanctions-against-icc-judges-and-prosecutors
- Tier 2. Official UN Special Rapporteur position statement. Useful for the normative framing and for establishing the international consensus that the sanctions violate international legal standards.

**24. Knight First Amendment Institute — "Trump Administration Concedes That US Researchers May Engage With Sanctioned UN Official"**
- URL: https://knightcolumbia.org/content/trump-administration-concedes-that-us-researchers-may-engage-with-sanctioned-un-official
- Tier 2. Documents the government's concession on researcher engagement — an important limit on the sanctions' scope that civil society organizations may not have fully incorporated into their compliance assessments.

## Zone 3: International Precedent — Primary Sources

**25. Patberg, Markus — "Treaty Withdrawal and Institutional Regression: What Role for the Democratic Underlabourer?" (2025)**
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17550882251351919
- Tier 1. Journal of International Political Theory, Vol. 21(2). Peer-reviewed. The democratic theory framework for diagnosing treaty withdrawal as a regressive political act. Patberg's "democratic underlabourer" concept — the actor who diagnoses structural injustice and creates political conditions for its remedy — is directly applicable to Phase 3's movement coalition framing.
- Quality: Peer-reviewed by JIPT; University of Hamburg (Patberg) has strong record in international political theory. Author is publicly accessible for correspondence at wiso.uni-hamburg.de.

**26. "Unilateral Withdrawals from Multilateral International Treaties, 1945–2024" — Global Governance: A Review of Multilateralism and International Organizations, Vol. 31, Issue 2 (2025)**
- URL: https://brill.com/view/journals/gg/31/2/article-p163_3.xml
- Tier 1. Peer-reviewed empirical dataset of 945 withdrawals from 206 treaties (1945–2024). Critical for establishing whether the January 2026 US action (66 organizations simultaneously) is historically unprecedented in scale. Essential reading before writing Phase 3's comparative precedent section.
- Gap: Full text may require institutional library access. Confirm availability via research institution proxy before July 15.

**27. "Responding to Unilateral Challenges to International Institutions" — International Studies Quarterly, Vol. 69, Issue 2 (2025)**
- URL: https://academic.oup.com/isq/article/69/2/sqaf022/8099996
- Tier 1. Peer-reviewed. Maps three institutional responses: accommodation, inaction, and forceful response. Directly applicable to the ICC, ECHR, and other bodies' responses to the US and Hungary withdrawal episodes.
