## Email Template

Dear [Reviewer Name/Title],

I am writing to request your expertise in a critical medical accuracy review for an open-access offline medical reference project.

### Project Overview

The **open-repo project** is building a comprehensive offline archive (ZIM format — used by Kiwix, a Wikipedia-style reader) containing life-critical reference materials for individuals in austere and resource-limited environments. This includes wilderness survival, emergency medicine, seed preservation, food safety, and botanical knowledge.

The **medical reference module** is the highest-priority component and will include:

**Article categories:**
- Drug monographs (30-50 WHO Essential Medicine List drugs, with adult/pediatric dosing)
- Condition guides (20-30 articles covering trauma, infection, dehydration, respiratory, environmental emergencies)
- Procedure guides (10-15 articles: wound care, CPR, splinting, triage, airway management)

**Total scope:** 60-95 field-applicable medical articles in the initial v1 release

### Medical Accuracy Requirements

All dosing tables and procedural protocols will be sourced directly from:
- WHO Model Formulary (2008 + supplements)
- WHO Essential Medicines List (2023)
- MSF Clinical Guidelines (medicalguidelines.msf.org)
- ICRC First Aid guidelines (for trauma/wound care)
- Wilderness Medical Society practice guidelines

We require a **medical subject matter expert review** to confirm:

1. **Dosing accuracy**: All drug monographs use verbatim source values (no rounding or interpolation). Adult and pediatric dosing stratified by weight where applicable.
2. **Contraindication completeness**: Critical contraindications and drug interactions documented accurately.
3. **Procedure appropriateness**: Field protocols are safe and accurate for austere environments (e.g., improvised wound closure, triage criteria without laboratory testing).
4. **Disclaimer adequacy**: Each article carries a clear disclaimer that this is a reference for austere environments only and that professional medical consultation is preferred whenever available.
5. **Evacuation criteria**: When escalation to professional medical care is non-negotiable.

### Reviewer Scope & Timeline

**Scope**: Review and provide feedback on draft article categories, outline, and sample monographs (NOT full implementation code).

**Deliverables for reviewer**:
