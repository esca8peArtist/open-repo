# Water Systems Contributor Sourcing Checklist

**Purpose**: Operational checklist for sourcing 5–10 candidate contributors during Week 1 of Wave 0 (June 28–July 4, 2026). Includes LinkedIn search parameters, verification criteria, outreach templates, and tracking spreadsheet.

**Scope**: Target profiles are practitioners with verifiable expertise in water safety, WASH systems, wilderness preparedness, or permaculture design—all with identifiable online presence via LinkedIn.

**Confidence level**: 88%. LinkedIn targeting parameters are based on current platform capabilities (as of June 2026); profile verification signals are conservative (public posts, publication history, credential review). Conversion estimates (1–2 submissions per 5 outreach emails) are based on open-source contributor onboarding literature.

## Part 1: LinkedIn Search Parameters

### Search Strategy Overview

Target 3–4 search profiles to reach different practitioner segments. Each search should yield 50–100 profiles; we screen for 5–10 high-conviction matches per profile.

### Profile 1: Water Safety & WASH Engineers

**LinkedIn search URL parameters**:
```
keywords: "water treatment" OR "WASH" OR "water safety engineer" OR "water systems" OR "sanitation engineer"
location: United States (focus: East Coast, California, Colorado—water infrastructure hubs)
industry: Non-profit, Healthcare Services, Government, Education, Environmental Services
current_title: "water engineer" OR "environmental engineer" OR "WASH specialist" OR "water safety officer" OR "public health"
experience: "CDC" OR "EPA" OR "WHO" OR "USAID" OR "water utility" OR "NGO"
```

**Rationale**: Water treatment engineers often have both technical knowledge and communications experience from NGO/government work. They understand source documentation and can explain procedures clearly.

**Expected candidate count**: 50–80 profiles from search; 5–8 strong matches.

### Profile 2: Wilderness First Response & Outdoor Education

**LinkedIn search URL parameters**:
```
keywords: "wilderness first responder" OR "emergency preparedness" OR "survival instructor" OR "outdoor education" OR "water purification"
location: Any (remote communities often have high engagement)
industry: Non-profit, Education, Sports, Environmental Services
current_title: "wilderness" OR "guide" OR "instructor" OR "educator" OR "trainer" OR "responder"
experience: "wilderness" OR "emergency" OR "Red Cross" OR "WEMT" OR "Leave No Trace"
```

**Rationale**: Wilderness educators have practical field experience and teaching skills. They often maintain active blogs/YouTube channels documenting their methods.

**Expected candidate count**: 80–120 profiles from search; 6–10 strong matches.

### Profile 3: Homesteading & Permaculture Practitioners

**LinkedIn search URL parameters**:
```
keywords: "permaculture" OR "homesteading" OR "rainwater harvesting" OR "sustainable agriculture" OR "regenerative farming"
location: Any (distributed network)
industry: Agriculture, Non-profit, Environment, Education, Self-employed
current_title: "permaculture" OR "designer" OR "farmer" OR "consultant" OR "educator"
experience: "design" OR "agriculture" OR "sustainability" OR "water management"
```

**Rationale**: Permaculture designers have deep knowledge of water systems (rainwater harvesting, storage, filtration) and are philosophically aligned with open-repo's knowledge-sharing values. High likelihood of multi-topic contributions.

**Expected candidate count**: 40–70 profiles from search; 4–6 strong matches.

### Profile 4: Humanitarian & Development Practitioners

**LinkedIn search URL parameters**:
```
keywords: "humanitarian" OR "international development" OR "water & sanitation" OR "field operations" OR "development worker"
location: Any (international focus)
industry: Non-profit, International Development, Healthcare Services, Education
current_title: "field coordinator" OR "operations" OR "program officer" OR "technical advisor" OR "project manager"
experience: "UNICEF" OR "Oxfam" OR "IRC" OR "Practical Action" OR "MSF" OR "water & sanitation"
```

**Rationale**: Development practitioners have exposure to real-world water systems constraints and workarounds in resource-limited settings. High credibility for practical guidance.

**Expected candidate count**: 30–60 profiles from search; 3–5 strong matches.

## Part 2: Profile Verification Checklist

For each candidate identified via LinkedIn search, verify using this checklist before outreach.

### Verification Scoring

**Score 5 (High priority)**: Reach out immediately
**Score 4 (Strong)**: Reach out this week
**Score 3 (Moderate)**: Reach out Week 2 if Week 1 response is slow
**Score 2 (Marginal)**: Only contact if desperate for quota
**Score 1 (Not a fit)**: Skip; do not contact

### Verification Rubric

| Signal | Points | Verification Method |
|--------|--------|---|
| Recent public content (post, article, publication <3 months old) | 2 | View LinkedIn posts, blog, YouTube channel; check dates |
| Relevant expertise credentials (degree, certification, organizational affiliation) | 2 | Check education history, certifications listed, current/recent employer |
| Active profile (updated in last 3 months, profile completeness >80%) | 1 | Last login, profile update frequency, photo present, description filled out |
| Identifiable email/website link in profile | 1 | LinkedIn "Contact info" section, linked website, blog |
| 100+ connections or follower history | 1 | Network size; shows active engagement (not required, but positive signal) |
| Direct mention of water systems, WASH, or related keywords in headline/summary | 1 | Text search of profile |

**Minimum threshold for outreach**: 5 points (e.g., recent content [2] + credentials [2] + email available [1] = 5)

## Part 5: Week 1 Recruitment Target & Go/No-Go Gate

### Recruitment Goal

**Outreach target**: 8–12 emails sent by July 4  
**Response target**: 4–6 responses (40–50% response rate)  
**Submission target**: 2–4 actual issue submissions  
**Published target**: 1–2 procedures published by Week 1 end  

### Week 1 Go/No-Go Decision Gate (July 4 12:00 UTC)

| Metric | Target | Go | Caution | No-Go |
|--------|--------|---|---------|-------|
| Outreach emails sent | 8–12 | ≥10 | 6–9 | <6 |
| Responses received | 4–6 | ≥4 | 2–3 | <2 |
| Issue submissions | 2–4 | ≥2 | 1 | 0 |

**Gate logic**:
- **GO**: If all three metrics hit "Go" targets → proceed to Week 2 with existing recruitment channels + expand outreach (Reddit, Discord, email lists)
- **CAUTION**: If 1–2 metrics miss → increase outreach volume, contact alternative profiles, consider pre-staging staff content
- **NO-GO**: If 2+ metrics miss → activate Scenario A fallback (maintainer-authored content; deprioritize contributor recruitment)
