## Sequencing Strategy (Why This Order Matters)

### Wave 1: Tier 1A — Immigration Legal Aid (Days 1–7)

**Why first**: Direct client relationships with undocumented populations facing ELITE targeting. Legal aid organizations have established channels for distributing client resources and can integrate Part 0 (data broker opt-outs) immediately.

**Expected outcomes**:
- NILC: Forward to member organizations (reach: 50+ affiliated legal programs)
- CLINIC: Network-wide distribution to 400+ affiliates
- RAICES: Forward to regional chapters + communications network
- ILRC: Host as internal resource, forward to collaborating organizations
- NLG: Forward to specialized legal audience

**Multiplier effect**: Single successful engagement with a national org reaches 50–400+ people in the legal community.

---

### Wave 2: Tier 1B — Community-Based Organizations (Days 8–14)

**Why second**: Ground-level organizations with direct trust relationships in immigrant communities. Faster distribution velocity than formal legal organizations; easier to adapt resources for community workshops.

**Expected outcomes**:
- Make the Road: Share in networks, adapt for community workshops
- United We Dream: Forward to DACA-focused network, potential featured resource
- CASA: Distribute through community education infrastructure
- Sanctuary networks: Direct share to interfaith alliances and community groups
- Community ed programs: Integrate into workshops and client training

**Multiplier effect**: Community organizations reach the people most vulnerable to ELITE targeting through trusted channels.

---

### Wave 3: Tier 1C — Mutual Aid Networks (Days 15–21)

**Why third**: Mutual aid networks reach people not accessible through formal legal or community organizations. Fastest amplification velocity; security-conscious audiences understand and share Tier 2/3 material.

**Expected outcomes**:
- National Bail Fund: Direct share to member bail funds
- Local mutual aid: Direct community member distribution via Signal/Slack/Telegram
- Food Not Bombs chapters: Street-level distribution
- Anarchist Black Cross: Prison support + security culture networks

**Multiplier effect**: Fastest share velocity; reaches directly impacted populations outside formal institutional channels.

## Personalization Framework (By Institution Type)

### For 1A (Immigration Legal Aid)

**Lead with**: Part 0 (actionable opt-outs) + primary-source structure of threat model  
**Emphasize**: Legal-citability (designed for briefs and advocacy), California path (AB 60/AB 1766 → DROP), implementation verification (fraud prevention)  
**Avoid**: Overexplaining Palantir (they already know). Heavy technical language.

**Key hook**: "This threat model is designed to be citable from primary documents and the implementation guide includes verification checkpoints."

---

### For 1B (Community-Based Organizations)

**Lead with**: Accessibility ("no technical expertise required," "2–4 hours," "immediate impact")  
**Emphasize**: Workshoppability (can be taught in 2-hour community session), language roadmap (Spanish on July 2026), harm reduction framing  
**Avoid**: Corporate language. Heavy technical detail in opening.

**Key hook**: "Part 0 is workshoppable — a 2-hour community session can walk through opt-outs together. No technical background required."

---

### For 1C (Mutual Aid Networks)

**Lead with**: Part 0 as highest-ROI for broadest population. Explicit about limitations (targets bulk surveillance, not targeted investigation)  
**Emphasize**: Security culture (assume audience knows Signal/Tor/GrapheneOS), harm reduction, immediate action potential  
**Avoid**: Institutional framing. Corporate language. False confidence claims.

**Key hook**: "Part 0 is the highest-leverage action for the broadest population. Tier 2/3 are for people with specific reason to believe they're under active investigation."
