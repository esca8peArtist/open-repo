# Cybersecurity for Election Workers
**A one-page guide for election offices and poll workers**

---

## The Current Situation

Federal cybersecurity support for elections has been significantly reduced. CISA lost over 1,000 staff positions. The EI-ISAC (the primary cybersecurity assistance program for election offices) has been defunded. Your office now carries more responsibility for its own security.

This guide covers the highest-impact actions for two different roles: election administrators (full-time staff) and poll workers (temporary staff on election days).

---

## FOR ELECTION ADMINISTRATORS

**1. Multi-factor authentication on all election system accounts** (most important action)

Every account that can access your election management system must require a second factor — a code from an app, not a text message. Text message codes can be intercepted. Use Google Authenticator, Authy, or a hardware key (YubiKey).

- Enable MFA immediately for: election management system login, voter registration database access, your election office email, and any state-level reporting systems.

**2. Phishing awareness — do not click unexpected links**

The most common attack on election offices is phishing: an email that looks like it is from a state agency, a vendor, or a colleague, but contains a link that steals your login credentials. Before clicking any link in an email, hover over it to see the actual web address. If in doubt, go directly to the website by typing the address yourself.

**3. Keep election systems off the public internet during elections**

Election management systems that tabulate votes should not be connected to the internet during election operations. If your system requires an internet connection for any purpose, document exactly when, why, and who authorized it.

**4. Incident reporting**

Know who to call. Save these contacts:
- Your state election director's office (cybersecurity contact)
- MS-ISAC 24/7 Security Operations Center: 1-866-787-4722
- CISA (even with reduced capacity): cisa.gov/report

---

## FOR POLL WORKERS

**1. Do not connect personal devices to election equipment**

Your personal phone, tablet, or laptop should never connect to election networks, USB ports on election equipment, or the polling place Wi-Fi if it is the same network that election devices use.

**2. Do not discuss voter records or poll results on personal messaging apps**

Any election data — voter turnout numbers, reports of equipment issues, or any official records — should go through official channels only (your election office's designated reporting method), not through personal text messages or apps.

**3. Report anything suspicious immediately**

If you see someone attempting to photograph equipment, connect a device to election systems, or behave suspiciously around voting equipment or poll books, report it immediately to your chief judge or election office contact. Write down what you saw, when, and who was present.

**4. Know the chain of command**

Before election day, confirm: Who is your chief judge? Who do you call if equipment fails? Who do you call if you observe a potential security incident?
