# Basic Privacy Actions for Everyone
**The three most useful things you can do in under one hour**

---

## Why This Matters

Companies called "data brokers" collect your home address, phone number, relatives' names, and daily location from dozens of sources — and sell this information to anyone who pays, including law enforcement agencies. You can make yourself harder to find in these databases, and it costs nothing.

This guide focuses on three actions that have the most impact for the least effort. You do not need to be at risk of surveillance to benefit from these.

---

## Action 1: Remove Your Address from Data Broker Websites (45 minutes)

Data brokers like Spokeo and Whitepages publish your home address, past addresses, relatives, and phone number — and ICE, employers, stalkers, and scammers all use these sites. You can ask each site to remove your information.

Do these first — they take 2 minutes each:

- **Spokeo**: spokeo.com — search your name, find your listing, scroll to the bottom and click "Remove this listing"
- **Whitepages**: whitepages.com/suppression_requests — enter your name and address, submit removal request
- **BeenVerified**: beenverified.com/opt-out — search your name, find your profile, submit opt-out
- **FastPeopleSearch**: fastpeoplesearch.com — search your name, click "Remove My Info" at the bottom
- **PeopleFinder**: peoplefinder.com — search your name, find your listing, click "Remove my record"
- **Intelius**: intelius.com/opt-out — enter your name and submit an opt-out request

Each site will send a confirmation email. Save these for your records. Note: data brokers re-scrape data periodically, so repeat these opt-outs every 6–12 months.

**Start at OptOutPrescreen.com** before any of the above — this is a federally regulated opt-out from credit bureau marketing databases. It takes 5 minutes and reduces your data footprint across Equifax, Experian, Innovis, and TransUnion.

## Action 2: Use Signal for Sensitive Conversations (10 minutes to set up)

Signal is a free messaging app that encrypts your calls and messages so that your carrier, your internet provider, and most government agencies cannot read the content — even if they request it. The government can request records from Signal; Signal keeps only your account creation date and last connection time, not your messages.

**When to use Signal instead of regular calls or texts:**
- Anything related to your health or medical care
- Conversations with an attorney
- Financial discussions
- Anything you would not want read in a court filing

**How to get Signal**: Go to signal.org (not a search engine — type it directly) and download the app.

**After installing, do this:**
- Enable disappearing messages: open any conversation > tap the contact name > Disappearing messages > set to 1 week
- Enable a PIN: Signal Settings > Account > Signal PIN

Ask the people you communicate with to also install Signal. It only provides protection when both sides are using it.

## Action 3: Reduce Location Data Your Phone Collects (5 minutes)

Your phone continuously collects and shares your location with dozens of apps, many of which sell that data. These three settings reduce that exposure significantly.

**On iPhone:**
- Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services > set to "Off" for apps that do not need it; set to "While Using" (not "Always") for apps like maps
- Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services > System Services > Significant Locations > turn Off
- Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking > turn off "Allow Apps to Request to Track"

**On Android (varies slightly by phone model):**
- Settings > Location > App permissions > review each app and set to "Only while using" or "Deny"
- Settings > Location > Google Location History > turn Off

**Bonus**: If you use Google Maps, remove your home and work address from the saved locations. Google collects and retains this data.
