# Technology Safety for Domestic Violence Survivors

**STOP AND READ THIS FIRST**: Are you reading this on a phone or computer that your abusive partner uses, or that they pay for? If so, they may be able to see what you are reading. If possible, use a library computer, a friend's phone, or a device they do not know about.

**If you are in immediate danger: Call 911 or text START to 88788 (National DV Hotline, free, confidential, 24/7)**

---

## What Your Partner May Be Able to See

If they installed monitoring software (called "stalkerware"), they may see:
- Your location right now and everywhere you have been
- All your text messages, even on encrypted apps
- Everything you type, including your passwords
- Your phone calls and voicemails

If they know your Apple ID or Google account password, they can see your location without touching your phone.

---

## The Most Important Rule

**Do not remove stalkerware or change your accounts before making a safety plan with an advocate.**

When surveillance is cut off suddenly, some abusers escalate to physical violence. The order matters:
1. Talk to an advocate first
2. Make a safety plan
3. Then change your technology — in the right order

---

## Your First Step

Call or text the National Domestic Violence Hotline: **1-800-799-7233** or text **START to 88788**

Tell them you want to talk about "technology safety." They have advocates who specialize in this. Chat is also available at thehotline.org (silently, without calling).

---

## If You Are in a Safe Place and Ready to Act

Work through these steps with an advocate. The correct order:

**Step 1: Change your email password first** (use a device they do not have access to)
Go to your email account settings and change the password. Also sign out of all other sessions.

**Step 2: Change your Apple ID or Google account password**
This stops them from seeing your location via Find My or Find My Device.

**Step 3: Check your phone plan**
Call your carrier. Ask if you are on someone else's account. If yes, you may need a separate plan to stop them from seeing your call records and location.

**Step 4: Check your car**
GPS trackers can be hidden in wheel wells, under bumpers, or inside the car. If you have concerns, a local domestic violence organization can help you search.

**Step 5: Get a new phone number or a second number**
Apps like Google Voice give you a second number for free. You can use this number for shelter arrangements, attorney contacts, and other sensitive communications.
