# Communications Security for Labor Organizers
**A one-page guide for union campaigns and worker organizing**

---

## Why This Matters

Employers fighting union campaigns hire consultants who monitor company email, Slack, and social media for organizing activity. If you discuss your campaign on company systems before filing a petition, the employer may see it. More importantly: anything you discuss on company email, company Slack, or company Wi-Fi can be accessed by the employer — it is their property.

Government surveillance of labor organizing is also documented. DHS has issued administrative subpoenas to Google, Meta, Reddit, and Discord seeking to unmask organizers. Drones have been deployed over protests. Location data from mobile phones has been used to map who attended labor actions.

This guide focuses on the highest-impact protections for an organizing campaign.

---

## The Ground Rule: Never Organize on Company Systems

Company email, company Slack, company Microsoft Teams, and any device the employer owns or issued to you — all of this can be monitored by the employer. Use personal devices and personal accounts for all organizing communications.

---

## Four Actions to Do Before Your First Organizing Meeting

**1. Use Signal for all organizing committee communications** (free, 10 minutes to set up)

Signal is an encrypted messaging app. Your carrier, the employer, and most government agencies cannot read Signal messages without accessing your device directly. Download Signal from Signal.org (not the app store search — go directly to signal.org to avoid fake versions).

Settings to enable after installing:
- Set disappearing messages to 1 week for all organizing conversations
- Enable a PIN (Signal Settings > Account > Signal PIN)
- Do not link Signal to your work phone number — use a personal number

**2. Create a separate Google or email account for organizing**

Do not use your work email, your email associated with your employer-issued accounts, or any email you signed up for using your work computer. A new Gmail or ProtonMail account takes 5 minutes to create. Use this account only for organizing.

**3. Use the Signal group for your organizing committee, not a group text**

Group texts (SMS) are not encrypted. Your carrier keeps records of group text messages. An employer's attorney can subpoena those records. Move your organizing committee to a Signal group before your first formal meeting.

**4. Establish a "need to know" rule**

Not every member of the organizing committee needs to know every contact or every detail of the campaign strategy. If law enforcement or management pressures one person, limiting what they know limits what can be disclosed. Agree as a committee: who knows the full contact list? Who knows only their immediate contacts?

---

## At Protests and Public Actions

- Your vehicle license plate can be photographed and tracked across multiple events by automated plate readers. If your car's presence at protests is a concern, consider carpooling in someone else's vehicle or using public transit.
- Your phone transmits location data continuously. If you are in a high-risk situation, turn your phone off (not just silent) before arriving at a rally location.
- Drones have been deployed at protests. Wearing identical-colored outer layers as other participants makes movement tracking harder from above.

---

## If You Are Approached by Law Enforcement

You have the right to remain silent. You are not required to answer questions about your organizing activities, the identities of other organizers, or the status of your campaign. Say: "I am exercising my right to remain silent. I want to speak with an attorney." Then stop talking.
