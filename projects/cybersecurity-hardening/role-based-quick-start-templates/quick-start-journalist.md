# Source Protection and Device Security for Journalists
**A one-page guide for reporters, filmmakers, and freelancers**

---

## The 2026 Threat Landscape

Three threats have escalated for journalists in the United States:

1. **CBP border device searches require no warrant.** A CBP officer at any airport or border crossing can search your phone, read your messages, copy your contacts, and share everything with DHS — no warrant, no probable cause required.

2. **FBI warrantless access to your communications.** Under FISA Section 702, the FBI can search databases of collected communications for your name without a warrant. If your sources are outside the United States, assume those communications may have been collected.

3. **Administrative subpoenas to social media platforms.** DHS has issued hundreds of subpoenas to Google, Meta, Reddit, and Discord seeking to unmask anonymous accounts. Reddit, Meta, and Google have voluntarily complied with some of these requests.

There is no federal shield law. Federal courts have not established a clear rule protecting journalists from these searches.

---

## Five Actions to Take Before Your Next Story

**1. Use Signal for source communication** (not regular calls, not SMS, not email)

Signal encrypts your calls and messages. The government cannot read the content of Signal messages without accessing your physical device. Download Signal from signal.org. Verify your safety number with each source in person or on a separate channel before sharing sensitive information.

**2. Never cross a U.S. border with source materials on your devices**

Before any international travel: back up your device to an encrypted drive at home, then do a factory reset on your travel phone. Reinstall only the apps you need. Do not restore from backup until you have cleared customs. This "clean device" protocol is the only reliable protection against CBP device searches.

**3. Use SecureDrop for receiving sensitive documents**

SecureDrop is a whistleblower submission system that strips metadata from documents and allows anonymous, encrypted submission. If you are a journalist at a news organization with a SecureDrop instance, publicize your SecureDrop address to potential sources. If you are a freelancer, the Freedom of the Press Foundation maintains a directory of news organizations with SecureDrop: securedrop.org/directory.

**4. Set Signal disappearing messages to 24 hours for source conversations**

In Signal: open a conversation > tap the contact name > Disappearing messages > set to 1 day. This means that if your device is seized and unlocked, messages older than 24 hours are already deleted. FBI case records show that Signal's servers retain only account creation date and last connection time — the content is not accessible to them.

**5. Separate your source contacts from your regular contacts**

Do not save sources' real names in your phone's regular contacts. Use code names, numbers, or Signal's note feature to store contextual information separate from the contact name that appears in a device search.

---

## If CBP Stops You at the Border

You are not required to provide your device passwords to CBP. However, CBP can detain you and your device if you refuse. The practical guidance:

- State: "I do not consent to a search of my device."
- Ask for a supervisor and request a receipt for any device that is detained.
- Contact your news organization's legal team immediately.
- If you are a freelancer: contact the Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press (rcfp.org) legal defense hotline — they provide free legal support for journalists facing border searches.
