# Phase 3 Piloting Architecture
## Tier 3 Expansion: 20-30 Organizations Over August-December 2026

**Executive Summary**: Phase 3 scales distribution from Tier 2's ~50 organizations to Tier 3's 20-30 organizations across policy, labor, and academic sectors. Pilot launch begins August 1, 2026, with three sequential waves targeting distinct cohorts based on readiness metrics from Phase 2. Success metrics, contingency triggers, and go/no-go decision points are pre-committed. This document is the binding execution framework for H2 2026.

**Phase 3 positioning**: Tier 3 organizations (policy think tanks, labor federations, law schools, academic programs) do not reach at-risk populations directly. Instead, they shape the policy, legal, and educational infrastructure determining whether commercial data surveillance becomes regulated, litigated, and taught as a constitutional problem. A policy brief from the Brennan Center citing the corpus moves surveillance reform into the legislative record. A lawsuit briefed with FOIA documentation from the corpus becomes case law. A curriculum integrating the threat model into a law clinic affects 50+ future lawyers over a decade. Tier 3 adoption is durable infrastructure, not one-time engagement.

**Phase 3 differs from Phase 1–2**:
- Longer engagement cycles (6–12 months, not 6–8 weeks)
- Smaller cohort (20-30 vs 45-50 organizations)
- Higher personalization per organization (named adoption use case required upfront)
- Adoption measured by policy/legal/academic output, not engagement signals
- Messaging variant testing by sector (policy vs. labor vs. academic framing)
- Contingency tied to Phase 2 outcomes, not Phase 3 engagement

## Section 1: Tier 3 Cohort Selection Matrix

### 1A. Pre-Screened Candidate Organizations (25 Total)

These organizations are vetted against the Phase 3 selection criteria: sector coverage, adoption infrastructure, messaging fit, and Phase 2 adjacency. Scoring uses a simplified matrix from the Tier 2 architecture with Phase 3-specific adaptations.

**Selection Criteria Definitions**:
- **Adoption Infrastructure** (1–5): Does the organization have named people, documented processes, and institutional infrastructure to consume the corpus and produce policy/legal/academic output?
- **Sector Gap** (1–5): Does this organization fill a sector or geographic gap not already covered by Phase 1–2 or other Phase 3 contacts?
- **Output Potential** (1–5): Can this organization produce policy briefs, litigation frameworks, or curricula citing the corpus within 12 months?
- **Phase 2 Adjacency** (1–5): Are there relationships established during Tier 2 that can warm the Phase 3 outreach?
- **Resource Availability** (1–5): Is the organization adequately staffed for a 6–12 month engagement?

Scoring: 20–25 = High Priority, 15–19 = Medium Priority, 10–14 = Lower Priority. Target Phase 3 cohort includes all High Priority organizations and selective Medium Priority candidates to reach 20-30 total.

## Section 4: Resource Scaling Analysis

### 4A. Execution Team Scaling (Tier 1 → Tier 3)

**Tier 1 Model (May–June 2026)**:
- Team: 1 person (Anya) + 0.5 executive producer (coordinator)
- Time commitment: ~15 hours/week (outreach, call coordination, daily reporting)
- Contact volume: 45 organizations, 5 weeks
- Outcome: 25 contacts reading, 3–8 showing organizational adoption signal

**Tier 2 Model (June–August 2026)**:
- Team: 1 person (Anya) + 0.5 coordinator + 1 part-time sector specialist (labor/academic)
- Time commitment: ~20 hours/week (3 pilot groups, messaging customization, ongoing engagement)
- Contact volume: 50 organizations, 8 weeks
- Outcome: 25–35 reading, 5–10 organizational adoption signals

**Tier 3 Model (August–December 2026)**:
- Team: 1 person (Anya) + 0.5 coordinator + 1 full-time sector specialist (labor + policy + academic combined)
- Time commitment: ~25 hours/week (3 waves, 6-month engagement cycles, outcome tracking)
- Contact volume: 20–30 organizations, 18 weeks (staggered, not simultaneous)
- Outcome: 15–20 reading, 5–8 organizational adoption signals

**Key scaling differences**:
- Tier 3 has LONGER engagement cycles (6 months vs 6 weeks). This means simultaneous execution of Wave 1 follow-up, Wave 2 outreach, and Wave 3 planning.
- Tier 3 requires HIGHER personalization per organization (50–100 minutes per org vs 30 minutes Tier 1–2).
- Tier 3 requires SECTOR EXPERTISE. A single generalist cannot credibly brief policy think tanks, labor federations, and law clinics simultaneously. Resource allocation: 60% policy/academic, 40% labor.

### 5A. Phase 3 KPIs by Wave

| Metric | Wave 1 Target | Wave 2 Target | Wave 3 Target | Measurement Method |

### 5B. Phase 3 Adoption Scoring (Applied at 6-Month Checkpoint)

Each organization is scored 1–5 on three dimensions:

**Dimension 1 — Engagement Depth** (1–5 scale):
- 5: Named staff person assigned; regular check-ins established; corpus integrated into existing workflow
- 4: Substantive engagement; identified use case; 1–2 staff involved
- 3: Read corpus; general interest; no specific use case identified
- 2: Minimal engagement; forwarded materials internally but not beyond
- 1: No engagement (non-respondent, soft decline, read not confirmed)

**Dimension 2 — Output Signal** (1–5 scale):
- 5: Public output citing corpus (policy brief published, litigation memo filed, curriculum deployed, member training completed)
- 4: Output in development; credible timeline to publication (<90 days)
- 3: Use case identified; output planned but no timeline committed
- 2: General interest in future collaboration; no specific output committed
- 1: No output signal

**Dimension 3 — Network Multiplier** (1–5 scale):
- 5: Organization has referred corpus to 5+ downstream contacts; visible sector-wide adoption signal
- 4: Organization has made 2–4 referrals; peer organization adoption influenced
- 3: Internal distribution; forwarded to staff but no external referrals
- 2: Individual read; not organizational distribution
- 1: No multiplier signal

**Adoption Score = (D1 + D2 + D3) / 3, on 1–5 scale**
- 4.5–5.0 = **Durable Adoption**: Proceed to Phase 4 sustained engagement
- 3.5–4.4 = **Established Adoption**: Include in Phase 4 with monitoring; plan 6-month touch-base
- 2.5–3.4 = **Conditional Adoption**: Approach with revised value proposition; may suit Tier 4 (lower-priority sectors)
- <2.5 = **Limited Adoption**: Include in post-Phase-3 evaluation; consider for next round if circumstances change
