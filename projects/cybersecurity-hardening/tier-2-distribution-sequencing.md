## Executive Summary

Phase 1 distribution targets 45 individual leaders across 7 constituencies (law schools, AG offices, immigration legal services, mutual aid networks, security researchers, journalism, policy think tanks). Phase 2 extends impact through **organizational partnerships** — bringing frameworks directly into institutional decision-making, governance, and operations.

**Key insight from Phase 1 infrastructure**: The Tier 1 Engagement Success Metrics framework (Session 879) already defines 3-factor readiness scoring for Tier 2 candidate identification:
- **Engagement depth**: Opened email, clicked link, replied with substantive feedback
- **Integration signal**: Cited framework in external work, shared with colleagues, requested adaptations
- **Network multiplier**: Leads other organizations, has convening authority, speaks at conferences

**Tier 2 opportunity**: Use Phase 1 response patterns to automatically identify which Tier 1 contacts become Tier 2 institutional bridges. No new outreach strategy needed — organic emergence from Phase 1 engagement.

## 1. Tier 2 Candidate Identification (3-Factor Readiness Model)

### 1.1 Data Source: Phase 1 Engagement Scoring

From `tier-1-success-metrics.md` (Session 879), each Tier 1 contact is scored on a 0–5 scale:
- **0**: No engagement (bounce, no open)
- **1**: Minimal (email opened only)
- **2**: Interested (click through to framework, no reply)
- **3**: Engaged (substantive reply with questions/feedback)
- **4**: Integration signal (cited in own work, shared with others, requested adaptations)
- **5**: Adoption (deployed in institutional context, leadership endorsement)

### 1.2 Tier 2 Readiness Composite Score

**Formula**: `(engagement_depth × 0.4) + (integration_signal × 0.35) + (network_multiplier × 0.25)`

**Thresholds**:
- **High Readiness** (≥3.5): Tier 2 invite in Wave 1 (Days 8–14 post-Phase-1-launch)
- **Medium Readiness** (2.5–3.4): Tier 2 invite in Wave 2 (Days 21–28)
- **Low Readiness** (<2.5): Tier 2 follow-up only if integration signals emerge later

### 1.3 Factor Definitions & Measurement

**Engagement Depth** (0–5 scale):
- 0–1: No engagement or single email open
- 2–3: Click-through engagement without substantive reply
- 4: Substantive reply with questions, objections, or implementation ideas
- 5: Multi-message thread with detailed feedback; demonstrates framework comprehension

**Measurement**: Days 7, 14, 21, 28 email engagement checkpoints (from Phase 1 risk playbook).

**Integration Signal** (0–5 scale):
- 0–1: No signal
- 2: Shares framework link with colleagues (inferred from reply threads)
- 3: Cites framework in own external work (policy papers, communications, training materials)
- 4: Requests adaptations for institutional context
- 5: Deploys framework in operational/governance context; seeks input on updates

**Measurement**: (a) Monitor Tier 1 emails for outbound forwarding/sharing language, (b) Google Scholar + institutional website search post-Days 14, 28 for citations, (c) Track feature requests/customization asks in Day 14, 28, 35 replies.

**Network Multiplier** (0–5 scale):
- 0–1: Individual contributor, no known conveying authority
- 2: Speaks at professional conferences or publishes in high-profile outlets
- 3: Leads small working group or convenes within own organization
- 4: Convenes across multiple organizations; referenced as expert in media
- 5: Recognized field leader; convenes national initiatives; major media presence

**Measurement**: Pre-Phase-1 research (LinkedIn, organizational roles, speaker databases) + post-Phase-1 monitoring for media mentions, speaking engagements, institutional roles.

## 4. Tier 2 Launch Timeline & Sequencing

### 4.1 Phase 1→2 Transition (Days 1–7 Post-Phase-1 Launch)

| **Day** | **Trigger** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Phase 1 gist live | Tier 1 emails sent to 45 contacts |
| Days 1–7 | Phase 1 initial engagement | Monitor opens, clicks, bounces. Begin engagement scoring. |
| Day 7 | Engagement snapshot | Identify highest-engagement tier 1 contacts for early Tier 2 outreach |

### 4.2 Tier 2 Wave 1 Invitations (Days 8–14)

**Target**: High-readiness Tier 1 contacts (expected 13–20 from pool of 45).

**Outreach mechanism**:
1. **Day 8**: Personalized email from Phase 1 sender to 13–20 high-engagement contacts. Subject: "Expanding [Framework Name] — Your Institutional Leadership Opportunity."
2. **Content**: (a) Framework highlights from Phase 1, (b) Tier 2 opportunity (organizational partnership vs. individual awareness), (c) Specific institutional anchor partner role (e.g., "Clinic director integration," "AG policy leadership"), (d) Next steps (brief call, sample materials).
3. **CTA**: "Can we explore how [Framework] can strengthen your organization's decision-making? Calendly link."

**Expected response rate**: 50–70% reply (higher engagement than Tier 1, since contacts are already screened for engagement).

### 4.3 Tier 2 Candidate Conversations (Days 15–28)

**Structure**:
- **Day 15–21**: Tier 2 candidate calls (30 min). Understand: organizational priorities, decision-making pathways, specific materials needed, timeline for integration.
- **Day 28**: Institutional anchor partner designation. Formalize: which organizations are official Tier 2 partners, what support/materials do they need, when do they plan institutional presentation/adoption?

**Outcome**: 8–12 institutional anchor partners ready for implementation phase.

## 7. Risk Mitigation & Contingencies

### 7.1 Risks

| **Risk** | **Trigger** | **Mitigation** |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 2 candidate burnout** | >3 candidate conversations decline invitation | Adjust Wave 2 timeline; add 2-week pause after Wave 1 to absorb initial partners |
| **Customization scope creep** | Partners request extensive adaptations | Establish "customization menu" (3 standard adaptations per type) and limit to-one-per-partner initial integration |
| **Slow institutional decision-making** | Partners delay institutional approval (>60 days) | Early indicator at Day 42 follow-up; extend timeline expectation; offer smaller pilot scope (1 department vs. whole org) |
| **Tier 2 coordination overhead** | Managing 10–12 partners creates unsustainable workload | Create "Tier 2 implementation playbook" (template responses, batch outreach, automated updates) |

### 7.2 Contingency Scenarios

**Scenario A: Phase 1 approval delays beyond 2 weeks**
- Tier 2 launch timeline shifts. Adjust target dates proportionally.
- Prepare Tier 2 materials in parallel while waiting for Phase 1 approval.

**Scenario B: Fewer Tier 2 candidates emerge (only 8 high-readiness contacts)**
- Proceed with quality over quantity. Focus intensive support on 8 partners to maximize depth of adoption.
- Plan Wave 2 recruitment for Days 45–60 (lower-threshold candidates who showed moderate engagement).

**Scenario C: Institutional partner deprioritizes after initial commitment**
- May happen (organizational priorities shift). Continue support, but do not pursue forced integration.
- Document decision for future reference; maintain relationship for potential future engagement.
