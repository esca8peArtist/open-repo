# Tier 3 Expansion Outline

**Scope**: 30 organizations across three sectors over 16 weeks. Pre-condition: Tier 2 underway (minimum 2A digital rights and 2D journalist organizations contacted). Recommended launch: 6–10 weeks after Tier 2 begins.

**Strategic purpose**: Tier 2 amplifies the corpus to technical practitioners. Tier 3 embeds it in the institutions that shape policy, law, and labor rights. These organizations do not reach at-risk individuals directly — they influence the structural conditions determining whether commercial data broker-to-law enforcement surveillance is regulated, litigated, or taught as a constitutional problem.

**Why 30 organizations**: The TIER3_DISTRIBUTION_PREP.md inventory identifies 30 organizations that have clear alignment with the corpus, verified contact patterns, and institutional capacity to produce the medium-term adoption artifacts (policy briefs, litigation frameworks, curricula, member training) that distinguish Tier 3 success from Tier 1–2 amplification. A campaign targeting more than 30 organizations would require a level of personalization that cannot be sustained without sacrificing contact quality.

## Contact Strategy

### Contact Research Methodology

Tier 3 contacts change more frequently than Tier 1–2. Research each contact before sending:

1. Check organization annual report (most publish annually) — look for staff list, program director names
2. Check recent conference speaker lists (Tech Policy Institute, Privacy Law Scholars Conference, CPDP, FPF annual summit) — active participants are correct contacts
3. Check organization "People" or "About" page — focus on Program Director, Research Director, Communications Director
4. For law clinics: check PACER or CourtListener for recent clinic cases — confirms active practice areas and relevant faculty
5. Verify on LinkedIn only to confirm role and tenure, not for outreach

**Contact verification priority order** (before each send wave):
- Georgetown CPT — verify current director (director has changed historically)
- ACLU SPT — Esha Bhandari, Director (confirm still in role)
- AFL-CIO Tech Institute — Lauren McFerran, Executive Director (in role from Feb 2026)
- NDWA — verify current communications or organizing staff contact
- Brennan Center — Liberty & National Security Program current staff

### Outreach Sequencing Within Tier 3

1. **Policy organizations first** (3A): Weeks 6–8 after Tier 2 launches. Policy organizations are the highest-impact institutional contacts and their engagement provides credibility for subsequent labor and academic outreach.

2. **Labor organizations second** (3B): Weeks 8–10. Particularly AFL-CIO Tech Institute, UFW, NDWA. Labor contacts respond faster than academic; lead with direct member protection value for NDWA/UFW.

3. **Academic law and policy third** (3C): Weeks 10–14, timed to fall semester if possible (mid-August for best academic contact window). Contact academic program staff, not department-level email lists.

4. **Second wave with endorsements** (Month 4–6): Once any Tier 2 or Tier 3 organizations have published citations or acknowledgments, include those references in second-wave Tier 3 outreach. A citation from EFF or Freedom of the Press Foundation materially strengthens the Tier 3 academic and think tank case.

### Email Tone by Sector

- **Policy organizations**: Formal, evidence-first. Open with the primary source claim: "Every capability claim in the threat model traces to a FOIA disclosure, government contract, or court filing." Frame the corpus as a technical foundation for their policy work, not a finished product.
- **Labor organizations**: Direct and action-oriented. Lead with what members can do right now (Part 0 opt-outs). For tech worker unions, add the accountability dimension. Avoid think tank language.
- **Academic**: Scholarly framing. Connect to existing published work from that institution. Open with a specific legal theory question the corpus raises, not a general "this is a useful resource" pitch.

## Success Metrics

**Short-term (Weeks 1–6, active send period)**:
- Response rate from policy organizations: target 20%+ (2–3 of 12)
- Response rate from labor organizations: target 25%+ (2 of 8)
- Response rate from academic programs: target 15%+ (1–2 of 10)

**Medium-term (Months 3–6)**:
- One policy brief or white paper cites the corpus
- One law school clinic expresses interest in a case or research project related to ELITE documentation
- One labor organization distributes Part 0 to members via internal communications
- One academic course integrates the threat model as a case study

**Long-term (Months 6–12)**:
- Legislative testimony or agency comment letter cites the corpus
- Academic paper on commercial surveillance law cites the threat model
- Journalism school integrates source protection material into digital security curriculum
- Coalition of Tier 3 organizations produces joint policy advocacy based on corpus documentation

**The defining long-term outcome**: Georgetown CPT, ACLU SPT, Brennan Center, or New America OTI engages substantively and cites the corpus in public-facing work. This is the event that puts ELITE documentation into the evidentiary record for congressional hearings and regulatory proceedings.
