# Tier 3 Distribution Prep: Policy, Labor, and Academic Law

This document consolidates the Tier 3 distribution strategy. Tier 3 targets institutions that shape long-term policy, legal norms, and labor rights frameworks. These organizations do not reach the at-risk population directly; they influence the structural conditions that determine how surveillance systems are built, funded, challenged, and constrained.

**Status**: Materials prepared. Do not begin Tier 3 outreach until Tier 2 is underway. The recommended gap between Tier 2 launch and Tier 3 launch is 6–10 weeks.

**Strategic note**: Tier 3 contacts work on longer timelines than Tier 1 or 2. A policy organization may take months to publish analysis; a law school clinic may engage over a semester. Success metrics here are different: policy briefs, institutional partnerships, curricula adoption, and legislative testimony are all longer-horizon outcomes.

---

## Why Tier 3 Matters

**Policy influence**: Think tanks and policy organizations produce the briefs that legislators read, the testimony that appears in congressional hearings, and the white papers that shape agency rulemaking. Getting the ELITE threat model into policy conversations means the surveillance infrastructure documented in the corpus becomes part of the evidence base for future regulation of commercial data broker-to-law enforcement data flows.

**Labor protection**: Tech workers build these systems. Gig workers and contingent workers are targeted by the same commercial surveillance infrastructure. Labor organizations — particularly those with tech worker organizers — have interest in both the worker angle (are your members building tools used to target immigrants without warrants?) and the direct protection angle (are your members or their communities surveilled by commercial data brokers?).

**Institutional adoption**: Law school clinics can turn the threat model into litigation frameworks. Policy schools can build case studies. Journalism schools can integrate the opsec content into security training for student journalists. These adoptions create lasting distribution channels that outlive any single outreach effort.

**Credibility compounding**: By the time Tier 3 outreach begins, Tier 1 and Tier 2 engagements will have generated citations, acknowledgments, or publications from organizations with credibility in the policy space. Those references can be included in Tier 3 outreach.

## Email Templates (Tier 3)

Tier 3 templates are longer and more institutionally formal than Tier 1 or 2. Policy and academic contacts expect evidence, context, and clear relevance to their specific work before engaging.

and legal use.

   with citations

   through full device hardening
3. Implementation guide (~1,030 lines): Step-by-step technical setup with
   verification checkpoints

The threat model section may be useful to [Organization]'s policy work because:
[PERSONALIZE: e.g., "it documents the commercial data broker-to-law-enforcement

specific, FOIA-documented case study for the broader surveillance infrastructure
questions your recent [publication] examined" / etc.]

## Contact Research Methodology

Tier 3 contacts are harder to verify than Tier 1 or 2 because:
- Think tank staff change frequently (especially with political transitions)
- Law clinic directors rotate between faculty and visiting positions
- Labor organization tech committees often aren't publicly listed

Use the following research approach to verify contacts before sending:

**1. Annual reports** (most policy organizations publish annually)
- Look for: staff list, program director names, recent publications
- URL patterns: [org].org/annual-report, [org].org/about/staff

**2. Conference speaker lists**
- Recent conferences: Tech Policy Institute, Privacy Law Scholars Conference, CPDP, Future of Privacy Forum annual summit
- If a person from the organization spoke at a 2025-2026 conference, they are active and likely the right contact

**3. Organization "About" or "People" pages**
- Focus on: Program Director, Research Director, Communications Director
- Secondary: Fellows, Visiting Researchers (they often have more bandwidth for new engagement)

**4. FOIA and litigation records** (for law clinics)
- Search for clinic name in PACER or CourtListener
- Recent cases indicate active practice areas and may reveal relevant faculty

**5. LinkedIn** (use with caution)
- Verify role and tenure, not for outreach itself
- Useful for confirming the person you found in an annual report is still at the org

**6. Google Scholar** (for academic contacts)
- Search for the clinic/program name + recent years
- Published papers on surveillance, immigration enforcement, or commercial data indicate the right researcher

## Success Metrics for Tier 3

Tier 3 success has a longer time horizon. Indicators over a 6–12 month window:

**Short-term (1–3 months)**:
- A policy organization acknowledges and begins internal review of the threat model
- A law clinic director responds with interest in a specific Fourth Amendment or privacy law question raised by the ELITE documentation
- An AFL-CIO Tech Institute or union safety committee requests a version adapted for member education

**Medium-term (3–6 months)**:
- A policy brief or white paper cites the corpus in a public report
- A law school clinic takes on a related case or research project
- An academic course integrates the threat model as a case study
- A labor organization distributes Part 0 (data broker opt-outs) to its members via internal communications

**Long-term (6–12 months)**:
- Legislative testimony or agency comment letters cite the corpus
- An academic paper analyzing ELITE or commercial data broker law cites the threat model
- A journalism school integrates the source protection material into digital security curriculum
- A coalition of Tier 3 organizations produces joint policy advocacy based on the corpus documentation

**The most important long-term outcome**: The FOIA-sourced threat model documentation becomes part of the evidentiary record in legislative or regulatory proceedings on commercial data broker oversight. This requires at least one policy organization (Brookings, ACLU SPT, New America OTI, or Georgetown CPT) to engage substantively and cite the corpus in public-facing work.

## Why Tier 3 Is Distinct from Tier 2

Tier 2 amplifies to larger technical and media audiences. Tier 3 shapes institutions.

| Dimension | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Primary audience reached | Security-literate individuals | Policymakers, legislators, regulators, lawyers |
| Response timeline | Days to weeks | Weeks to months |
| Success indicator | Links, citations, training integration | Policy briefs, litigation, curricula, testimony |
| Outreach tone | Peer technical, journalist informational | Formal, evidence-first, institution-aware |
| Contact research difficulty | Moderate | High (staff turnover, academic calendars) |
| Best timing | Anytime | Start of semester, conference season |

The rationale for the gap between Tier 2 and Tier 3 is credibility compounding: when a Tier 3 contact asks "who else has engaged with this?", having EFF, Freedom of the Press Foundation, or a security researcher community response available makes the Tier 3 case stronger.
