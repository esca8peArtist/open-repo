# Phase 2 Distribution Sequencing
## Multi-Tier Multi-Wave Outreach for Maximum Institutional & Grassroots Impact

**Executive Summary**

Phase 2 orchestrates the expansion from Phase 1's focused 25-organization Tier 1 outreach (immigration legal aid, community organizations, mutual aid networks) into a scaled 100+ organization campaign spanning policy institutions, civil liberties organizations, labor unions, disability advocates, and media/research networks. Distribution is sequenced across four waves over 13 weeks (June 1 — August 31), with hard decision gates at weeks 2, 5, and 8 to assess adoption signals, messaging resonance, and contingency pivot requirements.

**Foundational principle**: Phase 2 succeeds when Phase 1 adoption signals are clearly identified and used to inform wave sequencing. This is not a broadcast calendar — it is a signal-responsive escalation framework. If Phase 1 stalls, Phase 2 pivots from expansion to recovery. If Phase 1 succeeds, Phase 2 amplifies through identified early adopters and sector leaders.

**Key constraint**: Phase 2 does not run sequentially after Phase 1 — it runs in parallel. Pilot contacts (FPF, NLG, CLS) are engaged during Phase 1 Weeks 4–11, producing feedback that refines the Phase 2 full wave messaging before Tier 2 Group B launches July 15.

## Section 4: Feedback Loop Design & Adoption Signal Detection

### Adoption Signal Hierarchy

**Level 1 (Weak Signal)**: Contact replies expressing interest or asking a question
- Action: Respond within 24h; offer 20-min call; track engagement score

**Level 2 (Medium Signal)**: Contact mentions implementing or distributing to colleagues
- Action: Schedule implementation or training call; offer to customize; ask for feedback timeline
- Example: "The clinic directors meeting is in 3 weeks — I'll walk through the Part 0 protocol then"

**Level 3 (Strong Signal)**: Contact mentions using in a specific institutional context (litigation brief, policy memo, client intake, training curriculum)
- Action: Offer to co-author or review draft; offer to provide customized documentation
- Example: "We're integrating this into our legal observer training"

**Level 4 (Critical Signal)**: Downstream amplification — contact mentions citing in published material, media mention, referral to another organization
- Action: Log as credibility signal; request permission to feature in testimonial or case study; ask for feedback on impact

### Real-Time Tracking: Adoption Signal Dashboard

Maintain a simple weekly tracker during Waves 2–4:

| Organization | Wave Sent | Signal Level | Evidence | Follow-Up Action | Timeline |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|----------|------------------|----------|

| EFF | 2B | Level 2 | "Shared with journalism desk" | Request feedback timeline | 2 weeks |

**Weekly synthesis report** (every Friday during Waves 2–4):
- New Level 3+ signals identified?

### Feedback Surveys (Weeks 4–8, Tier 2 Pilot)

Three-question short feedback form (sent to FPF, NLG, CLS after orientation sessions):

1. **Most usable section?** (Which playbook section did you find most directly applicable to your practice or team's work?)
2. **Unverifiable claim or gap?** (Did you identify any claim that seemed unsourced or any important gap in the documentation?)
3. **Integration plan in 60 days?** (What's your realistic commitment — will you integrate this into training, policy, distribution, or no further action?)

**Timing**: Send surveys within 24 hours after each orientation session (Week 4–6). Compile responses into synthesis document by July 8 (end of Week 6, before Wave 2B launch).

**Refinement triggers**: If 2+ pilot orgs identify the same gap or request the same customization, that becomes a Wave 2B messaging update.

## Section 5: Risk Mitigation & Contingency Pivots

### Risk 1: Phase 1 Adoption Stalls (Low Early Reply Rate)

**Trigger**: Gate 1 (June 7) click rate <20% OR Gate 2 (June 15) reply rate <10%

**Impact on Phase 2**: Delays all Tier 2 waves by 2 weeks; pivot messaging emphasis

**Contingency Response**:
1. Extend Phase 1 follow-up loop through July 1 instead of June 21
2. Do NOT launch Tier 2 Group B on July 15 — defer to August 1
3. Run diagnostic on Phase 1 messaging (review subject lines, opening sentences; test sector-specific variants on follow-ups)
4. Launch pilot (FPF, NLG, CLS) on normal schedule (Week 4), but with messaging A/B test based on Phase 1 learnings
5. Use pilot feedback (Week 6) to refine Wave 2B messaging before August 1 launch

**Outcome**: Phase 2 still launches but on 2-week delay; Tier 3 slides to September 1

---

### Risk 2: Pilot Adoption Low (under 40%)

**Trigger**: Gate 2 assessment (July 8–15) shows 1 of 3 pilot orgs engaging substantively

**Impact on Phase 2**: Messaging is misaligned; full Tier 2 wave likely to underperform

**Contingency Response**:
1. Pause Wave 2B launch (scheduled July 15) by 1 week
2. Run 1-on-1 diagnostic calls with non-responsive pilot orgs:
   - Wrong staff function reached? (Identify alternative contact in the organization)
   - Subject line/framing not resonating? (Test alternative framing in 1-on-1 call)
   - Timing bad? (Ask if there's a better window to revisit in August)
3. Compile pilot feedback (even minimal) + diagnostic insights
4. Refine Wave 2B templates based on diagnostic findings
5. Launch Wave 2B on August 1 (2-week delay) with refined messaging

**Expected outcome**: Reduced Wave 2B response rate (20–30% vs. 40%+ target), but messaging is more aligned when Tier 3 launches

---

### Risk 3: Certain Sectors Unresponsive (e.g., Labor, Disability Advocates)

**Trigger**: Wave 2B data shows 0 responses from Steady Majority sector contacts (SEIU, DREDF, etc.)

**Impact on Phase 2**: Tier 2 coverage is incomplete; Tier 3 sector-specific messaging needs adjustment

**Contingency Response**:
1. Identify why sector is unresponsive:
   - **Wrong contact function reached?** Re-research and find alternative contact (e.g., if messaging reached SEIU communications, find SEIU organizing training coordinator)
   - **Subject line/framing not resonating?** Pilot organizations in that sector didn't show strong adoption signal — hold off on Wave 2B sends to that sector; move them to Wave 3 with revised framing
   - **Timing bad?** (e.g., SEIU in active campaign window) — defer to August 1
2. For Wave 3 (July 16+): Apply sector-specific messaging test. Example: if disability advocates are unresponsive to "threat model" framing, test "accessibility and surveillance intersection" framing instead.
3. Do NOT remove sector from Wave 3–4 plans; simply pivot messaging.

---

### Risk 4: Media Amplification Spike (Unexpected)

**Trigger**: Tier 1 or Tier 2 adopter mentions corpus in media, triggering inbound inquiries or secondary coverage

**Opportunity**: This is a positive inflection point, but requires rapid response

**Proactive Response**:
1. Monitor Google Alerts (set during Phase 1) for "ELITE address confidence," "Palantir ICE ELITE," "DROP platform immigration," and "cybersecurity hardening resource"
2. If media mention detected:
   - Identify which organization made the public statement
   - Determine if statement is accurate; if not, respond quickly to clarify
   - Use the mention as a credibility signal for downstream Tier 2/3 contacts ("Recently cited in [media outlet]")
   - Offer to brief the journalist or media outlet on additional context
3. Update Phase 2 outreach messaging to reference the media mention (credibility amplification)

---

### Risk 5: Contact Fatigue (Organizational Burnout from Email Waves)

**Trigger**: By Wave 3, some organizations have received multiple emails (Phase 1 adjacent, Tier 2 invite, Tier 2 follow-up, Tier 3 launch)

**Prevention**:
1. **Deduplication check**: Before sending any Wave 2, 3, or 4 email, verify that organization was NOT contacted in Phase 1 or Tier 2 Wave 2B
2. **Maximum 2 contacts per organization**: Unless an organization has publicly requested additional contacts, do not send to more than 2 different staff functions within same organization (e.g., both the policy director AND the communications director)
3. **Escalation by referral only**: If targeting multiple staff functions within same organization, only do it if a prior contact explicitly refers you to the second person (e.g., "Talk to [colleague], they handle training")

## Section 6: Integration with Phase 1 — How Phase 2 Amplifies Phase 1 Impact

### Phase 1 → Phase 2 Transmission Mechanisms

**1. Direct Referral Pathway**
Phase 1 early adopters (who mention forwarding to colleagues or ask for customizations) become warm introduction sources for Phase 2 contacts in their network.

Example: NILC (Phase 1, Tier 1A) mentions integrating Part 0 into their network. When contacting regional law schools (Tier 2 Wave 2B), reference: "NILC is using this resource — wanted to ensure you had it as well."

**2. Adoption Signal Harvesting**
Phase 1 measurables → Wave 2 messaging refinement:
- If Phase 1 shows strongest adoption in legal aid sector (not mutual aid), Wave 2B emphasizes litigation-support angle more heavily
- If Phase 1 Tier 1A regions show faster adoption (South vs. Northeast), Wave 2 prioritizes Tier 2 contacts in high-response regions first

**3. Credibility Compounding**
Each phase's credible early adopters become endorsers for the next phase's frontier.

Phase 1: "25 immigration legal aid organizations have reviewed this corpus"
Phase 2A: "FPF, NLG, and CLS have integrated this into their practice"
Phase 2B: "EFF, ACLU, and Just Futures Law are using this for litigation support"
Phase 3: "Over 50 organizations including [leaders] recommend this resource"

**4. Messaging Feedback Loop**
Pilot feedback (Week 6) directly refines Wave 2B templates (Week 7) before launch:
- If pilot shows need for "accessibility guide" (how to use corpus without technical training), Wave 2B includes accessibility version
- If pilot shows confusion about Section X, Wave 2B simplifies that section's framing
- If pilot shows demand for customization (sector-specific versions), Wave 2B offers customization explicitly

## Section 10: Key Wave Insights & Contingency Risks Summary

### What Could Go Wrong — and What We'll Do About It

**Risk 1: Phase 1 Outreach Stalls**
- **Likelihood**: Medium (20–25% probability)
- **Impact**: Delays entire Phase 2 by 2 weeks; undermines Tier 2 credibility signal
- **Contingency**: Extend Phase 1 follow-up to July 1; use pilot feedback to refine messaging; launch Wave 2B on August 1 instead of July 15
- **Mitigation**: Monitor Phase 1 click rate daily (not just Gates); pivot subject lines by Day 5 if trend is <20%

**Risk 2: Pilot Engagement Low**
- **Likelihood**: Low (10–15% probability, assuming FPF/NLG contacts are high-quality)
- **Impact**: Wave 2B messaging is misaligned; full Tier 2 wave underperforms
- **Contingency**: Run diagnostic calls with non-responsive pilot orgs; apply findings to Wave 2B; delay to August 1
- **Mitigation**: Verify pilot contacts are correct staff function (security team at FPF, not communications); personalize invitations heavily

**Risk 3: Sector-Specific Unresponsiveness**
- **Likelihood**: Medium (25–30% probability, especially for labor unions and disability orgs)
- **Impact**: Tier 2 coverage is incomplete; Phase 3 targets fewer sectors
- **Contingency**: Pivot messaging for unresponsive sectors in Wave 3; do NOT remove sector; simply reframe
- **Mitigation**: Phase 2B includes A/B testing for at least 2 different sector framings; don't rely on single message

**Risk 4: Contact Fatigue / Overlap**
- **Likelihood**: Low (5–10% probability if tracking sheet is maintained)
- **Impact**: Contacted organization becomes annoyed by multiple emails; marks corpus as spam or low-trust
- **Contingency**: Strict deduplication before each wave; maximum 2 contacts per organization; escalation only by referral
- **Mitigation**: Weekly review of contact list for duplicates; maintain master contact registry across all waves

**Risk 5: Media Spike (Positive Unexpected)**
- **Likelihood**: Low (10–15% probability, but high upside if occurs)
- **Impact**: Sudden inbound interest; opportunity for credibility amplification
- **Contingency**: Use media mention as credibility signal in downstream Phase 2 outreach
- **Mitigation**: Monitor Google Alerts continuously; have media response protocol ready (draft quote, offer briefing)
