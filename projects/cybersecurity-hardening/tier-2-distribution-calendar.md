# Tier 2 Distribution Calendar: 4-Week Rollout

**How to use**: This calendar is designed to be immediately executable. Each week has a specific sector focus, a list of contacts to send, and a follow-up structure. The sequence is ordered by expected response speed and strategic value — digital rights first (fastest, highest impact), journalists second (time-sensitive story angles, quick wins), academic third (slow conversion, start early), researcher communities fourth (ongoing engagement, no hard deadline).

**Assumptions**:
- Tier 1 outreach has been completed or is underway (Tier 2 is a follow-on, not a replacement)
- User has the corpus Gist URL ready: https://gist.github.com/esca8peArtist/e90dd6a0bd6805e0ddbe0e8d1eu7d108
- User has 1-2 hours available per week for send execution and response tracking
- User is sending from a personal email address, not a domain email — adjust signature accordingly

**Immediate action required**: DEF CON 34 CFP closes May 1, 2026. If a talk submission is planned, it must happen before this calendar's Week 1 (or simultaneously). Do not wait for Week 4.

## Follow-Up Structure (Post-Campaign)

### 1-Week Follow-Up Protocol (applies to all sectors)

Send 7 days after initial outreach to non-responding contacts. Keep to 3 sentences:
1. Brief reminder of what you sent and the corpus link
2. One new piece of context if available (e.g., "since my last email, STOP's Michelle Dahl confirmed the corpus is being reviewed by their research team" — use social proof where legitimately available)
3. Routing request: "If this belongs with a different person at your organization, please feel free to forward or let me know who to contact."

Do not apologize for following up. Do not write "I know you're busy." Follow-up is normal professional practice.

### 2-Week Check-In (for engaged contacts only)

If a contact replied positively but has not taken an action (e.g., expressed interest in curriculum integration, routing to another team member), send a brief 2-week check-in that advances the conversation with a specific offer:
- "I'm happy to prepare a one-page summary formatted for syllabus distribution" (academic)
- "I can provide the original FOIA documents cited in the threat model if that would be useful" (digital rights / journalist)
- "If it would help, I can join a brief call to walk through the countermeasures methodology" (researcher / FPF training)

### 3-Month Re-Engagement

For academic contacts that expressed interest but didn't convert, re-engage in September 2026 for fall semester planning. Reference the initial exchange: "We corresponded in May — I wanted to follow up now that fall curriculum planning is underway."
