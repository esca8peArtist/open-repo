# Phase 2 Deployment Readiness

**Target launch**: 4 weeks after Phase 1 Tier 1 outreach begins (concurrent with the Week 4 follow-up loop)

**Decision gate**: Full Tier 2 launch is gated on the Week 7 Phase 1 adoption assessment (see PHASE_2_SEQUENCING.md Section 2). However, pilot outreach to 3–5 organizations can begin at Week 4 without waiting for the gate. The gate only governs full-wave Tier 2 expansion.

**Contingency**: Per tier-2-launch-checklist.md, Tier 2 can launch independently if Phase 1 stalls beyond June 3, 2026, given the June 12 FISA Section 702 reauthorization deadline creating time-bounded policy urgency.

## Section 2: Four Playbook Integration Steps

### Step 2.1 — Journalist Security Playbook

**Distribution channel**: Freedom of the Press Foundation (FPF), Committee to Protect Journalists (CPJ), Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press (RCFP), PEN America. Contacts documented in TIER2_DISTRIBUTION_PREP.md Tier 2D.

**Integration sequence**:
1. **Week 4 (pilot)**: Send to FPF security team at security@freedom.press with specific review ask: CBP device protocol, Signal safety number verification, SecureDrop deployment for small newsrooms. Frame as pilot with feedback request.
2. **Week 6 gate**: If FPF engagement is substantive (feedback received or review confirmed), proceed to full Tier 2D wave.
3. **Full Tier 2D wave (Weeks 6–8)**: CPJ, RCFP, PEN America, journalism school security trainers. Use sector-specific template from TIER2_MESSAGING_TEMPLATES.md.
4. **Gist integration**: Link journalist playbook from a dedicated note on the Gist, or distribute as a separate PDF/Gist. Do not replace the core corpus Gist — the playbook is additive.

**Section 702 timing**: June 12, 2026 FISA deadline creates an urgency window for journalist security outreach. The playbook's Section 1.2 on warrantless FBI queries of Section 702 databases (including queries targeting U.S. journalists) is directly relevant to the policy moment. Send before June 12 if possible.

**Pre-distribution check**: Confirm Section 702 status as of actual send date (current: 45-day extension, expires mid-June 2026; reform amendment for U.S. person queries failed Senate 42–50).

### Step 2.2 — Whistleblower Security Playbook

**Distribution channel**: Government Accountability Project (GAP), Project On Government Oversight (POGO), National Whistleblower Center, congressional oversight staff, FPF (SecureDrop infrastructure link).

**Integration sequence**:
1. **Week 5 (pilot)**: Send to GAP and POGO with specific framing: "The WPA coverage gap table in Section 3 (probationary period gap, intelligence community gap, press disclosure gap) is immediately relevant to attorneys advising current federal employees. The parallel construction risk discussion in Section 2.3 fills a documented gap in most existing whistleblower guides."
2. **Whistleblower-journalist pair**: Distribute to FPF simultaneously with or immediately after the journalist playbook. The two playbooks form a source-journalist pair — the whistleblower playbook covers the source side of SecureDrop; the journalist playbook covers the newsroom side. FPF can validate both.
3. **Congressional staff track**: Per TIER2_DISTRIBUTION_PREP.md Tier 2B, distribute to congressional oversight staff contacts. The DOGE/SSA whistleblower cases (Charles Borges OSC complaint, Daniel Berulis NLRB case) are documented in the playbook and are directly relevant to ongoing oversight committee work.
4. **Full wave (Weeks 7–9)**: Broader distribution after GAP/POGO pilot feedback.

**Pre-distribution check**: Confirm GAP's operational capacity given DOGE-era DOJ restructuring effects. Verify current contact (GAP website for current program leadership).

### Step 2.3 — DV Survivor Safety Playbook

**Distribution channel**: NNEDV Safety Net Project (techsafety.org), National Domestic Violence Hotline, state DV coalition networks, DV legal advocates, shelter technology safety staff.

**Required review before distribution**: This playbook is flagged for NNEDV Safety Net review before any distribution. The safety planning framework (specifically the escalation risk discussion — removing stalkerware can trigger physical violence escalation) and device replacement timing recommendations require validation by DV practitioners with real-world safety planning experience.

**Integration sequence**:
1. **Before Week 4 pilot**: Contact NNEDV Safety Net Project at techsafety.org with a review request. Frame as: "We have produced a technology safety planning guide for DV survivors and advocates. We want NNEDV Safety Net to review the safety planning framing and escalation risk guidance before distribution to ensure it is consistent with current best practice."
2. **If NNEDV engages**: Incorporate feedback before any distribution. The NNEDV review is the distribution authorization gate for this playbook.
3. **If NNEDV does not respond within 3 weeks**: Escalate to Coalition Against Stalkerware (stopstalkerware.org) as a secondary reviewer. Do not distribute without at least one practitioner review.
4. **Full wave (Weeks 8–12, contingent on review)**: State coalition networks, DV hotline partner organizations, shelter staff training programs.

**Standalone distribution note**: This playbook is a distinct outreach track from the core corpus immigration/activism audience. Do not distribute to Tier 1 immigration legal aid contacts — the audience is DV advocates and survivor services, not immigration legal services.

**Spanish translation**: If Spanish translation is produced (see Section 4), the DV playbook's Part 0 (safety planning protocol) is the highest-ROI translation target within Phase 2, after the immigration playbook's Part 0.

### Step 2.4 — Financial Resistance Playbook

**Distribution channel**: Nonprofit networks (National Council of Nonprofits, Bolder Advocacy / Alliance for Justice), labor organizations (AFL-CIO Tech Institute, SEIU, CWA), mutual aid fund managers, legal defense funds.

**Required review before distribution**: Flagged for nonprofit tax counsel review before distribution. The financial and tax claims (Schedule B confidentiality, IRS structuring law, Monero entry/exit vulnerability, FinCEN SAR filing triggers) are accurate at a general level but application to specific organizational situations requires practitioner review. Legal disclaimers are present throughout; a practitioner review by a nonprofit tax attorney strengthens distribution confidence.

**Integration sequence**:
1. **Before Week 6**: Contact Bolder Advocacy (Alliance for Justice) or National Council of Nonprofits with a review request. Frame as: "We have produced a financial privacy guide for advocacy organizations and mutual aid networks. We want a nonprofit tax attorney to review the financial claims before distribution."
2. **If review confirms**: Proceed to labor and nonprofit distribution wave.
3. **Labor track (Weeks 7–10)**: AFL-CIO Tech Institute, SEIU California, CWA/CODE-CWA, UFW, NDWA. Use Template 3B from TIER3_DISTRIBUTION_PREP.md (labor template is directly applicable here for the labor track).
4. **Policy track (concurrent with labor)**: Lawyers Committee for Civil Rights Under Law, Center for Constitutional Rights, Brennan Center. Frame around the IRS LCA Palantir documentation as a FOIA-grounded civil rights research resource.

**IRS timeline note**: The financial resistance playbook is not time-critical in the same way the journalist playbook is time-critical (FISA deadline). However, any IRS enforcement action against a prominent advocacy organization in 2026 would create a news cycle urgency window. Monitor for such events and be prepared to accelerate distribution in that context.

## Section 5: Spanish Translation Status

**Current status**: Not yet produced. Documented as future expansion in SCENARIO_PLAYBOOK_INDEX.md.

**Recommended sequence for Spanish translation** (highest ROI first):

1. **Immigration playbook Part 0** (data broker opt-outs) — the single highest-ROI translation. The undocumented immigrant population who most needs this content is disproportionately Spanish-speaking. Estimated: 1,500–2,000 words.

2. **DV survivor playbook safety planning section** — second highest ROI. The stalkerware threat and safety planning framework are as relevant in Spanish-language DV survivor communities as in English-language. Estimated: 1,000 words.

3. **Core corpus Part 0** (in opsec-playbook.md) — third. The broader immigration legal aid audience (Tier 1A) can distribute a Spanish-language Part 0 to clients immediately.

**Translation decision point**: Translation requires either a human translator with DV/legal fluency or an AI translation reviewed by a Spanish-speaking legal advocate. Do not distribute machine-translated safety-critical material (especially DV playbook) without human review.

**Timeline**: No firm deadline. Documented as July 2026 expansion possibility in Tier 1B outreach templates (language roadmap note: "Spanish on July 2026"). Treat this as a July quarterly review item.
