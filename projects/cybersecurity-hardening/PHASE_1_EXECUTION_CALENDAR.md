## Part 1: Overview

### What Phase 1 Entails

Phase 1 delivers the OpSec corpus — a three-document set (threat model, countermeasures playbook, implementation guide) documenting Palantir's ELITE system, NSA Section 702 targeting, and commercial data broker pipelines — to 25 high-leverage policy contacts over a 15-day active window. These contacts span three sectors:

- **Senate offices and senate staff** (estimated 8–10 contacts): Legislative staff working on digital privacy, civil liberties, or immigration oversight. Target function is the staff member handling digital surveillance policy, not the press office.
- **Think tanks and policy organizations** (estimated 8–10 contacts): Organizations producing surveillance policy research, civil liberties analysis, or immigration enforcement analysis. Target function is the fellow or senior analyst, not communications.
- **Law schools with civil liberties or surveillance law clinics** (estimated 5–7 contacts): Clinics running active immigration cases, digital rights litigation, or surveillance law coursework. Target function is the supervising attorney or clinic director.

The 25 contacts are pre-screened. Contact verification status, personalized email drafts, and response templates are ready in `TIER1_OUTREACH_PREPARED.md` and `TIER1_OUTREACH_EXECUTION_PLAN.md`.

**What Phase 1 is not**: Phase 1 is not immigration legal aid outreach (that is Tier 1A/1B/1C in a parallel track), not Tier 2 pilot delivery (Group A began May 28), and not Tier 2 full expansion (Gate 2 decision is June 15). Phase 1 is the high-leverage policy-contact cohort whose adoption generates downstream credibility for every subsequent tier.

## Part 8: Contingency Playbook

All contingency protocols are pre-committed. Do not improvise under pressure.

### Scenario 1: Key Contact Does Not Respond (No Click, No Reply)

**Trigger**: Named contact with a verified email shows no Bitly click and no reply after 10 days.

**Steps** (in order; do not skip ahead):
1. **Verify routing** (Day 8): Was the email sent to the right function? Senate staff = legislative assistant or counsel handling digital policy, not press. Think tank = named fellow or program director, not communications. Law school clinic = supervising attorney or clinic director, not general admissions.
2. **Send Template R2 follow-up** (Day 8 for Day 1 sends): Different subject line, one sentence referencing recent organizational activity, Calendly link.
3. **If still no response by Day 14**: LinkedIn message (one only) for think tank contacts with public LinkedIn profiles. For Senate staff, do not use LinkedIn — use the Senate office's public website to identify correct routing.
4. **If still no response by Day 19**: No third outreach. Close the contact in the spreadsheet as "Unresponsive — closed." Do not count toward campaign failure unless >40% of contacts are in this bucket.

**Escalation threshold**: If 8+ of 25 contacts (>30%) show zero engagement by Day 14, the issue is systemic, not individual. Run the Scenario A diagnostic from `TIER_2_EXPANSION_ARCHITECTURE.md` Section 5.

---

### Scenario 2: Adoption Rate 50% Lower Than Target

**Trigger**: Gate 2 (June 15) shows click rate 20–30% and meetings <2 (i.e., adoption is roughly half the 40% click / 3-meeting target).

**Diagnostic sequence** (from `TIER_2_EXPANSION_ARCHITECTURE.md` Scenario A):
1. **Click-through analysis by sector** (30 min): Open Bitly. Which sector — Senate, think tank, law school — has the lowest click rate? If sector-specific, the subject line is not resonating for that sector. Test a sector-specific variant on remaining sends.
2. **Meeting no-show rate** (15 min): Of scheduled meetings, how many completed vs. cancelled? If no-show rate >25%, the scheduling process is not qualifying intent. Add a pre-call question before confirming time.
3. **Feedback quality** (20 min): Are replies generic ("looks interesting") or specific ("the Part 0 opt-outs would be useful for our intake forms")? Generic-only = framing is too abstract. Test a more concrete opening that names a specific use case.

**Lever 1 — Sector-specific intensification**: Rewrite follow-up emails for the underperforming sector with a different hook. Do not re-send the same email.
**Lever 2 — Meeting conversion strengthening**: Replace all "happy to discuss" CTAs with specific time offers containing a date, duration, and deliverable.
**Lever 3 — Framing simplification**: Add a one-paragraph "why this matters for your specific work" to each follow-up, personalized per organization.

**Escalation**: If all three levers applied and adoption still <35% by Day 28, pause Tier 2 and run diagnostic sessions with 3 early adopters. Document findings before proceeding.

---

### Scenario 3: Political Environment Shifts

**Trigger**: A major public event (congressional hearing, media story, executive order) related to ICE surveillance, Palantir, or immigration enforcement occurs during June 1–15.

**Assessment**: A political trigger is an acceleration signal for the think tank and Senate cohorts. Policy contacts engage more readily when the topic is in the news.

**Response** (activate within 24 hours of the event):
1. **For contacts already in the pipeline**: Update the follow-up email to reference the event: "Given the [hearing/story/order] this week, the ELITE documentation I sent earlier may be immediately relevant to your current work."
2. **For un-sent contacts**: Add a single sentence to the opening of their initial email referencing the triggering event. This is not opportunistic — it is relevant personalization.
3. **Do not alter the corpus itself** in response to a news cycle. Stability of documentation is a credibility asset.
4. **If the event is adversarial** (e.g., disclosure that a contacted organization is under surveillance): Reach out proactively with support offer. Do not use the event for recruitment purposes.

---

### Scenario 4: Media Angle Dominates

**Trigger**: A Tier 1 or Tier 2 contact generates media coverage of the corpus or threat model before the campaign has completed.

**Assessment**: This is a positive trigger and an acceleration signal. Media coverage of the corpus by a Tier 1 policy contact validates the documentation before Tier 2 Group B launches.

**Response**:
1. **Do not contact the journalist** (unless you initiated the connection). Let the contact manage their press relationship.
2. **Use the coverage in remaining outreach**: "The threat model was recently cited in [publication] — I wanted to share it with you given your related work." One sentence; do not lead with the media mention.
3. **Accelerate Tier 2 Group B timing**: If Tier 1 policy adoption generates media coverage during June 1–15, bring Group B launch timeline forward. Notify user with the media signal and the acceleration recommendation.
4. **Log the coverage** in Tab 4 (Adoption Signals) immediately with date, publication, context, and whether the contacting organization was the source.

---

### Cascade Procedures: Accelerated vs. Decelerated Follow-Up

**Accelerated follow-up** (when Gate 1 or Gate 2 exceeds targets):
- If Gate 1 click rate >50% (12+ clicks from 25 sends): Send Stage 1+ follow-up emails to high-engagement contacts on Day 5 rather than Day 8. Warm contacts should be engaged before they cool off.
- If ≥5 meetings scheduled by Day 10: Prioritize meeting completion over new sends in Week 2. Do not let scheduled calls accumulate while you are still sending.
- If Bitly shows a spike suggesting internal forwarding (multiple clicks in short succession from the same time zone): Contact the originating organization the same day. Ask who else received it.

**Decelerated follow-up** (when below targets):
- If Gate 1 click rate <20%: Pause all sends for 48 hours. Do not follow up while deliverability is unresolved.
- If 3+ bounces in Wave 1–2: Stop Wave 3 sends. Re-validate all remaining contact emails before proceeding.
- If a Stage 1+ contact goes silent after requesting a meeting: One re-engagement email only, sent 5 business days after the originally scheduled call time. If no response, close as Declined.
