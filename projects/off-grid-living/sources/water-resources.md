# Water Domain: Primary Source Index

Midwest / Zone 5 focus. Covers short-term purification, hand pumps, well drilling
(DIY and contracted), long-term storage, and water testing (with Midwest
agricultural-runoff emphasis). All sources verified as of May 2026.

Relevance ratings are 1–5 for Midwest grid-failure water needs specifically.

---

## Section 1: General Emergency Water Preparedness

### FEMA — "Food and Water in an Emergency"
- **URL:** https://www.fema.gov/pdf/library/f&web.pdf
- **Format:** PDF (FEMA publication)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Storage quantities (1 gal/person/day minimum), container selection,
  how to treat tap water before storage, boiling, bleach disinfection, and solar
  disinfection (SODIS). The foundational civilian emergency reference — short,
  practical, no fluff.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Good for households still on municipal water who want to
  prepare for short outages. Does not address well drilling, hand pumps, or
  surface-water treatment at depth.
- **Gap:** Does not cover agricultural nitrate contamination or long-term off-grid
  water sourcing.

### Ready.gov — Water Preparedness
- **URL:** https://www.ready.gov/water
- **Format:** Web (HTML, printable)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Per-person storage recommendations, container types, treatment
  methods, what to do if you suspect your water is contaminated. Kept current
  by FEMA. More accessible than the PDF above; links to related resources.
- **Relevance: 3/5.** Good entry-level reference. Very general; no Midwest or
  rural-specific content.

### EPA — "Emergency Disinfection of Drinking Water"
- **URL:** https://www.epa.gov/ground-water-and-drinking-water/emergency-disinfection-drinking-water
- **Direct PDF:** https://www.epa.gov/sites/default/files/2017-09/documents/emergency_disinfection_of_drinking_water_sept2017.pdf
- **Format:** Web page + PDF
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Bleach ratios for disinfection (8 drops/gallon unscented 6% bleach,
  or 6 drops at 8.25%), boiling times by altitude, iodine tablet use, filtering
  before chemical treatment. Authoritative and specific — use these numbers, not
  blog posts.
- **Relevance: 5/5.** The single most important short-term treatment reference.
  Bookmark this.

### CDC — "How to Make Water Safe in an Emergency"
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/about/index.html
- **Format:** Web (HTML)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Boiling (rolling boil 1 minute; 3 minutes above 6,500 ft elevation),
  chemical disinfection, commercial tablets, filtering. Parallels the EPA page
  with CDC framing. Additional WASH (Water, Sanitation, Hygiene) emergency
  training resources linked from the training page:
  https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/php/training/index.html
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Solid confirmation source; use alongside EPA for the
  authoritative treatment numbers.

## Section 2: Comprehensive Field Manuals (Free Download)

### "Where There Is No Doctor" — David Werner, Carol Thuman, Jane Maxwell
- **Publisher:** Hesperian Health Guides
- **ISBN:** 978-0-942364-15-6 (print edition)
- **Official 2025 PDF:** https://hesperian.org/wp-content/uploads/pdf/en_wtnd_2025/en_wtnd_2025_fm.pdf
- **Internet Archive (full book):** https://archive.org/details/WhereThereIsNoDoctor-English-DavidWerner
- **Format:** PDF (free download, official Hesperian release)
- **Free:** Yes (Hesperian explicitly releases PDFs at no cost)
- **Covers:** Village-level public health. Water sanitation chapter covers water
  source protection, simple filtration, boiling, bleach treatment, latrine
  placement relative to wells. Also covers diarrheal illness treatment —
  critical in contamination scenarios.
- **Relevance: 3/5 for water specifically; 5/5 as a household medical reference.**
  Water content is basic; its real value is managing the disease consequences
  of waterborne contamination.
- **Note:** The 2025 FM (front matter) link above confirms active distribution.
  Download the full book from the Internet Archive link.

### "The Humanure Handbook" — Joseph C. Jenkins (4th Edition)
- **Official free online version:** https://humanurehandbook.com/contents.html
  (read chapter-by-chapter as linked PDFs)
- **4th edition PDF contents page:** https://humanurehandbook.com/downloads/H4/Contents_HH4.pdf
- **Internet Archive (3rd edition):** https://archive.org/details/humanurehandbook0000jose
- **Format:** PDF (chapter downloads) / HTML
- **Free:** Yes — author explicitly provides it free
- **Covers:** Composting human waste. Relevant to the water domain because
  improper waste disposal is the primary route to well contamination in off-grid
  settings. The book's sanitation model eliminates a major fecal-oral
  contamination vector.
- **Relevance: 3/5 for water directly; essential companion for well-water
  protection.** If you have a shallow well, this book tells you how to keep
  your own waste from fouling it.

## Section 3: Well Construction — Official Government Guides

### EPA — "Manual of Water Well Construction Practices"
- **Author/Publisher:** U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, Office of Water
  Supply (1975, rev. 1976)
- **EPA Publication No.:** EPA-570/9-75-001
- **Official NEPIS text version:** https://nepis.epa.gov/Exe/ZyPURL.cgi?Dockey=2000J6P4.TXT
- **Internet Archive (full PDF):** https://archive.org/details/manualofwaterwel00unit
- **Direct PDF (rexresearch mirror, 2nd ed):**
  https://www.rexresearch1.com/WellsPumpsLibrary/ManualfWaterWellconstr.pdf
- **Format:** PDF / text
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** All major well construction methods — cable tool, rotary, jetting,
  driven point, dug wells. Well casing standards, grouting, development, and
  pump installation. Written for field use, not just engineers. The regulatory
  and technical foundation for well construction in the US.
- **Relevance: 5/5.** The most complete free technical reference for well
  construction. Essential if hiring a driller (lets you evaluate their work)
  or attempting DIY methods.
- **Confidence:** High — EPA publication, multiple hosting sources confirm same
  document.

### USDA / NRCS — "Conservation Practice Standard: Water Well (Code 642)"
- **URL:** https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/sites/default/files/2022-10/Water_Well_642_NHCP_CPS_2020.pdf
- **Format:** PDF
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** USDA construction standards for agricultural water wells. Covers
  casing depth, grouting requirements, well head protection, and compliance
  standards. Useful for understanding what a professional well should meet.
- **Relevance: 3/5.** More of a regulatory standard than a how-to guide. Use it
  to evaluate a driller's proposal or a well already on a property.

### USDA National Engineering Handbook — Part 631, Chapter 32: "Well Design and Spring Development"
- **URL:** https://forestrywebinars.net/webinars/planning-designing-and-installing-shallow-wells/NEH%20Part%20631%20Ch%2032%20Well%20Design%20and%20Spring%20Development.pdf/at_download/file
- **Format:** PDF
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Shallow well design, spring boxes, driven points, and development
  procedures. More practical than the Code 642 standard. Includes yield
  estimation and development techniques.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Solid technical depth on shallow systems — the most common
  DIY target.

### USDA Forest Service — "Guide for Fabricating and Installing Shallow Ground Water Observation Wells"
- **URL:** https://www.fs.usda.gov/rm/pubs/rmrs_rn009.pdf
- **Format:** PDF
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** DIY installation of shallow monitoring wells using hand tools.
  Describes fabrication of a manual driving/jetting tool from hardware-store
  materials. Though framed as monitoring wells, the mechanics are identical to
  a driven point production well.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Underrated resource. The tool fabrication diagrams are
  useful for anyone attempting a driven-point installation without renting
  equipment.

## Section 4: DIY Well Drilling — Jetting and Driven-Point Methods

### Mother Earth News — "Install a Sand-Point Driven Well"
- **URL:** https://www.motherearthnews.com/homesteading-and-livestock/drive-a-sand-point-well-zbcz1507/
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Sand-point (driven-point) well installation for shallow water
  tables. Four hammering methods (T-post driver, tripod pulley, rope-lowered
  weight, maul). Five-foot galvanized pipe sections driven in sequence.
  Soil limitations: works in coarse sand/gravel; fails in clay or rock.
  Realistic depth ceiling: 30 ft typical, 50 ft maximum under ideal conditions.
  Yield is low; multiple wells can share one pump.
- **Relevance: 5/5.** The most practical single article for Midwest homesteaders
  with sandy loam or glacial till overlying the water table. Much of rural
  Illinois, Indiana, and Iowa fits this profile.
- **Key limitation noted in the article:** Yield is described as "relatively
  low" and the water table fluctuates seasonally.

### Backwoods Home Magazine — "Drive Your Own Freshwater Well" (Len McDougall)
- **URL:** https://www.backwoodshome.com/drive-your-own-freshwater-well/
- **Published:** Issue #128, March/April 2011
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Step-by-step driven-well installation. Pilot hole augering,
  driving the well point, adding pipe sections, sealing joints, detecting
  water, installing a pitcher pump. Includes legal reminder (permits vary
  by county).
- **Relevance: 5/5.** Highly practical, well-written, peer-reviewed by
  decades of Backwoods Home readership. Print and save this one.

### drillyourownwell.com — DIY Jetting Technique
- **URL:** https://drillyourownwell.com/the-how-to-drill-a-well-technique/
- **Format:** Web site (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Water-jetting method using PVC Schedule 40 pipe as both drill
  stem and bit. Water is pumped down through the pipe; cuttings return up
  the annular space. Two-hose variant described for more effective spoil
  removal. Hole size approximately 2 inches. Effective to 30–40 ft; difficult
  beyond 40 ft; not suitable for clay or rock.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Best suited for sandy aquifer zones. The site has a Q&A
  section with real user results.
- **Honest limitation:** Forum users report the borehole collapses overnight in
  some soil types, requiring re-drilling. The 10–12 hour per well reality is
  documented in community discussion.
- **For deeper work (60–300 ft):** The site recommends air-rotary methods
  covered at HowToDrillAWell.com.

### Leaf House Tea — "4 Low-Cost Ways to Install a Shallow Sand Point Well"
- **URL:** https://leafhousetea.com/sand-point-well-installation-methods/
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Comparison of four methods — augering, driving, jackhammering,
  and jetting — with cost tables and materials lists for each. The most useful
  cost-comparison resource found.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Good starting point for deciding which method fits your
  site conditions and budget before committing to a particular approach.

### YouTube — "How to Drill a Well — Most Powerful Homemade Jet" (Complete Guide)
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3pOSzBgB8WU
- **Format:** YouTube video
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Homemade jet well drilling rig. Shows the complete build and
  drilling process. High production quality, complete guide format.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Watch alongside the drillyourownwell.com text guide.
  Seeing the rig in operation resolves many questions the text leaves ambiguous.

### YouTube — "Jetting a Shallow Well DIY"
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1fKjkmSySaE
- **Format:** YouTube video
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Real-world jetting of a shallow well, DIY setup. No polish —
  straight documentation of the process.
- **Relevance: 3/5.** Useful for seeing actual soil conditions and what
  "getting stuck" looks like in practice.

### Instructables — "Make an O-ringer PVC Manual Lift Pump for Your Well Cap"
- **URL:** https://www.instructables.com/Make-an-O-ringer-PVC-Manual-Lift-Pump-for-Your-Wel/
- **Schematic PDF (direct):**
  https://content.instructables.com/FB2/L13R/IQ9PM9HW/FB2L13RIQ9PM9HW.pdf
- **Format:** Web guide with photos + PDF schematic

## Section 6: Long-Term Water Storage

### ibctanks.com — "Are IBC Totes Good for Long-Term Water Storage?"
- **URL:** https://www.ibctanks.com/knowledge-base/are-ibc-totes-good-for-long-term-water-storage
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** IBC tote selection for potable water storage. Key distinction:
  "rebottled" (new inner tank, reused cage) = safe for potable water;
  "reconditioned" (cleaned used tank) = not safe for drinking water.
  HDPE material requirements, rotation schedule (every 6 months minimum),
  light-blocking requirements. 275-gallon capacity per tote.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** IBC totes are the most cost-effective large-volume storage
  option for rural Midwest. Used food-grade totes routinely available at $50–100
  on Craigslist/Facebook Marketplace. Critical reading: the food-grade
  identification rules before purchasing any used tote.

### tank-depot.com — "How to Identify Food-Grade IBC Totes"
- **URL:** https://www.tank-depot.com/blog/how-to-identify-food-grade-ibc-totes-your-ultimate-guide-to-safe-liquid-storage
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Visual identification markers for food-grade IBC totes. Resin codes,
  label reading, UN certification marks, previous-contents indicators.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** Read this before buying any used tote. One wrong purchase
  contaminates stored water with chemical residue.

## Section 7: Rainwater Collection

- **Publications list:** https://rainwaterharvesting.tamu.edu/publications/
- **Texas Guide to Rainwater Harvesting PDF:**
  https://lubbock.tamu.edu/files/2011/10/RainHarv.pdf

- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** System sizing math, first-flush calculations, tank selection,

  catchments under 50,000 sq ft. The most comprehensive free rainwater
  harvesting resource in the US extension system.
- **Relevance: 4/5 for Midwest.** Designed around Texas rainfall patterns but

  35–45 inches/year, sufficient for meaningful collection. State law references

### Midwest Rainwater Collection Legality (Summary)
- **State-by-state guide:** https://worldwaterreserve.com/is-it-illegal-to-collect-rainwater/
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Midwest status as of 2026:**
  - **Illinois:** Legal; systems over 5,000 gallons require permit; indoor
    non-potable use must comply with Illinois Plumbing Code
  - **Indiana:** No restrictions; encouraged
  - **Iowa:** Legal; no restrictions found for personal use
  - **Wisconsin:** Legal for non-potable outdoor use; no permit required
  - **Ohio:** Legal per Ohio Revised Code 3701.344; potable use requires
    compliance with private water system rules
  - **Minnesota:** Legal; check local municipality for additional rules
- **Relevance: 3/5** (informational/legal; not a how-to guide)
- **Note:** County and municipal rules can be more restrictive than state law.
  Verify locally before building a large system.

## Section 8: Water Testing — Agricultural Runoff and Nitrates

### EPA — "Protect Your Home's Water" (Private Wells)
- **URL:** https://www.epa.gov/privatewells/protect-your-homes-water
- **Format:** Web page (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** What to test for in private wells, when to test (annually for
  coliform, nitrates, TDS, pH), how to find a certified lab, contaminant
  sources. Key fact: the EPA maximum contaminant level for nitrate is
  10 mg/L (as nitrogen). Above this level is dangerous for infants
  (methemoglobinemia / "blue baby syndrome").
- **Relevance: 5/5 for Midwest agricultural areas.** Row-crop farming with
  synthetic nitrogen fertilizer dominates the Midwest landscape. Shallow wells
  (<100 ft) in corn/soy areas face the highest nitrate risk.

### CDC — "Guidelines for Testing Well Water"
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/drinking-water/safety/guidelines-for-testing-well-water.html
- **Format:** Web (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** Annual testing schedule, what events trigger immediate testing
  (flooding, nearby well contamination, land-use changes), list of contaminants
  by well type and region.
- **Relevance: 4/5.** The "test more frequently after flooding or heavy rainfall
  if near agricultural land" note is directly applicable to Midwest spring
  flooding events.

### Minnesota Department of Agriculture — Well Testing for Nitrates
- **Main program page:** https://www.mda.state.mn.us/water-testing-nitrate
- **Private well program:** https://www.mda.state.mn.us/private-well-testing-home-water-treatment
- **Township Testing Program:** https://www.mda.state.mn.us/township-testing-program
- **Format:** Web (free)
- **Free:** Yes (MDA provides free or subsidized testing in high-risk townships)
- **Covers:** Free nitrate testing clinics (on-site results in under 5 minutes),
  township-level testing program covering over 70,000 wells in vulnerable
  groundwater areas, referral to certified labs for confirmatory testing.
- **Relevance: 5/5 for Minnesota.** Directly applicable; MN has one of the
  best-funded state agricultural-well testing programs in the country.
- **Transferable note:** Iowa and Wisconsin run similar programs through their
  DNRs. Contact your county Soil and Water Conservation District (SWCD) first —
  they often have loaner test kits or subsidized testing for row-crop-adjacent
  properties.

### Iowa State University Extension — Private Well Water Testing
- **URL:** https://naturalresources.extension.iastate.edu/encyclopedia/private-well-water-testing
- **Private Well Stewardship Program:** https://naturalresources.extension.iastate.edu/programs/private-well-stewardship-program
- **Iowa DNR Private Well Program:** https://www.iowadnr.gov/environmental-protection/water-quality/private-well-program
- **Format:** Web (free)
- **Free:** Yes (Iowa's Private Well Grant Program provides free annual testing)
- **Covers:** Iowa's statewide free testing for bacteria (total coliform, E. coli)
  and nitrates. Arsenic, manganese, and PFAS testing also available (less frequent).
  Test in spring specifically for nitrates — spring snowmelt and rainfall flush
  surface nitrogen into shallow aquifers.
- **Relevance: 5/5 for Iowa.** Iowa has intense row-crop agriculture and among
  the highest nitrate contamination rates in the Midwest.
- **How to access:** Contact your county Department of Public Health; a county
  Environmental Health Specialist collects the sample for you.

### Mid Atlantic Water — "How to Test for Nitrates in Well Water"
- **URL:** https://midatlanticwater.net/blogs/faqs/how-to-test-for-nitrates-well-water
- **Format:** Web article (free)
- **Free:** Yes
- **Covers:** At-home nitrate test strips vs. certified lab testing, what the
  numbers mean, safe levels (under 10 mg/L for adults; under 5 mg/L if you
  have infants), treatment options (reverse osmosis or distillation are
  effective; boiling concentrates nitrates and makes it worse).
- **Relevance: 4/5.** The "boiling concentrates nitrates" warning is critical
  and counterintuitive — this is a common mistake in emergency scenarios.

## Coverage Gaps and Research Notes

**What this document does not yet cover:**

1. **Cable-tool (percussion) well drilling** — the older, slower method that
   works in rock where jetting fails. No strong free DIY guide found. The EPA
   Manual of Water Well Construction Practices (Section 3 above) covers it
   technically; practical DIY video content is sparse.

2. **Hiring a driller — how to evaluate bids** — the NGWA Well Owners Guide
   touches on this; a dedicated "questions to ask a driller" resource was not
   found in free form. The EPA Manual gives you enough technical vocabulary to
   ask intelligent questions.

3. **Spring box construction** — the USDA NEH Chapter 32 covers spring
   development briefly. No dedicated free guide found for the Midwest context.

4. **Berkey / gravity filter systems** — multiple homestead channels cover these
   but manufacturer documentation is marketing-heavy. NSF/ANSI 53 and 58
   certification is the standard to look for; the CDC and EPA pages above
   describe the certification system.

5. **PFAS testing** — mentioned by Iowa Extension as available through their
   grant program but not covered in depth here. PFAS in agricultural areas is
   an emerging issue (biosolids application). A dedicated PFAS-well-testing
   source should be added when this document is expanded.

6. **Midwest-specific aquifer maps** — the USGS National Water Information
   System (https://waterdata.usgs.gov) has well log data by county that shows
   typical depths to water, which is essential before choosing a drilling method.
   Not fully researched in this pass.
