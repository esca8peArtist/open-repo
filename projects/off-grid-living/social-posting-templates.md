# Off-Grid Living Guide — Social Posting Templates

Use these templates as written or adapt the angle. Each platform section has 3–4 variants with different hooks. Pick the one that fits your account's existing voice best.

GitHub URL throughout: `https://github.com/esca8peArtist/off-grid-living-guide`

---

## Reddit Templates

Reddit posts should be text posts (not link posts) where possible. The link goes at the bottom of the body, not in the title. Titles should be factual and specific — not promotional.

---

## Hacker News Templates

HN posts are a title only (URL submission) or a text post ("Ask HN" / "Show HN"). Use the Show HN format for a project release.

HN titles should be factual, specific, and avoid marketing language. The comments are the post — your job is to write a clear, honest title and then participate in the discussion.

---

**Variant A (recommended)**

Title: `Show HN: A 17-domain open-source guide to off-grid living (CC BY-SA 4.0)`

First comment (post this yourself immediately after submitting):
> This started as a research project and grew into a comprehensive reference. 17 domains covering everything from site selection and shelter through communications, security, and nuclear preparedness — each domain is a full standalone document with real cost breakdowns, regional variations, and decision matrices.

> The goal was to avoid the fragmentation problem: most off-grid resources are isolated. You find a guide for wells, another for solar, another for food preservation. They don't connect. This guide treats the systems as interconnected — your energy choices constrain your water heating, your water situation constrains food production, and so on.
>
> CC BY-SA 4.0 — completely free. Contributions welcome via GitHub issues and PRs.

**Title**: `I built a 17-domain open-source guide to off-grid living — here's how it's structured`

**Tags**: `opensource`, `productivity`, `beginners`, `community`

**Body outline**:

> **Why I built this**
> [2–3 sentences: most off-grid resources are fragmented; this treats systems as interconnected; wanted something that could be freely adapted and taught from]
>
> **What it covers**
> [List the 17 domains briefly — 2–3 words each, no need to explain every one]
>
> **How it's structured**
> [Each domain is a standalone Markdown file with YAML frontmatter, cross-references, cost tables, regional variations, and decision matrices. The repo is flat — no build process, no dependencies, just files.]
>
> **What makes it different from most guides**
> [The interconnected systems framing — energy choices cascade into water heating into food preservation into disaster resilience]
>
> **Current gaps and how to contribute**
> [Regional coverage gaps, invite PRs and issues]

> **Where to find it**
> [GitHub link, license note]
