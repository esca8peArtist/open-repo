**Analysis Date**: June 27, 2026  
**Framework Applied**: phase-2-prioritization-framework.md (Sections 1–2)

- 17 completed Phase 1 domain documents (2,100–1,100 lines each)

## Executive Summary

Phase 1 is structurally complete (17 interconnected domains, 25,000+ lines of content). The framework identifies three distinct Phase 2 expansion paths: 
- **Path A** — Depth expansion (deepen existing domains with community-identified gaps)
- **Path B** — Adjacent domains (add genuinely new 18th–20th domains)
- **Path C** — Case studies and implementation guides

Based on structural analysis of Phase 1 documents and gap patterns evident in the distribution planning materials, **Path A (depth expansion) is the recommended Phase 2 approach**. This analysis identifies the top 5 domains most likely to benefit from Phase 2 deepening, ranked by strategic fit and evidence of community demand.

## Section 1: Gap Analysis from Phase 1 Document Coverage

### 1.1 Methodology

Analyzed each of the 17 Phase 1 domains for:
1. **Scope and section coverage** — what subsections exist?
2. **Depth indicators** — are sections end-to-end or simplified?
3. **Regional variation coverage** — how many climate zones / geographies handled?
4. **Livestock/specialty content** — are niche production systems covered?
5. **Disaster integration** — does content connect to Domain 15 (Disaster Scenarios)?

### 1.2 Coverage Findings

#### **High-coverage domains** (comprehensive, well-structured, cross-linked):
- **Domain 3 (Water Systems)**: Well-structured by source type (wells, springs, rainwater). Includes drilled well depth by region, pump sizing, testing protocols, emergency reserves. Good disaster tie-in.
- **Domain 4 (Food Production)**: Caloric math foundation is explicit. Includes regional crop tables, zoning strategies, rotation planning. Livestock section present but depth limited (see 1.3 below).
- **Domain 6 (Energy & Power)**: Load calculation methodology detailed. Sizing formulas, battery types, cost comparisons. Regional solar/wind variation noted.
- **Domain 15 (Disaster Scenarios)**: Central hub; explicitly connects to all other domains with cascade effects.

#### **Medium-coverage domains** (core content present, but specialized topics simplified):
- **Domain 7 (Heating & Cooling)**: Covers wood stoves, passive solar, thermal mass. Seasonal protocols present. *Gap: limited detail on radiant floor systems, masonry heater design variations.*
- **Domain 9 (Waste & Sanitation)**: Composting toilets, greywater, septic systems covered. *Gap: depth of composting toilet design options (bucket systems vs. sawdust vs. composting vaults) is summary-level.*
- **Domain 13 (Community & Governance)**: Consensus models, mutual aid frameworks, skill inventory. *Gap: limited real-world case studies of multi-household properties or shared infrastructure.*
- **Domain 14 (Finances & Trade)**: Budgeting, income strategies, bartering frameworks. *Gap: detailed cost-of-living analysis by region; tax implications vary widely but covered only at high level.*

#### **Lower-coverage domains** (narrower scope or emerging specializations):
- **Domain 11 (Communications)**: Ham radio, GMRS, Starlink, EMP hardening covered. *Gap: decision matrix for choosing between these options is limited; integration with other systems undersolved.*
- **Domain 12 (Security & Defense)**: Threat matrices, perimeter design, OPSEC. *Gap: regional security variations (urban retreat vs. rural isolation, international contexts) are limited.*
- **Domain 16 (Skills & Knowledge)**: Tier 1–4 learning paths framework present. *Gap: specific techniques for teaching children off-grid skills; fitness protocols for off-grid labor.*

#### **Domains with identified content gaps from distribution materials**:

From the distribution campaign plan and social posting templates, the following topics appear in marketing angles but are either lightly covered or cross-domain:

| Topic | Primary domain | Coverage status | Evidence |
|-------|---|---|---|
| Small livestock (chickens, rabbits, goats, pigs) | Domain 4 | Summary in caloric tables | Distribution template: "livestock integration... mentioned as gap in community requests" |
| Permaculture design / integrated systems | Cross-domain (1, 4, 7) | Mentioned but not structured as methodology | Common off-grid community request; appears in Phase 2 framework examples |
| Rainwater legal status by state | Domain 3 | Regulations noted, but appendix per-state missing | Phase-2 framework example: "add rainwater harvesting legal status by state appendix" |
| Budget case studies ("$45K cabin build") | All domains | Not present in Phase 1 | Path C example in framework; distribution materials emphasize need |
| Regional guides beyond 2 existing | Domain 1 + all | Pacific NW and Upper Midwest regional guides exist; others missing | Distribution: "gaps: Great Plains, Gulf Coast humid subtropical, mountainous West" |

## Section 4: Path Selection and Top 3–5 Candidates

### 4.1 Recommended Expansion Path

**Primary: Path A (Depth Expansion) + Secondary: Path C (Case Studies)**

**Rationale**:
1. **Path A is optimal for Phase 2 entry** because:
   - All candidates have high strategic fit (improve existing domains, no new framework needed)
   - Urgency signals are clear (repeated in framework examples and distribution planning)
   - Effort is medium (200–600 lines per deepening), not high
   - Success signals are measurable (GitHub issues on Path A content drop to <1/month)

2. **Path C amplifies Path A** because:
   - Budget case studies are explicitly high-engagement format (3–5x engagement multiplier, framework p. 165)
   - Case studies are "distribution fuel" — shareable stories that drive organic reach
   - Low effort if sourced from community (ideal model per framework p. 163)
   - Validates content from Phase A in real-world context

3. **Path B (new domains) deferred** because:
   - No candidate has 8+ independent signals required by framework filter (p. 149)
   - Permaculture and multi-household governance are specialized, not universal
   - Better to deepen Phase 1 adoption and then re-assess demand in Month 2–3

## Section 5: Phase 2 Prioritized Candidates (Top 3–5)

### 5.1 Tier 1 — Build First (High urgency + High fit)

#### **Candidate 1: Livestock Integration Expansion (Domain 4)**

**Current state**: Domain 4 (Food Production) includes livestock in caloric tables (eggs, rabbit/poultry, 80 lb meat). No specialization by animal type.

**Phase 2 scope**: 
- Dedicated subsection: "Small Livestock Systems" (300–400 lines)
- Specifics: chickens (layer vs. meat, coop design, costs), rabbits (hutch systems, breeding cycles), goats (dairy, meat, minimum land), pigs (seasonal, feed requirements, housing)
- Real costs by animal (2026 pricing)
- Regional integration matrices (cold-climate winter housing requirements, hot-climate shade/water needs)
- Disaster impact analysis: egg production under stress, water access constraints

**Effort estimate**: 400–600 lines (30–40 hours primary research + writing)

**Success signal**: When published, expect 2–3 GitHub issues on livestock topics; if issues drop to 0–1/month within 2 months of publication, saturation confirmed.

**SEO/reach impact**: High — "raising chickens off-grid," "rabbit meat production," "goat dairy costs" are all independently searchable, long-tail terms.

---

#### **Candidate 2: Rainwater Harvesting Legal Status by State (Domain 3)**

**Current state**: Domain 3 covers rainwater collection operation (first-flush, storage, treatment). Legal status mentioned at state-policy level but not tabulated.

**Phase 2 scope**:
- Appendix: "Rainwater Harvesting Legal Status by State (2026)" 
- 50-state summary: restrictions (volume caps, catchment rules, permit requirements), exemptions (residential use, agricultural), regional patterns (Western states vs. Eastern)
- Cost of compliance (permit fees, testing)
- Workaround strategies where restrictions are severe
- Integration with Domain 1 (Site Selection) — legal status affects water design choice

**Effort estimate**: 300–400 lines (20–30 hours legal research + compilation)

**Success signal**: Direct traffic to rainwater-specific searches; GitHub issues referencing this appendix.

**SEO impact**: Very high — "rainwater harvesting laws by state" is a high-search-volume query with no comprehensive free resource.

---

#### **Candidate 3: Composting Toilet Design Depth (Domain 9)**

**Current state**: Domain 9 (Waste & Sanitation) covers composting toilets at summary level. Mentions "design options" but lists only bucket, sawdust.

**Phase 2 scope**:
- Subsection: "Composting Toilet Systems: Design Comparison" (400–500 lines)
- Options compared: bucket/portable, sawdust vault, continuous-flow compost toilet, waterless urinal + separate solid system, commercial alternatives (Nature's Head, Separett)
- Cost breakdown per system (materials, labor, maintenance cost over 10 years)
- Cold-climate adaptation (freeze tolerance, winter operation)
- Failure modes and troubleshooting
- Real examples: which system for which household size / situation
- Disaster integration: operation during power outage, water shortage

**Effort estimate**: 400–500 lines (25–35 hours research + writing)

**Success signal**: Engagement on composting toilet-specific GitHub issues; bookmark/share rate for this section.

**SEO impact**: High — "composting toilet design," "off-grid toilet options," "waterless toilet cost comparison" are strong search signals.

---

### 5.2 Tier 2 — Build Next (High urgency + High fit, lower effort)

#### **Candidate 4: Budget Case Studies (Path C — All domains)**

**Format**: Standalone case study documents, not domain expansions. Example structure:

**Case Study 1**: "Budget Off-Grid Cabin in the Pacific Northwest: $45,000 5-Year Build"
- House: 600 sq ft, site selection to finished (timeline)
- Water: spring development cost + redundancy
- Energy: 3 kW solar + 20 kWh battery (actual costs, sourcing)
- Food: first 3 years (what grew, what failed, why)
- Heating: wood stove + passive solar (efficiency data)
- Final cost breakdown by domain
- Lessons learned: what to redo, what worked perfectly

**Effort estimate**: 400–600 lines per case study (40–50 hours if self-sourced). 30–40 hours if crowd-sourced from community contributor.

**Success signal**: Social media performance. Framework states case studies outperform documentation by 3–5x on engagement (p. 165). Expected: 500–2,000 views per case study post on Reddit, 3–10x higher bookmark rate than domain docs.

**Community sourcing model** (ideal per framework p. 163): Issue a Reddit Discussion or HN "Ask HN: Share your off-grid build cost data" asking for real numbers. Compile 3–5 community responses into structured case studies with contributor credits.

**Recommended: 2–3 case studies for Phase 2 Wave 1** (Pacific Northwest, Upper Midwest cold climate, Southwest desert).

---

#### **Candidate 5: Regional Guides (Expansion)**

**Current state**: Two regional guides exist in Phase 1:
- `regional-guide-upper-midwest.md`
- `regional-guide-southwest-desert.md`

**Phase 2 scope**: Add 2–3 new regional guides per distribution gap analysis (p. 80):

1. **Great Plains** (KS, NE, SD, ND, OK, TX Panhandle)
   - Specific: wind resource assessment, drought-resistant crops, aquifer water status, extreme temperature swings
   
2. **Gulf Coast Humid Subtropical** (LA, MS, AL, coastal TX, coastal SC)
   - Specific: hurricane season protocols, mold/humidity management, crop options for humidity
   
3. **Rocky Mountain / High West** (CO, MT, WY, UT)
   - Specific: altitude-specific heating, short growing season, solar resource variation

**Effort estimate**: 600–800 lines per region (40–50 hours research + writing)

**Success signal**: Organic traffic from regional searches; new issues filed from those regions.

**SEO impact**: Very high — "off-grid living in Montana," "homestead budget Pacific Northwest" drive long-tail discovery and link-backs from regional forums.

### 6.3 Decision Gate: Go/No-Go for Phase 2

Before committing to Phase 2 Candidates 1–5, the project should confirm:

1. **Phase 1 distribution has achieved traction** (250+ stars, 10+ issues)
2. **Community interest is validated** via issue patterns and distribution channel feedback
3. **Maintenance bandwidth exists** for Phase 2 (editor has time to QA contributions, review PRs, respond to issues)

If Phase 1 publication results in <100 stars or <5 issues after 30 days across all distribution channels, **reassess distribution strategy before Phase 2 development begins**. The guide may need a README rewrite, different framing, or secondary distribution push (r/solarpunk, r/SHTF, Medium article) before Phase 2 work is justified.

## Appendix A: Comparative Benchmarking

The framework recommends checking what top 3–5 competing resources cover. This analysis identified:

**Common guide types** (and their Phase 1 analogs):
- Mother Earth News Homesteading (comprehensive multi-topic) — Phase 1 covers more depth per domain
- Homesteading Institute courses (specialized, paywalled) — Phase 1 is free, CC BY-SA; no competitive threat
- FEMA/CDC preparedness guides (disaster-focused) — Phase 1 Domains 15–17 integrate disaster content across all systems
- Reddit community wikis (crowdsourced, inconsistent quality) — Phase 1 structure is coherent, professionally edited

**Gaps in competitors that Phase 1 fills**:
- Integrated systems thinking (how energy affects water affects food) — Phase 1 unique
- Real cost data with region variation — Phase 1 comprehensive; competitors often date-stamped 5+ years old
- Off-grid focus (not just homesteading or preparedness) — Phase 1 explicit scope

**Gaps in Phase 1 that competitors cover** (Phase 2 candidates):
- Detailed livestock guides (separate specialty blogs do this well)
- State-by-state legal appendices (some state extension offices do this, fragmented)
- Cost-specific case studies (rare; when they exist, high engagement)
- Permaculture design methodology (permaculture.org and specialty guides, niche)

**Conclusion**: Phase 1 has strong competitive positioning. Phase 2 Candidates 1–5 address the most common gap pattern (depth + specialized subsystems + real examples).
