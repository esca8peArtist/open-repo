# Distribution Execution Playbook

## Relationship to Existing Toolkits

This playbook extends `social-media-execution-toolkit.md` (Reddit, HN, GitHub strategy) and `phase-2-social-media-execution-toolkit.md` (community engagement, metrics monitoring). Do not duplicate that work here. Those documents govern the Reddit/HN/GitHub layer. This playbook governs the six remaining platforms that require separate strategy: YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Substack, and Medium — plus an updated Twitter/X section informed by 2026 algorithm changes.

**Lead finding**: LinkedIn and Substack should launch before video platforms. Credibility infrastructure (professional network presence + email list) makes every subsequent video platform post more credible and provides a warm audience for cross-promotion. The sequence is: LinkedIn (Week 1) → Substack (Week 2) → Medium (Week 2) → Twitter/X threads (Week 3) → Instagram Reels (Week 4) → TikTok (Week 5) → YouTube (Week 6+).

### Content Multiplier Principle

One domain document produces approximately 6–8 pieces of content across formats:
1. LinkedIn carousel (PDF, 8–12 slides with key framework)
2. Substack newsletter section or dedicated issue
3. Medium article (800–1,200 words, SEO-optimized)
4. Twitter/X thread (6–8 tweets with key findings)
5. Instagram carousel or Reel (15–30 seconds, visual hook)
6. TikTok short (15–60 seconds, educational hook)
7. YouTube Short (under 60 seconds, discovery)
8. YouTube long-form (8–20 minutes, comprehensive walkthrough)

Not every domain justifies all eight. Prioritize the formats that match the content type. Visual and procedural domains (water, shelter, tools, food) work well for video. Systems and frameworks (governance, finances, communications) work better for LinkedIn and Substack.

## Part 3: Platform Playbooks

### 3.1 LinkedIn

**Algorithm state (May 2026)**: LinkedIn replaced its ranking system with a new AI model (360Brew) that classifies posts by "topic DNA" and distributes based on demonstrated expertise rather than network size. Organic reach dropped ~50% overall, but creators with consistent topic focus are seeing stronger niche distribution. Company pages receive approximately 5% of feed allocation vs. 65% for personal profiles — always post from personal profile.

**Best content formats**: PDF carousel posts lead with 6.6% average engagement rate. Native video follows at 5.6%. Posts with external links receive ~60% less reach — keep links in comments, not in the post body.

**Posting cadence**: 3–4 posts per week maximum. One genuinely valuable post outperforms five average ones. Quality beats frequency under the 2026 algorithm.

**Best posting times**: Tuesday through Thursday, 8 AM–12 PM local time. The algorithm tests posts with 2–5% of your network in the first 60 minutes; only 5% of posts that underperform in the first hour recover to broader distribution.

**Hashtag strategy**: 3–5 hashtags per post. Use a mix: 1 niche (#OffGridLiving, #Homesteading), 1 mid-tier (#Sustainability, #SelfSufficiency), 1 broad (#FutureOfLiving, #Resilience). Avoid overused generic tags (#Productivity, #Leadership) — they no longer drive discovery in 2026.

**Sample post template — Domain 6 (Energy)**:
> Most people size their off-grid solar system for average use.
>
> That's the mistake.
>
> Here's the framework I use instead: [3-second hook]
>
> The real number is your 3-day worst-case load during a low-sun period.
>
> Here are the 5 steps: [numbered list, 3–5 items]
>
> I documented the full calculation method (with real numbers) in our free open-source guide. Link in comments.
>
> #OffGridLiving #SolarEnergy #EnergyResilience

**PDF carousel template**: 8–12 slides. Slide 1: bold claim or surprising finding. Slides 2–9: one key point per slide, each self-contained. Slide 10: "The full domain document is free on GitHub" with the URL. Save as PDF and upload natively.

**Engagement tactic**: Reply to every comment within the first hour. Ask a genuine follow-up question in your reply. The algorithm reads comment conversations as engagement signals and extends distribution.

### 3.2 Substack

**Platform state (May 2026)**: Substack Notes has become the primary discovery channel, driving 62% of new subscribers vs. only 4% from reading newsletter posts first. Notes is the micro-content feed within Substack; think Twitter-length posts that live inside the Substack ecosystem. The Recommendations tool creates a built-in referral loop: when someone subscribes to a newsletter that recommends yours, Substack shows them a one-click subscribe option.

**Setup sequence** (do before first post):
1. Create publication. Name suggestion: "Off-Grid Systems" or "The Grid-Free Guide"
2. Write a clear "About" page: what it covers, who it's for, publishing cadence
3. Enable paid tier only after reaching 3,000–5,000 free subscribers — do not set up paid earlier
4. Post 2–3 Notes per day in the first week to seed the discovery algorithm
5. Recommend 3–5 related Substack publications (sustainability, homesteading, preparedness newsletters) immediately — this activates the Recommendations loop

**Content approach**: Each newsletter issue should be one domain deep-dive or one cross-domain framework. Not a summary — a genuine expansion beyond what's in the GitHub document. The Substack version should contain at least one original insight, case study, or calculation not in the GitHub version. This gives readers a reason to subscribe rather than just bookmarking the repo.

**Posting cadence**: Once per week, consistently. The algorithm rewards predictable cadence over volume.

**Issue structure**:
1. One-sentence summary of the issue's core finding
2. The full deep-dive (800–1,500 words)
3. "This issue builds on Domain [N] of the free off-grid guide on GitHub" (link to specific document, not the repo root)
4. One question at the end: "What aspect of [domain] do you want me to go deeper on next?"

**Cross-platform integration**: End every LinkedIn carousel and Twitter thread with "The newsletter version (with more detail) is on Substack — link in bio." This makes Substack the destination for people who want depth.

**Sample Notes strategy**: Post 2–3 short observations per day from your research process. Examples: "Interesting: FEMA's guidance on water storage assumes 1 gallon/day/person but field data from long-term off-gridders suggests 3–5 gallons is realistic." These brief posts build authority and drive profile visits that convert to free subscribers.

### 3.3 Medium

**Platform role (2026)**: Medium is primarily an SEO play. Articles take 2–6 weeks to reach peak Google visibility, then deliver passive traffic for months or years. The off-grid and homesteading keyword space has moderate search competition — articles targeting long-tail queries ("composting toilet vs. septic system off-grid," "how many solar panels for off-grid cabin") can rank on page 1 within 4–8 weeks of publication.

**Topic selection for SEO**: Target long-tail queries with clear search intent. Each article should answer one specific question a person actively types into Google.

| Domain | Recommended Article Title | Search Intent |
|--------|---------------------------|---------------|
| 06 | "How to Size a Solar System for Off-Grid Living (Real Numbers)" | Informational + transactional |
| 03 | "Off-Grid Water Filtration: What Actually Works for Long-Term Use" | Informational |
| 01 | "How to Choose Land for Off-Grid Living: 9 Criteria That Matter" | Informational |
| 09 | "Composting Toilet vs. Septic: What Off-Grid Homesteaders Actually Choose" | Comparison |
| 17 | "Nuclear Preparedness: What FEMA and NCRP Actually Recommend" | Informational (high demand) |
| 13 | "Governance Models for Off-Grid Communities" | Informational (low competition) |

**Article structure**: 800–1,200 words. Lead with the answer (inverted pyramid). Include one data point or calculation from the domain document. End with: "This article is based on Domain [N] of the Off-Grid Living Guide, a free open-source project on GitHub [link]." The link is low-PageRank compared to external site links, but the GitHub attribution builds discoverability and is honest.

**Publication strategy**: Submit articles to relevant Medium publications (The Startup, The Ascent, Predict) after initial posting. Being accepted by a publication with 100K+ followers multiplies initial distribution by 5–10x. The off-grid / sustainability angle fits The Ascent (personal finance + lifestyle angle) and One Zero (technology + society).

**Cadence**: One article per week for the first 8 weeks. After that, one article per 2 weeks is sufficient to maintain SEO momentum.

### 3.4 Twitter/X

**Algorithm state (May 2026)**: The algorithm explicitly boosts threads, giving them more impressions per tweet than standalone posts. Text-first content outperforms video by 30% on this platform. Replies are the highest-value engagement signal (worth 150x likes in the algorithm's ranking). External links in the main tweet body significantly reduce reach — place all links in reply tweets.

**Thread format**: 4–7 tweets is the engagement sweet spot (35% higher per-tweet engagement than standalone posts or longer threads). Start with a strong hook tweet that works as a standalone claim. Each subsequent tweet should be self-contained.

**Best times**: Wednesday 9 AM is the single highest-engagement slot. Secondary: Tuesday–Thursday 12–6 PM local time.

**Posting cadence**: Daily posting drives 40% higher average engagement per tweet. Minimum 3x per week for meaningful presence. Post a thread once per week on a specific domain; post shorter standalone observations on non-thread days.

**Link strategy**: Never put the GitHub link in the main tweet of a thread. Post the thread (tweets 1–7), then reply to your own thread with: "Full document: [GitHub link]. Free, CC BY-SA 4.0." This preserves thread reach while still capturing clicks.

**Sample thread structure — Domain 17 (Nuclear)**:
> Tweet 1: "Most people's nuclear preparedness plan is wrong. Here's what the NCRP (the authority on this) actually recommends: [thread]"
> Tweet 2: "First: distance beats sheltering in most scenarios. The NCRP recommends evacuation if you're within X miles of a detonation and have 15+ minutes warning."
> Tweet 3-6: One key finding each, sourced to NCRP/FEMA
> Tweet 7: "I documented the full protocol in Domain 17 of the Off-Grid Living Guide (see reply for link)"

**Hashtag strategy on X**: 1–2 hashtags maximum. X's algorithm treats heavy hashtag use as spam in 2026. Use #OffGridLiving and either #Preparedness or #Homesteading depending on the domain.

**Community engagement**: Reply substantively to 5–10 posts per day in the off-grid, homesteading, and preparedness niches. Replies from non-followers are how new people discover your profile. "Hot takes" and contrarian observations consistently outperform informational tweets in the first-hour engagement window.

### 3.5 Instagram

**Algorithm state (May 2026)**: The number-one ranking signal is "sends per reach" — how often people DM your post to a friend. Create content specifically designed to make people think "I need to send this to someone." Reels with strong 3-second hold rates (above 60%) outperform weak-hold Reels by 5–10x in total reach. The algorithm now pushes Reels aggressively to non-followers; for new accounts, Reels are the primary discovery mechanism.

**Content mix**: 3–4 Reels per week, 2–3 carousels, 1–2 static posts per week for optimal algorithm engagement and community depth.

**Best times**: 8 AM–12 PM Monday–Thursday for Reels. Wednesday and Thursday have the strongest overall performance. Evening posting (6–9 PM) also performs well for lifestyle niches.

**Reel format for off-grid content**:
- 15–30 seconds optimal for discovery; 60–90 seconds for depth
- Hook in the first 3 seconds: show the end result first, then the process
- Text overlay for silent viewing (majority of viewers watch without sound)
- Call to action in caption: "Save this for when you're planning your water system"

**Carousel format**: Carousel posts drive saves and shares more than Reels. Use for frameworks and decision matrices: "9 questions to ask before buying land for off-grid living" (one question per slide). Final slide: "Full guide is free on GitHub — link in bio."

**Hashtag strategy**: 5–10 hashtags per post using the pyramid approach: 2–3 niche (#OffGridHomestead, #SolarCabin), 3–4 mid-tier (#OffGridLiving, #SustainableLiving), 1–2 broad (#Homesteading, #SelfSufficiency). Caption keywords matter more than hashtags for Instagram Search in 2026 — write naturally for the search terms your audience uses.

**Sample Reel concept — Domain 3 (Water)**:
> Hook (0–3s): Show a clear glass of water being filled from a gravity filter
> Middle (3–20s): Split-screen showing 4 filtration methods with pros/cons text overlay
> End (20–30s): Text card: "Full water systems guide — free on GitHub (link in bio)"
> Caption: "The filter you choose depends on your water source. Here's the decision framework from our off-grid water guide. Save for your build planning."

### 3.6 TikTok

**Algorithm state (May 2026)**: TikTok now uses account-level niche consistency to build an "authority score" — posting consistently about one topic causes the algorithm to distribute your content to users who have demonstrated interest in that topic. Video completion rate is the single most important ranking signal. Caption keywords (not just hashtags) feed directly into TikTok's search index.

**Niche positioning**: Establish the account as an "off-grid systems" account, not a lifestyle vlog. The systems and practical-how-to angle distinguishes from the crowded lifestyle homesteading content and attracts the preparedness overlap audience.

**Hashtag pyramid (3–6 hashtags per post)**:
- Niche (under 50M views): #OffGridHomestead, #OffGridBuild, #SolarOffGrid
- Mid-tier (50M–500M): #OffGridLiving, #Homesteading, #SelfSufficiency
- Broad (500M+): #Survival, #Sustainability

**Caption keywords**: Write the caption as if answering a search query. Example: "How to size a solar system for off-grid living — the calculation that most guides skip."

**Posting cadence**: 3–5 times per week for meaningful growth. Daily posting drives niche authority acceleration. Post at 6–10 PM local time for maximum initial velocity.

**Video format**: Start with a genuine hook that promises a specific outcome ("Watch this before you buy your first solar panel"). Under 60 seconds for discovery videos; under 3 minutes for deep-dive how-to content. Use text overlays throughout — TikTok's own analytics show 65%+ of views are sound-off.

**Sample TikTok concepts by domain**:
- Domain 05: "I preserved food for 3 years without electricity — here's every method I used" (storytelling hook)
- Domain 06: "The solar sizing mistake that almost cost me $3,000" (loss-aversion hook)
- Domain 10: "5 tools every off-grid homestead actually needs vs. what YouTube tells you" (contrarian hook)
- Domain 16: "Skills I wish I had before going off-grid — honest tier list" (tier list format)

**Trending audio**: Use TikTok's "Trending" tab to find audio that fits your niche. Instructional content works well with minimalist music (no lyrics). News and informational content sometimes pairs well with trending ambient sounds. Check weekly — trending audio changes every 5–7 days.

### 3.7 YouTube

**Algorithm state (May 2026)**: YouTube is in an aggressive new-creator support phase. The algorithm now tests videos with small audiences first, caring more about viewer response than subscriber count or upload history. Satisfaction signals now outweigh watch time as the primary ranking factor. Creators posting both Shorts and long-form videos grow subscriber bases 3x faster than single-format creators. Shorts generate up to 40% more channel discovery.

**Launch sequence**: Do not launch YouTube until Week 6+. The platform requires the highest production investment. By Week 6, LinkedIn, Substack, and Instagram have created a warm audience that will seed early YouTube engagement — which matters enormously for algorithmic distribution.

**Content strategy**: Lead with the highest-search-volume topics. Domain 06 (Energy/Solar) and Domain 01 (Site Selection) are the two highest-search-volume topics in the niche. Start there before producing videos on lower-search topics.

**Long-form video formats**:
- "Complete guide to [domain]" walkthrough: 12–20 minutes, structured like a mini-course
- "Real numbers: [domain]" cost/spec analysis: 8–15 minutes, data-heavy
- Case study format: "Here's what a real off-grid system looks like, by the numbers"
- Q&A format: pull top questions from Reddit and GitHub issues and answer on camera

**YouTube Shorts strategy**: Produce 2–3 Shorts per week as clips from long-form videos or standalone tips. Shorts and long-form algorithms are fully decoupled in 2026 — optimize each separately. A Short is not a preview of a long video; it should stand alone as complete content.

**Playlist structure**: Build playlists by domain from Day 1. Viewers who watch multiple videos in a series signal strong interest; the algorithm learns this pattern and recommends the series more broadly. Suggested playlists: "Off-Grid Energy Systems," "Water and Sanitation," "Food Production," "Disaster Preparedness."

**Upload cadence**: One long-form video per week plus 2–3 Shorts. Consistency matters more than frequency — a predictable once-per-week schedule outperforms sporadic bursts.

**SEO for YouTube**: Video titles should include the exact search phrase the audience would type. "How to Size a Solar System for Off-Grid Living" beats "Domain 6: Energy and Power." Descriptions should include the GitHub URL, timestamps, and 200+ words of keyword-rich content.

**Week 5**
- Launch TikTok. Post first 5 videos in the first week to seed the niche authority signal. Domains 05, 10, 16 work best for initial TikTok content (visual, how-to format). Post daily for first 2 weeks.

**Week 6**
- Launch YouTube. Publish first long-form video (Domain 06: solar sizing complete walkthrough). Publish 2 Shorts (cut from the long-form).

**Weeks 7–8**

**Milestone check at end of Week 8**: YouTube channel launched with 2+ videos, TikTok 200+ followers, Instagram 150+ followers, Substack 300+ subscribers, Medium 3 articles indexed and receiving organic traffic.

### Weeks 9–12: Cross-Platform Flywheel

**Weeks 9–10**
- All platforms now active. Focus shifts from launching to optimizing based on data.
- Identify best-performing platform by engagement rate and time invested. Allocate an extra 30% of time to that platform.
- Submit Medium articles to appropriate publications (The Ascent, One Zero) if not already done.
- Begin newsletter pitch to external newsletters (Homestead Honey, Prepared Not Scared) per existing toolkit. Use GitHub star count and Substack subscriber count as social proof.
- YouTube: third long-form video. Begin building series structure with playlists.

**Weeks 11–12**
- Conduct first cross-platform referral campaign: "This week I'm going deep on Domain [N]. Full thread on Twitter, carousel on LinkedIn, newsletter on Substack, video on YouTube. Start with whichever format you prefer."
- Review 90-day metrics (see Part 5).
- Decide Phase 3 scope: Which platforms justify increased investment? Which domains should get expanded content treatment?
- Begin planning first "community content" piece: a synthesis post that directly responds to the most common questions received across all platforms.
