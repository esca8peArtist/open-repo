# Off-Grid Living: A Comprehensive Guide

A complete, practical guide to designing, building, and maintaining an off-grid, self-sufficient homestead. This guide covers everything from site selection through disaster preparedness — with real costs, sourcing information, skill requirements, and actionable protocols for every major domain of self-sufficient life.

## Who This Is For

- **Homesteaders** planning a transition to off-grid living
- **Preppers** building resilience and redundancy
- **Farmers and ranchers** interested in energy independence and sustainability
- **Communities** organizing mutual aid and collective resilience
- **Anyone** seeking to understand the full scope of off-grid operations

## How to Use This Guide

### Start Here
- **New to off-grid living?** Read **Site Selection** first, then **Shelter**, then **Water**. These three domains form the foundation.
- **Already have land?** Jump to the domain most relevant to your immediate need.
- **Planning a specific system?** Each domain has a full technical breakdown with costs and specifications.

### Navigation
- Each file has a `cross-refs` field (in the front matter) listing related domains.
- Follow cross-references when you need deeper context on a related topic.
- **Master Outline** (`master-outline.md`) has additional project scope, development roadmap, and domain summaries.

### Implementation
- Most domains include:
  - **Practical specifications** — actual dimensions, materials, wiring diagrams
  - **Cost breakdowns** — material and labor costs for common configurations
  - **Regional variations** — climate-specific strategies (cold, hot/arid, Pacific NW, Appalachia)
  - **Decision matrices** — how to choose between options
  - **Sourcing information** — where to find supplies, tools, and services
  - **Skill requirements** — what you need to know to do it yourself

### Disaster Sections
- **Disaster Scenarios** (Domain 15) connects all other domains to specific disaster types.
- Use this to understand cascade effects and identify critical priorities.
- Contains decision matrices for different scenarios (power outage, pandemic, civil unrest, nuclear).

## Key Features

✅ **Evidence-based** — all specifications, costs, and protocols are sourced from real systems, government guidelines, and field experience

✅ **Actionable** — not theoretical. Every section explains how to actually do it, with material lists and cost estimates

✅ **Interconnected** — understand how systems interact (e.g., energy choices affect water heating, affect food preservation, affect medical capacity)

✅ **Disaster-ready** — every domain includes protocols for grid-down, no-resupply scenarios

✅ **Community-focused** — governance, skill-sharing, and mutual aid are integrated throughout
