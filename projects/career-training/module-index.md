# Career Training Module Index — Reading Order by Career Path

> **How to use this document.** Three reading paths are defined below — Residential GC, Industrial GC, and Specialty Subcontractor to PM. Each path lists modules in recommended reading order with estimated hours, prerequisites, and cross-references to the most relevant case-study scenarios. Start with the path closest to your current role or target role; do not read all 33 modules front to back.
>
> **Curriculum totals**: 33 modules (~336,000 words), 1 case-study workbook (150 scenarios, ~121,000 words). All modules carry `status: reference`, created May 2026.

## Path 1: Residential GC (Starting to Growth)

**Target student**: A tradesperson going independent, a construction manager starting their first GC firm, or an experienced laborer stepping into a supervisory-and-ownership role. California-specific throughout.

**Total estimated time**: 120–140 hours over 14–16 weeks at 8–10 hours/week.

---

### Phase 1 — Prerequisites: How the Industry Works (Weeks 1–4, ~35 hours)

Read these four modules before anything else. They establish the mental model for all later material: how drawings work, what a GC legally owns, how California codes are structured, and how sites are set up. Do not skip ahead into technical or business modules before completing this phase.

| Order | Module | File | Est. Hours | Key Topics | Case-Study Scenarios |
|------:|--------|------|:----------:|------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Architecture for the GC | `06-architecture-for-contractors.md` | 8 hrs | Drawing sets, AIA phases, RFI/submittal process, coordination gaps | Scenarios 6.x — drawing conflict and RFI disputes |
| 2 | Residential GC: Scope, Trades, and People Management | `07-residential-gc-scope-and-management.md` | 10 hrs | GC role, trade sequencing, inspection discipline, checklists | Scenarios 7.x — sub performance, sequencing failures |
| 3 | California Building Codes, Permits, and Inspections | `09-codes-permits-inspections-california.md` | 9 hrs | Title 24, CRC, CBC, CalGreen, permit workflow, inspections | Scenarios 9.x — permit and inspection disputes |

## Path 3: Specialty Subcontractor to Foreman to PM

**Target student**: An electrician, plumber, framer, mechanical technician, or civil worker moving toward a foreman or project manager role. Goal: learn everything that surrounds the trade — contracts, schedule logic, other trades' work, business infrastructure — without re-learning the trade itself.

**Total estimated time**: 112–130 hours over 12–14 weeks.

---

### Phase 1 — Prerequisites: Reading the Full Job (Weeks 1–2, ~15 hours)

The single most common gap for tradespeople moving to foreman: they can read their own sheets and none of the others. Fix this first.

| Order | Module | File | Est. Hours | Key Topics | Case-Study Scenarios |
|------:|--------|------|:----------:|------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Architecture for the GC | `06-architecture-for-contractors.md` | 7 hrs | Drawing conventions, plan types, detail reading, coordination drawings | Scenarios 6.x — drawing and coordination disputes |
| 2 | Reading and Interpreting Construction Specifications | `24-reading-construction-specifications.md` | 8 hrs | Spec sections for your trade, submittal requirements, quality standards, RFI process | Scenarios 24.x — spec interpretation for sub scope |

*Phase complete when*: You can pick up an unfamiliar full drawing set and read from cover sheet to detail, not just your trade sheets.

| Module | Regulatory Reference | Review Cadence |
|--------|---------------------|----------------|
| 09 | California Title 24 (energy code) | Triennial — 2025 code cycle in effect Jan 2026 |

| 22 | WUI zone maps, CALFIRE zone designations | As updated |
