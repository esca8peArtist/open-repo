# Career Training Module Index & Study Guide

## Overview

This document provides a master index of all 28 career-training modules covering industrial and residential general contracting. Use this as your entry point to the curriculum — select your career path, follow the recommended reading order, and dive into individual modules.

## Recommended Reading Paths

### Path A: Industrial General Contractor (8-10 modules, 40-50 hours)

**Phase 1: Industrial GC Foundations (12-16 hours)**
1. Module 01 — Industrial GC Foundations: Role, Contracts, and Estimating
2. Module 04 — Codes, Regulations, Business, and Career Path (codes section focus)
3. Module 12 — Setting Up a GC Business in California

**Phase 2: Technical Execution (12-16 hours)**
4. Module 02 — Field Execution of Mechanical and Electrical Work
5. Module 03 — Industrial Construction PM, Scheduling, and Safety
6. Module 06 — Architecture for the GC (drawing reading fundamentals)

**Phase 3: Supporting Disciplines (8-12 hours)**
7. Module 05 — Civil Engineering for the GC (if brownfield site work involved)
8. Module 13 — Construction Law and Lien Rights

**Advanced (Optional, 4-8 hours)**
- Module 24 — Reading and Interpreting Specifications
- Module 17 — Construction Technology and Software
- Module 29 — Dispute Resolution, Claims, and Schedule Delay Management *(strongly recommended for public works GCs)*
- Module 31 — Prevailing Wage and Federal Labor Compliance *(required if bidding public works)*
- Module 33 — Quality Assurance and Quality Control Procedures

**Estimated time**: 40–50 hours over 6–8 weeks

## Identified Coverage Gaps and Opportunities for Module Development

### Gap 1: Dispute Resolution, Claims, and Schedule Delay Management
**Current coverage**: Module 13 introduces lien rights and dispute resolution, but lacks procedural detail.

**Proposed scope**: 
- Claim documentation and procedures (daily reports, delay impacts, costs)
- Schedule analysis and CPM for delay claims
- Constructive acceleration and time-impact analysis
- Contractor vs. owner-caused delays (risk allocation)
- Mediation and arbitration procedures
- Recovery strategies and negotiation tactics

**Estimated effort**: 2,000–2,500 words, 3–4 hours reference value

**Audience**: GCs seeking to minimize claim losses and manage disputes

---

### Gap 2: Subcontractor Management and Trade Coordination
**Current coverage**: Modules 07, 03 discuss coordination but lack systematic management procedures.

**Proposed scope**:
- Pre-qualification and vetting procedures
- Subcontract terms and flow-down requirements
- Pay application review and lien waiver administration
- Progress tracking and performance management
- Coordination meetings and communication protocols
- Performance bonds and defaults

**Estimated effort**: 2,500–3,000 words, 4–5 hours reference value

**Audience**: Residential and industrial GCs managing complex multi-trade projects

---

### Gap 3: Prevailing Wage and Federal Labor Compliance
**Current coverage**: Module 14 covers Cal/OSHA but not prevailing wage (Davis-Bacon, California Prevailing Wage Act).

**Proposed scope**:
- Davis-Bacon (federal projects) requirements and documentation
- California Prevailing Wage Act (state projects, local jurisdictions)
- Certified payroll and wage determinations
- Penalties and audit procedures
- Project eligibility and reporting

**Estimated effort**: 1,500–2,000 words, 2–3 hours reference value

**Audience**: GCs bidding public works (schools, roads, utilities, federal contracts)

---

### Gap 4: Change Order Management and Value Engineering
**Current coverage**: Modules 01, 08 touch on change orders and cost control; Module 20 discusses design-build, which includes value engineering, but lacks systematic procedures.

**Proposed scope**:
- Change order triggers and procedures
- Cost impact analysis and pricing strategies
- Negotiation tactics and documentation
- Change order forms and administration
- Value engineering methodology (cost reduction without scope loss)
- Design alternatives and trade-off analysis

**Estimated effort**: 2,000–2,500 words, 3–4 hours reference value

**Audience**: GCs seeking to manage scope creep, improve margins, and maintain owner relationships

---

### Gap 5: Quality Assurance and Quality Control Procedures
**Current coverage**: Modules 07, 16, 23 mention QC; Module 24 discusses specifications, but lacks QC systematic procedures.

**Proposed scope**:
- QC program structure (inspections, testing, documentation)
- Inspection protocols by trade (framing, electrical, plumbing, concrete, etc.)
- Testing requirements and acceptance criteria
- Non-conformance procedures and corrective actions
- Final inspection and punch-list management
- Warranty claims and customer communication

**Estimated effort**: 2,500–3,000 words, 4–5 hours reference value

**Audience**: GCs seeking to reduce warranty claims and improve project quality

## Recommendations for Study Path Optimization

### For First-Time Industrial GC (No residential background)
**Start with**: Modules 01 → 04 → 12 (4–5 weeks)
**Then**: Modules 02 → 03 → 06 (4–5 weeks)
**Optional advanced**: Proposed modules on Subcontractor Management, Disputes, and QC.

### For Residential GC Expanding to Industrial
**Start with**: Current industrial modules (01–04), then apply to industrial projects.
**Focus**: Modules 02, 03, 04 (field execution, PM, codes).

### For Residential GC Seeking to Specialize
**Choose one path**:
- **Solar specialist**: 27 + 18 + 22 + 11
- **Renovation specialist**: 16 + 10 + 15 (if ADU renovation market)
- **Luxury homes**: 20 + 23
- **Hillside/difficult sites**: 26 + 10 + 05

### For Project Managers and Superintendents
**Curriculum**: 07, 03, 21, 24, and proposed Subcontractor Management, QC, and Disputes modules.
