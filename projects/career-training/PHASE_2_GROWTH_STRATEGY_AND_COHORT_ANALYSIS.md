# Phase 2 Growth Strategy and Cohort Analysis

**Starting condition**: GitHub Pages site live (July 2026). Jekyll curriculum published. 38 modules, 150-scenario workbook. Kit.com email infrastructure active. Zero subscribers at launch.

**Goal**: 2,000 active learners (defined as email subscribers who have opened at least one email in the prior 30 days) by December 31, 2026.

**Confidence level**: 75%. Strategy is grounded in SaaS education precedents and documented channel performance data. The 2,000-learner target is achievable but requires consistent execution across at least two acquisition channels in parallel. Single-channel execution (LinkedIn only, or partnership only) will likely reach 600-900 by year-end.

---

## Most Important Finding

Partnership outreach to construction associations and community colleges is the highest-ROI subscriber acquisition channel available for this curriculum. A single confirmed newsletter mention from a state-level construction association (AGC California, CFCA, NARI) typically produces 50-200 new subscribers from a single send. At current lead-magnet conversion rates (3-5% of visitors), you would need 4,000-6,700 organic site visitors to produce the same result. Begin partnership outreach in Week 3, before the LinkedIn content library is fully built.

## Cohort Segmentation: Three Primary Personas

Phase 2 operates with three learner personas. Messaging, email cadence, and revenue conversion strategy differ substantially across all three.

### Persona 1: The Learner

**Who they are**: Individual construction professionals — specialty subs, field workers, aspiring GCs — using the curriculum to advance their career. They are 25-45 years old, likely working in the field or at a mid-tier construction firm. Many are California-based (curriculum is California-weighted for CSLB licensing, Title 24, Cal/OSHA).

**What they want**: Practical, immediately applicable knowledge. They are not academic learners. They want to know what to do when a lien waiver dispute arises, not the academic theory of lien rights. The 150-scenario workbook is their primary entry point.

**Email list share**: 60-70% of total subscribers.

**Conversion path**: Free module reading → email signup → welcome sequence → module completion → case study engagement → paid certification (Phase 3+).

**Failure mode**: Passive consumption. A learner who reads one module and never returns is not a "learner" in any meaningful sense. The activation threshold is five modules completed (see Module Completion Patterns below).

**Email signup conversion from site visit**: 5-8% of unique visitors (above average for education sites; this audience visits with intent rather than casual curiosity, which improves conversion).

### Persona 2: The Instructor

**Who they are**: Community college faculty, union training coordinators, vocational school instructors, and independent construction educators who want to use the curriculum to teach a structured course. Typically 35-55 years old. They evaluate content for quality and legal/regulatory accuracy, not entertainment value.

**What they want**: Curriculum structure (syllabi, module maps, assessment rubrics), legal accuracy, and instructor guides. They do not want another platform — they want content they can use inside their existing teaching workflow.

**Email list share**: 5-10% of total subscribers, but 30-40% of revenue potential. An instructor who assigns the curriculum to 20 students and pays a site license is worth 20x a single learner subscription.

**Conversion path**: Site discovery → instructor resources page → email signup (instructor-specific form with `instructor` tag) → instructor monthly digest → license purchase or formal partnership.

**Revenue implication**: Instructor licensing is the primary B2B revenue path. Target pricing: $150-500/year per instructor for full module access and assessment rubrics. This is priced below LinkedIn Learning institutional pricing ($299-500/seat/year) while offering more construction-specific depth.

**Failure mode**: Instructors who visit once, find no instructor-specific resources, and leave without signing up. The fix is to create an instructor landing page before Phase 2 launch — a dedicated page at `/for-instructors/` that speaks directly to their use case and includes a path-tagged signup form.

### Persona 3: The Contractor (Business-Focused Learner)

**Who they are**: GC business owners and project managers who want to improve their company's systems, compliance posture, or team capabilities. They are using the curriculum organizationally, not just personally. California-licensed GCs with 5-50 employee companies are the primary sub-segment.

**What they want**: Compliance tools (CSLB, Cal/OSHA, lien rights) and ready-to-use systems (scheduling templates, change order procedures, subcontract frameworks). They have money but limited time. They will pay for convenience and organization.

**Email list share**: 10-20% of subscribers.

**Conversion path**: LinkedIn post referral or association mention → site → module browse → email signup → digest → bulk module access or team subscription inquiry.

**Revenue implication**: Contractors are the pay-per-module or bulk-license purchasers. A contractor who buys modules for 3 employees at $15/module equivalent generates $45-135 per module cluster. This is the segment most likely to pay for live instructor sessions when that feature launches.

**Failure mode**: They sign up, receive the welcome sequence, and never return because the content is not directly tied to an action they need to take this week. The fix is to make Module 9 (California Codes and Permits) and Module 13 (Lien Rights) the prominently-marketed entry modules for this persona.

## Module Completion Patterns: Evidence-Based Estimates

No platform publishes a single "modules completed per user per year" metric, but the following data points allow calibrated estimates.

**LinkedIn Learning**: The platform reports users watch an average of 52 minutes of content per week, and their AI-personalized paths drove a 42% higher completion rate than unguided browsing. The median LinkedIn Learning user (enterprise account) completes 2-4 courses per year based on observable badge and certificate data. For self-directed free access platforms, completion rates are lower.

**MOOCs and self-paced courses**: Academic research on MOOC completion rates consistently finds 10-15% of enrolled learners complete full courses. Self-paced paid courses average 10-20% full completion. The dropout funnel is front-weighted: most attrition occurs before the second module.

**O'Reilly platform**: Structured learning paths (linear consumption) account for 55% of usage; self-directed browsing accounts for the remaining 45%. Certificate seekers — learners with an explicit credential goal — complete 8-12 modules per engagement period based on Ruzuku platform analysis of completion gap research.

**Estimates for this curriculum**:

| Milestone | Estimated % of Signups Who Reach It | What it Means |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|---------------|
| 1 module completed | 35-50% | Low-friction entry. Reached by anyone who clicks a module from the welcome email. |
| 5 modules completed | 12-20% | Commitment threshold. This learner has chosen a path and is using it. |
| 15+ modules completed | 4-8% | Invested learner. Likely to complete their path, recommend to peers, and convert to paid products. |
| All 38 modules | Under 2% | Completionist. Rare; valuable for case studies and testimonials. |

**Practical implication**: The email sequence must get subscribers to module 1 within 7 days of signup. The Day 4 case study email (existing design) is the right mechanism — it creates a pull that makes clicking a module the path of least resistance. First-week module engagement is the single best predictor of 90-day retention.

## Revenue Model: Freemium + Certification + Instructor Licensing

### Phase 2 Revenue Structure (July-December 2026)

Phase 2 does not require paid products at launch. The first 90 days are pure audience-building. Revenue activation begins at 300-500 subscribers when there is enough of an audience to sell to and enough signal about what they want.

**Freemium tier (always free)**: All 38 modules, the case study workbook, and all learning path guides remain free. This is the moat — free access is what generates the word-of-mouth and association endorsements that drive subscriber growth.

**Revenue tier 1 — Certification track** (target launch: Month 4-5, ~300 subscribers):
- 1-week intensive + proctored exam + digital certificate
- Recommended price point: **$97-127** per certification track
- Rationale: Google Career Certificates are $49/month; vocational self-paced courses run $99-$299; the sweet spot for a niche professional credential is under $150 (impulse-buy range) but above $75 (credibility threshold). The $97 price point has the strongest conversion precedent in the $50-$150 vocational range.
- Format: Asynchronous self-paced intensive (5-7 modules completed in sequence with end-of-module checks) + a 60-minute online proctored exam (administered via a low-cost service like Honorlock or ProctorFree, ~$5-15/exam to deliver)
- Net margin at $97: approximately 85% after delivery costs
- Target: 50-100 certifications by December 2026 = $4,850-$12,700 gross revenue

**Revenue tier 2 — Instructor live sessions** (target launch: Month 5-6, when 5+ active instructors identified):
- 90-minute live video session with a subject-matter expert (sourced from instructor persona subscribers who have identified themselves)
- Recommended price: $35-75 per session
- Format: Group session (10-30 learners per session caps keep quality high; 10-learner minimum required for session to run)
- This requires finding 3-5 instructors who want to teach. The instructor discovery funnel (email tag `instructor` + monthly instructor digest) surfaces them organically.

**Revenue tier 3 — Bulk/institutional licenses** (target launch: Month 6+):
- Community college departments, union training programs, contractor companies
- Price: $150-500/year per instructor seat (gives access to assessment rubrics, editable syllabi, instructor guide)
- This is the highest-margin revenue line but requires a sales conversation, not just a purchase button

**Monthly subscription model (deferred)**: Not recommended for Phase 2. Subscriptions require a continuous content production commitment (new content monthly) that conflicts with the existing static-site model. The freemium + certification + institutional license model generates higher ARPU from a smaller audience without ongoing content creation pressure.

**Pay-per-module model (deferred)**: The entire curriculum is currently free. Paywalling individual modules would reduce the size of the audience available for certification upsells. The free-module-to-paid-certification funnel has better math than a pay-per-module model at this audience size.

## Instructor Revenue Sharing Model

### Platform Comparison

| Platform | Instructor % | How It Works | Notes |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Skillshare | ~30% of revenue pool | Share of watchtime minutes; pool-based | Changed from per-minute flat rate to revenue share in Jan 2024 |
| Udemy (marketplace) | 37% when Udemy drives sale; 97% when instructor drives sale | Per-transaction; instructors who market their own courses keep nearly all | Subscription pool: dropped to 15% of revenue pool by Jan 2026 |
| Teachable | 80-95% | Creator keeps revenue minus transaction fees; Teachable takes 0-5% fee depending on plan | Highest payout; creator owns the customer |
| Kajabi | ~95% | Creator keeps all revenue minus payment processing (2.7-2.9% + $0.30) | No platform fee; highest margin but $149+/month platform cost |

**Recommended model for this platform**: A hybrid of the Teachable and Udemy "instructor-drives-sale" structures. Because the curriculum is the platform and instructors would be providing live sessions to an already-existing email list audience:

- Instructor-sourced certification cohort (instructor recruited the students from their own network): **instructor keeps 75%**
- Platform-sourced certification cohort (students from the email list; instructor provides only labor): **instructor keeps 40-50%**
- Live session (group Q&A): **instructor keeps 60%**, platform keeps 40% (covers the booking infrastructure and email promotion)

**Rationale**: The Teachable model (80%+) makes sense when the instructor owns the audience. Here, the platform owns the email list, which is where most learners will come from. A 40-50% platform share is justified when the platform is sourcing the students. As the instructor builds their own following within the curriculum audience, their split can be renegotiated upward.

**Failure trigger**: If fewer than 3 instructors are active by Month 6, the instructor session model should be deprioritized. The operator can self-host sessions until the instructor pipeline develops.

## Growth Channels: Prioritization and Projections

### Channel Ranking by Expected ROI (Months 1-9)

| Channel | Expected Subscribers | Cost | Time to First Result | Priority |
|---------|---------------------|------|---------------------|----------|
| Partnership outreach (associations, community colleges) | 300-800 | Low (time cost only) | 4-8 weeks | 1 |
| LinkedIn organic (personal profile, 3x/week posting) | 200-500 | Low-medium ($15/mo Buffer + time) | 6-12 weeks | 2 |
| Reddit organic (r/Construction, r/generalcontractor) | 100-300 | Very low | 2-4 weeks per post | 3 |
| Email referral loop (subscriber shares case studies) | 150-400 | Zero | Grows with list size | 4 |
| YouTube (Module overview videos) | 100-300 | Medium (time + equipment) | 3-6 months | 5 |
| Paid Google/LinkedIn Ads | 200-600 | $300-600 total | Immediate | 6 |

**SEO note**: GitHub Pages sites on new domains (including custom domains pointed to GitHub Pages) typically take 3-6 months to begin ranking for target keywords. For this curriculum, the most likely ranking keywords — "California GC license preparation," "construction PM training," "CSLB license modules" — face competition from established training providers. Organic search will not be a meaningful traffic driver before Month 4-5. Do not invest in SEO optimization at the expense of partnership outreach in Phase 2.

**Paid acquisition note**: Google Ads CPC for construction training keywords runs $2-15 depending on specificity. "HVAC certification" and "electrician courses" are $5-15. Niche terms like "California GC pre-licensing training" or "construction PM certification" are $2-5 (lower competition). A $300 monthly Google Ads budget targeting 3-5 niche keywords could produce 60-150 clicks/month; at a 5-8% email signup rate, that is 3-12 new subscribers per $300 spent. Cost-per-subscriber from paid ads: $25-100. Partnership outreach cost-per-subscriber: effectively $0-2 per subscriber (time only). Run paid ads only after organic channels are producing data about what messaging converts.

### Path to 2,000 Subscribers by December 31, 2026

**Month 1-2 (July-August)**: Infrastructure live, outreach begins.
- Target: 75-150 subscribers
- Primary channels: LinkedIn posting begins, partnership outreach emails sent (10/week)
- First partnership confirmation expected by end of Month 2

**Month 3-4 (September-October)**: Channel amplification.
- Target: 400-700 total subscribers
- Partnership mentions produce spike traffic; Reddit seeding ongoing
- YouTube decision made at Month 3 gate (proceed if email list > 200 and LinkedIn consistent)
- Certification track launched when list reaches 300

**Month 5-6 (November-December)**: Scale.
- Target: 1,000-1,500 total subscribers
- Email referral loop active (subscriber base large enough to produce meaningful share activity)
- Instructor live sessions launched
- Paid LinkedIn post boost ($100/month) if organic growth is below target

**Month 7-9 stretch (if launch was July 2026, full 9 months through end of March 2027)**: The 2,000-subscriber target is more realistic over 9 full months than by December. If Phase 1 launches in July 2026, December 2026 is only 5-6 months of growth. The December 2026 realistic target is 1,200-1,800 subscribers with strong channel execution.

**Revised target recommendation**: Set the December 2026 checkpoint at 1,000-1,500 subscribers and the end-of-Q1-2027 target at 2,000. This is realistic without paid acquisition; achieving 2,000 by December requires either a major partnership win (AGC California newsletter, a community college program adoption) or paid acquisition acceleration starting Month 3.

## Failure Triggers and Escalation Protocol

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Escalate |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Week 8 subscriber count | 100+ | 50-99 | Below 50 |
| Monthly subscriber growth rate | 30%+ | 15-29% | Below 15% |
| Day 7 email open rate | 40%+ | 30-39% | Below 30% |
| Module click rate from email | 20%+ | 10-19% | Below 10% |
| Partnership responses (Month 2) | 3-5 active conversations | 1-2 | 0 |

**If Week 8 subscribers < 50**: Problem is site traffic, not email conversion. Double LinkedIn posting cadence and invest 5-10 hours/week in Reddit seeding for 30 days before any other change.

**If Day 7 open rate < 30%**: Problem is email quality or list composition (people signed up for the lead magnet, not the curriculum). Audit the Day 0 subject line; test a plain-text version against the current formatted version. If the issue persists after 2 subject line tests, re-examine lead magnet alignment.

**If partnership outreach produces 0 responses after 20 emails**: Personalization is insufficient. Shift from "we built a curriculum" messaging to "I can send your members a free 1-page CSLB licensing summary from Module 9 as a member resource — no strings." This is a more concrete value exchange.
