# Construction Career Training Curriculum

A **production-ready, comprehensive training program** for construction professionals who want to move into general contracting, project management, or business ownership.

**33 modules | 150 case studies | 3 complete learning paths | ~457,000 words**

---

## Choose Your Path

This curriculum is designed for three distinct career trajectories. Select the path that matches your background and goals.

### 🏗️ Industrial GC Path
**For**: Project managers, field supervisors, and specialty subcontractors ready to manage large commercial or industrial projects.

**What you'll learn**:
- Heavy civil, industrial, and infrastructure project management
- ASME codes, complex scheduling (CPM), and regulatory compliance
- Leading large teams across multiple sites
- Financial management for $10M+ projects

**Time commitment**: 60–80 hours  
**Difficulty**: Advanced  
**Best for**: Experienced construction professionals ready to scale

**[Start Industrial Path →](navigation/industrial-path.md)**

---

### 🏘️ Residential GC Path
**For**: Residential construction professionals, remodelers, and custom home builders ready to start or expand their own business.

**What you'll learn**:
- Residential construction from foundation to closeout
- California licensing, bonding, and business setup
- Estimating, job costing, and profitability
- Handling clients, permits, inspections, and warranty management

**Time commitment**: 50–70 hours  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate  
**Best for**: Residential specialists building GC skills

**[Start Residential Path →](navigation/residential-path.md)**

---

### 🔧 Specialty Sub → PM Path
**For**: Electricians, plumbers, framers, HVAC techs, and specialty contractors moving into project management or foreman roles.

**What you'll learn**:
- Transitioning from trade to leadership
- Managing teams, budgets, and schedules
- Understanding contracts, lien rights, and legal protection
- Building a construction business from specialty trade

**Time commitment**: 40–60 hours  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate  
**Best for**: Trades professionals stepping into management

**[Start Specialty Sub → PM Path →](navigation/specialty-path.md)**

## What's Inside

### 33 Modules Across All Paths
Core modules cover:
- **Foundations**: Contracts, estimating, field execution, project management, codes
- **Residential Specialty**: GC operations, MEP systems, sustainable building, renovation, solar
- **Industrial & Commercial**: Heavy civil, ASME codes, large-team management
- **Business**: Licensing, company setup, financial management, business development
- **Specializations**: Technology, wildfire-resistant construction, luxury homes, hillside sites

### 150 Case Studies & Scenarios
Real-world construction problems with complete worked answers:
- Sticky estimating situations (underquote recovery, scope creep)
- Schedule conflicts (delays, concurrent trades, permits)
- Financial challenges (cash flow, job loss mitigation)
- Team management and conflict resolution
- Regulatory and compliance scenarios

### Production-Ready Resources
- **Templates**: Subcontract, lien waiver, change order, inspection checklist
- **Glossary**: Construction terminology with California regulations flagged
- **Instructor Guide**: Teaching methodology, sample syllabus, assessment rubrics
- **Forms Library**: DIY forms for independent GCs and small shops

## How to Use This Curriculum

1. **Pick your path** based on your background and goals (see above)
2. **Read modules in order** — each builds on the previous
3. **Work through case studies** after every 2–3 modules
4. **Use templates and resources** for real projects
5. **Reference the glossary** anytime you need clarity

**Estimated time**: 40–80 hours depending on path  
**Study pace**: 5–10 hours per week (4–8 week program) or self-directed  
**Best for**: Self-study, training programs, or team onboarding

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a construction background?**  
A: Yes. This curriculum assumes construction field experience. It's designed for people moving into management or business roles, not entry-level workers.

**Q: How long does it take?**  
A: 40–80 hours depending on your path. Most people complete it in 4–8 weeks at 5–10 hours per week.

**Q: Can I use this for my team?**  
A: Yes. See the [Instructor Guide](instructor-guide/how-to-teach.md) for team training options and customization suggestions.

**Q: Is this California-specific?**  
A: Yes. Modules focus on California requirements (CSLB, Title 24, Cal/OSHA, permits). Federal requirements and general construction principles are included for reference.

**Q: What if I need templates or forms?**  
A: See [Resources → Templates](resources/templates/) for subcontracts, lien waivers, change orders, and more.

**Q: How do I get help?**  
A: Start with the [FAQ page](navigation/faq.md) or check the [Quick Start Guide](navigation/quick-start.md) for next steps.
