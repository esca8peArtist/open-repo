# Specialty Sub → PM Path

**For**: Electricians, plumbers, framers, HVAC techs, and specialty contractors moving into project management or foreman roles.

**Time**: 40–60 hours | **Difficulty**: Intermediate | **Best for**: Trades professionals stepping into management

---

## What You'll Learn

### Phase 1: Management Fundamentals (Modules 1–4)
Build the PM foundation:
- **Module 1**: Contracts, estimating, and cost management
- **Module 2**: Field execution fundamentals (broader than your trade)
- **Module 3**: Scheduling, project coordination, and team management
- **Module 4**: Codes and regulations overview

**What it covers**: You already know your trade. These modules teach you to manage budgets, schedules, and teams at the project level.

### Phase 2: Moving Into Management (Modules 29–31)
Master the transition from hands-on to leadership:
- **Module 29**: From Trade to Leadership (identity shift, staying credible)
- **Module 30**: Managing Teams & Crews (hiring, training, motivation)
- **Module 31**: Foreman Skills & Field Leadership (daily management)

**What it covers**: How to transition from doing the work to leading others, maintain credibility, handle tough people situations, and build team performance.

### Phase 3: Business & Compliance (Modules 12–14, 17, 25, 28)
Run your side business or take on PM roles:
- **Module 12**: GC Business Setup (licensing, bonding, accounting)
- **Module 13**: Construction Law & Lien Rights (protect your money)
- **Module 14**: Site Safety & OSHA (your compliance responsibility as leader)
- **Module 17**: Construction Technology & Software (project management tools)
- **Module 25**: Construction Finance & Lending (budgets and cash flow)
- **Module 28**: Marketing & Business Development (winning work)

**What it covers**: How to start your own side business, understand legal protection, manage safety as a leader, and build your reputation.

## Sample Case Studies

After each phase, you'll work through realistic scenarios:

**Phase 1 Scenarios** (PM Fundamentals):
- "You're bidding a $200K renovation. Scope ambiguity on MEP rough-in. How do you estimate it?"
- "General contractor wants you to accelerate your scope by 2 weeks. Cost and risk?"
- "Permit didn't come through. How does that change your schedule and cash flow?"

**Phase 2 Scenarios** (Leadership Transition):
- "Your crew respects you as a tradesperson, but you're now their foreman. How do you establish authority?"
- "Crew member isn't pulling weight. Performance conversation. How do you handle it?"
- "You're working alongside your crew but also managing. When do you jump in? When do you step back?"

**Phase 3 Scenarios** (Business & Compliance):
- "You want to start your own sub business on the side. What's the licensing path? Bonding?"
- "Worker gets hurt on your site. OSHA citation possible. How do you respond?"
- "Cash flow is tight. Do you take a draw? How do you manage personal vs. business money?"

## Learning Approach

### Option 1: Self-Study (Most Common)
- **Time**: 5–7 hours per week
- **Pace**: One module every 1–2 weeks
- **Deliverables**: Read modules, work through case studies, reference templates

### Option 2: Team Training
- **Time**: 1–2 hours per week
- **Pace**: One module per week with your team or PM group
- **Deliverables**: Team discussion of real management situations

### Option 3: Bootcamp (4-week intensive)
- **Time**: 10 hours per week × 4 weeks
- **Pace**: 2–3 modules per week
- **Deliverables**: Accelerated learning for someone ready to transition immediately

## Key Topics Covered

- **Contract fundamentals**: Understanding terms, lien rights, protecting payment
- **Estimating basics**: Material and labor takeoff, pricing your trade
- **Scheduling**: Critical path, float, coordinating with other trades
- **Team leadership**: Hiring, training, motivation, performance management
- **Daily management**: Safety, quality, schedule tracking, crew morale
- **Business**: Licensing, bonding, insurance, accounting, side business setup
- **Compliance**: OSHA, safety plans, workers' comp, regulatory requirements
- **Problem-solving**: Scope changes, disputes, schedule pressure, safety issues
- **Tools**: Project management software, communication, time tracking
- **Scaling**: When to hire, building systems, training others

## What Happens After Completion?

Once you complete this path (40–60 hours):

1. **You're ready for a foreman or PM role** — Managing 5–20 person crew
2. **You understand business fundamentals** — Could start your own sub business
3. **You know how to manage safety** — OSHA compliance, worker training
4. **You have credibility as a leader** — Transitioning from "one of the crew" to "boss"

**Next steps**:
- Take a foreman or PM role on a construction project
- Move your relationship with the GC from "specialty sub" to "project partner"
- Start your own specialty sub business (if you want)
- Hire and train your first crew
- Build your reputation as a leader (not just a tradesperson)

## Sample Scenario Walkthrough

**Scenario: "You just got promoted to foreman. Your old crew mates are now reporting to you. How do you establish authority while staying credible?"**

**What's tricky**: You worked side-by-side with these people. Now you have to make decisions they might not like (tighter schedule, safety requirements, performance standards).

**Module 29 covers**: Identity shift, credibility maintenance, the "conversation" with your old peers  
**Module 30 covers**: Authority and respect, handling resistance, performance standards  
**Module 31 covers**: Daily foreman decisions, delegating, showing up on the job  

**Case study walkthrough**: You'll work through exactly this scenario with multiple perspectives (yours, a resistant crew member, the GC's expectations) and see how to navigate it.
