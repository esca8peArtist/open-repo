# Frequently Asked Questions

---

## General

**Q: What is this curriculum?**  
A: A production-ready, comprehensive training program for construction professionals moving into general contracting, project management, or business ownership. 33 modules, 150 case studies, ~457,000 words across three learning paths.

**Q: Who created this?**  
A: Developed by construction industry professionals with 50+ years combined experience in residential, commercial, and industrial construction.

**Q: How much does it cost?**  
A: The curriculum is available for free as an open educational resource. See [License](#) for usage terms.

---

## Choosing a Path

**Q: I'm not sure which path is right for me. Where do I start?**  
A: Start with the [Quick Start Guide](quick-start.md). It has a 5-minute assessment to help you pick your path.

**Q: Can I do multiple paths?**  
A: Yes! The foundation modules (1–4) are shared across all paths. After that, you can mix and match. Most people complete one path fully before exploring another.

**Q: I work in a specialty trade. Which path should I take?**  
A: Start with [Specialty Sub → PM Path](specialty-path.md). It's designed specifically for electricians, plumbers, framers, HVAC techs, and other trades professionals moving into management.

**Q: I'm already a GC. Will this curriculum help?**  
A: Yes, if you want to deepen your skills or train your team. The [Instructor Guide](../instructor-guide/how-to-teach.md) covers team training setups. Experienced GCs often use it as a reference for specific topics or to train their next-gen PMs.

## Time & Pace

**Q: How long does it take?**  
A: Depends on your path:
- **Residential GC**: 50–70 hours (5–7 weeks at 10 hrs/week)
- **Industrial GC**: 60–80 hours (6–8 weeks)
- **Specialty Sub → PM**: 40–60 hours (4–6 weeks)

**Q: How many hours per week do I need?**  
A: Most people dedicate 5–10 hours per week. You can adjust:
- Fast-track: 10–15 hours/week (done in 4 weeks)
- Flexible: 3–5 hours/week (done in 10–15 weeks)
- Part-time: 1–2 hours/week (done in 20+ weeks)

**Q: Can I do this at my own pace?**  
A: Absolutely. All modules are self-contained. Read them in order or jump around based on your needs.

**Q: Do I have to complete the whole thing?**  
A: No. Pick individual modules or topics you need. However, foundation modules (1–4) should come first to establish context.

## Content & Resources

**Q: Is this California-specific?**  
A: Modules emphasize California requirements (CSLB licensing, Title 24, Cal/OSHA, permitting). General construction principles and federal requirements are included for reference. Non-California residents can still use the curriculum, but some content won't apply directly.

**Q: Do you have templates and forms?**  
A: Yes. See [Resources → Templates](../resources/templates/) for:
- Subcontract templates
- Lien waiver forms
- Change order templates
- Inspection checklists
- Safety plan outlines

**Q: Is this material specific to residential, or does it apply to commercial?**  
A: Each path focuses on a specific market:
- **Residential GC Path**: Residential homes, remodels, custom builds, ADUs, solar
- **Industrial GC Path**: Heavy civil, commercial, industrial, $10M+ projects
- **Specialty Sub → PM Path**: Any project type, focus on leadership transition

General principles (contracts, safety, scheduling, finance) apply across all markets.

**Q: Where can I get more help on a specific topic?**  
A: Each module lists recommended resources and references at the end. See [Resources](../resources/) for additional templates, checklists, and links.

## Case Studies

**Q: How many case studies are there?**  
A: 150 total scenarios with worked answers, organized by module. Each scenario presents a real construction problem with multiple perspectives.

**Q: Are the case studies real situations?**  
A: Yes. They're based on actual construction project challenges and are written from experience, not hypothetical.

**Q: Do I have to work through all the case studies?**  
A: No, but it's recommended. Case studies are where you apply what you learned in modules. Work through 2–3 per week as you progress through the curriculum.

**Q: Can I use these in my own training program?**  
A: Yes. See [Instructor Guide](../instructor-guide/how-to-teach.md) for permission and best practices for using cases with your team.

## Cost & Business Questions

**Q: Should I start my own GC business? When am I ready?**  
A: This curriculum prepares you with the knowledge. Readiness depends on:
- Experience in your market (3+ years typical)
- Capital for licensing, bonding, insurance
- Business planning and financial reserves
- Market conditions and demand

See [Module 12: GC Business Setup](../modules/12-gc-business-setup-california.md) for California-specific requirements.

**Q: How much does California CSLB licensing cost?**  
A: Approximately $300–$1,000 depending on contractor type and exam preparation. See [Module 12](../modules/12-gc-business-setup-california.md) for current fees.

**Q: Do I need a general contractor's license to do residential work?**  
A: In California, yes, if you're contracting work for others. License requirements vary by project type and value. See [Module 12](../modules/12-gc-business-setup-california.md).
