# Residential GC Path

**For**: Residential contractors, remodelers, and custom home builders ready to start or scale their own business.

**Time**: 50–70 hours | **Difficulty**: Intermediate | **Best for**: Residential specialists building GC skills

---

## What You'll Learn

### Phase 1: Construction Fundamentals (Modules 1–4)
Build the foundation every GC needs:
- **Module 1**: Contracts, estimating, and cost management
- **Module 2**: MEP systems and residential field execution
- **Module 3**: Scheduling, project coordination, and team management
- **Module 4**: Codes and regulations overview

**What it covers**: You'll understand contracts (protecting yourself and your business), how to estimate accurately, schedule work, and navigate regulations.

### Phase 2: Residential Operations (Modules 7–11)
Deep dive into running residential projects:
- **Module 7**: Residential GC scope and complete project management
- **Module 8**: Estimating residential jobs and financial management
- **Module 9**: Codes, permits, and inspections (California-specific)
- **Module 10**: Residential structural systems
- **Module 11**: MEP systems (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) in residential

**What it covers**: How to manage a residential project from start to finish, coordinate trades, handle permits, and understand structural and MEP work.

### Phase 3: Business Setup & Specializations (Modules 12–28)
Start and scale your residential business:
- **Module 12**: Business setup (licensing, bonding, accounting)
- **Module 13**: Construction law and lien rights
- **Module 14**: Site safety and OSHA compliance
- **Module 15**: ADU and infill development (high-growth California segment)
- **Module 16**: Renovation and remodel project management
- **Module 17**: Construction software and technology tools
- **Module 18**: Sustainable and green building
- **Module 19**: Outdoor living and specialty scopes
- **Module 20**: Custom home design-build management
- **Module 21**: Project closeout and warranty management
- **Module 22**: Wildfire-resistant construction (California requirement)
- **Module 23**: Luxury residential management (premium market)
- **Module 24**: Reading construction specifications
- **Module 25**: Construction finance and lending
- **Module 26**: Hillside and difficult site construction
- **Module 27**: Residential solar and battery storage
- **Module 28**: Marketing and business development

**What it covers**: Everything you need to start a residential business, manage special project types (remodels, ADUs, luxury homes), navigate California regulations, and market your services.

---

## Sample Case Studies

After each phase, you'll work through real-world scenarios:

**Phase 1 Scenarios** (Fundamentals):
- "Bid $150K for a kitchen remodel. Found hidden rot behind cabinets. Budget blown. How do you recover?"
- "Homeowner wants to add scope mid-project. How do change orders work?"
- "General permit versus specialty permits for this project? What's required in California?"

**Phase 2 Scenarios** (Operations):
- "Framing crew finished 2 weeks early. MEP not ready. How do you use the float?"
- "Plumber is backed up for 3 weeks. Delays your interior work. What's your recovery plan?"
- "City is scheduling final inspection. You're still waiting on one trade. What do you do?"

**Phase 3 Scenarios** (Business):
- "Should you start your own GC business? What are the licensing and bonding requirements?"
- "Homeowner sues for defect after closeout. How do you handle warranty claims?"
- "Competitor is underbidding you. How do you compete without cutting your margin?"
- "Should you go solar? Market demand and your value proposition?"

## Learning Approach

### Option 1: Self-Study (Most Common)
- **Time**: 5–10 hours per week
- **Pace**: One module every 1–2 weeks
- **Deliverables**: Read each module, work through 2–3 case studies per week, save templates for your projects

### Option 2: Team Training
- **Time**: 2 hours per week × 25–35 weeks
- **Pace**: One module per week with your crew or PM team
- **Deliverables**: Team discussion of real scenarios from your current jobs

### Option 3: Formal Course (8-week intensive)
- **Time**: 6–8 hours per week × 8 weeks
- **Pace**: 3–4 modules per week with quizzes and projects
- **Deliverables**: See [16-week syllabus](../instructor-guide/sample-syllabus.md) (adapt for 8 weeks)

## Key Topics Covered

- **Estimating**: Residential takeoff, budget accuracy, contingency management
- **Scheduling**: Coordination of 8–15 trades, CPM basics, float and critical path
- **Contracts**: Understanding owner-GC agreements, protecting lien rights, managing change orders
- **Permitting**: California building permits, title 24, inspections, expediting
- **Quality**: Specifications, inspections, punch lists, warranty management
- **Teams**: Managing subcontractors, crews, suppliers, and customer relationships
- **Business**: Licensing (CSLB), bonding, insurance, company structure, accounting
- **Safety**: Site safety plans, OSHA compliance, worker training
- **Technology**: Project management software, estimating tools, accounting systems
- **Specialties**: Remodels, ADUs, luxury homes, solar, wildfire-resistant, accessible design
- **Marketing**: Lead generation, reputation management, bid strategy

## What Happens After Completion?

Once you complete the residential path (50–70 hours):

1. **You can manage residential projects independently** — Estimating to closeout
2. **You understand California licensing and business setup** — Ready to start your own GC business
3. **You know how to handle specialties** — ADUs, remodels, solar, luxury homes, difficult sites
4. **You have templates and checklists** — Contracts, specs, permits, warranties, bid strategy

**Next steps**:
- Start your own residential GC business (or scale your current one)
- Manage larger or more complex projects (ADUs, custom homes, renovations)
- Hire and train your own PM or foreman
- Expand into specialty niches (luxury, sustainable, solar)
