## Three Career Stories

### Story 1: From Electrician to Project Manager
*"I've been an electrician for 12 years. I'm good at my trade, but I want to lead teams and run jobs. I don't want to stay hands-on forever."*

**Your path**: [Specialty Sub → PM Path](specialty-path.md)  
**Why**: Covers transitioning from hands-on trade to management, leading teams, understanding contracts and business. 40–60 hours, intermediate difficulty.  
**First module**: Module 29 — From Trade to Leadership

**You'll learn**:
- How to manage teams and still do the work (transition period)
- Contracts and lien rights (protect yourself)
- Job costing and profitability
- Managing relationships with GCs and other trades

---

### Story 2: From Carpenter to Residential GC
*"I've been doing residential remodels for 10 years. I want to start my own business, but I need to understand the business side — licensing, bonding, estimating, dealing with customers."*

**Your path**: [Residential GC Path](residential-path.md)  
**Why**: Comprehensive coverage of residential construction business. Includes licensing (CSLB), estimating, job costing, managing clients, California regulations. 50–70 hours, intermediate.  
**First module**: Module 01 — Foundations (Contracts & Estimating)

**You'll learn**:
- How to estimate residential jobs accurately
- California licensing and bonding requirements
- Managing customer relationships and expectations
- Setting up your business (licenses, insurance, accounting)
- Handling permits, inspections, and warranty

---

### Story 3: From Field Supervisor to Industrial GC
*"I've been supervising crews on commercial and industrial projects for 8 years. Now I want to move into project management — handling budgets, schedules, and client relationships on $10M+ projects."*

**Your path**: [Industrial GC Path](industrial-path.md)  
**Why**: Covers heavy civil, commercial, and industrial project management at scale. Includes ASME codes, CPM scheduling, regulatory compliance, managing large teams. 60–80 hours, advanced.  
**First module**: Module 01 — Foundations (Contracts & Estimating)

**You'll learn**:
- Managing $10M+ projects and large teams
- ASME codes and complex compliance requirements
- CPM scheduling and managing schedule pressure
- Financial management and cost control
- Working with owners, architects, and other stakeholders

## Which Path Is Right for You?

| **Criteria** | **Residential GC** | **Industrial GC** | **Specialty Sub → PM** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Current role** | Residential contractor, remodeler, custom home builder | Commercial/industrial supervisor, PM | Electrician, plumber, framer, HVAC, trade lead |
| **Career goal** | Start/grow own residential business | Lead large commercial/industrial projects | Transition into management/foreman role |
| **Project size** | $100K–$5M residential | $5M–$50M+ commercial/industrial | $100K–$5M (any type) |
| **Team size** | 5–20 people | 20–100+ people | 5–15 people |
| **Time to completion** | 50–70 hours (5–7 weeks at 10 hrs/week) | 60–80 hours (6–8 weeks) | 40–60 hours (4–6 weeks) |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate | Advanced | Intermediate |
| **Best for** | Residential specialists | Large project expertise seekers | Trades professionals moving to management |

## Learning Format

You can use this curriculum in three ways:

### 1️⃣ Self-Study (Most Common)
- Read modules at your own pace (5–10 hours per week)
- Work through case studies
- Reference templates and resources as needed
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks

### 2️⃣ Team Training
- Use with your team or crew
- Work through one module per week in a group setting
- Discuss case studies and real scenarios from your projects
- **Timeline**: 8–16 weeks (1 module per week)
- See [Instructor Guide](../instructor-guide/how-to-teach.md) for team setup

### 3️⃣ Formal Training Program
- Use in a classroom or structured course
- Adapt the [16-week syllabus](../instructor-guide/sample-syllabus.md) for your program
- Use assessment rubrics for grading
- **Timeline**: 16 weeks (1–2 modules per week)

## Sample Module Preview

To get a feel for the content, here's a preview of what modules cover:

**Module 01 — Foundations: Contracts & Estimating** (7 hours)
- How to read and negotiate construction contracts
- Types of contracts (lump sum, cost-plus, unit price, time & materials)
- Estimating accuracy and managing underquotes
- Contingency planning
- **Case study**: "You quoted $150K, then found hidden conditions. What do you do?"

**Module 07 — Residential GC Scope & Management** (8 hours)
- Managing the full residential project lifecycle
- Scheduling, coordinating trades, managing suppliers
- Quality control and inspections
- Working with customers and architects
- **Case study**: "Schedule slipped 3 weeks. Framing and MEP overlap. How do you recover?"

## Not Sure? Take the Quick Assessment

Answer these 3 questions:

1. **What's your main goal right now?**
   - A) Start my own business → Residential GC Path
   - B) Move into management → Specialty Sub → PM Path
   - C) Lead large projects → Industrial GC Path

2. **What projects do you typically work on?**
   - A) Residential homes, remodels → Residential GC Path
   - B) Any type (I do the work myself) → Specialty Sub → PM Path
   - C) Commercial, industrial, heavy civil → Industrial GC Path

3. **What's your biggest skill gap right now?**
   - A) Business, licensing, profitability → Residential GC Path
   - B) Managing people and budgets → Specialty Sub → PM Path
   - C) Large project management and compliance → Industrial GC Path

**Count your A's, B's, and C's. Your most common letter is your path!**
