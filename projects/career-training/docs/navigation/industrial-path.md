# Industrial GC Path

**For**: Project managers, field supervisors, and specialty subcontractors ready to manage large commercial or industrial projects.

**Time**: 60–80 hours | **Difficulty**: Advanced | **Best for**: Large project expertise

---

## What You'll Learn

### Phase 1: Construction Fundamentals (Modules 1–6)
Build the foundation every GC needs:
- **Module 1**: Contracts, estimating, and cost management
- **Module 2**: MEP systems and field execution fundamentals
- **Module 3**: Scheduling, CPM basics, and project management theory
- **Module 4**: Codes and regulations overview
- **Module 5**: Civil engineering fundamentals (heavy civil focus)
- **Module 6**: Reading and working with architects

**What it covers**: You'll understand contracts, basic estimating, scheduling theory, and how to read plans — the foundation for all industrial project work.

### Phase 2: Industrial Specialization (Modules 28, 32–33)
Deep dive into large project management:
- **Module 28**: Marketing and winning large projects
- **Module 32**: Industrial project controls, compliance, and risk management
- **Module 33**: ASME codes and heavy civil project management

**What it covers**: Managing $10M+ budgets, complex schedules with 100+ crew members, regulatory compliance, ASME codes, and stakeholder management.

---

## Sample Case Studies

After each phase, you'll work through 20–30 real-world scenarios:

**Phase 1 Scenarios** (Contracts & Execution):
- "You're bidding a $25M industrial project. Scope is ambiguous. How do you handle estimating uncertainty?"
- "Change order #47 from the owner was never approved by your CFO. It's $300K. How do you handle it?"
- "Your subcontractor is behind schedule and pushing for concurrent work on critical path. What's your play?"

**Phase 2 Scenarios** (Industrial PM):
- "Your mega-project is 12 weeks behind. Owner wants acceleration. Crew fatigue is rising. Options?"
- "OSHA citation for fall protection during heavy formwork. How do you respond and update your program?"
- "Buried utility line discovered during excavation. Project timeline impact? Financial recovery?"

## Learning Approach

### Option 1: Self-Study (Most Common)
- **Time**: 2–3 hours per week × 25–30 weeks
- **Pace**: One module every 1–2 weeks
- **Deliverables**: Read each module, work through 2–3 case studies per week, reference templates as needed

### Option 2: Team Training
- **Time**: 2 hours per week × 30–40 weeks
- **Pace**: One module per week in a group setting
- **Deliverables**: Team discussion of cases, real-project problem-solving, collaborative learning

### Option 3: Formal Course (16-week intensive)
- **Time**: 4–6 hours per week × 16 weeks
- **Pace**: 1–2 modules per week with quizzes and projects
- **Deliverables**: See [16-week syllabus](../instructor-guide/sample-syllabus.md)

## What Happens After Completion?

Once you complete all 9 modules (60–80 hours):

1. **You understand large project management** — Budgets, schedules, compliance, teams
2. **You're ready for PM roles on $10M+ projects**
3. **You know when to escalate issues** and have frameworks for complex decisions
4. **You have templates** for major contracts, risk registers, and compliance checklists

**Next steps**:
- Take a PM role on a $10M+ commercial or industrial project
- Reference the curriculum for specific situations (lien rights, OSHA issues, cost control)
- Share with your team for ongoing professional development

## Who Uses This Path?

This path is designed for:

✅ **Commercial project managers** learning to scale from $1M–$10M to $10M–$50M+  
✅ **Field supervisors** moving into PM roles on large projects  
✅ **Specialty subcontractors** winning and managing prime contracts  
✅ **Civil contractors** managing heavy infrastructure and industrial work  
✅ **Training programs** for large construction companies preparing next-gen PMs
