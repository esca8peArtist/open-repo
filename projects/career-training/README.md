# Industrial and Residential GC Career Training — Complete Curriculum

Welcome to the construction career training curriculum. This is a complete, self-directed learning program designed for three distinct career paths: **Industrial GC**, **Residential GC**, and **Specialty Subcontractor to PM**. The curriculum consists of 33 instructional modules (~336,000 words), 150 case-study scenarios, and detailed reading sequences tailored to your role.

**Start here.** Choose your career path below, then follow the recommended reading sequence for your role. You do not need to read all 33 modules — each path specifies a focused sequence appropriate for your goals.

## Curriculum Overview

### What You Get

- **33 instructional modules** covering industrial GC, residential GC, business operations, legal compliance, and specialty scopes
- **150 case-study scenarios** with worked answers — decision-based learning from real construction situations
- **Three complete learning paths** — each with prerequisites, recommended reading order, and cross-references to case studies
- **Production-ready documents**: comprehensive module index, gap analysis, and prioritized new module recommendations
- **All modules are California-specific** where applicable (codes, licensing, labor law, permitting)

### Completion Criteria

Each learning path defines a clear "phase complete when" milestone. You'll know you're ready to advance when you can:

- **Residential GC, Phase 1**: Walk a set of drawings, name the trades in sequence, describe the permit workflow, and read a soils report summary
- **Industrial GC, Phase 1**: Identify the correct ASME code for a piping service, structure a prime contract, and manage a CPM schedule update
- **Specialty Sub, Phase 1**: Pick up an unfamiliar full drawing set and read from cover sheet to detail, not just your trade sheets

A tradesperson (pipefitter, millwright, electrician, HVAC technician) moving into a GC or PM role on industrial projects — refineries, manufacturing plants, power generation, food and beverage, pharmaceutical, or heavy industrial facilities. ## Path 3: Specialty Subcontractor to Foreman to PM

### Target Student

An electrician, plumber, framer, mechanical technician, or civil worker moving toward a foreman or project manager role. Goal: learn everything that surrounds the trade — contracts, schedule logic, other trades' work, business infrastructure — without re-learning the trade itself.

**Total estimated time:** 112–130 hours over 12–14 weeks.

### Phase 1 — Prerequisites: Reading the Full Job (Weeks 1–2, ~15 hours)

The single most common gap for tradespeople moving to foreman: they can read their own sheets and none of the others. Fix this first.

| Order | Module | File | Est. Hours | Key Topics | Learning Outcome |
|------:|--------|------|:----------:|------------|------------------|
| 1 | Architecture for the GC | `06-architecture-for-contractors.md` | 7 hrs | Drawing conventions, plan types, detail reading, coordination drawings | You can read any discipline's drawings and spot coordination issues |
| 2 | Reading and Interpreting Construction Specifications | `24-reading-construction-specifications.md` | 8 hrs | Spec sections for your trade, submittal requirements, quality standards, RFI process | You understand spec obligations and submittal requirements |

**Phase 1 complete when:** You can pick up an unfamiliar full drawing set and read from cover sheet to detail, not just your trade sheets.

### Phase 2 — Understanding the Project (Weeks 3–5, ~24 hours)

Learn how the GC sees the job. Understanding your GC's problems makes you a better sub — and prepares you to manage a project yourself.

| Order | Module | File | Est. Hours | Key Topics | Learning Outcome |
|------:|--------|------|:----------:|------------|------------------|
| 3 | Residential GC: Scope, Trades, and People Management | `07-residential-gc-scope-and-management.md` | 9 hrs | GC's trade coordination challenges; inspection discipline; read from the GC's perspective | You understand the GC's constraints and sequencing logic |
| 4 | Industrial Construction PM, Scheduling, and Safety | `03-project-management-scheduling-safety.md` | 8 hrs | CPM logic, critical path, float; how your trade's work fits the master schedule | You can read a CPM schedule and understand your role |
| 5 | Civil Engineering for the GC | `05-civil-engineering.md` | 7 hrs | Site sequencing context; why the project is sequenced the way it is | You understand site preparation and earthwork context |

**Phase 2 complete when:** You understand how the GC manages the project and where your trade fits into the critical path.

### Phase 3 — Trade-Adjacent Technical Modules (Weeks 6–8, ~20 hours)

Choose two or three modules most relevant to your trade. All are self-contained.

**For Electrical or Solar Workers:**
- Module 11: Residential MEP Systems (`11-residential-mep-systems.md`) — 10 hrs — MEP coordination with plumbing/HVAC
- Module 27: Solar PV and Battery (`27-residential-solar-battery-storage.md`) — 6 hrs — NEC 690, interconnection, Title 24

**For Mechanical, Plumbing, or HVAC Workers:**
- Module 11: Residential MEP Systems (`11-residential-mep-systems.md`) — 10 hrs — Coordination with electrical and structural
- Module 02: Field Execution, Mechanical/Electrical (`02-field-execution-mechanical-electrical.md`) — 9 hrs — ASME codes and commissioning

**For Framers or Structural Workers:**
- Module 10: Structural Systems (`10-structural-systems-residential.md`) — 9 hrs — Load path, connectors, seismic design
- Module 05: Civil Engineering (`05-civil-engineering.md`) — 8 hrs — Site-to-structure interface and foundations

**For Civil, Grading, or Drainage Workers:**
- Module 05: Civil Engineering (`05-civil-engineering.md`) — 8 hrs — Earthwork, drainage, site sequencing
- Module 26: Hillside and Difficult Sites (`26-hillside-difficult-site-construction.md`) — 8 hrs — Geotechnical and retaining wall management

**For Finish, Renovation, or Carpentry Workers:**
- Module 16: Renovation and Remodeling (`16-renovation-remodel-guide.md`) — 8 hrs — Existing conditions and phasing
- Module 19: Outdoor Living and Specialty (`19-outdoor-living-specialty-scopes.md`) — 8 hrs — Specialty scope coordination

Understanding your legal position as a sub, and how money flows from owner to GC to you. This knowledge protects your employer now and your own business eventually.

## How to Use This Curriculum

### Option 1: Self-Directed Study

1. Choose your career path above
2. Follow the recommended reading order — don't skip phases
3. After each phase, test yourself using the corresponding case-study scenarios
4. Track your completion in a spreadsheet or note app
5. Take the completion milestone test before advancing to the next phase

### Option 2: Classroom or Group Training

1. An instructor selects modules for a cohort (e.g., "Industrial GC Path")
2. Assign modules in phase sequence — 2–3 weeks per phase
3. Use the case-study workbook for in-class discussion and group problem-solving
4. Final capstone: students present a project estimate and contract analysis drawn from their own market

### Option 3: Reference Lookup

1. Use the Module Quick-Reference to find topics you need right now
2. Jump to that module section
