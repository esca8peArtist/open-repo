- Women's Health: lemon balm (*Melissa officinalis*), red clover (*Trifolium pratense*), damiana (*Turnera diffusa*)

- Styled flat-lay: herbs + guide mockup on natural surface

- White balance: consistent within each bundle set; warm 5000–5500K preferred

## ROLE 2: Wellness Content Writer — Women's Health Bundle (Writer #1)

**Upwork title**: Wellness Content Writer — Women's Herbal Health Guide (Lemon Balm, Red Clover, Damiana)

**Project type**: Fixed-price
**Contract length**: 3 weeks (July 5 – July 26, 2026)
**Posted budget**: $950 gross
**Estimated hours**: 50–60 hours

---

Seedwarden publishes digital medicinal herb guides on Etsy. The Women's Health bundle covers lemon balm (*Melissa officinalis*), red clover (*Trifolium pratense*), and damiana (*Turnera diffusa*).

We need a wellness writer who can produce educational, evidence-grounded content for a reader with a moderate herbal background — not clinical marketing copy, not blog-level generalities.

**Your deliverables**

One manuscript: 3,600–3,800 words

Species sections (400–500 words each):

- Lemon balm (*Melissa officinalis*): Traditional uses in women's health (anxiety, menstrual cramping, thyroid interaction notes), active constituents (rosmarinic acid, flavonoids), cultivation and harvest, preparation methods (tea, tincture, glycerite), contraindications — TSH-binding inhibition is mandatory and must appear as a named contraindication, not in body text only.

- Red clover (*Trifolium pratense*): Isoflavone content and phytoestrogen activity, menopausal symptom research (hot flash evidence with limitations of current trials stated honestly), traditional use vs. modern evidence framing, drug interactions (warfarin, hormone therapy), harvest and preparation (blossom infusion, standardized extracts vs. whole-plant).

- Damiana (*Turnera diffusa*): Traditional use history (Mexican and Central American traditions), current evidence status — must include this verbatim statement: "Clinical evidence for damiana's effects on human health is limited. Traditional use is documented across multiple cultures, but double-blind trial data is sparse. This guide documents traditional applications, not proven clinical outcomes." Contraindications (diabetes medication interaction, blood sugar effects). Cultivation notes (frost-tender; annual in USDA Zones 8 and below).

Supporting sections:
- Contraindication reference table (all three species, all populations: pregnancy, nursing, hormone-sensitive conditions, drug interactions)
- Preparation methods overview (tea, tincture, glycerite, capsule) with dosage ranges
- Sourcing and sustainability sidebar (red clover: widely cultivated; damiana: Mexican wild-harvest sustainability notes)
- Sources and further reading (minimum 6 primary or peer-reviewed sources cited)

**Mandatory content requirements**

- Latin binomials italicized on every first mention
- Damiana limited-evidence statement: verbatim as written above — do not paraphrase
- Lemon balm thyroid interaction: named contraindication, not footnote
- FTC compliance: no unqualified therapeutic claims. All claims framed with evidence tier: "Traditionally used to...," "Research suggests...," "Preliminary evidence indicates..."
- Contraindication language: "Avoid during pregnancy" or "Consult a healthcare provider before use with [drug class]" — not "is safe for most people"

**Style**

- Educational, accessible, herbalist-level detail
- Warm but not promotional
- US English, AP style for numbers
- Text only — no image placeholders; design team handles layout

**Payment schedule**

- 50% ($475) on contract signature (by July 5)
- 50% ($475) on final manuscript approved after all revisions (by July 26)

**Work-for-hire notice**

All content is owned by Seedwarden on delivery. No republishing rights. No byline unless separately negotiated.

**How to apply**

1. Links to 3 published articles on women's health or herbal medicine (verifiable publication links required)
2. Brief description of your experience with FTC compliance in health content
3. Confirmation you can start July 5 and deliver a complete draft by July 21 (yes/no)
4. Your proposed rate

**Contract length**: 6 weeks (July 7 – August 14, 2026)
**Posted budget**: $850 gross
**Estimated hours**: 25–35 hours (5–7 hours per week)

---

Seedwarden publishes digital medicinal herb guides on Etsy. We are launching four Q3 bundles and need a credentialed herbalist to review each manuscript for botanical accuracy, contraindication completeness, drug interaction accuracy, dosage safety, and CITES/endangered species compliance.

**Bundles for review (in delivery order)**

1. Women's Health: lemon balm (*Melissa officinalis*), red clover (*Trifolium pratense*), damiana (*Turnera diffusa*)
2. Respiratory Health: elderberry, mullein, echinacea (*E. purpurea* / *E. angustifolia*), thyme

4. Immunity Support: echinacea, ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*), elderberry, goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*)

**Your deliverables**

Four review reports — one per bundle — delivered as Google Doc inline comments plus a summary email.

Each review covers:
- Species identification accuracy (correct Latin binomials, family, synonyms if used in text)
- Traditional use accuracy vs. manuscript claims
- Active constituent correctness (mechanism claims match current literature)
- Contraindication completeness (drug interactions, population-specific cautions, pregnancy/nursing flags)
- Dosage appropriateness (preparation-specific dosage ranges within safe limits)
- FTC compliance flags (any unqualified claims needing evidence-tier rewording)
- Conservation status accuracy (UpS At-Risk species correctly flagged: *Actaea racemosa*, *E. angustifolia*, *Hydrastis canadensis*)
- CITES compliance: goldenseal (*H. canadensis*) is CITES Appendix II — confirm sourcing language recommends cultivated-only sources throughout

**Priority flags per bundle**

- Women's Health: lemon balm TSH-binding inhibition (confirm citation); damiana blood sugar interaction (verify mechanism and current evidence tier); red clover phytoestrogen framing (hormone-sensitive conditions contraindication specificity)
- Respiratory: echinacea autoimmune contraindication (verify wording is unambiguous); raw elderberry toxicity (sambunigrin — confirm safety language is adequate); *E. angustifolia* vs. *E. purpurea* conservation distinction
- Sleep & Nervines: valerian CNS depressant potentiation (list specific drug classes: benzodiazepines, barbiturates, alcohol minimum); passionflower MAOI interaction (confirm SSRI co-use language is explicit); lavender Silexan vs. DIY preparation distinction
- Immunity Support: ashwagandha thyroid hormone interaction (Hashimoto's contraindication language); goldenseal drug interactions (multiple CYP450 substrates — verify completeness); CITES language for goldenseal throughout
