# Canva Execution Playbook: Phase 2 Lifestyle Photography

**What this document is**: A step-by-step Canva guide for producing Etsy slots 4 and 5 across all 21 Seedwarden products, following the approved hybrid strategy. Physical photos for 15 products (Clusters A, B, C). Stock compositing for 6 products (Clusters D, E). This playbook covers every Canva step from account setup through final export.

**What this document is not**: A photography guide. That is covered in [LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md](./LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md) and [PHASE2_PHOTOGRAPHY_EXECUTION_PLAN.md](./PHASE2_PHOTOGRAPHY_EXECUTION_PLAN.md).

**Prerequisite state**: Slots 1–3 are complete for all 21 products (tablet cover mockup, interior grid, phone mockup). See [MOCKUP_STRATEGY.md](./MOCKUP_STRATEGY.md) for details. You are filling slots 4 and 5 only.

**Output**: 42 Etsy-ready images — two per product, one for slot 4 (lifestyle/flat-lay), one for slot 5 (in-use/contextual). All exported at 2400×2400px.

## Section 1: Canva Setup

### 1.1 Account Tier Decision: Free vs. Pro

**Start with Canva Free.** The free tier covers everything in this playbook except one feature: background removal. That feature matters for compositing product overlays onto stock images (the Cluster D/E workflow in Section 4). Here is the decision tree:

- If you are doing compositing work (stock photo background + product PDF overlay): Pro is worth $15/month for one month, then cancel. Background Removal is the feature you need, and it is a 3-second click versus a 15-minute manual workaround.
- If you are only compositing physical photos where the product already appears in the shot: Free is sufficient. No background removal needed.
- If you have already purchased Adobe Creative Cloud or have access to Photoshop/GIMP: skip Canva Pro entirely and use those tools for compositing. Use Canva Free for layout, text, and export only.

**Canva Pro: $15/month (billed monthly, cancel after one month).** Do not pay annually. This is a single-sprint production tool, not a recurring workflow.

To upgrade: canva.com > Settings > Billing & Plans > Try Canva Pro.

### 1.2 Brand Kit Creation

The Brand Kit is how you ensure every image across all 21 products looks like the same brand without manually entering color codes every time. Set it up once at the start, use it throughout.

Navigate to: canva.com > Brand Hub (left sidebar) > Create a Brand Kit.

**Brand colors to enter** (from the established Seedwarden palette):

| Color Name | Hex Code | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Forest Green | #143b28 | Primary brand color, text on light backgrounds |
| Deep Ink Green | #1A3A2A | Alternate primary, header backgrounds |
| Warm Cream | #F5EDD6 | Background fields, text on dark backgrounds |
| Parchment | #EDE0C4 | Secondary background, subtle panels |
| Sage | #8FA882 | Accent, divider lines, subtle overlays |
| Burnt Sienna | #A0522D | Accent for product-specific highlights |

Add all six. In Canva's Brand Kit color section, click "+ Add color" and paste each hex code. Label them with the names above so you can identify them without memorizing codes.

**Brand fonts** (enter these even if using free fonts, so they are pinned for quick access):

- Heading font: Playfair Display (available free in Canva) — use for product name overlays on lifestyle images
- Body font: Lato or Source Sans 3 (both free in Canva) — use for taglines, secondary text, call-to-action text on phone mockup designs
- Accent / label font: Cormorant Garamond (free in Canva) — use sparingly for elegant label text on flat-lay images

**Brand logo**: Upload your Seedwarden logo file (PNG with transparent background) to the Brand Kit. If you do not yet have a final logo file, upload a placeholder — the Brand Kit logo field is optional and can be updated later without affecting existing designs.

### 1.3 Team and Collaboration Setup

If you plan to outsource any of this work to a Fiverr or Upwork designer:

1. In Canva, go to Settings > Teams > Invite Team Member.
2. Enter the designer's email address and set role to "Editor" (not Owner).
3. Share the specific Brand Kit with the designer so they pick up your exact colors and fonts without a briefing document.
4. Create a shared folder: Canva Home > Projects > New Folder > Name it "Seedwarden Phase 2 Lifestyle". Share this folder with the designer.
5. Upload all source photos (physical shots from your camera roll, sourced stock images) into this shared folder before handing off.

This setup means the designer works in your Brand Kit, in your folder, with your assets. The output files land in the same folder. No handoff friction, no version control issues.

If working solo, skip the team step. The shared folder is still useful for organizing your own work — create it regardless.

### 1.4 Template Library Strategy

Do not start each of the 21 products from a blank canvas. You will create three master templates (one per workflow), then duplicate them 20 times each. The master template approach is the single biggest efficiency lever in this entire playbook.

**Three master templates to create:**

1. `SW-Master-Tablet` — 1280×960px, for composited tablet-in-scene images
2. `SW-Master-Phone` — 1170×2532px, for phone frame images cropped to square for Etsy
3. `SW-Master-FlatLay` — 2400×2400px, for flat-lay lifestyle images (direct photo compositing)

Templates live in your shared Seedwarden folder. After creating each master, right-click > Make a Copy 20 times. Rename each copy to the product slug before editing (e.g., `sw-seed-saving-field-manual-slot4`). This prevents overwriting the master template mid-batch.

## Section 2: Tablet Mockup Workflow

This section covers the compositing step for products where the lifestyle photo shows a tablet or printed page in the scene. It applies to all 15 physical-photo products (Clusters A, B, C) where a tablet was present during the shoot.

**Canvas size**: 1280×960px (4:3 landscape — this is exported and then cropped to 2400×2400 square at export stage).

**Estimated time per image**: 10–15 minutes once the master template is built. First image: 25–35 minutes (template creation). Image 2 through 21: 8–12 minutes each with template reuse.

### 2.1 Create the Master Tablet Template

Open Canva > Create a design > Custom size > 1280 × 960 px.

**Step 1 — Upload your base lifestyle photo.**
Click "Uploads" in the left sidebar > Upload files > select the flat-lay or contextual photo for your first product. Drag it onto the canvas. Resize it to fill the entire 1280×960 canvas. This is the background layer — everything else sits on top of it.

To fill without distortion: click the image > select "Edit image" > choose "Fit" or drag the corner handles until the image fills the canvas edge to edge without white bars. If the photo is taller than wide, position it so the focal point (props, hands, product) is centered vertically.

**Step 2 — Add the product overlay.**
If the tablet was physically present in the shot and the product PDF is visible on screen, no overlay is needed — the product is already in the photo. Move to Step 4.

If the tablet was in frame but the screen content is not readable, or if you are compositing a printed-page look over a prop shot:
- Upload the product's PDF cover as a PNG (export one page of the PDF as PNG first, at 150 DPI).
- In Canva: Uploads > Upload the PNG > drag onto canvas.
- Resize the PNG to roughly 30–40% of the canvas width so it reads clearly but does not overpower the lifestyle context.
- Position it in the upper-left or center of the frame, overlapping a natural surface (wooden table, fabric).
- Apply a very subtle drop shadow: select the image > Edit image > Shadow > Offset shadow, opacity 30%, blur 12px, offset X 4, offset Y 6. This makes the PNG look like it is sitting on the surface rather than floating.

**Step 3 — Add a subtle brand text element (optional but recommended).**
In the lower-right corner, add the product name in Playfair Display, 18–22px, Deep Forest Green (#143b28), 70% opacity. This is not a headline — it is a quiet brand attribution that becomes visible when the buyer zooms in.

Do not add large text overlays to lifestyle images. The lifestyle photo's job is emotional connection, not information delivery. Text should be present but not dominant.

**Step 4 — Typography sizing for Etsy thumbnails.**

Etsy displays all listing images at 170×170px in search results. At that size, any text smaller than 24px is invisible. Any text smaller than 18px at 2400×2400px export resolution becomes unreadable when compressed for thumbnail display.

Rules for any text you add to lifestyle images:
- Minimum size: 18px at the design canvas dimensions (Canva's font size number)
- Product name / brand attribution: 18–22px, lower corner, low opacity
- If adding a tagline or category label (e.g., "Seed Saving Guide"): 28–36px, positioned in upper third
- Never layer more than two text elements on a lifestyle image. The photo is the content.

**Step 5 — Quality check before saving as master.**

Before duplicating this template 20 times, verify:
- [ ] The lifestyle photo fills the entire canvas with no white edges
- [ ] The product overlay (if present) has a drop shadow and is positioned on a natural surface
- [ ] Any text elements use Brand Kit colors and Brand Kit fonts
- [ ] The composition is not centered — the product should be at the intersection of thirds (rule of thirds)
- [ ] Nothing in the frame competes with the product: no visible brand logos on props, no cluttered corners

**Step 6 — Save as template.**
Do not save yet. Right-click the design in Canva's project folder > "Add to folder" > Seedwarden Phase 2. Then duplicate 20 times.

### 2.2 Canva Efficiency Shortcuts

**Copy/paste elements across designs**: With multiple designs open in tabs, use Cmd+C / Ctrl+C to copy a text element, switch tabs, Cmd+V / Ctrl+V to paste. The pasted element retains font, size, color, and position. This is how you replicate the product-name text across all 21 designs without retyping.

**Batch-update brand colors**: If you decide to change the text color or adjust opacity after building 10 designs, use "Find and replace" in Canva Pro. In Free tier, you will need to update each manually — this is why setting Brand Kit colors correctly at the start saves significant time.

**Grid view**: In your Canva project folder, enable grid view to see all 21 designs as thumbnails. This makes it easy to spot inconsistencies in layout, text size, or image composition before exporting.

**Keyboard shortcuts worth memorizing**:
- T: Add text box
- R: Add rectangle
- Cmd+D / Ctrl+D: Duplicate element
- Cmd+Z / Ctrl+Z: Undo
- Cmd+Shift+H: Flip horizontal
- Cmd+G / Ctrl+G: Group selected elements
- Cmd+] / Ctrl+]: Bring forward (layer ordering)

### 2.3 Export Settings

After completing all 21 tablet lifestyle images:

File > Download > PNG > 2400×2400px equivalent.

Canva's export works in terms of design size, not DPI. Your design is 1280×960px — to export at 2400×2400, you need to:
1. Change canvas size to 2400×2400px before exporting (Resize button in top bar > Custom size > 2400×2400 > Resize)
2. Export as PNG
3. Confirm file size is under 2MB (check in your Downloads folder after exporting)

If over 2MB: export as JPEG at 90% quality instead. For Etsy, JPEG is acceptable and loads faster.

**Estimated total time, tablet workflow**: 25–35 minutes for master template + (10–12 minutes x 20 additional products) = approximately 3.5–4.5 hours for all 21 tablet-slot images.

## Section 3: Phone Frame Workflow

The phone mockup workflow produces images for a secondary lifestyle context: showing the product as someone would view it on their phone. This is distinct from the programmatically generated phone mockup in slot 3 — the slot 5 phone lifestyle image places the phone in an environmental context (on a potting bench, beside a coffee cup, held in a hand).

**Canvas size**: 1170×2532px (iPhone 13 native resolution ratio). This gets exported and cropped to 2400×2400 at final export.

**Estimated time per image**: 15–20 minutes for the first image (frame creation), 10–12 minutes per subsequent image with template reuse.

**When to use this workflow**: Use it for slot 5 on products where a phone-in-environment image is stronger than a flat-lay. This is most relevant for the Clusters B and C products (urban growing, food preservation), where a phone sitting beside a mason jar of ferments or next to a seedling tray is a relatable, practical image.

### 3.1 Create the Phone Frame

Canva does not have a built-in iPhone 13 mockup element in the free tier. You have two options:

**Option A — Canva's mockup generator (Pro only)**: If using Pro, search "iPhone mockup" in the Elements sidebar. Canva has pre-built phone frame graphics. Drag one onto your canvas and replace the placeholder screen content with your product's PDF cover PNG.

**Option B — Find a free mockup template (Free tier)**: Search "free iPhone 13 mockup PNG transparent background" on Pixeden (pixeden.com) or Mockup World (mockupworld.co). Download a PNG with a transparent background. Upload to Canva. This gives you the phone frame as an element you can layer over any background photo.

After placing the phone frame element on your canvas:

**Step 1 — Add background context.**
Upload a lifestyle photo (the background environment: a wooden table, balcony railing, kitchen counter). Place it as the bottom layer filling the full 1170×2532px canvas. Use the same photo as the slot 4 image or a second angle from the same shoot.

**Step 2 — Place the phone frame.**
Drag the iPhone mockup PNG onto the canvas. Resize it so the phone occupies roughly 50–60% of the canvas height. Position it slightly left of center or slightly right — not dead-center. Dead-center reads as product photography; offset reads as lifestyle.

**Step 3 — Insert the product screen.**
Export your product's PDF cover as a PNG (one page, 150 DPI). In Canva, upload this PNG and place it behind the phone frame but in front of the background photo. Resize and position it to align with the phone's screen area. This simulates the product being displayed on the phone.

To align precisely: select both the phone frame PNG and the product screen PNG > right-click > Group. This locks their relative position so you can move both together without misalignment.

**Step 4 — Text placement for phone readability.**

On phone images, text readability is constrained because the canvas is tall and narrow. Text placement rules:

- Do not place text over the phone frame — it competes with the product content on the screen.
- If adding a category label or tagline, place it in the upper 20% of the canvas, above the phone. Use Playfair Display, 36–44px at 1170px canvas width, Deep Forest Green.
- If adding a call-to-action (e.g., "Instant Digital Download"), place it in the lower 15% of the canvas, below the phone. Use Lato, 28px, Warm Cream (#F5EDD6) on a semi-transparent Deep Forest Green bar (rectangle element at 70% opacity).
- CTA text is optional for slot 5. The lifestyle image's primary job is emotional context; the listing description handles CTA language.

**Step 5 — Crop for Etsy export.**

Your design is 1170×2532px. To export at 2400×2400 for Etsy, you need to crop the canvas to square. Two approaches:

- Resize the canvas to 2400×2400 in Canva (Resize > Custom) and reposition the phone and background to fit the square. This works if your background image is wide enough to fill the square without cropping the phone.
- Alternatively, export the full 1170×2532px design, then crop to 2400×2400 in any image editing tool (Squoosh, Preview on Mac, Photos on iPhone). Center the crop on the phone and the key lifestyle props.

The square crop will cut off the top and bottom of the phone image. Frame the phone at the vertical center of your canvas, with enough background visible on the sides to establish the lifestyle context.

**Estimated total time, phone workflow**: 20 minutes for master template + (10–12 minutes x 20 additional products) = approximately 3.5 hours for all 21 phone-lifestyle images. This workflow is optional — only execute it if slot 5 is currently empty for any product. If slot 5 is being filled by a second flat-lay variant (tablet workflow), skip the phone frame workflow entirely.

## Section 4: Interior Grid Workflow (Flat-Lay Stock Compositing)

This workflow applies to the 6 Cluster D and E products (Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual, Meat/Fish Preservation Field Manual, Hunting/Fishing/Trapping Manual, Survival Garden Regional Plans, Native Plants Regional Guide, and the hunting/foraging content product). These use stock photos as the background context rather than physical photos.

**Canvas size**: 1200×900px for layout purposes, exported and expanded to 2400×2400 at final export.

**Estimated time per image**: 15–20 minutes including sourcing confirmation and compositing.

### 4.1 Stock Photo Compositing in Canva

By the time you reach this workflow, you will have already sourced and saved the stock images for all 6 products (see PHASE2_PHOTOGRAPHY_EXECUTION_PLAN.md, Week 1 tasks). The images are in `/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/stock/`.

**Step 1 — Upload the stock photo.**
Canva > Uploads > Upload file. Select the stock image for the first product (e.g., `small-scale-livestock-field-manual-lifestyle-1.jpg`).

Drag onto the canvas. Fill the full 1200×900 canvas. Adjust position so the strongest compositional element (a relevant prop, a landscape, a person's hands) is in the upper two-thirds.

**Step 2 — Export the product PDF cover as PNG.**
Before this session, export each product's PDF cover at 150 DPI as a PNG. Tool: any PDF reader (Preview on Mac, Adobe Reader). Save to a working folder on your desktop. You will upload these PNGs into Canva one at a time.

Upload the product's cover PNG into Canva. Drag it onto the canvas.

**Step 3 — Remove the background from the PDF cover PNG (Pro only).**
If the PDF cover has a green (#143b28) background that does not blend with the lifestyle photo, use Canva's Background Remover: select the PNG > Edit image > BG Remover > Apply. This strips the background so only the cover art and text float in the scene.

If using Canva Free: do not try to fake this step. Instead, place the cover PNG at 80% opacity and apply a subtle drop shadow (Edit image > Shadow > Offset, 25% opacity, 8px blur). At 80% opacity, the green background becomes semi-transparent and reads as a "printed page" overlaid on the scene rather than a digital sticker. This is a viable compositing technique without Pro.

**Step 4 — Position the product.**
Place the product PNG in the lower-left or lower-right third of the canvas, sized at 25–35% of canvas width. It should appear as a natural object in the scene — a field guide sitting on a log, a printed chart on a workbench, a laminated reference card propped against a jar.

Apply the drop shadow settings from Section 2. Add a very slight rotation (3–5 degrees) to make the overlay look like it has been casually placed rather than precisely positioned.

**Step 5 — Consistency across 6 stock products.**

The consistency check for stock-composited images is different from physical photos because the backgrounds are stylistically varied (each stock image has its own lighting, color temperature, and composition). The thread of consistency comes from:

- The product overlay size: always 25–35% of canvas width
- The product overlay position: always lower-left or lower-right corner
- The drop shadow settings: always the same values
- The product text overlay: always the same font, size, and position

Create a "consistency strip" — a thin horizontal bar at the bottom of every design in Deep Forest Green (#143b28), 60px tall, containing the brand name "Seedwarden" in Warm Cream (#F5EDD6), Playfair Display, 18px, centered. This strip is the same on every image across all 21 products, physical and stock alike. It is the visual anchor that makes a set of stylistically different photos read as a single coherent brand.

### 4.2 2x2 Preview Grid for Multi-Image Consistency Check

Before exporting, create a single 2×2 preview grid to check visual consistency across any four images at once. This takes 5 minutes and catches layout problems before they hit Etsy.

In Canva, create a new design at 2400×1800px. Add four grid cells (use the Grid layout element or manually add four 1200×900px rectangles with 10px gutters). Drop in four of your lifestyle images — mix physical and stock images — and view them together. If any image looks dramatically different in color temperature, brightness, or product placement, note it and fix it before exporting the full batch.

This preview grid is for your eyes only — do not export it to Etsy.

## Section 5: Batch Workflow and Efficiency

### 5.1 Master Template Approach

The master template approach is the difference between 20 hours of production and 10. Here is the exact discipline:

1. Create `SW-Master-Tablet` first. Perfect it entirely before touching any product-specific content. Every spacing decision, every text size, every shadow setting should be final.
2. Duplicate the master 20 times before editing any copy. In Canva: right-click the master in the project folder > "Make a copy" > repeat 20 times. Rename each copy immediately to its product slug.
3. Edit each copy by replacing only the variable elements: the background photo, the product PDF cover PNG, and the product name text. Every structural element (canvas size, text position, shadow settings, brand bar) stays identical.

This means you never rebuild a layout. You only swap content.

### 5.2 Work Order for Efficiency

Execute in this order to minimize context-switching:

**Round 1 (Days 1–2)**: Build all three master templates. Do not produce a single product-specific image yet. Test each master with a sample product. Finalize. Then duplicate.

**Round 2 (Day 3)**: Complete all 6 stock-composited images (Clusters D, E). These come first because the stock images are already sourced and saved, and the compositing step is the bottleneck. Clearing this cluster early means the physical photo products (which depend on your shooting schedule) do not hold up the entire batch.

**Round 3 (Days 4–5)**: Complete the 15 physical photo products after the shooting session is done. Process all Cluster A products together, then B, then C. Do not mix clusters mid-session — each cluster's photos share the same background color palette and props, so batch editing is faster within a cluster.

**Round 4 (Day 6)**: Export all 42 images. Run QA checklist (Section 6). Fix any issues. Export corrections.

### 5.3 Pro Batch Export

Canva Pro includes a "Bulk create" feature that can replace text and images across multiple designs from a spreadsheet. This is useful if your 21 product names and their associated image files are organized in a CSV.

To use Bulk Create:
1. In Canva, open a master template.
2. Click "Apps" in the left sidebar > Bulk Create > Upload a CSV.
3. Your CSV should have columns: `product_name`, `photo_filename`.
4. Map the CSV columns to the Canva text element (product name) and image element (background photo).
5. Canva generates all 21 variants automatically.

This feature works well for the text substitution but is less reliable for image substitution when the photos vary significantly in aspect ratio or composition. Test it on 3 products before committing to a full batch run.

### 5.4 Outsourcing Consideration: Fiverr vs. Solo

If time is the binding constraint, outsourcing the Canva production work to a Fiverr designer is a legitimate option. Here is the cost and time analysis:

**Solo production:**
- Time: 15–20 hours total (master template creation + 21 products x 3 workflows + QA + export)
- Cost: $0 (Canva Free) or $15 (one month Canva Pro)
- Control: Complete
- Timeline: 1 week if dedicated, 2 weeks if fitting around other work

**Fiverr designer:**
- Time: 2–4 hours of your time (briefing, asset handoff, review, revision rounds)
- Cost: $50–$150 for all 42 images (search "Etsy digital product mockup" or "Canva lifestyle mockup design" on Fiverr; filter by 4.8+ star sellers with relevant portfolio)
- Control: Moderate — you direct via brief, they execute, you approve
- Timeline: 3–5 business days for delivery, plus 1–2 rounds of revision
- Best option if: you have budget but not time, or if Canva feels friction-heavy after the first few hours

**What to include in a Fiverr brief:**
- Link to this playbook (share the file or paste the relevant sections)
- Your Canva Brand Kit share link
- The product names and their associated lifestyle photos as a .zip file
- The three canvas sizes (tablet, phone, flat-lay)
- The brand consistency strip specification (green bar, Seedwarden text, cream color)
- Export specs: 2400×2400px PNG or JPEG under 2MB

**Solo is recommended for this sprint** because the production work teaches you the template system. Future phases (Phase 3 social media content, seasonal refreshes) will reuse the same templates. If you outsource Phase 2, you will need to re-brief a designer every time you need new content. Learning the templates once costs 15–20 hours but pays back across every future production session.

### 6.3 Etsy Thumbnail Preview Test

1. Open the exported file in any image viewer.
2. Resize your view to 170px wide (on Mac: View > Fit to Width, then zoom to 7% of a 2400px image).
3. Or: take a screenshot of the export and paste it into a blank Canva canvas alongside a reference thumbnail from Etsy's search results. Scale your image to match the reference size.
4. Ask: Is the product visible? Is the lifestyle context clear? Is there any text that was supposed to be readable but now is not?

If the product overlay is too small to read in thumbnail view, go back and increase its size to at least 35% of canvas width.

### 6.4 Brand Consistency Verification

- [ ] No image reads as aspirational / Pottery Barn aesthetic — props look working, not decorative
- [ ] No competing brand logos visible in any frame
- [ ] Physical photo products (Clusters A, B, C) look warmer and more tactile than stock photo products (Clusters D, E) — this is expected and acceptable; the visual vocabulary does not need to be identical across clusters

## Section 7: Tools and Resources

### 7.1 Canva Alternatives (If Not Using Canva)

- **Adobe Express (free tier)**: Adobe's browser-based tool. Similar feature set to Canva Free. Stronger font library. Background remover available on the free tier in Express (unlike Canva Free). Good option if the Canva Free tier is too limited and you do not want to pay for Canva Pro.
- **Figma (free tier)**: More powerful layout tool, steeper learning curve. Worth using if you have Figma experience already. Not recommended for lifestyle photo compositing — its strength is UI/vector design, not photo editing.
- **GIMP (free, desktop)**: Open-source photo editor. Handles background removal, compositing, and export at any resolution. Time-intensive to learn if unfamiliar, but has no capability limitations. Use if you want a one-time setup with no monthly cost and full control.
- **Photoshop (if already subscribed)**: Best tool for compositing (background removal, perspective matching, lighting adjustment). If you have Creative Cloud, use Photoshop for compositing and Canva for any text overlay work.

For this playbook, Canva Free covers 80% of the work. The only genuine gap is background removal, which Canva Pro ($15/month) or Adobe Express Free solves cleanly.
