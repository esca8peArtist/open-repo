## Section 1 — What Track B Feedback Is Available by June 1 and How to Read It

Track B launched May 30. By June 1 evening you have 48–72 hours of real data. That is Day 3 of the contingency monitoring cycle defined in `CONTINGENCY_DECISION_THRESHOLDS.md`. Here is what is meaningful at that horizon and what is not.

### What Is Meaningful at Day 3

**Gist view count.** The Day 3 threshold is 70+ views for a GREEN signal, 30–70 for YELLOW, below 30 for RED. Views at this stage reflect distribution reach — who clicked through from Reddit, herbalist outreach emails, Instagram posts, and social shares. A view count above 70 at Day 3 tells you the distribution network is alive. Below 30 tells you the email and social posts did not travel, which is usually a sending or posting failure rather than a product problem.

**Organic sharing.** If any of the 15 pre-staged influencer contacts or any member of the organic audience has reshared the Gist URL or mentioned Seedwarden in their own channels without being asked, that is the strongest possible Day 3 signal. Organic sharing at this stage indicates genuine product-market fit for the free Zone Card distribution format. One organic share is more meaningful than 200 passive views.

**Influencer response rate.** Of the 15 herbalist influencers contacted during the May 28–30 outreach window, how many have responded by June 1? A response — even a polite "thanks, will look at it" — confirms the contact list is live and reachable. Zero responses at 72 hours suggests either delivery failures (check spam) or that the outreach angle needs adjustment.

**Kit email subscribers.** Each zone card download through the Kit landing page converts an anonymous visitor into a named subscriber. The number of subscribers added between May 30 and June 1 represents the actual conversion rate from the distribution push. Even 10–15 new subscribers at Day 3 is a functional baseline.

### What Is Not Meaningful at Day 3

Etsy traffic and sales are not meaningful yet if no Etsy listing was live at May 30 launch. The Zone Cards were distributed as a free Gist download to build the list before Etsy listings go live. Do not draw conclusions from Etsy silence at Day 3.

Reddit upvote counts are noisy at 48–72 hours because posts decay quickly. Check for removal notices — a removed post explains low counts and requires a format adjustment, not a messaging change.

Instagram follower count is not a Day 3 signal for a new account. Growth at this stage is measured in single digits and reflects algorithm cold-start, not product quality.

### The One Question to Answer From Day 3 Data

> Did the outreach reach real people who engaged with it, or did it go into a void?

If Gist views are above 30 AND at least one influencer responded AND Kit has at least a handful of new subscribers: the distribution system is working. Phase 3 messaging and audience strategy are validated. Proceed with June 1 decisions at full confidence.

If Gist views are below 30 AND no influencer has responded AND Kit is at zero: diagnose the sending failure before adjusting Phase 3 positioning. The most common causes are: outreach emails were not sent, posts contained a broken Gist URL, or the Reddit post was filtered. Fix the distribution failure first. Phase 3 is not affected by a Track B sending failure — but Phase 3 messaging optimization does depend on having real audience signal, which you will get from Day 7 and Day 14 instead.

## Section 2 — How Phase 3 Messaging Differs From Track B

This section exists because Track B and Phase 3 serve fundamentally different audiences. Track B distributed free Zone Cards to build a broad list of gardeners, foragers, homesteaders, and preppers. Phase 3 sells paid medicinal herb guides to certified herbalists, integrative health practitioners, and herbalism students. These are different people with different purchase triggers, different vocabulary, and different trust criteria.

Getting Phase 3 messaging right is the highest-leverage optimization available between May 30 and June 22. It costs nothing and affects every email, post, and Etsy listing that goes out during the sprint.

### Track B Audience: What Drove Them

Track B's Zone Quick-Start Cards succeeded with a self-reliance and seasonal-gardening message. The core appeal was practical utility: what to plant right now, in your specific zone, presented in a dense one-page reference format. The audience responded to the free + useful + zone-specific combination. They are motivated by self-sufficiency, food security, and accessible gardening knowledge.

Track B influencer contacts were herbalists, but the product being shared (Zone Cards) was a generalist gardening resource. The herbalists shared it because their audience overlaps with the forager and homesteader communities — not because the product spoke to clinical practice.

### Phase 3 Audience: What Will Drive Them

Phase 3 bundles are clinical-grade medicinal herb guides targeting three segments (from `PHASE_3_AUDIENCE_STRATEGY.md`):

**Segment 1 — Certified Herbalists and Practitioners (RH AHG, clinical herbalists, apothecary operators)**: These buyers want professional recognition, not utility. They will evaluate whether a guide demonstrates depth of botanical knowledge, sourcing ethics, and FTC-compliant framing before they trust it with their practice. The conservation angle — specifically the United Plant Savers At-Risk status disclosures and Forest Grown Verified sourcing — is the fastest credibility signal with this segment. The phrase "PLR-free original research" is also a differentiator, because this audience encounters generic herb guides constantly and has developed a sharp filter for them.

**Segment 2 — Integrative Health Practitioners (NPs, RNs, Naturopathic Doctors)**: These buyers are looking for patient education resources, not personal study. They need guides they can hand to patients without fear of legal or professional liability. The absence of disease claims and the presence of proper FTC disclaimers is a feature, not a constraint, for this audience. Lead messaging should use the phrases "patient education resource" and "practitioner reference" rather than "herbalist guide." The white-label license option — which no other Etsy herb guide seller offers — is the key differentiator for this segment.

**Segment 3 — Herbalism Students and Self-Educators**: These buyers are in a progression. They purchase educational resources that align with the curriculum they are studying. The key message here is curriculum alignment: Seedwarden bundles map directly to the modules in major herbalism programs (Herbal Academy, Rosemary Gladstar's correspondence course, LearningHerbs). This framing differentiates from generic herb content by demonstrating that the guides serve a specific learning objective.

### The Three Messaging Shifts

**1. From "practical and free" to "professional and authoritative"**

Track B: "Free Zone Quick-Start Card — what to plant right now."  
Phase 3: "Practitioner-grade medicinal herb cultivation guide — original research, sourcing ethics, FTC-compliant."

The word "practitioner-grade" does significant work. It signals who the product is for (professionals), what standard it meets (clinical quality), and why it is different from the general herb content flooding the market.

**2. From "self-reliance" to "sourcing ethics and conservation"**

Track B spoke to food sovereignty and independent growing. Phase 3 should speak to the ethics of medicinal plant commerce. The United Plant Savers at-risk species disclosures (Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, Echinacea angustifolia) and the Forest Grown Verified sourcing citations are not compliance checkboxes — they are the content that separates a Seedwarden guide from anything a PLR seller produces. Lead with the conservation story, particularly in Week 1 social content targeting herbalists.

**3. From "broad zone-based audiences" to "specialty-segmented outreach"**

Track B sent the same zone card to everyone in their zone. Phase 3 outreach should be segmented by bundle theme and practitioner specialty. The Women's Health bundle targets herbalists with a women's health specialty; the Respiratory bundle targets practitioners working with immunology and respiratory conditions; the Sleep bundle targets those working with anxiety and sleep disorders. The AHG directory allows filtering by specialty — use it. A practitioner who focuses on women's health is three times more likely to respond to an email about the Women's Health bundle than to a generic "we have herb guides" email.

### How Track B Signals Affect Phase 3 Positioning

If Track B herbalist influencers responded positively to the Zone Card and shared it: their audiences are warm. The Phase 3 launch email to those influencers can reference the Track B reception: "The zone cards got a strong response from your community — I wanted to let you know the Phase 3 medicinal herb guides are live." This creates continuity and goodwill.

If Track B influencers did not respond: the Phase 3 approach for those contacts should be a fresh introduction framed around the medicinal herb guides specifically, not a follow-up on the zone cards. Herbalists who did not engage with a general gardening resource may engage strongly with a practitioner-specific guide.

If Track B Kit subscriber cohort shows a high proportion of herbalist-tagged subscribers (herbalist tag applied based on interest signals): this cohort is the Phase 3 warm launch list. They have already opted in and shown botanical interest. The pre-launch announcement email goes to this segment two weeks before June 22.

## Section 7 — What Phase 3 Success Looks Like at Each Gate

These are the meaningful milestones to evaluate at each checkpoint. They are not binary pass/fail — they are signals that tell you whether to hold course or adjust.

**June 29 (Day 8 — Women's Health upload)**: Listing live by noon ET. View count above 50 in first 12 hours (Etsy algorithmic placement signal). First purchase within 72 hours would confirm the keyword cluster is working. No purchase in 72 hours is common for new listings — it is not a signal to adjust.

**July 5 (End of Week 2)**: Three Kit email metrics to check: Email 1 (lead magnet delivery) open rate above 40%, Email 3 (conservation story) open rate above 30%, Email 5 (first offer) click rate above 8%. If Email 5 click rate is below 5%: the offer framing is weak. Adjust Email 6 (Day 21 cross-sell) language before it sends.

**July 6–7 (Respiratory upload)**: Two live listings gives Etsy the ability to link them in search results. Watch for cross-listing views — Women's Health buyers clicking through to Respiratory. This is the first repeat purchase signal. A buyer who views both listings within 7 days has a 20–30% conversion probability on the second bundle.

**July 13 (Sprint close)**: Three KPIs determine Phase 3 Month 1 success:
1. Total views across all live listings (target: 500+ across three bundles in 22 days)
2. Conversion rate (target: 2–4% of Etsy listing views to purchase)
3. Kit subscriber growth from Phase 3 lead magnets (target: 30+ new subscribers)

If all three are at or above target by July 13: Phase 3 is performing at baseline. Proceed with post-sprint institutional outreach as planned.

If total views are above 500 but conversion is below 2%: the audience is finding the listings but not converting. Review listing copy — specifically the price justification paragraphs and the first image. A low-conversion, high-view pattern usually means a pricing or first-image trust failure.

If views are below 200 at sprint close: the listings are not being discovered. This is an SEO problem. Run the keyword density check immediately and resubmit titles and tags. Consider a single $20–30 Pinterest promoted pin on the Women's Health listing.
