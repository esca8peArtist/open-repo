The May 30 launch proceeds if and only if all five of the following pass on the May 28 evening verification:

1. **At least 4 of 5 launch guides** have complete photo sets (minimum 3 confirmed photos each: one root or habit, one leaf or flower, one archival or contextual frame).

If criteria 1 fails (fewer than 4 guides photo-complete): launch with whatever guides are fully ready; do not launch partial guides. If criteria 2, 3, 4, or 5 fail: do not launch until resolved. These are not aspirational targets — they are binary pre-conditions.

**Fallback date**: June 7, 2026. No revenue impact relative to the May 30 target has been modeled for a 7-day slip. The June 7 path is documented in `TRACK_B_PRODUCTION_PIPELINE.md Section 6`.

## Part 2: Location Scouting

### Cluster Assignment

Three photography clusters are assigned for the May 17-19 shoot window. Each cluster targets a distinct ecosystem and species set. Note that most Appalachian at-risk species are NOT to be photographed in the wild — specimens are procured from commercial suppliers and photographed as controlled studio props. Field sessions capture habitat context, lookalike comparisons, and ecological setting shots only.

### Cluster 1 — Temperate Forest Understory (May 17)

**Target species context shots**: Ramps habitat (residual colony), Black Cohosh habitat (open woodland), Bloodroot and Trillium ecological context, Wild Ginger as a lookalike comparison plant.

**Three Candidate Locations (Appalachian Region)**

**Candidate A — Pisgah National Forest, North Carolina (Cove Creek / North Mills River area)**
- Public land status: National Forest, no photography permit required for non-tripod incidental photography
- Ecosystem: Mixed mesophytic Appalachian cove — the highest-diversity temperate forest type in eastern North America
- Species expected: Ramps habitat (look for residual leaf material or seed stalks), Trillium grandiflorum (late bloom or fruit stage), Black Cohosh emerging leaf clusters, Wild Ginger colonies
- Access: Forest Road 1206 to Cove Creek Group Camp trailhead; 2-wheel-drive accessible
- GPS anchor point: 35.578°N, 82.694°W (Cove Creek area)
- Permission required: None for National Forest incidental photography. Confirm no Active Wilderness Area overlap at intended trail before arrival via fs.usda.gov/recarea/nfsnc/recarea/?recid=48098
- Distance context: Asheville, NC is the nearest hub city (30 minutes)
- Contact for site conditions: Pisgah Ranger District 828-877-3265; confirm trail open and no seasonal closure

**Candidate B — Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Tennessee/North Carolina (Laurel Falls / Alum Cave Trail area)**
- Public land status: National Park — commercial photography permits required (NPS Commercial Photography Permit, $50-200, 14-day lead time). Apply at nps.gov/grsm permits if this site is selected
- Ecosystem: Classic Southern Appalachian cove and mixed mesophytic forest
- Species expected: All target species above; exceptional Ramps habitat documented in spring iNaturalist observations
- Access: Laurel Falls Trail from Little River Road trailhead; well-maintained, paved section
- Note: If commercial permit is not obtained 14 days before shoot, this site is not available. Candidate A (National Forest) requires no permit and is the preferred choice for May 17.
- Contact: GRSM permits office 865-436-1291

**Candidate C — Nantahala National Forest, North Carolina (Wayah Bald / Appalachian Trail corridor)**
- Public land status: National Forest, no photography permit required
- Ecosystem: High-elevation Appalachian hardwood forest and heath bald
- Species expected: Black Cohosh in forest understory; ramp habitat at lower elevations (below 3,500 ft); Wild Bergamot at bald edges in late May
- Access: USFS Road 69 to Wayah Bald trailhead; 4WD recommended after recent rain
- GPS anchor point: 35.180°N, 83.551°W
- Contact: Nantahala Ranger District 828-524-6441

**Recommended selection**: Candidate A (Pisgah NF, Cove Creek area) for May 17. No permit required, accessible road, documented Appalachian cove diversity. Contact ranger district by May 13 to confirm trail conditions.

### Cluster 3 — Studio / Controlled Indoor Session (May 19)

**Purpose**: Prop photography for all procured specimens (dried roots, seeds, fresh ramp leaves, live potted plants). This is not a field session — it is a controlled tabletop shoot.

**Setup requirements**:
- North-facing window or overcast-sky window for even indirect light (no direct sunbeam)
- Two surfaces: white foam board or white card for identification shots; dark linen cloth (olive or dark green preferred) for lifestyle shots
- Props: US quarter and ruler for scale; small spray bottle for leaf misting; kitchen cutting board for root cross-sections
- No tripod required; phone camera at 12MP or higher

**Location**: Any indoor space with access to a large north- or east-facing window. Home kitchen, dining table, or studio space. This session requires no scouting, no permits, and no travel.

### Prop and Lighting Checklist

**Surfaces**
- [ ] White foam board (18x24 inch or larger) — primary ID shot surface
- [ ] Dark linen cloth, olive or charcoal (1 yard minimum) — lifestyle and atmospheric shots
- [ ] Wooden cutting board or unfinished wood plank — secondary surface for root cross-sections

**Lighting**
- [ ] North-facing or east-facing window identified; no direct sunbeam crossing the board surface at shoot time
- [ ] Overcast or cloudy condition preferred for outdoor sessions (eliminates harsh shadow)
- [ ] No flash — diffused window light only for all specimen shots
