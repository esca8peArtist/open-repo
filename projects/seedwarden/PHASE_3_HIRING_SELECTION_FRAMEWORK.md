## 3. First-Pass Screening Criteria

Run on every application within 12 hours of posting. Applications that do not meet the pass criteria below go to a "maybe" list only.

**Photographer — PASS requires all three**:
- Portfolio shows at least 3 examples of flat-lay, lifestyle, or product photography (not exclusively portraiture or events)
- Confirms July 5 start availability (check their reply message)
- Proposed rate falls within $1,200–$1,800 range (or negotiable if slightly above)

**Writer 1 (Educational) — PASS requires all three**:
- Portfolio includes 2+ published articles on herbal medicine, wellness, or natural health
- Mentions familiarity with contraindications, safety, or FTC compliance in their reply
- Word count on at least 1 submitted sample is 1,500+ words

**Writer 2 (Recipes) — PASS requires all three**:
- Portfolio includes recipes or step-by-step preparation guides (food, herbal, or natural products)
- Reply confirms comfort with dosage and food safety guidelines
- Confirms metric + US standard measurement experience

**Writer 3 (Clinical) — PASS requires all three**:
- Portfolio includes drug interaction panels, evidence-based health writing, or clinical/pharmacy-adjacent content
- Mentions access to or familiarity with NatMed Pro, Micromedex, or equivalent database
- Reply demonstrates awareness of evidence-tier framing (not "X prevents Y" language)

**Herbalist Consultant — PASS requires both**:
- AHG, NAHA, or equivalent credential stated — OR 5+ years professional herbalism (verify via LinkedIn or portfolio website)
- Mentions contraindication checking, drug interactions, or toxicology in their reply

**Coordinator — PASS requires all three**:
- 2+ years project management or operations experience described
- Names at least one email marketing platform (Kit, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or similar)
- Mentions Etsy, Shopify, or similar e-commerce experience

## 4. Weighted Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to score each candidate who passes first-pass screening. Score each dimension 0–10, then calculate the weighted total. A single automatic disqualifier overrides the total score.

### Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | What to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio Quality | 40% | Does recent work match the quality and subject matter this role requires? (See role-specific scoring guides below.) |
| Relevant Experience | 30% | Years and depth of directly applicable experience — not general writing or general photography, but the specific domain. |
| Communication | 20% | Responsiveness of their application reply; clarity of their questions; professional tone; whether they read the posting carefully (mentioned specifics). |
| Availability | 10% | Can they confirm July 5 start? Do they have conflicts in the July 5 – August 10 window? |

**Weighted score** = (Portfolio × 0.40) + (Experience × 0.30) + (Communication × 0.20) + (Availability × 0.10)

**Tier assignments**:
- Tier 1 (7.5+): Extend offer immediately
- Tier 2 (6.5–7.4): Extend offer only if Tier 1 rejects
- Tier 3 (5.5–6.4): Contact only if Tier 1 and Tier 2 both reject
- Below 5.5: Do not contact

### Automatic Disqualifiers (Override Any Score)

- Unavailable to start by July 8 (any role)
- Rate proposed is more than 25% above the top of the rate range and refuses to negotiate
- No published portfolio available for review (claimed experience only, nothing verifiable)
- Any unqualified health claim in their own published writing ("X cures Y") without evidence-tier framing (writers and herbalist only)
- For Herbalist Consultant: No verifiable credential or professional experience description

### Portfolio Quality Scoring (by Role)

**Photographer (0–10)**:
- 9–10: Five or more recent flat-lay or lifestyle projects with botanical or natural subjects; professional resolution; consistent white balance; warm aesthetic matching Seedwarden reference images
- 7–8: Three to four qualifying projects; mostly professional quality; minor color inconsistency; generally warm natural aesthetic
- 5–6: Two to three product photography projects; acceptable resolution; some lighting issues; uncertain botanical aesthetic match
- 3–4: Mostly portraiture or events; one to two product examples; clear limitations; poor aesthetic match
- 0–2: No relevant flat-lay or product work

**Writer 1 — Educational (0–10)**:
- 9–10: Five or more published wellness/herbal articles using Latin names, primary source citations, and FTC-compliant language; at least two pieces exceed 2,500 words
- 7–8: Three to four wellness articles; science-backed; some contraindication coverage; 1,500+ word capacity; educational tone
- 5–6: Two to three health/wellness pieces; basic sourcing; limited contraindication coverage; 1,200+ word capacity
- 3–4: One to two wellness blog posts; minimal sourcing; no contraindication coverage
- 0–2: No published wellness or herbal writing

**Writer 2 — Recipes (0–10)**:
- 9–10: Five or more detailed recipes or preparation guides; metric and US measurements; dosage guidance; storage and shelf-life; FTC-compliant safety note present
- 7–8: Three to four qualifying recipes; mostly complete format; minor gaps in dosage or storage
- 5–6: Two to three recipes; basic format; no dosage guidance; inconsistent measurements
- 3–4: Recipe writing but no dosage, safety, or storage component
- 0–2: No recipe or step-by-step preparation writing

**Herbalist Consultant (0–10)**:
- 9–10: AHG/NAHA credential verified; three or more published or consulting projects; demonstrates drug interaction and CITES/conservation knowledge
- 7–8: Verified credential or 5+ years experience; two to three projects described; mentions contraindication knowledge
- 5–6: Credential unverified but plausible; one to two projects described; basic contraindication mention
- 3–4: Experience claimed but not verifiable; no publication or consulting history
- 0–2: Cannot verify any relevant credential or experience

**Coordinator (0–10)**:
- 9–10: Three or more email marketing campaigns (Kit/Mailchimp/ConvertKit/ActiveCampaign); two or more e-commerce projects (Etsy, Shopify, or equivalent); multi-project tracking demonstrated; professional communication examples shown
- 7–8: Two to three email campaigns; one to two e-commerce projects; good tracking history
- 5–6: One to two email campaigns; one e-commerce project; basic tracking
- 3–4: Email or e-commerce experience but not both
- 0–2: No email or e-commerce experience

### Scoring Example

**Candidate: Photographer applicant A**
- Portfolio Quality: 8/10 (four flat-lay projects; warm aesthetic; one white balance inconsistency)
- Relevant Experience: 9/10 (6 years product photography; previous botanical and food work)
- Communication: 8/10 (replied within 3 hours; asked specific question about herb specimen shipping)
- Availability: 10/10 (confirms July 5 start, no conflicts through August)

**Weighted score**: (8 × 0.40) + (9 × 0.30) + (8 × 0.20) + (10 × 0.10) = 3.2 + 2.7 + 1.6 + 1.0 = **8.5 — Tier 1**

## 5. Reference Check Procedure

Conduct reference checks for all Tier 1 candidates after they accept the offer, before contract generation. One reference per candidate is sufficient unless a concern emerged during portfolio review.

**Format**: Email (async-friendly) or 5-minute call. Use this template verbatim:

> "Hi [REFERENCE NAME],
>
> I am following up on a reference for [CANDIDATE NAME], who has applied for a [ROLE] project with Seedwarden, a botanical guide publisher. This is a project-based contract (6 weeks, July–August 2026).
>
> I have three quick questions:
> 1. Quality: How would you rate [CANDIDATE]'s work quality on a scale of 1–10?
> 2. Reliability: Did [CANDIDATE] deliver on schedule? Any missed deadlines or quality issues?
> 3. Communication: How responsive was [CANDIDATE] during the project?
>
> A reply by email is completely fine. Thank you."

**Document the response** (one sentence per reference):
- Example: "Photo project client (Jordan K.): 9/10 quality, on-time delivery, responsive to feedback. No issues."
- Example: "Editor (wellness platform): 8/10 writing quality, revised once as requested, consistent communication."

**Score impact**: If a reference rates quality or reliability below 6/10, reduce the candidate's overall score by one tier (Tier 1 becomes Tier 2; Tier 2 becomes Tier 3). Log in PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md.

## 6. Background Verification Checklist

Run this for all candidates before generating the contract. This is not a formal background check service — it is a 15-minute verification against publicly available information.

For all roles:
- [ ] Search candidate's name + primary platform (Upwork, LinkedIn, personal website) — do search results match what they described in their application?
- [ ] Check Upwork contractor profile: job success score, review history, any negative reviews (read any review below 4 stars)
- [ ] Search their portfolio URLs: are the published pieces actually authored by them? (Check bylines.)
- [ ] No obvious misrepresentation in application (claimed credential that does not appear in profile)

For Herbalist Consultant additionally:
- [ ] Search their name in AHG member directory (americanherbalistsguild.com) — OR search NAHA member directory (naha.org) — OR verify professional website shows practice history
- [ ] If neither directory match nor verifiable professional site: ask directly ("Can you point me to your credential certificate or your professional practice website?")

For Writers 1 and 3 (clinical content):
- [ ] Review 3 published articles for FTC compliance. Any "prevents," "cures," or "treats" claims without evidence-tier qualifier: flag and request explanation before offer
- [ ] Confirm the articles are genuinely theirs (byline matches name)

For Coordinator:
- [ ] Check LinkedIn or professional profile for described email marketing and e-commerce experience
- [ ] If no LinkedIn: verify Upwork work history shows described platforms

**Red flag**: Any misrepresentation of credential, publication, or platform experience — do not advance to contract regardless of score.

- Consulting: Revision or supplemental notes up to 1,000 additional words per bundle.

- Contractor returns pro-rated amount of any milestone paid for undelivered scope within 5 business days.
