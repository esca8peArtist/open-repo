## Template 3B — Course Correction Email

**When to send**: When a delivered draft or image set has a correctable issue. Send within 48 hours of receiving the deliverable — do not let a revision request linger.

**Subject**: [NAME] — [DELIVERABLE] revision notes

---

Hi [NAME],

Thank you for delivering [DELIVERABLE] on [DATE]. I have reviewed it and want to flag [NUMBER — "one" / "two" / "a few"] items before we move forward.

**[ISSUE 1 — be specific, not evaluative]**:
- Describe the exact problem: "The Ashwagandha section (page 4) includes the phrase 'Ashwagandha reduces cortisol.' This is a therapeutic claim without evidence-tier framing. Per our FTC Quick Reference: this needs to read 'Research suggests ashwagandha may support cortisol regulation — see [citation]' or equivalent."
- Describe the fix needed: "Please revise this claim and any similar unqualified therapeutic statements throughout the bundle. I have flagged three others in the attached notes."

**[ISSUE 2 — if applicable]**:
- Describe the problem: "Session 1 image `respiratory-flatlay-03.jpg` has a visible shadow across the guide cover text from the overhead lamp. The text is partially unreadable at Etsy thumbnail size (170×135px)."
- Describe the fix: "Can you reshoot that angle with the lamp moved further left, or send an alternate frame from the same session if you have one? I need this resolved before I can approve Session 1."

**What is working well**:
- [ONE POSITIVE NOTE — even in a correction email, acknowledge what landed right. This keeps the working relationship constructive.]

**Revision due date**: [DATE — give 2–3 days for writers; 3 business days for photographers]

I want to stay on the [DATE] delivery schedule for the next bundle. Let me know if there are any questions on the revision notes.

[YOUR NAME]
Seedwarden

## Quick Reference — Which Template to Send When

| Situation | Template | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Candidate passes evaluation, offer ready | Template 1 — Offer Email | Within 24 hours of decision |
| Active production week — Monday | Template 2 — Weekly Check-In | Every Monday, Weeks 2–4 |
| Deliverable received, quality good | Template 3A — Praise | Within 48 hours of receipt |
| Deliverable received, needs revision | Template 3B — Course Correction | Within 48 hours of receipt |
| All deliverables complete, final payment pending | Template 4 — Final Handoff Checklist | Before releasing final payment |
| Bundle goes live on Etsy | Template 5 — Payment + Testimonial | Within 7 days of launch |

**Tone note across all templates**: Specific over vague. "The Echinacea section handles the two-species distinction correctly" is more useful than "great work." Contractors who receive specific feedback deliver better work in the next round.
