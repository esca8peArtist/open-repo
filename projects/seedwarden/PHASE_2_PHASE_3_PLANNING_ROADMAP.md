# Seedwarden Phase 2 and Phase 3 Planning Roadmap
## Track-Independent Planning — July 2026 through March 2027

**Prepared**: June 4, 2026 by seedwarden-agent (claude-sonnet-4-6)

**Purpose**: This document designs Phase 2 (July–December 2026) and early Phase 3 (January–March 2027) content strategy, business model, revenue projections, and constraint analysis in a form that is valid regardless of which track (A, B, or both) activates first. The Track A/B decision governs Phase 1 launch mechanics only. Phase 2 begins June 22 with the medicinal herbs writing sprint regardless of which track activated Phase 1.

**Relationship to existing documents**: This document extends the horizon of PHASE_1_TO_PHASE_2_TRANSITION_ROADMAP.md (v3.0), which closes at the July 13 sprint end. It does not modify existing operational checklists, gate criteria, or Phase 3 (June 22–July 13) production timelines documented elsewhere. ## Section 1: Phase 2 Content Strategy and Scope (July 1–December 31, 2026)

### 1.1 Guide Count: From 18 to 40–50

Phase 1 delivers 18 wild edibles and zone-card guides as the lead magnet and initial product catalog. Phase 2 (the June 22–July 13 writing sprint) delivers 3–5 medicinal herb bundles covering 14 species. The path from 18 to 40–50 total guides by December 31, 2026 follows three production waves:

**Wave 1 — June 22–July 13 (already planned)**: 3 medicinal herb bundles (Option C scope)
- Women's Health Bundle: Black Cohosh, Vitex, Red Clover, Calendula, Lavender (5 species)
- Respiratory Health Bundle: Elderberry, Mullein, Echinacea ×2, Thyme (4–5 species)
- Sleep and Nervines Bundle: Valerian, Passionflower, Lemon Balm, Lavender condensed (4 species)
- Total new species: 14. Upload window: June 29 (Women's Health), July 6–7 (Respiratory), July 13 (Sleep)

**Wave 2 — August 1–31 (botanical identification guides, already planned in PHASE_4_BOTANICAL_IDENTIFICATION_GUIDE_ROADMAP.md)**: 18 single-species identification guides
- Wave 2a (9 guides) uploaded August 1; Wave 2b (9 guides) uploaded August 31
- These are 4–6 page PDF field references, not clinical monographs — production velocity is higher than clinical bundles
- The 18 species are drawn from Phase 3 herb list and North American native medicinal priority list
- Each guide adds one Etsy listing; collectively they expand the catalog to 36–37 total products

**Wave 3 — October 1–November 30 (Phase 2 second bundle batch)**: 2–3 additional therapeutic bundles
- Immunity and Adaptogens Bundle: Astragalus, Ashwagandha, Eleuthero, Reishi (4 species)
- Digestive Health Bundle: Marshmallow Root, Slippery Elm, Ginger, Fennel, Chamomile (4–5 species)
- Optional third bundle contingent on writing capacity: Skin and Lymphatic Bundle (Cleavers, Red Clover [repeat], Calendula [repeat], Burdock)
- Upload target: October 1 (Immunity), November 1 (Digestive), November 30 (Skin/Lymphatic if in scope)
- Total with Wave 3: 40–50 guide products depending on species overlap and third bundle execution

**What categories does Phase 2 expand into?**

Phase 1 = wild edibles and zone-specific growing calendars (homesteader/forager audience)
Phase 2 Wave 1 = clinical herb bundles (women's health, respiratory, sleep — broadest buyer interest)
Phase 2 Wave 2 = single-species botanical ID guides (forager/naturalist/educator audience; Tier 1 practitioner tools)
Phase 2 Wave 3 = immunity/adaptogens and digestive health bundles (high-search-volume clinical categories)

The category sequence is deliberate: Wave 1 captures the highest search-volume clinical categories (sleep, respiratory, women's health are the top 3 herbalism search clusters on Etsy). Wave 2 adds a product type (ID guides) that serves a different buyer persona. Wave 3 fills the clinical bundle catalog with two high-LTV categories that practitioners consult frequently.

### 1.2 Bundle Structure: Keep the 5-Bundle Architecture

The existing 5-bundle framework documented in the project (Apartment Grower, Food Sovereignty, Forager Starter, Prepper/Homesteader, and the planned Practitioner 3-Bundle Library) should be retained as the organizational spine. Phase 2 adds medicinal herb bundles on top of this structure; it does not replace it.

**Recommended Phase 2 bundle architecture (end of 2026)**:

| Bundle | Price | Contents | Audience |
|--------|-------|----------|----------|
| Wild Edibles Starter Bundle | $30–35 | Wild Edibles guide + Zone Quick-Start Card + Foraging Field Journal | High-intent forager; gift buyer |
| Women's Herb Bundle | $35–40 | Women's Health clinical bundle + Botanical ID guides for included species | Women's wellness; holistic health buyer |
| Respiratory and Immune Bundle | $35–40 | Respiratory + Immunity bundles combined | Cold/flu season; homesteader; prepper |
| Sleep and Nervous System Bundle | $28–32 | Sleep and Nervines + 2 ID guides | Wellness buyer; stress/anxiety audience |
| Practitioner Library Bundle | $120–150 | All 3–5 clinical bundles + 5 priority ID guides | RH, student herbalist, clinical practitioner |

**Bundle evolution rule**: Do not introduce a new bundle structure until Phase 1 bundle data (months 1–3) reveals which bundles convert and which do not. The Apartment Grower and Food Sovereignty bundles from Phase 1 are the data baseline. If they underperform relative to individual sales, the Phase 2 bundle discount depth needs adjustment. If they overperform, the Phase 2 Practitioner Library pricing ($120–150) is likely conservative.

### 1.3 Distribution: August–November

Phase 2 distribution follows the seasonal content calendar established in SEEDWARDEN_PHASE_2_CONTENT_ROADMAP.md v2.0. Key distribution milestones in the August–November window:

- **August 1**: Botanical ID guides Wave 1 live (9 guides). Tier 1 and Tier 2 practitioner subscriptions launch (per PHASE_4_PRACTITIONER_TIER_PROGRESSION.md). AHG Symposium outreach begins (August 14 Symposium window is the primary Tier 2 acquisition event; promotional pricing $125/year for annual subscriptions during August 14–16).
- **August 14**: AHG Symposium week. Mentor must be named in all published listings by August 12. This is the non-negotiable credibility gate for practitioner channel credibility.
- **September–October**: Fall harvest seasonal lift. Respiratory and Immunity bundles align with cold/flu-season search intent build from September onward. Digestive Health bundle uploaded October 1 into the seasonal lift window.
- **October 1**: Tier 3 Clinical Specialist tier launches. International Traditions library must be assembled by this date.

### 1.4 Early Evaluation: October–November Data Read

The Phase 2 early evaluation window (October–November) should answer four questions:

1. **Which clinical bundle category is converting at highest rate?** Women's Health, Respiratory, or Sleep? The highest-converting category gets Tier 2 content deepened first and gets featured in AHG Symposium outreach collateral.
2. **Are botanical ID guides generating net-new buyers or upselling existing buyers?** If ID guide buyers are primarily new-to-Seedwarden (trackable via Etsy buyer history), they represent a forager/naturalist audience that Phase 3 should court directly. If they are primarily existing buyers, they are an LTV driver for the existing herbalist cohort.
3. **What is the Tier 2 subscriber conversion rate from Etsy buyers?** Target: 8–12% of clinical bundle buyers upgrade to Tier 2 within 90 days. Below 5% signals the Kit Day 30 upgrade email copy needs revision. Above 12% signals the Practitioner Library Bundle and Tier 2 annual pricing should be promoted more aggressively.
4. **Has any single influencer share generated a measurable traffic spike?** If yes, identify that influencer as a priority Phase 3 affiliate partner candidate.

## Section 2: Practitioner Tier Progression (RH/Specialist)

### 2.1 Tier Architecture (Sourced from PHASE_4_PRACTITIONER_TIER_PROGRESSION.md)

The three-tier system is already fully designed. This section summarizes the credential requirements, content gates, and pricing relevant to Phase 2/3 planning.

**Tier 1 — Herbalist** (entry-level, no credential required)
- Access: Any Phase 2/3 bundle (individual Etsy purchase) + 5 complimentary botanical ID guides
- Price: $20–22/bundle or $35 one-time Herbalist Starter Pack (3 bundles + 5 ID guides)
- Launch: August 1, 2026 (auto-qualifies existing buyers with 3+ bundles)
- LTV target: $30–65 before upgrade; 8–12% upgrade to Tier 2 within 6 months

**Tier 2 — Registered Herbalist (RH)**
Credential requirements (any of the following):
- AHG Registered Herbalist (RH designation) — verified against AHG public directory
- NAHA Professional Member Level 5 — verified against NAHA find-a-practitioner directory
- Herbal Academy Advanced Herbal Certificate — verified via program completion email

Content access gates (Tier 2 unlocks):
- All Phase 2/3 bundle library (5 bundles, all published)
- All 18 botanical ID guides
- Drug interaction and contraindication database (searchable, covers 8 drug classes and high-interaction herbs)
- Monograph library: Commission E summaries, WHO TRM summaries, BHP summaries, ESCOP summaries
- Ayurvedic and TCM cross-reference at introductory level
- Clinical case study library (5–8 cases per bundle theme at launch, expanding quarterly)

Pricing:
- Monthly: $18/month
- Annual: $165/year ($13.75/month effective)
- AHG Symposium promotional pricing (August 14–16 only): $125/year
- Platform: Launch on Gumroad (10% fee); migrate to Kit Commerce when 50+ subscribers

Revenue targets:
- 75–100 Tier 2 subscribers by December 31, 2026
- At 75 subscribers (50 monthly, 25 annual): $900 + ~$344 = ~$1,240/month effective recurring
- At 100 subscribers: $1,500–1,700/month effective

**Tier 3 — Clinical Specialist (ND/PhD/CNS)**
Credential requirements (any of the following):
- Licensed Naturopathic Doctor (ND) in any AANP-regulated state
- MD/DO with documented integrative medicine focus
- Registered Dietitian with CNS credential
- PhD in botanical medicine, pharmacognosy, or ethnobotany (academic affiliation required)
- FNIMH or MNIMH (UK); DAc or equivalent in TCM for TCM module; Vaidya through NAMA for Ayurvedic module
- Discretionary: 10+ years documented clinical practice + published clinical/academic contribution

Content access gates (Tier 3 adds to Tier 2):
- Full International Traditions Library (Commission E full text where public-domain, WHO TRM full text, complete Ayurvedic Materia Medica, full TCM clinical module)
- Dosing protocol documentation (evidence-based, ND-level prescribing reference, with FTC-required clinical-use disclaimers)
- Research database (curated PubMed search strategies, clinical trial abstracts, evidence-tier ratings)
- Limited content publishing rights (1,500 words/quarter for professional newsletters, clinic handouts, conference materials)
- Peer review network participation with subscription discount for accepted reviews

Pricing:
- Monthly: $55/month
- Annual: $550/year ($45.83/month effective)
- Academic/nonprofit rate: $35/month or $350/year
- Launch: October 1, 2026 (International Traditions library assembly complete)

Revenue targets:
- 25–40 Tier 3 subscribers by December 31, 2026
- At 25 subscribers: ~$1,100–1,375/month effective recurring

### 2.2 Credential Verification Workflow

Verification is currently manual (2–5 business days for Tier 2; 5–10 business days for Tier 3). The workflow is:

1. Subscriber submits application form (Typeform or Google Form) with credential claim and identifier
2. Operator cross-checks against public directory (AHG, NAHA, state license board, institutional email)
3. Approved: access code activated; onboarding sequence begins
4. Declined: specific guidance provided; appeals reviewed within 5 business days

Manual verification is sustainable up to approximately 200 Tier 2/3 verifications per quarter. Beyond that scale, a random-audit self-attestation model or a credential verification API (Verosint, Certif-ID) should be evaluated. This threshold is not expected before mid-2027 at Phase 3 growth rates.

## Section 3: Business Model Refinement

### 3.1 Revenue Architecture by Source

Phase 2 generates revenue from four distinct streams that should be tracked separately:

| Stream | Mechanism | Margin | Phase 2 target (Dec 31 run rate) |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------------------------------|
| Etsy individual sales | Cold organic search, email referral, social referral | ~88% (net of Etsy fees) | $500–1,200/month |
| Etsy bundle sales | Same channels; higher AOV | ~88% | $200–500/month additional lift |
| Tier 2 subscriptions | Gumroad/Kit; converted from Etsy buyers via Kit email sequence | ~90% (Gumroad 10% fee) | $1,240–1,700/month |
| Tier 3 subscriptions | Direct outreach, AHG/AANP channels | ~90% | $1,100–2,200/month |
| Mountain Rose Herbs affiliate | Kit Sequence C emails; social bio link | 10% commission | $100–300/month |
| **Total combined** | | | **$3,140–5,900/month by Dec 31** |

These ranges are wide because the primary variable is the Tier 2 subscriber count at AHG Symposium. The Symposium is the single highest-leverage event in the Phase 2 calendar — 25 annual subscriptions at $125 (promotional) = $3,125 in one week, plus 25 subscribers generating $2,750/year in recurring revenue. The pre-Symposium outreach quality (Mentor named in listings by August 12) is the primary determinant of Tier 2 subscriber acquisition velocity.

### 3.2 Pricing Elasticity — Tier 2 Monthly Subscription

The three pricing scenarios for Tier 2 monthly ($18, $29, $39) have different market implications:

**$18/month**: Positions below most professional tool subscriptions (Natural Medicines Database is $200–300/year for individual practitioners). Appeals to AHG RH students and early-career herbalists who are cost-sensitive. Reduces friction at first conversion but limits ARR per subscriber. At 100 subscribers: $1,800/month = $21,600/year.

**$29/month**: Aligns with mid-tier professional SaaS pricing ($29 is the Kit Creator price point — practitioners who already use Kit understand this tier). Reduces casual conversion but attracts more committed practitioners. At 100 subscribers: $2,900/month = $34,800/year. Break-even for writing a second Tier 2 content batch (30 hours at $30/hour = $900) achieved in approximately 10 days at this price.

**$39/month**: Approaches the mid-tier professional CE subscription range (AHG CE bundles, Herbal Academy advanced memberships). Likely to reduce conversion rate significantly for early-career practitioners. Justified only if Tier 2 content is demonstrably deeper than alternatives at the same price. The drug interaction database alone does not justify $39/month without the full monograph library being operational.

**Recommendation for Phase 2 launch**: Start at $18/month with $165/year ($13.75/month). This prioritizes conversion rate and subscriber count over per-subscriber revenue during the launch phase. Re-evaluate at 100 subscribers (estimated Month 5–6 post-launch): if 90-day churn is below 8%, test $24/month for new subscribers while grandfathering existing at $18. The annual pricing discipline ($165/year = 24% savings) is the primary LTV driver.

### 3.3 Affiliate Channel

**Mountain Rose Herbs** (10% commission, 24-hour cookie) is the primary affiliate channel. Kit Sequence C deploys to subscribers with the "60-days-active" and "etsy-purchaser" tags. Current target: 0.5–2.0% click-through rate from affiliate email links.

**Strictly Medicinal Seeds** (Oregon-based, USDA Organic certified) is the secondary affiliate channel. Seed-specific sourcing email (Kit Sequence C Email 1) targets subscribers in planting zones 3–7 who have purchased cultivation-focused guides.

**AHG and NAMA directories as distribution channels** (not affiliate): The AHG member directory (americanherbalistsguild.com/directory) and NAMA practitioner directory (namacb.org) are outreach channels for Tier 2/3 direct subscription offers, not affiliate relationships. The channel value is practitioner-to-practitioner credibility, not commission revenue. Do not treat these as affiliate channels — cold outreach to the AHG directory should focus on Tier 2 content value and peer review network participation as the primary offer.

Is there better reach than AHG/NAMA directories? The NUNM alumni network, Bastyr University continuing education mailing list, and OANP (Oregon Association of Naturopathic Physicians) newsletter represent higher-density concentrations of Tier 3-eligible practitioners in a single channel. However, access to these channels requires either a formal institutional relationship or a paid advertisement — neither is available at Phase 2 launch. AHG cold outreach remains the most accessible high-credential channel at scale.

### 3.4 International Channel Sequencing

**Canada — June 2026**: Canadian practitioners are eligible for Tier 1 and Tier 2 subscriptions immediately. Etsy Canada listings are automatic (same listing, Canadian currency conversion by Etsy). The AHG has Canadian members (AHG is US-based but does not restrict membership geographically). No separate Canadian launch required — Canadian subscribers are captured by the existing infrastructure.

**Australia — October 2026**: The National Herbalists Association of Australia (NHAA) represents approximately 600 registered herbalists. NHAA credential is internationally recognized and should be added to the Tier 2 credential list for Australian practitioners. Etsy Australia listings are automatic. A targeted Kit email broadcast in September 2026 to existing subscribers outside North America (tagged by timezone in Kit) can surface the Tier 2 offer to Australian subscribers. No separate launch infrastructure required.

**UK/EU — 2027**: The NIMH (National Institute of Medical Herbalists) is already listed in the Tier 2 and Tier 3 credential verification protocols. UK practitioners can subscribe to Tier 2 and Tier 3 from Day 1 of the August 1 launch. The EU/European market requires GDPR compliance confirmation (Gumroad is GDPR-compliant; Kit is GDPR-compliant; Etsy handles European VAT automatically). No structural barrier exists, but a targeted EU outreach campaign is a Phase 3 (January 2027) initiative, not Phase 2. European Commission E content in the Tier 2 monograph library is the most natural entry point for European herbalist outreach messaging.

## Section 4: Phase 3 Content Outline (January–March 2027)

The term "Phase 3" in this section refers to the January–March 2027 period, not to the June 22–July 13, 2026 writing sprint (which this project's existing documents call "Phase 3" using the older nomenclature). ### 4.1 Botanical Identification Guides (15–20 guides)

The 18 botanical ID guides from August 2026 (Phase 2 Wave 2) complete the North American native medicinals series. The January–March 2027 production wave adds:

**Visual keys and harvesting protocol guides** (10 new species): Focus on medicinal plants with poor existing Etsy coverage and high herbalist search demand. Priority species selection criteria:
- UpS "At-Risk" or "To-Watch" status signals practitioner concern and drives content sharing
- Commission E positive monograph coverage means European practitioners need the ID guide for clinical use
- At least one lookalike species that is commonly confused in the field (ID confidence content performs well)

Priority candidate species for January–March 2027 wave:
1. American Ginseng (Panax quinquefolius) — UpS At-Risk; high practitioner demand; complex lookalike (wild sarsaparilla)
2. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) — UpS At-Risk; forested habitat; extreme lookalike risk (false rue anemone)
3. Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) — already in Phase 2 Women's Health bundle; companion ID guide adds cross-sell
4. Wild Bergamot (Monarda fistulosa) — native pollinator companion plant; distinctive; beginner-accessible
5. Joe-Pye Weed (Eutrochium purpureum) — high landscape and ethnobotany interest; underserved in ID guide market
6. Blue Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) — UpS At-Risk; frequently confused with Black Cohosh; safety-critical content
7. Wild Ginger (Asarum canadense) — common in deciduous forest; distinctive; edible and medicinal
8. Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) — North American native; historical medicinal use; safe for harvest
9. Spikenard (Aralia racemosa) — UpS To-Watch; Appalachian tradition; aromatic root
10. Pipsissewa (Chimaphila umbellata) — shade-tolerant native; woodland medicinal; low Etsy competition

**Habitat and harvesting content sourcing strategy**:
- Wikimedia Commons CC-licensed photographs (already established as the primary photo source in the current standing task for habit photos)
- USDA PLANTS database (plants.usda.gov) for range maps, habitat notes, and conservation status — all public domain
- iNaturalist (inaturalist.org) observation records for phenology data (bloom timing, habitat associations)
- UpS conservation status from United Plant Savers (unitedplantsavers.org) — freely available Species At-Risk list

### 4.2 International Herb Traditions (Monographs, Regional, Traditions Bundles)

Three bundle concepts for the January–March 2027 window:

**Monographs Bundle** (Tier 2 access, also sold individually): Curated collection of Commission E positive monograph summaries for the Phase 2 herb roster, formatted as practitioner-usable reference cards. Each card: herb name, Commission E indication, contraindications, dosing range (Commission E dose), interaction alerts, and evidence tier. 15 herb cards covering all Phase 2 species with Commission E coverage. Format: single PDF, 30–40 pages. Price: $22 individual, included in Tier 2 subscription.

**Regional Bundle — North American Native Traditions**: Ethnobotanical use documentation for Phase 2 and Phase 3 species across Indigenous and European-settler traditional medicine systems. Focus on well-documented sources (Daniel Moerman's Native American Ethnobotany database is comprehensive and freely accessible; USDA PLANTS cites ethnobotanical uses). Each entry includes the traditional use, the culture attributed, and a sourcing note distinguishing traditional knowledge documentation from clinical recommendation. 10–12 species. Format: PDF, 40–50 pages. Price: $25 individual, included in Tier 2 subscription.

**Traditions Bundle — Ayurvedic and TCM Cross-Reference**: The 5-herb preview in Tier 2 content (from PHASE_4_INTERNATIONAL_TRADITIONS_SOURCING.md) scales to a 12–15 herb cross-reference document for the January 2027 bundle. Each herb entry: Ayurvedic counterpart (Sanskrit name, dosha affinity, taste, traditional use), TCM counterpart (Pinyin name, channel affinity, energetic character, classical formula appearances). Sourcing per PHASE_4_INTERNATIONAL_TRADITIONS_SOURCING.md: original summaries of public-domain Ayurvedic texts, Bensky and Gamble abstractions for TCM (cited, not reproduced). Format: PDF, 50–70 pages. Price: $30 individual, included in Tier 3 subscription. Tier 2 receives an introductory 5-herb sample.

**Which traditions first?**: European (Commission E, ESCOP) first — already documented, copyright-clear sourcing strategy established, and the primary need for AHG RH and NIMH-credentialed practitioners. Ayurvedic second — NAMA practitioners (Ayurvedic) represent the next-largest US practitioner audience with a specific need for quality Ayurvedic cross-references. TCM third — requires the most specialized knowledge base and sourcing (Bensky and Gamble, Huang Huang) and serves a smaller, more specialized practitioner cohort in the current subscriber base.

### 4.3 Specialist Tier Content (Beyond RH — ND/CNS/PhD)

Phase 3 adds two content types that are exclusive to Tier 3:

**Clinical case study library expansion**: From 5–8 cases per bundle theme at Tier 3 launch (October 2026) to 25–30 cases by March 2027. Sources: AHG Herbalist Quarterly published cases (abstracted and anonymized with attribution); peer-reviewed literature (PubMed); Phase 3 peer-reviewer network contributions. Format: 2–4 paragraphs per case (patient presentation, clinical reasoning, herb selection, outcome, practitioner notes). Production velocity: 2–3 cases per week during the January–March 2027 writing window. Total investment: 15–20 writing hours to reach 25 cases.

**Advanced pathophysiology integration**: For 5 Phase 2 species with the richest RCT literature (Valerian, St. John's Wort, Echinacea, Black Cohosh, Elderberry), produce extended clinical monographs (8–12 pages each) incorporating mechanism of action, pharmacokinetic data, clinical trial summaries, and drug-herb interaction depth. These are Tier 3-exclusive documents. Each monograph is linked to the relevant Phase 2 bundle in the Tier 3 subscriber portal. Production estimate: 8–10 hours per monograph; 5 monographs = 40–50 hours. This is the primary writing commitment of the January–March 2027 window alongside the 10 botanical ID guides.

**Research paper integration**: Curated PubMed search strategy documents for each Tier 3 monograph species (search string, date range, MeSH terms, key RCTs to retrieve). These are 1–2 page reference documents, not written summaries. Production time is minimal (1–2 hours each), but they provide significant practitioner value and differentiate Tier 3 from any existing commercial herbalism subscription.

### 4.4 ZIM Platform Integration

The Phase 4 Botanical Identification Guide Roadmap (existing document) specifies that guides should be structured with consistent HTML-compatible markdown for ZIM packaging. The January–March 2027 botanical ID guide batch should:

1. Use the same 5-section format (Identity at a Glance, Visual ID Key, Habitat and Range, Harvesting Protocol, Evidence Summary) to enable batch ZIM packaging without reformatting
2. Include USDA PLANTS range map citations (URL-referenced, not embedded images) — ZIM packagers will resolve these to cached versions
3. Avoid Etsy-specific CTAs in the guide body text (ZIM distribution removes the commercial context; CTAs would appear broken in the offline format)

The ZIM submission target is the OpenZIM library (openzim.org) and the Kiwix library (kiwix.org/en/opds). Submission requires a consistent content structure and a clear open-license declaration. The Phase 3 botanical ID guide batch should be designated CC BY-SA 4.0 for the ZIM distribution (the Etsy commercial listing is the paid access point; the ZIM version is the open-access version). This is the "open-repo botanical feature" mentioned in the orchestrator task brief.

Whether the ZIM and Etsy versions should be identical or whether the Etsy version should include practitioner-specific content that the ZIM version omits is a decision the operator should make before January 2027 production begins. The recommendation here is that the ZIM version matches the Etsy guide exactly (no content withholding from the open version) and that the Etsy listing's value-add is the format (print-ready PDF with cover design) and the practitioner-bundle context, not the information itself.

## Section 5: Constraint Analysis

### 5.1 Writing Capacity

The Phase 1–3 writing arc through March 2027:

| Phase | Task | Hours | Timeline |
|-------|------|--------|----------|
| Phase 2 Wave 1 (existing plan) | 3 medicinal herb bundles, Option C scope | 36–44 hrs | June 22–July 13 |
| Phase 2 Wave 2 (botanical ID guides) | 18 single-species ID guides at 2–3 hrs each | 36–54 hrs | July 14–August 31 |
| Phase 2 Wave 3 (second bundle batch) | 2–3 therapeutic bundles (immunity, digestive, skin/lymphatic) | 24–36 hrs | October 1–November 30 |
| Phase 3 botanical ID guides | 10 additional ID guides | 20–30 hrs | January–March 2027 |
| Phase 3 advanced monographs | 5 extended clinical monographs (8–12 pages each) | 40–50 hrs | January–March 2027 |
| Phase 3 case studies | 25 case study summaries | 15–20 hrs | January–March 2027 |
| Phase 3 tradition bundles | Monographs, Regional, Traditions PDFs | 20–30 hrs | January–March 2027 |
| **Total through March 2027** | | **191–264 hrs** | |

Is 191–264 hours through March 2027 realistic? The breakpoint is in the three distinct production windows:

- **June 22–July 13 (22 days)**: 36–44 hours = 1.6–2.0 hrs/day. This is the most intensive sprint of the entire arc and was already assessed as achievable (PHASE_1_TO_PHASE_2_TRANSITION_ROADMAP.md describes this as "survivable for 22 days" at 8–9 hrs/day writing during the sprint, with social and admin time included). The document here records only the writing component at 36–44 hrs; the 8–9 hrs/day figure includes all other activity.
- **July 14–August 31 (48 days)**: 36–54 hours = 0.75–1.1 hrs/day. Botanical ID guides are shorter and more formula-driven than clinical bundles. This is a sustainable pace alongside community builder recruitment (opens July 1) and social posting.
- **October–November (60 days)**: 24–36 hours = 0.4–0.6 hrs/day. A very light pace; two therapeutic bundles can be produced at this rate while maintaining Phase 2 social distribution and monitoring.
- **January–March 2027 (90 days)**: 95–130 hours = 1.0–1.4 hrs/day. Achievable without a second writer at a sustained but not sprint pace. The advanced monographs are the most time-intensive component (40–50 hours); these should be front-loaded in January to avoid the spring/planting content season competing for writing time in March.

**Assessment**: The total writing commitment (191–264 hours through March 2027) is realistic for a solo operator averaging 1–2 hours of writing per day across the timeline, with two intensive sprint windows (June 22–July 13 and January 2027). It does not require a second writer. ### 6.4 Diminishing Returns: When Does Phase 4 Make More Sense Than Deepening Phase 2/3?

The point of diminishing returns for deepening Phase 2/3 content appears at approximately:

- **Tier 2 saturation**: 200+ subscribers at $18/month. The AHG RH addressable audience in the US (4,000–6,000 members) puts the realistic addressable Tier 2 market at 500–2,000 subscribers given outreach intensity. Deepening content benefits the existing subscriber base but does not substantially attract new subscribers beyond this point without a new channel (e.g., AHG institutional partnership, academic curriculum adoption).
- **Etsy guide saturation**: 50+ guides covering all major clinical herb categories and the primary botanical ID guide species. Beyond 50 guides, incremental catalog expansion has diminishing SEO benefit because the herb categories begin to overlap and each new guide competes with existing guides for the same search queries.
- **Phase 4 expansion makes more sense when**: Monthly recurring subscription revenue exceeds $5,000/month (post-Phase 3, expected February–March 2027), a new clinical category emerges with demonstrated search demand that Phase 2/3 doesn't cover (likely: mushroom/fungi therapeutics, flower essences, or topical preparations), or an international partnership (NIMH, NHAA) offers a credibility halo that justifies a dedicated international product line.

The Phase 4 expansion signal to watch is the Tier 3 subscriber mix. If Tier 3 subscribers are predominantly NDs (rather than PhD researchers or CNS practitioners), they are signaling unmet needs in clinical dosing protocols and pharmacokinetic content — the content direction that defines a Phase 4 ND-specialist tier expansion. If they are predominantly academic researchers, Phase 4 should prioritize the research database depth and literature curation over clinical dosing.
