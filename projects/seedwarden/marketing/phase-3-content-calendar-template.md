# Seedwarden Phase 3 — Content Calendar Framework

This template extends the existing Day 1–30 launch calendar into a reusable framework for ongoing Phase 3 content production. The launch calendar (`marketing/social-media-calendar.md`) covers the opening month. This document provides the repeating structure, seasonal adjustments, and product-to-theme mapping that governs Month 2 onward.

---

## 1. Content Mix Ratios

The Phase 3 content mix is calibrated for a growing account in the homesteading/urban gardening niche. It is based on the engagement benchmarks and algorithm behavior documented in `phase-3-social-media-growth-strategy.md`.

| Category | Target Share | Posts per 30-Day Month | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Educational (how-to, explainer) | 35% | 10–11 posts | Highest completion rate; builds authority |
| Values / political | 15% | 4–5 posts | Drives follows from aligned audience; keep factual |
| Product feature | 20% | 6 posts | One per product cluster; never more than 2 in a row |
| Behind the scenes / personal | 15% | 4–5 posts | Trust-building; balances the product posts |
| Community / engagement | 10% | 3 posts | Polls, Q&A, seed swap mentions; invite participation |
| Relatable / entertaining | 5% | 1–2 posts | Algorithm reward for completion; keeps feed human |

**Rule**: Never post two product features consecutively. Always sandwich a product post between at least one educational or community post.

## 2. Posting Frequency by Platform

Derived from Phase 3 strategy research. The cadences below apply once the initial 20–30 piece content bank (Month 1) is built.

### TikTok
- **Month 1 (Launch)**: 5–7 videos per week (daily if possible — algorithmic momentum)
- **Month 2–3 (Steady state)**: 4–5 videos per week
- **Optimal posting times**: Tuesday–Friday, 7–9 am and 7–9 pm local time; avoid Monday and Sunday for new posts
- **Video length**: 30–60 seconds hard maximum. Educational hooks in first 3 seconds. Single topic per video.
- **Production approach**: Batch-film 5–7 videos in one session per week. Phone camera, one good window, no studio required.

### Instagram
- **Reels**: 3–4 per week (primary growth lever; Reels generate 67% of Instagram's total engagement)
- **Carousels**: 1–2 per week (educational content, infographics, comparison charts)
- **Stories**: Daily (behind-the-scenes, polls, quick updates — relationship layer)
- **Feed posts**: Taper to 1 static per week max; Reels dominate
- **Aesthetic note**: Earth tones, natural light, real photography. Canva templates acceptable for carousels; avoid corporate stock photos for anything personal-brand-oriented.

### Pinterest
- **Cadence**: 5–7 fresh pins per week, spaced consistently (schedule via Tailwind or Pinterest native scheduler)
- **Format**: Vertical 2:3 ratio. Text overlay with search-intent phrase. Product mockup images (already generated from Phase 2) are the core visual.
- **Linking**: Every pin links to the specific Etsy listing — not the shop homepage. Reduce clicks to purchase.
- **Do not**: Post 30 pins in one day and nothing for two weeks. Pinterest rewards consistency over volume spikes.
- **Board coverage**: Rotate pins across all 7 boards each week (see Pinterest board structure in `marketing/social-media-calendar.md` Part 2).

## 3. Seasonal Content Angles — May 2026 Window

Phase 3 launches after 1–2 months of Etsy conversion data. If Phase 1 goes live in late April/early May 2026, Phase 3 organic social begins in May/June. The following seasonal angles are specific to the May–July 2026 window.

### May 2026: Late Spring Planting Prep
The highest-intent gardening moment of the year. People are moving seedlings outdoors, making last frost judgments, and buying seeds they should have started weeks ago. Content angles:

- "Last frost passed — here's what to direct sow right now" (educational, high search intent)
- "I started seeds too late — here's how to catch up" (relatable; drives seed starting kit traffic)
- "What to plant in May if you're in [Zone 5 / 6 / 7]" (zone-specific content; create 3 short variations)
- "Hardening off: the step most first-time growers skip" (educational, high completion rate)
- Companion planting angle: "What to plant next to your tomatoes before it's too late" — links to Companion Planting Chart ($5)

### June 2026: Garden Launch and First Harvest
Content shifts from preparation to active growing. First harvests are social media gold — community engagement peaks.

- "First harvest from seed" content format (encourage followers to share their own)
- "My container setup — a real mid-season look" (behind the scenes)
- Preservation pivot: "What to do when your lettuce bolts" (intro to fermentation/preservation content)
- Succession sowing: "You can sow a second round of beans right now" (educational)
- Seed saving awareness: "Mark your best tomato plant now — here's why" (preview of seed saving content)

### July 2026: Peak Season and Companion Planting Payoff
Peak growing season. Content focuses on managing abundance, troubleshooting, and building toward preservation.

- Hot sauce angle: "I grew my own hot peppers and made this" (Grow Your Own Hot Sauce product feature)
- Food preservation introduction: first wave of "what to do with your harvest" content
- Seed saving process content: fermentation jars, drying trays, the visual satisfying process
- "What I'd do differently this season" (honest retrospective; trust-building)
- Community: seed swap planning content (fall swaps start organizing in summer)

### Evergreen Angles (any month)
These perform consistently regardless of season and can fill any week:

- Food sovereignty education (seed consolidation, heirloom history, Percy Schmeiser)
- "Heirloom vs. hybrid vs. GMO" explainer variations
- Beginner guides: "start here if you've never grown food before"
- Product-as-solution: pair a common gardener problem with the relevant guide
- Founder story / shop origin content (one rotation per month maximum)

## 6. Content Production System

### Weekly Batch Session (1.5–2 hours)

One session per week covers the full week's production:

1. **Film all Reels** (45–60 min): 4–5 short videos in a single shoot. One setup, multiple topics. Phone propped up, one good window, seed packets and guides as props.
2. **Write captions** (20 min): Pull from the content calendar. Keep a swipe file of hashtag stacks (organized by post category) so you're not building from scratch each time.
3. **Build Pinterest pins** (20 min): Canva. Vertical 2:3, product mockup image, text overlay with search phrase. Three variants per product: different background color, different text angle, different mockup orientation. Schedule via Tailwind.
4. **Queue IG carousels** (if that week's plan includes one): Export carousel slides from Canva, schedule in Later or Meta Business Suite.

## 7. Tracking and Optimization

At the end of each 30-day block, record the following before building the next month's calendar:

| Metric | Where to find it | Action threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Top 3 TikTok videos by completion rate | TikTok Business analytics | Replicate format; double this content type next month |
| Instagram posts by saves | IG Insights | Saved = educational value. More carousels on this topic. |
| Pinterest: top boards by link clicks | Pinterest Analytics | More pin variants for top boards; rotate lower boards |
| Etsy traffic by source | Etsy Stats > Traffic Sources | Identifies which platform is actually driving clicks |
| Etsy conversion rate by listing | Etsy Stats > Listings | If TikTok drives views but conversions are low, the listing needs work — not the content |

**The only metric that pays rent is Etsy click-throughs that convert.** Use TikTok follows and Pinterest impressions as leading indicators, but optimize final decisions based on Etsy referral data.
