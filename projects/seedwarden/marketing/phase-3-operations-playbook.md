## Part 1: TikTok Content Creation System

### Video Production Specs (Shoot-Ready Reference)

Every Seedwarden TikTok video uses the same technical setup. Standardizing this eliminates pre-shoot decisions and makes batch filming sustainable.

**Camera setup**:
- Device: iPhone or any modern Android with 1080p or better
- Orientation: 9:16 vertical, always
- Resolution: 1080 x 1920 px minimum; record in the highest quality the device supports
- Frame rate: 30fps standard; 60fps optional for close-up prop shots
- Stabilization: prop phone against a book stack, or use a $12 phone tripod. Hand-held is acceptable for walking shots but creates inconsistency across videos
- Distance from lens: arm's length for talking-head; 12–18 inches for flat-lay close-ups
- Export format: MP4, H.264. No platform watermarks. CapCut and native camera apps export correctly by default.

**Lighting setup (non-negotiable)**:
- Natural window light, shoot between 9am and 2pm
- Window should be to the side, not directly behind — back-lit shots create silhouettes
- Overcast days are better than direct sun (even light, no harsh shadows)
- If natural light is consistently unavailable: one 45W daylight LED softbox at 45 degrees, positioned at chest height. Do not use ring lights — the catch-light pattern reads as influencer content, not educational.

**On-screen text (captions)**:
- Add auto-captions via TikTok's in-app captioning or CapCut before posting. Non-negotiable for accessibility and for the 40% of viewers who watch without sound.
- Captions font: white with black outline. Keep text within the center safe zone (rows 15–75%, center 60% of width). See `phase-3-platform-asset-specs.md` for exact safe zone dimensions.
- Do not add text overlays that duplicate the spoken caption word for word — use text overlays only for emphasis words, on-screen counters, or slide headers.

**Video length rule**: Maximum 60 seconds. Hard stop. If a topic cannot be covered in 60 seconds, it is two videos. TikTok's algorithm rewards completion rate above all else. A 30-second video completed by 70% of viewers outranks a 90-second video completed by 20%. The homesteading niche specifically overindexes on short-form educational content — the 2026 platform data confirms 21–60 second videos generate the highest completion in this category.

**Batch filming protocol (weekly, one session)**:
1. Pick one surface and one light setup. Do not move between videos — consistency of environment reads as brand coherence to first-time visitors.
2. Script the first 3 seconds of each video before filming. The hook is the only scripted element. Everything else is spoken naturally.
3. Film 5–7 videos per session without reviewing footage between takes. Review after the batch is complete. This avoids the trap of endless retakes that kills momentum.
4. Total filming time per week: 45–60 minutes. Editing time: 20–30 minutes. Weekly time investment: under 2 hours.

### Upload Schedule Optimization

Based on TikTok algorithm data for the homesteading niche:

**Best posting days**: Tuesday through Friday. Monday and Sunday consistently underperform for educational content in this niche. Saturday morning (8–10am) is an exception — it outperforms Tuesday–Friday for "weekend project" content (seed saving, container setup).

**Best posting times** (local time, whichever zone the account targets most):
- Morning window: 7:00–9:00 am (catches commuters and early risers before the day starts)
- Evening window: 7:00–9:00 pm (highest engagement volume, most competitive)
- For Phase 3 with limited posting volume (4–5 per week): prioritize the evening window. The morning window is secondary.

**What to avoid**: Posting at 12pm–2pm (lowest engagement period for this niche), posting on Sunday evening (algorithm deprioritizes new content going into low-traffic Monday), and posting more than one video within a 4-hour window (TikTok distributes one video at a time per account — rapid posting dilutes each video's initial push).

**Scheduling tool**: TikTok's native scheduler is sufficient. Set posts 24–48 hours in advance. Do not schedule more than 7 days ahead — the platform's scheduler has historically de-prioritized far-scheduled content.

### Engagement Response Protocol

TikTok's algorithm weights comment response rate as a ranking signal. Comments responded to within 60 minutes of posting get distributed to a second audience wave. These templates minimize time while maintaining authentic voice.

**Comment type: genuine question about content**
Response: Answer the question directly in 1–2 sentences. If the answer is complex, add "I made a whole guide on this if you want the full process — link in bio." Never paste this CTA without answering the question first.

**Comment type: personal experience share ("This happened to me too")**
Response: Acknowledge specifically. "The paper towel method is so tempting and so wrong — I did the same thing. The fermentation jar is the only reliable method." Use their specific detail if they shared one. Do not paste a link.

**Comment type: skepticism or pushback**
Response: Engage without defending. "Fair — [acknowledge their point]. The context I was missing when I started was [brief explanation]. Does that change anything for you?" Never dismiss, never lecture.

**Comment type: "Where can I buy this?" or "Do you have a guide?"**
Response: "Yes — it's in the Etsy shop, link in bio. It's [price], PDF download, instant access." Brief. Always include price — it filters intent and prevents wasted clicks.

**Comment type: tagging a friend**
Response: Welcome the tagged person. "@[username] glad you found your way here — if you're just starting out, the [product name] is where I'd begin, it covers [brief description]." Tag the original commenter only, not both.

**DM protocol**:
All product-inquiry DMs get the same three-part response: (1) acknowledge, (2) specific recommendation based on what they said, (3) price and link. Keep under 40 words. Longer responses reduce reply rates.

DM template for "I want to start growing but don't know where to start":
> "Start with the Apartment Seed Starting Kit ($9) — it's designed for exactly that situation. Walks you through soil, containers, and what to plant in sequence. Here's the link: [Etsy URL]. Any questions, just reply here."

DM template for "Do you ship seeds?":
> "Everything is digital — PDF downloads, instant access after purchase. No physical shipping, no waiting. Full catalog at [Etsy URL]."

### Story Mechanics (Daily)

Stories are the relationship layer, not the discovery layer. They should take under 10 minutes per day to produce.

**Daily Story structure (3–5 slides, total 60 seconds or less)**:

1. **The check-in slide**: One image or 5-second video from the current season. Seed germination tray, a mason jar, a plant update. No caption needed except maybe a date or location. This is the "I'm here and alive" signal.

2. **The engagement slide**: One interactive element per day, rotating:
   - Poll: Two options, gardening-related ("tomatoes first or peppers first?")
   - Question sticker: Open-ended ("What's confusing you about starting seeds?")
   - Quiz: One gardening fact with 3 answer options (educational + fun)
   - Countdown: Link to a new product listing going live, or a seasonal milestone

3. **The link slide** (2–3 times per week, not every day):
   - One product, one image, one link sticker. "This is the [product name] — $[price]. If you're [specific situation], it's the right guide." That is the entire copy.
   - Do not post link slides every day — it trains the audience to skip Stories because they expect a sales pitch.

4. **Repurposed Reel clip** (when a Reel performed well that day):
   - Export the strongest 15-second clip from the week's TikTok, post as a Stories video with "full version in the feed" text. Drives Reel views from the Stories audience.

**Behind-the-scenes Story content from Phase 2 raw photos**:
- In-progress prop arrangements before the shoot (seeds spilled across a cutting board, mason jars lined up before placement)
- "This is what the product actually looks like on a tablet" — show the open PDF on a real screen
- Outtakes: the photo that didn't work (too dark, awkward composition) — these perform well because they humanize the process
- The editing screen: Lightroom or Snapseed with the lifestyle photo visible — "before" and "after" swipe

### Bio Link Strategy

The Instagram bio gets one link. Rotate it based on current priority. Do not use a Linktree in Phase 3 — the extra click reduces conversion by 30–40% compared to a direct Etsy link.

**Phase 3 bio link rotation**:

- **Default (90% of the time)**: Direct Etsy shop URL. `etsy.com/shop/seedwarden`
- **During a lead magnet push** (when driving email sign-ups): Kit landing page URL for the Seedwarden Starter Pack. Switch back to Etsy after the campaign week ends.
- **During a product launch** (new listing going live): Direct URL to the specific new listing page. More specific = higher conversion.

**Bio copy** (140 characters max):
```
Heirloom seeds + food sovereignty guides for city growers
PDFs for apartments, balconies, containers
↓ Browse all guides
```

This copy is direct, specific, and addresses exactly who the account is for. "City growers" is the audience filter. "PDFs for apartments" signals the product format and eliminates confusion about physical products. The arrow drives the bio link click.

**Lead magnet email CTA in bio** (for the email push week):
Change the arrow line to: `↓ Free guide: 5 heirloom varieties for small spaces`

## Part 3: Pinterest Pin Strategy

### Pin Design Specs and Templates

**Required dimensions**: 1000 × 1500 px (2:3 ratio). This is the algorithm-preferred format — Pinterest buries 1:1 and landscape pins in favor of 2:3 vertical. All pin exports from Canva should use this template size.

**Text overlay formula**:
```
[Search-intent headline — 5-8 words]
[Supporting line — benefit or method — 8-12 words]
[Brand name small at bottom — "Seedwarden | Etsy"]
```

Example: "How to Save Tomato Seeds at Home / Fermentation method — works for every open-pollinated variety / Seedwarden | Etsy"

**Font requirements**: Bold or semi-bold sans-serif (Montserrat Bold, DM Sans, or Open Sans Bold all work in Canva). Minimum effective size at 1000px width: 48px for headline, 32px for subline. Test readability by viewing the pin at 25% zoom — if you can't read the headline, it's too small for mobile thumbnail.

**Color and contrast rule**: Every pin must pass the 4.5:1 contrast ratio test. Practical rule: white text on a dark overlay (overlay at 50–70% opacity) over any lifestyle photo. Or dark brown/forest green text on a cream or light natural background. Never light text on a medium-tone photo background — it fails on mobile.

**Seedwarden pin color palette**:
- Background overlays: `#2D2D2A` (charcoal, 55% opacity), `#3B4A3F` (forest green, 50% opacity)
- Text: `#FFFFFF` (on dark overlay) or `#1A1A18` (on light background)
- Accent: `#C4A35A` (warm brass/wheat — use for product price or brand name callout)
- Never: neon, stark white backgrounds, cool blue tones — inconsistent with Seedwarden's earthy brand

### Batch Creation from Phase 2 Assets (63 Pins — Month 1)

21 products × 3 pin variants = 63 pins. At 2 per day, this covers the full first month of Pinterest with zero new content creation.

**Time to create all 63**: 3–4 hours in a single Canva batch session. Setup takes 30 minutes (build the three base templates); each pin after that takes 3–4 minutes (swap photo, change headline text, adjust overlay).

**The three-template system**:

Build these three Canva templates once. Reuse for every product.

**Template 1 — Product mockup pin** (commercial intent, direct Etsy link):
- Background: product lifestyle photo from Phase 2 (`etsy-ready/[product-slug]-slot4.jpg`)
- Text overlay: dark overlay at 55% opacity over bottom 40% of image
- Headline: product title reformatted as search phrase ("Seed Saving Guide PDF — Heirloom Varieties")
- Subline: key benefit ("Covers fermentation method, storage, and viability testing")
- CTA: price tag graphic in brass color — "$14 | Instant Download"
- Brand: "Seedwarden | Etsy" in small text at very bottom
- Link: direct to specific Etsy listing

**Template 2 — Benefit-focused pin** (broader keyword targeting, same listing):
- Background: same lifestyle photo or a different Phase 2 raw photo from the same cluster
- Headline: search intent phrase, not product title ("How to Save Heirloom Tomato Seeds")
- Subline: outcome/result ("Step-by-step fermentation method + storage guide")
- No price callout — this pin targets discovery, not purchase intent
- Link: same Etsy listing
- This pin captures users at an earlier stage of intent than Template 1

**Template 3 — Educational/hook pin** (longest lifespan, highest repin potential):
- Background: a props-only shot from Phase 2 (no tablet, no product overlay) — seed envelopes, mason jars, dried peppers
- Headline: curiosity or list hook ("7 Seeds Worth Saving Before Fall")
- Subline: implicit product tie-in ("Complete guide — link below")
- No explicit price
- Link: Etsy listing description (which contains the educational content) or a future blog post
- This pin accumulates repins for months because it looks editorial, not commercial

**Build order for 63 pins** (aligned with PHASE2_PRODUCT_PRIORITIES.md tier structure):

1. Tier 1 products first (21 pins, Days 1–10.5): Survival Garden, Hunting Manual, Livestock Manual, Meat/Fish Preservation, Harvest Preservation

### Rich Pins Setup (Etsy Catalog Integration)

Pinterest's Rich Pins for products pull live price and availability data directly from the linked page. For Etsy sellers, this means the pin automatically shows the current price and "In stock" status below the image — this is a meaningful click-through rate signal.

**Setup (one-time, 20 minutes)**:

1. Go to Pinterest Business account → Settings → Claimed accounts
2. Add your Etsy shop URL as a claimed website (Etsy supports this via meta tags that Etsy automatically generates)
3. Go to developers.pinterest.com/tools/url-debugger/ and submit one Etsy listing URL
4. Pinterest validates the Rich Pin metadata from Etsy's product pages automatically
5. Once validated, all pins linking to Etsy listings will display price and availability

**Article Pins** (for future use when a blog exists): Not applicable in Phase 3. Enable when a seedwarden.co website with blog content exists.

2. In Email 5, update the "if you're new to heirloom growing / if you're ready to start saving seeds / if you're interested in containers" recommendations to match Phase 1 sales data (which products actually convert with which audience segments).

Link in bio." This P.S. is seasonal and should be updated each quarter.

### Weekly Newsletter Template

Begins in Week 5 of Phase 3 (after welcome sequence runs its course for initial subscribers). Cadence: weekly, every Thursday. Thursday sends consistently outperform Monday–Wednesday in open rate for this audience segment based on Kit's niche benchmark data for lifestyle/education lists.

**Template structure** (800–1,000 words):

---

**Subject line formula**: One of three types, rotating:
- Curiosity gap: "The seed I almost didn't grow this year (and why I changed my mind)"
- Practical value: "3 things to do in your garden this week"
- Story lead: "My first attempt at seed saving was a disaster. Here's what I got wrong."

**Body structure**:

**Section 1 — The growing update (150–200 words)**
What is happening in the garden or workspace right now. Specific, not generic. "The fermented tomato seeds I started in March are germinating at 94% — higher than packet-purchased seeds in every comparison I've done." This section is never promotional. It is the "I'm a real person" credential.

**Section 2 — One technique or piece of information worth knowing (300–400 words)**
One useful thing the subscriber can apply this week. Matches the season. Examples:
- May: "How to harden off seedlings without losing them to transplant shock"
- June: "Mark your best tomato plant now — here's the 2-minute seed saving setup that prevents you from forgetting which one it was"
- July: "The lacto-fermentation setup that works in any kitchen"
This section is the reason subscribers stay subscribed. Never cut it short for a product mention.

**Section 3 — Product spotlight (100–150 words)**
One product, one paragraph. Lead with a specific use case ("If you're at the end-of-summer harvest overwhelm stage, the Harvest Preservation Field Manual is exactly what you need right now"). Include price, a direct Etsy link, and one sentence about what's inside. Never hype. Never scarcity tactics.

**Section 4 — The mailbag (optional, 100–150 words when used)**
Answer one subscriber question from the previous week's reply emails or Story Q&As. Attribute by first name only ("Sarah asks: does companion planting actually work or is it just aesthetics?"). The mailbag builds community and increases the reply rate on the newsletter itself, which improves deliverability.

**Closing**: One line. Not a signature block. "Good growing, [your name]." No Unsubscribe guilt-tripping. No "If you found this valuable..." phrasing. Just close.

---

**Newsletter subject line swipe file** (pre-built, pull from this list):

- "What the seed packet doesn't tell you about storage"
- "Three heirloom varieties I'm growing this season (and why I picked them)"
- "The container size question I get most often"
- "I made a mistake with my bean seeds. Here's what happened."
- "Why seed saving feels hard (and the one step that makes it easier)"
- "The difference between fermenting hot sauce and fermenting tomato seeds"
- "A question from a subscriber I couldn't answer in one line"
- "What to do when your lettuce bolts"
- "This is why zone maps feel confusing (and how to actually use them)"
- "What I'd plant if I were starting over with one 5-gallon bucket"

### Segment Strategy

Kit allows tagging subscribers based on behavior. Set up three segments on Day 1:

**Segment A — "Interested in seed saving"**: Triggered when a subscriber clicks any link in the welcome sequence related to the Seed Saving Field Manual, Food Sovereignty Starter Guide, or Anti-Catalog. Suppress from beginner apartment-growing content. Weight toward seed-focused product feature emails.

**Segment B — "Urban/apartment growers"**: Triggered when a subscriber clicks anything related to Container Blueprint, Apartment Plant Catalog, Apartment Growing Complete Guide, or Apartment Seed Starting Kit. Weight toward apartment-growing seasonal content.

**Segment C — "Food preservation interested"**: Triggered when a subscriber clicks anything related to Harvest Preservation, Fermented Harvest, or Grow Your Own Hot Sauce. Weight toward preservation seasonal content (peak: August–October).

**Implementation**: In Kit, create three "Click triggers" (one per segment). When any subscriber clicks a link tagged with that segment's relevant products, Kit automatically adds the segment tag to their profile. This requires no subscriber action — it is invisible behavioral segmentation.

**Segment use in practice**: Once segments are populated (4–6 weeks of data), split the Thursday newsletter by segment: Segment A gets the seed-saving-weighted version, Segment B gets the apartment-growing version, Segment C gets the preservation-weighted version. The framework is the same — only the product spotlight section (100–150 words) changes between versions. This takes 15 extra minutes per week and measurably improves click rates.

### Win-Back Campaign (Non-Openers)

Activate in Month 3 of Phase 3. Target: subscribers who have not opened any of the last 6 emails.

**3-email win-back sequence** (send over 10 days, then remove non-responders from list):

**Win-back Email 1 — Subject: "Still there?"**
Body (100 words max): "I've been sending emails for a few months and I realize some of them might not be landing for you. Before I clean up my list, I want to ask: are you still growing anything this season? If so, hit reply and tell me what's happening in your garden — I read every response. If not, no hard feelings — the unsubscribe link is at the bottom and I won't take it personally. I'd rather have a small list of people who actually care about this stuff than a large one that isn't reading it."

**Win-back Email 2 — Subject: "One last thing before I go quiet" (Day 5)**
Offer a free resource they haven't seen: a single-page printable (companion planting reference, seed storage chart, or succession sowing schedule — built in Canva in an afternoon). No purchase required. This is the re-engagement offer. Subscribers who open and click are moved back to active status. Non-openers proceed to Email 3.

**Win-back Email 3 — Subject: "I'm removing you from this list (not rudely)" (Day 10)**
Body: "I'm cleaning up my list to keep only people who find this useful. If you want to stay subscribed, click this button: [Stay Subscribed]. If not, no action needed — you'll be removed automatically in 48 hours." Subscribers who do not click are removed from the list via Kit's automation. This is the cleanest implementation — it gives one final opt-in and removes non-responders automatically.

**Why this matters**: A healthy Kit list with 60%+ open rates and active engagement delivers better in newsletters (higher inbox placement, lower spam filtering) than a large, disengaged list. Every 90 days, run the win-back flow.

## Part 6: Quick Reference — Decision Trees

These are the triage rules for the most common Phase 3 operations decisions.

**"Should I post today even though I'm short on time?"**
Yes, if you can do a 30-second talking-head video with one point and a product name in the last 5 seconds. No, if posting something mediocre means missing a higher-quality post later in the week. Post only when you have something worth saying, but never go more than 3 days without posting during Phase 3 Month 1 — the algorithm treats 3-day gaps as inactivity.

**"Which product should I feature this week?"**
Check Etsy stats first: which product had views but no purchases in the last 7 days? That product has traffic but is not closing — a lifestyle-focused social post may be the push it needs. If no clear conversion lag, default to the product rotation in the 30-day calendar.

**"A video got unexpectedly high views. What do I do?"**
Immediately post a follow-up video on the same topic. Different angle, different detail, same subject. High-performing content in this niche consistently generates repeat-performance follow-ups on the same topic within 48 hours. Do not post a product pitch as the follow-up — extend the education first.

**"My Pinterest views are flat after 2 weeks."**
Check: Are pins linking to specific Etsy listings (not the shop homepage)? Are you posting consistently (2+ per day), not in batches? Are board names keyword-optimized, not generic? Are pins 2:3 ratio, not square? Any one of these is typically the cause. Fix the most obvious issue first. Pinterest takes 2–4 weeks to show traction even with correct setup.

**"An influencer responded positively. What's the next step?"**
Reply within 24 hours. Confirm which products they want (free digital download — generate a complimentary link via Etsy's "gift" feature or email them the PDF directly). Agree on format (TikTok video, IG Reel, or their preference). Set a soft timeline: "No rush — whenever it fits your content schedule, ideally within the next 4–6 weeks." Then update the outreach tracker. Do not follow up more than once if they go quiet — a second follow-up 3 weeks later is acceptable, a third is not.

**"I have no Phase 2 lifestyle photos yet. Can I still start Phase 3?"**
No. Phase 3 content without lifestyle assets relies entirely on talking-head videos and product mockups. This is viable but meaningfully lower-converting than content with real lifestyle photography as B-roll. Delay Phase 3 by 1–2 weeks to complete Phase 2 photography (minimum: Tier 1 products — 5 lifestyle images). These 5 images alone unlock 15+ pieces of content.
