## What Seedwarden Is

Seedwarden is an Etsy shop selling digital guides for people growing food in cities and small spaces. Everything is a PDF download: seed saving manuals, container gardening plans, heirloom variety guides, food preservation handbooks, urban growing planners.

The brand is not a homestead. It is an apartment. The content is for people who want to grow food in whatever space they have — balconies, fire escapes, south-facing windows — without pretending they have a farm. The politics are real: seed consolidation, heirloom variety loss, food sovereignty. But the tone is practical, not preachy.

Products range from $5 (Companion Planting Chart) to $22 (Survival Garden Regional Plans bundle). Most fall between $8 and $18. These are impulse-range digital purchases for a buyer who just watched a video about seed saving and wants to go deeper.

## What Makes These Products Different

A few things that creators in this space have responded to:

**Honest small-space framing.** Every guide assumes the reader is in an apartment or a small urban plot, not a half-acre. There is no fake-aspirational "imagine your sprawling garden" framing. This matters to your audience because they are tired of being sold to by content that ignores their actual conditions.

**Food sovereignty angle.** Three companies (Bayer, Corteva, ChemChina) now control 60% of the global commercial seed market. Between 1903 and 1983, 93% of vegetable varieties available in the US disappeared. The Seedwarden guides — especially the Food Sovereignty Starter Guide ($8) and the Anti-Catalog: 30 Heirlooms Worth Growing ($10) — address this directly. If your audience cares about food systems, this hooks.

**Practical depth.** The Seed Saving Field Manual ($14) covers fermentation method for tomatoes, dry processing for beans and peppers, long-term storage, and viability testing. It is not a 10-slide overview — it is a working manual. Creators who have received free copies consistently comment that the depth surprised them for the price point.

**Genuine price accessibility.** $5–$22 is buy-on-impulse territory. No paywall, no subscription. One purchase, instant PDF download, usable offline. This means higher conversion rates from your recommendation than physical products or high-ticket digital courses.

## Suggested Video Angles

These are specific, field-tested ideas based on what performs well in the homesteading/urban gardening niche. Each one creates a natural bridge from the video content to the product.

**1. "I saved seeds for the first time using this guide"**
Show the fermentation method (slice tomato, seeds into water jar, 2–3 days, rinse and dry). Narrate with the Seed Saving Field Manual open on your phone or tablet. Ending: "I used this guide, the PDF walks you through every variety, link in bio." Works as a 45-second Reel or a 3-minute YouTube video.

**2. "These heirloom varieties actually have stories"**
Pull 3–4 varieties from the Anti-Catalog (Cherokee Purple, Mortgage Lifter, Dragon Tongue beans, Fish Pepper) and give their origin in 10 seconds each. "Mortgage Lifter — a gardener in the 1930s developed this by crossing 4 varieties over 6 years. He sold $1 seedlings and paid off his mortgage. You can save the seeds every year." Product tie-in is organic: "I found these in Seedwarden's heirloom guide."

**3. "Starting a container garden from zero — what I actually need"**
Walk through the Container Growing Blueprint Pack. Show one of the five layout plans (window box, 5-gallon bucket, half barrel). Point out the companion planting specs and watering schedules. This works as a "setup" video for spring audiences and for beginners who feel overwhelmed by container options.

**4. "I grew my own hot peppers and made this"**
Show a pepper plant in a container, harvested peppers, and the lacto-fermentation process (peppers, water, salt, a jar). Tie to the Grow Your Own Hot Sauce guide ($15). Visual content. High completion rate. The fermentation jar shot is compelling.

**5. "This is why I stopped buying seeds every year"**
Explain open-pollinated vs. hybrid vs. GMO (the 2-minute version). Show the visual difference between saved seeds and a commercial packet. End on: "Once you understand this, you can get off the seed-buying treadmill — this guide explains the whole process." Tie to Seed Saving Field Manual.

**6. "What to plant in a container garden this month (honest list)"**
Seasonal angle. "Cherry tomatoes in a 5-gallon bucket — yes. Corn — no, and here's why." Run through 6–8 vegetables with real container compatibility ratings. Ties to Container Growing Blueprint Pack or Companion Planting Chart.

**7. "How I planned my entire growing year in one afternoon"**
Walk through the 12-Month Urban Growing Planner. Show the monthly sow dates, succession planting notes, zone-by-zone adjustments. Appeals to the planner/organizer type in your audience. Works well in January–March (new year planning) or as a fall-reset angle.

## Partnership Structures

Three options, depending on how you prefer to work. All are negotiable.

### Option 1 — Gift + Affiliate (no upfront cost to you)
- You receive free copies of any 2–3 Seedwarden guides your audience would find most relevant
- If you find genuine value, post one honest video or Reel at your discretion
- You receive a unique discount code or tracked link; Seedwarden pays 25% commission on all sales through your code, tracked for 60 days
- No posting obligation — if the product doesn't resonate, no post needed
- Best for: creators who want to try before committing

### Option 2 — Flat Fee + Affiliate
- One dedicated TikTok or Instagram video featuring a Seedwarden product
- Flat fee: $150–$300 depending on your audience size and engagement rate
- Plus 15% commission on tracked sales for 60 days
- Usage rights: Seedwarden may use the video as an ad creative (with your approval) for up to 90 days
- Best for: creators who prefer payment certainty and have an audience that converts on product recommendations

### Option 3 — Ongoing Affiliate Partnership
- No upfront fee. Custom affiliate link tracked via [UTM / third-party tracker]
- 25% commission on all sales, tracked indefinitely
- Seedwarden provides new product launches, seasonal promotion prompts, and updated creative assets quarterly
- Best for: creators who include regular product links in bio or description and have an audience that browses recommendations

**Commission note on Etsy**: Etsy does not have a native affiliate system. Tracked links use UTM parameters with a shared tracking spreadsheet (honor system) or a third-party tool like Pretty Links on a landing page. This is disclosed upfront.
