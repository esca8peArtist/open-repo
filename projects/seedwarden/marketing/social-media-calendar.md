### Day 10 — Values / Political
**Format**: Static image with text overlay

**Hook**: *"Percy Schmeiser saved his own seed. Monsanto sued him. Here's what happened."*

**Caption**:
Percy Schmeiser was a canola farmer in Saskatchewan. He'd been saving his own seed for over 40 years. In 1997, Monsanto investigators found patented genetics in his field — seeds that had blown in from neighboring farms.

Monsanto sued him for patent infringement.

The Canadian courts ultimately ruled for Monsanto on the patent question. Schmeiser spent the rest of his life speaking at the UN and advocating against corporate seed patents. He died in 2020.

His case is the clearest example of what happens when seed patents meet the biological fact that plants don't respect property lines.

This isn't history. The legal framework that made it possible is still in place.

Save seeds. It's legal. It's important. And every open-pollinated variety you grow gives you that option.

### Connecting Pinterest to Etsy Traffic

Pinterest pins have a long shelf life — a pin created today can surface in search results months or years later. The strategy:

1. **Every pin links directly to the relevant Etsy listing**, not the Etsy homepage. Reduce friction to purchase.
2. **Use keyword-rich pin titles and descriptions** — Pinterest's algorithm is search-based, not social. Write for what people type, not what sounds good.
3. **Create pinnable versions of your Instagram carousels** — the same educational content that performs on Instagram can be reformatted as tall (2:3 ratio) Pinterest graphics.
4. **Repin your own content to multiple boards** when it fits — a seed saving guide pin belongs on both the Seed Saving board and the Heirloom Seeds board.
5. **Consistent pinning beats burst activity** — 5–7 pins per week, spaced out, outperforms 50 pins on one day. Use a scheduler (Tailwind or Pinterest's native scheduler) to maintain cadence without daily effort.
