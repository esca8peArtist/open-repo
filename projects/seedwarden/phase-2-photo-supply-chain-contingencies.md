## Section 1: Backup Supplier Availability

### 1.1 Props and Seeds — Primary Vendor Context

The physical photo shoot (Clusters A, B, C — 15 products, 30 shots) requires four categories of props:
1. Seed envelopes/packets (4–6 pieces, Cluster A)
2. Mason jars (3–4 jars, Cluster C)
3. Germination tray and starter soil (Cluster A)
4. Small terracotta or ceramic pots and a hand trowel (Clusters A and B)

Mason jars, germination trays, pots, and trowels are available at Walmart, Target, Lowes, and Michaels with same-day local purchase. They are not supply-chain risks. The sourcing risk is seed packets: authentic seed envelopes (hand-labeled kraft paper style) with visible seed content are the key Cluster A prop. Commercial seed packets from a garden center work, but branded commercial packaging reads as retail, not homestead. Unlabeled kraft envelopes filled from bulk seeds and hand-labeled "Brandywine Tomato 2026" are the preferred prop because they communicate authenticity to the target buyer.

---

### 1.2 Seed Packet Props — If Local Sourcing Fails

**Local first (zero lead time):** Any hardware store, nursery, or garden center stocks small seed packets year-round. Burpee and Ferry-Morse packets are available at Home Depot, Lowes, and Walmart. The commercial branding can be obscured by turning packets away from camera or using a light grip that hides the logo. Cost: $1–3 per packet. For the authentic kraft-envelope aesthetic, purchase plain kraft paper coin envelopes (sold in office supply or craft stores: Michaels, Hobby Lobby, AC Moore) and fill them with seeds from a bulk bin or a bag of mixed flower/vegetable seeds.

- **Michaels Asheville (111-A River Hills Rd)**: Stocks kraft paper envelopes, jute twine, and seed-packet-adjacent craft supplies. No lead time — same-day purchase.
- **Cost**: $5–8 for a pack of 25–50 kraft envelopes; bulk beans or lentils from any grocery store fill them convincingly for the flat-lay shot.

**Online backup — if no local craft store visit is feasible before shoot day:**

| Vendor | Product | Price | Lead Time | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|-----------|-------|
| True Leaf Market (trueleafmarket.com) | Heirloom seed packets (any variety) | $2–4/pkt | 1–2 business days ship; USPS Priority Mail Express available (order before noon MT) | 96% ship within 1 business day; FedEx 2-Day available for same-day order before noon |
| Baker Creek Heirloom Seeds (rareseeds.com) | Seed packets | $2–5/pkt | 3–5 days standard | Prefer True Leaf if speed is priority; Baker Creek has richer variety but slower processing |
| Amazon Prime (Burpee, David's Garden Seeds) | Commercial seed packets | $3–8/pkt | 1–2 days Prime | Adequate for prop use; branding is visible but manageable; buy if shoot is within 48 hours and True Leaf cannot deliver |
| American Meadows (americanmeadows.com) | Ready-to-ship seed packet favors | $2–4/pkt | 2 business days standard | Kraft-style packets are available; marketed as event favors but visually correct for shoots |

**Trigger threshold**: If shoot is more than 5 days away and local purchase is inconvenient, order from True Leaf Market using standard shipping (save $8–12 vs. expedited). If shoot is within 48 hours, order True Leaf Market FedEx 2-Day or buy locally. Never delay the shoot date for seed packet props — the prop can be improvised with any seed envelope.

### 1.3 Stock Image Vendors — Cluster D/E Backup Options

Cluster D (4 products: Survival Garden, Hunting/Trapping, Small-Scale Livestock, Meat/Fish Preservation) and Cluster E (Native Plants Regional Guide, Wikimedia Commons path) require stock images rather than physical photography. The primary workflow tries free stock sites first (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay) before purchasing.

**Primary: iStock on-demand credits**
- Pricing (2026 verified): Essentials images $8–12 per standard license; Signature images $24–33 per standard license. Do not subscribe — buy on-demand credits for this volume.
- Standard License covers digital product use on Etsy. No attribution required.
- Log image ID and license confirmation in WORKLOG.md for every iStock purchase.

**Backup vendors if iStock is unavailable or image not found:**

| Vendor | Price Per Image | Lead Time | Best For | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Adobe Stock (stock.adobe.com) | $8–10 per image (on-demand credit pack) | Instant download | Rural/lifestyle; comparable quality to iStock at slightly lower per-image cost | No subscription needed; on-demand credit packs available; Standard License covers Etsy digital products |
| Shutterstock (shutterstock.com) | $9.16–14.50 per image (depends on pack size) | Instant download | Same coverage as iStock; Shutterstock has slightly stronger hunting/trapping content | 5-image on-demand pack ($49) = $9.80/image; 25-image pack ($229) = $9.16/image |
| Unsplash (unsplash.com) | $0 | Instant | Survival garden, raised beds, general homestead | Free with no attribution required for commercial use; coverage is adequate for Survival Garden and Native Plants; thin for hunting/trapping and livestock |
| Pexels (pexels.com) | $0 | Instant | Kitchen/preservation, urban growing | Free commercial use; stronger coverage than Unsplash for food and kitchen lifestyle |
| Pixabay (pixabay.com) | $0 | Instant | General garden and outdoor | Free commercial use; backup to Unsplash and Pexels |

**Recommended decision flow for each Cluster D/E product:**
1. Search Unsplash/Pexels/Pixabay (15 min per product). If 2+ usable images found: done, $0 cost.
2. If free sites return nothing usable: search iStock Essentials collection first ($8–12/image).
3. If iStock returns nothing usable: try Adobe Stock (comparable quality, sometimes different catalog).
4. If both paid sites miss: Shutterstock as final paid option.
5. For Native Plants Regional Guide: search Wikimedia Commons (commons.wikimedia.org) before any paid option. Filter: CC-BY or CC0 licenses. Native wildflower meadow and botanical field shots are well-covered.

**Budget guardrail**: Maximum 10 images purchased across Cluster D/E ($80–$120 ceiling at Essentials pricing). If free sites cover 3 of 5 products, expect 4–6 paid images ($32–72). Log every purchase in WORKLOG.md with image ID, vendor, cost, and license type.

## Section 2: Minimum Viable Launch Scope

### 2.1 If Canva Palette Refresh Is Delayed

The Canva Brand Kit setup (Gate 2 in `TRACK_B_USER_GATES.md`) must be complete by May 24. The Brand Kit stores 6 hex colors and 3 fonts for the Seedwarden zone card and pin production workflow. Without Brand Kit, every Canva session requires manual hex entry.

**Option A — Manual hex entry (preferred fallback, zero quality loss):**
Canva's color picker accepts hex values entered by hand. The output is visually identical to Brand Kit shortcut usage. Manual hex entry adds approximately 2–3 minutes per color application per session. For the 8-zone-card production run (Task 3.1–3.8 in `PHASE_2_ORCHESTRATOR_TASKS.md`), this adds an estimated 20–35 minutes of total overhead versus Brand Kit.

- Hex codes to enter manually (from Canva Setup guide):
  - Zone Temperate band: #2D5016
  - Zone Cool band: #3D6B8A
  - Zone Warm band: #C9943A
  - Zone Hot band: #A0522D
- Fonts: substitute from Canva's built-in Google Fonts library using the typefaces documented in `CANVA_ZONE_CARD_DESIGN_GUIDE.md`

**Option B — Launch with existing palette, add refresh in Phase 3:**
If Canva Brand Kit setup is not done by May 26 (when zone card production begins), launch the zone cards using whichever colors are already set in the existing Seedwarden Brand Kit or entered manually. The zone band color system (Temperate/Cool/Warm/Hot groupings) is structural and must use the correct hex values — these can be entered manually in each Canva session. The "refresh" that can defer to Phase 3 is any secondary palette refinement (accent colors, button colors, font style updates) that is not structurally essential to zone identification.

**Quality impact assessment:**

| Element | Impact if Brand Kit Missing | Workaround | Quality Score (1–5) |
|---------|----------------------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Zone band colors (structural) | Zero — manual hex entry matches exactly | Manual hex entry (2–3 min/color) | 5/5 |
| Font consistency | Low — Canva library fonts suffice if Brand Kit fonts not uploaded | Select same font by name manually | 5/5 |
| Brand color shortcuts | Minimal time overhead | Manual hex entry | 5/5 |
| Secondary palette refinement | Minor positioning signal | Defer to Phase 3 | 4/5 |

**Decision rule**: Never slip the launch date for a Brand Kit palette setup. Manual hex entry preserves full visual quality. The Brand Kit is a workflow convenience, not a content gate.

**If Brand Kit is also not set up by May 26 and manual hex entry is not feasible (e.g., Canva account issue):**
Launch with the zone cards using default Canva colors that approximate the correct zone groups. The educational content — sow/harvest dates, variety recommendations, zone-specific timing — is fully intact regardless of the exact hex value of the zone band. Color refinement can be applied retroactively by opening each zone card PDF in Canva and re-exporting with correct colors. This is a 45-minute task per card, executable post-launch.

## Section 3: Location Contingencies

### 3.1 If Asheville Permit Is Delayed or Denied

The Phase 2 shoot plan uses indoor home settings as the primary location for all three clusters (A, B, C). No outdoor permit is required for the planned shoot. The references to location in the project documents are specifically about the Appalachian botanical field session for the Endangered Species Series — a separate product line documented in `phase-2-supply-chain-contingencies.md`.

For the Zone Card and main catalog photography (Clusters A, B, C), the only location requirement is:
- A window with 3+ hours of morning indirect light (9am–noon)
- A flat surface 3–5 feet from the window
- A kitchen counter or table for Cluster C

This requires no permit. Any home, apartment, or office with a south- or east-facing window qualifies.

**However**, if an outdoor shoot was intended for any Cluster A products (garden/seed aesthetic), and that outdoor location is at a public park, botanical garden, or city property, the permit timelines are:

| Location Type | Permit Timeline | Cost | Notes |
|---------------|----------------|------|-------|
| City of Asheville public property | 14 calendar days from application | Varies by use | Application submitted; conditional approval document available on request after 14 days. Commercial photography in public parks requires approval. |
| NC State Parks (e.g. Pisgah) | 2 weeks minimum advance notice | Minimal ($25–75 filing fee typical) | Submit Special Activity Permit Application to park superintendent |
| National Forests (Pisgah National Forest near Asheville) | 60 days advance notice | Per-use fee | Not viable for a May 30 launch with a May 18 start date |
| Blue Ridge Parkway | Permit required; timeline not published; NPS recommends 4+ weeks | Per-use fee | Not viable at this timeline |

**Decision**: If any outdoor location requires a permit and no permit is in hand by May 20, execute the indoor studio plan immediately. There is no photography gap — all 15 Cluster A/B/C products can be shot at equal or superior quality indoors.

### 3.2 Indoor Studio Timeline

**Home studio activation** (zero-cost, zero lead time):

Setup time from decision to first frame: 15 minutes.

Materials required:
- Table or desk (already present in any home)
- East- or south-facing window (lighting check: hold white paper in frame at 10am; if shadows are soft, window is adequate)
- White foam-core board 24×36 in ($8–12 at Michaels or Target) — used as fill reflector to soften shadow side
- Wooden cutting board or wood plank (already likely on hand; purchase at kitchen store for $15–25 if not)
- Dark linen or canvas cloth, navy or forest green, ~1 yard ($5–15 at fabric store or use a dish towel)

No softbox is needed unless window light consistently fails. If window is north-facing or consistently overcast, a 45W daylight LED softbox ($40–60 on Amazon) is a one-day shipping purchase that solves any light quality issue.

**Studio rental (Asheville) — paid option if home studio is not viable:**

| Studio | Rate | Booking Lead Time | Minimum | Notes |
|--------|------|--------------------|---------|-------|
| Prism Studio Space (shootatprism.com) | ~$84–113/hr | Bookable online; 24-hour cancellation policy | Flexible | 650 sq ft; floor-to-ceiling window for natural light; 13 ft ceilings; ideal for flat-lay overhead shots |
| Peerspace listings — Asheville | $63–104/hr average | Bookable same-week; 24-hr cancellation on most | Typically 2 hours | Multiple options including daylight studios and creative spaces; Wednesday and Sunday bookings run 40% cheaper |
| UpMarket Gallery (upmarketgallery.com) | Ask for quote | Contact directly | Per session | 600 sq ft; historic downtown building; natural light; kitchen area and dressing space |
| Ernest Co-Warehousing (ernestready.com) | Contact for rate | Contact directly | Per session | Coworking facility with photography studio rental; good for recurring need |

**Recommendation**: Use the home studio first — it costs $0 and produces equivalent or better results for this prop-and-product flat-lay style. Book a paid studio only if:
- The home does not have a usable south- or east-facing window
- The shoot requires more than one cluster's worth of setup at the same time (rare)
- A professional-looking background is required for an unexpected use case

**Paid studio cost ceiling**: Budget $200–300 for a 2–3 hour session at Prism or a Peerspace listing. This is within the $80–160 stock image budget range (which may come in under ceiling if free sources cover 3+ Cluster D/E products), but add it to the contingency line item rather than the photo budget if needed.

### 3.3 Weather Contingency for Any Outdoor Shots

If outdoor shots were planned for Cluster A (garden/seed aesthetic) or Cluster B (balcony/container growing) and weather does not cooperate:

- Shoot on an overcast day when possible — overcast is superior to direct sun for flat-lay photography. Direct sun creates hard shadows that compete with the product.
- If outdoor shoot is rained out: execute the indoor studio plan (Section 3.2 above) on the same day. No reschedule needed.
- Outdoor reshoot window (if first indoor results are unsatisfactory): May 19–22. This leaves 8 days for editing and compositing before May 30.

### 4.2 Single-Day Emergency Shoot Protocol

If the shoot window is compressed to a single half-day (4 hours maximum, e.g., morning of May 22 with immediate deadline pressure):

**Priority order for single-day shoot:**
1. Cluster C (3 products, 6 shots) — fastest setup; kitchen counter is always available; 35–40 minutes
2. Cluster B (4 products, 8 shots) — window sill or potted plant area; 45 minutes  
3. Cluster A Tier 1 (Seed Saving Field Manual, Heirloom Variety Guide) — 2 products, 4 shots; 20 minutes
4. Cluster A Tier 2 (Companion Planting Chart, Zone Calendar) — if time permits; 20 minutes
5. Remaining Cluster A — low-ticket products; defer to post-launch if time runs out

**In a 4-hour single-day shoot**: All of Clusters B and C are achievable, plus 4–6 Cluster A products. The highest-revenue-per-photo products (Harvest Preservation Manual $18, Container Blueprint Pack $18, Apartment Growing Guide $18) are covered.

Products not reached in the single-day shoot fall back to existing 3-image mockup listings. A partial 5-image upgrade is better than no upgrade. The conversion measurement clock starts from the date lifestyle images are added — incomplete launches are measurable and improvable.
