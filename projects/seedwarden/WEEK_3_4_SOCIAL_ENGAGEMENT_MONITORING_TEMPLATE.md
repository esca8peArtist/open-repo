## SECTION 5: RED/YELLOW DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURES — WEEKS 3-4

### RED Trigger: Engagement <5% on 2+ Consecutive Same-Type Posts

**This is the primary algorithm suppression signal for Weeks 3-4.**

By Week 3-4, you have 8+ posts of data. Two consecutive RED posts of the same type (e.g., Educational posts 11 AND 14 both <5%) confirms algorithmic pattern.

**Diagnostic steps**:

1. **Confirm content type consistency**: Are both RED posts the same type? If yes, type-level issue. If no, random.

2. **Compare posting times**: Were both posted at 9am ET? If yes, try 12pm ET for next post of that type (timing issue test).

3. **Compare hook strength**: What was the opening line of each RED post? Is the hook weaker than GREEN posts? (Example: "Here's some information about valerian" = weak vs. "Valerian root concentration peaks in year 2-3 fall — here's the science" = strong)

4. **Repost test**: Repost the second RED post (slightly revised hook) at 12pm ET within 24 hours. If engagement improves 3+ points → timing issue. If stays RED → content issue.

5. **Action for next post of that type**:
   - Timing issue: Shift that content type to 12pm ET for 2 weeks; re-measure
   - Content issue: Increase specificity (more Latin names, more specific data, more personal observation)

---

### YELLOW Trigger: Declining Week-over-Week Engagement on Same Content Type

**Example**: Educational engagement rate Week 1: 9.2%, Week 2: 8.1%, Week 3: 6.8%  
This is a 2.4% week-over-week decline indicating audience fatigue on educational format.

**Action**:
1. Pause that content type for 1 week (no new Educational posts Jul 27-Aug 2)
2. Replace with 2 testimonial or engagement posts in that week slot
3. Reintroduce educational content Aug 3+ with a different topic angle or format (shorter post, bullet-point format instead of narrative)

## SECTION 9: CONTINGENCY TRIGGERS — WEEKS 3-4 SOCIAL

### Trigger 1: LinkedIn Algorithm Flagged (<8% engagement for 3+ consecutive posts)

**This is a pattern that may indicate LinkedIn account suppression, not just underperformance.**

**Diagnostic**:
1. Check LinkedIn creator analytics: "Impressions" vs. "Engagement" — if impressions are also falling, account may be suppressed
2. Review recent posts for policy violations (no direct buy-now links in post body — first-comment strategy required)
3. Review posting frequency: >1 post/day can trigger LinkedIn suppression
4. If account suppression suspected: pause posting for 5 days, then resume with educational-only content

**Recovery**:
- 5-day pause
- Resume with high-value educational post (long-form, 400+ words, no external links)
- Monitor for 48 hours before adding promotional posts back

---

### Trigger 2: Instagram Engagement Drops >50% from Week 2 Baseline

**Diagnostic**: Check if Instagram changed algorithm (search "@latermedia" or "@buffer" for platform news). Check if specific hashtags were flagged.

**Recovery**: Remove flagged hashtags; replace with alternative set. Test posting without hashtags for 2 posts (measure if impressions recover).

---

### Trigger 3: Testimonial Post Engagement <5%

**Root cause**: Testimonial lacks specificity or feels inauthentic.

**Immediate action**:
1. Review the testimonial: does it have (a) specific herb, (b) specific outcome, (c) grower context, (d) full name?
2. If missing 2+ elements: do not repost; request more detailed testimonial from next buyer
3. For next testimonial post: provide buyer with these instructions: "Please include: which herb you grew/used, what you noticed (be specific), your gardening zone or region, and your full name. Vague testimonials underperform."
