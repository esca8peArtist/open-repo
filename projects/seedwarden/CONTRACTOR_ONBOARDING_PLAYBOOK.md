## FTC Compliance and Confidentiality (All Contractors)

Include in all onboarding kits. Do not modify these sections.

### FTC Compliance Requirements

All content you create (text, recipes, images, social posts) may be used in promotional contexts (Etsy, email, social media). FTC regulations require:

- **Sponsored content**: Any post or content promoting Seedwarden products requires FTC disclosure (#ad, #sponsored, or equivalent). Seedwarden will add these if missing — but inform us immediately if you're unsure whether disclosure is required for a specific piece.
- **Health claims**: Avoid definitive medical claims. Use evidence-tier framing: "Traditionally used to support...," "Research suggests...," "May support..." Do not use "cures," "treats," or "prevents" without evidence qualifier.
- **Endorsements**: If you personally recommend a product in content, disclose that this is sponsored work.
- **Review responsibility**: Herbalist consultant ensures all therapeutic language is FTC-compliant before launch. Writers: self-review against the FTC guide provided in your onboarding kit.

### Confidentiality and Work-for-Hire

- **Ownership**: All deliverables (photos, manuscripts, recipes, social posts, review notes) are owned by Seedwarden after delivery and payment.
- **Commercial rights**: Seedwarden may use all content commercially (Etsy, marketing, social, derivatives, advertising) without additional permission or licensing fees.
- **Confidentiality**: Do not discuss project details, deliverables, rates, timelines, or launch dates publicly until Seedwarden announces launch on Etsy (target: July 2026 onward).
- **No republishing**: You may not republish your work elsewhere (portfolio, website, publications) without written permission from Seedwarden.
- **Attribution**: Attribution is not guaranteed. Seedwarden may use content with or without byline at its discretion.

## Contingency and Replacement Procedures

**If contractor misses deliverable deadline (>24h late):**

- Day 1 (24h late): Coordinator sends reminder. Contractor has 24h to submit a revised delivery date.
- Day 2 (48h late): You follow up directly. Contractor has 48h final window.
- Day 4 (72h late / deadline missed): Activate contingency:
  - Photographer: Contact backup photographer; assign to remaining sessions
  - Writer: Contact Tier 2 writer candidate; offer emergency scope (1 bundle vs. 2–3)
  - Herbalist: Internal review or defer to Week 5 (less critical path)
  - Coordinator: Absorb tasks internally or hire last-minute admin support
  - Social Media Manager: Temporarily absorb posting from content calendar; defer engagement replies

**If contractor quality fails gate review twice:**

1. First fail: detailed feedback; contractor revises within 24–48h
2. Second fail: schedule call within 24h to discuss. Options: (a) reduce scope, (b) extend timeline, (c) transition to backup contractor
3. Implement decision within 48h of the call

**If contractor becomes unavailable unexpectedly:**
- Request estimated return date; adjust timeline
- If >5 days unavailable: activate backup immediately
- Adjust final payment proportionally if scope is reduced
