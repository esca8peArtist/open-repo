# Phase 3 Second Writer — Candidate Profiles and Hiring Checklist

**Prepared**: May 21, 2026
**Applicability**: This document is relevant ONLY if Option B (5-bundle sprint with two writers) is selected by May 30.
**Writer confirmed deadline**: June 1, 2026 — if no writer is confirmed by this date, default to Option C immediately. The June 22 sprint briefing window requires at least three weeks for onboarding, writing sample review, and brief alignment.
**Sprint period**: June 22 – August 1, 2026 (writer produces Respiratory, Immunity, and Digestive bundles)

---

## Lead Finding

A second writer for Option B must meet a non-negotiable baseline: herbalist certification (AHG Registered Herbalist, NAHA, or clinical equivalent) OR clinical background (RN, ND, or MD) with documented medicinal herb specialty, AND demonstrated experience writing contraindication panels at practitioner-grade depth. The contraindication requirement is the hardest filter — it eliminates most freelance health writers immediately. The most likely sourcing path is the AHG member directory (americanherbalistsguild.com/find-an-herbalist) or instructor networks at Chestnut School or Herbal Academy. Budget: $80–$100/hour for credentialed herbalist writers, or $1,400–$2,000 flat-rate for the three-bundle scope (Respiratory, Immunity, Digestive).

## Section 2: Non-Negotiable Qualification Criteria

The following criteria are hard filters. Candidates who do not meet all of them are not qualified for this engagement.

### Hard Filters

**1. Herbalist credential OR clinical background**
- Acceptable: AHG Registered Herbalist (RH); NAHA certified aromatherapist is NOT sufficient; North American Institute of Medical Herbalism (NAIMH) graduate; Chestnut School Professional Herbalist certification; Herbal Academy Intermediate + Advanced + Entrepreneur track completion (combined, not individually); ND or MD with documented botanical medicine specialty; RN or PA with functional medicine + botanical specialty
- Not acceptable: wellness blogger without credential; nutrition coach; certified health coach; life coach; "self-taught herbalist" without peer-reviewed or clinical publication record

**2. Medicinal herbs contraindication expertise (CRITICAL)**
- Must be able to write herb-drug interaction tables from primary sources without prompting
- Must understand GABA/serotonin pathway interactions (Passionflower, Valerian, Lemon Balm)
- Must understand thyroid-herb interactions (Ashwagandha, Lemon Balm)
- Must understand CITES Appendix II and UpS sourcing ethics context for the guide framing
- Contraindication incompleteness creates FTC and tort liability. This is the hardest and most important filter.

**3. FTC-compliant health claims writing**
- Must demonstrate awareness of FTC health claim standards for educational products
- Must be willing to use the FTC Quick Reference language from PHASE_3_MEDICINAL_HERBS_CRITICAL_PATH.md Appendix A without modification
- Sample test: ask the candidate to rewrite "Elderberry prevents colds" in FTC-compliant form. Correct answer: "Studied in randomized controlled trials for cold duration and severity outcomes." An answer that includes "may help prevent" or "supports immune function" without evidence-tier framing fails this filter.

**4. Writing sample — published medicinal herb educational content**
- Must provide at least one published guide, educational article, or clinical monograph on a specific medicinal herb at practitioner-grade depth
- Sample must include Latin binomials, contraindications, and evidence citations
- Wikipedia-style or listicle content does not qualify
- Blog posts qualify only if they demonstrate clinical-level depth (herb-drug interactions table, species differentiation, cultivation guidance)

**5. Availability — June 22 to August 1**
- Must be available for 4–6 week sprint starting June 22
- Remote work capability required (no in-person requirement)
- Must be reachable for revision requests within 24–48 hours during the sprint
- Must be available for a June 22 briefing call (1 hour) before writing begins

### Preferred (Not Required)

- Experience writing for Etsy sellers or educational product platforms
- Familiarity with PDF guide production (Canva or equivalent)
- Familiarity with Etsy SEO keyword integration
- Clinical practice experience (adds credibility for the practitioner-tier reviewer mention)

## Section 3: Candidate Sourcing Paths

### Path A: AHG Member Directory (Highest Credential Density)

The American Herbalists Guild member directory (americanherbalistsguild.com/find-an-herbalist) lists Registered Herbalists by specialty and location. Filter for "clinical practice" and "medicinal herbs" specialties.

**Search approach**:
1. Visit americanherbalistsguild.com/find-an-herbalist
2. Filter specialty: Clinical Herbalism, Medicinal Herbs
3. Look for members who list "teaching," "writing," or "education" as a secondary activity — these are the candidates who are most likely to be interested in a writing project
4. Contact by email; do not cold-call; reference the AHG credential as shared context

**Expected response rate**: 15–25% from RH members who list education as an activity. The AHG community is service-oriented; a credentialed educational guide request is more warmly received than a standard freelance pitch.

### Path B: Herbalism School Instructor Networks

Three schools have instructor networks with documented writing capability and practitioner-grade credentials:

**Chestnut School of Herbal Medicine** (chestnutherbs.com)
- Contact: partnerships@chestnutherbs.com
- Request: referral to instructors or senior alumni with writing availability for a 4–6 week educational guide project
- Credential depth: Chestnut's instructors carry RH or clinical practitioner credentials; their curriculum is bioregional and clinical — aligned with Seedwarden's content style
- Note: Juliet Blankespoor (Chestnut founder) is already a Tier 1 outreach target in the Practitioner Relationship Roadmap — this is a parallel track, not a conflict

**Herbal Academy** (theherbalacademy.com)
- Contact: partnerships@theherbalacademy.com
- Request: referral to advanced-level instructors or alumni with clinical writing experience
- Note: Herbal Academy graduates range widely in depth; request specifically for instructors (not recent graduates) or Advanced + Clinical Herbal Medicine program alumni
- Credential depth: Lower than Chestnut for clinical contraindication work; higher than most general wellness writers

**North American Institute of Medical Herbalism (NAIMH)** (naimh.com)
- Contact: direct faculty email (listed on website)
- Higher clinical depth than general herbalism schools; graduates are prepared for clinical practice
- Smaller network; response time may be slower

### Path C: Academic Herbalists (Published Researchers)

Academic herbalists produce peer-reviewed content at the highest evidence-tier level. The tradeoff: they are unlikely to take a $1,600–$2,000 writing engagement, and their writing style may require significant reframing for a consumer-facing educational product.

**Targeting criteria**: Look for PhD students or postdoctoral researchers in ethnobotany or pharmacognosy who have published educational content (extension publications, public outreach pieces) in addition to academic papers. These individuals bridge the evidence-tier depth and accessible writing style.

**Sources**: University extension publications (herbalism, ethnobotany sections); ResearchGate profiles in herbal medicine; Herb Society of America publication contributors.

### Path D: Freelance Platform Filters

If Path A–C do not produce a candidate by June 1, use Upwork or Contra with strict filters:

- Job title filter: "Medical Herbalist," "Herbalist Writer," "Botanical Medicine Writer"
- Required: Must answer screening question "What is the CITES Appendix II listing and why does it matter for a Goldenseal cultivation guide?" Correct answer demonstrates species-level knowledge required for the Immunity bundle
- Required: Must answer screening question "Write a FTC-compliant version of 'Valerian cures insomnia'." Correct answer: "Studied in clinical trials for sleep-related outcomes, with mixed results across study designs"
- Do not use Fiverr for this engagement — the contraindication requirement cannot be quality-verified at Fiverr volume and price points

## Section 5: Bundle Assignment and Briefing Protocol

### Bundle Assignment Logic

The user retains Women's Health and Sleep — the two bundles requiring the most accurate FTC and contraindication work that the user can self-verify. The second writer receives Respiratory, Immunity, and Digestive:

- **Respiratory** is the lowest-risk bundle for writer delegation: the Echinacea two-species comparison requires species-level accuracy, but the contraindication surface is smaller than Immunity or Women's Health
- **Immunity** is the highest-risk bundle for writer delegation: the Goldenseal CITES sidebar and Ashwagandha thyroid warning are the two most legally and clinically sensitive passages in Phase 3. The writer must produce these sections with the verbatim CITES language from Appendix A and the specific thyroid warning framing from the Critical Path. These sections require a mandatory accuracy review by an RH or ND before upload.
- **Digestive** is the most accessible bundle for delegation: lowest FTC risk, no CITES complexity, shared species (Calendula, Lemon Balm) with established guide content

### Briefing Package Contents

Deliver to the confirmed writer by June 15 (one week before sprint start):

1. `phase-3-medicinal-herbs-content-outline.md` — complete content outline for all three bundles, section by section
2. PHASE_3_MEDICINAL_HERBS_CRITICAL_PATH.md Appendix A — FTC Quick Reference and mandatory warning language
3. CITES sidebar verbatim (Goldenseal, mandatory in Immunity body)
4. Attribution log template (PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md) — writer is responsible for noting any sources they cite in their drafts
5. Phase 3 color palette and PDF export spec (5MB limit, 2400×2400px images, no placeholder text)
6. Writing velocity target: 300–350 words/hour, with pre-compiled outlines reducing research load
7. Contraindication review protocol: submit all contraindication sections to user for review before finalizing each bundle; user retains final accuracy approval authority on all medical content

### Revision and Approval Protocol

- Writer submits first draft of each bundle to user within 7 days of sprint start for that bundle
- User reviews contraindication panels and CITES sidebar first; other content reviewed subsequently
- One revision cycle per bundle is included in scope; additional revisions billed at hourly rate
- User retains final upload authorization — writer does not have Etsy access
- If writer-produced content fails the FTC Quick Reference check on review, user makes corrections; writer does not charge for corrections to their own FTC-language errors (specify in contract)

## Section 6: Contract and Payment Structure

### Recommended Engagement Structure

**Flat-rate per bundle** is recommended over hourly for this engagement. It aligns incentives: the writer is motivated to produce efficiently; the user is not exposed to open-ended hourly billing.

| Bundle | Flat Rate (recommended) | Hourly Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Respiratory Health | $400–500 | 12–14 hrs × $35–40/hr |
| Immunity Support | $500–600 | 10–12 hrs × $45–55/hr |
| Digestive Support | $350–450 | 9–11 hrs × $38–45/hr |
| **Total** | **$1,250–1,550** | |

If the writer has RH (AHG) credential and clinical practice experience, rates should be at the higher end ($1,400–$2,000 total) — the credential is worth paying for in the Immunity bundle specifically. A lower-cost writer without the contraindication depth costs more in revision cycles and risk exposure than the savings on their day rate.

### Payment Milestones

- 25% on contract signing (June 1 expected)
- 25% on delivery of first bundle draft (Respiratory, expected July 1–2)
- 25% on delivery of second bundle draft (Immunity, expected July 8)
- 25% on delivery of third bundle draft and all revision cycles complete (Digestive, expected July 20)

### Contract Must-Haves

- Scope: three bundles, stated word counts (3,600 / 3,800 / 3,600), specified delivery dates
- Work-for-hire clause: all content is Seedwarden's property on delivery; writer retains no rights to reuse
- Accuracy representation: writer warrants that contraindication content is accurate and that they have the stated credentials
- FTC language compliance: writer agrees to use FTC Quick Reference framing; user approval required before finalization
- Revision scope: one revision cycle per bundle included; additional revisions at hourly rate
- Confidentiality: writer does not disclose Seedwarden content before launch
- No attribution requirement (unless agreed otherwise) — the guide carries Seedwarden branding, not writer attribution
