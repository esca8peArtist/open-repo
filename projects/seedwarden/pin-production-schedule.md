### Template Build Session Plan

**Total template build time**: 3–4 hours for all 5 masters. This is a one-time investment — all subsequent batch production is duplication and content-swap only.

Build all masters in a single focused session rather than spread across days. Reason: Brand Kit colors and font sizes need to be calibrated once visually (the spec numbers approximate optimal sizing; pixel-level rendering varies by font). Completing all 5 templates in one session means calibration is consistent across the set.

**Recommended session structure**: 3-hour block with one break after Template 2.

**Total batch production time**: 5–7 hours across 3–4 sessions.

**Pre-condition**: All 9 master templates built (Part A complete).

**Session target**: 12–15 pins per 90-minute session (from `phase-2-canva-pin-production-checklist.md` Section 3).

**Batch Session 1 — Product Mockup Pins (Templates 1 + 3 partial)**

**Batch Session 3 — Carousel Covers (Template 5)**
- Target output: 4–6 carousel covers with all inner slides (4–5 slides per carousel = 16–30 total carousel files)
- Time: 35–50 min per carousel × 4 carousels = 2.5–3 hours
- Plan: Build 1 carousel per session; do not attempt 2 carousels in the same session
- Topic suggestions for first 4 carousels (drawn from `phase-2-social-content-calendar-60day.md` Day 3 specification):
  1. "5 Guides for People Growing Their Own Food" — Tier 1 product carousel
  2. "3 Types of Food Preservation (and When to Use Each)" — Cluster C educational
  3. "How to Start Saving Seeds This Season" — Cluster A educational

**Batch Session 4 — Lifestyle Flat-Lay Pins (Template 3, remaining 5 products)**
- Target output: 5 additional lifestyle flat-lay pins as Cluster D/E stock images become available
- Time: 3–5 min each × 5 = 15–25 min
- Ready to start: After Cluster D stock image compositing is complete (Stock Sprint 1 + compositing)
- Full 10-product lifestyle pin batch completes when at least the top-10-revenue products have slot 4 images

## Part C — Scheduling Tool Selection

### Requirements

- Schedule Pinterest pins across multiple weeks (evergreen repinning preferred)
- Schedule Instagram posts (carousels, single images, stories)
- Low cost — ideally free or under $20/month to stay within operational budget
- No TikTok scheduling required for this batch (TikTok native drafts are sufficient for Phase 2 volume)

### Options Evaluated

#### Option 1: Later Starter — $18/month

**Platforms supported**: Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok, LinkedIn, X (Twitter)
**Pinterest features**: Native Pinterest scheduling, board selection, pin description editor, best-time suggestions
**Instagram features**: Feed posts, Reels scheduling (with notification workflow for Reels), Stories (via mobile push notification)
**Pin volume**: Later Starter allows 30 posts per social profile per month. At 7–10 pins per week, this is 30–40 pins per month — the Starter limit would be hit at higher-volume weeks.
**Verdict**: Borderline on volume at peak production. Adequate for Phase 2 if pinning is paced at 5–7 pins/week (30 per month per profile). At 7–10 pins/week, consider Later Growth ($40/month) or a free alternative.

#### Option 2: Pinterest Native Scheduler (free)

**Platforms supported**: Pinterest only
**Features**: Schedule up to 100 pins, 2 weeks in advance, directly in Pinterest Business (no third-party app required)
**Limitations**: No Instagram scheduling, no analytics dashboard beyond Pinterest native, no best-time recommendations
**Verdict**: Best option for Pinterest-only scheduling. Zero cost. For Phase 2 where Pinterest is the primary platform, this is sufficient. Instagram posts can be scheduled in-app (Instagram natively supports post scheduling for Business accounts via Meta Business Suite).

#### Option 3: Meta Business Suite (free, Instagram + Facebook only)

**Platforms supported**: Instagram and Facebook only
**Features**: Schedule feed posts, carousels, and Reels up to 75 days in advance; analytics dashboard
**Limitations**: Pinterest not included
**Verdict**: Free and fully functional for Instagram scheduling. Use in combination with Pinterest native scheduler.

#### Option 4: Buffer Free Plan (free)

**Platforms supported**: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, Mastodon (Pinterest NOT included on free plan)
**Free tier**: 3 social channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel
**Verdict**: Pinterest absence rules this out as the primary tool for Phase 2. Not recommended.

### Recommendation: Pinterest Native + Meta Business Suite (combined, free)

For Phase 2 scope (Pinterest primary, Instagram secondary), the free combination covers all needs:
- Pinterest pins: scheduled via Pinterest Business native scheduler (up to 100 pins, 2 weeks out)
- Instagram posts and carousels: scheduled via Meta Business Suite (free, up to 75 days)
- TikTok: use TikTok native drafts (no scheduling tool needed at Phase 2 volume)

**Later Starter upgrade trigger**: If Phase 2 Pinterest volume exceeds 30 pins/month consistently OR cross-platform analytics become necessary for Phase 3 planning, upgrade to Later Starter ($18/month). This trigger is expected in Week 4–6 of production if all 50–60 pins are queued for aggressive early distribution.

**Decision**: Proceed with Pinterest Native + Meta Business Suite (free). Revisit at Week 4 checkpoint.

## Part D — Launch Date Estimate

### Phase 2 Pin Launch Scenarios

**Scenario A — Week 1 Launch (fastest, recommended)**

Conditions: User completes Canva Brand Kit setup within the next 1–2 days. Templates built Day 1–2. Batch Session 1 (Template 1, 21 product pins) runs Day 2–3.

- **First pins published**: Day 3–4 from today (approximately May 3–4, 2026)
- **Initial batch size**: 21 product mockup pins (using existing slots 1–3 mockup images)
- **Format**: Template 1 only — product name, mockup image, price, URL
- **Assessment**: These pins are functional and ready even without lifestyle photography. They establish the Pinterest presence and begin SEO indexing immediately.

**Scenario B — Week 2 Launch (with partial lifestyle images)**

Conditions: Cluster D stock sprint completes in Week 2. Lifestyle images composited. Template 3 pins (lifestyle flat-lay, 5 Cluster D products) available.

- **Additional pins published**: 5 lifestyle flat-lay pins for top-revenue products
- **Total published by end of Week 2**: ~50 pins (Template 1: 21 product pins + Template 2: 10 educational + Template 4: 5 values pins + first carousel)
- **Assessment**: This is the target state for a "soft launch" with a real, diverse pin library.

**Scenario C — Full Library Launch (Week 4)**

Conditions: Physical shoots complete (Clusters A, B, C). All 42 slot 4/5 images edited. Full 50–60 pin batch built.

- **Total pins published**: 50–60 unique pins (100–120 files across both formats)
- **Assessment**: Full library launch. All 5 template types represented. Lifestyle-heavy pins (Template 3) for all 10 priority products.

### Recommended Launch Approach

Do not wait for Scenario C to begin publishing. Launch Scenario A (Week 1, Template 1 pins) immediately after Brand Kit setup. This serves three purposes:

1. Pinterest SEO indexing takes 30–60 days to compound. Starting Week 1 versus Week 4 adds 3 weeks of compounding.
2. Template 1 product pins are sufficient to test which products generate saves, clicks, or follows — this data informs which Template 3 lifestyle pins are most urgent to produce.
3. Early pin engagement (saves, repins) will happen organically even with mockup images — Seedwarden's product aesthetic is clean enough that styled mockups outperform many competitor pins.

**Launch sequence**:
- Week 1: Publish Template 1 (21 product pins) + Template 4 (5 values pins) — these are fully unblocked
- Week 2: Add Template 2 (educational pins) as Cluster D compositing is complete
- Week 3–4: Add Template 3 (lifestyle flat-lays) and Template 5 (carousels) as physical shoots complete

## Part E — Quality Gate: Spot-Check Protocol

Before any pin batch is published, run the following 2–3 pin spot-check for brand alignment with Phase 1 aesthetic:

**Pins to spot-check (select from first completed batch)**:
1. One Template 1 pin — the highest-ticket product (Survival Garden Regional Plans, $22)
2. One Template 4 values pin — the Deep Forest Green background variant
3. One Template 2 educational hook pin — flat background variant

**Spot-check criteria** (adapted from `phase-2-canva-pin-production-checklist.md` Section 5):

| Check | Pass Criterion |
|-------|----------------|
| Color match | Deep Forest Green (#143b28) reads as clearly dark green, not black or navy |
| Color match | Warm Cream (#F5EDD6) reads as warm-toned, not pure white |
| Typography legibility | All text readable at 100% zoom without squinting |
| No text overflow | No text cut off at zone edges or canvas boundaries |
| Product name matches listing | Etsy title matches pin text (shortened if over 50 characters) |
| Logo visibility | Logo visible with 60px clear space on all sides |
| Zone separation | Zone 3 product name and Zone 4 CTA are visually distinct; no zone bleed |
| Brand consistency with Phase 1 | Earthy, tactile, working-tool aesthetic (not aspirational or clinical) |

**Failure response**: If any of the 3 spot-check pins fail, do not publish the full batch. Fix the issue at the master template level first, then regenerate. A master-level fix propagates correctly to all duplicated production pins — verify 2–3 sample production pins after the master fix before re-approving the batch.
