## Response Tracking During the Timeline

### June 24–26 (Warm Window) — What to Expect

**Typical response breakdown** (based on contractor pool scoring + prior outreach experience):

| Response Type | Expected Count | Response Quality | Probability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enthusiastic + All Info** | 3–5 | High (immediate "yes", full pricing, clear availability) | 40–50% |
| **Interested + Clarification Needed** | 2–3 | Medium (interested but needs scope/budget clarified) | 25–30% |
| **Neutral + Slow Response** | 1–2 | Low (responds but non-committal, needs follow-up) | 10–15% |
| **No Response Yet** | 1–2 | Unknown | 10–15% |

**What to do for each response type**:

1. **Enthusiastic + All Info**: Score immediately using PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md. Treat as high-priority candidate. Update RESPONSE_TRACKING_TEMPLATE.md: [Response Status] = "ACCEPTED", [Fit Score] = score, [Rank] = position.
   
2. **Interested + Clarification Needed**: Reply within 24 hours with answers to their questions (use email template from PHASE_3_COMMUNICATION_TEMPLATES.md). Ask them to confirm final pricing, availability, deliverables by June 26 EOD so you can finalize decisions by June 28.
   
3. **Neutral + Slow Response**: Reply same day, re-emphasize timeline ("Decision deadline June 28 EOD, production starts July 1"). Ask for confirmation by June 27 noon to remain in consideration. Do not push hard — tone is "first-come, first-decided" (fair and professional).
   
4. **No Response by June 26 EOD**: Mark as potential non-responder. Send one follow-up email June 27 morning: "Quick follow-up: Are you available for July 1–August 1 project? Decision deadline is June 28 EOD." If no response by June 27, 6pm, mark as "No Response" and activate backup candidate.

## Scoring Reminder During Timeline

**Use PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md for all scoring decisions**. During the timeline above, you will score contractors at three points:

1. **June 25 EOD** (warm window preliminary scoring) — score early responders to identify front-runners
2. **June 27, 6pm** (cool window final scoring of non-backup candidates) — update scores as more info arrives
3. **June 28, 8am–11am** (final scoring before selection) — calculate final scores for all respondents, rank, and decide

**Scoring formula**: (Portfolio Fit × 0.35) + (Availability × 0.30) + (Budget Fit × 0.20) + (Success Prob × 0.15)

**Scoring thresholds**:
- **8.0–10.0**: Hire immediately (top tier, highest fit)
- **7.0–7.9**: Hire (good fit, strong availability)
- **5.5–6.9**: Hire if role gap exists or top candidates decline (acceptable fit, some trade-offs)
- **Below 5.5**: Pass unless extreme role shortage (poor fit, not recommended)

## Contingency: "What If" Scenarios

### Scenario 1: All Top 5 Candidates Respond "No" or Don't Respond by June 26 EOD

**Action**:
1. Do not panic. Activate backup candidates immediately (June 27 morning, not waiting until June 27 6pm).
2. Send backup outreach emails to next 5–8 candidates from PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_POOL.md backup section.
3. Mark backup emails with subject line **[URGENT — 1-DAY DECISION WINDOW]** to signal priority.
4. Offer expedited decision: "We will confirm final contract by June 28 noon; production begins July 1."
5. Set response deadline: June 28, 10am (even tighter than normal backup window).
6. If ANY of the backup tier responds by June 28, 10am, score and select immediately. Do not wait for all backups to respond — select the highest-scoring backup candidate who confirms availability.

**Contingency resource**: PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md (contains full backup playbook and alternative contractor paths)

---

### Scenario 2: Responses Arrive Slowly (June 24–25 with No Responses)

**Action**:
1. Do not assume silence = decline. Many professionals respond by June 26–27 even if quiet through June 24.
2. June 26 morning: Send one "gentle reminder" email (not pushy) to all 11 contractors who haven't responded yet. Subject: "Quick check-in: Medicinal herb project outreach — decision timeline." Keep tone neutral: "If interested, please reply by June 27 EOD so we can finalize selections for July 1 start."
3. Resume normal monitoring. Do not activate backups until June 27, 6pm cutoff unless a role is entirely uncovered (e.g., zero photographer responses by June 27 noon).

---

### Scenario 3: Responses Arrive, But All Scores Are Below 5.5

**Action**:
1. Do not hire someone with a score below 5.5 unless there is a critical role gap (e.g., zero photographer responses).
2. Activate all backup candidates immediately (June 27 morning).
3. If backup tier also scores below 5.5, escalate to user for decision: "All candidates score below acceptable threshold (5.5). Options: (A) delay production 2–3 weeks to find better fits, (B) hire lowest-score candidate with contingency time buffer in production timeline, or (C) restructure Phase 3 scope to eliminate the weak role (e.g., produce guides without photos)."
4. Document decision and reasoning in CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_DECISION_LOG.md.

---

### Scenario 4: Top Candidate Confirms Availability But Asks for Higher Rate Than Budget

**Action**:
1. Do not automatically reject. Check budget cushion in PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md, Track Budget sections.
2. If there is a $100–$300 cushion in the track budget, offer a 10–15% rate increase: "We can stretch to [higher rate] given your strong fit."
3. If no cushion, offer alternatives: (A) reduced scope (e.g., 4 instead of 6 photos), (B) payment plan (half upfront, half on completion), or (C) trade-off (accept lower rate in exchange for clear, documented deliverables and milestone-based review).
4. If candidate will not negotiate, mark as "Budget Fit" = low score, move to next candidate.
5. Document all rate negotiations in RESPONSE_TRACKING_TEMPLATE.md notes field.
