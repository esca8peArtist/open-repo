# Phase 3 Option Analysis
## Sprint Scope · Cost · Timeline · Revenue

**Prepared**: May 21, 2026
**Decision deadline**: May 30, 2026
**Execution window**: June 22 – August 3, 2026 (full 5-bundle sequence)
**Source document**: PHASE_3_MEDICINAL_HERBS_CRITICAL_PATH.md v6.0

## Lead Finding

Option C (3-bundle solo: Women's Health + Respiratory + Sleep) is the lowest-risk path to a July 13 practitioner-tier unlock at zero out-of-pocket cost. The 90-day revenue difference versus a full 5-bundle launch is approximately $745 — this gap closes by September from ongoing sales momentum on the three live bundles. Option B (5-bundle with a second writer) accelerates the full library to August 15 and cuts the user's writing load in half, but requires a $1,600–$2,000 writer hire confirmed by June 1 and a practitioner-grade herbalist available on four weeks' notice. Option A (5-bundle solo) is viable only if 5+ hours of daily writing is confirmed available starting June 22 — the pace requirement is the primary risk factor.

## Section 3: Recommendation Framework

### Primary Decision Driver: Writing Availability

The single most important question before May 30 is not which option maximizes 90-day revenue — it is whether 5+ hours per day of focused writing is available starting June 22 and sustained through July 5.

- If yes, with high confidence: Option A is viable. The 90-day revenue difference (~$745 vs. Option C) captures the full library market window without any writer hiring overhead.
- If no (or uncertain): Option C is the correct choice. The pace gate at June 24 (Women's Health must be at 2,500 words EOD D3) is the built-in enforcement mechanism — if pace is short at that check, Option C activates automatically regardless.
- If writing is available but parallel work is constrained: Option B is the right choice only if a qualified writer is available by June 1 and the user's freed writing hours go directly to high-leverage activities (practitioner outreach, affiliate setup, practitioner-tier collateral).

## Section 5: Bundle-by-Bundle Demand Rationale

The Option C bundle selection (Women's Health, Respiratory, Sleep) was not arbitrary — it reflects three independent demand signals and the lowest contraindication-risk combination:

**Women's Health (June 29)**
- Black Cohosh is an uncontested Tier 3 Etsy keyword (high practitioner-buyer intent, low competition). No other Phase 3 bundle has this keyword profile.
- Leads forager cohort cross-sell because practitioner-tier buyers represent the highest repeat-purchase probability.
- Peer review requirement is highest here (RH reviewer for Black Cohosh and Vitex content). Front-loading ensures the reviewer relationship is established before other bundles go live.

**Respiratory Health (July 6–7)**
- Elderberry cold-season research intent builds July–August — the timing is intentional, not arbitrary.
- The Echinacea two-species comparison (E. purpurea vs. E. angustifolia) is a practitioner-differentiation signal that competitors do not provide.
- Optional peer review only — lower FTC and contraindication complexity than Women's Health or Immunity.

**Sleep and Nervines (July 13)**
- July burnout-resolution peak is the strongest seasonal alignment of the three.
- Activates the practitioner tier ($120–$150 per bundle) on July 15, meeting the 3-bundle clinical library minimum.
- Mandatory sedative drug interaction warning (Valerian + CNS depressants) and MAOI note (Passionflower) make this the bundle that most requires user-authored accuracy — not a good candidate for writer delegation.

**Immunity (July 20) and Digestive (August 3)**
- Both are by-design post-sprint, not deferred due to failure.
- Immunity's Goldenseal CITES sidebar and Ashwagandha thyroid warning are the most precision-critical content in Phase 3. These sections benefit from the additional time available under Option C.
- Digestive's Dandelion cross-sell hook (FORAGER20 Kit automation trigger) is aligned to the August 3 upload date regardless of option selected.
