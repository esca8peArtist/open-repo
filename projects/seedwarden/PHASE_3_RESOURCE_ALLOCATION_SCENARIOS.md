## Section 3: Photography Sequence

### 3.1 Pre-Sprint Photography Track (June 3–21)

Photography runs before the sprint and does not compete with writing time. It is not a launch gate — all 14 unique species have verified Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA or iNaturalist CC-BY fallback sufficient for guide launch quality.

**Phase 1: Props Acquisition (June 3–9, 2 hours)**

Budget: $60–$100.
- Kraft paper roll (background) — hardware store or Amazon
- Mortar and pestle (stone or ceramic, medium size)
- Glass jars (4 oz, 6-pack — wide-mouth Ball or similar)
- Natural linen fabric (20"×30" minimum)
- Wooden tray or cutting board (for props staging)
- White foam core reflector card (18"×24")
- Digital spray bottle for herb freshening (optional)

Setup location: north-facing window with diffuse natural light, 9am–2pm window. No artificial lighting required.

**Phase 2: Seedling Photography (June 3–9, 4 hours)**

Seeds to sow by May 26 (germination 2–4 weeks, results available by June 3–9):
- Calendula — fast germinator (10–14 days)
- Red Clover — 14–21 days
- Lemon Balm — 14–21 days
- Thyme — 14–21 days
- Lavender — slow (21–28 days; buy nursery start if seed-started plants are not ready by June 3)

Target: 30–40 images per session; cull to 20–30 keepers. Shot list:
1. Seedling in cell tray (full tray, multiple seedlings)
2. Single seedling in pot (clean background)
3. Leaf close-up at 1:1 or macro (identifying features)
4. Seedling bundle on linen (4–6 seedlings styled)

**Phase 3: Mature Specimen Photography (June 10–16, 5 hours)**

Black Cohosh arrives June 21–28 if ordered May 25. For the June 10–16 window, photograph any Tier 3 nursery plants acquired locally (Vitex, Lavender, Lemon Balm, Thyme). Shot list per species:
1. Full plant habit (showing growth form)
2. Leaf detail (shape, texture, color)
3. Flower or fruit cluster (if present)
4. Stem close-up (bark, texture, nodes)

Photograph Black Cohosh within 3 days of arrival (plant deteriorates rapidly post-shipping). If arrival is after June 21, use iNaturalist CC-BY Appalachian range images for sprint and photograph live specimen for v1.1 update.

**Phase 4: Dried Herb Studio Session (June 17–21, 3 hours)**

Mountain Rose Herbs order arrives June 17–18 (ordered June 15). This is the highest-impact single photography session — it produces flat-lay content for all five bundle Etsy listing images.

Setup by bundle theme:
- Women's Health: linen background, lavender bundles, calendula flowers, mortar with vitex berries
- Respiratory: white background with elderberry stems, mullein leaf, glass jar with echinacea
- Sleep: dark wood background, valerian root, passionflower dried, lemon balm sprigs, lavender
- Immunity: kraft paper background, goldenseal root (dried if Path 2), ashwagandha root pieces, glass jar with elderberry
- Digestive: warm kraft paper, dandelion root dried, ginger rhizome, calendula, lemon balm

Per-session targets:
- 5 flat-lay hero shots (one per bundle) — primary Etsy listing image, slot 1
- 5 close-up ingredient shots — Etsy listing image slots 2–3
- 5 lifestyle shots (journal, wooden spoon, warm mug styled with herbs) — Etsy listing image slots 4–5
- Total: 60–80 raw; cull to 25–40 keepers across all bundles

**Phase 5: Photo Editing (June 19–21, 3 hours)**

Apply consistent warm preset to all keepers:
- Exposure: +0.2
- Highlights: –20
- Shadows: +15
- White Balance: +200K warmer (if shot under cool natural light)
- Clarity: +10 (adds texture detail for herb photography)

Export settings:
- Resolution: 2400×2400px minimum (Etsy listing image standard)
- Format: JPG, 90% quality
- Color space: sRGB

Create `PHOTO_MANIFEST.csv` on June 20 logging all keepers: filename, species, bundle assignment, shot type, source (studio or CC), CC license if applicable.

## Section 5: AHG Peer Reviewer Coordination

### 5.1 Why This Is Non-Negotiable

The HERBALIST_NETWORK_ECOSYSTEM_MAPPING.md Section 7.3 documents the binary credibility filter applied by registered herbalists to any practitioner-tier purchase. A single sentence in the Etsy listing — "Reviewed by [Name], RH (AHG), [specialty]" — is the single most important conversion lever for the $120–$150 practitioner bundle tier. Without it, AHG directory cold outreach will convert at low rates regardless of content quality.

The reviewer does not need to review every word. They need to confirm accuracy on the high-liability sections: Black Cohosh conservation sidebar, Vitex invasive-species framing, Goldenseal CITES sidebar (if Immunity bundle is in scope), and Ashwagandha thyroid warning.

### 5.2 Recruitment Timeline

**June 8 — Initial Outreach (8 target practitioners)**

Send personalized email to 8 AHG-directory RH practitioners with Women's Health or Reproductive Health specialty. Source from directory.americanherbalistsguild.com, filter by specialty.

Email content:
- Brief introduction and guide purpose (2 sentences)
- Explicit request: "Would you be willing to review a 2-page excerpt from our Black Cohosh section for botanical nomenclature and contraindication accuracy? This would take approximately 15–20 minutes and we would credit you in the published listing as reviewer."
- Attach PDF excerpt: Black Cohosh identification, 150-word conservation sidebar, contraindication block (approximately 400 words)
- Response deadline: June 18

**June 15 — Follow-Up Wave**

- Re-contact non-respondents from June 8 with brief follow-up (1 sentence)
- Expand outreach to UpS practitioner network: email info@unitedplantsavers.org requesting referral to any member willing to review Black Cohosh content for conservation accuracy
- Expand to Herbal Academy affiliate contact (partnerships@theherbalacademy.com) requesting a reviewer referral from their instructor network

**June 21 — Deadline**

Reviewer written confirmation must be in hand before sprint Day 1. "Written confirmation" means an email from the reviewer stating they have reviewed the attached excerpt and attest to its botanical and contraindication accuracy. Log confirmation sender, date, and credential in WORKLOG.md.

If no reviewer is confirmed by June 21: begin sprint June 22 as planned. Upload Women's Health on June 29 without reviewer quote. Add reviewer quote to listing description retroactively when secured. This is the PHASE_3_GANTT_TIMELINE.csv Contingency 6 scenario — a revenue ceiling risk, not a sprint blocker.

### 5.3 Feedback Cycle Window

If a reviewer responds with requested edits (expected: 0–3 minor clarifications per section):
- Edits are likely to be: species authority formatting, contraindication specificity, or evidence tier attribution
- Revision time budget: 1–2 hours per reviewer comment set
- Sprint absorption: the self-edit revision buffer (1.5–2 hours embedded per bundle) can absorb up to 2 hours of reviewer-driven revisions
- If revisions exceed 2 hours: flag as a quality signal and expand the revision window; the June 29 upload date is protected by the self-edit buffer and the 2-day float on D6–D7

Email the PHASE_3_HERBALIST_NETWORK_PRESTAGING.md contact list (ND or RH with clinical herbal medicine focus) by July 2 with the Immunity draft Goldenseal CITES sidebar and Ashwagandha thyroid warning sections.
