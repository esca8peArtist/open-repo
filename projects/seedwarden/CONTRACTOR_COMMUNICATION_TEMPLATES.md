# Contractor Communication Templates — Q3 Launch

**How to use**: Templates are labeled A through F. Each has a "When to send" note, a subject line, and a body. Fill every [BRACKET] field before sending. Do not skip any field — blanks in sent emails erode professional credibility and create ambiguity around payment and deliverables.

## Template D: Quality Gate Revision Request

**When to send**: Within 24 hours of reviewing a deliverable that does not pass its quality gate. Never wait more than 48 hours — delayed feedback is more disruptive than fast feedback on a failure.

**Subject**: [NAME] — [DELIVERABLE] revision notes (due [REVISION DATE])

---

Hi [NAME],

Thank you for delivering [DELIVERABLE] on [DATE]. I've reviewed it and have [NUMBER] items to flag before I can approve.

**Issue 1 — [SPECIFIC ISSUE, not evaluative]**:
- What the problem is: [Describe exactly. Examples:]
  - "The ashwagandha section (page 3) includes the sentence 'ashwagandha reduces cortisol levels.' Per our FTC guide, this is a therapeutic claim without evidence-tier framing. It needs to read 'Research suggests ashwagandha may support cortisol regulation' or equivalent, with a citation in the same sentence."
  - "Image `respiratory-flatlay-03.jpg` has a shadow crossing the guide title text. At Etsy thumbnail size (170×135px), the text is unreadable. Please reshoot this angle or submit an alternate frame from the same session."
  - "The damiana section does not include the required explicit limited-evidence statement. The contract brief specifies this must read: 'Clinical evidence for damiana's effects on human health is limited. Traditional use is documented across multiple cultures, but double-blind trial data is sparse.' Please add this as a named paragraph, not a footnote."
  - "The passionflower section mentions MAOI interactions in a parenthetical in the body text. Per our FTC and safety standard, drug interactions of this severity must appear in the named Contraindications section, not body text. Please move it and expand."

**Issue 2 — [if applicable]**:
- [Describe exactly as above]

**What is working**:
[ONE SPECIFIC POSITIVE NOTE — even in a revision email. Examples:]
- "The elderberry section's treatment of the sambunigrin toxicity is exactly right — specific, clearly framed, not alarmist."
- "The lighting in images 1, 2, and 4 is strong — consistent white balance and the warmth reads well."
- "The review notes for the women's health lemon balm section are actionable — writer will know exactly what to change."

**Revision due**: [DATE — give 48 hours from this email for writers and herbalist; 24 hours for photographers (typically one reshoot or correction)]

I want to approve this and release your [PAYMENT MILESTONE] as soon as the revision comes back clean. Let me know if there are any questions about the specific issues above.

[YOUR NAME]

## Quick Reference — Template Selection Guide

| Situation | Template | Send within |
|---|---|---|
| Candidate selected; ready to extend offer | Template A — Offer Letter | 4 hours of decision |
| Contract signed; kickoff call completed; July 6 | Template B — Week 1 Orientation | Same day as kickoff call |
| Deliverable received; passes quality gate | Template C — Gate Passed | 24 hours of receipt |
| Deliverable received; needs revision | Template D — Revision Request | 24 hours of receipt |
| Contractor is 24h past delivery deadline with no notification | Template E — Escalation Notice | Immediately upon 24h mark |
| All deliverables complete; final payment ready | Template F — Final Payment + Testimonial | Before releasing final payment |

**Tone standard across all templates**: Specific over vague. "The elderberry section correctly cites the Cochrane review on duration of illness" is more useful than "great work." Specific feedback — positive and corrective — is what makes contractors better on the next deliverable and more likely to re-engage for Phase 4.

**FTC and CITES language in these templates is verbatim and intentional.** Do not paraphrase, shorten, or remove these sections. The specificity around damiana's limited-evidence disclaimer, goldenseal's CITES status, and endangered species sourcing language reflects real regulatory and legal considerations, not editorial preference.

**Response SLA**:
- Incoming contractor email: respond within 24 hours
- Incoming deliverable: review and send Template C or D within 24 hours (48 hours maximum)
- Escalation (Template E): if no contractor response within 24 hours of sending, activate CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md — do not send a second escalation email
