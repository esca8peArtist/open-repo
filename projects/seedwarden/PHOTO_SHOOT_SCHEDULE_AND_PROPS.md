## Part 3: Germination Tray Setup — Start Today (April 30)

This is the only action with a hard deadline before the May 10 shoot. Start the germination tray today.

**What you need:**
- Any small tray, shallow container, or egg carton (anything that holds soil)
- Potting mix or seed-starting mix (standard bagged potting soil works)
- Basil seeds (find at any grocery store in the herb/spice section — McCormick and similar brands sell basil seeds, or buy a seed packet from a garden center for $1–$3)
- Alternatively: lettuce or radish seeds (faster germination — 3–5 days vs. 5–7 for basil)

**Setup (5 minutes):**
1. Fill the tray with moistened potting mix, approximately 1 inch deep
2. Scatter seeds across the surface — no need for precise spacing
3. Press lightly to ensure seed contact with soil
4. Cover with plastic wrap or a clear container lid (creates a mini-greenhouse)
5. Place in the warmest spot in the house (top of the refrigerator, near a heating vent, or any warm surface)
6. Keep moist but not wet — check daily and mist if the surface is drying

**Timeline to May 10 shoot:**
- Day 1 (April 30): seeds sown
- Day 5–7 (May 5–7): germination visible; remove plastic cover
- Day 10 (May 10): seedlings have their first true leaves — ideal for photographing

**If seeds do not germinate by May 8:** The germination tray is an optional prop. Products 5 and 7 are both improved by it but are not blocked by its absence. Check that the tray stayed moist and warm; if not, resow a second tray immediately. The shoot proceeds either way.

## Part 6: Post-Shoot File Handling

### Immediate Transfer After Each Session

Transfer photos from your phone to your computer immediately after each cluster session — do not wait until all three clusters are shot. Transfer method:
- USB cable (fastest and most reliable)
- AirDrop or Google Photos sync (slower but wireless)
- iCloud or Google Photos auto-upload (runs in background — confirm it is enabled before shoot day)

### Editing Batch Sequence

Edit in cluster batches, not photo by photo:
1. Import all Cluster A photos (up to 20 raw shots including test shots)
2. Edit one Cluster A shot to final look (apply standard Seedwarden adjustments from PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md Part 1)
3. Copy settings and paste to all other Cluster A photos
4. Review individually and adjust any outliers
5. Export all Cluster A photos
6. Repeat for Cluster B, then Cluster C

**Standard Seedwarden editing adjustments** (from PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md Part 1):
- Exposure: +0.2 to +0.5
- Contrast: +20
- Highlights: -30
- Shadows: +20
- White balance: 5500–6000K
- Green saturation: +12
- Blue saturation: -10
- Crop to 1:1 square before export
