# Photo Shoot Planning
## Seedwarden Phase 2 — Lifestyle Image Shot List

**Purpose**: This document is the scene-by-scene production guide for the 15 physical product lifestyle shots that will fill Etsy slots 4 and 5 for Seedwarden's five core product clusters. It is written for the person setting up and shooting — no prior photography experience assumed. The hybrid photography approach from LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md is the operating assumption: physical photography for Clusters A, B, and C (15 products); stock compositing for Clusters D and E (6 products). This doc covers only the physical photography scope.

**Decision note**: LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md is awaiting user approval. This doc advances production planning so that once the user confirms the physical photography route, the shoot can begin immediately with no additional planning work needed.

## Part 1: Technical Specifications

All physical lifestyle photos must meet these specs before upload to Etsy.

### Output Requirements

| Parameter | Spec | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Final resolution | 2400 x 2400 px minimum | Etsy recommends 2000px shortest side; 2400 gives cropping headroom |
| Aspect ratio | 1:1 (square) primary, 4:3 secondary for Pinterest | Etsy thumbnail crops to square; 4:3 serves Pinterest repurpose |
| File format | JPEG | Etsy requirement; JPEG at 85–90% quality is lossless at display size |
| File size | Under 1 MB per image | Etsy upload speed; buyers see full-res only on click |
| Color mode | sRGB | Screen display standard; do not use Adobe RGB (shifts on non-color-managed screens) |
| Minimum megapixels at capture | 12 MP | iPhone 14, iPhone 15, Pixel 7, Galaxy S22 or newer all qualify |

### Capture Settings

Shoot in **portrait mode for close-ups** (subjects closer than 18 inches). Shoot in **standard photo mode for flat lays** (overhead shots from 24+ inches — portrait blur does not work correctly at that distance and will blur your props instead of the background).

**White balance**: Set manually to "Daylight" or "Cloudy" rather than "Auto." Auto white balance drifts between frames and makes batch editing harder. A consistent warm daylight setting (5500–6000K) matches Seedwarden's brand tone.

**Exposure**: Slightly expose for the highlights (the brightest element in frame). Underexposure recovers better in editing than blown highlights. If shooting indoors near a window, the bright window itself should read as soft glow, not white wash.

### Editing Pipeline

All 15 images should be edited in a single batch session, not image by image. The recommended workflow:

1. Import all images from one cluster into Lightroom Mobile (free) or Snapseed (free)
2. Edit one image from the cluster to final look using the adjustments below
3. Save as a preset or copy settings
4. Paste preset to all remaining images in the cluster
5. Export at 2400px on the long edge, JPEG quality 88–90%

**Standard adjustments for all Seedwarden lifestyle images:**
- Exposure: +0.2 to +0.5 (keep shadows open; earthy not dramatic)
- Contrast: +20 (adds richness without harshness)
- Highlights: -30 (recovers window blow-out)
- Shadows: +20 (opens mid-shadow detail)
- White balance: 5500–6000K (warm daylight; avoid cool/blue tones)
- Green saturation: +12 (makes foliage and herbs pop slightly)
- Blue saturation: -10 (counteracts any sky or cool cast)
- Crop to 1:1 square before export

## Part 2: Location and Prop Sourcing

### Shooting Locations by Cluster

**For all indoor shots (Clusters A, B, C):**

- **Primary location**: Kitchen table or work surface, positioned 3–5 feet from the largest window in the house. Mid-morning light (9am–12pm) is ideal — soft, directional, warm without being orange.
- **Secondary location**: Potting bench (garage or outdoor covered area). Good for seed-related products; works even on overcast days.
- **Avoid**: Directly in front of a window (silhouettes the subject), under overhead kitchen lights only (warm from above, shadows below), outdoors in full direct sun (harsh shadows and reflections).

**Backdrop surfaces — have at least two ready:**

| Surface | Best for | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Raw wood cutting board (large, 18"+ wide) | All three clusters; the most versatile option | $0 (likely on hand) or $15–25 |
| Dark linen/canvas cloth (navy, charcoal, forest green) | Clusters A and C; makes seed envelopes and jars pop | $10–15 at a fabric store |
| Terracotta-colored tile or stone board | Cluster B urban/container content | $0–20 depending on what's available |
| Weathered concrete or stone (outdoor patio, step) | Any outdoor flat lay | $0 |

Do not use:
- White seamless paper — reads as commercial product photography, not lifestyle
- Patterned tablecloths — competes with props and product
- Shiny surfaces — introduces reflections that are difficult to remove

### Master Props List

Source these before the shoot. Most should be on hand or available at a local hardware/garden store.

**Cluster A — Seeds and Garden (8 products):**
- Seed envelopes (plain kraft, labeled or unlabeled; 5–10)
- Small ceramic or terracotta pots (2–3, 4-inch size)
- A trowel or hand cultivator
- Germination tray with actual sprouts (optional but excellent — 7-day basil or lettuce sprouts are easy to grow in advance)
- Mason jar filled with saved seeds (any large seeds — squash, bean, sunflower work visually)
- Sprig of fresh herbs (basil, parsley, rosemary — whatever is accessible)
- A handwritten seed label or small chalkboard marker tag

**Cluster B — Urban and Container Growing (4 products):**
- Potted herb (mint, basil, or trailing rosemary — something with visual interest)
- Small watering can (metal or ceramic preferred over plastic)
- Pair of gardening gloves (worn, not new — new gloves read as props; worn ones read as use)
- A grow tray or seedling flat
- Apartment context: window sill, balcony railing, or small shelf visible in background

**Cluster C — Food Preservation (3 products):**
- Three to four mason jars with contents (ferments, pickles, dried herbs, or hot sauce — anything amber, red, or green reads well)
- A canning funnel or jar lifter visible in frame
- Fresh chili peppers or garlic bulbs
- Small wooden cutting board or spoon
- A cloth napkin or cheesecloth (textural element)

## Part 4: Batch Shooting Schedule

Organize the shoot into three sessions, one per cluster, on three separate days or in a single long Saturday with cluster transitions.

| Session | Cluster | Products | Estimated Time | Time of Day |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Session 1 | A — Seeds and Garden | 8 products (16 shots) | 3.5–4.5 hours | 9am–1pm (window light peak) |
| Session 2 | B — Urban/Container | 4 products (8 shots) | 1.5–2 hours | 9am–noon or 3pm–5pm |
| Session 3 | C — Food Preservation | 3 products (6 shots) | 1.5–2 hours | Any time (kitchen shoot) |

**Within each session:**
- Set up cluster backdrop and props fully before shooting any product (20–30 min per cluster).
- Shoot all Slot 4 images for the entire cluster before moving to Slot 5 (reduces props reset time).
- Transfer photos immediately after each session — do not wait until all three sessions are complete before editing.

**Setup time by cluster:**
- Cluster A: 25 min (wood surface, seeds, envelopes, mason jar, pot — all must be gathered and arranged)
- Cluster B: 20 min (potted plants, containers, window positioning)
- Cluster C: 15 min (mason jars, cutting board, cloth napkin — fastest cluster to set up)

## Part 5: Styling Guidelines

### Universal Rules (All 15 Products)

1. **No competing text in frame.** Seed envelopes should not have visible brand names from commercial suppliers. Tape over or turn away from camera. No grocery store labels on jars.

2. **Hands in frame communicate use.** Any shot where hands appear should show them mid-action, not posed. Holding a pencil over a page works. Cupped hands holding seeds works. Hovering a hand next to a product does not work — it looks staged.

3. **One clear focal point per shot.** Props exist to support the product image, not to share equal visual weight. If the frame feels busy, remove one prop.

4. **Consistent angle within a cluster.** All Slot 4 images for Cluster A should be shot from the same overhead angle. All Slot 4 images for Cluster C should be shot from the same 45-degree angle. Viewers browsing the shop across multiple listings should feel visual consistency.

5. **No perfectionism on props arrangement.** A slightly rumpled cloth napkin, a pencil that rolled slightly off-axis, a jar that is not perfectly aligned with the frame edge — these all read as authentic. A perfectly symmetrical arrangement reads as staged.

6. **Avoid monoculture greens.** Do not let the entire frame be one flat shade of green (happens easily with herb props). Vary texture: rough clay pot, smooth jar glass, soft linen, raw wood grain.

### Per-Cluster Tone Guidance

| Cluster | Tone | Color Direction | Common Mistake to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — Seeds/Garden | Earthy, active, productive | Warm browns and greens; ochre seed envelopes | Over-styling — too many props |
| B — Urban/Container | Modern, achievable, space-efficient | Terracotta accents, bright green plants, urban neutrals | Generic apartment look — needs to read as someone's actual space |
| C — Food Preservation | Warm, artisanal, satisfying | Amber and red from ferments and peppers; dark wood | Dark studio look — needs warmth from the food itself |

## Part 6: Best Practices from Etsy Lifestyle Research

The following practices are consistent with Etsy's own seller research and independent conversion analysis for digital products in the craft/artisan/independent seller market:

**Etsy's 5+ image rule.** Listings with five or more images outperform three-image listings by 20–40% in conversion. Physical lifestyle shots fill slots 4 and 5 to reach that threshold for all 21 products.

**Lifestyle shots convert 3x better than flat lays alone.** For digital products specifically, the flat lay (slot 1) establishes what the product is. The lifestyle shot (slot 4) answers whether it is for someone like the buyer. Both are necessary; neither alone is sufficient.

**Authenticity outperforms polish in craft/independent markets.** Seedwarden's brand positioning (honest, independent, anti-corporate) means an image shot in an actual kitchen with actual preserved food will consistently outperform a professional iStock image of someone else's kitchen. The brand signal and the image signal must match.

**Hands are the fastest authenticity cue.** A disembodied tablet on a table signals "mockup." The same tablet held in a hand near a garden signals "real person using this." The difference is one prop (a willing hand and a 3-second timer) and the conversion impact is measurable.

**Thumbnail crops at 1:1.** All composition decisions should be tested at a mental 1:1 square crop from the center. The product must be visible and recognizable in the thumbnail version of the image. If the product image (tablet screen, printed page) gets cropped out in the square version, recompose.

## Part 7: Post-Shoot File Handling

### Naming Convention

All final exported lifestyle images should be saved to:
`/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/etsy-ready/`

Filename format: `[product-slug]-slot[number]-lifestyle.jpg`

Examples:
- `seed-saving-field-manual-slot4-lifestyle.jpg`
- `seed-saving-field-manual-slot5-lifestyle.jpg`
- `fermented-preserved-harvest-handbook-slot4-lifestyle.jpg`
- `grow-your-own-hot-sauce-slot5-lifestyle.jpg`

### WORKLOG Entry Per Image

When logging in WORKLOG.md, each lifestyle image entry should include:
- Product name
- Slot number (4 or 5)
- Shot description (one sentence)
- Any post-processing notes (e.g., "warmed white balance significantly in editing")
- Approval status (draft / approved / uploaded)

### Integration with Etsy Upload Sequence

Physical lifestyle photos are Etsy slot 4 and slot 5. They do not replace slots 1–3 (mockups, already complete). At upload time:
- Slot 1: Cover mockup (complete)
- Slot 2: Interior page mockup (complete)
- Slot 3: Phone mockup (complete)
- Slot 4: Lifestyle flat lay or scene shot (this document)
- Slot 5: In-use or detail shot (this document)

Upload via Etsy listing editor: drag slot 4 and slot 5 positions specifically. Do not replace slots 1–3.
