# Species Guide Template — Single Medicinal Herb

**Template version**: 1.0  
**Use case**: Individual species guides (extracted from bundles or developed independently)  
**Target word count**: 400–500 words  
**Est. development time**: 3–5 hours (can reuse content from bundle, then expand and contextualize)  
**Status**: Supplementary tier (develop after 5 bundles launch)

---

## Overview

Single-species guides are deeper dives into one medicinal plant. They serve practitioners who:
- Want focused content on their favorite or most-used herb
- Need portable reference (smaller than full bundles)
- Are creating curriculum or teaching materials

These guides can be **extracted from bundle content** (reduce development time to 2 hours) or 
**developed independently** (full 3–5 hours).

---

## Structure (400–500 words total)

### 1. Quick Reference (50–75 words)

Condensed fact box at the top:

```
QUICK REFERENCE — [Species Name]

Common names: [Common names used in English-speaking regions]
Scientific name: [Genus species, with author if relevant]
Plant family: [Family name, e.g., Ranunculaceae]
Growing zones: [USDA hardiness, e.g., 3–9]
Plant height: [Typical mature height, e.g., 2–4 feet]
Life cycle: [Annual/biennial/perennial]
Medicinal parts: [Leaf, root, flower, seed — what you harvest]
Harvest season: [Month range, e.g., July–September]
Primary preparation: [Tea, tincture, oil, etc.]
Common name origin: [Etymology if interesting, optional]
```

**Word count**: 60–75 words

---

### 2. Identification (75–100 words)

Visual description for confident field identification:

```
IDENTIFICATION

[Detailed appearance description covering:]
- Overall growth habit and size
- Leaf shape, texture, veining, color (top and underside)
- Stem color, texture, nodes/internodes
- Flower color, structure, bloom season
- Fruit/seed structure (if relevant)
- Distinctive scent, taste (if safe), or tactile features

Lookalikes: [Name any plants easily confused with this species; explain differences clearly]

Natural range: [Geographic region where it grows wild, if applicable]

Where to find it: [Habitat—forest floor, meadow, disturbed ground, etc.; when to observe it seasonally]

Identification timing: [Best time of year to identify confidently—usually flowering or fruiting season]
```

**Word count**: 85–110 words

---

### 3. Cultivation (80–120 words)

How to grow from seed or divisions:

```
CULTIVATION

Growing zones: [USDA zones where this thrives]

Site requirements: [Sun/shade preference, soil type, moisture, pH]

Propagation: [Seed vs. division vs. transplant; provide step-by-step for primary method]

Planting timeline: [When to sow/plant in your region; seasonal cues]

Spacing & establishment: [Distance between plants, timeline to maturity, years to first harvest]

Maintenance: [Watering, feeding, pest management, pruning if applicable]

Recommended suppliers: [2–3 suppliers with websites, species availability]

Notes: [Any special considerations—slow establishment, pest pressure, drought/wet tolerance, etc.]
```

**Word count**: 100–130 words

---

### 4. Active Constituents & Traditional Use (75–100 words)

What makes this plant medicinal:

```
ACTIVE CONSTITUENTS & TRADITIONAL USE

Primary compounds: [Alkaloids, glycosides, polysaccharides, etc., + brief explanation of function]

Mechanism of action: [How does this compound affect the body—research-backed or traditional understanding]

Traditional use: [Where this herb was used historically—which culture, time period, condition]

Modern clinical research: [1–2 sentence summary of clinical trial findings, if any; citation not required]

Modern herbalism: [Contemporary use in Western herbalism, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, etc.]
```

**Word count**: 85–105 words

---

### 5. Contraindications & Cautions (50–75 words)

Safety information:

```
CONTRAINDICATIONS & CAUTIONS

Pregnancy/lactation: [Safe/avoid/use with caution]

Drug interactions: [Any known interactions with medications]

Allergies/sensitivities: [Cross-reactivity risks, plant family allergies]

Adverse effects: [Possible side effects, overdose signs]

Contraindicated for: [Any specific conditions or populations where use is inadvisable]

[Use FTC-compliant language: "traditionally used for", "studied for", NOT "treats" or "cures"]
```

**Word count**: 60–80 words

---

### 6. Harvesting & Drying (75–100 words)

When and how to harvest:

```
HARVESTING & DRYING

What to harvest: [Which part of the plant—leaf, root, flower, seed]

When to harvest: [Seasonal timing, plant maturity cues, daily timing if relevant]

How to harvest: [Technique—careful cutting, digging, gentle handling to preserve plant]

Post-harvest handling: [Cleaning, initial processing before drying]

Drying method: [Temperature range (hang-dry, dehydrator, room temperature); timeline]

Storage: [Container, conditions, shelf life; signs of degradation]

Yield: [Example: "one mature plant yields ~30g dried leaf per year"]
```

**Word count**: 85–110 words

---

### 7. Preparation & Dosage (75–100 words)

How to use the herb:

```
PREPARATION & DOSAGE

Primary preparation: [Tea, tincture, oil, capsule, etc.—most common method]

Recipe: [Specific proportions: e.g., "1 tsp dried leaf per 8 oz hot water, steep 5–10 min"]

Dosage (educational): [Dose range used by experienced herbalists, e.g., "20–40 drops tincture 2–3× daily"]

Duration: [How long to use: "6–12 weeks before evaluating effectiveness"]

Timing: [Any specific guidance on when to take (morning/evening, with food, etc.)]

Alternative preparations: [2–3 other valid methods and their advantages/disadvantages]

Palatability/taste: [How this herb tastes; any tips for palatability]
```

**Word count**: 90–120 words

---

### 8. Sourcing & Conservation (50–75 words)

[Include ONLY for at-risk species: Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, E. angustifolia]

```
SOURCING & CONSERVATION

Status: [Is this species wild-harvested, cultivated, or both?]

Conservation concerns: [Any threats to wild populations; sustainability status]

Sourcing guidance: [Cultivated vs. wild-harvested; preferred suppliers]

Certifications: [FGV (Forest Grown Verified), organic, fair-trade status if available]

If at-risk: [Explicitly recommend cultivated sources only; provide supplier links]

Resources: [United Plant Savers, Forest Farming information, etc.]
```

**Word count**: 65–85 words

## Implementation Notes

### Source Material

Single-species guides can be developed in two ways:

**Option A: Extract from bundle** (2–3 hours)
1. Copy the herb profile section from the relevant bundle (3.1–3.8 from medicinal-bundle-outline-template.md)
2. Condense overview paragraph (3.1) to 50 words
3. Keep identification (3.2), cultivation (3.3), harvest (3.4), constituents (3.5) largely as-is
4. Trim contraindications (3.6) to core information
5. Keep sourcing section (3.7) if at-risk species
6. Convert practitioner notes (3.8) to dosage/preparation guidance (section 7)
7. Total content reuse: ~70%; development time: 2–3 hours

**Option B: Develop independently** (3–5 hours)
1. Write from scratch for an herb not yet covered in bundles
2. Use same structure as above
3. Research from sourcing guide, botanical references, practitioner experience
4. Full development time: 3–5 hours
5. Can be published immediately or incorporated into future bundle work

### Etsy Product Strategy

**Publication timing**: After 2–3 bundles are live with reviews  
**Pricing**: $10–$14 per species guide (lower than bundle, smaller product)  
**Bundling**: After 5+ individual guides, create a "Species Guide Collection" bundle  
**Upsell path**: Individual bundle → Individual species guides → Collection bundle → Practitioner 10-pack

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Word count: 400–500 words (sections 1–8)
- [ ] Identification section includes 2–3 distinguishing features
- [ ] Cultivation information is specific to this species (not generic)
- [ ] Dosage uses educational/practitioner language (not medicinal claims)
- [ ] At-risk species include conservation section (section 8)
- [ ] All sources cited (URLs required for supplier links)
- [ ] No prohibited medicinal claims ("treats", "cures", etc.)
- [ ] Preparation method includes specific proportions (not vague "as needed")
- [ ] Contraindications section complete (pregnancy, drug interactions, allergies)
