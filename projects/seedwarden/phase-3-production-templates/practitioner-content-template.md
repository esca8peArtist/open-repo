### Part 1: Practitioner License & Scope Statement (2–3 pages, 600–800 words)

**Purpose**: Establish credibility with practitioner audience and manage liability

```
PRACTITIONER LICENSE & SCOPE STATEMENT

Who This Is For

This complete medicinal herbs bundle is designed specifically for:
- Registered Herbalists-AHG (American Herbalists Guild credential holders)
- Licensed acupuncturists and Traditional Chinese Medicine practitioners using herbal medicine
- Naturopathic doctors (state-licensed where applicable)
- Midwives and doulas integrating herbal support into reproductive healthcare
- Nurses with herbal certification or herbalism training
- Integrative healthcare providers seeking herbal protocols
- Clinical herbalists and herbalism educators

This bundle is NOT for:
- Self-care home herbal use (purchase individual bundles instead)
- Veterinary use (not formulated for animal medicine)
- Pharmaceutical or clinical trial purposes
- Marketing or therapeutic claims in unlicensed contexts

Licensing & Scope Notes

Medicinal herb practitioners operate under different regulatory frameworks depending on location and credential:

REGISTERED HERBALIST (RH-AHG, American Herbalists Guild)
- Credential: American Herbalists Guild Registered Herbalist standard
- Scope: Educational guidance on herbal use; typically not prescriptive
- Use: This guide as educational reference and client education material
- Liability: Operate under your own professional liability insurance

LICENSED ACUPUNCTURIST / TCM PRACTITIONER
- Credential: State license (varies by state)
- Scope: Herbal medicine typically within scope of practice for licensed acupuncturists
- Use: This guide as clinical reference; adapt monographs to TCM framework
- Liability: Covered under acupuncture professional liability; document herbal recommendations in patient charts

LICENSED NATUROPATHIC DOCTOR (where licensed)
- Credential: ND license (Oregon, Washington, Vermont, US Virgin Islands, and other states)
- Scope: Herbal medicine within scope in licensed states
- Use: This guide as clinical reference and patient education
- Liability: Covered under ND professional liability; document recommendations

MIDWIFE / DOULA
- Credential: CPM (Certified Professional Midwife) or CEUS-certified doula
- Scope: Herbal support for reproductive health within scope of midwifery practice
- Use: This guide for labor support, postpartum recovery, breastfeeding; Women's Health 
  bundle is especially relevant
- Liability: Document recommendations; work within your scope as defined by your credential and state law

NURSE WITH HERBAL CERTIFICATION
- Credential: RN + Herbal certification (various programs)
- Scope: Herbal education and support within nursing scope; not prescriptive
- Use: This guide as clinical reference for patient education
- Liability: Practice within nursing scope; hospital policies may restrict herbal recommendations

FTC Compliance & Therapeutic Claims

This guide is educational and informational. All recommendations in these guides comply with 
FTC guidance on dietary supplements and herbal products:

✓ Permitted language:
- "Traditionally used for..."
- "Studied in clinical trials for..."
- "Supports..." (structure/function)
- "Contains [compound] which is researched for..."

✗ Prohibited language (do not use):
- "Cures [disease]"
- "Treats [disease]"
- "Prevents [disease]"
- "Diagnostic claims" (diagnosing or suggesting diagnosis)
- "Drug claims" (comparing to pharmaceuticals or claiming drug-like action)

If you are using this guide to provide practitioner services, consult your own legal counsel 
regarding claims appropriate to your license and jurisdiction.

This guide contains FTC-compliant language throughout. If you adapt content for client materials, 
maintain this language and include appropriate disclaimers.

Informed Consent & Client Documentation

As a practitioner, you are responsible for:
1. Obtaining informed consent from clients before herbal recommendations
2. Documenting herbal recommendations in client records
3. Tracking client progress and adverse effects
4. Referring to medical doctors when appropriate
5. Maintaining professional boundaries and scope of practice

Sample informed consent language:

"Herbal medicine has a long history of use. Clinical research on some herbs is robust; 
on others it is limited. I am recommending these herbs based on traditional use and 
available research. You have the right to decline herbal recommendations and seek 
conventional medical care instead. Herbs can interact with medications and may not be 
appropriate for all individuals. I will ask you questions about your health, medications, 
and allergies to ensure safe recommendations. Please inform me immediately if you 
experience adverse effects."

[Document client signature and date if implementing formal consent forms]

Your Responsibility & Liability

This guide is a reference tool. You remain responsible for:
- Your own continuing education and training
- Knowledge of your state's legal scope of practice
- Professional liability insurance appropriate to your credential
- Client safety and appropriate referrals
- Documentation and record-keeping
- Compliance with FTC, FDA, and state regulations

Seedwarden provides this educational content in good faith. Seedwarden assumes no 
liability for how practitioners use this information or how clients respond to herbal 
recommendations made by practitioners. You, the practitioner, are responsible for the 
care you provide and the recommendations you make.

Resources for Continuing Education & Legal Compliance

- American Herbalists Guild (americanherbalistsguild.com) — RH-AHG credential, professional standards
- American Herbal Products Association (ahpa.org) — Industry standards and best practices
- Botanical Medicines (formerly Herbal Medicine: Biomolecular and Clinical Aspects) — 
  Peer-reviewed reference
- American Botanical Council (herbalgram.org) — Clinical data and safety information
- State/local licensing boards — For scope of practice in your jurisdiction
- Professional liability insurance — Appropriate to your credential (herbalists, acupuncturists, 
  midwives, etc.)
```

### Part 3: Formulation Guide (8–12 pages, 2,000–2,500 words)

**Purpose**: Teach practitioners how to combine herbs from this bundle into effective formulas

Example excerpt for Women's Health:

```
FORMULATION GUIDE — WOMEN'S HEALTH

Multi-Herb Formulations Using Herbs From This Bundle

Herbalists often combine multiple herbs to create synergistic effects. This section provides 
example formulations using 2–3 herbs from the Women's Health bundle.

FORMULA 1: Cycle Regulation Support

For clients with: Irregular cycles, long cycle days (35+ days), sparse periods, mild PMS

Herbs & ratios:
- Vitex (berry tincture): 30 mL (primary—seed remedy)
- Red Clover (infusion or capsule): 2 cups daily (nutritive, phytoestrogen)
- Lemon Balm (tea, from Sleep bundle if available): 1 cup evening (nervous system support)

Dosage:
- Vitex tincture: 30 drops 2–3× daily (total: 60–90 mL per month)
- Red Clover infusion: 2 cups daily (2 oz dried herb per liter water)
- Lemon Balm: 1 cup before bed
- Duration: 3–4 months before evaluating effectiveness

Rationale:
- Vitex acts on luteinizing hormone (LH), supporting progesterone production
- Red Clover provides iron, minerals, and mild hormone-modulating effects (phytoestrogens)
- Lemon Balm supports nervous system (often underlying irregular cycles is stress-related)
- This combination is gentle and takes time; not indicated for quick symptom relief

Adjustments:
- If client reports anxiety: Increase Lemon Balm or add Lavender
- If heavy bleeding develops: Add Calendula (astringent support) or refer to healthcare provider
- If no change after 4 months: Reassess—may need different primary herb (Black Cohosh, etc.)

Assessment cues to track:
- Cycle length (shorter, longer, or more regular?)
- Flow (change in amount or duration?)
- Mood/PMS (better, same, or worse?)
- Energy (any change?)

FORMULA 2: Menopausal Hot Flash Support

For clients with: Hot flashes, night sweats, hormonal mood swings, perimenopausal transitions

Herbs & ratios:
- Black Cohosh (decoction or tincture): 30 mL daily
- Lavender (tea or tincture): 20 drops 2–3× daily OR 1 cup tea before bed
- Red Clover (infusion): 2–3 cups daily (nutritive, cooling)

Dosage:
- Black Cohosh decoction: 1 cup 3× daily (simmered 0.5 tsp root per cup) OR 
  30 drops tincture 3× daily
- Lavender: 20 drops tincture 2–3× daily OR 1 cup strong tea before bed
- Red Clover: 2–3 cups infusion daily (1 Tbsp dried flowers per cup, steeped 10–15 min)
- Duration: 6–12 weeks for noticeable effect

Rationale:
- Black Cohosh is the primary—research-backed for hot flashes and night sweats
- Lavender cools and calms (useful for daytime flashes and sleep disruption)
- Red Clover is nutritive and cooling (supports the body during hormonal transition)
- This formula addresses symptom relief AND underlying nutritional support

Adjustments:
- If anxiety or insomnia is primary: Emphasize Lavender; add Lemon Balm (1 cup tea)
- If heavy sweating (drenching night sweats): Increase Red Clover to 4 cups daily
- If mood swings (irritability, depression): Add Lemon Balm or consider referral for mental 
  health support
- If no improvement after 6 weeks: Evaluate adherence; consider adding Vitex or trying higher 
  Black Cohosh dose; consider medical evaluation if severe

Assessment cues:
- Hot flashes: Frequency per day? Duration? Severity (minor warmth vs. drenching)?
- Night sweats: Every night? Frequency per night? Require changing sheets?
- Mood: More stable, same, or worsening?
- Sleep: Better, same, or worse?
- Other symptoms: Vaginal dryness, joint pain, brain fog (common in menopause—not addressed 
  by this formula, may need additional support)

[Additional formulas for postpartum support, fertility support, cycle pain support, etc.]
```

```
FOREST GROWN VERIFIED (FGV) & CONSERVATION SOURCING GUIDE

What is FGV?

Forest Grown Verified (FGV) is a third-party certification program operated by United Plant 
Savers (UpS). It certifies that medicinal plants were:
1. Grown in a forest farming system (under existing tree canopy)
2. Verified by UpS inspectors against sustainability standards
3. NOT wild-harvested (or wild-harvested with documented sustainable management)

Why FGV Matters

For at-risk woodland species (Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Echinacea angustifolia), FGV 
certification ensures:
- Sustainable sourcing (not depleting wild populations)
- Habitat preservation (forest farming mimics native habitat)
- Higher therapeutic quality (forest-grown plants often have higher active compound concentrations)
- Ethical alignment with conservation values

Where to Find FGV Certified Material

Visit: unitedplantsavers.org/forest-grown-verified/

Current FGV participants (as of 2026):
- [List active FGV operations in Appalachia, Pacific Northwest, other regions]
- [Include contact info and which species they produce]

FGV In Your Practice

- Cite FGV certification when recommending sources to clients (builds trust with practitioner-minded buyers)
- Prioritize FGV-certified material when available and affordable
- Educate clients about why Forest Grown Verified matters for conservation
- Consider partnership with FGV farms if your practice grows medicinal herbs
```

## Assembly & Implementation Guide

### How to Create a Practitioner 10-Pack

**Step 1: Compile existing content** (1 hour)
- Gather 5 completed bundle guides (Women's Health, Respiratory, Immunity, Sleep, Digestive)
- Each bundle is 3,500–3,800 words in PDF format
- Save as separate files for assembly

**Step 2: Write practitioner-specific sections** (3–4 hours)
- Part 1: License & Scope Statement (write from template above, customize for your context)
- Part 2: Assessment frameworks (adapt template for each bundle)
- Part 3: Formulation guide (select 4–5 example formulas per bundle)
- Part 4: Reference tools (assemble from template sections)

**Step 3: Assemble final document** (1–2 hours)
- Combine all parts in order: License → Assessment → Formulation → 5 Bundles → Reference Tools
- Create table of contents with page numbers
- Add page breaks between major sections
- Number pages consecutively

**Step 4: Export to PDF** (30 minutes)
- Export from Google Docs, LibreOffice, or Word as PDF
- Confirm: No formatting errors, all images embedded, TOC links work (if digital PDF)
- Final file size: ~12–15 MB (acceptable for Etsy delivery, max 25 MB)

**Step 5: Upload to Etsy** (30 minutes)
- Create listing: "Practitioner Bundle — Women's Health Medicinal Herbs Complete Collection"
- Price: $120–$150
- Provide sample pages (screenshot Part 1 + table of contents) in listing graphics
- Use tags: practitioner, herbalist, herbal medicine, RH-AHG, clinical herbalism, etc.

**Total development time**: 6–8 hours per practitioner 10-pack (reusing 5 existing bundles)
