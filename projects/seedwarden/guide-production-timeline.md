## Part 1: Production Order — Species Sequence

**Why Ginseng first**: American Ginseng has the highest search volume of the five species. It is the anchor title for the Appalachian Medicinals series, and the Ginseng guide establishes the master Canva template from which all four subsequent guides are duplicated. Getting it right on Day 1 sets the production pace and eliminates repeated design decisions.

**Why Ramps last**: Ramps is the most culturally compelling guide but the one with the most variable photography sourcing (seasonal leaves; EFN seed availability uncertain). By producing it last, any late-arriving photography can be incorporated without impacting the other four guides. If ramps photography is incomplete by May 29, this guide soft-launches as "coming June 6" without affecting the four-guide May 30 release.

## Part 2: Daily Production Targets

### May 26 — Master Template Day (American Ginseng)

The Ginseng guide is the most time-intensive because every design decision is made fresh. Budget 5–6 hours.

**Template decisions to lock on May 26** (do not revisit in subsequent guides):
- Font pair: header font / body font (use existing Seedwarden Brand Kit if already established in Canva; if not, set it now and lock it)
- Color palette: primary color / accent / conservation status badge color (suggested: deep forest green primary, warm cream background, amber accent for conservation status callout)
- Section structure: Cover / Species Overview / Conservation Status / Identification Guide / Cultivation Method / Harvest Ethics / Cultural History / Seed Sourcing / Disclaimer
- Photo placement grid: define exactly where each of the 6 photo angles appears in the guide (full-plant: page 3, root close-up: page 7, etc.)
- Conservation status badge: a visually distinct callout box that clearly states CITES Appendix II / UpS At-Risk / State status — this is the highest-value information for buyers comparing wild-harvest versus cultivated plants
- Attribution footer: standard format for all iNaturalist CC-BY photo credits

**May 26 end-of-day deliverable**: Ginseng guide complete and exported as PDF (draft quality for internal review). Upload to Google Drive or Dropbox for review on May 27.

### May 27 — Goldenseal (Template Duplication)

Duplication from the master makes May 27 considerably faster than May 26. The only design work is substituting species-specific content.

**Critical content for Goldenseal**:
- CITES Appendix II listing (1997) — note that cultivated material is unrestricted; only wild-harvest roots trigger CITES
- State endangered status: Connecticut, Georgia, Maine, Massachusetts, Minnesota, New Jersey, North Carolina
- Yellow root color callout — this is the primary ID feature; the photography from Cluster 2 should be prominent here
- Commercially available seeds: Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Prairie Moon Nursery, Johnny's Selected Seeds

**May 27 end-of-day deliverable**: Goldenseal guide draft exported and uploaded for review.

### May 28 — Black Cohosh (Full-Content Day)

Black Cohosh has the strongest "cultivation solution" story of the five species. 95% of all commercial black cohosh raw material is still wild-harvested despite two decades of conservation concern (source: `endangered-species-candidate-list.md` Framework 1). This statistic should appear prominently in the guide.

**Critical content for Black Cohosh**:
- UpS At-Risk status; Massachusetts Endangered
- The 95% wild-harvest statistic and its implications
- Menopausal and hormonal health use context — note traditional use without making therapeutic claims; add standard educational disclaimer
- Cultivation guide: establishment from seed (stratification requirements), partial shade requirement, 2–3 years to first harvest

**May 28 internal review**: After completing Black Cohosh draft, conduct a 30-minute review of all three guides against the source document (`endangered-species-candidate-list.md`). Verify: all conservation status statements are accurate, all supplier names are spelled correctly, no therapeutic claims appear without the educational disclaimer.

### May 29 — Bloodroot and Ramps (Sprint Day)

May 29 is the production sprint — two guides in one day. Feasible because the master template is fully established and duplication is the primary task. Budget 5–6 hours total with a break between guides.

**Bloodroot critical content**:
- Toxicity note: sanguinarine is toxic — include prominently with clear disclaimer ("For identification and cultivation only; not for internal use without qualified guidance")
- 1-week seed viability window: this is the strongest cultivation education hook (explains why commercial seed is rare and why rhizome division is the standard propagation method)
- Historical dye and dental-care use: rich content for the cultural history section

**Ramps critical content**:
- Quebec commercial harvest prohibition — the most dramatic regulatory action of the five species
- NY *Allium tricoccum var. burdickii* endangered listing
- Cherokee *wasdi* harvesting traditions: Ecology & Society 2023 paper (cited in `endangered-species-candidate-list.md`) is the source
- 5-year regeneration timeline: this is why pulling the whole plant is ecologically catastrophic — cutting only the leaves allows the bulb to survive and regenerate

### May 30 (Before 10:00am) — QA and Upload

Two hours maximum for final QA and Etsy upload. This must be complete before 10:00am launch.

**Full-set QA checklist**:
- [ ] All iNaturalist CC-BY photo attributions present in every guide (format: "Photo: [Observer Name], iNaturalist, CC BY 4.0")
- [ ] All BHL illustration credits present or public domain status noted
- [ ] No placeholder text remaining (search "TBD," "INSERT," "[Name]" in each PDF before export)
- [ ] Footer URLs point to live Etsy store URL and Kit landing page URL
- [ ] Educational disclaimer on every guide: "This guide is for educational purposes. Consult a qualified practitioner before using any plant medicinally."
- [ ] Conservation status language is accurate and current for all five species
- [ ] Species name spellings correct (Latin names, common names)
- [ ] Bundle listing cover image ready for the 5-guide Appalachian Medicinals bundle Etsy listing

## Part 4: Contingency Decision Tree

### If Photo Shoot Delays to May 27–28

**Scenario**: Photo shoot was not completed by May 25 and photography is not available until May 27–28.

**Impact assessment**:
- Guide production cannot begin until May 28 at the earliest
- 5 guides in 2 days (May 28–30) is not achievable at 1 guide/day pace
- May 30 launch with all 5 guides: NOT FEASIBLE

**Recommended contingency — June 6 launch**:
- Launch June 6 with all 5 guides instead of May 30 with partial set
- Revenue impact: approximately $1,200 delayed by 1 week (based on projected Phase 2 Wave 1 first-week revenue at 8% email list conversion on 50 subscribers at $18–32 price point)
- Quality impact: zero — guides are complete before launch
- Announcement: update Etsy listings to "launching June 6" and send a pre-launch teaser email to the Kit list on May 30 ("Appalachian Medicinals guides — launching in 7 days")
- Decision deadline: if photo processing is not complete by May 26 at 9:00am, announce June 6 launch immediately. Do not attempt a partial May 30 launch with 1–2 guides unless ginseng and goldenseal are both fully edited.

**Minimum viable May 30 launch**: If Ginseng AND Goldenseal are both edit-ready by May 26, a 2-guide soft launch is viable. Launch May 30 with Ginseng + Goldenseal; announce remaining 3 guides releasing June 6. Revenue impact is smaller than full delay, but buyer expectation management requires explicit communication in the listing copy and launch email.

### If Suppliers Miss Delivery (Plants Arrive May 20 Instead of May 18)

**Scenario**: Plant specimens or props arrive May 20 (shoot start day) instead of May 18.

**Impact**: Specimens are not pre-inspected or acclimatized. Shoot proceeds with unacclimatized plants.

**Recovery**:
- Contact 2nd and 3rd vendors on May 19 if primary orders have not confirmed shipping by May 17
- Compressed shoot window: May 21–23 instead of May 20–24 (3 active shoot days, losing 1 day of float)
- Float days May 24–25 absorb re-shoots
- Guide production still begins May 26 on schedule
- No June 6 slip required for a 2-day sourcing delay

**Escalation path if all vendors fail**:
- May 19: order dried specimens from Mountain Rose Herbs (2-day priority ship)
- May 19: purchase any available native plants at local garden center (same-day)
- Compressed shoot May 21–22 using retail dried specimens + iNaturalist CC-BY archive for all in-habitat shots
- Guide production begins May 26 as planned
- No launch date impact — dried specimens are photographically adequate for guide-interior identification sections
