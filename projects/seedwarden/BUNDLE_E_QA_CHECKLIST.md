## Section 2: Safety & Accuracy Verification

Run these checks for every guide after completing Section 1.

### Safety Notes Audit (Per-Guide)

For each guide, verify these specific items are present and explicit. Vague language does not pass.

**Garlic Mustard Safety**
- [ ] Allergy note present: "No common allergic reactions documented" or equivalent explicit statement (not silent omission)
- [ ] Lookalike note: Hedge Garlic, Money Plant (*Lunaria annua*) compared — or confirmed "No toxic lookalikes at the leaf stage"
- [ ] Habitat contamination warning present: "Avoid harvesting near roadsides with heavy vehicle traffic or herbicide-treated areas"
- [ ] Raw vs. cooked: Confirmed safe raw (it is)
- [ ] Harvest stage safety: "Mature plants past flowering are bitter, not toxic — avoid for palatability, not safety"

**Japanese Knotweed Safety**
- [ ] Allergy note present
- [ ] Lookalike note: Common lookalikes (Buckwheat family members) explicitly compared, or confirmed "Hollow bamboo-like stem with swollen nodes is unambiguous"
- [ ] Oxalic acid content noted: "Contains oxalic acid in significant quantity — limit consumption if prone to kidney stones; cooking reduces oxalate load"
- [ ] Habitat contamination: Stream-bank harvesting caution (runoff from upstream), industrial site avoidance
- [ ] Invasive root fragmentation warning: "Do not compost knotweed roots or stems — any fragment can regenerate a new stand"

**Autumn Olive Safety**
- [ ] Allergy note: Some individuals react to Elaeagnus family; note present
- [ ] Lookalike note: Silverberry (*Elaeagnus commutata*) is also edible — note the comparison; Buffaloberry (*Shepherdia argentea*) can be confused in certain regions — note if applicable
- [ ] Oxalic acid note: Low in Autumn Olive, but mention in context of preparation
- [ ] Berry stage safety: "Unripe (green) berries are astringent and may cause stomach upset"
- [ ] Habitat: "Avoid berries from roadsides or fence lines adjacent to treated land"

**Purslane Safety**
- [ ] Allergy note present
- [ ] Lookalike note: **Spurge (*Euphorbia* spp.) is the critical toxic lookalike** — must be explicitly described. Spurge has milky sap; Purslane does not. This distinction must be in the guide.
- [ ] Oxalic acid: Purslane is moderately high in oxalic acid — note for kidney stone-prone individuals, recommend cooking
- [ ] Habitat: "Garden purslane is safe; roadside or industrial-adjacent purslane may carry soil contamination — wash thoroughly and avoid if in doubt"
- [ ] The milky sap / clear sap distinction for Spurge confusion is **mandatory** — if missing, the guide cannot ship

**Multiflora Rose Safety**
- [ ] Allergy note: Rose family allergies documented; note for people with known rose allergies
- [ ] Lookalike note: Native roses (*Rosa carolina*, *Rosa blanda*) are also edible — note comparison, not confusion risk; Wild Parsnip (*Pastinaca sativa*) is not a rose lookalike but grows in similar habitats — note habitat context
- [ ] Petal harvest: "Avoid commercially sprayed roses — only harvest from confirmed unsprayed areas. The presence of other invasives nearby does not confirm absence of herbicide treatment."
- [ ] Hip seeds: "Rose hip seeds/achenes contain hair-like fibers that cause irritation — strain all hip preparations through a fine sieve before consuming"
- [ ] The hip seed fiber warning is **mandatory** — if missing, the guide cannot ship

## Section 6: Escalation Protocol

**Trigger**: Any guide is less than 75% complete OR any mandatory safety item is missing at May 14, 4:00 PM local time.

### Severity Classification

**Critical (blocks launch)**: Missing mandatory safety warning (Spurge distinction for Purslane, hip seed warning for Multiflora Rose). Do not launch without these — fix immediately, even if it pushes launch to May 20.

**Significant (1-day delay acceptable)**: Section missing more than 100 words, recipe count below minimum, no seasonal dates. Fix before upload. May 19 delay to May 20 is acceptable per sprint plan Risk 3.

**Minor (post-launch fix)**: Tone slightly academic in 1-2 sentences, one recipe's storage note missing. Log the issue, ship anyway, update the digital file within 48 hours of launch.

### If More Than 1 Guide Fails the Gate at May 14 EOD

Priority order for completion (most seasonally urgent first):
1. Japanese Knotweed — window closes mid-June
2. Garlic Mustard — window closing now
3. Purslane — available all summer, but spurge safety check is mandatory before shipping
4. Multiflora Rose — petals window closing late June
5. Autumn Olive — harvest is July-September; can launch with a note "berries ready August"

**Minimum viable launch**: 3 guides complete + 2 delayed = launch May 19 with announcement email stating "Autumn Olive and [Species X] guides ship May 22 — included free with your purchase."

### Peer Review Pairing

If capacity allows, assign review pairs to catch what self-review misses:
- Pair A: Garlic Mustard reviews Japanese Knotweed (both spring-harvest, similar safety profile — reviewer will catch seasonal inconsistencies)
- Pair B: Autumn Olive reviews Purslane (both summer/late season — reviewer will catch timing gaps)
- Multiflora Rose: self-review only (unique dual-season structure; cross-species reviewer adds little value)

Peer review is optional but recommended for the safety sections. A second reader for the Purslane Spurge distinction and the Knotweed oxalic acid note is worth 10 minutes.
