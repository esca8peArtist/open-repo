# Track B Independent Launch Roadmap

**What this document is**: A standalone launch plan for Seedwarden's social media presence and lifestyle photography that can be executed immediately, whether or not Phase 1 Etsy listings are live. It assumes the worst-case scenario: Phase 1 remains blocked indefinitely. It plans accordingly.

**What Track B is**: Phase 2 social content (Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest), lifestyle photography sourcing for Etsy slots 4-5, and email list building. The 60-Day Social Content Calendar (phase-2-social-content-calendar-60day.md) is the primary execution document. This roadmap provides the launch conditions, sequencing decisions, and contingency thinking that the calendar does not address.

**Why Track B can launch independently**: Track B's primary assets — the content calendar, pin templates, Canva brand kit, and photography strategy — do not reference live Etsy listings as a prerequisite for content production. They reference them as CTAs. The posts, Reels, carousels, and pins can all be produced, scheduled, and published regardless of whether an Etsy shop is live. The only thing that changes is the call to action at the end of each post.

## Part 1: Independent Launch Conditions

Track B requires five conditions to launch. None of them depend on Phase 1 Etsy status.

**Condition 1: Social media accounts exist**

At minimum, one platform account must be active and in good standing. The priority order is Instagram, then Pinterest, then TikTok. Instagram is the core platform because Reels drive follower growth and the feed/Stories ecosystem gives the content calendar its full structure. Pinterest and TikTok are distribution amplifiers.

Account handle target: `seedwarden` on all three platforms. If unavailable, `seedwarden.co` or `seedwarden.seeds` are acceptable alternatives. The handle should match across all platforms.

Time to complete: 30-60 minutes for account creation across all three platforms.

**Condition 2: Canva Brand Kit is configured**

The pin templates in pin-template-specs.md and all carousel designs depend on the Brand Kit containing the correct hex codes, fonts, and logo. Without this setup, each design element must be specified manually, which multiplies production time per post.

Brand Kit setup is documented in CANVA_EXECUTION_PLAYBOOK.md Section 1.2. It is a one-time configuration requiring approximately 30 minutes. After setup, every template inherits the brand elements automatically.

**Condition 3: A link-in-bio destination exists**

Every post CTA directs traffic somewhere. If the Etsy shop is not live, the destination must be something else. Options in priority order:

Option 3a (preferred if shop is live): Direct Etsy shop link. All social bios point to `etsy.com/shop/seedwarden`.

Option 3b (if shop is not live, email list exists): Kit or Mailchimp email capture page. "Join the list to know when the shop opens." This is the recommended Track B fallback. An email list subscriber who signs up before the shop opens is worth more than a cold visitor who arrives after launch — they already opted in.

Option 3c (if neither shop nor email list is live): A Linktree or similar link page with two items: (1) "Get notified when we launch" linking to a simple Google Form or Mailchimp form, and (2) "About Seedwarden" linking to a placeholder About page or a detailed Instagram/TikTok bio.

A link-in-bio destination can be live within 15 minutes using Kit's free tier (email-list-building-playbook.md covers the setup). This should be the first Track B action taken.

**Condition 4: At least 5 pieces of content are produced before the first post goes live**

Do not launch an account and post one video, then go silent for a week while producing the next piece. Accounts with content gaps in the first two weeks lose algorithm momentum during the period when the algorithm is most actively learning who to recommend the account to.

The minimum viable content bank before first post:
- 2 Reels (Day 1 and Day 4 from the 60-Day Calendar)
- 1 Carousel (Day 3)
- 10 Pinterest pins (Day 2 batch-build from the Calendar)
- 3 days of Stories (can be produced on Day 1 of posting)

Total production time: 4-6 hours. This can be done in a single focused session the day before launch.

**Condition 5: The first post is the origin story**

Do not lead with product promotion on a new account. The first post for Seedwarden is the origin story Reel: why this shop exists, what the creator got angry about, what problem it solves. This post has no CTA to buy anything. Its only job is to make the viewer feel like they have found something they have been looking for.

This is not a principle — it is a data-backed platform mechanic. TikTok and Instagram Reels algorithms measure completion rate and share rate on the first post to calibrate who the account reaches. An origin story with emotional resonance drives both shares (viewers send it to others) and saves (viewers return to it). A product promotion post typically performs poorly on a zero-follower account because there is no trust context.

The Day 1 Reel script hook from phase-2-social-content-calendar-60day.md is: "I started a seed shop because I got mad about something. Here's what." This is the correct entry point.

## Part 2: Launch Sequence — First 21 Days

The following sequence is designed for Track B operating independently, with Phase 1 Etsy status unknown. It adapts as Phase 1 comes online.

### Days 1-3: Foundation and First Content

**Day 1**:
- Complete social account setup (all three platforms) if not done
- Set up Kit email capture page: landing page title "Know when Seedwarden opens" + one-line description + email field + submit button
- Add Kit link to all social bios
- Produce Day 1 Reel: origin story (script in 60-Day Calendar Day 1)
- Produce Day 1-7 Pinterest pin batch (10-12 pins using existing mockup images, specs in pin-template-specs.md)
- Post Day 1 Reel to Instagram and TikTok (natively, separate uploads)
- Do NOT promote products yet; this post is purely narrative

**Day 2**:
- Pinterest batch production: Build 5-7 product pins using existing mockup images (specs from pin-template-specs.md Template 1: Product Mockup Pin)
- Save all Pinterest pins to draft; do not publish yet
- If Phase 1 Etsy listings are live: update all pin links to point to specific listings
- If Phase 1 not live: link all pins to Kit email capture page instead
- Post 3-5 Instagram Stories: behind-the-scenes of the pin build process (screenshot of Canva, quick walkthrough of the product catalog)

**Day 3**:
- Produce Day 3 Carousel: "5 guides for people who want to grow more of their own food this year" (5 slides, one per lead product, using mockup images)
- Post Carousel to Instagram
- Publish Day 2 Pinterest pins (space 2 hours apart through the day)

### Days 4-7: Education and Values Content

The 60-Day Calendar's Day 4-7 content focuses on education and values. This content performs independently of whether the shop is live because it does not have a direct-purchase CTA. Its job is to grow followers and build trust.

Day 4 content: TikTok native re-upload of Day 1 Reel with adapted caption
Day 5 content: Instagram Stories series (behind-the-scenes, poll sticker: "Do you prefer seeing a product in real life vs. a clean mockup?")
Day 6 content: Publish remaining Day 2 Pinterest pins
Day 7 content: Instagram single image post — strongest existing mockup image with product description caption

**Adapting Day 7 for mockup-only mode**: The 60-Day Calendar specifies using "the strongest lifestyle photo from the Tier 1 batch" for Day 7. If lifestyle photos do not yet exist, use the Survival Garden Regional Plans mockup (survival-garden-regional-plans-mockup.png) — it is the most visually compelling of the existing tablet mockups and represents the highest-priced single listing. The caption adapts: "This guide covers 7 US growing regions. If you've been trying to figure out what to actually plant for food security in your specific climate — this is the one."

### Days 8-14: Expanding the Content Bank

By Week 2, the account has an established posting pattern. The primary job of Week 2 is expanding the content types:

- Produce the first educational infographic-style Carousel: pick a topic from the existing product content (seed saving basics, heirloom vs. hybrid breakdown, companion planting quick guide)
- Begin sourcing stock lifestyle images from Unsplash and Pexels (per LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md Section 1) for use in Week 3+ posts
- If Phase 1 Etsy is live: update all social bios from Kit link to Etsy shop link and begin including "link in bio" CTAs in captions
- If Phase 1 not live: maintain Kit link; shift framing from "opening soon" to "early access list" — subtly implies scarcity and increases perceived value of subscribing

### Days 15-21: Converting from Audience-Building to Traffic-Driving

By Day 15, the account has a content history. The algorithm has seen the posting pattern and begun calibrating the audience. Days 15-21 shift focus from pure awareness content to traffic content:

- Publish first direct product-feature posts (Day 16 and Day 19 in 60-Day Calendar)
- Begin including prices in captions ("The Companion Planting Chart is $5 — link in bio")
- Run first Poll on Instagram Stories: "Are you buying seeds this spring?" — use the response to identify active-grower audience segment
- Pinterest: begin building boards organized by theme (Seed Saving, Container Gardening, Food Preservation, Homesteading, Survival Prep) and distribute product pins across relevant boards

## Part 3: Audience Building and Etsy Audience Overlap

A critical question for Track B planning is whether the social audience is the same as the Etsy buyer audience. The answer determines whether social follower growth is a leading indicator of Etsy sales or a separate, parallel metric.

**The overlap is significant but not complete.**

The Etsy buyer universe for Seedwarden products (homesteaders, urban growers, preppers, food sovereignty advocates) is the same demographic that follows homesteading and food-sovereignty content on Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest. The person who searches "seed saving field manual" on Etsy and the person who follows "seed saving" content on Instagram are frequently the same person at different points in their decision process.

However, Etsy and social media represent different phases of buyer intent:

- Etsy traffic is high-intent: buyers arrive on Etsy already ready to purchase something specific
- Social media traffic is discovery: followers arrive at Seedwarden before they have a specific purchase intent; social content creates the need by educating and building desire

The implication is not that social followers do not buy. They do. The implication is that the conversion path is longer for social-sourced buyers (typically 3-7 touchpoints) versus Etsy-organic buyers (typically 1-2 touchpoints). A follower who sees the origin story Reel, saves the companion planting Carousel, watches two more videos, and then clicks the link-in-bio to the Etsy shop is converting after 4 touchpoints. That is normal and acceptable — the math still works because social followers cost nothing to acquire when the content is organic.

**Cross-promotion strategy**:

The two audiences (Etsy organic + social) reinforce each other in both directions:

Direction 1 (Social to Etsy): Social content drives discovery traffic to the Etsy shop. Every Reel, pin, and Carousel that includes the shop link or "link in bio" is a potential Etsy session. The 60-Day Calendar is designed to build this pipeline: educational content drives followers, product-feature content converts followers to shop visitors, Etsy shop converts visitors to buyers.

Direction 2 (Etsy to Social): Etsy sellers can include a note in their delivery message or digital file directing buyers to Instagram or Pinterest for more content. A buyer who purchased the Companion Planting Chart and follows Seedwarden on Instagram is a warm re-purchaser candidate — they already know the brand quality and are predisposed to buy again when the next product is featured.

**Practical cross-promotion actions**:
- Add to every PDF digital product (in header or footer): "Follow @seedwarden on Instagram for weekly growing guides"
- In Etsy listing descriptions: "See more @seedwarden on Instagram + Pinterest"
- In Kit welcome email (first email in sequence from email-list-building-playbook.md): "Follow us on Instagram and Pinterest for the weekly content that didn't make it into the guides"

This creates a loop: Etsy buyer → social follower → future Etsy buyer.

**Email list is the bridge**: The Kit email list sits between social and Etsy in the funnel. A social follower who subscribes to the email list converts to a known lead with an email address. That lead can be reached directly with new product announcements, bundle launches, and seasonal content. The email-list-building-playbook.md documents a full welcome sequence (Day 0-10) that converts subscribers into buyers over their first 10 days on the list.

## Part 4: If Track B Succeeds Independently — Should Phase 1 Approach Change?

This question arises when Track B is generating real follower growth and email signups but Phase 1 has still not launched. At what point does Track B success create pressure to revisit Phase 1 decisions?

There are three signals that specifically indicate Track B success is creating Phase 1 urgency:

**Signal 1**: Instagram or TikTok account reaches 500+ followers before Phase 1 is live.

At 500+ followers, a subset of that audience is actively expecting a shop to exist. Link-in-bio clicks increase. DMs asking "where can I buy your guides?" begin arriving. At this point, the longer Phase 1 waits, the larger the conversion loss from unmonetized warm traffic.

**Recommended response to Signal 1**: If Phase 1 Etsy remains blocked, activate an alternative storefront on Gumroad (gumroad.com) or Payhip (payhip.com). Both require only a payment email to sell digital files. No verification hold, no listing requirements comparable to Etsy's. Upload the 6 lead product PDFs, set prices as documented, and update the link-in-bio to point to the Gumroad/Payhip shop. This is not the preferred long-term platform — Etsy's marketplace distribution is valuable — but it captures revenue that would otherwise be lost while the Etsy account verification resolves.

**Signal 2**: Email list reaches 100+ subscribers before Phase 1 is live.

An email list of 100+ subscribers with a completed welcome sequence (email-list-building-playbook.md) represents a launch asset. When Phase 1 does launch, those 100 subscribers receive the launch announcement email (Day 7 in the welcome sequence, or a standalone broadcast). Expected conversion from a warm email list on launch day is 2-8%, meaning 2-8 immediate sales from the list alone. This is a meaningful launch lift.

At 100 subscribers, reconsider whether the Etsy verification timeline can be accelerated by contacting Etsy support directly with verification documentation. The email list's size creates concrete urgency to resolve the account issue.

**Signal 3**: A single organic Pinterest pin breaks 1,000 impressions within 30 days.

Pinterest pins compound over time — a pin with early strong impressions continues accumulating traffic for months. If one pin crosses 1,000 impressions organically, it signals that the content-market fit for that specific product theme is strong. That pin should be duplicated into a promoted pin (minimum $5/day on Pinterest Ads, recommended budget: $25 for a 5-day test per marketing/social-media-calendar-may-july-2026.md).

At this signal level, Phase 1 launch is urgent because the promoted pin traffic is being sent to either a Kit signup page or a Gumroad storefront — converting at lower rates than an Etsy listing with social proof. Get Phase 1 live before investing any paid promotion budget.

**How Track B success changes Phase 1 approach**:

Track B success does not change the Phase 1 product selection, pricing, or listing copy — those decisions were made based on market research and are not subject to revision based on social performance. What changes is the launch framing:

- A Phase 1 launch with 500+ social followers behind it becomes a newsworthy event for the audience. The Day 3 social announcement ("shop is live") generates more engagement from an existing audience than from zero followers.
- Products that are performing well in Track B content (high saves, high shares, many questions in comments) are the products to lead with in Phase 1 bundles and cross-sell copy.
- Products with weak Track B signal (low save rate, few comments) should be reviewed for title and mockup quality before being featured heavily in launch week content.

## Part 5: Contingency Triggers and Escalation Logic

Track B is a contingency plan. It has its own contingency triggers.

**Track B Trigger 1 — Platform account creation blocked**:

If the social accounts cannot be created (platform ban, handle unavailable), pivot to email list as the sole Track B workstream. Kit email capture + newsletter-style content (send monthly email with educational content from the product catalog) can build an audience without any social media presence. Slower growth, but no platform dependency.

**Track B Trigger 2 — Consistent content production is unsustainable**:

If the posting cadence drops below 2 posts per week on Instagram for more than 2 consecutive weeks, pause active posting and shift to Pinterest-only operation. Pinterest pins are evergreen — a batch of 20 pins scheduled over two weeks via Pinterest native scheduler continues driving traffic without daily active management. This allows Track B to continue generating value at near-zero ongoing time commitment.

**Track B Trigger 3 — Lifestyle photography investment decision point**:

Lifestyle photography (Tier 1 stock images from Unsplash/Pexels, or DIY flat-lay shoot) requires a time investment decision. Per LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md, the stock approach is 3-5 hours for sourcing, compositing, and exporting 6 images. The DIY shoot approach is 4-8 hours including setup, shooting, editing, and exporting.

Do not invest this time until one of these signals is met:
- An Etsy listing has reached 50+ views without a single sale (lifestyle photo is the most likely conversion lever)
- Instagram follower count is above 200 (lifestyle images significantly increase the save rate on single-image posts, which compounds follower growth)
- The first product pin on Pinterest has been shared to 3+ boards by other users (indicates aesthetic appetite that lifestyle images would satisfy)

Before these signals, continue using mockup images. Mockup images are sufficient for audience building. Lifestyle images are for conversion optimization.

**Track B Trigger 4 — Phase 1 launches mid-Track B execution**:

This is the ideal scenario. When Phase 1 goes live during active Track B execution, make three immediate changes:
1. Update all social bios from Kit capture link to direct Etsy shop link
2. Post a dedicated "shop is live" announcement on all three platforms (can be same-day, use Day 3 content from 60-Day Calendar adapted with actual launch date)
3. Send launch email to Kit list (template: email-list-building-playbook.md Day 7 email in welcome sequence, or standalone broadcast if subscribers joined before the sequence)

Then continue the Track B content cadence unchanged. The only difference post-launch is that CTAs now drive directly to Etsy instead of to a capture page.

## Part 6: 90-Day Track B Success Thresholds

These are the metrics that confirm Track B is working independently, at 30, 60, and 90 days from first post.

| Metric | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Instagram followers | 100+ | 300+ | 600+ | Instagram Insights |
| Instagram Reel avg. reach | 500+ | 1,200+ | 2,500+ | Instagram Insights |
| Pinterest monthly views | 2,000+ | 8,000+ | 20,000+ | Pinterest Analytics |
| Pinterest total pins live | 30+ | 75+ | 150+ | Pinterest Analytics |
| TikTok followers | 75+ | 250+ | 600+ | TikTok Analytics |
| Email list subscribers | 30+ | 100+ | 250+ | Kit Dashboard |
| Etsy sessions from social (if live) | 30+ | 100+ | 250+ | Etsy Stats Traffic Sources |

**What to do if metrics fall below threshold at Day 30**:

The most common cause of Day 30 underperformance is low posting frequency (under 3 posts per week) or low content quality on the first Reel (which sets algorithm calibration for the account). If follower count is below 50 at Day 30:

1. Review the Day 1 Reel. Did it have a strong hook in the first 3 seconds? Was the completion rate above 40%? If completion rate is below 25%, delete and repost with a stronger hook.
2. Increase Pinterest pin frequency to 3+ pins per day for one week (using native scheduler, takes 30 minutes to batch-schedule).
3. Add a location tag to Instagram posts (city or region — this adds local discoverability).
4. Use TikTok's Promote feature for $2-5 on the best-performing post to date (this is not a sustained ad strategy, it is a one-time signal to the algorithm that paid promotion is associated with this account, which can lift organic reach).

**What success looks like at Day 90**:

A Track B account at Day 90 thresholds (600 Instagram followers, 20,000 Pinterest monthly views, 250 email subscribers) is a functioning early-stage audience. It is not a large audience. But it is an audience with documented interest in Seedwarden's content, and it is an audience that has been built without any paid advertising spend. The revenue potential of a Phase 1 launch into this audience versus a cold launch is significantly higher. Phase 3 social strategy (phase-3-social-media-growth-strategy.md) takes over at this point and operates at a higher cadence.
