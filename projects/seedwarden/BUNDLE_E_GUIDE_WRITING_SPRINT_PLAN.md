---

### Habitat
[150 words: Where does this species thrive? Urban disturbed ground, forests, wetlands, roadsides? Soil type? Shade/sun? Density (does it grow in patches or scattered?)]

[Timeline with specific dates]
- **Peak season**: [Month range]
- **Optimal harvest window**: [specific 2-4 week window; e.g., "May 1-20 for Garlic Mustard shoots"]
- **What happens after peak**: [does it become woody? unpalatable? toxic? example: "By late May, Garlic Mustard bolts and becomes fibrous"]
- **Regional variation**: [if applicable; e.g., "Northeast peaks 2 weeks earlier than Midwest due to temperature lag"]

---

## Harvest Method

### What to Harvest
[150 words: Which plant parts are edible? Entire plant or specific parts?]

**Target tissue**: [shoots, leaves, berries, roots, etc.]
**Quantity per harvest**: [realistic amount per plant visit; e.g., "take 30% of leaves, leave 70%"]
**Tools needed**: [knife, gloves, basket, etc.]

### How to Harvest Ethically
[Harvesting reduces invasive spread — frame as ecological benefit. Include rotation guidance if applicable.]

## Section 5: Risk Mitigation for Compressed Timeline

### Risk 1: Running Out of Writing Time
**If end of May 14, guides are incomplete:**
- Deprioritize: Autumn Olive (least seasonal pressure — available through August)
- Fast-track: Garlic Mustard, Japanese Knotweed, Purslane (seasonal window closing)
- Contingency: Launch May 19 with 3 guides complete; email subscribers May 22 with "Japanese Knotweed + Autumn Olive guides shipping May 24" (2 delayed guides)
- **Impact on revenue**: Minimal (3-guide bundle still validates system; customer expectations managed with delay notification)

### Risk 2: Wikimedia Images Missing or License Issues
**If photo sourcing fails for any species:**
- Fallback 1: Use Phase 1 guide photos (if available from seedwarden asset library)
- Fallback 2: Use iNaturalist CC-BY images (searchable by species name + license)
- Fallback 3: Commission emergency Canva graphic (species illustration, botanical drawing style) — 15 min per species
- **Impact**: Guides still ship May 19, with hand-drawn or stock illustrations instead of nature photos (acceptable for digital product pricing)

### Risk 3: Quality Gate Fails on May 14 EOD
**If prose is rough, spelling errors present:**
- Do not delay launch
- Mark guides for "light editing May 15 afternoon" (2-3 hours)
- Ship May 20 instead of May 19 (1-day delay, acceptable per BUNDLE_E_PUBLICATION_PACKAGE.md)
- Notify early email subscribers (send May 19 EOD: "Guides ship tomorrow morning — final quality pass complete")

### Risk 4: User Approves Launch, Then Feedback Requires Rewrites
**If user reviews guides May 13-14 and requests major changes:**
- Categorize feedback: Critical (safety/accuracy) vs. Polish (phrasing, tone)
- Rewrite critical items (even if it delays launch 24 hours to May 20)
- Defer polish items to post-launch (can update digital product files same day)
