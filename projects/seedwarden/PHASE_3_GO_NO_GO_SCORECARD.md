## Section 1: Scope Go/No-Go

Answer the three scope viability questions. Each question gates a scope option.

---

### Question 1.1 — Option A Viability (5 Bundles, Single Writer)

> Can you commit to 5+ focused, uninterrupted writing hours per day from June 22 through July 5 (14 consecutive days)?

"Focused hours" means: no email, no admin tasks, no supplier calls, no photography sessions during the writing block. A 5-hour writing block is the minimum required to sustain Women's Health and Immunity writing at the pace needed to hit the June 29 first upload date.

Check project history: Has any Phase 1 or Phase 2 sprint been sustained at 5+ hours/day writing for 14 consecutive days? If no prior sprint has validated this pace, the answer to this question should be No.

| Answer | Implication |
|---|---|
| YES — I can confirm 5+ hrs/day, June 22–July 5 | Option A remains viable; proceed to Q1.2 |
| NO — I cannot confirm this pace | Option A is off the table; proceed directly to C2 |
| UNSURE | Treat as No — Option A requires an affirmative commitment, not a hope |

**Your answer**: [ ] YES / [ ] NO / [ ] UNSURE

---

### Question 1.2 — Option B Viability (5 Bundles, Second Writer)

> Is a qualified herbalist writer — with FTC-compliant language competency, practitioner-level botanical accuracy, and availability from June 22 – July 13 — identified, contacted, and confirmed by today?

The viability bar for Option B is specific: the contractor must be named, available, and have agreed in principle to the engagement by May 30. A contractor who is "probably available" or "I have a lead on someone" does not meet this bar. The contractor must pass a test section review by June 5 (3 days before sprint briefing materials are due).

If the contractor cannot be confirmed today, Option B is not available for the June 22 sprint. This is not a judgment — it is a logistics constraint set by the 10–16 hour onboarding requirement.

| Answer | Implication |
|---|---|
| YES — contractor named and confirmed today | Option B viable; proceed to Q1.3 |
| NO — no contractor confirmed | Option B off the table; proceed to C2 if Q1.1 was also No |
| PARTIALLY — contractor identified but not confirmed | Treat as No |

## Section 10: Contingency — If May 30 Decision Is Delayed

If the decision cannot be made on May 30 (vendor responses not received, or user unavailable), these defaults activate automatically on June 1 at 9:00 AM local time:

| Decision | Automatic Default |
|---|---|
| Scope | C2 — 3-bundle focus, single writer |
| Goldenseal path | Path 2 — Wikimedia CC |
| Designer scope | 3-bundle RFQ sent to all 5 Priority 1–2 designers immediately |
| Palette | canva-phase-3-adaptation-guide.md hex codes confirmed as-is |

**Why these defaults are safe**: C2 is the lowest-risk combination. Its pre-sprint window has no hard deadlines before June 7. A 1-day decision delay under C2 costs nothing. A 1-day decision delay under A2 is manageable (pace gate is June 24, not June 1). The only combination where a 1-day delay causes measurable harm is B2 (contractor onboarding loses 1 day of the June 1–21 preparation window).

If the decision is delayed beyond June 3: activate C2 unconditionally. Options A2 and B2 require a minimum of 18 clear pre-sprint preparation days (June 3–21) to be viable. A decision after June 3 reduces this window below the minimum.
