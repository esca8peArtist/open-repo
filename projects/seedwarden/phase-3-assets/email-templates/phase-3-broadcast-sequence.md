### Email 2 — Day 3
**Subject**: The Black Cohosh harvest window is July–September — here's why timing matters
**Preview**: Most wild harvesters get this wrong. Here's the cultivation difference.

---

Hi {{ subscriber.first_name }},

One piece of practical intelligence from the Women's Health Herbs Guide:

Black Cohosh root is harvested in fall — specifically, after the aerial parts have died back in the second or third growing season. The root requires 3–5 years of establishment to develop full content, and wild harvesters shortcut this constantly by pulling first-year or second-year root that has not yet fully developed.

This is one reason the supplement market has a Black Cohosh quality problem alongside its sourcing ethics problem: the most easily harvested roots (surface, accessible, young) are not the best roots.

For cultivation growers, the timing sequence is:
- Year 1: establish from nursery-propagated root division or seed (seed requires cold stratification, 18-month germination — root division is faster)
- Year 2: let the plant establish without harvest
- Year 3–4: first root harvest in October after aerial dieback

The patience required is also the conservation mechanism. No commercial wild harvester waits four years. The cultivated grower does.

The full Women's Health Herbs Guide has the complete cultivation timeline, companion planting recommendations for shade-woodland gardens, and the forest farming setup for serious apothecary production.

More on the conservation story next week.
