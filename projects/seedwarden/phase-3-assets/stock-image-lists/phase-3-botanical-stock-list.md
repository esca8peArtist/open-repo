# Phase 3 Botanical Stock Image List — Wikimedia Commons Sources

**Prepared**: May 9, 2026
**License policy**: All images in this list are either (a) public domain (pre-1928 botanical illustrations), (b) CC BY or CC BY-SA licensed from Wikimedia Commons, or (c) CC0 from Unsplash/Pexels. Commercial use permitted for all. Verify license on Wikimedia page before downloading. Log each download in WORKLOG.md per the standard photo logging protocol.

**Download procedure**: Wikimedia Commons — go to the URL listed, click the file name, click "Download" at top-right, select "Full resolution." Save to `projects/seedwarden/assets/botanical-photos/phase-3/[species-slug]/`.

## Priority 1: Bundle Cover Anchor Images (Required before Etsy listing upload)

### 1. Black Cohosh — Women's Health Bundle Cover

**Common name**: Black Cohosh
**Latin binomial**: *Actaea racemosa* (syn. *Cimicifuga racemosa*)
**Use**: Bundle 1 cover anchor illustration, Etsy slot 3, zone card Zone A

**Primary source (recommended)**:
- Wikimedia Commons search: `Actaea racemosa botanical illustration`
- Look for: "Actaea racemosa" in Sturm's Deutschlands Flora (public domain, 1796). High-resolution botanical plate.
- Backup: "Actaea racemosa 2" by Krzysztof Ziarnek, Kenraiz (CC BY-SA 4.0) — in-habitat flowering plant, good zone card use.
- Backup 2: iNaturalist observation photos for *Actaea racemosa* in forest understory — filter by CC BY license.

**Image notes**: The tall white bottlebrush flower spike is the identification anchor. Cover image should show flower spike clearly. Root close-up is secondary (reserve for zone card back or interior page).

---

### 2. Elderberry — Respiratory Bundle Cover

**Common name**: Elderberry
**Latin binomial**: *Sambucus nigra* / *Sambucus canadensis*
**Use**: Bundle 2 cover anchor, Etsy slot 3, zone card Zone B

**Primary source**:
- Wikimedia Commons: search `Sambucus nigra berries`
- "Sambucus nigra - köögikärbseseen 2" (CC BY-SA 3.0) — dark berry clusters, high quality.
- Backup: "Sambucus canadensis" USDA-NRCS PLANTS Database image (public domain).
- Backup 2: Wikimedia "Elderberry (Sambucus nigra)" by Rudolphous (CC BY-SA).

**Image notes**: Dark purple berry cluster is the visual anchor. Do NOT use elderflower images for the cover — berries are the primary Etsy search visual for this category. Flower images are good for slot 3 or zone card front.

---

### 3. Goldenseal — Immunity Bundle Cover

**Common name**: Goldenseal
**Latin binomial**: *Hydrastis canadensis*
**Use**: Bundle 3 cover anchor, Etsy slot 3, zone card Zone A

**Primary source**:
- Wikimedia Commons: search `Hydrastis canadensis`
- "Hydrastis canadensis — Köhler's Medizinalpflanzen" (public domain, 1887 botanical illustration) — highly detailed, museum quality.
- Backup: "Hydrastis canadensis USDA-NRCS" (public domain) — habitat photo showing leaf structure and red berries.

**Image notes**: The Köhler's Medizinalpflanzen illustration is the recommended cover image — it shows root, leaf, and flower in a single plate, demonstrating the full plant that the conservation story is about. The CITES II and UpS At-Risk badges make the illustration format more appropriate than a casual photo.

---

### 4. Passionflower — Sleep/Nervines Bundle Cover

**Common name**: Passionflower
**Latin binomial**: *Passiflora incarnata*
**Use**: Bundle 4 cover anchor, Etsy slot 3, zone card Zone C

**Primary source**:
- Wikimedia Commons: search `Passiflora incarnata`
- "Passiflora incarnata" by Aeantoinette (CC BY-SA 4.0) — full flower open, purple and white, unmistakable.
- Backup: "Maypop Passiflora incarnata" by Kenpei (CC BY-SA 3.0) — flower close-up.
- Backup 2: USDA-NRCS Plants Database photo (public domain).

**Image notes**: The passionflower is the single most visually distinctive species in the Phase 3 catalog — use the most dramatic, fully-open flower photo available. This is a thumbnail anchor that will stop scroll on Pinterest and Etsy. Do not substitute a leaf or vine photo.

---

### 5. Dandelion Root — Digestive Bundle Cover

**Common name**: Dandelion
**Latin binomial**: *Taraxacum officinale*
**Use**: Bundle 5 cover anchor, Etsy slot 3, zone card Zone B-C

**Primary source**:
- Wikimedia Commons: search `Taraxacum officinale botanical`
- "Taraxacum officinale" — multiple public domain botanical illustrations available (Köhler's, Sturm's).
- Recommend: illustration that shows both the above-ground plant AND the root in cross-section — this is the unique differentiator of the digestive bundle (the root preparation is the subject, not the common dandelion flower).
- Backup: "Dandelion" by Böhringer Friedrich (CC BY-SA 2.5) — photo of plant in habitat.

**Image notes**: The root is the hero of this bundle. If illustration shows root clearly, use illustration. If only flower photos are available, use a photo that includes both flowers and a visible root section.

**Recommended source for all zone card illustrations**: Biodiversity Heritage Library (biodiversitylibrary.org) — search species name, filter for high-resolution botanical plates. All BHL content is public domain or CC-licensed. Free download.

**Alternative**: Wikimedia Commons "Flora Batava," "Köhler's Medizinalpflanzen," or "Sturm's Deutschlands Flora" series — all public domain, high quality.
