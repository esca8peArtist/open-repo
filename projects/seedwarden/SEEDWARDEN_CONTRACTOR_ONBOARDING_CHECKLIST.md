# Seedwarden Contractor Onboarding Checklist — Week 1–6 Integration Playbook

**Objective**: Onboard 6 contractors within 24 hours of contract signature (target July 1). All tools, access, and orientation materials are staged before each contractor's kickoff call. First deliverables due July 5–8. No manual steps after onboarding — every contractor should know exactly what to deliver, where to send it, and when, before they end their kickoff call.

**Six contractor roles**:
1. Photographer
2. Writer #1 (Women's Health + Immunity)
3. Writer #2 (Respiratory + Sleep)
4. Writer #3 (Digestive + Practitioner)
5. Herbalist Consultant
6. Logistics Coordinator

**Onboarding kit contents** (send as email with shared drive link, July 1 same-day as signature):

- Role-specific style guide and content brief
- Tool access and shared folder link
- FTC compliance one-pager (writers and herbalist)
- Key dates, milestones, payment schedule
- Escalation contacts

### Style Guide + Quality Rubric (Send with Onboarding Kit)

Include the same quality rubric used in the job posting success criteria section for each role. Contractors must receive this before their first delivery so they can self-assess before submitting.

Writers: include a 1-page FTC compliance guide with this content:

**FTC compliance — mandatory language standards:**

All therapeutic claims must be framed with an evidence tier. The following language patterns are required:
- Use: "Traditionally used to support...," "Research suggests...," "Preliminary evidence indicates...," "Studies have shown mixed results on..."
- Do not use: "prevents," "cures," "treats," "eliminates," "guarantees" without an evidence-tier qualifier
- Contraindication language: "Avoid during pregnancy" or "Consult a healthcare provider before use with [drug class]" — not "is generally safe"
- If a species has limited evidence (e.g., damiana), state the limitation explicitly and do not imply clinical equivalence to studied herbs

## Contingency and Replacement Procedures

### If Contractor Misses Delivery (>24h Late)

Day 1 (24h late): Coordinator sends reminder (same day). Contractor has 24h to submit revised delivery date.
Day 2 (48h late): Direct follow-up from you. Contractor has 48h final window.
Day 4 (72h late / deadline missed): Activate contingency:

- Photographer: Contact Wikimedia Commons for CC-licensed botanical images as emergency fill; source from backup Thumbtack photographer for future sessions
- Writers: Contact second-tier writer candidate or reduce scope to condensed 2,000-word format for missed bundle; activate solo fallback per PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md
- Herbalist: Internal review using mandatory content checklist from SEEDWARDEN_CONTRACTOR_JOB_DESCRIPTIONS.md (self-flag protocol); defer herbalist to Immunity and Digestive bundles only (highest-risk)
- Coordinator: Absorb tasks internally; hire last-minute admin support if needed (1-day Upwork post)

### If Quality Fails Gate Review Twice

First fail: Send detailed feedback; contractor revises within 24–48h.
Second fail: Schedule call with contractor to assess root cause. Options in order:
1. Reduce scope (fewer images per session, fewer recipes, fewer species per manuscript)
2. Extend timeline by 3–5 days if cascade impact is acceptable
3. Transition to backup contractor (transfer work in progress or restart)

Decision made jointly within 24 hours of second-fail review. Implement within 48 hours.

### Payment Hold and Release Logic

Trigger Payment Hold when: contractor misses 2 consecutive check-in replies without explanation, or quality fails gate review for the second time.

Communicate Payment Hold via the template above. Log Hold date in tracking sheet (Notes column: "Payment Hold — [reason] — [date]").

Release Hold when: contractor delivers missing item or passes revision. Release same day as resolution. Log in tracking sheet: "Payment Hold released — [date] — [reason resolved]."

Do not threaten Hold without activating it if the trigger condition is met. An activated Hold that is not communicated is not a Hold.

### Refund Logic (If Contract Terminated)

If contract is terminated before any deliverable is received: return 25% deposit in full within 48h.
If contract is terminated after partial delivery: pay pro-rata for accepted deliverables only; return deposit balance.
If contractor abandons (no contact for 5+ days after escalation): retain deposit as contractual penalty per contract terms; document in WORKLOG.md.
