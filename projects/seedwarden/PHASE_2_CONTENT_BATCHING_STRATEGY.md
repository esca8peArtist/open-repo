# Phase 2 Content Batching Strategy
## Weekly Production Schedule — June 22 Activation

**Purpose**: Remove production bottleneck as Phase 2 adds 2.5–3 hours/day distributed
content output to the existing 1.3h/day Phase 1 baseline. This document defines how to
produce a full week of content in one concentrated batch session rather than as daily reactive
tasks.

**Core principle**: Batch production by content type, not by calendar day. Producing all
TikTok scripts in one 45-minute session is 60% faster than writing one TikTok script per day
across four sessions. The discipline of batching converts content from a constant drain to a
scheduled, bounded production window.

**Phase 1 operational baseline** (confirmed from transition roadmap, Week 1 actual data):
- Monitoring/analytics: 25–35 min/day
- Customer response: 10–20 min/day
- Social scheduling checks: 20 min/day
- Total Phase 1 ops: ~1.3h/day

**Phase 2 target**: Add 2.5–3h distributed production per day using batching to contain
peak effort to two concentrated sessions per week.

## Section 2: Turnaround Time Per Content Type

All estimates are benchmarked against the Phase 1 social velocity data (from PHASE_1→PHASE_2
transition roadmap: 30–45 min/day social posting at Phase 1 scale) and adjusted for Phase 2
volume with a 20% padding factor.

### TikTok Video (30–60 sec)

| Stage | Time | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Hook + script writing (one video) | 8 min | Template-driven (see Section 5 hook library) |
| Script for all 4 videos in one session | 25 min | Batch gain: writing 4 sequentially is faster than 4 isolated sessions |
| B-roll or field footage collection | 0 min (batch day only) | Film all raw clips in one outdoor session; no additional collection needed if B-roll library pre-built |

**Single-video turnaround** (if produced daily): 45–55 min each, 3h total for 4 videos.
**Batch gain**: 55 minutes saved per week through consolidated session work.

## Section 3: Weekly Batch Schedule

Phase 2 content production runs on two batch sessions per week, not daily production.
All other time is Phase 1 operations (monitoring, customer response, scheduling checks).

### Monday: Primary Production Session (2h 30 min — 9:00–11:30 AM)

**Block purpose**: Produce all video content for the week.

| Time | Activity | Output |
|------|----------|--------|
| 9:00–9:25 | Script all 4 TikTok hooks + bodies | 4 complete scripts |
| 9:25–9:57 | Film all 4 TikTok on-camera segments (8 min/video) | 4 raw recordings |
| 9:57–10:45 | Edit all 4 TikToks (12 min/video) | 4 edited videos |
| 10:45–10:57 | Add captions, thumbnails, hashtags to all 4 | 4 TikTok-ready files |
| 10:57–11:03 | Cross-post 3 TikToks to Instagram (remove watermark, adapt caption) | 3 IG Reels |
| 11:03–11:33 | Produce 1 original Instagram-only Reel + caption | 1 IG Reel |
| 11:33–11:38 | Schedule all 4 TikToks + 4 Instagram Reels in Buffer/Later | 8 posts scheduled |
| **Buffer** | Review weekly strategy, adjust hooks based on prior week performance | — |

**Total output from Monday session**: 4 TikTok videos + 4 Instagram Reels — all scheduled
for the week ahead.

### Thursday: Static + Pinterest Session (1h 25 min — 10:00–11:25 AM)

**Block purpose**: Produce all static/carousel and Pinterest content.

| Time | Activity | Output |
|------|----------|--------|
| 10:00–10:25 | Produce 1 Instagram carousel (5-slide, Canva clone) | 1 carousel |
| 10:25–10:35 | Select lifestyle photo + write caption for 1 Instagram static | 1 static post |
| 10:35–10:43 | Captions + hashtags for both Instagram posts | 2 captioned posts |
| 10:43–11:01 | Produce 3 Pinterest pins (6 min each, Canva template) | 3 pin graphics |
| 11:01–11:13 | Pinterest titles + descriptions (SEO keyword copy) | 3 pins ready |
| 11:13–11:25 | Schedule Instagram posts (Thu/Sat) + 3 Pinterest pins | 5 assets scheduled |

**Total output from Thursday session**: 2 Instagram posts + 3 Pinterest pins — all scheduled.

### Daily Micro-Tasks (15–20 min/day, not batched)

These cannot be batched because they require same-day responsiveness:

| Task | Time | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Reply to TikTok + Instagram comments (priority) | 10 min | Days 1–14 of any new video: TikTok comment velocity is a ranking signal |
| Instagram Stories (repurpose existing content) | 3 min | Pull from last 48 hours of posts; add a poll or countdown sticker |
| Check engagement metrics (save rate, completion rate) | 5 min | Flag any video performing above 3% comment-to-view — boost with pinned reply |

**Daily micro total**: 15–20 min. This is already accounted for within the Phase 1 monitoring
baseline (social engagement check was already ~5 min/day; extended to 15–20 for Phase 2 volume).

## Section 4: Batch Size Optimization

### Why 4 TikTok Videos Is the Optimal Weekly Batch

**Below 4 (2–3/week)**: TikTok algorithm 2026 preferences accounts that post 4–7x/week for
consistent Interest Graph distribution. At 3 videos/week, organic reach per video is 15–20%
lower than at 4/week (Statusphere 2025 benchmarks). The batch session overhead (setup,
mental mode-switch) is roughly 20 min regardless of how many videos are produced — producing
2 instead of 4 wastes that fixed overhead cost.

**Above 4 (5–7/week)**: Marginal algorithm gain exists at 7/week for accounts under 10K
followers, but quality degrades when production speed increases above ~4 videos per session.
Script quality is the primary driver of completion rate (60%+ target); speed-forcing a 5th
video in one session risks hook quality. Hold at 4 until a second batch session is warranted
by channel growth (when TikTok followers exceed 5K, evaluate adding a Wednesday micro-session
for 1–2 additional videos).

**Batch break-even analysis**:
- 4 videos per Monday session: 2h 5min total = 31 min per video
- 4 videos produced daily (1/day, Mon–Thu): ~45 min each = 3h total
- Batch saves 55 minutes per week, or ~3.5 hours per month

### Why 3 Pinterest Pins Is the Optimal Weekly Batch

Pinterest indexing lag is 3–5 business days. Pinning more than 3–5 per week on a new account
(under 3 months old, less than 50 pins on any given board) does not accelerate indexing and
may trigger the platform's spam filter on accounts without engagement history. Scale to 5–7
pins/week after accounts reach 200 total pins (approximately Week 10 of Phase 2 if 3/week
from launch).

## Section 5: Hook Library and Script Templates

### TikTok Hook Templates (rotate weekly, avoid repetition)

All hooks are under 8 words. Hook is delivered in the first 1.5 seconds, on-camera.

| Hook Type | Template | Best For |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Identification challenge | "You walked past this today and didn't know it." | Field ID videos, Forager cohort |
| Seasonal urgency | "[Species] season ends in [X] weeks — here's how to find it." | Ramp, elderberry, nettle windows |
| Contrast | "This is what the generic guide looks like vs. the real one." | Guide preview, Homesteader cohort |
| Safety/surprising fact | "This plant looks like [toxic species]. Here's how to tell them apart." | Safety content, builds trust |
| Local relevance | "Zone [X] foragers — here's what's edible in your area right now." | Zone-specific, Homesteader/Forager |
| Process reveal | "I preserved 20 pounds of [vegetable] in 25 minutes. Here's how." | Preservation season, July–September |
| Behind-the-scenes | "45 days since launching. What's actually selling and what isn't." | Transparency, saves + follows |

**Rule**: Never use the same hook type twice in a single week's 4 videos. Vary the format.

### Instagram Caption Structure

```
[Hook sentence — mirrors TikTok hook but written form; 80 characters or fewer]
[Line break]
[2–3 sentences of educational content or story — the body]
[Line break]
[CTA — one action: "link in bio," "save this," "which zone are you in?"]
[Line break]
[3–5 hashtags]
```

**Caption length**: 150–250 words. Instagram Reels with longer captions (2026) show higher
save rates among the Homesteader cohort because the educational content in the caption is
saved alongside the Reel for reference.

### Pinterest Pin Title Formula

```
[Keyword phrase] + [Qualifier] + [Year]
```

Examples:
- "Wild Edibles Identification Guide for Beginners — North America 2026"
- "Zone 5 Seed Saving Calendar — What to Harvest in August"
- "Free-Range Foraging Safety Guide — Lookalike Species Comparison"

**Board placement rule**: Each pin goes to the board that best matches its primary keyword,
not the board with the most existing pins. Over-pinning to one board reduces per-board reach.

## Section 6: Equipment Requirements

Phase 2 production at 4 TikTok + 6 Instagram + 3 Pinterest per week requires minimal
dedicated equipment — the bottleneck is time and workflow, not hardware.

### Minimum Equipment (Production Capable Today)

| Item | Use | Already Available |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| Smartphone (iPhone 12 or equivalent, 4K capable) | TikTok and Instagram Reel filming | Yes — standard |
| Ring light or north-facing window (natural light) | On-camera talking head segments | North-facing window is free; ring light $25–40 if needed |
| Tripod or phone stand | Stable framing for talking head segments | Phone stand: $12–18 |
| Lavalier mic or earbuds with inline mic | Audio quality for on-camera segments | Earbuds inline mic is sufficient; wired lav: $15–30 |
| CapCut (free, mobile or desktop) | TikTok editing, caption auto-generation, template system | Free |
| Canva (free tier or Pro) | Pinterest pins, Instagram carousels, static posts | Free tier sufficient for Phase 2 |
| Later or Buffer (free tier) | Scheduling for Instagram and Pinterest | Free tier up to 10 scheduled posts/platform |

### Optional Upgrades (After TikTok followers reach 2K)

| Item | Justification | Cost |
|------|--------------|------|
| Shotgun mic (Rode VideoMicro) | Audio quality improvement for field videos | $60–80 |
| B-roll archive day (half-day outdoor shoot, seasonal) | Pre-built footage library for 4–6 weeks of field clips | Time: 3h. No cost if existing access to field/garden. |
| Canva Pro subscription | Removes background, brand kit lock, Magic Resize for cross-platform | $15/mo; justified when pin production exceeds 10/week |
| Tailwind (Pinterest scheduler) | Smart loop pinning, board analytics, SEO pin score | $24/mo; justified after 500 monthly impressions/week |

### B-Roll Library as a Production Multiplier

A half-day field filming session (3 hours, 30 min/species, 6 species) produces enough raw
B-roll for 3–4 weeks of TikTok and Instagram videos. This is the highest-leverage equipment
investment: spending 3 hours once eliminates the "I need to film outside" dependency from 12
future batch sessions. Schedule one field B-roll day per month, keyed to the seasonal content
calendar (May: spring greens; July: preservation; September: fall harvest).

## Section 7: Parallel Production Workflows

### Wave 1 → Checkpoint → Wave 2 (Weekly)

Batch production inside each Monday session follows a parallel-first pattern.

**Wave 1 (parallel where possible)**:
- Write all 4 TikTok scripts simultaneously (one script per slot in the same doc — context-switching cost is low when all 4 are the same week's theme)
- While scripts are written, phone records field B-roll loop from library in background (no attention required)

**Checkpoint** (5 min): Review 4 scripts for hook variety (no duplicate hook types), seasonal
accuracy, and CTA clarity. Fix before filming — reshooting costs more than rewriting.

**Wave 2 (sequential)**:
- Film 4 on-camera segments in order (sequential because camera setup stays fixed; filming back-to-back in one setup is faster than multiple setups)
- Edit all 4 in CapCut (sequential; each uses the same template — apply template, drop in footage, adjust text overlays)

### Cross-Platform Repurposing Path

Every piece of content flows downhill through platforms in order of production cost:

```
TikTok (highest effort — original production)
    |
    v
Instagram Reel (medium effort — cross-post, adapt caption)
    |
    v
Instagram Story (low effort — pull frame, add sticker)
    |
    v
Pinterest Video Pin (minimal effort — upload same vertical file, SEO title)
```

This path means 1 TikTok video generates 3 additional assets with under 15 minutes of
marginal work. Three of the four weekly TikTok videos follow this full path. One video per
week is Instagram-native (different aspect ratio, different hook) to maintain platform
authenticity.

### Theme-of-the-Month System

Rather than producing each week in isolation, Phase 2 operates on a monthly theme that
provides creative continuity across all 13+ weekly assets.

| Month | Theme | TikTok Angle | Instagram Angle | Pinterest Angle |
|-------|-------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| June | Spring foraging + companion planting | Field ID videos | Planting process Reels | "What to plant together" pins |
| July | Preservation season begins | Fermentation process | Jar-packing Reels | "Summer harvest preservation" pins |
| August | Peak harvest + prepper activation | Volume harvest processing | Before/after transformation | "Fall prep" gift pins |
| September | Fall garden + livestock | Zone-specific fall tasks | Chicken prep/root harvest Reels | Seasonal recipe preservation pins |

**Monthly content prep session** (1 hour, first Monday of each month, before regular batch):
Choose 4 TikTok hook templates for the month, confirm 3 Instagram carousel topics, pin 12
pre-planned Pinterest titles. This 1-hour investment eliminates decision fatigue during all
8 weekly Monday sessions that follow.

## Section 8: Production Quality Gates

Before any asset is scheduled, it passes a 3-check gate. Each check takes under 30 seconds.

### TikTok Gate
1. Hook lands in first 1.5 seconds (not 3, not 2 — watch back at 1.5 sec mark and confirm you've said the hook)
2. Captions are readable on a 4-inch screen (test: zoom phone to 50%)
3. CTA is present and specific ("link in bio for the guide" — not "check the link")

### Instagram Gate
1. First frame is static-compelling (Reels autoplay muted; first frame must stop a scroll)
2. Caption has a line break after the first sentence (Instagram caption compression hides body text without this)
3. Hashtags are in caption body, not comment section (2026 algorithm guidance)

### Pinterest Gate
1. Title contains at least one primary keyword in the first 4 words
2. Pin image passes the thumbnail test (readable at 150×225px — Pinterest's browse grid size)
3. Description first sentence contains secondary keyword (Pinterest's text index reads first 100 characters)
