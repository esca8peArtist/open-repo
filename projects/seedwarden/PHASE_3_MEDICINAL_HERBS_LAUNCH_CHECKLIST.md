### 2.3 Writing House Style — Non-Negotiable Rules

Apply to every species section in every bundle:

1. **Person**: Second person ("you") for practical cultivation and harvesting sections. Third person for historical use and research sections.
2. **No marketing language** in guide body. Marketing copy lives in the Etsy listing only.
3. **Contraindications section is mandatory** for every species. Omitting it creates liability exposure. Header: "Note: This section describes traditional and historical use and does not constitute medical advice."
4. **FTC framing**: Never write "treats," "cures," "prevents," or "relieves" in relation to a health condition. Write "traditionally used for," "studied in [research context] for," or "contains [compound] studied for." See Appendix A of `phase-3-medicinal-herbs-critical-path.md` for the full FTC quick reference.
5. **Active constituent descriptions**: Describe what is in the plant and what research has studied. Do not assert efficacy. Example: "Contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in vitro and in clinical settings for antimicrobial activity" — not "antimicrobial."
6. **Conservation sidebars**: Placed immediately after identification section for At-Risk species (Black Cohosh, Echinacea angustifolia, Goldenseal). Before cultivation content.
7. **Sources section**: Every bundle ends with inline or listed citations. No footnotes. iNaturalist, USDA PLANTS, UpS, PubMed as primary references.
