### FAQ Section

**Q: Will these herbs prevent me from getting sick?**  
A: No single herb prevents illness. These herbs build immune resilience—your body's capacity to recognize, respond to, and recover from threats. Resilience reduces severity and duration, not occurrence. Combined with sleep, nutrition, and hygiene, they're powerful. Alone, they're not magic.

**Q: How long do I need to take these before they work?**  
A: Foundational immunity herbs (Astragalus, Reishi) work best over months, not days or weeks. Start in summer/early fall for winter resilience. Acute-response herbs (Elderberry, Echinacea) work faster but are used differently. See protocol timing in guide.

**Q: Can I take these with my vitamins or other supplements?**  
A: Generally yes, but some interactions are possible. Check with your healthcare provider or pharmacist if taking multiple supplements or medications. The guide includes known interactions.

**Q: Are these suitable for autoimmune conditions?**  
A: Autoimmune conditions require immune regulation, not stimulation. Some herbs here (like Reishi) regulate rather than stimulate, but this is complex. Consult your rheumatologist or specialist before use if you have an autoimmune diagnosis.

**Q: Why is Reishi so expensive?**  
A: Reishi cultivation takes 18–24 months and requires specialized growing conditions (temperature, humidity, substrate control). Premium cultivated Reishi costs significantly more than wild-harvested (which has sustainability concerns), but the quality and potency are incomparable. Our Reishi is cultivated for maximum bioavailability.

**Q: How do I know if these herbs are working?**  
A: With foundational herbs, results are subtle: less frequent illness, faster recovery, better energy. With acute-response herbs (Elderberry), you notice shorter cold duration or reduced severity. Keep a simple log of illness frequency/duration over 3 months.

**Q: Can I take these if I'm pregnant or nursing?**  
A: Some herbs are safer in pregnancy/nursing than others. See the comprehensive safety table in your guide. Consult your midwife or OB/GYN before use.

### FAQ Section

**Q: Will these herbs make me sleepy during the day?**  
A: Not if dosed correctly. These are nervine tonics, not sedatives at low doses. A small cup of tea won't cause daytime drowsiness. Higher doses or tinctures taken in the afternoon might. See dosing guidelines in your guide.

**Q: How long does it take for these herbs to work?**  
A: Depends on the herb. Passionflower and Valerian can work within 30 minutes to an hour. Skullcap works better over weeks of consistent use. For best results, start your protocol 2–3 weeks before you need it (not the night before a big presentation).

**Q: Can I use these with sleep medications?**  
A: Possibly, but check with your doctor or pharmacist. Some interactions are possible, especially with benzodiazepines or prescription sleep aids. The guide includes known interactions. Never combine without medical supervision.

**Q: Will I become dependent on these herbs like I would with sleeping pills?**  
A: Herbs don't create the same chemical dependency as medications, but your nervous system can become habituated (expecting the signal to sleep). The guide teaches rotation protocols and dosing variation to prevent habituation. These herbs are meant as a bridge to better sleep habits, not a permanent crutch.

**Q: Why do some of these herbs have an earthy or bitter taste?**  
A: Bitter taste is actually the sign that you have real, potent herbs (not flavored supplements). The compounds responsible for sleep effects often taste earthy or slightly bitter. You can add honey, lemon, or other herbs to improve the taste—see recipes in your guide.

**Q: How much herb do I need per cup of tea?**  
A: Varies by herb. Chamomile: 1 tablespoon per cup. Valerian root: 1–2 teaspoons per cup. Passionflower leaf: 1–2 teaspoons per cup. See dosing chart in your guide. Start lower and adjust up.

**Q: Can I mix all five herbs together or should I use them separately?**  
A: Both approaches work. See "formulation frameworks" in your guide—some combinations are synergistic, others are best used separately. You'll learn which combinations target which sleep patterns.

**Q: Are these safe for kids or teens?**  
A: Chamomile is safe for all ages. Others depend on age and condition. See the age-guidance section in your safety table. Consult a pediatric herbalist or healthcare provider for children under 12.

**Q: How do I store sleep herbs?**  
A: Cool, dark, dry place in sealed glass jars (included). Keep away from light and moisture. Properly stored, they remain potent for 1–2 years. Avoid storing in the bedroom (humidity from sleep can degrade quality).
