## Plant Sourcing

### Which Species Are in Phase 2 Round 1

Four species make up Appalachian Medicinals Wave 1. They were selected from the 15-candidate endangered species list based on three criteria: commercial seed availability from cultivated (not wild-harvested) sources, photo access within the May window, and combined storytelling strength as a conservation collection.

- **American Ginseng** (*Panax quinquefolius*) — CITES Appendix II anchor species; 200 years of documented overharvest; forest-farming revival narrative. Dried root is the primary prop.
- **Goldenseal** (*Hydrastis canadensis*) — CITES Appendix II; state-endangered in seven states; bright yellow root makes it the most visually distinctive guide in the set.
- **Black Cohosh** (*Actaea racemosa*) — United Plant Savers At-Risk; 95% wild-harvested despite decades of conservation concern; potted live plant is the preferred photography prop.
- **Ramps** (*Allium tricoccum*) — commercially banned in Quebec; culturally resonant Appalachian foodway; fresh farmers market leaves are the primary photography prop.

### Vendor Identification and Lead Times

Three vendors cover all four species. No single vendor supplies everything; orders must be split.

**Mountain Rose Herbs** (mountainroseherbs.com) — primary source for dried roots. Ginseng whole root: $14–22/oz. Goldenseal dried root: $14–20/oz. Ships USPS Priority 2–3 days from Eugene, Oregon. No minimum order. Dried roots are available year-round; zero availability risk. Order by May 11 for May 13–14 arrival.

**Strictly Medicinal Seeds** (strictlymedicinalseeds.com) — primary source for Black Cohosh live plant and Goldenseal seeds. Black Cohosh potted plant: $8–12, ships May through July. Goldenseal seeds (20-pack): $20. Processing 2–3 business days plus 6–9 transit days = 8–12 days total. Order by May 10 for May 18–22 arrival; add "ship ASAP" to order notes.

**Prairie Moon Nursery** (prairiemoon.com) — secondary source for Ramps bulblets. Bulblets (6–10 pack): price TBD; confirm availability by phone or online before ordering. Lead time 7–14 days. Order by May 10 for May 17–24 arrival. Availability is seasonal and varies week to week in May — call to confirm before placing the order.

**Ramps fresh leaves** — purchased locally from farmers markets or specialty grocery stores (Whole Foods, co-ops) on May 18 or 19. Cost $3–8. This is the highest-value ramps prop because fresh broad leaves are immediately recognizable in guide photography. Do not refrigerate for more than 48 hours before the shoot.

### Order Timeline

Orders must be placed no later than May 10 for all items requiring mail delivery. Mountain Rose Herbs dried roots can be ordered as late as May 11 given the 2-day shipping window. Ramps fresh leaves are purchased locally on May 18–19 — no mail order needed. iNaturalist CC-BY photo curation (5–8 Research Grade observations per species) and Biodiversity Heritage Library botanical illustrations are digital and can be gathered on any day before May 15.

### Contingency: If a Vendor Cannot Supply in Time

**Ginseng seeds not available**: This is expected. Ginseng seed is a fall crop; spring stock is depleted at most vendors. The dried whole root (Mountain Rose Herbs, 2-day ship) is the superior identification prop. Skip seeds entirely if unavailable; the guide is fully produceable without them.

**Black Cohosh plant does not arrive by May 18**: Order from Prairie Moon Nursery as backup (3-pack seedlings). If both vendors miss delivery: purchase a live Black Cohosh or native Actaea specimen from a local garden center. If no local source exists, execute the indoor shoot using only iNaturalist CC-BY habitat photography and dried specimens — adequate for all guide interior sections.

**Ramps bulblets not available from Prairie Moon**: Use Etsy search "ramps bulbs Appalachian" — small growers list cultivated bulbs. Verify "cultivated, not wild-harvested" before ordering. Last resort: iNaturalist CC-BY spring archive (2020–2025 observations) covers ramp leaf identification fully without any physical prop.

**All-vendor miss by May 17**: Place emergency Mountain Rose Herbs order on 2-day Priority ship (arrives May 19). Source any remaining props at local garden center or specialty grocery same-day. Compress the shoot to May 21–23. No launch date impact — dried specimens and iNaturalist CC-BY are photographically sufficient for all four guides.

## Location Scouting and Logistics

### Geographic Cluster: Where in Appalachia

The shoot is centered in Asheville, North Carolina, the only practical base for this timeline. Three shoot clusters operate from this hub:

- **Cluster 1 — Forest Floor** (May 20, Asheville Botanical Garden): Field photography of Ginseng, Goldenseal, and Ramps in naturalistic Appalachian woodland understory. The Garden holds 600+ native Appalachian species across 10 acres.
- **Cluster 2 — Root and Seed** (May 21, indoor window studio): All four species, root close-ups and seed panels on a dark wood board surface 2–3 feet from an east- or south-facing window.
- **Cluster 3 — Stem and Leaf** (May 22, Asheville Botanical Garden morning + indoor afternoon): Black Cohosh compound leaf arrangement, stem detail, rhizome.

### Access Permissions

The Asheville Botanical Garden (151 W.T. Weaver Blvd, Asheville, NC 28804) requires a commercial photography permit submitted at least 7 days in advance. The permit application deadline for a May 20 shoot is **May 12**. Apply via ashevillebotanicalgarden.org/facility-rental and include: shoot dates May 20–21, crew size 1–2 persons, equipment (camera, portable reflector, no mounted lights), and intended use (botanical guide photography for digital educational publication sold on Etsy). Fee: $25–75 per 2-hour session; budget $75–150 for a full shoot day.

Critical permit constraint: National Forest commercial photography requires 60-day advance notice (deadline was March 21 — passed). NC State Parks require 14-day notice (deadline was May 6 — passed). Do not shoot on those lands.

Backup location if the Garden cannot confirm by May 13: United Plant Savers (UpS) maintains a network of forest farm sanctuary members in western North Carolina. Email UpS member services and request a private farm introduction for botanical photography. Written landowner permission is sufficient for commercial photography. Begin UpS outreach on May 10 in parallel with the Garden application.

### Logistics: Travel, Accommodation, Equipment

The Garden is 5 minutes from Asheville city center. No overnight accommodation is required if the photographer is based within 2 hours of Asheville. If traveling from further, stay in Asheville May 19–22 (3 nights). Cluster 2 (indoor studio) can be executed at any accommodation with a window — no fixed location.

Equipment transport is a single carry load: camera or smartphone, portable foam-core reflector, silnylon tarp (ground protection), spray bottle, botanical scale (ruler), external drive, laptop for on-site culling. No studio lighting required outdoors; portable LED panel used for indoor Cluster 2 only.

### Team

Minimum viable crew: 1 person with tripod. Preferred: 2 people (photographer plus props handler). The props handler manages specimen placement, scale references, spray bottle, and reflector positioning while the photographer reviews framing. A 2-person crew cuts shoot time by roughly 30% and increases the number of usable selects per session. If solo: use a tripod, remote shutter, and plan 30–45 additional minutes per cluster.

## Photo Shoot Sequence

Each species requires 30 selects covering six angle types: full plant in habitat, leaf detail, flower or fruit (sourced from iNaturalist CC-BY archive for all four species — spring bloom window has passed), root or rhizome, seed or seed pod, and habitat context. Five frames minimum per angle type. Shoot 30–40 RAW frames per angle to guarantee 5 selects after culling.

**Props list per cluster**: Dark-stained hardwood board (Cluster 2 surface), white foam-core reflector, botanical ruler (15 cm metric), clean nitrile gloves (hand-in-frame ID shots), spray bottle with water (moistens dried roots, freshens ramp leaves), silnylon tarp (ground protection, Cluster 1), brown kraft paper (flat-lay backdrop alternative).

**Lighting setup**: Natural north or northeast window light for Cluster 2 indoor. White balance locked at 5500K for all clusters. No direct flash. Foam-core reflector opposite window fills shadows. Portable LED panel used only for indoor Cluster 2 in low-light conditions. Overcast daylight is strongly preferred for Cluster 1 field work — harsh midday sun creates unflattering shadows on root and leaf texture.

**Backup shots if conditions are bad**: Weather failure on any single field day shifts that cluster to the next day (May 20 weather failure shifts Cluster 1 to May 21; Cluster 2 moves to May 20). Full three-day weather failure activates indoor-only mode: Cluster 2 is always executable; Clusters 1 and 3 field habitat shots are sourced from iNaturalist CC-BY archive. The guide is fully produceable in this scenario with no launch date impact.
