## Workstream A: Plant Sourcing

### What to Order and Why

The shoot requires two types of plant material: **live specimens for in-context photography** (field cluster) and **prop material for identification close-ups** (indoor cluster). You do not need to grow any plants — all material is for photography only.

The four core guides require the following physical materials:

| Species | Prop Type | Purpose | Order Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| American Ginseng | Dried whole root + stratified seeds (if available) | Root ID close-up; seed identification panel | Dried root from Mountain Rose Herbs; seeds from Wisconsin Grown Ginseng LLC |
| Goldenseal | Dried root (bright yellow) + seeds | Yellow root ID close-up; seed panel | Dried root from Mountain Rose Herbs; seeds from Strictly Medicinal Seeds |
| Black Cohosh | Live potted plant (preferred) or seeds | Full-plant composition; leaf detail | Live plant from Strictly Medicinal Seeds (ships May); seed packet as backup |
| Ramps | Fresh leaves from farmers market + bulblets | Leaf identification; bulb ID close-up | Local farmers market (fresh); Prairie Moon Nursery (bulblets) |

For complete vendor details, lead times, and ordering instructions, see `phase-2-plant-sourcing-vendor-list.md`.

### Order Deadlines

| Order | Deadline | Vendor | Expected Arrival | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dried ginseng root (1 oz) | May 11 | Mountain Rose Herbs (2-day ship) | May 13–14 | None — available year-round |
| Ginseng seeds (stratified, if available) | May 10 | Wisconsin Grown Ginseng LLC | May 17–20 | Critical — spring stock limited; order immediately |

| BHL botanical illustrations | May 10 (begin) | biodiversitylibrary.org | Immediate (digital) | None |

**Total cash outlay**: $65–$115 (see `phase-2-plant-sourcing-vendor-list.md` for itemized breakdown).

### Plant Handling on Arrival

When plant specimens arrive (May 13–21):
1. Inspect immediately — photograph packaging damage if present; note in WORKLOG.md
2. Dried roots: store at room temperature in paper bag or open kraft envelope; do not refrigerate
3. Live plants (Black Cohosh): place in east- or south-facing window; water lightly; acclimatize 48 hours before photographing
4. Fresh ramp leaves: refrigerate in a damp paper towel; photograph within 24–48 hours of purchase
5. Seeds: keep in original packaging; photograph in packaging for the seed panel shots; do not open until shoot

## Workstream B: Location Scouting

### Primary Location: Asheville Botanical Garden (North Carolina)

The primary shoot location is the Asheville Botanical Garden (151 W.T. Weaver Blvd, Asheville, NC 28804). With 600+ Appalachian species in a naturalistic woodland setting, this is the only public location in the region with:
- Documented collections of all four core species in habitat context
- A streamlined commercial photography permit process (7-day lead time)
- Adequate logistics (free parking, restrooms, shelter from rain, cell signal)

For the complete scouting assessment including backup locations, see `phase-2-location-scout-report.md`.

### Permit Action Items

| Action | Deadline | How |
|---|---|---|
| Submit commercial photography application | May 12 | ashevillebotanicalgarden.org/facility-rental — include: shoot dates May 20–21, crew size (1–2 persons), equipment list (camera/smartphone, portable reflector, no lights), intended use (botanical guide photography for digital educational publication) |
| Pay session fee | May 12 (with application) | $25–75 per 2 hours; budget $75–150 for full shoot day |
| Receive written confirmation | May 13–14 | Garden events manager responds within 1–3 business days |

**If botanical garden is unavailable**: Fall back to private forest farm access via United Plant Savers referral (outreach begins May 10 in parallel). Written permission from a forest farm landowner is the most photographically rewarding option. See `phase-2-location-scout-report.md` Part 3 for the UPS outreach template.

**Permit deadline warning**: National Forest commercial photography requires 60 days advance notice (deadline March 21 — passed). NC State Parks require 14 days (deadline May 6 for May 20 shoot — passed). Do not attempt to shoot on these public lands without permits. Use Asheville Botanical Garden or private land only.

## Workstream C: Photo Shoot and Processing

### Three-Cluster Architecture

The shoot is organized into three clusters across three active shoot days (May 20–22), with May 23–25 as float and processing days.

| Cluster | Day | Location | Species | Primary Shot Types |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cluster 1 — Forest Floor | May 20 (Sat) | Field — Asheville Botanical Garden or forest farm | Ginseng, Goldenseal, Ramps | Full plant in habitat, leaf detail, colony/patch view, root in situ |
| Cluster 2 — Root and Seed | May 21 (Sun) | Indoor — home window setup | All 4 species | Root close-up, seed close-up, dried specimen, scale reference, botanical illustration |
| Cluster 3 — Stem and Leaf | May 22 (Mon) | Field (AM) + Indoor (PM) | Black Cohosh | Compound leaf arrangement, stem detail, rhizome, habitat context |

For the full 30-shot sequence table, camera settings, equipment list, and SOP, see `phase-2-photo-shoot-checklist.md`.

- 10:15–11:00am: Goldenseal root sequence (yellow root critical — verify color accuracy at full zoom)

### Photo Sourcing Tiers (Running in Parallel May 7–14)

In parallel with shoot preparation, curate the digital photo sources that provide flower/bloom shots (impossible in late May for most species), habitat views, and archive botanical illustration.

**Tier 1 — Botanical Garden Partnership (contact by May 7)**
- Email Missouri Botanical Garden (pr@mobot.org) and North Carolina Botanical Garden by May 7
- Request: high-resolution CC-licensed or free-use commercial photos of the four core species
- Response window: May 7–14
- Not required for launch — this is a quality upgrade, not a dependency

**Tier 2 — iNaturalist CC-BY Curation (execute May 7–9)**
- Filter: `photo_license=cc-by` AND `quality_grade=research` for each species
- Target: 5–8 Research Grade CC-BY observations per species, covering all 5 angle types
- Attribution format: "Photo: [Observer Name], iNaturalist, CC BY 4.0"
- Log every selected photo in WORKLOG.md: observer name, iNaturalist URL, license confirmed, intended guide section

**Tier 3 — Public Domain Botanical Illustrations (execute May 7)**
- Source: Biodiversity Heritage Library (biodiversitylibrary.org) and Flickr archive
- Key resource: Ginseng and Goldenseal Growers' Handbook (1912) — BHL item 116021 — contains engraved illustrations of ginseng and goldenseal
- All pre-1928 material is public domain — no attribution required
- Download at highest available resolution; clean paper background in Curves/Levels if needed

By May 14: photo pool is locked — minimum 4 photos per species from digital sources before the shoot supplements with prop photography.

## Workstream D: Guide Production

### Canva Guide Production — May 15–25

Guide production begins May 15, independent of the shoot. The Canva master template is built on available BHL illustrations and preliminary iNaturalist CC-BY photos. Final photos from the shoot slot into the established template May 26.

### Priority Sequence

Four guides are produced in this order, with each subsequent guide duplicated from the prior one:

1. **American Ginseng** — first and most time-intensive (builds the master template); highest search volume; anchor title for the series
2. **Goldenseal** — CITES Appendix II documentation and yellow-root ID photography make this the most research-dense guide
3. **Black Cohosh** — strong "cultivation solution" story; 95% wild-harvest statistic is the lead content hook
4. **Ramps** — last position because ramps photography is most variable (seasonal leaves; potential EFN seed stock delay); also the most culturally compelling guide, benefiting from the refined template established in guides 1–3

### Canva Workflow: 8 Zones Per Guide

Each guide is structured identically across all four species. Maintain zone numbering and section order; vary only species-specific content.

| Zone | Section | Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | Hero image, species name, Latin name, "Know it. Grow it. Protect it." tagline, Seedwarden logo | Full-bleed hero from shoot or iNaturalist CC-BY |
| 2 | Quick ID | Leaf shape, height, habitat, color photos, lookalike warning box | Yellow callout box for lookalike warning — critical for all four species |
| 3 | Ecology | Native range, habitat preference, seasonal timing, relationship to human use | USDA Plants Database range map (public domain) |
| 4 | Cultivation | Propagation method, stratification requirements, grow timeline, shade percentage | Forest-farming method for all four species |
| 5 | Conservation | CITES/UpS/ESA/state status, harvest ethics, cultivation as conservation action, poaching reporting | Most important zone — place prominently; red/yellow/green status table |
| 6 | Practical Use | Ethnobotanical uses, modern applications, preparation methods, contraindications box | FTC-compliant framing: "traditionally used to support..." not therapeutic claims; red disclaimer box required |
| 7 | Sourcing Guide | Verified cultivated vendors with URLs, sustainability context | Verify all URLs are live before export |
| 8 | Further Learning | Books, scientific articles, UpS membership, conservation organizations | UpS CTA in every guide |

### Proofreading and Approval Gate

Before export to PDF:
- Read each guide start-to-finish on a phone screen (simulates buyer experience)
- Check all Zone 7 vendor URLs for live links (use a different browser tab)
- Search for placeholder text: "TBD," "INSERT," "[Name]," "XX" — zero should remain
- Confirm educational disclaimer appears in Zone 6 of every guide
- Confirm CC-BY attribution appears in image caption or footnote for every iNaturalist photo
- Export test PDF and open in a PDF viewer — confirm no font substitution, no text overflow

**Who proofreads**: Self-review minimum. If a second reader is available, have them read Zone 5 (Conservation) and Zone 6 (Practical Use) only — these are the highest-risk sections for inaccuracy or unsupported claims.
