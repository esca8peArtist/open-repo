# Phase 3 Outreach Templates

**Prepared**: June 6, 2026
**Usage instructions**: Each template below is a working draft. Customize the `[BRACKETED FIELDS]` before sending. ## Template 3 — Habitat Specialist

**Use for**: Arthur Haines, Rebecca Lexa (habitat track), Conservation Job Board / SERCAL referrals, state native plant society contacts, and any ecological restoration practitioner whose portfolio includes educational content production.

**Customization note**: This template is calibrated for a scoped, bounded engagement — a habitat content review and regional plant selection contribution — not a full guide authorship commission. Habitat specialists typically respond better to a clearly bounded scope than to an open-ended writing request.

---

**Subject line**: Native plant habitat content — regional guide contribution, June 2026 start

---

Hi [SPECIALIST'S FIRST NAME],

I found your work via [SOURCE: your website / arthurhaines.com / Conservation Job Board / CNPS / NW-SER / referral from X] and wanted to reach out about a content contribution opportunity that I think aligns well with your expertise.

I run Seedwarden, a botanical education series producing downloadable field guides on native plants, wild edibles, and medicinal herbs. Phase 3 of the series — covering wild edibles and medicinal herbs documentation — launches June 22. I am looking for a habitat specialist to contribute [SCOPE: e.g., USDA zone-specific plant habitat notes / native plant selection sidebar content / habitat ecology context for Northeastern species / regional foraging site assessment criteria].

**What the contribution involves**:
The guides include a habitat and regional context section for each plant covered. I need a specialist who can verify and augment this content for [REGION: e.g., Northeast / Pacific Northwest / Southeast / Midwest] with:
- Accurate USDA hardiness zone ranges for featured species
- Habitat types where each species is typically found (meadow, woodland edge, riparian, disturbed ground, etc.)
- Native range vs. naturalized range distinctions where relevant
- Ethical wildcrafting notes specific to regional habitat pressure (at-risk habitat designations, conservation area restrictions)
- Native plant selection recommendations for readers who want to grow featured species in their region

**Scope and deliverable**:
- [NUMBER: 3–8] plant species covered for [REGION] region
- Format: 200–400 words of habitat notes per species, plus a 400–600 word regional plant selection sidebar
- Total: approximately [TOTAL WORDS: 1,000–3,800] words of habitat-specific content
- One revision cycle included

**Timeline**:
- Contract signed: by June 17
- Delivery: [DATE: July 8 / July 15 — set based on candidate and production schedule]
- Review cycle: 2 weeks

**Budget**: $[AMOUNT: $400–$900] flat rate for the full regional scope. I am open to hourly if you prefer.

**Work terms**: Work-for-hire. All content becomes Seedwarden property on delivery. Your credential or affiliation may be listed in the guide's acknowledgments section (optional — let me know your preference).

I have reviewed your work on [REFERENCE SPECIFIC WORK: e.g., "Ancestral Plants" / "The Everyday Naturalist" / your property consultation practice] and believe your [REGION] regional expertise is a strong match for what the Phase 3 guides need.

Would you be open to a brief conversation to discuss whether the scope is a fit? I can send the full content outline, species list, and an example of the habitat section format in advance.

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
