The four species in the Immunity bundle are:

**Echinacea** (*E. purpurea* and *E. angustifolia* — treated as separate entries, because they are clinically and ecologically distinct). The Cochrane review (Karsch-Völk, 2015) and the Rauš 2021 trial comparing echinacea to oseltamivir are both referenced. So is the Asteraceae allergy caution, which is frequently omitted.

**Ashwagandha** (*Withania somnifera*). The Rasayana context, withanolide RCT evidence, and — critically — the mandatory thyroid medication and pregnancy contraindications. This is the most commonly underspecified safety note in ashwagandha marketing.

**Elderberry** in its immunity framing (distinct from the Respiratory bundle, which covers raw berry toxicity and sambunigrin). Here the focus is fermentation, immune activation, and the 2019 Hawkins meta-analysis.

**Goldenseal** (*Hydrastis canadensis*). The bundle includes a 200-word CITES Appendix II sidebar — written for direct distribution to clients. It covers the international trade status, why wild-harvested material is the sourcing problem, and how to find Forest Grown Verified certified suppliers. The CITES sidebar is in the guide because practitioners who recommend goldenseal should be explaining this to their clients, not leaving it to chance.
