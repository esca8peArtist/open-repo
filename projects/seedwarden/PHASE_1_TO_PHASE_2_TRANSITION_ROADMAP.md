# Phase 1 to Phase 2 Transition Roadmap

**Prepared**: June 3, 2026
**Phase 1 window**: June 3 – July 31, 2026 (12 weeks)
**Phase 2 research window**: July 1 – August 31, 2026 (parallel production model)
**Phase 2 launch readiness target**: September 15, 2026
**Central finding**: Phase 2 content production can begin in parallel with Phase 1 execution — Phase 1 metrics do not gate Phase 2 timing, they gate Phase 2 strategy. The specific Phase 2 go-to-market approach (warm list leverage vs. cold Etsy discovery vs. commercial licensing) is determined by Day 7 and Day 30 Phase 1 engagement data. Writing capacity and summer photography access are the only hard constraints.

**Critical platform constraint**: Kit's free plan allows only one email sequence and one basic visual automation. Adding a Phase 2 herbalist nurture sequence (20+ emails) requires either upgrading to Kit Creator at $33/month or running Phase 2 as broadcast emails tagged to herbalist-segment subscribers. This infrastructure decision must be made by June 14.

## Section 3 — Platform Capacity Assessment

### Etsy Shop: Phase 1 + Phase 2 Simultaneous Operation

**Capacity**: Fully viable. Etsy has no documented maximum listing count. Phase 1's 6 listings remain live during Phase 2 with zero overhead unless customer questions arise (budget 15 minutes/day for inbox monitoring).

**Upload spacing**: Phase 2 upload dates should be spaced 7 days apart (e.g., September 6, September 8, September 10) so each new listing gets approximately 72 hours of algorithmic recency boost before the next one publishes. Do not batch-publish all Phase 2 listings on one day.

**File handling**: All Phase 2 PDFs target 682–754 KB, well within Etsy's 20MB-per-file limit.

**Fulfillment ceiling**: Digital product delivery is fully automated by Etsy. There is no fulfillment ceiling on simultaneous Phase 1 + Phase 2 orders. The only overhead scales are customer inbox (15 min/day at under 50 orders/month) and review response time. These do not prevent Phase 2 research.

### Kit Email Service: One List, Two Products

**Free plan ceiling**: 10,000 subscribers, unlimited broadcasts, 1 sequence, 1 basic automation. At projected Phase 2 launch list size of 50–300 subscribers, Kit free tier is not a capacity constraint.

**The one real constraint**: The single sequence slot is occupied by the Phase 1 welcome sequence. Phase 2 herbalist funnel requires either a Kit Creator upgrade ($33/month) or broadcast-based delivery. This is not a launch blocker but is a budget decision that should be made by June 14.

**Can one email list handle two different products without degrading engagement?** Yes, with segmentation. Tag Phase 1 buyers with `native_plants_buyer` and Phase 2 buyers with `herbalist_buyer`. Send product-specific content to each segment. Unsegmented subscribers (email-only, no purchases) receive both product lines — this is appropriate because they opted into the Seedwarden brand, not a specific product category.

**Engagement risk**: Sending both native plants content and medicinal herb content to an unsegmented list degrades engagement only if subscribers feel the two topics are unrelated. Botanical content to outdoor/garden audience is cohesive enough that cross-topic sends should not produce meaningful unsubscribe spikes. Monitor unsubscribe rate after the first Phase 2 broadcast — above 1% per send warrants tighter segmentation.

### Social Media: Simultaneous Phase 1 Community + Phase 2 Content Production

**Daily posting capacity**: 1–3 feed posts per day, up to 5 stories/day, 4–7 Reels per week on Instagram. Pinterest supports unlimited daily pins.

**Solo operator bandwidth**: Phase 1 social content (community engagement, daily posts) can coexist with Phase 2 research if social posts are batch-created weekly (90-minute Monday batch session). During the August writing sprint, reduce Phase 1 social to 1 post/day baseline — writing hours take priority. Phase 2 social content should not be created during the August sprint at all; write it in bulk during the September 1–5 float period before launch.

**Influencer relationship management**: Maintaining Phase 1 influencer contacts while building Phase 2 herbalist practitioner relationships requires approximately 2–3 hours/week of correspondence. This is manageable solo if batched to 30-minute daily email windows. Above 300+ subscribers with Phase 1 affiliate relationships active, consider a part-time research assistant (see Scenario F below).

### Team Bandwidth: Solo Operator Ceiling

**Sprint commitment (August 1–31)**: Heavy writing days are 6–8 hours. Light days (design, upload prep) are 4–5 hours. Upload days are 8–9 hours.

**Parallelism rule**: Design, photography (morning sessions), supplier follow-ups, and social scheduling can run in parallel with writing. They must be scheduled outside the writing block (mornings 8–10am or evenings), not during it.

**Phase 1 customer inbox overhead during sprint**: At under 50 orders/month, budget 15 minutes/day for Etsy inbox. At 51–200 orders/month, budget 30–45 minutes/day. At 200+ orders/month, inbox management alone prevents full-day writing sprints — see Scenario D and F below.

## Section 4 — Risk Scenarios and Decision Trees

### Scenario A: Slow Phase 1 Uptake — Day 7 Engagement Below 15%

**Definition**: Email open rate below 15% on welcome sequence AND fewer than 5 Kit subscribers at Day 7 AND zero Etsy sales in first 7 days despite 30+ views.

**Root cause**: Almost always an SEO failure (listing titles not matching active search terms) or a first-image trust failure, not a product quality problem. Email below 15% is likely a spam delivery or broken sender authentication issue.

**Impact on Phase 2**: Phase 2 content research (July) can proceed. Phase 2 writing sprint (August) should not begin until the Day 30 Phase 1 audit shows conversion above 1.5%. If Phase 1 conversion remains below 1.5% at Day 30, revise listing copy before uploading Phase 2 guides — the same structural problems will reproduce.

**Decision tree**:
- Day 7 email open below 15% → Check SPF/DKIM sender authentication in Kit settings before any other action
- Day 7 zero sales with 30+ views → Audit listing mockup quality and price competitiveness against top 10 Etsy results for primary keyword
- Day 30 conversion below 1.5% → Run Phase 1 listing optimization sprint (rewrite title, test new mockup, add social proof language) before Phase 2 uploads begin
- Phase 2 research (July): proceed regardless. Photography and species research are not affected by Phase 1 metrics.
- Phase 2 writing sprint (August): proceed unless Day 30 conversion below 1.5% AND Day 30 email open below 20% simultaneously. In that case, delay writing sprint start by 2 weeks to resolve Phase 1 structural issues first.

**Does slow Phase 1 trigger Phase 2 deferral to October?** No. Below 50 subscribers, Phase 2 changes its promotional strategy — it does not change its launch date. The September 15 target holds across all Phase 1 outcomes.

### Scenario B: Fast Phase 1 Growth — 500+ Orders/Month by Day 45

**Definition**: Phase 1 generates 500+ orders/month by mid-July (approximately Day 45 from June 3 launch).

**Context**: At $12 AOV, 500 orders/month = $6,000 gross monthly revenue, approximately $4,920 net after Etsy fees. This is exceptional early performance and fundamentally changes the Phase 2 decision calculus.

**Impact on Phase 2**: Three-way decision fork.

**Decision tree**:
- 500+ orders/month, solo operator: Inbox management (500 orders/month × average 5% question rate = 25 customer inquiries/month) is manageable but grows. Begin Phase 2 research as planned in July. Do NOT attempt solo 5-bundle Phase 2 writing sprint — compress to 3-bundle minimum. At this order volume, revenue justifies a part-time research assistant ($15–20/hour, 10 hours/week) for species research and photography to free up writing capacity.
- 500+ orders/month, content licensing interest: At this revenue level, commercial partners (regional garden centers, herbalist schools, wellness publishers) may approach for licensing or wholesale. Before licensing any content, consult the existing `AFFILIATE_PARTNERSHIP_FRAMEWORK.md`. Commercial licensing requires a separate pricing structure ($500–2,500/license for unlimited distribution rights) and is not compatible with Etsy's standard digital download model. Evaluate on a case-by-case basis — do not accept the first inbound offer.
- 500+ orders/month with review velocity (20+ reviews): Phase 2 goes live with social proof as a credibility signal. Pre-launch email to Phase 1 buyers ("You already love the garden guides — here's what's coming next") is a high-conversion mechanism at this subscriber/buyer scale.

**Does >500 orders/month accelerate Phase 2 to August (ahead of September 15)?** Only for the first bundle. Women's Health bundle could upload August 25–31 (one week ahead of target) if writing sprint starts August 1 and pace gate at August 6 is met. Do not compress all Phase 2 uploads to August — the Etsy algorithmic recency boost requires spacing.

### Scenario C: Etsy Algorithm Change Impacts Phase 1 Discovery

**Definition**: Phase 1 listings drop 30%+ in weekly views after an Etsy algorithm update.

**Detection signal**: Shop views drop more than 30% week-over-week without any seller-side listing changes.

**Decision tree**:
- Step 1: Check Etsy Seller Community forums for platform-wide reports in the same week. If widespread, this is a platform event — wait 7 days before making changes.
- Step 2: If isolated to Seedwarden listings: renew all listings manually to force re-indexing. Check that no tags have been auto-removed for policy compliance.
- Step 3: Activate Pinterest as backup traffic source. A batch of 15–20 new pins takes 2 hours and begins driving traffic within 30 days. Pinterest is algorithm-independent from Etsy.
- Step 4: If views remain below 50% of baseline after 14 days: run a $20–30 Pinterest promoted pin test on the highest-converting Phase 1 listing.
- Phase 2 impact: If Phase 1 is in algorithm-recovery mode when Phase 2 launches (September 15), still publish on schedule. New listings receive an Etsy recency boost at publication — Phase 2 launches are not disadvantaged by a Phase 1 algorithm event. The two listing batches are algorithmically independent.

**Does an Etsy algorithm change trigger Phase 2 pivot to another platform?** Only if Phase 1 shop views remain below 50% of pre-event baseline for 30+ consecutive days despite re-optimization. In that case, evaluate Gumroad or Payhip as secondary storefronts. This threshold would not be reached before September 15.

### Scenario D: Fulfillment Overload Blocks Phase 2 Research Time

**Definition**: Phase 1 order volume exceeds 100 orders/month by Day 45, generating customer inbox load that consistently consumes more than 1.5 hours/day — preventing Phase 2 research or writing blocks.

**Context**: Digital product delivery is fully automated by Etsy. "Fulfillment overload" for digital products means customer support (download issues, refund requests, questions about content), not physical packing. At 100 orders/month with a 5% question rate, this is 5 inquiries/month — easily managed in 15 minutes/day. The real threshold is higher: 300–500 orders/month generates 15–25 monthly inquiries, which is still manageable solo at 30–45 minutes/day. True fulfillment ceiling begins at 1,000+ orders/month.

**Decision tree**:
- Under 100 orders/month: zero impact on Phase 2 research or writing time
- 100–300 orders/month: budget 30 minutes/day for inbox. Phase 2 writing sprint can proceed at full capacity.
- 300–500 orders/month: budget 45 minutes/day for inbox. Reduce Phase 2 daily writing target from 5 hours to 4 hours. Writing sprint extends by 3–4 days but September 15 launch readiness is still achievable.
- 500–1,000 orders/month: inbox management plus review response consumes 90+ minutes/day. At this level, Phase 2 3-bundle scope is still achievable but 5-bundle scope requires either a part-time assistant (Scenario F) or a September 15 target for 3 bundles and November 1 target for the full 5.
- Above 1,000 orders/month: reconsider the business model entirely. At 1,000 orders/month ($12,000 gross), investing $500–1,500/month in a virtual assistant to handle customer support and Phase 2 research is directly ROI-positive.

**Does order volume above 100/month delay Phase 2 start to August?** No. Phase 2 research (July) requires only 1–2 focused hours/day — well within available time even at 500 orders/month. The writing sprint (August) is the high-time-commitment phase. If order volume is above 300/month in August, extend the sprint by 1 week (August 1 through September 7 instead of August 31) rather than compressing daily writing hours.

### Scenario E: Seasonal Photography Bottleneck

**Definition**: Phase 2 wild edibles photography must occur in summer (June–August) because key species are dormant in fall and unavailable for fresh photography in winter.

**Seasonal constraint**: Wild edibles and medicinal herb habit photography is strictly seasonal. Species like Black Cohosh, Elderberry, Echinacea, and Mullein are best photographed June–August. September photography is possible for some species but with reduced visual quality. October through April photography is not viable for outdoor habitat shots of most Phase 2 target species.

**Decision tree**:
- Photography sessions scheduled July 15–31: optimal. Fresh habit photos available for all 3-bundle minimum species.
- Photography sessions not scheduled by August 15: activate Wikimedia Commons CC backup. All 14 Phase 2 medicinal species have CC-licensed habit photos at publishable quality. Do not delay writing sprint for photography.
- Photography sessions scheduled but species not available locally: order dried herb samples from Mountain Rose Herbs ($73–107 for 11 species) for studio still-life photography. Dried herb studio photos are an acceptable substitute for habit photos in a PDF guide context — they show the plant material the buyer will actually use.
- Phase 2 research not started by July 31: Phase 2 writing sprint in August must use 100% Wikimedia Commons CC images. This does not affect quality materially — original photography is a differentiator but not a requirement.

**Seasonal hard deadline**: If Phase 2 photography for any species has not been sourced (original or Wikimedia CC confirmed) by September 1, that species is excluded from the September 15 launch and added to a November Phase 2.2 update. Do not delay launch to photograph missing species — ship with what is ready.

### Scenario F: Team Expansion — Part-Time Research Assistant

**Definition**: Phase 2 content production requires hiring a part-time research assistant when writing sprint demands exceed solo operator capacity.

**Trigger criteria**: Hire a part-time research assistant only if ALL of the following are true simultaneously:
- Phase 1 Day 30 subscriber count is above 150 (validates audience demand exists for Phase 2)
- Phase 1 monthly revenue is above $1,500/month (validates revenue to fund the hire)
- Phase 2 scope is 5 bundles (not 3-bundle minimum) — the 3-bundle scope is achievable solo without a hire

**Role scope**: A part-time research assistant (10 hours/week at $15–20/hour = $600–800/month) handles species research compilation (safety data, FTC compliance notes, botanical cross-references), Wikimedia Commons photo sourcing and attribution logging, and Etsy draft listing setup. They do NOT write guide content — the botanical accuracy and editorial voice require the primary author.

**Hiring timeline if triggered**:
- July 1: Post a targeted listing on Upwork or Contra (not general job boards — target herbalists, botanical illustrators, forager community members with research background)
- July 7–14: Interview and onboard
- July 14 onward: Research assistant active on species research, Wikimedia sourcing, and attribution logging
- September 1: Research assistant transitions to Etsy listing setup and QA support for launch

**Does Phase 2 require a hire to launch by September 15?** No. The 3-bundle minimum scope is achievable solo in 36–44 writing hours. A hire is an accelerant for the 5-bundle scope, not a requirement for the baseline launch.

## Section 5 — Phase 2 Activation Recommendation

Phase 2 content research should begin July 1, 2026 regardless of Phase 1 engagement outcomes. The June 3–July 31 Phase 1 window provides sufficient time to generate Day 7 and Day 30 engagement data that shapes Phase 2 go-to-market strategy without delaying Phase 2 production. Above the target threshold (150+ subscribers, 2.5%+ conversion), proceed to 5-bundle scope with a research assistant. At or below the minimum threshold (50–150 subscribers, 1.5–2.4% conversion), proceed with 3-bundle scope solo.

**Confidence level: 85%.** The primary uncertainty is Phase 1 conversion rate — new Etsy shops without reviews underperform the platform benchmark by 30–50% in Month 1. If Phase 1 conversion is consistently below 1.5% at Day 30, one week of listing copy revision before the August writing sprint is warranted, which pushes the sprint start to August 8 and the September 15 target to September 22. All other Phase 2 activation paths have predictable timelines.

The one infrastructure decision with a hard deadline is the Kit Creator plan upgrade: this must be decided by June 14 (upgrade if subscribers at 100+, defer if under 50). Missing this decision does not block Phase 2 launch but limits the herbalist email funnel to manual broadcasts through the launch period.
