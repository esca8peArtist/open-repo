# Seedwarden B2B Distribution & Wholesale Strategy

**Prepared**: May 5, 2026  
**Horizon**: Months 1–6 post-Phase-1-launch (June–November 2026)  
**Purpose**: Production-ready playbook for B2B channel development. Enables execution immediately after Phase 1 Etsy launch is stable (30–60 days post-launch).

---

## Most Important Finding

The B2B opportunity for Seedwarden's digital product catalog is real but channel-dependent: **affiliates and content licensing to media publishers are highest-ROI with lowest support overhead**, while seed cooperative and extension office channels require more relationship time but generate larger per-deal revenue. The quick-win path is affiliate partnerships with existing homesteading Substack, newsletter, and YouTube creators (commission-based, no upfront work), followed by content licensing deals with seed companies and garden media. Extension office and seed cooperative partnerships are Month 3–6 targets — they require grant cycle awareness and longer sales cycles.

The most important calibration: Seedwarden's guides are premium-positioned (research-backed, design-quality, regional specificity) — they do not belong in PLR bulk markets. Any B2B licensing must protect this positioning. The pricing framework below is built around a **floor rule**: no distribution channel should receive Seedwarden content at a price that would undercut the Etsy retail price if surfaced publicly.

## Part 1 — B2B Partner Identification and Channel Map

### Channel 1: Seed Suppliers (Johnny's, Fedco, Baker Creek, Bootstrap Farmer, Botanical Interests)

**What they currently do**: These companies produce their own in-house educational content (growing guides, planting charts, seasonal newsletters) as customer acquisition and retention tools. None of them operate a content marketplace or resell third-party digital guides as a revenue line. Their digital content is currently a marketing cost, not a revenue center.

**The opportunity**: Bundled digital guide add-ons at checkout ("Add the Seed Saving Field Manual for $4 with your order"), or co-branded educational content (Fedco-branded version of Seedwarden's Heirloom Seed Guide distributed to their 60,000+ catalog recipients). The content licensing pitch is distinct from the affiliate pitch — content licensing means they pay once for rights to distribute; affiliate means they earn a commission when they send buyers to Seedwarden's store.

**Fedco specifically**: Fedco is a consumer-worker cooperative (40% worker-owned, 60% consumer-owned), values-aligned with Seedwarden's food sovereignty and seed saving positioning. Their catalog serves approximately 60,000 households, primarily in the Northeast. They are structurally receptive to cooperative-economy framings and not purely profit-maximizing — which means the pitch angle can emphasize member value over margin.

**Bootstrap Farmer**: Runs a 10% commission affiliate program on greenhouse/supply orders. Average order value exceeds $120. They are already affiliate-program-literate. The pitch here is different: Bootstrap Farmer customers are semi-commercial growers who would benefit from Seedwarden's Seed Saving Field Manual and Commercial-Scale Harvest Preservation guides. An affiliate arrangement (Bootstrap Farmer earns 25–30% per Seedwarden sale they refer) is lower friction than a licensing deal.

**Botanical Interests** (now part of Epic Gardening): Runs a two-tier creator program — 15% affiliate commission for creators, higher for ambassadors. They already send traffic to third-party content. The pitch angle is to get Seedwarden listed as a recommended resource in their creator newsletter (The Epic Gardening Thymes). This is relationship-based marketing, not direct licensing.

**Baker Creek**: Mission-aligned (heirloom seed preservation, food sovereignty, anti-GMO). They publish a print magazine (Heirloom Gardener) with a large readership in Seedwarden's exact customer cohort. A licensing deal — Baker Creek distributes a Seedwarden-branded native plants guide insert in their catalog — is plausible. Their audience skews toward Seedwarden's Homesteader and Food Sovereignty cohorts.

**Realistic B2B take rate for this channel**: 15–30% commission on referred sales (affiliate model) OR a one-time content licensing fee of $500–2,500 per guide for co-branded distribution to their list.

### Channel 2: Seed Cooperatives and Regional Seed Libraries

**What they currently do**: Seed libraries (there are approximately 700+ in the U.S., operated through public libraries, academic institutions, and community organizations) distribute seeds, seed envelopes, and growing instructions. Most have very limited budgets — the average library-based seed library operates on $0–500/year in materials budget. Academic seed libraries (like the Eckerd College Seed Library) occasionally partner with extension services and professors.

Going to Seed's 2024–2025 initiative is the best market precedent: they converted digital crop guides into printable seed-saving zines and distributed 2,000+ copies to 24 seed libraries nationwide. The economics were grant-funded, not commercial. This is the structural reality of the seed library channel: they will accept free or grant-subsidized content, but do not have budget to license commercial content at meaningful rates.

**The opportunity is indirect**: Seed libraries are better approached as brand-building and list-building channels than revenue channels. The model: Seedwarden offers a co-branded "Seed Library Starter Guide" (a stripped-down, printable version of the Seed Saving Field Manual) at no cost to participating seed libraries, with a QR code linking back to Seedwarden's full product catalog. This creates distribution of approximately 50–500 copies per library partner, generates email list opt-ins, and establishes Seedwarden's credibility in the seed saving space.

Snake River Seed Cooperative (Idaho) and similar regional cooperatives are slightly better commercial targets — they have members who pay annual dues and receive member benefits. A "digital library" of Seedwarden guides as a member benefit (Seedwarden receives a flat licensing fee from the cooperative, e.g., $200–500/year for unlimited digital access for members) is a viable structure. The cooperative benefits from member value; Seedwarden receives recurring revenue and brand exposure.

**Realistic B2B take rate for this channel**: $150–500 per cooperative partner per year (site license / member benefit). Seed library channel is brand-building, not revenue.

### Channel 3: Agricultural Extension Networks

**How extension offices work**: The Cooperative Extension System is a federal-state-county partnership funded through USDA NIFA grants and state university budgets. NIFA appropriated $561.7 million for extension activities in FY2024. Extension offices produce their own educational content — primarily free factsheets, bulletins, and workshops — as their primary mission. They are not structured to purchase or license commercial educational content.

**The realistic partnership model**: Extension offices can be partners in grant-funded initiatives, not direct commercial customers. USDA NIFA competitive grants (e.g., New Technologies for Ag Extension, funding opportunity USDA-NIFA-OP-010186) have funded digital content initiatives. The path to extension partnership is:

1. Identify a state land-grant university with an active extension digital content initiative (e.g., NC State Extension, UC Cooperative Extension, Cornell Cooperative Extension).
2. Propose a content collaboration — Seedwarden contributes adapted digital guides as educational materials; the extension office provides co-branding, distribution via their county networks, and potentially grant funding.
3. The grant covers content adaptation and distribution costs; Seedwarden receives attribution, list-building, and a modest licensing fee ($500–2,000 per grant cycle).

Cornell Cooperative Extension has operated a seed library in partnership with Master Gardener volunteers, distributing vegetable and pollinator seed packets. This creates an entry point: Seedwarden's Zone Quick-Start Cards and Companion Planting Charts are exactly the supplementary materials extension seed libraries need.

**Timeline reality**: Extension office partnerships require 6–18 months for grant cycles and institutional approval. Do not treat this channel as a Month 1–3 opportunity. It is a Month 6–18 channel with high credibility value.

**Realistic B2B take rate for this channel**: $500–2,000 per grant-funded project. Long sales cycle; treat as credibility investment.

### Channel 4: Garden Centers and Homesteading Retail

**Market context**: The garden center platform market was valued at approximately $11 billion in 2024, projected to reach $26.91 billion by 2033. Independent garden centers are undergoing digital transformation — many are adding e-commerce, QR-code-linked product information, and digital gift cards.

**The opportunity**: Seedwarden's guides are not a natural fit for traditional wholesale into physical garden centers (no physical SKU, no planogram placement, no retailer margin on a digital download without a mechanism). The more tractable model is a **QR code insert program**: garden centers purchase a bundle of co-branded "quick reference card" inserts (physical, printed by Seedwarden's POD partners) that point to a landing page where buyers can purchase the full digital guide. The garden center earns a commission on digital sales referred by the insert. The physical card costs approximately $0.50–1.00 (POD, laminated), and the garden center keeps 20–30% of the digital sale.

An alternative: digital gift cards for garden center checkout — "Give the gift of gardening knowledge." Garden centers already sell physical gift cards and are receptive to adjacent digital SKUs.

**Homesteading retail chains** (Country Life Natural Foods, Tractor Supply's online partners, rural feed stores): Tractor Supply operates a significant e-commerce presence with digital content partnerships. Their customer base overlaps heavily with Seedwarden's Homesteader and Survival Prepper cohorts. An affiliate arrangement — Seedwarden guides listed in a "Digital Resource" section of a homesteading retail partner's website — is a viable structure.

**Realistic B2B take rate for this channel**: 20–30% commission to retailer on referred digital sales. Low revenue per partner initially; volume requires multiple retailer partners.

### Channel 5: Homesteading / Permaculture Media (Blogs, YouTube, Substack, Newsletters)

**This is the highest-ROI, lowest-overhead B2B channel.** The homesteading content creator ecosystem is large, affiliate-literate, and actively seeking revenue diversification.

**Commission benchmarks from active homesteading affiliate programs**:
- The Homestead Education: up to 50% on digital products
- The Free Range Life: 35% on all purchases
- Wandering Hoof Ranch: 30% commission
- Rich Soil: 40% on video sets
- A Modern Homestead: 40% on books
- Mama on the Homestead: 20% commission
- Homesteaders of America: 10% on memberships

The market rate for homesteading digital product affiliates is **25–40%**. Seedwarden should offer 30% as a standard rate, with 35% for high-volume publishers (defined as 10+ sales/month).

**Target publisher types and specific prospects**:

1. **Substack newsletters**: Backyard Farming Connection (Gretchen, backyardfarming.substack.com) covers modern homesteading and gardening with an active paid subscriber base. Seed Library Network (seedlibrarynetwork.substack.com) publishes content directly aligned with Seedwarden's seed saving catalog. These are warm audiences — no cold pitching required, just an authentic collaboration email.

2. **YouTube channels**: The homesteading YouTube space includes channels with 100K–2M+ subscribers covering exactly Seedwarden's product categories (seed saving, foraging, food preservation, survival gardening). Micro-channels (10K–100K subscribers) are more receptive to affiliate partnerships with smaller creators and often have higher engagement rates per subscriber.

3. **Pinterest/blog creators**: The "Gardening Know How" style blog ecosystem creates affiliate link embeds naturally. Fine Gardening already operates an affiliate link program (Amazon Associates + partners). A Seedwarden affiliate partnership can be embedded in existing blog content without requiring new content creation from the partner.

4. **Permies.com community**: Permies is the largest permaculture and homesteading online community (forum-based, approximately 1 million registered users). Their digital marketplace has featured homesteading bundles — the 2024 Homesteading + Permaculture Bundle was listed directly on Permies and priced at $65+ for 35+ items. Seedwarden products could be included in future community bundles, or Seedwarden could list directly in their digital market.

**Realistic B2B take rate for this channel**: Affiliates retain 30–35%; Seedwarden retains 65–70% of sale price, minus Etsy/platform fees. Net margin on an affiliate-referred $12 sale: approximately $6.50–7.20 (vs. $10.61 direct on Etsy).

## Part 2 — Wholesale Pricing Matrix and Margin Models

### Seedwarden's Etsy Direct-to-Consumer Baseline

| Product Type | Etsy Price | Etsy Net (after ~10.5% fees) | Margin (zero COGS digital) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single guide (low tier) | $5–9 | $4.20–7.90 | ~90% |
| Single guide (mid tier) | $10–13 | $8.70–11.40 | ~90% |
| Single guide (premium) | $14–22 | $12.20–19.30 | ~90% |
| Bundle (2–4 products) | $25–45 | $21.80–39.55 | ~90% |
| Zone Calendar (tiered) | $7–18 | $6.10–15.80 | ~90% |

Digital products have essentially zero marginal COGS — each additional copy sold costs nothing. This means any B2B channel that generates incremental sales is additive margin, even at deeply discounted wholesale prices, provided the deal does not cannibalize existing Etsy retail sales.

---

### Wholesale Pricing Framework — By License Type

**Principle**: Floor price is 40% of Etsy retail for any license. This ensures Seedwarden earns more than platform fees cover on a direct sale, even at maximum discount. Below this floor, Seedwarden would be better off declining and investing the time in direct Etsy SEO.

#### Model A — Affiliate Referral (Partner Earns Commission, Seedwarden Sells)

The affiliate earns a percentage of the Seedwarden retail price. Seedwarden continues to sell at full retail; the affiliate earns a cut. Platform fees still apply. Best for: media publishers, blogs, YouTube channels, Substack newsletters.

| Product | Etsy Retail | 30% Affiliate Commission | Etsy Fees (est.) | Seedwarden Net |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $7.99 guide | $7.99 | $2.40 paid to affiliate | $1.08 | $4.51 |
| $12.99 guide | $12.99 | $3.90 | $1.66 | $7.43 |
| $18.99 guide | $18.99 | $5.70 | $2.24 | $11.05 |
| $32 bundle | $32.00 | $9.60 | $3.62 | $18.78 |

Affiliate commission is paid by Seedwarden from gross revenue. The affiliate link directs buyers to Seedwarden's Etsy shop or an independent Gumroad/Payhip store (where Etsy fees are avoided, improving Seedwarden's net by ~$1.00–2.00 per sale).

**Recommendation**: For affiliate tracking, set up on Gumroad or Payhip (each offers native affiliate tracking tools) as a parallel sales channel. This allows affiliate link tracking without Etsy's fee structure.

---

#### Model B — Site License (Partner Pays Once, Distributes to Their Audience)

The partner pays a one-time fee and distributes a specified number of copies (or unlimited copies to their defined membership base). Best for: seed cooperatives, academic seed libraries with small budgets, regional gardening clubs.

| Product | Etsy Retail | Recommended Site License | License Scope | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $7.99 single guide | $7.99 | $199–499 | Up to 500 downloads | 25–62x retail; justifiable for list reach |
| $12.99–18.99 guide | avg. $15 | $499–999 | Up to 500 downloads | 33–67x retail |
| Full catalog (21 products) | $250 retail value | $1,500–3,500/year | Unlimited member access | Recurring; cooperative member benefit model |
| Bundle (4 products) | $32 | $800–1,500 | Up to 300 downloads | Per-event educational license |

**The $7.99 guide example the brief asks about**: For a seed cooperative wanting to bundle Seedwarden's $7.99 Companion Planting Chart with new member kits:
- A cooperative with 200 members: a $299 one-time license covers all current members for 1 year. That is $1.50/member — clearly viable for a membership organization that charges $20–50/year in dues.
- A cooperative with 500 members: a $499 site license — $1.00/member. At this price point, the cooperative has almost no barrier to saying yes.

---

#### Model C — Co-Branded Content License (Partner Rebrands/Distributes Under Their Name)

The partner pays for the right to apply their branding to Seedwarden's content. Content structure and accuracy are Seedwarden's; cover, header, and attribution can be customized. Best for: seed companies wanting to distribute educational content to their catalog customers (Baker Creek, Fedco, regional seed suppliers).

| Product | Co-Brand License Fee | Terms | Brand Constraint |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single guide (co-branded cover only) | $750–1,500 one-time | 1-year distribution, PDF only | Must retain "Content by Seedwarden" in footer |
| Single guide (full co-brand, partner's name primary) | $1,500–3,000 | 1-year, PDF only, no sub-licensing | Must retain attribution in footer/metadata |
| Catalog of 5 guides (co-branded) | $5,000–8,000/year | Annual renewal, PDF + print rights | Attribution required; Seedwarden listed as author |
| White-label (partner claims authorship entirely) | Not offered | — | Brand dilution risk unacceptable at this stage |

**White-label / full PLR is not recommended for Phase 2.** Seedwarden's content is its primary competitive moat. Selling the authorship away — even at a premium — risks the brand positioning that makes the Etsy direct-to-consumer channel work. A competitor could rebrand Seedwarden's content and list it on Etsy at a lower price. The co-brand model (partner branding prominent, Seedwarden attribution retained) captures the revenue without the brand risk.

---

#### Model D — Wholesale Bundle for Events/Conferences

Homesteaders of America Annual Conference, permaculture convergences, and regional seed swaps attract 200–2,000 participants who are in Seedwarden's exact customer cohort. Conference organizers can purchase a digital bundle license for attendee gift bags.

| Bundle | Etsy Value | Wholesale Conference License | Per-Attendee Cost | Volume |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter Bundle (3 guides) | $24 | $300–600 (200 attendees) | $1.50–3.00/attendee | 200 |
| Full Food Sovereignty Bundle | $30 | $500–1,000 (200 attendees) | $2.50–5.00/attendee | 200 |
| Premium 6-guide bundle | $60 | $800–1,500 (200 attendees) | $4.00–7.50/attendee | 200 |

Conference organizers can access downloads via a one-time link or redemption code. No ongoing support required — this is close to fire-and-forget once the system is set up.

## Part 4 — Digital Affiliate and Referral Program Design

### Commission Structure

| Volume Tier | Monthly Sales Referred | Commission Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 1–9 sales/month | 30% |
| Partner | 10–29 sales/month | 33% |
| Premier | 30+ sales/month | 35% |
| Bundle/Event license referral | Per event | 15% flat on license fee |

A flat 30% starting commission is competitive with the homesteading affiliate market (benchmarks: 20–50%, median approximately 30%). Do not go above 35% — above this level, Seedwarden's net margin per sale after platform fees approaches the floor where it is no longer profitable to run an affiliate channel.

---

### Technical Setup (Low-Cost, Phase 2-Ready)

**Option A — Gumroad (Recommended for Phase 2)**  
Gumroad has native affiliate tracking built in. Sellers can generate unique affiliate links per partner, set commission rates, and view real-time dashboard for each affiliate. Fee: 10% Gumroad fee on each sale (higher than Etsy's ~10.5%, but avoids Etsy's listing constraints). Setup time: 2–4 hours to import product catalog and configure affiliate links.

**Option B — Payhip**  
Payhip offers affiliate tracking with a flat 5% fee (lower than Gumroad). Slightly less polished UX, but fully functional for affiliate management. Setup time: similar to Gumroad.

**Option C — ThriveCart (if scaling to Phase 3)**  
ThriveCart is a premium cart/affiliate platform with one-time lifetime pricing (~$495). Worth considering if affiliate revenue exceeds $500/month. Full feature set: upsells, subscriptions, affiliate management, coupon codes.

**Recommendation for Phase 2**: Set up Gumroad as a parallel sales channel. All affiliate links point to Gumroad, not Etsy. This avoids Etsy's constraints on external affiliate systems and gives Seedwarden full control over affiliate tracking data.

**Coupon code alternative**: For affiliates who prefer simplicity, assign a unique coupon code (e.g., BACKYARD20) and track manually in a spreadsheet. Commission is paid monthly via PayPal based on tracked coupon code usage. This works for 1–10 affiliates; beyond that, automated tracking is worth the platform investment.

## Part 5 — Resale Rights Licensing Strategy

### What Seedwarden Should and Should Not License

**License types available (from least to most permissive)**:

| License Type | What Partner Can Do | Risk Level | Recommended? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Affiliate (referral) | Send traffic, earn commission | Minimal | Yes — core strategy |
| Site license | Distribute to defined membership | Low | Yes — for cooperatives |
| Co-brand license | Add their logo/name to cover | Medium | Yes — with attribution requirement |
| Resell Rights (RR) | Resell as-is, no modification | High | No in Phase 2 |
| PLR / White-label | Rebrand fully, claim authorship | Very High | No at any stage |
| Master Resell Rights (MRR) | Resell product + resell the rights | Very High | No at any stage |

**Why PLR/white-label is excluded**: Seedwarden's content quality and regional specificity are the primary brand differentiators. If a partner can rebrand Seedwarden's Seed Saving Field Manual as their own and sell it on Etsy, they become a competitor. At Phase 2 scale, this risk is asymmetric — the revenue from one PLR deal does not compensate for the brand damage of having a white-labeled copy undercutting Seedwarden on Etsy.

**What is viable**: Restricted Resell Rights under a custom contract — the partner can sell Seedwarden's guide through their specific platform (their website, their Shopify store) at their own price, and pay Seedwarden a per-sale royalty OR a one-time license fee. The contract specifies: no listing on Etsy, no further sub-licensing, attribution to Seedwarden required, 1-year term with renewal option.

**One-time license vs. ongoing royalty**:

For Phase 2, **one-time license fees are operationally preferable**. Royalties require tracking, reporting, and enforcement. One-time fees are clean — pay once, done. The pricing premium for one-time vs. royalty:

| Model | Partner Pays | Seedwarden Earns | Operational Burden |
|---|---|---|---|
| Royalty (15% of partner's sales) | Nothing upfront; ongoing % | Recurring revenue | High — tracking, enforcement |
| One-time license (1 year) | $750–3,000 upfront | Lump sum | Low — contract only |
| One-time license (perpetual) | $2,000–7,500 upfront | Lump sum | Low — contract only |

Perpetual one-time licenses for specific guides are appropriate if the partner is a large, established organization (a seed company with 60,000+ catalog customers) and the fee is commensurate. Perpetual licenses should always be limited to a defined use case (e.g., "distribution as a catalog insert to Fedco catalog subscribers") — not open-ended distribution rights.

**Branding requirements on all licenses**:
- Minimum: "Content developed by Seedwarden (seedwarden.etsy.com)" in the footer of every distributed page.
- Preferred: Seedwarden wordmark (small) on the cover alongside partner branding.
- Prohibited on any license: Removal of Seedwarden attribution, sale on competing platforms, sub-licensing to third parties.

## Part 6 — Implementation Roadmap

### Quick Wins: Months 1–3 (Easiest Channels First)

**Priority 1 — Affiliate Program Setup (Weeks 1–2)**  
Invest 2–4 hours to set up a Gumroad or Payhip store with the full Phase 2 catalog. Configure affiliate tracking. Create a simple affiliate onboarding page (1 page explaining the program, commission rate, how to get links). This is the foundation everything else runs on.

**Priority 2 — Affiliate Outreach to Homesteading Media (Weeks 2–4)**  
Identify 20–30 Substack newsletters, YouTube channels, and blogs in the homesteading/seed saving niche. Send personalized Template D emails in batches of 10/week. Target creators with 5K–100K followers for maximum ROI. Expected response rate: 10–20% (2–6 interested partners per batch). Even 3–5 active affiliates generating 5–10 sales/month each yields $150–600/month in incremental Seedwarden revenue at essentially zero marginal cost.

**Priority 3 — Permies.com Engagement (Weeks 3–6)**  
Engage authentically in Permies.com forums before pitching anything. Post genuinely helpful content in seed saving and homesteading threads. After 2–4 weeks of authentic engagement, introduce Seedwarden's products naturally. Explore the Permies digital marketplace for product listing.

**Priority 4 — Bootstrap Farmer and Botanical Interests Affiliate Pitch (Month 2)**  
These companies already operate affiliate programs — they are pre-qualified as affiliate-literate. The pitch is simple: "Would you add a 'recommended digital resources' section to your newsletter and include Seedwarden, with 30% commission for referred sales?" Bootstrap Farmer's average order value exceeds $120; a customer who just spent $120 on greenhouse supplies is primed for a $15 seed saving guide.

---

### Month 3–6: First Formal Partnerships

**Seed Cooperative Licensing (Month 3–4)**  
After affiliate program is running, approach 5–8 regional seed cooperatives and seed library networks with the free trial offer (Template B). Convert 2–3 to paid site licenses at $199–499/year. This generates $400–1,500 in recurring annual revenue from a channel that requires minimal ongoing maintenance.

**Baker Creek / Fedco Content Licensing Discussion (Month 4–6)**  
These are higher-stakes conversations requiring a stronger Etsy track record as social proof. Approach after Seedwarden has at least 25–50 reviews on Etsy — this is the credibility signal that makes a seed company take the partnership seriously. Initial ask: start with a simple affiliate arrangement (no risk for them), with the path to a co-branded content license as the upsell if the affiliate relationship proves valuable.

**Conference / Event Bundle Licensing (Month 4–6)**  
Homesteaders of America Annual Conference typically occurs in fall. Contact their events team by Month 3 to pitch a digital bundle for attendee gift bags. This is a single transaction ($300–1,000) with zero ongoing support required. Other targets: regional permaculture convergences, seed saving conferences (Seed Savers Exchange annual gathering), and county fair homesteading workshops.

## Part 7 — Channel Comparison: Go/No-Go Criteria

| Channel | ROI (Revenue/Time) | Support Overhead | Time to First Revenue | Go/No-Go |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Affiliate — media publishers | High | Very Low | 2–6 weeks | GO — top priority |
| Seed cooperative site license | Medium | Low | 6–12 weeks | GO — Month 2–3 |
| Seed supplier affiliate/licensing | Medium-High | Medium | 8–16 weeks | GO — Month 2–4 |
| Conference/event bundle | Medium | Very Low | 8–20 weeks | GO — opportunistic |
| Garden center QR insert | Low-Medium | Medium | 12–24 weeks | CONDITIONAL — only if retail partnership materializes organically |
| Extension office partnership | Low (cash) / High (credibility) | High | 6–18 months | DEFER to Month 6+ |
| PLR / white-label resale | Short-term Medium | High | — | NO — brand risk |
| Permies.com marketplace listing | Medium | Low | 4–8 weeks | GO — low effort |

**The highest-leverage starting position**: Affiliate outreach to homesteading media + Gumroad setup. This channel has the fastest time to revenue, lowest operational overhead, and scales without additional Seedwarden time investment once affiliates are activated. Every subsequent B2B channel builds on the credibility and revenue baseline that affiliates establish.
