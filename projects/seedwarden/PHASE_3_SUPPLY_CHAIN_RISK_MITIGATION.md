## Failure Scenarios and Responses

### Scenario 1: Mountain Rose Herbs Delays Entire Order

**Symptom**: No shipping confirmation email by June 18 (3 business days after June 13 order).

**Response**:
1. June 18: Call MRH customer service; ask for ship date
2. June 18 (same day if no ship date given): Place Frontier Co-op order for all 11 species simultaneously
3. Frontier 3–5 day ship = June 22–24 arrival — compresses dried herb studio session to Week 1 parallel (June 23–25), not pre-sprint
4. Writing is unaffected. Photography compresses to in-sprint schedule. No upload dates change.

### Scenario 2: Passionflower Aerial Herb OOS at Both MRH and Frontier Co-op

**Symptom**: Both suppliers confirm OOS when checking.

**Response**:
1. iNaturalist CC-BY southeastern US Passiflora incarnata photographs are among the best of any Phase 3 herb — no dried aerial herb specimen is needed for launch quality
2. The guide body describes preparation of dried aerial herb from cultivated plants — this content stands without a dried prop photograph
3. Log CC fallback in PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md; update PHOTO_MANIFEST.csv; note in guide sources section
4. Zero impact on writing, design, or upload dates

### Scenario 3: Local Garden Center Has No Elderberry in Stock

**Symptom**: June 1 phone check confirms no 2-gal elderberry potted shrubs available.

**Response**:
1. Check 2–3 more garden centers within reasonable distance
2. Check Tractor Supply or Rural King (farm stores often carry elderberry)
3. If no local stock by June 8: Wikimedia CC-BY-SA Sambucus nigra berry cluster images are exceptional — several are resolution-suitable for guide hero image and listing slot 3
4. Log CC fallback June 8; no further action needed for sprint launch
5. Order local elderberry shrub when stock becomes available for v1.1 photo upgrade in August

### Scenario 4: Strictly Medicinal Confirms Summer Shutdown for All Transplants

**Symptom**: SM response email confirms no live transplant shipping June–August.

**Response**:
1. Activate Prairie Moon June 1 check for Echinacea purpurea, Passionflower, and Valerian (all three are Prairie Moon species)
2. If Prairie Moon also unavailable: all three have confirmed CC fallback at launch quality — log in attribution log June 8
3. Consider SM seeds for fall growing (available summer per catalog) — cite in guide content as cultivation source
4. Impact: zero on sprint writing, design, or upload dates

### Scenario 5: NativeWildflowers.net Black Cohosh OOS by June 8

**Symptom**: Website shows OOS when placing order.

**Response**:
1. Check current stock before assuming — call or email NWF to confirm
2. If confirmed OOS: iNaturalist CC-BY Actaea racemosa Appalachian range photos are abundant and high quality June–August
3. Log CC fallback immediately; Women's Health guide content (conservation sidebar, cultivation notes) is unchanged

- The only difference versus nominal plan: Etsy listing photos are all CC-sourced rather than a mix of studio and CC
