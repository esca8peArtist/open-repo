# Phase 3 Hiring Timeline and Selection Rubric

**Objective**: Hire 6 contractors (1 photographer, 2 writers, 1 herbalist, 1 coordinator, +1 fallback photographer) by July 1, 2026, to meet production deadlines for five medicinal herb bundles launching July–August.

**Critical constraint**: All contracts must be signed and deposits sent by July 1. This provides zero delay buffer for onboarding before first deliverables due July 5–8.

### Screening Criteria (First Filter)

Run on all applications within 12 hours of posting:

- **Photographer**: Portfolio must show flat-lay or product photography (minimum 5 examples). Must confirm July 1 start availability.
- **Writer #1**: Portfolio must include 2+ published wellness/herbal health articles. Must confirm herbalism knowledge level.
- **Writer #2**: Portfolio must include recipes or preparation guides. Must confirm step-by-step instruction experience.
- **Herbalist**: Must have AHG/NAHA credential or 5+ years professional experience (verified via profile or LinkedIn). Must cite contraindication knowledge.
- **Coordinator**: Must have 2+ years project management or operations experience. Must confirm Etsy and email platform familiarity.

**Quick-list threshold**: Score 7+ per PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md rubric (Portfolio Fit ≥7, Availability ≥7, Budget Fit ≥7).

### Portfolio Audit Checklist

For each accepted candidate, audit 5 recent portfolio pieces against role-specific criteria:

**Photographer** (5 recent projects):
- [ ] At least 3 show botanical or natural product flat-lay (not portraiture, not architecture)
- [ ] Image resolution appears professional (1600px+ width minimum)
- [ ] Color consistency across images in same project (white balance stable)
- [ ] Props & composition match Seedwarden's aesthetic (warm, accessible, not overly stylized)
- [ ] No harsh shadows or low-light issues in botanical close-ups

**Writer #1 (5 recent articles)**:
- [ ] Minimum 3 articles use scientific/Latin names and sources
- [ ] At least 1 article discusses contraindications or safety
- [ ] Tone is educational (not marketing hype, not overly academic)
- [ ] Word count targets: At least 1 article ≥2,500 words (demonstrates capacity for 3,600-word bundles)
- [ ] No unqualified health claims (checked for FTC compliance language)

**Writer #2 (5 recent recipes/guides)**:
- [ ] At least 3 include step-by-step instructions with ingredient lists
- [ ] At least 2 include dosage or serving guidance
- [ ] Language is clear and accessible (non-specialists can follow)
- [ ] Measurements use both metric and US standard (or adaptable)
- [ ] Format allows photo illustration (recipes mention visual elements or plating)

**Herbalist** (professional background):
- [ ] Credential verified via AHG/NAHA directory or professional website
- [ ] Published work or consulting experience listed (at least 3 projects/publications)
- [ ] Demonstrates knowledge of contraindications (LinkedIn or portfolio mentions drug interactions, safety, toxicology)
- [ ] Experience with endangered/at-risk species noted (CITES, conservation language)

**Coordinator** (5 most relevant projects):
- [ ] At least 2 projects show email marketing platform experience (Kit, Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign)
- [ ] At least 1 project shows Etsy store management or similar e-commerce coordination
- [ ] Demonstrated ability to track multiple concurrent timelines (project plans, status reports)
- [ ] Communication examples show professional, diplomatic tone

### Reference Check Template

**Call or email format** (5 minutes, async-friendly):

"Hi [REFERENCE NAME], I'm checking a reference for [CANDIDATE NAME] who has applied for a [ROLE] project with Seedwarden. Could you answer 3 quick questions?

1. **Quality**: How would you rate [CANDIDATE]'s work quality on a scale of 1–10?
2. **Reliability**: Did [CANDIDATE] deliver on time? Any missed deadlines or quality issues?
3. **Communication**: How responsive was [CANDIDATE] to feedback or questions during the project?

Feel free to reply by email if a call is inconvenient. Thanks!"

**Document responses** (1 sentence per reference):
- Example: "Photo project client: 9/10 quality, on-time delivery, responsive to feedback."
- Example: "Editor feedback: 8/10 writing, revised twice as requested, clear communication."

## Selection Rubric — Scoring Framework

Use this rubric to compare candidates within each role and make final hiring decisions.

### Dimensions (4 equally weighted at offer stage; portfolio audit adds final veto power)

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Portfolio Fit** | 35% | 0–10 scale | Does previous work match quality standard? (See role-specific audit checklists above) |
| **Availability** | 30% | 0–10 scale | Can they start July 1? Deliver on July 5–8 deadlines? Zero flexibility. |
| **Budget Fit** | 20% | 0–10 scale | Does proposed rate fall within budget range? (See PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md for ranges) |
| **Communication** | 15% | 0–10 scale | Responsiveness to initial outreach? Professional tone? Asks clarifying questions? |

**Overall Score** = (Portfolio × 0.35) + (Availability × 0.30) + (Budget × 0.20) + (Communication × 0.15)

**Hiring threshold**:
- **Score ≥7.5**: Tier 1 — extend offer immediately
- **Score 6.5–7.4**: Tier 2 — offer if tier 1 rejects within 48 hours
- **Score <6.5**: Deprioritize; contact only if all tier 1–2 candidates reject

### Score Calculation Example

**Photographer candidate A**:
- Portfolio Fit: 8/10 (3 flat-lay projects match Seedwarden aesthetic; professional resolution)
- Availability: 9/10 (confirms July 1 start, flexible for additional shoots)
- Budget Fit: 8/10 (proposed $1,400 fits $1,200–1,800 range)
- Communication: 8/10 (replied within 2 hours, asked clarifying questions about props)

**Overall**: (8 × 0.35) + (9 × 0.30) + (8 × 0.20) + (8 × 0.15) = 2.8 + 2.7 + 1.6 + 1.2 = **8.3/10 — Tier 1 offer**

## Portfolio Fit Scoring Guide

### Photographer Portfolio Fit (0–10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **9–10** | 5+ recent flat-lay/product projects with botanical subjects; professional resolution; consistent color; warm aesthetic matching Seedwarden style |
| **7–8** | 3–4 flat-lay or lifestyle projects; mostly professional quality; minor white balance inconsistencies; aesthetic generally aligned |
| **5–6** | 2–3 product photography projects; acceptable resolution; noticeable lighting issues; unclear botanical aesthetic match |
| **3–4** | Mostly portraiture or events; 1–2 product examples; technical limitations (resolution, color); poor botanical fit |
| **0–2** | No relevant flat-lay or product work; heavily artistic/stylized; cannot assess botanical photography capability |

### Writer Portfolio Fit (0–10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **9–10** | 5+ published wellness/herbal articles; uses scientific names, cites sources, includes contraindications; 2,000+ word pieces; FTC-compliant language |
| **7–8** | 3–4 wellness articles; mostly science-backed; some contraindication coverage; 1,500+ word pieces; tone is educational |
| **5–6** | 2–3 articles on health/wellness; basic sourcing; limited contraindication depth; 1,200+ word capacity; tone accessible but variable |
| **3–4** | 1–2 wellness pieces or blog posts; minimal sourcing; no contraindication discussion; unclear long-form capacity |
| **0–2** | No published wellness/health writing; unclear if capable of evidence-based herbal content |

### Coordinator Portfolio Fit (0–10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **9–10** | 3+ email marketing campaigns (Kit/Mailchimp/ConvertKit); 2+ e-commerce operations projects (Etsy or Shopify); demonstrated multi-project tracking; professional communication examples |
| **7–8** | 2–3 email campaigns; 1–2 e-commerce projects; good project tracking; responsive communication |
| **5–6** | 1–2 email campaigns; 1 e-commerce project; basic tracking capability; acceptable communication |
| **3–4** | Email or e-commerce experience, but not both; limited multi-project examples; variable communication |
| **0–2** | No email or e-commerce experience; unclear if capable of managing concurrent contractor timelines |

## Contingency Triggers

**If tier 1 candidate rejects offer** (within 48-hour window):
1. Log rejection in PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md with reason (if provided)
2. Immediately send tier 2 offer (new 48-hour window starts)
3. If tier 2 also rejects, move to tier 3 (fallback candidates from PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SELECTION_SCORECARD.md)
4. If all tiers reject by Jul 4 12pm: **Activate contingency role** (e.g., solo photographer with extended timeline, or fallback writer)

**If no qualified candidates available by Jul 4 12pm**:
- Photographer: Post to Thumbtack in addition to Upwork; accept tier-3 candidate (score 6.0–6.9)
- Writers: Consider splitting content between remaining writers or reducing scope per bundle (see PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md)
- Herbalist: Defer to week 2–3 review phase if needed; internal review acceptable fallback
- Coordinator: Delay Etsy uploads 2–3 days; absorb tasks internally if unable to hire
