# Track B — Herbalist Audience Email Teaser

## When to Use This Template

This template is for direct email outreach to:
- **AHG (American Herbalists Guild)** chapter email lists or individual members you have a direct relationship with
- **NAMA (North American Mycological Association)** practitioners and foragers
- **Online herbalist community members** you are connected with via email (Discord server members who shared their email, newsletter subscribers, past customers)

This is NOT the Kit automation welcome sequence. This is a one-time personal broadcast or individual outreach sent before or on launch day.

---

## Subject Lines (A/B test — use one)

**Option A**: Free zone-specific guides for every bioregion — preview before launch
**Option B**: I built something for your growing zone (Zones 3–10, free PDF)
**Option C**: What zone are you? Free quick-start cards just launched for all 8 USDA zones

## Email Body

---

Hi [First Name / "Fellow herbalist" if broadcasting to a list],

I wanted to share something with you before the wider launch — a free resource I've been building that I think is directly useful for your practice.

Seedwarden just completed Zone Quick-Start Cards for all 8 USDA hardiness zones (Zones 3–10). Each card is a single-page PDF you can print and post in your growing space or share with students and clients.

**What each card includes:**

- Last frost and first frost dates specific to your zone
- What to plant, direct sow, start indoors, or harvest right now (calibrated to May 2026)
- Storage and preservation timing matched to your zone's harvest window
- Heirloom variety spotlight — regionally tested open-pollinated varieties with days-to-maturity

[**Personalization hook — insert one of the following based on recipient:**]
- *For AHG members*: "Zone [X] covers [their region] — the card includes several species that AHG practitioners in that bioregion work with regularly, including timing windows that account for your specific frost calendar."
- *For NAMA practitioners*: "The foraging timing sections are calibrated to regional seasonality — if you teach wild plant identification, these cards give your students a one-page reference for what's happening in their zone right now."
- *For community members*: "I've been following [their community / forum / Discord] for a while and thought this would land well with people who already think in growing zones."

The cards are completely free — no email required, direct PDF download. I'm sharing them with community members like you first, before the public launch on May 30, because I'd rather they land in the hands of people who will actually use them.

**Download your zone's card here:** [Gist link or seedwarden.co/zone]

If you find it useful and want to share it with your chapter, students, or community, I would genuinely appreciate that — but no obligation. The goal was to make something practical that herbalists and foragers in every bioregion could actually use in the field.

If you have feedback — anything that's wrong, anything that would make the card more useful for your practice — reply to this email. I read everything and the cards can be updated.

Thank you for the work you do.

[Your name]
Seedwarden | seedwarden.co/zone

P.S. If you want to share the full set with your chapter or students, there are 8 separate cards (Zones 3–10) — all free, all at the same link.

## Sending Notes

- **Solo outreach**: For individual contacts, customize the personalization hook fully. Takes 2–3 minutes per contact.
- **List broadcast**: For a small list (under 100), use Kit or Gmail with the base template. Insert the personalization hook that applies to the majority of your list.
- **AHG chapters**: Contact chapter coordinators first (use TRACK_B_HERBALIST_OUTREACH_MATRIX.md Template 3) and ask if they can circulate to members. Do not cold-email individual members without a warm intro.
- **NAMA**: NAMA has regional chapters — contact chapter chairs and offer to share the cards as a free foraging reference for members.
- **Timing**: Send May 28–29 for pre-launch preview framing. Send May 30 for launch-day announcement framing. Do not send the same email twice to the same recipient.
