# Phase 2 Mockup Production Plan

**Purpose**: This document maps every one of the 21 Phase 2 products to its full mockup production requirements. It specifies which mockup types each product needs, which products require physical photography versus stock/AI composites, the production method for each variant, and the complete output file specification.

Mockup slots 1–3 (tablet cover, interior grid, phone frame) are already complete for all 21 products. This plan covers the remaining production work: Etsy slots 4–5 (lifestyle images) and the social media content variants derived from the same shoot.

---

#### 1. Survival Garden Regional Plans — $22

**Cluster**: D (stock sourcing)
**Method**: Stock composite — iStock or Pexels
**Rationale**: Regional landscape and survival garden settings require outdoor rural staging not available in a kitchen/balcony setup. Stock is the correct method.

**Slot 4 — Lifestyle/flat-lay**:
- Source image: iStock search "raised bed garden rows regional" OR "homestead garden planning aerial" — filter for horizontal or square crop
- Composite: Product PDF cover (or printed pages showing a regional map or planting plan) overlaid in the scene as though spread on a garden planning table outdoors
- Canva workflow: Section 4 of CANVA_EXECUTION_PLAYBOOK.md (stock compositing)
- Key element: the image should show the "planning" moment — map or layout pages in context, not just a garden photo without the product

**Slot 5 — In-use/contextual**:
- Source image: iStock search "hands over garden layout" OR "garden planning table outdoor"
- Composite: guide pages visible, hands interacting
- Alternative if compositing looks unconvincing: a clean product cover art overlay in the lower-right corner of the garden image, with "Digital Download" label in Seedwarden green — simpler but effective

**File outputs**: `survival-garden-regional-plans-slot4.jpg`, `survival-garden-regional-plans-slot5.jpg`
**Social variants**: Crop slot 4 to 1080×1350px for Instagram; export 1000×1500px variant for Pinterest pin
**Production time estimate**: 2–3 hours (stock search + compositing + export)
**Physical photography needed**: No

#### 2. Hunting, Fishing and Trapping Manual — $20

**Cluster**: D (stock sourcing — iStock priority)
**Method**: Stock composite — iStock
**Rationale**: Hunting and trapping lifestyle content is thin on free stock sites. iStock has commercial-quality images for this niche. This product gets 2 iStock credits before any others.

**Slot 4 — Lifestyle/flat-lay**:
- Source image: iStock search "fly fishing stream rustic" OR "outdoor field guide jacket pocket" — rustic and functional, not aspirational
- Composite: guide cover overlaid on a rock or pack surface in the outdoor scene, or visible in an open jacket pocket
- Do not use: hunting trophy images, anything that reads as competitive sport rather than subsistence/self-sufficiency

**Slot 5 — In-use/contextual**:
- Source image: iStock search "fishing guide hands open water" OR "outdoor identification guide nature"
- The guide should appear to be actively consulted — not posed

**File outputs**: `hunting-fishing-trapping-manual-slot4.jpg`, `hunting-fishing-trapping-manual-slot5.jpg`
**Production time estimate**: 2.5 hours (iStock search + purchase + compositing)
**Physical photography needed**: No

#### 5. Harvest Preservation Field Manual — $16

**Cluster**: C (physical photography — kitchen setting)
**Method**: Physical photography
**Rationale**: A kitchen with mason jars and canning equipment is a completely stageable physical setting. This is one of the most visually strong opportunities for authentic product photography.

**Slot 4 — Lifestyle/flat-lay**:
- Surface: kitchen counter or wooden kitchen table
- Props: 3–4 mason jars (one with visible contents — fermented vegetables, canned tomatoes, or dried herbs), a canning funnel or jar lifter, the guide pages spread flat
- Overhead shot, soft window light from the left
- Key element: the jars should look used — real contents visible, ring slightly worn, not commercial-label jarred goods

**Slot 5 — In-use/contextual**:
- 45-degree angle: one hand holding a mason jar, guide open in background on the counter
- OR: flat-lay with hands visible pouring or stirring something near the guide

**Physical shoot requirements**: Kitchen counter, 3–4 mason jars, canning equipment (funnel, tongs, or lifter), guide pages printed or tablet showing PDF. Estimated setup time: 15 minutes. Shoot time: 25 minutes.

**File outputs**: `harvest-preservation-field-manual-slot4.jpg`, `harvest-preservation-field-manual-slot5.jpg`
**Production time estimate**: 45 minutes physical shoot + 30 minutes editing
**Physical photography needed**: Yes — Cluster C shoot session

## AI-Generated Composite Option

For products where stock compositing consistently looks unconvincing — particularly for Cluster D products where the guide overlay in a rural setting is hard to make realistic — an AI image generation approach is a viable alternative.

**Appropriate use cases**: Generating a scene of a person reading a guide in a field, or a guide spread on a camp table outdoors, where the specific product cover can be placed in post-compositing. This avoids the need to make a real stock image look as though the product is in it.

**Tools**: Midjourney, DALL-E 3, or Adobe Firefly. For any tool that generates a plausible setting, the product cover is then composited in using Canva or Photoshop. The generated background is not the product; the composited product cover is.

**Licensing**: AI-generated images used as backgrounds do not carry license restrictions (no photographer attribution needed). Verify the specific tool's terms of service regarding commercial use before relying on this approach.

**When not to use**: Etsy's policies prohibit AI-generated images from being the primary product image. Slots 1–3 (the mockup images) are fine; for slots 4–5, if an AI-generated image is the sole background, label it as "digitally rendered" or ensure the composited physical product cover is clearly the primary visual element.

**Recommended use**: Only for Cluster D products where no suitable stock image exists after exhausting iStock credits. Not a first-choice method.
