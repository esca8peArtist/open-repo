## Risk Assessment

### Ginseng Seeds — CRITICAL Risk

**Problem**: American Ginseng seed is a fall crop. Most vendors sell out of stratified spring stock by March–April. Strictly Medicinal does not stock spring ginseng seed reliably.

**Mitigation**:
- Check Wisconsin Grown Ginseng LLC (wisconsingrownginseng.com) first on May 10 — they maintain stratified spring stock at $18–28 per 50-seed packet
- Also check Dairyland Ginseng (dairylandginseng.com) — larger quantities (1/4 lb minimum) but spring stratified stock available
- **If both are out of stock**: the dried ginseng root from Mountain Rose Herbs ($14–22) is the stronger identification prop. Ginseng root is more visually distinctive than ginseng seeds for guide photography. Skip seeds; use root.

### Ramps — Medium Risk

**Problem**: Experimental Farm Network ramps seeds are sold out as of early May 2026; restock is unpredictable.

**Mitigation**:
- Ramps fresh leaves from farmers market or specialty grocery are the highest-value ramps visual prop — readers recognize leaves immediately. Purchase May 18–19 (within 24–48 hours of the May 20 shoot).
- If Prairie Moon bulblets are unavailable, search Etsy for "ramps bulbs Appalachian" or "wild leek bulbs" — small Appalachian native plant growers list on Etsy. Verify "cultivated, not wild-harvested" before ordering.
- **Last resort**: iNaturalist CC-BY spring archive photos (2020–2025) cover ramps leaf identification fully. No physical props required for guide production.

### All-Vendor Miss

**Scenario**: Primary and secondary vendors all miss delivery by May 18.

**Response**:
- May 17: Order dried specimens from Mountain Rose Herbs on 2-day Priority ship (expected arrival May 19); purchase native plants at local garden center same-day if available in the Appalachian region
- May 19: Purchase fresh ramp leaves at any specialty grocery or co-op; purchase additional dried roots retail
- Compressed shoot: May 21–23 using retail dried specimens + iNaturalist CC-BY for habitat shots
- **No launch date impact** — dried specimens are photographically adequate for all guide-interior identification sections

## Inbound Logistics and Handling

### Packaging Inspection (on Arrival)

For each package received:
1. Photograph the sealed package and note arrival date in WORKLOG.md
2. Open and inspect: check for damage, mold, dried-out roots, dead live plants
3. If damaged: photograph and contact vendor immediately; most medicinal herb suppliers have replacement policies
4. Log in WORKLOG.md: species, vendor, order date, arrival date, condition

### Storage by Material Type

| Material | Storage Method | Max Hold Before Shoot |
|---|---|---|
| Dried roots (ginseng, goldenseal) | Room temperature, paper bag or kraft envelope; no refrigeration; no plastic sealing | Indefinite — dried root is stable |
| Live potted plants (Black Cohosh) | East- or south-facing window; water lightly; acclimatize 48 hours before shooting | 2–3 weeks if cared for properly |
| Fresh ramp leaves | Refrigerator wrapped in damp paper towel; loosely bagged | 48 hours maximum |
| Seeds (any species) | Original packaging; room temperature; dry location | Weeks — do not open until shoot day |
| Ramps bulblets | Refrigerator (40–45°F) or cool, dark location; moist but not wet | 1 week |

### Fragility Notes

- **Bloodroot rhizome** (if ordered): the orange-red color oxidizes after cutting; photograph the cut cross-section within 30 minutes of cutting. Do not pre-cut — cut on shoot day.
- **Fresh ramp leaves**: they wilt within hours at room temperature. Refrigerate until the moment of use; mist lightly with water spray bottle immediately before photographing.
- **Goldenseal dried root**: naturally bright yellow, but color deepens when lightly moistened. A light mist from a spray bottle before photographing brings out the yellow for ID shots.
