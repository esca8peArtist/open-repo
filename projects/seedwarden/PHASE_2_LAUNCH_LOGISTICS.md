### 3.4 Lighting and Camera Setup

**Window setup**: Kitchen table or work surface positioned 3–5 feet from largest east- or south-facing window. Shoot between 9am–12pm for soft directional light. White foam-core board (24×36 in) on the shadow side as fill reflector.

**Camera settings**: White balance — Daylight or Cloudy preset (not Auto). Self-timer — 3 seconds. If using smartphone: lock exposure and white balance before each shot. iPhone: tap-hold to lock AE/AF; Android: use Pro mode to lock WB at 5500K.

**Camera minimum spec**: iPhone 12 or Android equivalent (12MP+). Macro clip lens (Moment or Xenvo) recommended for root and seed close-ups. External macro lens produces noticeably better detail than digital zoom.

**Editing preset (apply consistently to all 30 frames)**:
- Exposure: +0.2 to +0.5
- Contrast: +20
- Highlights: -30
- Shadows: +20
- White balance: 5500–6000K
- Green saturation: +12
- Blue saturation: -10
- Crop to 1:1 square before export

## Section 6: Risk Mitigations

### Risk 1: Vendor Delays — Specimens Arrive After May 20

**Trigger**: Strictly Medicinal Seeds or Prairie Moon Nursery order has not shipped by May 16.

**Mitigation by species**:
- **Black Cohosh live plant delayed**: Proceed with iNaturalist CC-BY archive for habit and leaf shots. The guide does not require the live plant if iNaturalist supply is adequate. No schedule impact.
- **Ramps bulblets delayed**: Fresh leaves from the farmers market (purchased May 18–19) are a superior photography prop. No schedule impact.
- **Mountain Rose Herbs dried roots delayed**: This is the highest-risk single-vendor miss. Contact Mountain Rose Herbs directly by May 14 if no shipping confirmation. If not shipped by May 14, cancel and purchase goldenseal and ginseng dried roots at a local health food store (Whole Foods, co-op, herb shop — goldenseal dried root is commonly stocked in specialty retail). Zero schedule impact if local fallback is executed within 24 hours.
- **All vendors miss**: Execute Mountain Rose Herbs 2-day Priority ship emergency order on May 19; purchase any available native plants at local garden center May 19; compressed shoot May 21–23 using retail dried specimens + iNaturalist CC-BY for habitat shots. No launch date impact.

### Risk 2: Location Access Delays

**Trigger**: Asheville Botanical Garden permit is not approved and no private forest farm host is confirmed by May 18.

**Mitigation**: Activate indoor window studio immediately upon this confirmation. The field shoot is an upgrade, not a production dependency. All guide-interior photography (root props, leaf detail, seeds) is produceable indoors at equivalent or better quality. Habitat context photography comes from iNaturalist CC-BY archive. Zero launch date impact.

**Recovery**: If the field session is missed entirely, use iNaturalist CC-BY observations for all habitat context shots. Filter by: taxon name + license=CC-BY + quality_grade=research + photos=true. Taxon IDs are documented in ENDANGERED_SPECIES_PHOTO_PIPELINE.md. Log all CC-BY attributions in WORKLOG.md before placing any photo in a guide.

### Risk 3: Photo Quality or Reshoot Requirement

**Trigger**: After reviewing May 14–18 lifestyle shoot photos, multiple shots are out of focus, underexposed, or fail the scale bar visibility check.

**Reshoot timeline**: May 19 is the float day for a lifestyle shoot reshoot. If a full-cluster reshoot is needed, May 19 can absorb it without impacting the May 20 Appalachian session start. A partial reshoot (5–8 specific shots) can be completed in under 2 hours.

**Mitigation**: Before the shoot, run one test frame and review at 100% zoom to confirm focus and exposure. Do not begin the full Cluster A session without a confirmed test frame. This takes 5 minutes and catches setup errors before 16 shots are taken incorrectly.

**Processing quality gate**: Discard any frame where focus fails on the primary subject, exposure requires more than +1 stop recovery, or the scale bar is cropped out of a frame that requires it. Do not publish noisy, over-processed frames — discard and reshoot.

**Mitigation**: If Canva has account-level issues, Figma is the alternative — it has a free tier and a native plant/botanical template library.
