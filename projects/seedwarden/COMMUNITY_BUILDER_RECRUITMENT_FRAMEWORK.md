# Community Builder Recruitment Framework
## Phase 3 Scaling — July–August 2026

**Prepared**: May 31, 2026
**Context**: Phase 1 (Track B) launches June 1, 2026 with solo operation. This framework activates after a 30-day traction assessment (earliest: July 1, 2026). Phase 3 minimum viable team: 2 content creators + 1 practice builder + 1 mentor.

---

## Section 1: Herbalist Ecosystem Segmentation and Recruiter Profiles

### 1.1 Why Three Distinct Profiles

The herbalist ecosystem is not one audience — it is three overlapping professional tiers with different motivations, credibility anchors, and working styles. A single recruitment message fails all three. The compensation model that works for a TikTok-native content creator will insult a 30-year clinical practitioner. The workload that energizes a student mentor will overwhelm a licensed ND with a full patient caseload.

The HERBALIST_NETWORK_ECOSYSTEM_MAPPING.md identifies 25 top voices and 5 audience segments. Mapping those segments onto builder roles reveals three functional profiles that Seedwarden Phase 3 requires, in this priority order:

1. **Content Creator** — produces Zone guides, short video, and case studies; holds and grows an audience
2. **Practice Builder** — runs or participates in herbal programs; recruits practitioners; expands Zone scope
3. **Mentor** — provides quality oversight, field credibility, and community stewardship

Each profile is defined in full below, including skills needed, compensation expectations, realistic workload, and track record indicators.

---

### 1.2 Profile A: Content Creator

**Role in Phase 3**: Generate Zone-specific guides (Zones 6–12), short-form educational videos (Instagram Reels, TikTok), and case studies from personal practice or research. The Content Creator is Seedwarden's primary audience growth engine in Phase 3.

**Target persona**: A practicing or student herbalist with an established Instagram or TikTok following (>10K followers). Existing media production comfort. History of affiliate content or educational posts. Comfortable on camera but does not need to be a broadcaster. The ecosystem mapping identified John Gallagher (LearningHerbs, 269K IG) and Jade Alicandro (Milk and Honey Herbs, 13K IG, high engagement) as archetypes at opposite ends of the audience scale — Phase 3 needs builders closer to Alicandro's profile (10K–30K, high engagement, clinical voice) rather than macro influencers who command $500–$2,000 per post.

**Credential requirement**: No formal credential required, but active practice (3+ years) or enrollment in a recognized program (Herbal Academy, Chestnut School, CSCH). Track record indicators:

- Instagram or TikTok account >10K followers with herbalism content primary
- At least 6 months of consistent posting history
- Prior affiliate or sponsored content experience (even a single partnership counts)
- Engagement rate >3% (comments + shares relative to follower count — the ceiling on purchased audiences)
- At least one piece of educational content with documented reach (screenshot of views or impressions shared in application)

**Deliverables per month**:
- 1–2 Zone guide contributions (structured template provided; see Onboarding Section)
- 4–8 short-form videos cross-promoting Zone guides (Reels, TikToks, or Pinterest video pins)
- Monthly progress report: guide views, video reach, affiliate conversions attributed

**Estimated workload**: 8–15 hours/week. Guide research and writing (4–6 hr/guide), video scripting and filming (1–2 hr/video), community engagement (2–3 hr/week). This is a material commitment — not a side task. The compensation structure must reflect that.

**Compensation expectation benchmark**:
- At 10K–30K followers with moderate engagement, comparable freelance herbalism content work pays $200–$600/month
- Affiliate-only arrangements without guaranteed base are rejected by 70%+ of working content creators at this tier
- Seedwarden pre-revenue state means a pure revenue-share offer carries no floor; this is the primary recruitment friction

**Compensation model (Seedwarden recommendation)**: $150 guaranteed monthly base + 25% revenue share on guide sales attributed to their Zone(s) via tracking link. Base covers minimum viable time cost; revenue share creates upside alignment. At launch (zero attribution), $150/month is the floor. At $1,200/month attributed guide revenue, total compensation reaches $450/month — well above the $200 minimum viable threshold.

**Escalation path**: If 3-month attributed revenue exceeds $2,000/month consistently, renegotiate to $0 base + 35% revenue share, which yields $700+/month and removes the fixed cost from Seedwarden's P&L.

### 1.3 Profile B: Practice Builder

**Role in Phase 3**: Expand Seedwarden's practitioner network — recruit other practitioners to use guides in their clinical practice, run referral programs within professional associations (AHG, AANP chapters), and assist in building Zone-specific practitioner directories. The Practice Builder is a business development function inside a clinical professional's existing network.

**Target persona**: A licensed or credentialed herbalist (RH preferred, or ND/NP with active herbal practice), educator running an herbal program, or community herbalism school instructor. Operating within an active professional network (AHG chapter membership, herbal school faculty role, or state-level ND association activity). Tieraona Low Dog (MD, RH) represents the credential ceiling; a Colorado School of Clinical Herbalism instructor or an AHG chapter coordinator represents the realistic mid-tier recruit.

**Credential requirement**: AHG membership (Registered Herbalist preferred; active member acceptable) OR current enrollment/faculty status at a recognized clinical herbalism program OR active ND/NP license in a state with full ND scope. This credential requirement is non-negotiable — the Practice Builder's value is their professional network credibility.

**Deliverables per month**:
- 2–5 new practitioner referrals to Seedwarden (tracked via unique referral code or landing page)
- 1 practitioner-network touchpoint per month: newsletter feature, workshop mention, or AHG chapter outreach on Seedwarden's behalf
- Quarterly: participation in a 30-minute call to share network intelligence (which bundles practitioners are requesting, what practitioner objections surface)

**Estimated workload**: 3–6 hours/week. Lower production burden than the Content Creator but requires active professional relationship maintenance. The Practice Builder is making personal introductions, which means their own credibility is on the line. This creates a higher quality bar but lower volume.

**Compensation expectation benchmark**:
- Clinical practitioners with AHG RH credential charge $75–$150/hour for consulting work
- $200–$500/month for 3–6 hours/week work is below their consulting rate but justified as a part-time community role they do alongside existing practice
- Referral-only compensation without a base retainer is functionally the same as an affiliate program — it does not constitute recruitment

**Compensation model (Seedwarden recommendation)**: $250 monthly retainer + $15 per qualified practitioner referral who makes a first purchase. "Qualified" = the referred practitioner completes at least one transaction (not just an email list signup). At 5 referrals/month and average transaction $28, total monthly compensation: $325. At 10 referrals: $400. Retainer floor covers relationship maintenance even in low-referral months.

**Track record indicators**:
- Active AHG or AANP chapter membership verifiable via directory
- History of workshop, school, or event-based teaching (even 2–3 documented events)
- Prior affiliate or referral relationship with another herbal brand (Mountain Rose Herbs, Herbal Academy, LearningHerbs) is a strong positive signal
- LinkedIn or professional bio showing multi-year professional network tenure

### 1.4 Profile C: Mentor

**Role in Phase 3**: Quality gatekeeper. Reviews Zone guide submissions from Content Creators for botanical nomenclature accuracy, evidence-tier correctness, contraindication completeness, and FTC language compliance before publication. Provides community stewardship — handles escalating conflicts, sets content tone standards, and acts as the named expert whose credibility backs the Seedwarden brand.

**Target persona**: An experienced clinical herbalist with 20+ years of practice. David Winston (54 years clinical practice, RH AHG, author of seven books) is the aspirational example. More realistically, a senior AHG member with 20–30 years practice experience, recognized within at least one regional herbalism community. Does not need a large social audience — the Mentor role is editorial and relational, not media-facing.

**Credential requirement**: AHG Registered Herbalist (mandatory) with a minimum 20 years of active clinical or teaching practice. Peer-reviewed publication history is a strong plus but not required. Named in at least one published herbal text, school curriculum, or recognized professional publication. The named reviewer on the Seedwarden website and product listings is this person — their credibility is public-facing, so vetting must be thorough.

**Deliverables per month**:
- 2–4 guide quality reviews (30–60 minutes per review): check Latin nomenclature with species authority, evidence-tier citations, contraindication tables, FTC language, regional accuracy
- 1 brief written quality note per reviewed guide (3–5 bullet points): what passed, what was corrected, what to flag for the Content Creator
- Available for 1 escalation call/month (15 minutes) if a community conflict or quality dispute arises

**Estimated workload**: 4–8 hours/month. The Mentor role is designed to be a light-touch advisory engagement compatible with an active clinical practice schedule.

**Compensation expectation benchmark**:
- Senior herbalists who consult or peer-review for academic publications typically receive honoraria of $100–$300 per review
- A named advisory board role for a wellness brand at this scale might pay $200–$500/month
- The Mentor is not a full-time employee and should not be recruited with full-time expectations

**Compensation model (Seedwarden recommendation)**: $75 per completed guide review, paid monthly. At 2 reviews/month: $150. At 4 reviews/month: $300. This is an honorarium structure — per-deliverable rather than a retainer. This respects the Mentor's time and aligns payment with actual output. Additionally: named credit on every reviewed guide listing ("Reviewed by [Name], RH (AHG), [specialty], [years] years clinical practice") is non-financial but professionally meaningful compensation that every experienced herbalist values.

**Track record indicators**:
- AHG Registered Herbalist designation verifiable in AHG directory
- 20+ years documented clinical or teaching practice (bio, curriculum vitae, or school affiliation)
- Prior peer review, curriculum development, or publication credit in herbalism
- Availability for monthly deliverables confirmed in writing before offer acceptance
- No active commercial conflicts that would create bias in guide reviews (e.g., does not own a competing guide product)

## Section 2: Compensation Analysis and Model Selection

### 2.1 Five Platform Model Analysis

The following five platforms represent the range of compensation structures active in the herbalism and adjacent creator economy markets. Each model has been evaluated for take rate (platform cut from revenue), minimum viable earnings for a part-time contributor, and scalability to 3–5 builders.

---

**Model 1: Patreon (Subscription Revenue Share)**

Patreon charges 10% platform fee (flat rate for creators joining after August 4, 2025) plus approximately 3% payment processing, for a total creator loss of 12–13% per dollar of subscriber revenue. A creator with 200 patrons at $5/month gross earns $1,000/month before fees, netting approximately $870.

- **Take rate**: 12–13% total (platform + payment)
- **Minimum viable earnings per builder**: Requires 40–100 patrons at $5/month to reach $200–$500/month net — viable only for Content Creators with an existing audience, not a launch condition
- **Scalability to 3–5 builders**: Each builder runs their own Patreon; Seedwarden has no direct cost but also no control over pricing or messaging. Revenue is unpredictable from Seedwarden's perspective.
- **Fit for Seedwarden**: Low. Patreon requires the builder to have an established paying subscriber base before earnings materialize. In a pre-launch environment, no builder will be willing to build a Patreon from zero on Seedwarden's behalf. Patreon is a solo creator monetization tool, not a team compensation structure.

---

**Model 2: Herbal Academy Affiliate + Content Revenue Model**

Herbal Academy pays affiliates 15% commission on referred course sales, with a 21-day cookie window. A typical Herbal Academy course sells for $47–$397. An affiliate who refers 10 course purchases at $150 average in a month earns $225 in affiliate commissions.

Herbal Academy content creators (instructors, not affiliates) receive a negotiated flat fee or revenue share per course published — exact terms are not publicly disclosed, but comparable platforms (Udemy, Teachable) pay instructors 25–97% depending on whether the sale was organically generated by the instructor's own audience (97% at Udemy via instructor link) or generated by platform marketing (37% at Udemy). The Herbal Academy model sits closer to the instructor-owns-audience end of this range.

- **Take rate**: 85% platform / 15% affiliate for referrals; estimated 40–60% platform / 40–60% instructor for original content
- **Minimum viable earnings per builder**: At 15% commission, reaching $300/month requires $2,000/month in referred course sales — achievable only for established content creators with >20K followers actively recommending courses
- **Scalability to 3–5 builders**: Herbal Academy's model scales for them, not for Seedwarden. If Seedwarden builders all become Herbal Academy affiliates, Herbal Academy gets the audience growth and Seedwarden earns nothing. This is a competitor model, not a template.
- **Fit for Seedwarden**: Moderate as a reference point for affiliate commission rates in the herbalism space (15% is the market standard). Seedwarden should benchmark its own affiliate rate at 20–25% to be above market.

---

**Model 3: Skillshare Creator (Royalty Pool / Watch-Time Model)**

Skillshare allocates approximately 20–30% of total platform subscription revenue to a creator royalty pool, distributed proportionally by minutes watched per creator per month. In 2025, this yields $0.05–$0.10 per minute watched. A creator with 5,000 minutes watched per month earns $250–$500 before taxes.

- **Take rate**: 70–80% platform (pool allocation); effective creator share is audience-dependent
- **Minimum viable earnings per builder**: 2,000–5,000 monthly watch minutes needed to reach $100–$250 — requires a course library and sustained audience referral. New courses take 3–6 months to accumulate watch minutes.
- **Scalability to 3–5 builders**: Each builder runs independently on Skillshare; Seedwarden cannot manage this model as a team compensation structure.
- **Fit for Seedwarden**: Low for direct replication. Relevant as a model for understanding that time-based compensation ($0.05–$0.10/minute) is common in creator economies. Seedwarden can apply analogous logic: pay Content Creators per guide submitted (a per-deliverable rate) rather than per view, which removes audience-dependency from the compensation equation.

---

**Model 4: Teachable / Kajabi (Creator Keeps Revenue — Flat Fee Platform)**

Both Teachable and Kajabi charge the course creator a platform fee (0–7.5% depending on plan tier) rather than taking a percentage of revenue. The creator owns the pricing, the audience relationship, and the revenue. An instructor on Teachable Pro selling a $50 course keeps approximately $47–$50 per sale (0% transaction fee on Pro plan; payment processing ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction).

- **Take rate**: 0–7.5% platform fee (plus payment processing ~3%)
- **Minimum viable earnings per builder**: There is no platform-mandated minimum — the creator sets their price. A Teachable instructor selling 10 guides at $20 earns $185 after fees. Teachable's model is fully self-directed; earnings depend entirely on the creator's own sales volume.
- **Scalability to 3–5 builders**: Each builder operates a separate Teachable/Kajabi account; no unified team management. Not structured for a distributed team.
- **Fit for Seedwarden**: Low for team management, but highly relevant as a benchmark for what creators in the space expect when they run their own platform. Content creators with established Teachable/Kajabi operations will see Seedwarden's revenue-share offer through the lens of "what would I give up from my own platform?" That number is near 100% of revenue. Seedwarden's 25% revenue-share model is less economically favorable than a creator's own platform — the compensating factor must be Seedwarden's existing audience and distribution reach.

---

**Model 5: Direct Hybrid Retainer + Performance Bonus (Substack, Newsletter Creators)**

The newsletter creator model (Substack, Ghost, Kit/ConvertKit Commerce) combines a base subscription income with performance bonuses from affiliate commissions or sponsored posts. Established Substack writers with 2,000–5,000 free subscribers and a 5–10% paid conversion rate earn $1,000–$5,000/month. Mid-tier newsletter writers (500–2,000 subscribers) earn $200–$800/month.

The hybrid structure used by these creators: base revenue from paid subscribers (recurring) + variable from affiliate placements + occasional flat-fee sponsorships. This is the closest existing model to what Seedwarden should offer its builders.

- **Take rate**: Substack takes 10%; Ghost takes $9–$25/month flat fee. Payment processing adds ~3%.
- **Minimum viable earnings per builder**: With a base of 200 paid subscribers at $5/month, a creator earns $900/month gross before platform and processing fees — this is the newsletter equivalent of a living wage side income.
- **Scalability to 3–5 builders**: Seedwarden can run a shared newsletter infrastructure (Kit/ConvertKit) with each builder managing their Zone-specific list segment. This scales operationally.
- **Fit for Seedwarden**: High — the hybrid retainer + performance model maps directly onto what Seedwarden needs. Seedwarden is not asking builders to build their own subscriber base from zero; it is offering them access to Seedwarden's existing and growing audience in exchange for content production. The retainer covers time cost; the performance component creates upside.

---

### 2.2 Compensation Model Recommendation for Seedwarden

**Seedwarden's constraint**: Pre-launch revenue is unknown. No existing user base size confirmed. Guaranteeing large fixed compensation without revenue to cover it creates a cash-flow risk.

**The core tension**: Builders who accept pure revenue share are speculators, not partners. Experienced practitioners do not speculate on untested platforms. A meaningful fixed base is required to attract quality builders — but the base must be low enough to be cash-flow-safe.

**Recommendation: Tiered Hybrid Model**

| Tier | Trigger | Base | Performance | Max expected monthly |
|------|---------|------|-------------|---------------------|
| Tier 1 (Launch) | Phase 3 months 1–3 | Content Creator: $150 / Practice Builder: $250 / Mentor: $75/review | Rev share (25% CC, $15/referral PB) | $300–$450 |
| Tier 2 (Growth) | 90 days post-onboarding, if attributed revenue > $1,000/mo | Content Creator: $0 base + 35% / Practice Builder: $250 + $20/referral / Mentor: $100/review | Higher rev share | $450–$700 |
| Tier 3 (Scale) | 6 months, if attributed revenue > $3,000/mo | Content Creator: negotiated flat fee per guide | Performance-dominant | $700+ |

**Pre-revenue state mitigation**: In months where total Seedwarden guide revenue is zero (impossible after any launch traction but theoretically possible in week 1), the fixed base payments still apply. To fund these: Tier 1 commitments total $550–$700/month maximum (1 CC + 1 PB + 1 Mentor at 4 reviews). This is the full fixed cost of Phase 3 team operations before any revenue share kicks in. At $28 average guide price and a 5-sale-per-day run rate (achievable given Etsy traffic from Phase 1–2 SEO foundation), monthly gross is $4,200 — more than adequate to cover Tier 1 base commitments.

**Pure revenue share rejection**: Rejected. No experienced practitioner will accept pure revenue share from a pre-scale brand with unverifiable attribution. Pure revenue share is appropriate at Tier 3 scale, not at launch.

**Hourly + performance rejection**: Rejected. Hourly billing creates an incentive to work slowly and requires time-tracking infrastructure Seedwarden does not have. The per-deliverable model (per guide, per review, per referral) is superior for quality alignment.

## Section 4: Onboarding Checklist and Friction Reduction

**Target**: Full onboarding setup in under 30 minutes per builder. Painful onboarding is the single largest post-recruitment failure mode in distributed content teams.

### 4.1 Pre-Onboarding (Before Builder Accepts Offer)

- [ ] Seedwarden sends builder a Zone guide template file (Google Docs link, view-only preview) during negotiation phase — eliminates "what exactly am I committing to?" objection before offer acceptance
- [ ] One sample completed Zone guide provided as quality benchmark — builder evaluates format fit before any obligation
- [ ] Compensation calculation worked example provided in plain text: "If your Zone generates $800/month, here is your exact payment" — no ambiguity

### 4.2 Onboarding Session (Day 1 — 20–30 minutes total)

| Step | Owner | Time | Notes |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| 1. Platform account creation | Seedwarden creates; builder receives invite link | 3 min | Builder does not self-register; Seedwarden controls account permissions. Content Creators get author + publish rights. Practice Builders get view-only access. Mentors get review-queue access only. |
| 2. Payment method setup | Builder completes Stripe or PayPal onboarding link | 5–8 min | Seedwarden sends pre-filled onboarding link. Stripe preferred (instant transfer, lower fee than PayPal). Direct bank transfer acceptable for Mentors (per-review honorarium). |
| 3. Brand guidelines document | Builder receives 1-page PDF | 2 min | Logo use policy, approved terminology (no "heals," "cures," "treats"), evidence-tier language examples, species nomenclature standard (specify author authority). Read time: 5 minutes. |
| 4. Zone assignment | Seedwarden emails builder their Zone(s) | 1 min | Content Creator receives one Zone assignment at onboarding; additional Zones unlocked at 3-month review. Practice Builder assigned to their existing geographic professional network (no new Zone imposed). Mentor assigned no Zone — reviews all submissions. |
| 5. Content calendar access | Seedwarden shares Google Calendar link | 1 min | Shows upcoming guide publication windows and any event tie-ins (AHG Symposium, Herbstalk) that should inform guide timing. Builder adds their own submission dates. |
| 6. Communication channel | Seedwarden adds builder to designated channel (email thread or Discord DM — no Slack; reduces app sprawl) | 1 min | One channel only. Seedwarden sends monthly content calendar on the 1st. Builder sends submission on agreed date. |
| 7. QA and publication process | Builder reads 1-page workflow summary | 3 min | Content Creator submits guide → Mentor reviews within 5 business days → Seedwarden publishes or returns with notes → Content Creator has 3 business days for revisions → Seedwarden publishes. |
| 8. Escalation path | Provided in onboarding document | 1 min | Platform issue: email [Seedwarden contact]. Payment delay: email [Seedwarden contact] by the 5th of the following month if not received by the 1st. Content conflict or quality dispute: escalate to Mentor, then Seedwarden. |

**Total onboarding time**: 17–25 minutes. Under 30-minute target is achievable if the Stripe onboarding link is pre-populated and the Zone guide template is sent during negotiation (not at onboarding).

### 4.3 Ongoing Friction Reduction

- **Payment cadence**: Monthly, on the 1st, for the prior calendar month. No "waiting for a check" — automated Stripe payout on the 1st. Base payments process automatically; revenue share payments require one monthly calculation (Seedwarden runs this, not the builder).
- **Guide submission format**: Google Docs only. No file attachments, no PDFs, no Word docs. Builder submits a link to a shared Docs file in their designated template folder. Seedwarden comments directly in the document. No email attachment chains.
- **Revision limit**: Two rounds maximum per guide. If a guide fails two rounds of Mentor review, Seedwarden and the builder discuss whether the guide topic is a scope mismatch or whether the builder needs additional support before submitting in that category.
- **API and CMS access**: Not provided to builders in Phase 3. Content Creators write in Google Docs (converted to Seedwarden format by Seedwarden). Practice Builders have no CMS access. Mentors access a read-only review queue. This limits risk from account access issues and simplifies the permission model significantly.

### 5.4 Tracking Dashboard

Monthly tracking requires approximately 11 minutes:
- 3 minutes: Pull Etsy sales report, filter by tracking link/referral code per builder
- 3 minutes: Review builder monthly reports (screenshot submissions via email or Discord DM)
- 3 minutes: Log KPIs in master tracking spreadsheet (one tab per builder, 5 rows per KPI)
- 2 minutes: Flag any red-flag conditions for follow-up

Quarterly synthesis (30 minutes):
- Which builders are above target across 3+ KPIs? Candidates for Tier 2 upgrade.
- Which builders are below target on 2+ KPIs? Schedule a support call before considering termination.
- Which KPIs are systematically below target across all builders? Likely a Seedwarden process problem, not a builder problem (e.g., if attributed sales are low for all creators, the tracking link setup may be broken).

## Section 6: Recruitment Timeline and Success Criteria

### 6.1 Phase Gate: When to Recruit

**Do not recruit before**: 30 days of Phase 1 Track B traction data. Minimum readable signal: 100 Etsy views and 3+ sales across any guide category. Recruiting builders before any data exists means compensating people to build on a foundation you cannot validate.

**Recruitment window opens**: July 1, 2026 (30 days post-June 1 launch), conditional on:
- Etsy shop operational with at least 3 guide listings live
- At least 1 buyer review posted (social proof for builder recruitment messaging)
- Monthly revenue trajectory positive (even $50–$100 in the first 30 days)

If Phase 1 traction is zero at July 1 (no views, no sales), delay recruitment 2 additional weeks. Use that time to diagnose the distribution problem — builder recruitment cannot fix a broken funnel.

### 6.3 Minimum Viable Team for Phase 3 Launch

Phase 3 cannot operate without this combination:

- **2+ Content Creators** — provides guide production redundancy (if one misses a deadline, the other covers)
- **1 Practice Builder** — activates the AHG/AANP practitioner channel; without this role, cold practitioner outreach is Seedwarden brand-to-stranger with no warm introduction layer
- **1 Mentor** — required before any AHG directory cold outreach begins; without a named reviewer, practitioner conversion rate on the $120–$150 bundle tier will be materially lower

**If only 1 Content Creator is available by August 1**: Delay practitioner-tier outreach by 2 weeks. One creator can sustain guide production but limits Zone coverage and creates single-point-of-failure risk.

**If no Mentor is secured by August 1**: Do not begin AHG directory cold outreach. Instead, run solo practitioner outreach using Phase 3 content without reviewer naming — accept a lower conversion rate for the first 30–60 days while Mentor recruitment continues.

### 6.4 Fallback: Solo Operation Extension to July

If Phase 3 recruitment fails entirely (zero builders onboarded by August 1), solo operation can continue through July with the following accommodations:

- Reduce guide publishing target from 2/month to 1/month (solo operator sustainable rate)
- Focus outreach on Segments 3 and 5 (Wildcrafters and Community Educators) — these segments do not require a named clinical reviewer for credibility, unlike Segments 1 and 2
- Delay practitioner-tier AHG directory outreach until Mentor is secured — avoid burning the AHG cold-outreach channel with unreviewed content
- Use the extended solo window to generate 30-day traction data that strengthens the builder recruitment pitch ("here is what the platform has already done in 60 days")

**Risk if Phase 3 stalls past September**: AHG Symposium (August 14–16) is the single highest-value practitioner gathering of the year. Missing this window with no Phase 3 team operational means the next equivalent opportunity is the UpS conference cycle (November–December) and the next AHG Symposium (August 2027). Not fatal, but a 12-month delay in practitioner-tier revenue growth.

The realistic risk is not platform collapse — Seedwarden's Etsy infrastructure and Phase 1–2 SEO foundation continue generating passive revenue regardless of Phase 3 team status. The risk is opportunity cost: Phase 3 team scales revenue 3x–6x faster than solo operation. Each month of delay costs approximately $2,000–$5,000 in incremental revenue potential at the Phase 3 trajectory modeled in the Audience Strategy document.
