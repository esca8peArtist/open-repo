# Seedwarden Phase 3 Product Expansion Roadmap
**Month 3–6 Post-Phase-1 Launch — July through October 2026**

**Context**: Phase 1 launched with 21 digital products and five defined bundles on Etsy. Phase 2 completed mockup tooling (tablet and phone frames) and lifestyle photography planning. Phase 3 social media growth strategy is research-complete. This roadmap addresses the parallel track: **product catalog expansion** to compound revenue from a live, converting store.

This document is operational, not aspirational. Every recommendation in it is bounded by the actual Phase 1 product catalog, the confirmed Etsy market research, the existing content pipeline, and realistic solo-operator capacity.

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 3 product expansion runs July through October 2026 — months 3 through 6 after a May 2026 Phase 1 launch. The strategic logic is:

1. **Months 1–2 (May–June)**: Let Phase 1 run and collect conversion data. Do not expand yet. Identify which products convert and which keyword clusters drive traffic.
2. **Month 3–4 (July–August)**: Expand depth within established winning categories. Add derivative products that require minimal new content creation. Launch the preservation season push.
3. **Month 5–6 (September–October)**: Move into adjacent categories. The fall window opens demand for foraging, seed saving, and homesteader gift positioning. New SKU categories go live.
4. **Month 7+ (November onward)**: Harvest holiday revenue from the expanded catalog. The Phase 3 product additions become the next-year baseline.

The Phase 3 revenue target is **$1,800–$3,200/month gross by Month 6 (October 2026)**, up from an estimated **$400–$800/month Phase 1 baseline**. This requires approximately 3–5x traffic growth (driven by Phase 3 social media) combined with a 20–30% increase in average order value from the expanded catalog and improved bundle offerings.

July and August are the peak preservation season. Etsy search volume surges for canning, fermentation, and harvest preservation keywords. Seedwarden already has three preservation products (Harvest Preservation Field Manual, Fermented Harvest Handbook, Meat & Fish Preservation Field Manual) and one bundle (Preservation Bundle at $38). ## Part 3: Pricing Strategy

### 3.1 Premium Positioning Rationale

Phase 1 pricing ($5–$22) is competitive and correct for launch. Phase 3 products should test the upper edge of the premium segment ($14–$16) for content-dense guides, with bundle economics driving the high-ticket transactions.

The etsy-seo-market-research.md notes that Seedwarden may be underpriced relative to content depth. Phase 3 is the moment to test price increases on three specific products:

- **Native Plants Regional Guide**: Raise from $18 to $22. The 7,717-line, 9-region, 80-plant guide is priced at the bottom of the premium segment. Adding the photo-complete wild-edibles content and the individual region listings provides pricing leverage. The individual listings at $12 make the complete guide at $22 look like a genuine deal by comparison.
- **Survival Garden Regional Plans**: Raise from $22 to $24. The individual region listings at $5.99 make the complete bundle at $24 feel like a 72% discount ($5.99 × 7 = $41.93 implied individual total). This is correct tiered pricing.
- **Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual**: Raise from $20 to $22. The most specialized product in the catalog. Low competition, high perceived value, specialist buyer who expects to pay more. Test at $22 in September and monitor conversion rate.

All three price increases should be tested in Month 5 (September) with a 30-day observation window before deciding whether to hold or revert.

### 3.2 Bundle Economics

Phase 3 bundles are designed to achieve a 40–50% average order value lift over individual product purchases. The mechanics:

| Bundle | Individual Total | Bundle Price | Discount % | Target AOV Lift |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|------------|----------------|
| Preservation Season Starter (P3-B1) | $28 | $22 | 21% | Converts $9 buyers to $22 |
| Master Preserver Bundle (P3-B2) | $90 | $52 | 42% | Converts $13–16 buyers to $52 |
| Expanded Homesteader Gift Set (P3-26) | $106 | $62 | 41% | Gift buyer anchor product |

The key insight from the existing bundle economics: the discount percentage matters less than the framing. A $52 bundle framed as "save $38 on a $90 library" converts better than a $25 bundle framed as "save $6." The Phase 3 master bundles are designed to make the savings feel substantial and the product collection feel complete.

## Part 4: Supplier Sourcing and Vendor Analysis

Seedwarden is a digital-only business. There is no physical inventory, no warehouse, and no supplier relationships in the traditional sense. "Supplier" in this context means: platform vendors, tool vendors, and content-input sources.

### 4.1 Platform and Tool Vendors

**Etsy** (primary sales platform)
- Cost: $0.20 listing fee per listing per 4 months + 6.5% transaction fee + 3% + $0.25 payment processing
- Phase 3 impact: 26 new listings (7 preservation derivatives, 9 native plants regional, 7 survival garden regional, 3 new bundles) = ~$5.20 in listing fees for the first 4-month period. Trivial cost.
- Lead time: Immediate. Listings can go live same day as created.

**ConvertKit / Kit** (email platform)
- Current plan: Free tier (up to 1,000 subscribers) is adequate through Month 4–5. At 500+ subscribers, upgrade to Creator plan ($25/month) for automation access beyond what the free tier allows.
- Phase 3 impact: The expanded product catalog requires 3 new post-purchase email variants (preservation buyer path, foraging buyer path, seed library buyer path). These are low-effort additions to the existing email-automation-blueprint.md architecture.

**Canva** (mockup and marketing design)
- Phase 3 requires: 26 new product mockups (tablet frame, phone frame), 78 Pinterest pin variants (3 per new product). Estimated 8–10 hours of batch Canva work across the Phase 3 launch period.
- Cost: Canva Pro at $13/month if not already subscribed. Worth it for batch asset creation.

**iNaturalist and Wikimedia Commons** (botanical image sources)
- Wild Edibles Photo Pack (P3-20) and Wild Edibles Quick Reference (P3-19) draw on assets already downloaded and logged in WORKLOG.md.
- No additional sourcing cost. Attribution page required in all derivative PDFs.

## Part 6: Fulfillment Workflow

### 6.1 Current Fulfillment Baseline (Phase 1)

All products are digital PDFs delivered automatically through Etsy's digital download system. The fulfillment workflow is fully automated: buyer purchases → Etsy triggers file delivery → buyer downloads PDF. Zero manual intervention required per order.

Phase 3 does not change this architecture. The expanded SKU count (from 21 to approximately 47 products + regional variants) adds complexity only in listing management, not in order fulfillment.

### 6.2 Inventory Management for Expanded SKU Count

With ~47 Etsy listings by October 2026, SKU management becomes a real operational consideration. The following system keeps it manageable:

**SKU naming convention:**
- Format: `SW-[CATEGORY]-[NUMBER]-[VARIANT]`
- Examples: `SW-PRES-01` (Preservation, product 1), `SW-NAT-05-NE` (Native Plants, listing 5, Northeast variant), `SW-BDLP3-B2` (Bundle, Phase 3, Bundle 2)

**PDF version control:**
- All PDFs stored in `scripts/output/` with the existing naming convention.
- Phase 3 derivatives: name clearly as derivatives (e.g., `beginner-canning-guide.pdf`, `fermentation-starter-guide.pdf`).
- Regional variants: same PDF as parent, with a note in the Etsy listing management system (or a simple spreadsheet) mapping each listing to its source PDF.

**Listing management spreadsheet (new tool required):**
Create `projects/seedwarden/data/etsy-listing-inventory.csv` to track all Etsy listings with columns: SKU, Etsy listing ID (once live), product title, source PDF path, price, tags used, mockup file path, phase launched, last updated.

This spreadsheet is the single source of truth for listing management. Any price change, tag update, or title revision is logged here before being applied on Etsy. Prevents the accidental use of outdated tags or overwriting of optimized copy.

### 6.3 Photography and Mockup Workflow for 26 New Listings

26 new Phase 3 products require:
- 26 tablet mockups (using existing `generate_mockups.py` with `--frame` default)
- 26 phone mockups (using `generate_mockups.py --frame portrait`)
- 78 Pinterest pin variants (3 per product, Canva batch session)
- Interior page mockup for content-dense products (P3-22, P3-23, P3-25) — 3 additional mockups showing interior pages

Workflow: Generate all mockups in a single batch after all PDFs are finalized. Do not generate mockups before PDF is in final state. The generate_mockups.py pipeline is already set up for batch runs; the Phase 3 batch adds new PDF filenames to the input list.

### 6.4 Packaging and Kitting (Digital Context)

"Kitting" for digital products means bundling multiple PDFs into a ZIP archive for delivery. The existing bundle products deliver multiple PDFs as a ZIP. Phase 3 does not change this — the Master Preserver Bundle (7 PDFs) and Expanded Homesteader Gift Set (7 PDFs) use the same ZIP delivery mechanism as existing bundles.

ZIP file naming convention: `seedwarden-[bundle-slug]-bundle.zip`

One new consideration: the Wild Edibles Photo Pack (P3-20) delivers JPEG files, not PDFs. Etsy supports ZIP delivery for image packs. The ZIP should include: 18 JPEG files at web-resolution, a plain-text attribution file (`ATTRIBUTION.txt`) documenting the Wikimedia Commons source URL and license for each image, and a simple `README.txt` explaining the CC BY-SA license terms and how buyers may use the images.
