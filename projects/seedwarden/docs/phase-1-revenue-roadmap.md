# Seedwarden Phase 1 Revenue Roadmap
**90-Day Forecast | May–July 2026 | Production-Ready by May 1**

**Context**: Phase 1 launches in May 2026 with 21 digital products and 5 bundles on Etsy. All products have PDFs, mockups, and listing copy. Pending blockers: user tag corrections + Etsy account verification. This roadmap is the single reference for tracking financial performance and making Phase 2 go/no-go decisions.

## Part 1: Baseline Conversion Rate Estimates by Cohort

Conversion rate inputs are drawn from three sources: Etsy digital product benchmarks (0.5–5% depending on shop age and listing quality), the customer cohort profiles in `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md`, and the product-specific positioning in `product-audit-2026-04-11.md`.

**Etsy Conversion Benchmarks (Digital Products):**

| Shop Stage | Typical Conversion Rate | Basis |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| New shop, no reviews | 0.4–0.8% | Etsy digital product historical averages |
| Established shop (6+ months, 20+ reviews) | 1.5–2.5% | Post-social-proof threshold |
| Proven shop (50+ reviews, ads active) | 3.0–5.0% | Top-quartile digital sellers |

**Seedwarden Phase 1 Baseline: 0.8% conversion** (realistic for a new shop with strong listing copy and mockups but zero reviews).

**Per-Cohort Conversion Estimates:**

### Cohort 1: High-Intent Forager (20–25% of traffic)
- Products: Native Plants Regional Guide, Wild Edibles section of other guides, Regional Self-Sufficiency Bundle
- Conversion rate: **20–25%** within-cohort (of visitors who arrive already intent on foraging content)
  - Rationale: Forager traffic arrives from high-intent searches ("native edible plants field guide," "foraging guide by region"). These buyers have a specific need and our product directly addresses it. Cart abandon rate is modeled at <5%.
  - AOV: $25–$40 (frequently bundles or premium products)
  - Expected repeat rate: 40% (seasonal foraging patterns drive quarterly return)

### Cohort 2: Survival Prepper (15–20% of traffic)
- Products: Hunting/Fishing Manual, Livestock Manual, Meat/Fish Preservation Manual, Homesteader's Complete Bundle
- Conversion rate: **15–20%** within-cohort
  - Rationale: Prepper buyers are research-intensive before purchase (10–15% abandon rate at price sensitivity). However, when they do convert, they tend to buy multiple items or bundles. The Homesteader's Complete Bundle at $50 is the natural destination product.
  - AOV: $35–$60 (high bundle affinity)
  - Expected repeat rate: 60% (event-driven, returns during news cycles, seasonal hunting prep)

### Cohort 3: Homesteader (30–35% of traffic)
- Products: Seed Saving Manual, Fermented Harvest, Harvest Preservation, Companion Planting Chart, multiple bundles
- Conversion rate: **30–35%** within-cohort
  - Rationale: Homesteader traffic is the broadest and most purchase-ready cohort. Etsy homesteading category has strong buyer intent. Our positioning (practical, anti-corporate, not padded content) differentiates within a category that often delivers generic content. This is the highest-converting cohort because products match what they're already doing.
  - AOV: $18–$35 (project-driven, incremental purchases)
  - Expected repeat rate: 70% (quarterly project cycles, consistent engagement with the brand)

### Cohort 4: Gift Buyer (15–20% of traffic)
- Products: Bundles, the $5 Companion Planting Chart, Hot Sauce Guide, Apartment Grower Bundle
- Conversion rate: **15–20%** within-cohort
  - Rationale: Gift buyers are browsing, not searching. They need a product that signals clear giftability. Our bundles ($28–$50) are well-positioned for this but the gift buyer has higher cart abandonment (50%+ modeled). They convert when the price point and positioning align with occasion (Mother's Day, holiday).
  - AOV: $25–$50 (gift price point)
  - Expected repeat rate: 10% (occasion-driven, returns annually at most)

**Blended Store Conversion Rate Model (Phase 1 Month 1):**
- Cohort-weighted average: 0.8% (conservative) to 1.2% (realistic)
- Basis: Etsy organic search drives 85–90% of new-shop traffic; most first-week traffic is cold (no prior brand awareness). The within-cohort rates above apply to traffic already pre-qualified by search intent. The blended 0.8% accounts for all traffic including casual browsers.
- Progression assumption: Blended conversion improves as reviews accumulate (+0.2–0.4% per 10 reviews received) and as listing quality signals improve with Etsy's algorithm.

## Part 2: Month-by-Month Revenue Projections (3 Scenarios)

### Key Input Parameters

**Product catalog:**
- 21 individual products
- 5 bundles (Apartment Grower $32, Food Sovereignty $30, Regional Self-Sufficiency $28, Preservation $38, Homesteader's Complete $50)
- Price range: $5 (Companion Planting Chart) to $50 (Homesteader's Complete Bundle)
- Average individual product price: ~$12.40 (weighted by price distribution)
- Average bundle price: ~$35.60

**Traffic assumptions (Etsy organic, no paid ads, no social in Month 1):**
- Month 1 views: 300–500 (conservative for a new Etsy shop, digital product category)
  - Basis: Etsy research consistently shows new digital shops receiving 200–600 organic views in Month 1 before the algorithm establishes a ranking pattern. 400 is the midpoint and the working assumption.
- Traffic growth rate: +20–40% per month (as listings age and collect algorithm signals)
- Individual listing views: Distributed across 26 listings (21 products + 5 bundles); top 3 listings capture ~35% of total views (typical for new shops with no clear breakout product yet)

**Fee structure:**
- Etsy transaction fee: 6.5% of sale price
- Etsy payment processing: 3% + $0.25 per order
- Etsy listing fee: $0.20 per listing per 4 months (one-time at launch for all 26 listings = $5.20 total; negligible)
- Net margin per $1.00 gross: approximately $0.905 minus $0.25 per order (so at $13.50 average order: net ~$11.98, or ~88.7% net margin after all fees)

## Part 3: Customer Acquisition Cost Analysis by Channel

### Phase 1 Channel Stack (No Paid Ads)

**Channel 1: Etsy Organic Search (Primary)**
- CAC: $0
- Rationale: No ad spend. All acquisition through Etsy algorithm ranking and organic search.
- Volume driver: Number of listings × quality of keyword targeting × conversion rate feedback loop
- Phase 1 investment: Zero ongoing cost. Upfront investment was product creation (already complete).
- Limitations: Etsy organic for a new shop is slow. The algorithm needs 30–60 days to assess listing quality before meaningful rankings appear. Month 1 traffic is a function of listing freshness boost (24–48 hours per new listing) plus whatever residual keyword matching occurs.

**Channel 2: Pinterest Organic (Secondary)**
- CAC: $0
- Rationale: Organic Pinterest pins have a 4+ month lifespan and compound over time. No ad spend in Phase 1.
- Volume contribution: Minimal in Month 1 (pins need to age), potentially 50–150 additional monthly views in M2–M3 if the 63-pin batch from `PHASE2_TO_PHASE3_TRANSITION.md` is executed.
- ROI: Extremely high. One batch Canva session (3–4 hours) producing 63 pins results in 6–18 months of passive referral traffic.
- Phase 1 action: Build 63 pins in Month 1 or early Month 2. The full protocol is in `PHASE2_TO_PHASE3_TRANSITION.md`.

**Channel 3: Email List (Tertiary, Early Stage)**
- CAC for new subscribers: $0 (organic lead magnet pickup through Etsy PDF end-page)
- Revenue contribution in Phase 1: Minimal. List will be 0–50 subscribers by end of Month 1. Email channel becomes meaningful at 200+ subscribers.
- LTV leverage: Each subscriber who enters the welcome sequence (per `email-automation-blueprint.md`) receives the SEEDWARDEN15 coupon. Coupon redemption rate target: 8%+ (Email 5). At 50 subscribers end-of-Month-1, that's 4 incremental purchases from email in Month 1.

### LTV Estimates by Cohort

LTV calculated over 24 months using repeat rate and AOV assumptions from `growth-metrics-framework.md` and `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md`.

**LTV Formula**: LTV = (AOV × Purchase Frequency × Retention Months) × Net Margin

| Cohort | First Purchase AOV | Retention Rate | Annual Purchases | 24-Month LTV (Gross) | 24-Month LTV (Net) |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| High-Intent Forager | $28 | 40% | 2.0 | $56 + $22 (repeat) = $78 | ~$69 |
| Survival Prepper | $42 | 60% | 2.5 | $42 + $63 (repeat) = $105 | ~$93 |
| Homesteader | $22 | 70% | 3.0 | $22 + $46 (repeat) = $68 | ~$60 |
| Gift Buyer | $30 | 10% | 1.1 | $30 + $3 (repeat) = $33 | ~$29 |

**Homesteader cohort is the highest-LTV target** despite lower first-purchase AOV because the 70% retention rate and 3x annual purchase frequency compound over 24 months more than the prepper's higher per-order value.

**Key LTV implication for Phase 1**: Every homesteader customer acquired in Month 1 has an expected 24-month gross LTV of $68. At a $0 CAC (Etsy organic), the payback period is the first purchase — making Etsy organic the most efficient acquisition channel available.

## Part 4: Payback Period and Break-Even Analysis

### Phase 1 Cost Baseline

**Sunk costs (already invested, not recoverable):**
- Content creation: All 21 products written and PDF-generated. Time investment is complete.
- Mockup generation: All 21 tablet mockups + 21 phone mockups generated.
- Listing copy: All 26 listings (21 products + 5 bundles) written and ready to upload.
- These costs are sunk. The break-even analysis focuses on ongoing costs only.

**Ongoing costs per month:**

| Cost Item | Monthly Amount | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Etsy listing fees | ~$0.43/month | $5.20 for all 26 listings per 4-month period |
| Etsy transaction + processing fees | ~11.3% of gross revenue | Deducted from each sale |
| ConvertKit (email platform) | $0 | Free tier up to 1,000 subscribers |
| Domain / hosting | $0 | No separate site in Phase 1 |
| Canva | $0–$13 | Already subscribed or free tier |
| **Total monthly fixed overhead** | **~$0.43–$13.43** | Essentially zero until paid tools are added |

**Effective break-even: first sale.** Because Phase 1 has no fixed monthly costs beyond the trivial listing renewal fee, every sale above zero is net positive. The meaningful break-even question is: when does revenue recover the opportunity cost of the development time already spent?

### Time-Investment Payback (Opportunity Cost Break-Even)

Estimating total development hours for Phase 1:
- Product creation (21 products): ~300 hours (estimated across all sessions)
- Listing copy + SEO research: ~40 hours
- Mockup generation + tooling: ~25 hours
- **Total: ~365 hours of development investment**

At a $40/hour reference rate (skilled freelance content creation):
- Implied investment value: $14,600

At the realistic scenario ($180/month run rate by M3):
- Break-even point (time to recover implied investment at run rate): 81 months (6.75 years)

This calculation demonstrates why a direct hourly-rate payback framework is the wrong model for digital products. The correct model is: Phase 1 is a catalog asset. Its value compounds as the catalog matures, social media amplifies traffic, and reviews accumulate. The $180/month M3 run rate is a launch-week number. The growth-metrics-framework.md targets $1,500–$3,000/month by Month 12. At $2,000/month annual:
- **Effective payback on implied investment: ~7–8 months from the Month 12 run rate**

**Etsy fee break-even**: Every product that sells at its listed price more than covers Etsy fees. The fee structure (11.3% effective) is not a break-even risk.

### Product Development Cost for Phase 1 Launch

Going forward, each new product requires ongoing development time. Phase 3 development load (per `docs/phase-3-product-expansion-roadmap.md`) is ~130–140 hours over 4 months. At a $40/hour reference rate: $5,200–$5,600 in development time for Phase 3. At the M6 revenue target of $1,800–$3,200/month:
- Phase 3 development investment is recovered in 2–3 months at the M6 run rate.

## Part 5: Phase 1 to Phase 2 Transition Criteria

### Go/No-Go Gates (Numeric)

**Month 1 Gate (June 1 check — based on first 30 days of Phase 1 data):**

| Metric | Target | Hold Threshold | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|----------------|
| Total sales | ≥20 | <10 | <10 = product-market fit not established; audit listings before expanding |
| Conversion rate | ≥0.8% | <0.5% | <0.5% = listing quality problem (images, titles, or pricing) |
| Number of 5-star reviews | ≥3 | 0 | 0 reviews at 20+ sales = post-purchase sequence not running |
| Pinterest monthly views | ≥5,000 | <1,000 | <1,000 = Pinterest setup incomplete or pins not indexed |

**If Month 1 Gate met (≥20 sales):** Proceed to Phase 2 lifestyle photography planning. Begin building 63-pin Pinterest batch.

**If Month 1 Gate NOT met (<10 sales):** Hold Phase 2. Diagnose: run the Section 5 listing health audit from `analytics/monthly-metrics-checklist.md`. Identify which listings have views but zero conversions. Fix cover images or titles on those listings before expanding.

---

**Month 2 Gate (July 1 check — based on cumulative 60-day data):**

| Metric | Green-Light | Yellow (Watch) | Red (Hold Phase 2) |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Cumulative sales | ≥50 | 30–49 | <30 |
| Blended conversion rate | ≥1.0% | 0.7–0.9% | <0.7% |
| Email subscribers | ≥80 | 40–79 | <40 |
| Repeat buyer rate | ≥15% | 8–14% | <8% |
| AOV trend | Increasing | Flat | Declining |

**Green-light (≥50 cumulative sales):** Authorize Phase 2 lifestyle photography sprint. Proceed with Phase 3 planning in parallel.

**Yellow (30–49 sales):** Continue Phase 2 photography prep autonomously but do not commit iStock budget (per `PHASE2_PHOTOGRAPHY_EXECUTION_PLAN.md`). Phase 3 planning proceeds in parallel.

**Red (<30 sales):** Hold Phase 2 photography investment. Redirect effort to listing optimization: re-shoot cover mockups for 3 lowest-performing listings, run title A/B test using Etsy Experiments, audit tag alignment with eRank.

---

**Month 3 Gate (August 1 check — based on cumulative 90-day data):**

| Metric | Target | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Cumulative sales | ≥100 | Validates organic reach and product-market fit |
| Repeat customer rate | ≥15% | Signals retention and brand loyalty formation |
| Top-3 product revenue share | ≥35% of total | Identifies winners for ad scaling |
| Email list size | ≥150 subscribers | Validates lead magnet and organic email growth |
| Pinterest organic referrals | ≥50 clicks/month to Etsy | Validates Pinterest as a growth channel |

**If Month 3 cumulative ≥100 sales and repeat rate ≥15%:** Full green-light for Phase 3 product expansion. Begin Phase 3 product development sprint (preservation derivatives, regional listings).

**If Month 3 cumulative ≥100 sales but repeat rate <15%:** Investigate post-purchase sequence. Check whether Automation 2 (post-purchase email) is firing. Check cross-sell copy in listing descriptions. Phase 3 expansion proceeds but email retention must be diagnosed in Month 4.

---

### Phase 2 Photography Decision Logic

The investment decision for Phase 2 lifestyle photography (iStock credits for 6 stock-photo products: $0–$150 total) is triggered by Month 2 gate only if:
- Month 2 cumulative ≥50 sales (green-light or yellow), AND
- At least one of the 5 highest-ticket products (Survival Garden $22, Hunting Manual $20, Livestock Manual $18, Meat/Fish Preservation $18, Harvest Preservation $16) is in the top 3 revenue-generating listings

The second condition ensures the iStock investment goes to products that are already showing commercial signal, not to products that need more fundamental help.

## Part 6: Monthly KPI Dashboard Specification

### Dashboard Design Principles

1. 90-minute maximum to complete (per `monthly-metrics-checklist.md` protocol)
2. Every metric has a clear action threshold — no metric exists purely for observation
3. Metrics are layered: revenue (what happened) → conversion (why) → retention (what's sticky) → acquisition (how to grow)
4. Data sources are Etsy Seller Dashboard + Kit (ConvertKit) exports. No third-party tools required in Phase 1.

### 12 Core KPIs

| # | Metric | Definition | Healthy Range | Alert Threshold | Action if Triggered |
|---|--------|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Blended Conversion Rate** | Total orders / Total listing views × 100 | 0.8–2.5% | <0.5% | Audit top-5 listings by views; check cover images and title keywords |
| 2 | **Average Order Value (AOV)** | Gross revenue / Total orders | $14–$35 | <$12 | Increase bundle visibility; add bundle mention to individual listing descriptions |
| 3 | **Repeat Buyer Rate** | Returning buyers / Total unique buyers × 100 | 15–25% by M3 | <8% by M3 | Diagnose post-purchase email sequence; check coupon redemption rate |
| 4 | **Email Signup Rate** | New Kit subscribers / New Etsy buyers × 100 | 20–35% | <10% | Audit lead magnet CTA placement in PDFs; verify Kit form link is live in listing description |
| 5 | **Pinterest Saves per Listing** | Total saves from Etsy listing analytics | 5+ per listing/month | <2 | Pin variants need better hook text; test benefit-first pin vs. product mockup pin |
| 6 | **Pinterest Outbound Clicks** | Monthly referral clicks from Pinterest to Etsy (via Etsy Stats traffic sources) | 25+ by M2, 75+ by M3 | <10 by M2 | Verify pins are indexed; add UTM tracking to bio link |
| 7 | **Top Listing Concentration** | Revenue from top 3 listings / Total revenue × 100 | 30–50% | >70% | Diversify: invest more mockup work in listings outside the top 3 |
| 8 | **Listing Health Score** | Count of listings with conversion rate ≥0.8% / Total listings | ≥30% of listings | <15% of listings | Triage by views: listings with 50+ views and <0.5% conversion need intervention first |
| 9 | **Review Accumulation Rate** | Cumulative reviews by month | M1: 3+, M2: 10+, M3: 20+ | 0 reviews at 20+ sales | Verify Automation 2 (post-purchase email) is running; manually send review request to first 5 buyers |
| 10 | **Net Revenue per Order** | (Gross revenue − Etsy fees) / Orders | ~88–91% of AOV | <85% | Usually means a refund event; check for chargebacks or download failures |
| 11 | **Email Open Rate (Welcome Sequence)** | Email 1 open rate for subscribers acquired this month | 45%+ | <30% | Test subject line; check spam folder routing; simplify Email 1 to a single CTA |
| 12 | **Bundle Revenue Share** | Bundle order revenue / Total gross revenue × 100 | 20–35% by M3 | <10% by M3 | Increase bundle visibility; add bundle upsell mention to the 5 lowest-AOV individual listings |

### Alert Logic and Decision Rules

**Red Alert (immediate action required — same week):**
- Blended conversion rate drops below 0.5% for two consecutive weeks: audit cover images and titles on top-viewed listings within 48 hours.
- Zero reviews after 30+ cumulative sales: manually verify Automation 2 is firing in Kit. Send one manual review request to the most recent 10 buyers.
- Email open rate below 30% (Email 1): the deliverability problem overrides all other email metrics. Check spam classification with a test send to personal email.

**Yellow Alert (address within the current month):**
- AOV below $12 for two consecutive months: cross-sell copy in listing descriptions needs revision. Test adding "complete the collection" bundle mention.
- Pinterest outbound clicks below 10 by Month 2: add one dedicated Pinterest promotion week; reshare the lead magnet pin.
- Repeat buyer rate below 8% by Month 3: the post-purchase sequence is the likely failure point. Run through the Kit automation diagnostic in `email-automation-blueprint.md` Part 4.

**Green Signal (accelerate):**
- Any listing achieves >3% conversion rate over 30 days: add it to Etsy Ads at $1/day to test paid amplification.
- Pinterest outbound clicks exceed 100/month: prioritize creating 3 additional pin variants for the top-traffic-driving product.
- Repeat buyer rate exceeds 25% by M3: segment the VIP cohort and create a dedicated upsell email with early access to Phase 3 products.

### Monthly Dashboard Execution Sequence

1. Pull Etsy CSV exports + Kit subscriber export (15 min — pre-work from `monthly-metrics-checklist.md`)
2. Calculate all 12 KPIs and log to Monthly Data Log table (20 min)
3. Check all alert thresholds — flag any red or yellow alerts (5 min)
4. Review top-3 and bottom-3 listings by conversion rate (10 min)
5. Check cohort health: count returning buyers this month (10 min)
6. Document actions arising in Priority 1/2/3 format (10 min)
7. Set next month's targets based on current trajectory (10 min)
8. **Total: 80 min**

- **6-month target**: $600–$1,200 gross/month (October 2026)
- **12-month target**: $1,500–$3,000 gross/month (April 2027)

### Phase 1 Trajectory vs. Year 1 Targets

**Is the realistic Phase 1 trajectory aligned?**

Phase 1 realistic scenario (M3 run rate: $180/month) extrapolated linearly to Month 12:
- This would produce ~$1,500–$2,000 annual gross revenue — well below the $8,000 floor.

However, Phase 1 is not a linear growth scenario. The business plan assumes Phase 3 product expansion (47 listings by October 2026), social media activation (Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram from Month 3), and seasonal demand spikes (preservation season July–September, holiday November–December) compound the baseline.

**The Year 1 math requires Phase 3:**

| Phase | Period | Monthly Contribution | Annual Contribution |
|-------|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Phase 1 only (21 products, no social) | May–July | $60–$180/month | $360–$540 |
| Phase 1 + Phase 2 photos + early social | August–September | $400–$700/month | $800–$1,400 |
| Phase 1 + 2 + Phase 3 products + active social | October–April | $1,000–$2,500/month | $6,000–$15,000 |
| **Year 1 Total (realistic scenario)** | | | **$7,160–$16,940** |

**Conclusion: Phase 1 revenue trajectory is aligned with Year 1 targets IF:**
1. Phase 2 photography is executed in Months 2–3 (per the green-light gate conditions)
2. Phase 3 products launch on schedule (July–October, per `docs/phase-3-product-expansion-roadmap.md`)
3. Social media activation (Pinterest batch, TikTok, Instagram) begins by Month 3

The $8,000 Year 1 floor is achievable in the realistic scenario. The $30,000 ceiling requires the optimistic Phase 3 trajectory with active social media driving traffic — achievable but requires all three conditions above to execute on schedule.

**Phase 1 specifically contributes $360–$1,200 of Year 1 revenue** (Months 1–3, before Phase 3 compounds). This is a small share of the annual number, which is correct — Phase 1 is not designed to be the revenue engine. It is designed to prove product-market fit, accumulate reviews, and provide the data needed to make Phase 3 bets confidently.
