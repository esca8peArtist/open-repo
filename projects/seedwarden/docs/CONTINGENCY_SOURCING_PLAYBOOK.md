# Contingency Sourcing Playbook
## Phase 3 Botanical Illustration — Four Scenario Response Procedures

**Prepared**: June 10, 2026
**Purpose**: Deterministic response procedures for each failure mode in the contractor sourcing process. Each scenario has a trigger condition, assessment procedure, and ready-to-send outreach templates. No open-ended decisions are required when a scenario triggers — the procedure is pre-authorized.

---

## How to Use This Playbook

Each scenario has:
- A **trigger condition** with an explicit date and measurable threshold
- A **go/no-go assessment** if applicable
- An **execution procedure** with specific actions in sequence
- **Ready-to-send outreach templates** that can be copied and sent without additional drafting

Check this playbook at each gate date: June 12, June 15, June 17, and throughout the sprint.

---

## Scenario A: Primary Channels Return Fewer Than 5 Qualified Candidates by June 12

### Trigger Condition
June 12 EOD: Upwork posting (live since June 10) has produced fewer than 3 proposals with publication-quality portfolios, AND the Tier A direct outreach (Anna Farba, Joséphine Klerks, Adrian White) has produced fewer than 2 substantive replies.

This means: 5 days of primary sourcing has not produced enough candidates to conduct a proper comparison evaluation. Do not wait until June 15 to respond — activate this scenario on June 12 EOD if the threshold is met.

### Assessment (5 minutes)
Count qualified proposals (Upwork) + substantive replies (direct outreach). If combined count is below 5 candidates who have shared portfolio links showing publication-quality botanical illustration work, Scenario A triggers.

### Execution Procedure

**Step 1: Boost Upwork posting (30 minutes)**
Activate Upwork's promoted jobs feature on the existing posting. This increases the posting's visibility in candidate search results. Log the boost activation date in WORKLOG.md.

**Step 2: Post on Reddit r/HireAnArtist (10 minutes)**
Use the Reddit outreach post template below. Post between 10am and 2pm ET on a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday for maximum visibility on this subreddit.

**Step 3: Instagram hashtag outreach (45 minutes)**
Search the following hashtags on Instagram: #botanicalillustration, #medicinalplantillustration, #herbalistillustrator, #scientificbotanicalillustration, #inkbotanical. Identify 8–10 accounts with a post history showing plant accuracy and line art work. Send the Instagram DM template below. Send all DMs on the same day.

**Step 4: ASBA referral request (10 minutes)**
Email memberservices@asba-art.org using the ASBA Referral Request template below. The American Society of Botanical Artists has close to 2,000 members — one referral from this organization will surface a publication-quality candidate immediately.

**Step 5: Log in WORKLOG.md**
"Scenario A activated June 12 — primary channels below threshold. Backup channels activated: Upwork boost, Reddit r/HireAnArtist post, Instagram DM outreach to [X] accounts, ASBA referral request sent."

## Scenario B: Tier A Candidates Quote Above $1,350

### Trigger Condition
One or more Tier A candidates reply with a rate quote that, after Upwork fees (5%) or direct, exceeds the $1,350 hard ceiling. Common form: "My rate for this scope is $X,000" where X×1,000 > $1,350.

This scenario activates mid-search, not at a specific date. It can trigger any day between June 10 and June 15.

### Assessment
Before classifying as Scenario B, attempt one round of scope negotiation per candidate. The goal is to fit within budget by reducing the illustration count rather than the per-illustration rate.

**Negotiation procedure (per over-budget candidate)**:

1. **Anchor to 10 illustrations**: "Our budget is $1,000–$1,350 for the full project. If that's below your rate for 18 plates, could you scope with us for 10 plates at publication quality, with a per-illustration rate we could add to for additional pieces if needed?"

2. **Offer the rate transparency**: "Your per-illustration rate at 18 plates is approximately [$X/illustration]. At that rate, our budget covers approximately [budget ÷ per-illustration rate] plates. Would you be open to a 10–12 plate initial scope with an option to add plates at the same rate?"

3. **Frame the fourth-bundle expansion**: If the candidate quotes $1,500–$2,000 for 18 plates but their per-illustration rate is within the per-plate budget: "We may expand to a second series (additional species) in early August at the same rate. Would you consider the initial scope at $1,350 with the understanding we'd discuss the expansion separately?"

**If negotiation produces no agreement (candidate holds above $1,350 after scope reduction):**

### Scenario B Fallback Options

**Option B1: Solo 9-week sprint with reduced illustration scope**

The PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md solo model produces all five medicinal herb guide bundles without a contractor. Illustrations in the solo model are sourced from Wikimedia Commons CC-licensed photographs (already established as the project's botanical photography source). The Phase 3 guides do not require original commissioned illustrations — they can launch with high-quality CC-licensed botanical photography as the visual layer.

Risk profile: The guides using CC photography rather than commissioned line art have lower perceived premium positioning than guides with original commissioned illustration. This affects the visual distinctiveness of the Etsy listings but does not affect the textual content quality.

Recovery procedure: If the solo model launches without commissioned illustration, original illustration can be added as a second edition (PDF update) after Phase 3 closes. This is the same listing-update recovery mechanism documented in the solo fallback architecture for partial-content uploads.

**Option B2: Less-experienced illustrator at a mentored rate**

If Tier A candidates are over budget, identify a mid-tier Upwork candidate (portfolio shows botanical accuracy but less publication-polish) willing to work with editorial feedback. The risk profile for this option:
- Risk: First-draft quality may require 2–3 rounds of revision per illustration, adding 20–30% to the effective engagement time
- Risk: Scientific accuracy review burden falls entirely on the user — the illustrator's plant knowledge must be confirmed via the screening questions before hire
- Recovery: Build 2 revision rounds into the contract terms (explicit in the Upwork contract) and allocate 10 hours of user review time in the sprint schedule for illustration feedback
- Rate range for this tier: $15–$28/hour, putting 80–120 hours at $1,200–$3,360. For a fixed-scope engagement (12 plates, 45–60 hours), this tier fits at $675–$1,680. The lower half of this range is budget-compatible.

**Scenario B decision rule**: If no Tier A candidate fits the budget after one round of scope negotiation (by June 13), activate Option B1 (solo + CC photography) unless a mid-tier Upwork candidate meets the botanical accuracy screening questions. If a passing mid-tier candidate exists, activate Option B2. Log the decision in WORKLOG.md.

## Scenario C: Fewer Than 3 Confirmed Candidates by June 17 Decision Gate

### Trigger Condition
June 17 EOD: Fewer than 3 candidates have completed the screening process (portfolio reviewed, screening questions answered, discovery call conducted), OR no candidate has agreed to contract terms within budget.

This is the hard deadline. If this scenario triggers, the solo fallback activates immediately.

### Execution Procedure

**Step 1: Final assessment (30 minutes before EOD June 17)**
Count confirmed candidates (all screening criteria met, rate within budget, availability confirmed). If below 3:
- Is any one candidate ready to sign? If yes: send the contract now, do not wait.
- If no candidate is ready to sign: proceed to solo fallback activation.

**Step 2: Solo fallback activation**
Log in WORKLOG.md: "Scenario C triggered — June 17 EOD. [X] candidates in pipeline, none confirmed. Solo fallback activated per PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md. 5-bundle solo sprint June 22 – September 24 at 12 hrs/week. Phase 4 start adjusted to October 1."

**Step 3: Stop all active contractor outreach**
Do not continue running both planning tracks simultaneously after June 17. Exception: if a strong unsolicited candidate appears between June 17 and June 22, apply the PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_DECISION_TREE.md one-call go/no-go rule (score ≥75, confirmed availability, rate within ceiling → hire; otherwise do not proceed). After June 22, the search window is fully closed.

**Step 4: Confirm sprint preparation is complete**
All sprint preparation actions from PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md Section 6 must be verified:
- CC images pre-staged (all 14 species confirmed in assets/)
- Canva palette loaded
- Attribution log complete
- Content outlines finalized for all 5 bundles

**Step 5: Update Phase 4 planning dates**
Per PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md Section 5: Phase 4 start October 1, Wave 1 launch November 1, Phase 5 start February 1, 2027.

### Note on Illustration Under Solo Fallback
The solo model produces all five guide bundles using CC-licensed botanical photography from Wikimedia Commons — the same sourcing path established for the wild edibles habit photos in the earlier phases. Original commissioned illustration is not a prerequisite for guide launch. All five bundles can reach Etsy with CC photography as the visual layer. Commissioned illustration becomes a v2.0 upgrade for any bundle that performs well in organic discovery (identified by the August analytics check).

## Scenario D: Contractor Drops During Sprint (Post-June-22)

### Trigger Condition
After June 22, the confirmed contractor:
- Misses two consecutive delivery milestones without advance notice
- Notifies the user they cannot continue due to personal circumstances, workload, or other reasons
- Delivers work that fails botanical accuracy review on two consecutive drafts (not one revision cycle but two)
- Goes unresponsive for 5+ days during the sprint without prior notice

### Detection and Response Window
The sprint uses four delivery milestones. Dropout can be detected at any milestone. The response window between detection and solo fallback activation is 48 hours — enough time to attempt contact and assess whether recovery is possible, but not enough time to re-run the full contractor search.

**Dropout detection procedure**:

Day 1 (detection): Contractor misses a milestone or notifies of inability to continue.
- Attempt contact by phone, email, and Upwork message within 4 hours
- Send the Contractor Check-In template (Template D1)

Day 1 EOD: If no response and no partial delivery exists: begin preparing solo fallback immediately. Do not wait for Day 2.

Day 2: If contractor responds with a revised delivery date within 3 days: grant the extension once. Log it. If contractor does not respond by Day 2 EOD: activate solo fallback.

### Illustration Replacement Procedure

If dropout occurs during the illustration sprint, the recovery options depend on which milestone was missed:

**Dropout before any delivery (before first plate delivered)**: Full solo fallback (CC photography). No partial contractor work to salvage. Log all contractor communication and preserve the contract for potential deposit recovery through Upwork's dispute process.

**Dropout after partial delivery (1–8 plates delivered)**: Partial solo recovery. The delivered plates are usable. The remaining plates default to CC photography. Guides can launch with a mixed illustration set (commissioned plates + CC photography) without relaunching as a full revision. Note which plants have commissioned illustrations in the guide attribution section.

**Dropout after substantial delivery (9+ plates delivered)**: Minimal disruption. Attempt to complete the remaining 3–9 plates via one of the following:
1. Upwork replacement hire: post an urgent "small scope" job (3–9 plates, $150–$400, 2-week turnaround). Many Upwork illustrators will take small urgent commissions.
2. Upwork original contractor's portfolio: contact illustrators from the original Upwork proposal pool who were not selected — one may be available for a small urgent scope.
3. CC photography for the remaining species: use existing photo attribution workflow to fill the remaining species.

### Financial Recovery
If a contractor drops before completing the scope and payment milestones were structured per the recommended 25%/25%/25%/25% payment structure:
- If dropout occurs before Milestone 1 delivery: request deposit return through Upwork's dispute resolution. Upwork escrow protects the client's funds for the undelivered milestones.
- If dropout occurs after Milestone 1 delivery but before Milestone 2: retain the delivered work; release only the proportional payment for completed deliverables. Upwork's contract terms allow for partial payment dispute if deliverables are incomplete.
- Do not pay the final milestone until all plates are confirmed received and QA-checked (resolution, format, file completeness).
