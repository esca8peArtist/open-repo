## Section 1 — Keyword Analysis: How Botanical Illustrators Search Upwork

Upwork's search algorithm weights job title and skill tags. Candidates searching for illustration work on Upwork use the following term clusters, in rough order of frequency:

**High-volume botanical illustration terms** (use in job title and skills):
- "botanical illustration"
- "botanical artist"
- "plant illustration"
- "scientific illustration"
- "natural history illustration"

**Niche terms that surface the right candidate tier** (use in description body):
- "medicinal plant illustration"
- "herbal illustration"
- "publication-quality line art"
- "pen and ink botanical"
- "ink illustration plants"
- "black and white illustration"
- "scientific botanical drawing"

**Women's health alignment terms** (use in description; not in title):
- "herbalism illustration"
- "women's health plants"
- "traditional herbal medicine illustration"
- "plant medicine art"

**Terms to avoid in the title** (attract wrong candidates):
- "watercolor botanical" — attracts decorative artists, not publication-quality line art specialists
- "floral illustration" — attracts wedding/event illustrators
- "botanical print" — attracts surface pattern designers
- "plant drawing" — too broad; attracts hobbyist illustrators

### Recommended Job Title
"Botanical Illustrator — Medicinal Plants, Publication-Quality Line Art, Women's Health Focus"

This title hits three keyword clusters (botanical illustrator, medicinal plants, publication-quality line art) and includes the women's health focus that will self-select candidates with relevant plant knowledge. It is under 80 characters, fitting within Upwork's title display.

## Section 2 — Competitor Pricing Analysis

Based on research into comparable botanical illustration projects on Upwork and adjacent freelance platforms, here is what comparable projects are paying in 2025–2026:

### Low-Tier Projects (Entry-Level, Decorative Style)
- **Scope**: 5–15 simple botanical drawings, watercolor or digital
- **Budget range**: $150–$500
- **Candidate profile**: Beginning illustrators, Fiverr-crossover candidates, hobbyist botanical artists
- **Why this tier is wrong for Phase 3**: These candidates produce decorative work, not scientifically accurate species identification-quality illustrations. A Black Cohosh illustration that cannot be used to identify the species has no value in a women's health guide.

### Mid-Tier Projects (Publication-Quality, Specialist Style)
- **Scope**: 8–20 detailed botanical line art illustrations for books, educational materials, or product packaging
- **Budget range**: $500–$2,000
- **Candidate profile**: Illustrators with 3–8 years of professional experience, documented publication or product work, scientific or editorial illustration background
- **This is the Phase 3 target tier**: The $1,000–$1,350 budget fits squarely in the middle of this range, which positions the posting as competitive for mid-tier candidates without overpaying for the top of the range.

### Expert/Premium Projects (Fine Art + Scientific, High Exclusivity)
- **Scope**: 5–15 illustrations with exclusive licensing, publication in books or academic work
- **Budget range**: $2,000–$8,000+
- **Candidate profile**: Established botanical illustrators with book credits, gallery exhibitions, or scientific institution commissions (ASBA members, natural history museum work)
- **Why this tier is out of scope**: The Phase 3 budget does not reach this tier. Attempting to recruit from this tier will result in non-competitive proposals or ignoring.

### Calibration Note
For a publication-quality botanical line art set of 12–18 illustrations (the realistic output volume within the 80–120 hour scope), the market rate from the mid-tier pool is:
- $55–$100 per illustration at publication quality
- 80–120 hours at $12–$17/illustration average = plausible at $1,000–$1,350 flat rate

The flat-rate framing in the job posting is critical. An hourly framing at $25–$35/hour for 80–120 hours implies $2,000–$4,200 — above budget. Framing as a fixed-price project with a defined deliverable count keeps the posting competitive within the budget.

## Section 4 — Optimal Project Description (200–300 Words, Copy-Paste Ready)

The following description is formatted for direct paste into Upwork's job creation form. It is 280 words — within the 200–300 word target range that performs best on Upwork (long enough to pre-qualify candidates, short enough to read fully).

---

**POSTING TITLE**: Botanical Illustrator — Medicinal Plants, Publication-Quality Line Art, Women's Health Focus

**POSTING BODY**:

We are creating a series of medicinal herb educational guides focused on women's health plants, published as PDF digital downloads through an established Etsy-based botanical education brand.

We need a botanical illustrator to produce publication-quality line art illustrations of key women's health medicinal plants: Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa), Vitex (Vitex agnus-castus), Red Raspberry Leaf (Rubus idaeus), Red Clover (Trifolium pratense), Evening Primrose (Oenothera biennis), and related species. Species accuracy is essential — these illustrations will appear alongside clinical-level botanical text and must allow readers to identify the plant correctly.

**What we need**:
- Botanical line art illustrations suitable for print-quality PDF interiors and digital cover images
- Black-and-white or limited-palette line art style (pen and ink, or digital equivalent)
- High-resolution deliverables: 300 DPI minimum, TIFF or layered PNG
- Latin name labeling optional (preferred where space allows)
- Creative autonomy welcomed: bring your own compositional voice within species-accuracy constraints

**What we value in this collaboration**:
- Botanical knowledge alongside illustration skill: you understand the plant, not just its visual form
- Interest in women's health plants and traditional herbal medicine
- Experience producing educational or publication material (book, guide, course, or product)
- Reliable communication and milestone delivery on a June–September timeline

**Budget**: $1,000–$1,286 for the full project scope. Fixed-price contract preferred.

**To apply**: Share two portfolio examples showing botanical accuracy in your illustration work — ideally medicinal or edible plants. Include a brief note on any background you have in herbalism or botanical science.

---

**SCREENING QUESTIONS** (add to Upwork proposal screening):

1. "Describe one botanical plant in your portfolio where you paid particular attention to accuracy of leaf shape, venation, or stem structure. Which plant was it, and why did that accuracy matter for the end use of the illustration?"

2. "Are you familiar with any of the following species: Black Cohosh, Vitex, Red Raspberry Leaf, Red Clover? Have you illustrated any of them previously?"

These questions screen for botanical accuracy orientation and women's health plant familiarity without requiring a clinical credential — the correct filter for an illustration (not writing) engagement.

## Section 6 — Proposal Scoring Guide

When proposals arrive, score each on this five-point checklist before scheduling discovery calls:

| Criterion | Score (0–2) | What to Look For |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Portfolio botanical accuracy | 0–2 | Can you identify the plants depicted? Are leaf margins, stem structure, or flower morphology correctly rendered? |
| Portfolio line art quality | 0–2 | Is the work publication-quality? Clean lines, consistent weight, no pixelation at print resolution? |
| Screening question answers | 0–2 | Did they describe a specific plant + accuracy concern? Do they know any Phase 3 species? |
| Budget fit | 0–1 | Quoted within $1,000–$1,286? Or close enough to negotiate? |
| Communication quality | 0–1 | Is the proposal specific to this posting or generic copy-paste? |

**Score threshold**: 7–8 = Discovery call. 5–6 = Portfolio review before deciding on call. Below 5 = Decline.

**Automatic decline criteria** (regardless of score):
- Portfolio shows only decorative/surface pattern work with no evidence of scientific accuracy concern
- Proposal does not reference the women's health plant focus
- Quoted rate exceeds $1,286 with no negotiation signal
