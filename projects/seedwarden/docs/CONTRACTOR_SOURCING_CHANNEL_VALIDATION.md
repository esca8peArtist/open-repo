## Important Clarification: Contractor Role

The six channels assessed below are being evaluated for **botanical illustrators** producing publication-quality line art, not the herbalist writer role documented in PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SOURCING_STRATEGY.md. The illustration contractor scope is:

- 80–120 hours of original botanical illustration work
- Focus: women's health plants (Black Cohosh, Vitex, Red Raspberry Leaf, Evening Primrose, Red Clover, and related species)
- Style requirement: publication-quality black-and-white or limited-palette line art suitable for PDF guide interiors and Etsy listing imagery
- Sprint window: June 22 – September 24, 2026

AHG, IHA, Chestnut School, and Herbal Academy are primarily herbalist/practitioner networks. Their relevance for **illustration** sourcing is indirect — they surface candidates who combine botanical knowledge with illustration skill, which is the correct target profile for Phase 3 (medically accurate depictions of women's health plants require species-level knowledge, not just technical drawing skill). This validation assesses each channel through that lens.

## Channel 1: American Herbalists Guild (AHG) Member Directory

**URL**: americanherbalistsguild.com/directory/
**Canonical redirect**: directory.americanherbalistsguild.com → americanherbalistsguild.com/directory/

### Directory Access
The AHG directory is publicly searchable without login. The directory platform states that visitors can explore listings by location, specialty, or herbal tradition. Basic browsing does not require AHG membership. Member management features (adding/editing listings) require membership login. No paywall blocks candidate search for external users.

Specialty filter categories include Clinical Herbalism, Women's Health, Medicinal Herbs, Respiratory, and Immune System. The directory does not have a dedicated "botanical illustration" or "botanical art" filter — this is a practitioner credentialing directory, not an illustration marketplace. However, some AHG Registered Herbalists carry dual backgrounds in botanical art or scientific illustration; these candidates are present in the directory but are not indexed by illustration specialty.

**Workaround**: Filter by specialty (Women's Health, Medicinal Herbs) and look for members who list "education," "writing," or "botanical art" in their bio descriptions. This requires reading individual profiles rather than filtering directly — estimate 2–3 hours of directory browsing to surface 15–20 candidates worth reviewing.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
3–5 hours total for identification phase: 2–3 hours directory browsing to identify candidates with illustration in their profiles, 1–2 hours writing and sending personalized outreach. Expect first replies within 5–7 days (AHG community is active but practitioners respond at practitioner pace, not freelancer platform pace). Interview-to-offer window: 3–5 additional days. Realistic time-to-confirmed-contractor: **12–16 days from outreach send**.

If outreach goes out June 10, realistic contract signed date is June 22–26 — which falls outside the June 17 decision gate. AHG is therefore a **background channel** for Phase 3 illustration, not a primary fast-path channel. Its value is referral quality, not speed.

### Candidate Density
Moderate-to-scarce for the specific combination of herbalist credential + illustration skill. The AHG directory has 400–600 active Registered Herbalists. Of these, an estimated 10–20 have documented illustration or botanical art backgrounds — perhaps 2–4% of the listed population. Total addressable candidates: approximately 10–20 nationally. This is low volume but high fit when candidates are found.

### Price Point Expectations
AHG members with RH credentials charge professional rates. For botanical illustration work (as opposed to clinical writing), the typical range is lower than their writing rates: $35–$75/hour for illustration commissions from credentialed herbalist-illustrators. At 80–120 hours, this yields $2,800–$9,000 at standard rates — well above the $1,000–$1,350 Phase 3 budget. AHG channel candidates are therefore price-mismatched unless:
1. The engagement is scoped to a subset of illustrations (not the full 80–120 hours)
2. The candidate is an early-career RH who values portfolio development
3. The project is framed as a discounted educational/community engagement

### Recommendation
**Rank: 4 of 6.** Use AHG directory for referrals and warm outreach, not as a primary sourcing channel for illustration at this budget. Contact 5–8 AHG members who list education or art in their profiles and ask if they know botanical illustrators in the network. One referral from a trusted AHG member is worth more than 20 cold Upwork proposals.

## Channel 2: International Herb Association (IHA)

**URL**: iherb.org/membership-directory/

### Directory Access
**Access barrier confirmed**: The IHA membership directory page currently shows "Coming Soon." The directory is not yet operational as of June 2026. This means the IHA member directory cannot be used for direct candidate search at this time.

The IHA does maintain a conference speaker list (annual Herb Growing and Marketing Conference) and a Facebook group (International Herb Association) that may surface member contacts. Conference speaker pages and social channels are publicly accessible. However, a searchable, filterable directory is unavailable.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
Because the directory is offline, the IHA path requires indirect engagement: (1) browsing past conference speaker lists for herbalist-educators who may do illustration, (2) contacting IHA directly at their listed contact email to request member referrals, or (3) posting in the IHA Facebook community. This is a 2–3 day research and outreach process with uncertain response rate. Realistic time-to-identified-candidate: **10–20 days**. Not a viable primary channel given the June 17 decision gate.

### Candidate Density
IHA members skew toward commercial herb growers, buyers, and educators — not botanical illustrators. The IHA describes its membership as culinary and medical herbalists, teachers, writers, authors, photographers, horticulturalists, botanists, and herb business owners. Botanical illustrators are not a named category. Candidate density for the specific Phase 3 need (botanical illustration, women's health, publication quality): **scarce**.

### Price Point Expectations
IHA members in the botanical art space tend to be independent educators or commercial growers; those who do illustration as a secondary service may quote lower rates ($25–$55/hour) than AHG practitioners. However, finding them requires the workaround path described above.

### Recommendation
**Rank: 6 of 6.** IHA directory is non-operational. Do not use as a primary sourcing channel. Activate only if AHG outreach and Upwork both fail by June 12: post a brief in the IHA Facebook group asking for referrals to botanical illustrators who specialize in medicinal plants.

## Channel 3: Chestnut School of Herbal Medicine — Instructor Network

**URL**: chestnutherbs.com
**Contact**: partnerships@chestnutherbs.com

### Directory Access
Chestnut does not maintain a public-facing directory. The school has a core team of approximately 8 instructors documented on their website, plus a broader alumni network. Access to that alumni network requires direct contact with the school through the partnerships email. There is no browse-and-search function; access is referral-only through the school's gatekeeping.

The Chestnut School is documented in the Phase 3 context as the single best channel for the **herbalist writer track** (Goldenseal CITES, Immunity bundle). For illustration, its relevance depends on whether any instructors or senior alumni combine clinical herbalism with botanical art skills. Chestnut's curriculum includes detailed botanical photography and botanical graphics — some instructors are likely to know professional botanical illustrators in the Appalachian/Southeast network.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
Contact partnerships@chestnutherbs.com with a referral request. The school's response window is typically 2–5 business days. If a referral is provided, outreach to the referred illustrator and interview would add another 5–7 days. Realistic time-to-identified-candidate: **8–14 days from first contact**, which is tight against June 17 but achievable if the email goes out June 10.

This channel's value is referral quality: a Chestnut-referred illustrator arrives pre-vetted for species-level botanical accuracy, which is the hardest criterion to verify through a cold portfolio review.

### Candidate Density
Not a high-volume channel. Expect 1–3 referrals from Chestnut if the school is actively helpful. Even a single strong referral from this channel is worth pursuing given the species-accuracy requirement for women's health plant illustration.

### Price Point Expectations
Chestnut-affiliated illustrators or alumni are likely to price similarly to AHG-adjacent practitioners: $35–$65/hour or flat-rate commissions at $75–$150/illustration. The same budget mismatch applies as AHG unless scope is constrained. Frame the project as a partial engagement (10–20 key illustrations at publication quality) rather than the full 80–120 hour scope in the first contact.

### Recommendation
**Rank: 3 of 6.** High-quality referral potential, but slow response and narrow candidate pool. Email partnerships@chestnutherbs.com on June 10. Do not rely on this channel as the primary path, but treat any referral it produces as a Tier A candidate.

## Channel 4: Herbal Academy — Illustration Track

**URL**: theherbalacademy.com
**Contact**: partnerships@theherbalacademy.com
**Relevant course**: Botanical Drawing for Herbalists Course (theherbalacademy.com/product/botanical-drawing-for-herbalists-course/)

### Directory Access
The Herbal Academy does not maintain a public alumni directory. The organization has a team page listing staff and instructors, but alumni contact is not publicly searchable. The Herbal Academy explicitly offers a Botanical Drawing for Herbalists Course, which produces a pool of graduates with exactly the combined botanical knowledge + illustration skill that Phase 3 needs.

More directly relevant: the Herbal Academy features named botanical illustrators in their blog content. Joséphine Klerks (JosephineKlerks.com, @soulart.klerks) contributed a spring drawing exercise piece to the Herbal Academy blog. She is an herbalist-illustrator based in Sweden who describes work in watercolor, ink, and colored pencil with women's health and "Divine Feminine" plant themes. Her style leans spiritual-interpretive rather than scientific-line-art — she may not be the right fit for publication-quality botanical line art, but she is a documented example of the herbalist-illustrator profile the Herbal Academy network produces.

The faster path through Herbal Academy is not alumni search but instructor referral: email partnerships@theherbalacademy.com and ask whether any of their botanical illustration course instructors or Botanical Drawing for Herbalists graduates take freelance commissions.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
Email response from Herbal Academy partnerships: 3–5 business days. Referral outreach and candidate evaluation: additional 5–7 days. Realistic timeline: **10–14 days to confirmed candidate**. Tight against June 17 but achievable.

### Candidate Density
Moderate. The Botanical Drawing for Herbalists Course has graduated multiple cohorts of herbalist-illustrators. The Herbal Academy network is large (tens of thousands of students) and skews toward the women's health and plant medicine niche — a strong demographic match for Phase 3. However, most graduates are hobbyists or early-career illustrators, not publication-quality professionals. Filtering for professional publication-quality work within this pool requires careful screening.

### Price Point Expectations
Herbal Academy alumni-illustrators are more likely to be within the Phase 3 budget than AHG RHs or Chestnut instructors. Early-career herbalist-illustrators with 2–5 years of practice may quote $20–$45/hour or flat project rates in the $800–$1,500 range for a 10–15 illustration set. This is budget-compatible if the project is scoped as a defined set of illustrations rather than an hourly engagement.

### Recommendation
**Rank: 2 of 6** for botanical illustration specifically. The Herbal Academy has the highest concentration of the women's health plant + illustration combination in any network channel. Email partnerships@theherbalacademy.com on June 10. Simultaneously, reach out directly to Joséphine Klerks at JosephineKlerks.com for a cold inquiry (her style may be adaptable to the project's visual tone if she has line art in her portfolio alongside her spiritual botanical work).

## Channel 5: Upwork — Freelance Platform Search

**URL**: upwork.com/hire/botanical-illustration-freelancers/

### Directory Access
Fully public browsing. Upwork's freelancer marketplace allows any visitor to search profiles, view portfolios, and read reviews without creating an account. Posting a job to receive proposals requires an account. The platform shows 27+ botanical illustration freelancers as of early 2026, with more available through adjacent search terms (botanical art, scientific illustration, plant illustration, medicinal illustration).

Upwork is the only channel in this set where you can browse ranked, reviewed candidates with full portfolio visibility before making any contact. This makes it the fastest channel for initial candidate identification.

**Fee structure (client-side)**: Upwork charges clients a 5% marketplace fee on US contracts. A contractor quoting $1,200 costs the project $1,260 after fees. Maximum contractor quote to stay within the $1,350 ceiling: $1,286.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
Job post live to first proposals: **24–72 hours** for a well-written posting with a realistic budget. Screening proposals: 2–4 hours. Screening calls with top 3–5 candidates: 1–2 days. Offer and contract: 1 day. Total from posting to signed contract: **5–8 days** under favorable conditions. This is the fastest channel and the only one reliably capable of meeting the June 17 gate if the posting goes live June 10.

### Candidate Density
Abundant. Upwork has documented botanical illustration freelancers at multiple experience and price tiers. The platform enables search by skill tag (botanical illustration, scientific illustration, botanical art, plant illustration, line art, pen and ink illustration). Women's health plant specialization is not a searchable filter but can be assessed through portfolio review. Estimated pool of qualified candidates matching the Phase 3 profile: 10–25 within budget, 3–8 at publication quality within budget.

### Price Point Expectations
Upwork botanical illustration rates range from $15–$60/hour with a median around $25. However:
- Entry-level ($15–$25/hour): adequate for digital botanical illustrations but may not meet publication-quality line art standards
- Mid-tier ($25–$45/hour): the primary target range for Phase 3; experienced botanical illustrators with documented publication or product work
- Expert ($45–$80+/hour): established illustrators who likely exceed the $1,350 budget for 80–120 hour scope

For a flat-rate project at $1,000–$1,350 covering 12–18 publication-quality line art illustrations (the realistic deliverable count within 80–120 hours), Upwork candidates in the $25–$40/hour equivalent tier are the target. At $35/hour × 37 hours (a constrained scope), the project fits at $1,295.

### Recommendation
**Rank: 1 of 6.** Upwork is the primary channel. Post the job listing on June 10 with the optimized description (see UPWORK_JOB_POSTING_OPTIMIZATION.md). It is the only channel that can reliably surface candidates before the June 17 decision gate. All other channels run in parallel as sourcing supplements.

## Channel 6: Toptal — Pre-Vetted Freelancer Network

**URL**: toptal.com/designers/illustration

### Directory Access
Toptal does not offer public profile browsing for unregistered visitors in the same way Upwork does. Access to the full freelancer network requires submitting a client intake request through toptal.com/hire. Toptal's matching team then surfaces 2–3 pre-screened candidates within 48–72 hours. You interact with Toptal through an account manager, not through self-directed directory search.

Toptal does have public-facing "best illustrators" showcase pages (toptal.com/designers/illustration) showing 11 featured illustrators, but these are curated showcases rather than searchable directories.

### Time-to-Hire Estimate
Toptal's matching process: **48–72 hours** to receive first candidate matches. Discovery calls with matched candidates: 1–2 days. A 2-week no-risk trial period is available. Realistically, contract-to-start timeline is **5–10 days** after initiating contact — comparable to Upwork but with less candidate volume and higher prices.

If Toptal contact is initiated June 10, first candidate matches arrive June 12–13, allowing discovery calls June 13–15 and a signed contract by June 17 at the latest. This timeline is achievable but has no slack.

### Candidate Density
Toptal claims fewer than 3% of applicants pass their vetting process. The illustration talent pool is intentionally small and curated. Toptal does not specifically vet for herbalist knowledge or botanical accuracy — their vetting is for general design quality and communication. The pre-vetting is useful for general illustration quality but does not substitute for the botanical accuracy screening that Phase 3 requires (run the same species ID screening question used in the herbalist writer track on any Toptal candidate).

### Price Point Expectations
Toptal adds approximately 30–50% above the contractor's direct rate as a platform markup. The client is quoted a blended rate that does not show this breakdown separately. Toptal illustrators are positioned in the $60–$150/hour range on the client-facing side. At the Phase 3 budget ($1,000–$1,350), even the lowest Toptal illustrator rate of $60/hour produces a 17-hour engagement — insufficient for the 80–120 hour scope.

Toptal is therefore **budget-incompatible with the full Phase 3 illustration scope**. It can be used for a constrained premium engagement (5–8 hero illustrations at publication quality) if the illustrated content is the strategic priority rather than volume coverage.

A $500 refundable deposit is required to initiate the Toptal matching process, credited toward the first invoice. The $79 monthly subscription fee applies.

### Recommendation
**Rank: 5 of 6.** Toptal is not budget-compatible with the full scope. Activate only if Upwork produces no acceptable candidates by June 13 and the user is willing to scope down the illustration deliverables to a premium subset (cover illustrations + 5–6 key species). Even in that scenario, the $1,350 ceiling may be challenging to hold given Toptal's platform markup.

## Backup Sourcing: Community Channels

If Upwork and the herbalism school networks produce fewer than 5 qualified proposals by June 12, activate the following backup channels in order:

### Reddit

**r/botany** (reddit.com/r/botany): Active community of botanists and botanical artists. Post a "Commission Request" thread with project scope, budget range, and portfolio requirements. Response time: 24–72 hours. Community leans toward scientific illustration appreciation; several members are professional botanical illustrators. Posting format: brief 2–3 sentence description of the project + "PM me if you illustrate medicinal plants at publication quality."

**r/Illustration** (reddit.com/r/Illustration): General illustration community. Botanical illustration is a common sub-niche. Post in the weekly commission or job threads (these exist as recurring threads in this community). Response time: 24–72 hours.

**r/HireAnArtist** (reddit.com/r/HireAnArtist): Dedicated hire/commission thread subreddit. Structured for project listings. Post with title: "Botanical Illustrator Needed — Women's Health Plants, Publication-Quality Line Art, $1,000–$1,350." Response time: 12–48 hours for well-specified posts.

**r/freelance**: Post asking whether the community knows vetted botanical illustrators who combine herbalism knowledge with scientific illustration. Referral-seeking post, not a job posting.

### Instagram

Search hashtags: #botanicalillustration, #medicinalplantillustration, #herbalistillustrator, #scientificbotanicalillustration. Identify 5–10 illustrators with portfolio posts showing plant accuracy and line art quality. Send DM with a brief project inquiry. Response rate from Instagram cold DM: 20–35% within 48 hours from active accounts.

### Etsy / ASBA

The American Society of Botanical Artists (asba-art.org) has a Find a Teacher page and a member gallery. While not a hire-by-directory service, contacting ASBA directly (memberservices@asba-art.org) with a referral request may surface publication-quality illustrators within the membership. The ASBA member base includes close to 2,000 botanical artists across 39 countries — the largest single concentration of vetted botanical art talent available.
