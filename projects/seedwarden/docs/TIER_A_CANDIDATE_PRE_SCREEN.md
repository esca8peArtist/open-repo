# Tier A Candidate Pre-Screen
## Phase 3 Botanical Illustration — Women's Health Plants

**Prepared**: June 10, 2026
**Outreach start**: June 10, 2026
**Decision gate**: June 17, 2026 EOD
**Tier A definition**: Candidates whose portfolios and public profiles demonstrate publication-quality botanical illustration work with documented plant species accuracy. Confirmed availability and budget compatibility must be verified by June 15.

---

## Screening Methodology

The AHG and IHA member directories were assessed for botanical illustrator profiles. The IHA directory is currently offline ("Coming Soon" as of June 2026). The AHG directory is publicly searchable but indexes practitioners by herbalist specialty, not illustration skill. Botanical illustrators operating within the herbalism community are more easily identified through the Herbal Academy's content networks and through direct web research than through the AHG directory alone.

The three Tier A candidates below were identified through:
1. Herbal Academy blog contributors and course network (Herbal Academy channel)
2. Direct web research on botanical illustrators specializing in medicinal and women's health plants
3. The existing PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SOURCING_STRATEGY.md research (Adrian White — cross-track candidate)

Each candidate has a public portfolio accessible for pre-screening review. All three align with the Phase 3 women's health plant focus to a degree that justifies immediate outreach.

## Tier A Candidate 1: Anna Farba

**Website**: annafarba.com
**Contact**: a@annafarba.com
**Portfolio relevant to Phase 3**: annafarba.com/medicinal-plants-healing-herbs
**Style**: Hand-painted watercolor + pen-and-ink line art; medical/editorial botanical illustration
**Source channel**: Direct web research — appeared in botanical illustrator search results

### Background

Anna Farba is a professional freelance botanical and food illustrator working for packaging, publishing, and beauty clients. Her specific medicinal plant portfolio (annafarba.com/medicinal-plants-healing-herbs) includes documented illustrations of:
- Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)
- Elderflower/Elderberry (Sambucus nigra)
- St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum)
- Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale)
- Burdock (Arctium lappa)
- Calendula (Calendula officinalis)
- Goldenrod (Solidago sp.)
- Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica)
- Chicory (Cichorium intybus)
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)
- Rhodiola rosea
- Holy Basil/Tulsi (Ocimum tenuiflorum)
- And additional species

Her plant list overlaps directly with Phase 3 women's health bundle species. Several of the plants she has illustrated — Elderflower, Dandelion, Calendula, St. John's Wort — appear in or adjacent to the Phase 3 women's health guide content.

She explicitly offers both custom commission work and licensing of existing illustrations, and her "custom botanical drawing in black and white" page describes publication-quality pen-and-ink deliverables at high resolution (300 DPI minimum, TIFF/PNG/vector formats).

### Portfolio Assessment

**Women's health plant alignment**: Moderate-to-strong. Her medicinal plant portfolio does not include Black Cohosh, Vitex, or Red Raspberry Leaf specifically — the three most central Phase 3 women's health species. However, her demonstrated proficiency with Ashwagandha, Elderflower, Calendula, and St. John's Wort establishes that she can illustrate botanical complexity at the species-specific accuracy level. She is not an herbalist-illustrator (her background appears to be fine art + botanical illustration, not clinical herbalism), but her plant selection shows consistent interest in the medicinal plant space.

**Publication quality**: Confirmed by client roster (publishing, beauty, editorial) and format deliverables (TIFF/PNG at print resolution, vector if needed). Her work is used in product packaging and books, which sets a publication-quality baseline.

**Line art capability**: Confirmed by her "custom botanical drawing in black and white" service page. She offers both watercolor and black-and-white line art commissions.

**Estimated rate**: Anna Farba does not publish rates; fees are custom-quoted based on usage scope, exclusivity, and duration. For comparison context, a 12–18 illustration set at publication quality from a professional working at this level typically ranges $1,500–$4,000 at commercial rates. She may be above the Phase 3 budget ceiling at standard rates. The strongest outreach angle is: (1) scoped project rather than hourly, (2) non-exclusive educational license, and (3) women's health plant focus as a shared interest that may motivate a favorable rate for a project aligned with her existing portfolio subject matter.

**Action for June 10 outreach**: Email a@annafarba.com using the Tier A Initial Outreach Email template (see CONTINGENCY_SOURCING_PLAYBOOK.md Appendix A). Reference her medicinal plants portfolio specifically. Ask whether she is available for a June–September educational guide project, describe the scope as 12–15 illustration plates at her quoted rate, and request her current project availability before discussing budget.

## Tier A Candidate 2: Joséphine Klerks (SoulArt Klerks)

**Website**: JosephineKlerks.com
**Instagram**: @soulart.klerks
**Facebook**: Josephine Klerks SoulArt Klerks
**Contact**: Via JosephineKlerks.com contact form
**Source channel**: Herbal Academy blog contributor (appeared in Herbal Academy drawing exercise post, June 2026 research)

### Background

Joséphine Klerks is a herbalist-illustrator based in Sweden who operates SoulArt Klerks, a print and textile studio. She describes her background as "mixed heritage — Finnish, Dutch, and Indonesian" with a creative practice centered on botanical art and herbalism. Her work mediums include watercolor, ink, colored pencils, pastel crayons, and digital drawing. She has documented interest in herbal pigment-making (creating custom watercolors from plants).

She is enrolled in the Herbal Academy's Botanical Skin Care Course and completed Asia Suler's Intuitive Plant Medicine course. She has been featured as a guest contributor on the Herbal Academy blog for botanical drawing exercises — specifically a spring equinox drawing exercise.

### Portfolio Assessment

**Women's health plant alignment**: Strong thematic alignment. Her documented work includes pieces titled "Hawthorn Spirit" and "Mother Rose" — both of which reflect women's health and plant medicine themes. Her artistic framing explicitly centers the "Divine Feminine" and "plant spirits," which aligns with the women's health focus of Phase 3 better than any other known candidate.

**Publication quality**: Her work has been featured by the Herbal Academy (an established herbal education brand) and she operates a print and textile studio, which implies print-resolution capability. However, her work leans toward intuitive botanical art rather than scientific line art. The critical question for Phase 3 is whether she can produce species-accurate line art for plant identification purposes alongside her more interpretive work.

**Line art capability**: Ink is listed as one of her mediums. Whether she has a documented portfolio of botanical line art at publication quality (as distinct from interpretive watercolor compositions) requires direct inquiry.

**Herbalist background**: Confirmed. She is actively studying herbalism at the Herbal Academy and has completed plant medicine coursework. This is the clearest herbalist-illustrator combination of the three Tier A candidates — she brings both the plant knowledge and the illustration skill.

**Estimated rate**: Unknown. Sweden-based; rates may be quoted in EUR or SEK. For a freelance illustrator with her profile (active print studio, Herbal Academy featured artist), European freelance illustration rates for educational commissions are typically €40–€90/hour or flat-rate per illustration. At the low end (€40/hour equivalent), the Phase 3 budget is compatible for a 25–30 illustration hour scope. Currency conversion and payment logistics (EUR to USD via Wise or similar) must be confirmed.

**Limitation**: She is Sweden-based, which introduces time zone coordination (CET = UTC+1/+2) and payment logistics. Neither of these is a blocking issue for a remote illustration engagement, but both must be addressed in the contract.

**Action for June 10 outreach**: Contact via JosephineKlerks.com contact form or @soulart.klerks Instagram DM. Lead with the Herbal Academy connection and the women's health plant focus. Ask whether she does commissioned botanical illustration work alongside her print studio, and whether she would be interested in a June–September illustration series for an herbal educational guide.
