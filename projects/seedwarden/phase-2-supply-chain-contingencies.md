**Detection trigger**: No shipping confirmation email within 48 hours of order placement,

**Contact before activating backup**: Call 503-549-9559 or email orders@mountainroseherbs.com.
Confirm whether the order is in transit and get a revised delivery estimate. If it is
scheduled to arrive May 17 or earlier, hold — the shoot window is May 19–22 and a May 17
arrival still clears the 48-hour acclimatization window for dried specimens (which require no
acclimatization; they can be photographed immediately on arrival).

**Backup vendor: Starwest Botanicals** (starwest-botanicals.com)
- Products: Dried ginseng root (whole) and dried goldenseal root — both in stock year-round
- Price: Ginseng $12–19/oz, Goldenseal $14–22/oz (comparable to Mountain Rose Herbs)
- Processing: 1–2 business days from Sacramento, CA warehouse
- Shipping: USPS Priority Mail (3–5 days to most of US) or FedEx Ground
- Lead time total: 4–6 days from order to delivery
- Reliability: High — established organic supplier, similar certification standards
- Action: Place order same day as Mountain Rose miss confirmation. Cancel Mountain Rose
  when Starwest ships.

**Backup vendor: Richters Herbs** (richters.com — Ontario, Canada)
- Products: Dried medicinal herb roots including goldenseal and American ginseng
- Note: Richters ships internationally (Canada to US); customs transit adds 2–4 days over
  domestic options. Lead time: 7–10 days. Only use if both domestic options fail.
- Price: Comparable to Mountain Rose Herbs; duty costs minimal for small quantities
- Action threshold: Richters is a tertiary option. Activate only if Starwest Botanicals
  is also out of stock or cannot ship within the required window.

**Local retail (Portland / major-city buyers) — same-day option**:
Goldenseal dried root is stocked at natural food retailers in bulk herb sections: Whole
Foods Market, independent co-ops, and dedicated herb shops. Ginseng dried root is less
common in bulk but is stocked at Korean grocery stores as whole ginseng root (fresh or
dried Korean red ginseng), which is visually equivalent for photography.
- Cost: $18–30/oz (30–50% retail premium over mail-order)
- Lead time: Zero — same-day purchase
- Action threshold: If no domestic mail-order vendor can deliver by May 20, execute local
  retail purchase the same day the decision is made. Log vendor name, cost, and purchase

**iHerb and Vitacost — not recommended for this use case**:
iHerb (iherb.com) and Vitacost (vitacost.com) stock processed/packaged supplement forms
of ginseng and goldenseal (capsules, tinctures, powders) rather than whole dried root.
The whole root structure — the forked ginseng root morphology and the yellow goldenseal
root cross-section — is the essential visual element for guide photography. These retailers
do not reliably carry photogenic whole root specimens. Check Korean grocery stores for whole
ginseng root before falling back to iHerb or Amazon.

**Amazon Prime — emergency only**:
Frontier Co-op and Starwest Botanicals sell through Amazon Prime with 1–2 day delivery.
Quality is equivalent to direct-vendor purchase. Cost is 15–30% higher due to marketplace
markup. Use if local retail fails and time is critical.

### 1.2 Strictly Medicinal Seeds and Prairie Moon Nursery — Fallbacks If May 20–27 Window Is Missed

These vendors supply the live endangered species specimens: Black Cohosh potted plant
(Strictly Medicinal, strictlymedicinalseeds.com) and Ramps bulblets plus Trillium bare root
(Prairie Moon Nursery, prairiemoon.com). Lead times of 7–12 days (Strictly Medicinal) and
7–14 days (Prairie Moon) mean orders placed May 13 have expected arrivals of May 20–25 and
May 20–27, respectively.

**Maximum acceptable slip**: May 28. If either vendor's tracking shows delivery after May 28,
activate iNaturalist CC-BY sourcing for that species immediately — do not wait for the delivery.

**Fallback vendor: Etsy native plant growers**
- Search terms: "black cohosh live plant," "ramps bulbs cultivated," "trillium grandiflorum
  live," "native plant appalachian live"
- Filter: Eastern US sellers (NC, VA, WV, TN, KY, PA preferred for transit speed and
  seasonal compatibility)
- Before ordering, message the seller to confirm: (a) cultivated origin, not wild-harvested,
  (b) can ship within 2 business days, (c) actively growing, not dormant
- Lead time: 3–7 days if seller ships within 2 days (seller location determines transit)
- Cost: $10–25 per species (comparable to commercial nurseries)
- Action: If Strictly Medicinal and Prairie Moon both show no tracking number by May 19,
  search Etsy same day and place orders.

**Fallback vendor: Local native plant society plant sales**
Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) is sold at native plant society spring sales through May in
the Appalachian region. Zero lead time; call ahead for availability.
- NC Native Plant Society chapters (ncnps.org) — spring plant sales active through May
- Western Carolina Botanical Club — periodic plant exchanges
- Regional botanical garden sales and university extension plant sales
- Cost: $4–12 per plant (lowest cost option)
- Limitation: Availability is unpredictable; no advance inventory confirmation

**Fallback: Fresh ramp leaves from farmers market (Ramps only)**
Fresh ramp leaves from a farmers market or specialty grocery (Whole Foods, Asian grocery,
co-op) are a superior photography prop over mail-order bulblets — buyers recognize the
distinctive leaf shape immediately. Purchase May 18–19 regardless of Prairie Moon order
status. Refrigerate wrapped in damp paper towel. Photograph within 24 hours of purchase.
Cost: $3–8. Lead time: zero.

**Fallback: iNaturalist CC-BY sourcing sprint (all species)**
For every at-risk species where live plant fails to arrive, iNaturalist CC-BY Research Grade
observations provide equivalent coverage. Filter URL structure:
`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_id=[ID]&license=CC-BY&quality_grade=research&photos=true`

Species-specific supply assessment:
- Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa): abundant — high observation density in Appalachian region;
  leaf habit, flower raceme, and colony shots well covered across multiple observers
- Trillium grandiflorum: very abundant — the most-photographed woodland wildflower in eastern
  North America; all growth stages covered
- Ramps (Allium tricoccum): good — late April through May observations are richest; archived
  seasons available; leaf habit fully covered
- Bloodroot (Sanguinaria canadensis): good for bloom stage; thinner for rhizome/root
  cross-section — BHL 19th-century botanical illustration fills this gap

**Action threshold**: If either Strictly Medicinal or Prairie Moon shows no tracking number
by May 19, execute iNaturalist sourcing sprint for the affected species immediately. A sprint
covers 3 species in 2–3 hours. No guide production delay results.

### 1.3 Plant Substitution Matrix

If a species is entirely unavailable (physical specimen and iNaturalist CC-BY both
insufficient), the following substitutions maintain zone-card and guide viability without
compromising the educational content standard. Each substitution is assessed for botanical
accuracy, conservation relevance, and photo sourcing feasibility.

| Primary Species | Substitution | Substitution Rationale | Zone-Card Viability | Notes |
|-----------------|-------------|------------------------|--------------------|----|
| American Ginseng (Panax quinquefolius) | Asiatic Ginseng (Panax ginseng) dried root | Same genus; near-identical root morphology; easy to photograph; available at Korean grocery stores | Maintained — note species origin clearly; both are CITES Appendix II | Use only for prop photography; update attribution to "Panax ginseng (substitute for comparative morphology)" |
| Goldenseal root (Hydrastis canadensis) | Turmeric root (Curcuma longa) | Not botanically related, but both show intense yellow root cross-section; use only if goldenseal root photo is urgently needed for proof-of-concept | Partial — must explicitly caption as turmeric analogy, not goldenseal | Last resort; very low preference. Goldenseal CC-BY from iNaturalist is strongly preferred |
| Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) live plant | White Baneberry (Actaea pachypoda) OR Bugbane (Actaea simplex) | Same genus; compound leaf structure is visually similar at a distance; widely available at native plant nurseries | Partial — leaf structure differs in detail; adequate for habit photos but not leaf-detail close-up | Caption carefully; suitable for lifestyle/habitat context shots only |
| Ramps (Allium tricoccum) leaves | Wild garlic (Allium vineale) OR chives (Allium schoenoprasum) | Same genus; linear leaf shape; similar cross-section for identification comparison | Partial — leaf width and tip shape differ from ramps; usable only for scale reference or multi-species comparison | Fresh ramp leaves from farmers market are strongly preferred; use Allium substitute only if no ramps sourced by May 20 |
| Trillium grandiflorum | Trillium erectum OR Trillium sessile | Same genus; same three-petal, three-leaf structure; T. erectum is available in Appalachian native plant sales | Maintained for habit and structure shots — note species clearly | iNaturalist has T. grandiflorum in abundance; substitution needed only for live specimen photography |
| Bloodroot (Sanguinaria canadensis) rhizome | BHL 19th-century botanical illustration (public domain) | No botanical substitute has equivalent orange-red sap; illustration is the correct fallback and is historically accurate | Fully maintained — BHL illustrations are visually appropriate for conservation-themed guides | BHL illustration reference: Flora of North America, Sanguinaria canadensis plate; full citation in ENDANGERED_SPECIES_PHOTO_PIPELINE.md |

**Zone-card viability rule**: A guide maintains zone-card viability if the primary identification
features (leaf shape, root color, flower structure) are visually represented with accurate
attribution. Substitutes are acceptable when captioned correctly. No substitution is
acceptable without explicit labeling.

## Section 2: Minimum Viable Launch — Canva Palette Refresh Delay

### 2.1 Scenario: Endangered Species Series Palette Not Set Up by May 26

The Canva Brand Kit "Endangered Species Series" palette adds 7 colors specific to the
conservation-themed series:
- Deep Forest Floor: #1C2B1A
- Aged Bark: #3B2A1A
- Pale Parchment: #F0E6C8
- Sage Mist: #7A9A6E
- Bone White: #F5F1E8
- Conservation Red: #8B2000
- Species Gold: #C49A2A

If Brand Kit setup is not complete before May 26 guide production begins, two options exist:

**Option A — Manual hex entry (preferred fallback, zero quality loss)**
Canva's color picker accepts hex values typed by hand. Adding approximately 2–3 minutes per
color application per session, for a 5-guide series, this adds an estimated 30–50 minutes of
total production overhead versus Brand Kit shortcut usage. The visual output is identical —
the same hex values appear in the final PDF regardless of input method.

**Option B — Launch with existing Seedwarden palette, refresh in Phase 3**
The existing Seedwarden Brand Kit palette is a complete botanical design palette. The
Endangered Species extension signals conservation-education intent through Conservation Red
and Species Gold. Without these two colors, the series reads as a standard botanical guide
rather than a conservation-themed one. This is a positioning signal, not an educational
content gap.

Quality impact rating (1–5 scale):
- Educational content quality: 5/5 unchanged
- Visual distinctiveness from generic botanical guides: 3/5 (reduced by ~40%)
- Conservation-education positioning: 3/5 (Conservation Red status callout boxes become
  generic red; Species Gold name accents revert to standard gold or are omitted)
- Buyer perception on Etsy: 4/5 (buyers in this market do not recognize palette specifics)

| Guide | Palette Dependency | Can Launch with Existing Palette | Notes |

## Section 4: Critical-Path Compression Options

### 4.1 Which Guides Can Compress from 15 Days to 10 Days Without Quality Loss

The 15-day production window (May 15–30) was designed with daily milestones for procurement,
photography, and Canva work. A compressed 10-day window (May 21–30) is achievable for
specific guides without quality loss. The constraint is photo sourcing — Canva production
itself is fast once photos are confirmed.

| Guide | Standard Window | Compressed Window | Quality at Compression | Compression Bottleneck |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| American Ginseng | May 15–26 (sourcing + production) | May 21–26 | Full — dried root arrives by May 15; photos confirmed by May 21 | Canva template Day 1 (May 26) is the Canva constraint, not sourcing |
| Goldenseal | May 15–27 | May 22–27 | Full — same sourcing path as Ginseng | Canva Day 2 (May 27); no compression needed |
| Black Cohosh | May 20–28 | May 22–28 | Full — iNaturalist CC-BY is the complete photo set; confirmable in 1 hour any time | No physical specimen required; 0-day sourcing lead time |
| Bloodroot | May 22–29 | May 24–29 | Full if BHL illustration is used; reduced if rhizome photo required | Rhizome photography timing (30-minute oxidation window); BHL eliminates this constraint |
| Ramps | May 24–29 | May 24–29 | Full — fresh market leaves are zero lead-time; iNaturalist CC-BY archive available | No compression needed; already a sprint day |

**Result**: All 5 guides are achievable in a 10-day compressed window (May 21–30) without
quality loss, provided iNaturalist CC-BY is accepted as the baseline photo source and the
Ginseng/Goldenseal dried roots arrive by May 21 via local retail if mail-order fails.

### 4.2 Writing Parallelization — How Many Guides Can Run Simultaneously

Canva guide production is structured as sequential template duplication — each guide copies
the previous one and substitutes species-specific content. Parallelization across guides
requires multiple Canva files open simultaneously, which is technically possible but increases
error risk (wrong-guide edits, inconsistent formatting decisions).

**Recommended parallelization level: 2 guides maximum**

- Day 1 (May 26): American Ginseng master template (solo; no parallelization — this establishes
  all design decisions)
- Day 2 (May 27): Goldenseal (solo; applies Ginseng decisions to new species; 3–4 hours)
- Day 3 (May 28): Black Cohosh AND internal QA pass on Ginseng + Goldenseal (parallel; user
  runs Canva while reviewing prior guides in a second window)
- Day 4 (May 29): Bloodroot AM + Ramps PM (sequential within the day; not true parallelization
  but both in the same session day — acceptable)

**If stacked delays require compressing Days 3–4 into a single day (May 28 or May 29)**:
Black Cohosh and Bloodroot can both be produced in a single uninterrupted day (combined 5–7
hours; both are short template duplications from established master). Do not attempt this if
the master template is not finalized — errors cascade to all subsequent guides.

**Writing capacity ceiling without quality loss**: 2 guides per day for days 2–4; 1 guide
per day for Day 1 (master template). Attempting 3 guides in a single day introduces
attribution errors, inconsistent conservation status language, and formatting drift. Maintain
the 2-guide daily ceiling regardless of schedule pressure.

### 4.3 Canva Design Batching — Zone-Card Template Finalization to Parallel Application

The zone-card production workflow documented in CANVA_ZONE_CARD_BATCH_WORKFLOW.md and
ZONE_CARD_PRODUCTION_TIMELINE.md describes the batch approach: finalize one zone-card master
template, then apply to all guides in parallel by duplicating and substituting species-specific
zone and range data.

**Batch application timeline under compressed schedule**:

1. May 24 (Brand Kit setup day): After completing Brand Kit setup (45–90 min), build the
   zone-card master template as a second task (estimated 1–2 hours). Both tasks share the
   same Canva session and same palette; completing them together saves a context-switch.

2. May 26 (Canva Day 1): After completing the Ginseng master guide template, duplicate the
   zone-card template for Ginseng and apply species-specific zone hardiness data (USDA zones
   3–8 for American Ginseng in Appalachian range). This is a 20–30 minute addition to Day 1.

3. May 27–29: Each subsequent guide day includes a 20–30 minute zone-card duplication at
   end of day. Five zone cards total: approximately 2.5 hours of zone-card work distributed
   across 4 production days, or concentrated in a single 2–3 hour session on May 25 if the
   template is ready.

**Compression option**: If production time is tight on May 27–29, produce all 4 remaining
zone cards (Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, Ramps) in a single 2–3 hour batch session
on the morning of May 29 or morning of May 30 before the QA pass. Zone cards are structurally
simpler than full guides and lend themselves to rapid duplication once the template is finalized.

## Section 7: Cost-Impact Analysis

### 7.1 Backup Supplier Price Premium

| Scenario | Baseline Cost | Backup Cost | Price Premium | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Mountain Rose Herbs miss → Starwest Botanicals | $28–42 (ginseng + goldenseal, 1 oz each) | $26–41 + $10–15 expedited shipping | $10–15 premium (shipping differential) | Starwest unit cost is comparable; premium is shipping |
| Mountain Rose Herbs miss → local retail | $28–42 | $36–60 (retail markup 30–50%) | $8–18 premium | Zero wait time; worth the premium for certainty |
| Mountain Rose Herbs miss → Amazon Prime | $28–42 | $32–50 + $0 shipping (Prime) | $4–8 premium | 1–2 day delivery; Frontier Co-op or Starwest via Amazon |
| Richters Herbs (Canada, tertiary) | $28–42 | $30–45 + $12–20 international shipping + 2–5 days customs | $14–23 premium | Only if all domestic options fail; high lead time risk |
| Strictly Medicinal / Prairie Moon miss → Etsy native plant seller | $16–27 (bulblets + live plant) | $20–50 + $8–15 shipping | $12–38 premium | Wide range depending on seller; message before ordering to confirm price |
| All live plant vendors miss → iNaturalist CC-BY only | $16–27 | $0 | -$16–27 savings | No cost; 2–3 hour sourcing sprint; no quality loss for guide production |

**Total maximum backup supplier premium** (worst case, all vendors miss, all physical props
replaced through retail or expedited alternatives): $40–75 above baseline. This is well within
the 15% contingency buffer documented in PHASE_2_LAUNCH_LOGISTICS.md Section 1.4 ($13–24 on
a $101–187 total budget). The contingency buffer is insufficient to cover the full worst case;
however, the realistic scenario is a partial miss requiring $15–25 in backup costs.

### 7.2 Location Pivot Cost

| Scenario | Permit / Location Cost | Indoor Studio Cost | Net Cost Difference |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Asheville Botanical Garden permit (field shoot) | $75–150 per day | — | — |

### 7.3 Design Quality Impact — Acceptable-But-Suboptimal vs. Delayed Launch

This analysis is the core of the palette refresh decision (Section 2).

| Approach | Launch Date | Design Quality Score | Revenue Estimate (First 30 Days) | Risk |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|----------------------------------|------|
| Full Endangered Species palette (as planned) | May 30 | 100% | Baseline | Lowest |
| Manual hex entry (Brand Kit not set up) | May 30 | 100% (same output) | Same as baseline | Very low (30 min extra work) |
| Existing Seedwarden palette (Brand Kit delayed) | May 30 | ~80% | ~5% below baseline (reduced premium positioning) | Low — quality is still publication-grade |
| Delay launch for Brand Kit setup | June 4–10 | 100% | ~15–25% below baseline (lost launch momentum) | Moderate — timing matters for Kit email cohort |

**Conclusion**: A design quality slip from 100% to 80% costs an estimated 5% of first-30-day
revenue. A 5-day launch slip costs an estimated 15–25% of first-30-day revenue. The correct
decision is always to launch on schedule with the available design quality. Never delay a
launch for a design element that has a same-quality workaround (manual hex entry) or an
acceptable-quality fallback (existing palette).

## Summary: The One Rule

Every supply chain contingency documented above has a fallback that preserves the May 30
launch date. The only scenario that moves the launch date is Canva production not starting
by May 27 — and even then, a 3-guide May 30 soft launch preserves the marketing event.

**The May 30 launch event happens on May 30. Period.**

The supply chain is a quality multiplier, not a launch gate. Physical specimens improve
guides. iNaturalist CC-BY photos make guides. Dried roots from local retail are photographically
equivalent to mail-order roots. The indoor studio produces root and leaf photos equal to or
better than field conditions.

When in doubt about any supplier, vendor, or design question: execute the fallback now,
not later. Decisions made by May 25 cost nothing. Decisions deferred to May 28 cost
production time.
