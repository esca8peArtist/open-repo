**Respiratory Health — Clear Airways, Immune Resilience, and Lung Support** covers 8 herbs with enough cultivation and preparation depth to grow, harvest, and use them correctly.

## BUNDLE 3 — Sleep & Nervines: Herbal Support for Restful Sleep and Nervous System Resilience

### Primary Headline Options

**A**: "Grow the herbs that support deep rest — valerian, passionflower, lemon balm, and five more"
**B**: "Sleep & Nervines: because the sleep market got the herbs right but the timing wrong"
**C**: "Eight nervine herbs, with the cultivation and preparation depth to actually use them"

*Recommended: Option B for email/social landing (distinctive hook); Option A for Etsy (clear and searchable).*

### Subheadline

"Valerian, Passionflower, Lemon Balm, Lavender, Hops, California Poppy, Skullcap, Chamomile — cultivation, harvest, and preparation for your specific sleep challenge."

### Body Copy (~300 words)

Not all sleep herbs work the same way — and most guides do not tell you that.

Hypnotics (like valerian) induce sleep. Anxiolytics (like passionflower) calm the nervous system. Adaptogens (like ashwagandha) build resilience over time. Using an adaptogen when you need a hypnotic gives you a protocol that does not work.

**Sleep & Nervines — Herbal Support for Restful Sleep, Calm Nervous System, and Stress Resilience** maps these distinctions precisely, so you reach for the right herb at the right time.

**8 herbs covered in full**: Valerian Root (Valeriana officinalis), Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata), Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis), Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia), Hops (Humulus lupulus), California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica), Skullcap (Scutellaria lateriflora), and Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla / Chamaemelum nobile).

**The valerian section**: Explains why stored valerian root is more potent than fresh — valerenic acid concentration increases during drying. This is why commercial fresh-dried valerian often underperforms compared to root stored for 1–2 years. You will not find this explained anywhere else in a consumer guide.

**The chamomile section**: German and Roman chamomile have different phytochemistry (apigenin vs. bisabolol as primary actives). This guide covers both, with cultivation and harvest notes for each.

**Sleep herb timing**: Lemon balm in the afternoon. Valerian 30–60 minutes before bed. Skullcap for anxiety and nervous tension (not sleep induction). California poppy for restlessness and light sleep. Timing is the protocol.

**Safety**: MAOI interactions for passionflower. Sedative drug interactions for valerian. Pregnancy cautions for California poppy. Drug interaction section is complete and specific.

**5 preparations**: Sleep tea blend, valerian-passionflower tincture, lemon balm sleep syrup, lavender-infused pillow spray, multi-herb daytime nervine formula.
