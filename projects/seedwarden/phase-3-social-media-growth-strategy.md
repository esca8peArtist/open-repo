# Seedwarden Phase 3: Social Media & Paid Advertising Growth Strategy

**Context**: Phase 1 launches imminently (8 text-heavy digital products + native plants guide). Phase 3 activates after 1-2 months of Etsy conversion data. This document is the execution blueprint: research done, playbook ready, no discovery needed at launch time.

---

## 1. TikTok Competitive Landscape

### The Niche Is Real and Large

Homesteading on TikTok is not a hobbyist backwater. The hashtag `#homestead` alone has 5.4 billion views across 458,000+ posts. `#homesteading` adds another 2.4 billion views. Aggregated across related tags — `#farmlife`, `#selfsufficient`, `#offgridliving`, `#seedsaving`, `#foodpreservation` — the combined viewership is in the tens of billions. TikTok's algorithm actively amplifies this content because food-growing, self-sufficiency, and cottagecore content generates strong completion rates and emotional engagement. Gen Z and Millennials are the primary audiences, and both skew toward practical skills they can apply immediately — exactly what Seedwarden's products address.

### The Top Creators and What They're Doing

The dominant accounts in this space are not prepping-first or survival-first — they are lifestyle-first with practical skill content embedded. The largest is **Hannah Neeleman (@ballerinafarm)**, a former NYC ballet dancer turned Utah cattle-farm wife with 10.5 million followers. Her content is visually stunning lifestyle footage — family dinners, farm mornings, bread-baking — with almost no product sales in the frame. She is aspirational, not instructional. At 6.5 million followers, **Matt Mathews (@matt_mathews)** blends Southern humor with farm life and has built a mainstream crossover audience. Neither of these is a direct Seedwarden competitor or partnership target — they are too broad, too expensive, and too entertainment-focused.

More relevant are mid-tier accounts in the 500K–2M range. **Morgan Gold (@goldshawfarm)** has 1.7–2.2 million followers across sources and produces Vermont farm lifestyle content that includes livestock, food preservation, and seasonal rhythms. **@itsbreellis** (772K) operates as a backyard homesteader from Kansas and consistently covers urban-scale skills like container gardening and seed saving. **@barefeetandmimosas** (400K+) covers kitchen preservation, food storage, and homeschooling — a near-perfect audience overlap with Seedwarden's customer profile. **@thermal\_and\_oaks** (367K) focuses on non-toxic living and chicken coops, explicitly targets health-conscious homesteaders, and already uses product links in bio. **@can't\_contain\_us** (638K) documents container-house homesteading, making them a direct match for Seedwarden's apartment and container gardening products.

### Content Format Analysis

The top-performing content in this niche follows a predictable formula: short-form educational video (21–60 seconds) with a strong 3-second hook, practical demonstration, and a result the viewer can apply immediately. The specific content categories that generate the highest completion rates are: food preservation demonstrations (canning salsa, fermenting vegetables), seed-saving tutorials, "what to do with your harvest" guides, and "things I wish I knew before starting a homestead" lists. Process content — timelapse of a raised bed being built, a jar of sauerkraut fermenting — performs extremely well because it is satisfying to watch even without sound.

TikTok's 2025 algorithm rewards completion rate above all else. A 30-second video where 70% of viewers watch to the end will outrank a 3-minute video with 20% completion. For Seedwarden, this means: never make a video for TikTok that is longer than 60 seconds. Teach one thing, show the result, end. The hook must be a question or a statement that creates curiosity in the first 3 seconds: "This is why your seeds keep dying" or "You're spending $40/month on something you can preserve yourself."

### Hashtag Strategy

The optimal TikTok hashtag stack for Seedwarden content uses 3–5 tags total, mixing one broad tag, 2–3 mid-tier niche tags, and one micro-niche tag. A working example:

- Broad (billions of views): `#homesteading` or `#farmlife`
- Mid-tier (millions of views): `#seedsaving`, `#foodpreservation`, `#growyourownfood`
- Micro-niche (hundreds of thousands of views): `#urbangardening`, `#containergarden`, `#heirloomseeds`

Avoid: `#fyp`, `#viral`, `#foryou` — these are widely reported to have no algorithmic effect and dilute hashtag budget. The 2026 data from Sprout Social and Sked Social confirms 3–5 highly relevant niche hashtags outperform 10–30 mixed tags.

### Posting Frequency and Monetization Thresholds

For a new TikTok account, the recommendation from platform-level data is daily posting for the first 60–90 days to build algorithmic momentum, then stepping back to 4–5 videos per week once consistent traction is established. This is the single largest time investment in Phase 3 organic. Batch production — filming 5–7 short videos in a single session once per week — makes this sustainable.

TikTok's Creator Fund (now the Creativity Program) requires 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in the prior 30 days to activate. At 10K followers, platform-native revenue is negligible: $50–$300/month from the Creativity Program. But the commercial value is not platform payouts — it is the ability to drive traffic to Etsy via bio link. At 10K–50K followers, brand deals pay $100–$500 per sponsored video; at 50K–100K, $500–$2,000. For Seedwarden in Phase 3, the near-term goal is not brand sponsorships — it is using TikTok as a free traffic funnel to Etsy listings.

Engagement rates confirm this is achievable: TikTok accounts under 100K followers average 7.5% engagement — more than double the rate of million-follower accounts. Educational content specifically averages 9.5% engagement. Seedwarden's content is definitionally educational. A small, targeted account in this niche can drive disproportionate Etsy click-through.

## 2. Instagram and Pinterest Strategy

### Instagram: Discovery to Consideration

Instagram's role in the Seedwarden funnel is mid-funnel: someone finds Seedwarden on TikTok or Pinterest, visits the Instagram page to assess legitimacy, and then decides whether to click through to Etsy. This means Instagram content must be polished enough to act as a portfolio, even if it doesn't drive significant discovery volume on its own.

The homesteading niche average engagement rate on Instagram is in the range of 2–5% for mid-size accounts, compared to the platform-wide average of 0.79%. This is a niche where audiences are genuinely interested and accounts are not saturated with corporate content. Reels are the dominant growth lever: Meta's Q3 2025 report shows Reels generate 67% of Instagram's total engagement. Static posts and carousels should be used for educational value (infographics, plant zone maps, fermentation charts) but Reels drive discoverability.

The effective Instagram hashtag stack for homesteading uses 5–10 tags: mix of `#homesteading` (millions of posts), `#seedsaving`, `#foodpreservation`, `#growyourownfood`, `#heirloomgardens`, `#urbanhomestead`, `#sustainableliving`, and one or two account-size-appropriate micro-tags under 100K posts. Posting hashtags in the first comment rather than the caption is a style preference that reduces visual clutter without affecting distribution.

Posting cadence for a growing Instagram account: 3–4 Reels per week, 1–2 carousel posts with educational content, and daily Stories. Stories are where audience relationship is built — behind-the-scenes product creation, polls ("Which guide would you use first?"), Q&A, and informal updates. A consistent aesthetic (earth tones, simple typography, real photography rather than stock) reinforces the Seedwarden brand identity.

### Pinterest: The Highest-ROI Organic Channel for Etsy

Pinterest is structurally different from TikTok and Instagram: it is a search engine, not a social feed. Users arrive with intent — they are looking for "seed saving guide," "container garden plan," or "food preservation printable." They click out at much higher rates than from any other social platform. One Etsy seller documented growing monthly views from 500 to 50,000 using Pinterest alone, attributing this almost entirely to keyword-optimized vertical pins linked directly to Etsy listings. Pinterest drives 33% more referral traffic to e-commerce than Facebook.

The pin lifespan is the key structural advantage: while a TikTok video has a 24–72 hour active window before it dies algorithmically, a Pinterest pin accumulates clicks and impressions for months to years. One well-optimized pin for "seed saving field manual" can drive Etsy clicks for 18+ months. This is compounding, passive traffic that requires upfront creation but no ongoing maintenance.

The Pinterest-to-Etsy funnel is direct: pin links directly to the Etsy product listing page. No intermediate blog or landing page needed. For each of Seedwarden's 20 products, a minimum of 3–5 pin variations should be created (different visual angles, different text overlays, different color backgrounds) and distributed across relevant boards. Boards should be named with keyword intent: "Seed Saving Guides and Resources," "Food Preservation Printables," "Container Gardening Plans," "Urban Homesteading Downloads."

Content format for Pinterest: vertical image (2:3 ratio), text overlay with the product benefit or a search-intent phrase, clean product mockup showing the guide open on a tablet or phone. Video pins (5–10 seconds of pages flipping, or a timelapse of a garden growing) are prioritized by Pinterest's algorithm. Carousel pins work well for bundles. Posting cadence: 2–3 fresh pins per day, or 30–50 pins per month scheduled via Tailwind. Consistency matters more than volume spikes.

### Content Differentiation Across Platforms

The same core content serves all three platforms but must be reformatted:

| Platform | Format | Tone | Primary Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 30–60s vertical video | Immediate, hook-driven, educational | Discovery + traffic spike |
| Instagram Reels | 15–45s video | Slightly more polished, personal | Trust building + community |
| Instagram Carousels | 5–10 static slides | Educational, authoritative | Saves + shares |
| Pinterest | Vertical image or 5–10s video | Search-intent, benefit-forward | Long-tail Etsy traffic |

A single content piece — say, a tutorial on seed saving — can be shot as a 45-second TikTok, trimmed to 20 seconds for Instagram Reel, turned into a 6-slide carousel ("6 Seeds Worth Saving Before Winter"), and then the best frame becomes a Pinterest pin with the text "Download the Seed Saving Field Manual — $14."

## 3. Competitive Creator Breakdown

### The Landscape by Tier

Understanding what works at different follower tiers is more actionable than chasing top-level creators.

**Tier 1 — Under 50K followers (where Seedwarden starts)**

Accounts in this tier have the highest engagement rates (often 7–17%) and the most direct community relationship. **Shaye Elliott (@shayeelliott)** on Instagram has 85K followers built around "farm life in Washington" and a philosophy of simple living — she has translated this into a paid membership community. Her path: consistent lifestyle content for 3–4 years, then productization. The lesson for Seedwarden is that even small audiences convert well if the content is specific and trust is built through consistency.

**Tier 2 — 100K–400K followers**

**@thermal\_and\_oaks** (367K TikTok) is the most instructive case at this tier. Her content is practical homesteading with a non-toxic living angle; she drives product traffic via bio links and uses TikTok as discovery. **@barefeetandmimosas** (400K TikTok) overlaps heavily with Seedwarden's audience: kitchen preservation, fermentation, food storage. Both demonstrate that mid-tier accounts in this niche have sufficient scale to sell digital products without brand deals.

**Jill Winger (@theprairiehomestead)** is the canonical case study for monetization in this niche. She has 191K Instagram followers, a top-ranked podcast ("Old Fashioned on Purpose"), and a best-selling cookbook. Her Prairie Homestead site generates over 1 million monthly visits. She started as a blogger, built audience through consistent practical content for years, then productized through a book, cookbook, and digital courses. The digital product playbook she runs — blog + email list + social proof — is exactly what Seedwarden should build toward in Phase 4+. For Phase 3, the key insight is that Jill's audience trusts her because she gave away enormous amounts of useful content for free before asking for money.

**Tier 3 — 500K–2M followers**

**@can't\_contain\_us** (638K TikTok) documents container-house homesteading and is a direct audience match for Seedwarden's container gardening and apartment growing products. **@itsbreellis** (772K TikTok) is a backyard homesteader covering skills that map directly to Seedwarden's product catalog. Neither has publicly disclosed revenue, but accounts in this range with affiliate links and product sales can generate $3,000–$10,000/month from digital product commissions alone.

**@goldshawfarm** (1.7–2.2M TikTok, Morgan Gold) monetizes through farm-direct meat sales and sponsored content — not digital products. But his content format and engagement dynamics are instructive: Vermont farm life, seasonal storytelling, authentic footage over produced video. His success confirms that authenticity and specificity of place beat production quality in this niche.

### What the Successful Ones Have in Common

Five traits appear consistently across successful homesteading creators who monetize digital products:

1. **Hyper-specific niche within homesteading**: Not "homesteading" broadly but "backyard chickens in a suburb," "apartment fermentation," or "seed saving for heirlooms." Specificity drives the right audience.
2. **Practical content with immediate applicability**: Every piece of content teaches something the viewer can do today. No abstract philosophy — show the method, show the result.
3. **Consistent daily or near-daily presence over 12–24 months before significant monetization**: The audience builds slowly, then compounds. Patience is the most common success factor.
4. **Email list as the real asset**: Every successful creator in this tier treats social media as a funnel to an email list. Social platforms can ban accounts or change algorithms; email lists cannot be taken away. Phase 4 for Seedwarden should include email capture.
5. **Product pricing that matches audience trust level**: Successful digital product sales in this niche start at $5–$15 (entry-level trust) and grow to $20–$50+ (established trust). Seedwarden's pricing ($5–$22) is correctly positioned for an unknown brand.

## 4. Paid Advertising Strategy

### The Correct Sequencing: Organic First, Paid Second

The most reliable failure mode for small Etsy sellers is running paid ads to listings that haven't been proven to convert organically. Ads amplify what is already working — they cannot fix a bad listing, wrong pricing, or unappealing mockups. The prerequisite for Phase 3 paid advertising is that at least 2–3 products have demonstrated an organic conversion rate (views to purchases) of at least 1–2%. Phase 1 data will reveal which products are converting. Phase 3 paid ads should start with those proven winners only.

### Etsy Ads: The Starting Point

Etsy's internal ad system (onsite ads) is the lowest-friction starting point because it requires no external campaign management and the targeting is handled by Etsy's own buyer data. For digital products specifically, Etsy ads are structurally advantageous: with 85–95% margin and a break-even ROAS of just 1.05–1.18, almost any click that converts to a sale is profitable. The target ROAS for digital products is 2.0+, which means generating $2 in revenue per $1 spent. The average Etsy CPC is $0.20–$0.50 for most niches.

Starting budget: $3–$5/day per listing, limited to 2–3 proven listings. Run for a minimum of 30 days before making optimization decisions — the Etsy algorithm needs time to calibrate. The critical discipline: do not increase budgets before the algorithm has learned. Patience here directly affects efficiency.

The benchmark ROAS for Etsy overall is approximately 2.8x. For high-margin digital products, anything above 2.0x is profitable. Any listing consistently below 1.5x ROAS after 45 days should be paused and the listing revised (better images, stronger title, different tags) before re-running ads.

### Facebook and Instagram Ads: Phase 3 Testing Budget

Facebook/Instagram ads reach buyers who are not already on Etsy searching — they are being introduced to a need they didn't know they had. This makes creative execution more important: the ad must explain the product, convey value, and create urgency, all in 5–15 seconds of video or a single image.

The "Shopping, Collectibles & Gifts" industry (Etsy's closest Facebook category) has the most favorable benchmarks in the 2025 WordStream dataset: average CPC of $0.34 (lowest of all industries), CTR of 4.13% (highest of all industries), and year-over-year CTR increase of 146%. This is the ad universe Seedwarden competes in. These numbers suggest Facebook/Instagram ads for Etsy digital products are more cost-efficient than most sellers assume.

Audience targeting for Seedwarden Facebook ads: start with interest stacking. Layer 3–4 related interests: `Homesteading` + `Seed saving` + `Food preservation` + `DIY projects`. Add demographic overlay: age 28–55, household income middle-to-upper tier (homesteaders who buy books tend to be financially stable), interest in sustainability and self-sufficiency. Exclude existing purchasers (Etsy customer list, if exportable). Lookalike audiences from Etsy buyer lists become available once there are 100+ Etsy customers — this is a Phase 4 tactic.

Starting budget: $150–$300/month on Facebook/Instagram ads in Phase 3 testing. This is enough for meaningful data at $0.34–$0.87 CPC without significant financial risk. Run 2–3 ad variations against the same audience (different creative, same product) to identify which hook converts. Kill anything that doesn't hit 2.0x ROAS within 21 days.

### Pinterest Ads: The Highest-Intent Paid Channel

Pinterest ads are underused by small Etsy sellers and represent the best cost-per-acquisition among paid channels for this niche. The average Pinterest CPC is $0.05–$0.50 for lifestyle and DIY categories — 30–40% cheaper than equivalent Facebook/Instagram targeting. Average cost per conversion is approximately $8.00, making Pinterest the most cost-effective paid channel for driving purchase-intent traffic.

Pinterest ads work as "promoted pins" — they look identical to organic pins and appear in search results and home feeds of users who are actively searching for relevant content. This is the critical structural difference from Facebook: Pinterest users are in a discovery/planning mindset with purchase intent; Facebook users are being interrupted. For a product like "Seed Saving Field Manual" or "Container Growing Blueprint Pack," a promoted pin appearing when a user searches "seed saving guide" is far more likely to convert than a Facebook ad appearing mid-scroll.

Pinterest ad structure for Seedwarden: one promoted pin per product, targeting keyword clusters (not interest targeting) — search terms like "seed saving guide," "food preservation printable," "container gardening plan." Budget: $50–$100/month per tested product. Minimum weekly budget of $50 recommended to gather meaningful data. At $8 average CPA and $11 average product price, this produces roughly 1.4x ROAS — marginally profitable. But the organic residual value of a promoted pin (it continues generating free clicks after the ad budget runs out) extends the effective ROI significantly.

### Metrics That Matter

In priority order for Phase 3:

1. **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**: Revenue generated per dollar spent. Target 2.0+ for digital products. The North Star metric.
2. **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: Total ad spend divided by number of purchases. Should be below 50% of product price to be structforably profitable.
3. **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Indicates whether creative is resonating. Below 1% on Facebook/Instagram means the ad needs new creative. Below 0.5% means the audience targeting is wrong.
4. **Conversion Rate from ad click to purchase**: Should be at least 2–3% for a well-optimized Etsy listing. If CTR is high but conversion is low, the problem is the listing, not the ad.

Vanity metrics to ignore: impressions, reach, follower count. These do not pay bills.

## 5. Influencer Partnership Opportunities

### Why Micro-Influencers Are the Right Tier

In the homesteading and sustainable living niche, micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) outperform macro-influencers on every metric that matters for digital product sales:

- Engagement rates 2–3x higher than accounts over 1M followers (7.5%+ vs. 2.88%)
- Audience trust is higher because the creator has a personal relationship with followers
- Cost is lower: $100–$500 per Instagram post, $200–$800 per TikTok video
- Niche specificity means a 30K-follower seed-saving creator's audience is already pre-qualified for Seedwarden's products

The research confirms: 64% of marketers partner with micro-influencers, and 47% report the strongest results from this tier. Brands combining affiliate and influencer strategies generate 46% higher affiliate-based sales than those using affiliates alone.

### Partnership Structures for Digital Products

For Seedwarden at Phase 3 stage, three partnership models are viable:

**Model 1: Pure affiliate (commission-only)**. The creator receives a unique discount code or affiliate link. They promote the product to their audience; Seedwarden pays 20–30% commission per sale. No upfront cost. Best for testing whether a creator's audience converts. Downside: lower motivation for creator than flat-fee deals. Etsy does not have a native affiliate program, so this requires using a third-party link tracker (Gumroad, Payhip, or a simple UTM-tagged Etsy URL with honor-system reporting).

**Model 2: Product gift + affiliate**. Send the creator a free copy of 2–3 products ($20–$40 value). Ask for an honest review video if they find value in it. Add a 20% affiliate commission. This requires no cash outlay and creates authentic reviews. It does mean a creator might receive free products and post nothing — the acceptance rate for gift-only deals is approximately 30–50%.

**Model 3: Flat fee + affiliate hybrid**. Pay $100–$250 for a dedicated TikTok or Instagram video, plus 15–20% commission on tracked sales. This is the model that produces the most motivated creators and the most polished content. Total budget for testing 3–5 creators in Month 2 of Phase 3: $500–$1,500.

### Creator Outreach: Direct vs. Platform

At Phase 3 budget levels, paid influencer platforms (SARAL at $12,000/year, Grin at $25,000–$40,000/year) are not appropriate. These tools are designed for brands with dedicated influencer marketing teams and five-figure monthly budgets.

The right approach at Phase 3 is direct outreach. Identify 10–15 target creators manually using the following criteria:
- 10K–100K followers on TikTok or Instagram
- Content covers seed saving, food preservation, foraging, container gardening, urban homesteading, or prepping
- Posts regularly (minimum 3x/week)
- Engagement rate above 4% (use Social Blade or creator.co for free estimates)
- Has linked products in bio or mentioned purchasing books/guides (indicates monetization-receptive audience)

Reach out via Instagram DM or email (usually in bio). Keep the pitch under 5 sentences: who you are, what the product is, what you're offering (free product, commission), and what you're asking for (one honest video if they find value). Expect 20–30% response rate and 10–15% agreement rate. From 15 outreach messages, expect 2–4 active partnerships.

Accounts worth targeting as initial outreach candidates based on audience overlap: creators in the `#urbanhomestead`, `#seedsaving`, `#foodpreservation` spaces with 15K–75K followers. Search TikTok and Instagram directly using these hashtags, sort by recent posts, identify accounts with consistent posting and above-average engagement, then check if they have any monetization signals (Linktree in bio, mention of books or courses, Amazon affiliate links).

## 6. Phase 3 Implementation Blueprint

### Month 1: Infrastructure and Organic Foundation

Before any paid dollar is spent, three infrastructure pieces must be in place:

1. **Social media accounts created and optimized**: TikTok Business account, Instagram Business account, Pinterest Business account — all with consistent handle (seedwarden or seedwarden.co), bio that includes the Etsy shop URL, and a profile image that matches the Etsy shop branding.

2. **Content bank pre-built**: Create 20–30 short-form videos before the first post goes live. This prevents the "week 3 burnout" that kills most small brand social accounts. Content themes for Batch 1: one video per product in the catalog (what it is, what problem it solves), 3–5 "educational" videos with no product pitch (how to save tomato seeds; why most seed packets fail; the difference between heirloom and hybrid), and 2–3 "founder story" videos introducing the brand.

3. **Pinterest infrastructure built**: 10–12 boards created with keyword-optimized names. 3–5 pins per product, pointing directly to Etsy listings. Pin creation can be batch-done in a weekend using Canva (vertical format, product mockup images already created in Phase 1 are the core visual asset).

Month 1 posting cadence: TikTok 5–7 videos/week, Instagram 3–4 Reels + daily Stories, Pinterest 2–3 pins/day. Month 1 goal is not sales — it is data. Which videos get the most views? Which products get the most clicks? What questions do commenters ask? This data guides Month 2 content and ad targeting.

### Month 2: First Paid Testing + Influencer Outreach

By Month 2, Phase 1 Etsy data should show which 2–3 products have the best organic conversion rates. These are the ad candidates.

**Paid ad testing budget for Month 2**: $300–$500 total.
- Etsy internal ads: $150–$200 ($5–$7/day across 2–3 products)
- Pinterest ads: $100–$150 ($50–$75 on 2 products)
- Facebook/Instagram: $50–$150 (one campaign, one audience, 2 creative variations)

Influencer outreach begins in Month 2: contact 15 targeted creators with the gift + commission offer. Fulfillment of free products costs $0 (digital downloads). Track all outreach in a simple spreadsheet: creator name, handle, follower count, date contacted, response, status, sales generated.

Month 2 success threshold: at least one paid channel showing ROAS above 2.0. If none hit that threshold, the products are not the problem (they are converting organically) — the creative or the targeting is the problem. Revise creative before scaling budget.

### Month 3: Scale Winners, Kill Losers

Month 3 is the optimization phase. By now:
- Organic content data shows which content types drive Etsy clicks
- Paid ad data shows which products and audiences are profitable
- 1–3 influencer partnerships are active or completed

Actions:
- Increase budget by 25–50% on any ad campaign consistently showing 2.5x+ ROAS
- Pause any ad campaign below 1.5x ROAS after 30 days
- Double down on the 2–3 content formats that generated most organic Etsy click-throughs
- Negotiate repeat content with any influencer whose audience converted
- Build an email capture mechanism (free resource download, waitlist for upcoming product) to begin transitioning social audience to owned audience

### Budget Allocation Framework

For a Phase 3 budget of $500–$1,000/month:

| Channel | Allocation | Amount ($500 total) | Amount ($1,000 total) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Etsy internal ads | 30% | $150 | $300 |
| Pinterest ads | 20% | $100 | $200 |
| Facebook/Instagram ads | 20% | $100 | $200 |
| Influencer gifting + small flat fees | 20% | $100 | $200 |
| Content tools (Canva Pro, scheduling) | 10% | $50 | $100 |

This allocation prioritizes Etsy internal ads because they are lowest-risk (Etsy's own buyer data), followed by Pinterest (high purchase intent, low CPC), followed by Facebook/Instagram testing. Influencer budget is kept small in Phase 3 — it scales in Phase 4 once Seedwarden has social proof (reviews, follower count, brand story).

### Success Metrics (Phase 3 Scorecard)

Track monthly:

| Metric | Month 1 Goal | Month 3 Goal |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok followers | 500 | 2,000+ |
| Instagram followers | 300 | 1,500+ |
| Pinterest monthly views | 5,000 | 25,000+ |
| Pinterest clicks to Etsy | 50 | 300+ |
| Etsy ad ROAS | n/a | 2.5+ |
| Pinterest ad ROAS | n/a | 2.0+ |
| Active influencer partnerships | 0 | 2–4 |
| Etsy reviews (total) | 10+ | 30+ |

The Pinterest monthly views metric is leading — it grows before sales grow, and it signals whether the content is indexing in Pinterest's search engine.

### Failure Modes and Early Warnings

Phase 3 fails in four recognizable patterns:

**Failure Mode 1: Content production collapse.** The creator starts strong (20 videos in Month 1) then drops to 1–2 videos per week by Month 2 due to burnout or competing priorities. Mitigation: batch production, content calendar scheduled 2 weeks in advance, ruthless simplicity of video format (film on phone, no editing beyond trim and caption).

**Failure Mode 2: Ads running to unconverted listings.** Paying for clicks to listings with poor mockups, weak titles, or mismatched tags is burning money. The tell: CTR is acceptable (1%+) but Etsy conversion rate is below 0.5%. Fix the listing before increasing ad budget.

**Failure Mode 3: Chasing vanity metrics.** Optimizing for TikTok followers instead of Etsy clicks. A video with 100K views that generates zero Etsy traffic is a pleasant distraction. Track Etsy referral traffic in the Etsy stats dashboard (Settings > Stats > Traffic Sources) to distinguish social impact from noise.

**Failure Mode 4: Influencer partnerships with audience mismatch.** A homesteading creator with 80K followers in the UK audience will not drive Etsy sales for products that reference USDA zones and North American planting calendars. Verify creator audience geography before outreach. Ask directly: "What percentage of your audience is in the US?" or check their content for geographic signals.
