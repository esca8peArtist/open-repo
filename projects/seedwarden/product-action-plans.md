# Product 1: Native Plants Regional Guide
**File:** `products/native-plants-regional-guide.md`
**PDF:** `scripts/output/native-plants-regional-guide.pdf` (37 pages)
**Suggested Price:** $18

---

## Content Status

The guide is substantively complete: 80+ plant entries across 9 US regions, identification features, habitat, season, uses, preparation, and safety. Dangerous lookalike pairs are documented with warnings. The appendix includes universal foraging safety rules, a lookalike comparison table, a required-preparation table, and photo source guidance.

**What's in place:**
- Photo placeholder system (`[PHOTO: description]`) throughout — consistent, clearly labeled
- Death Camas / Common Camas warning (potentially fatal confusion)
- Regional field guide bibliography by region
- Free photo source directory (iNaturalist, USDA PLANTS Database, Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center)

---

## Outstanding Items Before Launch

### High Priority
- [ ] **Photo sourcing pass** — Work through placeholders systematically. Pull CC0 / public domain images from iNaturalist (license filter: CC0 or CC-BY) and USDA PLANTS Database. Rebuild PDF after inserting images. Target: at least 1 photo per plant entry; 2+ for any plant with a dangerous lookalike.
- [ ] **Legal disclaimer** — Add a liability disclaimer on the first content page. Standard foraging guide language: "This guide is for educational purposes. Always verify plant identification with multiple sources before consuming any wild plant. The author assumes no responsibility for illness or injury resulting from misidentification." A version of this should appear on the Etsy listing as well.
- [ ] **Safety section prominence** — Confirm the "Six Questions Before You Eat" universal safety rules appear early in the document (currently in appendix). Consider moving a condensed version to page 2 or 3, immediately after the introduction.

### Medium Priority
- [ ] **Regional accuracy spot-check** — Have at least one person familiar with each region review the plant list. Plants listed for the Southwest Desert region should be verified against a native plant database (Lady Bird Johnson, Calflora, SEINet) for accuracy in range and season.
- [ ] **Lookalike table completeness** — Confirm all major dangerous lookalike pairs are represented in the appendix table. Current critical inclusions: Death Camas/Common Camas, False Morel/True Morel, Jack-o-Lantern/Chanterelle, Lily of the Valley/Ramps, Poison Hemlock/Queen Anne's Lace. Add: Water Hemlock vs. Wild Parsnip, Amanita phalloides (Death Cap) vs. edible button mushrooms if not present.
- [ ] **Mushroom section review** — Mushroom identification carries higher risk than plant identification. Consider adding a note that fungi are outside standard foraging liability and require spore prints plus expert verification.

### Low Priority / Optional
- [ ] **Region index** — Consider a one-page visual index showing which plants appear in which regions, for buyers who want to use the guide in a specific region only.
- [ ] **Printable format** — Review PDF layout at actual print size (A4 / Letter). Some photo placeholders may create awkward blank space. Consider adding a "print-friendly" rendering option (without images) as a second download.

# Product 2: Apartment Plant Catalog
**File:** `products/apartment-plant-catalog.md`
**PDF:** `scripts/output/apartment-plant-catalog.pdf` (31 pages)
**Suggested Price:** $14

---

## Content Status

The catalog is complete: 17 edible plant entries and 20+ decorative plant entries. Each entry includes varieties, light/water/temperature specs, container requirements, display options, care instructions, propagation, and common problems. Additional sections: pet toxicity reference table, difficulty quick reference, display and design strategies (vertical growing, window gradient, hanging/railing).

This product is the most launch-ready of the three — no photos required, content is self-contained, and liability risk is low compared to the foraging guide.

---

## Outstanding Items Before Launch

### High Priority
- [ ] **Verify pet toxicity table accuracy** — Cross-reference the toxicity classifications against ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center database (aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants). This table will be relied on by buyers with pets; accuracy is critical and errors here carry real liability risk.
- [ ] **Add a seasonal growing calendar** — A simple one-page table showing which of the edible plants can be grown in each season (spring/summer/fall/winter) by light category. This makes the guide significantly more actionable for buyers who purchase in a specific season.

### Medium Priority
- [ ] **Edible variety audit** — Confirm all edible plant entries include at least one variety recommendation for very limited light (north-facing windows / minimal indirect). A buyer in a dim apartment should still be able to find something they can grow.
- [ ] **Container sizing table** — Add a quick reference table showing minimum container size per plant. Currently this is embedded in individual entries; a summary table at the front would make the guide more navigable.
- [ ] **Common problem / pest index** — Brief table at the end: Pest/Problem → Affected Plants → Organic Solution. Fungus gnats (most soil plants), root rot (overwatered succulents), spider mites (dry conditions), aphids (most edibles). Cross-reference with entries already written.

### Low Priority / Optional
- [ ] **"Starter kit" page** — One page recommending a suggested combination of 5–7 plants for a first-time apartment grower (one herbs window box, one hanging plant, one decorative). Remove decision paralysis. Could also become a separate free lead magnet.
- [ ] **IKEA / common container compatibility note** — Brief note on which common low-cost containers (IKEA FEJKA, basic terracotta, 5-gallon bucket) work for which plant categories. Practical and shareable.

# Product 3: Survival Garden Regional Plans
**File:** `products/survival-garden-regional-plans.md`
**PDF:** `scripts/output/survival-garden-regional-plans.pdf` (25 pages)
**Suggested Price:** $18 (full) or sold individually at $5/region

---

## Content Status

The guide is complete for five regions: Houston TX, NW Arkansas, SE Wisconsin, Central Wisconsin, and Central Michigan. Each region has a full climate profile, seed list with variety recommendations, monthly planting calendar, System 1 (minimal improvement / mounded rows) and System 2 (raised beds / full improvement) layout schematics with ASCII grid and legend, and companion planting rationale. Universal sections at the back cover seed saving, root cellar storage, Three Sisters planting sequence, and four-bed crop rotation.

The ASCII schematics render well in PDF with ASCII fallback characters. The document is dense with specific, actionable data.

---

## Outstanding Items Before Launch

### High Priority
- [ ] **Decide on single product vs. individual region pricing** — Two viable strategies:
  - **Option A:** Sell all 5 regions as one $18 PDF (current approach) — best value perception, broader appeal
  - **Option B:** List each region separately at $5.99 each — buyers in one region only pay for what they need; also creates 5 separate Etsy listings (5x the SEO surface area); offer a discounted bundle link in each listing
  - **Recommendation:** Option B for SEO reach, with an "all regions" bundle at $18. Create 6 listings total.
- [ ] **Add 2–3 more regions** — The five current regions are Midwest/South-heavy. High-demand regions currently missing:
  - Pacific Northwest (Portland / Seattle area) — very large urban gardening market
  - Mid-Atlantic / Northeast (Philadelphia / DC / New York) — high-density urban market
  - Northern California (Sacramento / Bay Area) — enormous gardening audience
  - These would significantly expand the product's market reach. Each region is roughly 4–6 hours of writing work.
- [ ] **Schematic clarity review** — The ASCII grid schematics use 2-letter crop codes. Verify the legend is immediately adjacent to each schematic in the PDF (not just defined once at the top). A reader should never have to flip pages to decode a schematic.

### Medium Priority
- [ ] **Caloric output table for all regions** — Currently only Houston has a detailed caloric output estimate table. Add equivalent tables for all five regions (or a single comparative table in the introduction) — this is one of the most compelling data points in the guide.
- [ ] **System 2 schematics** — System 2 (raised beds) currently describes the layout in prose rather than providing a separate ASCII grid. Add System 2 schematics for each region to match System 1 presentation. This is the most significant content gap.
- [ ] **"What if I only have X space" guidance** — Brief section addressing plots smaller than 800 sq ft: how to scale down to 400 sq ft or 200 sq ft (one 4'×50' bed). Makes the guide accessible to people without full backyard space.

### Low Priority / Optional
- [ ] **Printable layout pages** — Extract just the schematics and legends into a separate printable insert (clean formatting, no headers/footers) so buyers can pin them to a wall or garden shed board while planting.
- [ ] **First-year vs. established garden differences** — Brief note that the seed lists and companion planting guilds assume some soil improvement already done. A first-year note would set expectations for buyers starting from raw lawn.
