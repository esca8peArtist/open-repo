## Section 1 — Timeline: Three Phases

### Phase 1: Active Search (June 8–17, 9 days)

The search window is tight. Nine days is enough to surface 4–6 viable candidates and close on one if outreach goes out on Day 1 and follow-up is disciplined. The failure mode is delayed outreach start — every day of delay compresses the evaluation window.

**June 8 — Day 1 (Search launch)**

All outreach goes out on Day 1, not Day 2 or Day 3. The platforms and channels below have different response lag times; the only way to have responses by June 12 is to send by June 8.

- Send direct outreach to 6–8 local/regional photographers identified via Instagram search (see Section 2 below)
- Post job listing on two platforms: Thumbtack and Bark (both surface local photographers quickly)
- Send inquiry to local herbalist networks or co-ops asking for photographer referrals (warm referral converts faster than cold platform outreach)
- Post in relevant Facebook groups: "Botanical Photography," "Herbal Business Network," and local/regional small business photography groups
- Check Etsy seller community boards — many Etsy product photographers take freelance sessions

**June 9 — Day 2 (First response window)**

- Monitor inboxes; respond to any replies within 4 hours (slow response loses candidates who are also fielding other inquiries)
- Send follow-up to any pre-vetted photographers identified before June 8 whose Day 1 outreach may have gone to spam
- Post in a second set of Facebook groups if Day 1 posting drew zero responses

**June 10–11 — Days 3–4 (Screening round)**

- Score all respondents using the screening rubric (Section 4 below)
- Candidates scoring above threshold: request portfolio review and schedule a 20-minute discovery call
- Candidates below threshold: send polite decline, log reason in WORKLOG.md
- Begin short-list of top 2–3 candidates for deeper evaluation

**June 12–13 — Days 5–6 (Evaluation and portfolio review)**

- Conduct 20-minute discovery calls with short-listed candidates
- Review portfolio: check for botanical, product, and flat-lay experience specifically
- Send the equipment and technical spec sheet (from `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_PREBID_MATERIALS.md`) to top 2 candidates; ask for a bid against the spec

**June 14 — Day 7 (Bid due date)**

- Bids due from all candidates who received the spec sheet
- Score bids on the evaluation rubric (Section 4)
- Select primary contractor; begin contract preparation

**June 15 — Day 8 (Contingency gate)**

- If no bids received by EOD June 15, activate contingency path A (self-shoot model) immediately — do not wait for June 17
- If 1+ bids received but none meet criteria, activate contingency path B (stock + Wikimedia-only model for Round 1)
- Log contingency status in WORKLOG.md

**June 17 — Day 9 (Hard deadline)**

- Contract signed and 25% deposit paid to selected contractor
- OR contingency path confirmed active in writing (WORKLOG.md entry)
- No action after June 17 that delays June 22 sprint launch is acceptable

### Phase 2: Onboarding and Training (June 18–July 1, 9 days)

The onboarding window exists to get the photographer calibrated before first payment of deliverables is due. A contractor who has never shot botanical flat-lay for Etsy needs 3–5 days of alignment before the camera is picked up. A contractor who does regular product photography needs 1–2 days.

**June 18 — Onboarding Day 1**

- Send the full onboarding package (see onboarding checklist, Section 5)
- Schedule briefing call: 45 minutes, walking through the shoot brief, style reference images, technical specs, and the naming convention
- Confirm shoot dates (target: first shoot within the June 22–25 window)
- Confirm props list and who sources what (owner provides: herbs, guide printout mockup, jars; photographer provides: surface, backdrop, lighting)

**June 19–20 — Onboarding Days 2–3**

- Photographer reviews shot list and prepares equipment
- Owner sources and stages props: dried herb bundles (Elderberry, Mullein, Echinacea) for Respiratory bundle shoot; mortar and pestle, glass apothecary jars, natural linen surface
- Owner prepares guide printout mockup: print one page of the Respiratory bundle guide at full color, fold/bind loosely to simulate a finished guide booklet
- Confirm shoot location: home studio (recommended for control) or outdoor garden setting with overcast light (natural diffusion)

**June 21 — Onboarding Day 4**

- Test shoot or phone/video preview: photographer shares 2–3 test images from their setup with correct surface and lighting. Owner approves or requests adjustment (surface color, light temperature, or prop arrangement). This catches style mismatches before the full shoot day.
- If test images are not available (remote contractor): review portfolio images against the style spec one final time; confirm white balance and composition expectations in writing before June 22

**June 22–24 — Sprint launch parallel track**

- Sprint Day 1 (June 22): herbalist writer begins Respiratory bundle manuscript. Photo shoot is not Day 1 — it follows manuscript completion by approximately 10 days
- Photo contractor is on standby during this window; shoot date confirmation is Day 0 of the 10-day photo delivery timeline (per `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHER_AND_SOURCING_RUNBOOK.md` Section 4)

**June 25–July 1 — Final calibration and first shoot window**

- By June 28 (target): Respiratory bundle manuscript first draft complete (contractor writing pace assumed)
- July 1: First photo shoot session (Respiratory bundle). This is the earliest date the shoot can happen while still fitting the 10-day delivery timeline ahead of the July 11 Respiratory upload target

## Section 2 — Contractor Profile

The right photo contractor for Phase 3 is not necessarily a dedicated botanical photographer. Product photography and food styling skills translate directly to medicinal herb flat-lay. The must-have qualifications are narrower than they might appear.

### Must-Have Qualifications

**Flat-lay product photography experience**
The contractor must have a portfolio showing top-down flat-lay shots — not just portraits, events, or general lifestyle. Flat-lay is a specific composition discipline. Herb products can be tested against kitchen, cosmetics, or food flat-lay portfolios for style match. Any flat-lay portfolio with props and a clean background indicates the spatial composition skill is present.

**Natural light or soft-box capability**
Seedwarden guides use warm, soft lighting without harsh shadows. Photographers who primarily shoot with on-camera flash or mixed artificial lighting produce images that will not match the brand aesthetic. The portfolio should show: diffuse shadows, warm tones, visible texture on surfaces. Rejection signal: portfolio shows harsh shadows or overly blue/cool white balance.

**Minimum resolution: 2000×2000px delivered**
This is a technical floor for Etsy. Any serious photographer shoots above this resolution as a matter of course. The question is whether they understand the final output requirement and deliver JPEG at 80%+ quality, not heavily compressed exports.

**Local or willing to travel to shoot location**
The herbs must be physically present for the flat-lay shoot. A remote photographer cannot shoot live plants or dried herb bundles without the owner present. Priority: within 50 miles of shoot location. Acceptable: willing to shoot at owner's location (common for product photography).

**Commercial use license on all delivered images**
Standard product photography contracts include commercial use. Confirm explicitly in writing before signing. The Seedwarden Etsy listings and guide PDFs are commercial products — a "personal use only" delivery is not usable.

### Preferred Qualifications (Not Required)

**Etsy product photography experience**
A photographer who has shot for other Etsy sellers understands the 5-image listing format and the square crop requirement. They will naturally ask "which image is Slot 1?" rather than needing the format explained from scratch. This reduces briefing time by 30–45 minutes.

**Knowledge of herbs or botanical subjects**
Not required, but useful. A photographer who gardens or forages will recognize herb specimens and handle them correctly (not wilting, not crushing). This matters for fresh-herb shoots. For dried herb shoots, it is a minor advantage.

**Experience with editorial or cookbook styling**
Food styling and botanical flat-lay share the same discipline: props, texture, color contrast, and negative space. A photographer with editorial styling experience will often compose a better frame than a technically superior photographer who lacks styling instinct.

### Disqualifying Conditions

- Portfolio shows exclusively portraits, events, or architectural work with zero product experience
- Portfolio shows heavy HDR or over-saturation processing (visual style is incompatible with Seedwarden brand)
- Cannot confirm commercial use license in their standard contract
- Cannot shoot within the July 1–8 window (Respiratory bundle shoot)
- Quoted rate above $400/session without elite portfolio justification

## Section 3 — Search Strategy: Platforms and Outreach Sequence

### Primary Channels (Use all on Day 1)

**Channel 1: Instagram — Local botanical and product photographers**

Search Instagram with the following hashtags, filtered to recent posts (past 30 days) from accounts with 200–5,000 followers (small professional photographers, not stock agencies):

- `#botanicalphotography` + local city or region tag
- `#productphotographyetsy`
- `#herbalphotography`
- `#flatlayphotography` + local city tag
- `#cottagecorephotography` (aesthetic match for Seedwarden brand)

For each promising account found: check their profile for a contact email or DM option. Send the DM template from `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_OUTREACH_SCRIPT.md`. Target 8–10 outreach messages on Day 1.

Expected response rate: 15–30% from accounts that actively post for clients. Most photographers who book project work respond to DMs within 24–48 hours.

**Channel 2: Thumbtack**

Thumbtack (thumbtack.com) surfaces local service providers by category. Use it with these search terms:

- "Product photographer" + city/ZIP
- "Etsy product photography"
- "Botanical photographer"

Thumbtack allows you to post a project brief and receive quotes within 24 hours. Set the project category to "Photography > Product photography." Budget field: enter "$150–350 per session" to pre-filter out wedding/event photographers who price at $1,000+ per day.

Response time on Thumbtack is typically same-day or next-day; it is the fastest response channel in this search.

**Channel 3: Bark**

Bark (bark.com) is a second local service marketplace with strong product photography coverage. Create a brief with the same parameters as Thumbtack. Some photographers list on both platforms; do not send duplicate outreach to the same individual across channels.

**Channel 4: Facebook Groups**

Target four groups on Day 1:

1. "Botanical Photography" (public group, active community of botanical and nature photographers)
2. "Herbal Business Network" (herbalists who run Etsy businesses often know product photographers who already understand the botanical context)
3. A regional/local small business Facebook group (search "small business [city] photographers" — most cities have a local chamber or entrepreneur group where photographers advertise)
4. "Etsy Sellers Community" — many Etsy sellers photograph their own products or know photographers who specialize in Etsy listings

Post format: short (3–5 sentences), specific about what you are looking for, with a budget range stated. Vague posts asking "does anyone know a photographer?" draw no responses. Posts that say "I am looking for a product photographer for medicinal herb flat-lay, half-day session, ~$150–250, commercial license required, June–August shoot window" draw specific qualified responses.

**Channel 5: Warm Referral (Herbalist Network)**

If you have any existing contacts in the herbalism, food preservation, or homesteading space — including anyone you have contacted as part of the Phase 3 practitioner outreach — ask directly: "Do you know a photographer who shoots botanical or product flat-lay work?" Referrals convert at 3–5x the rate of cold outreach. One referral from an herbalist who has worked with a photographer before is worth more than 10 cold DMs.

### Secondary Channels (Activate June 10 if primary channels yield fewer than 3 candidates)

**Etsy sellers who produce high-quality product listings**: Contact them via Etsy message asking who photographs their products. Many small Etsy sellers self-shoot and will share photographer names from their network.

**Local camera clubs**: Search "photography club [city]" — many clubs have members who do freelance work. Posting a paid project on a camera club forum often draws responses within 48 hours.

**Behance/500px**: Both platforms have portfolios searchable by style. Search "flat lay herbs" or "botanical product" and contact photographers directly. These platforms are slightly slower than Instagram for response but have higher-quality portfolio depth.

## Section 4 — Evaluation Criteria

### Screening Rubric (50-point scale)

Score each candidate before scheduling a discovery call. A score of 35 or above proceeds to discovery call. Below 30 is a disqualifying rejection.

| Category | Criterion | Maximum Points | Scoring Guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Portfolio Match** | Flat-lay product photography in portfolio | 20 | Dedicated flat-lay portfolio with props = 20; 2–3 flat-lay examples within a general portfolio = 14; No flat-lay, product only from angles = 6; No product work at all = 0 (disqualifying) |
| **Technical Output** | Image quality and resolution evidence | 15 | Portfolio images clearly 2000px+ with correct color, clean exposure = 15; Good exposure but unclear resolution = 10; Visible quality issues (heavy noise, over-processing) = 3; No portfolio available = 0 (disqualifying) |
| **Availability** | Confirmed July 1–8 window availability | 10 | Explicitly confirmed in first contact = 10; "Probably available, will confirm" = 6; Not yet confirmed = 3; Unavailable for window = 0 (disqualifying) |
| **Commercial License** | Confirms commercial license in standard contract | 5 | Confirmed in writing = 5; Standard contract says "personal use" but willing to negotiate = 2; Refuses commercial license = 0 (disqualifying) |

**Total: 50 points**

**Threshold decisions**:
- 40–50: Priority candidate. Proceed directly to bid request; skip discovery call if portfolio is clear.
- 35–39: Discovery call candidate. 20-minute call to confirm style alignment and technical workflow.
- 30–34: Borderline. Only proceed if fewer than 2 candidates are above threshold.
- Below 30: Decline. Do not invest time in a discovery call.

### Bid Evaluation Criteria

After receiving bids from the top 2–3 candidates, evaluate on the following dimensions:

**Cost within budget**: Session fee must fit within $150–$350 per half-day session. Total engagement cost (3–4 sessions) must not exceed $1,200. Bids above $400/session require elite portfolio justification and explicit user approval before proceeding.

**Edit turnaround commitment**: Seedwarden needs edited images within 3 days of each shoot. A photographer who delivers in 2 days is preferred over one who delivers in 5 days, even at equal quality. Ask for turnaround commitment in writing in the bid.

**Revision policy**: The contract should include at minimum one round of minor crop/color adjustments at no additional charge. Photographers who charge per revision above baseline create unpredictable cost exposure.

**References or repeat clients**: A photographer who has worked with the same client multiple times has demonstrated reliability. Ask for one reference from a repeat client.

## Section 5 — Onboarding Checklist

Use this checklist when onboarding a confirmed contractor. Complete all items in the June 18–21 window (before first shoot).

### Training Path (June 18–21)

**Day 1: Documentation delivery**

Send the following files as a single onboarding package (PDF or Google Drive folder):
- Shoot brief (from `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_PREBID_MATERIALS.md`)
- Naming convention reference (from `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHER_AND_SOURCING_RUNBOOK.md` Section 6)
- Style reference images: 6–8 images from Seedwarden brand aesthetic (hand-picked from Wikimedia or Unsplash that match the warm, botanical, natural-linen aesthetic)
- Technical specifications sheet: resolution, color space, file format, delivery method
- Bundle shoot schedule: which herbs are in each shoot, what props the owner provides vs. photographer

**Day 1: Briefing call (45 minutes)**

Agenda:
1. Walk through one sample shot list (Respiratory bundle — 5 required images)
2. Review style references together; confirm aesthetic alignment
3. Confirm what the photographer is bringing vs. what the owner is providing
4. Confirm delivery method (Google Drive folder preferred; Dropbox acceptable)
5. Confirm payment schedule and invoice process
6. Set date and time for first shoot

**Day 2–3: Props and plant staging**

Owner action — not contractor:
- Source dried Elderberry sprigs, Mullein leaves, and Echinacea flowers from Phase 3 plant supply
- Obtain: mortar and pestle, 2–3 glass apothecary jars (amber or clear), natural linen surface (light beige or off-white, minimum 24"×24")
- Print guide cover mockup (Respiratory bundle) in color; fold and bind loosely
- Confirm shoot location: home studio window light or outdoor shaded garden area

**Day 3: Test image review (optional but recommended)**

Ask the contractor to share 2–3 test shots from their studio setup before the first paid shoot. Review for: light quality (soft, warm), surface color (neutral, not cool-gray), composition (clean negative space, herbs in focus). Approve or request one adjustment. This 10-minute review prevents a full reshoot.

**Day 4: Final confirmation**

- Shoot date and time locked
- Props staged and ready
- Contractor has shot list in hand
- Owner has printed mockup guide ready
- Payment method confirmed (preferred: PayPal or Venmo for speed; Zelle if photographer is local US)

### Platform Setup (for contractor — minimal)

No platform-specific setup is required for the photographer. Delivery is via Google Drive folder or Dropbox. The contractor does not need access to Etsy, Canva, Kit, or any Seedwarden platform. The only technical requirement is delivery of JPEG files at the correct resolution to a shared Drive folder.

**Drive folder structure for photo delivery**:
```
Seedwarden-Phase3-Photos/
  respiratory/
    [delivered images named per convention]
  immunity/
    [delivered images]
  digestive/
    [delivered images]
```

Naming convention reminder (from `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHER_AND_SOURCING_RUNBOOK.md`):
`[bundle-slug]-[image-type]-[sequence].jpg`
Example: `respiratory-flatlay-01.jpg`, `respiratory-lifestyle-02.jpg`

## Section 6 — Contingency Paths

### Contingency Path A: Self-Shoot Model

**Activation trigger**: No contractor confirmed by June 15 EOD, OR confirmed contractor cancels before July 1.

**What changes**: Owner conducts all photo shoots. Timeline from manuscript to delivered photos compresses from 10 days (with contracted photographer) to 8 days (self-shoot, per `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHER_AND_SOURCING_RUNBOOK.md` Section 4).

**Equipment needed**:
- Camera or smartphone with manual focus capability (iPhone 13+ or equivalent is sufficient for 2000px deliverable)
- Natural window light source (large north- or east-facing window, or overcast outdoor light)
- White foam board or natural linen surface (24"×24") — available at any craft store for $10–20
- Props from bundle plant supply (already staged for contractor use)
- Free editing: Lightroom Mobile (free tier) handles white balance, exposure, and crop in 15–20 minutes per image set

**Time cost**: 3–4 hours per bundle (shoot + edit). Three bundles = 9–12 hours total across the sprint.

**Revenue impact**: None. Photo quality from self-shoot is sufficient for Etsy listing requirements if natural window light is used and editing is done carefully. The visual difference between a professional flat-lay and a careful self-shoot on a good surface with good light is small at Etsy listing size.

**When to activate**: June 15 EOD, without waiting for June 17. A contractor confirmed June 16–17 compresses the June 18–21 onboarding to 2 days — workable but not preferred.

### Contingency Path B: Wikimedia/iNaturalist-Only Round 1

**Activation trigger**: No contracted or self-shoot photography is possible for the July 1 Respiratory bundle upload (e.g., key props unavailable or owner schedule conflict).

**What changes**: Respiratory bundle launches with Wikimedia CC-licensed species habit photos only — no flat-lay cover or lifestyle images. The Etsy listing uses habit photos in all 5 listing slots rather than the preferred flat-lay/lifestyle combination.

**Impact on conversion**: Estimated 15–25% lower click-through rate on the listing compared to a professional flat-lay cover. This is an acceptable Round 1 trade-off if it avoids delaying the upload.

**Recovery**: After Round 1 upload, schedule one self-shoot session (half-day) and update Etsy listing photos for Respiratory bundle. Update does not require a new listing — Etsy allows photo replacement on existing active listings.

**Do not activate Path B for Immunity or Digestive bundles unless Respiratory bundle photo data confirms the conversion impact is acceptable.**

### Contingency Path C: Budget Escalation

**Activation trigger**: Only one qualified contractor available, and their bid is $400–$500 per session (above the $350 ceiling).

**Decision logic**:
1. Negotiate scope reduction: offer 2 sessions (Respiratory + Immunity) instead of 3–4 for a total not exceeding $800. Digestive bundle photos come from a second contractor or self-shoot.
2. If contractor will not negotiate below $400/session: activate Path A (self-shoot) for all bundles. The cost difference does not justify the quality premium at Phase 3 sales volume.
3. Do not pay more than $1,200 total for the photo contractor engagement in Phase 3.

### Contractor Dropout Mid-Sprint

**Trigger**: Contractor completes Respiratory bundle shoot but cancels before Immunity shoot.

**Response** (within 24 hours of cancellation):
1. Confirm whether delivered images from Respiratory shoot are in Drive folder and cleared for use. Pay the Respiratory shoot invoice immediately.
2. Activate self-shoot model for remaining bundles. Do not attempt to find a replacement contractor on a 3-day timeline.
3. Log dropout in WORKLOG.md with the date and reason (if given).
4. If contractor holds any unbilled advance payments: issue a demand for return within 7 days per contract terms (see `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_PREBID_MATERIALS.md` payment terms section).
