## 1. Credibility Filter Assessment

The herbalist practitioner market operates on a binary credibility filter. A guide either passes a 10-second RH-level scan or it does not. Understanding this filter determines who you approach first, what you offer them, and what their endorsement actually signals to their audience.

### What the Filter Tests

A practitioner scanning a product description checks four things in order:

1. **Botanical nomenclature** — Is the Latin binomial present and correct, with species authority? *Cimicifuga racemosa* alone (outdated) fails; *Actaea racemosa* L. with the correct authority passes.
2. **Evidence-tier attribution** — Are sources tiered (meta-analysis > RCT > monograph > traditional use) or just cited as "studies show"? The former passes; the latter is dismissed as consumer-grade.
3. **Contraindication completeness** — Are herb-drug interactions and contraindications present? Absence of this table is a hard fail for clinical practitioners who may distribute material to patients.
4. **Sourcing ethics statement** — For at-risk species (Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, American Ginseng), is there an explicit statement about cultivated vs. wildcrafted origin? Absence on these species signals amateur content to the practitioner who knows UpS listings.

### Who Validates in This Market

**Primary validators (Tier 1 credibility gatekeepers):**
- **AHG Registered Herbalists** — The RH credential is the highest voluntary herbalism credential in the US. A single sentence naming one RH reviewer in a product listing ("reviewed for botanical nomenclature and contraindication accuracy by [Name], RH (AHG)") collapses the main friction point for the entire $120–$150 tier. Target: one RH reviewer confirmed before June 22 launch.
- **Naturopathic Doctors (ND) with botanical specialty** — Licensed practitioners whose endorsement signals to the 7,500+ US ND community. Higher authority than RH for the clinical evidence audience but longer outreach cycle.
- **PhD ethnobotanists or phytochemists** — Academic credibility for the evidence-tier citations. Cassandra Quave (Emory) and Christopher Hobbs (UMass Amherst) are the two Phase 3-relevant targets in this category.

**Secondary validators (audience trust builders):**
- Herbalism school instructors (Herbal Academy, Chestnut School) — Their recommendation to alumni functions as curriculum endorsement.
- Conservation network voices (UpS, United Plant Savers) — Their signal is specifically about sourcing ethics, not clinical accuracy; complementary to RH validator, not a substitute.

**What validation is NOT sufficient for the practitioner tier:**
- General wellness influencer endorsement (even 1M+ followers) — Does not pass the practitioner credibility filter without clinical framing.
- School-level enrollment but no credential — Signals emerging status to practitioners; useful for student-tier marketing, not the $120–$150 bundle.

### Minimum Viable Credibility for June 22 Launch

One confirmed RH (AHG) reviewer named in each product listing unlocks the practitioner channel. This is the single highest-leverage pre-launch action. Everything else in this roadmap layers on top of that baseline.

## 3. Pre-Outreach Strategy

### The Bundle Offer

Every outreach message should lead with a clear, low-friction offer. The "free guide + affiliate setup" bundle is the proven mechanism from Phase 2 launch outreach (documented in LAUNCH_WEEK_INFLUENCER_OUTREACH.md). Adapt for Phase 3 medicinal herbs context:

**Standard offer (Tier 1 and Tier 2):**
> "I'd like to send you complimentary access to the [specific bundle] — [Women's Health / Respiratory / Sleep] — in exchange for an honest look at it. No posting requirement. If you find it useful for your work or your audience, I can set up a 15% affiliate code ([THEIRNAME]15) so your followers can purchase with a discount."

**Credibility reviewer offer (Tier 1 clinical practitioners):**
> "I'm looking for one RH-level reviewer to confirm botanical nomenclature and contraindication accuracy before launch. This is not a paid position — I'm offering complimentary access to all five bundles and a named attribution in every product listing ('reviewed for accuracy by [Name], RH (AHG)') in recognition of the time. Would that be something you'd be open to discussing?"

**Key personalization rules:**
- Reference a specific post, podcast episode, or piece of their work by topic (not title).
- Match the bundle to their documented specialty — do not pitch Women's Health to a Respiratory-focused practitioner.
- Do not lead with Seedwarden's story — lead with what they get.
- Keep the message under 150 words. Practitioners are busy.

### Timing the Outreach Message

**What to emphasize by phase:**

Before launch (pre-June 22):
- Emphasize the practitioner resource angle and review opportunity.
- Emphasize the credibility reviewer ask — this is time-sensitive to the launch date.
- Do not push affiliate yet — lead with the resource value.

Launch week (June 22–28):
- Shift to affiliate offer — "The Women's Health bundle is live. Here's your affiliate code. Your followers get 15% off."
- Include a live Etsy listing link. Social proof begins to build.

Post-launch (July–August):
- Shift to podcast pitch for August–September placement (4–12 week lead time from pitch to air date).
- Emphasize first reviews ("RH practitioners already using the guide in their practice").
- Lead with conservation framing for UpS and conservation-adjacent voices (August AHG Symposium window).

### Fresh vs. Dried Decision Matrix (Messaging Framing)

When practitioners ask about sourcing authenticity — which clinical practitioners will — the answer must be consistent:

| Species | Seedwarden Guide Recommendation | Sourcing Rationale |
|---------|--------------------------------|-------------------|
| Black Cohosh | Dried root (rhizome), cultivated source | At-risk wild population; cultivated = ethical + consistent quality |
| Goldenseal | Dried root, certified organic cultivated or FGV-grown | CITES Appendix II; cultivated is legal, ethical, and supply-chain stable |
| Elderberry | Fresh or dried both appropriate; dried for shelf stability | Not at-risk; either source acceptable; guide addresses both uses |
| Echinacea (angustifolia) | Dried root only | Highest active constituent concentration in root; leaf/flower lower potency |
| Echinacea (purpurea) | Fresh aerial parts or dried | More commercially available; different phytochemistry from angustifolia |
| Ashwagandha | Dried root, certified organic | Not a US native; cultivation is appropriate; organic cert matters |
| Valerian | Fresh root tincture or dried root | Fresh tincture preferred for higher valerenic acid retention |

This matrix pre-empts the most common practitioner objection before they raise it. Include a version of this table in product listings and pitch emails.

### Risk Activation Dates

Two risk activation dates bear on outreach timing:

- **June 8**: Goldenseal sourcing path must be confirmed (cultivated specimen or CC photo). Outreach messages referencing Goldenseal before June 8 should not promise a specific sourcing recommendation — confirm path first.
- **June 15**: Canva palette lock. All mockup images used in pitches after June 15 should use the locked palette. Do not send pitches with prototype visuals that will not match the final product.

## 5. Credibility Filter Assessment — Phase 4 Implications

Phase 4 expands into exotic medicinal herbs. The credibility requirements become more demanding, not less. A Phase 3 botanical guide for Black Cohosh can be published with one named RH reviewer. A Phase 4 guide covering Kava or Kratom requires a pharmacognosy review and an explicit scope-of-practice statement. Phase 4 validation contacts to cultivate now (relationships begun in Phase 3 become Phase 4 validators):

| Phase 4 Category | Credibility Requirement | Pre-Cultivate Contact |
|-----------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Adaptogens (Rhodiola, Eleuthero) | RH + ND reviewer; evidence-tier citations for adaptogen research | David Winston (Adaptogens book author) |
| Anti-inflammatory herbs (Boswellia, Turmeric) | Phytochemistry accuracy; COX-2 mechanism citations | Christopher Hobbs (PhD) |
| Nervines + Anxiolytics (Kava, Ashwagandha at higher dose ranges) | Liver toxicity warnings; herb-drug interaction tables; scope-of-practice statement | Jenn Dazey (ND, RH, Bastyr) |
| Psychoactive/Schedule-adjacent (Kratom) | Legal review required; may not be viable for Etsy | Do not approach influencers pre-legal review |

**David Winston** (AHG RH; 54 years practice; *Adaptogens* book author; mailing list + author platform) is the single most valuable Phase 4 relationship to begin cultivating in Window 3. He is educator-oriented and willing to engage with educational projects. The introductory touchpoint should be recognition of his *Adaptogens* work in the Immunity bundle's adaptogen sidebar — not a sales ask.
