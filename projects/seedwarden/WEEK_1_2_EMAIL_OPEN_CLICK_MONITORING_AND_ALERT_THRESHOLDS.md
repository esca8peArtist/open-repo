# Week 1-2 Email Open/Click Monitoring and Alert Thresholds

**PURPOSE**: Real-time mechanical monitoring of email engagement metrics with automatic alert triggers. No subjective judgment — metrics either cross thresholds or they don't. When a threshold is crossed, activate specific Tier 1 or Tier 2 response actions. All alerts logged with timestamp and action taken.

**KEY PRINCIPLE**: Week 1-2 emails (Women's Health June 29, Respiratory July 6) are peak performance windows. Open rates 22-28% are realistic; anything below 20% indicates a content/audience/timing issue requiring immediate investigation and response.

**BENCHMARK BASELINE** (from Q2 2026 Phase 2 campaigns):
- Average email open rate: 22-28% (industry range: 15-25%)
- Average click rate: 3-5% (industry range: 2-5%)
- Expected launch email boost: +5-10% opens, +2-3% clicks (first in series)
- Week 2+ decline: -2-4% opens (expected decline after launch momentum)

---

## ALERT THRESHOLD DEFINITIONS

### THRESHOLD LEVELS

**GREEN ZONE** (On-target or exceeding):
- Open rate: ≥22%
- Click rate: ≥3%
- Status: No action required; continue current strategy

**YELLOW ZONE** (Below target, investigation needed):
- Open rate: 20-21.99%
- Click rate: 2-2.99%
- Status: Investigate cause; prepare Tier 1 response; plan adjustment for next send

**RED ZONE** (Significantly below target, immediate action required):
- Open rate: <20%
- Click rate: <2%
- Status: Activate Tier 2 rapid-response; investigate root cause; execute same-day response

## ALERT TRIGGER RESPONSES

### YELLOW ALERT — Email reaches YELLOW thresholds (20-21% opens OR 2-2.99% clicks)

**Decision window**: Check at 24-hour mark (early warning). Confirm at 72-hour final mark (trigger action).

**Root cause assessment** (investigate which is likely):

**Cause 1: Subject line clarity**
- Is subject line clear and enticing? Example good: "Respiratory Health — Herbs for clear airways"
- Is subject line generic? Example bad: "New bundle available"
- Assessment: Subject clarity STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK

**Cause 2: List quality/engagement**
- Are recipients engaged with previous emails (Women's Health)?
- Are there new unsubscribes/complaints? (indicates poor message-audience fit)
- Assessment: List quality GOOD / ADEQUATE / POOR

**Cause 3: Send time**
- Is 9am ET optimal for your audience? (Could peak be 8am or 10am?)
- Are opens concentrated at specific time (indicating time zone issues)?
- Assessment: Send time OPTIMAL / ADEQUATE / SUBOPTIMAL

**Cause 4: Email body copy**
- Is preview text compelling? Example: "Elecampane, thyme, mullein, and more — cultivation through preparation"
- Is CTA button clear and prominent? (Target: "GET THE [BUNDLE] BUNDLE")
- Assessment: Copy STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK

**Cause 5: Content/audience mismatch**
- Did subject line promise something email doesn't deliver?
- Example: Subject says "Today's launch" but email is educational background info?
- Assessment: Message match STRONG / ADEQUATE / MISMATCH

**TIER 1 RESPONSE** (execute at 72-hour mark if YELLOW)

1. **Document root cause**: ____

2. **Adjust next send** (Week 3 Sleep bundle, July 13):
   - [ ] A/B test subject line: Keep original vs. try [alternative subject] with 50/50 split
   - [ ] Adjust send time: Try 8:30 AM ET OR 9:30 AM ET instead of 9:00 AM ET
   - [ ] Segment audience: Try sending promotional version to segment A, educational to segment B
   - [ ] Improve CTA: Change button text from [current] to [alternative]

3. **Document decision**: Week 3 adjustment: [Describe change] | Expected improvement: +__% opens

4. **Log to learning system**: Add to PHASE_3_EMAIL_PERFORMANCE_INSIGHTS.md for Phase 4 reference

---

### RED ALERT — Email reaches RED thresholds (<20% opens OR <2% clicks)

**Decision window**: Escalate immediately at 24-hour mark if RED.

**Root cause assessment** (prioritized order):

**Priority 1: Deliverability crisis** (ISP blocks, spam folder)
- Check Kit dashboard: Deliverability % (should be 98%+)
- Is email reaching inboxes or spam folder? (request test opens from friends)
- Assessment: Deliverability GOOD / POOR
- Action if POOR: Contact Kit support, investigate ISP blocks, resend to engaged segment

**Priority 2: Severe content mismatch**
- Is subject line misleading? (causes delete without open)
- Is subject line generic or unsent-looking? (deleted by filters)
- Assessment: Subject line STRONG / WEAK
- Action if WEAK: Send follow-up email same day with different subject, offering same bundle

**Priority 3: Audience problem**
- Is list quality degraded? (old addresses, inactive subscribers)
- Are unsubscribes spiking? (indicates poor message fit)
- Assessment: List HEALTHY / DEGRADED
- Action if DEGRADED: Segment to engaged-only subscribers for follow-up

**TIER 2 RAPID-RESPONSE** (execute within 24 hours if RED)

1. **Same-day follow-up email**:
   - [ ] Subject line: "You asked about [bundle topic] — here's what customers are downloading"
   - [ ] Copy: Short, social-proof focused ("This bundle has been popular; here's why...")
   - [ ] CTA: Direct Etsy link + urgency ("Available for 7 more days")
   - [ ] Send time: 2 hours after initial send (if initial was morning, send at 11am ET)
   - [ ] Audience: Only to people who OPENED first email (retargeting)

2. **Parallel social media push**:
   - [ ] LinkedIn post highlighting same bundle + Etsy link
   - [ ] Instagram post with testimonial ("Customer feedback on why people love this bundle")
   - [ ] Post within 2 hours of initial email send
   - [ ] Purpose: Diversify channel if email alone underperformed

3. **Root cause investigation**:
   - [ ] Check Kit technical stats (spam score, rendering issues)
   - [ ] Request test opens from 3 friends on different email providers (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo)
   - [ ] Check if subject line hit spam triggers (ALL CAPS, $$, too many exclamation marks)

4. **Decision on future sends**:
   - [ ] A/B test subject line on Week 3 send (Sleep, July 13)
   - [ ] Try segmentation: Send "Practitioners" version to professional audience, "Gardeners" version to consumer audience
   - [ ] Adjust sending frequency (if sending too many emails, might need rest between sends)

5. **Log escalation**: PHASE_3_EXECUTION_LOG.md (daily task log)
   - [ ] RED alert triggered: [Date], [Time]
   - [ ] Cause identified: [Description]
   - [ ] Tier 2 action taken: [Action 1, Action 2, Action 3]
   - [ ] Expected outcome: [Prediction for follow-up metrics]
