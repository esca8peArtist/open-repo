## Portfolio Audit Checklist

Run this checklist on every Tier 1 candidate before scoring. Require 5 portfolio samples minimum for all roles except Social Media Manager (2 accounts suffice).

### Photographer (5 recent projects required)

- [ ] At least 3 samples show botanical, food, or natural-product flat-lay photography (not portraiture or events)
- [ ] Resolution appears professional (assess via display quality; ask for source dimensions if unclear)
- [ ] White balance is consistent within each project set (no visible color drift between frames in a series)
- [ ] Composition and props fit Seedwarden's warm, accessible, naturalist aesthetic (compare to reference images provided at onboarding)
- [ ] No harsh shadows across central subjects in botanical close-ups
- [ ] If any people appear in samples: confirm model releases were obtained (ask directly)

**Disqualifying**: Work that is exclusively portraiture, architecture, or event photography with no product/flat-lay samples.

### Women's Health Writer (5 published articles required)

- [ ] At least 3 articles cover women's health, herbal medicine, or integrative wellness topics
- [ ] At least 2 articles use Latin binomials for plant species mentioned
- [ ] At least 1 article mentions contraindications or drug interactions for an herb or supplement
- [ ] FTC-compliant language visible: "may support," "research suggests," "traditionally used" — not "cures" or "prevents" without evidence qualifier
- [ ] Word count: at least 1 article exceeds 2,000 words (demonstrates capacity for 3,600-word manuscript)

**Disqualifying**: Articles that make unqualified health claims ("eliminates," "cures," "guarantees") without evidence-tier framing, or articles with no citations.

### Respiratory & Sleep Writer (5 published articles required)

- [ ] At least 3 articles cover herbal medicine, natural health, or wellness topics with evidence-based framing
- [ ] At least 1 article addresses drug interactions or safety considerations for an herb
- [ ] Uses primary sources or named databases (NatMed Pro, Cochrane, WebMD clinical, Examine.com, or equivalent)
- [ ] Tone is educational and accessible, not academic-only or marketing-heavy
- [ ] Word count: at least 1 article exceeds 2,500 words

**Disqualifying**: Blog content only with no evidence citations, or articles that recommend supplements as replacements for medication.

### Herbalist Consultant (credential verification required)

- [ ] Verify AHG Professional Member status via ahg.memberclicks.net (public directory) OR NAHA membership verification
- [ ] If unaffiliated with AHG/NAHA: request documentation of 5+ years clinical herbalism (practice records, school faculty position, peer-reviewed publication authorship, or client testimonials from at least 3 long-term clients)
- [ ] At least 3 professional projects or publications listed (published monographs, consulting references, continuing education delivery)
- [ ] Demonstrates drug interaction knowledge in profile or portfolio (mentions NatMed Pro, Micromedex, clinical databases)
- [ ] CITES or endangered species knowledge: look for UpS membership, UpS At-Risk list familiarity, or conservation language in published work

**Disqualifying**: Self-described "herbalist" with no verifiable credential and no professional project history.

### Logistics Coordinator (3–5 professional references or projects required)

- [ ] At least 2 projects show email marketing platform management (Kit, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or equivalent)
- [ ] At least 1 project shows e-commerce coordination (Etsy, Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon seller)
- [ ] Evidence of managing concurrent timelines (project tracking, multi-contractor coordination, or VA with multiple simultaneous clients)
- [ ] Communication samples show professional, concise tone
- [ ] Stated availability matches Monday + Thursday coordination windows required

**Disqualifying**: No email marketing platform experience, or only buyer-side Etsy experience.

### Social Media Manager (2 accounts managed required — links mandatory)

- [ ] 2 Instagram accounts provided with verifiable links (active accounts, not deleted or private)
- [ ] At least 1 account is in wellness, botanical, food, natural products, or lifestyle niche
- [ ] Engagement quality visible: thoughtful comments from followers, community interaction (not just likes)
- [ ] Content is varied: mix of educational, promotional, and lifestyle posts (not exclusively product ads)
- [ ] FTC compliance visible in existing content: #ad on sponsored posts, no unqualified health claims in wellness accounts managed
- [ ] Posting cadence: consistent (5+ posts per week) in the accounts shown

**Disqualifying**: Accounts provided are inactive, under 3 months old, or have follower counts purchased (check for sudden follower spikes in public tools like Social Blade).

## Reference Check Protocol

Send to 2 references per Tier 1 candidate (email preferred; phone optional). Response within 24 hours or skip and proceed.

**Reference request email template**:

Subject: Quick reference check — [CANDIDATE NAME]

Hi [REFERENCE NAME],

I'm checking a reference for [CANDIDATE NAME] who has applied for a [ROLE] project with Seedwarden, a digital herbal guide brand. The project runs July–August 2026.

Three quick questions — reply by email is fine, takes about 3 minutes:

1. Quality: On a scale of 1–10, how would you rate [CANDIDATE NAME]'s work quality for the project you worked on together?
2. Reliability: Did [CANDIDATE NAME] deliver on time? Any missed deadlines or unexpected quality issues?
3. Communication: Was [CANDIDATE NAME] responsive to feedback and questions during the project?

Feel free to add anything else that would help me understand what it's like to work with them.

Thank you — I appreciate your time.

[YOUR NAME]
Seedwarden

**Log responses** (one sentence per reference per criterion):
- Example: "Editor (The Herbal Dispatch): 9/10 quality, always on-time, revised twice without issue, very responsive."
- Example: "Client (Botanical co.): 8/10, one deadline pushed 2 days but communicated early, professional throughout."

**Minimum bar**: At least 1 of 2 references must confirm on-time delivery and professional communication. A single strong reference with no opposing signal is sufficient to proceed.

## Scoring Rubric

Score each candidate across 4 dimensions. Use the decision matrix below to determine tier assignment.

### Dimensions and Weights

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio Fit | 40% | Does prior work match quality and subject matter required? (Audit checklist above is the scoring basis) |
| Availability | 30% | Can they start July 5–6 and commit to all delivery dates? Hard requirement; score 0 if unavailable |
| Budget Fit | 20% | Does proposed rate fall within posted budget ±10%? See rate negotiation rules below |
| Communication | 10% | Response time, clarity of application, questions asked (relevant to role, not generic) |

### Scoring Scale (per dimension, 0–10)

**Portfolio Fit**:
- 9–10: Exceeds requirements; prior work directly matches subject matter and quality level
- 7–8: Meets requirements; prior work shows clear capability; minor gaps in subject match
- 5–6: Acceptable; prior work shows general capability but limited subject-matter match
- 3–4: Weak match; significant gap between prior work and this project's requirements
- 0–2: Disqualifying issues (see audit checklist disqualifiers above)

**Availability**:
- 10: Confirmed July 5 start, all delivery dates confirmed, no conflicts stated
- 7–9: July 5 start confirmed; minor uncertainty on one delivery date (can be resolved in kickoff call)
- 4–6: Unclear start date; or one delivery date in question (significant risk)
- 0–3: Cannot confirm July 5 start or has confirmed conflict with a delivery date

**Budget Fit**:
- 10: Proposed rate is at or below posted budget
- 8–9: Proposed rate is 1–5% above posted budget (within negotiation range)
- 6–7: Proposed rate is 6–10% above posted budget (borderline; negotiate down before offer)
- 4–5: Proposed rate is 11–15% above posted budget (decline or hard-negotiate)
- 0–3: Proposed rate is more than 15% above posted budget (do not extend offer without scope adjustment)

**Communication**:
- 9–10: Applied within 12 hours of posting; asked 1–2 specific, relevant questions; portfolio links valid and relevant
- 7–8: Applied within 24 hours; application is complete and professional; links work
- 5–6: Applied within 48 hours; application is adequate but generic
- 3–4: Application is incomplete (missing link or confirmation) or has grammar/clarity issues
- 0–2: No response to outreach or application is obviously templated with no role-specific content

### Final Score Calculation

Overall Score = (Portfolio × 0.40) + (Availability × 0.30) + (Budget × 0.20) + (Communication × 0.10)

### Decision Matrix

| Score | Decision | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 8.0–10.0 | **HIRE (Tier 1)** | Extend offer within 4 hours of scoring. This candidate meets all criteria. |
| 6.5–7.9 | **INTERVIEW** | Conduct 10-minute screening call to resolve one or two scoring gaps before deciding. If resolved, extend offer. If not, move to next candidate. |
| Below 6.5 | **REJECT** | Do not extend offer. Send polite decline: "Thank you for applying — we've filled this role." |

### Rate Negotiation Rules

- **Within posted budget**: Accept without negotiation.
- **1–10% above posted budget**: Negotiate down. Email: "Our budget for this role is $[X]. Can you work within that? We can offer [one small sweetener: e.g., faster payment timeline, strong reference at close]."
- **More than 10% above posted budget**: Do not hire unless this candidate is the only qualified applicant and no Tier 2 candidate exists. In that case, escalate scope or timeline before accepting rate overage.
- **Do not split the difference** on rate if it moves the total cost more than 10% above your budget. The Q3 contractor budget is finite.

### Objective Rejection Criteria (any one = automatic REJECT regardless of score)

- Cannot confirm July 5 start
- No portfolio link provided or all links broken/private
- Portfolio shows zero relevant work (no flat-lay, no herbal writing, no wellness brand management)
- Proposed rate exceeds posted budget by more than 20%
- Reference check reveals missed deliverables on a comparable project with no prior notification
- Application appears copy-pasted with no mention of Seedwarden, herbal medicine, or the role specifics
