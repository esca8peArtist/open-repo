# Phase 2 Day-by-Day Execution Plan
## May 10 – May 30, 2026

**Purpose**: A working document you open every morning and close every evening. Each day block specifies exactly what to do, in what order, with which tool, and what "done" looks like. No ambiguity, no judgment calls deferred to launch day.

**Time reality**: The total execution window is 21 days. Actual work hours are 36–45 hours distributed across that window — an average of 1.5–2 hours per day with three heavier days (the photo shoot and two Canva sprint days). Every task block shows an estimated time so you can protect the right days on your calendar.

**Reading this document**: Each day block is self-contained. If you complete a day early, you may pull forward tasks from the next day's block. Do not skip forward past a dependency gate (marked with a lock symbol) — those are tasks that cannot begin until a prior deliverable exists.

## Days 1–2: Photo Shoot (May 10–11)

**Total estimated time**: 8–10 hours across both days.

### Day 1 — Saturday May 10

**8:30am — Setup (20 min)**
- Lay out Cluster A props on the shoot surface: seed envelopes (filled and sealed), 2–3 terracotta or ceramic pots, small trowel or hand rake, germination tray, mason jar with saved seeds, printed product pages, wooden surface or linen swatch.
- Position your window or light source. For flat-lay shots: shoot directly overhead with camera parallel to the surface. Place a white foam board on the opposite side of the window to bounce fill light — this eliminates harsh one-sided shadows.
- Take one test shot and review on camera screen at 100% zoom. Confirm: sharp focus at product center, no blown highlights on white props, warm color temperature (not blue-toned).

**9:00am–1:30pm — Session 1: Cluster A (16 shots, 8 products)** (4.5 hours)
- Products 1–8: Seed Saving Field Manual, Heirloom Variety Selection Guide, Anti-Catalog: 30 Heirlooms, Companion Planting Chart, Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar, Food Sovereignty Starter Guide, Apartment Seed Starting Kit, 12-Month Urban Growing Planner.
- Shot sequence per product: flat-lay first (overhead, product centered at intersection of thirds, props surrounding), then contextual (product in use — hands holding a page, guide open next to germination tray).
- After each pair of shots: review on camera screen, delete clearly unusable frames, continue. Do not do detailed editing here — only gross culls.
- Reshoot flag: if any shot has soft focus on the product face or harsh specular highlights on glossy props, note the product slug and reshoot before moving to the next product. Fixing in editing is 10x harder than a 90-second reshoot.

**1:30pm — Transfer (15 min)**
- Connect camera to computer (USB or SD card). Import all Cluster A files to: `/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/raw/cluster-a/`
- Verify file count: you should have 30–50 RAW files (multiple attempts per composition).
- Do not delete originals from the camera card until all 30 final exports are confirmed.

**3:00pm–5:00pm — Session 2: Cluster B (8 shots, 4 products)** (2 hours)
- Products 9–12: Container Growing Blueprint Pack, Apartment Growing Complete Guide, Apartment Plant Catalog, 12-Month Urban Growing Planner (second angle).
- Props: potted herb (basil, mint, or rosemary in 4–6 inch container), balcony railing or window ledge as background, second printed product page.
- Natural afternoon light is acceptable for Cluster B — the urban/container aesthetic benefits from slightly warmer late-afternoon tones.
- Transfer immediately after session.

**Day 1 success criteria**: 24+ RAW files imported across Clusters A and B. All 8 Cluster A products have at least one usable flat-lay and one usable contextual shot. No critical focus errors on any product.

**Troubleshooting — Day 1**:
- If window light is overcast or flat: this is ideal for flat-lay shots. Do not add artificial light unless shadows are completely absent. Overcast equals natural softbox.
- If window light is too harsh (direct sun): hang a white bedsheet over the window to diffuse. Translucent white curtains work the same way.
- If germination sprouts are too sparse or leggy: substitute fresh sprouting seeds in a shallow dish (soak overnight). The close-crop shot of sprouting seeds reads well and does not require a full tray of 13-day growth.

## Days 3–4: Image Processing and Export (May 12–13)

**Total estimated time**: 3–4 hours.

### Day 3 — Monday May 12

**Batch editing (2–2.5 hours)**
- Open all 30 selects in your editing application.
- Create one edit preset using the best Cluster A image as the anchor. Settings: Exposure +0.3 to +0.5 (lifestyle photos should be 10–15% brighter than a metered exposure), Highlights -20, Shadows +25, White Balance temperature 5200–5500K (warm, not neutral), Saturation -5 (earthy tones, not saturated), Clarity +10 (adds micro-contrast that makes props read as tactile).
- Sync this preset to all Cluster A images. Adjust individually if any shot is an outlier.
- For Cluster B: apply the same preset, then adjust white balance individually if late-afternoon shots are significantly warmer than Cluster A.
- For Cluster C: kitchen light is often warmer than window light. Pull White Balance temperature down to 5000–5200K if needed. The mason jar contents should look appetizing, not orange.

### Day 4 — Tuesday May 13

**Export and file naming (1–1.5 hours)**
- Export all 30 files: 2400px on long edge, 1:1 crop (square), JPEG, 88–90% quality.
- Naming convention (required for Canva import and Etsy upload tracking):
  - `[product-slug]-lifestyle-flat.jpg` (slot 4)
  - `[product-slug]-lifestyle-context.jpg` (slot 5)
  - Use the exact slugs from CANVA_EXECUTION_PLAYBOOK.md Section 6.1.
- Save to: `/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/etsy-ready/`
- Run file size check: all files must be under 2MB. Use Squoosh (squoosh.app) to compress anything over 2MB without visible quality loss.
- Confirm file count: 30 JPEG files, 2 per product, 15 products.

**Day 4 success criteria**: 30 JPEG files in `/etsy-ready/`, correctly named, all under 2MB. Photos are ready for Canva import and Etsy upload.

## Days 15–19: Kit Email Platform Setup (May 15–24, parallel track)

The Kit setup track runs in parallel with Canva zone card production. These tasks do not depend on zone cards being finished — they depend only on having a Kit account and the zone card file URLs (which are obtained after export, not during design).

**Total estimated time**: 3–4 hours distributed across 5 days.

### Day 15 — May 15 (after Canva Brand Kit work, same day)

**Kit account creation and authentication (45 min)**
- Go to kit.co. Click "Start for free."
- Account email: wanka95@gmail.com (confirmed from KIT_SETUP_NOTES.md).
- Sender name: Seedwarden.
- Time zone: your local time zone.
- After account creation, navigate to Settings > Email Settings > Custom Sender Domain.
- Add SPF record to your domain's DNS: `v=spf1 include:sendgrid.net ~all` (Kit uses SendGrid infrastructure). Log into your domain registrar (Namecheap, GoDaddy, Google Domains, or wherever the domain is registered) and add the SPF record as a TXT record.
- Add DKIM record: Kit displays a CNAME record in Settings > Email Settings > DKIM Signing. Copy and paste it into your DNS registrar exactly. DNS propagation takes 24–48 hours — add these records on Day 15 and verify on Day 17.
- If no custom domain exists yet: use wanka95@gmail.com as the sender address. This is acceptable for launch. Migrate to a custom domain before the list reaches 500 subscribers.

**Tool command**: DNS changes require logging into your domain registrar's control panel. No API key is required for this step.

**Expected output**: Kit account created, SPF and DKIM records added to DNS. Email deliverability setup is in progress (24–48h for DNS propagation).

**Troubleshooting**: If the SPF record conflicts with an existing SPF record (error: "only one SPF record allowed"), merge the records. Existing SPF record `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all` + Kit requirement becomes `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com include:sendgrid.net ~all`. Never add two separate SPF records.
