# Seedwarden Q3 Social Engagement Tracking

**Purpose**: Daily engagement monitoring and algorithmic detection for Phase 3 bundle launches (60 posts, 90+ days). Identifies underperforming content, timing issues, and content-type effectiveness to enable real-time pivots and algorithm-aware optimization.

**Engagement rate threshold**: LinkedIn algorithm suppresses posts with <8% engagement. This system identifies GREEN (>8%), YELLOW (5–8%), and RED (<5%) performance within 24–48 hours of posting.

**Expected baseline**: Seedwarden target audience (herbalism professionals, home growers, educators) typically shows 6–12% engagement on educational content during launch phase. Promotional content averages 3–5%. Community testimonials average 8–14%.

## Section 2: Engagement Rate Thresholds & Decision Tree

### LinkedIn Algorithm Engagement Threshold

**Threshold Definition**: LinkedIn's organic distribution algorithm prioritizes posts with engagement >8% in the first 24 hours. Posts with <5% engagement are deprioritized in followers' feeds, reducing downstream visibility.

### Three-Tier Status System

#### GREEN Status: >8% Engagement Rate

**Indicator**: Post is being organically amplified by the algorithm. Content resonates with audience.

**Action**: No intervention needed. Track this post type for replication. Note:
- Content format (length, structure, question-driven?)
- Posting time (is 9am ET optimal for this content type?)
- Content topic (which herb, which seasonal context?)

**Decision**: Continue baseline posting schedule. This content type is working.

**Success markers**: Engagement rate >8%, viral coefficient >0.30, profile visits >200.

#### YELLOW Status: 5–8% Engagement Rate

**Indicator**: Post is performing adequately but not being strongly amplified. Borderline distribution. Content may have minor optimization opportunities.

**Action**: Monitor next post of similar type within 48–72 hours. Look for:
- Is the issue timing? Post same content type at different time (test 12pm ET or 5pm ET).
- Is the issue format? Try different headline structure or hook.
- Is the issue topic? This specific herb/season performed weaker than similar posts.

**Decision**: Adjust one variable (time, format, or topic mix) in next post and re-measure. Do NOT pivot broadly yet.

**Success markers if trend continues**: After 2 consecutive YELLOW posts of same type, escalate to RED protocol.

#### RED Status: <5% Engagement Rate

**Indicator**: Algorithm is suppressing post distribution. Either content issue or significant timing mismatch.

**Action**: Investigate root cause immediately:
1. **Repost test (24h later)**: Repost similar content at alternate time (12pm ET if originally 9am; 5pm ET if originally 12pm). Track engagement. If engagement improves 3+ points, it's a timing issue. If engagement stays <5%, it's a content issue.
2. **Content review**: Compare to GREEN posts. What is missing? (Educational depth? Personal connection? Call-to-action clarity?)
3. **Format audit**: Is the post text too long? Too short? Does it have a question hook? Do comments on the post suggest confusion or pushback?

**Decision tree**:
- If repost at different time improves to 5–8%+ → timing issue. Shift that content type's posting schedule for next week.
- If repost shows no improvement → content issue. Adjust content type ratio for next week (reduce this category, increase high-performing category).
- If algorithm deprioritization suspects (very high Likes but very low Shares) → possible LinkedIn penalty (reuse of exact copy across posts, too-frequent posting). Audit posting frequency and uniqueness.

**Success markers after pivot**: Next post of adjusted type should reach 5%+ within 24–48h.

### Metrics Explanation

## Section 5: Algorithm Detection & Pivot Decision Tree

### Red Flags: Algorithm Suppression Signals

#### Signal 1: Engagement <5% on 2+ Consecutive Posts

**What it means**: Algorithm is deprioritizing your content. Either the audience response is weak (indicating poor content) OR the platform is throttling distribution.

**Diagnostic test**: Repost similar content at a different time. If engagement improves 3+ percentage points → timing issue. If engagement stays <5% → content issue.

**Action path**:
1. Within 24 hours of RED status on second consecutive post, prepare a repost of the second post at an alternate time (if originally 9am, repost at 12pm or 5pm).
2. Measure engagement on repost within 24–48 hours.
3. If repost improves: update posting schedule for that content type starting Week 4.
4. If repost stays red: escalate to content audit.

#### Signal 2: High Likes, Very Low Shares

**What it means**: Audience finds content personally valuable (likes = personal resonance) but NOT network-valuable (shares = network worth). Indicates the post may be too niche, too controversial for open sharing, or lacking clear network value statement.

**Example**: A post gets 150 likes but only 2 shares on a 5,000-follower account. Viral coefficient is 0.01. This signals the audience liked it personally but did not forward it.

**Causes**: 
- Post is too polished/professional (people reshare raw/authentic content more)
- Missing clear "why I am sharing this" signal
- Audience fears judgment for resharing (sensitivity around topic)
- No explicit CTA ("share this if you agree" or "forward to someone who needs this")

**Action**:
- If this pattern emerges: add explicit share CTA in next week's similar posts. Example: "Share this with anyone learning herbalism — save them weeks of research."
- A/B test: try posting same content with explicit CTA vs. implicit CTA in Week 5+.

#### Signal 3: Declining Engagement on Repeated Content Type

**What it means**: You have established a pattern (e.g., "I post plant-science deep dives Tuesdays at 9am") and engagement is declining week-over-week on that pattern.

**Example**: 
- Week 1 Tuesday post (plant science): 10% engagement
- Week 2 Tuesday post (plant science): 8.5% engagement
- Week 3 Tuesday post (plant science): 6.5% engagement

This is a trend line decline indicating algorithm fatigue on the content type or time slot.

**Action**:
- Pause the repeated post type for 1 week. Replace with a different content type at that time slot.
- Reintroduce the paused content type in Week 5 at a new time or with a different angle.
- Measure if engagement recovers. If yes, it was audience fatigue on the repetitive schedule. If no, the content type itself needs rethinking.

### Content Issue vs. Timing Issue: Diagnostic Decision Tree

```
RED Status Post (<5% engagement) Received
    │
    ├─ YES: Repost at alternate time within 24h
    │  │
    │  ├─ YES: Engagement improves to 5%+
    │  │  └─ → TIMING ISSUE
    │  │     Action: Shift this content type to new time slot starting Week 4+
    │  │
    │  └─ NO: Engagement stays <5%
    │     └─ → CONTENT ISSUE
    │        Action: Review content depth, clarity, relevance vs. HIGH-PERFORMING posts
    │
    └─ Maybe: Unsure which variable to change
       │
       ├─ Compare to high-performing posts of same TYPE
       │  (Does high-performing testimonial have longer story? Better detail? More specific outcome?)
       │
       ├─ Compare to high-performing posts of same TIME
       │  (Do other 9am ET posts perform better? If yes, it's content. If no, it's timing.)
       │
       └─ If still unclear: Change ONE variable only
          (Either time OR format OR topic) in next post, re-measure
```

### Content Issue: Adjustment Strategies

If diagnostic confirms CONTENT issue:

1. **Depth**: Compare low-performing post to high-performing educational post. Is the low-performing post too brief? Too introductory? Add 50–100 words of detail in next similar post.

2. **Specificity**: Compare low-performing post to high-performing post on same topic. Does the high-performer mention specific plant Latin names, zones, harvest months? Mirror that specificity in next post.

3. **Hook/Opening**: Does the low-performer start with a weak hook? ("Here's how to grow valerian") vs. high-performer hook ("Valerian root concentration peaks in year 2 or 3 fall, and here's why"). Strengthen the opening in next post.

4. **Audience fit**: Is the content addressing the right audience? (E.g., a post aimed at "herbalism students" got low engagement on a account that has 80% home-grower followers.) Shift the angle in next post.

5. **Call-to-action**: Does the post have a clear Etsy link, guide reference, or engagement question? Posts without CTAs typically underperform. Add a natural CTA in next similar post.

### Timing Issue: Adjustment Strategies

If diagnostic confirms TIMING issue:

1. **Primary shift**: Move the underperforming content type to the time slot where the repost performed better. For Week 4+, schedule all posts of that type at the new time.

2. **A/B sustained test**: For 2 weeks, post half of that content type at 9am ET and half at the alternate time. Measure which time consistently outperforms. Lock in the winner.

3. **Document by content type**: Maintain a "posting time winner" list:
   - Educational — Cultivation: 9am ET (if baseline holds)
   - Educational — Plant Science: [TBD by Week 2]
   - Testimonial: [TBD by Week 3]
   - Promotional: [TBD by Week 2]
   - YouTube Engagement: variable (post all week)

## Section 6: Content Type Mix Recommendation & Adjustment Rules

### Baseline Week 1–2 Content Mix (From PHASE_3_SOCIAL_MEDIA_CONTENT_CALENDAR.md)

- **60% Educational** (15 posts out of 25 planned, excluding YouTube engagement): plant science, cultivation, harvest, preparation, seasonal guidance
- **20% Testimonial** (5 posts): grower stories, practitioner quotes, customer success
- **20% Promotional** (5 posts): bundle launches, Etsy links, practitioner tier offers

*Note*: YouTube Community Tab engagement posts (5 planned) are separate from the 25 above and serve a community function; they are not part of the content-mix percentage calculation.

### Adjustment Rules: Dynamic Mix Rebalancing

**Rule 1: If Educational Engagement >8% for 3+ consecutive posts**

Educational content is resonating. Increase educational ratio from 60% to 70%.

**Implementation**: 
- Reduce promotional posts from 20% to 15%.
- Keep testimonial at 15%.
- Rebalance in Week 4 onward.
- Example: 70% educational (14 posts/week), 15% testimonial (4 posts/week), 15% promotional (4 posts/week).

**Rationale**: Educational content builds trust, demonstrates expertise, and attracts followers who want long-term value. Higher engagement on educational content suggests audience is skewed toward learners/practitioners.

**Rule 2: If Promotional Engagement <5% for 2+ consecutive posts**

Promotional content is underperforming. Reduce or reformat.

**Implementation**:
- Reduce promotional posts from 20% to 10%.
- Do NOT eliminate promotional entirely (revenue link is necessary).
- Increase educational to compensate (from 60% to 70%).
- Test reformatted promotional posts: instead of "Here's the bundle link," try "This is what the Immunity bundle reader did differently this fall" (promotional with story angle).

**Rationale**: Pure promotional content on LinkedIn struggles because the audience expects thought leadership, not sales pitches. Promotional content wrapped in context (story, seasonal relevance, use case) performs better.

**Rule 3: If Testimonial Engagement >10% for 2+ consecutive posts**

Testimonials are your highest-engagement content type. Increase frequency.

**Implementation**:
- Increase testimonial from 20% to 30%.
- Reduce educational from 60% to 55%.
- Continue at 15% promotional.
- Rebalance in Week 5 onward.
- Example: 55% educational (11 posts/week), 30% testimonial (6 posts/week), 15% promotional (4 posts/week).

**Rationale**: User-generated content (or quoted testimonials) outperforms brand-generated content on LinkedIn because it signals social proof and community. If testimonials drive the most engagement, lean into them.

**Rule 4: If Testimonial Engagement <5% or Declining Week-over-week**

Testimonials are underperforming. Audit and adjust.

**Possible causes**:
- Testimonials lack specificity (vague praise vs. concrete outcome)
- Testimonials feel inauthentic or overly polished
- Testimonials are from non-relevant personas (e.g., herbalism student testimonial to a home-grower audience)

**Implementation**:
- Require testimonials to include: specific herb, specific result, specific zone/context, and full name (not "Maria T." — full "Maria Thompson, Zone 6 Pennsylvania").
- Use unedited, natural voice (misspellings, casual tone OK if authentic).
- Target testimonials by persona: home grower testimonials for weeks targeting home growers; educator testimonials for weeks targeting educators.
- If engagement still low, reduce testimonial ratio back to 15% and investigate other content types.

**Rule 5: Seasonal Flexibility**

Content mix may shift by season within Phase 3:
- **Weeks 1–3 (late June–mid-July, pre-launch)**: 50% educational, 30% testimonial, 20% promotional (building credibility before launches)
- **Weeks 4–6 (July–Aug, launch phase)**: 40% educational, 30% testimonial, 30% promotional (heavier promotion during launch windows)
- **Weeks 7–12 (Aug–Sep, post-launch, seasonal pivot)**: 70% educational, 15% testimonial, 15% promotional (back to thought leadership focus, harvest season content heavy)
- **Weeks 13–18 (Sep–Oct, fall + close)**: 60% educational, 25% testimonial, 15% promotional (wrap-up, next-season planning, Phase 4 teaser)

**Note**: These are guidelines. Actual mix should respond to engagement data, not follow a calendar blindly.

## Section 8: Cross-Platform Posting Checklist

### Pre-Post Launch Checklist

Use this checklist for every post published during Phase 3 (60 posts across 90 days).

#### Content Preparation (24–48 hours before post)

- [ ] **Copy finalized**: Post text has been proofread, word count verified (200–300 words for LinkedIn educational posts)
- [ ] **Visual prepared**: Image/graphic is ready and optimized for platform (LinkedIn: 1200×627px; Instagram: 1080×1350px square or carousel)
- [ ] **Hashtags curated**: 
  - LinkedIn: 3–5 hashtags max; include #seedwarden, industry tags (#herbalism #medicinalherbs), niche tags (#habitat #conservation if applicable)
  - Instagram: 8–10 hashtags; include #seedwarden plus 7–9 topic-specific tags
- [ ] **CTA identified**: Post has a natural call-to-action (Etsy link in comment, engagement question, profile visit invitation)
- [ ] **Scheduling platform loaded**: Post queued in Buffer, Later, or native platform scheduler
- [ ] **Scheduling time confirmed**: Platform time is set to 9am ET (or alternate time if A/B testing)
- [ ] **Cross-platform variant reviewed**: If posting to multiple platforms, each variant is platform-optimized (shorter/punchier for Instagram; longer/more detailed for LinkedIn)

#### LinkedIn Post Specifics

- [ ] **Headline hook**: First 50 characters are compelling (not "Here's a post about valerian" but "Valerian root concentration peaks in year 2—here's why your harvest timing matters")
- [ ] **First-comment strategy**: If posting a promotional link, plan to add CTA in first comment (not in post body) to avoid algorithm suppression for link posts. Example: Reply to your own post with Etsy link + brief CTA within 5 minutes of publishing.
- [ ] **Engagement bait included**: Post includes a question to encourage comments (e.g., "What medicinal herb surprised you this season?")
- [ ] **Hashtags placed at end**: Hashtags should be in a separate paragraph at the end, not scattered through post body
- [ ] **Tone verified**: Post matches audience expectations (professional but conversational; thought leadership, not sales)
- [ ] **Formatting clean**: Line breaks, bullet points, and white space make post scannable (LinkedIn feeds heavily penalize wall-of-text posts)

#### Instagram Post Specifics

- [ ] **Caption length optimized**: 80–150 words; longer captions get cut off on mobile, requiring "see more" click
- [ ] **Hashtags in first comment**: Post caption first, then reply to own post with hashtags (improves algorithmic distribution)
- [ ] **Visual quality high**: Image is clear, well-lit, on-brand aesthetic (not stock photo unless captioned as such)
- [ ] **Emoji use appropriate**: 1–3 emojis max; use strategically (🌿 for herbalism context, not random decorative emoji)
- [ ] **Link in bio updated**: If post has a specific Etsy link, update Instagram bio "Link in Bio" to point to that product/landing page (or use Linktree)
- [ ] **Story accompanying post** (optional): Quick behind-the-scenes or post preview story can drive traffic to feed post

#### YouTube Community Tab Specifics (Engagement Posts)

## Section 9: LinkedIn-Specific Optimization Tactics

### LinkedIn Algorithm Behavior: Key Facts

1. **First 24 hours critical**: 80% of a LinkedIn post's engagement happens in the first 24 hours. Initial engagement velocity signals the algorithm whether to promote or bury the post.

2. **Engagement weighted differently**: LinkedIn rewards *comments* more heavily than likes. A post with 10 comments and 50 likes is algorithmically stronger than a post with 5 comments and 200 likes.

3. **Link posts suppressed**: Posts with external URLs in the body text are deprioritized by 30–40% vs. link-free posts. First-comment strategy (adding link in comment instead of post body) mitigates this suppression.

4. **Connection-native content prioritized**: LinkedIn prefers content that keeps users on the platform (text, images, video) over content that drives off-platform (external URLs). Long-form text and video get 2–3x the distribution of link posts.

5. **Recency matters**: LinkedIn's algorithm decays post visibility after 48 hours. Posts older than 1 week receive minimal organic distribution unless they receive a significant engagement spike.

### Tactic 1: First-Comment Strategy (Link Placement)

**What**: Instead of embedding an Etsy link in the post body (which triggers algorithmic suppression), post the link as a comment on your own post within 5 minutes of publishing.

**Why**: LinkedIn treats native content (text, image) as more valuable than outbound links. By separating the link into a comment, the post itself remains link-free and receives better distribution. The link is still highly visible (appears immediately, often pinned to top).

**Implementation**:
1. Write and publish post WITHOUT any URL.
2. Within 5 minutes, reply to your own post with comment like: "The Women's Health bundle is available here: [ETSY_LINK]. Use code LAUNCH2026 for 15% off."
3. Pin this comment to the top (LinkedIn allows post author to pin comments).
4. Verify that the link comment is visible and clickable.

**Expected impact**: Post body remains link-free (eligible for full algorithmic distribution) while link remains prominently visible (not hidden in third-tier replies).

### Tactic 2: Engagement Bait (Question-Driven Posts)

**What**: End the post with a genuine question that invites substantive comments.

**Why**: Comments are the highest-weighted engagement signal for LinkedIn. Posts that spark 15+ comments get 5–7x better distribution than posts with the same number of likes but 2–3 comments.

**Implementation**:
1. End educational posts with a question tied to the content. Example (from Post 4): "What medicinal herb surprised you this season — either in how easy it was to grow, how much it produced, or what you learned about it?"
2. Avoid yes/no questions (they don't invite discussion): "Have you grown medicinal herbs?" is weak. "What is the one herb you wish you had grown this season?" is strong.
3. Tag 2–3 relevant people or company accounts who might answer (increases chance they see the question and respond).
4. Pin the post to your profile header for 24–48 hours (only top-performing posts; prevents clutter).

**Expected impact**: 8–15 comments per 4,000-follower post (vs. typical 2–5 comments without bait).

### Tactic 3: Hashtag Strategy (Niche + Industry + Branded)

**What**: Use 3–5 hashtags max, segmented by category.

**Why**: LinkedIn's hashtag algorithm changed in 2024. Overly salesy or spam hashtags (#herbalism #medicinalherbs on every post) get suppressed. Specific, strategic hashtags drive better results.

**Implementation**:

**Tier 1 — Always include**:
- `#seedwarden` (brand hashtag; helps followers find your content and builds brand visibility)

**Tier 2 — Industry + Niche (rotate 2–3 per post)**:
- `#herbalism` (broad; use on educational and testimonial posts)
- `#medicinalherbs` (broad; use on educational and testimonial)
- `#herbgarden` (grower-focused; use when addressing home growers)
- `#plantscience` (practitioner-focused; use on deep-dive scientific posts)
- `#botanicals` (commercial grower focus; use when addressing commercial cultivators)

**Tier 3 — Topic-specific (use when relevant)**:
- `#harvest` (harvest timing, preparation posts)
- `#womenshealth`, `#respiratory`, `#immunity`, `#sleep` (bundle-specific)
- `#foraging` (foraging safety posts)
- `#herbaleducation` (educator testimonials, practitioner tier)
- `#habitat` `#conservation` (rare native plant posts; secondary in Phase 3, primary in Phase 4)

**Example hashtag strategy for an educational post on root harvest timing**:
"#seedwarden #herbgarden #harvest"

**Example hashtag strategy for a testimonial from a practitioner**:
"#seedwarden #herbalism #herbaleducation"

**Expected impact**: Niche hashtags (#herbgarden, #plantscience) reach more qualified audiences than generic hashtags (#herbalism). Expected reach increase: 15–30% from hashtag strategy alone (measured as post impressions).

### Tactic 4: Posting Time — Weekday + Hour Optimization

**Optimal times for professional/educational content** (herbalism falls into this category):

| Time | Rationale | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **8–10am ET** | Peak LinkedIn usage (pre-work check) | Educational, testimonial, thought leadership |
| **12–1pm ET** | Lunch break (secondary peak) | Quick tips, promotional CTAs, shorter posts |
| **5–6pm ET** | After-work wind-down (tertiary peak) | Longer content, community engagement, questions |

**Weekday performance**:
- **Monday–Tuesday**: Generally strongest engagement (users return to LinkedIn after weekend)
- **Wednesday**: Stable engagement
- **Thursday**: Slight dip (weekend anticipation)
- **Friday**: Significant dip (users transitioning to weekend)
- **Saturday–Sunday**: Minimal engagement (professionals off platform)

**Recommendation for Seedwarden**:
- **Baseline**: Post Tuesday–Wednesday at 9am ET (avoids Monday chaos, hits mid-week engagement peak)
- **Secondary**: Post Thursday at 9am ET (still acceptable, slight dip OK)

### Tactic 5: Content Formatting for LinkedIn Mobile (60% of traffic)

**What**: LinkedIn traffic is 60% mobile, 40% desktop. Formatting must optimize for small screens.

**Why**: Wall-of-text posts get scrolled past on mobile. Formatted, scannable posts get read and engaged with.

**Implementation**:
1. **Short paragraphs**: 1–2 sentences per paragraph max (vs. traditional 5–7 sentences).
2. **White space**: Use line breaks generously (mobile screens cut off text; readers need visual break points).
3. **Bullet points**: Use for lists, not long sentences. Instead of "The three reasons roots should be harvested in fall are that the plant pulls energy down, active compounds concentrate, and storage begins," write:
   - Active compounds concentrate in fall
   - Energy translocates from aerial parts to roots
   - Storage organs are at maximum concentration
4. **Bold key terms**: "The harvest window for **valerian root is year 2 or 3 in fall**. Here's why..." (helps mobile scanners find key info quickly).
5. **Avoid dense paragraphs**: LinkedIn font is small on mobile. Dense text = not read.

### Tactic 6: Video Content (If Adding to Phase 3)

**Note**: Phase 3 calendar does not include video posts. If Seedwarden decides to add short-form video, use these guidelines.

**YouTube Shorts format** (15–30 seconds):
- **Hook in first 2 seconds**: Viewer decides to watch or scroll within 2 sec. No slow intros.
- **Text overlay throughout**: Audio alone insufficient; mobile users may have sound off. Use clear, large text.
- **CTA in last 3 seconds**: "Click link in bio to get the guide" or "See more in the full article."
- **Performance threshold**: <3% click-through rate (CTR) indicates video is not driving traffic. Threshold: aim for 5–8% CTR on YouTube Shorts with clear Etsy CTA.

**Video engagement metrics** (different from static post metrics):
- **View count**: Total plays
- **Average watch time**: % of video viewed on average (aim: >70% watch time = people watching to the end)
- **Click-through rate (CTR)**: (Link clicks) / (Video views) × 100. Threshold: 3%+ is acceptable; <3% means video is not driving traffic.

#### Video Performance Decision Rules

**If CTR <3% on 2+ consecutive video posts**:
- Video format is not driving traffic to Etsy.
- Action: Revert to static posts with text CTAs (likely more effective for this audience).
- Alternative: Redesign video with stronger CTA (larger text, clearer offer, time-limited framing).

**If Average Watch Time <40%**:
- Hook is too slow or content is not compelling in first 5 seconds.
- Action: Shorten intro, tighten pacing, add visual hooks (plant closeups, hands harvesting) in first 3 seconds.

**If Completion Rate >75% but CTR <3%**:
- People watch the entire video but don't click the link.
- Possible causes: CTA is unclear, offer is not compelling, link placement is hard to see.
- Action: Redesign CTA (make it bigger, clearer, or more urgent: "Get the Immunity bundle — limited stock, link in bio").

## Glossary of Key Metrics

| Term | Definition | How to Calculate | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Engagement Rate** | % of followers who engaged (liked, commented, or shared) | (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers × 100 | >8% (GREEN) |
| **Viral Coefficient** | Ratio of shares to likes; indicates network forwarding | (Shares + Saves) / Likes | >0.25 (good) |
| **Profile Visit Spike** | Increase in profile visits from a post | Current week visits – Previous week average | >200 visits/post (good signal) |
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | % of viewers who clicked the Etsy link | (Link clicks) / (Post impressions) × 100 | 2–5% for educational; 1–3% for promotional |
| **Follower Growth Rate** | Daily average followers gained | (Weekly followers gained) / 7 | +10–20/week during launch phase |
| **Comment Depth** | Average length/quality of comments | Qualitative: short (1–2 words), medium (1 sentence), long (2+ sentences) | Long comments = better engagement signal |
| **Share-to-Like Ratio** | How often post is shared vs. liked | Shares / Likes | >0.10 is good (means 1 share per 10 likes) |
