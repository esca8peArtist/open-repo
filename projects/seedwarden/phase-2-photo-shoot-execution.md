# Phase 2 Photo Shoot Execution Guide

**Purpose**: Everything needed to plan, execute, and process the May photo shoot without referencing other documents mid-session. Carry this document on shoot days alongside PHOTO_SHOOT_CHECKLIST.md. All scene descriptions and file-naming conventions are here.

**Total shots required**: 30 RAW compositions (2 per product, 15 products)
**Total shoot time**: 9-11 hours across 2 days
**Equipment**: Smartphone camera (12MP or newer). No professional equipment required.
**Budget for props**: $0 if fully stocked; $40-97 if sourcing most items new.

---

## Part 1: Location Setup and Requirements

### Window Requirements

All three clusters can be shot indoors in a standard home. The only location requirement is a window.

**Cluster A (Seeds and Garden) — highest requirement**:
- East- or south-facing window
- Minimum 3 hours of unobstructed morning light (9am-12pm)
- Window must be within 3-5 feet of a flat work surface (kitchen table, dining table, or folding table)
- Optional: white foam board or white poster board on the opposite side of the window to bounce fill light and soften shadows

Test the window before shoot day: at 10am, position a white piece of paper on the surface and take a test shot. If the paper renders as warm white (not blue or orange) with soft shadows, the window is correct. If shadows are harsh and dark, tape a white bedsheet over the window or reschedule to an overcast day.

**Cluster B (Urban/Container) — lower requirement**:
- Window sill, balcony railing, or small shelf with any natural light
- Late afternoon (3pm-5pm) is acceptable — the urban aesthetic benefits from slightly warmer tones
- If using a balcony: avoid direct sun (creates harsh shadows). Overcast or open shade is ideal.

**Cluster C (Food Preservation) — no light dependency**:
- Kitchen counter near any window
- Afternoon light is fine for this cluster
- No morning light window required

**No outdoor location booking required**: If an outdoor garden or balcony is available, Cluster A flat-lay shots can be stronger outside on an overcast day. Direct sun should be avoided for flat-lay compositions.

---

### Surface Setup

**Primary surface (Clusters A and C)**: Raw wood cutting board, 18 inches or wider. Position directly on the work surface, placed 3-5 feet from the window. The wood texture reads authentically in frame and does not reflect light like laminate surfaces.

**Secondary surface (alternate Cluster A shots)**: Dark linen or canvas cloth (navy, charcoal, or forest green), 1 yard minimum. Lay flat on the table as an alternative to the wood board when shooting products that benefit from contrast.

**Cluster B surface**: Window sill, balcony railing ledge, or small shelf. The surface does not need to be prepared — the context is the prop.

## Part 2: Complete 30-Shot Shot List

Two shots per product. Shot A is always a flat-lay (overhead, product centered). Shot B is always a contextual/in-use shot (product engaged — held, open, propped next to related props).

### Cluster A — Seeds and Garden (16 shots, 8 products)

**Product 1: Seed Saving Field Manual**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Manual cover placed at intersection of rule-of-thirds. Surrounding props: mason jar with large saved seeds (squash or bean), 3 kraft seed envelopes labeled "Brandywine Tomato 2026," small terracotta pot with soil, fresh herb sprig. Background: wood cutting board.
- Shot B (contextual): Two hands hold the manual open to the seed anatomy diagram page. Germination tray visible in background left. Background: dark linen.

**Product 2: Heirloom Variety Selection Guide**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Tablet propped at 45-degree angle showing the variety comparison page of the guide. Terracotta pots on either side, small trowel in frame, one kraft seed envelope. Background: wood board.
- Shot B (contextual): Hands hold printed cover page of the guide. Germination tray with seedlings visible in background. Fresh herb sprig in lower right corner of frame.

**Product 3: Anti-Catalog — 30 Heirlooms Worth Growing**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Printed cover page centered on wood board. Single seed envelope, mason jar with visible seeds, handwritten label "Brandywine Tomato 2026" prop in lower frame.
- Shot B (contextual): Cover page held in one hand, pencil in the other (as if annotating). Background: dark linen with shallow depth of field.

**Product 4: Companion Planting Chart**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Full-size printed chart spread on wood board (must print at full 8.5×11 for legibility). Small terracotta pot on the chart, trowel at an angle, mason jar of seeds beside. The chart text must be readable — no folds or creases.
- Shot B (contextual): Hands spread the chart flat on a surface. Germination tray visible above the hands in frame.

**Product 5: Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Printed monthly spread (April or May) placed on wood board. Germination tray of 10-day sprouts in background. Pencil resting diagonally across the calendar spread.
- Shot B (contextual): Hand points to a date on the printed calendar spread. Small seedling pot beside the hand. Background: dark linen.

**Product 6: Food Sovereignty Starter Guide**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Printed cover + one interior list page fanned open on wood board. Mason jar of large seeds, seed envelopes fanned out below.
- Shot B (contextual): Two printed pages held open toward the camera. Fresh herbs in background. Natural morning light creating a warm, documentary feel.

**Product 7: Apartment Seed Starting Kit**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Printed cover on dark linen. Germination tray of fresh sprouts beside it. 3 kraft envelopes fanned around the cover. Small trowel crossing one corner.
- Shot B (contextual): Hand holds the printed cover while the other hand places seeds into a germination cell. Close crop on the hands and the tray.

**Product 8: 12-Month Urban Growing Planner**
- Shot A (flat-lay): Printed monthly spread on wood board. Pencil resting on the spread. Small terracotta pot in upper left corner. One seed envelope in lower right.
- Shot B (contextual): Planner spread open on a surface, pencil resting in the spine. Hand reaching in from frame edge as if about to write. Background: blurred window light.

## Part 3: Shot Technique Reference

### Flat-Lay Setup (Overhead)
- Camera positioned directly above the surface, parallel to the surface (not angled)
- Use the self-timer (3 seconds) to avoid camera shake from pressing the shutter
- Stack books under the phone to achieve the correct height (28-36 inches above surface is typical)
- Rule of thirds: place the product cover at the intersection of the third lines, not centered
- Props surround the product — they do not cover it
- No more than 6 prop elements in any flat-lay (fewer is often stronger)

### Contextual Shot Setup (In-Use)
- Camera at a slightly lower angle than flat-lay (25-35 degrees off horizontal works)
- For hand shots: use the self-timer; position hands first, then start the timer
- Maintain consistency in hand positioning across clusters (same person's hands if possible)
- Depth of field: for sharp product + blurred background, open aperture to f/1.8-2.8 if camera allows; on smartphones, hold focus point close to the product
- Avoid camera strap, jewelry, or watches in frame

### Lighting Adjustments
- **Overcast day**: ideal for flat-lays. Do not add artificial light. The soft, diffuse light eliminates harsh shadows.
- **Direct sun**: hang a white bedsheet over the window to diffuse. Do not shoot in direct sun for flat-lay compositions.
- **Too dark**: do not use the phone's flash — it creates flat, clinical light. Instead, bring a second light source: a desk lamp with a warm-white (2700-3000K) bulb, positioned above and to the side.
- **White balance**: set manually to Daylight (5500K) or Cloudy (6000K) before the first shot of each cluster. Do not use Auto — Auto shifts throughout the session and produces inconsistent color between shots.

## Part 4: Editing Workflow

### Software Options (Choose One)

- **Lightroom Mobile** (free, iOS/Android): best option for batch editing with preset sync. The "Sync Settings" feature applies identical adjustments to all Cluster A shots from one edit.
- **Snapseed** (free, iOS/Android): good alternative if Lightroom is unavailable. Use the "Selective Adjust" tool for targeted corrections.
- **Apple Photos** (iOS): acceptable for basic exposure and color correction. Lacks batch sync — must edit each image individually.

### Standard Seedwarden Edit Settings

Apply these to the first Cluster A image. Then sync to all Cluster A images. Adjust Cluster B and C individually for any white balance outliers.

| Adjustment | Value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Exposure | +0.3 to +0.5 | Lifestyle photos should be 10-15% brighter than a metered exposure |
| Contrast | +20 | Adds definition between props |
| Highlights | -20 to -30 | Recover any blown white surfaces |
| Shadows | +20 to +25 | Lift shadow detail in props and paper text |
| White balance (temp) | 5200-5500K | Warm but not orange; earthy tones |
| White balance (tint) | 0 to +5 | Slight green tint removal if under LED lights |
| Saturation | -5 | Earthy, de-saturated look; avoid oversaturated greens |
| Green saturation | +12 | Lifts plant/herb colors specifically without oversaturating props |
| Clarity | +10 | Micro-contrast makes props read as tactile |
| Crop | 1:1 square | Required for Etsy square format |

**Cluster B adjustment**: If late-afternoon shots have significantly warmer tones than Cluster A, pull White Balance temperature down 200-300K (to 5000-5200K) to harmonize.

**Cluster C adjustment**: Kitchen light is often warmer than window light. If mason jar brine looks orange, pull White Balance to 4800-5000K until it reads as neutral-warm (not orange).

### Batch Editing Sequence

1. Import all Cluster A images into editing app
2. Select the best Cluster A flat-lay image as the anchor edit
3. Apply adjustments above to the anchor image
4. Sync all adjustments to all other Cluster A images at once (Lightroom: long-press the edited image > Copy Settings > select all other Cluster A images > Paste Settings)
5. Review each Cluster A image individually; adjust any outliers (±0.3 exposure is usually the only per-image correction needed)
6. Export all Cluster A images before moving to Cluster B
7. Repeat for Cluster B, then Cluster C

## Part 6: Day-of Troubleshooting

**Germination tray shows too few sprouts**: Substitute fresh sprouting seeds in a shallow dish — soak dried beans or lentils overnight. The close-crop shot of plump sprouting seeds reads well and does not require a full 10-day germination tray.

**Window light too harsh (direct sun)**: Tape or clip a white bedsheet over the window. A translucent white curtain works the same way. Do not proceed without diffusion — direct sun creates shadows that are difficult to retouch.

**Focus soft on product face**: Use the tap-to-focus function on the phone (tap on the product cover in the viewfinder before capturing). Re-shoot immediately if any shot has soft focus on the product title. Fixing focus in post is impossible.

**Props looking too staged/perfect**: Introduce natural imperfection — slightly crumple one printed page, let one seed envelope sit at an angle rather than perfectly parallel, let a fresh herb sprig droop naturally. Authentic imperfection reads more credibly than overly precise arrangements.

**No contextual shot available for a product**: Two flat-lay variants from slightly different angles (one overhead, one at 15 degrees) are acceptable for Etsy slot 5. Two flat-lays from the same session are better than a weak or blurry contextual shot.

**Session runs long and natural light fades**: Continue shooting for Cluster C under kitchen overhead light if available (afternoon light is acceptable for this cluster). For Clusters A and B, reschedule to the next morning rather than shooting in artificial light — consistency across the Cluster A set matters more than completing the session on one day.

**Phone storage runs out mid-shoot**: Transfer photos to computer mid-session. Alternatively: delete obviously unusable shots (motion blur, extreme overexposure) in camera immediately after capture to free space. Keep at minimum 3 attempts per composition before deleting.
