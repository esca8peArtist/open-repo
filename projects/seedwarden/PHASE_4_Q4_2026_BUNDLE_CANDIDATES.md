## Part 1 — Q4 Bundle Selection Framework

### Why Q4 2026 Is a Distinct Planning Window

Phase 3 runs June 29–August 3 with six bundles and six contractors. Q4 2026 is a separate production window that begins immediately after Phase 3 closes. The eight-week production gap (August 4–October 7) must carry enough writing work and photography to stock the Q4 slate before the October 8 launch date. Any bundle requiring new contractor photography that is not in production by August 15 will miss the October window — lead time for photography session booking, editing, and layout is a minimum of six weeks.

The Q4 opportunity is real and confirmed by demand data. Herbal supplement search interest reaches its annual peak in December (normalized value 100 out of 100), with the ascent beginning in August–September and accelerating through October–November. Elderberry and echinacea specifically spike 3–4 weeks before peak cold season (mid-November in the northern US), meaning the Q4-A Winter Immunity Defender must be live by October 8 to capture research traffic before buyers have already purchased elsewhere. Etsy seasonal digital product data shows wellness content peaking in November–December, with some sellers reporting 40–50% of annual revenue in that eight-week window.

### Four Scoring Dimensions (Mechanistic)

Each Q4 candidate is scored on four dimensions with numeric weights. A total score of 70+ is the GO threshold for the Q4 slate.

**Dimension 1 — Demand Signal (25 points)**
Measured by: seasonal search pattern strength (high/medium/low), Reddit community volume (r/herbalism, r/homesteading, r/WildEdibles), Etsy listing density in the keyword space (under-served = higher score), and Q4 specificity (does the demand spike concentrate in Q4, or is it year-round?).
- Year-round demand with Q4 spike: 18–22 pts
- Q4-specific demand with strong signal: 20–25 pts
- Moderate or seasonal but weak signal: 10–17 pts
- Low or unclear signal: 0–9 pts

**Dimension 2 — Profitability (25 points)**
Measured by: estimated margin on a digital guide at Seedwarden's price point ($20–$40), production hours required, and whether Phase 3 assets can be reused (reducing time-to-margin). Digital guides have near-zero unit COGS — profitability is driven by production hours and break-even unit count. Gross margin ≥ 60% on time-invested basis required for 20+ points.
- ≤ 8 production hours, reuses Phase 3 assets, prices at $26–$40: 22–25 pts
- 8–18 production hours, partial asset reuse, prices at $22–$32: 15–21 pts
- 18–30 production hours, new species/new photography needed: 8–14 pts
- 30+ production hours, full new photography commitment: 0–7 pts

**Dimension 3 — Contractor Capacity (25 points)**
Measured by: photography dependency (new vs. reuse), writing complexity (seasonal framing vs. new species research), and Phase 3 contractor availability August–September.
- No new photography, seasonal writing only (4–8 hrs writing): 22–25 pts
- Wikimedia CC photos acceptable, moderate writing (8–16 hrs): 16–21 pts
- One new photography session needed, complex writing (16–24 hrs): 8–15 pts
- Multiple new photography sessions + new species research: 0–7 pts

**Dimension 4 — Differentiation vs. Phase 3 (25 points)**
Measured by: topic overlap with the six Phase 3 bundles (Women's Health, Respiratory, Immunity, Sleep & Nervines, Digestive Support, and the sixth bundle), unique Q4 positioning, and buyer audience distinction (gift-givers are additive; repeat practitioners are cross-sell; pure overlap is low differentiation).
- Zero species overlap, novel Q4 framing, new buyer sub-segment: 22–25 pts
- Low overlap, seasonal reframe unlocks new positioning: 16–21 pts
- Moderate overlap, same audience, incremental content: 8–15 pts
- High overlap, same audience, minimal new framing: 0–7 pts

### Launch Sequence Rationale

**October 8 — Q4-A (Winter Immunity Defender)**: Timed one week before the October 15 peak demand window opens. This is the highest-demand seasonal entry point; listings need 7–10 days to reach full Etsy indexing before buyers are actively searching in volume.

**October 20 — Q4-B (Holiday Stress Relief)**: Follows Q4-A with two weeks of gap time. Each new listing triggers Etsy's 72-hour discovery window — staggering launches captures the algorithmic boost from each listing individually rather than diluting across simultaneous uploads.

**November 1 — Q4-D (Gift-Giver's Collection) and Q4-C (Digestive + Holiday Eating)**: Both live before Black Friday (November 27). Gift-Giver's Collection must be live for the full pre-holiday window; Q4-C captures the holiday eating search traffic that builds through November.

**November 15 — DIY Winter Herb Garden (stretch)**: Latest viable launch for any meaningful Q4 traffic. This is a stretch target; it adds a new buyer persona (gardener/homesteader) who is distinct from the therapeutic buyer.

**Hold — Q4-E Practitioner Collection**: Decision point July 15. If Phase 3 contractors confirm August capacity, production begins August 1 with a November 15 launch. If August capacity is unavailable, Q4-E rolls to Q1 2027.

## Part 4 — Contractor Resource Plan

### Phase 3 Contractor Footprint (Baseline)

Phase 3 runs six contractors across three tracks: photography (up to three contractors from the Phase 3 register — Upwork photographer, Kriss MacDonald licensing, Thumbtack fallback), writing (Rebecca Lexa, Arianna Collins, Adrian White), and habitat specialist (Arthur Haines, Conservation Board referral). The Phase 3 sprint closes August 3.

### Q4 Contractor Requirements by Bundle

**Q4-A, Q4-B, Q4-D (Tier 1 bundles)**: Zero new contractor dependency. All three are writing-only, reuse existing photography, and can be produced by the user directly or by a returning Phase 3 writer on a per-project rate. Estimated writing hours: 15–20 hours total across all three Tier 1 bundles. No hiring needed.

**Q4-C (Tier 2)**: Peppermint and Fennel photography is Wikimedia CC self-sourced (both species are extensively documented with CC-licensed habit photographs on Wikimedia Commons and iNaturalist). Writing: 12–16 hours for two new species profiles + holiday eating protocol. No new contractor needed if user has writing capacity; if not, any Phase 3 writer can execute these profiles.

**DIY Winter Herb Garden (stretch)**: Writing only, no photography. 10–14 hours cultivation writing. A returning Phase 3 writer at their established per-word rate is the appropriate resource.

**Q4-E Practitioner Collection (conditional)**: This is the only Q4 bundle requiring Phase 3 contractor involvement. Five new species photography sessions = minimum two Phase 3 photographer sessions in August; five new species writing profiles = 25–35 hours from a Phase 3 clinical writer (Rebecca Lexa or Adrian White at their established rates, approximately $400–600 for this scope). If Q4-E is committed, contractor onboarding must begin August 1.

### Onboarding Timeline for Q4 Hiring

For Q4, the standard rule is: hire three to four weeks before the bundle's production start date, not before the launch date. Production timelines:

- Q4-D production starts July 15 — no hiring needed (user direct)
- Q4-A production starts July 22 — no hiring needed (user direct)
- Q4-B production starts August 5 — no hiring needed (user direct)
- Q4-C production starts August 15 — if delegating to a Phase 3 writer, re-engage by August 5 (one week notice, not new hiring)
- Q4-E production starts August 1 (if committed) — Phase 3 contractor re-engagement by July 25, no new onboarding required if Phase 3 contractors confirmed available

**No net-new contractor hiring is required for the Q4 2026 bundle slate.** The entire Q4 slate from Tier 1 through Tier 2 is executable by the user directly or by reengaging Phase 3 contractors on existing rate terms. New contractor hiring for 2027 is a separate planning item covered in PHASE_4_2027_ROADMAP.md.

### "Same Six + Additional" vs. Rotate Decision

**Recommendation: Same six plus selective additions for Q4.** The six Phase 3 contractors know Seedwarden's quality standards, FTC language requirements, CITES framing, and production workflow. Replacing high-performing Phase 3 contractors in Q4 with new hires costs 2–3 weeks of onboarding overhead and introduces quality risk during the most revenue-critical window of the year. The rotation question is the right one for 2027, not Q4 2026.

The only scenario where new contractor hiring is warranted before Q4 is if multiple Phase 3 contractors decline Q4 re-engagement by August 1. In that case, the PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SOURCING_STRATEGY.md backup roster (Kolabtree, Toptal, AHG directory) is the fastest reactivation path, not a new search from scratch.
