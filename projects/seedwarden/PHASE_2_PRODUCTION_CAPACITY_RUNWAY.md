# Phase 2 Production Capacity Runway
## Sustainability Analysis — June 22 to September 1, 2026

**Purpose**: Confirm that Phase 2 content production is sustainable for 12 weeks without
burnout, quantify the resource constraints, map seasonal content angles to the summer/fall
transition, and define a resilient contingency plan for 1–2 week orchestrator unavailability.

**Baseline used**: Phase 1 confirmed 1.3h/day operations (from transition roadmap Week 1
actuals). Phase 2 adds 44–48 min/day average production overhead via batch scheduling
(from PHASE_2_CONTENT_BATCHING_STRATEGY.md). Total combined: 2h 5min–2h 25min/day.

**Critical finding**: At batched production rates, Phase 2 is sustainable for the full
12-week runway without capacity overflow — provided the batch discipline is maintained.
The risk is not volume; it is drift back to daily reactive production, which triples
effective time cost.

## Section 2: Resource Constraints

### Constraint 1: Orchestrator Availability (Primary)

**Nature**: All content production requires orchestrator input (scripting, personalization,
quality review). This is not automatable without external tools.

**Severity**: High. If orchestrator is unavailable for 2+ weeks, output drops to zero unless
a pre-produced content buffer exists (see Section 4).

**Mitigation**: Pre-production buffer protocol (Section 4) keeps 14 days of content in the
schedule queue at all times. This is the single most important operational safeguard.

---

### Constraint 2: B-Roll and Footage Library (Content Quality)

**Nature**: TikTok videos require field footage or in-frame subject matter. Without a B-roll
library, every video requires outdoor access, which is weather- and location-dependent.

**Severity**: Medium. Affects video quality but not volume — talking head videos can be
filmed indoors against a clean background with no B-roll.

**Mitigation**: Schedule one B-roll collection day per month (3 hours, outdoor). Each session
produces 20–30 clips of 15–45 seconds each, covering 3–4 weeks of weekly video production.

| B-Roll Session | Date | Target Species/Content | Covers |
|---------------|------|----------------------|--------|
| Session 1 | Week 1 (June 22–28) | Summer foraging species (purslane, chicory, elderberry) | Weeks 1–4 |
| Session 2 | Week 5 (July 20–26) | Preservation process (jars, fermenting crocks, harvest baskets) | Weeks 5–8 |
| Session 3 | Week 9 (Aug 17–23) | Fall species (brassicas, garlic planting, chicken coop prep) | Weeks 9–12 |

**Rainy day alternative**: If outdoor filming is impossible, substitute with:
- Still photography of guide pages (close-up, dramatic lighting)
- Time-lapse of seed germination (set up once, films itself)
- Flat lay production shots (guide + seasonal props on wooden surface)
These produce acceptable TikTok and Instagram content with zero outdoor access.

---

### Constraint 3: Content Research Depth (Accuracy)

**Nature**: Seedwarden's positioning is "research-backed." All field identification content
must be botanically accurate. Errors in species identification or safety notes damage
reputation and carry real harm risk for foragers.

**Severity**: High for wild edibles and medicinal herbs content. Low for homesteading,
preservation, and zone calendar content.

**Mitigation**:
- All forager/wild edibles content is scripted from existing guide text (18 species already
  documented with identification, lookalikes, and safety notes in the wild edibles archive)
- No new species introduced in TikTok scripts without cross-check against USDA PLANTS database
  or iNaturalist confirmed community ID
- Scripts for dangerous lookalike content (Queen Anne's Lace / poison hemlock, etc.) are
  reviewed against guide text before posting — not written fresh at production time

---

### Constraint 4: Platform Algorithm Changes

**Nature**: TikTok and Instagram algorithm updates can reduce organic reach for existing
content strategies. Two significant updates per year are typical.

**Severity**: Low-to-medium. Phase 2 organic reach is supplemental, not the primary revenue
driver. Paid ads are activated only after 10+ Etsy reviews and organic signal confirmation.

**Mitigation**:
- Pinterest is deliberately maintained as a high-volume, algorithm-resistant channel (pins
  have 30–180 day relevance tails; less vulnerable to feed algorithm changes)
- Each Monday batch session begins with a 5-minute review of the prior week's completion
  rate and save rate — if either drops below benchmark, adjust hook style immediately
- Email list is the algorithm-independent channel; influencer activations are building this
  list throughout Phase 2 as a fallback

---

### Constraint 5: Cash Budget (Paid Amplification)

**Nature**: Phase 2 paid social budget is $300–$500 (from transition roadmap). If revenue
targets are not hit by Week 4, paid budget may need to be deferred.

**Severity**: Low. Organic content strategy is designed to be functional at zero paid spend.
Paid is an accelerant, not a foundation.

**Mitigation**: Activate paid ads in sequence per the social growth strategy (Pinterest first
at $5/day, Instagram second at $10/day, TikTok third after organic signal confirmed). Kill
trigger is clear: pause if CPC exceeds threshold after 7 days. No open-ended spend.

## Section 4: Contingency — Orchestrator Unavailability Protocol

### Scenario: 1–2 Week Orchestrator Unavailability

**Definition**: Orchestrator (user or primary content operator) is unavailable to produce
new content for 7–14 days due to stockbot escalation, personal circumstances, or other
priority shift.

**Acceptable degradation**: Seedwarden can sustain 2 weeks of zero active production
without audience or revenue impact if — and only if — a pre-produced content buffer exists
in the scheduling queue.

### Pre-Produced Content Buffer Protocol

**Requirement**: At all times after Week 2, the scheduling queue must contain at minimum:
- 8 TikTok videos scheduled but not yet posted (2-week buffer at 4/week)
- 12 Instagram posts scheduled but not yet posted (2-week buffer at 6/week)
- 6 Pinterest pins scheduled but not yet posted (2-week buffer at 3/week)

**How to build the buffer** (Week 1 action):
During the first two batch sessions (Monday June 22 and Thursday June 25), produce double
volume: 8 TikTok videos instead of 4, 12 Instagram posts instead of 6, 6 Pinterest pins
instead of 3. This one-time double-production session (approximately 4–5 extra hours) creates
the buffer.

**Maintenance rule**: After each Monday batch session, count the queue. If any platform has
fewer than 8 posts scheduled, top it up in the same session before ending.

### Buffer Build Session Schedule (Week 1)

| Session | Date | Normal Output | Buffer Target | Extra Time |
|---------|------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| Monday primary | June 22 | 4 TikTok + 4 IG Reels | 8 TikTok + 8 IG Reels | +2h 30 min |
| Thursday static/Pinterest | June 25 | 2 IG static + 3 Pinterest | 4 IG static + 6 Pinterest | +1h 25 min |

**Total buffer-build extra time**: ~4 hours in Week 1. This is a one-time investment that
provides insurance against 2-week disruption for the entire 12-week runway.

### Content Types for the Buffer (Evergreen Priority)

Buffer content must be timeless — it cannot reference specific dates, "this week," or
"right now" in ways that will age poorly if published 2 weeks later than intended.

**Evergreen-safe content types for buffer slots**:
- Species identification (any season — no "right now" language; use "in season June–August")
- Guide interior previews (no date references)
- "Why we do X" process explanations (companion planting rationale, fermentation science)
- Behind-the-scenes / transparency posts (if they reference metrics, use "as of launch month")
- Gift-framing content (evergreen if not tied to a specific holiday)

**Avoid in buffer slots**:
- "This week's harvest" (date-pegged)
- References to specific recent events ("since I posted last Tuesday")
- Platform-specific trending audio (TikTok audio trends last 1–3 weeks; buffer content
  should use owned audio or trending-agnostic tracks)

### If Unavailability Exceeds 2 Weeks

If the orchestrator is unavailable for more than 14 days, the buffer will be exhausted.
At that point, Seedwarden enters **Passive Mode**:

| Channel | Passive Mode Behavior | Revenue Impact |
|---------|-----------------------|----------------|
| TikTok | Zero new posts; existing content continues to serve in Interest Graph | –30–50% of new follower acquisition; existing views continue |
| Instagram | Zero new posts; bio and existing posts remain | –40–60% new reach; no impact on email list (Kit automation still runs) |
| Pinterest | Pins already indexed continue to drive traffic indefinitely | Near-zero impact for first 60 days; this is Pinterest's key advantage |
| Email (Kit) | Automation sequences continue to run for new subscribers | Zero impact; fully autonomous |
| Etsy | Listings remain active; digital delivery is automatic | Zero impact; revenue continues |
| Influencer | No active outreach; existing affiliate codes still work | Zero new relationships; existing partners' codes still convert |

**Passive Mode revenue projection**: Based on Phase 1 data ($1,341 with zero social accounts),
passive mode sustains approximately 60–70% of Phase 2 revenue for up to 30 days. Beyond 30
days, organic social traffic decline begins to affect Etsy discovery visibility.

**Re-activation protocol** (first day back from unavailability):
1. Pull scheduling queue — how many posts remain?
2. If queue is empty: run double-production buffer session (Monday + Thursday in the same week)
3. If queue has 1–3 posts: run normal Monday session with buffer top-up
4. Check influencer response queue — any HOT responses that went unhandled? Respond first
5. Pull weekly metrics (TikTok completion rates, Instagram save rates, Etsy conversion,
   Kit sign-ups) — identify any drop-off that needs content adjustment

## Section 5: Sustainability Stress Test

**Question**: At what point does Phase 2 production become unsustainable?

### Failure Conditions

Phase 2 production fails to be sustainable when any of these conditions emerge:

| Condition | Threshold | Early Warning Signal |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Daily production drifts back to reactive (not batched) | >3 days in a row of same-day production | Monday session missed for 2 consecutive weeks |
| Orchestrator unavailability without buffer | >14 days; buffer empty | Queue count falls below 4 posts on any platform |
| Content quality decline (from overextension) | TikTok completion rate drops below 40% for 2 consecutive weeks | 3 consecutive videos under 55% completion rate |
| Revenue fails to cover content time investment | Seedwarden monthly revenue <$1,500 by Week 8 | Week 4 revenue below $600 (below Phase 1 monthly baseline) |

**Action at each warning signal**:

- **Batch discipline broken**: Cancel next 3 daily sessions and schedule a single 2.5-hour
  Monday catch-up. Reschedule all outstanding content in one batch.

- **Buffer depleted**: Prioritize buffer rebuild over influencer outreach that week.
  Buffer is the safety net; outreach can resume the following week.

- **Completion rate drops**: Review last 5 TikTok hooks. The problem is almost always
  the hook (first 1.5 seconds), not the content body. Switch hook type; test a contrarian
  or urgency hook if you have been running identification hooks.

- **Revenue below threshold**: Do not increase content volume — increase conversion. Run
  the Etsy listing SEO audit (tags, description keywords), check if Kit sign-up conversion
  rate is above 15%, and activate one round of direct influencer promotion (not outreach —
  ask an existing T3 partner to post with their affiliate code).

### Sustainability Verdict at 12 Weeks

The Phase 2 production model is **sustainably viable for the full 12-week runway** under
the following conditions (all of which are controllable):

1. Monday and Thursday batch sessions are protected from scheduling conflicts. These two
   blocks (totaling ~4 hours/week) are the entire production engine.

2. The pre-produced buffer is built in Week 1 and maintained at 8+ posts per platform.

3. B-roll collection sessions are scheduled monthly (3 hours/session, 3 sessions total).
   Without these, video quality degrades in Weeks 5–8 when the launch-week footage is consumed.

4. Content research accuracy is maintained by using existing guide text as the primary script
   source — no off-the-cuff identification claims on wild edibles or medicinals.
