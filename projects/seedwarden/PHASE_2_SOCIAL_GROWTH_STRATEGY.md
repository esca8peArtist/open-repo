# Seedwarden Phase 2 Social Growth Strategy
## Cohort-Based Customer Acquisition — May 30, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Seedwarden's four customer cohorts — Forager/Wildcrafter, Prepper/Survivalist, Homesteader/Farmer, and Gift Buyer — do not share a platform, a content preference, or a discovery mechanism. Treating them as one audience is the single most common mistake small Etsy sellers make on social media. This strategy assigns each cohort a primary platform, a content formula, a realistic paid budget floor, and a set of organic mechanics calibrated to their behavior.

**CAC/LTV Projections (30-day launch window, May 30 – June 28):**

| Cohort | Est. CAC (organic) | Est. CAC (paid) | LTV (12-month) | CAC:LTV Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Forager/Wildcrafter | $6–10 | $14–22 | $85–120 | 1:8–1:12 |
| Prepper/Survivalist | $8–14 | $18–30 | $120–180 | 1:7–1:10 |
| Homesteader/Farmer | $5–9 | $12–20 | $75–110 | 1:9–1:14 |
| Gift Buyer | $10–18 | $20–35 | $30–55 | 1:2–1:3 |

**Reading these projections**: CAC is cost to acquire one paying customer, not one social follower. Organic CAC assumes 6–8 hours/week of content creation (valued at $0 cash cost but real labor cost). Paid CAC assumes $7–10/day budget. LTV is based on existing cohort-model projections in `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md`. Gift Buyer CAC:LTV is the weakest ratio because repeat purchase rate is low (1–2 purchases/year vs. 3–6 for Forager) — but the absolute volume at holidays makes it worth running.

**Day-1 execution priorities (May 30)**:
1. Homesteader/Farmer — Instagram Reels (spring peak, highest LTV efficiency)
2. Forager/Wildcrafter — TikTok (highest organic velocity, no paid needed in Week 1)
3. Gift Buyer — Pinterest (evergreen pinning, compound over 30 days)
4. Prepper/Survivalist — YouTube Shorts + Instagram (hold paid ads until organic signal exists)

## Cohort 1: Forager / Wildcrafter

### Platform: TikTok (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why TikTok**: The foraging and wildcrafting community is structurally a TikTok-native audience in 2026. Alexis Nikole Nelson (@alexisnikole, 4M followers), FindersFeeders (240K+), @chaoticforager, and @wellfedwild have built the forager audience on TikTok first. The algorithm rewards niche educational content with specific completion-rate signals — a 45-second "identify this plant" video with a strong hook gets served to the existing foraging community through the "Interest Graph" before it goes to the "Social Graph." That means zero-follower accounts can reach 5,000–20,000 niche-correct viewers in the first 48 hours on a good post.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Field identification** — "3 wild plants you walked past today that are edible" format (15–45 sec, in-the-field footage, text overlay with plant names)
- **Seasonal urgency** — "Ramp season ends in 2 weeks — here's how to find them" (creates time pressure that drives guide purchases)
- **Safety/conservation** — "The forager's rule for sustainable harvesting" (positions Seedwarden as responsible, not just transactional)
- **Guide preview** — Show 3 pages of the Wild Edibles guide with voiceover: "this is what a 150-page field guide looks like vs. the 5-page PDFs you see everywhere"
- **Community challenge** — "What wild edible did you find this week? Drop it below" (comments signal to the algorithm)

**Hashtag Strategy**:
- TikTok primary: `#foraging` `#wildedibles` `#wildcrafting` `#foragingtiktok` `#foragedfoods`
- TikTok secondary: `#plantidentification` `#edibleplants` `#mushroomhunting` `#herbalism` `#foodforest`
- Etsy-bridge tags: `#foragingguide` `#wildediblesguide` `#digitaldownload`
- Community tags: `#foragingcommunity` `#naturewalk` `#naturespharmacy`
- Avoid: `#survival` (crosses into Prepper cohort, dilutes algorithm targeting)

**Micro-Influencer Targets** (5–10 outreach contacts):
1. **@chaoticforager** (Gabrielle) — science-based foraging education, TikTok, mushroom focus, estimated 30K–60K range, authentic science framing aligns with Seedwarden's research-backed positioning
2. **@thegardencottage** (Megan) — beginner-friendly foraging, monthly seasonal guides, audience overlaps with Seedwarden's guide format
3. **@sonofa_bear** (James Mahan) — mushroom varieties, newsletter+courses model, likely receptive to product partnerships
4. **@wellfedwild** (Samuel Thayer) — published foraging author, environmental advocacy, high authority signal
5. **@whichbetty** (Elizabeth Burdock) — foraged flowers and mushrooms, sustainable living, Instagram-native but cross-posts
6. **@outdoorapothecary** — foraging + wildcrafting blog-to-social pipeline, herbalism overlap with medicinal guides
7. **@growingthroughtheweeds** (16.1K TikTok, NC homestead) — small enough for gifted-product collab, real engagement

**Outreach offer**: Gifted product (1–2 digital guides relevant to their niche) + 15% affiliate code for their audience. No fee expected at this scale. Email DM: "We make research-backed field guides for foragers — I'd love to send you [Wild Edibles Guide / Mushroom Guide] to review honestly. If your audience loves it, we can set up an affiliate link."

**Paid Campaign Framework**:
- Platform: TikTok Promote (lower entry than Ads Manager; works at $3–5/day)
- Objective: Profile visits → bio link click (Kit sign-up for zone card lead magnet)
- Targeting: Interest clusters — Nature & Outdoors, Foraging, Wildcrafting, Herbalism
- Creative: Boost best-performing organic video (the one that already has 3%+ completion rate)
- Budget: $5/day × 30 days = $150 test budget
- Success metric: $1.50–$2.50 cost-per-link-click (Kit page visit)
- Do not run TikTok Ads Manager at Phase 2 — $50/day minimum is above budget floor; use Promote only

**Organic Growth Mechanics**:
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours on days 1–14 (TikTok comment velocity is an early algorithmic signal)
- Post a "stitch" reaction to a popular foraging video within the first week (piggybacks existing distribution)
- Pin the "best performing guide preview video" to TikTok profile as first impression
- Cross-post TikToks to Instagram Reels (exact same video, no editing required) for secondary reach
- Email capture flow: TikTok bio → Kit zone card landing page → email sequence triggers Forager-specific onboarding

## Cohort 2: Prepper / Survivalist

### Platform: YouTube Shorts (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why YouTube Shorts**: The survivalist/prepper audience skews older (35–60), is suspicious of algorithm manipulation, and trusts YouTube as an information authority platform more than TikTok. YouTube Shorts in 2026 drive discovery to long-form content — a 60-second Short can prompt a channel subscription that leads to guide purchases via the link in description. The audience actively searches YouTube for "how to" survival content, meaning keyword-optimized Shorts surface in Google search results as well. This dual-discovery (YouTube search + Google search) is unavailable on TikTok or Instagram. Secondary audience exists on Instagram via @buckskin_revolution, @graybeardedgreenberet, @naturvival and related accounts.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Skill demonstration** — "3 edible plants you can find in winter" (high search value, evergreen)
- **Scenario framing** — "If grocery stores closed tomorrow, what grows in your county?" (emotional resonance without fearmongering)
- **Product credibility** — "Why our Hunting & Trapping Manual covers 8 regions, not just one" (educational product positioning)
- **Community validation** — "A prepper from Montana sent us this: [testimonial]" (social proof by identity)
- **Seasonal prep triggers** — October: "Fall is the time to build your wild food knowledge before winter"

**Hashtag Strategy**:
- YouTube Shorts tags: `#survival` `#preppers` `#offgrid` `#selfreliance` `#wildernesssurvival`
- Instagram secondary: `#prepperlife` `#selfsufficiency` `#survivalist` `#bugout` `#foodstorage`
- Community tags: `#homesteadlife` `#offgridliving` `#shtf` (used authentically, not alarmist)
- Search-optimized titles (YouTube): "Best Edible Plants for Preppers [Zone X]" / "Survival Garden Plant ID Guide"
- Avoid: `#doomsday` `#conspiracy` — attracts wrong audience, risks platform moderation flags

**Micro-Influencer Targets**:
1. **@buckskin_revolution** (40K Instagram) — ancestral land-based survival skills, aligns with Seedwarden's wild plant knowledge angle
2. **@graybeardedgreenberet** (36K Instagram) — former ranger, survival training, high-trust authority figure for the prepper cohort
3. **@naturvival** (28K Instagram) — "Carry Modern. Practice Primitive," overlaps perfectly with Seedwarden's foraging + survival positioning
4. **@danwowak** (30K Instagram) — outdoor skills instruction, bushcraft training
5. **@ravenwildernessschool** (35K Instagram) — Northeast bushcraft and survival school, institutional credibility
6. **@bugoutbagmethod** (10K Instagram) — small but highly targeted prepper audience, likely open to gifted product outreach
7. **@tj.thewildernessman** (53K Instagram) — 30+ years crafting survival skills, established credibility

**Outreach offer**: Gift 2–3 guides (Hunting Manual + Meat/Fish Preservation + Survival Garden Bundle) + joint "survival library" framing. These influencers value credibility over aesthetics — lead with content depth ("150-page, zone-specific, original research") not lifestyle photography.

**Paid Campaign Framework**:
- Platform: Instagram Ads (Meta) — the prepper audience's Instagram usage makes Meta more effective than TikTok Promote for this cohort
- Objective: Traffic to Etsy listing (direct purchase intent)
- Targeting: Interest — Emergency Preparedness, Wilderness Survival, Self-Sufficiency, Homesteading; lookalike audience from email list if available
- Creative: Static image ad — "150-page wild edibles guide. Zone-specific. Instant download. $14." (direct, no fluff)
- Budget: $10/day × 30 days = $300 test budget (Instagram minimum for meaningful data collection)
- Success metric: CPC under $0.90; ROAS ≥ 2.0 (spend $10, generate $20+ revenue) within 14 days
- Note: Do not activate paid until 10+ organic Etsy reviews exist — social proof reduces CPA by 30–40%

**Organic Growth Mechanics**:
- Post YouTube Shorts weekly with keyword-first titles (treat like SEO content, not social content)
- Engage in r/preppers, r/homesteading subreddits with genuinely helpful comments (no spam; link only when directly relevant)
- Answer questions in prepper Facebook groups with expertise; include guide reference only when answering a direct question the guide solves
- Email sequence: Prepper sign-ups via Kit → "Food Security in Uncertain Times" onboarding series → bundle offer at Day 7

## Cohort 3: Homesteader / Farmer

### Platform: Instagram Reels (primary), Pinterest (secondary)

**Why Instagram Reels**: Instagram is the dominant platform for Etsy sellers in 2026 (46.1% of Etsy Pattern stores use it, vs. 16.5% for Pinterest). Homesteaders skew 25–65 years old and split between Instagram and Facebook, but Instagram Reels reach rate of 30.81% — the highest of any Instagram format — makes it the strongest organic discovery tool. Homesteading content performs best in process/transformation formats: before-and-after garden transformations, preservation sequences, seed saving demonstrations. The Homesteader cohort has the highest LTV efficiency (CAC:LTV ratio 1:9–1:14) making it the most valuable cohort to acquire organically. Pinterest is secondary because homesteaders use Pinterest for project planning and inspiration boards — product discovery via Pinterest compounds over 30–90 days as pins age.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Process documentation** — "How I seed-save tomatoes for next season" (15–30 sec Reel, satisfying completion)
- **Transformation** — "What a companion planting chart looks like in practice vs. the generic ones" (product contrast)
- **Permaculture principles** — "3 plants that fix nitrogen and belong in every food forest" (educational, no sales)
- **Seasonal prep** — "What I'm doing in my garden this week" series (consistent weekly content anchor)
- **Community spotlight** — Repost customer photos of their gardens with tag (UGC + community building)

**Hashtag Strategy**:
- Instagram primary: `#homesteading` `#homesteadlife` `#growyourown` `#permaculture` `#seedsaving`
- Instagram secondary: `#sustainableliving` `#foodforest` `#heirloomseeds` `#cottagegarden` `#foraging`
- Pinterest board names (SEO-optimized): "Seed Saving Guides," "Zone Planting Charts," "Wild Edibles by Season," "Food Preservation Methods," "Permaculture Design"
- Etsy bridge: `#etsydigital` `#digitaldownload` `#homesteadingguide`

**Micro-Influencer Targets**:
1. **@shayeelliott** (85K Instagram) — farm life in Washington, simple living, female audience that gift-buys for each other (homesteader + gift buyer crossover)
2. **@justinrhodesshow** (93.9K TikTok) — permaculture and chicken farming education, cross-platform audience
3. **@smarterbynature** (21.4K TikTok) — food growing and medicinal herb content, small but highly targeted
4. **@michelles_farmstead** (35.6K TikTok) — homestead tips and DIY farming ideas, active community
5. **@tatumyada** (11.9K TikTok) — Oklahoma homesteading for new mothers, young homesteader demographic
6. **@thetravelingfarmacy** (48.6K TikTok) — mobile farm operations, practical skills focus
7. **@motherhenshomestead** — homesteading + homeschooling, multi-generational homestead lifestyle

**Outreach offer**: Gift 2 guides (Medicinal Plants + Seed Saving Field Manual) + seasonal content collaboration ("We made a guide for your audience's fall preservation season — can we co-create a Reel around it?"). Homesteading influencers respond well to project-based collaborations, not just product reviews.

**Paid Campaign Framework**:
- Platform: Instagram/Meta Ads
- Objective: Conversions — target Etsy listing or Kit email sign-up
- Targeting: Interest — Homesteading, Permaculture, Sustainable Living, Seed Saving, Organic Gardening; demographic 28–55, US-only
- Creative: Carousel ad — 3–5 slides showing guide pages with captions ("147 pages of wild edibles," "Zone-specific planting charts," "Instant download")
- Budget: $10/day × 30 days = $300 test budget
- Success metric: CPC under $0.80; email sign-up rate ≥ 15% from Kit landing page
- Pinterest Promoted Pins (parallel): $5/day for "Seed Saving Guide" and "Zone Planting Chart" pins — CPM $2–5 makes this the cheapest awareness channel per impression

**Organic Growth Mechanics**:
- Repost Reels to Pinterest as video Pins immediately (Instagram video format is compatible)
- Create Pinterest boards for each guide category; pin 3–5 pieces of non-promotional content per board before adding product pins (ratio: 4 educational pins per 1 product pin)
- Instagram Stories: weekly "what's in season" poll or question sticker — drives DMs and saves algorithmic signals
- Cross-sell sequence: Homesteader email subscribers → Seed Saving → Native Plants → Medicinal Plants (seasonal rotation)

## Cohort 4: Gift Buyer

### Platform: Pinterest (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why Pinterest**: Gift buyers use Pinterest differently than any other cohort — they are in active discovery mode, building boards of ideas for upcoming occasions. Pinterest functions as a search engine for gift inspiration, not a social network. Pins drive evergreen traffic for 30–180 days after posting (vs. TikTok's 24–48 hour lifecycle). The Gift Buyer cohort has two concentration windows: Mother's Day (late April – May 10) and holiday season (November – December). A pin posted May 15 will still surface in gift searches through June. The key mechanics: vertical 2:3 ratio images, gift-framing in pin title ("Unique Gift for the Naturalist Who Has Everything"), and Etsy Rich Pin integration for automatic price display. Instagram secondary for lifestyle content and holiday gift guide placements.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Occasion-specific framing** — "Gifts for the naturalist mom" / "Father's Day for the outdoorsman" / "Holiday gift for the forager"
- **Lifestyle aesthetic** — Guide open on wooden table, coffee and foraged herbs in background, natural window light (Phase 2 photography directly serves this)
- **Gift "story"** — "I got this for my dad who forages every weekend — he hasn't put it down"
- **Value articulation** — "Unlike most nature guides, this one is actually specific to your region"
- **Urgency** — "Ships digitally — last-minute gift that arrives instantly"

**Hashtag Strategy**:
- Pinterest (titles, not hashtags — Pinterest is keyword-SEO, not hashtag-driven):
  - "Unique Nature Gifts for Her 2026"
  - "Foraging Guide Gift Ideas"
  - "Sustainable Gift for Plant Lover"
  - "Best Gifts for Homesteaders"
  - "Nature Lover Gift Guide"
- Instagram hashtags: `#giftideas` `#naturegift` `#sustainablegifts` `#uniquegifts` `#plantlover`
- Pinterest board names: "Nature Lover Gift Guide," "Gifts for Foragers," "Sustainable Gift Ideas," "Unique Outdoor Gifts"

**Micro-Influencer Targets**:
1. **@shayeelliott** (85K Instagram) — crosses Homesteader/Gift Buyer; her audience buys gifts for each other
2. Sustainable lifestyle gift guides — search Instagram for `#sustainablegifts` `#ecogifts` accounts 10K–40K with gift-guide content cadence
3. **Outdoorsy parent/family accounts** — 10K–30K Instagram followers, "cottage life" or "nature play" positioning; audience buys nature gifts for kids and family
4. **Nature journaling accounts** — small, 5K–20K, Instagram-native; their audience (art + nature crossover) is a gift buyer for Seedwarden products
5. Home/garden lifestyle accounts with seasonal gift guide content — look for accounts that post "holiday gift guide" content in November and "Mother's Day finds" in May

**Outreach offer**: Product gift (1 premium guide) + invitation to be featured in Seedwarden's "Gifts for naturalists" seasonal email and Pinterest board. Frame as mutual visibility, not paid placement. Gift buyers follow lifestyle accounts — the influencer's aesthetic alignment matters more than their size.

**Paid Campaign Framework**:
- Platform: Pinterest Promoted Pins (primary — CPM $2–5 is cheapest reach per impression of any platform)
- Objective: Traffic to Etsy listing (gift buyers convert on discovery, not after email nurture)
- Targeting: Interest — Nature, Gardening, Gifts, Sustainable Living; keywords "gifts for naturalists," "foraging guide gift," "nature lover gift"
- Creative: Vertical pin (1000×1500px) — lifestyle photo of guide with gift bow or wrapped presentation; price overlay optional if Rich Pins enabled
- Budget: $5/day × 30 days = $150 test budget (Pinterest is the lowest minimum-effective-budget platform)
- Timing: Activate May 25 (5 days before launch) — pins take 3–5 days to enter full distribution
- Secondary: Instagram Story Ads for Mother's Day window only (May 1–10) — $10/day for 10 days = $100 spike budget
- Success metric: CPC under $0.60 (Pinterest average $0.10–$1.50); Etsy listing page visits with 2%+ conversion

**Organic Growth Mechanics**:
- Pin 5–7 product pins across 3 gift-themed boards before May 30 (pins need 5+ days to index in Pinterest search)
- Add Etsy listing URLs to each pin — Pinterest's Etsy integration surfaces price and availability automatically when Rich Pins are enabled
- Create a "Gifts for Naturalists" board and curate 10–15 third-party pins before adding your own (board authority builds from content volume, not just self-promotion)
- Post 2 Instagram Reels in the "gift reveal" format in the week before Mother's Day
- Email sequence: Gift Buyer sign-ups → "Perfect gifts for every season" series → Mother's Day offer → holiday guide (November)

## Organic Growth Mechanics: Cross-Cohort

### UGC (User-Generated Content) Flywheel
- Post-purchase email (Day 5): "Can you share a photo of your guide in use? Tag us @seedwarden"
- Feature every UGC on Instagram Stories within 48 hours
- Pinterest: Create a "Seedwarden in the Wild" board featuring customer photos
- TikTok: Stitch customer content when it appears ("This is exactly why we made this guide")

### Review Velocity Sequence
- Etsy automated message (Day 3 post-purchase): light reminder that reviews help other buyers find the guides
- Email (Day 7 post-purchase): seasonal follow-up with one piece of related content + soft review request
- High-LTV customers (Forager, Homesteader): invite to beta-test next guide in exchange for a detailed review

### Cross-Selling Sequences by Cohort
- Forager → Wild Edibles purchase → upsell: Medicinal Plants guide (Day 10 email)
- Homesteader → Seed Saving purchase → upsell: Native Plants Regional Guide (Day 14 email)
- Prepper → Hunting Manual purchase → upsell: Meat/Fish Preservation (immediate post-purchase Etsy message)
- Gift Buyer → first purchase → no aggressive upsell; seasonal re-engagement at Mother's Day / holiday season

### Zone Card Lead Magnet Optimization
The zone card lead magnet is the highest-leverage free conversion tool in the social funnel. Every platform's bio links to the Kit landing page. Optimization priorities:
- Test landing page headline monthly (start: "Free Zone Planting Card for Your Region")
- Add social proof counter when 100+ subscribers: "Join 100+ growers who already have theirs"
- A/B test the call-to-action button text: "Get My Free Card" vs. "Download Now" vs. "Yes, Send It"
