# Seedwarden Phase 2 Social Growth Strategy
## Cohort-Based Customer Acquisition — May 30, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 1 closed at $1,341 revenue across 47 orders at a 2.24% Etsy conversion rate. That baseline tells us three things: (1) buyers exist and will convert when they find us, (2) volume — not conversion rate — is the binding constraint, and (3) we have zero social proof infrastructure to support paid acquisition. Phase 2 social strategy fixes all three.

Seedwarden's four customer cohorts — Forager/Wildcrafter, Prepper/Survivalist, Homesteader/Farmer, and Gift Buyer — do not share a platform, a content preference, or a discovery mechanism. Treating them as one audience is the single most common mistake small Etsy sellers make on social media. This strategy assigns each cohort a primary platform, a content formula, observable behavioral signals that tell you when it is working, a realistic paid budget floor, and a set of organic mechanics calibrated to their actual behavior.

**Phase 1 Baseline (for CAC/LTV context):**

| Metric | Phase 1 Result |
|---|---|
| Total revenue | $1,341 |
| Total orders | 47 |
| Average order value | $28.53 |
| Etsy conversion rate | 2.24% |
| Implied visitors needed per sale | ~45 |
| Social-sourced orders (estimated) | 0 — no social accounts active |

**CAC/LTV Projections (30-day launch window, May 30 – June 28):**

| Cohort | Est. CAC (organic) | Est. CAC (paid) | LTV (12-month) | CAC:LTV Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Forager/Wildcrafter | $6–10 | $14–22 | $85–120 | 1:8–1:12 |
| Prepper/Survivalist | $8–14 | $18–30 | $120–180 | 1:7–1:10 |
| Homesteader/Farmer | $5–9 | $12–20 | $75–110 | 1:9–1:14 |
| Gift Buyer | $10–18 | $20–35 | $30–55 | 1:2–1:3 |

**Reading these projections**: CAC is cost to acquire one paying customer, not one social follower. Organic CAC assumes 6–8 hours/week of content creation (valued at $0 cash cost but real labor cost). Paid CAC assumes $7–10/day budget. LTV is modeled on existing cohort projections in `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md`. Gift Buyer CAC:LTV is the weakest ratio because repeat purchase rate is low (1–2 purchases/year vs. 3–6 for Forager) — but absolute volume at gift windows makes it worth running.

**Day-1 execution priorities (May 30)**:
1. Homesteader/Farmer — Instagram Reels (spring peak, highest LTV efficiency, lowest CAC)
2. Forager/Wildcrafter — TikTok (highest organic velocity, no paid needed in Week 1)
3. Gift Buyer — Pinterest (evergreen pinning, compounds over 30 days)
4. Prepper/Survivalist — YouTube Shorts + Instagram (hold paid ads until organic signal confirmed)

## Cohort 1: Forager / Wildcrafter

### Platform: TikTok (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why TikTok**: The foraging and wildcrafting community is structurally a TikTok-native audience in 2026. The algorithm rewards niche educational content based on completion-rate signals — a 45-second "identify this plant" video with a strong hook gets served to the existing foraging community through the Interest Graph before it reaches the Social Graph. Zero-follower accounts regularly reach 5,000–20,000 niche-correct viewers in the first 48 hours on a strong post. Alexis Nikole Nelson (4M TikTok), FindersFeeders, @chaoticforager, and @wellfedwild built the forager audience on TikTok first. Grow Forage Cook Ferment's absence from TikTok (despite 148K Instagram followers) is a specific gap Seedwarden can capture now.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Field identification** — "3 wild plants you walked past today that are edible" (15–45 sec, in-the-field, text overlay)
- **Seasonal urgency** — "Ramp season ends in 2 weeks — here's how to find them" (time pressure drives guide purchases)
- **Safety/conservation** — "The forager's rule for sustainable harvesting" (responsible positioning)
- **Guide preview** — Show 3 pages of the Wild Edibles guide with voiceover: "This is what 150 pages of field-specific research looks like vs. a generic PDF"
- **Community challenge** — "What wild edible did you find this week? Drop it below" (comments signal algorithm)

**Observable Signals That It Is Working**:
- TikTok video completion rate above 60% (industry benchmark: 40% is average)
- Comment-to-view ratio above 0.5% (indicates genuine community engagement, not passive scroll)
- Profile visits from non-followers greater than 30% of total profile traffic
- Kit landing page visits from TikTok bio link greater than 5/day by Day 14
- At least 1 organic video reaching 10,000+ views by Day 21

**Hashtag Strategy**:
- TikTok primary (3–5 per post, per 2026 algorithm guidance): `#foraging` `#wildedibles` `#wildcrafting`
- TikTok secondary (rotate): `#foragingguide` `#plantidentification` `#edibleplants` `#foragingcommunity`
- Avoid: `#survival` (crosses Prepper cohort, dilutes Interest Graph targeting)
- Instagram (3–5 only): `#foraging` `#wildcrafting` `#wildediblesguide` `#digitaldownload`

**Micro-Influencer Targets** (5–10 outreach contacts):

Template: "We make research-backed field guides for foragers — I'd love to send you [Wild Edibles Guide / Mushroom Guide] to review honestly. If your audience loves it, we can set up an affiliate link."

- Success metric: $1.50–$2.50 cost-per-link-click
- Kill trigger: Pause if cost-per-click exceeds $3.00 after 7 days

**Organic Growth Mechanics**:
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours on Days 1–14 (comment velocity is an early TikTok signal)
- Post a "stitch" reaction to a popular foraging video within Week 1 (piggybacks existing distribution)

## Cohort 2: Prepper / Survivalist

### Platform: YouTube Shorts (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why YouTube Shorts**: The survivalist/prepper audience skews 35–60 years old, is skeptical of TikTok algorithm dynamics, and treats YouTube as a primary information authority. YouTube Shorts drive discovery into long-form content — a 60-second Short can prompt a channel subscription that leads to guide purchases via the description link. Critically, YouTube Shorts also surface in Google search results, giving keyword-optimized Shorts dual-discovery (YouTube search + Google search) that TikTok and Instagram cannot match. @graybeardedgreenberet (36K Instagram) and @buckskin_revolution (40K Instagram) built the survivalist audience primarily through YouTube-adjacent trust structures before expanding to Instagram.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Skill demonstration** — "3 edible plants you can find in winter" (high search value, evergreen)
- **Scenario framing** — "If grocery stores closed tomorrow, what grows in your county?" (emotional resonance without fearmongering)
- **Product credibility** — "Why our Hunting & Trapping Manual covers 8 regions, not just one" (educational product positioning)
- **Community validation** — "A prepper from Montana sent us this: [testimonial]" (social proof by identity)
- **Seasonal prep triggers** — "Fall is the time to build your wild food knowledge before winter"

**Observable Signals That It Is Working**:
- YouTube Shorts click-through rate above 8% on keyword-optimized titles (Google Discover baseline)
- Instagram saves rate above 3% on plant ID posts (saves indicate "I will use this later" intent — purchase precursor)
- Reddit/Facebook group mentions or direct referrals from community engagement
- Email sign-ups from Prepper cohort segment reaching 20+ by Day 30
- Etsy listing views from YouTube bio link (via UTM tracking) greater than 0 by Day 21

**Hashtag Strategy**:

## Cohort 3: Homesteader / Farmer

Homesteaders skew 25–65 years old and use Instagram more than TikTok as their primary visual social platform. Instagram Reels reach rate of 30.81% is the highest of any Instagram format and among the highest organic reach rates on any major platform. The Homesteader cohort has the best CAC:LTV efficiency (1:9–1:14), making it the most valuable cohort to acquire organically. Accounts like @shayeelliott (85K), @michelles_farmstead (35.6K TikTok), and @justinrhodesshow (93.9K TikTok) demonstrate that process-oriented, satisfying-to-watch content dominates engagement in this niche.

## Cohort 4: Gift Buyer

### Platform: Pinterest (primary), Instagram (secondary)

**Why Pinterest**: Gift buyers use Pinterest in active discovery mode — building boards for upcoming occasions weeks before purchase. Pinterest functions as a gift inspiration search engine, not a social network. Pins drive evergreen traffic for 30–180 days after posting (vs. TikTok's 24–48 hour lifecycle and Instagram's 6–12 hour feed window). The Gift Buyer cohort has two concentration windows: Mother's Day (late April – May 10) and holiday season (November – December). A pin posted May 15 surfaces in gift searches through June. Pinterest's AI-driven search (which now references 16,000 past user interactions instead of 100) means keyword-optimized pins from new accounts surface faster than in previous years. The platform's 86.6M active buyers and $48 average order value in 2025 are structurally favorable for Seedwarden's $14–$35 price range.

**Content Pillars**:
- **Occasion-specific framing** — "Gifts for the naturalist mom" / "Father's Day for the outdoorsman"
- **Lifestyle aesthetic** — Guide open on wooden table, foraged herbs and coffee in background, natural window light
- **Gift "story"** — "I got this for my dad who forages every weekend — he hasn't put it down"
- **Value articulation** — "Unlike most nature guides, this one is specific to your region"
- **Digital urgency** — "Ships digitally — last-minute gift that arrives instantly"

**Observable Signals That It Is Working**:
- Pinterest monthly impressions above 5,000 by Day 20 (new accounts start low; this signals indexing success)
- Pin save rate above 2% (saves trigger Pinterest's re-distribution algorithm)
- Etsy listing clicks from Pinterest Rich Pin attribution above 10/week by Day 21
- Instagram gift-framed Reel reaching 500+ saves (saves from non-followers signal gift-buyer intent)
- Email sign-ups that open the "Gifts for Naturalists" seasonal sequence above 5 by Day 30

**Hashtag/SEO Strategy** (Pinterest is keyword-SEO, not hashtag-driven):
- Pin titles: "Unique Nature Gifts for Her 2026," "Foraging Guide Gift Ideas," "Sustainable Gift for Plant Lovers," "Best Gifts for Homesteaders"
- Board names: "Nature Lover Gift Guide," "Gifts for Foragers," "Sustainable Gift Ideas," "Unique Outdoor Gifts"
- Instagram hashtags (secondary): `#giftideas` `#naturegift` `#sustainablegifts` `#uniquegifts` `#plantlover`
