# Phase 1 and Track B Parallel Execution Plan

**Purpose**: This document defines the exact week-by-week task split between Phase 1 (Etsy live + email campaign) and Track B (social media, mockup finalization, pin production) for the four-week launch window. It answers the operational question the user must decide: run both tracks simultaneously, or complete Phase 1 before starting Track B?

**Decision framework**: Both paths are documented. The concurrent path is recommended if 8+ hours per week are available. The sequential path is recommended if less time is available or if Phase 1 shows conversion problems in Week 1 (see Risk Gate 1 below).

## Section 2: Float Analysis

Float is the number of days a task can be delayed without affecting the critical path to the nearest milestone (first sale for Phase 1, first social post for Track B).

| Task | Assigned Track | Float | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Etsy account verification | Phase 1 | 0 — critical path | Binary gate; all Phase 1 blocked without it |
| Etsy shop setup (banner, policies) | Phase 1 | 0 | Must precede any listing upload |
| Upload 6 Phase 1 listings | Phase 1 | 0 | First sale impossible without at least 1 listing live |
| Email automation (Kit welcome sequence) | Phase 1 | **5 days** | Can begin Day 1 (concurrent with uploads) or deferred to Day 6 without missing any email to buyers; first sale triggers the sequence only after it is armed |
| Social account creation | Track B | **2 days** | Can defer to Day 3 if user is focused on Etsy in Days 1–2 |
| Canva Brand Kit setup | Track B | **2 days** | No content production begins without it, but it takes only 30 minutes when done |
| Mockup finalization (Canva export) | Track B | **1 day** | Mockups already exist; any final export variant has 1 day float |
| Day 1 Reel filming | Track B | **1 day** | First social post requires the Reel; can film Day 1 or Day 2 of launch week |
| Pinterest pin batch (5–7 pins) | Track B | **7 days** | Pins take 7–30 days to generate traffic; a 7-day delay has no discernible impact on Month 1 pin traffic |
| Instagram Carousel (Day 3 content) | Track B | **1 day** | Specifically scheduled for Day 3 of the 60-day calendar; 1-day slip is acceptable |
| Reddit/Facebook community outreach | Phase 1 | **5 days** | Week 2 organic outreach; can begin any day in Week 2 without critical path impact |
| Phase 2 backlog product uploads | Phase 1 | **7 days** | Begin in Week 2 after Phase 1 is live; specific day does not matter |

**Critical path to first Etsy sale**: Etsy account active → shop setup (30–45 min) → Listing 1 uploaded → shop switched to public. Earliest possible first sale: Day 1, approximately 3 hours after starting shop setup. Everything on the Track B list has positive float relative to this critical path.

**Critical path to first social post**: Social accounts created → Canva Brand Kit set up → Day 1 Reel filmed → uploaded to Instagram + TikTok. Earliest possible first post: 5–6 hours from decision to proceed. Pinterest pins have a separate critical path (Brand Kit → pin templates → batch production → scheduling) with an estimated completion time of 4–5 hours.

## Section 3: Week-by-Week Parallel Execution Plan

The following plan assumes 8 hours per week of available user time (the "Scenario 2" baseline from concurrent-track-execution-plan.md Part 4). Time allocations adjust for lower or higher availability — see Section 4.

### Week 1: Phase 1 Launch Live + Track B Setup

**Phase 1 Week 1 goals**: All 6 listings uploaded and shop live. Email automation armed and triggering. First views and first sales data flowing.

**Track B Week 1 goals**: Accounts created. Canva Brand Kit configured. Day 1 Reel filmed and posted. First 5–7 Pinterest pins queued.

**Day-by-day task split**:

| Day | Phase 1 Tasks | Track B Tasks | Estimated Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (Monday) | Verify Etsy account active. Complete shop setup (banner, announcement, about, policies). Upload Listings 1–2 (Companion Planting + Food Sovereignty). | Create Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok accounts. Set up Canva Brand Kit (30 min). Create Kit email landing page (15 min). | 3.5–4 hours |
| Day 2 (Tuesday) | Upload Listings 3–4 (Anti-Catalog, Seed Saving). Arm Kit welcome sequence — test with dummy subscriber to confirm trigger fires. | Film Day 1 Reel (30–45 seconds, origin story). Export as 1080×1920px MP4. | 2–3 hours |
| Day 3 (Wednesday) | Upload Listings 5–6 (Apartment Plant Catalog, Survival Garden). Switch shop to public at noon. Post launch announcement on at least one social platform. | Upload Day 1 Reel to Instagram and TikTok. Record and post Day 1–2 Stories (behind-the-scenes). | 2.5–3 hours |
| Day 4 (Thursday) | Monitor: check Etsy Stats for first views. Log in customer-analytics.csv any orders received. | Build 5–7 Pinterest pins in Canva using Tier 1 mockup images and Template 1 (Product Mockup Pin). Schedule to publish Days 4–7 spaced 2 hours apart. | 2–3 hours |
| Day 5–7 | Monitor views and orders. No upload action required unless a listing shows zero views (renew listing if so). | Build Day 3 Carousel in Canva (5 slides, one per Tier 1 product). Post Carousel to Instagram on Day 7. Begin pin batch for Week 2 (5 additional pins). | 1.5–2 hours |

**Week 1 human time total**: 11–15 hours. This is above the Scenario 2 baseline of 8 hours because it includes all one-time setup tasks (Etsy shop setup, social account creation, Brand Kit, Kit landing page). Week 2 onward drops to 6–8 hours/week as setup tasks are complete.

**Week 1 non-negotiable tasks** (do not defer regardless of time pressure):
- Etsy shop goes public by Day 3
- Kit welcome sequence is armed before first sale (do not let a sale trigger the sequence before it is configured)
- Day 1 Reel is posted to Instagram and TikTok (not Day 4 or Day 7 — algorithm calibration requires early posting activity)

### Week 2: Email Nurture + Social Organic Outreach + Track B Pin Production

**Phase 1 Week 2 goals**: Upload 5–8 Phase 2 backlog products. Send first Kit newsletter to subscribers. Begin Reddit and Facebook group outreach with non-promotional content that links back to the lead magnet.

**Track B Week 2 goals**: Production of 5–8 additional Pinterest pins. Post Day 4 Reel (hybrid/open-pollinated explainer). Begin Instagram and TikTok organic posting cadence (every other day minimum).

**Task allocation**:

**Phase 1 tasks — Week 2**:
- Upload backlog products 7–11 (Seed Swap Hosting Kit, Zone-by-Zone Calendar, Apartment Seed Starting Kit, 12-Month Urban Growing Planner, Container Growing Blueprint Pack). Refer to PHASE_1_EXECUTION_READY.md Part 2 for upload order and tag sources. Estimated time: 90 minutes for 5 listings at 15–18 minutes each.
- Send Kit newsletter Week 2: a short educational email (not a sales pitch) featuring one tip from the Seed Saving Field Manual guide — links to the Etsy listing. Estimated time: 30 minutes to write and schedule.
- Reddit organic outreach: post a helpful, non-promotional reply or thread in r/homesteading or r/vegetablegardening. The post should contain useful information from one of your guides and include your Kit landing page link only in the bio or as a secondary mention. No spam. Estimated time: 30–45 minutes per community per week.
- Facebook group outreach: identify 2–3 homesteading or foraging Facebook groups and post a helpful question-and-answer format post. Same non-promotional principle. Estimated time: 30 minutes.

**Track B tasks — Week 2**:
- Canva pin batch production: build 5–8 additional pins. Mix Template 1 (Product Mockup) and Template 2 (Educational Hook). Schedule for Week 2 distribution. Estimated time: 1.5–2 hours.
- Day 4 Reel (hybrid vs. open-pollinated explainer): the script is in phase-2-social-content-calendar-60day.md. Film, edit, post to Instagram and TikTok. Estimated time: 1–1.5 hours.
- Instagram Day 8 post (single image): product spotlight — use the Seed Saving Field Manual mockup. Caption from 60-day calendar. Estimated time: 20 minutes.

**Week 2 total estimated time**: 6–8 hours (within the Scenario 2 baseline).

**Week 2 milestone check** (from phase-1-success-metrics-dashboard.md Section 6):
- At least one listing has 50+ cumulative views
- At least 10 email signups have arrived
- At least 1 sale has occurred

If any of these three milestones is not met by Day 14, initiate Risk Gate 1 (see Section 5).

### Weeks 3–4: Conversion Optimization + Track B Pin Schedule

**Phase 1 Weeks 3–4 goals**: Product page A/B testing on lowest-converting listing. Email sequence refinement based on open rate data (if Email 1 open rate is below 30%, rewrite subject line). Upload remaining backlog products.

**Track B Weeks 3–4 goals**: Pin social schedule fully operational — daily Instagram and Pinterest drops. Pre-loaded HubSpot or Pinterest native scheduler with 5–8 pins per week. Lifestyle photo trigger assessment (check which Etsy listing has 50+ views — that listing is the photography priority).

**Phase 1 Weeks 3–4 tasks**:
- If any listing has received 100+ views and zero or one sale: test a new title for that listing. Change only the title (not tags or description) and monitor for 7 days. This is the minimum-variable test you can run without confounding your data.
- Upload remaining backlog products 12–19 in the order specified in PHASE_1_EXECUTION_READY.md Part 2. This completes the full catalog.
- Review Kit email sequence performance: which email in the 5-email welcome sequence has the lowest open or click rate? Rewrite only that email's subject line and preview text. Leave the body unchanged. Measure the change over the next 50 subscribers.
- Set up bundle listings B1, B2, B3 from bundle-listings.md once all individual listings are live (needed before bundle listings can go up on Etsy).

**Track B Weeks 3–4 tasks**:
- Daily Instagram drops: every day, post one item from the 60-day calendar (alternating Reel, Carousel, single image, and Stories). Use Later, Buffer, or Pinterest native scheduler to queue posts for the full week in a single 1-hour session.
- Pinterest daily pins: schedule 1–2 pins per day (7–10 per week). At this cadence, you will have 20–28 pins live by end of Week 4. Pinterest traffic begins building from Week 3 pins in Week 4 and beyond.
- Lifestyle photo trigger assessment: check Etsy Stats. If any listing has 50+ cumulative views with a conversion rate below 1%, that listing needs lifestyle photography. Source 2–3 Unsplash or Pexels stock images for that product's aesthetic (per LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md). This replaces mockup-only images in Etsy slots 4–5 for that listing.

**Weeks 3–4 total estimated time**: 5–7 hours/week.

## Section 4: Resource Requirements by Track

### Human Time Per Week — Both Tracks Active

| Week | Phase 1 Hours | Track B Hours | Total Hours | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 7–8 hours | 5–6 hours | 12–14 hours | Highest week due to one-time setup |
| Week 2 | 3–4 hours | 3–4 hours | 6–8 hours | Routine steady state begins |
| Week 3 | 2–3 hours | 3–4 hours | 5–7 hours | Catalog upload nearing completion |
| Week 4 | 2–3 hours | 3–4 hours | 5–7 hours | Optimization mode; minimal new production |

**Minimum viable time to run both tracks**: 6 hours/week (Week 2 onward). Below 6 hours/week, the sequential path is recommended (complete Phase 1 first, then start Track B).

**What gets cut first if time is short**:
1. Reddit and Facebook outreach (Track B) — highest time-per-result ratio in early weeks
2. Instagram Reels (Track B) — high production time; defer to when conversion data shows which products to promote
3. Phase 2 backlog uploads beyond Products 7–11 (Phase 1) — not urgent; any upload after Week 3 is still within normal launch rhythm

**What must never be cut**:
- Daily Etsy Stats check (8 minutes, non-negotiable for anomaly detection)
- Kit email sequence functionality check (once per week, 5 minutes)
- Pinterest pin batch (2 hours/week minimum — the only Track B activity with significant organic ROI in the 30-day window)

## Section 5: Risk Gates — When to Pause Track B

Risk gates are pre-defined thresholds at which Track B work stops and all available time redirects to Phase 1 optimization. This prevents resource fragmentation when Phase 1 needs triage.

### Risk Gate 1: Phase 1 Conversion Below 1% at End of Week 2

**Trigger condition**: At Day 14, if the overall shop conversion rate (total orders / total views × 100) is below 1% AND at least 200 views have accumulated across all listings, Risk Gate 1 is active.

**Action when Gate 1 triggers**:
- Suspend all non-critical Track B activities: pause Instagram Reels production, pause Pinterest batch expansion, do not start new social platforms
- Maintain only: Pinterest pins already scheduled (do not pull them — they are zero marginal-time to let run), and Kit email sequence (it runs automatically)
- Focus all available human time on Phase 1 conversion audit: title rewrite, mockup quality check, price comparison against top 10 Etsy results, description clarity review
- Set a 7-day sprint. At Day 21, reassess. If conversion has moved above 1%, resume Track B.

**Why this threshold**: A conversion rate below 1% after 200 views means the listing is reaching buyers but not convincing them. Adding more traffic (Track B's purpose) to a non-converting listing is a waste. Fix the conversion mechanism before amplifying the audience.

### Risk Gate 2: Phase 1 Conversion Below 1% at End of Month 1

**Trigger condition**: At Day 30, if overall conversion remains below 1% despite the Week 2–3 optimization sprint.

**Action when Gate 2 triggers**:
- Full Track B pause: all new Track B production stops. Social accounts remain active (existing posts continue running) but no new content is produced.
- Escalate to listing audit: review all 6 Phase 1 listings against competitors using Etsy search results directly. If pricing is the issue (your $18 listing is next to $5 listings with 50 reviews), consider testing a price reduction on one listing only.
- Evaluate whether the problem is listing quality or category selection (wrong keywords for the actual buyer intent). If keyword mismatch, rebuild tags from scratch using Etsy search suggestions for your top 2 keywords.
- Do not restart Track B until conversion is above 1% on at least one listing.

### Risk Gate 3: Etsy Account Blocked or Verified Shop Unavailable

**Trigger condition**: Phase 1 cannot go live due to account issues (see PHASE_1_EXECUTION_READY.md Action 2).

**Action when Gate 3 triggers**: This is the Track B-first scenario defined in concurrent-track-execution-plan.md Risk 1.
- Launch Track B immediately and in full — all 8 hours of weekly capacity goes to Track B
- Change CTA from "link in bio to shop" to "link in bio to join the waitlist"
- Set 21-day deadline: if Phase 1 is not live within 21 days of Track B launch, evaluate Gumroad or Payhip as the launch platform (per TRACK_B_LAUNCH_STATUS.md Risk 2)

## Section 6: Concurrent vs. Sequential Mode — Decision Summary

### Concurrent Mode (Recommended at 8+ hours/week)

Run Phase 1 and Track B simultaneously starting Week 1. The key advantage: email subscribers acquired in Week 1 via the Kit landing page become warm traffic for the Etsy shop when it goes live — or when the shop link updates from "coming soon" to active. Every email subscriber acquired before the first sale is a compounding asset.

**Concurrent mode prerequisites** (must be true before starting):
- Etsy account is active and verified (no payment hold)
- At least 8 hours/week are reliably available in Weeks 1–4
- Phase 1 conversion is not yet at risk (i.e., the shop is not already live with sub-1% conversion)

### Sequential Mode (Recommended at under 8 hours/week, or if Phase 1 is already live with conversion problems)

Complete Phase 1 first: all 6 listings live, Kit welcome sequence armed, first views and first sale achieved. Then begin Track B. The advantage: Phase 1's critical path requires only 3 focused sessions (approximately 3 hours total per concurrent-track-execution-plan.md Part 1). The disadvantage: delay in building the social audience means Track B's pin traffic will arrive later.

**Sequential mode timeline**: If Phase 1 takes 7 days to complete (Days 1–7), Track B can begin Day 8. Pinterest pins launched in Day 8 generate organic traffic by Day 15–38 (the standard Pinterest indexing window). The delay is real but recoverable — it affects the April/May traffic curve, not the September endangered species launch.
