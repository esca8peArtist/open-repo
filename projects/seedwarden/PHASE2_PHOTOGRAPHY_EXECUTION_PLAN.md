## Overview

Phase 2 photography fills Etsy listing image slots 4 and 5 for all 21 products. Slots 1–3 are complete (tablet cover mockup, interior grid, phone mockup). Slots 4–5 are the lifestyle images that answer the buyer question: "Is this for someone like me?"

This plan operationalizes the hybrid approach from LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md: physical photography for 15 products (Clusters A, B, C) and stock images for 6 products (Clusters D, E). Everything here is a sequenced, executable checklist that can be picked up immediately after strategy approval.

**Total time budget**: 10–14 hours across 3 weeks, mostly concentrated in one or two shooting sessions.
**Total cost budget**: $80–$160 (iStock credits for Cluster D/E gaps; everything else sourced free or on hand).

## Section 1: Hybrid Approach Logistics

### What Goes Physical vs. Stock

The deciding factor is whether the required setting — the physical environment in the photo — can be staged in a kitchen, on a balcony, or in a small garden. If yes: physical. If not: stock.

**Physical (15 products — Clusters A, B, C):**
- Cluster A — Seeds/Garden (8 products): Seed Saving Field Manual, Heirloom Variety Selection Guide, Anti-Catalog: 30 Heirlooms, Companion Planting Chart, Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar, Food Sovereignty Starter Guide, Apartment Seed Starting Kit, 12-Month Urban Growing Planner
- Cluster B — Container/Urban Growing (4 products): Container Growing Blueprint Pack, Apartment Growing Complete Guide, Apartment Plant Catalog, Seed Swap Hosting Kit
- Cluster C — Food Preservation (3 products): Fermented & Preserved Harvest Handbook, Harvest Preservation Field Manual, Grow Your Own Hot Sauce

These require: a wooden surface or kitchen table, seed packets, mason jars, small pots or a tray with seedlings, and a tablet or a few printed pages to represent the product. All of this is either on hand or sourced locally for under $30.

**Stock (6 products — Clusters D, E):**
- Cluster D — Survival/Homestead (4 products): Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual, Meat/Fish Preservation Field Manual, Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual, Survival Garden Regional Plans
- Cluster E — Wild/Native Plants (2 products): Native Plants Regional Guide, and Native Plants content within the Hunting/Trapping guide

These require rural property, livestock, processing facilities, or wilderness — settings that cannot be faked on a kitchen table and for which iStock has the strongest commercial-quality library.

### Physical Logistics: What You Need Before Shooting Day

**Props by cluster (source these before the shoot, not during):**

Cluster A props:
- 4–6 seed envelopes or small seed packets (any variety, from the garden or a hardware store)
- One germination tray with or without seedlings (a damp seedling tray with some starter mix works)
- One small ceramic pot or terracotta pot (4–6 inch)
- One small garden trowel or hand fork
- One mason jar loosely filled with seeds (dried beans work if no seed stock is available)
- A printed page or two from one of the PDFs (optional, but adds authenticity)

Cluster B props:
- 2–3 potted herbs or small houseplants (basil, mint, or any small leafy plant)
- A window with decent natural light, or a balcony railing
- One grow light or grow light tube (if available — not required)

Cluster C props:
- 3–4 mason jars: ideally one with something fermented or canned inside, one empty, one with dried herbs
- A kitchen cutting board or wooden surface
- One or two empty hot sauce bottles (any brand; covered or turned away from camera)
- Dried chilis or a few fresh peppers (seasonal availability permitting)

**Tablet setup:**
For any shot where the digital product needs to appear in the frame, use a tablet or laptop displaying the PDF cover. Open the file in full-screen mode before the shoot; set screen brightness to maximum. This is cleaner than printing pages for most shots, and it explicitly communicates "digital download."

**Printed page option:**
For flat-lay shots, printing 2–3 pages of the product is also effective and may look more natural in an overhead flat-lay than a glowing screen. Print in color if possible. A single printed page of the Companion Planting Chart next to a small trowel and some seed envelopes is a complete, strong flat-lay.

## Section 2: Timeline Breakdown (3-Week Window)

The 3-week window runs from the day Phase 2 is approved to the day lifestyle images go live on Etsy. The tasks inside the weeks are parallel-capable where noted.

### Week 1 — Stock Images for Clusters D and E

**Goal**: 12 stock images sourced, licensed, saved, and logged (2 per product, 6 products = 12 images). These run in parallel with any remaining Phase 1 upload activity and do not require any physical setup.

**Day 1 — Free sources first:**
- Search Unsplash, Pexels, and Pixabay for each of the 6 products.
- Specific search strings to use:
  - Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual: "livestock farm", "backyard chickens", "goats homestead", "small farm animals"
  - Meat/Fish Preservation Field Manual: "meat smoking", "fish preservation", "dry curing", "salted meat", "smoking rack"
  - Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual: "fly fishing stream", "foraging basket wild plants", "hunting field", "trap outdoor"
  - Survival Garden Regional Plans: "regional garden", "survival garden", "homestead aerial", "raised bed garden rows"
  - Native Plants Regional Guide: "wildflower meadow", "native plant field", "foraging plants natural", "wild herbs forest"
- For each product: save 3–5 candidate images, keep 2 strongest.
- If free sites have usable images for all 6: skip Day 2 iStock step. Total spend: $0.
- Save all sourced images to `/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/stock/`
- Filename convention: `[product-slug]-lifestyle-1.jpg`, `[product-slug]-lifestyle-2.jpg`
- Log every image in WORKLOG.md: species or subject, source URL, license type, any attribution requirement.

**Day 2 — iStock fill-in (only if free sources fall short):**
- For any product where free sources returned no usable image, purchase iStock credits.
- Do not subscribe to iStock monthly plan. Use on-demand credit packs (~$12–$15 per image for standard license).
- Purchase only for the specific gaps identified on Day 1. Expected gaps: 3–6 images (primarily Cluster D survival/livestock).
- iStock search strings for gaps:
  - Livestock: "small farm homestead lifestyle" + filter for horizontal or square
  - Meat preservation: "artisan charcuterie", "home meat curing", "smokehouse exterior"
  - Hunting/trapping: "rural hunting lifestyle", "fishing stream rustic"
- Total expected iStock spend if 4–6 images needed: $48–$90.
- Save to the same `/marketing/lifestyle-photos/stock/` directory.

**Day 3 — Composite stock images with product overlay:**
- For each of the 12 stock images, composite the product's PDF cover (or a page from the interior) into the scene.
- Tool: Canva (free tier is sufficient for this; use the Smart Mockup or photo editing tools). Photoshop or GIMP work too.
- The stock image is the background; the product appears as a printed page or tablet sitting in the scene.
- Export at 2400×2400px square, JPEG at 90% quality.
- Save to `/projects/seedwarden/marketing/lifestyle-photos/etsy-ready/`
- Filename convention: `[product-slug]-slot4.jpg`, `[product-slug]-slot5.jpg`

**End of Week 1 deliverable**: 12 Etsy-ready lifestyle images for all 6 Cluster D/E products.

### Week 2 — Physical Photography for Clusters A, B, C

Batch shooting by cluster minimizes setup time. Set up all props for a cluster, shoot all products in that cluster, move to the next.

**Shooting Day 1 (recommended: a morning with good window light) — Cluster A:**

Setup time: 25–30 minutes.
- Spread a wooden cutting board or linen cloth on a table near the best natural light window.
- Arrange seed envelopes, the germination tray, a small pot, and the trowel within arm's reach.
- Open the first product PDF on a tablet, or set aside a printed page.

Per-product shooting (8 products, ~8 minutes per product = ~65 minutes total):
- Flat-lay shot: Lay the tablet or printed page flat on the wooden surface, arrange 2–3 relevant props around it, photograph from directly overhead (stand on a chair, hold phone at arm's length looking straight down). Take 5–8 frames; keep 2.
- Contextual shot: Hold the tablet or page in one hand while your other hand interacts with a prop (picking up a seed envelope, touching a pot). This creates the "in use" signal. One hand is enough — use a tripod or prop the phone against something for this.

Move to Cluster B props after all 8 Cluster A products are shot. Do not break setup between products — keep the same window and surface. This is the discipline that keeps batch shooting efficient.

**Cluster B (4 products, ~45 minutes total):**
- Move setup near a window sill with herbs or a balcony door.
- Flat-lay on a plant table surface (wood or tile works).
- Contextual: hand holding tablet near a potted plant, or pages spread open on a table with a small herb pot nearby.

**Cluster C (3 products, ~35 minutes total):**
- Kitchen counter or table with mason jars and cutting board.
- Flat-lay: overhead shot of the product page or tablet next to a mason jar and some dried herbs or peppers.
- Contextual: hand opening a mason jar, tablet propped against a jar, or a spread of pages near canning equipment.

**Total shooting time, Day 1: approximately 2.5–3.5 hours including setup.**

**Shooting Day 2 (editing):**
- Import all photos from phone to computer or editing app.
- Open Lightroom Mobile, Snapseed, or VSCO.
- Edit one image from each cluster to final look using the following parameters:
  - Exposure: +0.3 (open up shadows)
  - Contrast: +20 (richness without harshness)
  - Highlights: -30 (pull back overexposed windows)
  - White balance: warm (toward 5500K — earthy, not clinical)
  - Saturation: greens +12, yellows +10, blues -10
- Save those settings as a preset. Paste preset to all remaining images in each cluster.
- Final crop: square (1:1) for all Etsy-bound images.
- Export at 2400px on the long edge, JPEG 90%.
- Run all exports through Squoosh (squoosh.app) to confirm files are under 1MB.
- Total editing time: 1.5–2.5 hours.

**Shooting Day 3 (backup, if Day 1 lighting or angles were insufficient):**
- Review all exports. Identify any images that did not work: too dark, props cluttered, product not clearly visible.
- Reshoot those specific shots only. Typically 2–4 images need reshooting.
- This day is a buffer. If Day 1 went well, skip it.

**End of Week 2 deliverable**: 30 Etsy-ready lifestyle images for all 15 Cluster A/B/C products (2 per product = 30 images total, before compositing overlay).

---

### Week 3 — Final Compositing, QA, and Upload

**Days 1–2 — Compositing:**
- For any physical photo where the product needs to be overlaid as a page or screen (rather than appearing literally in the shot), composite the product cover into the image.
- Not every physical photo needs compositing — if a tablet was in the shot, the product is already there. Compositing is only for images where the product is implied but not literal.
- Same workflow as Week 1: Canva or Photoshop, export at 2400×2400px.

**Day 3 — Final quality check (see Section 5 for full QA checklist):**
- Verify all 42 images exist (2 per product × 21 products = 42).
- Verify all images are at correct dimensions (2400×2400px).
- Verify all filenames follow the convention (`[product-slug]-slot4.jpg`, `[product-slug]-slot5.jpg`).
- Spot-check 6–8 images at full size for: product visibility, no competing brands in frame, natural lighting, no cluttered backgrounds.

**Days 4–5 — Etsy upload:**
- Open each listing in Etsy Seller Dashboard.
- Add lifestyle image to slot 4, in-use or contextual image to slot 5.
- Confirm thumbnail crop shows the product clearly in the 1:1 Etsy grid view.
- Do not change other listing elements during this upload — isolate the photography variable for clean conversion measurement.

**End of Week 3 deliverable**: All 21 Etsy listings updated to 5-image listings. Conversion measurement clock starts.

## Section 3: Equipment and Location Setup

No equipment purchases are required to start. The minimum viable setup for all 15 physical products:

**Required (should be on hand):**
- Smartphone with decent camera (iPhone 12+, any modern Android). Portrait mode for close-ups.
- One clean wooden surface: a cutting board, a plank of reclaimed wood, or an outdoor picnic table.
- Natural window light: any window with indirect daylight (not direct sun). Shoot between 9am–2pm.

**Optional upgrades (buy only if natural light is consistently poor):**
- 45W daylight LED softbox, ~$40–$60 on Amazon. Position at 45 degrees from the subject. One light is sufficient; avoid ring lights.

**Backdrop by cluster:**
- Cluster A (seeds/garden): wooden surface is perfect. Outdoors on a potting bench or garden table is even better.
- Cluster B (urban growing): window sill, balcony railing, or indoor plant shelf. The background can include plants — this adds authenticity.
- Cluster C (food preservation): kitchen countertop or wooden table. Mason jars in background are a feature, not a problem.

**What to avoid:**
- White seamless paper backdrop (reads as product photography, not lifestyle)
- Ring light (creates a distinct catch-light that reads as influencer content)
- Overhead fluorescent lighting without supplemental warm light

## Section 4: iStock Credit Purchasing Strategy

### Do Not Subscribe — Buy Credits on Demand

The iStock Essential subscription (~$40/month, 10 downloads) makes sense only if you are confident you need 10+ images per month. For this project, the maximum need is 6 images (Cluster D/E gaps after free sources are exhausted). On-demand credits are cheaper at this volume.

**On-demand credit pricing (as of April 2026):** approximately $12–$15 per standard-license image.

**Allocation strategy for $80–$160 budget:**

If free sources cover 3 of 6 Cluster D/E products (likely outcome for Survival Garden and Native Plants, which have decent free stock coverage):
- Buy 3 images × 2 slots each = 6 images maximum at $12–$15 each = $72–$90 total.
- This leaves $70–$88 for buffer or future stock needs.

If free sources cover only 1–2 products:
- Buy up to 10 images at $12–$15 each = $120–$150.
- This is within the $160 ceiling but tight. Prioritize: Livestock Manual and Hunting/Trapping Manual are the hardest to cover with free stock. These get iStock credits first.
- Meat/Fish Preservation and Survival Garden have more free-stock coverage — exhaust those sources before purchasing.

**Which products to prioritize iStock credits for (ranked by free-stock gap risk):**

1. Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual — livestock imagery is thin on Unsplash/Pexels; iStock has strong backyard poultry and small farm lifestyle content.
2. Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual — hunting content is uncommon on free sites; fishing is better covered but trap/snare imagery is rare.
3. Meat/Fish Preservation Field Manual — artisan charcuterie and smokehouse lifestyle photography is a gap on free sites.
4. Survival Garden Regional Plans — free sites have generic raised-bed content; region-specific survival garden imagery is thin but not absent.
5. Native Plants Regional Guide — Wikimedia Commons is the correct source here (botanical accuracy over lifestyle aesthetics). This may need zero iStock spend. Source Wikimedia first.

**License verification checklist for each iStock purchase:**
- Confirm "Standard License" is selected (covers Etsy digital product use).
- Confirm "No further license required" for web and digital use.
- Note the iStock image ID and download URL in WORKLOG.md.
- iStock Standard License covers: digital distribution in products sold for a fee. This explicitly covers Etsy digital download products. No attribution required.

## Section 5: Quality Assurance Checklist

Run this checklist on every image before uploading to Etsy. An image that fails any item should be replaced or edited, not published.

### Technical Specs
- [ ] Image dimensions: exactly 2400×2400px (1:1 square)
- [ ] File format: JPEG (not PNG for Etsy slots 4–5; PNG is fine but JPEG is smaller and loads faster)
- [ ] File size: under 1MB per image (run through Squoosh if over 1MB)
- [ ] Color mode: RGB (not CMYK)
- [ ] No visible pixelation at 100% zoom

### Etsy Compliance
- [ ] No text overlay that duplicates the listing title (Etsy's guidelines flag keyword stuffing in images)
- [ ] No watermarks or photographer branding visible in frame
- [ ] No visible competing brand logos or product labels in frame (turn or obscure any branded items)
- [ ] No misleading imagery (the product is digital — the lifestyle image should not imply a physical item will arrive)
- [ ] No NSFW content (not applicable to this catalog, but flagged as a note)

### Conversion-Focused Angles
- [ ] Product is visible and recognizable in the frame (tablet screen, printed page, or clearly implied context)
- [ ] Props support the product's theme — they should tell the same story the product title tells
- [ ] Lighting is soft and natural (no harsh shadows cutting across the product)
- [ ] Hands, if present, look working — unpainted, mid-task, grounded
- [ ] Background is clean enough not to distract; busy backgrounds are acceptable only if they are on-theme (a cluttered garden bench is fine; a messy kitchen counter with random objects is not)
- [ ] Composition: product is positioned in the center or upper third of the frame; not cut off at edge

### Brand Consistency
- [ ] Tone is earthy and practical, not aspirational or polished
- [ ] No shot reads as wealthy homestead (Pottery Barn aesthetic); Seedwarden's audience values function over display
- [ ] Warm color temperature throughout (5500–6000K range); no cold blue tones

### License and Attribution Log
- [ ] Every stock image has a logged entry in WORKLOG.md with source URL and license type
- [ ] CC BY-SA images have attribution noted for inclusion in product credits page (applies to Wikimedia sources from prior sessions)
- [ ] iStock images have the image ID and standard license confirmation logged

## Section 6: Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Physical photos come out unusable (poor lighting, cluttered background, product not readable)

**Mitigation**: The buffer in Week 2 (Shooting Day 3) is specifically for reshoots. Additionally, keep all physical photo candidates — even mediocre shots — until compositing is done. A mediocre flat-lay can become a usable image when the product overlay is added.

**Fallback**: If physical photos fail for a specific product, fall back to stock. Every physical-photo product has stock-site coverage on Unsplash or Pexels. The fallback costs $0 and 30 minutes of search time. Physical photography is preferred but not irreplaceable.

### Risk 2: iStock runs out of budget before all Cluster D/E gaps are filled

**Mitigation**: The priority order in Section 4 ensures the hardest-to-cover products (Livestock, Hunting) get credits first. If budget runs out, Survival Garden Regional Plans and the Native Plants guide have adequate free-stock coverage. Launch without the last 2–3 images rather than overspend.

**Fallback**: For any product where neither physical nor purchased stock is ready by Week 3, launch with 3 images (slots 1–3 are complete) rather than delay the 5-image upgrade for the whole catalog. A partial Phase 2 rollout is better than a delayed one.

### Risk 3: Shooting window is not available (scheduling, weather, seasonal light)

**Mitigation**: All Cluster A/B/C shoots can be done indoors with a softbox. Window light is preferred but not required. The entire 15-product physical set can be shot in a single indoor session if outdoor shooting is unavailable.

**Fallback**: If the shooting window cannot be scheduled within 3 weeks, complete stock-only workflow for all 21 products. This takes 1–2 days, costs $0–$120, and still moves all listings from 3-image to 5-image. The conversion lift is slightly lower than physical photos but still meaningful.

### Risk 4: Compositing creates an unconvincing product-in-scene appearance

**Mitigation**: Test the compositing approach on one product before committing to all 12 stock images. If the overlay looks obviously digital (wrong perspective, mismatched lighting, hard edges), switch to a clean product mockup approach: place the product cover art as a flat 2D overlay in the corner of the stock image, clearly labeled "Digital Download." This is less immersive than a 3D composite but still communicates context and product.

### Risk 5: Phase 1 conversion data suggests lifestyle photos are low priority

**Mitigation**: LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md Section 5 defines the measurement framework. If Phase 1 data shows that listings with 3 images are converting at 3%+, Phase 2 photography is optional, not urgent. The 3-week window preserves this optionality — the work can be deprioritized without sunk cost if the data tells a different story.
