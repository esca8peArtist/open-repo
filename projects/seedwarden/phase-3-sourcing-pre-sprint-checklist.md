### Action IM-1: Resolve Goldenseal Image Sourcing Path

**Deadline**: June 8 (hard)
**Why zero float**: Goldenseal is CITES Appendix II. The only legally safe image sources are: (a) Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA images of cultivated or wild material with explicit license, (b) iNaturalist CC-BY observations, or (c) direct permission from a botanical garden or FGV-certified farm. Wild-harvested images without explicit sourcing documentation are a conservation ethics problem in a guide that is explicitly calling readers to buy only cultivated material.

**Decision tree**:
- **Path A (recommended, immediate)**: Confirm Wikimedia CC path. Known good CC images exist:
  - Eric Hunt CC-BY-SA 4.0: habit shots (search "Hydrastis canadensis" on Wikimedia Commons)
  - H. Zell CC-BY-SA 3.0: flower image
  - USDA NRCS: public domain range and habitat photos
  - Action: Search `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:Search?search=Hydrastis+canadensis+habit`, identify the best available habit shot, confirm CC license, download, log in Attribution Log. Done.

- **Path B (supplementary to Path A)**: Email botanical garden media contacts for educational-use permission:
  - NC Botanical Garden: media@ncbg.unc.edu — request educational license for Hydrastis canadensis habitat photos
  - Missouri Botanical Garden: media@mobot.org — same request
  - Email template: "I am preparing an educational guide on cultivated medicinal herbs for Seedwarden, a digital educational publisher. The guide specifically advocates for cultivated-source Goldenseal and FGV-certified farms as an alternative to wild-harvested material. I would like to request permission to use a habit photograph of Hydrastis canadensis from your institution's collection in the PDF guide, with full attribution to [Institution Name]. Would you be able to share a CC-licensed or permission-granted image for this purpose?"
  - Lead time: Botanical garden responses typically take 5–14 business days. Email June 5–6 to receive a response by June 19 at the latest.

- **If no botanical garden response by June 14**: Default to Wikimedia CC Eric Hunt images. Confirmed sufficient quality for guide. Log this decision in WORKLOG.md.

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Wikimedia Commons Goldenseal search completed; best habit image identified
- [ ] Eric Hunt CC-BY-SA 4.0 image (or equivalent) confirmed and downloaded to `assets/phase-3-medicinal-herbs/goldenseal/`
- [ ] Attribution Log row for Goldenseal habit: no blank fields
- [ ] NC Botanical Garden email sent (optional upgrade path)
- [ ] Missouri Botanical Garden email sent (optional upgrade path)

**Risk**: LOW for guide launch. HIGH for conservation optics if a poor-quality or ambiguously licensed image is used. The Eric Hunt Wikimedia image is excellent quality and explicitly CC-BY-SA 4.0. Path A alone is sufficient.

### Action IM-2: Resolve Black Cohosh Image Sourcing Path

**Deadline**: June 8 (hard)
**Why zero float**: Black Cohosh is UpS At-Risk and Massachusetts Endangered. Like Goldenseal, image sourcing must use cultivated or explicitly wild-sourced CC-licensed material. The conservation sidebar in the Women's Health bundle is the highest-profile conservation content in Phase 3 — the images need to visually reinforce the cultivated-source message.

**Sourcing approach**:
- **Primary**: Wikimedia Commons — search `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:Search?search=Actaea+racemosa+habit`
  - Multiple CC-BY-SA images of the flower spike (white bottle-brush raceme, June–August) and forest floor habit exist
  - The June–August bloom window images are the most distinctive and recognizable
  - Target: one forest-floor habit image showing compound leaves and stem structure
- **iNaturalist fallback**: `https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/52534-Actaea-racemosa`
  - Filter for CC-BY license (not CC-BY-NC)
  - Rich results from Appalachian and Great Lakes states, especially June–August
  - Confirm per-observation license (iNaturalist licenses are per-observation, not per-species)
- **Direct contact (optional upgrade)**: Vermont Wildflower Farm (vermontbutterflyfarm.com) — educational use request for growing-stock photos. Not required for launch quality.

**Lead time**: Wikimedia Commons download is immediate. iNaturalist download is immediate (if observation is CC-BY). Direct contact is 5–14 days.

**File naming convention**:
- `[species-slug]-root.jpg` (root/rhizome shots)
- `[species-slug]-dried.jpg` (dried herb studio shots)
- `[bundle-slug]-flatlay.jpg` (bundle arrangement shots)

**Practical notes**:
- Shoot at minimum 2400×2400px at 300 DPI
- Apply warm preset in editing: +0.2 exposure, -20 highlights, +15 shadows
- Export final edited files to `assets/phase-3-medicinal-herbs/[species-slug]/`
