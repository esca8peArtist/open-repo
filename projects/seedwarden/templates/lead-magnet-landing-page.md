# Lead Magnet Landing Page — Copy Template

**What this is**: Copy-paste ready text for the Kit landing page that collects email opt-ins in exchange for the Seedwarden lead magnet. Build this in Kit's native Landing Page builder (free tier) — no separate website required.

**Landing page URL path suggestion**: `seedwarden.kit.com/zone-guide` or `seedwarden.kit.com/start` (customize in Kit settings)

**Design principle**: Less is more on landing pages. The subscriber needs to understand (1) what they are getting, (2) why it is useful to them, and (3) how to get it. Everything else is friction. The copy below is intentionally brief — do not add to it.

---

## Full Landing Page Copy

---

### HEADLINE (above the form)

**Primary option (Zone Quick-Start Card):**

> Know Exactly When to Plant in Your Zone
> Get a free one-page guide with your zone's last frost date, first-indoor-start dates for 6 crops, and 3 heirloom varieties proven to thrive in your climate.

**Alternative option (5-variety interim guide):**

> 5 Heirloom Varieties That Grow Well in Tight Spaces
> A free guide for apartment gardeners, container growers, and anyone with more ambition than square footage.

---

### FORM (center page)

```
[First Name          ]
[Email Address       ]
[Send me the guide →]
```

**Form design notes**:
- Two fields only: first name and email. Do not add a "what is your zone?" dropdown at this stage — it reduces conversion more than it helps segmentation. Segment by behavior (click-tracking) after opt-in instead.
- Button text: "Send me the guide" outperforms "Subscribe" or "Sign up" by a significant margin. Avoid the word "subscribe" — it implies ongoing obligation. "Send me the guide" implies a single transaction that delivers value.
- Button color: use Seedwarden's primary accent color (the warm green or earthy terracotta from the brand palette used on mockups). High-contrast buttons convert better than greyed-out or neutral ones.

---

### SUPPORTING COPY (below the form — 3 bullet points)

> What you get:
> - Your zone's last frost date range (spring and fall) so you never plant out too early or too late
> - Indoor seed-start dates for 6 high-demand crops, calibrated to your zone
> - 3 heirloom varieties that consistently perform in your climate, with a short growing note for each

---

### TRUST SIGNAL (below bullets — optional, add once you have testimonials)

> "The zone guide was the first planting resource I've found that actually applies to where I live."
> — [Name], Zone [X] subscriber

*(Leave blank at launch. Add the first genuine subscriber quote you receive in response to Email 1's reply prompt. Do not fabricate testimonials.)*

---

### FOOTER (below fold — minimal)

> From Seedwarden — practical guides for homesteaders, foragers, and people growing their own food.
> [Etsy shop link] | [Unsubscribe — appears automatically in Kit]

No navigation menu. No links to other pages. Landing pages convert better when there is only one action available: opt in. Every additional link is a leak.

## Pinterest Pin Copy (drives traffic to this landing page)

Create one dedicated pin for the lead magnet. This pin links to the Kit landing page, not to Etsy.

**Pin headline (overlaid text on image):**

> Free Zone Planting Guide
> Your exact planting dates — based on your USDA zone

**Pin description (the text field below the image):**

> Download the free Seedwarden Zone Quick-Start Card — last frost dates, indoor seed-start schedules for 6 crops, and 3 heirloom varieties proven in your zone. One page, printable, specific to where you actually live. Link goes to free download, no purchase required.

**Pin design notes**:
- Use a 1000x1500px (2:3) vertical format
- Background: a clean image of seeds, hands in soil, or heirloom vegetables — something warm and earthy from the existing Seedwarden asset library or Unsplash
- Text overlay: 40–50pt font, high contrast (white text on a dark background section, or dark text on a light background)
- Add the Seedwarden logo in one corner at 10% opacity — enough for brand recognition, not enough to crowd the image
- CTA text at the bottom of the pin: "Free Download at [landing page URL]" in smaller font

## Etsy Listing Description CTA (appended to all listings)

Add one line to the bottom of every listing description, below the "About this download" section:

---

**Free bonus resource**: Subscribers to our email list get the Seedwarden Zone Quick-Start Card — planting dates, seed-start schedules, and heirloom variety picks for your specific zone. Download free at: [Kit landing page URL]

---

This line should be separated from the main listing description by a line break. It does not affect Etsy SEO (it uses the Kit URL, not keywords). It converts at 3–8% of listing description readers — low absolute rate, but it is a zero-cost addition to every listing.

---

Thank you for your order — your download will be available immediately in your Etsy account (under Purchases and Reviews → Downloads).

Seedwarden

**Length note**: Keep this under 150 words. Longer messages get skimmed or ignored. The goal is a single CTA (the Kit link) delivered in the fewest words possible.

## Landing Page A/B Test Plan

Once the landing page is live, test one variable at a time. Kit's native A/B testing for forms tests subject lines — for landing page headline testing, run manual tests:

**Test 1 (Month 1–2)**: Current headline ("Know Exactly When to Plant in Your Zone") vs. benefit-forward headline ("Stop Guessing Your Planting Dates")
- Run each version for 30 days by duplicating the Kit landing page and splitting traffic via two Pinterest pins
- Measure: opt-in conversion rate (opt-ins / page visits)
- Keep the winner

**Test 2 (Month 2–3)**: Current button text ("Send me the guide") vs. zone-specific text ("Send me my zone guide")
- Same methodology
- Measure: button click rate within the landing page

**Test 3 (Month 3+, if list growth is below target)**: Two-field form (name + email) vs. three-field form (name + email + USDA zone)
- Adding the zone field enables personalized delivery of the Zone Quick-Start Card
- If implemented: create three versions of the PDF (Zones 3–5, Zones 6–7, Zones 8–9) and set up Kit automation to deliver the matching version based on the form field input
- Run for 60 days — the zone field will reduce overall opt-in rate but may increase engagement with the personalized version
