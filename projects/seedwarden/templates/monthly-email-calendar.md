# Monthly Email Content Calendar — Template

**What this is**: A reusable monthly planning template for Seedwarden's email content. Fill in one calendar per month during the last week of the prior month. Planning time: 30–45 minutes per month.

**The rule**: Newsletters are informational. Broadcasts are promotional. No more than one broadcast per week, and no broadcast on the same week as a newsletter that promotes a product directly. The subscriber's inbox is not a storefront — it is a relationship that earns the right to sell.

**Cadence**: 4–5 emails per month total (4 weekly newsletters + 0–1 seasonal broadcast). This is sustainable at a solo operator pace.

## Subject Line Formula Bank

**Formula 1 — Curiosity gap question** (best for educational topics)
Structure: [Surprising or counterintuitive question that the email answers]
Examples:
- "Why your seedlings look that bad (and it's not overwatering)"
- "What most foraging guides get wrong about ramps"
- "Is your seed saving destroying your harvest?"

**Formula 2 — Specific tip** (best for practical technique emails)
Structure: How to [do specific thing] in [timeframe or with specific constraint]
Examples:
- "How to ferment hot peppers in 4 days with no equipment"
- "How to identify 3 edible weeds you probably already have"
- "How to calculate planting dates for any zone in 2 minutes"

**Formula 3 — Story hook** (best for narrative/personal emails)
Structure: [Specific event] that [result or discovery]
Examples:
- "The seed I almost threw away — and what it taught me about variety selection"
- "The batch that failed and why I now use a different brine"
- "The foraging mistake that sent me back to basics"

**Formula 4 — Direct statement** (best for broadcast campaigns)
Structure: [What the email contains, stated plainly]
Examples:
- "Preservation Bundle — 20% off this week only"
- "New guide: Beginner Canning from scratch"
- "Black Friday: 25% off everything in the catalog"

**Formulas to avoid**:
- "Don't miss..." — implies the reader is obligated
- "You won't believe..." — clickbait; this audience is sophisticated and will not click
- Subject lines with ALL CAPS words — spam filter trigger
- Excessive exclamation points (one maximum; zero is fine)
- "[FIRST NAME], check this out!" — the personalization token at the start of a subject line is overused and no longer lifts open rates

## Technique Topic Bank

A rolling bank of technique topics for newsletter Section 2. Each topic should be covered in 200–350 words — specific, practical, and based on Seedwarden's existing guide content. Do not repeat topics in the same quarter.

**Seed saving and growing:**
- Seed drying methods by crop type (flat vs. ferment vs. thresh)
- Isolation distance requirements for common vegetables
- How to read germination rate on a seed packet
- Soil temperature vs. air temperature for planting decisions
- The difference between determinate and indeterminate tomatoes for seed saving
- Cover cropping for soil health — which crops to use by season
- Succession planting math for continuous lettuce harvest

**Preservation:**
- Water bath vs. pressure canning — which foods require which
- Lacto-fermentation science — why salt concentration matters
- Dehydration temperature guide by food category
- How to test pectin level in fruit for jam making
- Salt-to-weight ratios for different ferment styles
- Freezer burn prevention for meat and fish

**Foraging and native plants:**
- The five-feature identification method for wild edibles
- Lookalike plant pairs and how to tell them apart
- Seasonal foraging windows by region (spring ephemerals, summer berries, fall roots)
- How to process stinging nettles safely
- Which urban "weeds" are actually edible
- Ethical foraging practices (leave enough to propagate, don't over-harvest)

**Homesteading systems:**
- Zone planning for a productive backyard in any USDA zone
- Companion planting rationale — what the research actually shows vs. folk wisdom
- Small-space livestock options (quail, rabbits, meat chickens) and their requirements
- The survival garden calorie math — how much space to feed one person for a year
- Water storage and filtration basics for off-grid readiness

## Monthly Planning Checklist

Run this checklist during the last week of each prior month. Estimated time: 30–45 minutes.

- [ ] Pull Kit analytics from the current month — note top open rate subject, top click-through topic
- [ ] Identify the seasonal theme for next month using `marketing/annual-product-plan.md` and `marketing/product-calendar-2026-2027.json`
- [ ] Select one featured product for newsletter spotlight rotation (choose a product with seasonal alignment — do not feature preservation products in March)
- [ ] Fill in the four subject lines for next month's newsletters using the formula bank above
- [ ] Fill in the four technique topics — ensure no repeat from the prior 90 days
- [ ] Determine whether a broadcast is warranted this month (check the broadcast schedule; maximum one per month)
- [ ] If broadcast: draft the subject line and CTA; create any Etsy coupon codes needed; set send date
- [ ] Load newsletters into Kit as drafts so they are ready to send without a writing session each Thursday
- [ ] Check list size and calculate growth rate from prior month — if below 15%, increase Reddit participation or Pinterest lead magnet pin promotion
