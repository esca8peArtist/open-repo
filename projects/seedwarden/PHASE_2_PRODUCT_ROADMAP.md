# Seedwarden Phase 2 Product Roadmap

**Prepared**: June 17, 2026
**Scope**: Phase 2 content, features, monetization, and resource planning (July 2026 – March 2027)

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 2 moves Seedwarden from transactional product sales ($270/day Phase 1 baseline) to a mixed revenue model with recurring subscription revenue as the anchor. Target December 31, 2026 run rate: **$3,140–5,900/month** ($800+ monthly from subscriptions, remainder from Etsy sales, affiliates, and partnerships).

**Content production plan**: 191–264 hours across three priority tiers. Practitioner subscription tiers (Tier 2 @ $18/mo, Tier 3 @ $55/mo) launch August 1 (Tier 2) and October 1 (Tier 3), targeting 75–100 Tier 2 and 25–40 Tier 3 subscribers by year-end.

**Critical path**:
1. Medicinal herb bundles Wave 1 (June 22–July 13, 36–44 hours, highest-intensity sprint)
2. Practitioner tier credibility gate (mentor/affiliation by August 12)
3. AHG Symposium outreach (August 14–16 acquisition window)
4. Institutional partnerships initiation (Fall 2026)

**Success gated on**:
- Phase 1 sustained traction (target ≥50 cumulative sales by July 1)
- Phase 2 photography gate decision (iStock budget authorization by July 1)
- Practitioner mentor/affiliation confirmed (August 12 hard deadline)

## 2. Feature Roadmap

### Search and Filter (August–October 2026)

**Option A (Recommended): Structured tags + landing page filter**
- Static landing page on Carrd or Notion
- Database organized by region, category (edible/medicinal/native), season, format
- Provides filtering experience Etsy search cannot offer
- 4–6 hours setup, zero ongoing cost
- **Recommend Option A** — matches solo-operator capacity constraints

**Option B (Medium-resource): Gumroad/Ko-fi storefront**
- Parallel sales channel with native filtering
- 6–10 hours setup
- Activate August 1 with practitioner tier subscriptions

### Geographic and Hardiness Zone Navigation

- Each botanical ID guide includes USDA hardiness zone badge + range map citation
- 15-minute addition per guide in template
- Static landing page sortable by USDA zone
- Regional listing variants provide direct geographic navigation

### Mobile-Friendly Design

- All PDFs readable on mobile without pinch-to-zoom
- One-column layout, 11pt minimum body text, high contrast
- Botanical ID guides at 1x1 square ratio for social preview
- Flashcard Set designed at 4x6" (standard index card ratio)
- No app development warranted — **ZIM/Kiwix offline format is Phase 2 equivalent** (January 2027 submission)

### API and Data Export (Phase 3, not Phase 2)

- Establish consistent JSON schema for species records now (zero additional time per guide)
- Creates data asset for future institutional licensing in Phase 3
- Foundation for API layer once 100+ species documented

### Community Contribution Mechanisms (Phase 2: Structured only)

**Peer reviewer network (gated, credentialed)**
- 3–5 peer reviewers per quarter
- Subscription discount incentive for accepted reviews
- Operator reviews all submissions before publication
- Not a community platform — editorial process with external contributors

**Observer network via iNaturalist**
- Encourage buyers to log observations on iNaturalist
- Tag with project-specific identifier
- Zero infrastructure to build
- Data flows into iNaturalist's database for range verification

## 6. Key Risks and Decision Points

**Risk 1: Phase 1 traction does not sustain** (HIGH IMPACT)
- Mitigation: July 1 Gate 2 check (need ≥50 cumulative sales). If <30 sales, hold iStock investment and optimize listings before expanding content.

**Risk 2: Practitioner credibility gate not met by August 12** (HIGHEST IMPACT)
- AHG Symposium outreach opens August 14. Without named mentor/clinical advisor in published listings, Tier 2 conversion targets will fail.
- Mitigation: Commit to mentor identification by August 1 (hard deadline). If unavailable, offer "coming soon" tier launch (August 15) instead of August 1 to preserve credibility.

**Risk 3: Writing sprint burnout June 22–July 13** (MEDIUM IMPACT)
- The 36–44 hour sprint over 22 days is the most intensive production window. Quality will determine Tier 2 retention over following 6 months.
- Mitigation: Scope to exactly 3 bundles. Defer Wave 2 to October rather than compressing everything into summer.

**Risk 4: Etsy policy or algorithm change** (MEDIUM IMPACT)
- Current state: 100% distribution through Etsy.
- Mitigation: By December 31, target Etsy to represent no more than 40% of monthly revenue. Practitioner tier subscriptions are Phase 2 hedge against Etsy dependency. At Dec 31 targets, Etsy = 15–40% of revenue.

## 7. Competitive Positioning

**vs. iNaturalist** (citizen science observation platform, 200M+ observations):
- Seedwarden is NOT competing on species breadth or observation density
- Seedwarden advantage: practitioner-formatted, decision-ready reference documentation vs. raw observation data
- iNaturalist is a data source for Seedwarden, not a competitor

**vs. USDA PLANTS database** (authoritative, free, comprehensive US plant reference):
- Seedwarden advantage: interpretation and application (how to use the plant) vs. taxonomy and range
- USDA PLANTS is a reference source Seedwarden builds from

**vs. Audubon guides** (curated, accessible field guides for beginners):
- Seedwarden's practitioner tier goes in opposite direction (depth vs. accessibility)
- Seedwarden's entry tier (zone cards, quick reference) competes on regional specificity + practical application + production freshness
- Seedwarden serves beginner → intermediate → advanced practitioner progression (all three tiers with same brand)

**Unique space**: Layered, tiered reference resource that grows with the buyer from curious beginner to credentialed practitioner. No single existing product serves all three tiers with systematic species coverage and brand consistency.
