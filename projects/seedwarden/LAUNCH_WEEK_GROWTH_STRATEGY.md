## Most Important Finding

The launch week strategy's highest-ROI action is not posting content — it is the 48 hours of targeted comment engagement on the top-10 foraging and homesteading accounts before the first Seedwarden post goes live. This "warm entry" tactic (genuine, substantive comments on competitor and peer content 2–3 days before launch) costs zero dollars, requires 2–3 hours total, and ensures that the most likely first followers of Seedwarden have already seen the brand name once in a context that is helpful rather than promotional. It is the single most underused launch tactic in niche educational content.

## Part 3: Press Release Angles

Seedwarden's May 30 launch has four distinct press angles, each targeting a different media category. Pick one as the primary angle per press contact.

### Angle 1 — The Midwest Food Sovereignty Angle
**Headline**: "Midwest Field Guides Fill the Geographic Gap in America's Foraging Revival"
**Pitch**: The wild foods movement has produced national celebrities (Alexis Nikole Nelson, 4.5M TikTok followers) but almost no resources specifically for the 65+ million people who live in Zones 4b–6a of the Midwest. Seedwarden's Zone 5-specific guides are the first commercially available research-backed digital field guides calibrated to Midwest growing conditions.
**Media targets**: Midstory (midstory.org), Racket MN, regional sustainability newsletters (e.g., Illinois Natural History Survey community notes), Midwest farm media (AgDaily, Farm Flavor)

### Angle 2 — The Digital Skills Economy Angle
**Headline**: "Independent Research Brand Bypasses Traditional Publishing to Bring Field Guide Depth to Etsy"
**Pitch**: Traditional botanical field guides take 5–10 years to publish, go out of print, and do not cover Zone-specific seasonal windows. Seedwarden is building the reference library that regional foragers and homesteaders cannot find in bookstores — and delivering it as an instant download for $8–$22.
**Media targets**: Etsy seller community media (Etsy Seller Handbook blog, Make It On Etsy podcast), small business and creator economy media

### Angle 3 — The Food Security Trend Angle
**Headline**: "44% of American Families Plan to Grow Their Own Food in 2025 — Here's the Research That Helps Them"
**Pitch**: Lead with the 44% statistic (PHS, 2025), connect to the information gap for new growers who lack region-specific reference resources. Seedwarden is serving the growing audience of people who want to forage and grow food but lack the zone-specific knowledge to do it safely and successfully.
**Media targets**: General wellness and sustainability media (Treehugger, Organic Authority, MindBodyGreen, local news "smart living" sections), homesteading publications (Hobby Farms, Backyard Poultry)

### Angle 4 — The Wild Food Safety Angle
**Headline**: "Before You Eat That Wild Plant: How Region-Specific Field Guides Reduce Foraging Risk"
**Pitch**: Foraging apps like iNaturalist have limitations for edibility assessment; most foraging guides are national with generic lookalike warnings. A Zone 5 field guide can name the specific dangerous lookalike that grows in the same habitat in the same season as the edible species — national guides cannot.
**Media targets**: Outdoor and safety media (REI Co-op Journal, Outside, field and stream publications), regional naturalist society newsletters

## Part 4: Organic Growth Levers

### The Warm Entry Tactic (Pre-Launch)
Before posting the first Seedwarden post, spend 2 hours on Day –2 (two days before launch) engaging authentically in the comment sections of the 15 priority influencer accounts above. Rules for this engagement:
- Comment must be substantive — answer a question someone asked in the comments, add a relevant species note, or provide a specific Zone 5 data point
- Do not mention Seedwarden in these comments
- Do not link to anything
- The goal is that these creators' audiences have seen the @seedwarden account name once in a non-promotional context before the first Seedwarden content appears

This creates brand familiarity without advertising. When a forager later sees a Seedwarden Reel in their Explore feed, the name is not new.

### The Dangerous Lookalike Reel
The highest organic growth lever for Launch Week is a single piece of content: "The 3 plants people most commonly mistake for [edible species] in the Midwest." This content category:
- Has high completion rate (people stay until they have learned the safety information)
- Has high share rate (foragers send safety content to friends who forage with them)
- Has high save rate (reference content people want to access later)
- Has zero commercial feel (no product pitch required)
- Has direct product bridge ("everything in this guide has dedicated dangerous lookalike sections — link in bio")

Post this video on Day 1 or Day 2 of launch week. It is the highest-leverage single content execution in the launch window.

### The "Zone 5 This Week" Weekly Series
A simple, consistent weekly series framing: "What to forage in Zone 5 this week." Post every Tuesday. The content is a 30–45 second Reel showing 1–3 species that are at peak in Zone 5 at the current date, with identification features, habitat notes, and safety note. No production complexity required. This series has built-in time urgency (it is literally relevant right now), high share potential (Zone 5 foragers share it to their regional foraging groups), and a natural content pillar that requires no planning beyond knowing the seasonal calendar.
