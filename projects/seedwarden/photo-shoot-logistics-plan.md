# Photo Shoot Logistics Plan
## Phase 2 — Appalachian Medicinals, May 20–25, 2026

**Shoot window**: May 20–25, 2026
**Species**: American Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, Ramps
**Success criteria**: 30+ usable shots per species (150+ total), consistent color balance across all five species, at least 6 shot angles per species (full plant / leaf / flower / root / seed / habitat context)
**Hard cutoff**: All shooting complete by end of May 25 to allow processing May 26 and Canva start May 26

This document is the shoot-day operations guide. It defines the cluster breakdown, daily schedule, props list, equipment checklist, team roles, and the per-species shot requirements. Carry this document (or a printed copy) on every shoot day.

---

## Part 1: Cluster Breakdown and Shoot Logic

### Why Three Clusters

Five species photographed in one session produces an exhausting, rushed shoot with inconsistent quality. Three clusters, organized by habitat compatibility and props requirements, allow the photographer to fully reset between sessions and maintain visual consistency.

**The three-cluster logic**:
- **Cluster 1 — Forest Floor Species** (Day 1, field location): Ginseng, Goldenseal, Ramps. All three are woodland understory plants with similar habitat — north-facing slopes, leaf litter, dappled light. Photographing them in sequence at the same field location creates visual consistency and minimizes location changes. Props: forest floor background material (actual leaf litter from site), root specimens, botanical illustration prints.
- **Cluster 2 — Root and Rhizome Close-ups** (Day 2, indoor or semi-indoor): All five species, root/rhizome/seed close-up sequences. This cluster is purely props-based — no live plants required. Best executed indoors with controlled lighting for consistent color accuracy across all five species. Props: dark wood board, dried roots, seeds, kraft envelopes, botanical scale.
- **Cluster 3 — Ephemeral and Stem Species** (Day 3, field or indoor): Black Cohosh, Bloodroot. Black Cohosh has distinctive palmate leaves photographable in May; Bloodroot's white bloom has typically passed by late May (use iNaturalist CC-BY archive for flower shots), but the leaves and orange rhizome are May-photographable. Grouping these two together allows the field component to be a shorter, more targeted session.

### Cluster Assignment Summary

| Cluster | Species | Day | Location | Primary Shots |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cluster 1 — Forest Floor | Ginseng, Goldenseal, Ramps | Day 1 (May 20) | Field — Asheville Botanical Garden or forest farm | Full plant in habitat, leaf detail, colony/patch view, root in situ |
| Cluster 2 — Root and Seed | All 5 species | Day 2 (May 21) | Indoor — window light setup | Root close-up, seed close-up, dried specimen, scale reference, botanical illustration |
| Cluster 3 — Ephemeral/Stem | Black Cohosh, Bloodroot | Day 3 (May 22) | Field + indoor | Leaf arrangement, stem detail, rhizome cross-section, habitat context |

Days 4–5 (May 23–25) are float days for re-shoots, additional angles, and supplemental hero shots for the Etsy listing bundle cover.

## Part 2: Daily Shoot Schedule

### Day 1 — May 20 (Saturday): Cluster 1 — Forest Floor

**Location**: Asheville Botanical Garden (primary) or private forest farm (backup)
**Call time**: 8:00am at location
**Shoot window**: 9:00am – 1:30pm (morning light; avoid midday direct sun)
**Session 2 (optional)**: 3:00pm – 5:00pm if afternoon is overcast

**Hour-by-hour**:
- 8:00am: Arrive at location; unload equipment; do initial light test shot to confirm white balance setting
- 8:30am: Walk the site with the props bag; identify 3–4 specific shooting locations (north-facing understory backdrop, root-in-soil foreground, leaf-litter flat surface)
- 9:00am: Begin American Ginseng sequence — habitat backdrop first, then leaf detail, then root prop, then seed prop. Capture 30–40 RAW frames (cull to 10–12 selects per species)
- 10:15am: Transition to Goldenseal — same backdrop with slightly different foreground arrangement. Bright yellow root is the key prop; ensure it is in frame for at least 10 of the Goldenseal shots.
- 11:30am: Transition to Ramps — if fresh ramp leaves are on hand, this is the most photogenic cluster. Full leaf spread flat on forest floor with leaf litter background; then vertical shot of leaf with light background (overcast sky if shooting from below canopy level); then bulb/bulblet close-up.
- 12:45pm: Full review of Day 1 frames on camera LCD or laptop (bring laptop). Flag any re-shoot needs for the afternoon session or Day 4 float.
- 1:30pm: Morning session closes. Transfer all images to laptop immediately (do not leave on camera card).
- 3:00pm: Optional afternoon session if light is overcast (overcast is superior to sunny for forest floor shots — eliminates dappled shadow pattern). Capture additional angles or any re-shoots flagged at 12:45pm.
- 5:00pm: Day 1 complete. Drive home. Back up all images to a second location (external drive or cloud) before end of day.

**Day 1 target**: 80–120 RAW frames total (Ginseng + Goldenseal + Ramps combined); cull to 30–36 selects.

### Day 2 — May 21 (Sunday): Cluster 2 — Root and Seed

**Location**: Indoor — home or apartment window setup
**Call time**: 9:00am (setup)
**Shoot window**: 9:30am – 1:00pm (morning window light)

**Hour-by-hour**:
- 9:00am: Set up dark wood board on table 2–3 feet from east or south window. Position white foam-core reflector opposite window to fill shadows. Set white balance to Daylight (5500K).
- 9:30am: Begin American Ginseng root sequence — dried whole root on dark wood, close-up at 45° angle, overhead flat-lay, root with scale reference (ruler or coin), root with seed envelope.
- 10:15am: Goldenseal root sequence — yellow root color is distinctive; capture at least 15 frames at different angles to ensure the yellow is accurately rendered. Note camera white balance — goldenseal root at 5000K can shift toward green; adjust to 5500K if needed.
- 11:00am: Bloodroot rhizome sequence — orange-red cut cross-section is the ID feature. If rhizome was not obtained from vendor, use BHL botanical illustration as a prop in frame (print on 8x10 paper). Capture the cut cross-section (if available) at extreme close-up (macro or portrait mode on smartphone).
- 11:45am: Ramps bulb/bulblet sequence — cluster of bulblets on dark wood; individual bulb with roots visible; bulb cross-section if feasible.
- 12:30pm: Black Cohosh seed head close-up (if seed head was obtained); dried root pieces if available; botanical illustration alongside specimen.
- 1:00pm: Day 2 complete. Image transfer and backup.

**Day 2 target**: 60–80 RAW frames; cull to 25–30 selects covering root/seed angles for all 5 species.

### Day 3 — May 22 (Monday or Tuesday): Cluster 3 — Leaf and Stem

**Location**: 50% field (Asheville Botanical Garden or forest farm), 50% indoor
**Call time**: 8:00am at field location
**Shoot window**: 9:00am – 12:00pm (field); 2:00pm – 4:00pm (indoor supplement)

**Hour-by-hour**:
- 9:00am: Black Cohosh — distinctive palmate compound leaf arrangement. Five leaflets on a single stem. Photograph leaf from above (flat-lay on forest floor); then at eye level against blurred background; then single leaflet close-up showing tooth margin. Capture 25–30 RAW frames.
- 10:30am: Bloodroot — leaves are still present in late May (heart-shaped with distinctive lobing). If rhizome was obtained, photograph rhizome in situ (partially buried in soil with leaf emerging). Capture 20–25 frames.
- 12:00pm: Field session closes. Transfer images.
- 2:00pm: Indoor supplement — Black Cohosh and Bloodroot dried specimen arrangements; botanical illustration prints as props. These supplement the field shots with close-up detail that is cleaner in controlled lighting.
- 4:00pm: Day 3 complete. Image transfer and full backup.

**Day 3 target**: 50–70 RAW frames; cull to 20–25 selects.

### Days 4–5 — May 23–24: Float and Hero Shots

Float days are used for three purposes:
1. Re-shooting any frames from Days 1–3 flagged as inadequate during culling
2. Bundle cover hero shots — a single flat-lay composition featuring specimens from all five species together on a dark wood or slate surface, styled for the Etsy listing cover image
3. Additional angles at any species where fewer than 30 usable selects exist after Days 1–3

**Bundle cover shot (Day 4, 1–2 hours)**: Set up a styled flat-lay with all five species represented — dried ginseng root, goldenseal root, bloodroot rhizome (or illustration), ramps leaves or bulblets, black cohosh seed or leaf. Dark slate or wood board background. This image is the Etsy listing cover for the 5-guide Appalachian Medicinals bundle.

## Part 5: Team Roles

### Minimum viable crew: 1 person

All five days can be executed by a single person. The main constraint is simultaneous tasks — photographing a prop arrangement while adjusting the reflector position requires either a second person or a tripod. A smartphone tripod ($10–20 on Amazon) solves the tripod constraint.

### Recommended crew: 2 people

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Photographer | Camera settings, composition, light assessment, shot sequencing |
| Props handler | Arranging specimens, adjusting reflector, marking approved shots, managing props bag, writing down shot notes (species / angle / frame count) |

The props handler role can be filled by any person willing to follow directions and handle plant specimens carefully. No botanical or photography expertise required — the photographer directs all arrangements.

### Backup plan if crew member is ill

If the props handler is unavailable: photographer operates solo with tripod and remote shutter trigger. This extends each day's shoot by approximately 1 hour. Float days (May 23–24) absorb this. The photography does not fail for a one-person crew.

## Part 6: Shot Requirements and Success Criteria

### Per-Species Shot Checklist (Minimum 30 Shots Per Species)

Each species needs the following 6 angle types, with 5 frames minimum per angle (for culling), yielding 30 selects minimum per species.

| Angle | Description | Source | Minimum Frames |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Full plant in habitat | Complete plant in natural or naturalistic setting | Field shoot OR iNaturalist CC-BY archive | 5 |
| 2. Leaf detail | Single leaf showing shape, texture, margin | Field shoot | 5 |
| 3. Flower or fruit | In-season bloom or seed head | iNaturalist CC-BY archive (spring bloom typically past in late May) | 5 |
| 4. Root or rhizome | Dried or fresh specimen on dark background | Indoor Cluster 2 | 5 |
| 5. Seed or seed pod | Seeds in envelope, scattered on wood, or in close-up | Indoor Cluster 2 | 5 |
| 6. Habitat context / colony | Wider view showing habitat or growing context | Field shoot OR iNaturalist CC-BY archive | 5 |

**Total per species**: 30 frames minimum (6 angles x 5 frames). Cull these to 6–8 finals for the guide (one per angle) plus 2–3 alternates.

### Success Criteria — Launch Gate

The shoot is considered successful (and guide production can begin) when the following conditions are met:

- [ ] 30+ usable selects per species (150+ total across all five species)
- [ ] All six angle types represented for each species (substituting iNaturalist CC-BY archive for flower shots where specimens are not in bloom)
- [ ] Color balance is consistent across all five species: white balance 5500–6000K, no obvious color cast differences between species sessions
- [ ] All root and rhizome shots: specimen is clean, in focus, and shows the distinctive ID color (ginseng tan/cream; goldenseal yellow; bloodroot orange-red)
- [ ] All images transferred and backed up to two locations (laptop + external drive) by end of May 25
- [ ] No remaining single points of failure: if any one image file is lost, alternative frames exist for that angle and species

### What Constitutes a Re-shoot Requirement

Flag a sequence for re-shoot (on float days May 23–24) if:
- Fewer than 3 usable frames exist for any angle type for any species
- White balance is visibly inconsistent within a species session (compare on laptop at full resolution — LCD preview is not reliable)
- Focus is soft on the primary feature (root ID color, leaf margin texture) in all frames for that angle
- Props arrangement is visually confusing or the species identity is not clear from the shot

**Standard editing adjustments** (consistent with existing Seedwarden photo style from PHOTO_SHOOT_SCHEDULE_AND_PROPS.md):
- Exposure: +0.2 to +0.4
- Contrast: +20
- Highlights: -30 to -40
- Shadows: +20 to +30
- White balance: 5500–6000K
- Vibrance: +10 (not saturation — vibrance preserves skin tones if hands are in frame)
- Crop: leave as native aspect ratio for guide interiors; 1:1 square for Etsy listing hero images
