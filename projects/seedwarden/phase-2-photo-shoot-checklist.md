# Phase 2 Photo Shoot Checklist
## Appalachian Medicinals — May 20–22, 2026

**Carry this document on all shoot days alongside `phase-2-location-scout-report.md`.**  
**Total shots required**: 30 final selects (minimum 6 angles × 5 frames per species = 30 per species; cull to 6–8 finals per species for the guides)  
**Total shoot time**: 9–11 hours across 3 days  
**Equipment philosophy**: Smartphone camera is sufficient; light is the only limiting factor

---

## Part 1: 30-Shot Sequence Table

Two shot types per angle: A (flat-lay or overhead, surface-placed specimen) and B (contextual, 25–35 degrees off horizontal, prop in naturalistic position). Execute both types for each angle. Five frames minimum per angle before moving on.

### Angle Type 1 — Full Plant in Habitat

**May 20 field only** — this angle cannot be replicated indoors. Photograph each species with its naturalistic context visible in the frame.

| Species | Shot A (flat-lay / overhead) | Shot B (contextual) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ginseng | Compound leaf arrangement laid on leaf-litter surface; 5 leaflets spread naturally | Standing plant (cultivated prop) against forest understory backdrop; vertical orientation | Root collar visible at soil line if possible |
| Goldenseal | Paired leaves on dark forest-floor surface; large, distinctively veined | Leaf held at angle showing both upper and underside texture | Ground-level perspective emphasizes the low, woodland character |
| Black Cohosh | Palmate compound leaf on surface; 5 leaflets in natural spread | Leaf group in situ against filtered canopy light | Distinctive toothed leaf margin must be visible at zoom |
| Ramps | Fresh leaf bundle, spread flat; all leaves parallel | Leaf in upright position against dark soil or leaf litter | Broad strap shape; smooth texture; onion smell is the ID key (note in caption) |

---

### Angle Type 2 — Leaf Detail

**May 20 field + May 22 indoor** — close-up of single leaf showing shape, texture, and margin detail.

| Species | Shot A (overhead, lit from side) | Shot B (contextual, slight angle) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ginseng | Single leaflet from compound leaf; vein pattern visible | Leaflet pinched between fingers showing thickness | Smooth surface; palmate veins |
| Goldenseal | Single leaf; large size (6–12 inches); distinct veining | Leaf edge showing scalloped margin | Large leaf is the key visual ID — include ruler or hand for scale |
| Black Cohosh | Single leaflet; sharply toothed margin visible | Leaflet held against light background showing silhouette | Double-toothed margin is the ID feature |
| Ramps | Single leaf; smooth, broad, elliptical | Leaf cross-section tip showing tapered end | Pair with bulb prop in same frame if possible |

### Angle Type 4 — Root or Rhizome

**May 21 indoor only** — all root photography done indoors with controlled lighting and dark wood/velvet background.

| Species | Shot A (overhead flat-lay, dark background) | Shot B (45-degree angle, ruler in frame) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ginseng | Dried whole root on dark wood; clean background | Root with coin or ruler showing scale (2–4 inch typical) | Tan/cream color; branched form ("manlike" shape is the ID story) |
| Goldenseal | Dried yellow root on dark velvet | Root cut cross-section if available; yellow interior visible | Yellow color is the primary ID feature; lightly mist with water to intensify color |
| Black Cohosh | Dried root pieces or seed head (if available) | Root arranged with botanical illustration prop | Dark root less visually distinctive; emphasis on the seed head if available |
| Ramps | Bulblet cluster on dark wood | Individual bulb with roots visible; cross-section | Small white bulb with purple-red sheath; similar to spring onion in form |

### Angle Type 6 — Habitat Context

**May 20 field + iNaturalist CC-BY backup** — wider view showing growing habitat or colony.

| Species | Shot A (wide, 3–5 feet from subject) | Shot B (ground-level perspective) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ginseng | Single plant prop against forest floor context; understory visible behind | Low-angle: stem emerges from leaf litter layer | North-facing slope context implied by light direction |

| Black Cohosh | Full stem height visible with forest backdrop | Looking up through compound leaves toward canopy | Vertical height (3–8 feet when mature) is the scale story |
| Ramps | Patch of ramp leaves spreading across forest floor | Individual leaves emerging from leaf litter at ground level | Carpet effect — ramps grow in dense patches |

## Part 2: Camera Settings Reference

### White Balance — Lock Before First Shot, Do Not Change

| Cluster | Location | White Balance Setting | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cluster 1 (Forest Floor) | Field — May 20 | 5500K (Daylight) | Matches filtered woodland light; prevents color drift between shots |
| Cluster 2 (Root/Seed) | Indoor — May 21 | 5500K (Daylight) | Window light at 9am–1pm is approximately 5500K |
| Cluster 3 (Stem/Leaf) | Field AM + Indoor PM — May 22 | 5500K (Daylight) | Keep consistent with Clusters 1 and 2 for matching edits |

**Do not use Auto White Balance**. Auto drifts between frames and makes batch editing exponentially harder. Set manually before the first shot of each cluster and do not touch it again during that cluster.

### Aperture and Mode by Shot Type

| Shot Type | Distance to Subject | Camera Mode | Aperture | Depth of Field |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Flat-lay overhead (any angle) | 24 inches or more above surface | Standard photo mode | f/5.6–f/8 (or auto if phone) | Deep — everything in focus |
| Root / seed macro | 4–12 inches from subject | Portrait mode or macro | f/1.8–f/2.8 | Shallow — background blur; subject sharp |
| Full plant contextual | 18–36 inches | Standard mode | f/4–f/5.6 | Medium — subject sharp; background softened |
| Ground-level wide (habitat) | 24–48 inches | Standard mode | f/8 or auto | Deep — full scene in focus |

### Exposure Baseline

- **Exposure**: 0 to +0.3 (lifestyle photos should be slightly brighter than metered; do not expose to the histogram edge)
- **ISO**: 100–200 (morning window light or overcast field); never exceed 800 — noise at ISO 800+ is visible at Etsy zoom levels
- **Shutter speed**: Any speed above 1/125 if handheld; use 3-second self-timer for all two-hand or tripod shots to eliminate camera shake

### Editing Preset (Apply in Lightroom Mobile after Shoot)

| Adjustment | Value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Exposure | +0.3 | Open up forest-floor shadows slightly |
| Contrast | +20 | Definition between props; root texture |
| Highlights | -30 | Recover any blown root color (goldenseal yellow can overexpose) |
| Shadows | +25 | Lift shadow detail in leaf margins and soil |
| White balance temp | 5500K | Earthy, not clinical |
| Vibrance | +10 | Enhances plant color without oversaturating skin tones |
| Green saturation | +12 | Lifts leaf color specifically |
| Clarity | +10 | Micro-contrast; makes roots and seeds read as tactile |
| Crop | Native ratio for guide interiors; 1:1 for Etsy listing covers |

## Part 3: Gear Checklist

### Camera and Storage

- [ ] Smartphone (iPhone 12+ or equivalent Android; 12MP or higher) — charge to 100% the night before each shoot day
- [ ] Confirm 3 GB+ free storage before Cluster 1; do not fill the phone between Day 1 and Day 2 without transferring
- [ ] Memory card (if shooting with dedicated camera): 32 GB minimum; 2 cards (alternate between cards beyond 200 frames)
- [ ] Laptop for on-site culling — Day 1 midday review is not optional; flag re-shoots while props are still staged
- [ ] External hard drive — end-of-day backup; never leave a shoot day with images on one device only

### Lighting and Reflectors

- [ ] White foam-core board (24x36 in) — fill reflector opposite window for indoor sessions; folds flat for field transport
- [ ] Portable LED panel (battery-powered, 45W minimum) — field fill light on overcast days; available on Amazon $30–80
- [ ] Spray bottle with water — light misting of leaves and roots brings out color and adds visual life to dried specimens

### Stabilization

- [ ] Phone tripod or clip adapter (~$10) — required for any two-hand shot; any standard tripod works
- [ ] Alternatively: stack of hardcover books at counter height + vertical cookbook propped as phone support — adequate for indoor sessions
- [ ] Kneeling pad — extended time at ground level for low-angle habitat shots

### Field Supplies

- [ ] Silnylon tarp (6x8 ft minimum) — equipment shelter during light rain; also works as a clean backdrop surface on forest floors
- [ ] Offline map downloaded before departure — many Appalachian forest locations have no cell signal
- [ ] Portable phone battery — field sessions run 6+ hours; standard phone battery will not last without supplemental charging
- [ ] Paper bag for specimens in transit — dried roots travel well in kraft bags; avoid plastic

### Props (Have All Ready by May 18)

- [ ] Dark wood board (18x18 in minimum) — primary surface for all indoor close-ups
- [ ] Dark velvet cloth (small swatch) — alternative background for seed macro shots
- [ ] Botanical scale / ruler (15 cm) — size reference in root and seed shots
- [ ] Kraft paper seed envelopes (10–15) — seed display props; pre-label 4 envelopes by species
- [ ] Dried ginseng root (1 oz) — Mountain Rose Herbs; tan/cream color
- [ ] Dried goldenseal root (1 oz) — Mountain Rose Herbs; bright yellow
- [ ] Black Cohosh live plant or seed packet — from Strictly Medicinal Seeds
- [ ] Ramps fresh leaves — purchase May 18–19; refrigerate; bring to shoot in damp paper towel
- [ ] Ramps bulblets — Prairie Moon Nursery
- [ ] Ginseng stratified seeds (if available) — Wisconsin Grown Ginseng LLC
- [ ] Goldenseal seeds — Strictly Medicinal Seeds
- [ ] Botanical illustration printouts — 1 per species; color, 8.5x11; source from BHL (public domain)

## Part 5: Batch Naming Convention and Post-Processing SOP

### File Naming Convention

All exported JPEGs must follow this convention before uploading to Canva or the shared drive:

```
[species-slug]-[angle-type]-[sequence-number].jpg
```

**Species slugs** (use exactly as written):
- `ginseng` (American Ginseng / Panax quinquefolius)
- `goldenseal` (Goldenseal / Hydrastis canadensis)
- `black-cohosh` (Black Cohosh / Actaea racemosa)
- `ramps` (Ramps / Allium tricoccum)

**Angle type codes** (use exactly as written):
- `habitat` — full plant in naturalistic habitat context
- `leaf` — single leaf or leaf detail
- `flower` — flower or fruit (sourced from iNaturalist archive)
- `root` — root or rhizome close-up
- `seed` — seed or seed pod
- `context` — wider habitat or colony view

**Sequence numbers**: two digits, zero-padded (01, 02, 03...)

**Examples**:
- `ginseng-root-01.jpg` — first ginseng root close-up
- `goldenseal-leaf-03.jpg` — third goldenseal leaf detail
- `black-cohosh-habitat-01.jpg` — first Black Cohosh full-plant habitat shot
- `ramps-seed-02.jpg` — second ramps seed/bulblet image

**iNaturalist archive images**: prefix with `inat-` to distinguish from own photography:
- `inat-ginseng-flower-01.jpg`
- `inat-ramps-flower-01.jpg`

**BHL illustration images**: prefix with `bhl-`:
- `bhl-goldenseal-illustration-01.jpg`

### Post-Processing SOP

**Step 1 — Transfer (end of each shoot day)**
1. Connect phone/camera to laptop via USB or AirDrop
2. Import all RAW files to `/projects/seedwarden/assets/phase-2-guide-photos/[species-slug]/raw/`
3. Count frame totals — confirm transfer is complete
4. Copy entire raw folder to external hard drive
5. Upload to cloud (Google Drive or iCloud) before sleeping

**Step 2 — Initial Cull (same evening or next morning)**
1. Open Lightroom Mobile or Lightroom Classic
2. Star-rate each image: 5-star = select; 1-star = discard; 3-star = maybe
3. Target: 30 selects minimum across all species (5 per angle type per species)
4. Flag any re-shoot gaps immediately so they can be addressed on the next shoot day

**Step 3 — Color Correction (apply Lightroom preset to all 5-star selects)**
Apply the preset values from Part 2: Exposure +0.3, Contrast +20, Highlights -30, Shadows +25, White Balance 5500K, Vibrance +10, Green Saturation +12, Clarity +10.
First, do a manual anchor edit on the best ginseng root flat-lay (reference image). Batch-apply to all other Cluster 2 root images. Adjust Cluster 1 and 3 field images individually where outdoor lighting differs.

**Step 4 — Export**
- Long edge: 2000px minimum (use 2400px for cover images)
- Format: JPEG
- Quality: 90%
- Color space: sRGB
- Export to: `/projects/seedwarden/assets/phase-2-guide-photos/[species-slug]/finals/`

**Step 5 — Rename**
Rename each exported file using the naming convention above before placing in Canva. Use Lightroom's built-in filename template or rename in Finder/File Explorer after export.

**Step 6 — Attribution Log**
Log each iNaturalist CC-BY image in WORKLOG.md immediately after downloading (not after the guide is built). Do not let attribution logging accumulate — it becomes error-prone if delayed.

### Canva Upload

After all finals are exported and renamed:
1. Log into Canva (wanka95@gmail.com)
2. Open the Brand Kit > Media uploads
3. Create a folder named `Phase 2 Guide Photos — [Species Name]` for each species
4. Batch-upload all finals for that species to the correct folder
5. Confirm folder thumbnail previews are correct before closing
6. All guide builds in Canva should pull images from these named folders — do not upload the same image twice under different file names
