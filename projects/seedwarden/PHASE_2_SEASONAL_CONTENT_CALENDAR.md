# Phase 2 Seasonal Content Calendar
## May – October 2026 — Product Launch Timing, Content Hooks, and Email Sequences

**Purpose**: This is the 6-month rolling content and product launch calendar for Phase 2. It coordinates three tracks simultaneously: (1) which Phase 2 products to make visible when, (2) what seasonal content to produce for social media and email, and (3) how email nurture sequences align to seasonal buyer behavior. The goal is to have Seedwarden's content surface at the moment each customer cohort is most motivated to act.

**Context**: The 60-day social content calendar (phase-2-social-content-calendar-60day.md) handles the day-by-day post schedule for the first two months. This document operates at the month-level strategic layer — it tells you which product to highlight each month, why, and through which channels, with email sequences mapped to the seasonal buying pattern.

**Calendar assumption**: Phase 1 Etsy listings go live in early May 2026. Social media starts driving organic traffic mid-May. Email list grows from the Zone Quick-Start Card lead magnet starting Week 4. Month 1 = May 2026.

---

## Overview: Seedwarden's Seasonal Rhythm

Four customer cohorts have different seasonal buying patterns. The calendar is built around their peaks, not a generic content cadence.

| Month | Primary Cohort Active | Buying Driver | Key Products in Season |
|---|---|---|---|
| May | Homesteader, Forager | Spring planting urgency, foraging peak | Zone Calendar, Wild Edibles, Seed Saving |
| June | Homesteader, Forager | Garden established, early harvest preparation | Companion Planting, Container Growing, Forager bundle |
| July | All cohorts | Summer garden management, early preservation | Harvest Preservation, Fermented Harvest, Hot Sauce |
| August | Homesteader, Gift-adjacent | Harvest peak, canning season | Full preservation stack, Harvest Season Bundle |
| September | Homesteader, Prepper | Fall harvest, preservation urgency, winter prep | Livestock, Survival Garden, Prepper content |
| October | Prepper, Gift Buyer | Winter preparedness, early holiday discovery | Survival Garden, Prepper Bundle (if launched), gift-framing begins |

**Compound effect**: Each month's content should reference what is coming next month. A May post about seed starting ends with "next month we'll cover companion planting when everything is in the ground." This serializes the content and creates returning audience behavior.

## Month 1 — May 2026: Spring Planting Urgency

### Product Focus

**Lead product**: Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar ($7–18 tiered)
**Supporting product**: Apartment Seed Starting Kit ($9), Companion Planting Chart ($5)
**Lead magnet push**: Zone Quick-Start Card (primary acquisition tool this month)

**Rationale**: May is peak seed-starting urgency for Zones 4–7 (the majority of the US gardening population). Buyers who find Seedwarden in May via "zone 5 planting calendar" or "what to plant in May" are high-intent — they have a problem (what to plant right now) and Seedwarden has the most precise solution (zone-specific calendars). The Zone Quick-Start Card is the entry point; the full Zone Calendar is the paid conversion.

### Social Content Pillars — May

**Pillar 1: "What to plant in [Zone X] in May"** (4 posts, one per zone cluster)
Format: Short-form video or carousel
Hook: "Zone [5] growers — here's what goes in the ground this week." Show the specific planting tasks from the Zone-by-Zone Calendar for each zone cluster. This is educational content that doubles as a product demonstration. The product title and Etsy link appear at the end of each post.
Platforms: Instagram Reels, TikTok, Pinterest (static pin with zone-specific text overlay)

**Pillar 2: Seed saving basics** (2 posts)
Format: Carousel or talking head video
Hook: "You don't need to buy seeds every year. Here's how to save them." Entry-level content for buyers who are discovering seed saving for the first time. Cross-links to the Seed Saving Field Manual.

**Pillar 3: Apartment growing in May** (2 posts)
Format: Window sill or balcony shots with text overlay
Hook: "Growing in an apartment in May — what's actually possible without a yard." Addresses the urban growing cohort directly. Cross-links to the Apartment Seed Starting Kit and Container Blueprint Pack.

**Pillar 4: Zone Quick-Start Card promotion** (2 posts)
Format: Simple text-on-background or product shot
Hook: "Free download for Zone [X] growers — your one-page planting guide for right now." These are explicit lead magnet promotion posts. Direct traffic to the landing page.

### Email Sequence — May Subscribers (New Subscribers via Zone Card)

Subscribers who join in May via the Zone Quick-Start Card receive this 3-email onboarding sequence:

**Email 1 (immediate)**: Welcome + Zone Card delivery
Subject: "Your Zone [X] Quick-Start Card is ready"
Content: Download link, one paragraph on what's inside, one line: "If you want the full month-by-month calendar for Zone [X], the Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar has every planting week from January through December — [Etsy link]."

**Email 2 (Day 5)**: Seed saving hook
Subject: "You're already saving seeds — you just don't know it yet"
Content: Short explanation of what seeds are worth saving from a spring garden (tomatoes, peppers, beans). Brief case for the Seed Saving Field Manual. No hard sell — this email educates. CTA: "If you want the full system: [Etsy link]."

**Email 3 (Day 12)**: Companion planting hook
Subject: "The plants that grow better together (and the ones that don't)"
Content: Three companion planting pairs with the reasoning (basil + tomato, beans + corn, dill + cucumber). Tie to Companion Planting Chart. CTA: "The full chart covers 30+ pairings for any garden layout: [Etsy link] — it's $5."

### Platform-Specific May Strategy

**Pinterest**: Pin volume is highest this month — 15 pins minimum. One pin per product featured in the Zone Calendar (May planting windows for each crop). Evergreen content that continues to drive traffic long after the month ends.

**Instagram/TikTok**: Focus on Pillar 1 (zone-specific planting content). This type of content has the highest share rate on both platforms in spring because people share "this is for my zone" content with friends who garden in the same zone.

**Email**: Onboarding sequence for new Zone Card subscribers. No promotional email until Day 12 — allow two weeks of value delivery before a product pitch.

## Month 2 — June 2026: Early Harvest and Garden Management

### Product Focus

**Lead product**: Companion Planting Chart ($5) — low barrier, high shareability for established gardens
**Supporting product**: Container Growing Blueprint Pack ($12), Apartment Growing Complete Guide ($13)
**Bundle launch**: Spring Forager Bundle ($22) — see PHASE_2_BUNDLE_STRATEGY.md

**Rationale**: By June, most gardeners in Zones 4–8 have their spring planting in the ground. The next problems are garden management (companion planting, pest pressure, succession planting) and early harvest decisions (what to do with peas and lettuce before they bolt). The Companion Planting Chart is June's anchor product because it is universally applicable at this garden stage.

The Spring Forager Bundle launches in June because May–June is peak spring foraging season in most of the US. Wild garlic, dandelion, stinging nettle, chickweed, and elder flower are all at peak. Buyers are actively foraging and actively searching for guides.

### Social Content Pillars — June

**Pillar 1: Companion planting** (3 posts)
Format: Infographic-style carousel or short video
Hook: "These three plants make your tomatoes produce more." Companion planting content drives high engagement — it is counterintuitive, specific, and immediately actionable. The Companion Planting Chart is the obvious CTA for every post in this series.

**Pillar 2: What to do with too much produce** (2 posts)
Format: Talking head or carousel
Hook: "Your peas are ready, your lettuce is bolting — here's what to do before it's all gone." Succession planting, blanching and freezing, early fermentation. Bridges the gap between growing and preservation — sets up the Harvest Season Bundle announcement in July.

**Pillar 3: Spring foraging is live** (2 posts)
Format: Field photography or nature walk video
Hook: "Five edible plants you can find right now in [Region X]." Feature specific species from the Wild Edibles guide (use the habit photos from the wild edibles archive). Each plant shown is in the guide — the post is simultaneously educational and a product preview.

**Pillar 4: Small space update** (1 post)
Hook: "One month in — here's what a container garden in [X square feet] looks like." Progress post for apartment growing cohort. Authentic, behind-the-scenes. Links to Container Blueprint Pack and Apartment Growing Complete Guide.

### Email Sequence — June Nurture (Month 2 subscribers)

**Existing subscribers** (joined in May): continue to month 2 nurture.

**Email 4 (Week 5, Day 33)**: Foraging hook
Subject: "Five things you can eat for free right now"
Content: Feature three to five spring edibles with identification notes (drawn from Wild Edibles guide content). Brief and specific — not a comprehensive botany lesson. CTA: "The full identification guide for wild edibles is [Etsy link]."

**Email 5 (Week 6, Day 40)**: Companion planting tip
Subject: "Plant these together this week"
Content: Two companion planting examples with one-sentence rationale for each. Seasonal hook: "This works right now if you still have transplant space in early June." CTA: "The full companion planting chart is [$5 at Etsy link]."

**Email 6 (Week 8, Day 54)**: Preservation season preview
Subject: "Before your garden runs away from you — what to do with the overflow"
Content: Short preview of what happens in July and August if there is no preservation plan: volunteer tomatoes, bolting greens, too many cucumbers. Frame the Harvest Preservation Field Manual as the planning tool for what is coming. CTA: "[Etsy link] — the full preservation guide covers canning, fermenting, and dehydrating for whatever your garden throws at you."

## Month 3 — July 2026: Preservation Season Begins

### Product Focus

**Lead product**: Fermented and Preserved Harvest Handbook ($13)
**Supporting product**: Harvest Preservation Field Manual ($15), Grow Your Own Hot Sauce ($10)
**Bundle launch**: Harvest Season Bundle ($28) — all three preservation guides
**Phase 3 data collection begins**: By July 1, Phase 1 has 45+ days of live data. Pull first cohort analysis.

**Rationale**: July is when preservation urgency begins. Early summer vegetables are producing: cucumbers, zucchini, green beans, early tomatoes. Buyers who have never preserved before are overwhelmed. Buyers who have preserved before are planning their supplies. Both groups are searching for guides in July. Seedwarden's three preservation products are the most relevant catalog cluster this month.

### Social Content Pillars — July

**Pillar 1: Fermentation introduction** (3 posts)
Format: Process video — hands in frame packing a jar
Hook: "No equipment. No canning pot. Here's how to preserve vegetables in 20 minutes." Lacto-fermentation is the lowest barrier entry point in the preservation category. These posts drive the most new-to-preservation engagement because they make the process feel achievable. Every post ends at the Fermented Harvest Handbook or the Harvest Season Bundle.

**Pillar 2: Hot sauce season** (2 posts)
Format: Garden-to-bottle narrative
Hook: "Your pepper plants are about to go crazy. Here's what to do with them." Features the Grow Your Own Hot Sauce guide. High shareability because hot sauce is a social food — people share what they made and who they made it with.

**Pillar 3: Harvest Season Bundle announcement** (2 posts)
Format: Flat lay lifestyle shot + text overlay
Hook: "Three guides, everything you need for preservation season — $28 for all three." Direct product promotion posts. Run in Week 2 and Week 4 of July. These are the only explicit promotion posts this month.

**Pillar 4: Phase 1 data milestone** (1 post)
Format: Transparent behind-the-scenes
Hook: "We've been open for 45 days. Here's what we've learned." A candid shop update post. Builds trust and connection. Not a product promotion. These posts consistently outperform product posts for saves and follows because they let buyers see the person behind the shop.

### Email Sequence — July Nurture

**Email 7 (Week 9, Day 61)**: Preservation season arrival
Subject: "Your cucumber and bean harvest is going to be overwhelming. Here's the plan."
Content: Frame the preservation season in concrete terms. "Most Zone 5 gardeners get their first cucumber overwhelm in the second week of July. Here's what to do with 30 cucumbers in a week." Lacto-fermented pickles in a short explanation. CTA: "[Etsy link] for the full fermentation guide, or get all three preservation guides together at $28: [bundle link]."

**Email 8 (Week 11, Day 75)**: Hot sauce angle
Subject: "Your peppers are going to make better hot sauce than anything in the grocery store"
Content: One-paragraph case for making your own hot sauce. Brief process. CTA: "[Etsy link] Grow Your Own Hot Sauce guide — covers pepper growing, fermentation, and finishing."

## Month 4 — August 2026: Harvest Peak and Prepper Season Preview

### Product Focus

**Lead product**: Harvest Preservation Field Manual ($15) — the comprehensive reference for the full harvest season
**Supporting product**: Harvest Season Bundle ($28) — second month of the bundle
**Prepper preview**: Survival Garden Regional Plans ($22), Hunting Fishing Trapping Manual ($20)

**Rationale**: August is the peak canning month for most of the US (Zones 4–8). Social searches for "canning tomatoes," "how to can green beans," and "water bath canning guide" all peak in August. Seedwarden's preservation catalog is in peak demand. The Prepper cohort also activates in late summer as fall approaches — survivalists think about winter supplies in August and September.

### Social Content Pillars — August

**Pillar 1: Peak harvest preservation** (3 posts)
Format: Quantity-and-volume angle — "what to do with 30 pounds of tomatoes"
Hook: "Tomato harvest is coming in. Here's how to turn it into 12 months of sauce." High-specificity posts perform better than general "how to preserve food" content. Feature specific volumes, specific times, specific methods.

**Pillar 2: Prepper activation** (2 posts)
Format: Forward-looking, winter preparation framing
Hook: "August is when serious preppers start building their winter supplies. Here's the plan." Feature the Survival Garden Regional Plans and the Hunting Manual. This content intentionally reaches the prepper cohort before the rest of the market does — early September is when prepper content volume spikes on social; Seedwarden can capture attention in August while CPM is lower.

**Pillar 3: Gift buyer preview** (1 post)
Format: Gift framing — "the best gift for the person who grows their own food"
Hook: "If someone in your life grows their own food, here's the gift they actually want." Early gift content targeting the Holiday cohort. November and December will be obvious gift months; August plants the seed in gift buyers who plan early.

### Email Sequence — August

**Email 9 (Week 13, Day 89)**: Harvest season anchor
Subject: "The August canning problem — and the most efficient fix"
Content: Frame the harvest overwhelm in concrete, sympathetic terms. Three practical options for anyone facing too much produce. CTA: Harvest Season Bundle at $28 for anyone who wants the full preservation reference.

**Email 10 (Week 15, Day 103)**: Prepper pivot
Subject: "If you're reading this in August, you're already behind on winter prep"
Content: Tone shift — direct, practical, prepper-adjacent. Frame August as the right time to audit food security for the coming winter. CTA: Survival Garden Regional Plans + Hunting Manual cross-promotion.

## Month 5 — September 2026: Fall Harvest and Sustainability Review

### Product Focus

**Lead product**: Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual ($15) — fall livestock management (chickens into winter)
**Supporting product**: Meat and Fish Preservation Field Manual ($20), Survival Garden Regional Plans ($22)
**Email milestone**: Month 5 is the first time email list should have meaningful volume (100+ subscribers from Zone Card). Begin segmented sends by zone.

**Rationale**: September is the second peak for the homesteader cohort. Fall crops are in (brassicas, root vegetables, winter squash). Livestock decisions happen before winter (culling, feed supply). The prepper cohort's second annual activation window is September–November. Seedwarden's livestock, preservation, and survival catalog become primary.

### Social Content Pillars — September

**Pillar 1: Fall garden planting** (2 posts)
Hook: "September is actually your second planting season. Here's what goes in now." Zones 6–9 have a full fall growing season (kale, spinach, garlic). This content is less applicable to Zones 3–5 but is highly seasonal for the Southern US, Pacific Coast, and transition zone.

**Pillar 2: Livestock and winter prep** (2 posts)
Hook: "Backyard chickens need different care when frost is coming. Here's the checklist." Feature the Livestock Field Manual content. This reaches the subsection of the homesteader cohort with small livestock — a highly engaged, underserved audience on Etsy.

**Pillar 3: Zone Quick-Start Card refresh** (2 posts)
Hook: "Updated your Zone Card for fall? Here's what to plant this month." The Zone Quick-Start Card "This Month" tasks are updated monthly — September's update gives this content a reason to post and a reason to re-engage existing subscribers. Forward traffic to the landing page for fall sign-ups.

**Pillar 4: Transparency post** (1 post)
Hook: "Month 5. Here's what is actually selling and what isn't." Second behind-the-scenes store update. Builds community and shows buyers the real experience of running a small independent shop.

### Email Sequence — September (Segmented by Zone)

September is the first month to use zone-based segmentation. Subscribers in Zones 3–5 get different fall content than subscribers in Zones 7–10.

**Zone 3–5 subscribers**:
Subject: "Your fall gardening window is almost closed — here's what to get in before frost"
Content: Specific tasks for cool-zone fall gardening (garlic planting, cover crops, root vegetable storage). CTA: Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar for the fall planting schedule.

**Zone 6–8 subscribers**:
Subject: "Fall is actually a second growing season in your zone — here's what to plant now"
Content: Fall brassicas, garlic, succession greens. CTA: Zone Calendar + Companion Planting Chart for fall layout.

**Zone 9–10 subscribers**:
Subject: "October and November are your best growing months — here's the fall setup"
Content: Zone 9–10 fall is high-season gardening. Cool-weather crops, tropical perennial management. CTA: Zone Calendar + Container Blueprint Pack.

## Content Production Infrastructure

### Batch Production Schedule

Content does not need to be produced week-by-week. Batch production by cluster dramatically reduces total time:

| Batch | Content Type | Produce Once | Distributes Through |
|---|---|---|---|
| Photography batch (Session 1–3) | All 15 lifestyle photos | One month | Every subsequent month |
| Zone Card monthly update | 8 zone card refreshes | 20 min per month | Email + social simultaneously |
| Pinterest pins | 5 pins per product | 3 hours total | Scheduled across the 6-month window |
| Email sequences | All 13 emails | Written once before Month 1 | Automated delivery via Kit |

The emails listed above should be written in full before Phase 1 launch — even though they are scheduled to send 5 months apart, writing them in one session ensures consistent voice and correct cross-references between emails.

### Social Media Scheduling

Pinterest is the highest-priority scheduling task because pins have long relevance tails — a May pin about Zone 5 planting will continue to drive traffic in September when another grower plans their fall garden. Use Pinterest's native scheduler (free) to load all May and June pins in a single session at the end of April.

Instagram and TikTok require fresh, timely content — pre-scheduling 4 weeks in advance is practical; pre-scheduling 6 months is not advisable for platforms where trends and platform algorithms shift. Schedule 4 weeks out, reassess monthly.

### Zone Card Monthly Refresh

The Zone Quick-Start Card's "This Month" block is the one element that requires monthly maintenance. The monthly update workflow (from ZONE_CARD_PRODUCTION_TIMELINE.md) takes 20 minutes for all 8 zones. Set a recurring calendar reminder for the first of each month. The updated cards improve email open rates (subscribers in May will re-open a June card if they know it has been refreshed), and the refresh post on social gives a low-effort content opportunity each month with a natural CTA.
