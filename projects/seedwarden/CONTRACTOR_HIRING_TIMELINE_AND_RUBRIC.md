## Portfolio Audit Checklist

Run on every Tier 1 candidate before offer. Require minimum 5 portfolio samples for all roles except Social Media Manager (2 accounts).

### Photographer (5 recent projects)

- [ ] At least 3 samples show botanical, food, or natural-product flat-lay (not portraiture or events)
- [ ] Resolution appears professional (assess via display quality; ask for source dimensions if unclear)
- [ ] White balance is consistent within each project set (no visible color drift between frames in a series)
- [ ] Composition and props fit Seedwarden's warm, accessible, naturalist aesthetic
- [ ] No harsh shadows across central subjects in botanical close-ups
- [ ] If any people appear: confirm model releases were obtained (ask directly)

**Auto-reject**: Work exclusively portraiture, architecture, or event photography with zero product/flat-lay samples.

### Women's Health Writer (5 published articles)

- [ ] At least 3 articles cover women's health, herbal medicine, or integrative wellness
- [ ] At least 2 articles use Latin binomials for plant species mentioned
- [ ] At least 1 article mentions contraindications or drug interactions for an herb or supplement
- [ ] FTC-compliant language visible: "may support," "research suggests," "traditionally used" — not "cures" or "prevents" without evidence qualifier
- [ ] At least 1 article exceeds 2,000 words (demonstrates capacity for 3,600-word manuscript)

**Auto-reject**: Articles making unqualified health claims ("eliminates," "cures," "guarantees") without evidence-tier framing, or articles with no citations.

### Respiratory & Sleep Writer (5 published articles)

- [ ] At least 3 articles cover herbal medicine, natural health, or evidence-based wellness
- [ ] At least 1 article addresses drug interactions or safety for an herb
- [ ] Uses primary sources or named databases (NatMed Pro, Cochrane, Examine.com, or equivalent)
- [ ] Tone is educational and accessible (not academic-only or marketing-heavy)
- [ ] At least 1 article exceeds 2,500 words

**Auto-reject**: Blog content only with no evidence citations; articles recommending supplements as replacements for medication.

### Herbalist Consultant (credential verification required)

- [ ] Verify AHG Professional Member status via ahg.memberclicks.net (public directory) OR NAHA membership verification
- [ ] If unaffiliated with AHG/NAHA: request documentation of 5+ years clinical herbalism (practice records, school faculty position, peer-reviewed publications, or 3+ long-term client testimonials)
- [ ] At least 3 professional projects or publications listed (monographs, consulting references, continuing education delivery)
- [ ] Demonstrates drug interaction knowledge in profile (mentions NatMed Pro, Micromedex, clinical databases)
- [ ] CITES or endangered species knowledge: UpS membership, At-Risk list familiarity, or conservation language in published work

**Auto-reject**: Self-described "herbalist" with no verifiable credential and no professional project history.

### Logistics Coordinator (3–5 professional references or projects)

- [ ] At least 2 projects show email marketing platform management (Kit, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or equivalent)
- [ ] At least 1 project shows e-commerce coordination (Etsy, Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon seller)
- [ ] Evidence of managing concurrent timelines (multi-contractor coordination, or VA with multiple simultaneous clients)
- [ ] Communication samples show professional, concise tone
- [ ] Stated availability matches Monday + Thursday coordination windows

**Auto-reject**: No email marketing platform experience, or only buyer-side Etsy experience.

### Social Media Manager (2 active accounts required — links mandatory)

- [ ] 2 Instagram accounts provided with verifiable links (active, not deleted or private)
- [ ] At least 1 account is in wellness, botanical, food, natural products, or lifestyle niche
- [ ] Engagement quality visible: thoughtful comments from followers, community interaction
- [ ] Content is varied: mix of educational, promotional, and lifestyle posts (not exclusively product ads)
- [ ] FTC compliance visible in existing managed content: #ad on sponsored posts, no unqualified health claims
- [ ] Posting cadence: consistent (5+ posts per week) in the accounts shown

**Auto-reject**: Accounts provided are inactive, under 3 months old, or show purchased followers (check Social Blade for sudden follower spikes).

## Reference Check Protocol

Send to 2 references per Tier 1 candidate (email preferred; phone optional). Responses required within 24 hours or skip and proceed.

**Reference request email template**:

Subject: Quick reference check — [CANDIDATE NAME]

Hi [REFERENCE NAME],

I'm checking a reference for [CANDIDATE NAME] who has applied for a [ROLE] project with Seedwarden, a digital herbal guide brand. The project runs July–August 2026.

Three quick questions — reply by email is fine, takes about 3 minutes:

1. Quality: On a scale of 1–10, how would you rate [CANDIDATE NAME]'s work quality on the project you worked on together?
2. Reliability: Did [CANDIDATE NAME] deliver on time? Any missed deadlines or unexpected quality issues?
3. Communication: Was [CANDIDATE NAME] responsive to feedback and questions during the project?

Feel free to add anything else that would be helpful.

Thank you.

[YOUR NAME]
Seedwarden

---

**Log responses** (one sentence per reference per criterion):
- Example: "Editor (The Herbal Dispatch): 9/10 quality, always on-time, revised twice without issue, very responsive."
- Example: "Client (Botanical Co.): 8/10, one deadline pushed 2 days but communicated early, professional throughout."

**Minimum bar**: At least 1 of 2 references must confirm on-time delivery and professional communication. A single strong reference with no opposing signal is sufficient to proceed.

## Scoring Rubric — 5-Point Scale Per Dimension

Score each candidate across 4 dimensions. Each dimension is scored 1–5 (not 0–10) for simplicity.

### Dimensions and Weights

| Dimension | Weight | What to Assess |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio Quality | 40% | Does prior work match quality and subject matter required? (Use audit checklists above as scoring basis) |
| Timeline Fit | 30% | Can they start July 5–6 and commit to all delivery dates? Hard requirement — score 1 if unavailable |
| Communication Clarity | 15% | Response time, clarity and completeness of application, quality of questions asked |
| Price Reasonableness | 15% | Does proposed rate fall within posted budget ±10%? See rate negotiation rules below |

### 5-Point Scoring Scale

**Portfolio Quality**:
| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Exceeds requirements; prior work directly matches subject matter and quality level (e.g., photographer with 5+ botanical flat-lay portfolios) |
| 4 | Meets requirements; clear capability with minor subject-matter gaps |
| 3 | Acceptable; general capability but limited subject-matter match |
| 2 | Weak match; significant gap between prior work and project requirements |
| 1 | Disqualifying issues (see auto-reject criteria per role above) |

**Timeline Fit (Q3 timeline)**:
| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | July 5 start confirmed; all delivery dates confirmed; no conflicts |
| 4 | July 5 start confirmed; minor uncertainty on one delivery date (resolvable in kickoff call) |
| 3 | July 5 start likely; one delivery date in question (moderate risk) |
| 2 | Unclear start date; or confirmed conflict with a delivery date |
| 1 | Cannot confirm July 5 start or has confirmed conflict with multiple delivery dates |

**Communication Clarity**:
| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Applied within 12 hours of posting; asked 1–2 specific role-relevant questions; all portfolio links valid |
| 4 | Applied within 24 hours; application complete and professional; links work |
| 3 | Applied within 48 hours; application adequate but generic |
| 2 | Application incomplete (missing link or confirmation) or has clarity issues |
| 1 | No response to outreach; application obviously templated with no mention of Seedwarden or role specifics |

**Price Reasonableness**:
| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Proposed rate at or below posted budget |
| 4 | Proposed rate 1–5% above posted budget (within easy negotiation range) |
| 3 | Proposed rate 6–10% above posted budget (borderline; negotiate before offer) |
| 2 | Proposed rate 11–15% above posted budget (decline or hard-negotiate) |
| 1 | Proposed rate more than 15% above posted budget (do not hire without scope adjustment) |

### Final Score Calculation

Overall Score = (Portfolio × 0.40) + (Timeline × 0.30) + (Communication × 0.15) + (Price × 0.15)

Maximum possible: 5.0

### Hire/Interview/Reject Decision Matrix

| Score | Decision | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 4.0–5.0 | **HIRE (Tier 1)** | Extend offer within 4 hours of scoring. Meets all criteria. |
| 3.0–3.9 | **INTERVIEW** | Conduct 10-minute screening call to resolve one or two scoring gaps. If resolved, extend offer. |
| Below 3.0 | **REJECT** | Do not extend offer. Send polite decline: "Thank you for applying — we've filled this role." |

### Worked Example

**Photographer candidate A**:
- Portfolio Quality: 4 (3 botanical flat-lay portfolios; professional resolution; minor prop style differences)
- Timeline Fit: 5 (confirms July 5 start, all 4 delivery dates confirmed)
- Communication Clarity: 4 (replied within 18 hours; application complete; asked about prop sourcing)
- Price Reasonableness: 5 (proposed $1,380 against $1,400 budget)

Overall: (4 × 0.40) + (5 × 0.30) + (4 × 0.15) + (5 × 0.15) = 1.60 + 1.50 + 0.60 + 0.75 = **4.45 — Tier 1 HIRE**

**Writer candidate B**:
- Portfolio Quality: 3 (3 wellness articles; basic contraindication coverage; no articles >2,000 words)
- Timeline Fit: 2 (July 5 start uncertain; says "likely available" but no confirmation)
- Communication Clarity: 4 (clean application; asked reasonable question about word count)
- Price Reasonableness: 3 (proposed $1,200 against $950 budget — 26% over)

Overall: (3 × 0.40) + (2 × 0.30) + (4 × 0.15) + (3 × 0.15) = 1.20 + 0.60 + 0.60 + 0.45 = **2.85 — REJECT**

## Rate Negotiation Rules

- **Within posted budget**: Accept without negotiation.
- **1–10% above posted budget**: Negotiate down. Email: "Our budget for this role is $[X]. Can you work within that? We can offer [one small sweetener: e.g., faster payment timeline, strong reference at close, earlier final payment trigger]."
- **More than 10% above posted budget**: Do not hire unless this candidate is the only qualified applicant and no Tier 2 candidate exists. In that case, escalate scope or timeline before accepting the rate overage.
- **Do not split the difference** on rate if it moves total cost more than 10% above budget. The Q3 contractor budget is $5,950 gross — finite.

## Automatic Rejection Criteria

Any one of the following = reject immediately, regardless of score:

- Cannot confirm July 5 start
- No portfolio link provided or all links broken/private
- Portfolio shows zero relevant work for the role
- Proposed rate exceeds posted budget by more than 20%
- Reference check reveals missed deliverables on a comparable project with no prior notification to client
- Application appears copy-pasted with no mention of Seedwarden, herbal medicine, or role specifics

## Background Verification Procedures

For Tier 1 candidates, run the following before extending offer:

**Credential verification (Herbalist Consultant only)**:
- AHG directory check: ahg.memberclicks.net
- NAHA directory check: naha.org/membership-directory
- If neither: request documentation and 3 professional references with verifiable contact information

**Portfolio link verification (all roles)**:
- Confirm all portfolio links are publicly accessible (not 404, not login-gated)
- For social media accounts: check last post date (must be within 30 days for active claim)
- For Upwork profiles: check job success score (aim for 90%+) and review count (minimum 3 reviews)

**Identity verification (all roles)**:
- Confirm contractor name matches Upwork profile name
- Note profile country and timezone (relevant for check-in window alignment)
- For any candidate proposing an alias: confirm legal name for contract signature

**FTC compliance check (Writers only)**:
- Review all 3+ portfolio articles for FTC language compliance
- Flag any article using unqualified claims ("cures," "prevents," "eliminates" without evidence qualifier)
- A single disqualifying article is not auto-reject if the candidate acknowledges the standard and provides evidence of current compliance in other work
