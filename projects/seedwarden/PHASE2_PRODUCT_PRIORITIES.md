# Seedwarden Phase 2 Product Priority Matrix
**Status**: Ready — applies immediately after LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md approval
**Prepared**: April 27, 2026

---

## Prioritization Framework

Two factors determine sequencing: conversion potential and product price. The rationale is straightforward — a lifestyle image that moves one additional sale per month is worth more on a $20 product than on a $5 product. Combined with what is known about Etsy search demand and buyer behavior for this catalog, this produces three priority tiers.

**Factor 1 — Price point:** High-ticket products ($18–$22) get lifestyle photos first. The revenue upside per converted sale is highest. Even a 1–2 sale per month increase justifies the photography investment immediately.

**Factor 2 — Search intent and listing maturity:** Products with strong existing keyword demand and a defined buyer intent benefit most from lifestyle photos, because the photos tip undecided visitors who are already browsing. Products where search intent is highly specific (the buyer already knows exactly what they want) are lower priority — they will convert on description alone.

**Factor 3 — Phase 1 sales data (apply after first 30 days live):** Once Phase 1 is live, replace estimated priority with actual data. Products with views but low conversion rate are the highest-priority lifestyle photo candidates. Views indicate buyers are finding the listing; low conversion indicates something in the listing — most likely the 3-image set — is not closing them.

## Tier 1 — Photograph First (High Ticket + High Conversion Potential)

These 5 products get lifestyle photography in Week 1–2 of Phase 2. Combined, they represent roughly 55–60% of expected monthly revenue at conservative volume.

**1. Survival Garden Regional Plans ($22)**
- Highest price in catalog. Every additional sale is $22 gross.
- Strong evergreen search intent ("survival garden plan," "regional food garden"). Buyers are comparison shopping between guides; a lifestyle photo showing the guide being used in a real planning context is the decisive trust signal.
- Stock photography required (Cluster D). Week 1, Day 1–2 in the execution plan.
- Target: slot 4 = rural raised-bed garden or regional landscape; slot 5 = printed guide pages spread on a table with a hand marking zones.

**2. Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual ($20)**
- Second-highest price. Cluster D — stock required.
- Buyer profile: experienced outdoors person who evaluates field guides critically. Lifestyle photos showing the guide in a field context (not a studio) communicate authenticity to this audience more than to any other in the catalog.
- iStock is the appropriate source; free sites have weak coverage for hunting lifestyle.
- Target: slot 4 = streamside fishing or outdoor field context; slot 5 = guide pages open on a camp table or in a jacket pocket.

**3. Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual ($18)**
- Cluster D — stock required.
- Buyers are smallholders or aspiring homesteaders actively researching before getting animals. A photo of a backyard chicken flock or small goat pen with the guide present is directly relevant to their current decision-making moment.
- iStock priority target per the budget strategy.
- Target: slot 4 = small farm lifestyle (backyard chickens, goats, or ducks); slot 5 = guide open next to a feed bucket or animal area.

**4. Meat/Fish Preservation Field Manual ($18)**
- Cluster D — stock required.
- Preservation skills have surged in search interest. A smokehouse, curing rack, or salt-box visual is distinctive enough to stand out from generic food photography.
- iStock for smokehouse/artisan charcuterie content.
- Target: slot 4 = artisan curing or smoking setting; slot 5 = guide pages visible alongside preservation equipment.

**5. Harvest Preservation Field Manual ($16)**
- Cluster C — physical photography possible (kitchen with mason jars and canning equipment).
- Strong seasonal demand in late summer/fall; purchasing before peak season matters. But lifestyle photos improve off-season browsing too.
- Physical shoot: kitchen table, mason jars, dehydrating tray or canning pot.
- Target: slot 4 = flat-lay of guide pages with full jars and preservation tools; slot 5 = hands lifting a mason jar, guide open in background.

## Tier 2 — Photograph Second (Mid-Ticket + Strong Audience Match)

These 7 products follow Tier 1 immediately, completing the physical photography batches. Combined, they cover the core of Clusters A and C.

**6. Fermented & Preserved Harvest Handbook ($13)**
- Cluster C — physical. Kitchen table with fermentation crocks, mason jars, weights.
- Strong audience overlap with Tier 1 preservation products. Buyers often browse multiple preservation listings; consistent lifestyle visuals across these products builds brand cohesion.

**7. Grow Your Own Hot Sauce ($15)**
- Cluster C — physical. Kitchen setting with dried chilis, hot sauce bottles, cutting board.
- Passionate niche with high click intent. The hot sauce category has visual distinctiveness that stock photography underserves — fresh and dried peppers next to the guide is an immediately recognizable signal.

**8. Seed Saving Field Manual ($14)**
- Cluster A — physical. Seed envelopes, germination tray, wooden surface.
- Core catalog product with the highest intrinsic search volume. Strong Phase 1 candidate; lifestyle photos here compound the listing's existing traffic.

**9. Native Plants Regional Guide ($18)**
- Cluster E — Wikimedia Commons preferred for botanical accuracy.
- Higher price justifies the sourcing effort. Source pressed-plant or field habitat photos from Wikimedia under appropriate licenses (CC0 or CC BY preferred).
- Lower priority than Tier 1 only because the guide's conversion strength comes from content depth; the lifestyle photo is additive rather than decisive.
- Target: slot 4 = native wildflower meadow or habitat photo (Wikimedia field shot); slot 5 = foraging basket or field identification scene.

**10. Heirloom Variety Selection Guide ($11)**
- Cluster A — physical. Seed envelopes, small pots, wooden surface. Shoot alongside Seed Saving Manual (same props, same setup).

**11. Apartment Plant Catalog ($14)**
- Cluster B — physical. Potted houseplants, window sill, modern indoor setting.
- Strong spring demand; apartment growers are the highest-density Etsy buyer segment for this price range.

**12. Container Growing Blueprint Pack ($12)**
- Cluster B — physical. Shoot alongside Apartment Plant Catalog.

## Tier 3 — Photograph Third (Lower Ticket or Lower Conversion Priority)

These 9 products represent strong catalog depth but have either lower price points where lifestyle photo ROI is thinner, or they share enough visual overlap with earlier batches that they can be shot in the same sessions without additional setup.

**13–15. Cluster A remaining products** (Food Sovereignty Starter Guide $8, Anti-Catalog: 30 Heirlooms $10, 12-Month Urban Growing Planner $7): Shoot during the same Cluster A session as the Seed Saving Manual. These are lower-priced products where lifestyle photos improve conversion modestly rather than decisively. The marginal cost of shooting them during an existing Cluster A session is near zero.

**16–17. Cluster B remaining products** (Apartment Growing Complete Guide $13, Seed Swap Hosting Kit $10): Shoot during the same Cluster B session as Apartment Plant Catalog and Container Blueprint. The Seed Swap Kit benefits from a specific lifestyle angle — a table with seed envelopes being sorted, a small community gathering setup — which is achievable but slightly more involved than a standard indoor plant shot.

**18–19. Cluster A remaining products** (Companion Planting Chart $5, Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar $7–$18): The Companion Planting Chart is a low-priced impulse buy where lifestyle photos have limited ROI. Zone Calendar is a higher-value product in the multi-zone bundle version ($18), making lifestyle photos slightly more important there. Shoot both during the Cluster A batch — they require no additional props.

**20–21. Apartment Seed Starting Kit ($9) and any remaining products**: Complete the catalog during Cluster A/B sessions. No additional setups required.

## Week 1 Critical Mass: 5–7 Products for Immediate Impact

The single most important action at Phase 2 launch is getting lifestyle images live on the highest-ticket products before spending time on the low-ticket tail. The first 5 products to receive lifestyle images (Tier 1 above) represent roughly $84–$94 in combined list price. If lifestyle images add even 1 additional sale per month across these five products, the monthly revenue lift is $80+, which covers the iStock budget in the first month.

**Week 1 sequencing:**
- Days 1–3: Source and composite all Cluster D/E stock images (Survival Garden, Hunting, Livestock, Meat/Fish Preservation). Upload to Etsy slots 4–5 on these listings immediately.
- Days 4–5: Schedule physical shoot for Harvest Preservation (Cluster C — the 5th Tier 1 product). Shoot, edit, upload.

By end of Week 1: five of the highest-revenue-potential listings have moved from 3-image to 5-image. This is the critical mass that makes Phase 2 measurable before the full 3-week window closes.

**Tracking note**: Record the date each lifestyle image is added to each listing. The before/after conversion measurement window (documented in LIFESTYLE_PHOTOGRAPHY_STRATEGY.md Section 5) starts from these dates, not from the beginning of Phase 2.
