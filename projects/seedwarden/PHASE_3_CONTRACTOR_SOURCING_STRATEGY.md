## Lead Finding

The hardest filter in this search is not credential — it is contraindication research rigor. Freelance health writers who can produce clinical-grade contraindication panels (herb-drug interaction tables from primary sources, CITES Appendix II context, pregnancy and nursing safety rated by evidence tier) are a subset of approximately 10–15% of credentialed herbalist writers. The fastest path to this subset is the AHG member directory filtered by specialty, Chestnut School of Herbal Medicine instructor network, and Herbal Academy Advanced + Clinical alumni. Upwork and Toptal searches are viable with strict screening questions, but require more initial filtering effort. Budget 2–3 hours of vetting work per day for the first five days of the search window to process all inbound candidates efficiently.

## Section 1 — Search Channels (Six Verified Sources)

### Channel 1: American Herbalists Guild (AHG) Member Directory

**URL**: directory.americanherbalistsguild.com  
**Access**: Public directory; searchable by specialty and location  
**Credential density**: Highest available for US herbalist writers. AHG Registered Herbalist (RH) credential requires minimum 1,200 hours of training plus examination. The directory lists approximately 400–600 active RH members, of whom 30–50 list teaching, writing, or education as a secondary activity — the target population.

**Search procedure**:

1. Go to directory.americanherbalistsguild.com
2. Filter by specialty: Clinical Herbalism, Women's Health, Medicinal Herbs, Respiratory, Immune System
3. For each candidate who lists writing, teaching, or education in their profile: copy name, contact method, and specialty to the contractor tracking log
4. Target 15–20 initial contacts; expect 3–5 substantive replies within the first response window

**Outreach framing for AHG directory contacts**: Lead with the shared credential community. Reference the AHG credential as context, not as a filter. Herbalists who have invested in their RH credential are responsive to projects that take clinical accuracy seriously. The phrase "practitioner-grade contraindication standards" signals that this is not a generic content writing job.

**Expected response rate**: 15–25% from RH members who list education as an activity. Timeline: send Monday morning, expect first replies by Wednesday of the same week.

**Sample outreach subject line**: "Medicinal herb guide writer needed — AHG practitioner-grade accuracy standards"

### Channel 6: Upwork and Toptal — Freelance Platform Filters

**URLs**: upwork.com, toptal.com  
**Commission rates**: Upwork charges 5–20% service fee to the client (on top of contractor rate); Toptal charges a placement fee of 10–15%. Budget for this: add 12–18% to any quoted rate to estimate true cost. A contractor quoting $1,200 on Upwork costs the project approximately $1,344–$1,440 including fees.

**Platform strengths**: Both platforms offer contract management, payment escrow, and dispute resolution. Upwork is higher volume with a wider range of quality. Toptal claims pre-vetted top 3% of freelancers, but this vetting is general writing quality, not herbalism credential depth — do not assume Toptal vetting replaces the screening questions below.

**Search procedure on Upwork**:

1. Post a job (not a search) with the following title: "Medicinal Herbalist Writer — Practitioner-Grade Educational Guides"
2. Job description must include: "Applicants must answer two screening questions before their proposal is reviewed"
3. Screening question 1: "What is the CITES Appendix II listing and why does it matter for a Goldenseal cultivation guide? Answer in 3–5 sentences."
4. Screening question 2: "Rewrite the following sentence in FTC-compliant form: 'Elderberry prevents colds and flu.' Your rewrite should accurately represent the evidence tier."
5. Filter proposals: read screening question answers first. Candidates who cannot answer Question 1 do not have the species-level knowledge required for the Immunity bundle. Candidates who fail Question 2 will create FTC liability.
6. Minimum hourly rate filter: $40/hr. Below this threshold, contraindication research quality is unlikely to meet the standard.

**Search procedure on Toptal**: Contact Toptal's client intake team directly at toptal.com/hire. Specify "medical herbalist writer with clinical contraindication experience" as the specialization. Toptal's matching process will surface 2–3 pre-screened candidates within 48–72 hours. Apply the same screening questions during the intro call.

**Do not use Fiverr**: Contraindication research depth cannot be quality-verified at Fiverr volume and price points. The risk of inaccurate drug interaction content is not worth the cost savings.

## Section 2 — Vetting Rubric (Point System)

Each candidate is scored on a 100-point scale. A score of 75 or above is required for contract consideration. A score below 60 disqualifies the candidate regardless of other factors.

### Vetting Rubric

| Category | Criterion | Maximum Points | Scoring Guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Credential** | Verified herbalist or clinical credential | 20 | AHG RH = 20 pts; ND or MD with botanical specialty = 18 pts; Chestnut Professional Herbalist = 16 pts; Herbal Academy Advanced + Clinical = 12 pts; No verifiable credential = 0 pts (disqualifying) |
| **Portfolio Depth** | Quality of medicinal herb writing samples | 20 | Published guide with contraindications + Latin binomials + evidence citations = 20 pts; Blog posts with clinical depth = 14 pts; General wellness writing = 6 pts; No samples = 0 pts (disqualifying) |
| **Contraindication Rigor** | FTC test result + CITES test result | 25 | Perfect score on both screening questions = 25 pts; Correct FTC, partial CITES = 18 pts; Partial FTC, correct CITES = 15 pts; Partial on both = 8 pts; Fails either = 0 pts (disqualifying if FTC failed) |
| **Deadline Commitment** | Explicit confirmation of June 22–August 1 availability | 15 | Confirmed in writing with no hedging = 15 pts; Confirmed with minor schedule caveats = 10 pts; "Probably available" without confirmation = 3 pts; Unavailable = 0 pts (disqualifying) |
| **Scope Consistency** | Demonstrated ability to handle 3-bundle scope (11,000 words, 4–5 weeks) | 12 | Past project of similar scope cited with deliverables = 12 pts; Shorter but similar projects = 8 pts; No comparable prior scope = 3 pts |
| **Preferred Qualifiers** | Etsy/digital product experience; clinical practice; references provided | 8 | All three = 8 pts; Two of three = 5 pts; One = 2 pts; None = 0 pts (not disqualifying) |

**Total: 100 points**

**Threshold decisions**:
- 75–100: Proceed to interview. Candidate is a qualified lead.
- 60–74: Conduct a brief screening call (15 minutes) before committing to a full interview. Low scores in contraindication rigor are harder to recover from than low scores in scope consistency.
- Below 60: Do not proceed. Log rejection reason in the contractor tracking log and move to next candidate.

### Disqualifying Conditions (Immediate Elimination)

A candidate is eliminated immediately — regardless of total score — if any of the following are true:

1. Cannot name any CITES Appendix II listed plant species or explain why cultivation sourcing matters for at-risk herbs
2. Fails the FTC test (rewrites "Elderberry prevents colds" as "may help prevent colds" or similar — the correct answer requires evidence-tier framing, not hedging language)
3. Cannot provide at least one published writing sample with species-specific contraindication content
4. Is unavailable for any part of the June 22 – August 1 sprint window
5. Has no verifiable credential (no AHG RH, no ND, no academic publication record, no professional herbalism school completion certificate that can be cross-referenced)

## Section 3 — Interview Checklist

### Pre-Interview Preparation (15 minutes)

Before the interview call:
- Score the candidate on the vetting rubric using their screening question answers and any portfolio materials they have submitted
- Review their writing sample: check for Latin binomials, contraindication depth, evidence citation format, and FTC language usage
- Note which bundle(s) they are most qualified to write based on their specialty

### Interview Questions (Structured, 45 minutes)

**Opening (5 minutes)**

Introduce the project: Seedwarden produces practitioner-grade medicinal herb guides for Etsy distribution. The Phase 3 sprint runs June 22 through August 1. The contractor scope is three bundles: Respiratory Health (Elderberry, Mullein, Echinacea purpurea/angustifolia, Thyme — 3,600 words), Immunity Support (Echinacea, Ashwagandha, Elderberry, Goldenseal — 3,800 words), and Digestive Support (Dandelion, Calendula, Lemon Balm, Ginger — 3,600 words). Total: 11,000 words across 4–5 weeks.

**Critical Questions — Must Ask All**

**Q1: 5-bundle writing capacity in 4–5 weeks**
"Our scope is 11,000 words across three bundles. At 300–350 words per hour on pre-outlined medicinal herb content, that is approximately 31–37 hours of writing. Can you commit to that pace within the June 22 – August 1 window? What does your weekly writing capacity look like for this sprint?"

Target answer: Confirms 8–10 hours per week availability for the sprint period, or demonstrates a comparable pace from a prior project. Candidates who answer "I write fast" without a concrete hour estimate should be pressed for specifics.

**Q2: Contraindication research rigor standard**
"Our Immunity bundle requires a Goldenseal section with a CITES Appendix II sidebar — verbatim language about why CITES Appendix II listing matters for cultivation sourcing, not trade restriction. It also requires an Ashwagandha section with a thyroid hormone interaction warning at a specific framing level. How would you research and write these sections? What primary sources would you use?"

Target answer: Mentions NatMed Pro (Natural Medicines database) or Memorial Sloan Kettering Integrative Medicine herb database as primary contraindication sources. Can explain the difference between CITES trade regulation and the cultivation sourcing message it creates for Goldenseal guides. Candidates who say they would "Google it" or reference only one general source fail this question.

**Q3: Deadlines non-negotiable confirmation**
"The Respiratory bundle delivers July 1, Immunity July 8, Digestive July 20. These dates align with our Etsy upload schedule and cannot slip without cascading all upload dates. If you take on this contract and find yourself running behind on the Respiratory bundle by June 28, what do you do?"

Target answer: Proactively escalates at least 3 days before a deadline, not the day of. Has a clear process for communicating status — not just delivering late. Candidates who treat late delivery as primarily a writing quality trade-off (slower = better) rather than a project management failure are a risk signal.

**Q4: Pricing flexibility for scope expansion**
"Our scope is currently three bundles. If the project performs well and we want to add a fourth bundle — Sleep and Nervines (Valerian, Passionflower, Lemon Balm, Lavender, 3,500 words) — in early August, what would that look like in terms of rate and timeline?"

Target answer: Provides a specific rate for the fourth bundle (not "we can discuss"). This tests whether the candidate has a consistent internal rate logic. A candidate who quotes $300 per bundle for three bundles and then quotes $500 for the fourth is showing inconsistent pricing. A candidate who quotes proportionally (same per-word rate) is showing disciplined scope management.

**Q5: FTC compliance familiarity**
"Can you give me an example of a therapeutic claim you have had to reframe for FTC compliance in a consumer-facing educational guide? What was the original claim and what was your rewrite?"

Target answer: Gives a specific example. The rewrite should demonstrate evidence-tier framing (citing research type, not just hedging with "may"). A candidate who cannot give a specific example has not written FTC-compliant herb content at the level this project requires.

**Q6: Work-for-hire and confidentiality**
"The contract will include a work-for-hire clause — Seedwarden retains all intellectual property rights on delivery. The content will not carry your byline unless we agree otherwise. Content must be kept confidential before launch. Are those terms acceptable?"

Target answer: Direct yes. Any hesitation about IP terms or a counter-offer for byline credit should be clarified and documented before the contract is signed.

**Closing (5 minutes)**

Ask for references: "Can you provide one reference from a previous educational writing client — someone who can speak to your ability to meet deadlines and produce accurate botanical content?" Log reference name and contact method.

Ask for timeline to contract: "If we move forward, we would want to sign a contract and pay the 25% deposit by June 12 at the latest. Does that timeline work for you?"

## Section 4 — Rate Benchmarking and Negotiation

### Market Rate Context

**Credentialed herbalist writer rates (US market, 2026)**:
- AHG Registered Herbalist: $60–$100/hour for clinical content; $40–$65/hour for educational content
- ND with botanical specialty: $75–$120/hour for clinical content; $50–$80/hour for educational content
- Chestnut Professional Herbalist / advanced certification: $35–$60/hour for educational content
- Herbal Academy Advanced + Clinical alumni: $25–$45/hour depending on experience depth

**At flat-rate for the 3-bundle scope (31–37 writing hours)**:
- AHG RH at $40/hr flat-rate: $1,240–$1,480 (above ceiling)
- AHG RH at $35/hr equivalent: $1,085–$1,295 (within ceiling)
- Chestnut Herbalist at $32/hr equivalent: $992–$1,184 (within target range)
- Mid-credential at $28–$32/hr: $868–$1,184 (at lower bound)

**Target range**: $1,000–$1,350 for full 3-bundle scope  
**Hard ceiling**: $1,500 for exceptional credential (AHG RH + clinical practice + published monograph)  
**Floor**: Do not go below $900 for an unverified credential or a first-time medicinal herb writing project

### Commission Analysis (Upwork and Toptal)

**Upwork fees (client-side)**:
- Upwork charges a 5% fee to clients on US-based contracts as of 2026
- A contractor quoting $1,200 on Upwork = $1,260 true cost to the project
- Maximum budget on Upwork to stay within $1,350 ceiling: quote the contractor $1,286 or less

**Toptal fees**:
- Toptal charges 10–15% above the contractor rate as a placement/matching fee
- A contractor billing $1,100 through Toptal = $1,210–$1,265 true cost
- Toptal is appropriate only if Channels 1–5 fail to produce a qualified candidate by June 12

**Direct (off-platform) rate advantage**: Candidates sourced through AHG directory, Chestnut School, or Herbal Academy alumni can be engaged directly with no platform commission. This allows the full $1,000–$1,350 budget to go to the contractor, which makes the project more competitive than a platform listing at the same headline rate.

### Negotiation Framing

**Opening position**: Present the scope as a flat-rate engagement with milestone payments. Do not offer hourly unless the contractor specifically requests it. Hourly billing creates open-ended exposure if writing pace is slower than estimated.

**Payment structure to offer**:
- 25% on contract signing (deposit)
- 25% on delivery of Respiratory first draft (approximately July 1)
- 25% on delivery of Immunity first draft (approximately July 8)
- 25% on delivery of Digestive final draft, all revisions complete (approximately July 20)

**When candidate quotes above ceiling ($1,500+)**:
"Our budget for this engagement is $1,000–$1,350 for the three-bundle scope. We are not able to go above $1,350 for this contract. However, if the project performs well, we are planning a fourth bundle (Sleep and Nervines, similar scope) in early August with the same payment structure, which would bring the total engagement to $1,350–$1,800 for four bundles. Would you be willing to take the three-bundle contract at $1,350 with the understanding that we will discuss the fourth bundle separately?"

**When candidate quotes below floor ($800–)**: Probe why. A credentialed herbalist writer quoting below $800 for 11,000 words of clinical botanical content is either underestimating the work, misunderstanding the scope, or does not have the contraindication depth the project requires. Re-administer the screening questions before proceeding.

**Scope expansion pricing**: If the user decides to expand to Women's Health or Sleep bundles during the sprint, the negotiated per-word rate from the original contract sets the baseline. Do not renegotiate the rate mid-sprint — only the total scope changes.
