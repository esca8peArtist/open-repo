# Seedwarden Phase 3 Product Expansion Roadmap

**Phase 3 activates in July 2026** — approximately 60 days after Phase 1 launch. The gate condition is 30+ days of live Etsy conversion data. This document provides the strategy behind each expansion decision: which product categories, which launch order, how pricing evolves, which customer cohorts drive what, and what success looks like in numbers.

This roadmap covers Month 3 (July 2026) through Month 8 (December 2026). The detailed per-product specifications live in `phase-3-product-specifications.json`.

---

## Part 1: Strategic Context — Why These Categories

Phase 1 launched 21 products covering the full homesteading spectrum: gardening, seed saving, food preservation, foraging, livestock, hunting, and community organizing. Phase 3 does not start from scratch. Every Phase 3 product category was chosen because it deepens an existing strength or fills a gap that Phase 1 data is expected to reveal.

The four customer cohorts defined in `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md` each have different expansion vectors:

- **High-intent forager** (20–25% of customers, 4–6 purchases/year): wants more species coverage, regional depth, and identification tools. This cohort is responsible for the foraging-focused Phase 3 products: the Wild Edibles Quick Reference, Native Plants flashcards, regional listing variants, and the Medicinal Herb Guide.
- **Survival prepper** (15–20% of customers, 2–3 purchases/year, $45–$100 AOV): buys in volume and responds to bundle depth. The Master Preserver Bundle ($52) and the Expanded Homesteader Gift Set ($62) are designed for this cohort's bulk-purchase pattern. Event-driven purchasing means they often return during news cycles without needing re-acquisition.
- **Homesteader** (30–35% of customers, the highest-LTV cohort at $60 net/24 months per customer): project-driven and incremental. The Seed Library Organization System and Medicinal Herb Guide speak directly to the "what do I build next?" mindset. The Homestead Skills Assessment and Learning Roadmap is the gateway product designed specifically to guide homesteaders through the full catalog.
- **Gift buyer** (15–20% of customers, 1–2 purchases/year): needs high-perceived-value bundles and holiday positioning. The Expanded Homesteader Gift Set at $62 is the Phase 3 anchor product for this cohort, launching October 15 ahead of the holiday window.

### Which Phase 1 Data Triggers Phase 3

Phase 3 product selection is conditional on Phase 1 data signals. The following Phase 1 outcomes activate specific Phase 3 responses:

| Phase 1 Signal | Phase 3 Response |
|---|---|
| Native Plants Regional Guide in top 3 converting products | Accelerate regional listing batch (P3-05 to P3-11) to Month 3 |
| Preservation products (Harvest Preservation, Fermented Harvest) generating >20% of revenue | Launch all 4 preservation derivatives in July as planned; do not delay |
| Forager cohort shows repeat purchase within 60 days | Prioritize Wild Edibles Quick Reference and Flashcard Set |
| Prepper cohort buying bundles at 40%+ of prepper revenue | Launch Master Preserver Bundle in Month 4; add expanded gift bundle for October |
| Gift buyer cohort shows holiday-window spike (October data) | Confirm Expanded Homesteader Gift Set for October 15 launch |
| Any Phase 1 product converting below 0.5% after 45 days | Pause Phase 3 expansion; fix listing copy and mockups first |

The last row matters: Phase 3 should not expand a catalog that is not converting. If overall Phase 1 conversion is below 0.5% (below average for digital products on Etsy), the first action is listing optimization — not new SKU launches.

## Part 2: Product Categories and Sequencing

### Month 3 (July 2026): Preservation Season + Regional Expansion

July is the highest-leverage Phase 3 launch window. Two forces converge: preservation season peaks (canning, fermentation, and dehydrating searches spike in July–August) and the early-summer foraging window (wild edibles identification interest peaks June–September). Both can be served with minimal new content creation.

**Category A: Preservation Sub-Niche Derivatives (4 products)**

These derive directly from existing Phase 1 content. They require 4–7 hours of editing and PDF generation per product, not original research.

- Beginner Canning Quick-Start Guide ($9): Entry-point canning guide for buyers who find the full Harvest Preservation Manual (at $16) intimidating. Expands the canning section of that manual into 35 standalone pages. First-purchase gateway product targeting "beginner canning guide" — a high-volume, moderate-competition keyword.
- Fermentation Starter Kit Guide ($8): Isolates and expands the lacto-fermentation section from the Fermented Harvest Handbook. Covers five first ferments (sauerkraut, kimchi, pickles, fermented hot sauce, brined vegetables) for buyers who want vegetables-only fermentation, not the full preservation spectrum.
- Dehydrating and Drying Field Guide ($11): Expands the dehydrating section of the Harvest Preservation Manual into a standalone 40-page reference. Dehydrating is underserved on Etsy relative to canning. The audience — backpackers, off-grid homesteaders, food-storage preppers — is adjacent to Seedwarden's core buyers but converts to more specific keyword searches.
- Pressure Canning Meat and Poultry Guide ($13): Merges the meat-handling sections of Meat/Fish Preservation Manual with the pressure canning methods in Harvest Preservation Manual. Addresses a genuine safety gap: most Etsy canning guides avoid meat because of safety complexity. Seedwarden's audience (hunters, livestock raisers) needs exactly this. Low competition.

**Category B: Regional Listing Variants (14 new listings)**

Regional variants create new keyword surfaces without new content. The existing Native Plants Regional Guide and Survival Garden Regional Plans each cover multiple US regions in a single PDF. Listing each region as a separate Etsy product generates region-specific titles and tags (e.g., "Pacific Northwest foraging guide," "Texas survival garden plan") that the single combined listing cannot capture.

This strategy is already proven by the Survival Garden Regional Plans structure (documented in `product-audit-2026-04-11.md`). Phase 3 expands it to both major multi-region products:

- 7 Survival Garden regional listings at $5.99/region (TX, SE, NE, Midwest, PNW, Mountain West, Mid-Atlantic)
- 7 Native Plants regional listings at $12/region (NE, SE, Midwest, Great Plains, SW, PNW, Mountain West)

All 14 listings deliver the same complete PDF as the parent product. The pricing differential ($5.99 vs. $22 for Survival Garden; $12 vs. $18 for Native Plants) is intentional: it makes the complete bundle look like a deep discount by comparison, and it serves buyers who only need one region at a lower commitment price.

Development time per regional listing: 1–1.5 hours (title, tags, description, mockup variant). Total for all 14: ~19 hours.

**Month 3 Wild Edibles Product:**

- Wild Edibles Quick Reference ($10): 22-page visual identification guide using the 18 wild-edibles habit photos completed in Session 560 (all CC BY-SA licensed from Wikimedia Commons). One page per species: habit photo, common name, species name, 3 identification features, harvest season, edible parts, preparation method. Launches July 15 into peak foraging season. This is the only Month 3 product that requires layout-intensive new work (10 hours); all other Month 3 products are editing-of-existing-content.

### Month 4 (August 2026): Bundle Launches + Photo Pack

August is the peak of preservation season. Product launches in August are shorter-lead additions that compound the July preservation push.

- Preservation Season Starter Bundle ($22): Bundles the three beginner preservation derivatives (Beginner Canning + Fermentation Starter + Dehydrating Guide). Individual total $28, bundle saves $6. The bundle conversion mechanic is not the dollar savings — it is the framing: "Complete beginner's preservation bundle for the 2026 harvest season" answers the question "where do I start?" for new buyers.
- Master Preserver Bundle ($52): Seven preservation products including all three Phase 1 preservation manuals plus Grow Your Own Hot Sauce plus the three July derivatives. Individual total $90; saves $38 (42%). This is the high-ticket gift and committed-buyer product. The $38 savings figure is the primary conversion driver — the number reads as substantial.
- Wild Edibles Habitat Photo Pack ($14): All 18 wild-edibles habit photos delivered as a ZIP (18 JPEG files + ATTRIBUTION.txt + README.txt explaining CC BY-SA license terms). Audience: bloggers, homeschool curriculum creators, foraging instructors, and content creators who need licensed botanical photography without stock-photo costs. This is a niche product — not expected to be a top revenue driver — but it has zero additional development work and serves a legitimate buyer need.
- Native Plants Identification Flashcard Set ($12): 40+ printable identification cards (3x5" format) derived from the Native Plants Regional Guide. Audience: teachers, homeschoolers, nature journalers, foraging instructors. Design-intensive (14 hours) due to card layout work. Can slip to September without consequence.

### Month 5 (September 2026): Adjacent Categories

September brings seed-saving season (gardeners transitioning from harvest to saving) and the early fall gift-browsing window. Month 5 products move into adjacent categories that stretch the catalog beyond pure field manuals.

- Seed Library Setup and Organization System ($14): Covers what happens after seed saving — building a home seed library. Includes 10 printable templates (envelope labels, catalog cards, germination log, viability chart, variety description card, trade/swap sheet). This addresses the "I saved the seeds but now what?" gap that the existing Seed Saving Field Manual does not fill. Serves the homesteader and forager cohorts; launches September 1 at the peak of seed-saving season.
- Medicinal Herb Growing and Harvest Guide ($14): 56-page guide covering 20 commonly grown medicinal herbs from growing through processing and storage. No medical claims — the guide covers cultivation and preservation only, consistent with Seedwarden's educational-reference tone. This is the only genuinely new-research product in Phase 3 (12 hours of writing; requires Wikimedia Commons image sourcing for 20 herb photos). Launches September 15. If Phase 1 data shows no interest in botanical-adjacent products, deprioritize.
- Regional Forager Bundle ($26): Native Plants Regional Guide ($18) + Wild Edibles Quick Reference ($10). Shallow 7% discount — framed as convenience bundling, not deep savings. Consider deepening to $24 (14% off) if initial conversion is low.
- Homestead Skills Assessment and Learning Roadmap ($10): The catalog navigation product. A self-assessment and 12-month learning pathway covering 100 homesteading skills across 8 categories. Every section cross-references specific Seedwarden products at the relevant skill level. This is simultaneously a standalone value product and the most powerful cross-sell driver in the Phase 3 catalog. Priced at $10 to be a low-friction second purchase for any first-time buyer.

### Month 6 (October 2026): Gift Season Positioning

October's two launches finalize Phase 3 and set up the November/December revenue peak.

- First-Year Food Preservation Planner ($12): 58-page printable planner organized around the preservation calendar (July through February). Monthly sections with harvest tracker, preservation goals, supplies checklists, batch log, and next-year notes. Designed as a tactile, journal-like product that buyers print and bind — this gives it higher gift potential than a reference manual. Launches October 7.
- The Homesteader's Complete Reference Set ($62): Seven-guide expanded gift bundle (four from existing Phase 1 Homesteader's Complete Bundle plus Wild Edibles Quick Reference, Medicinal Herb Guide, and Homestead Skills Roadmap). Individual total $106; saves $44 (41%). This is the holiday anchor product — the most complete and highest-priced item in the Phase 3 catalog. Launches October 15 to capture the early gift-browsing window documented in `marketing/annual-product-plan.md`.

## Part 3: Pricing Strategy

### Phase 3 Pricing vs. Phase 1

Phase 1 pricing ($5–$22) was set conservatively for launch. The etsy-seo-market-research.md notes that Seedwarden is likely underpriced relative to content depth — competitors charge $15–$25 for guides with one-third the content. Phase 3 tests price increases on three products and holds mid-tier pricing ($10–$14) for new additions.

Phase 1 products remain at their existing prices during Month 3–4. Phase 3 introduces three price tests on August 15:
- Native Plants Regional Guide: $18 → $22 (rationale: individual regional listings at $12 create favorable contrast — the complete 9-region guide at $22 is clearly the better value)
- Survival Garden Regional Plans: $22 → $24 (individual regions at $5.99 imply $41.93 total, making $24 for the bundle read as a 43% discount)
- Hunting, Fishing and Trapping Manual: $20 → $22 (most specialized, lowest-competition product; specialist buyers tolerate higher prices)

Revert trigger for all three: conversion rate drops more than 25% vs. 30-day July baseline. Decision date: September 28 — before October 1 holiday season launch.

### Phase 3 New Product Pricing Rationale

Phase 3 new products are deliberately priced in the $8–$14 mid-tier, not the $16–$22 premium tier:
- Entry derivatives ($8–$9): Beginner Canning, Fermentation Starter. Entry price for keyword-rich, high-volume search terms. Gateway products that lead to $38–$52 bundle upgrades.
- Mid-tier new products ($10–$14): All other Phase 3 originals. This range is below the "significant purchase decision" threshold for the homesteader and forager cohorts — the same logic that made the $5 Companion Planting Chart a high-converting entry product applies here.
- Bundle anchors ($22–$62): The three new Phase 3 bundles plus the expanded gift set. High perceived savings (21%–42% off individual totals) drive AOV lift.

Target margin for all Phase 3 digital products: 85%+ after Etsy fees (~15% combined). All Phase 3 products are digital PDFs with no COGS beyond development hours; marginal cost per sale is $0. Effective gross margin on a $12 product after Etsy fees: approximately $10.20 (85%).

### Seasonal Pricing Calendar

| Period | Action |
|---|---|
| July 1 | Launch preservation derivatives and regional listings at full price |
| August 1 | Launch Preservation Season Starter Bundle ($22) |
| August 15 | Price increase tests go live (Native Plants, Survival Garden, Hunting Manual) |
| August 15–31 | 15% preservation season sale to email list only (not public Etsy sale) |
| October 15 | Expanded Homesteader Gift Set live; begin gift positioning across all bundles |
| November 27 | Black Friday 20% storewide sale (aligns with annual-product-plan.md) |

## Part 4: Supplier Sourcing

Seedwarden is digital-only. There is no physical inventory, no MOQ, and no supplier in the traditional sense. "Sourcing" means: platforms, tools, and content-input sources.

**Primary Platform: Etsy**
- Cost per new listing: $0.20 listing fee for 4 months + 6.5% transaction fee + 3%+$0.25 payment processing
- Phase 3 total listing fees for 26 new SKUs: approximately $5.20 for the first 4-month period
- Lead time to live: same-day
- No approval delays for digital PDF listings

**Email Platform: Kit (ConvertKit)**
- Free tier (up to 1,000 subscribers) is adequate through Month 5. Upgrade to Creator plan ($25/month) when subscribers exceed 1,000 or when Phase 3 behavioral-segment automations are needed
- Phase 3 adds three new post-purchase email paths: preservation buyer, foraging buyer, seed-saving buyer

**Design and Mockup: Canva**
- Canva Pro ($13/month): required for batch mockup generation and Pinterest pin creation
- Phase 3 batch: 26 tablet mockups, 26 phone mockups, 78 Pinterest pin variants (3 per new product)
- Estimated 8–10 hours of batch Canva work across the Phase 3 launch window

**Image Sourcing: Wikimedia Commons**
- Wild Edibles Quick Reference (P3-19): 18 habit photos already downloaded and logged in WORKLOG.md. Zero additional sourcing cost.
- Medicinal Herb Guide (P3-23): 20 herb photos needed. Wikimedia Commons search protocol already established from Sessions 560 and earlier. Estimated 2 hours sourcing time.
- All Wikimedia Commons images are CC BY-SA 3.0 or 4.0. Attribution page required in all derivative PDFs — this is a hard requirement before publication.

**Content Sources: Existing Catalog**
All preservation derivatives (P3-01 through P3-04), regional listing variants (P3-05 through P3-18), and the Seed Library System (P3-22) derive from existing Phase 1 product files. Zero external research vendors needed for these. The only products requiring new external research are the Medicinal Herb Guide (P3-23) and the Wild Edibles Quick Reference layout work (P3-19).

**COGS Targets:**
Since all products are digital, COGS is expressed in development-hours cost at an imputed rate of $25/hour (the opportunity cost floor for a solo operator):
- Preservation derivatives: $125–$175/product (5–7 hours). COGS recovers in 14–20 sales at $9–$13 price points.
- Regional listings: $25–$38/listing (1–1.5 hours). COGS recovers in 5–7 sales at $5.99–$12 price points.
- Original new-content products (P3-22, P3-23, P3-24, P3-25): $200–$300/product (8–12 hours). COGS recovers in 15–25 sales.

## Part 5: Customer Feedback Integration

Phase 1 generates three types of data that inform Phase 3 product selection:

**1. Etsy listing analytics (views, conversion rate, revenue per listing)**
The top 3–5 converting listings by Month 2 (June 2026) indicate which categories and price points are working. If preservation products dominate, accelerate the preservation derivative launches. If the foraging/regional guides dominate, front-load the regional listing batch and the Wild Edibles Quick Reference.

**2. Customer cohort survey responses**
The post-purchase survey (documented in `customer-cohort-analysis-framework.md`) asks buyers to self-identify: foraging, prepping, homesteading, or gift-giving. By Month 2, 20–40 survey responses should identify which cohort is overperforming the pre-launch model. The cohort with the highest repeat purchase rate within 60 days gets Phase 3 product investment priority.

**3. Etsy message and review content**
Buyers who contact a seller often reveal unmet needs directly. Common patterns in the homesteading/foraging niche: "Does this cover [specific region]?" (signals demand for regional specificity), "Is there a beginner version?" (signals entry-product gap), "Does this include [specific technique]?" (signals sub-niche derivative opportunity). Every Etsy message received in Months 1–2 should be logged in a simple notes doc — this qualitative data complements the quantitative conversion data.

**Decision rule: If a Phase 3 product's triggering signal does not appear in Phase 1 data, that product moves to Phase 4.** The Medicinal Herb Guide (P3-23) is the most conditional product in Phase 3. If Phase 1 data shows no demand for botanical-adjacent products (no forager cohort signal, no native plants purchases), deprioritize and reallocate the 12 development hours elsewhere.

## Part 9: Competitive Differentiation

Phase 3 products differentiate from Phase 1 in two structural ways:

**From comprehensive to specific.** Phase 1 products are comprehensive field manuals (50–200+ pages). Phase 3 derivatives are specific: the Beginner Canning Guide addresses one problem for one audience. This specificity matches the buyer-search pattern on Etsy (people search for "beginner canning guide," not "complete preservation library") and creates more keyword surfaces per hour of development.

**From national to regional.** The regional listing variants are Phase 3's highest-ROI product category: they create 14 new Etsy listings from two existing PDFs in approximately 19 hours of work. Each listing captures region-specific search traffic (e.g., "Texas survival garden plan," "Pacific Northwest foraging guide") that the generic national listing cannot appear for. Etsy's personalization layer actively shows region-relevant results to buyers — this is a structural platform advantage for regional variants.

**Phase 3 vs. competitors on Etsy.** The competitive analysis from `etsy-seo-market-research.md` identifies two competitor failure modes that Phase 3 exploits:

1. Most preservation competitors sell comprehensive books or thin checklists. The 30–50 page derivative guide (positioned as a practical, intermediate reference) is underserved.
2. Most foraging and regional garden competitors sell generic national guides. Regional specificity with honest depth (not just "zone 7 plants: tomatoes, peppers, squash") differentiates Seedwarden at the listing-title level, which is the highest-weight Etsy search factor.

Phase 3 does not introduce physical products, services, or subscriptions. All Phase 3 products remain digital PDFs or ZIP packages, maintaining the zero-marginal-cost, high-margin structure that makes digital products viable at Seedwarden's current scale.

## Part 10: Risk Management

**Risk 1: Phase 1 conversion below 0.5%**
Signal: Fewer than 20 sales in the first 45 days. Response: Pause Phase 3 development. Redirect hours to Phase 1 listing optimization (mockup images, title rewrites, tag refinement). Do not expand a catalog that is not converting.

**Risk 2: Social media traffic fails to materialize by Month 3**
Signal: Etsy stats show less than 20% of traffic from social sources by July 1. Response: Phase 3 timeline adjusts — launch only the two highest organic-SEO-value products (Beginner Canning Guide and regional listing batch) which can rank on Etsy search without social traffic. Deprioritize the photo pack and flashcard set.

**Risk 3: Price increase on Phase 1 products damages conversion**
Signal: 30-day comparison (August 15 to September 15) shows conversion drop of more than 25% on affected products. Response: Revert all three price increases by September 28 before the holiday season. Regional variants remain live at their lower price points — they protect entry conversion even if the premium complete-guide listing is reverted.

**Risk 4: Development overload collapses the timeline**
Phase 3 requires 125–140 hours of development across 16 weeks — approximately 9 hours/week. This is sustainable for a solo operator only if content editing and listing creation are batched efficiently. Mitigation: do all regional listing creation in a single 19-hour batch session, not distributed across weeks. Generate all July PDFs before creating any listings. Create all Canva mockups in one session after all PDFs are finalized.

## Part 11: Execution Timeline Options

The task brief calls for post-Phase-1 decision-making flexibility. The four options below are not guesses — they are structured responses to specific Phase 1 outcome scenarios. Select the option that matches the Phase 1 signal set observed at Week 2–3.

---

### Option A: Conservative (Phase 1 underwhelms)

**Trigger condition**: Phase 1 reaches fewer than 20 sales in the first 45 days, or overall conversion rate below 0.5% after 30 days.

**Logic**: Do not expand a catalog that is not converting. Every development hour spent on Phase 3 products has an opportunity cost. If Phase 1 is underperforming, the root cause is almost certainly listing quality (mockup images, title copy, keyword tags) — not catalog breadth. Fixing Phase 1 listings returns 3–5x more revenue per hour than launching new Phase 3 products.

**What launches**: Regional listing variants only (P3-05 to P3-18). These are the sole exception because they require no new content and add keyword surface area with minimal risk — they live or die on Etsy search rank, not on conversion optimization of existing listings.

**What pauses**: All 12 new Phase 3 content products. Development does not begin until Phase 1 reaches 0.75%+ conversion on at least 5 listings.

**Timeline adjustment**: Phase 3 content launch shifts to Month 5–6 (September–October) rather than Month 3. The October gift set and preservation planner still hit the holiday window because those were already the last scheduled launches.

**Revenue impact**: Conservative scenario targets (from `phase-3-product-specifications.json`) — approximately $900–$1,100/month gross in the July–August window rather than $1,100–$1,800. The gap comes from missing the preservation season peak with underdeveloped new products.

**Decision date**: July 1 — 45 days post-Phase-1 launch. If conversion is below 0.5%, activate Option A. If it exceeds 0.5%, move to Option B or C.

---

### Option B: Standard (Phase 1 converts at expected range)

**Trigger condition**: Phase 1 reaches 20–50 sales in the first 45 days, overall conversion rate 0.5%–1.5%, no single category overwhelmingly dominant.

**Logic**: Proceed with the Phase 3 plan as written in Parts 2 and 7. This is the baseline scenario; all dates and development hours in Part 7 assume Option B.

**What launches**: All products on their planned schedule — regional listings July 1, preservation derivatives July 15, Wild Edibles Quick Reference July 15, then bundled progression through October.

**Development priority order**: Regional listings (highest ROI/hour) → preservation derivatives (seasonal window dependency) → foraging products (mid-season) → original new-content products (no hard seasonal urgency except Medicinal Herb Guide in September).

**Timeline**: 125–140 development hours over 16 weeks (July–October). Approximately 9 hours/week.

**Decision date**: No special decision needed — proceed per Part 7. Review at August 28 analytics check to determine if any products need listing revision before Option B continues into Month 5.

### Option C: Aggressive (Phase 1 overperforms)

**Trigger condition**: Phase 1 reaches 50+ sales in the first 45 days, conversion rate above 1.5% on multiple products, or one cohort (forager, prepper, or homesteader) visibly overrepresented in purchases.

**Logic**: When Phase 1 proves strong demand, Phase 3 should front-load its highest-cohort-match products rather than following the seasonal-calendar sequence. The goal is to capture repeat purchases from the overperforming cohort before they find a competitor.

**What changes from Standard**:
- Compress July development into 2 weeks instead of 4: hire a Canva contractor for mockup batch (estimated $200–$300 for 26 mockups at $8–$12/mockup), freeing the operator for content writing.
- If forager cohort overperforms: prioritize Wild Edibles Quick Reference and Native Plants Flashcard Set before preservation derivatives. Launch both by July 8 instead of July 15 and August 21.
- If prepper cohort overperforms: move Master Preserver Bundle to July 21 rather than August 15. The bundle requires no new products — it is a listing that references already-developed products.
- If homesteader cohort overperforms: move Homestead Skills Assessment forward to August 1 instead of September 28. This is the cross-sell multiplier — getting it live sooner compounds every other product's AOV.

**Timeline**: Same 125–140 hours total but compressed into 10–12 weeks rather than 16. Requires 12–14 hours/week during the peak July–August window.

**Revenue target**: Optimistic scenario — $1,900/month gross by July, $2,500 by August, $3,800 by October.

**Decision date**: June 15 (2 weeks after Phase 1 launch, before Phase 3 development begins). If 14-day conversion rate is above 1.5% on 3+ products, activate Option C.

### Option D: Focused Single-Cohort (one cohort dominates)

**Trigger condition**: Phase 1 data at Week 3 shows one cohort accounting for 50%+ of purchases. This is the highest-signal scenario — it means Phase 3 can optimize for depth in one direction rather than breadth across all four cohorts.

**Logic**: Cohort concentration in Phase 1 is rare (the product mix was specifically designed to spread across cohorts) but possible if a single product goes viral on one platform or a niche community links to one specific guide. If it happens, responding with cohort-specific product depth is higher-return than the full 26-SKU spread.

**Forager-dominant scenario**: Launch Wild Edibles Quick Reference, Native Plants Flashcards, Medicinal Herb Guide, and 7 Native Plants regional listings in July (cohort-focused batch). Defer all preservation derivatives and Survival Garden regional listings to Month 5–6.

**Prepper-dominant scenario**: Launch all 4 preservation derivatives, 7 Survival Garden regional listings, and the Master Preserver Bundle in July. Defer foraging products and the Medicinal Herb Guide until Phase 1 forager signal appears.

**Homesteader-dominant scenario**: Front-load Seed Library Organization System and Homestead Skills Assessment and Learning Roadmap to July (both can be developed in 18 hours). These two products cross-sell the entire existing catalog and generate AOV lift faster than any other Phase 3 products. Defer foraging and preservation derivatives to August.

**Gift-buyer-dominant scenario**: This scenario is unlikely in May–June (off-season for gift buying). If it appears in October Phase 1 data, the Expanded Homesteader Gift Set should launch as early as October 7 rather than October 15 to capture extra weeks of holiday window.

**Timeline**: Same total development hours, but 60–70% concentrated in the dominant-cohort product cluster in Month 3–4. Less context-switching means faster per-product output.

**Revenue target**: Between moderate and optimistic scenarios depending on cohort, because the concentrated product depth drives higher repeat purchase rates from the dominant cohort (estimated 25–35% 90-day repeat rate vs. the 15% standard baseline).

**Decision date**: Week 3 post-Phase-1 launch. If any cohort exceeds 45% of observed purchases, activate Option D. If no single cohort dominates, revert to Option B or C.
