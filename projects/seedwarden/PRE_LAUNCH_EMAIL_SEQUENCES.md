### B2 — Day 3

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Subject | Evidence tiers: what Gold, Silver, and Bronze actually mean in practice |
| Preview text | Not all "studies show" claims are equal. Here is how we label the difference. |
| CTA button text | See the full guide sample |
| CTA button URL | `[ETSY_PRACTITIONER_BUNDLE_URL]?utm_source=kit&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=practitioner_tier&utm_content=email2` |

**Body**:

One of the clinical community's legitimate frustrations with herbal reference materials is the undifferentiated evidence claim. "Studies suggest Valerian may support sleep" tells you nothing about whether those studies were placebo-controlled, how large they were, or whether the finding replicated.

Seedwarden's evidence tiers:

**Gold**: At least one peer-reviewed randomized controlled trial (RCT) with statistically significant results. Primary sources cited by name (journal, year, sample size). Example: Valerian (*Valeriana officinalis*) — Gold for sleep latency reduction in primary insomnia (multiple RCTs, n=100+ in at least two independent trials).

**Silver**: Observational studies, systematic reviews without RCTs, or pharmacological mechanism studies. The evidence is real and peer-reviewed — the trial design is not controlled. Example: Lemon Balm (*Melissa officinalis*) — Silver for anxiety and sleep; observational cohort data consistent but no large RCT as of 2025.

**Bronze**: Traditional use with historical consistency but no modern clinical trial. Referenced to traditional materia medica sources and ethnobotanical documentation. Example: Mugwort (*Artemisia vulgaris*) — Bronze for dream vividness / sleep; traditional Eclectic and European use, no controlled clinical trial data.

Why this matters in practice: when a patient asks "is there evidence for this?", your answer is different for each tier. Gold means you can cite studies. Silver means you can say "the observational data is consistent." Bronze means you are recommending based on traditional use with transparency about the evidence level.

This is the system. It works in clinical settings because it matches the real landscape of herbal evidence — not everything is RCT-proven, and pretending otherwise is its own kind of problem.
