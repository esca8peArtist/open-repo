## Message Tuning by Response Type

### If prospect says: "What exactly do you need?"
**Reply**: Be very specific. Choose ONE of these three and offer flexibility:

```
I can be flexible on format. Pick what works best for you:

1. **A formal review** (500–1,000 words) evaluating our approach against [their expertise area]
2. **An endorsement quote** (150–300 words) for our launch marketing
3. **A featured collaboration** (we co-write a section on [topic])

Any of these works for our June 22 launch credibility.

Which resonates?
```

### If prospect says: "Interesting but I'm swamped right now"
**Reply**: Reduce friction. Offer time-saving option:

```
I understand — your time is valuable. How about a quick async approach?

I'll send you a draft review (that I write in your voice, based on interviews with you). You edit + approve. 
This way you get the credit without starting from scratch. 

Does 30 min of editing work better than 3 hours of writing?
```

### If prospect says: "Not aligned with my current projects"
**Reply**: Reframe. Use different angle:

```
I hear you. One different angle:

Your audience [describe their audience] is exactly who needs this guide. Even if you're not available for a formal review, would a quick quote be possible? ("Seedwarden's approach to [topic] meets [their standard]")

Just 100–150 words. This helps your followers without derailing your projects.

Interested?
```

### If prospect says: "Who else is endorsing this?"
**Reply**: Build social proof narrative (tactfully):

```
Great question. Right now we're starting with [name of anyone who has said yes or even expressed interest].

We want to build credibility thoughtfully — starting with practitioners whose standards align with ours, not mass-endorsements.

That's why we're reaching out to [their specific expertise area] — you're the standard we want to match.

Does that philosophy resonate?
```
