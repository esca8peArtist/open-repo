# Phase 3 Medicinal Herbs — Writer Load Model

**Prepared**: June 5, 2026
**Total writing scope**: 56–66 adjusted hours (18,300 words; adjusted for shared-species efficiency)
**Decision point**: June 19 (Phase 2 Day 14 checkpoint GO/NO-GO) → execution model confirmed by June 21
**Sprint start**: June 22, 2026

## Executive Summary

Three writer paths can deliver Phase 3 medicinal herbs content. The choice depends on the user's available hours per week, budget, and risk tolerance for content quality on the technically complex sections. All three paths deliver the full 5-bundle suite by August 3 with the recommended model.

The critical constraint is not total hours — it is the concentration of complex writing in Week 1. The Women's Health bundle (14–16 hours) contains the two most research-dense species in Phase 3 (Black Cohosh at 700 words with Cherokee framing and conservation sidebar; Vitex at 600 words with invasive-watch note and dopaminergic mechanism). Any writer, regardless of path, must handle these sections with care.

**Recommendation**: Hybrid path. The user handles all narrative, historical framing, and contraindications sections (the highest-liability content). A contractor handles the first drafts of cultivation specifications and active-constituent descriptions (the most technical, least liability-sensitive content). This splits the work approximately 50/50, delivers the full suite in 4–5 weeks at roughly $700–990 in contractor cost, and keeps the user's cognitive load at a level compatible with simultaneously monitoring Track B launch metrics through June 19.

If no qualified contractor is available by June 19, default to the solo path at 12 hours/week (not 10). The difference between 10 and 12 hours/week is the difference between a 6–7 week timeline (full launch August 17–24) and a 5–5.5 week timeline (full launch August 3–10). Given that the target herbalist-season window is pre-fall (August–September), the 12 hours/week solo pace is the minimum to hit that window cleanly.

## Section 1: Understanding the Writing Load

Before comparing models, it is important to understand what the 56–66 adjusted hours actually covers and where the cognitive load concentrates.

### What the hours include

Every bundle's hour estimate includes: research integration (pulling from compiled outlines and source materials), first draft writing, contraindications verification (cross-checking drug interactions and pregnancy cautions against current herbalism references), FTC compliance review (every therapeutic claim must use qualifying language — "traditionally used for" not "treats"), and one self-edit pass.

What they do not include: Canva design (tracked separately at 12.5 hours), Etsy upload administration (tracked separately at ~10 hours), or photography (pre-sprint track, already planned).

### Where cognitive demand concentrates

Not all hours are equal. The Phase 3 content has three distinct cognitive modes:

**Mode 1 — Research-dense narrative (highest cognitive load)**: These sections require synthesis of ethnobotanical records, conservation status, and FTC-compliant historical framing. Examples: Black Cohosh Cherokee traditional use, Goldenseal CITES sidebar, Ashwagandha Rasayana context. A writer cannot produce these sections well when fatigued or distracted. Estimated: 30% of total hours.

**Mode 2 — Technical specification writing (medium cognitive load)**: Active constituent descriptions, cultivation zone specifications, harvest timing tables. These are precise but follow a template and require accuracy more than creativity. Examples: thymol/carvacrol framing for Thyme, valerian root drying temperature specifications, echinacea zone-by-zone cultivation notes. A contractor with a good outline can handle these competently. Estimated: 40% of total hours.

**Mode 3 — Template-driven sections (lower cognitive load)**: Preparation methods, seasonal timing, sources sections. These follow the same structure in every bundle and can be produced quickly once the research is compiled. Estimated: 30% of total hours.

The hybrid model exploits this distinction: the user handles Mode 1 (narrative, historical, contraindications), the contractor handles Mode 2 (technical specifications), and both contribute to Mode 3 (preparation methods and sources).

### The Track B cognitive load problem

From June 5–19, the user is managing the Track B launch: email response, sales metrics review, Day 3/7/14 checkpoint preparation, influencer engagement. This is not light work — it requires daily attention and reactive decision-making. Phase 3 planning actions (supplier orders, image sourcing) are low-cognitive-load tasks that can run concurrently. But beginning sustained writing during this window would divide creative energy at a critical moment.

The practical implication: Phase 3 writing begins June 22 as planned (post-Day 14 checkpoint). Pre-sprint sourcing and preparation actions (this checklist, attribution log) are the only Phase 3 tasks that compete for attention June 5–19.

## Section 2: Solo Model (User at 10–12 Hours/Week)

### Model definition

The user writes all 56–66 hours of content without contractor assistance. Design (12.5 hours) and upload administration (10 hours) run in parallel but are also solo. Total production hours: 78–88 hours.

### Sustainable daily hour targets

**10 hours/week scenario**: 1.5–2 hours/day, 6 days/week. This is the conservative scenario from the project header.
- Week 1: 10 hours → Women's Health at 14–16 hours (requires a partial Week 2 spillover)
- Actual timeline: 56–66 hours at 10 hours/week = 5.6–6.6 weeks of pure writing time
- With design and admin: approximately 7–8 weeks from June 22 = full launch mid-August to late August
- Risk: Women's Health cannot be completed in Week 1 at 10 hours/week. With 14–16 hours needed and only 10 hours in a week, Women's Health takes 1.5 weeks. The June 29 upload target slips to July 6–7. All other uploads slide accordingly.

**12 hours/week scenario** (recommended minimum for solo path): 2 hours/day, 6 days/week.
- Week 1: 12 hours → Women's Health at 14–16 hours requires 5–6 hours on two consecutive weekend days to complete by June 28. Achievable.
- Actual timeline: 56–66 hours at 12 hours/week = 4.7–5.5 weeks of pure writing time
- With design and admin: approximately 5.5–6.5 weeks from June 22 = full launch early-to-mid August
- This timeline is compatible with the August 3 Digestive upload target.

**14 hours/week scenario** (upper end of sustainable solo pace): 2.5 hours/day, 6 days/week, or 3 hours/day 5 days/week.
- Most realistic representation of what the critical path analysis calls the "5 hours/day Week 1" pace — that is a sprint pace for the highest-intensity days, not the average.
- At 14 hours/week: 56–66 hours = 4–4.7 weeks = full launch late July to early August
- Matches the sprint roadmap calendar precisely

**Writer fatigue profile for solo path**:

| Week | Bundle(s) | Writing Hours | Fatigue Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 (June 22–28) | Women's Health (partial Respiratory) | 14–15 | Medium | Fresh start; Black Cohosh conservation sidebar is the most demanding section |
| Week 2 (June 29–July 5) | Respiratory (complete) + Immunity + Sleep | 18–20 | High | Peak load week; three bundles in progress |
| Week 3 (July 6–13) | Digestive + QA + uploads | 14–16 | Medium-high | Digestive benefits from shared species; QA is mechanical not creative |

**Week 2 is the highest-fatigue risk period**. At 18–20 hours of writing in one week (plus design and administration), the solo path is demanding. Mitigation: schedule the Immunity and Sleep bundles in the correct order (Immunity benefits from Respiratory research; Sleep benefits from Women's Health Lavender). Do not shuffle the sequence to create variety — the efficiency savings from in-sequence writing reduce the Week 2 hour burden.

**Motivational sustainability**: Solo writing on research-dense content for 5+ weeks requires sustained motivation. Risk factors: writer fatigue after Week 2–3, diminishing enthusiasm for the 4th and 5th bundles, competing priorities (Track B sales response, customer emails). Mitigation strategies:
- Set a fixed writing window each day (same 2-hour block) to create habit rather than motivation dependency
- Treat each bundle as a distinct project with its own "done" celebration (Etsy listing live = milestone achieved)
- Keep the Digestive bundle (Week 3) short in the schedule — its 9–11 adjusted hours make it the fastest finish of the five, which provides a motivational payoff after the Week 2 intensity

## Section 3: Contractor Model (40 Hours/Week)

### Model definition

A contractor writes the bulk of the 56–66 adjusted hours (approximately 45–55 hours). The user reviews, revises, and approves all content — particularly the contraindications sections and any section making a therapeutic claim. Total contractor writing: 45–55 hours. User review time: 10–15 hours.

### Contractor profile requirements

The contractor must meet minimum qualifications for Phase 3 content:
- Experience writing about herbal medicine, botanical supplements, or plant science — not general health/wellness copywriting
- Familiarity with FTC compliance requirements for dietary supplement and traditional-use claims (or willingness to follow explicit style guidelines)
- Ability to write from a detailed outline and source material without drifting into efficacy claims
- Turnaround capacity: 40+ hours/week for 2–3 weeks of concentrated work

**This is a specialized brief**. Generic freelance writers without botanical knowledge will produce content that requires heavy revision — offsetting the time savings. The Phase 3 content is not difficult to find on the internet, but distinguishing correct botanical information from the abundant incorrect internet content requires domain knowledge.

**Where to find qualified contractors**:
- American Herbalists Guild (americanherbalistsguild.com) — practitioner directory; some members offer writing services
- Upwork: search "herbal medicine writer" or "botanical content writer" — filter for writers with demonstrated familiarity with supplement compliance
- The Herbal Academy (theherbalacademy.com) — has a practitioner community; ask in their forums
- Local herbalism schools — recent graduates with writing skills who need portfolio work

**Briefing the contractor**: The Phase 3 content outline (`phase-3-medicinal-herbs-content-outline.md`) is the brief. It specifies every section, word target, and content note for all 14 species. A qualified contractor can work from this document without additional research sessions. Provide: the content outline, the FTC language quick reference (Appendix A of the sprint roadmap), and the house style rules (second person for practical sections, third person for historical sections; no marketing language in guide body).

### What the contractor writes vs. what the user must retain

**Contractor can draft**:
- Cultivation specifications (zone requirements, soil conditions, spacing, propagation methods, harvest timing)
- Active constituent descriptions (following FTC-compliant framing from the quick reference)
- Preparation methods (infusion, decoction, tincture processes — these are procedural descriptions)
- Identification sections (botanical description, look-alike notes — factual content)
- Sourcing recommendations (supplier references already documented in the sourcing guide)

**User must write or approve with line-by-line review**:
- All contraindications sections — liability exposure is the primary risk here; a contractor writing "black cohosh is safe during pregnancy" when it is contraindicated during pregnancy would be a serious error
- Conservation sidebars (Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, Echinacea angustifolia) — these make specific conservation claims that must be verified against current UpS status
- The Goldenseal CITES sidebar — verbatim text that must be legally accurate
- Traditional use sections where ethnobotanical framing is nuanced (Black Cohosh Cherokee use, Goldenseal Eclectic tradition)
- The dandelion forager cross-sell hook (this is strategic positioning content, not botanical information)

### Contractor timeline

At 40 hours/week, a contractor completes 56–66 adjusted hours in:
- 56 hours ÷ 40 hours/week = 1.4 weeks
- 66 hours ÷ 40 hours/week = 1.65 weeks
- With review cycles: approximately 2–2.5 weeks from handoff to user-approved drafts

Upload sequencing then drives the actual launch timeline. Even if all content is written in Week 1 of contractor engagement, the staggered Etsy upload schedule (Women's Health June 29, Respiratory July 6–7, etc.) is not compressed by faster writing — you cannot upload more than one listing per week without sacrificing algorithmic discovery window.

**Practical contractor timeline**:
- June 22: Contractor begins Women's Health bundle
- June 24–25: User reviews Women's Health draft, revises contraindications sections
- June 26–27: Contractor begins Respiratory + Immunity simultaneously (parallel drafting)
- June 28: Women's Health final approval + PDF export
- June 29: Women's Health upload live
- June 30 – July 5: User reviews Respiratory and Immunity; contractor drafts Sleep and Digestive
- July 6–7: Respiratory upload live
- July 8–10: User reviews Sleep and Digestive
- July 13: Sleep upload live
- July 20: Immunity upload live
- August 3: Digestive upload live

To recover the $1,125–$1,650 contractor fee requires 10–17 complete sales (all 5 bundles). ### Contractor risk profile

**Primary risk**: Domain knowledge gap. A contractor unfamiliar with botanical medicine may write cultivation notes that are technically incorrect (e.g., recommending alkaline soil for Black Cohosh, which requires acidic pH 5.5–6.5). The content outline includes specific cultivation parameters — the contractor must follow them precisely, not paraphrase from general internet sources.

**Mitigation**: Provide the content outline as the sole reference document. Specify in the brief: "Use only the parameters in this outline for cultivation specifications; do not supplement from other sources without flagging for review."

**Secondary risk**: FTC compliance drift. Even with the quick reference, a contractor may write sentences that make efficacy claims rather than historical or research descriptions. Every bundle requires a full FTC review pass.

**Mitigation**: User conducts line-by-line FTC review of every bundle. Budget 2 hours per bundle for this review = 10 additional user hours.

**Contractor model verdict**: Viable at $1,350–$1,650 total cost. Requires a qualified contractor found and briefed before June 22. The search, interview, and briefing process takes 3–5 business days minimum — begin contractor search immediately (June 5–10) if this model is chosen. If no qualified contractor is confirmed by June 17, default to hybrid or solo.

## Section 4: Hybrid Model (User 20 hrs/Week + Contractor 20 hrs/Week)

### Model definition

The user and contractor split the writing work by section type. The user writes all sections requiring domain judgment or highest liability (Mode 1 sections: narrative, historical, contraindications). The contractor writes first drafts of technical specification and template sections (Mode 2 and 3: cultivation, constituents, preparation methods). Total user writing hours: approximately 28–33. Total contractor hours: approximately 28–33.

### Section assignment matrix

| Section Type | User or Contractor | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction (each bundle) | User | Strategic positioning content; sets the tone for practitioner buyers |
| Species identification | Contractor (first draft) + User review | Factual, verifiable, low liability |
| Cultivation specifications | Contractor (first draft) + User review | Technical, outline-driven, low liability |
| Harvest and preparation | Contractor (first draft) + User review | Procedural, follows outline protocol |
| Active constituents | Contractor (first draft) + User FTC review | High FTC sensitivity; user does final qualifying-language check |
| Traditional use sections | **User writes** | Ethnobotanical nuance; Cherokee and Ayurvedic framing require sensitivity |
| Conservation sidebars | **User writes** | Conservation claims require verification; conservation story is a core differentiator |
| Contraindications sections | **User writes** | Highest liability content in the guide; no delegation |
| CITES Goldenseal sidebar | **User writes** | Legal accuracy requirement; mandatory verbatim text |
| Shared sections (preparation methods, seasonal timing) | Contractor (first draft) + User review | Template-driven, lower stakes |
| Sources section | Contractor compiles, user verifies PMIDs | Factual, outline-driven |
| Forager cross-sell hook (Dandelion) | **User writes** | Strategic revenue bridge; not botanical information |
| FTC compliance review | **User reviews every bundle** | Cannot delegate liability review |

**Estimated user hours by section type** (Mode 1 sections only):
- Introductions (5 bundles × ~30 min each): 2.5 hours
- Traditional use sections (14 species × ~30 min each, some species have longer framing): 8–10 hours
- Conservation sidebars (Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, Echinacea angustifolia): 2–3 hours
- Contraindications sections (14 species × ~20 min each): 4–5 hours
- CITES Goldenseal sidebar (mandatory verbatim + framing): 1–1.5 hours
- Forager cross-sell hook (Dandelion): 30 minutes
- FTC compliance review (5 bundles × 1.5–2 hrs each): 7.5–10 hours
- Self-edit passes (5 bundles × 1 hr each): 5 hours
- **Total user writing hours**: **30–37 hours**

**Estimated contractor hours** (Mode 2 and 3 sections):
- Identification sections (14 species × ~45 min each): 10–11 hours
- Cultivation sections (14 species × ~1 hr each): 13–14 hours
- Harvest and preparation sections (5 bundles × ~1.5 hrs each): 7–8 hours
- Active constituent first drafts (14 species × ~30 min each): 6–7 hours
- Shared sections first drafts: 4–5 hours
- **Total contractor hours**: **40–45 hours first draft** (some sections will require revision cycles)

**Revision overhead**: Budget 20–30% additional user time for revising contractor drafts (contractor writes 40 hours; user revises 8–12 hours). This is included in the 30–37 hour estimate above.

### Hybrid coordination protocol

**Handoff approach**:
- User provides content outline + FTC quick reference + house style rules
- User writes Mode 1 sections for each bundle (working from June 22 calendar)
- Contractor drafts Mode 2/3 sections in parallel, starting with Women's Health
- User reviews contractor sections before bundle assembly; returns revisions within 24 hours
- Bundle assembly (combining user-written and contractor-revised sections into a single document) is a user task: 1–2 hours per bundle

**Communication overhead**: Budget 30 minutes/day for contractor communication (brief, feedback, revision tracking). Avoid long back-and-forth on single sections — if a section needs more than one revision cycle, it is faster for the user to rewrite it.

**Recommended tools**: Shared Google Docs for each bundle (one doc per bundle). User writes Mode 1 sections in highlighted text; contractor fills in Mode 2/3 sections in standard text. Comments for revision notes. This makes the merge step trivial.

### Hybrid timeline

The hybrid timeline is determined by the slowest parallel track. User writes Mode 1 sections at roughly 2 hours/day (conservative). Contractor writes Mode 2/3 sections at roughly 5–6 hours/day.

| Week | User Task | Contractor Task | Sprint State |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 (June 22–28) | WH intro + conservation sidebars + traditional use + contraindications (~12 hrs) | WH identification + cultivation + harvest + constituents first drafts (~15 hrs) | Women's Health bundle assembly by June 27 |
| Week 2 (June 29–July 5) | Resp/Immunity traditional use + contraindications (~10 hrs) | Resp/Immunity identification + cultivation + shared sections (~20 hrs) | Respiratory and Immunity assemblies by July 5 |
| Week 3 (July 6–13) | Sleep/Digestive traditional use + contraindications + FTC review all 5 (~12 hrs) | Sleep/Digestive identification + cultivation + preparation methods (~12 hrs) | All 5 bundles complete by July 10 |

**Full 5-bundle content complete by July 10** in hybrid model. Upload sequence (Women's Health June 29, Respiratory July 6–7, Sleep July 13, Immunity July 20, Digestive August 3) is unchanged — the staggered upload schedule is driven by Etsy algorithmic strategy, not writing completion.

## Section 6: Recommendation

**Recommended model: Hybrid**, conditional on finding a qualified contractor by June 17.

**Rationale**:

1. The hybrid model keeps the user on all sections where liability or strategic positioning is highest (contraindications, conservation sidebars, forager cross-sell hook, traditional use framing). These sections constitute Seedwarden's actual competitive differentiation — they are the reason a practitioner buyer chooses this guide over a generic PLR product.

2. The contractor handles cultivation specifications and constituent descriptions — sections that are verifiable, template-driven, and less dependent on the specific voice and judgment that make the guides valuable. A botanical writer following the content outline precisely can produce these sections competently.

3. The hybrid model reduces the user's Week 2 peak intensity from 18–20 writing hours to roughly 10–12 writing hours. This matters because Week 2 (June 29–July 5) also includes the Women's Health Etsy upload, early sales monitoring, and potentially the first buyer inquiries. Lower writing load in Week 2 leaves cognitive bandwidth for reactive business tasks.

4. At $1,000–$1,350 total contractor cost (realistic estimate), the hybrid model breaks even at 10–14 complete buyer sales across the suite. This is achievable within the first 30 days post-launch at the forager cohort attachment rate.

**If a qualified contractor is not confirmed by June 17**: Default to solo at 12 hours/week. Do not begin a contractor relationship after June 22 — onboarding and briefing time during Week 1 (the highest-intensity writing week) would disrupt rather than help the sprint. The contractor relationship requires setup time (briefing, review of first sample, style alignment) that is not compatible with simultaneously writing Women's Health at full intensity.

**If choosing solo**: Target 12 hours/week, not 10. The difference is one additional 30-minute session per day. At 12 hours/week, all five bundles finish by early August. At 10 hours/week, the Women's Health June 29 upload slips by approximately 1 week, which cascades through the entire calendar.
