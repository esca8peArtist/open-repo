## Lighting and Location Specifications

**Primary light source**: North-facing window, 9am–3pm. North-facing avoids direct sun hotspots. If south- or east-facing window only: shoot before 9am or after 3pm to avoid harsh directional shadows.

**Reflector placement**: White foamcore board or foam poster board (dollar store), positioned opposite window at 45 degrees. Fills shadows on the near side of the subject. Do not use silver reflector — too harsh for botanical subjects.

**Background options**:
- Kraft paper: earthy, neutral, pairs with Sage Green and Apothecary Gold palette. Use for Respiratory and Digestive.
- White linen napkin or tea towel: clean, clinical, pairs with Burgundy and Lavender palette. Use for Women's Health and Sleep.
- White ceramic plate or cutting board: clean surface for root close-ups.
- Dark wood cutting board: lifestyle shots requiring warmth and apothecary aesthetic.

**Camera settings** (phone or DSLR):
- Resolution: maximum available
- White balance: Daylight or 5500K — do not use Auto WB
- Avoid zoom above 2× on phones (quality degrades)
- Shoot multiple angles per subject: overhead flat-lay, 45-degree angle, close-up fill-frame. Cull to best per subject.

**Prohibited**: Busy backgrounds (kitchen counters with appliances visible), colored surfaces (shows through herbs), outdoor shots with variable light (inconsistent with Phase 2 aesthetic), deep filters or Instagram-style editing.

## Phase 2 Aesthetic Consistency Rules

Phase 2 used:
- Warm cream backgrounds (Warm Cream #F5EDD6)
- Deep Forest Green accent frames
- Natural surface textures (wood, linen, clay)
- No visible brand logos or retail packaging

Phase 3 adds:
- Clinical Cream backgrounds (#F9F5F0 — slightly cooler than Phase 2 Warm Cream)
- Burgundy and Sage accent elements in frame edges or props
- Apothecary styling (glass jars, mortar/pestle, ceramic dishes)

**What must stay the same**: Natural textures (linen, wood, kraft paper), single-herb or sparse-herb composition (never a busy pile), consistent exposure and warmth level, white or near-white background options.

**What changes**: Props shift from field/forager aesthetic (baskets, canvas bags) to apothecary/preparation aesthetic (mortar, glass jars, ceramic). Surfaces shift from outdoor wood to indoor table surfaces.
