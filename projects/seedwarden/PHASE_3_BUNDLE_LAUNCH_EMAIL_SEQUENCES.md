### GOLDENSEAL — Ethical Sourcing and Conservation

Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) is listed on CITES Appendix II as a plant whose export is restricted due to over-harvesting of wild populations. **Never harvest wild goldenseal.** Cultivated goldenseal is available from native plant nurseries and is legal to purchase, grow, and use. If you purchase goldenseal root or products containing goldenseal, verify the source is cultivated, not wild-harvested. Supporting cultivated sources creates economic incentive for restoration of wild populations.

**Cultivation notes**: Goldenseal prefers partial shade and moist soil; it is native to deciduous forests of Eastern North America. Seeds require 18-month stratification before germination. Commercial growers typically use root propagation (faster than seed). A mature goldenseal plant takes 4–5 years to reach harvestable size. If you harvest rhizome and leave roots in ground, the plant regenerates within 2–3 years.
