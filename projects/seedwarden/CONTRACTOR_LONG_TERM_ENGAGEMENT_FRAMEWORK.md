## Phase 3 → Phase 4 Transition, 2027 Cohort, Retention, and Contingency Staffing

**Activation timeline**: Assessment begins August 4, 2026 (day after Phase 3 close); 2027 cohort locked by August 15

## Part 1 — Phase 3 to Phase 4 Transition

### Assessment Framework: Which Phase 3 Contractors Advance?

Phase 3 runs eight contractor candidates across three tracks (photography, writing, habitat specialist) as listed in PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md. Assessment happens at the Phase 3 close (August 3). Four criteria determine which contractors advance to Phase 4 and in what capacity.

**Criterion 1 — Productivity Score (0–25 points)**
Measured by: bundles delivered vs. committed, word count vs. target, photo sessions vs. booked. A contractor who delivers three bundles on time at full word count scores 25. A contractor who delivers two of three bundles with one late delivery scores 16. A contractor who delivers one bundle or less scores 0–10 and is not retained.

**Criterion 2 — Iteration Cycle Count (0–25 points)**
Measured by: number of revision rounds required before final approval. Phase 3 quality standard is two revision cycles maximum. One round of revision = 25 pts. Two rounds = 18 pts. Three rounds = 10 pts. Four or more rounds = 0 pts (disqualifying for retainer; may continue as project-based Tier B at reduced rate).

**Criterion 3 — FTC/CITES Accuracy Rate (0–25 points)**
Measured by: number of FTC non-compliant phrases flagged in quality gate review, and number of CITES/at-risk sourcing errors corrected after initial delivery. Zero errors = 25 pts. One error corrected = 20 pts. Two errors = 13 pts. Three or more errors = 0 pts (disqualifying for clinical content; may continue on non-clinical topics only).

**Criterion 4 — Deadline Adherence (0–25 points)**
Measured by: delivery date vs. contracted date for each bundle milestone. On-time or early for all milestones = 25 pts. One late delivery within three days = 18 pts. One late delivery more than three days = 10 pts. Two or more late deliveries = 0 pts (disqualifying for retainer consideration).

**Phase 4 Tier Assignment by Score**:
- 85–100 pts: **Tier A Retainer** — offer quarterly retainer for 2027, right of first refusal on all bundles in their specialty
- 65–84 pts: **Tier B Project Regular** — engaged per bundle at established rates, advance notice before external sourcing
- 40–64 pts: **Tier B Probationary** — available for one Q4 2026 bundle on a probationary basis; re-score before 2027 commitment
- Below 40 pts: **Not retained** — log exit reason; do not re-engage without re-vetting from scratch

### Which Roles Are Candidates for Expanded Responsibility?

**Lead Photographer (elevated from Tier A Photographer)**: The highest-scoring Phase 3 photographer who demonstrates consistent composition standards (sharpness, color calibration, species isolation) and meets all five delivery windows becomes the Lead Photographer for 2027. Responsibilities expand to: reviewing other photographers' work for quality gate compliance, managing the 2027 Wikimedia CC photo sourcing process (identifying and attributing CC-licensed images for low-priority species), and conducting the photo brief orientation call for any new photographer hired in Q1 2027. No rate increase for this coordination role in 2027 — frame it as portfolio development (lead credit in project documentation) with a rate review after six months.

**Lead Clinical Writer (elevated from Tier A Writer)**: The highest-scoring clinical writer (likely Rebecca Lexa or Adrian White based on Phase 3 credential profile) becomes the content quality reviewer for 2027 bundles written by Tier B writers. Responsibilities: conduct one review pass on all new species profiles for FTC compliance and CITES accuracy before the user's editorial review. This is a paid review role: $50–$75 per bundle reviewed (1–2 hours per bundle). This creates a quality escalation layer that reduces user review burden as the 2027 bundle volume scales.

### Contractors to Retire or Benchmark

**Criteria for retirement**: Any Phase 3 contractor who scores below 40 on the assessment rubric should not be retained. The reason is not punitive — it is that the specific demands of Seedwarden's content (clinical contraindication depth, FTC precision, CITES awareness) are not a match for every credentialed herbalist. Log the exit reason in the contractor tracking log for future reference.

**Benchmarking vs. retirement**: A contractor who scores 40–64 ("Tier B Probationary") should be given one Q4 2026 probationary bundle before the retirement decision. If they deliver the Q4 bundle at or above threshold, they enter 2027 as Tier B. If the Q4 bundle requires more than two revision cycles or misses its deadline, the retirement decision is confirmed.

**Archiving contact**: Even retired contractors should be kept in the contractor tracking log as "inactive — archived." Circumstances change. A Phase 3 writer who was unavailable for Q4 2026 due to a competing commitment may be available for a Q2 2027 bundle. The vetting rubric score from Phase 3 remains the baseline; do not require a full re-vetting for a previously screened contractor returning after a gap.

## Part 2 — 2027 Contractor Cohort Expansion

### Scaling from 6 to 10–12 Contractors

Phase 3 has six contractor candidates (three photographers, three writers/specialists). The 2027 bundle slate (ten bundles) requires eight to ten production-active contractors across the year, plus two on-call bench contractors. The math: each bundle requires one writer (at minimum) and one photographer (or confirmed Wikimedia CC sourcing). Ten bundles across four quarters = roughly two to three active contractors per bundle per quarter. This is achievable with six strong Phase 3 retains + two to three new hires.

**Target 2027 cohort composition**:
- 2 Tier A retainers (clinical writer + photographer) from Phase 3
- 3–4 Tier B project regulars from Phase 3
- 2 new clinical writers hired Q1 2027 (for the Q2 and Q3 bundle expansion)
- 1 new photographer hired Q1 2027 (for Q2+ species that require fresh photography)
- 1–2 on-call bench contractors (writer + photographer) available from February

**Total 2027 active contractor pool**: 9–11 contractors.

### Recruiting Strategy for New 2027 Hires

**Primary channel**: Referral from Phase 3 contractors. A Phase 3 Tier A contractor who knows two or three other credentialed herbalist writers is the fastest and most reliable sourcing pipeline. Referral candidates skip the first two stages of the vetting rubric (portfolio review and cold screening) because the referring contractor vouches for clinical depth and professional reliability. Cost of referral: zero. Expected response rate: higher than cold AHG directory outreach because the introduction carries a trusted professional relationship.

**Secondary channel**: AHG directory + Chestnut School (unchanged from Phase 3 sourcing strategy). In February 2027, post the 2027 bundle scope (eight new bundles, quarterly cadence, retainer structure available) to the AHG directory and Chestnut School partnerships contact. The retainer offer is a material upgrade from Phase 3's flat-rate sprint structure — it attracts more established herbalist writers who value income stability over one-off project income.

**Tertiary channel**: Upwork job posting (same screening questions as Phase 3). Post in January 2027 for a February start.

**Absolute rule**: Do not lower vetting standards to fill the cohort faster. A Tier B writer who clears all five disqualifying conditions and scores 65–74 on the rubric is preferable to a lower-scoring candidate who is available immediately. A weaker hire creates more revision overhead than leaving a bundle on a slightly delayed timeline.

### 2-Week Onboarding Playbook for New Mid-Year Contractors

A contractor joining mid-year (e.g., a Q3 hire onboarded in June 2027) requires two weeks of orientation before their first bundle assignment begins.

**Week 1 — Brand and Standards Immersion**

Day 1–2: Deliver the Seedwarden Contractor Briefing Package (CBP). The CBP is the Phase 3 document set: PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_PREBID_MATERIALS.md (project overview, brand voice, audience description), the FTC Quick Reference (evidence-tier framing examples), and the CITES sidebar verbatim text (why sourcing framing matters for at-risk species). The new contractor reads these before any writing begins.

Day 3: Schedule a 60-minute orientation call. Agenda: (1) walk through one completed Phase 3 bundle as the quality exemplar (show what a "Pass" bundle looks like at the first-draft stage), (2) walk through one revision cycle from Phase 3 (show what feedback looks like and how it is addressed), (3) confirm the contractor's bundle assignment, delivery dates, and payment milestones.

Day 4–5: Contractor completes a paid test section (~600 words, one species profile from their assigned bundle). User reviews against the quality gate checklist (PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md Section 3). If the test section passes, proceed to full bundle assignment. If the test section fails two or more quality gates, the onboarding is paused and the failure mode is analyzed before proceeding.

**Week 2 — First Assignment Begin**

Day 6–10: Contractor begins writing the assigned bundle with a structured check-in on Day 8 (1,500–2,000 words delivered for early-stage quality check). The Day 8 check-in is not a formal revision — it is a trajectory confirmation that the contractor is hitting the correct depth and framing tone before investing full production hours.

Day 11–14: Contractor completes the first major species profile section and delivers for first-draft quality review. Feedback turnaround target: 48 hours. Contractor revises and delivers updated draft.

**Total onboarding cost**: Two weeks of the contractor's time before their first full delivery, plus approximately 3–4 hours of user time (orientation call, test section review, Day 8 check-in, first-draft review).

## Part 3 — Retention and Career Pathing

### What Retains Niche Herbalist Contractors Long-Term

Credentialed herbalist writers have two scarcities in their favor: their clinical depth is hard to replicate, and the job market for this specific skill intersection (AHG-level herbalism + FTC-compliant medical writing + digital publishing experience) is thin. This means they have meaningful market power despite operating as freelancers. The retention levers that work are non-transactional:

**Lever 1 — Published portfolio credit**: As described in PHASE_4_2027_ROADMAP.md Part 2, all 2027 bundles carry a "clinical review by [Name]" attribution in the PDF. For AHG Registered Herbalists, a published clinical reference is a professional credential asset — it demonstrates ongoing professional publication outside of clinical practice. This is not available from any other freelance content client in their niche. It costs Seedwarden nothing and has high perceived value to the contractor.

**Lever 2 — Annual scope preview**: Each November or December, share the upcoming year's bundle themes before recruitment begins. This gives Tier A and Tier B contractors first selection of the bundles they are most qualified for. A clinical writer with deep Cardiovascular herbalism background who is given first selection of the Q1 Cardiovascular bundle in December 2026 will turn down competing work to hold that slot. This costs Seedwarden one conversation per contractor per year.

**Lever 3 — Performance-linked rate escalation**: Tier A contractors who maintain 85+ quality gate scores across two consecutive quarters receive a 10% rate increase for the following quarter. Rate escalation is structured, not negotiated reactively — the contractor knows at onboarding that sustained quality translates to a defined rate increase. This prevents the mid-year "rate renegotiation" conversation that disrupts production timelines.

**Lever 4 — Revenue-share consideration for 2028**: If Seedwarden's digital catalog reaches $5,000+/month by Q4 2027, the Tier A writer who has contributed to the largest number of bundles in the catalog is a candidate for a royalty arrangement — a 2–3% royalty on ongoing sales from bundles they wrote. This is a 2028 planning item, not a 2027 commitment. But signaling it to Tier A contractors in 2027 ("if we reach scale target, we intend to convert key contributors to a revenue-share model") is a retention signal that differentiates Seedwarden from every other freelance content client they will encounter.

### Realistic Retention Rate Expectations

**Target**: 80% of Phase 3 contractors retained through Q4 2026; 70–75% retained through full-year 2027. These rates account for:
- Natural 15–20% attrition from life change, competing clients, or scope misalignment
- One or two contractors who Phase 3 performance assessment declines from Tier A to Tier B (not attrition, but a role change)
- The gap filled by Tier C on-call hires, which provide the 20% buffer

Do not target 100% retention — it is neither achievable nor desirable. Attrition introduces new candidates who may bring skills or specialties the original Phase 3 cohort lacked (example: a Q1 2027 hire with deep cardiovascular herbalism background might be stronger for the Cardiovascular bundle than any Phase 3 writer).

## Part 4 — Contingency Staffing

### The Critical Failure Scenario: Lead Contractor Dropout in Week 3 of Q4

The highest-risk contingency is a Tier A contractor exiting mid-production — after a bundle assignment has started but before first-draft delivery. In Phase 3, this risk is addressed by PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md (Wikimedia CC fallback, solo writing fallback). In 2027, the contingency architecture must be more robust because bundles are quarterly, not sprint-based — a mid-quarter dropout affects a specific bundle, not the entire production window.

**Contingency Layer 1 — The Tier C Bench (primary response)**

The two Tier C on-call contractors (one writer, one photographer) are specifically maintained to absorb Tier A/B dropout. When a Tier A dropout occurs:
- Day 1: User contacts Tier C on-call writer, provides the bundle assignment and the Phase 3 quality standard documents
- Day 1–2: Tier C writer completes the 2-week onboarding playbook accelerated to 5 days (since they are already pre-onboarded at the start of the year)
- Day 3–10: Tier C writer produces the bundle content at Tier C production rate ($700–$900 flat for a standard bundle)
- Timeline impact: maximum 2-week delay if dropout occurs in Week 3; 1-week delay if dropout occurs in Week 4 or later (Tier C can overlap with the delivery window)

**Contingency Layer 2 — The Wikimedia / AI Draft Assist Hybrid (backup)**

For bundles where no Tier C writer is available or the timeline cannot absorb a 2-week contractor transition, the solo production model from Phase 3 remains viable. The user writes the bundle with AI-assisted drafting for the species research backbone, then applies the FTC/CITES quality review pass independently. Timeline: 15–20 hours of direct user production. This is the same solo fallback documented in PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md.

**Contingency Layer 3 — AHG Directory Emergency Posting**

If both Layer 1 and Layer 2 are unavailable (e.g., the Tier C writer is also unavailable and the user's schedule cannot absorb solo production), post an emergency contract to the AHG directory and Upwork simultaneously with a 48-hour response deadline. Budget ceiling for emergency contract: 20% above standard rate to compress the sourcing timeline. Apply the full screening questions regardless of urgency — FTC and CITES accuracy failures are more costly to fix post-production than a brief timeline delay.

### Photographer Contingency

Photographer dropout is lower-risk than writer dropout for three reasons: (1) most 2027 bundles will carry Wikimedia CC photography as the primary image source; (2) new photography sessions are bookable on 48-hour notice in most metro areas via Thumbtack or similar; (3) a delayed photography session does not block writing production — the writer can deliver the full bundle text before photography is complete.

**Photographer dropout response**: Immediately activate Wikimedia CC sourcing for all species in the affected bundle. New photographer sourcing can proceed in parallel without a production deadline consequence. The bundle goes to Etsy upload with Wikimedia CC images and upgraded to commissioned photography in a later version if the bundle's revenue performance justifies the investment.

## Contractor Tracking Requirements

The PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md tracking format carries forward to 2027 with one addition: a **Retention Score column** updated quarterly. Retention Score = average of last four quality gate assessments (Productivity + Iteration + FTC Accuracy + Deadline). This score is the primary input to the Tier assignment review at the start of each quarter.

Review timing:
- **End of Phase 3 (August 4)**: Assign Phase 4 tiers; confirm Q4 2026 re-engagements
- **End of Q4 2026 (December 31)**: Confirm 2027 cohort; onboard new Tier C hires by January 15
- **End of Q1 2027 (March 31)**: First in-year tier review; adjust Tier assignments if any contractor has moved significantly on Retention Score
- **End of Q3 2027 (September 30)**: Pre-Q4 review; confirm Q4 2027 contractor availability before October production sprint begins
