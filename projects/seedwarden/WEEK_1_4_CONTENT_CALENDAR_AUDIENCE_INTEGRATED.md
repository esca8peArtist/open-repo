## PRE-LAUNCH TACTICAL SEQUENCE (May 27–29 — 48 Hours Before First Post)

### Why This Matters
The launch week strategy's highest-ROI action is NOT posting content — it is the 48 hours of targeted comment engagement on top-10 foraging and homesteading accounts BEFORE the first Seedwarden post goes live. This "warm entry" tactic (genuine, substantive comments on competitor and peer content 2–3 days before launch) costs zero dollars, requires 2–3 hours total, and ensures that the most likely first followers of Seedwarden have already seen the brand name once in a context that is helpful rather than promotional.

**Timeline**:
- May 27–28 (Tuesday–Wednesday): 48 hours of comment engagement
- May 29 (Thursday): Production finalization + scheduling
- May 30 (Saturday), 7:00am: First post goes live (Post 1, TikTok)

### Accounts to Monitor and Engage (Pre-Launch)

**Tier 1 (High Priority — engage both days)**:
1. @alexisnikole (TikTok, 4.5M) — Largest foraging creator; your audience watches here daily
2. @chaoticforager (TikTok, 30–60K) — Niche mushroom expert; highly engaged community

**Tier 2 (Medium Priority — engage one day)**:

### Engagement Script (Substantive Comments — Not Promotional)

**Format**: 1–2 sentence comment that demonstrates you've watched/read the content, asks a question, or adds useful information. Never mention Seedwarden or links.

**Examples (by content type)**:

**Plant identification posts**: "Question — I've seen this grow in disturbed areas in Zone 5 but only in spring. Does anyone know why it's not present in summer cultivation? [Your observation]"

**Foraging technique posts**: "This is the technique I use too, but I've had better results with [specific detail]. Has anyone noticed regional variation?"

**Product/resource recommendation posts**: "Adding this to my reference library — do you have a guide for Zone [your zone]? Most national guides miss the early frost timing we deal with."

**Why this works**: 
- Shows you're a thoughtful consumer of the content, not a bot
- Demonstrates you have specific regional/technical knowledge
- Signals to the algorithm that the account + comments are high-quality
- When these people encounter Seedwarden on May 30, they've seen your name in context and are more likely to click through

### Comment Engagement Metrics (What to Track)

Track which accounts respond to your comments and with what sentiment. These are highest-probability follow-back candidates. When you launch on May 30, prioritize following these accounts first (Instagram's follow-back algorithm gives a boost to accounts that have interacted with you).

**Target**: 15–20 substantive comments per day across 4–5 accounts = 30–40 total comments over 48 hours.

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC POSTING TIME OPTIMIZATION

### Instagram Optimization (From TRACK_B_AUDIENCE_RESEARCH.md + 2026 Algorithm Changes)

**Consumption Pattern**:
- Morning planning mode (9–11am): Active saving, high completion, reference/planning intent
- Evening discovery mode (6–8pm): Passive browsing, lower save rate, awareness-building

**Algorithm Weighting (2026)**:
- DM shares weighted 3–5x more than likes
- Completion rate critical (watch until end)
- Saves = highest-quality engagement signal

**Posting Time Strategy for Each Content Type**:

| Post Type | Optimal Time | Cluster Target | Algorithm Goal | Why This Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Educational (carousel, zone card, ID post) | 9:00–10:30am | Cluster 1, 2 | Maximize saves | Morning planning mode hits when homesteaders are building reference library |
| Lifestyle (before/after, product showcase, real use context) | 10:00–11:00am | Cluster 2, 4 | Maximize shares + DM forwards | Peak giftability evaluation time; "I want to send this to X person" |
| Brand/Launch content (announcements, shop updates) | 6:30–7:30pm | Cluster 1, 2, 3 | Maximize discovery reach | Evening discovery mode, lower competition, broader interest signaling |
| Direct sales (product pins, bundle showcase) | 9:00am OR 3:00pm | Cluster 4 | Maximize clicks + AOV | Morning planners + mid-afternoon impulse window |

**Shareability Framework for Every Instagram Post**:
Before posting, ask: "Who would a forager or homesteader SEND this to?" If the answer is "nobody" or "only if they're trying to promote it," reframe the post. Examples:
- ✗ "Check out our new guide" → ✓ "Send this to someone who keeps asking 'what's this plant?'"
- ✗ "Learn companion planting" → ✓ "If you garden alongside native plants, these combos actually work in Zone 5"
- ✗ "Shop now" → ✓ "I finally broke down companion planting by zone — here's what I learned"

### TikTok Optimization (From TRACK_B_AUDIENCE_RESEARCH.md + LAUNCH_WEEK_GROWTH_STRATEGY.md)

**Algorithm Reality (2026)**:
- Follower count is a non-factor; every video independently evaluated
- 70% completion rate threshold for FYP distribution
- Interest Graph, not Social Graph (follower count doesn't matter)

**Content Structure Rules**:
1. **First 45 seconds = everything**. Most useful information must land by :45, not in the final 10 seconds.
2. **Completion rate optimization**: If >70% of viewers finish, TikTok pushes to next test audience. If <50%, it stops.
3. **Hook quality = critical**: First 3 seconds determine 70% of completion.

**Optimal Posting Times for TikTok** (cluster-specific):

| Cluster | Optimal Time | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| Cluster 1 (Urban foragers, ages 26–38) | 7:00–8:00am, 6:00–7:00pm | Commute times + evening discovery |
| Cluster 2 (Homesteaders, ages 28–50) | 9:00–10:00am, 8:00–9:00pm | Morning routine, evening farm time break |
| Cluster 3 (Preppers, all day, peak 5–7pm) | 5:00–6:00pm | Evening content consumption |

**Posting Frequency**: 4–5 TikTok videos per week (not daily, but consistent). Consistency trains the algorithm that your content is reliable plant ID education, not random creator content.

**Topical Demand Monitoring**:
When @alexisnikole, @chaoticforager, or @wellfedwild post breakout content, TikTok surfaces similar content to satisfy demand. **Post complementary or response content within 24–48 hours** to ride the interest wave (check these accounts daily 5–6pm).

## HASHTAG STRATEGY BY POST (Integrated from LAUNCH_WEEK_GROWTH_STRATEGY.md)

### Instagram Hashtag Discipline Rules (2026)

**Rule 1**: Use exactly 5 hashtags (more than 5 suppresses reach; fewer wastes potential)
**Rule 2**: Never mix cohort tags in same post (#foraging + #prepper dilutes targeting; algorithm treats them as separate clusters)
**Rule 3**: Always include #seedwarden (build brand search from Day 1)
**Rule 4**: Rotate secondary 2–4 hashtags per post; primary hashtags stay constant

**Primary Hashtags (On Every Post)**:
- #foraging (612K+ posts)
- #homesteading (1M+ posts)
- #seedwarden (0 posts — build brand search)
- #wildedibles (150K–250K posts)
- One secondary based on content type (see below)

**Secondary Hashtags by Content Type** (pick ONE to rotate):

| Content Type | Hashtag | Volume | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seed content | #seedsaving (180K+) | Seed saving, homesteader cohort |
| Companion planting | #permaculture (350K+) | Food forest, integrated systems |
| Plant ID | #plantidentification (90K–120K) | Forager cohort, ID-specific |
| Foraging technique | #wildcrafting (200K+) | Foraging + medicinal plants |
| Zone/Native content | #nativeplants (280K+) | Native plants, regional guide |

**Example Instagram Hashtag Sets** (by week):

- Week 1 (brand launch): #foraging #homesteading #seedwarden #wildedibles #plantidentification
- Week 2 (seed focus): #foraging #homesteading #seedwarden #seedsaving #wildcrafting
- Week 3 (companion planting): #foraging #homesteading #seedwarden #permaculture #foodforest
- Week 4 (retention + lifestyle): #foraging #homesteading #seedwarden #nativeplants #growyourown

### TikTok Hashtag Strategy

**Primary hashtags (on every video)**:
- #foraging (highest-volume foraging tag)
- #wildedibles (plant ID videos)
- #homesteading (homestead + cultivation)

**Secondary hashtags** (rotate 1–2):
- #plantidentification (plant ID focus)
- #homestead (variant of primary, for algorithm confirmation)
- #seedsaving (seed content)
- #prepping (for Cluster 3 audience)

**Hashtag use model**: Caption keywords first (algorithm priority), hashtags as secondary confirmation signal.

### Pinterest Hashtag Strategy

Pinterest doesn't use hashtags in traditional sense. Instead:
- **Board names** = exact search phrases (e.g., "Zone 5 Gardening," "Plant Identification")
- **Pin descriptions** = 150–300 words, keyword-rich, natural language (includes relevant keywords naturally, not forced)
- **Pin titles** = lead with most specific keyword phrase (e.g., "Dangerous Look-alikes: Poison Hemlock vs. Wild Carrot in Zone 5")

**Example Pinterest board structure**:
- Heirloom Seeds & Growing Guides (primary product board)
- Zone 5 Gardening (regional planning)
- Plant Identification (educational + discovery)
- Foraging Guides (wild edibles)
- Companion Planting for Homesteads (technique)

## COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION CALLOUTS — Where to Emphasize the "Wild+Cultivated+Ecosystem" Bridge

### The Gap Seedwarden Fills (From COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING_ANALYSIS.md)

**Gap 1**: Zero competitors produce Zone 5/Midwest-specific digital products. @mnforager and @thehomesteadingrd create Midwest content but don't sell guides. **Your advantage**: "Research-backed Zone 5 field guides, no national generics."
