## Section 3: Risk Register

Six primary risks with likelihood, impact, mitigation, and escalation criteria. Risk scoring: Probability 1–3 × Impact 1–3 = Score. Score 4+ requires explicit contingency with a trigger action.

### Risk 1: Photo Shoot Weather Delay or Equipment Failure

**Scenario**: Outdoor photography for lifestyle or fresh plant shots is rained out or postponed. Camera or lighting equipment fails mid-session.

**Likelihood**: Medium (25–40%). Outdoor sessions in late June are subject to weather variability in most US regions. Equipment failure probability is lower but non-zero.

**Impact**: Low–Medium. Studio-only photography is unaffected by weather. Outdoor lifestyle shots affect Etsy listing slots 3–5 for the Women's Health bundle. Wikimedia CC covers all identification shots regardless.

**Score**: 4 — contingency required.

**Mitigation**:
- Schedule studio photography session (dried herbs, flat-lays) indoors June 17–21 as the primary session. Outdoor lifestyle shots are supplementary, not blocking.
- Pre-stage 3–5 Wikimedia CC and Unsplash CC0 practitioner lifestyle images for each bundle before June 17. These are documented in `phase-3-botanical-stock-list.md` and require zero photography time.
- Use dried herb specimens (Mountain Rose Herbs) for all preparation shots — weather-independent.

**Escalation criteria**: If outdoor session cannot occur before June 21 (end of pre-sprint window), substitute stock practitioner lifestyle images from the pre-staged Unsplash CC0 list. This costs 0 hours and 0 schedule impact. Document substitution in WORKLOG.md.

**If shoot slips 7+ days**: Use stock images for Women's Health and Respiratory bundle cover slots 3–5. Advance writing timeline by 2 days (photos no longer compete with writing on June 25–26). Log the decision.

**Impact**: Medium. Delayed dried herbs compress the studio photography session and delay hero image selection for cover design (begins June 23). Cover design sessions can be pushed 2–3 days without missing the July 3 design lock.

### Risk 3: Writing Overrun — Pace Falls Below 250 Words/Hour

**Scenario**: Actual writing velocity is 200–250 words/hour instead of 300–350, adding 15–20 hours to total sprint. Week 1 Women's Health writing is the highest-risk period — Black Cohosh and Vitex are the most research-intensive species in the sprint.

**Likelihood**: Medium (25–40%). Writing pace estimates are based on pre-compiled outlines. If outlines require more in-session research than expected, or if revision rounds are heavier, daily hours extend.

**Impact**: High. At 200 words/hour, Women's Health (3,800 words) requires 19 hours — 4–5 hours more than estimated. This directly impacts the June 28 export target and could push Women's Health upload from June 29 to July 1.

**Score**: 6 — contingency required.

**Mitigation**:
- Use `phase-3-medicinal-herbs-content-outline.md` as the writing template. Pre-compiled outlines reduce in-session research time from 3–4 hours per bundle to 1–2 hours.
- Front-load rough drafts in Week 1 — complete a full rough draft before revising anything. A complete rough draft of Women's Health at 250 words/hour is still uploadable; a polished partial draft is not.
- Reduce shared-species second-occurrence sections to 250 words minimum (from 300–400 target) if pace falls below 13 hours per bundle. This saves 4–8 hours with minimal quality impact.

**Escalation criteria — June 24 EOD Pace Gate**: If Women's Health word count is below 2,500 words at EOD June 24:
- Activate Option C (3-bundle scope: Women's Health, Respiratory, Sleep) on June 25 morning.
- Defer Immunity to August 3 and Digestive to August 17.
- Women's Health June 29 + Respiratory July 6–7 + Sleep July 13 upload dates are fully intact.
