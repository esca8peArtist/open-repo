## Part 4: Photography Task Breakdown

### Can This Be Done in One Day or Does It Need 3–4 Days?

Photography cannot be done in one day for a 5-bundle launch. Here is why: the five bundles require different shot types — fresh seedlings, mature flowering plants, dried herb flat-lays, and lifestyle/desk mockups. Seedlings are only available during germination windows (typically June 3–15 for seeds started late May). Mature flowering specimens have a different timing window. Dried herb studio work requires Mountain Rose Herbs delivery (June 17–21). These windows are non-overlapping.

The practical structure is a 4-week pre-sprint track running May 26 through June 21, followed by occasional in-sprint studio sessions during lighter writing days (June 23–26). No photography happens during the critical writing days (June 22–24, June 29–July 2).

### Four-Week Pre-Sprint Photography Track

**Week 1 (May 26 – June 2): Props acquisition and studio setup**
- Acquire: kraft paper (background material, 24x36 inch minimum), mortar and pestle (ceramic preferred), 4–6 small glass jars, natural linen cloth (30x30 inch), wooden serving board or tray, small reflector card. Budget $60–100.
- Confirm studio location: north-facing window, minimum 36x24 inch flat surface, test light quality at 9 AM, noon, and 2 PM. No ring light — natural diffuse light only.
- Download and verify Wikimedia CC-BY-SA images for all 14 species. Log each source URL, license, and filename in WORKLOG.md. This is the attribution logging required before any image can appear in a published guide.
- Target: studio location confirmed, 70% of props acquired, attribution log started.

**Week 2 (June 3–9): Seedling and juvenile state photography**
- Calendula, Red Clover, Lemon Balm, Thyme, Lavender seeds started May 26–27 should be showing true leaves.
- Shoot: seedling in germination tray (overhead), seedling in 4-inch pot (front angle), leaf close-up (flat on kraft paper). 2–3 hours of shooting.
- If Black Cohosh ordered June 8 arrives by June 20–22: photograph immediately on arrival.
- Target: 30–40 images captured, culled to 20–30 keepers at 2400x2400px.

**Week 3 (June 10–16): Mature and flowering specimen photography**
- Black Cohosh (if ordered May 25–June 8 arrives): photograph young plant, compound leaf structure, root division if visible.
- Elderberry from local nursery (purchased early June): photograph branch, leaf, stem. If Prairie Moon bare-root arrives: photograph on arrival.
- Any Tier 3 plants arriving early: photograph on receipt.
- iNaturalist CC-BY is the automatic fallback for any species not yet arrived: Passionflower (abundant SE US coverage), Valerian (Northeast US coverage), Echinacea both species.
- Target: 40–50 images captured, attribution notes for all stock sources logged.

**Week 4 (June 17–21): Dried herb studio session and full edit/cull**
- Mountain Rose Herbs dried herbs arrive June 17–21 (order by June 13 for East Coast 7–10 day transit).
- Studio session: 3 hours across June 17–18. Five bundle flat-lays (each bundle's herbs arranged on kraft paper or linen), mortar-and-pestle preparations shots (2–3 per bundle), small glass jar close-ups, lifestyle desk shot (guide mockup among dried herbs, notebook, mug).
- Full edit and cull: 100–150 raw images reduced to 50–60 keepers at 2400x2400px. Warm preset: +0.2 exposure, -20 highlights, +15 shadows. Export to five bundle folders.
- Flag cover hero image candidate for each bundle.
- Complete attribution log: every Wikimedia CC and iNaturalist source logged in WORKLOG.md with species, source URL, license type, filename, and date retrieved.

### Equipment and Props Checklist

- Camera or smartphone with manual exposure control (minimum 12 MP). No flash.
- Natural light source: north-facing window, minimum 2 feet wide.
- White foam-core or natural paper reflector (fill shadow opposite window).
- Backgrounds: unbleached kraft paper, natural linen cloth, weathered wooden board.
- Props: ceramic mortar and pestle, 4–6 small glass jars (4 oz preferred), dried botanical bundles from Mountain Rose Herbs order, small clay or ceramic bowl.
- Post-processing: Lightroom or equivalent. Consistent warm preset applied to all images.
- Storage: organized by bundle (WH/, RESP/, IMM/, SLP/, DIG/) with attribution CSV per folder.

## Part 6: Critical Path Analysis

### What Is Actually on the Critical Path

The critical path is the sequence of tasks with zero float. Any delay on the critical path delays the June 29 first upload date. Tasks off the critical path have float available.

**Primary critical path (controlling June 29 first upload):**

```
May 30: Scope decision confirmed (0 float — all production blocked)
    |
June 8: Goldenseal path confirmed (0 float — Immunity photography unresolved without)
    |
June 15: Elderberry + Mountain Rose Herbs ordered (0 float)
    |
June 17–21: Dried herb studio photography (1 day float)
    |
June 21: Canva brand kit loaded + one zone card export-tested (0 float after this date)
    |
June 22: Sprint Day 1 — Women's Health writing begins (0 float)
    |
June 23: Women's Health Day 2 — Vitex + Red Clover (1 day float)
    |
June 24: Women's Health Day 3 — Calendula + Lavender (0 float — pace gate)
    |
June 28: Women's Health self-edit pass complete (2 day float)
    |
June 29: Women's Health PDF export + upload to Etsy (0 float — MILESTONE 1)
```

**What compresses versus what serializes:**

Tasks that can be compressed (parallel execution possible):
- Cover design and zone card design run entirely parallel to writing. Design sessions are scheduled on the same days as writing but use different cognitive resources. A fatigued writer can shift to 1–2 hours of Canva design work without competing with writing.
- Photography (pre-sprint track) is entirely non-competing with writing. Four weeks of pre-sprint photography produce all needed assets before the first writing day.
- Bundle introductions (written June 1–21 pre-sprint) eliminate blank-page delay on Day 1 of each bundle. This compression saves approximately 45–60 minutes per bundle.

Tasks that are truly serialized (cannot be compressed):
- Women's Health writing must be substantially complete before Women's Health PDF export (June 28). The export-ready state requires writing, editing, design, and photo sourcing all resolved.
- Goldenseal decision (June 8) must happen before Immunity bundle photography strategy is resolved. If decision is delayed past June 8, photography path defaults to Wikimedia CC automatically — but the decision itself still gates the strategy.
- Writing sequencing for efficiency: Women's Health before Respiratory before Immunity (shared Echinacea content), and Women's Health before Digestive (shared Calendula content). These are efficiency recommendations, not hard dependencies — any bundle can be written out of sequence, it just costs 2–4 additional hours.

**The single hardest constraint in the sprint**: Women's Health Days 1–3 (June 22–24). These three days have zero float by Day 3. They require 14–15 hours of writing across three days (5 hours/day). If Day 3 falls below 2,500 total Women's Health words, the sprint scope must shift to Option C immediately — deferring Immunity and Digestive to August but preserving all three primary upload dates (June 29, July 6–7, July 13).

**Design bottleneck**: The Digestive bundle cover has zero float with a July 3 EOD design lock. This is the latest of all five covers and blocks all post-sprint design refinement. All five cover images must be final by July 3. After July 3, no design changes are made until post-launch.

## Part 9: Key Research Questions Answered

**Q1: How long for Mountain Rose Herbs + Strictly Medicinal to confirm supply?**
Mountain Rose Herbs: standard retail order processes within 1 business day; East Coast transit 7–10 business days. For June 21 arrival, order by June 13. Strictly Medicinal Seeds: USPS shipping with 2–3 week lead time typical for plant orders in spring/summer. June 8 order = late June to early July arrival.

**Q2: Minimum viable writing scope per bundle — can a bundle launch with 1 herb or must all herbs be ready simultaneously?**
All species in a bundle must be complete before upload. The bundle is a single PDF product — an incomplete bundle with placeholder text for one species cannot be uploaded to Etsy as a purchasable product. However, a bundle can be updated post-launch: if Goldenseal content needs revision, it can be replaced in the PDF and re-uploaded to Etsy without removing the listing. This means launch with the complete first-draft version, then use v1.1 updates to incorporate photo upgrades and any content refinements. A bundle does not need to be perfect at launch — it needs to be complete and FTC-compliant.

**Q3: Photography — 1 day or 3–4 days?**
3–4 weeks of pre-sprint sessions, not 1–4 days during the sprint. The different shot types (seedlings, mature flowering specimens, dried herb flat-lays) correspond to different timing windows that do not collapse into a single day. However, each individual session is 2–3 hours, not a full day. A realistic schedule is: one 3-hour seedling session (June 3–9), one 3-hour mature specimen session (June 10–16), one 3-hour dried herb studio session (June 17–21), and occasional 1–2 hour in-sprint sessions on lighter writing days. Total photography hours: approximately 36 pre-sprint plus 8–10 in-sprint = 44–46 hours across the 8-week window.

**Q4: Canva design — zone card template reusable (4–6 hours total) or per-bundle (20–30 hours)?**
Template is reusable. First-time Phase 3 adaptation of the Phase 2 template: 2 hours. Each of five zone card fills: 0.8 hours each. Total zone card time: 6 hours. Total design time including covers: 12.5 hours. Per-bundle from-scratch production would require 3–4 hours each = 15–20 hours. Template reuse is the correct approach.

**Q5: Supplier payment terms and hard budget constraints?**
No net-30 or advance purchase requirements. All suppliers (Prairie Moon, Strictly Medicinal, Mountain Rose Herbs, Frontier Co-op) charge at time of order via standard retail checkout. No supplier creates a budget gate beyond the order placement deadlines. The total procurement budget of $320–$485 is the full cash outlay.

**Q6: If Goldenseal is unavailable June 10, what is the recovery path?**
Three paths, in order of preference. Path A (substitute species within bundle): Barberry (*Berberis vulgaris*) also contains berberine, shares the antimicrobial-activity research framing, and has no CITES restrictions. The CITES Appendix II sidebar in the Immunity bundle (the most legally distinctive content in the portfolio) becomes a "why Goldenseal is preferred over Barberry for ethical reasons" comparison — this is actually stronger content for the practitioner audience. Sourcing risk: zero. Cost: negligible. Path B (launch without Goldenseal photography, Wikimedia CC only): guide launches with Wikimedia CC-BY-SA Goldenseal images. All guide text including the CITES sidebar is present. Photography upgrade comes post-launch as v1.1. Zero delay, zero revenue impact. Path C (delay Immunity bundle 2 weeks): Immunity uploads August 3 instead of July 20, Digestive uploads August 17. Revenue delay: $180–$350 in first-month gross at 3–5 unit sales. This path is least preferred because the Wikimedia CC option is genuinely sufficient for launch quality — delaying for photography is not warranted.
