# May 10 Go/No-Go Decision Framework

**Prepared**: May 7, 2026
**Decision date**: May 10, 2026 (3 days from now)
**Decision question**: Is May 30, 2026 still the viable Phase 2 launch date?

This document gives you a binary answer by end of May 9 evening. You either enter the May 10 photo shoot with confidence in May 30, or you formally shift to June 6 and adjust the next 23 days accordingly. There is no third state.

---

## The Decision Is Not "Launch vs. No Launch"

Before reading the criteria: the May 30 launch is not at risk of complete failure. Phase 2 infrastructure is 100% complete. What is at stake is the *scope* of launch day.

- **May 30 full launch**: Social accounts live, email automation collecting subscribers, zone cards delivering, photo shoot complete, all Etsy products with lifestyle images, full 60-day content schedule loaded.
- **May 30 soft launch (June 6 full launch)**: Social accounts live, email automation live, zone cards delivering, Cluster D+E Etsy images uploaded, some social content live — but lifestyle photography for Clusters A, B, C not complete. Etsy slots 4-5 for 12 products still showing only 3 images.
- **June 6 full launch**: Everything above, plus all 30 lifestyle photos edited, exported, uploaded to Etsy, and social content fully scheduled.

The go/no-go at May 10 determines which of these you are executing. Both are legitimate. The decision is about setting accurate expectations and not trying to do May 30 full launch from a June 6 starting position.

## Section 1: Criteria for a May 10 "Go" Decision

All five items below must be TRUE by end of day May 9 (before the shoot begins May 10 morning). These are verifiable facts, not estimates.

### Criterion 1: Social Accounts Exist and Are Business Type

**How to verify**: Open Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest from a logged-out browser. Confirm each of these URLs resolves to a live Seedwarden profile:
- instagram.com/seedwarden (or fallback handle)
- tiktok.com/@seedwarden (or fallback)
- pinterest.com/seedwarden (or fallback)

**What "Business" means**: On Instagram, the profile shows category text below the name (e.g., "Product/service"). On TikTok, the profile has the Business label in settings. On Pinterest, the Analytics tab appears in the top navigation.

**Minimum acceptable**: Two of three platforms live. Instagram is the non-negotiable minimum. TikTok can follow one to two weeks later without affecting May 30 metrics.

**If this criterion fails**: Do not attempt May 30 full launch. Social accounts are not optional — without them, there is nowhere for any traffic to land and no mechanism for the Day 1 Reel to generate subscribers.

### Criterion 2: Kit Landing Page Is Published and Accessible

**How to verify**: Open an incognito browser window. Navigate to the Kit landing page URL. Confirm the page loads with the headline "Your Free Zone Quick-Start Card", the three-field form (first name, email, growing zone), and the "Send My Zone Card" button. Fill in the form and confirm it submits without error. You do not need the full automation working by May 10 — the landing page must simply be live and collecting form submissions.

**What "published" means**: The URL is publicly accessible without a login. Anyone who clicks the bio link reaches the form.

**If this criterion fails**: Email list building has not started. Every day the landing page is not live is a day of subscriber momentum lost.

### Criterion 3: Bio Links Are Active on All Live Platforms

**How to verify**: Open each live social account in a logged-out browser. Click the link in bio. Confirm it opens the Kit landing page, not a broken URL or placeholder.

**This is a 10-minute task after Criteria 1 and 2 are met.** If accounts are live but bio links are not set, complete this immediately — it takes longer to notice the problem than to fix it.

### Criterion 4: Canva Brand Kit Exists with All Colors, Fonts, and Logo

**How to verify**: Log in to canva.com. Open Brand Hub. The Seedwarden Brand Kit should show:
- 10 color swatches (6 brand colors + 4 zone band colors), or minimum 6 brand colors
- 3 fonts: Playfair Display (heading), Lato (body), Cormorant Garamond (accent)
- The seedwarden_logo_1.png uploaded to the Logos section

**Why this matters at May 10**: Without the Brand Kit, zone card builds take 10-15 minutes longer per card. More importantly, having it ready means the Zone 5 master card is either complete or substantially in progress, which is what Criterion 5 checks.

**If Brand Hub is unavailable on your Canva tier**: Start building zone cards with manually configured styles. Do not wait for Brand Hub. A zone card built without a Brand Kit is identical to one built with it — Brand Kit is just a shortcut.

### Criterion 5: Zone 5 Master Card Build Has Started (50% Minimum)

**How to verify**: Open Canva. The Zone 5 master zone card template should exist as a design in your account. It should show a header with the Seedwarden logo, a zone band in the Temperate green (#2D5016), at minimum the frost date section and the "Best crops right now" section populated with Zone 5 content from CANVA_ZONE_CARD_BATCH_WORKFLOW.md.

**"50% complete" means**: The layout is established, the brand elements are placed, and at least two of the four content sections are populated with actual Zone 5 data (not placeholder text).

**Why this gates the decision**: Zone 5 is the most time-consuming card (150-180 min) and all other zone cards clone from it. If Zone 5 has not been started by May 10, there is no realistic path to completing all 8 cards by May 17, which means Kit Email 1 variants cannot be built until May 20-21 at the earliest, which means Kit automation cannot be wired and tested until May 24-25, which leaves only 5 days for the pre-launch buffer. That is a compressible but uncomfortable margin.

## Section 3: Recovery Procedures for Each Failed Criterion

### If Criterion 1 Fails (Social Accounts Not Created)

**Recovery window**: May 10 can still be a go if accounts are created before 8am May 10 (prior to shoot start).

**Action**: Create Instagram and Kit landing page during the evening of May 9 (60-90 min). Set a phone alarm for 6:30am May 10. Complete the remaining platforms before the shoot begins at 9am.

**Triage rule**: Instagram + Kit landing page is the minimum viable set. TikTok and Pinterest can follow May 11-12. Do not delay the shoot to create accounts.

**If accounts cannot be created until after May 10**: Begin Criterion 1 on May 11. Shift to June 6 launch scope. Social accounts being 10 days late is a 10-day delay in list-building momentum. You can still launch May 30, but launch day has no subscriber list to email and no social posting history — it is a Day 1 from scratch. That is viable but not what Phase 2 was designed to deliver.

### If Criterion 2 Fails (Landing Page Not Published)

**Recovery window**: Landing page can be built in 30-45 minutes. Build it first thing May 10 before leaving for the photo shoot setup if necessary.

**Action**: The landing page spec is fully written in TRACK_B_EMAIL_AUTOMATION_KIT_GUIDE.md Step 3. It requires no copywriting decisions. Open kit.co, follow the steps, publish. If the landing page form is not ready to test by May 10, the automation timeline slips by one week.

**Acceptable May 10 state**: Landing page published with the form live. The automation (zone routing, Email 1 delivery) does not need to be wired by May 10 — that is a May 17-22 task. The page just needs to collect form submissions.

### If Criterion 3 Fails (Bio Links Not Set)

**This is a 10-minute fix.** Go to each live social account, edit the profile, add the landing page URL. This cannot block the May 30 decision on its own — it only fails if Criteria 1 and 2 also fail, in which case those are the real problems.

### If Criterion 4 Fails (Brand Kit Not Set Up)

**Recovery**: Set up the Brand Kit during the lunch break on May 10 (1:30pm-3:00pm, between Cluster A and Cluster B shoots). The 30-minute Brand Kit setup does not require the computer — it can be done on a phone or tablet. Begin Zone 5 card build May 11 afternoon after Cluster C shoot.

**Impact**: Zone 5 master card build shifts from target start May 8 to actual start May 11. This compresses the zone card build window by 3 days. Zone 5 must be complete by May 12, Zones 6, 3, 4 by May 14, Zones 7, 8, 9, 10 by May 17. This schedule is achievable but requires 60-90 minutes of Canva work each evening May 11-17.

### If Criterion 5 Fails (Zone 5 Master Card Not Started)

**Recovery**: Begin Zone 5 card on May 10 evening after the shoot. The shoot ends at 5pm. Set aside 90 minutes (5:30pm-7:00pm) to establish the Zone 5 layout and complete at least the header, zone band, and frost date section.

**Impact on May 30**: If Zone 5 build starts May 10, the full 8-card set cannot realistically be complete before May 18-19. Google Drive upload and Kit email building shifts to May 19-20. Kit automation wiring shifts to May 22-23. End-to-end testing shifts to May 23-24. This leaves 6 days of buffer before May 30. That is tight but viable if no other items slip.

**Red line**: If Zone 5 has not been started by May 12, shift to June 6 for the Kit automation component. You can still launch May 30 with social accounts and Etsy updates, but the email automation will not be fully live and tested by May 30.

## Section 4: Contingency Launch Date — June 6, 2026

If the May 30 Go/No-Go assessment on May 9 shows three or more criteria failing, or if the shoot cannot happen on May 10-11, the formal contingency launch date is June 6, 2026.

### What June 6 Means in Practice

June 6 is not a failure. It is the documented contingency from the beginning of Track B planning (TRACK_B_FINAL_EXECUTION_GUIDE.md Section 4). The delay adds 7 days to every remaining task window.

**What changes with a June 6 launch**:

| Item | May 30 Plan | June 6 Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| Photo shoot dates | May 10-11 | May 17-18 (same format, same props, same shot list) |
| Image editing | May 13-14 | May 19-20 |
| Etsy lifestyle image uploads (A, B, C) | May 22-25 | May 28-June 3 |
| Kit automation live and tested | May 22 | May 28-29 |
| Launch-week scheduling loaded | May 25-29 | June 1-5 |
| Kit launch broadcast | May 30 | June 6 |
| Instagram launch post | May 30 | June 6 |

**What does NOT change with June 6**:
- Social account creation — still urgent, still needs to happen today or tomorrow
- Brand Kit setup — still needs to happen by May 8-9
- Cluster D+E compositing — still targets May 15
- Zone card builds — still targets May 8-17 (this window is unchanged)
- Kit account and landing page — still need to be live this week (bio links must work as soon as accounts exist)

**The key insight**: Shifting to June 6 does not pause everything. It only moves the shoot-dependent items. Account creation, Brand Kit, zone card builds, and Kit automation setup all proceed on the same timeline regardless of whether the final launch date is May 30 or June 6.

### What a June 6 Shift Impacts Externally

- The social accounts will have 3 extra weeks of content before launch day — which is better, not worse.
- The email list will have had more time to build before the launch broadcast.
- The Week 2 checkpoint metrics (May 14 target: 50 Instagram followers, 500 Pinterest monthly views) remain valid as a mid-point measurement even if the final launch shifts.
- Etsy sales from Clusters D+E can begin May 15 regardless of the June 6 shift — those images upload independently.

### Announcing a June 6 Shift

No public announcement is necessary. Seedwarden has no subscribers yet and no content live. There is no audience to inform. Simply update the launch date in WORKLOG.md and proceed with the June 6 adjusted timeline.

All other May 7 actions are in the day-by-day checklist in may-30-launch-readiness-audit.md.

## Section 6: Making the Call on May 9 Evening

At the end of May 9, run the decision matrix in Section 2. Score each criterion as True or False. Apply the result:

- 5/5 True: May 30 full launch confirmed. Begin shoot May 10 with no adjustments.
- 4/5 True (one failure): Review the failed criterion against the recovery procedures in Section 3. Apply the appropriate recovery. Most single-criterion failures are recoverable within 48-72 hours without shifting the launch date.
- 3/5 True or fewer: Formally shift to June 6. Update WORKLOG.md with the date and criteria that failed. Do not attempt to run the May 30 timeline on a 3-criteria-deficient base — the compression will produce errors that are harder to fix than the original delays.

**The decision framework is designed so you spend under 10 minutes making the call.** The data collection (checking each criterion against a live URL or open application) takes 20 minutes. The decision itself, once the data is in, follows directly from the matrix. There is no judgment call required.
