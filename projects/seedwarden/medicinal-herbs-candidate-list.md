# Medicinal Herbs Candidate List — Phase 3

**Prepared**: May 7, 2026
**Scope**: 12 primary species for Phase 3 medicinal guide production. Selected for: (a) Etsy keyword demand in the herbalist/practitioner niche, (b) available cultivated seed/plant sources for ethical cultivation content, (c) sufficient photo sourcing paths (Wikimedia Commons, Unsplash, botanical gardens), and (d) meaningful differentiation from existing PLR content (i.e., a conservation or cultivation angle that PLR misses).

Each species is mapped to one of the five Phase 3 bundle themes. Species appearing in multiple bundles are noted.

---

## Species 1 — Black Cohosh (*Actaea racemosa*)

**Bundle theme**: Women's Health (primary); Immunity (supporting)
**Conservation status**: United Plant Savers At-Risk; Massachusetts Endangered; ~95% wild-harvested commercially
**Cultivation source availability**: Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Fedco, Vermont Wildflower Farm
**Photo sourcing**: iNaturalist CC-BY (abundant June–August bloom); Vermont Wildflower Farm contact for growing-stock photos; Wikimedia Commons has multiple CC-licensed habit images
**Guide angle**: The 95% wild-harvest statistic is the hook. Forest farming and shade-garden cultivation is practical and well-documented. The menopausal herb market's conservation blind spot is the differentiation no PLR guide covers. Pair with the UpS Forest Grown Verified certification program as the sourcing recommendation.
**FTC note**: Traditional and historical use claims only. Do not frame as a menopausal symptom treatment; frame as "historically used for women's hormonal health in Cherokee and Appalachian folk medicine."

**Margin model**:
- As part of Women's Health bundle at $22: development cost allocated at ~$40/species (6 species at ~$280 total bundle COGS). Unit break-even: 13 sales. Month 3 target: 25+ sales.
- As standalone single-herb guide at $16: break-even ~22 units.

## Species 2 — Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*)

**Bundle theme**: Immunity (primary); Respiratory Health (secondary)
**Conservation status**: CITES Appendix II (wild roots/rhizomes require export permit; cultivated unrestricted); endangered in 7 states; UpS At-Risk
**Cultivation source availability**: Johnny's Selected Seeds, Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Southern Exposure Seed Exchange
**Photo sourcing**: NC Botanical Garden (media contact), Missouri Botanical Garden (media contact), iNaturalist CC-BY. Forest farming operations in WV/KY welcome to approach for photo permission.
**Guide angle**: CITES Appendix II status is the lead — it explains why wild goldenseal is legally regulated at international trade level and why cultivated sources matter. The guide teaches forest farming technique alongside the conservation story. The UpS Forest Grown Verified goldenseal program in Virginia is a concrete sourcing recommendation buyers can act on.
**FTC note**: Antibiotic-property research exists in the literature (berberine studies). Frame as "contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in laboratory settings for antimicrobial properties" — not "treats infections." The distinction is material for FTC compliance.

**Margin model**: Identical to Black Cohosh. As bundle component: ~$40 allocated COGS. As standalone guide at $16: break-even 22 units.

## Species 3 — Elderberry (*Sambucus nigra* and *S. canadensis*)

**Bundle theme**: Respiratory Health (primary); Immunity Support (primary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Common native shrub. Cultivated varieties widely available.
**Cultivation source availability**: All major nurseries; multiple cultivated varieties (Adams, Bob Gordon, Bob's Select) documented for high berry yield. Extremely accessible for home cultivation content.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons has abundant CC-licensed habit and flower images. iNaturalist CC-BY rich. Unsplash has lifestyle elderberry photography.
**Guide angle**: Highest-searched medicinal herb on Etsy in the immune support category. The cultivation guide (elderberry shrub establishment, harvest timing, processing) is the differentiation — most Etsy elderberry guides are syrup recipes, not cultivation manuals. Pair with elderflower culinary use (connects to forager cohort).
**FTC note**: Elderberry has the most studied immune-related evidence of any herb in this list (Sambucol clinical trials for flu duration). Frame as "studied in randomized controlled trials for cold and flu duration" — not "prevents or treats colds." Cite the studies descriptively without making the disease claim.

**Margin model**: As Respiratory bundle component and Immunity bundle component — appears in two bundles, enabling cross-listing. Bundle pricing unchanged; double the keyword surfaces for one content investment.

## Species 4 — Echinacea (*Echinacea purpurea*, *E. angustifolia*)

**Bundle theme**: Immunity Support (primary); Respiratory Health (supporting)
**Conservation status**: *E. angustifolia* is UpS At-Risk (overharvested prairie species); *E. purpurea* is common and widely cultivated. The guide distinguishes the two species explicitly — a content angle that PLR misses entirely.
**Cultivation source availability**: All major seed suppliers carry *E. purpurea*; *E. angustifolia* from Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds (less available). Native range restoration content possible for *E. angustifolia*.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons abundant for both species. iNaturalist CC-BY rich.
**Guide angle**: The "two echinacea" story — one threatened, one common — maps directly onto the cultivation-vs-wildcrafting theme. Practitioners specifically want to know which species is which and which to source ethically.
**FTC note**: Most heavily researched herb in this list. Multiple meta-analyses on cold duration. Frame as "studied for immune function support" — not "prevents colds." The research exists; the claim just needs appropriate qualification.

**Margin model**: Bundle component. Primary revenue driver in Immunity bundle.

## Species 5 — Valerian (*Valeriana officinalis*)

**Bundle theme**: Sleep and Nervines (primary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Common naturalized species across North America.
**Cultivation source availability**: Widely cultivated; most herb nurseries stock starts. Root-division propagation is the standard cultivation method — content-rich guide angle.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons CC-licensed habit and flower images available. Unsplash has lifestyle photography with lavender/valerian aesthetic.
**Guide angle**: The "sleep herb" identity is strong but the cultivation content is thin on Etsy. Most valerian guides describe the supplement form, not how to grow the root. A cultivation-focused guide with harvest timing (roots in second year, autumn) and basic drying method differentiates immediately.
**FTC note**: Sleep claims are sensitive. Frame as "traditionally used as a sleep-supporting herb in European herbalism" — not "treats insomnia." The word "sleep" can appear in the bundle title (Sleep and Nervines Guide) as a theme descriptor; it becomes a medical claim only if attached to a treatment promise.

**Margin model**: Sleep bundle anchor species. Bundle at $20: break-even 16 units.

## Species 6 — Lemon Balm (*Melissa officinalis*)

**Bundle theme**: Sleep and Nervines (primary); Digestive Support (secondary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Common garden herb; widely naturalized.
**Cultivation source availability**: Universal. Every herb nursery carries it; seeds at all suppliers.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons abundant. Unsplash lifestyle available.
**Guide angle**: The forager cohort overlap is strong — lemon balm is edible and the tea preparation is the gateway use. The nervine and carminative properties connect Sleep and Digestive bundles. A single species page can be shared between two bundles (with minor framing adjustments) for zero additional production cost.
**FTC note**: Mild claims territory. "Used traditionally for relaxation and digestive comfort" is accurate and within historical use framing.

**Margin model**: Dual-bundle leverage — content produced once, appears in two $20 bundles. Effective COGS halved per bundle.

## Species 7 — Lavender (*Lavandula angustifolia*)

**Bundle theme**: Sleep and Nervines (primary); Women's Health (supporting)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Native to Mediterranean; widely cultivated.
**Cultivation source availability**: Universal. Zone 5–9 cultivation well-documented; container growing content for zones 3–4.
**Photo sourcing**: Best Wikimedia Commons sourcing of any herb on this list. Lifestyle photography on Unsplash abundant.
**Guide angle**: Lavender is the highest-recognition herb in the wellness market. The cultivation guide (English lavender vs. French vs. hybrid varieties, zone-specific growing, distillation vs. fresh harvest) adds depth that PLR ignores. The Women's Health connection (hormonal balance support in traditional herbalism) is a secondary but real use.
**FTC note**: Very mild claim territory. "Used traditionally for relaxation and stress support" is the standard framing. The Sleep bundle title can include "Lavender" — that is a product description, not a medical claim.

**Margin model**: Sleep bundle co-anchor. High visual appeal drives clickthrough from thumbnail.

## Species 8 — Mullein (*Verbascum thapsus*)

**Bundle theme**: Respiratory Health (primary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Common roadside biennial; invasive in some western states.
**Cultivation source availability**: Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds, and most herb suppliers. Seed collection from wild plants in non-invasive-listed states is legal and free.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons exceptional — the distinctive tall flower spike is widely photographed. iNaturalist CC-BY rich.
**Guide angle**: The "roadside apothecary" framing is strong — mullein is legally harvestable, recognizable, and under-documented as a cultivation subject. The respiratory use (leaf tea for dry coughs; ear oil from flowers) is one of the most commonly searched folk remedies. Differentiates Respiratory bundle from an elderberry-only guide.
**FTC note**: "Traditionally used for respiratory support" — standard folk medicine historical use framing. The ear oil preparation involves oleic acid maceration, which is a food preparation method, not a drug manufacturing claim.

**Margin model**: Respiratory bundle component. Co-anchor with Elderberry.

---

## Species 9 — Calendula (*Calendula officinalis*)

**Bundle theme**: Women's Health (supporting); Digestive Support (supporting)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Annual garden flower; one of the easiest herbs to grow.
**Cultivation source availability**: All seed suppliers. High seed yield; self-sowing. Zero-cost seed saving angle for forager/homesteader overlap.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons excellent. iNaturalist CC-BY rich. Unsplash lifestyle abundant.
**Guide angle**: The gateway medicinal herb. The salve preparation and infused oil are beginner-accessible applications with strong visual appeal (orange petals, golden color). Women's health connection (lymphatic support, traditionally used for menstrual comfort in European herbalism) plus digestive anti-inflammatory properties make this a dual-bundle species.
**FTC note**: Gentle anti-inflammatory historical use claims are within bounds. Topical applications (salve) are not dietary supplement claims and do not trigger the same FTC framework — they are cosmetic/personal care claims, which fall under different (FDA cosmetics) rules. A guide describing how to make calendula salve for dry skin is not a health claim issue.

**Margin model**: Dual-bundle leverage. Women's Health and Digestive bundles both benefit.

## Species 10 — Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*)

**Bundle theme**: Immunity Support (supporting); Women's Health (supporting)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk in North America (native to India/Middle East; cultivated commercially in US). Adaptogen category is the fastest-growing segment in herbal supplements market.
**Cultivation source availability**: Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Mountain Rose Herbs bulk supply (dried root). Cultivation guides for zones 8–11 (perennial) and 5–7 (annual). Growing season content.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons has habit images. iNaturalist CC-BY moderate.
**Guide angle**: Highest-search-volume adaptogen on Etsy. The practitioner market — RNs and NPs in integrative health — specifically looks for ashwagandha guidance because patients are frequently asking about it. A cultivation-focused guide that connects the plant to the supplement form fills the gap between the "take this supplement" content and the "here is what grows it" knowledge.
**FTC note**: Adaptogen claims are a nuanced area. "Traditionally classified as a Rasayana herb in Ayurvedic tradition" is acceptable. "Reduces cortisol" as a product claim would require clinical trial substantiation. The guide can describe the existing evidence (multiple RCTs on stress and sleep markers) without asserting the product makes that claim.

**Margin model**: Premium species for the Immunity bundle — adds practitioner credibility and search volume.

---

## Species 11 — Passionflower (*Passiflora incarnata*)

**Bundle theme**: Sleep and Nervines (primary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk at the species level. *P. incarnata* is native to the southeastern US; considered Vulnerable in some northern states at the edge of its range.
**Cultivation source availability**: Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds. Vine growth habit; zone 6–10 perennial, annual in zones 4–5.
**Photo sourcing**: Wikimedia Commons excellent (visually striking purple and white flower). iNaturalist CC-BY abundant.
**Guide angle**: The most visually distinctive species in the Sleep bundle. The flower photograph is the thumbnail anchor for the bundle listing. Cultivation guide includes trellis requirements and zone-specific hardiness — practical content that no PLR guide includes.
**FTC note**: "Traditionally used as a nervine and sleep-supporting herb in American folk medicine" — within bounds. Clinical studies exist (PubMed) on anxiolytic properties; describe the research descriptively without making the anxiety treatment claim.

**Margin model**: Sleep bundle visual anchor. High thumbnail click value.

---

## Species 12 — Dandelion (*Taraxacum officinale*)

**Bundle theme**: Digestive Support (primary)
**Conservation status**: Not at risk. Common; cosmopolitan distribution.
**Cultivation source availability**: Universal. The "grow your own" angle is actually counterintuitive (dandelion grows itself); the guide focuses on harvest timing, root vs. leaf vs. flower use, and bitters preparation.
**Photo sourcing**: Already in the Seedwarden wild edibles photo archive (Session 560 — dandelion was one of the original 18 wild-edible species). Zero additional photo sourcing required.
**Guide angle**: The bridge species between the wild-edibles forager catalog and the medicinal herbs catalog. A buyer who purchased the Wild Edibles Quick Reference already knows dandelion as food; the medicinal guide extends the relationship to liver-supporting bitters, roasted root coffee substitute, and diuretic folk use. Cross-sell path: Wild Edibles Quick Reference ($10) → Digestive Support Herbs Bundle ($20). Email Day 14 sequence trigger.
**FTC note**: "Traditionally used as a digestive bitter and diuretic in European herbalism" — well within historical use bounds. The bitters preparation is a culinary category, not a drug preparation.

**Margin model**: Bridge species. Enables cross-sell from existing wild-edibles buyers with zero new customer acquisition cost. The highest-ROI species in terms of net LTV contribution.
