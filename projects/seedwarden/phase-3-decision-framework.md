# Seedwarden Phase 3 Decision Framework

This document translates Phase 1 performance data into a specific Phase 3 execution option. By June 15, you will have 30+ days of live conversion data, post-purchase survey responses, and cohort signals. This framework lets you move from data to decision in 30 minutes, with zero ambiguity about what to execute next.

---

## Decision Architecture

### The Four Options

**Option A: Conservative**
- **Scenario**: Phase 1 underperforms or shows unclear product-market fit
- **Development scope**: 14 regional listing variants only (no new content products)
- **Timeline**: July 1–October 31, 2026 (16 weeks)
- **Total dev hours**: 19 hours (all regional listing creation)
- **M6 revenue target**: $900–$1,100/month gross
- **Go-forward assumption**: Phase 1 issues are solvable; defer content expansion until Phase 1 stabilizes

**Option B: Standard**
- **Scenario**: Phase 1 at expected range (20–50 sales, balanced cohorts)
- **Development scope**: 12 new products + 14 regional listings + 3 bundles (full Phase 3 schedule)
- **Timeline**: July–December 2026 (26 weeks, with 16-week execution window)
- **Total dev hours**: 125–140 hours
- **M6 revenue target**: $1,800–$2,500/month gross
- **Go-forward assumption**: Phase 1 validates core positioning; expand across all cohorts and categories

**Option C: Aggressive**
- **Scenario**: Phase 1 overperforms (50+ sales, 1.5%+ conversion on 3+ products)
- **Development scope**: 12 new products + 14 regional listings, compressed timeline, possible contractor support
- **Timeline**: July–November 2026 (22 weeks execution, 2-week compression)
- **Total dev hours**: 120 hours (2-week sprint + possible mockup outsourcing)
- **M6 revenue target**: $2,800–$3,800/month gross
- **Go-forward assumption**: Market pull is strong; maximize first-mover advantage with compressed launch

**Option D: Focused Single-Cohort**
- **Scenario**: One cohort dominates Phase 1 (45%+ of purchases)
- **Development scope**: 60–70% development effort concentrated on cohort-specific products; core products from other cohorts deferred
- **Timeline**: Varies by cohort; typically 18 weeks for focused execution
- **Total dev hours**: 95–110 hours
- **M6 revenue target**: $2,200–$3,200/month gross
- **Go-forward assumption**: Market signals a clear preference; move faster by reducing decision scope

## DECISION TREE

### Branch A: Sales Volume (Most Important Signal)

**How many total sales did Phase 1 achieve in its first 30 days?**

#### A1: Fewer than 10 sales in 30 days
- **Conversion rate**: Likely <0.4%
- **Diagnosis**: Product-market fit not established
- **Decision**: **OPTION A (Conservative)**
- **Action required before Phase 3**:
  1. Audit Phase 1 listings for structural problems (mockup quality, title keywords, pricing vs. competition, description hook)
  2. Run the listing health diagnostic in `monthly-metrics-checklist.md` Part 3
  3. Fix the 3 lowest-performing listings (by views per conversion)
  4. Do NOT launch Phase 3 content until Phase 1 conversion rate improves to ≥0.6%
- **Phase 3 scope if proceeding**: Launch regional variants only (SW-P3-R01 to SW-P3-R14). These add keyword surface area with zero content creation burden. Do not commit to new content products until Phase 1 is stable.
- **Timeline**: Phase 3 content deferred to September–October at the earliest

#### A2: 10–20 sales in 30 days
- **Conversion rate**: 0.4–0.7% (below target, but improving)
- **Diagnosis**: Listings are working but need optimization
- **Decision**: **OPTION A + Conditional Upgrade**
- **Action required**:
  1. Review Section 5 Risk Assessment in `phase-3-product-expansion-roadmap.md` (Risk 3: Specific Phase 1 Product Underperforms)
  2. Identify which Phase 1 products have views but zero conversions
  3. Fix top 2–3 underperforming listings before launching Phase 3
  4. Establish a 0.8% conversion baseline on 80%+ of Phase 1 listings before content expansion
- **Phase 3 scope**: Regional variants launch July 1 as planned (no dependencies on Phase 1 performance). Content products deferred to August at the earliest. Decision to proceed to Option B can be made July 15 if Phase 1 fixes show results.
- **Go/no-go date**: July 15 check-in. If Phase 1 conversion rate reaches 0.8% by July 15, upgrade to Option B. If still below 0.6%, stay with Option A.

#### A3: 20–50 sales in 30 days
- **Conversion rate**: 0.7–1.5% (at or near target)
- **Diagnosis**: Product-market fit is forming; cohort composition not yet clear
- **Decision**: **OPTION B (Standard)** — full Phase 3 roadmap
- **Action required**:
  1. Proceed with July 1 regional variant launch
  2. Analyze Phase 1 cohort composition: which products generated the 20–50 sales?
  3. If homesteader cohort is >35% of sales, consider front-loading Seed Library System and Homestead Skills Roadmap to Month 3 (July 15 rather than October)
  4. If forager cohort is >25% of sales, front-load Wild Edibles Quick Reference and Native Plants Flashcards
  5. Otherwise, execute the standard Phase 3 schedule per `phase-3-product-expansion-roadmap.md` Section 4
- **Phase 3 scope**: 12 new products + 14 regional listings on the standard schedule (Section 4, Timeline)
- **Timeline**: Full Phase 3 development July–December per Section 4 schedule
- **Revenue expectation**: $1,800–$2,500/month by October (M6)

#### A4: 50+ sales in 30 days
- **Conversion rate**: 1.5%+ on multiple products (overperforming)
- **Diagnosis**: Clear product-market fit; strong demand signal
- **Decision**: **OPTION C (Aggressive)** or **OPTION D (Focused)**
- **Next step**: Go to Branch B below to refine between C and D

---

### Branch B: Cohort Concentration (Secondary Signal)

**Applies if Phase 1 achieved 50+ sales OR the post-purchase survey shows clear cohort identity.**

Analyze the 20–50 post-purchase survey responses (or email messages if survey is incomplete). Assign each respondent to a cohort:
- **High-Intent Forager**: Mentions foraging, wild plants, identification, regional expertise
- **Survival Prepper**: Mentions food security, preparedness, storage, off-grid, self-sufficiency
- **Homesteader**: Mentions gardening, small-scale farming, seasonal planning, projects
- **Gift Buyer**: Mentions buying for someone else, gift recipient identity

**What percentage of Phase 1 buyers fall into each cohort?**

#### B1: Balanced cohorts (no single cohort >40%)
- **Decision**: **OPTION B (Standard)** or **OPTION C (Aggressive)** depending on sales volume (from Branch A)
- If A4 (50+ sales) + balanced cohorts: **OPTION C**
- If A3 (20–50 sales) + balanced: **OPTION B**
- **Implication**: Launch all Phase 3 products across all cohorts on schedule. No prioritization required.

#### B2: One cohort 40–55% of Phase 1 sales
- **Decision**: **OPTION D (Focused Single-Cohort)** if A4; **OPTION B** if A3
- **Which cohort**: Identify the dominant cohort from the 20–50 survey responses
- **Phase 3 adjustment**:

**If Forager-dominant (40–55%):**
- Priority 1 (July): Wild Edibles Quick Reference, 7 Native Plants regional listings
- Priority 2 (August): Native Plants Flashcard Set, Regional Forager Bundle
- Priority 3 (September): Medicinal Herb Guide
- Defer: All preservation derivatives, Homestead Skills Roadmap (move to December if time permits)
- Reasoning: Foragers have high search intent and low price sensitivity for quality. Maximize the foraging product depth first.

**If Prepper-dominant (40–55%):**
- Priority 1 (July): All 4 preservation derivatives (Beginner Canning, Fermentation Starter, Dehydrating, Pressure Canning)
- Priority 2 (July): 7 Survival Garden regional listings, Master Preserver Bundle
- Priority 3 (August): Expanded Homesteader Gift Set
- Defer: Flashcards, Medicinal Herb Guide, Seed Library System
- Reasoning: Preppers buy volume and bundles. Maximize preservation product depth and bundle options first.

**If Homesteader-dominant (40–55%):**
- Priority 1 (July): Seed Library Organization System, Homestead Skills Assessment and Learning Roadmap
- Priority 2 (August): Preservation Season Starter Bundle, regional variants
- Priority 3 (September): Medicinal Herb Guide
- Defer: Flashcards, Photo Pack, single-method preservation guides
- Reasoning: Homesteaders are AOV multipliers. The Skills Roadmap drives bundle adoption. Front-load the catalog navigation tool.

**If Gift-buyer-dominant (40–55%):**
- Priority 1 (August): All bundle products — Preservation Season Starter, Master Preserver, Expanded Homesteader Gift Set
- Priority 2 (September): First-Year Food Preservation Planner (gift-positioned)
- Priority 3 (October): Regional Forager Bundle, other bundles
- Defer: Flashcards, Photo Pack, individual specialty guides
- Reasoning: Gift buyers make high-value single purchases. Maximize bundle options and gift-category visibility.

#### B3: One cohort >55% of Phase 1 sales
- **Decision**: **OPTION D (Focused Single-Cohort)**
- **Implication**: Concentrate 70% of Phase 3 development hours on the dominant cohort's product stack in July–August. Secondary cohort products move to September–October. Non-dominant cohorts are lower priority.
- **Development time savings**: By focusing the first 6 weeks on a single cohort, context-switching overhead is minimized, resulting in 5–10% faster per-product output. This can enable the compressed timeline even with the same ~130 hours.
- **Risk**: If Phase 1 data changes in August (e.g., a second cohort suddenly spikes), be prepared to shift gears. Option D is flexible mid-execution — it's a sequencing choice, not a hard constraint.

### Branch C: Repeat Purchase Rate (Validation Signal)

**Applies after 45 days of Phase 1 data (approximately June 15).**

Check Kit (ConvertKit) for repeat buyers: how many Phase 1 customers have made a second purchase within 30–45 days?

| Repeat Rate | Interpretation | Phase 3 Implication |
|---|---|---|
| <8% | Post-purchase sequence may not be firing correctly | Diagnose email automation before Phase 3 launch; proceed with Option A or B |
| 8–15% | Baseline repeat rate; some cross-sell friction | Proceed with Option B; audit email Day 7 cross-sell messaging |
| 15–25% | Healthy repeat rate; Phase 1 cross-sell working | Proceed with Option B or C; email automation is solid |
| >25% | Strong repeat rate; brand loyalty forming | Proceed with Option C or D; email list is becoming an asset |

**Action if repeat rate <8%**: Do NOT defer Phase 3. Instead, diagnose post-purchase sequence in parallel. Check in Kit that Automation 2 (post-purchase email) is triggering. Review Email 3 and Email 7 for cross-sell copy effectiveness. If emails are not delivering, troubleshoot deliverability (spam folder, suppression lists) before launch day for Phase 3 Day 1 emails.

### Branch D: Etsy Message Volume (Qualitative Signal)

**Listen to Etsy messages for product gap signals.**

| What Buyers Are Asking | What It Means | Phase 3 Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Is there a beginner version?" (re: any comprehensive guide) | Buyers are price-sensitive or intimidated by page count | Preserve derivatives (Beginner Canning, Fermentation Starter) should launch July 15, not August 1 |
| "Does this cover [specific region]?" | Buyers want regional specificity | Regional variants should launch July 1 as planned; highest-ROI action |
| "Can you add [herb/species]?" | Buyers want more depth in an existing category | Medicinal Herb Guide should launch July 15 if this appears 3+ times; move from September to July |
| "Do you have a gift bundle?" | Gift buyers are searching for premium options | Expanded Homesteader Gift Set should launch October 1 (confirmed — proceed) |
| "How do I organize my saved seeds?" | Seed-saving buyers are hitting the post-save use case | Seed Library Organization System critical for repeat rate; front-load to August if this appears 2+ times |
| No repeat questions; minimal message volume | Phase 1 buyers are satisfied; no obvious gaps | Proceed with standard Phase 3 schedule |

## Reversal Triggers

**If conditions change during Phase 3 execution, reverse course:**

| Trigger | Action | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Selected Option B; Phase 1 conversion drops below 0.5% in weeks 5–8 | Pause Phase 3 content launches; investigate Phase 1 issue | Phase 1 degradation suggests a structural problem (e.g., listing visibility, pricing sensitivity) |
| Selected Option C; development velocity is 30% slower than planned | Extend timeline to 14 weeks instead of 10; do not compress further | Burnout and quality issues arise from over-compression |
| Selected Option D (single cohort); second cohort reaches 35%+ by Month 2 (August) | Shift Phase 3 Mix: keep Tier 1 focus, add top 2 products from secondary cohort in Tier 2 | Market signal changed; be flexible to capture the new opportunity |
| Any Phase 3 product launches with <0.5% conversion after 30 days | Pull from paid promotion; diagnose listing quality before future launches | Low-converting products consume marketing budget with no return; fix first, then relaunch |
| October gift season shows >50% gift-buyer traffic | Front-load Expanded Homesteader Gift Set to October 7 (if available); add gift keywords to all listings October 1 | Gift season is concentrated; capitalize fast |

## Summary: Fast Decision Path

**Minimum viable decision-making process (if you're time-constrained):**

1. **Count Phase 1 sales in 30 days**: <10 → A | 10–20 → A+ | 20–50 → B | 50+ → C or D
2. **Check repeat rate**: If <8%, diagnose email before proceeding
3. **Scan post-purchase survey**: Any cohort >40%? If yes, consider D
4. **Scan Etsy messages**: Any pattern of product gap questions? Adjust Tier 1 priorities if yes
5. **Decide**: A / B / C / D
6. **Go**: Execute per the Option's timeline and scope
