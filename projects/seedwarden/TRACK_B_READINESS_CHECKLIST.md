### 1E. Location Prep Guide

**Before Session 1 (Cluster A):**
- Identify the window in the house with the largest, most diffused light exposure (north or east facing is softer than south or west)
- Clear the surface area within 5 feet of that window: the shooting surface should have 24 inches minimum of clear working space
- Test window light at 9am–11am the day before the shoot if possible — look for soft directional light without harsh shadows on the surface
- Do not use a bathroom or any room with strong artificial light competing with window light

**Between sessions (cluster transitions):**
- Props for the completed cluster can be set aside but do not need to be stored formally — organize by cluster in a box or tray
- Do not disassemble the window light setup between Session 1 (Cluster A) and Session 2 (Cluster B) — the same window can serve both if sessions are on the same day
- Cluster C (kitchen) transitions to a kitchen surface — this is the only cluster where the location itself changes meaningfully

**At the start of each session:**
- Allow 20–25 minutes for cluster setup before any photos are taken
- Shoot all Slot 4 images for the full cluster before moving to Slot 5 — reduces reset time by 40%
- Take one test shot and review it at full zoom on the phone screen before proceeding to confirm: focus on the product image, white balance is warm not cool, no competing labels or text visible in frame

### 1F. Photography Consistency Guide

These practices maintain visual coherence across all 15 photos so the full shop looks like one coordinated body of work, not 15 unrelated shots.

**White balance**: Set manually to "Daylight" or "Cloudy" (5500–6000K) at the start of Session 1 and do not change it for the duration of the shoot. Auto white balance will drift between frames and create batch editing inconsistency. Cluster C can go slightly warmer (6000–6500K) to reinforce its amber-and-red tone target.

**Angle consistency**: All Cluster A Slot 4 images should be shot from exactly overhead (90 degrees). All Cluster B Slot 4 images should be scene-based (45-degree angle). All Cluster C Slot 4 images should match: overhead for flat lays (Products 13 and 15) and 45-degree for scene-based (Product 14). Do not mix overhead and angled shots within a cluster's Slot 4 images — it reads as inconsistent in the shop.

**Shadows**: Soft window light creates natural shadows that add depth. Do not diffuse or eliminate them (that creates a flat, studio look). The exception: if shadow falls directly across the product text (the seed saving chart, the calendar dates), reposition the product or rotate it in the frame until the text is lit.

**Focal point discipline**: The product image (the PDF page or tablet screen) is the clear focal center in every single shot. If the frame feels crowded, remove one prop — do not add a second focal point.

**Hands in Slot 5**: Any hand visible in Slot 5 images should be mid-action (writing, holding seeds, packing a jar, lifting a tray). A hand that is posed or hovering reads as staged. The 3-second timer allows the shooter to start the action and hold it through the capture.

**Do not edit between sessions**: Shoot all 15 products before any editing. Editing one cluster's photos and matching the others to them is faster than editing each cluster independently. The batch edit pipeline (import all, edit one, paste to all) is documented in PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md Part 1.

### 2E. Pre-Build User Decision Required

There is one decision to make before the Canva build begins that affects all 8 cards:

**Decision: Canva Free vs. Canva Pro?**

The workflow in ZONE_CARD_PRODUCTION_TIMELINE.md was written for Canva Pro (Brand Kit availability). **If Canva Pro is desired**: Pro subscription is $15/month (monthly) or $120/year. Given the zone card system requires 4 weeks of build time, a single month of Pro ($15) covers the full build. Cancel after Week 4.

## Section 3: Parallel vs. Sequential Execution

These two production tracks (photo shoot and Canva build) are fully independent. They share no assets and no dependencies. Both can proceed simultaneously.

**Recommended parallel sequence:**

```
Week 0 (Day 1):
  USER: Brand Kit setup in Canva (30 min)           [unblocks Canva track]

Week 0–1:
  USER: Props sourcing + printed page prep           [unblocks photo shoot]
  CANVA TRACK: Begin Zone 5 master template build    [start after Brand Kit live]

Week 1:
  PHOTO SHOOT: Session 1 — Cluster A (3.5–4.5 hr)  [any day window light is good]
  CANVA TRACK: Complete Zones 5 and 6               [independent of photo shoot]

Week 2:
  PHOTO SHOOT: Sessions 2 and 3 — Clusters B and C  [can be same day, ~3 hrs]
  CANVA TRACK: Zones 3, 4, 7, 8

Week 3:
  EDITING: Batch edit all 30 photos, export to etsy-ready/
  CANVA TRACK: Zones 9, 10 + full review + print test

Week 4:
  UPLOAD: Etsy slot 4 and 5 images (pending account verification)
  CANVA TRACK: Kit email delivery setup + landing page launch
```

**Critical dependency note**: Etsy upload (Track A) is still blocked on account verification. Photo shoot completion does not unblock uploads — both the photo assets AND the verified Etsy account are needed for upload. Completing the shoot now means zero additional delay once the account becomes available.
