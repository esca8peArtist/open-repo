Design (12.5 hours) and photography (8–10 hours sprint) have enough float that writing delays do not compound into design or photo delays.

### Sequential vs. Parallel Dependencies

The five bundles cannot be written fully in parallel by a single writer. However, the dependency structure is shallow — each bundle requires only that its unique species research be compiled, not that any other bundle be complete.

**True sequential dependencies** (cannot start until predecessor is finished):
- None between bundles. Each bundle is independent in its research phase.

**Research cross-references** (not blocking, but efficiency gains from sequencing):
- Immunity writing benefits from completed Respiratory writing because Echinacea's first-occurrence content (written in Respiratory) is reused in condensed form. Writing Respiratory before Immunity saves approximately 2 hours.
- Digestive writing benefits from completed Women's Health writing for Calendula and Sleep writing for Lemon Balm. Writing Women's Health and Sleep before Digestive saves approximately 3 hours.
- Recommended write order: Women's Health → Respiratory → Immunity → Sleep → Digestive. This ordering maximizes shared-species savings and front-loads the two highest-complexity bundles into Week 1 when daily pace is freshest.

**Contraindication cross-checking**: The Immunity bundle requires cross-checking Ashwagandha's thyroid contraindication against the Lemon Balm TSH note in Sleep (both affect thyroid patients). This is a 15-minute check, not a sequential dependency — flag the contraindication during Immunity writing and confirm during Sleep writing.

**Day 3 pace gate** (June 24): If Women's Health is below 2,500 words at EOD June 24, Option C (3-bundle scope, defer Immunity and Digestive) is the correct response. This gate is early enough that the Women's Health → Respiratory → Sleep upload sequence (June 29, July 6–7, July 13) remains fully intact regardless of which option activates.

## Section 5: Risk Analysis and Mitigation

**Risk scoring**: Probability (1=15–20%, 2=25–40%, 3=50%+) × Impact (1=Low, 2=Medium, 3=High) = Score. Score 4+ requires explicit contingency.

### Risk 1: Herb Delivery Delays

**Description**: One or more physical herb specimens arrive late, delaying photography. Most Tier 1–2 specimens (Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Elderberry) ordered June 8–15 have expected arrival dates of June 21 – July 9. Any delay past June 21 compresses the pre-sprint studio session.

**Probability**: 2 (25–40%). Prairie Moon's spring season extends through mid-July for select species; East Coast transit adds 7–10 days; backorder risk for conservation species.

**Impact**: 1 (Low). Writing and design are unaffected. Only studio photography of fresh specimens is delayed. All 14 species have Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA or iNaturalist CC-BY photo coverage at guide quality.

**Score**: 2 (below trigger threshold).

**Mitigation**:
- Order Mountain Rose Herbs dried herbs by June 13 (not June 15) to guarantee arrival before June 21 studio session. Dried material covers flat-lay and preparation shots for all bundles.
- Pre-stage Wikimedia Commons sourcing links for all 14 species before June 22. All identification shots are accessible within minutes of sprint start.
- Build 3-day recovery window into each supplier tier by ordering ahead of the photography deadline.

**Contingency trigger**: Any order without confirmed ship date by June 21 → activate photo substitution immediately. Log substitution decision in WORKLOG.md with date and rationale.

**Can shooting be split across Week 1 and Week 2?** Yes, without any impact on writing or design. Fresh specimen shots can be deferred to any available day through July 12.

## Appendix A: FTC Language Quick Reference

All five bundles must use qualifying language for any therapeutic claims.

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|------------|
| "Black Cohosh relieves menopause symptoms" | "Traditionally used in Cherokee and Appalachian folk medicine for women's hormonal transitions" |
| "Elderberry prevents colds" | "Studied in randomized controlled trials for cold duration outcomes" |
| "Valerian cures insomnia" | "Studied in clinical trials for sleep-related outcomes with mixed results" |
| "Goldenseal is antimicrobial" | "Contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in vitro and in clinical settings for antimicrobial activity" |
| "Ashwagandha reduces cortisol" | "Contains withanolides, compounds studied in randomized controlled trials for stress-related physiological markers" |

**Mandatory in every bundle**: copyright and disclaimer page (identical language across all five bundles), contraindications section per species (with FTC disclaimer header), sources section at back of guide.

**Goldenseal CITES sidebar** (mandatory in Immunity bundle): "Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*) is listed in CITES Appendix II. International trade in wild-harvested material requires export permits. This guide recommends cultivated sources only. Forest Grown Verified (FGV) certified sources are available via the United Plant Savers participant directory at unitedplantsavers.org/forest-grown-verified."
