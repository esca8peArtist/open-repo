# Canva Pro Brand Kit Setup Guide

**Purpose**: Once social accounts exist (Gate 1) and Canva Pro trial is active, this guide
walks through Brand Kit configuration from login to verification in 30–40 minutes.

**Why this document exists**: The Brand Kit is a one-time configuration that makes every
subsequent Canva session faster and more consistent. Without it, every zone card, Pinterest
pin, and carousel requires manual hex-code entry for each colored element — approximately
30–50 manual entries across zone card production alone. With the Brand Kit, color is applied
in one click from the palette.

**Who does this**: You (the user). The agent cannot access Canva's UI.

**Total time**: 30–40 minutes.

## Part 2: Create the Brand Kit

1. Log into `canva.com` with `wanka95@gmail.com`
2. In the left sidebar, click "Brand Hub"
   (If Brand Hub is not visible: click the grid/waffle icon top-left > look for Brand Hub

## Part 3: Add Colors

The Brand Kit accepts hex codes. Click "Add a color" (or the "+" icon in the Colors
section), paste the hex code, press Enter. Repeat for each color below.

Seedwarden requires 10 colors total: 6 brand colors and 4 zone band colors. All 10 must
be in the Brand Kit so zone card production requires zero manual color entries.

### Brand Colors (add all 6)

| Name to enter in Canva | Hex Code | Primary use |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Forest Green | `#143b28` | Headers, borders, column headings |
| Deep Ink Green | `#1A3A2A` | Body text on dark backgrounds |
| Warm Cream | `#F5EDD6` | Card background — all 8 zone cards |
| Parchment | `#EDE0C4` | Variety spotlight section background |
| Sage | `#8FA882` | Dividers, icons, subtle callouts |
| Burnt Sienna | `#A0522D` | Zone 9/10 band color, accent elements |

### Zone Band Colors (add all 4)

These four colors are used exclusively as the 12px horizontal rule in zone cards.
Each zone card uses exactly one band color based on its climate group.

| Name to enter in Canva | Hex Code | Zones that use this color |
|---|---|---|
| Zone Band — Cool | `#3D6B8A` | Zones 3 and 4 |
| Zone Band — Temperate | `#2D5016` | Zones 5 and 6 |
| Zone Band — Warm | `#C9943A` | Zones 7 and 8 |
| Zone Band — Hot | `#A0522D` | Zones 9 and 10 (same hex as Burnt Sienna — add separately with this label) |

**After entering all 10 colors**: The Brand Kit colors section should show 10 swatches.
If any swatch is missing, re-enter that color before proceeding.

**Verification shortcut**: Take a screenshot of the colors section. Compare it to this
table. Each hex code should be visible when you hover over the swatch.

## Part 4: Add Fonts

In the Brand Kit Fonts section, click "Add a font" and search by name. All three fonts
below are available free in Canva's library — no upload is needed.

| Role | Font name | Search term |
|---|---|---|
| Heading | Playfair Display | "Playfair Display" |
| Body | Lato | "Lato" (fallback: "Source Sans 3" if Lato unavailable) |
| Accent | Cormorant Garamond | "Cormorant Garamond" |

**Designate roles**: After selecting each font, assign it to its role (Heading / Body /
Accent) using the dropdown next to the font name in the Brand Kit.

**Note on Montserrat**: ZONE_CARD_PRODUCTION_TIMELINE.md (an earlier document) lists
Montserrat as the body font. The current spec from CANVA_SETUP_STATUS.md and
TRACK_B_USER_GATES.md supersedes this — use Lato (or Source Sans 3) as the body font.
Montserrat is acceptable as a substitute if you prefer it, but do not mix both in the
same document.

## Part 5: Upload the Logo

1. In the Brand Kit Logos section, click "Upload a logo"
2. Locate `projects/seedwarden/logos/seedwarden_logo_1.png` on your computer
   (If not already downloaded: navigate to that path in the project folder and download it)
3. Upload the PNG file. Canva accepts PNG with transparent backgrounds.
4. Wait for the upload to complete. A thumbnail should appear in the Logos section.
5. Verify the thumbnail shows the wordmark clearly at small size.

**If you have both a transparent-background PNG and a white-background version**: Upload
both. Canva stores multiple logo variants per Brand Kit. The transparent version is
preferred for most designs; the white version prevents conflicts on dark backgrounds.
