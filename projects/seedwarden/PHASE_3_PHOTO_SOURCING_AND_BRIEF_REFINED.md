## Part 1: Sourcing Hierarchy and Platform Coverage

### Platform Rankings by Botanical Coverage Quality

**Tier 1 — Wikimedia Commons**
Best platform for medicinal herb habit photos. The botanical garden photography community contributes very high-quality CC-licensed images that rival professional photography for common medicinal species. The coverage for all 12 Phase 3 primary species is sufficient for a complete launch (no species is unrepresented). Preferred license: CC-BY-SA or CC-BY. CC0 is acceptable but rare for high-quality botanical images.

Coverage confidence by species:
- Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa): Excellent — multiple CC-BY-SA habit images including Appalachian forest-floor context
- Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis): Moderate — sufficient for cover use; fewer options than Black Cohosh
- Elderberry (Sambucus nigra): Excellent — abundant flower and berry cluster images
- Echinacea purpurea: Excellent — garden context images widely available
- Echinacea angustifolia: Good — prairie context images available, fewer than E. purpurea
- Valerian (Valeriana officinalis): Good — European botanical images dominant
- Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata): Excellent — the flower is widely photographed
- Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis): Good — adequate coverage
- Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia): Excellent — best coverage of any herb in the portfolio
- Mullein (Verbascum thapsus): Excellent — the second-year spike is iconic and widely photographed
- Calendula (Calendula officinalis): Excellent — garden images abundant
- Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale): Already in Seedwarden archive (Session 560); no new sourcing required

**Tier 2 — iNaturalist**
Best for North American native species in natural habitat context. Essential for Black Cohosh (Appalachian forest floor), Passionflower (southeastern US maypop populations), and Echinacea angustifolia (prairie context). Search filter: License = CC-BY (not CC-BY-NC — the NC restriction prohibits commercial use). Sort by "Faves" to surface highest-quality observations.

Coverage confidence by species:
- Black Cohosh: Excellent — hundreds of CC-BY observations in natural habitat
- Echinacea angustifolia: Good — concentrated in Great Plains observations
- Passionflower: Excellent — southeastern US observations abundant
- Mullein: Excellent — nationally abundant, many CC-BY observations
- Dandelion: Excellent (and already archived)

**Tier 3 — Unsplash / Pixabay (lifestyle and flat-lay only)**
Best for apothecary aesthetic props, lifestyle scenes (herbal tea steeping, mortar and pestle), and interior page mood images. Not suitable for identification-quality botanical images. All Unsplash images are CC0 / commercial use free. Good search terms: "dried herbs flat lay," "apothecary herbs," "herbal infusion," "botanical still life."

Use case for Phase 3: Etsy listing slots 4–5 (lifestyle flat-lay, in-use contextual) rather than interior PDF pages. The bundle guides themselves should use Tier 1–2 images for identification sections.

**Tier 4 — Botanical Garden Direct Contact**
For Goldenseal only. The NC Botanical Garden (media@ncbg.unc.edu) and Missouri Botanical Garden (media@mobot.org) both have Goldenseal photography in their archives. Contact for educational use permission for high-resolution images by June 7 (one week before the June 8 Goldenseal decision deadline). If no response by June 8, proceed with Wikimedia CC path automatically.

## Part 2: Per-Bundle Cover Image Selection

### Women's Health Bundle — Cover Image

**Hero image target**: Black Cohosh in forest-floor context (preferred) or Calendula flowers as fallback.

Black Cohosh sourcing path:
- Primary: Wikimedia Commons search "Actaea racemosa" — filter CC-BY or CC-BY-SA — look for images showing the full flowering spike in a forest understory setting. Target resolution: minimum 2400px on shortest side.
- Secondary: iNaturalist search "Actaea racemosa" — filter CC-BY — sort Faves — look for observations in June-July bloom window showing the bottle-brush raceme.
- Selection criterion: The conservation story (overharvested woodland species) should be visible in the image context — a forest-floor setting reinforces the conservation message better than a garden setting.

Calendula fallback (if Black Cohosh image insufficient):
- Unsplash search "calendula flowers" — dozens of high-quality lifestyle options. The warm orange palette aligns with the Deep Burgundy header color.

Supporting images for Women's Health interior:
1. Vitex in flower — Wikimedia "Vitex agnus-castus" — look for the lavender flower spikes on a shrub or small tree
2. Red Clover flowering — Wikimedia "Trifolium pratense" — pink globe flowers with the characteristic chevron leaf marking
3. Dried Black Cohosh root — original from Mountain Rose Herbs studio session (June 17–21)
4. Lavender field or garden row — Unsplash "lavender garden" — the premium lifestyle aesthetic

### Respiratory Bundle — Cover Image

**Hero image target**: Elderberry berry clusters in late-ripening stage (dark purple clusters beginning to droop).

Sourcing path:
- Primary: Wikimedia "Sambucus nigra" or "Sambucus canadensis" — filter CC-BY-SA — look for the flat-topped berry clusters against dark-green foliage.
- Secondary: iNaturalist "Sambucus canadensis" — CC-BY — late August/early September observations when berries are ripe.
- Selection criterion: The visual should show the berry clusters clearly enough for identification use. A background of forest or garden is better than a close-up isolation.

Supporting images for Respiratory interior:
1. Mullein second-year spike — Wikimedia "Verbascum thapsus" — the tall spike is immediately recognizable. Look for images showing both the rosette context (ground level) and the spike.
2. Echinacea purpurea in flower — Wikimedia — the drooping ray petals around an orange-bronze cone. The image should distinguish E. purpurea (purple rays) from Rudbeckia (yellow rays) — a common confusion point.
3. Dried elderberries in a bowl — original from Mountain Rose Herbs studio session
4. Thyme plant habit — Wikimedia "Thymus vulgaris" — small leaves on woody stems with pink flowers

### Immunity Bundle — Cover Image

**Hero image target**: Goldenseal in forest farming context (preferred) or Ashwagandha with berry clusters (fallback).

Goldenseal sourcing path:

Ashwagandha fallback:

Supporting images for Immunity interior:

2. Goldenseal yellow rhizome — diagnostic yellow-orange cross-section. Original from live specimen if ordered; Wikimedia botanical illustration if not.

### Studio Setup Instructions

**Lighting**: Natural window light is preferred over artificial for the apothecary aesthetic. Position the setup 18–24 inches from a north or east-facing window for even indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight (harsh shadows destroy the apothecary still-life aesthetic). If natural light is insufficient, use a portable daylight LED panel at 5600K.

**Camera**: Phone camera at the highest available resolution (Portrait mode or manual mode if available). Use a tripod or stable surface for close-ups. Distance for flat-lays: 18–24 inches above the subject, directly overhead.

**Export specifications**: Export or save all images at the highest available JPEG quality. Target minimum 2400×2400px for Canva use. Do not apply heavy filters — the natural herb colors (the orange of calendula, the yellow of Goldenseal root, the purple of elderberries) are the palette.

**Session duration**: 3 hours for all five bundle shot lists (approximately 30 images total, yielding 20–25 keepers).

### Cull and Edit Protocol

After the studio session, cull immediately (same day or within 24 hours). Target: keep the best 20–25 images from 30+ captured.

Cull criteria:
- In focus on the subject (dried herbs must be sharp, not backgrounds)
- Correct exposure (no blown highlights on light-colored herbs like calendula or lavender)
- Clean background (no visible product tags, tools, or non-prop items)
- Color accuracy (elderberries should be dark purple, not brown; goldenseal should be yellow-orange)

Edit criteria (minimal): Slight contrast enhancement, slight warmth increase (add 5–10 points toward warm on color temperature if image feels cold), remove dust spots visible at zoom. Do not heavily filter.

## Part 4: Stock Platform Analysis — Best Coverage by Category

### Which Platform Has Best Coverage for Which Content Type

| Content Type | Best Platform | Second Best | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Habit photos (full plant in context) | iNaturalist | Wikimedia | iNaturalist has more natural habitat context; Wikimedia has more garden/cultivated context |
| Close-up botanical detail | Wikimedia | iNaturalist | Wikimedia's scientific community uploads more detail shots |
| Root/rhizome identification | Wikimedia botanical illustrations | Studio original | Botanical illustrations often more useful than photos for root ID |
| Flat-lay / apothecary aesthetic | Studio original | Unsplash | Unsplash "dried herbs" searches return generic lifestyle images; original is better |
| Lifestyle (in-use, preparation scenes) | Studio original | Unsplash | Original always preferred; Unsplash as fallback |
| Seasonal context (in-flower, late-summer harvest) | iNaturalist | Wikimedia | iNaturalist observation dates are filterable by month |
| Zone-specific regional context | iNaturalist | None equivalent | Filter by location/region for zone-specific identification |

### Platform-Specific Search Tips

**Wikimedia Commons — Maximizing results**:
- Search by scientific name, not common name. "Actaea racemosa" returns more relevant results than "black cohosh."
- After searching, click "Filter" and select CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, or CC0 under License.
- Sort by "Most recently featured" to surface high-quality nominations from Wikimedia's editorial community.
- Check "File:" namespace results — sometimes the best images are linked from species articles but don't appear in direct searches.

**iNaturalist — Maximizing results**:
- After searching a species, click "Photos" tab (not "Observations" tab) to see photos sorted by quality.
- Apply filter: "Quality Grade = Research Grade" — these are observations confirmed by at least two independent identifiers, meaning the species ID is reliable.
- Filter "License = CC BY" (not CC-BY-NC, not CC-BY-NC-SA — non-commercial licenses prohibit Seedwarden's commercial use).
- Sort by "Faves" — the iNaturalist community's most-liked photos tend to be the best quality.

**Unsplash — Efficient search**:
- Useful search terms for the apothecary aesthetic: "mortar pestle herbs," "dried botanicals," "herbal medicine," "apothecary bottles," "herb garden flat lay."
- Attribution is not legally required for Unsplash images but is recommended. Format: "Photo by [Name] on Unsplash."
- All Unsplash images permit commercial use under the Unsplash License (effectively CC0 for commercial purposes).

## Part 5: Image Organization and Attribution Log Template

### File Naming Convention

All downloaded or shot images are named by species slug and image type:

```
[species-slug]-[image-type]-[source].jpg

Examples:
  actaea-racemosa-habit-wikimedia.jpg
  goldenseal-root-studio.jpg
  elderberry-berries-inaturalist.jpg
  passionflower-flower-wikimedia.jpg
  calendula-petals-studio.jpg
```

Image type codes: `habit` (full plant in context), `flower` (flower close-up), `root` (root/rhizome), `flatlay` (studio flat-lay), `preparation` (in-use preparation scene)

Source codes: `wikimedia`, `inaturalist`, `unsplash`, `studio`, `ncbotgarden`

### Attribution Log Format (PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md)

Create this file at: `/projects/seedwarden/assets/phase-3-medicinal-herbs/PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md`

```
| Filename | Bundle | Usage | Source | License | URL | Attribution Line |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| actaea-racemosa-habit-wikimedia.jpg | Women's Health | Cover hero | Wikimedia Commons | CC-BY-SA 4.0 | https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:... | [Artist Name], CC BY-SA 4.0 |
| passionflower-flower-wikimedia.jpg | Sleep | Cover hero | Wikimedia Commons | CC-BY-SA 4.0 | https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:... | [Artist Name], CC BY-SA 4.0 |
...
```

Every image used in any Seedwarden Phase 3 guide or Etsy listing must be documented here before the guide PDF is exported. Undocumented images block the PDF export stage.

### Attribution Page Format (inside each bundle PDF)

At the back of each bundle guide (before the Sources section), include one paragraph:

"Photography credits: [Species] images sourced from Wikimedia Commons under Creative Commons licenses (CC-BY-SA) and iNaturalist (CC-BY). Full attribution list available at seedwarden.com/credits. Studio photography by Seedwarden, 2026. Unsplash images under the Unsplash License."

This protects against any attribution dispute by directing interested parties to the online credits page rather than filling the PDF with lengthy per-image attribution lines.

## Part 6: Mockup Image Compilation Reference

### Purpose of Mockup Images

Phase 3 bundles need three types of mockup images for Etsy listing slots:
- Slot 1: Cover image (PDF guide on a tablet or device mockup — shows the guide as a product)
- Slot 2: Interior spread preview (two pages from the guide, showing layout quality)
- Slot 3: Zone card preview (the companion zone card, showing the calendar utility)
- Slot 4: Lifestyle flat-lay (dried herbs and the guide together — studio original)
- Slot 5: In-use scene (practitioner or herbalist at a desk with the guide and herbs)

### Mockup Resources

**Device mockups (Slot 1)**:
- Use the Phase 2 tablet mockup template (already in the Canva Brand Kit). Substitute the Phase 3 bundle cover image. No new mockup purchase needed.
- The tablet mockup should show the cover image clearly — use the A4-portrait mockup angle (slightly elevated, slight angle) for best cover legibility.

**Interior spread (Slot 2)**:
- Export two interior pages from each completed bundle guide at 72dpi.
- Arrange side-by-side in Canva at 2400×2400px.
- Add a subtle drop shadow to make the pages appear as physical sheets.
- The interior spread is the quality signal — buyers who click on Slot 2 are evaluating whether the guide is dense and well-formatted, not decorative.

**Slot 5 lifestyle mockup**:
- Use Unsplash "herbalist desk" or "natural medicine study" images as backgrounds.
- Composite the guide PDF cover over the background.
- The Canva Pro background removal tool makes this a 5-minute task per bundle.
- Alternative: use the studio session Slot 5 shot directly without compositing.
