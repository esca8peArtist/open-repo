## Platform KPIs + Decision Tree + Week 2 Replication Checklist

**Purpose**: Daily per-post tracking for Week 1-2 social posts (Jun 29 – Jul 12). Three platform sections: LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube. Each section includes KPI targets, decision tree, and a Friday replication analysis comparing all three platforms to identify Week 2 best-performing content types.

## PART 1: PLATFORM-SPECIFIC KPIs

### LinkedIn — Primary Platform

**Audience**: Herbalism professionals, herbalism students, home growers, integrative health educators.

**Engagement rate formula**: (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers × 100

| Content Type | GREEN | YELLOW | RED | Notes |
|-------------|-------|--------|-----|-------|
| Educational (plant science, cultivation) | >8% | 5–8% | <5% | Algorithm suppresses posts <8% at 24hr |
| Promotional (bundle launch) | >3% | 2–3% | <2% | Lower expected — focus on CTA clicks, not rate |
| Testimonial / Community | >10% | 5–10% | <5% | Highest performing type when specific |
| YouTube cross-post | >6% | 3–6% | <3% | Platform mismatch possible; link in first comment |

**Additional KPIs tracked per post**:
- Profile visits (indicator of audience curiosity, visible in LinkedIn Analytics)
- Follower change (net gain/loss on post day; visible in Creator Analytics > Followers)
- Viral coefficient: (Shares + Saves) / Likes — target >0.1 for content worth replicating

**LinkedIn-specific rules**:
- Never put Etsy link in post body — first-comment strategy required (LinkedIn suppresses external links in post body)
- Post Etsy link as first comment within 5 minutes of publish
- Post only once per day maximum; twice triggers suppression
- Best posting time for this audience: 9am ET Tuesday/Thursday/Friday (from Phase 3 baseline research)

---

### Instagram — Secondary Platform

**Audience**: Visual herb enthusiasts, home growers, lifestyle buyers.

**Engagement rate formula**: (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Followers × 100

| Content Type | GREEN | YELLOW | RED | Notes |
|-------------|-------|--------|-----|-------|
| Educational (carousel or single image) | >6% | 3–6% | <3% | Saves are highest-value signal on Instagram |
| Promotional (bundle launch) | >3% | 2–3% | <2% | Same as LinkedIn; lower expected |
| Testimonial (quote card) | >8% | 4–8% | <4% | |

**Primary value signal on Instagram**: Saves + Shares (not Likes). A post with 40 saves and 60 likes outperforms a post with 200 likes and 0 saves in algorithm weight.

**Additional KPIs tracked per post**:
- Saves count (most important single metric for educational content)
- Shares (Stories reshares count here if trackable)
- Profile visits from post (visible in Instagram Insights > Posts > View Insights)
- Story replies or DMs following post publish (manual count — check message requests)

**Instagram-specific rules**:
- Hashtags in first comment (not post body) — prevents caption feeling cluttered and avoids potential hashtag shadowban
- Use 3–5 highly relevant hashtags rather than 30 generic ones
- Best posting time: 8–10am ET or 7–9pm ET (test both in Week 1, confirm in Week 2)
- Reels vs. static: if a static post hits RED, test Reel format for same content in Week 2 (Reels receive 3–5x more impressions in current algorithm)

---

### YouTube — Tertiary Platform (Community Tab + Long-Form)

**Audience**: Home growers and herbalism students researching in-depth.

**Primary metric for Community Tab posts**: Comment count (not engagement rate — YouTube Community Tab does not show a standard engagement rate).

| Content Type | GREEN | YELLOW | RED | Notes |
|-------------|-------|--------|-----|-------|
| Community Tab question post | ≥10 comments (48hr) | 5–9 comments | <5 comments | Reply to every comment within 24hr |
| Long-form video (if used) | Watch time >5 min avg | 2–5 min | <2 min | Subscriber growth: target +2–5/week during launch |

**Additional KPIs for Community Tab**:
- Comment quality: count comments with specific herb mentioned (signal of engaged audience)
- Your reply rate: target 100% within 24hr (critical for algorithm recognition of engagement)
- Cross-post signal: did the Community Tab question drive any LinkedIn comments when cross-posted?

**YouTube-specific rules**:
- Community Tab posts work best as open questions or polls — avoid promotional copy here
- Best time: 12pm ET (midday) — YouTube Community Tab audience is more evening/weekend but midday catches lunch break
- Subscribe CTA: add to end of every Community Tab post ("Subscribe for weekly herb profiles")

## PART 3: DECISION TREE PER PLATFORM

### LinkedIn Decision Tree

```
T+24hr engagement rate:

>8% (Educational) or >3% (Promotional):
  → GREEN. Log "GREEN — no change." Continue scheduled cadence.

5–8% (Educational) or 2–3% (Promotional):
  → YELLOW.
  → Check: Was first-comment Etsy link added within 5 min? (If no, add now.)
  → Check: Post time — was it 9am ET? If earlier or later, note for next week's timing test.
  → Action: No repost. Adjust timing for next educational post if this is the 2nd YELLOW in a row.

<5% (Educational) or <2% (Promotional):
  → RED.
  → Step 1: Check impressions in LinkedIn Analytics — are impressions also low, or just engagement?
    IF impressions low (< 50% of follower count): distribution problem. Check: post frequency (>1/day?)
    IF impressions normal but engagement low: hook problem. Review first line of post.
  → Step 2: For educational RED: repost with revised opening line (different hook) at 12pm ET same day or next day.
  → Step 3: For promotional RED: no repost. Note for Email 2 prep (subject line or CTA issue may be parallel).
  → Step 4: Log in PHASE_3_EXECUTION_LOG.md with timestamp.

2 consecutive RED posts same content type:
  → Pattern signal. Apply YELLOW adjustment for that content type:
  → Educational: shift posting time from 9am ET to 12pm ET for 1 week; re-measure.
  → Promotional: review post copy framework — is the lead line benefit-focused or feature-focused?
```

---

### Instagram Decision Tree

```
T+24hr engagement rate:

>6% (Educational) or >3% (Promotional):
  → GREEN. Log and continue.

3–6% (Educational) or 2–3% (Promotional):
  → YELLOW.
  → Check: Were hashtags placed in first comment (not body)? If not, edit post body now.
  → Check: Did caption have a clear CTA or question in final line? (Drives comments.) If not, add comment reply with a question to prompt responses.
  → Action: No immediate repost. Test Reel format for next same-type post.

<3% (Educational) or <2% (Promotional):
  → RED.
  → Step 1: Is saves count >5 despite low total rate? If YES, content is valuable but reach is limited. Issue: distribution. Try posting as Reel or Carousel.
  → Step 2: Is saves count also 0–2? Audience relevance issue. Review: who is following this account vs. target audience?
  → Step 3: Repost as Reel (even a simple slideshow) within 48 hours. Compare Reel engagement to static post engagement.

Saves+Shares signal override:
  → If overall engagement rate is YELLOW (3–6%) BUT Saves+Shares > 20 (on a sub-500 follower account):
  → Reclassify as GREEN behavior. Saves/Shares indicate content is being bookmarked for later use.
  → This is the most valuable Instagram signal for educational content. Prioritize it.
```

---

### YouTube Community Tab Decision Tree

```
T+48hr comment count:

≥10 comments:
  → GREEN. Log and continue.
  → Action: Reply to each comment within 24hr. Every reply extends the post's visibility window.

5–9 comments:
  → YELLOW.
  → Action: Reply to each comment within 12hr.
  → Action: Cross-post the same question to LinkedIn with attribution: "We asked on YouTube Community — sharing here for broader conversation. [question]"
  → Cross-post drives LinkedIn engagement AND YouTube subscribers.

<5 comments:
  → RED.
  → Step 1: Reply to every comment received (even 1–2). Signal activity.
  → Step 2: Tag 2–3 relevant people in a reply: "@[name] I'd love your perspective on this."
  → Step 3: Cross-post to LinkedIn (same as YELLOW, but use more urgency: "This question on YouTube generated interesting conversation — weighing in here:").
  → Step 4: For next YouTube Community post, shift to a poll format (binary choice) rather than open question. Polls have lower friction and generate 3–5x comments vs. open questions.
```
