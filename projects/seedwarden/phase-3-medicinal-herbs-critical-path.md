## Executive Summary

Phase 3 produces five medicinal herb guide bundles for the Etsy herbalist market: Women's Health, Respiratory Health, Sleep and Nervines, Immunity Support, and Digestive Support. Total content output: 18,300 words across five PDFs. Total production time: 68–79 hours across a 22-day sprint (June 22–July 13, 2026).

**The binding constraint is writing.** Writing takes 56–66 hours (adjusted for seven species that appear in two bundles each). Design takes 12.5 hours and runs in parallel. Photography is a pre-sprint track that does not block writing or design. Upload administration takes 6 hours and is staggered across 7-day intervals to preserve each listing's Etsy algorithmic discovery window.

**Both launch gates are cleared.** Phase 3 does not need to wait for Phase 2 performance data — the forager cohort and native plants conversion rates already meet the thresholds. The only remaining gate is the user's scope decision by May 30.

**The minimum viable launch** if capacity is uncertain: Women's Health + Respiratory + Sleep. These three bundles represent the highest Etsy search intent, upload June 29 / July 6–7 / July 13, and require 37–43 hours of writing. Immunity and Digestive defer to July 20 and August 3 at no cost to launch timeline quality.

**Revenue model at steady state** (12 months post-launch): 5 bundles × $20–22 average price × estimated 20–35 sales/bundle/month = $2,000–$3,850/month. Break-even per bundle at 13–16 sales (includes production time at $25/hour equivalent).

## Section 4: Photography Staging

### Photography Strategy

Indoor studio is the primary plan. No bundle requires outdoor location photography. All identification, habit, and preparation photography can be completed with north-facing window light plus one reflector. This eliminates permit risk, weather risk, and travel cost.

**The practical constraint on live specimens**: Most ordered plants arrive at or after sprint end (July 13–20). The sprint runs primarily on Wikimedia Commons CC and iNaturalist CC-BY photos for identification and habit content, plus studio-shot dried herb preparations staged with Mountain Rose Herbs stock. Live specimens arriving July 12–20 become v1.1 quality upgrades post-launch, not launch blockers.

## Appendix A: FTC Language Quick Reference for Guide Writing

All five bundles must use qualifying language for any therapeutic claims. Non-compliant language creates product liability exposure on Etsy.

| Do NOT write | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "Black Cohosh relieves menopause symptoms" | "Traditionally used in Cherokee and Appalachian folk medicine for women's hormonal transitions" |
| "Elderberry prevents colds" | "Studied in randomized controlled trials for cold duration outcomes" |
| "Valerian cures insomnia" | "Studied in clinical trials for sleep-related outcomes with mixed results" |
| "Goldenseal is antimicrobial" | "Contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in vitro and in clinical settings for antimicrobial activity" |
| "Ashwagandha reduces cortisol" | "Contains withanolides, compounds studied in randomized controlled trials for stress-related physiological markers" |

**Mandatory in every bundle**: Copyright and disclaimer page (identical language across all five bundles), contraindications section for every species (with FTC disclaimer header), sources section at back of guide.

**Goldenseal CITES sidebar (mandatory in Immunity bundle)**: "Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*) is listed in CITES Appendix II. International trade in wild-harvested material requires export permits. This guide recommends cultivated sources only. Forest Grown Verified (FGV) certified sources are available via the United Plant Savers participant directory at unitedplantsavers.org/forest-grown-verified."

**Ashwagandha pregnancy/thyroid warning (mandatory)**: "Individuals who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or managing a thyroid condition should consult a qualified healthcare provider before using ashwagandha preparations."

**Sleep bundle drug-interaction warning (mandatory, all four species)**: "Individuals taking prescription sedatives, benzodiazepines, or sleep medications should consult a healthcare provider before use. This herb has traditionally been used for sleep and relaxation and does not constitute a medical treatment."
