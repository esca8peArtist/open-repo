## Executive Summary

Phase 3 is authorized to proceed. Both demand-validation gates are cleared with margin. The June 22 – July 13 sprint is feasible for a single writer producing all five bundles at 4–5 focused hours per day.

**The binding constraint is writing** (56–66 hours adjusted, 64–74 raw), not design (12.5 hours) or photography (pre-sprint parallel track that does not compete with sprint writing time). The critical path runs from Women's Health writing complete through PDF export to June 29 upload, with Respiratory and Sleep following at 7-day intervals. Design and photography have float ranging from 1–14 days. Writing has zero float on critical path days.

**Both launch gates are already met.** Phase 3 does not need to wait for Phase 2 performance results. The forager cohort and native plants conversion numbers from Phase 1 already exceed both thresholds. The only remaining gate is the scope decision by May 30.

**Can all 5 bundles ship by July 13?** Yes — with the critical path analysis showing Women's Health, Respiratory, and Sleep live by July 13 and Immunity and Digestive in post-sprint staging for July 20 and August 3. Three listings live by sprint end is the standard definition of "launch complete."

**What if writing slips 1 week?** The July 20 contingency path absorbs a full week of overrun with zero upload sequence compromise. All five bundles remain on track for August 3 full launch. The November–December holiday review accumulation window is fully preserved.

**What if suppliers slip?** A 3-day built-in recovery window separates each supplier deadline from its downstream photography gate. Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA coverage is verified sufficient for all 14 unique species — supplier specimens are quality upgrades for post-launch v1.1, not launch blockers.

**Three decisions are required by May 30** and are marked `[DECISION]` throughout this document.

## Appendix A: FTC Language Quick Reference for Guide Writing

All five bundles must use qualifying language for any therapeutic claims.

| Do NOT write | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "Black Cohosh relieves menopause symptoms" | "Traditionally used in Cherokee and Appalachian folk medicine for women's hormonal transitions" |
| "Elderberry prevents colds" | "Studied in randomized controlled trials for cold duration outcomes" |
| "Valerian cures insomnia" | "Studied in clinical trials for sleep-related outcomes with mixed results" |
| "Goldenseal is antimicrobial" | "Contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in vitro and in clinical settings for antimicrobial activity" |
| "Ashwagandha reduces cortisol" | "Contains withanolides, compounds studied in randomized controlled trials for stress-related physiological markers" |

**Mandatory in every bundle**: Copyright and disclaimer page (identical language across all five bundles), contraindications section for every species (with FTC disclaimer header), sources section at back of guide.

**Goldenseal CITES sidebar (mandatory in Immunity bundle)**: "Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*) is listed in CITES Appendix II. International trade in wild-harvested material requires export permits. This guide recommends cultivated sources only. Forest Grown Verified (FGV) certified sources are available via the United Plant Savers participant directory at unitedplantsavers.org/forest-grown-verified."
