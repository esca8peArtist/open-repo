# Phase 3 Pricing and Negotiation Ranges

**Prepared**: May 21, 2026
**Reference purpose**: Use this document alongside the vendor negotiation templates when evaluating quotes received May 22–27. All ranges are grounded in Seedwarden's Phase 2 sourcing research and AHG market rates as of May 2026.

## Section 1: Goldenseal Wholesale and Specimen Pricing Ranges

Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*) pricing varies substantially by form (live specimen vs. dried root), quantity, and cultivated vs. wild-harvested certification. All ranges below assume cultivated-origin stock only — wild-harvested pricing is irrelevant to Seedwarden's sourcing policy.

### Live Specimen Pricing (Path 1)

**Single-unit (1 rhizome division or seedling plug)**

| Supplier | Expected Unit Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prairie Moon Nursery | $15–22 | Bare-root rhizome division; spring stock; single-unit ordering available |
| Strictly Medicinal Seeds | $12–18 | Seedling plug or potted start; certified organic nursery-propagated |
| Regional Etsy native plant sellers | $10–18 | Variable quality; confirm cultivated before ordering; always request seller's growing statement |
| Local Appalachian native plant nurseries | $8–16 | If available regionally; lowest cost; no shipping risk |

**Anchor price target**: $14 for a healthy rhizome division or seedling plug from a verified cultivated nursery.
**Walk-away price**: $25+ for a single unit with no COA or cultivated certification. There is no scenario in which paying $25+ per unit for unverified stock is justified when verified alternatives are available at $12–18.

**5-unit quantity (if bulk is needed for photography variety)**

| Quantity | Expected Range | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|
| 5-unit Prairie Moon order | $60–85 total (bulk pricing may apply) | $95+ with no bulk discount |
| 5-unit Strictly Medicinal | $50–75 total | $85+ with no bulk discount |

**Note**: For Phase 3 photography, a single rhizome division is sufficient for all planned shots (rhizome structure, hand-scale comparison, early leaf emergence). A 5-unit order is not necessary unless the user intends to gift plants or establish a demonstration growing bed. The per-unit rate is for reference only.

**Negotiation leverage — live specimen**:
- Lead-time flexibility: offering to be flexible on ship-week (e.g., "any week in June") allows the supplier to ship on their schedule, which sometimes triggers goodwill discounts or priority fulfillment
- Order bundling: adding Black Cohosh or Wild Ginger to the same Prairie Moon order often unlocks free shipping on orders above $50
- Educational framing: Prairie Moon and Strictly Medicinal both respond favorably to requests from educators; a brief sentence noting the guide's conservation messaging often results in staff going out of their way to confirm cultivated certification in writing

### Dried Root Pricing (Path 2 Supplement or Path 1 Alternative)

Dried Goldenseal root is a viable photographic subject (the bright yellow interior is the species' most distinctive visual feature) and is the primary sourcing path under Path 2 for dried-specimen photography.

| Quantity | Expected Price | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|

| 4 oz | $42–65 (bulk) | $70+ for 4 oz; switch to 1 oz at that point |

**Anchor price target**: $16/oz for certified organic cultivated-origin dried root with Certificate of Analysis available on request.

## Section 2: AHG Peer Reviewer Honorarium Tiers

Reviewer compensation is structured to be fair to the reviewer while remaining proportional to the revenue impact they create. The practitioner-tier buyer segment ($120–$150 bundle set) requires one named RH or ND reviewer to convert — the honorarium investment pays for itself within 8–10 practitioner-tier sales.

### Honorarium Framework by Reviewer Type

**Tier 1: RH (AHG) Independent Practitioner**

| Scenario | Compensation | Value Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Standard RH review (one bundle, 30–45 min) | Complimentary 5-bundle set ($120–$150 value) | Fair exchange for 30–45 minutes and named listing credit |
| Extended RH review (two bundles, 60–90 min) | Complimentary set + $50 stipend | Appropriate for time above the 45-minute baseline |
| RH with affiliate arrangement (20% commission) | Complimentary set + affiliate link | No cash outlay; revenue sharing on referral sales |
| Walk-away: cash honorarium demand above $100 (RH tier) | Decline and offer affiliate-only | A cash demand above $100 from an RH without clinical or academic affiliation exceeds the return; move to next candidate |

**Tier 2: ND or Academic Faculty (Naturopathic Doctor, Herbalism Faculty)**

| Scenario | Compensation | Value Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Faculty review (one bundle, 45–60 min) | Complimentary set + $75–$150 honorarium | Faculty time carries institutional overhead; cash acknowledgment is appropriate |
| Bastyr or NAIMH faculty (named institution) | $100–$150 honorarium + set | Institutional affiliation increases the credential signal value; worth paying for |
| ND in clinical practice (not academic) | Complimentary set + $75 | Clinical ND adds significant credibility; $75 is appropriate for 45–60 minutes |
| Walk-away: ND demand above $200 for single-bundle review | Counter-offer: narrow the scope (contraindication sections only) at $150 | Scope reduction preserves the highest-value credential signal within the budget ceiling |

**Tier 3: MD-Level Reviewer (MD + RH, Board-Certified Integrative Medicine)**

| Scenario | Compensation | Value Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| MD review, narrow scope (two specific sections, 20–30 min) | $200–$250 honorarium + complimentary set | MD credential justifies premium; narrow scope controls time cost |
| MD review, full bundle (60–90 min) | $300–$400 honorarium + set | Appropriate for the single highest-authority review request; justified only if the MD credit is the primary launch marketing claim |
| Walk-away: MD demand above $500 for single bundle | Do not proceed | At $500+, a single review consumes margin on 3–4 practitioner-tier sales; not viable at current launch scale |
| Walk-away: MD unavailable before June 21 | Activate Tier 1 RH immediately | Launch deadline is hard; no MD credit is worth delaying past June 21 |

**Tier 4: PhD Researcher (Phytochemistry, Ethnobotany, Pharmacology)**

| Scenario | Compensation | Value Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| PhD review (one bundle, 45–60 min) | Complimentary set + $75–$150 honorarium | Academic orientation; cash acknowledges research contribution |
| PhD with publication record in species covered | $100–$150 + set | Highest academic credential signal; worth the premium |
| Walk-away: PhD demand above $200 for single bundle | Decline; use RH Tier 1 instead | PhD without clinical practice does not deliver proportionally higher practitioner conversion lift compared to an RH |

### Payment Timing Guidance

**When to pay upfront vs. on delivery**:

- Always pay the full complimentary bundle set upfront (on reviewer confirmation). The PDF is a zero-cost delivery; it demonstrates good faith and starts the review period.
- For cash honoraria: pay half on confirmation of review commitment (June 8 at the latest), half on receipt of written review confirmation (by June 21).
- Do not pay the full cash honorarium upfront for any reviewer. Protect against a non-response by holding the second payment until the written confirmation arrives.
- PayPal Goods and Services or Venmo are both acceptable. For faculty reviewers with institutional complications around personal payment receipt, offer a check mailed to their personal address or a donation to a named herbalism education fund.

---

### Leverage Points for Reviewer Negotiation

**Listing credit value**: The named listing credit ("Reviewed by [Name], RH (AHG), [specialty]") is a permanent credential association for the reviewer. Their name appears on every sale of the bundle. For practitioners who are building their name recognition, this is marketing value that is worth mentioning if they push back on compensation. Frame it as: "Your name will appear in a listing that our practitioner buyer audience sees on every purchase — that's a standing public endorsement of your expertise."

**Affiliate commission**: A 20% commission on practitioner-tier bundle sales ($120–$150 set) generates $24–$30 per sale. For an RH with an active email list or social following, projecting 5–10 referral sales per month implies $120–$300/month in ongoing passive income for a 30–45 minute one-time review. Frame this as the compensation structure for practitioners who have an existing audience — it aligns incentives and requires no upfront cash.

**Reciprocal promotion**: Offer to list the reviewer's practice, school, or book in the guide's resource section. A backlink or featured mention in a PDF reaching 200+ practitioner buyers has real marketing value for a clinical herbalist. This costs nothing and often resolves hesitation from practitioners who are not primarily motivated by cash.

## Section 3: Canva Designer Rate Cards

Phase 3 requires 5 bundle covers and 5 zone cards (or 3 of each under Option C). The rates below are grounded in Phase 2 Canva production research and current Etsy/Fiverr market rates for herbalist and wellness digital product design.

**Bundle cover (2400x2400px, Etsy primary listing image)**

| Designer Tier | Expected Rate / Cover | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|

| Designer Tier | Expected Bundle Rate | Walk-Away |

| Established freelancer | $900–$1,600 | $2,000+ without editable Canva files included |

**Three-bundle set (3 covers + 3 zone cards = 6 deliverables, Option C scope)**

| Designer Tier | Expected Bundle Rate | Walk-Away |

| Etsy botanical shop | $450–$840 | $1,100+ |
| Established freelancer | $550–$960 | $1,300+ |

**Cost delta between full and reduced scope**: A designer quoting in the middle of the Fiverr rate range should quote approximately $350–$500 less for the 3-bundle scope vs. the 5-bundle scope. If a designer quotes less than a 30% reduction for the 3-bundle scope relative to the 5-bundle quote, push back — the proportional reduction should be roughly 40% for 6 vs. 10 deliverables.

### Comparison: Freelance Fiverr vs. In-House Canva (Solo Execution)

| Approach | Time Cost | Cash Cost | Quality Risk | Revision Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Self-execute in Canva (Phase 2 template reuse) | 12.5 hrs (covers + zone cards) | $0 (Canva Free) or $15 (Canva Pro for 1 month) | Low (brand kit already built; templates proven) | Instant |
| Fiverr Level 2 herbalist designer | 0 hrs (user) + 1 hr brief | $500–$900 | Low-Medium (depends on portfolio match) | 24–48 hrs/revision |
| Etsy botanical designer | 0 hrs (user) + 1 hr brief | $750–$1,400 | Low (stronger portfolio depth) | 24–72 hrs/revision |

**When to hire vs. self-execute**: If writing sprint pace is already at 5+ hours/day (Option A solo sprint), the 12.5-hour design load is a genuine risk to writing output. Delegating design for $500–$900 buys back those 12.5 hours for writing and reduces the sprint's daily intensity from 7–8 hrs/day to 5–6 hrs/day. If the writing sprint is 3–4 hours/day (Option C or Option B), the 12.5-hour design load is manageable in parallel — self-execution is cost-optimal.

The break-even point: if the design cost is $600 and the writing velocity is 300 words/hour, $600 in design fees buys approximately 20 writing hours at a $30/hour opportunity cost equivalent — or approximately 5,000–6,000 additional guide words. That is roughly half a bundle's worth of writing. Under Option A (5-bundle solo sprint at maximum pace), this trade-off is marginally favorable toward delegation. Under Option C, it is not.

### Negotiation Leverage Points — Designer

**Scope optionality**: Tell the designer your final scope may be 3 or 5 bundles, to be confirmed by May 30. Ask them to quote both. This creates pricing anchors for the negotiation — a designer who quotes $800 for 5 bundles and $600 for 3 bundles has revealed their per-deliverable floor, which you can use to negotiate a better rate on the full set if the user selects Option A or B.

**July 3 hard deadline as leverage**: The July 3 hard delivery date is a real constraint but also a useful negotiation tool. A designer with current availability who needs work will accept a July 3 deadline — it is six weeks from May 22. A designer who hesitates on July 3 is either overbooked or underqualified for production-pace work. The deadline filters out low-commitment sellers before any money changes hands.

**Editable Canva file requirement**: Always require delivery of an editable Canva design file shared to your account, not just the exported PNG/PDF. This is a negotiation chip — some designers resist sharing editable files (it undermines their revision-billing model). If a designer refuses editable file delivery at no extra charge, increase their quote walk-away threshold by 20% to account for the revision cost exposure.

**Retainer framing**: For the right designer (high portfolio match, responsive, within rate range), offer a Phase 4 retainer mention in the negotiation. "If Phase 3 goes well, I will have Phase 4 category expansion work starting October 2026" is a real and credible statement given the Phase 4 planning already in motion. Retainer framing often unlocks a 10–15% discount on the Phase 3 engagement in exchange for right-of-first-refusal on Phase 4.

**Portfolio exclusivity trade**: Offer the designer permission to use the Phase 3 covers in their portfolio (with Seedwarden credit) in exchange for a 5–10% discount. Most designers are portfolio-building and value real client work they can display. The covers will be publicly visible on Etsy anyway — granting portfolio rights costs Seedwarden nothing.

## Section 4: Negotiation Leverage Points — Cross-Category Summary

These apply across all three vendor categories and should be kept in mind when evaluating any quote received May 22–29.

**Confirmation speed as leverage**: Any supplier or designer who can confirm availability and terms by May 27 (three days into the quote window) gets priority consideration. Slow responders self-select out. Do not chase non-responders — move to the next candidate on the list.

**Budget floor disclosure**: Do not disclose your budget ceiling to any vendor in the initial contact. The templates in `phase-3-vendor-negotiation-templates.md` are written to elicit a quote without revealing your ceiling. After receiving a quote, you can reveal a budget floor ("I have budgeted $X for this engagement") to anchor the negotiation downward.

**Timing as leverage across all categories**: All three vendor categories benefit from the May 22 send date. Suppliers and designers who receive an inquiry on May 22 with a May 29 response deadline and a June 1 start date perceive urgency — urgency often accelerates quote responses. The risk is that urgency also increases the likelihood of inflated quotes ("rush rate"). Counter this by including the phrase "I have quotes from three suppliers / designers and am selecting based on availability, price, and portfolio match" — this signals competitive evaluation without revealing specifics.

**Long-term relationship signal for suppliers**: Prairie Moon and Strictly Medicinal are both small family-run suppliers who respond well to relationship framing. A brief mention that Seedwarden's guide recommends only cultivated-origin Goldenseal and will direct readers to verified suppliers aligns Seedwarden's educational mission with the supplier's conservation orientation. This alignment often produces goodwill discounts (free shipping, priority fulfillment, an extra unit thrown in) that would not materialize from a purely transactional inquiry.
