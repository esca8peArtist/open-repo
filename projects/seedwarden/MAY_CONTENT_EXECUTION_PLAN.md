### Email 3 — Day 12 (12 days after sign-up)

**Subject**: The plants that grow better together — and the ones that don't

**From name**: Seedwarden

**Body**:

---

Hi [First Name],

Companion planting is one of those gardening practices that sounds like old folklore but has solid reasoning behind most of it.

Three pairs that actually work and why:

**Basil + Tomato**: Basil repels aphids and thrips. More practically, both plants like similar water schedules and temperatures, so they are easy to manage together. This pairing shows up in every zone.

**Beans + Corn**: Beans fix nitrogen in the soil. Corn is a heavy nitrogen feeder. The Three Sisters system (beans, corn, squash together) is the original companion planting framework — used by Indigenous growers in North America for centuries before European contact.

**Dill + Cucumber**: Dill attracts beneficial insects that prey on cucumber beetles — one of the most common cucumber pests. Do not plant dill near carrots or fennel; those are the antagonistic combinations.

The full Companion Planting Chart covers 30+ pairings, organized by crop family, with the reasoning behind each pair. At $5 it is the lowest-barrier purchase in the Seedwarden catalog and the one most gardeners find themselves using every week once it is on the wall:
