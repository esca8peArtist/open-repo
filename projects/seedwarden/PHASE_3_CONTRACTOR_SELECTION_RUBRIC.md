# Phase 3 Contractor Selection Rubric

**Purpose**: Score and rank all applicants for each of the 6 contractor roles. Use this rubric to assign candidates to Tier 1 (extend offer immediately), Tier 2 (backup offer if Tier 1 rejects), or Tier 3 (deprioritize).

**When to run**: During portfolio audit window (July 1–2). Score all candidates who pass first-pass screening within 24 hours of their application arrival.

**Passing threshold**: 60 points or higher (out of 100) for Tier 1 consideration. 45–59 points qualifies as Tier 2. Below 45 points: deprioritize.

---

## Scoring Matrix — Universal Dimensions (All 6 Roles)

Five dimensions apply to all roles. Point allocations differ by role to reflect what matters most for each track.

| Dimension | Max Points | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio Quality | 0–30 | Does previous work match the quality standard required for Seedwarden Phase 3? |
| Communication Responsiveness | 0–20 | How quickly and professionally did the candidate respond to the job posting and any follow-up? |
| Domain Knowledge | 0–20 | Does the candidate demonstrate relevant knowledge of their specific track (botany, herbs, operations)? |
| Timeline Feasibility | 0–20 | Can the candidate credibly commit to the hard delivery dates (July 5, 8, 12, 15, 24, August 3)? |
| Rate Fit | 0–10 | Does the candidate's proposed rate fall within the budget range for their role? |
| **Total** | **0–100** | |

**Tier assignments based on total score**:
- 75–100 points: Tier 1 — extend offer immediately, same day
- 60–74 points: Tier 1 — extend offer within 24 hours
- 45–59 points: Tier 2 — hold; send offer only if Tier 1 rejects within 48 hours
- Below 45 points: Tier 3 — contact only if all Tier 1 and 2 candidates reject

## Dimension 1: Portfolio Quality (0–30 Points)

### Photographer — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 27–30 | 5+ recent flat-lay or lifestyle product photography projects with botanical, food, or natural product subjects; professional resolution (2000px+); consistent color and white balance within each project; warm, natural aesthetic directly matching Seedwarden's reference images |
| 21–26 | 3–4 flat-lay or product projects; professional resolution; minor white balance inconsistencies acceptable; aesthetic generally aligned with Seedwarden style; 1–2 projects show botanical or natural subjects |
| 15–20 | 2–3 product photography projects; resolution meets minimum standard; lighting or color inconsistencies visible but not disqualifying; botanical or food photography evident in at least 1 project |
| 8–14 | Mostly portraiture, events, or architecture; 1–2 product examples; technical limitations (resolution, harsh shadows, inconsistent color); limited botanical aesthetic evidence |
| 0–7 | No flat-lay or product work visible; heavily artistic or stylized; cannot credibly assess botanical photography capability from portfolio |

**Automatic disqualifiers** (assign 0 regardless of other scoring):
- No portfolio link provided
- Portfolio link is broken or inaccessible
- Portfolio contains no examples relevant to product or nature photography

### Writer 1 (Educational) — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 27–30 | 5+ published wellness or herbal health articles; uses scientific/Latin plant names throughout; cites primary sources (peer-reviewed studies, recognized databases); includes contraindications or drug interactions; 2,000+ word long-form articles present; FTC-compliant language evident |
| 21–26 | 3–4 published articles on wellness or herbal topics; mostly science-backed with source references; some contraindication discussion; 1,500+ word pieces; tone is educational |
| 15–20 | 2–3 health or wellness articles; basic source referencing; limited contraindication depth; 1,200+ word capacity demonstrated; tone is accessible |
| 8–14 | 1–2 wellness blog posts; minimal sourcing; no contraindication discussion; unclear long-form capacity |
| 0–7 | No published wellness or health writing; no evidence of evidence-based writing capability |

### Writer 2 (Recipes/Prep Guides) — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|

| 15–20 | 2–3 recipe guides; step-by-step format; measurements present but inconsistent; accessible language with occasional jargon |
| 8–14 | 1–2 recipe examples; informal or inconsistent format; unclear dosage guidance |

### Writer 3 / Clinical Herbalist Writer — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |

### Herbalist Consultant — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |

| 21–26 | Credential verified or 5+ years practice confirmed; 2 consulting or review projects described; herbalism database familiarity demonstrated |
| 15–20 | Credential stated but unverified; 1 consulting project; general herbalism knowledge demonstrated |
| 8–14 | Herbal knowledge without consulting or review experience; CITES/UpS familiarity unclear |
| 0–7 | No credential, no review experience, and no conservation awareness demonstrated |

### Logistics Coordinator — Portfolio Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|

## Dimension 2: Communication Responsiveness (0–20 Points)

Applies identically to all 6 roles.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Responded to job posting within 4 hours; reply is professional, clear, and directly addresses all 5 application questions; tone is warm and business-appropriate; asks at least 1 clarifying question showing they read the brief |
| 14–17 | Responded within 12 hours; addressed most application questions; professional tone; minor formatting or completeness issues |
| 10–13 | Responded within 24 hours; addressed most questions; adequate professionalism; no clarifying questions but no red flags |
| 6–9 | Responded within 48 hours; partial response (skipped 1–2 questions); some informality acceptable but not unprofessional |
| 0–5 | Responded after 48 hours; missed multiple application questions; unprofessional tone; or did not respond to follow-up portfolio request |

**Communication red flags** (note separately, may lower tier regardless of score):
- Generic application (copy-paste boilerplate that ignores job-specific requirements)
- Evasive answers to specific questions (e.g., rates answered as "negotiable" without a range)
- Spelling or grammar errors that indicate carelessness, not non-native English
- Tone that is aggressive, informal to an inappropriate degree, or dismissive of requirements

## Dimension 4: Timeline Feasibility (0–20 Points)

Applies to all 6 roles, with role-specific hard dates.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Candidate explicitly confirms availability on specific dates: July 1 (contract start), first delivery date (July 5 for photographer, July 8 for writers), and all subsequent delivery dates through August 10. No schedule conflicts mentioned. Demonstrates availability for kickoff call within 48 hours of offer. |
| 14–17 | Confirms July 1 start and most delivery dates; minor ambiguity on 1–2 later dates but verbally addressable in kickoff call. |
| 10–13 | Confirms July 1 start; does not confirm specific delivery dates but states general availability; clarification required. |
| 6–9 | Confirms availability but with caveats (travel, overlapping projects, uncertain schedule for part of the timeline). |
| 0–5 | Cannot confirm July 1 start; or delivery dates conflict with existing commitments; or no availability response given. |

**Automatic timeline disqualifiers** (assign 0 regardless of other scoring):
- Cannot start July 1 and has no substitute start date that allows July 5–8 first delivery
- States they are unavailable for the entire first week of July
- References an overlapping engagement that would prevent their specific deliverable dates

## Dimension 5: Rate Fit (0–10 Points)

| Score | Role Budget Range | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 9–10 | Within range and below midpoint | Proposed rate is within the budget range and at or below the midpoint (maximum value at minimum cost) |
| 7–8 | Within range, at or above midpoint | Proposed rate is within range; at or above midpoint; still acceptable |
| 4–6 | Slightly above range (10–20% over) | Proposed rate is 10–20% above budget ceiling; negotiable to within range; candidate is otherwise strong |
| 1–3 | Significantly above range (20%+ over) | Proposed rate is more than 20% above budget ceiling; negotiation unlikely to close gap; deprioritize unless portfolio is exceptional |
| 0 | Did not provide rate | Candidate refused to provide a rate estimate; or stated rate is explicitly non-negotiable at a level above range |

**Role budget ranges for rate scoring**:

| Role | Budget Range |
|---|---|
| Photographer (3 sessions, 15 images) | $1,200 – $1,800 |
| Writer 1 (3 educational bundles, ~10,800 words) | $900 – $1,350 |
| Writer 2 (3 recipe sections, ~6,600 words) | $600 – $1,000 |
| Writer 3 / Clinical Herbalist Writer (3 clinical sections) | $1,000 – $1,350 |
| Herbalist Consultant (3 bundle reviews) | $400 – $800 |
| Logistics Coordinator (6 weeks, 8–10 hrs/week) | $1,600 – $2,400 flat, or $40–$60/hr |

## Reference Check Scoring

After portfolio audit, conduct one reference check per Tier-1 candidate (July 2). Ask 3 questions; score the response:

| Reference Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Quality rated 9–10/10; no missed deadlines; described as responsive and professional; client would rehire |
| 7–8 | Quality rated 7–8/10; delivered on time with minor issues; generally responsive; client would likely rehire |
| 5–6 | Quality rated 5–6/10; or delivered late once; or communication required follow-up; client is neutral on rehire |
| 0–4 | Quality rated below 5/10; or multiple missed deadlines; or communication described as poor; client would not rehire |

**Reference score threshold**: A reference score below 6 reduces the candidate's tier by one level regardless of application score. A Tier 1 candidate with a 5/10 reference becomes Tier 2.

## Final Hire Decision Matrix

After scoring all candidates per role, rank them within each role and assign tiers. Use this matrix to make the final hire decision:

| Rank | Score Range | Reference | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| #1 candidate, ≥75 pts, ref ≥7 | Tier 1 | Extend offer immediately, same day |
| #1 candidate, 60–74 pts, ref ≥6 | Tier 1 | Extend offer within 24 hours |
| #2 candidate, 45–59 pts | Tier 2 | Hold; extend offer if #1 rejects within 48 hours |
| #3+ candidate, <45 pts | Tier 3 | Do not contact unless all Tier 1 and 2 reject |
| No qualified candidates (all <45 pts) | — | Post second round or activate solo fallback (see PHASE_3_SOLO_FALLBACK_ARCHITECTURE.md) |
