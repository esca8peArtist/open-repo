## Section 1: Plant Sourcing Logistics

### The Core Decision: Live Plants vs. Existing Photo Sources

For photography purposes, the species in Wave 1 do **not** require you to grow live plants. The May 10–11 shoot window is too close to obtain meaningful plant material from seed (ginseng takes 18 months to germinate; ramps take 1+ year; goldenseal requires cold stratification). The photography plan uses existing photo sources — iNaturalist CC-BY, botanical illustration archives, and botanical garden partnerships — rather than propagated specimens.

**Live seed sourcing** has a different purpose: purchasing 5–10 seeds per species for the photography session creates physical prop material (seed close-up shots, seed-in-hand scale reference, seed packet labels as props). This is achievable on a 7–10 day order window and is the only live plant sourcing task before the shoot.

### Seed Sourcing: What to Order and When

The goal is prop seeds for photography — not growing plants. You need 5–10 seeds per species, delivered by May 9.

**Order by: May 8** (to ensure 7–10 business day delivery by May 9)

**American Ginseng** (*Panax quinquefolius*)
- Primary vendor: Strictly Medicinal Seeds (strictlymedicinalseeds.com)
- Availability note: Fresh stratified ginseng seed is a fall crop; Strictly Medicinal has historically stated "available September" for fresh seed. **Spring availability is limited or zero from most vendors.** If the website shows out-of-stock: skip ginseng prop seeds and use a botanical illustration or high-resolution iNaturalist CC-BY photo for seed reference shots.
- Fallback vendor for spring availability: Raintree Nursery, Pinetree Garden Seeds, or search "ginseng seeds buy spring" — some Appalachian forest-farming suppliers sell stratified seed year-round.
- Quantity: 10 seeds minimum (for photography only; no germination expected this window)
- Price range: $3.95–$26 per packet depending on size and stratification status
- Prop use: seed-in-hand close-up, seed-on-scale for size reference, seed packet as flat-lay prop

**Goldenseal** (*Hydrastis canadensis*)
- Primary vendor: Strictly Medicinal Seeds (20-seed packet, ~$20)
- Secondary: Prairie Moon Nursery (prairiemoon.com) — ships year-round, 1–3 business days processing, 7–14 day delivery
- Availability: Goldenseal seeds are generally available spring through fall from cultivated sources (unlike ginseng)
- Quantity: 20 seeds (standard packet is 20; all are props — no germination expected this window)
- Prop use: seed close-up, seed-to-rhizome size comparison, packet label as flat-lay element

**Black Cohosh** (*Actaea racemosa*)
- Primary vendor: Strictly Medicinal Seeds
- Secondary: Prairie Moon Nursery, Fedco Seeds (fedcoseeds.com)
- Availability: Black cohosh seed is spring-available from multiple cultivated sources; standard offering year-round
- Shipping note for Strictly Medicinal: plants ship mid-March through July; seeds ship more flexibly. Order by May 8.
- Quantity: 10–15 seeds for photography
- Prop use: seed close-up (distinctive, small black seeds), seed pod prop if available, packet label

**Ramps / Wild Leek** (*Allium tricoccum*)
- Primary vendor: Experimental Farm Network (store.experimentalfarmnetwork.org) — $4.25/packet (~45 seeds)
- **Availability warning**: EFN wild ramps seeds are currently sold out as of early May 2026. Check stock daily; they restock periodically.
- Secondary vendor: Prairie Moon Nursery ramps bulblets (spring-shipped), Vermont Wildflower Farm
- Tertiary: search Etsy for "ramps bulbs" or "wild leek bulbs" — small-scale native plant sellers often have spring bulblet stock
- Availability note: Ramps seed has complex dormancy (double dormancy — both root and shoot need cold periods). Seeds sold in spring are typically from the previous fall harvest and may not be germination-tested. Buy for photography props only.
- Quantity: 45 seeds (full packet) — photograph both loose seeds and paper packet
- Prop use: bulb/seed close-up, dried ramp leaf prop if available from a grocery store

### Tier 2: iNaturalist CC-BY Curation (Execute May 7–9)

iNaturalist's default license is CC-BY-NC (prohibits commercial use). However, many contributors have set CC-BY (fully open), which allows commercial use with attribution. Filter specifically for CC-BY observations.

**Search URL construction for each species**:

For American Ginseng CC-BY observations:
`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_id=116333&photo_license=cc-by&quality_grade=research`

For Goldenseal CC-BY observations:
`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_name=Hydrastis+canadensis&photo_license=cc-by&quality_grade=research`

For Black Cohosh CC-BY observations:
`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_name=Actaea+racemosa&photo_license=cc-by&quality_grade=research`

For Ramps CC-BY observations:
`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_name=Allium+tricoccum&photo_license=cc-by&quality_grade=research`

**Curation process**:
- Per species: target 5–8 Research Grade CC-BY observations with clear single-subject photography
- Required angles per species: full plant in habitat, leaf detail, flower or fruit, root/rhizome (where shown), seed or seed pod
- Attribution format: "Photo: [Observer Name], iNaturalist, CC BY 4.0"
- Log each selected photo: observer name, iNaturalist observation URL, license confirmed, intended guide section

**Ramps timing note**: Ramps bloom in early spring (March–April in Appalachia). iNaturalist CC-BY observations from previous springs will be the primary ramps photo source — search for Research Grade observations from 2020–2025 with CC-BY license. There should be several hundred observations available.

**Ginseng habitat privacy note**: Many ginseng observers deliberately obscure their location to prevent poaching. This means ginseng observations may have county-level rather than precise GPS data. This is fine for your use — you need plant photos, not location data.

### Tier 3: Public Domain Botanical Illustrations (Guaranteed Fallback — Execute May 7)

The Biodiversity Heritage Library (biodiversitylibrary.org) and its Flickr archive contain millions of public domain botanical illustrations, including 19th-century engravings and watercolors of all four Wave 1 species. These are public domain (pre-1928) and free for any commercial use without attribution requirements.

**Specific sources**:

- **Biodiversity Heritage Library Flickr**: flickr.com/photos/biodivlibrary — search by species common name. Tag search: "Panax quinquefolius," "Hydrastis canadensis," "Actaea racemosa," "Allium tricoccum"
- **BHL Web Archive**: biodiversitylibrary.org — search species name, filter to illustrated plates
- **Ginseng and Goldenseal Growers' Handbook (1912)**: biodiversitylibrary.org/item/116021 — public domain, includes engraved illustrations of both species
- **USDA Pomological Watercolor Collection**: loc.gov/collections/usda-pomological-watercolor — primarily fruit, but cross-botanical coverage is broad
- **Public Domain Review**: publicdomainreview.org/collections — curated botanical illustration sets

**How to use PD illustrations in guides**:
Public domain botanical illustrations carry a heritage quality that positions the guide content as scholarly and authoritative. Used alongside modern iNaturalist photography, they signal depth of research. Place one full-page botanical illustration per species as the "heritage reference" visual — then use modern CC-BY photos for identification and cultivation sections.

## Section 5: Guide Production — Canva Critical Path (May 15–20)

### Structure of Each Guide (8 Zones)

Each guide is a 25–35 page PDF with 8 content zones:

| Zone | Section | Content Summary | Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | Plant name, hero image, subtitle "Know it. Grow it. Protect it." | Low |
| 2 | Quick ID | Leaf shape, height, habitat, color photos, lookalike warning | Medium |
| 3 | Ecology | Native range, habitat preference, seasonal timing, relationship to humans | Medium |
| 4 | Cultivation | Propagation method, stratification, grow timeline, difficulty, forest-farming method | High |
| 5 | Conservation | CITES/UpS status, harvest ethics, cultivation as conservation, where to report poaching | High |
| 6 | Practical Use | Ethnobotanical uses, modern herbal applications, preparation, dosage, contraindications | High |
| 7 | Sourcing Guide | Where to buy cultivated seeds, reputable vendors, sustainability ratings | Medium |
| 8 | Further Learning | Books, scientific articles, conservation organizations, UpS membership | Low |

**Build order rationale**: Zones 5–6 (Conservation + Practical Use) are built first because they require the most research integration and set the authoritative tone. If Zones 5–6 are right, the rest flows from them. Zones 1–4 are more template-driven once the master structure is established.

### Canva Workflow: Critical Path

**Day 1 — May 15: Brand Kit lock + Zone 5–6 master template (3–4 hours)**
- Open Canva. Confirm Seedwarden Brand Kit is active (colors, fonts locked per `phase-2-canva-workflow.md` Part 1).
- Create a new design: A4 (210×297mm) or Letter (8.5×11in). Title: "Endangered Species Guide — MASTER TEMPLATE"
- Build Zone 5 (Conservation) first: 4-page section. Include:
  - Species status table (CITES/UpS/ESA columns, red/yellow/green color coding)
  - "Why This Matters" callout box (harvest crisis statistics — see `endangered-species-candidate-list.md` Framework 1)
  - Conservation actions checklist: grow cultivated, source from verified vendors, never harvest wild in state-listed areas, report poaching to state fish and wildlife
  - UpS membership CTA (unitedplantsavers.org)
- Build Zone 6 (Practical Use) second: 4-page section. Include:
  - Traditional use table (Cherokee/Iroquois/Appalachian usage by species)
  - Modern applications summary (herbalist-level, not medical claims — FTC-compliant framing required)
  - Preparation methods (tea, tincture, decoction, topical)
  - Contraindications box (red background, prominent) — required for goldenseal (do not use long-term; pregnancy contraindicated), black cohosh (hepatotoxicity rare but documented), ramps (allium caution for GERD)
  - Disclaimer footer: "For educational purposes only. Consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use."
- Export Zone 5–6 as draft PDF; review for readability at 100% zoom on phone screen.
- **Gate check**: Zone 5–6 reading length should feel authoritative but not overwhelming — target 8–10 minutes per zone at a leisurely pace.

**Day 2 — May 16: Zones 1–4 master template (2–3 hours)**
- Add Zone 1 (Cover): hero image full bleed, species name in primary font (32pt+), Latin name in secondary font (14pt), "Know it. Grow it. Protect it." tagline, Seedwarden logo footer
- Add Zone 2 (Quick ID): two-column layout, identification photo left, bullet list right. Include lookalike warning box (yellow background) — critical for safety: ginseng has several lookalikes; bloodroot and mayapple are toxic lookalikes in the Appalachian woodlands.
- Add Zone 3 (Ecology): native range map image (source from USDA Plants Database — public domain), habitat description, seasonal calendar strip
- Add Zone 4 (Cultivation): step-by-step numbered list, stratification table (species | cold period needed | warm period needed | germination rate), forest-farming shade percentage bar graphic

### Content Notes per Species

Use these to fill guide zones. All factual claims are sourced from `endangered-species-candidate-list.md` and the sources cited there.

**American Ginseng**
- Zone 5 lead stat: CITES Appendix II since 1975; wild harvest collapsed to below 20,000 lbs in 2024 (down from 62,000 lbs pre-2015 baseline, per American Ginseng Alliance)
- Zone 4 cultivation note: forest-farming under 80% canopy shade; seed requires 18-month dormancy; plant back red seeds where you find them
- Zone 6 use: adaptogenic, ginsenosides, Cherokee ceremonial plant; do not harvest before berries ripen; never dig in state-listed areas
- Zone 7 vendors: Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds (fall availability), Penn State Extension Forest Farming program

**Goldenseal**
- Zone 5 lead stat: CITES Appendix II since 1997; 95%+ of commercial goldenseal is still wild-harvested (Sustainable Herbs Initiative)
- Zone 4 cultivation: rhizome division preferred over seed; 5-year to first harvest; companion plant with ginseng
- Zone 6 use: berberine alkaloid antibacterial properties; short-term use only (2 weeks maximum traditional guideline); pregnancy contraindicated
- Zone 7 vendors: Strictly Medicinal Seeds ($20/20-seed packet), Johnny's Selected Seeds (organic roots), Prairie Moon Nursery

**Black Cohosh**
- Zone 5 lead stat: UpS At-Risk; Massachusetts-listed endangered; 95% wild-harvested despite 20+ years of conservation warnings (UpS)
- Zone 4 cultivation: partial shade (40–60% canopy), humus-rich soil, 3–5 years to bloom from seed
- Zone 6 use: traditional menopausal support; hepatotoxicity rare but real — include contraindication box; FTC framing: "has been traditionally used"
- Zone 7 vendors: Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Fedco Seeds, Vermont Wildflower Farm

**Ramps / Wild Leek**
- Zone 5 lead stat: Quebec commercial harvest banned after 20% of documented populations disappeared; NYC harvest prohibited from public land; West Virginia caps national forest harvest at one grocery bag per person
- Zone 4 cultivation: slow regeneration (5 years to reproductive maturity from seed); bulb division more practical for home cultivation; woodland understory, moist soil
- Zone 6 use: Appalachian foodways (ramp festivals); Cherokee wasdi harvesting traditions; culinary: raw or cooked, entire plant edible; caution for allium sensitivity
- Zone 7 vendors: Experimental Farm Network (when in stock), Prairie Moon Nursery, Strictly Medicinal Seeds, small Appalachian native plant nurseries via Xerces Society directory

## Section 10: Risk Mitigation and Contingencies

### Risk 1: Ginseng/Goldenseal prop seeds unavailable in spring

**Likelihood**: High for ginseng (fall crop); moderate for goldenseal.
**Impact**: Low. Prop seeds are optional.
**Response**: Use dried ginseng root (purchased from health food store) + printed BHL botanical illustration as ginseng prop. For goldenseal, the rhizome/root form is actually more visually distinctive than the seed — photograph a purchased goldenseal root supplement (or tincture bottle) alongside the BHL illustration.
**No schedule impact.**

### Risk 2: iNaturalist CC-BY photo pool insufficient (fewer than 3 per species)

**Likelihood**: Low for black cohosh and ramps (abundant observations); moderate for goldenseal (less photographed); low for ginseng.
**Impact**: Medium.
**Response tier 1**: Contact iNaturalist contributors who have CC-BY-NC observations and request individual permission for commercial use — many will grant it for a conservation-focused educational product.
**Response tier 2**: Commission one professional botanical photographer for $100–$200 flat fee. Search Instagram for #goldenseal or #hydrastis photographers; offer credit + guide copy + flat fee.
**Response tier 3**: Rely entirely on BHL public domain illustrations for that species. Aesthetically distinctive and no worse for the conservation-focused audience.
**Deadline trigger**: If by May 9 you have fewer than 3 usable CC-BY photos for any species, activate response tier 1 immediately.

### Risk 3: Botanical garden does not respond or declines

**Likelihood**: Moderate (4–8 week partnership timelines are the norm per `endangered-species-candidate-list.md`).
**Impact**: Low. Botanical garden photos are a quality upgrade, not a necessity.
**Response**: Proceed with Tier 2 + Tier 3 photo sources. Botanical garden partnership remains open for Wave 2 (Spring Ephemerals bundle, fall 2026) — begin outreach again in August 2026.
**No schedule impact.**

### Risk 4: Canva guide production takes longer than estimated

**Likelihood**: Moderate. 4 hours per guide is realistic for Zone 5–6 template creation; Zones 1–4 in a new guide may take 2–3 hours with all content already drafted.
**Impact**: Medium — risks pushing launch to June 6.
**Response**: The minimum viable launch is 2 guides live on May 30 (ginseng + goldenseal — the two highest market demand species). Black cohosh and ramps go live June 6. The bundle listing activates only when all 4 are live. Email the list on May 30 with 2 guides; send a June 6 follow-up email with "the collection is now complete."
**Float decision point**: If guides 3 and 4 are not exportable by May 27, trigger the 2-guide partial launch.

### Risk 5: Etsy account verification not complete before listing activation

**Likelihood**: Low if Phase 1 Etsy account is already verified.
**Impact**: High — no digital products can be sold without a verified account.
**Response**: This is a user gate action that must be resolved before any listing goes live. If Etsy 2FA / account verification is still pending (per `PHASE_2_LAUNCH_PREP.md`), resolve this as Priority 1 before any other Phase 2 work. Contact Etsy support immediately if the verification process is blocked.
**Cascade**: All other Phase 2 and endangered species launch work can proceed in parallel — but nothing goes live on Etsy until the account is verified.

### Worst-Case Stacked Scenario

Seeds unavailable + botanical garden no-response + Canva takes 5 hours per guide.

**Result**: Launch with iNaturalist CC-BY + BHL illustrations for all photos; 2 guides live May 30 (ginseng + goldenseal); full 4-guide collection live June 6. Bundle listing activates June 6. Revenue impact: 7-day delay in bundle sales; individual guide sales start May 30 as planned.

This outcome is entirely acceptable and was anticipated in `endangered-species-market-analysis.md` (September 2026 was the original Wave 1 launch target in that document — May 30 is already an aggressive acceleration).
