# Gate 2 — Canva Pro Decision Framework

**Read this May 19 morning. Decide by May 19 afternoon.**

---

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Start the 30-day free trial of Canva Pro on May 19. Do not pay anything yet.**

The decisive constraint is the Brand Kit color limit. Seedwarden's Brand Kit requires 10 colors (6 brand colors + 4 zone band colors). Canva's free tier limits Brand Kits to 1 kit with 3 colors. You cannot build the specified Brand Kit on the free tier without manually re-entering hex codes on every single design element — across 8 zone cards, 5+ Pinterest pin templates, Instagram carousels, and banners. That friction is real and cumulative.

The 30-day free trial of Canva Pro is available, requires a credit card to activate but does not charge until the trial ends, and can be cancelled at any time during the trial at no cost. Zone card production runs May 24–25. If all 8 cards are exported before May 30, you get the full value before any payment is required. The commit decision — whether to continue at $15/month or cancel — happens May 24 when you review what was actually produced. At the financial model's Month 6 revenue projection ($1,200–2,400/month gross), $15/month represents 0.6–1.2% of revenue, well below the threshold that warrants hesitation.

The free Canva tier is not sufficient. No credible alternative reaches the same workflow speed for this specific use case. Start the trial.

| Monthly | $15.00/month | $15.00 | Cancel anytime; no minimum term |

**Recommendation for Seedwarden**: Start monthly. At launch there is no revenue certainty. If Seedwarden reaches Phase 3 triggers (100+ subscribers, 10+ orders/month), switch to annual at that point and save $60/year.

### Free Trial (Confirmed)

- **Length**: 30 days
- **Credit card required to activate**: Yes — a card or PayPal must be on file
- **Charge if cancelled during trial**: None. Cancelling at any point before day 30 does not trigger a charge
- **How to cancel**: Canva > Settings > Billing > Cancel Plan
- **Access after cancellation**: Pro features remain active until the 30-day period expires, even if you cancel on day 1
- **Regional variation**: The trial may be 14 days in some regions. If the Canva.com interface shows 14 days rather than 30, proceed anyway — 14 days is sufficient to complete all zone card production (May 19 trial start + 14 days = June 2, which post-dates the May 25 zone card deadline)

### Tier Comparison for a 1-Person Operation

| Feature | Free | Pro ($15/mo) | Teams ($10/user/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand Kits | 1 kit, 3 colors max | 5 kits, unlimited colors | 100 kits |
| Templates | 250,000+ free | 610,000+ total | Same as Pro |

**Verdict on tier**: Pro is correct for a 1-person operation. Teams adds multi-seat collaboration and 100+ Brand Kits — neither is needed until Phase 3 team expansion. Do not pay for Teams.

### Features Specifically Relevant to Seedwarden Zone Cards and Social Assets

**Brand Kit (the decisive one)**: Pro allows unlimited colors per kit. Seedwarden's kit has 10 colors. Without Pro, you are limited to 3 colors in the kit and must manually enter hex codes (`#3D6B8A`, `#2D5016`, `#C9943A`, `#A0522D` for zone bands) every time you touch a zone card. Across 8 cards × multiple elements each, this is 30–50 manual hex entries with high error risk.

**Magic Resize**: Zone cards are 8.5×11 inches. Pinterest pins are 1000×1500 px. Instagram carousels are 1080×1080 px. The 30-day calendar calls for all three formats. With Magic Resize, you design once and generate all formats in one click. Without it, you rebuild each layout from scratch — estimated extra time: 45–90 minutes per format conversion.

**Content Planner (social scheduling)**: Posts to Pinterest, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and LinkedIn directly from Canva without Buffer or Later. If you are already using Buffer, this is redundant. If you have not set up Buffer yet, Canva Pro's native scheduler eliminates that Gate entirely.

**Premium templates**: The zone card design is being built from a custom canvas (8.5×11 US Letter) rather than a pre-built Canva template. Premium templates are not needed for the zone card production sprint. They become useful after launch for Pinterest pin variety and Instagram carousel variation.

**Background remover**: Not needed for zone cards (no photos with backgrounds in the zone card spec). Becomes useful for lifestyle photography mockups in Phase 2 social content.

**141M+ premium asset library**: The zone card icon set uses line-style botanical icons available in Canva's free library (seedling, calendar, jar, seed packet). No premium assets are required for zone cards. This feature becomes useful for Phase 3 premium imagery.

**Bottom line for Gate 2**: The only Pro feature you actually need for zone cards is the Brand Kit color unlock. Everything else is a Phase 3 convenience. The trial gives you access to all Pro features, but the Brand Kit alone justifies activating it.

## Section 2: Alternative Tools Analysis

If the Canva Pro trial is unavailable, refused, or the decision is a hard "no," here are the realistic alternatives evaluated specifically for zone-card production.

### Option A: Canva Free Tier with Manual Color Entry

**Cost**: $0

**How it works**: Create a Brand Kit with the 3 most-used colors (Deep Forest Green `#143b28`, Warm Cream `#F5EDD6`, Burnt Sienna `#A0522D`). For the 4 zone band colors, maintain a reference note and paste hex codes manually each time.

**Zone card viability**: Viable but painful. Each zone card requires 1 zone band color change — you enter the hex manually. Across 8 cards, that is 8 manual entries. Not a blocking problem.

**Batch workflow impact**: Each card duplication also requires manually resetting any zone-specific colored elements that were stored via Brand Kit on the previous card. Risk of color inconsistency increases.

**Recommended if**: You decide the trial is not worth the credit card entry, and you want to complete zone cards with zero cost.

**Not recommended if**: You plan to produce social content at the 4-posts/week Instagram + 7-10 pins/week Pinterest cadence in the 30-day calendar. At that volume, Brand Kit saves approximately 4–6 hours over June.

### Option B: Figma Free Tier

**Cost**: $0 (free); Pro is $12–15/editor/month annually

**Free tier limits**: 3 active team files. Personal drafts are unlimited. No team libraries, no branching, no Dev Mode.

**Zone card viability**: Figma can produce the zone card layout (8.5×11 portrait, custom canvas). However:
- Figma is vector-first, built for UI/UX, not print document layout
- PDF export from Figma free does not natively produce PDF Print at 300 DPI — you export as PDF but the resolution handling is less predictable than Canva's explicit "PDF Print" mode
- Learning curve for non-designers: significant. Canva's drag-and-drop is meaningfully faster for this use case
- Color tokens (Figma's equivalent of Brand Kit) are locked behind Pro in team files; in personal drafts you manage colors manually

**Verdict**: Do not switch to Figma for zone cards. It is a more powerful tool for the wrong use case (UI design), with a steeper learning curve and no print workflow advantage over Canva free. If you are already a Figma user, you know this already.

### Option C: Affinity Designer (Now Free)

**Cost**: $0 — Canva acquired Serif (Affinity) in 2024; core Affinity apps are now free. Advanced AI features require Canva Pro.

**Zone card viability**: Affinity Designer is a professional vector and layout application. It can produce the zone card design at print quality. Key facts:
- Requires download and install (desktop app, not browser-based)
- Learning curve is substantial — closer to Adobe InDesign than Canva
- No cloud Brand Kit equivalent; colors are managed per-file via swatches
- PDF export at 300 DPI is native and reliable
- No social scheduling, no Magic Resize equivalent
- Batch workflow (duplicate template, update per zone) is possible but slower than Canva for non-designers

**Verdict**: If you have prior graphic design experience and Affinity is already installed, this is a legitimate free alternative. If you are starting from scratch, the learning curve costs more time than the Canva Pro trial costs money. Do not install Affinity for this project unless you are already fluent in it.

### Option D: Procreate (iPad)

**Cost**: $12.99 one-time (iPad only)

**Zone card viability**: Poor. Procreate is a raster drawing app optimized for illustration and hand-drawn work. It lacks:
- Text layout tools suitable for multi-column document design
- Precise column and margin control
- Reliable PDF Print output at 8.5×11 with embedded fonts
- Template duplication workflow

**Verdict**: Not appropriate for zone cards. Procreate excels at botanical illustration for social content (hand-drawn plant sketches for Reels/TikTok), which is a Phase 3 opportunity — not a Gate 2 tool.

### Option E: Piktochart / Pixlr / Other Browser Tools

**Piktochart**: Infographic-focused. Free tier includes limited templates. Can produce single-page layouts but not optimized for print (PDF export quality is inconsistent). Not recommended.

**Pixlr**: Raster photo editor. No layout/document design capability relevant to zone cards. Not applicable.

**Adobe Express (formerly Spark)**: Free tier available. Similar to Canva in UX. Brand Kit on free tier is limited. The Canva ecosystem is more familiar and better documented for this specific use case.

**Pure HTML/CSS zone cards**: Technically possible — build a responsive HTML template, fill in per-zone data, print to PDF from browser. This is the developer path. Time investment to build: 3–5 hours. Output quality: depends on browser print rendering, which is not 300 DPI. Not recommended unless you have a specific reason to prefer a web-native format.

## Section 3: Zone Card Technical Specifications

This section documents the confirmed specs so the May 19–24 Brand Kit setup matches the May 24–25 production sprint exactly.

### Dimensions

**Format**: US Letter, portrait orientation
**Width × Height**: 8.5 inches × 11 inches
**Canvas setup in Canva**: Create design > Custom size > 8.5 in × 11 in

These are home-print dimensions, not retail card dimensions. Zone cards are not printed and mailed — they are downloaded as PDFs by email subscribers and printed at home, or read on-screen. The 8.5×11 format was chosen because:
- US Letter is the default paper size in North American home printers
- Portrait orientation fits the three-column layout with a substantial variety spotlight section at the bottom
- A subscriber can print it without any scaling or margin adjustment

(Standard retail "zone cards" used by nurseries are typically 4×6 or 5×7 postcard-format — those are point-of-sale displays, not lead magnets. Seedwarden's zone card is a printable PDF field guide, not a nursery postcard.)

### File Formats

| Output | Format | Settings | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary deliverable | PDF Print | 300 DPI, no crop marks, no bleed | Email delivery via Kit |
| Social preview | PNG | 1080×1080 px (crop header section) | Instagram/Pinterest preview |
| Pinterest pin | PNG | 1000×1500 px (portrait crop or redesign) | Pinterest pin image |

PDF Print at 300 DPI is available on both Canva Free and Canva Pro. The free tier does not include crop marks — but Seedwarden's zone cards do not require crop marks (home print, no bleed). This is a non-issue.

### Batch Design Workflow

The 8-zone workflow is: build one master template (Zone 5), then duplicate and update per zone. What changes per zone:

| Element | Changes per zone | Time to update |
|---|---|---|
| Zone number (72pt, Playfair Display) | Yes — the large "5", "6", etc. | 10 seconds |
| Zone color band (12px horizontal rule) | Yes — 4 color groups | 20 seconds |
| Region name | Yes — "Central Corridor", "Piedmont South", etc. | 10 seconds |
| Frost dates | Yes — 3 lines | 60 seconds |
| This Month tasks | Yes — 3 bullet points | 3–5 minutes |
| Quick-start crops | Yes — 3 entries | 3–5 minutes |
| Storage tips | Yes — 3 entries | 2–3 minutes |
| Variety spotlight | Yes — 3 varieties | 4–6 minutes |
| Header, footer, layout, fonts, colors | No — identical across all 8 | 0 |

Per-zone update time after master template is built: **15–20 minutes per card**. Total for 7 remaining cards after Zone 5 master: approximately 2–2.5 hours. All per-zone content is pre-written and copy-paste ready in `CANVA_ZONE_CARD_BATCH_WORKFLOW.md`.

### Mobile Responsiveness

Not applicable. Zone cards are static PDF downloads, not web pages. They are viewed in a PDF reader (phone, tablet, or desktop) or printed. No HTML responsiveness is required.

### Step 1: Is the 30-day free trial available at canva.com when you check May 19?

**How to check**: Go to canva.com > click "Try Canva Pro free for 30 days" (or equivalent offer on the homepage). If the offer is present and you can start it: proceed to Step 2.

### Step 2: Can you put a credit card on file temporarily and remove it or cancel if you decide not to continue?

**If YES**: Start the free trial immediately. You pay nothing until June 18 (30 days from May 19 start). Your Gate 2 Brand Kit setup happens May 19–24. Zone card production happens May 24–25. - If 8 zone cards are exported and you see the Brand Kit saved you significant time: keep Pro at $15/month. The Zone-by-Zone Seed Starting Calendar ($7/zone, $18 bundle) needs only 2 sales/month to cover the Pro subscription.

**If NO (no credit card available or not willing to put one on file)**: Go to Step 3.

---

### Step 3: Can the Canva Free tier complete the zone card production?

**Answer**: Yes, with a workaround. The free tier supports:
- 1 Brand Kit with 3 colors (insufficient for full spec — use 3 most critical colors)
- PDF Print export at 300 DPI (fully sufficient for zone card output)
- All required fonts (Playfair Display, Lato, Cormorant Garamond — all free in Canva)
- All required free icons (botanical line icons available in free library)
- Custom canvas dimensions (8.5×11 inches)

**The workaround**: Store hex codes for the 7 remaining colors in a plain-text note. Paste them manually when needed. The total number of manual entries across 8 zone cards is manageable (approximately 30–40 entries). This adds 10–15 minutes to total production time.

**If you choose the free tier**: Proceed to Section 5, Implementation Checklist, Option B (Free Tier Path).

## Section 6: Fallback Plan

**Situation**: Canva Pro trial is unavailable, credit card is not an option, and the free tier 3-color Brand Kit creates blocking problems during production.

**Path 1 — Canva Free with manual colors** (described in Option B above): This is not actually a blocking problem. It is an inconvenience that adds 15 minutes. Use it.

**Path 2 — Affinity Designer (if installed)**: Open Affinity Publisher or Designer. Create an 8.5×11 document. Build the zone card layout with the Seedwarden color palette stored as swatches (File > Swatches). Export as PDF. This produces identical output to Canva. Time investment is higher for initial setup if you are not familiar with Affinity.

**Path 3 — Delay Brand Kit, start zone card production immediately**: Build the Zone 5 master card using Canva Free with colors manually applied. Once the template is structurally correct, upgrade to Pro at any point to retroactively add Brand Kit. The upgrade applies immediately and the Brand Kit becomes available within the same session. You do not need to start over.

**Situation**: Zone cards are not ready by May 25.

**Impact**: Gate 3 (Kit Email 1 build) cannot be completed because the Kit email requires zone card PDF download links. The June 2 slip threshold still allows a June 2–6 launch with full infrastructure.

**Action**: If zone card production reaches May 27 and is not done, complete the Kit account setup in parallel (Phase 3C can start — account creation, tags, landing page, email sequence) while zone cards finish. The only Gate 3 step that requires zone card links is Email 1 body and the Kit automation trigger. Everything else can be built without the PDFs.
