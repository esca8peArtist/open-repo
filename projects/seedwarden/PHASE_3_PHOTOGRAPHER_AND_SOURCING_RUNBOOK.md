# Phase 3 Photographer and Sourcing Runbook

**Prepared**: May 31, 2026  
**Purpose**: Streamline Phase 3 photo sourcing across three channels (Wikimedia Commons CC, botanical garden direct contact, contracted photographer) and coordinate photographer workflow so the first bundle's photography is delivered within 10 days of author manuscript completion.

**Success criteria**: First bundle photos delivered within 10 calendar days of manuscript completion. All delivered photos logged in PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md before any guide is uploaded to Etsy.

---

## 1. Sourcing Checklist Per Bundle

For each bundle, three image categories are required:

| Category | Required count | Source priority | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cover illustration (main listing image) | 1 per bundle | Flat-lay photography (owner or contracted) | 7–10 days before upload |
| Per-species habit image (full plant) | 1 per species | Wikimedia CC-BY/SA (preferred) → iNaturalist CC-BY → contracted shoot | Source during Phase 2 observation window (May 30–June 20) |
| Per-species detail image (flower, root, or preparation) | 1 per species | Same sourcing priority as habit | Source during Phase 2 observation window |
| Practitioner lifestyle photos (guide in use) | 2–3 per bundle | Unsplash CC0 (preferred) → contracted photographer | Source before Week 1 upload |

**Minimum deliverable per bundle** (confirmed sufficient for Etsy listing per `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHY_STAGING_CHECKLIST.md`):
- 1 flat-lay cover photo (Etsy listing slot 1)
- 1 lifestyle photo (Etsy listing slot 2)
- 1 species collage or botanical detail (Etsy listing slot 3)
- 1 "inside spread" mockup showing guide interior page layout (Etsy listing slot 4)
- 1 practitioner/lifestyle use photo (Etsy listing slot 5)

## 2. Botanical Garden Contact List with Lead Times

Use these contacts when Wikimedia Commons coverage for a species is thin or when a specific photo type (forest farming, rare flower, root cross-section) is needed that CC archives do not have.

**Standard ask**: "I am producing an educational digital guide on [species common name] for home herbalists and small-scale growers. I am seeking permission to use one or two of your [garden/collection] photographs for non-commercial educational use. I will provide full photo credit in the guide and on our Etsy product listing. Would you be willing to grant a limited educational license for this use?"

| Organization | Contact | Species strength | Lead time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NC Botanical Garden (Chapel Hill, NC) | media@ncbg.unc.edu | Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, Bloodroot — Appalachian natives | 5–10 business days | NCBG has one of the strongest Black Cohosh photo archives in the Southeast. Educational use requests are typically granted with photo credit. |
| Missouri Botanical Garden (St. Louis, MO) | media@mobot.org | Goldenseal, Elderberry, Echinacea — comprehensive coverage | 7–14 business days | MoBot image library is large; request specific species and image type. Budget 2 weeks for response. |
| Vermont Wildflower Farm | Direct inquiry via website contact form | Black Cohosh cultivated growing stock | 3–5 business days | Specialty grower; informal contact more likely to succeed than formal media inquiry. Ask via their email contact form for a photo from their growing beds. |
| Mt. Cuba Center (Hockessin, DE) | communications@mtcubacenter.org | Echinacea angustifolia, native wildflowers, forest garden specimens | 5–7 business days | Mt. Cuba's garden photography documents natural habit; their Echinacea prairie restoration photos are strong. |
| Chicago Botanic Garden (Glencoe, IL) | media@chicagobotanic.org | Elderberry (native Sambucus canadensis), Passionflower, prairie herbs | 7–14 business days | Large media library. Specify "educational non-commercial digital guide" in request. |
| Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center (Austin, TX) | photos@wildflower.org | Passionflower (native range), Mullein, prairie and Eastern herbs | 5–10 business days | Strong Southern/Great Plains coverage; useful for species range diversity photos. |

**Lead time guidance**: If a botanical garden photo is needed for a bundle, send the inquiry at least 14 days before the upload target date. Inquiries sent with less than 10 days notice typically do not receive timely responses.

**Fallback**: If no response within 7 business days, proceed to Wikimedia Commons and iNaturalist paths. Do not delay production waiting for garden permission responses. Log the outreach date and status in WORKLOG.md.

## 3. Photographer Outreach Template and Shoot Brief

Use this template when contracting a local botanical or product photographer for Phase 3 flat-lay and lifestyle images.

### Outreach Email Template

```
Subject: Botanical Herb Guide Photography — [DATE RANGE] Session

Hi [Name],

I am producing a series of digital medicinal herb guides for home herbalists and small-scale
growers, launching on Etsy in [MONTH] 2026. I am looking for a photographer for a [1–2 day]
shoot of flat-lay and lifestyle images — dried herbs, growing plants, and simple preparation
setups.

Session details:
- Date(s): [PROPOSED DATE RANGE]
- Location: [HOME STUDIO / OUTDOOR GARDEN / YOUR CHOICE]
- Duration: [4–6 hours / 2 days at 3 hours each]
- Deliverables: [10–15] edited high-resolution images (minimum 2000×2000px for Etsy)
- Style: Clean flat-lay on natural-fiber surface or marble; lifestyle showing guide in use
  with plants and simple props (mortar and pestle, glass jars, dried herb bundles)
- License required: Full commercial use, unlimited Etsy and digital distribution

Budget: [$150–300] for the session — open to discuss based on your rates.

I can provide a detailed shot list and prop inventory before the session. All plants and
props are either owner-sourced or purchased; no live animals or location permits required.

Are you available for a [half-day / two half-day] session in [DATE RANGE]? Happy to discuss.

[Your name]
Seedwarden
```

### Shoot Brief (send with confirmed booking)

**Cover type**: Flat-lay on a natural linen or light marble surface. Central subject: the guide PDF mockup printout or a simulated guide cover surrounded by 3–5 herb specimens relevant to the bundle theme. Background: clean, uncluttered. Lighting: diffuse natural window light or soft box; no harsh shadows on guide cover text.

**Lifestyle type**: Guide open or partially folded, placed in a practical setting: beside a mortar and pestle, on a wooden workbench with herb bundles hanging, or open on a table with a tea cup and a fresh herb sprig. The subject is not the person — it is the guide in use in a herbalist's environment.

**Required shots per bundle** (minimum set):
1. Top-down flat-lay: guide cover visible, 3–5 herb specimens, natural surface
2. 45-degree angle flat-lay: slightly more depth; guide tilted to show page thickness
3. Lifestyle shot 1: guide open to a species section, surrounded by relevant herbs
4. Lifestyle shot 2: guide beside a preparation (tea, tincture jar, dried herb bundle)
5. Close-up detail: a single herb specimen from the bundle in foreground, guide blurred in background

**Technical specifications** (communicate to photographer before session):
- Minimum resolution: 2000×2000px (square preferred for Etsy; landscape also acceptable)
- Color space: sRGB
- File format: JPEG at maximum quality (or RAW + JPEG if editing is included)
- No heavy filters, presets, or HDR processing — clean, true-color output preferred
- White balance: daylight or cloudy (not auto — consistent across session)

## 4. Photo Shoot Timeline

**Standard 10-day delivery timeline** from manuscript completion to photos delivered:

| Day | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Manuscript first draft complete (author submits to orchestrator) | Author |
| Day 0–1 | Review manuscript; confirm herb list and bundle theme for shoot brief | Orchestrator |
| Day 1 | Send shoot brief to confirmed photographer; confirm date | Orchestrator |
| Day 2 | Order any missing props (dried herb bundles, jars, surface materials) | Orchestrator |
| Day 3–4 | Receive props; prepare herb specimens (dry or source fresh) | Orchestrator |
| Day 5 | Photo shoot (4–6 hours active) | Photographer / Owner |
| Day 6–8 | Photo edit and delivery (3 days post-shoot standard) | Photographer |
| Day 9 | Review delivered photos; request any reshoots or crop adjustments | Orchestrator |
| Day 10 | Photos finalized; upload to Etsy drafts | Orchestrator |

**If self-shooting** (no contracted photographer):
- Days 1–4: same (props, specimens, prep)
- Day 5: Self-shoot (half-day, 3–4 hours)
- Day 6–7: Edit in Lightroom or similar (2–3 hours)
- Day 8: Upload to Etsy drafts
- 2 days faster than contracted photography; total elapsed = 8 days

**Parallel path during Phase 2 observation window**: Wikimedia CC and iNaturalist sourcing for all 20+ herb habit photos should be completed before June 20 (see `PHASE_3_PRODUCTION_ACCELERATION_FRAMEWORK.md` Parallel Track 1). This means the flat-lay photography is the only outstanding photo task when June 20 data confirms the scenario.

## 5. Legal and Licensing Verification

All photos used in Seedwarden guides and Etsy listings require confirmed licensing before publication.

### Wikimedia Commons and iNaturalist

**Verify at the individual file level, not the species level.** A species may have some CC-BY-SA images and some All Rights Reserved images in the same Commons gallery. The license is per-file.

| License type | Permissible use | Attribution required | Commercial use | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CC0 (Public Domain) | Yes | No | Yes | Preferred for simplicity |
| CC-BY (Attribution) | Yes | Yes — author name + URL | Yes | Most iNaturalist research-grade observations use CC-BY |
| CC-BY-SA (ShareAlike) | Yes | Yes — author name + URL | Yes | Most Wikimedia Commons botanical photos use CC-BY-SA; ShareAlike means Seedwarden guides must also be shareable under equivalent license — compatible with Etsy digital distribution |
| CC-BY-NC (NonCommercial) | No | N/A | No | Do not use in any Seedwarden product. Flag any NC-licensed images found during sourcing. |
| All Rights Reserved / No License Stated | No | N/A | No | Do not use without explicit written permission |

**Logging requirement**: Every photo used in a Seedwarden guide must be logged in PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md (create if not present; use WORKLOG.md format) with: filename, species, source URL, author name, license, date accessed.

### CITES and At-Risk Species

Per `phase-3-medicinal-herbs-sourcing-guide.md`, Phase 3 includes two species with conservation significance that affect sourcing guidance in the guide text:

**Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*)**: Appendix II of CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species). This does not affect photo sourcing from Wikimedia Commons (photography is not trade). It does affect the guide text: the guide must recommend cultivated-source material and may not present wild harvest as an acceptable option for readers.

**Black Cohosh (*Actaea racemosa*)**: UpS At-Risk. Same restriction on guide text. Conservation sidebar mandatory in species section (see `PHASE_3_CONTENT_AUTHORING_TEMPLATE_KIT.md`).

**Echinacea angustifolia**: UpS Species of Concern (not At-Risk, lower classification). Conservation note recommended but not mandatory in guide text.

**No other Phase 3 herbs** have CITES or UpS listing concerns.

## 6. Photo Delivery Checklist

Run this checklist before uploading any photo to Etsy listings or embedding in a guide PDF.

### Technical Specifications
- [ ] Minimum 2000×2000px (square) or 2000×1333px (landscape) — Etsy minimum is 2000px on shortest side
- [ ] sRGB color space confirmed (not Adobe RGB or CMYK)
- [ ] JPEG format at 80%+ quality (not WebP, not TIFF for Etsy uploads)
- [ ] File size under 10MB per image (Etsy upload limit)
- [ ] No visible watermarks from stock sources

### Lighting and Background
- [ ] Primary subject clearly visible and in focus
- [ ] Background clean and uncluttered (for flat-lay shots)
- [ ] No harsh shadows obscuring guide cover text or herb identification features
- [ ] White balance consistent across the 5-image set for a given bundle

### Naming Convention

Use this naming convention for all Phase 3 product photos:

`[bundle-slug]-[image-type]-[sequence].jpg`

Examples:
- `womens-health-flatlay-01.jpg` — Women's Health bundle, top-down flat-lay, first image
- `respiratory-lifestyle-01.jpg` — Respiratory bundle, lifestyle shot, first image
- `womens-health-species-lavender.jpg` — Women's Health, species identification photo, Lavender
- `black-cohosh-habit.jpg` — Habitat photo for species section embed (follows convention from sourcing guide)

Bundle slugs:
- `womens-health`
- `respiratory`
- `sleep-nervines`
- `immunity`
- `digestive`

Image types:
- `flatlay` — top-down product flat-lay
- `lifestyle` — in-use lifestyle photography
- `species` — botanical species photo for guide interior
- `habit` — full-plant habit photo (Wikimedia CC or iNaturalist sourced)

### Attribution Logging
- [ ] Photo filename logged in PHOTO_ATTRIBUTION_LOG.md
- [ ] Source URL confirmed accessible (spot-check for Wikimedia Commons link rot)
- [ ] License type confirmed (CC0, CC-BY, CC-BY-SA — not NC)
- [ ] Author name recorded for attribution caption in guide

**Success criteria**: First bundle photography delivered within 10 days of manuscript completion. If self-shooting, 8 days. All delivered photos pass this checklist before any Etsy upload or guide embed.
