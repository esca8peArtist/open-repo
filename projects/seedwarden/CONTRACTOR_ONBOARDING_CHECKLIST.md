### Women's Health Writer Onboarding (July 5, evening)

**Pre-kickoff materials**:

- [ ] Content brief: Women's Health bundle — lemon balm, red clover, damiana
  - Word count: 3,600–3,800 words
  - Species sections: 400–500 words each
  - Required content per species: history, active constituents, primary traditional uses, evidence-tier summary (honest about limited evidence where applicable), contraindications with citation, cultivation and harvest notes, preparation methods
  - Supporting sections required: Contraindication reference table, Preparation methods (4 methods minimum), Sourcing and sustainability, Sources (6+ primary citations)
- [ ] FTC compliance one-pager:
  - Prohibited: "treats," "cures," "prevents," "eliminates" without evidence qualifier
  - Required framing: "Traditionally used to...," "Research suggests...," "Preliminary evidence indicates...," "May support..."
  - Damiana section: Must state explicitly that clinical evidence is limited
  - All therapeutic claims must include evidence tier in the same sentence or immediately following
- [ ] Reference sources list: NatMed Pro, United Plant Savers At-Risk list, Cochrane Library, MSK Integrative Medicine database, American Botanical Council HerbalGram database
- [ ] Sample species entry (from existing Seedwarden guide) showing correct format — tone, citation placement, contraindication formatting
- [ ] Mandatory contraindications list: Lemon balm thyroid interaction (TSH inhibition); red clover drug interactions (warfarin, hormone therapy); damiana blood sugar effects (diabetes medication interaction)

**Kickoff call script** (25 minutes):

1. Intro (2 min): "You're writing the Women's Health bundle — lemon balm, red clover, damiana. One manuscript, 3,600–3,800 words, educational and evidence-grounded."
2. Tone walkthrough (5 min): Show sample entry; explain the Seedwarden standard — "We're not marketing, we're not academic. We report what the evidence says, including when evidence is thin."
3. FTC compliance (5 min): Walk through one-pager; confirm contractor understands "damiana" requires explicit limited-evidence disclaimer
4. Contraindication standards (5 min): "Every contraindication must appear in its own named section or table. Not buried in body text. If you're not sure whether an interaction exists — look it up in NatMed Pro. If you find it, include it."
5. Schedule (5 min): "Draft due July 21. Revision round turnaround: 48 hours. Final manuscript July 26. If you need an extension beyond July 23 on the draft: email me by July 18 — not July 21 morning."
6. Q&A (3 min)

## Escalation Rules

**Trigger**: Contractor misses a delivery by 24 hours without prior notification.
**Action**: Coordinator sends reminder email immediately (Template D-1 in CONTRACTOR_COMMUNICATION_TEMPLATES.md). If no response or delivery within 24 hours of reminder: user follows up directly with 48-hour final deadline.

**Trigger**: Contractor misses a delivery by 72 hours (day 4 with no delivery).
**Action**: Activate contingency pool (see CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md). Do not wait for contractor to re-engage before activating contingency — activate simultaneously.

**Trigger**: Contractor fails quality gate twice on the same deliverable.
**Action**: User contacts contractor directly. Decision within 24 hours: (1) scope reduction — fewer deliverables at same rate, (2) extension — push deadline 5 days with written confirmation, or (3) transition to backup contractor. If transition: activate CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md immediately.

**Trigger**: Contractor misses 2 consecutive weekly check-in replies (no response to Monday email by Wednesday EOD for 2 weeks in a row).
**Action**: Coordinator escalates to user within 2 hours of Wednesday EOD. User sends direct email same day. If no response within 48 hours: assume availability issue; activate contingency pool.

**Pre-staged contingency pool** (activate in priority order):

1. Photographer backup: Post emergency Upwork job (48h turnaround for qualified applicants); or contact Thumbtack photographers in the same metro area
2. Writer backup: Tier 2 writer candidates identified during June 29–July 3 application review — keep their contact info in the tracker Notes column
3. Herbalist backup: AHG public directory emergency posting; or defer review to Week 4–5 (minimal impact if manuscripts not yet at revision stage)
4. Coordinator backup: Absorb tasks internally; or post Upwork express job (1 day to fill for VA-type work)
5. Social Media Manager backup: Reduce to 3 posts/week solo; or post Upwork express job

If fewer than 5 contractors are delivering on schedule at any point:
- Activate CONTRACTOR_DROPOUT_CONTINGENCY_ACTIVATION.md
- Fallback solo-written content from Q3_MEDICINAL_BUNDLES_COMPLETION_TRACKER.md covers any bundle missing contractor content by upload date
- Upload dates can flex ±3 days without impacting the next bundle's launch window
