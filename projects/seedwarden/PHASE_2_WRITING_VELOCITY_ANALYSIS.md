## Section 1: Species Priority Matrix

### Scoring Methodology

Four dimensions, each scored 1–5. Higher total score = higher priority.

- **(a) Market demand** — Etsy search volume / buyer-intent signals observed in Phase 1 bundle data and competitor landscape research. Weight: 30% of priority decision.
- **(b) Photo availability** — 5 = habit photo already in `assets/wild-edibles/` today (May 12). 3 = field session scheduled May 10–30. 1 = no photo, no session scheduled.
- **(c) Guide complexity** — 5 = simple safety profile, no lookalike risk, short field preparation needed. 1 = requires safety callout, toxic-lookalike comparison, two-stage photography, or herbalist review.
- **(d) Revenue per bundle** — estimated contribution to bundle sale price; species that anchor high-revenue bundles score higher.

**Conservative adjustment**: All time estimates padded 25% above sampled Phase 1 velocity (see Section 2).

**Photo inventory on hand (May 12)**: 18 habit JPEGs confirmed in `projects/seedwarden/assets/wild-edibles/`:
Allium tricoccum, Amaranthus retroflexus, Arctium lappa, Asclepias syriaca, Chenopodium album, Cichorium intybus, Daucus carota, Chamerion angustifolium, Reynoutria japonica, Fragaria virginiana, Helianthus tuberosus, Nasturtium officinale, Oxalis stricta, Portulaca oleracea, Stellaria media, Taraxacum officinale, Typha latifolia, Urtica dioica.

- Identification: 80–120 words

- Season: 30–50 words
- Edible Parts: 20–40 words
- Preparation: 60–100 words
- Safety: 30–60 words
- Notes (optional): 20–40 words

**Total per entry**: 300–500 words body text. With header, photo alt text, cross-reference sentences, and formatting: **~450–600 words per published entry.**

### Writing Time Estimates

**Sampled basis**: The regional guide at 59,843 words with full safety research represents the upper bound. The quick-reference at 143 words/entry is the lower bound. Phase 2 target falls between them.

**Observed writing blocks from WORKLOG.md**: The blueprint explicitly states "approximately 90 minutes per guide at the target word counts." This is the primary benchmark.

**Verification against word count**: 500 words × (60 min / typical professional writing rate of 400–600 words/hr for researched non-fiction) = 50–75 minutes of pure writing. Add Stage 4 photo-section mapping (5–10 min), QA pass (15 min), Canva placement (20 min) = **90–100 minutes per guide total**. This validates the blueprint's 90-minute estimate.

**Conservative padding (25%)**: 90 min × 1.25 = **112 minutes (~1 hour 52 minutes) per guide, conservative rate.**

**Velocity rates per week (5 working days, 1 guide session per day):**

| Rate | Guides/Day | Hours/Day | Guides/Week | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Conservative | 0.75 | ~2.3 hrs | 3–4 | Accounting for photo processing delays, verification waits |
| Moderate | 1.0 | ~1.9 hrs | 5 | One guide per session, no carry-over blocks |
| Aggressive | 1.5 | ~2.8 hrs | 7–8 | Two short guides per day (Wikimedia-sourced, simple safety) |

**Research time per species (Stage 1–3 pre-writing)**:
- Field-photo-dependent species: Stage 4 mapping = 5–10 min. Assume photos already culled. Net additional pre-writing time: 10 min.
- Wikimedia-sourced species: Source verification + WORKLOG.md entry = 20–30 min per species. Net additional: 25 min.
- Species with toxic lookalike: Add 15–20 min for safety section research and verification against iNaturalist / regional key.

**Editing cycles**: QA pass is built into the 90-min estimate. A second editing pass (for guides entering Canva + bundle listing) adds 20 min per guide. Plan one editing cycle per week for the previous week's output.

**Bundle creation time**: Each bundle requires assembling 5–7 guides into a single product listing. This involves: combined PDF compilation (30 min), bundle cover mockup in Canva (45 min), Etsy listing copy (30 min). Total: **~2 hours per bundle.** Budget this as a discrete task separate from per-guide writing.
