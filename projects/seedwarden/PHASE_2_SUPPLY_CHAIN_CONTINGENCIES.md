# Phase 2 Supply Chain Risk Management & Contingency Plans

**Prepared**: May 18, 2026  
**Launch target**: May 30, 2026  
**Days remaining**: 12  
**Purpose**: Pre-made decisions for every credible supply chain disruption. When a delay is
confirmed, find the scenario, execute the response, and update WORKLOG.md. No improvisation
under pressure.

**Critical path summary**: Guide PDF export (minimum 2 guides) is the only hard launch
blocker. Photography, plant sourcing, and Canva work all have documented fallbacks that
preserve May 30 viability. The supply chain is a quality multiplier, not a launch gate.

## Section 1: Backup Supplier Analysis

### 1.1 If Mountain Rose Herbs Is Delayed

Mountain Rose Herbs supplies the two foundational dried root props — American Ginseng whole
dried root (~$14–22/oz) and Goldenseal dried root (~$15–25/oz) — on a 2–3 day USPS Priority
lead time. They are the lowest-risk vendor in the entire sourcing matrix because of
year-round availability, same-day or next-day processing, and no seasonal stock limitations.
A delay from this vendor is a logistics failure (shipping disruption or order error), not a
supply failure.

**Detection**: If no shipping confirmation email arrives within 1 business day of placing the
order, contact Mountain Rose Herbs directly at orders@mountainroseherbs.com or call their
customer service line. Do not wait two days.

**Backup vendor A — Starwest Botanicals** (starwest-botanicals.com)  
Starwest is a direct competitor to Mountain Rose Herbs at comparable quality and price points.
They stock dried ginseng root and goldenseal root year-round with 1–2 day processing and
USPS Priority or FedEx shipping.

- Ginseng dried root (1 oz): $12–19 (sometimes lower than Mountain Rose; verify at order)
- Goldenseal dried root (1 oz): $14–22
- Lead time from order: 3–5 days USPS Priority from their Sacramento, CA warehouse
- Reliability: High — established supplier with similar organic certification standards
- Action threshold: If Mountain Rose Herbs order has not shipped by Day 2 after placement,
  place a parallel order with Starwest immediately. Cancel Mountain Rose when the Starwest
  order ships.

**Backup vendor B — Local health food stores and herb shops**  
Goldenseal dried root is commonly stocked in bulk herb sections at Whole Foods Market, co-ops,
independent natural food stores, and dedicated herb shops in most mid-size cities. Response
time: same day. Cost: $18–30 per oz (premium over mail-order due to retail markup), but zero
shipping wait.

- Ginseng dried root: less commonly stocked in bulk; often available in packaged supplement
  form. For photography, the key visual requirement is the root's tan/cream color and forked
  shape. A ginseng root purchased from a supplement retailer (Nature's Way, Solaray, or similar
  whole-root supplement) can function as a photography prop if the root structure is intact.
  Check Korean grocery stores, which reliably stock whole ginseng root (Korean red ginseng or
  fresh ginseng) in the produce or supplement section.
- Action threshold: If no shipping confirmation from any mail-order vendor by Day 2, execute
  local retail purchase that day. Zero schedule impact.

**Backup vendor C — Amazon (emergency only)**  
Frontier Co-op and Starwest Botanicals both sell dried roots via Amazon Prime with 1–2 day
delivery. Quality is equivalent; only use if the local retail option fails. Cost runs 15–30%
higher than direct from vendor.

**Quality impact assessment**: All three backup paths produce roots that are functionally
equivalent to Mountain Rose Herbs for photography purposes. The goldenseal yellow root color
and ginseng root structure are not vendor-specific visual properties. No guide content
quality degradation results from switching vendors. This is the lowest-severity supply chain
risk on the list.

### 1.2 If Strictly Medicinal Seeds and Prairie Moon Nursery Both Delay (May 20–27 Recovery Window)

These two vendors supply the live plant specimens: Black Cohosh potted plant (Strictly
Medicinal), and Ramps bulblets plus Trillium bare root (Prairie Moon). Lead times of 7–12
days (Strictly Medicinal) and 7–14 days (Prairie Moon) mean that orders placed by May 13
should arrive May 20–27. If both vendors miss this window simultaneously, the following
recovery path applies.

**Recovery supplier A — Etsy native plant sellers**  
Small Appalachian native plant growers list on Etsy with faster ship windows than commercial
nurseries and no minimum order requirements. Search terms: "black cohosh live plant," "ramps
bulbs cultivated," "trillium live plant," "native plant live." Filter by seller location
(eastern US states preferred for faster transit and seasonal compatibility). Before ordering,
message the seller to confirm: (1) cultivated origin, not wild-harvested, (2) can ship within
2 business days, (3) plants are actively growing and not dormant.

- Lead time: 3–7 days from order if seller ships within 2 days
- Cost: comparable to commercial nurseries ($10–25 per species)
- Action threshold: If Strictly Medicinal and Prairie Moon both show no shipping confirmation
  by May 19, execute Etsy search same day and place orders. Use the vendor communication
  template in PHASE_2_ENDANGERED_SPECIES_PROCUREMENT_TIMELINE.md Part 3.

**Recovery supplier B — Locally sourced substitutes (same-day)**  
In the Appalachian region, Black Cohosh (*Actaea racemosa*) is stocked at native plant
nurseries in late spring. Check regional sources by calling ahead: local native plant
societies, botanical garden plant sales, university extension plant sales, and independent
garden centers specializing in natives.

Organizations that sometimes sell at public plant sales in the Appalachian region:
- NC Native Plant Society chapters (ncnps.org) — spring plant sales through May
- Western Carolina Botanical Club — plant exchanges
- Regional land trust plant sales

These events are zero lead-time and often carry the exact species needed at lower cost than
mail-order ($4–12 per plant). The limitation is unpredictable availability.

**Recovery supplier C — iNaturalist CC-BY as permanent substitute (no shipping required)**  
For every species where the live plant contributes a habit photo or leaf detail photo,
iNaturalist CC-BY Research Grade observations provide equivalent coverage from multiple
geographic contexts, seasonal stages, and lighting conditions. Filter:

`https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?taxon_id=[ID]&license=CC-BY&quality_grade=research&photos=true`

Per-species taxon IDs are documented in ENDANGERED_SPECIES_PHOTO_PIPELINE.md. iNaturalist
CC-BY supply is assessed as:

- Black Cohosh: abundant (high observation density in Appalachian region; leaf habit well covered)
- Trillium grandiflorum: very abundant (most-photographed woodland wildflower in eastern NA)
- Ramps: good (late-April through May observations richest; archive photos available)
- Bloodroot: good for bloom-stage archive; thinner for rhizome/root

**Action threshold**: If both Strictly Medicinal and Prairie Moon have not shipped by May 21,
do not wait. Execute iNaturalist sourcing sprint immediately for Black Cohosh, Trillium, and
Ramps. Complete the sprint in a single 2–3 hour session per the ENDANGERED_SPECIES_PHOTO_PIPELINE.md
protocol. Log every sourced photo in WORKLOG.md with attribution (observer name, iNaturalist
URL, license type CC-BY, access date).

**Total recovery time if both vendors miss completely**: 2–3 hours of iNaturalist sourcing
plus same-day or next-day Etsy vendor order if physical specimens are still desired. No guide
launch date impact. The live plant props are an enhancement layer; iNaturalist provides the
baseline photo coverage that makes a complete guide.

### 1.3 iNaturalist and Local Forage as Species-Specific Fallbacks

For the Tier 1 wild edibles (chickweed, dandelion, watercress, and the other 17 species in
the Phase 2 Guide Content Blueprint), field photography of wild-growing specimens is the
primary photo sourcing strategy, not a fallback. This distinguishes the two production tracks:

**Track 1 (Appalachian Medicinals / at-risk species)**: procured specimens are the primary
path; iNaturalist CC-BY is the permanent supplement and the fallback for any species where
procurement fails.

**Track 2 (Tier 1 wild edibles)**: field photography is primary; iNaturalist CC-BY is the
fallback for species not yet in season, species outside the photographer's range, or species
that failed to photograph well in the field.

Local forage as a live-specimen source applies most directly to Ramps and Wild Bergamot:
Ramps can be purchased fresh at farmers markets (purchase within 48 hours of shoot; refrigerate
wrapped in damp paper towel). Wild Bergamot is stocked live at local garden centers through
June. Both are zero lead-time substitutes for mail-order if needed.

### 2.2 Medium Case — 1–2 Suppliers Delayed 3–5 Days: May 30 → June 4

**Trigger conditions**:
- Strictly Medicinal Seeds and/or Prairie Moon Nursery ship late; plants arrive May 24–26
  instead of May 20–22, OR
- Lifestyle photo shoot was delayed to May 21–22 (from the May 14–18 target), OR
- Canva Brand Kit setup was not completed until May 26 instead of May 24

**Impact on production calendar**:

| Track | Original | Revised | Delta |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Live plant photography | May 20–22 | May 24–26 (after arrival + 48hr acclimation) | +3–4 days |
| Appalachian botanical session | May 20–22 | May 24–26 | +3–4 days |
| Canva production start | May 26 | May 27 | +1 day |
| Canva production end | May 29 | May 30–31 | +1–2 days |
| Etsy upload | May 30, 10am | June 1, 10am | +2 days |

**Contingency adjustments for medium case**:

1. Compress Canva production from 5 days to 4 days by running Black Cohosh and Bloodroot
   as a single production day instead of two. Both are template duplications from the Ginseng
   master; each requires 2–3 hours, so a combined 5–6 hour day absorbs both. Requires a full

2. Launch with 3 guides on May 30 (Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh — these three have the

**Quality impact**: Medium case launches 3 of 5 guides fully photograph-complete on May 30,

**June 4 hard deadline**: If the medium-case scenario materializes, set June 4 as the

### 2.3 Worst Case — Multiple Delays Over 1 Week: June 10 Deferred Launch

**Trigger conditions**:
- Mountain Rose Herbs order not shipped by May 15 AND no local fallback executed, OR
- Lifestyle photo shoot has not occurred by May 22, OR
- Strictly Medicinal Seeds and Prairie Moon Nursery both deliver May 27–30 (after launch date), OR

- Any combination of two or more medium-case delays stacking simultaneously

**Deferred launch date: June 10, 2026**

June 10 is chosen over June 6 (the documented alternate date in june-6-contingency-path.md)
because the worst case involves procurement delays that push all photography to May 27–30,
leaving zero buffer for Canva production if launching June 6. June 10 provides an 11-day
Canva production window from May 30, which is comfortable even under worst-case photo
compression.

**What is available and launchable on May 30 with worst-case delays**:

Even in the worst case, the following are not affected by physical specimen delays:
- Ginseng guide: Mountain Rose Herbs dried root is the only prop required; if a local retail
  fallback was executed, this guide is fully photo-complete regardless of other vendor delays.
- Black Cohosh guide: iNaturalist CC-BY provides the complete photo set; no physical specimen
  is required to launch a high-quality guide.
- Any guide where the photo set is completed from iNaturalist CC-BY + BHL illustration only.

**Recommended May 30 minimum viable launch (worst case)**:

Publish 2 guides on May 30 (Ginseng and Black Cohosh; these two have fully independent photo
sourcing paths). Send the launch Kit email with these 2 guides available. Add a "complete
collection June 10" note to the email. Publish remaining 3 guides June 10 with a follow-up
broadcast email. This approach maintains the May 30 marketing momentum while deferring
photo-dependent production to a realistic timeline.

**Which guides compress from 15-day production to 10-day without quality loss**:

The 15-day production window (May 15–30) was designed with daily milestones for each
procurement, photography, and Canva step. A compressed 10-day window (May 21–30) is
achievable for the following guides without quality loss because their photo sourcing is
complete before the Canva production phase begins:

- **American Ginseng**: Mountain Rose Herbs root available by May 15; iNaturalist and BHL
  photos sourced in the same session. By May 21, all photos are confirmed. Canva production:
  5–6 hours. Can be completed in a single day.
- **Goldenseal**: Same sourcing path as Ginseng. Photos confirmed by May 15–17. Canva
  production: 3–4 hours from the Ginseng template. One half-day.
- **Black Cohosh**: No physical specimen required; iNaturalist CC-BY is the complete photo
  set. All photos confirmable in a 1-hour sourcing session at any point. Canva production:
  3–4 hours. One half-day.

The guides that cannot compress easily are Bloodroot (requires a rhizome prop for the
orange-red sap photo, which has a 30-minute oxidation window requiring careful scheduling)
and Ramps (requires fresh leaves purchased within 48 hours of the photo session, constraining
the timeline). These two should be produced after the core three even in a compressed window.

## Section 3: Critical-Path Options

### 3.1 If Canva Palette Refresh Is Delayed: Launch with Existing Palette

**Scenario**: The "Endangered Species Series" Canva Brand Kit palette (#1C2B1A Deep Forest
Floor, #3B2A1A Aged Bark, #F0E6C8 Pale Parchment, #7A9A6E Sage Mist, #F5F1E8 Bone White,
#8B2000 Conservation Red, #C49A2A Species Gold) has not been added to the Brand Kit by May 26
when guide production begins.

**Option A — Manual hex entry (recommended fallback)**:  
Canva's color picker accepts hex values by hand. The Endangered Species palette is documented
in CANVA_ENDANGERED_SPECIES_STYLE_GUIDE.md. A user who enters hex codes manually each time
adds approximately 2–3 minutes per color application but can produce an identical result to
Brand Kit usage. For a 5-guide series where each guide uses the same 7 colors in a consistent
template, the Brand Kit accelerates work; it is not technically required.

Quality impact: None on the final guides. Visual consistency is identical — the output is
the same hex values regardless of whether they came from the Brand Kit or were typed by hand.
The Brand Kit advantage is speed and error-prevention (no transposing hex digits); neither is
a quality gate.

**Option B — Launch with existing Seedwarden palette, refresh in Phase 3**:  
The existing Seedwarden Brand Kit palette (documented in CANVA_EXECUTION_PLAYBOOK.md) is a
fully functional botanical design palette. The Endangered Species Series palette is an
extension designed to signal conservation-education content specifically. If the extension is
not set up in time, launching with the existing Seedwarden palette is a viable option.

Quality impact: Minor. Buyers in the Etsy market for botanical guides will not recognize the
palette difference. The conservation-specific colors (Conservation Red #8B2000, Species Gold
#C49A2A) contribute to visual signaling that the series is conservation-themed; without them,
the series reads as a botanical guide without the conservation premium signal. This is a
positioning quality impact, not a content quality impact. The educational content of the
guide is unaffected.

Recommendation: Set up the Brand Kit by May 24. Estimated time: 45–90 minutes. If May 24 is
missed, use manual hex entry starting May 26. If both are infeasible, launch with existing
palette and add the Endangered Species palette in the Brand Kit before any Phase 3 production.

- Setup time: 15 minutes

- Habitat context fallback: iNaturalist CC-BY archive (filter by species, quality_grade=research,

**Action trigger**: If no location confirmation is received by May 17 (5-day forecast check
per the weather contingency schedule), activate the indoor studio immediately. Do not wait
for May 19 or May 20. The 3 days saved by early activation preserve float time for Canva
production if needed.

**What indoor studio cannot provide** (and the documented fallback for each):
- Wild Bergamot bloom-stage in prairie habitat: iNaturalist CC-BY July-peak archive photos
- Ramps woodland colony emergence shot: iNaturalist CC-BY March–May archive (excellent supply)
- Ginseng in hardwood forest context: iNaturalist CC-BY (abundant in Appalachian region)

None of these require a field shoot to produce. The indoor studio + iNaturalist path is fully

## Section 5: Activation Checklist

### 5.1 Under-15-Minute Decision Protocol

When a supply chain event is detected, run through this checklist in sequence. Target: decision
confirmed and contingency activated within 15 minutes of detection.

**Step 1 — Identify the supplier and the miss (2 minutes)**:
Name the vendor and the specific failure: missing shipping confirmation, delayed tracking,
damaged goods on arrival, or sold-out stock.

**Step 2 — Check the severity table (2 minutes)**:
Find the vendor row in Section 4.1 above. Read the "Recoverable?" column. If YES, proceed
to Step 3. If the scenario is outside the table, assess: does the miss affect guide PDF
production directly? If no, it is recoverable.

**Step 3 — Confirm the fallback path (3 minutes)**:
Identify the specific fallback from Section 1 or Section 3 that applies. It will be one of:
(a) alternate vendor order, (b) local retail purchase, (c) iNaturalist CC-BY sourcing sprint,
or (d) indoor studio activation. Write down the specific action.

**Step 4 — Execute or schedule the fallback (5 minutes)**:
For same-day fallbacks (local retail, iNaturalist): execute within the same session.
For next-day fallbacks (alternate vendor order): place the order before end of current day.
For guide production schedule adjustments: update the phase-2-day-by-day-execution.md
milestones with revised dates.

**Step 5 — Log in WORKLOG.md (3 minutes)**:
Entry format:
`[DATE] — CONTINGENCY ACTIVATED: [Vendor name] — [Failure type] — [Fallback path chosen] — [Action taken] — [Revised milestone dates]`
