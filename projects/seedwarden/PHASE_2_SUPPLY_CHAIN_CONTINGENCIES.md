## SECTION 2: Minimum Viable Launch Definitions

### 2.1 Guide Count — How Many Guides Are Needed to Launch?

**5-guide full launch** (target): American Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, Ramps.
All five on Etsy May 30. Full bundle listing active. Launch Kit broadcast references complete
Appalachian Medicinals collection.

**3-guide MVL** (acceptable): American Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh. These three share
the most independent photo sourcing paths (Mountain Rose Herbs dried roots + abundant iNat
CC-BY). Template duplication from Ginseng master makes Goldenseal and Black Cohosh fast. A
3-guide launch on May 30 with Bloodroot and Ramps releasing June 4 is a coherent "series
launch" — not a partial launch. Launch email copy reads: "First 3 guides available now.
Full 5-guide Appalachian Medicinals collection complete June 4." Revenue impact: minor; Kit
momentum is preserved because the May 30 broadcast still goes out.

**2-guide MVL** (minimum floor): American Ginseng + Black Cohosh. These two have fully
independent photo sourcing paths. Ginseng needs only the Mountain Rose Herbs dried root (or
local retail fallback). Black Cohosh needs only iNaturalist CC-BY (no physical specimen
required at all). A 2-guide launch is the minimum that supports a meaningful product offering
and a coherent launch announcement. A 1-guide launch delays purchasing intent rather than
enabling it — do not launch with 1 guide.

**Decision rule**: Never delay the launch date to add a guide. A 5-day date delay costs
approximately 15–25% of first-30-day revenue due to lost Kit email momentum and social
posting timing. A guide count reduction from 5 to 3 on May 30 (with June 4 completion)
costs an estimated 5–8% of first-30-day revenue — the math always favors launching on
schedule with available guides.

### 2.3 Photo Supply — Can We Launch with 100% iNaturalist CC-BY Photos?

Yes. iNaturalist CC-BY Research Grade photos are the baseline guide photo standard for this
series. They are not a compromise or a fallback-of-last-resort — they are how every prior

A guide with 5 well-selected iNaturalist CC-BY photos is publishable on Etsy and competitive

1. Proprietary (photographed by the user for Seedwarden)

No photo enters a guide without a logged attribution in WORKLOG.md.

### 4.3 Writing Speed: Can We Accept 10–12 Hours Per Guide Instead of 13–15?

The Phase 2 guides (Appalachian Medicinals) are Canva production tasks, not original writing
tasks — their content structure is templated, species content is pre-researched, and Canva
production time (2.5–6 hours per guide) is the binding variable. The 13–15 hour figure from
prior planning includes research, draft writing, and Canva production as a single estimate.

If we separate the tasks: a researcher-complete species profile (pre-done in prior sessions)
feeds directly into a Canva template duplication. The Canva work per guide is 2.5–6 hours,
not 13–15. There is no quality tradeoff in accepting the compressed per-guide Canva time
because the content quality is determined by the source research, not by the Canva session
duration.

**Conclusion**: The 10–12 hours per guide figure is not the right framing. The real constraint

### 4.4 Zone Card Production — Is the 8-Card Timeline Fixed or Compressible?

Zone card production (Gate 3 Phase A, 7.5–9 hours across May 24–25) is required for Kit
Email 1 delivery. The 8-card set is not compressible in count — all 8 USDA hardiness zones
(3–10) must exist for the zone-routing logic in Kit to function correctly. A subscriber
selecting Zone 7 must receive the Zone 7 card; sending a generic substitute breaks the lead
magnet mechanic.

However, within each card, production time can be compressed from the 1-hour-per-card
estimate to 45 minutes per card (total: 6 hours instead of 7.5–9 hours) if:
1. The Canva Brand Kit is fully active before the session starts (Gate 2 complete today)
2. The CANVA_ZONE_CARD_BATCH_WORKFLOW.md content tables are open and ready to copy-paste
3. No design decisions are made during the session — use the spec in CANVA_ZONE_CARD_DESIGN_GUIDE.md exactly

**Minimum viable zone card**: A zone card that contains (a) correct zone designation, (b)
first frost and last frost dates, (c) 2–3 plant recommendations, and (d) the Seedwarden logo
and download instructions is a publishable lead magnet. Additional design elements (zone band
color, extended plant list, visual hierarchy refinements) enhance quality. Production can
stop at the minimum viable version if time requires.

### 5.2 Severity Tiers

**Tier 0 — Zero impact (supply chain and location failures, R-01 through R-11)**  
All physical specimen delays, all location access denials, all live plant procurement misses,
all lifestyle photo delays, all iNaturalist sprint delays. Every item in this tier is
recoverable with documented fallbacks in under 3 hours. The collective probability of at least
one Tier 0 event occurring is approximately 80%. The probability that any Tier 0 event
damages the May 30 launch: 0%.

**Tier 1 — Minor impact (Canva start 1 day late, R-12)**  
A 1-day Canva start slip (May 27 instead of May 26) results in a 3-guide May 30 launch plus
2 guides on June 4. The May 30 marketing event proceeds. Revenue impact: 5–8% below baseline.
Probability: ~10%.

**Tier 2 — Moderate impact (Canva start 2 days late, R-13)**  
A 2-day slip requires emergency compression. 3 guides launch May 30, 2 launch June 4. Revenue
impact: 8–12% below baseline. Probability: ~5%.

**Tier 3 — Full launch impact (Canva not started by May 29, R-14)**  
Requires simultaneous failure of all pre-production tracks plus failure to execute iNat sprint
and/or local retail fallbacks. Probability: ~2%. Response: June 10 full launch at 100% quality.
All 5 guides available. Revenue impact: 20–30% below baseline first-30-day revenue.

**The critical observation**: Tier 3 requires every Tier 0 fallback to also fail simultaneously.
This is not a realistic scenario given the number of independent fallback paths. The dominant
risk is Tier 1 or Tier 2, both of which are recoverable with a 3-guide May 30 launch and
June 4 completion.
