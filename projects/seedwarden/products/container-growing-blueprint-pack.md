## Important Notice

These growing plans are educational content based on real-world container gardening experience. Your results will vary depending on your climate, light exposure, container quality, and growing conditions. This is not professional agricultural advice. Use these plans as a framework and adapt based on what you observe in your own setup.

---

## How to Use These Plans

Each plan in this pack gives you a complete picture of one container setup: what to grow in it, how to space plants, which plants work well together (and which don't), and how often to water. Print the pages for the containers you're working with and keep them with your supplies.

**Before you plant any container:**
1. Make sure it has drainage holes
2. Fill with quality potting mix, not garden soil
3. Place it in its final location before filling вҖ” containers are heavy once planted

The plans are written for standard growing season conditions in USDA Zones 5вҖ“7. Warmer zones can extend plantings; colder zones may need to shorten them.

### Planting Options (Choose One Per Bucket)

**Option A: One Tomato (Cherry or Compact)**

```
         Top View вҖ” 11" diameter
         в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
         в”Ӯ                     в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ         в—Ҹ           в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ       TOMATO        в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ      (center)       в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ                     в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ   * * * * * * * *   в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ  * BASIL FRINGE  *  в”Ӯ
         в”Ӯ   * * * * * * * *   в”Ӯ
         в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ

         в—Ҹ = 1 tomato plant, centered
         * = 3вҖ“4 basil plants around edge
```

Best varieties: Tumbling Tom, Tiny Tim, Patio, Yellow Pear, Sweetie

Companion planting: Basil planted at the edge of the bucket improves flavor (companion planting lore that many growers swear by), deters aphids, and gives you a bonus harvest. Plant 3вҖ“4 basil starts around the perimeter.

Spacing: Tomato centered, basil at 3вҖ“4" from the edge, spaced evenly around the circumference.

Watering: Daily in summer, especially once fruit is forming. 5-gallon buckets dry out fast in heat. A tomato plant in peak production needs close to 1вҖ“2 gallons of water per day in hot weather. Consider a self-watering insert or reservoir base.

Notes: Provide a cage or stake. Even compact cherry tomato varieties get large. A simple wire cage made from a 5-foot section of concrete reinforcing mesh is cheap and works perfectly.

Best varieties: Any sweet bell, Carmen, Shishito, Banana pepper, most hot peppers (jalapeГұo, serrano, cayenne)

Companion planting: Marigolds around the perimeter deter aphids and spider mites. Carrots grown in a second bucket nearby are said to improve pepper growth. Avoid fennel near peppers.

Spacing: Centered, with 2вҖ“3 marigold transplants tucked around the edge if desired.

Watering: Every 1вҖ“2 days in summer. Peppers are more drought-tolerant than tomatoes but still need consistent moisture for good fruit set. Inconsistent watering causes blossom drop.

Watering: Every 1вҖ“2 days; more in heat. Lettuce wilts quickly when dry but recovers well when watered promptly.

Succession note: Start a second lettuce bucket 3 weeks after the first for continuous harvest.

### Best Use Cases

Window boxes are ideal for trailing, compact, or shallow-rooted crops вҖ” lettuces, herbs, strawberries, radishes, flowers. They're not deep enough for tomatoes or peppers.

**Weight note:** A filled 24" window box can weigh 20вҖ“40 lbs. Make sure your railing bracket or window ledge mount can handle the weight. When in doubt, use a lightweight potting mix with added perlite, and avoid ceramic boxes.

Best tomato varieties for this size: Cherokee Purple, Black Krim, Brandywine (all will need staking), or any large cherry/cocktail tomato.

Spacing: Tomato centered, basil 6вҖ“8" to either side of tomato, marigolds filling the remaining perimeter.

Companion rationale: Basil is the classic tomato companion вҖ” anecdotally improves flavor, definitely improves the cook. Marigolds deter nematodes and aphids. Both are true companions, not just decoration.

Watering: Every day in summer, possibly twice a day during heat waves. A 15-gallon container with a large tomato plant in July needs 2вҖ“3 gallons of water per day. A self-watering reservoir insert is worth the investment for this setup.

Notes: Provide a 6-foot stake or large cage. Use two stakes if the plant grows particularly large. Top-dress with compost or worm castings monthly to maintain fertility through the season.

### Why Grow Bags?

Fabric grow bags air-prune roots вҖ” when roots reach the air at the bag's edge, they stop growing outward and branch inward instead, creating a denser, more efficient root system. This means you get better growth from smaller containers compared to plastic pots of the same size. They're also lightweight, cheap, and pack flat when not in use.

### General Grow Bag Notes

**Watering frequency:** Fabric grow bags dry out 20вҖ“30% faster than plastic containers of the same size because moisture evaporates through the sides. In peak summer, daily watering is normal. You can slow this down by placing bags on a tray with a small amount of water, or by clustering them tightly (the bags shade each other's sides, reducing evaporation).

**Fertilizing:** Because of frequent watering, nutrients wash out of grow bags faster than any other container type. Feed every 10вҖ“14 days with a balanced liquid fertilizer throughout the growing season.

**Winter storage:** Empty, wash, and fold flat. They store in almost no space and last 3вҖ“5 seasons with reasonable care.
