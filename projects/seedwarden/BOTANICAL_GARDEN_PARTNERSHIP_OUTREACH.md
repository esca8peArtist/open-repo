# Botanical Garden Partnership Outreach — Contact Opportunities & Proposal Templates

**Prepared**: June 24, 2026  
**Scope**: 14 primary contact opportunities across botanical gardens, native plant conservation organizations, and university extension programs — organized by partnership type, estimated response timeline, and strategic value.

**Lead finding**: Botanical garden partnerships open three value streams for Seedwarden simultaneously: (1) commercial-quality photo access for rare plant guides without per-photo licensing fees, (2) expert review relationships that confer conservation credibility on guide content, and (3) co-marketing and distribution pathways into the conservation-minded naturalist buyer segment. The Center for Plant Conservation's 2025 National Meeting drew 160+ conservation professionals — this is the credibility ecosystem Seedwarden needs access to, and it is accessible through the right introductory framing.

---

## 1. Partnership Framework — Value Exchange Model

Botanical gardens and conservation organizations receive limited commercial photography revenue and have constrained outreach budgets. Seedwarden's value proposition to them is:

**What Seedwarden offers**:
- Royalty-free attribution credit in every guide featuring their photographs or expertise (reaches motivated conservation-minded buyers at point of purchase)
- Free guide copies to education department staff for classroom/visitor program use
- Digital proceeds donation (offer 5–10% of gross sales from a specific guide to a partner organization's conservation fund — frame this in outreach as a specific, quantified offer: "5% of gross sales from the Goldenseal cultivation guide, estimated $200–400/year based on 50 sales at $26")
- Public acknowledgment in guide metadata and on Seedwarden Etsy listings (links to garden website)
- Co-promotion opportunity: Seedwarden emails its subscriber list about the garden's rare plant programs when the guide launches

**What Seedwarden receives**:
- Commercial-use photo license for 5–15 high-resolution images per species
- Optional: name of a garden botanist as a content reviewer (cited in guide as "reviewed by [Name], Conservation Botanist, [Garden Name]")
- Optional: access to seed or propagation material for documentation photography (on-site photography visit)
- Optional: joint educational event (webinar for Seedwarden email list; table at garden plant sale)

### Contact 6: Center for Plant Conservation (Escondido, CA / Network of 45+ Gardens)

**Why**: CPC is the national authority on rare plant conservation science with 160+ professional attendees at their 2025 National Meeting in St. Louis. CPC maintains the National Collection (safeguarding living collections of US rare plants across 45+ member gardens), the Rare Plant Academy (professional training), and a Rare Plant Reintroduction Database. A CPC relationship does not give photo access directly — but it gives access to the 45+ member gardens, each of which holds specific rare plant collections, AND provides professional credibility as an educational organization whose content is consistent with CPC standards.

**Contact pathway**:  
Website: saveplants.org  
Executive Director: saveplants.org/about/contact  
CPC Applied Plant Conservation Course (free resource, useful for guide research): saveplants.org/applied-course  
CPC newsletter archive: saveplants.org (June 2025 and August 2025 newsletters available)

**Species of relevance**: All species on UpS At-Risk / Critical list; CPC has documented programs for ginseng, goldenseal, lady's slipper, trillium, and oconee bells

**Proposed partnership type**: Formal recognition as "CPC-consistent content" (not an official CPC endorsement, but framed as "developed in accordance with CPC conservation standards"); access to CPC member garden contacts  
**Response timeline**: 6–10 weeks  
**Note**: CPC membership for small organizations is available; an annual CPC membership would give access to the professional network, CPC database, and networking at the annual meeting

## 7. Escalation — If Tier 1 Contacts Decline or Do Not Respond

If NC Botanical Garden, MoBG, or NativePlantTrust do not respond within 6 weeks:

**Fallback 1 — iNaturalist CC-BY heavy route**: Scale up contributor outreach (IENATURALIST_INTEGRATION_PLAYBOOK.md, Section 3) for all species where CC-BY observation counts allow it. For Tier 1 species (ginseng, ramps, bloodroot, trillium, black cohosh), this is fully viable.

**Fallback 2 — On-site guide photography**: Purchase cultivated specimens from Prairie Moon Nursery / Strictly Medicinal Seeds. Photograph in a studio or naturalistic backyard setting. This is legal, reliable, and produces consistent photo quality. For species available from ethical nursery sources, this eliminates the botanical garden dependency entirely.

**Fallback 3 — Commission botanical illustration**: Hire a botanical illustrator for the 1–2 species where both iNaturalist and botanical garden sourcing are insufficient (false unicorn, osha in its native elevation range). Botanical illustration is an established convention in plant guide publishing, confers additional premium perception, and costs $150–300 per plate from Etsy or Upwork botanical illustrators.
