# Phase 4 Go-to-Market Scenarios

**Prepared**: June 16, 2026
**Baseline assumptions carried forward**:
- Phase 3 (June 22 – July 13) delivers 5 medicinal herb bundles (64 species total)
- Phase 3 revenue by end of July: $800–$1,400/month (guide sales, Etsy)
- Seedwarden has existing Mountain Rose Herbs supplier relationship and Etsy shop
- No employees; solo operator; Canva Pro + Etsy infrastructure in place
- Email list size: growing through Phase 3 (target 500–800 subscribers by July 13)
- Available time for Phase 4: user-dependent (see decision tree, Section 5)

These four scenarios are **mutually exclusive** for Phase 4. Pick one.

---

## Scenario 1: Double-Down (Single-Category Excellence)

**Thesis**: Complete excellence in medicinal herbalism before expanding anywhere else.
All Phase 4 effort stays in the herbalism guide category — more depth, more species, higher
practitioner value — plus activating the B2B licensing channel that already exists but has not
been formally pursued.

### Product Roadmap

**July (Week 1–4)**: Derive 10–15 single-herb deep-dive guides from Phase 3 bundle content
(2–3 hours each to split and format; existing content, new surface area). List each at $14–$18.

**August**: Launch 2–3 additional themed bundles beyond the Phase 3 set (e.g., Skin & Wound
Herbs, Adaptogen & Nervous System, Children's Herb Safety). Each bundle: 12–16 hours to research
and write.

**September**: Activate B2B practitioner licensing. Create 10-pack license tier for top 3 Phase 3
bundles. Set up AHG directory outreach campaign (300 emails, 2–5% conversion target).
Offer white-label variant to 5–10 herbalist supply stores via direct email.

**Ongoing**: Gumroad as secondary sales channel (mirror Etsy listings; test higher pricing without
Etsy fee structure). Optional: Patreon at $5–$7/month for monthly plant profile content.

### Resource Requirements

- Hours: 20–35 hours/month (guide writing + B2B outreach + Gumroad setup)
- Skills: Writing (existing), Canva (existing), cold email (learnable in 1–2 hours)
- External costs: $0–$15/month (Gumroad free; optional Patreon Pro $12/month)
- Team size: Solo

### Revenue Projection

| Source | Conservative | Base | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 3 bundles (ongoing) | $800 | $1,200 | $1,600 |
| Single-herb guides (15 new listings) | $600 | $1,200 | $2,000 |
| New bundles (3 additional) | $400 | $800 | $1,400 |
| B2B practitioner licenses | $750 | $2,000 | $3,500 |
| **Total monthly by Sept 2026** | **$2,550** | **$5,200** | **$8,500** |
| **Annual run rate** | **$30–$40K** | **$55–$70K** | **$90–$100K** |

### Timeline to Break-Even

Guide writing costs are time only (imputed at $25/hour). Break-even on a new bundle (12–16 hours
at $25 = $300–$400 imputed) at $18 per sale: 17–22 units. At 50–100 sales/month across all
listings, break-even occurs within the first month of each listing going live. B2B licensing
channel break-even: first sale covers the setup (1–2 hours of outreach infrastructure = ~$50).

### Key Success Metrics (90 days)

- Total guide catalog: 25+ listings (up from Phase 3's 5 bundles)
- B2B practitioner accounts: 5–20 active
- Monthly revenue: $3,000–$5,000 by October 2026
- Etsy conversion rate: 1.5%+ maintained on top 3 bundles

### Failure Modes and Mitigations

| Failure Mode | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Guide market saturates before B2B activates | Medium | Launch B2B in parallel with guide expansion (July, not September) |
| B2B outreach converts at <1% | Medium | Supplement with Herbal Academy affiliate program as alternative B2B channel |
| Writing bandwidth bottleneck | Low (solo scale) | Reduce new bundles from 3 to 1; prioritize single-herb splits which are faster |

### Choose Scenario 1 if:
You have 20–35 hours/month available, want quality and depth over breadth, are not interested in
managing physical product inventory, and are comfortable with a 3–6 month B2B outreach ramp before
the new revenue channel matures.

## Scenario 2: Expand (Multi-Category, Parallel)

**Thesis**: Phase 4 adds three product lines simultaneously — practitioner training (certification
curriculum on Teachable), wellness subscription or physical product line (tea blends or skincare),
and B2B channel (bulk sales to apothecaries). Three parallel revenue streams, 3x risk, 3x ceiling.

### Product Roadmap

**July**: Launch Tea Blends physical product line on Etsy (4 SKUs from Phase 4 physical product
roadmap in PHASE_4_MARKET_RESEARCH.md). Source via Mountain Rose Herbs (existing supplier).
Simultaneously: begin outline for practitioner training course (5 modules, Teachable or Kajabi).

**August**: Launch Herbal Skincare (3 SKUs: Calendula Salve, Lavender Oil, Chamomile Mist).
Launch minimum viable practitioner training course Module 1–2 on Teachable ($97–$197 pilot price).
Begin Faire wholesale application and set minimum wholesale order at $150.

**September**: Full 5-module practitioner course live ($297 full price). Faire listing active for
tea blends and skincare. AHG outreach for course promotion and wholesale guide licenses.

### Resource Requirements

- Hours: 50–70 hours/month (guide writing + physical product sourcing and packing + course
  production + Faire setup + outreach)
- Skills: Video production or screencasting (new skill for course); physical product assembly;
  wholesale logistics
- External costs: $100–$300/month (Teachable $39/month + Mountain Rose Herbs reorder $200–$400 +
  Bramble Berry skincare supplies $75–$150 + Faire no upfront cost)
- Team size: Solo is possible but strained; 5–10 hours/month of contractor help recommended for
  packing/fulfillment (est. $150–$300/month)

### Revenue Projection

| Source | Conservative | Base | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 3 bundles (ongoing) | $800 | $1,200 | $1,600 |
| Tea blends (physical, Etsy) | $800 | $2,000 | $3,500 |
| Herbal skincare (physical, Etsy) | $600 | $1,500 | $2,500 |
| Practitioner training course | $600 | $2,000 | $5,000 |
| B2B wholesale (Faire + direct) | $500 | $1,200 | $2,500 |
| **Total monthly by Sept 2026** | **$3,300** | **$7,900** | **$15,100** |
| **Annual run rate** | **$40–$60K** | **$80–$120K** | **$150–$250K** |

### Timeline to Break-Even

Physical product investment: Mountain Rose Herbs order (~$200–$400) + Bramble Berry ($75–$150) +
packaging (~$100) = $375–$650 upfront. Break-even on physical products: 20–40 units sold across
tea and skincare lines. At projected 20–40 units/week by Month 2: 1–2 weeks to recoup investment.

Practitioner course investment: Teachable ($39/month) + ~40 hours course production ($1,000
imputed). Break-even: 5–10 course sales at $97–$197. At projected 10–20 sales/month: first month
profitable.

### Key Success Metrics (90 days)

- Physical product lines: 7+ SKUs live (4 tea + 3 skincare)
- Course: 20+ enrolled students in first 60 days
- Faire: 3–8 wholesale accounts activated
- Monthly revenue: $5,000–$8,000 by October 2026

### Failure Modes and Mitigations

| Failure Mode | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Quality degradation across 3 simultaneous launches | HIGH | Stagger launches: Tea July 15, Skincare Aug 15, Course Aug 1; do not rush any lane |
| Physical product inventory ties up cash | Medium | Start with 20-unit batches only; no bulk pre-orders until first 10 units sell |
| Course underperforms without video production skill | Medium | Launch as text-based course first ($97); add video modules in Month 2–3 |
| Practitioner course competes with Chestnut/Herbal Academy for credibility | High | Price lower ($97–$197 vs. their $297–$1,800); position as "field companion" not "certification" |

### Choose Scenario 2 if:
You have 50–70 hours/month available, have some tolerance for physical product logistics, want the
highest revenue ceiling by end of 2026, and can accept the execution risk of 3 parallel launches.

## Scenario 3: Sequential (Herbalism → Training → Subscription)

**Thesis**: One new product line per month, fully launched and stable before the next begins.
Lower risk, lower ceiling, sustainable solo-creator pacing. The staggered launch model reduces
quality degradation risk and preserves mental bandwidth.

### Product Roadmap

**July**: Complete medicinal herb guide catalog (Phase 3 follow-through: single-herb splits, 2
additional themed bundles, B2B licensing tier). Target: guide catalog at 20+ listings, B2B
outreach sent to 100 AHG practitioners, 5+ accounts converted.

**August**: Launch minimum viable practitioner training course on Teachable. 3 modules, text-based,
$97 pilot price. Collect feedback from first 10 students before adding modules or raising price.
Promote via email list and AHG contacts acquired in July.

**September**: Launch Patreon ($7/month tier). Exclusive content: monthly plant profile, seasonal
recipe, behind-the-scenes guide development. Drive Patreon from Etsy shop announcement, email
list, and YouTube short (1–2 per week, 3–5 minute plant spotlights).

### Resource Requirements

- Hours: 30–45 hours/month (increasing from July's 25–30 as course and Patreon layers in)
- Skills: Writing (existing); Teachable course setup (2–4 hour learning curve); Patreon setup
  (1–2 hours); light video for YouTube shorts (optional but high-ROI)
- External costs: $50–$150/month (Teachable $39/month + Patreon Pro $12/month + no physical inventory)
- Team size: Solo, fully manageable

### Revenue Projection

| Source | Conservative | Base | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 3 + guide catalog (July mature) | $1,200 | $2,500 | $4,000 |
| Practitioner course (Aug launch, Sept steady) | $400 | $1,500 | $3,500 |
| Patreon (Sept launch, 3-month ramp) | $200 | $700 | $2,000 |
| B2B practitioner licenses (July start) | $500 | $1,500 | $3,000 |
| **Total monthly by Oct 2026** | **$2,300** | **$6,200** | **$12,500** |
| **Annual run rate** | **$30–$50K** | **$65–$100K** | **$120–$150K** |

### Timeline to Break-Even

Each phase break-even is similar to Scenario 1 for guides and B2B. Course: 5 students at $97 =
$485, covering Teachable month + imputed planning time. Patreon: first 50 patrons at $7 = $350/month,
covering the $12 Patreon Pro fee and 4–6 hours of content creation ($100–$150 imputed).

**Full break-even on the sequential investment** (including all setup time): approximately Month 2–3
after each new channel launches, given conservative sales assumptions.

### Key Success Metrics (90 days)

- July gate: Guide catalog at 20+ listings; B2B outreach sent; 5+ practitioner accounts
- August gate: 10+ course students enrolled before September Patreon launch
- September gate: 50+ Patreon patrons within 30 days of launch
- Monthly revenue: $4,000–$6,000 by October 2026

### Failure Modes and Mitigations

| Failure Mode | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Course underperforms (fewer than 10 students in August) | Medium | Do not launch Patreon until course has 10+ students; validate demand first |
| Patreon content commitment becomes unsustainable | Medium | Pre-produce 2 months of Patreon content before launch; keep it simple (text plant profiles, not video) |
| Staggered launch loses momentum between phases | Low | July and August launches overlap; August content prep begins during July |

### Choose Scenario 3 if:
You have 30–45 hours/month available, prioritize quality and sustainable pacing over maximum speed,
want to validate each channel before committing to the next, and are not interested in managing
physical product inventory in Phase 4.

## Scenario 4: Platform Play (Creator Network)

**Thesis**: Seedwarden becomes a platform, not just a creator. Phase 4 recruits 5–10 contributing
creators (wildlife foraging, mushroom cultivation, permaculture, herbalism subniches), launches a
marketplace where Seedwarden takes 40% platform fee, and builds toward a multi-creator brand with
network effects.

### Product Roadmap

**July**: Platform architecture definition. Define contributor agreement (revenue share: creator 60%
/ Seedwarden 40%). Identify 5–10 creator candidates via Etsy, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube in
adjacent niches (wild mushrooms, permaculture, wildcrafting, homesteading).

**August**: Recruit first 3 creator-partners. Each contributes 1–2 guide products to the shared
catalog. Launch shared Shopify storefront (Seedwarden marketplace branding). Begin YouTube channel
with 2 plant-spotlight videos/week to build audience for platform launch.

**September**: Full platform launch with 3 creators + Seedwarden catalog (15+ total products).
Platform marketing: shared email list, cross-promotion between creators, coordinated social posts.
Begin Patreon as platform membership ($12–$20/month for full library access across all creators).

### Resource Requirements

- Hours: 60–80 hours/month (creator outreach + platform setup + own content + coordination)
- Skills: Contract/legal (contributor agreement — $150–$300 one-time legal consult); Shopify setup
  (~10 hours); creator management and communication
- External costs: $250–$500/month (Shopify $105/month + apps $100–$200 + legal consult one-time
  $150–$300 + email platform upgrade $30–$60/month)
- Team size: 1 platform operator (Seedwarden) + 3–10 contributing creators (independent contractors)

### Revenue Projection

| Source | Conservative | Base | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seedwarden own guides (40% margin on platform) | $500 | $1,500 | $3,000 |
| Creator guide sales (40% platform cut) | $400 | $2,000 | $6,000 |
| Platform membership / Patreon | $300 | $1,200 | $4,000 |
| B2B wholesale (platform brand) | $300 | $1,000 | $3,000 |
| **Total monthly by Oct 2026** | **$1,500** | **$5,700** | **$16,000** |
| **Annual run rate (if network effects work)** | **$20–$40K** | **$50–$100K** | **$200–$500K+** |

**Important caveat**: The optimistic scenario requires network effects to materialize — creators
bringing their own audiences to the platform. If creators do not have existing audiences, the
platform is just Seedwarden's own catalog with extra overhead and a shared revenue cut.

### Timeline to Break-Even

Platform setup investment: 60–80 hours in July–August ($1,500–$2,000 imputed) + $250–$500 cash.
Break-even requires platform revenue to cover ongoing operational cost (~$250–$300/month) AND
recover setup investment. At base scenario ($5,700/month by October), break-even on setup
investment: Month 2–3. BUT this assumes creators bring audiences. Without that, revenue underperforms.

### Key Success Metrics (90 days)

- Creator partners recruited: 3+ with existing audiences (minimum 1K Etsy reviews or 5K social following)
- Platform listings: 15+ (across Seedwarden + creator contributions)
- Patreon or membership: 50+ subscribers within 30 days of launch
- Monthly platform GMV: $3,000+ by October (Seedwarden keeps $1,200+)

### Failure Modes and Mitigations

| Failure Mode | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Chicken-and-egg problem: creators don't join without audience; audience doesn't come without creators | HIGH | Pre-recruit creators with existing audiences before announcing platform publicly |
| Creator quality below Seedwarden standard | High | Vet first product from each creator before publishing; content review gate required |
| Platform Shopify overhead exceeds early revenue | Medium | Don't launch Shopify until 3 creators are committed and first products ready; use Gumroad as temporary platform |
| Creator defection after learning the model | Low | Written agreement + delayed payout (first payment after 3 months of platform sales) |

### Choose Scenario 4 if:
You have 60–80 hours/month available, want the highest ceiling (platform economics vs. creator
economics), have strong relationship-building skills, and can tolerate 3–6 months before the
platform revenue model validates — with real risk of it not working without network effects.

## Section 6: Decision Tree

Use this to select your scenario before July 13, 2026.

**Step 1: How many hours per month can you reliably dedicate to Phase 4?**

- Less than 35 hours: go to Step 2.
- 35–55 hours: go to Step 3.
- More than 55 hours: go to Step 4.

**Step 2 (under 35 hours): Do you want physical product inventory or digital-only?**

- Digital-only, deepen quality: **Scenario 1 (Double-Down)**.
- Digital-only, staged new channels: **Scenario 3 (Sequential)**.

**Step 3 (35–55 hours): What is your primary goal — revenue speed or quality depth?**

- Maximum revenue by end of 2026, comfortable with execution risk: **Scenario 2 (Expand)**.
- Sustainable quality with multiple new channels: **Scenario 3 (Sequential)**.

**Step 4 (over 55 hours): Are you willing to recruit and manage other creators?**

- Yes, want platform/network economics, high risk tolerance: **Scenario 4 (Platform Play)**.
- No, want to focus on own content but add channels fast: **Scenario 2 (Expand)**.

**Tiebreaker — If you are between Scenario 1 and Scenario 3**:
Do you want to launch a practitioner training course in Phase 4?
- Yes: **Scenario 3** (Sequential gives you a structured August course launch).
- No: **Scenario 1** (Double-Down keeps all effort on guides and B2B).

**Conservative floor**: All four scenarios produce at least $2,000–$3,000/month by October 2026
on the conservative projection when combined with Phase 3's $800–$1,400/month base.

**Ceiling spread**: Scenario 4 has the highest ceiling ($200–$500K+ annualized) but also the
highest failure probability. Scenarios 1–3 have tighter, more reliable ranges
($30–$150K annualized) with substantially lower execution risk.
