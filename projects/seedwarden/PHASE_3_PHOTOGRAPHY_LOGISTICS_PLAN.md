## 3. Photography Location & Logistics

### 3.1 Location Selection — Appalachian Rare Plants Cluster

**Recommended Locations** (3-cluster shoot strategy):
1. **Cluster A — Medicinal Herb Native Habitat** (North Carolina Blue Ridge)
   - Location: Morikami Museum & Japanese Gardens OR Bamboo Grove State Nature Preserve (North Carolina)
   - Advantages: Humid, dappled light, existing medicinal herb plantings, zero permits needed
   - Species focus: Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh (shade-loving native habitat)
   - Shoot window: May 17–18 (1 day)

2. **Cluster B — Cultivated Medicinal Garden** (Kentucky)
   - Location: Shaker Village of Pleasant Hill medicinal herb demonstration garden OR University of Kentucky Botanical Garden
   - Advantages: Controlled environment, documented plant care, structured backdrop, public garden access
   - Species focus: Wild Bergamot, Red Clover, Ramps (cultivated species)
   - Shoot window: May 19–20 (1 day)

3. **Cluster C — Studio/Macro Setup** (Local Controlled Environment)
   - Location: Natural light studio or outdoor table with diffusion panels
   - Advantages: Control over angles, close-ups, leaf detail, season-specific backgrounds
   - Species focus: All species (root detail, seed close-ups, dried preparations)

## 4. Photo Shoot Planning — Detailed 90-Image Sequence

### 4.1 Shoot Statistics

- **Total Images**: 90 (30 per cluster)
- **Keepers Targeted**: 60–70 (66–77% keeper rate)
- **Per-Species Close-ups**: 5–8 angles (leaf detail, flower/seed, root if applicable, full plant, detail macro)
- **Composition Styles**: Habitat/native, cultivated/garden, isolated white/colored background, flat-lay (dried herbs), close-up macro
- **Backup Variants**: 2 angles per shot (safety redundancy for best-of-two)

### 4.2 Per-Cluster Shoot Plan

#### Cluster A — Habitat Shoot (May 17–18, 1 day)

**Species Target**: Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh (3 species × 10 images = 30 images)

| Time | Species | Shots (angle/style) | Notes |
|------|---------|-------------|-------|
| Sunrise (6:45–7:15) | Ginseng | 1. Full plant in habitat / 2. Leaf cluster close-up / 3. Soil/root context | Backlighting, dew highlights |
| 7:30–8:30 | Goldenseal | 1. Flower bud detail / 2. Leaf cluster / 3. Whole plant in shade / 4. Root (if available) | Macro lens 1:1 for root detail |
| 8:45–9:45 | Black Cohosh | 1. Flower spike (white, delicate) / 2. Leaf complex / 3. Whole plant in dappled light / 4. Seed pods (if in season) | Priority: flower definition |
| 10:00–11:00 | Repeat best 2 species | Safety variants (different angle, lighting) | Choose 2 with best keeper rate |
| Lunch | | | |
| 12:30–1:30 | Ginseng | Detail macro (leaf veins, seedling stage if available) / Ground context | Flat-lay variants |
| 1:45–2:45 | Goldenseal | Flower anatomy close-up / Leaf underside / Root preparation | Macro ring light if available |
| 3:00–4:00 | Black Cohosh | Seed pod close-up / Leaf edge detail / Height/scale context | Reference coin/ruler in some shots |

**Equipment List (Cluster A)**:
- Camera (DSLR or mirrorless, at least 18MP)
- Macro lens (100mm or 1:1 macro if available, OR telephoto + macro adapter)
- Diffusion panels or white bedsheet (3' x 3' minimum) for shade/backlighting control
- Tripod
- ND filter or polarizer (reduce glare, enhance green saturation)
- Backup SD cards (3x capacity needed)
- Spreadsheet or notebook: shot log with keeper assessment

**Weather Backup**: If May 17–18 forecast is poor (heavy rain, dense fog), reschedule to May 24–25 and shift Cluster C to May 26–28.

#### Cluster B — Cultivated Garden Shoot (May 19–20, 1 day)

**Species Target**: Wild Bergamot, Red Clover, Ramps, False Unicorn, (optional: Bloodroot if available) (5 species × 6 images = 30 images)

| Time | Species | Shots (angle/style) | Notes |
|------|---------|-------------|-------|
| Early morning | Wild Bergamot | 1. Flower cluster (pink/purple) / 2. Leaf arrangement / 3. Plant scale with garden context / 4. Flower detail macro | Saturated flower color key |
| Mid-morning | Red Clover | 1. Flower head close-up / 2. Leaf trio detail / 3. Whole plant in garden / 4. Flower cross-section | Classic rounded flower form |
| Late morning | Ramps | 1. Leaf cluster from above / 2. Flower umbel (white flowers) / 3. Whole plant in context / 4. Bulb detail (if lifted) | Delicate white flowers |
| Lunch | | | |
| Afternoon | False Unicorn (if available) | 1. Flower spike / 2. Leaf base / 3. Whole plant / 4. Root detail | Tall spiky flower form |
| Late afternoon | Bloodroot (if sourced) OR repeat best keeper | Red flower (unique) / Leaf context / Variants | Priority: unique red flower |

**Equipment**: Same as Cluster A + backup location contact info

#### Cluster C — Studio/Macro Setup (May 21–25, 5 days)

**Species Target**: All species (detailed macro, close-ups, styled variants) (90 species-specific images)

**Purpose**: Root detail, seed close-ups, dried herb preparations, white-background isolation shots, styled "flat-lay" herbal aesthetics

| Date | Shot Type | Species Focus | Volume |
|------|-----------|---------------|---------| 
| May 21 | Root detail macros | Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh | 12 images |
| May 22 | Seed pod / flower anatomy | All species | 15 images |
| May 23 | Dried herb preparations | All species (if harvestable) | 12 images |
| May 24 | White-background isolation | All species | 18 images |
| May 25 | Styled flat-lay arrangements | Herb bundles, therapeutic context | 18 images |

**Studio Setup Requirements**:
- Natural window light (north-facing for consistent, diffused light)
- White poster board or seamless background (3' wide minimum)
- Macro lens or macro adapter + extension tubes
- Ring light (optional, enhances detail photos)
- Reflectors (white card, silver reflector for fill light)

**Sample Styling Themes**:
- "Medicinal Garden Preparation" — herbs with mortar/pestle, dried bundle
- "Tea Ritual" — herbs in cup, steaming water, botanical background
- "Herbalist's Workspace" — roots, dried herbs, reference books, glass jars
- "Seasonal Harvest" — fresh vs. dried comparison, seasonal context

## 7. Risk Mitigation & Contingency Plans

### 7.1 Plant Sourcing Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Bloodroot/False Unicorn stock-out | 30% | Medium (unique species lost) | Pre-order by April 25; confirm secondary supplier |
| Seed viability low (<50%) | 15% | Low (aesthetic, not critical) | Order extra seeds (2x qty); test germination first |
| Plants arrive damaged/diseased | 10% | Medium (photo shoot delay) | Supplier guarantees + inspection on arrival; acclimate 1 week before shoot |
| Ramps sourcing delayed (seasonal) | 20% | Low (substitute with iNaturalist image) | Order by May 10; have backup photo sourced by May 5 |

**Mitigation Action**: By April 25, verify all suppliers have stock availability; place pre-orders for any species with <7-day lead time.

### 7.2 Photography Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Weather poor (rain, heavy overcast) | 40% (May spring weather) | Medium (1–2 day delay) | 5-day shoot window; if rainy May 17–20, shift to May 24–25 |
| Location unavailable (permits denied) | 10% | High (2-week research needed) | Secure 2 backup locations by May 12; have permission letters by May 14 |
| Camera/lens failure | 5% | High (critical equipment) | Carry spare SD cards; borrow backup camera from friend by May 14 |
| Insufficient keepers (>30% reject rate) | 25% | Medium (need to reshoot) | Low-risk: backup botanical garden images pre-sourced; worst case: use 60% botanical images + 40% personal |

**Mitigation Action**: Test all camera equipment May 14–15; scout locations in person if possible May 13–14; confirm permit letters May 12.

### 7.3 Guide Production Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Guide production takes >38 hours | 35% (research-heavy scope) | Medium (1–3 day slip) | Prioritize Women's Health + Respiratory; defer Immunity to June 16 |
| Photo selection criteria unclear | 15% | Low (manageable in May 29 review) | Create photo selection rubric (sharp, color accurate, compositionally strong) by May 27 |
| Canva template issues (fonts, sizing) | 10% | Low (1-day fix turnaround) | Build template May 6–8 (early); test with sample guide copy by June 1 |
| PDF export issues (file size, quality) | 5% | Low (can batch-optimize May 30) | Test PDF export workflow on first guide by June 2 |

**Mitigation Action**: Create detailed photo selection rubric by May 27; build Canva template by June 8 (2-day buffer before guide production).

### 7.4 Go-Live Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Etsy listing error (price, category, photo) | 10% | Low (can fix within 1 hour) | Use pre-filled template; test on dummy product June 13 |
| Email automation not triggered | 5% | Medium (missed lead gen) | Test Kit email workflows June 13; verify landing page link June 14 |
| High bounce rate from email | 20% | Medium (low list quality) | Segment email list by source; target warm audience first (existing Seedwarden list) |
| No sales on Day 1 | 80% (realistic for cold start) | Low (expected; Day 7–14 targets) | Educate customer expectation; track weekly cohorts (Week 1, Month 1 targets) |

**Mitigation Action**: Test all Etsy + Kit + email workflows June 13–14 on sandbox/test mode; create go-live checklist and sign-off form.
