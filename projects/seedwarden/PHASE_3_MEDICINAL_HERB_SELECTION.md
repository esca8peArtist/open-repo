# Phase 3 Medicinal Herb Selection — Final 7 for June 22 Sprint

**Prepared**: May 26, 2026  
**Decision required**: May 28-30 (user approval confirms this list and authorizes Sprint Day 1 writing on June 22)  
**Scope**: Top 7 herbs for the three first-sprint bundles. Herbs 8-12 from the candidate list are confirmed for post-sprint bundles (Immunity August 3, Digestive August 17) and are not in scope for this selection document.

The full 12-species candidate list exists at `medicinal-herbs-candidate-list.md`. This document does not repeat that research — it applies it to the specific question of which 7 herbs maximize sprint viability under the May 22 supplier constraints.

---

## Lead Finding

**Recommend: Elderberry, Echinacea, Lavender, Lemon Balm, Mullein, Passionflower, and Valerian as the 7 core herbs for the June 22 sprint.**

Black Cohosh and Goldenseal — the two highest-conservation-value herbs — are confirmed for the post-sprint Immunity and Women's Health v1.1 bundles but carry procurement constraints (both primary suppliers confirmed out of stock as of May 22) that make them risky sprint anchors. The 7 herbs below have either confirmed Tier 3 supplier availability, Wikimedia CC-BY-SA photo coverage, or both. None require live specimen photography to produce a launch-quality guide.

## 7-Herb Recommendation

### Herb 1 — Elderberry (*Sambucus nigra* / *S. canadensis*)

**Bundle assignment**: Respiratory Health (primary anchor); Immunity Support (secondary)  
**Etsy demand tier**: Highest. "Elderberry guide" and "elderberry growing" are among the top-searched herbal terms on Etsy in the immune-support category. Cross-listing to two bundles doubles the keyword surface for a single content investment.  
**Supplier status**: Local nursery 2-gallon potted shrub (June 15 order = July 1-5 arrival for v1.1 photo upgrade). Wikimedia CC-BY-SA Sambucus nigra photos are exceptional — flower clusters, berry clusters, full shrub — and are confirmed launch-quality. No live specimen required at sprint start.  
**Writing depth**: High. The cultivation guide (shrub establishment, coppicing, harvest timing, elderflower culinary use) is distinct from the syrup-recipe content that saturates Etsy. The native American elder (*S. canadensis*) differentiation adds a conservation note no PLR guide covers.  
**Practitioner interest**: Strong. Clinical herbalists cite the Sambucol randomized controlled trial evidence (cold/flu duration) and ask for cultivation-over-wildcrafting guidance. The FTC frame ("studied in RCTs for cold and flu duration") is the most defensible claim available for any herb in this list.  
**Audience segment**: Forager cohort (elderflower culinary bridge) + wellness practitioner cohort (evidence-based immune support)  
**Difficulty level**: Low-medium. No conservation complexity, no CITES issues, widely cultivated, excellent photo coverage.

---

### Herb 2 — Echinacea (*E. purpurea* + *E. angustifolia*)

**Bundle assignment**: Respiratory Health (primary); Immunity Support (primary)  
**Etsy demand tier**: Very high. One of the most-searched herbal supplement terms in North America. The Etsy herbalist market is saturated with supplement-framing guides — the cultivation manual angle is the differentiation.  
**Supplier status**: *E. purpurea* widely available at Strictly Medicinal and Prairie Moon (Tier 3, June 22 order = July 6-13 arrival for live specimen). Wikimedia CC-BY-SA purple coneflower photos are among the most abundant of any herb. No live specimen required for sprint launch.  
**Writing depth**: High. The "two echinacea" story — *E. purpurea* (common garden species) vs. *E. angustifolia* (UpS At-Risk prairie species) — is the content angle that no PLR guide covers and that practitioners specifically want. The prairie native restoration content adds depth for both the practitioner and the forager cohort.  
**Practitioner interest**: Very high. Echinacea is among the most-researched herbs; multiple meta-analyses on immune function. Practitioners need to explain the species distinction to patients. The guide fills that precise gap.  
**Audience segment**: Practitioner cohort (primary); forager cohort (prairie native restoration overlap)  
**Difficulty level**: Medium. Species distinction requires clear writing; FTC framing requires care ("studied for immune function support" not "prevents colds"). Conservation sidebar for *E. angustifolia* required.

---

### Herb 3 — Lavender (*Lavandula angustifolia*)

**Bundle assignment**: Sleep and Nervines (primary anchor); Women's Health (supporting)  
**Etsy demand tier**: Highest. Lavender is the highest-recognition herb in the wellness market and among the top-searched on Etsy in the herbal/bath/apothecary category. High search volume at low competition from cultivation-angle guides.  
**Supplier status**: Universal. Local nurseries, all seed suppliers. Photo sourcing: best Wikimedia Commons coverage of any herb in the full 12-species list. No procurement action required — lavender is available everywhere.  
**Writing depth**: High. The cultivation guide (English lavender vs. French vs. hybrid, zone-specific overwintering, distillation vs. fresh harvest, container growing for Zone 3-4) adds depth that no lifestyle guide or supplement guide covers. The Women's Health traditional use connection (relaxation, hormonal comfort in traditional European herbalism) provides the secondary audience hook.  
**Practitioner interest**: Moderate. Clinical herbalists use lavender as an introductory nervine herb and as a aromatherapy protocol component. The cultivation angle is less critical for this herb than for at-risk species — but the clear FTC framing model ("used traditionally for relaxation and stress support") makes it an easy guide to write.  
**Audience segment**: Lifestyle/wellness cohort (primary); practitioner cohort (nervine protocol reference)  
**Difficulty level**: Low. No conservation issues, universal availability, abundant imagery, mild claim territory.

---

### Herb 4 — Lemon Balm (*Melissa officinalis*)

**Bundle assignment**: Sleep and Nervines (primary); Digestive Support (secondary)  
**Etsy demand tier**: High. Lemon balm is among the top-searched herbs for anxiety and sleep support. The tea preparation is the gateway use; the forager/homesteader cohort already knows it as an edible.  
**Supplier status**: Universal. Every herb nursery carries it; seeds at all suppliers including Southern Exposure. Wikimedia Commons coverage excellent. Zero procurement friction.  
**Writing depth**: Medium-high. The dual nervine-and-carminative profile (relaxation + digestive comfort) allows the guide to appear in both the Sleep and Digestive bundles with minor framing adjustments — two bundles, one content investment. The forager cohort overlap (lemon balm is edible and tea-making is a direct extension of foraging knowledge) enables a cross-sell from the Phase 2 wild-edibles catalog.  
**Practitioner interest**: Moderate. A classic first herb for beginner practitioners. Guides that bridge the casual-buyer audience and the practitioner audience do well for nervine herbs — lemon balm is the best example of that bridge in this list.  
**Audience segment**: Forager cohort (tea/culinary bridge); beginner practitioner (entry-level nervine)  
**Difficulty level**: Low. No conservation issues, mild claims, excellent availability.

---

### Herb 5 — Mullein (*Verbascum thapsus*)

**Bundle assignment**: Respiratory Health (co-anchor with Elderberry)  
**Etsy demand tier**: High. Mullein leaf tea for coughs and mullein ear oil are among the most commonly searched folk remedies. The roadside-harvestable angle has very high forager-cohort appeal — this herb grows itself everywhere and is legal to harvest in most states.  
**Supplier status**: Prairie Moon and Strictly Medicinal both carry it as seed. Wikimedia Commons coverage is exceptional — the 6-foot second-year flower spike is one of the most dramatic and well-photographed biennial plants in North America. No live specimen needed for launch.  
**Writing depth**: High. The biennial lifecycle content (first-year rosette vs. second-year flower spike) is genuinely useful and under-documented. The ear oil preparation (oleic acid maceration in olive oil) is a culinary-category preparation, not a drug manufacturing process — no FTC complication. The "roadside apothecary" framing is a strong content angle.  
**Practitioner interest**: High. Clinical herbalists use mullein leaf in respiratory protocols. The guide is useful to both practitioners and foragers, which makes it a high-LTV species.  
**Audience segment**: Forager cohort (free harvestable); practitioner cohort (respiratory protocol)  
**Difficulty level**: Low-medium. Regional invasive weed status in some western states requires a sidebar note, but no CITES or conservation complexity.

---

### Herb 6 — Passionflower (*Passiflora incarnata*)

**Bundle assignment**: Sleep and Nervines (primary visual anchor)  
**Etsy demand tier**: High. Passionflower is among the most searched native herbs for anxiety and sleep support. The flower is visually the most striking of any herb in this list — the purple-and-white radial structure drives thumbnail click-through.  
**Supplier status**: Prairie Moon and Strictly Medicinal both carry transplants (Tier 3, June 22 order). Wikimedia Commons has multiple excellent CC-BY-SA images of the flower — confirmed launch-quality. The flower photograph is the Sleep bundle's listing thumbnail anchor; no live specimen is needed for launch, but the visual quality of the CC images is excellent.  
**Writing depth**: Medium-high. Cultivation guide includes trellis requirements, zone-specific hardiness (perennial in Zone 6-10, annual in 4-5), and the maypop fruit content that bridges the forager cohort. PubMed anxiolytic research exists and can be cited descriptively without making a health claim.  
**Practitioner interest**: High. Clinical herbalists use passionflower as a primary nervine. The native-range restoration content (*P. incarnata* is native to the southeastern US) gives the guide a conservation angle that supplement-framing competitors lack.  
**Audience segment**: Sleep/wellness cohort (primary); forager cohort (maypop fruit, edible native vine)  
**Difficulty level**: Medium. Zone-specific content required; FTC nervine framing required ("used as a nervine and sleep-supporting herb in American folk medicine").

---

### Herb 7 — Valerian (*Valeriana officinalis*)

**Bundle assignment**: Sleep and Nervines (co-anchor with Passionflower)  
**Etsy demand tier**: High. Valerian root is the most recognized sleep herb in the herbal supplement market. Most Etsy guides cover the supplement form; cultivation guides are thin.  
**Supplier status**: Prairie Moon and Strictly Medicinal carry transplants (Tier 3, June 22 order). Wikimedia Commons has good coverage of the flowering stalk. Local herb nurseries carry it. No procurement friction.  
**Writing depth**: High. The cultivation-focused angle (root division propagation, second-year harvest for root, autumn harvest timing, basic drying protocol) is genuinely differentiated from the supplement-framing content. The root is the medicinal part — guides that show you how to grow and harvest the root rather than just recommending a capsule are rare on Etsy.  
**Practitioner interest**: Moderate-high. Widely used in sleep protocols. Clinical herbalists routinely explain to patients why the capsule form is less effective than the whole-root preparation — this guide supports that conversation.  
**Audience segment**: Sleep/wellness cohort (primary); homesteader cohort (root propagation, harvest/drying protocols)  
**Difficulty level**: Medium. Sleep claims require careful FTC framing ("traditionally used as a sleep-supporting herb in European herbalism").

## What Is Not in This List (and Why)

**Black Cohosh**: Confirmed in the Women's Health bundle but primary suppliers (Prairie Moon, Mountain Rose Herbs dried root) are both out of stock as of May 22. NativeWildflowers.net has bareroot at $5.99, but the Women's Health bundle launch target was September 2026 in the original plan — it is not a June 22 sprint herb. Recommend confirming Black Cohosh sourcing path separately for the fall launch.

**Goldenseal**: Immunity bundle anchor. CITES Appendix II conservation significance makes it the most important herb in the portfolio for practitioner credibility, but the June 8 Goldenseal path decision (Path 1 live specimen vs. Path 2 Wikimedia CC) is a separate gate documented in `PHASE_3_SUPPLIER_CONFIRMATION_TRACKER.md`. Not a sprint-start herb.

**Ashwagandha**: Immunity bundle, post-sprint. Highest-search-volume adaptogen on Etsy, but belongs with Goldenseal in the post-sprint Immunity bundle. Document separately.

**Calendula, Dandelion**: Digestive and Women's Health bundles (post-sprint). Both have excellent supplier and photo availability — no blocking issues for their respective bundle launch dates.
