# Phase 3 Contractor Selection Scorecard

---

## Scoring Methodology

Each candidate is scored on four dimensions, weighted by their relevance to Phase 3 production risk:

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio Fit (0–10) | 35% | Phase 3 depends on visual quality (photographer) or content accuracy (writers/specialists) matching Seedwarden's established standard |
| Availability (0–10) | 30% | Compressed timeline (contracts June 28–30, production July 1–August 1) makes confirmed availability non-negotiable |
| Budget Fit (0–10) | 20% | All candidates must fit within track budget ceilings; over-budget candidates require negotiation overhead |
| Success Probability (0–10) | 15% | Pre-existing research estimates the likelihood of a positive response to outreach |

**Overall Score** = (Portfolio Fit × 0.35) + (Availability × 0.30) + (Budget Fit × 0.20) + (Success Prob × 0.15)

Score scale: 0–10. Scores of 7.0+ are high-priority hires. Scores of 5.5–6.9 are conditional hires. Below 5.5 are deprioritized unless higher-priority candidates are unavailable.

## Selection Logic and Recommendations

### Track A — Photographer (select 1 primary, 1 fallback)

**Primary recommendation**: Post Upwork and Thumbtack on Day 1 (June 22). These two channels have the highest combined success probability and fastest response cycles. A US-based candidate for in-person shoots must come from one of these two channels.

**Secondary recommendation**: Send licensing inquiry to Kriss MacDonald (Wild About Here) simultaneously. Her botanical art images are the best available match for guide interior pages and Etsy secondary slots. This is a parallel track — do not wait for in-person photographer before pursuing licensing.

**Licensing backup**: Contact Emma TS Robinson for licensing of existing herb images if Kriss MacDonald declines or does not carry the specific species needed. Her portfolio is an aesthetic match; logistics are manageable for digital licensing delivery.

**Day 2 action if Day 1 produces no Upwork/Thumbtack leads**: Browse PGPA directory and contact 3–4 members. This is a deeper but slower channel; allow 5–7 days for response.

**Contingency**: If no photographer is contracted by June 28, activate self-shoot model per `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SEARCH_ROADMAP.md` Section 6, Contingency Path A. Self-shoot is viable — do not wait past June 28 without triggering this fallback.

### Track B — Nature Writer (select 1 primary for natural history; 1 for clinical herbalist track)

**Natural history writer — Primary recommendation**: Contact Rebecca Lexa first (Day 1). Her Ten Speed Press credential is the strongest signal of quality and reliability in this track, and her Pacific Northwest expertise matches Phase 3 wild edibles regional chapters. Lead with a narrowly scoped engagement (3,000–4,000 words of field identification sections) to fit within budget constraints.

**Natural history writer — Fallback**: Contact Arianna Alexsandra Collins simultaneously (Day 1). Her active freelance practice and Northeast regional expertise provide complementary geographic coverage. Her rate is more likely to fit within the $400–$900 budget floor than Rebecca Lexa's published author rates.

**Day 2 contact**: Emily Han has the strongest content alignment (co-author of "Wild Remedies"), but the lowest availability confidence in this track. Contact Day 2 after confirming Rebecca Lexa's interest level.

**Clinical herbalist writer**: Contact Adrian White (Iowa Herbalist) on Day 1 using the AHG outreach template from `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SOURCING_STRATEGY.md`. Administer both screening questions (FTC and CITES) in first contact. Score on the 100-point vetting rubric before committing to an interview.

### Track C — Habitat Specialist (select 1 or use writer dual-track)

**Primary recommendation**: If Rebecca Lexa (Track B) is selected as nature writer, extend her engagement to cover Pacific Northwest habitat content as a dual-track add-on. This eliminates a separate Track C outreach entirely and reduces coordination overhead.

**Secondary recommendation**: Contact Arthur Haines for Northeast regional habitat content as a scoped accuracy review engagement (not full authorship). His academic credential is the strongest available for Northeast species habitat data; keeping the scope tightly bounded ($400–$600 flat for habitat notes across 5 Northeast species) makes the rate feasible.

**Parallel post**: Post on Conservation Job Board (Day 1) for multi-region ecological restoration practitioners. This surfaces candidates who are not findable by name and provides geographic coverage for Midwest and Southeast regions that Rebecca Lexa and Arthur Haines do not cover.

**Contact CNPS** for California/West regional referrals if Conservation Job Board post does not surface a West Coast specialist by June 25.
