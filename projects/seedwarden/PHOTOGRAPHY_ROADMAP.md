### CLUSTER A — Seeds/Garden (8 products)

**Shared setup**: Wooden cutting board or reclaimed wood plank surface. Large indirect-light window. #### Product 1 — Seed Saving Field Manual ($14)

**Primary use case**: Reference guide for selecting, harvesting, and storing seeds from food crops.

**Shot 1 — Flat-lay (SLOT 4)**
- Surface: Wooden cutting board
- Arrangement: Tablet showing PDF cover centered, 3 seed envelopes fanned to the left, one open seed envelope with seeds spilling slightly toward center, small trowel handle visible at bottom right corner
- Camera: Directly overhead (90 degrees), full square crop
- Lighting: Indirect window at 45 degrees, no direct sun on surface
- Key element: The seed envelopes must be clearly legible (hand-labeled preferred — any handwriting is more authentic than commercial font)

**Shot 2 — Hand/action (SLOT 5)**
- One hand holds a seed envelope open over the wooden surface; other hand or separate shot holds the tablet displaying the guide cover; seeds visible between the two hands
- Camera: Slightly off-axis, roughly 60-degree angle, not full overhead
- Lighting: Same window as Shot 1
- Key element: Hands should look working — no nail polish, dry/natural skin

**Shot 3 — Close-up detail (bonus)**
- Macro close-up of several seeds in the palm of one hand with the tablet's screen visible in the soft background (bokeh or slight blur)
- This is a social media asset, not for Etsy — can be captured in the same session

**Shot 4 — In-situ use (for social)**
- Tablet propped against a seed storage jar on a potting bench or outdoor table; seeds in various containers around it
- Best captured outdoors in natural light if available

**Styling notes**: Keep the seed envelopes unlabeled or hand-labeled to avoid brand confusion. Use actual seeds if available — white foam or plastic substitutes look artificial. No white backgrounds. Wooden grain visible in frame is a positive signal for Seedwarden's audience.

**Safety notes**: None — handling common food crop seeds.

#### Product 2 — Heirloom Variety Selection Guide ($14)

**Primary use case**: Buying and comparison guide for selecting heirloom vegetable varieties by region and season.

**Shot 1 — Flat-lay (SLOT 4)**
- Surface: Wooden cutting board
- Arrangement: Tablet showing guide cover flat, one or two fresh-cut vegetable specimens beside it (a sliced heirloom tomato or a small pepper if in season; if not in season, a jar of canned tomatoes or a dried seed pod works), seed packets in the background
- Camera: Directly overhead, square crop
- Lighting: Window light from left or right — not from behind the subject

**Shot 2 — Comparison shot (SLOT 5)**
- 2–3 different seed packets or seed envelopes laid out side by side, with the tablet in the upper center of the frame showing the guide open to an interior page (if visible on screen) or the cover
- Suggests the guide's function: comparison and selection
- Camera: Overhead or 45-degree angle

**Shot 3 — Scale reference**
- Tablet held flat against a garden tray or tray of seedling pots; shows the product's size relative to a working garden context
- Useful for communicating "this is a comprehensive guide, not a one-page cheat sheet"

**Styling notes**: Heirloom varietals have distinctive shapes and colors — a black krim tomato or striped zucchini alongside the guide communicates the guide's subject better than any text overlay. If seasonal produce is unavailable, seed catalogs or seed envelopes are a strong substitute. Avoid modern commercial seed brand packaging in frame (turn packets away or use hand-labeled ones).

**Safety notes**: None.

#### Product 3 — Anti-Catalog: 30 Heirlooms ($10)

**Primary use case**: Counter-programming to commercial seed catalogs — recommends 30 heirloom varieties worth growing based on flavor, resilience, and seed-saving suitability.

**Shot 1 — Flat-lay (SLOT 4)**
- Surface: Wooden table or outdoor picnic table
- Arrangement: Tablet with guide cover, a stack of commercial seed catalogs face-down (showing the "anti" positioning without showing competitor brands), 3–4 seed envelopes, one printed page from the guide visible beneath the tablet
- Camera: Overhead
- Lighting: Window or outdoor natural light (overcast day is ideal for flat-lays — no harsh shadow)

**Shot 2 — Conceptual contrast shot (SLOT 5)**
- Commercial seed catalogs in a loose pile on one side; tablet with Anti-Catalog cover on the other side; hands pushing the catalogs away or reaching for the tablet
- This communicates the product's positioning without a word of text
- Camera: 45-degree angle, landscape crop (then square-crop for Etsy; save the landscape for Pinterest)

**Styling notes**: The conceptual shot is the strongest possible asset for this product and worth 5–10 minutes of extra framing effort. The product's title does the work — the image just needs to support the narrative. If commercial catalogs are not on hand, a stack of printed pages with commercial seed packet images printed from a Google image search works as a stand-in. Do not show brand names clearly.

**Safety notes**: None.

#### Product 4 — Companion Planting Chart ($5)

**Primary use case**: Printable or digital reference chart for what to plant near what in a vegetable garden.

**Shot 1 — Wall chart in use (SLOT 4)**
- Print one copy of the companion planting chart PDF in color (8.5x11 or larger). Pin or tape it to a wooden wall, barn door, or fence board. Photograph from slightly below eye level so the wall/board texture is visible as background. This communicates "printable reference" immediately and is unique among Etsy listings in this category.
- Camera: Portrait or square crop, slight upward angle
- Lighting: Natural outdoor light or window from behind the camera

**Shot 2 — Flat-lay detail (SLOT 5)**
- A printed copy of the chart laid flat on a garden bench or table, one corner weighted down with a small trowel, 2–3 vegetables or herbs next to the relevant section of the chart
- Camera: Overhead, slightly close-cropped to show chart detail and props together

**Shot 3 — Gardening context (bonus/social)**
- A gardener (yourself, one-handed) holding the printed chart near a raised bed or garden row, as if consulting it mid-planting
- This is the clearest "in use" shot for this product and should be captured if opportunity exists

**Styling notes**: This is the one product where printing the file specifically for the shoot is worth doing. The chart's visual density and the act of holding or pinning it communicates product quality better than a tablet screen. Print at least A4/letter size. The wall-mount shot is differentiated — most Etsy listings for companion planting charts show them as mockup overlays; a real printed chart tacked to a wood surface is distinctive.

**Safety notes**: None.

This is a product where the flat-lay with the printed calendar page outperforms a tablet-only shot because the calendar format is visible at glance.

- Lighting: Window or diffuse indoor light

**Styling notes**: The "planning" ritual is this product's emotional hook. Props that signal planning — a mug, a pen, a calendar — are correct. Avoid seasonal specificity in the mug or calendar if you shoot outside of a particular season; a plain mug and a blurred calendar work year-round.

#### Product 13 — Fermented & Preserved Harvest Handbook ($18)

**Primary use case**: Comprehensive guide to fermentation and food preservation — lacto-fermentation, vinegar pickling, salt preservation, storage.

**Shot 1 — Kitchen preservation scene (SLOT 4)**
- Surface: Wooden kitchen counter or table
- Arrangement: Tablet showing guide cover centered; 3–4 mason jars around it — at least one with a visible ferment (sauerkraut, pickled vegetables, or brine visible through the glass), one with dried herbs or dried chilis; a cloth napkin or linen towel loosely tucked beside the jars
- Camera: Overhead or 45-degree angle
- Lighting: Kitchen window light or one warm LED

**Shot 2 — Hands-in-process (SLOT 5)**
- One hand pressing cabbage or vegetables into a mason jar (the physical act of packing a ferment); guide visible on tablet propped in the background on the counter
- If fermentation materials are not on hand: one hand placing a lid on a sealed jar works — the jar-sealing gesture is universally understood as preservation
- Camera: Eye-level or slightly above, close on hands and jar

**Shot 3 — Full preservation spread (bonus/social)**
- Wide-angle view of a counter covered in jars at various stages, guide open on tablet or printed pages spread at the back. This is a "whole system" shot ideal for Pinterest or Instagram.

**Styling notes**: The visible contents of the jars are the most important detail. Empty or water-filled jars look staged. Sauerkraut, pickled cucumbers, herb-infused vinegar, or even dried beans are all correct. A jar with bubbles visible (active fermentation) is the highest-impact prop if timing allows. Kitchen context — tiles, wooden surfaces, natural textures — is strongly preferred over a clean white background.

**Safety notes**: If photographing active ferments, note that some natural ferments (especially open-crock lacto-ferments) can splash. No specific safety hazard, but keep the jar sealed during the shoot.

## 3. Stock Photography Sourcing Plan — 6 Products

Six products cannot be staged authentically in a kitchen, balcony, or small garden setting. These get stock images sourced from free and paid sites. The sourcing plan below provides specific search queries, source priority, and budget allocation per product.

### Sourcing Protocol

Before purchasing anything:
1. Search all three free sources (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay) using all listed queries
2. For each product, collect 3–5 candidates from free sources and evaluate against the quality checklist
3. Only purchase iStock credits for products where free sources return zero usable results or where quality clearly falls short
4. Log every selected image in `projects/seedwarden/WORKLOG.md` with: product name, source URL, license type, filename saved as

**Slot 5 image**: Person handling a chicken or interacting with goats — the tactile "field manual" moment.

**Budget allocation**: This is the highest iStock risk product. If free sources have nothing usable: allocate $24–$30 (2 images) from the $80–$160 total.

## 5. Equipment and Setup Checklist

### Camera Selection

**Phone (recommended for most shots)**:
- iPhone 12 or newer: use Portrait mode for any shot where background blur is wanted; standard wide-angle lens for flat-lays
- Android equivalent: Pixel 6+, Samsung S21+, or any flagship with portrait/bokeh mode
- Key setting: grid lines on (for overhead shots — keeps the frame level), HDR off (HDR can make flat-lay shadows look artificial), format set to highest JPEG quality or RAW if editing flexibility is needed

**Dedicated camera (optional, use only if available)**:
- Mirrorless or DSLR with 35mm or 50mm prime lens (avoid wide-angle below 28mm for flat-lays — distortion)
- Aperture priority mode, f/5.6–f/8 for flat-lays (wide depth of field), f/1.8–f/2.8 for contextual shots with background separation
- ISO: keep below 800 to avoid visible grain

**Which to use**: If both are available, use the phone for quick contextual shots and the dedicated camera for hero flat-lays (slot 4 images). If only a phone is available, the phone is fully sufficient. Do not let camera choice delay the shoot.

---

### Lighting Setup

**Option A — Natural window light (best)**:
- Position the shooting surface 2–4 feet from the largest window available
- Shoot between 9am–2pm when window light is even
- If direct sun cuts across the surface (harsh diagonal shadows): hang a thin white curtain or tape white tissue paper over the lower pane — this diffuses the light without eliminating it
- Add a white foam board or white poster board on the far side of the subject from the window — this reflects fill light back into the shadow side and opens up dark areas without a second light source
- Cost: $0 (tissue paper or sheet) + $0–$5 (foam board from a dollar store)

**Option B — LED softbox (if natural light unavailable)**:
- 45W daylight LED softbox placed at 45 degrees to the subject, at roughly subject height or slightly above
- Temperature: set to 5500K (daylight balance) — do not use tungsten/warm setting on the softbox (it shifts to orange)
- One softbox is sufficient; a second softbox as fill is an improvement but not required
- Position: between the 10-o'clock and 2-o'clock positions relative to the subject when looking from above
- Cost: $35–$60 on Amazon; or borrow from a local library tool-lending program (increasingly common)

**What not to use**:
- Ring light: creates a circular catch-light in any reflective surface and in eyes; reads immediately as influencer/lifestyle content, not as Seedwarden's earthy/practical brand
- Phone flash or on-camera flash: creates flat, harsh light that eliminates depth and texture
- Overhead room lighting only (without supplemental window or softbox): creates muddy, low-contrast images that look unintentional

**Cluster A (seeds/garden)**:

- Cutting board as an embedded surface within the larger table scene

**Do not use for any cluster**:

- Black velvet or dark seamless: too dramatic; reads as jewelry photography
- Polished marble or granite: too aspirational, Pottery Barn aesthetic

### Post-Processing Workflow

**Step 1 — Import**:
- Transfer all photos from phone to computer or directly edit on phone
- Organize into folders by cluster: `cluster-a/`, `cluster-b/`, `cluster-c/`

**Step 2 — Select**:
- For each product, select the 2 best frames from each shot type (flat-lay, contextual)
- Delete or archive all other frames from that product before editing — editing only the selects avoids confusion during batch processing

**Step 3 — Edit first image per cluster**:

Standard parameters (apply in this order):
1. Exposure: +0.2 to +0.5 (lift shadows; avoid blowing highlights)
2. Contrast: +15 to +25 (richness without harshness)
3. Highlights: -20 to -40 (recover window or bright surface detail)
4. Shadows: +15 to +25 (open up prop shadows without flattening the image)
5. White balance: warm — 5500–6000K (earthy, not clinical); avoid the camera's auto white balance if it pulled cool/blue
6. Saturation: greens +10 to +15, yellows +10, blues -10 (earthy color profile; reduces digital-looking blue cast from windows or LED)
7. Crop: 1:1 square (do this last so you crop with final edit visible)

**Step 4 — Copy settings as preset**:
- In Lightroom Mobile: tap the three dots on the edited image, "Create Preset," name it "Cluster A" (or B, C)
- In Snapseed: after editing, tap "Export" then "Save Effects as Style"

**Step 5 — Paste preset to all images in cluster**:
- Select all remaining images from the same cluster
- Apply preset
- Review each image at 100% — the preset may need minor exposure adjustment per image if window light varied during the shoot

**Step 6 — Export**:
- Resolution: 2400px on the long edge
- Format: JPEG
- Quality: 90% (higher creates larger files with minimal visual benefit; lower creates visible compression artifacts at 100% zoom)
- Color space: sRGB (not Adobe RGB — Etsy displays sRGB)

**Step 7 — File size check**:
- Run all exports through Squoosh (squoosh.app — free, browser-based)
- Target: under 1MB per image
- If any file is over 1MB: reduce JPEG quality to 85% in Squoosh until under 1MB

**Step 8 — Rename**:
- Follow the naming convention: `[product-slug]-slot4.jpg`, `[product-slug]-slot5.jpg`
- Example: `seed-saving-field-manual-slot4.jpg`

**Free tool alternatives to Lightroom Mobile**:
- Snapseed (Google, free, iOS/Android): use Tune Image for all adjustments; use Selective for targeted area corrections; export at max quality
- VSCO (free tier): consistent preset-based workflow; slightly less granular control than Lightroom but faster
- RawTherapee (desktop, free, Windows/Mac/Linux): full RAW processing; steeper learning curve; use if working with RAW files from a dedicated camera

**Watermarking**: Not recommended for Etsy listing images. Watermarks reduce conversion rate without meaningfully protecting digital products. If theft is a concern, the correct response is a visible watermark on the product interior pages, not on marketing images.

### A/B Testing Plan (Phase 2 → Phase 3 handoff)

Etsy Experiments are available to shops that meet eligibility criteria (typically 100+ sales history and active listings). If the Seedwarden shop qualifies by the time lifestyle images are uploaded:

**Highest-value A/B test**: Hunting, Fishing & Trapping Manual ($20)
- Version A (control): Tablet cover mockup as primary image (slot 1)
- Version B (treatment): Lifestyle field image (fishing/foraging context) as primary image
- Metric: Views-to-purchase conversion rate over 30 days
- Hypothesis: Lifestyle image as primary will increase CTR from search results because it communicates use-context at thumbnail size, while the mockup communicates format (digital) — both are valid, but the field context may outperform for this audience

**Second-priority A/B test**: Survival Garden Regional Plans ($22)
- Version A: Tablet mockup
- Version B: Aerial or wide-angle garden stock image with product overlay
- Hypothesis: The scale and geographic specificity of the lifestyle image communicates the guide's scope in a way the tablet mockup cannot

**If Etsy Experiments are not available**: Run a manual rotation test by swapping the primary image (slot 1) for two 2-week periods and tracking conversion rate in Etsy Stats. This is noisier than a controlled Experiment but still generates directional data.

## 7. Social Media Double-Duty Asset Planning

The Phase 3 social media strategy (Session 501) requires lifestyle content at specific dimensions. The photography sessions in Phase 2 produce assets that can be repurposed without additional shoots if captured with this in mind.

### Capture Targets for Social Reuse

For each physical shoot session, capture at least one of the following alongside the Etsy-primary shots:

**Portrait orientation (vertical, 4:5 or 9:16)**:
- Hold the phone vertically for 2–3 frames of each contextual/action shot
- This creates a native Instagram Reels or TikTok still background without additional cropping
- Resolution target: 2400px minimum on the short edge (Etsy exports at 2400px on long edge — do not use Etsy exports for vertical social formats; export a separate vertical version)

**Wide/landscape orientation (16:9)**:
- Capture 1 wide establishing shot per cluster setup (before close-cropping for the product)
- This serves Pinterest (converted to 2:3 crop) and the Etsy shop banner (3360×840px)

**Video option (10–30 seconds)**:
- For Cluster C (food preservation): capture a 15-second video of hands packing a mason jar or pouring brine
- For Cluster A (seeds): capture a 10-second video of seeds being poured from an envelope
- These clips are TikTok-ready B-roll without any post-production beyond trimming
- Capture with phone oriented horizontally for TikTok (they convert vertical when cropped, but starting horizontal gives more flexibility)

### Format Reference

| Platform | Recommended Crop | Source Frame |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Etsy slot 4/5 | 1:1 (2400×2400px) | Square crop from any orientation |
| Etsy shop banner | 3360×840px | Wide landscape establishing shot |
| Instagram Reel | 9:16 (1080×1920px) | Vertical crop from portrait frame |
| Instagram static post | 4:5 (1080×1350px) | Slight vertical crop |
| Pinterest pin | 2:3 (1000×1500px) | Portrait crop |
| TikTok | 9:16 (1080×1920px) | Same as Instagram Reel |
| Pinterest video pin | 9:16 | Same as TikTok |

The single most valuable non-Etsy reuse: Pinterest pins for each product, created from the slot 4 flat-lay image cropped to 2:3. These point directly to the Etsy listing and generate long-tail purchase-intent traffic with no ongoing cost. See Phase 3 strategy document for full Pinterest keyword and board structure.
