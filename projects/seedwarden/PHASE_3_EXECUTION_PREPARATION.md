### 3.2 Research Depth Per Species Profile Section

Each species profile has 8 subsections. The research depth below is calibrated to the 2–4 hour per-species research-and-write cycle that keeps the bundle on the 12–16 hour development time target.

**Subsection 3.1: Identification and Botanical Description** (100 words)
- Sources: USDA PLANTS database (plants.usda.gov) for accepted botanical name, range map, and morphology. iNaturalist species account for common identification features. One academic flora (Flora of North America, or BONAP) for technical botanical description.
- Research time: 20–30 minutes. Pull USDA plants page, confirm Latin name, pull top iNaturalist observation images for visual reference.

**Subsection 3.2: Conservation Sidebar** (100–150 words — only for at-risk species)
- Sources: United Plant Savers (unitedplantsavers.org) At-Risk species list; Forest Grown Verified (FGV) supplier directory; CITES Appendices for Goldenseal (Appendix II). Massachusetts Endangered Plant List (for Black Cohosh state-level documentation).
- Research time: 20–30 minutes per conservation sidebar. Document the overharvest problem, cite the UpS status, and link to FGV as the solution.

**Subsection 3.3: Range and Habitat** (50 words)
- Sources: USDA PLANTS range map (pull the image reference); eFloras.org for native range description. This section is brief — 2–3 sentences max.
- Research time: 10 minutes.

**Subsection 3.4: Cultivation** (150 words)
- Sources: USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) Plant Guides (available for most native species at plants.usda.gov/plant-guides); University Extension publications (OSU Extension for Ohio woodland herbs; UI Extension for Iowa Zone 5 herbs); Strictly Medicinal Seeds planting notes (their catalog includes cultivation depth per species).
- Research time: 30–40 minutes. NRCS Plant Guide is the most efficient single source — it covers soil, water, shade, spacing, harvest in 1–2 pages per species.

**Subsection 3.5: Harvest and Preparation** (80 words)
- Sources: American Herbalists Guild preparation standards; NRCS Plant Guide harvest notes; practical confirmation from American Botanical Council's HerbalGram monograph database (herbalgram.org).
- Research time: 20 minutes. Do not include dosage — that is outside guide scope.

**Subsection 3.6: Active Constituents** (60 words)
- Sources: PubMed (pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — search "[species name] active constituents" OR "[species name] phytochemistry review". Target 1–2 peer-reviewed review papers per species. Do not use popular press sources for constituents section.
- Research time: 20–30 minutes. Read abstract + introduction only (not full study). Write constituent description at general level — no mechanism claims.

**Subsection 3.7: Traditional and Historical Use** (80 words)
- Sources: American Botanical Council's HerbalGram (herbalgram.org); Moerman's Native American Ethnobotany Database (naeb.brit.org) for indigenous use documentation; NCCIH Herbs at a Glance fact sheets for general background. Historical Eclectic Medicine texts (1850–1920, now public domain) for 19th-century use documentation.
- Research time: 25–35 minutes. Lead with the earliest well-documented use (often indigenous American or European). FTC framing: "traditionally used for X in [tradition]."

**Subsection 3.8: Contraindications** (80 words)
- Sources: NCCIH Herbs at a Glance (nccih.nih.gov/health/herbsataglance.htm); Natural Medicines database (if subscription available); PubMed case reports for serious contraindications (e.g., Black Cohosh liver toxicity case series). For drug interactions: HerbalGram monographs.
- Research time: 20–25 minutes. This is a liability section — be thorough. Every species gets one, with the standard header: "This section describes traditional cautions and does not constitute medical advice."
