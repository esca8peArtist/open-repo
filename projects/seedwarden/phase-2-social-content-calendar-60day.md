# Phase 2 — 60-Day Social Content Calendar

**Context**: This calendar begins when Phase 2 photography (slots 4–5) is live on Etsy for the first Tier 1 products. It is designed to run in parallel with Track A (account verification, tag corrections) — none of this content requires the Etsy store to be fully launched. Pinterest pins can be saved to draft, Instagram posts can be queued, and content can be produced days before it is needed.

**Calendar assumptions**:
- Lifestyle photos for Survival Garden, Hunting Manual, Livestock Manual, and Meat/Fish Preservation are complete before Day 1
- Cluster A physical photos (Seed Saving, Heirloom Guide) are complete by Week 2
- Cluster C photos (Harvest Preservation, Fermented Harvest, Hot Sauce) are complete by Week 3
- No paid advertising budget in Days 1–30; optional $25 Pinterest promoted pin in Days 31–60

**Brand voice reminder**: Warm, direct, slightly political but never preachy. Urban-centered, not aspirational homestead. The audience is doing this in an apartment or small yard, not on a farm. Every post should either teach something practical, share a values-aligned perspective, or show the product being used in a real context.

---

## Platform Cadence Overview

| Platform | Posts Per Week | Primary Format | Primary Goal |
|----------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| Instagram | 4–5 posts | Reels (growth), Carousels (saves), Stories (daily) | Audience building, Etsy traffic |
| Pinterest | 7–10 pins | Static product pins, educational pins | Evergreen Etsy traffic |
| TikTok | 3–4 videos | Short educational Reels (cross-post from IG) | Discovery, link-in-bio traffic |

**Cross-posting rule**: Every Instagram Reel is also posted to TikTok natively (uploaded separately, not linked — TikTok suppresses cross-platform links). Pinterest pins are produced from still frames extracted from Reels plus dedicated static pin designs.

**Story cadence**: Daily when active, minimum 5 stories per week. Stories are low-effort maintenance — behind-the-scenes of the photo process, product questions, poll stickers. They keep the account active between major posts.

### WEEK 1 (Days 1–7) — Phase 2 Launch Week

**Goal**: Announce that lifestyle photos are live, introduce the visual identity for Phase 2, drive first click-throughs to Tier 1 listings.

**Prerequisite**: Lifestyle photos live on Etsy for at least the first 2 Tier 1 products (Survival Garden, Hunting Manual) before Day 1 posts go live.

**Day 1 — Instagram Reel + TikTok**
Format: Reel, 30–45 seconds
Hook: "I just redesigned how my Etsy listings look. Here's why the original mockups were wrong."
Content: Talking head or B-roll showing the transition from mockup-only listings to the new lifestyle photos. Show a specific example — the before (clean mockup on white) and the after (lifestyle image of the guide in a real setting). Frame the change as "I wanted these to look like something a real person would use, not a stock photo."
CTA: "Link in bio — every listing now has 5 images."
Product tie-in: None specific — this post is about the store upgrade, which builds general credibility and curiosity.
Hashtags: `#etsydigitaldownload #etsyseller #growyourown #homesteading #urbanfarming #seedsaving #foodsovereignty #smallbusiness #etsyshop #seedwarden`
Pinterest: Extract a still frame for a "behind the scenes" pin

**Day 2 — Pinterest batch build session (no public post)**
Internal production: Build 5–7 Pinterest pins for Tier 1 products from the new lifestyle photos.
Pin 1: Survival Garden — product pin with lifestyle image, text overlay "Survival Garden Regional Plans — Plan your region's best food garden"
Pin 2: Hunting and Trapping Manual — educational pin "What to forage in your region + a full field guide"
Pin 3: Livestock Manual — product pin "Small-Scale Livestock Field Manual — chickens, goats, ducks for the small homestead"
Pin 4: Meat Preservation — educational pin "How to cure and smoke meat at home — complete guide"
Pin 5: Harvest Preservation — product lifestyle pin using new slot 4 image
Save all to draft; schedule or publish on Day 3.

**Day 3 — Instagram Carousel**
Format: Carousel, 5–7 slides
Hook: "5 guides for people who want to grow more of their own food this year"
Content: One slide per product (the 5 Tier 1 products), each slide showing the lifestyle photo + product name + one-sentence description of what it covers + price. Final slide: CTA to shop.
Design: Use the Seedwarden palette — Deep Forest Green (#143b28) background for text slides, lifestyle photos as full-bleed image slides alternating. Playfair Display for product names.
Caption: "We updated the shop with 5 new guides for people who are serious about growing more of their own food. From survival gardens to backyard livestock to hunting and foraging — these are the practical references, not the aspirational ones. Shop link in bio."
CTA: "Save this post if any of these are on your list."
Pinterest: The 5-slides-as-still-images format can be adapted directly into a carousel pin (Pinterest allows up to 5 images in a carousel pin format).

**Day 4 — TikTok (cross-post from Day 1 Reel)**
Upload Day 1 Reel natively to TikTok. Add TikTok-native caption (different text, same hook).
Note: Do not share the Instagram link. Upload the video file directly to TikTok for full distribution.

**Day 5 — Instagram Story series**
3–5 Stories: Behind-the-scenes of the photography process. Show the flat-lay setup, the props, the editing on Lightroom Mobile. Use the Poll sticker on one story: "Do you prefer seeing a product in real life vs. a clean mockup?" This generates engagement data and signals to the algorithm.

**Day 6 — Pinterest: publish Day 2 pins**
Publish the 5 pins built on Day 2. Space them 2 hours apart (Pinterest recommends spreading pins through the day, not dumping all at once).

**Day 7 — Instagram single image post**
Format: Single lifestyle photo (the strongest from the Tier 1 batch — likely the Harvest Preservation mason jar setup)
Hook: "This is what the guide looks like in a real kitchen."
Caption: Describe the product in 3–4 sentences. Price. CTA.
This post tests whether the standalone lifestyle image drives more saves/clicks than the carousel format — note the engagement difference in the WORKLOG.

## Posting Time Optimization

Based on the existing research in phase-3-social-media-growth-strategy.md and phase-3-platform-asset-specs.md, these are the optimal posting windows for Seedwarden's audience:

**Instagram**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday — 7:00–9:00pm local time. Sunday morning (9–11am) is a secondary strong window for saves and shares.

**TikTok**: Tuesday through Friday — 7:00–9:00pm is primary. Avoid Monday (lowest engagement day for educational content). Saturday morning (8–10am) is a secondary window if the primary window is not available.

**Pinterest**: Unlike Instagram and TikTok, Pinterest pin timing matters less than pin volume and quality. Pin distribution is handled by Pinterest's algorithm over days and weeks. Best practice: spread pins throughout the day (not all at once), and pin consistently (5–10 per day) rather than 50 pins one day and zero for a week.

## Product Showcase Sequencing Logic

The 60-day calendar introduces products in this order, which is designed to:
1. Launch with the highest-ticket products first (Days 1–7) for maximum revenue impact
2. Follow with the most educational content categories (seed saving) to establish authority
3. Time preservation content to build ahead of July–September peak demand season
4. Use the apartment/urban growing content when seasonal awareness for spring planting is still high

| Days | Product Focus | Rationale |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| 1–7 | Tier 1 products (Survival Garden, Hunting, Livestock, Meat Preservation, Harvest Preservation) | Highest-revenue products get the lifestyle photo announcement |
| 8–14 | Cluster A: Seeds, Heirloom, Companion Planting | Core values and educational content — builds trust and save rates |
| 15–21 | Cluster C: Preservation (Fermented Harvest, Hot Sauce) | Preservation anticipation before the July peak |
| 22–28 | Cluster B: Container, Apartment Growing | Spring planning season — apartment growers are most active |
| 29–35 | Analytics pause + optimization | Adjust based on real engagement data |
| 36–49 | Preservation deep-dive | July preservation season ramp |
| 50–60 | Phase 2 close, Phase 3 transition | Catalog review, Phase 3 teasers, content bank build |

## Content Pillars and Mix Targets

| Content Type | Target % | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Educational (how-to, explainer) | 35% | Highest save and share rate — builds evergreen audience |
| Product feature (lifestyle photos in context) | 20% | Drives direct Etsy clicks |
| Values / food sovereignty perspective | 15% | Brand differentiation — not every brand talks politics |
| Behind-the-scenes (shop process, photography) | 15% | Trust-building, humanizes the brand |
| Community (seed swaps, audience questions) | 10% | Engagement and loyalty |
| Relatable / entertaining | 5% | Balances the informational weight; algorithm booster |

**Cross-platform repurposing**: Every piece of content is produced once and adapted across platforms. A 45-second Reel becomes a TikTok, a cover image becomes a Pinterest pin, and the caption research becomes a Pinterest pin description. The production budget is one session per week for major content; minor adaptations happen during the same session.
