## Part 4: Writing Production Templates

Three templates must exist at `projects/seedwarden/phase-3-production-templates/` before sprint Day 1 (June 22). Create during the June 1–9 buffer window.

### Template 1: Medicinal Bundle Outline Template

**Filename**: `medicinal-bundle-outline-template.md`
**Purpose**: The scaffold every bundle follows. Opening with this template eliminates blank-page friction on sprint Day 1.
**Word count**: Template itself is ~500 words (placeholder text + section headers). Filled bundle is 3,500–4,000 words.
**Create by**: June 2

**Section structure** (all five bundles use identical order):

1. Front Matter (80–100 words) — copyright notice, FTC educational use statement, practitioner 10-pack license note
2. Introduction (180–220 words) — bundle framing, conservation thread, reader orientation, seasonal hook
3. Species Sections — 4–5 species entries per bundle (see species guide template below)
4. Preparation Methods (180–210 words) — 3–5 practical preparations applicable to the bundle's herbs
5. Practitioner Context (140–160 words) — why these herbs matter to RN/NP clinical herbalist buyers; AHG and ABC resource references
6. Seasonal Timing (90–110 words) — month-by-month planting and harvest calendar; Zone 5 as baseline; Zone 3 and Zone 8 variations noted for key species
7. Cross-Reference Notes (50–80 words) — which herbs appear in other Seedwarden bundles; cross-sell prompts
8. Sources (80–120 words) — all cited sources by URL or PMID; minimum 8–12 sources per bundle

**Total template word budget**: 3,800–5,400 words across all sections.

### Template 2: Species Guide Template

**Filename**: `species-guide-template.md`
**Purpose**: Per-species entry scaffold used inside each bundle. Also usable standalone for Tier 1 single-herb guides.
**Word count**: 650–900 words for first-occurrence species; 300–450 words for second-occurrence (shared species across bundles). Second-occurrence writing is 40% faster due to research reuse.
**Create by**: June 2

**Section structure** (within each species entry):

| Section | Word Target | Est. Hours | Key Rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 Identification and Botanical Description | 150–200 | 0.5 | Genus, family, growth habit, leaf and flower morphology, distinguishing features |
| 3.2 Range and Habitat | 80–100 | 0.25 | Native range, naturalized range, US USDA hardiness zones, habitat type |
| [Conservation Sidebar — at-risk species only] | 150–200 | 0.5 | Mandatory for Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, Echinacea angustifolia; optional for others |
| 3.3 Cultivation | 300–450 | 1.0–1.5 | Hardiness zones, soil pH, propagation method, time-to-harvest, yield per plant |
| 3.4 Harvest and Preparation | 120–150 | 0.4 | Specific plant part, timing, processing (drying, storage), no dosage information |
| 3.5 Active Constituents | 80–100 | 0.25 | Compound names, avoid efficacy framing ("contains berberine, studied in laboratory settings for...") |
| 3.6 Traditional and Historical Use | 250–350 | 0.8 | Named culture, named tradition, "historically used for" framing; no treatment claims |
| 3.7 Contraindications | 150–250 | 0.5 | Required for every species; verify against NCCIH and UpS before marking complete |
| **Species total** | **1,280–1,800** | **4.2–5.7** | First-occurrence; condensed versions are 50% shorter |

**Quality gate per species** (must pass before moving to next species):
- [ ] Cultivation section specifies hardiness zone range, soil pH, propagation option, and time-to-harvestable-plant
- [ ] Traditional use is framed as historical description ("used in Cherokee medicine for"), not as a contemporary efficacy claim
- [ ] Contraindications section verified against NCCIH and UpS (not written from memory)
- [ ] If species is UpS At-Risk: conservation sidebar present and 150 words minimum
- [ ] Active constituents section uses "studied in" or "contains" language; no "treats" or "cures" wording

### Template 3: Practitioner-Content Template

**Filename**: `practitioner-content-template.md`
**Purpose**: Template for the Practitioner Context section that differentiates Seedwarden bundles from consumer-only guides. This section is what makes the practitioner 10-pack licensing tier ($120–130) credible.
**Word count**: 280–360 words total (Practitioner Context section + embedded clinical education notes per species).
**Research depth**: Each species requires one peer-reviewed source citation (PMID or full citation). NCCIH monographs are the minimum standard. Peer-reviewed ethnobotanical journals (Journal of Ethnopharmacology, HerbalGram) should supplement for at-risk species.
**Create by**: June 6

**Section structure**:

```
Practitioner Context (140–160 words)
  - Opening: why clinical herbalists and integrative health practitioners
    seek guidance on this bundle's herb group
  - Middle: one specific clinical scenario where this guide serves patient
    education (e.g., "For the RN managing a patient asking about elderberry
    supplementation during respiratory illness season...")
  - Close: reference to AHG (americanherbalistsguild.com) and ABC
    (herbalgram.org) as continuing education resources; note the practitioner
    10-pack license for patient distribution use

Embedded Clinical Notes (120–200 words total, across species in the bundle)
  - Per at-risk species: one sentence on professional sourcing standards
    (FGV program, Herb Pharm, Pacific Botanicals as named examples)
  - Per species with drug interactions: one sentence note formatted as a
    clinical alert ("Clinical note: Passionflower has documented MAOI
    interaction potential; advise patients to disclose use to prescribing
    physician")
  - FTC framing for in-context research citations: "A 2016 Cochrane review
    of elderberry preparations found [result]; this does not constitute a
    medical claim and is provided for educational context"
```

**Research depth requirements per bundle**:

| Respiratory | 12 sources | Sambucol elderberry RCT (Zakay-Rones 2004); Cochrane echinacea review; NCCIH elderberry monograph |
