### Template 1: Medicinal-Bundle-Outline-Template.md

**Purpose**: Master template for any medicinal herb bundle (Women's Health, Respiratory, Immunity, Sleep, Digestive)

**Target word count**: 3,500–3,800 words per bundle  
**Target development time**: 12–16 hours per bundle (300–350 words/hour, research-dense content)

**Structure** (follows the established Phase 3 content outline structure):

```
MEDICINAL HERB BUNDLE OUTLINE — [Bundle Name]
Target: 3,500–3,800 words | Est. Development: 12–16 hours

1. FRONT MATTER (200–300 words)
   1.1 Copyright and disclaimer page
   1.2 How to use this guide (para 1-2)
   1.3 Quick reference — [bundle-specific table, e.g., "Women's Health Herbs at a Glance"]
   1.4 Table of contents

2. BUNDLE INTRODUCTION (400–500 words)
   2.1 Why these five herbs for [bundle theme] (60–80 words)
   2.2 How this guide is organized (40–60 words)
   2.3 The five herbs overview — one paragraph per herb (250–300 words total)
   2.4 How to read the herb profiles (60–80 words)

3. HERB PROFILE SECTION (2,200–2,600 words total)
   [Repeat 3.1–3.8 for each of the 5 species]
   
   3.1 HERB NAME (Latin + common names) — Overview para (150–200 words)
       - Historical context (80–100 words)
       - Modern use and research framing (50–80 words)
       - Why this herb is important for [bundle theme] (40–60 words)
   
   3.2 Identification & Habit (150–200 words)
       - Plant structure (height, growth habit, leaf shape, perennial/annual)
       - Identifying features (distinctive leaf, flower, root appearance)
       - Where it grows naturally (native range or cultivation zones)
       - Best time to observe the plant (seasonal identification cues)
   
   3.3 Cultivation (200–250 words)
       - Growing zones (USDA hardiness range if perennial; season if annual)
       - Site requirements (light, soil, moisture)
       - Propagation (seed, rhizome division, transplant)
       - Planting timeline (when to start, sow, transplant)
       - Maintenance (water, fertilize, pest management)
       - Recommended suppliers [CITE Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Prairie Moon, etc.]
   
   3.4 Harvest & Preparation (150–200 words)
       - What part to harvest (leaf, flower, root, seed)
       - When to harvest (seasonal timing, plant maturity cues)
       - How to harvest (technique, post-harvest handling)
       - Drying and storage (temperature, container, shelf life)
       - Preparation methods (tea, tincture, infused oil, etc.) — 2–3 main methods
   
   3.5 Active Constituents & Traditional Use (150–200 words)
       - Primary active compounds (alkaloids, polysaccharides, etc.) and their known actions
       - Traditional use in herbalism (TCM, Ayurveda, Western herbalism, folk traditions)
       - Modern clinical research status (1–2 studies cited, framing)
       - Connection to [bundle theme] (why this herb is relevant for this use category)
   
   3.6 Contraindications & Drug Interactions (100–150 words)
       - Pregnancy/lactation cautions (if relevant)
       - Drug interactions (specific drug classes if known)
       - Allergies/sensitivities (plant family, cross-reactivity)
       - Over-use cautions (if any)
       - Regulatory or legal notes (CITES, endangered status, etc.)
       [FTC language: "Traditionally used for...", "Studied in clinical trials for...", NOT "This herb cures...")
   
   3.7 Sourcing Considerations (80–100 words) [if at-risk species: Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, E. angustifolia]
       - Wild-harvested vs. cultivated (conservation preference)
       - FGV (Forest Grown Verified) if relevant (Goldenseal, Black Cohosh)
       - Recommended cultivated suppliers (Strictly Medicinal, Prairie Moon, Mountain Rose Herbs)
       - Organic/fair trade certification if available
   
   3.8 Practitioner Notes (100–150 words)
       - Dosage ranges (tea, tincture, extract — ranges for educated practitioners)
       - Combinations with other herbs (2–3 common pairings in [bundle theme] context)
       - Timeline for observable effects ("traditionally used over 6–12 weeks")
       - Assessment cues (how to know if this herb is working for the client)

4. SHARED SECTIONS (700–900 words total)

   4.1 Preparation Methods for [Bundle Theme] (300–400 words)
       - A comprehensive section covering the 3–4 main preparation methods used in this bundle
       - Example: Women's Health might focus on teas, tinctures, and infused oils
       - Example: Sleep might focus on herbal blends, tinctures, and sachets
       - Each method: rationale, basic recipe (proportions), timing, storage

   4.2 Growing Your Own vs. Sourcing (150–200 words)
       - Decision framework: time/space/cost for growing vs. purchasing
       - Economic comparison ($/month for dried bulk vs. growing from seed)
       - Quality consistency (home-grown variance vs. commercial consistency)
       - Yield expectations (e.g., "one goldenseal plant provides ~5g dried root/year")

   4.3 Seasonal & Lunar Timing Notes [if relevant to bundle theme] (100–150 words)
       - Any seasonal themes for this bundle (e.g., "Women's Health herbs often connect to menstrual cycles")
       - Harvest timing connection to traditional use
       - Planting calendar (reference to the Zone Card included with this bundle)

   4.4 Practitioner Context (150–250 words) [Differentiation for practitioner 10-pack tier]
       - Dosing for clinical use (if applicable)
       - Assessment frameworks (tongue diagnosis, pulse, or other traditional assessment)
       - Combination formulas (example practitioner formulations using 2–3 herbs from bundle)
       - Licensing/scope notes (registered herbalist credential context)
       - Continuing education resources (RHAg, AHG, etc.)

5. SOURCES & FURTHER READING (300–400 words)
   5.1 Primary sources (textbooks, peer-reviewed studies) — 10–15 items
   5.2 Supplier references (links to recommended cultivation sources)
   5.3 Certification/authority references (UpS, FGV, AHG, etc.)
   5.4 Additional resources (seed savers networks, conservation organizations, practitioner associations)

TOTAL: 3,500–3,800 words
```

**Quality gates**:
- [ ] All 5 species sections follow structure 3.1–3.8 (no skipped subsections)
- [ ] Contraindications section uses FTC-compliant language ("traditionally used", "studied in clinical trials")
- [ ] At-risk species (Black Cohosh, Goldenseal, E. angustifolia) include sourcing considerations section (3.7)
