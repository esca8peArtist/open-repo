# Seedwarden Track B Implementation Roadmap
## Non-Etsy Channel Rollout: Phase 2A through Phase 3

**Prepared**: May 7, 2026
**Decision context**: Phase 1 is live (47 orders, $1,341 gross). Phase 2 launches May 30, 2026. This roadmap governs the parallel development of non-Etsy distribution channels alongside and after Phase 2A Etsy focus.

---

## Phase 2A: May 30 – June 30, 2026 (Etsy Focus + Track B Research)

**Primary objective**: Phase 2A is Etsy-first. Track A (Etsy launch, photography, SEO, email) commands 80% of available time. Track B in this phase is research and light setup only — no major time investment, no new channel launches.

**Track B work permitted in Phase 2A**:

1. **Gumroad storefront setup** (2–4 hours, one-time): Create parallel Gumroad product listings for the full Phase 2 catalog. This is infrastructure for both the affiliate program (documented in B2B_DISTRIBUTION_STRATEGY.md) and the practitioner B2B channel. Do this in Week 1 of Phase 2A — it is a prerequisite for all B2B outreach.

2. **Patreon account creation and tier design** (2–3 hours): Set up the account, write tier descriptions, design the monthly content calendar. Do not launch publicly yet. Launch in Phase 2B.

3. **Amazon KDP account setup** (1–2 hours): Create KDP account. Do not upload any titles yet. Setup removes friction from Phase 2B launch.

4. **B2B prospect list** (1–2 hours): Compile list of 15–20 herbal practitioners and 8–10 herbal schools from AHG directory and web research. This is a spreadsheet, not an outreach campaign. Outreach begins in Phase 2B.

**Track B work NOT permitted in Phase 2A**:
- No Patreon public launch
- No B2B outreach emails
- No craft fair booth planning
- No KDP title uploads

**Rationale**: Phase 2A Etsy performance data (conversion rate, AOV, traffic sources) is the input that determines which Phase 2B channels to prioritize. Launching B2B channels before Etsy performance is measured creates execution noise and dilutes focus during the highest-impact launch window.

**Phase 2A exit condition**: June 30, 2026. Review Etsy analytics for the month. Apply the decision triggers below.

---

## Decision Triggers at June 30, 2026

These triggers determine the Phase 2B channel prioritization. They are binary and actionable — no ambiguity.

**Trigger 1: Etsy conversion rate below 1.5%**

If the June 30 Etsy conversion rate is below 1.5%, Track B becomes the primary revenue recovery strategy for July. Execute immediately:

- Launch Patreon membership publicly (Tier 1 priority)
- Begin B2B practitioner outreach batch 1 (10 outreach emails in Week 1 of July)
- Do not launch KDP or craft fairs — these have 60–120 day return timelines and will not help near-term

The logic: a <1.5% conversion rate on a well-listed Etsy shop indicates either insufficient traffic or weak social proof. B2B direct sales bypass both problems — no algorithm dependency, no review requirement. Patreon bypasses Etsy entirely and builds a subscriber base that compounds regardless of Etsy performance.

**Trigger 2: Etsy conversion rate 1.5–2.5% (on-target)**

Execute Phase 2B channel development on the planned July–August timeline. No acceleration, no deferrals. The three-channel pilot (Patreon + B2B practitioners + KDP Print one title) proceeds as scheduled below.

**Trigger 3: Etsy conversion rate above 2.5%**

Etsy is working above benchmark. Phase 2B channel work is additive, not compensatory. Prioritize Patreon (LTV layer on existing buyers) and skip the first KDP upload until Phase 3. Do not start B2B outreach until Patreon is running — Etsy buyer-to-Patreon-subscriber conversion is the highest-leverage next step when Etsy is performing.

**Trigger 4: Etsy conversion rate above 3% (outperforming)**

Etsy at >3% is genuinely exceptional for a shop under 12 months old. In this scenario: double down on Etsy SEO and Phase 2 bundle strategy as the primary work. Phase 2B channels are deferred to Phase 3. Patreon-only is the one allowed addition — purely to capture LTV from Etsy's existing buyer flow.

## Phase 2B: July – August 2026 (Top 3 Channel Pilots)

**Objective**: Pilot the three highest-priority non-Etsy channels in parallel. Gather 8-week performance data on each. Make Phase 3 scaling decisions based on measured results.

**The three channels** (selected based on highest margin + fastest time-to-revenue from the comparison matrix):

### Channel Pilot 1: B2B Direct (Herbalists and Practitioners) — July Week 1 Launch

**Week 1**: Send batch 1 of B2B outreach emails (10 practitioners from AHG directory). Use Template D from B2B_DISTRIBUTION_STRATEGY.md, adapted for practitioner positioning. Include sample guide attachment.

**Week 2**: Send batch 2 (10 herbal schools). Use education licensing framing (60-day free trial, then annual license).

**Weeks 3–8**: Follow-up sequence (Day 7, Day 14, Day 30 as documented in B2B_DISTRIBUTION_STRATEGY.md Part 3). Convert interested respondents to paid agreements.

**Success threshold at August 31**: 3+ paid practitioner clients OR 1 school site license signed. If below this threshold, deprioritize in Phase 3 — the audience may be too small for the sales cycle length to be worth it at current Seedwarden scale.

### Channel Pilot 2: Patreon Membership — July Week 1 Launch

**Week 1**: Announce Patreon publicly. Send launch announcement to Etsy buyers (via order confirmation follow-up), Kit email list, and Instagram/TikTok/Pinterest.

**Weeks 1–4**: Deliver first Patreon guide drop. Quality of the first drop sets retention. Use the June or July seasonal guide from the monthly content calendar (track-b-distribution-strategy.md Channel 4).

**Weeks 5–8**: Assess subscriber retention (month 1 to month 2 churn). If churn exceeds 30%, the monthly content drop is not valuable enough — revise the $10 and $18 tier offerings.

**Success threshold at August 31**: 25+ active subscribers at any paid tier. At 25 subscribers (blended average $11/month), monthly net = approximately $243. If subscriber count is growing each week, continue. If subscriber count plateaued below 15 after 6 weeks, pause and diagnose.

### Channel Pilot 3: Amazon KDP Print — One Title — July Week 2 Upload

**Week 2**: Upload first KDP Print title. Recommended: Seed Saving Field Manual, priced at $22.99 (120+ pages, full color). This is the highest-price justification for physical format.

**Weeks 2–8**: No active promotion required. Let Amazon's algorithm index the title. Spend 1 hour in Week 4 optimizing the KDP backend keywords using the same keyword research from Etsy SEO work.

**Success threshold at August 31**: At least 3 organic sales. If zero sales after 8 weeks, the title's category placement is wrong — adjust subcategory and keywords. If 3+ sales, continue and upload a second title in September.

## Phase 3: September 2026+ (Scale and Evaluate)

**Input**: Phase 2A Etsy performance data + Phase 2B pilot results from three channel tests.

**Scaling successful channels**:

- If B2B pilot met threshold: expand outreach to 30+ additional practitioners and 15+ additional schools. Develop the annual clinic license product formally.
- If Patreon met threshold: increase Patreon promotion within Kit email sequence (add Patreon mention to Day 7 email in welcome sequence). Target 50+ subscribers by November.
- If KDP met threshold: upload 2 additional titles. Consider IngramSpark for library/bookstore distribution of the Seed Saving Field Manual.

**New channels to evaluate in Phase 3** (not ready in Phase 2B):

- Craft fairs: Target fall homesteading events (Homesteaders of America Annual Conference, regional permaculture gatherings). Design access card physical product. Test one event.
- White-label licensing: With 25+ Etsy reviews established, approach 2–3 institutional targets for restricted resell license conversations.
- Education licensing expansion: If one school license signed in Phase 2B, use it as social proof for outreach to mid-size schools.

**Phase 3 success definition**: At least two non-Etsy revenue streams each generating $200+/month net by December 2026. This represents approximately 15–25% of total Seedwarden revenue from non-Etsy channels, reducing platform dependency from 100% to approximately 75–85%.

## Summary: Sequencing Logic

| Period | Primary Focus | Track B Action | Time Allocation |
|---|---|---|---|
| May 30 – June 30 | Phase 2A Etsy launch | Gumroad setup, Patreon setup (private), KDP account, B2B prospect list | 4–8 hours total |
| July (Phase 2B start) | Three channel pilots | B2B outreach batch 1, Patreon launch, KDP upload | 6–8 hours setup + 2–3 hours/week ongoing |
| August (Phase 2B cont.) | Pilot performance monitoring | Follow-up outreach, Patreon Month 2 drop, KDP keyword optimization | 2–3 hours/week |
| September+ (Phase 3) | Scale proven channels + add craft fairs / white-label | Based on August pilot results | Varies by channel mix |

**The invariant rule**: Never invest more than 20% of total Seedwarden time in Track B channels while Etsy conversion rate is below 1.5%. Etsy is the proven channel; Track B is the diversification layer. If Etsy is underperforming, it needs more attention first, not less.
