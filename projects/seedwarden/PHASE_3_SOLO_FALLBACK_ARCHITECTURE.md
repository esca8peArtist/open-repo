## Section 1 — Design Principles

### Why 12 Hours Per Week

The 12-hour weekly ceiling is not a stretch goal — it is the maximum sustainable writing pace for practitioner-grade medicinal herb content. The contractor search existed precisely because the original sprint (5+ hours per day, 6 days/week = 30+ hours per week) was identified as overloading for solo execution across 5 bundles.

The 12-hour model distributes the same total content across a longer calendar window:
- Contractor sprint: 6 weeks (June 22 – August 3), ~8–10 writing hours per week with contractor coverage of 3 bundles
- Solo model: 9 weeks (June 22 – September 24), 12 writing hours per week for all 5 bundles
- Total writing hours: comparable (contractor writes ~31–37 hours; solo model covers the same scope across 9 weeks at 12 hrs/week = 108 hours total including research, editing, and admin)

The 12-hour ceiling is non-negotiable for one reason: compressing the solo model below 9 weeks recreates the same overload that made a contractor attractive. If the pace feels too slow in Week 2, resist acceleration. The Women's Health June 29 upload date is the sole exception — that date has zero flex regardless of model.

### Weekly Hour Split

Each week's 12 hours are allocated as:
- **9 hours — Writing** (focused prose, self-edit, section completion)
- **2 hours — Admin** (attribution log updates, Etsy listing drafts, email responses)
- **1 hour — Monitoring** (Etsy analytics, AHG outreach follow-up, image sourcing checks)

This ratio is enforced weekly, not daily. Some days carry 3–4 hours of writing; some carry admin only. The weekly 9/2/1 split is what prevents the sprint from becoming consuming.

### Bundle Write Order Logic

The optimal write order for cross-species efficiency is:
**Women's Health → Respiratory → Immunity → Sleep → Digestive**

Rationale for this order:

1. **Women's Health first** (Days 1–14): Zero-float upload June 29. Non-negotiable starting point in any model. The template established in Women's Health (8-section architecture, FTC framing, contraindication register format) transfers directly to all subsequent bundles — first-bundle investment pays forward.

2. **Respiratory second** (Days 15–33): Three-species overlap with Immunity (Echinacea purpurea/angustifolia, Elderberry). Writing Respiratory first means Echinacea sections are researched and written once; the Immunity bundle cross-references rather than duplicates them. Estimated cross-species savings: 2–3 hours of research and 400–600 words of original writing.

3. **Immunity third** (Days 34–54): Echinacea and Elderberry sections can reference Respiratory with a single cross-reference sentence and a condensed 300-word summary. Goldenseal (unique to Immunity) and Ashwagandha require original research that does not overlap with other bundles. Immunity's position after Respiratory extracts maximum cross-species savings.

4. **Sleep fourth** (Days 55–75): Lemon Balm overlaps with both Women's Health (mild sedative framing) and Digestive (carminative framing). Writing Sleep before Digestive means Lemon Balm is researched once; Digestive cross-references. Lavender overlaps with Women's Health (previously written) — Sleep simply references the Women's Health section.

5. **Digestive fifth** (Days 76–98): Dandelion overlaps with Women's Health (Dandelion leaf diuretic framing). Lemon Balm is cross-referenced from Sleep. Ginger and Calendula are unique. Digestive's position last extracts all available cross-species savings from the four prior bundles.

**Cross-species savings summary** (WH→Resp→Immunity→Sleep→Digestive order):
- Echinacea: Respiratory (full write) → Immunity (300-word cross-reference, saves 600w original)
- Elderberry: Respiratory (full write) → Immunity (condensed cross-reference, saves 400w)
- Lemon Balm: Sleep (full write) → Digestive (cross-reference, saves 300w)
- Lavender: Women's Health (full write) → Sleep (reference, saves 200w)
- Dandelion: Women's Health (partial, diuretic context) → Digestive (expanded, saves 150w research)
- **Total estimated savings**: 1,650 words of original writing + 3–4 hours of research across bundles 3–5

## Section 4 — Scope Reduction Cascades

These cascades are pre-authorized procedures, not decisions requiring deliberation. When a trigger condition is met, the cascade activates. The cascade is logged in WORKLOG.md and implemented immediately.

### Cascade 1: Week 1–2 Behind Schedule (Activate Digestive Deferral)

**Trigger**: Women's Health below 2,500 words at June 24 EOD (D3 pace gate failure).

**What changes**:
- Immunity and Digestive are not deferred under solo fallback (they are already deferred to August 17 and September 24 respectively — this cascade pre-confirms those dates without acceleration pressure).
- Scope compression within Women's Health: Vitex 600w → 400w, Red Clover 400w → 250w. Excess content to v1.1 file.
- The June 29 Women's Health upload date is preserved regardless.

**What does not change**: Respiratory (July 13), Sleep (August 3), Immunity (August 17), Digestive (September 24) upload dates are unchanged.

**Savings**: 350 words of scope reduction in Women's Health recovers 1.5–2 hours of writing pace in Days 4–7.

**Log entry format**: "[Date] — Cascade 1 activated: Women's Health scope compression (Vitex 600→400w, Red Clover 400→250w). Excess content saved in WH-v1.1-expansion.md. D3 pace gate: [X] words. Women's Health upload June 29 preserved."

### Revenue Impact of 3-Month Phase 4 Delay

Phase 4 botanical identification guides (18 species, $8–$12 per guide, $7–$10 per bundle) represent a new revenue vector at Phase 3 scale. At 40 units per week per guide (conservative), a 3-month delay represents:
- Phase 4 Wave 1 (9 guides, launched August 1 contractor vs. November 1 solo): ~12 weeks × 40 units × $10 = $4,800 in foregone Wave 1 revenue across the delay window
- Phase 4 Wave 2 (9 guides, launched August 31 vs. December 1): ~13 weeks × 40 units × $10 = $5,200 in foregone Wave 2 revenue

**Total Phase 4 revenue impact of solo fallback**: approximately $10,000 in foregone revenue across the August–October delay window. This is the full cost of the solo fallback decision relative to the contractor model.

**Context**: This figure represents foregone revenue, not a cash loss. It represents revenue that would be generated in the August–October window under the contractor model but is shifted to November 2026–January 2027 under the solo model. The content is not lost — only delayed. Phase 5 Q4 2026 planning that assumed November 1 Phase 5 start should be updated to February 1, 2027 if solo fallback is activated.
