## Part B: Caption & Content Templates

### Template 1: Plant Profile Post
**Post type**: Single plant identification guide  
**Length**: 150-200 words  
**Tone**: Educational, conversational, actionable

**Caption seed**:
```
🌱 Meet [PLANT NAME]

Common name: [Plant]
Scientific name: [Botanical name]
Habitat: [Where it grows]

🔍 How to identify:
• [Identifying feature 1]
• [Identifying feature 2]
• [Identifying feature 3]

💡 Why it matters:
[1 sentence on uses — edible, medicinal, ecological value]

📍 When to find it: [Season/month]
🏞️ Where to find it: [Region/habitat description]

Have you spotted this plant? Share your habitat photos in the comments! 👇

#NativePlants #PlantIdentification #WildEdibles #ForagingGuide
```

**Variations for specific plants** (examples):
- **Common Milkweed**: "🦋 Meet Common Milkweed — the monarch butterfly's lifeline and a nutritious wild edible when harvested young."
- **Ramps (Wild Garlic)**: "🧅 Meet Ramps — spring's most foraged wild garlic, fragrant, delicious, and now protected in many areas. Learn the ethical harvesting rules."
- **Reishi Mushroom**: "🍄 Meet Reishi — the medicinal mushroom used in traditional medicine for 2,000+ years. Grow it at home or forage it wild."

### Template 2: Educational Carousel (5-slide structure)
**Post type**: Multi-slide learning series  
**Best for**: Instagram Carousel (5 slides max)  
**Engagement**: High (educate → show → prove → save)

**Slide structure**:
```
SLIDE 1: Title
"5 Ways to Identify [Plant Name]"
[Plant hero photo]

SLIDE 2: Feature 1
"Leaf shape: [description]"
[Detail photo]

SLIDE 3: Feature 2
"Stem characteristics: [description]"
[Detail photo]

SLIDE 4: Feature 3
"Smell/taste test: [description]"
[Optional: sensory photo]

SLIDE 5: Action CTA
"Found this plant? Save this carousel and share your spot! 📍"
"#PlantIdentification #WildEdibles #ForagingGuide"
```

**Specific carousel ideas**:
1. "5 Ways to Identify Poison Hemlock vs. Wild Carrot" (SAFETY angle)
2. "3 Edible Mushrooms You Can Forage This Spring" (ACTIONABLE)
3. "How to Propagate Native Plants from Cuttings" (DIY)
4. "Medicinal Plants in Your Backyard" (VALUE)
5. "Zone 5 Plants That Feed & Shelter Wildlife" (ECOLOGICAL)

### Template 3: Habitat Photo Showcase
**Post type**: Photo-first with location context  
**Length**: 80-120 words  
**Tone**: Documentary, place-focused

**Caption seed**:
```
📍 Habitat Spotlight: [Location description]

What I found: [3-4 plants visible in photo]
Why this spot matters: [Ecosystem significance]

This is a [type of habitat: wetland, forest, meadow] habitat in [region]. It's home to [key species] and provides [ecological services: pollinator support, soil building, etc.].

Want to explore similar habitats? [Specific call-to-action based on accessibility]

#HabitatPhotography #NativePlants #FieldGuide #NorthAmericanEcosystems
```

**Example fills**:
- "Wetlands Spotlight: Cattail Marsh in Minnesota — home to muskrats, herons, and 15+ edible/medicinal plants"
- "Forest Spotlight: Mixed Deciduous Forest Edge — spring ephemeral zone with Trillium, Jack-in-Pulpit, Bloodroot"
- "Prairie Spotlight: Restored Native Prairie — 40+ plant species in one square meter, carbon-rich soil rebuilding"

### Template 5: Seasonal Guide (All platforms)
**Post type**: Grid/collection showcase  
**Length**: 100-150 words  
**Tone**: Curated, actionable

**Caption seed**:
```
🌱 What's in Season: [SEASON] Edition

Right now, you can find & forage:
🫐 [Plant 1] — [use]
🌿 [Plant 2] — [use]
🍄 [Plant 3] — [use]
🌸 [Plant 4] — [use]

Best time to harvest: [timing]
Where to look: [habitat]
How to prepare: [brief how-to or link]

What are you finding in your area this [season]? Drop it in the comments! 👇

#[Season]Foraging #WildEdibles #PlantIdentification
```

**Example seasonal posts**:
- **Spring** (March-May): Ramps, fiddleheads, wild asparagus, chickweed, violets
- **Summer** (June-August): Berries, milkweed, edible flowers, mushrooms
- **Fall** (Sept-Nov): Nuts, seeds, mushrooms, roots, rose hips
- **Winter** (Dec-Feb): Evergreens, roots, stored seeds, hardiness plants

## Part C: Platform-Specific Content Themes

### Instagram Content Pillars (4 themes, rotate)
1. **"Plant Profile Monday"** — Single plant ID + uses (Template 1)
2. **"Habitat Wednesday"** — Location spotlight + ecosystem context (Template 3)
3. **"Carousel Thursday"** — Educational series, deep dives (Template 2)
4. **"Seasonal Spotlight"** — What to find + harvest tips (Template 5)

**Posting frequency**: 3-4 posts/week (or adjust based on user decision May 30)

### TikTok Content Pillars
1. **Plant ID Challenges** — "Can you identify this plant?" + reveal
2. **Quick Edible Tips** — "3 things you didn't know about [plant]"
3. **Foraging Hacks** — Fast tips, DIY, preparation methods
4. **Habitat Tours** — Walking through a meadow/forest, narrating plants
5. **Myth-busting** — "No, this is NOT poison ivy — here's what IS"

**Video format**: 15-60 seconds, fast-paced, captions, trending sounds (optional)

### Pinterest Content Pillars
1. **ID Guides** — Visual reference pins (Template 4)
2. **Seasonal Guides** — What to find when + how to prepare
3. **DIY & How-To** — Propagation, drying, storage, cooking
4. **Infographics** — Habitat maps, zone guides, timeline charts
5. **Blog Link Pins** — Link back to external guides (future)

**Pin format**: Vertical (1000 × 1500), text-heavy, clear and readable
