# Photo Shoot Checklist
## Seedwarden Phase 2 — Shoot-Day Execution Guide

**Purpose**: This is the checklist you carry into the shoot. PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md is the full reference document; this document is the condensed shoot-day guide — one checkbox per shot, props listed at the session level, and a time budget for each cluster. Print this document or open it on a second device during the shoot.

**What this document is not**: A re-statement of scene descriptions. All scene descriptions are in PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md. This document strips those down to the minimum information needed to execute each shot without losing time.

---

## Before You Start: Universal Shoot-Day Setup

Complete these steps once before the first cluster session begins. Time: 20–25 minutes.

- [ ] Phone charged to 100% (plug in overnight before shoot day)
- [ ] Phone storage: confirm at least 3 GB free (30 raw photos at 4–8 MB each = ~120–240 MB; 3 GB gives buffer)
- [ ] White balance set to "Daylight" or "Cloudy" in camera app (not Auto — see PHOTO_SHOOT_PLANNING.md Part 1)
- [ ] Timer set to 3 seconds (used for any two-hand shot; access via camera self-timer)
- [ ] Window identified: largest window in the house, light quality checked (morning light, not direct noon sun)
- [ ] Shot confirmation method chosen: after each shot, confirm the image is sharp at 100% zoom before moving the props

## Session 1: Cluster A — Seeds and Garden

**Day**: Schedule for a morning with available window light (9am–12:30pm block recommended)
**Total time budget**: 4.25–5.25 hours
**Setup time (do this before shooting any product)**: 25 minutes
**Shooting time**: 3.5–4.5 hours (16 shots across 8 products)
**Session transfer and edit**: 15 minutes (transfer photos immediately after session; do not wait)

### Session 1 Setup — Props to Assemble Before First Shot

Gather these items and have them physically staged on the shoot surface before starting:

- [ ] Raw wood cutting board or large wood surface (18" minimum width)

- [ ] 5–10 seed envelopes, plain kraft — pre-label at least three: "Brandywine Tomato 2026," "Dragon Tongue 2026," "Black Krim 2026"
- [ ] Small ceramic or terracotta pot (4-inch size, with soil)
- [ ] Trowel or hand cultivator
- [ ] Fresh herb sprig (basil, rosemary, or parsley)
- [ ] Handwritten seed label or small chalkboard tag
- [ ] Pencil
- [ ] Printed pages: Seed Saving Field Manual cover + interior page with seed diagram
- [ ] Printed pages: Heirloom Guide cover + variety comparison table page
- [ ] Printed pages: Anti-Catalog cover
- [ ] Printed pages: Companion Planting Chart (print full chart — this shot requires readable text)
- [ ] Printed pages: Zone Calendar (monthly spread, April or May)
- [ ] Printed pages: Food Sovereignty Starter Guide cover + interior list page

Position setup: wood surface is primary backdrop. Dark linen as secondary. Light source: window to the LEFT of the shooting surface.

## Batch Editing Session

**When**: After all three cluster sessions are complete (or after Sessions 2 and 3 on the same day)
**Time budget**: 1.5–2 hours for all 30 photos
**Tools**: Lightroom Mobile (free) or Snapseed (free)

### Editing Protocol

- [ ] Import all Cluster A photos into one album or batch
- [ ] Edit the best Cluster A flat lay photo to final look using these settings:
  - Exposure: +0.2 to +0.5
  - Contrast: +20
  - Highlights: -30
  - Shadows: +20
  - White balance: 5500–6000K
  - Green saturation: +12
  - Blue saturation: -10
  - Crop to 1:1 square
- [ ] Save as a preset or copy adjustments
- [ ] Paste preset to all remaining Cluster A photos (batch apply)
- [ ] Repeat: edit one Cluster B photo, save preset, batch apply to Cluster B
- [ ] Repeat: edit one Cluster C photo (warmer — 6000–6500K), save preset, batch apply to Cluster C
- [ ] Export all 30: 2400px long edge, JPEG quality 88–90%

| Day | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 morning | 9:00am–1:30pm | Session 1 (Cluster A — 16 shots) |

Two half-days is recommended because Cluster A is the most demanding session — attempting it after Sessions 2 and 3 increases the risk of fatigue-related errors on the most complex shots.

## Common Mistakes — Quick Reference

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Auto white balance drifts between shots | Set manually to Daylight or Cloudy before starting and do not change |
| Portrait mode at overhead distance | Use standard photo mode for flat lays (24" or more above surface) |
| Props competing with the product | Remove one prop — the product is always the focal center |
| Hands posed, not acting | Hands must be doing something: writing, holding a seed, pressing into soil |
| All props the same shade of green | Vary texture: clay pot + glass jar + linen + wood grain |
| Commercial brand names visible on envelopes | Tape over or turn away from camera before shooting |
| Frame looks busy | Remove one element — clarity is always better than abundance for conversions |
| Forgot to check 1:1 thumbnail crop | Do a mental crop from center — confirm product is visible in thumbnail version |
