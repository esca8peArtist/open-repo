# Phase 4 Practitioner Tier Progression
## 3-Tier Credentialed Pathway — Entry to Clinical Specialist

**Prepared**: June 1, 2026
**Launch target**: August 1, 2026 (Tier 1 automatic; Tier 2 with verification workflow)
**Revenue model**: Hybrid — Tier 1 Etsy one-time purchase, Tier 2 Gumroad or Kit monthly subscription, Tier 3 direct invoice or Gumroad premium tier
**Credential verification**: Manual review with directory cross-check (practicable at current scale); automated verification tools identified for Phase 5 if subscriber base exceeds 200 Tier 2/3 accounts

---

## Strategic Context

The Seedwarden practitioner audience divides cleanly into three credentialed strata. This was established in HERBALIST_NETWORK_ECOSYSTEM_MAPPING.md (v3.0): the clinical practitioner segment (RH, ND, NP) has a binary credibility filter that activates before price considerations enter. Tier 2 and Tier 3 exist to meet practitioners at their credential level with content matched to their clinical depth, and to create a defensible recurring revenue stream that grows independent of Etsy listing traffic.

The three-tier model solves a specific problem: Phase 3 bundles (one-time purchase, $20–22 each) are the floor. They reach students and enthusiasts but do not retain clinical practitioners as recurring subscribers or unlock the higher-LTV segment of the market. A subscription model at Tier 2 and Tier 3 creates monthly recurring revenue, reduces the single-purchase acquisition pressure, and gives practitioners a reason to remain engaged across Phase 4 content releases and international traditions integration.

**Revenue model compatibility**: Etsy does not support subscriptions natively. Tier 2 and 3 subscriptions are delivered via Gumroad (memberships feature, 10% fee) or Kit (ConvertKit Commerce, $0 + 0.5–3.5% depending on plan). The decision between Gumroad and Kit depends on whether the primary delivery is PDF access (Gumroad is simpler) or email-curriculum integration (Kit is better for sequenced delivery). This roadmap specifies both options per tier; final platform decision is the operator's call at August 1 launch.

## Tier 1 — Herbalist

### Who This Is For
Any practitioner, student, or serious enthusiast at any stage. No credential required. Tier 1 is the natural home for all existing Phase 3 buyers: they have already demonstrated willingness to pay for practitioner-level content at Etsy price points. Tier 1 is not a free tier — it is the existing Etsy product line reframed within a progression architecture.

### Content Access — What Tier 1 Unlocks
- Any Phase 3 medicinal herb bundle (individual purchase, $20–22 each)
- Any Phase 4 botanical identification guide (individual, $5–7; bundle $24–65)
- Phase 4 introductory content: therapeutic gap overview, species selection reasoning, cultivation basics
- Seedwarden email sequence (Kit integration): 7-email onboarding sequence covering practitioner vocabulary, sourcing ethics, evidence-tier framework introduction
- Monthly practitioner newsletter (new research digest, one new species highlight per month, community Q&A)

### What Tier 1 Does Not Include
- Drug interaction tables beyond the Phase 3 summary level
- Clinical contraindication database (searchable by drug class)
- Monograph-level content (Commission E, WHO TRM, British Herbal Pharmacopoeia summaries)
- International traditions cross-reference system (Phase 4 deliverable)
- Case study library
- Dosing protocols for clinical use

### Pricing and Structure
**Entry path 1 (Etsy)**: Individual bundle purchases. No subscription. No account required beyond Etsy account. Tier 1 benefits trigger automatically when 3 or more Seedwarden bundles are purchased — the user receives an email (via Kit) with a Tier 1 welcome sequence and the 5 complimentary botanical ID guides.

**Entry path 2 (direct)**: $35 one-time "Herbalist Starter Pack" (Kit or Gumroad product):
- Phase 3 Core Bundle (3-bundle selection of buyer's choosing)
- 5 botanical ID guides (forager-accessible species)
- Tier 1 newsletter subscription
- Access to Tier 2 upgrade prompt at 90 days

**Revenue per Tier 1 customer**:
- Etsy path: $20–22 per bundle, 1–3 purchases average, LTV $30–65 before upgrade
- Direct path: $35 one-time
- Upgrade conversion to Tier 2: target 8–12% of Tier 1 holders over 6 months

## Tier 2 — Registered Herbalist (RH)

### Who This Is For
Credentialed clinical herbalists holding one of the following recognized designations:
- AHG Registered Herbalist (RH) — highest voluntary herbalism credential in the US; 1,200+ documented training hours; peer-reviewed case studies
- NAHA Certified Herbalist Level 5 (Professional Member) — National Association for Holistic Aromatherapy; clinical herbalism track with documented case studies; less rigorous than AHG RH but recognized in the professional herbalism community
- Herbal Academy Advanced Herbal Certificate — formal completion of Clinical Herbalism program; recognized as entry-level practitioner qualification
- Licensed practitioners (RN, NP, LPN, PA) with documented herbalism continuing education: 30+ CE hours in botanical medicine from a recognized provider (AHG CE, Bastyr University CE, NUNM CE)
- International equivalent: NIMH (National Institute of Medical Herbalists, UK) membership; FNIMH, MNIMH designations; equivalent European national herbalism registrations

**Estimated addressable audience**: 4,000–6,000 AHG members (RH and general); 2,000–4,000 NAHA members at Level 4–5; 10,000–20,000 licensed practitioners with herbalism continuing education. Realistically reachable via Phase 3 outreach channels: 500–2,000 in the first 12 months.

### Content Access — What Tier 2 Unlocks
All Tier 1 content, plus:

**Full Phase 3 and Phase 4 Bundle Library**
- Access to all published Phase 3 bundles (5 bundles, current and future)
- Access to all published Phase 4 botanical ID guides (18 guides)
- Access to Phase 4 therapeutic bundles as published (Nervine, Inflammation, Adrenal/Metabolic, Skin/Lymphatic, Circulatory — per production schedule in PHASE_4_BOTANICAL_DIVERSIFICATION_STRATEGY.md)

**Drug Interaction and Contraindication Database**
- Searchable by herb name, drug class, and clinical condition
- Updated quarterly with new research findings
- Formatted for clinical distribution (FTC-compliant language, evidence-tier citations)
- Covers all Phase 3 and Phase 4 bundle species plus high-interaction herbs (St. John's Wort CYP450 interactions, licorice root pseudohyperaldosteronism, kava hepatotoxicity)
- Drug classes covered: SSRIs/SNRIs, anticoagulants (warfarin and NOACs), immunosuppressants, thyroid medications, cardiovascular medications, diuretics, oral contraceptives
- Source: Natural Medicines Database (institutional-grade summaries abstracted into practitioner-usable format); Drug-Herb Interactions (Stargrove, Treasure, McKee, 2nd ed.)

**Monograph Library (Phase 4 International Traditions — Tier 2 access level)**
- Commission E monograph summaries for all Phase 3 and Phase 4 species with Commission E coverage
- WHO Traditional Medicine monographs (accessible volumes, abstracted)
- British Herbal Pharmacopoeia summary entries
- European Scientific Cooperative on Phytotherapy (ESCOP) monograph summaries
- NOT included at Tier 2: full Ayurvedic Materia Medica mapping (Tier 3); TCM organ system clinical protocols (Tier 3); raw dosing tables for ND-level prescribing (Tier 3)

**Clinical Case Study Library**
- Anonymized case study summaries (2–4 paragraphs each) from Phase 3 and Phase 4 peer-reviewer network (see PEER_REVIEWER_RECRUITMENT_STRATEGY.md)
- 5–8 cases per bundle theme at Tier 2 launch; expanding quarterly
- Format: Patient presentation → clinical reasoning → herb selection → outcome → practitioner notes
- Not original Seedwarden case studies — curated from published AHG Herbalist Quarterly cases, peer-reviewed literature, and Phase 3 peer-reviewer contributions

**International Traditions Cross-Reference (Tier 2 partial access)**
- Phase 3 herbs mapped to their European Commission E indication
- Phase 3 herbs mapped to Ayurvedic traditional use (dosha framing, Sanskrit name)
- Phase 3 herbs mapped to TCM energetics (warm/cool, organ affinity) at the introductory level
- Full cross-reference system (deep-dive sourcing, clinical indications, contraindication mapping across traditions) is Tier 3

### Credential Verification Process — Tier 2

**Step 1 — Subscriber application**: Tier 2 subscription sign-up form (Gumroad or Kit form) captures:
- Full name
- Credential claimed (dropdown: AHG RH, NAHA Level 5, Herbal Academy Advanced, Licensed Practitioner with CE, International equivalent)
- Credential identifier if applicable (AHG RH application number, NAHA member number, Herbal Academy graduate year)
- Optional: public directory listing URL, school attended

**Step 2 — Directory verification**:
- AHG RH: Cross-check against AHG public member directory (directory.americanherbalistsguild.com — filterable by RH designation)
- NAHA Professional Level 5: Cross-check NAHA member directory (naha.org/find-a-practitioner); confirm Level 5 membership status
- Herbal Academy: Email verification to the applicant's program completion email; forward Herbal Academy certificate image
- Licensed practitioners: State license board lookup (AANP state licensing pages; individual state pharmacy/medical boards); CE documentation review (PDF of CE completion certificates)
- International: Case-by-case review against NIMH member directory (nimh.org.uk/find-a-herbalist) or equivalent national body

**Step 3 — Verification timeline**: 2–5 business days from application submission to access grant. During verification window, applicant receives a Tier 1 welcome package.

**Step 4 — Approval/decline communication**:
- Approved: Access code or Gumroad subscription link activated; onboarding email sequence begins (Tier 2 content orientation, database access instructions)
- Declined (insufficient evidence): Response with specific guidance ("Your AHG directory listing was not found under the name provided — please resubmit with your AHG member number")
- Appeals: Manual review by operator within 5 business days

**Verification at scale (>200 Tier 2 subscribers)**: Manual lookup becomes impractical beyond approximately 200 verifications per quarter. At that scale, automated solutions should be evaluated:
- AHG does not currently offer an API for directory lookup; this requires a manual verification step or a formal partnership arrangement
- Potential solution: Self-attestation with periodic random audit (10% of subscribers re-verified annually) and a community trust model; misrepresentation clause in terms of service
- Automated solution evaluation: Verosint (professional credential verification API, healthcare focus); Certif-ID (credential verification SaaS); both are overkill at current scale but noted for Phase 5

### Tier 2 Pricing

**Monthly subscription**: $18/month (Gumroad membership or Kit Commerce recurring)
**Annual subscription**: $165/year ($13.75/month effective — 24% savings vs. monthly)
**Promotional pricing for AHG Symposium window** (August 14–16, 2026): $125/year for annual sign-ups during the symposium week — the single highest-conversion window for RH-level practitioners

**Revenue model**:
- At 50 Tier 2 monthly subscribers: $900/month recurring
- At 100 Tier 2 subscribers (mix of monthly and annual): ~$1,500–1,700/month effective
- At 200 Tier 2 subscribers: ~$3,000–3,400/month effective
- Target: 75–100 Tier 2 subscribers by end of 2026

**Platform comparison**:
- Gumroad membership: 10% fee ($1.80/month on $18 subscription; $1.65/month on $165 annual). Simple to set up. Minimal friction for content delivery (PDF access by membership level). Limitations: no email sequencing built in; requires Kit integration for onboarding emails.
- Kit Commerce: 3.5% fee (lower) + $0.30 transaction fee at Creator Pro plan ($29/month). Better email integration. More complex setup. Recommended if Kit is already the primary email platform.
- Recommendation: Start with Gumroad for simplicity at launch; migrate to Kit Commerce once subscriber base exceeds 50 and email sequencing complexity increases.

## Tier 3 — Clinical Specialist (ND/PhD/CNS)

### Who This Is For
Licensed or credentialed clinical practitioners with prescribing authority or research roles:
- Licensed Naturopathic Doctors (ND) in regulated states (26 jurisdictions as of 2026, per AANP)
- Medical Doctors (MD/DO) with documented integrative medicine focus
- Registered Dietitians with Certified Nutrition Specialist (CNS) credential (Board for Certification of Nutrition Specialists)
- PhD researchers in botanical medicine, pharmacognosy, ethnobotany, or related fields (academic institution affiliation required)
- Advanced clinical herbalists: FNIMH (UK); DAc or equivalent in Traditional Chinese Medicine for the TCM module; Vaidya (AV) through NAMA for the Ayurvedic module
- Clinical Herbalists with 10+ years documented clinical practice and a published clinical or academic contribution (peer-reviewed article, textbook chapter, AHG Herbalist Quarterly publication): discretionary approval by operator

**Estimated addressable audience**: 7,500 licensed NDs in the US; 500–1,000 MDs with documented integrative focus; 2,000–3,000 CNS credential holders; 3,000–5,000 botanical medicine researchers globally. Realistically reachable through Phase 3 practitioner outreach: 50–200 in first 12 months.

### Content Access — What Tier 3 Unlocks
All Tier 1 and Tier 2 content, plus:

**Full International Traditions Library** (complete access)
- Commission E monographs: full text of English translation (where copyright-clear; Blumenthal edition abstracts for others)
- WHO Traditional Medicine Monographs (WHO-TRM): 28-volume series covering 130+ medicinal plants; all volumes in WHO's public access collection provided as organized, species-indexed reference
- Ayurvedic Materia Medica: Full dosha pairing, Rasa (taste), Guna (quality), Virya (potency), Vipaka (post-digestive effect) per Phase 3 and Phase 4 species with Ayurvedic counterpart; Charaka Samhita and Bhava Prakasha textual citations
- TCM full clinical module: Pinyin name, traditional channels, TCM energetics, organ system association, classical formulas each Phase 3 and Phase 4 species appears in; sourced from Bensky and Gamble *Chinese Herbal Medicine Materia Medica* and Huang Huang's *Ten Key Formula Families in Chinese Medicine*

**Dosing Protocol Documentation**
- Evidence-based dosing ranges compiled from Natural Medicines Database, Commission E, ESCOP, and clinical trial data
- Formatted for ND-level prescribing reference; includes dose-response considerations, preparation-type variations (tincture, standardized extract, bulk herb tea), and pediatric/geriatric adjustments where data exist
- IMPORTANT: These are reference documents, not prescribing recommendations. Each document carries a clear header: "This reference summarizes published dosing data for licensed clinical practitioners. It does not constitute medical advice or prescribing guidance."

**Research Database Access**
- Curated PubMed search strategies per Phase 3 and Phase 4 species (search strings + date ranges)
- Abstracts of key clinical trials (RCTs, systematic reviews, meta-analyses) with evidence-tier rating and plain-language summary
- Updates: quarterly new literature digest per bundle theme
- Herbalgram article archive links (American Botanical Council members-only content links for ABC member practitioners; Seedwarden provides the curation layer, not the paywall bypass)

**Limited Content Publishing Rights**
- Tier 3 subscribers may republish up to 1,500 words per calendar quarter from any Seedwarden guide in:
  - Their own professional newsletter or blog
  - Their clinic's patient education materials
  - Handouts distributed at professional conferences (AHG Symposium, AANP, NABNE, integrative medicine CME events)
- Attribution required: "Adapted from Seedwarden [Guide Name], [Year], seedwarden.com"
- May NOT republish: entire guides; dosing protocol documents; drug interaction database entries (liability)
- May submit corrections, updates, or clinical observations to the Seedwarden practitioner review queue — accepted submissions acknowledged and incorporated into guide updates with contributor credit

**Practitioner Peer Review Network Access**
- Tier 3 subscribers may volunteer as peer reviewers for new Phase 4 guide content
- Compensation: 20% discount on annual subscription renewal per accepted review (a $110 credit on the $550 annual rate)
- Peer review protocol: 2-hour commitment per guide; standardized review form covering botanical accuracy, clinical accuracy, FTC compliance, and sourcing ethics; feedback returned within 14 days

### Credential Verification Process — Tier 3

More rigorous than Tier 2 due to higher content access and publishing rights.

**Step 1 — Application**: Same form as Tier 2 with additional fields:
- State of licensure (for NDs and MDs)
- License number
- Institutional affiliation (for PhD/academic applicants)
- For "10+ year clinical herbalist" path: publication citation or CV

**Step 2 — Verification**:
- ND: State naturopathic board license lookup. AANP provides a state-by-state licensing board directory. Direct URL lookup available for Oregon (oregon.gov/obne), Washington (doh.wa.gov), California (baha.ca.gov), and all other regulated states.
- MD/DO: State medical board license verification (Docinfo.org, AMA DoctorFinder)
- CNS: BCNS credential verification at theacns.org (Board for Certification of Nutrition Specialists — public practitioner lookup)
- PhD: Institutional email confirmation; academic profile URL (Google Scholar, university faculty page)
- FNIMH/MNIMH: NIMH member directory lookup (nimh.org.uk/find-a-herbalist)
- AV (Ayurvedic Vaidya): NAMACB credential directory (namacb.org)
- Discretionary "10+ year clinical herbalist": Operator review of submitted CV + publication evidence; 5–7 day review window

**Step 3 — Timeline**: 5–10 business days. More complex cases (international credentials, discretionary path) may require up to 14 days.

### Tier 3 Pricing

**Monthly**: $55/month
**Annual**: $550/year ($45.83/month effective — 17% savings)
**Academic/nonprofit rate**: $35/month or $350/year (for PhD researchers at academic institutions or nonprofit clinic practitioners; requires institutional email verification)

**Revenue model**:
- At 20 Tier 3 monthly subscribers: $1,100/month recurring
- At 50 Tier 3 subscribers (mix): ~$2,500–2,800/month effective
- Target: 25–40 Tier 3 subscribers by end of 2026

**Launch strategy**: Tier 3 does not launch August 1 — the international traditions database (primary Tier 3 differentiator) is not assembled until October 1 per the Phase 4 International Traditions Sourcing roadmap. Tier 2 subscribers who meet Tier 3 credential standards are offered a Tier 3 early-access waitlist at the August 1 Tier 2 launch, with October 1 activation.

## Competitive Benchmarking

**Herbalists Without Borders premium content**:
- Model: Donation-based; no formal subscription tier
- Gap: No credential-gated content; no clinical depth differentiation
- Seedwarden advantage: Structured credential pathway with real content differentiation between tiers

**Gaia Herbs Practitioner Portal** (gaiapractitioner.com):
- Model: Free access for licensed practitioners (ND, MD, RD, ARNP, DC, LAc); requires license number verification
- Content: Professional pricing discounts, product certificates of analysis, monograph library
- Gap: Supplement-sale-oriented; not guide and education focused; no original clinical content
- Seedwarden advantage: Original guide content, botanical ID guides, international traditions cross-reference — Gaia's portal is a procurement tool, not an education platform

**Skillshare / Teachable (RH herbalism courses)**:
- Model: Course-based, one-time or annual platform subscription
- Typical RH-level courses: $97–$297 one-time per course
- Gap: Not credential-verified; not a subscription with ongoing content; not clinically focused (most herbalism courses on Skillshare are beginner-to-intermediate)
- Seedwarden advantage: Credential verification creates defensible moat; subscription model provides ongoing content rather than a static course

**Natural Medicines Database** (naturalmedicines.therapeuticresearch.com):
- Model: Institutional subscription ($950+/year for institutional; individual practitioner pricing ~$200–300/year)
- Content: Comprehensive drug-herb interaction database; dosing; efficacy ratings; safety
- Gap: Very expensive; general database, not botanical-herbalist-practitioner focused; no cultivation, foraging, or sourcing content
- Seedwarden advantage: Seedwarden is not trying to replace Natural Medicines Database — Tier 2/3 content cites Natural Medicines Database as a source and provides an abstracted, practitioner-digestible layer of its content in the herbalism tradition rather than the pharmaceutical reference tradition
