## Document 1 — Job Description

### Seedwarden — Product Photographer (Botanical Flat-Lay and Lifestyle)

**Project**: Seedwarden is a digital educational guide series covering medicinal herbs, native plants, and wild edibles. We are producing three guides for the June–August 2026 season: Respiratory Health, Immunity Support, and Digestive Support. Each guide sells as a digital PDF on Etsy.

**Role**: We are looking for a product photographer to shoot flat-lay and lifestyle images for each guide's Etsy listing. This is not a nature or landscape photography role — it is product photography using dried herbs, growing plants, and simple prop styling. Think cookbook flat-lay or cosmetics product photography, applied to medicinal herbs.

**What you will photograph**:

For each of the three bundles, we need 5 edited images:

1. Top-down flat-lay: Guide cover printout (mockup) centered on a natural linen or wood surface, surrounded by 3–5 herb specimens from the bundle. Background is clean and uncluttered. This is Etsy listing Slot 1 (the main listing image that appears in search results — most critical shot).
2. 45-degree angle flat-lay: Same arrangement as Shot 1, tilted to show page depth.
3. Lifestyle Shot A: Guide open to an interior page, on a work table with a mortar and pestle, an herb bundle, or a small jar of dried herbs. The guide is the subject, not a person.
4. Lifestyle Shot B: Guide beside a tea preparation or tincture setup — simple, warm, herbalist aesthetic.
5. Close-up detail: A single herb specimen from the bundle in sharp foreground focus; guide softly visible in background.

Total: 15 images across 3 shoots (one shoot per bundle, ~5 images per shoot).

**Timeline**:

- Shoot 1 (Respiratory bundle): July 1–5, 2026
- Shoot 2 (Immunity bundle): July 8–12, 2026
- Shoot 3 (Digestive bundle): July 20–24, 2026
- Edited image delivery: within 3 business days of each shoot

**Compensation**:

Flat rate per shoot session. Each session is a half-day (3–4 hours active shooting). Budget range: $150–$350 per session, depending on experience and equipment. Total engagement for 3 sessions: $450–$1,050.

A 25% deposit (first session) is paid on contract signing. Remaining balance per session is paid within 24 hours of each image delivery.

**What we provide**:

- All herb specimens (dried and/or fresh) for each shoot
- All props: mortar and pestle, glass apothecary jars, dried herb bundles, natural linen or wood surface
- Guide cover mockup (printed at full color and folded to simulate the final guide)
- Shot list for each bundle before each shoot
- Style reference images showing the aesthetic we are aiming for

**What you provide**:

- Camera body and lenses capable of delivering minimum 2000×2000px JPEG output
- Lighting: natural window light (highly preferred) or soft-box setup. No harsh on-camera flash
- Your time and editing (white balance correction, exposure adjustment, crop)
- Commercial use license on all delivered images (unlimited Etsy and digital product distribution)

**Location**: [Your city/region] or your home studio if you have the right light setup. We can bring all props and specimens to your location.

**What we are NOT looking for**: Portrait photographers, event photographers, nature photographers who do not have product experience, photographers who require a studio rental that adds to cost.

**To apply**: Reply with (1) a portfolio link showing flat-lay or product photography work, and (2) your rate for a half-day product shoot session with 5 edited deliverables. Applications without portfolio links will not be reviewed.

### Seedwarden Phase 3 — Photography Technical Specifications

Thank you for your interest in the Seedwarden photography project. Before submitting a bid, please review these technical requirements to confirm your equipment and workflow can meet them.

**Resolution and Format**

- Minimum delivered resolution: 2000×2000px (square crop) or 2000×1333px (landscape crop)
- Etsy requires the shortest side to be at least 2000px. Square (1:1) format is preferred for Slots 1–3.
- File format: JPEG at maximum quality (80%+ quality setting). Not WebP, not TIFF.
- File size: under 10MB per image (Etsy upload limit).

**Color and Lighting**

- Color space: sRGB (not Adobe RGB, not CMYK). If you shoot in a wider color space, export to sRGB for delivery.
- White balance: Daylight (5500K) or slightly warm (5800–6200K). Not auto-white-balance — consistency across the 5-image set for each session is required.
- No heavy presets, HDR processing, or vintage filters. Clean, true-color output.
- Preferred light: natural diffuse window light (north- or east-facing, or overcast outdoor) or equivalent soft-box. No harsh directional flash.

**Composition Requirements**

- Top-down flat-lay shots (Shots 1 and 2): Camera must be positioned directly overhead. A tripod is required for this setup.
- All shots: Primary subject (guide cover or herb specimen) must be in sharp focus. Background elements can be slightly soft but not motion-blurred.
- Background: clean, uncluttered. No distracting objects in frame. Natural fiber surfaces (linen, canvas) or light wood. No dark or patterned backgrounds unless specifically requested.

**Editing Expectations**

Light editing only:
- White balance correction (must be consistent across the session)
- Exposure adjustment (no blown highlights on the white/cream surface or the guide cover)
- Crop to correct aspect ratio
- Minor dust removal from surface if visible

Not included:
- Composite edits, object removal, or heavy retouching
- Color grading beyond white balance correction

**Delivery**

- Deliver to a shared Google Drive folder (link provided on booking confirmation)
- Folder name per bundle: `respiratory/`, `immunity/`, `digestive/`
- Filename convention: `[bundle-slug]-[image-type]-[sequence].jpg`
  - Example: `respiratory-flatlay-01.jpg`, `immunity-lifestyle-02.jpg`
- Turnaround: 3 business days from shoot date

**Confirming your equipment meets these specs**

When submitting your bid, please confirm:
- [ ] Camera sensor can deliver minimum 2000px on the short side (virtually all DSLR and mirrorless cameras; most high-end smartphones)
- [ ] You have a tripod for overhead flat-lay shots
- [ ] You can deliver JPEG at 80%+ quality in sRGB color space
- [ ] Your standard editing turnaround is 3 business days or less

## Document 5 — Q&A Template for Contractor Outreach

**Prepare answers to these common questions before any candidate asks. Reference during discovery calls.**

**Q: What kind of plants are we photographing?**
A: Dried medicinal herbs for each bundle — Elderberry, Mullein, and Echinacea for the Respiratory shoot; Echinacea, Ashwagandha, Elderberry, and Goldenseal for Immunity; Dandelion, Calendula, Lemon Balm, and Ginger for Digestive. Some specimens will be dried bundles, some will be fresh-cut. We provide all specimens — you do not need to source or purchase anything.

**Q: Do we need a studio? Can you come to me?**
A: We prefer to bring everything to your location if you have good natural window light. We carry all props: the herb specimens, the guide printout mockup, mortar and pestle, jars, and the surface. If you do not have a suitable light setup, we can host at our location. We are flexible.

**Q: What is the "guide mockup"?**
A: Each Seedwarden guide is a digital PDF. For the flat-lay photos, we print one page of the guide in full color and fold it to look like a bound booklet. This is what appears in the center of the flat-lay — it is the product being photographed. You are not photographing an actual printed book; you are photographing a printed mockup that simulates one. It looks like a thin saddle-stitched booklet.

**Q: Do you need video as well?**
A: Not for Phase 3. Photos only. If we expand to video content later in 2026, we will discuss that as a separate engagement.

**Q: What if a herb specimen wilts or does not photograph well on the day?**
A: We will have backup specimens for each herb. If a fresh specimen wilts, we substitute with a dried equivalent. We have researched sourcing for all Phase 3 species and have fallback options in stock.

**Q: Can I use these images for my portfolio?**
A: Yes, with two conditions: (1) you may not post them publicly until the Etsy listing is live (we will notify you when that happens), and (2) you credit them as "Product photography — Seedwarden digital guides." This is standard for commercial photography.

**Q: What if I am not available for one of the three sessions?**
A: We need all three sessions covered, but not necessarily by the same photographer. If you can commit to two sessions, we can discuss whether we source a second photographer for the third, or whether one session shifts in date. Let us know your availability for each date before we finalize.

**Q: Is there potential for ongoing work?**
A: Yes. Seedwarden plans a Phase 4 expansion in late 2026 covering additional herb categories. If Phase 3 photography goes well, you would be the first call for Phase 4. There is no guarantee, but the project is ongoing and expanding.
