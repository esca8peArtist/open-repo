# Phase 2 Production Timeline — Detailed Logistics

**Prepared**: May 7, 2026  
**Launch**: May 30, 2026 (23 days remaining)  
**Fallback launch**: June 7, 2026 (31 days — no revenue impact; see Section 5)  
**Wave 1 species**: American Ginseng, Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, Ramps, Wild Bergamot

This document extends `phase-2-production-execution-plan.md` with production-level detail on four topics not yet resolved to deadline specificity: plant sourcing order cutoffs, photography shoot scheduling, guide production critical path in Canva, and contingency decision logic. A companion logistics spreadsheet (`PHASE_2_SOURCING_LOGISTICS.csv`) covers per-species order details, supplier contacts, quantities, and cost totals.

The six-species scope reflects the task brief's Wave 1 definition. Wild Bergamot (*Monarda fistulosa*) is added to the original four Appalachian Medicinals because it carries lower photography risk (abundant iNaturalist coverage, no spring-timing constraint) and serves as the accessible entry-point guide for buyers new to the conservation content tier.

## Section 1: Plant Sourcing Timeline

### The Sourcing Constraint

Live plant material for guide photography is not achievable from seed at this stage — all six Wave 1 species require 12–24 months from seed to a photographable plant. Sourcing serves two narrower goals:

1. **Prop material for the May 10–11 shoot**: Seeds, dried roots, and any available bulblets are physical props for hero flat-lay shots and Etsy listing images. These must arrive by May 9.
2. **Demonstration specimens for the Canva guides**: High-quality root/rhizome close-up photography for identification sections. Dried commercial specimens (purchased at retail from herb suppliers) are acceptable and available same-week.

No live propagation timeline is required for the May 30 launch. This also means there is no cash outlay for live plants — only small prop seed orders and retail herb purchases.

### Per-Species Order Deadlines

All prop seed orders must be placed by **May 8** (Thursday) to meet a May 9–15 delivery window. All retail herb specimen purchases should be completed by **May 9** (Friday) — same-day local purchase preferred; Mountain Rose Herbs ships 2-day if local stores lack stock.

**American Ginseng** (*Panax quinquefolius*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–15  
Spring seed availability is critically limited (ginseng is a fall crop). Check Strictly Medicinal Seeds and Prairie Moon Nursery on May 7 before placing any order. If unavailable from commercial vendors, skip prop seeds entirely — a dried ginseng root purchased at retail (Whole Foods, herb shop, or Mountain Rose Herbs online at ~$12–20) is the superior photography prop in any case. The root communicates identification value; seeds alone do not. Cost: $12–20.

**Goldenseal** (*Hydrastis canadensis*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–15  
Spring availability confirmed from cultivated sources. Strictly Medicinal Seeds: $20.00 for 20 certified organic seeds. Prairie Moon Nursery is secondary. Johnny's Selected Seeds offers organic goldenseal root, which is more instructive for identification photography than seeds. Order the root in addition to or instead of seeds if timing allows delivery by May 10. Cost: $20–35.

**Black Cohosh** (*Actaea racemosa*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–14  
Spring-available from multiple cultivated sources. Strictly Medicinal Seeds primary. Prairie Moon Nursery and Fedco Seeds as secondaries. Strictly Medicinal also offers potted live plants shipped mid-March through July — if seeds are out, a small potted plant (~$8–12) is a photographic upgrade and still arrives in the window. Cost: $6–15.

**Bloodroot** (*Sanguinaria canadensis*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–15  
Spring ephemeral — seeds are viable for only 1 week post-collection; commercial spring seed availability is narrow. Prairie Moon Nursery and Strictly Medicinal Seeds carry seeds intermittently. Check both. Bloodroot rhizomes (the orange-red root that gives the plant its name) are the stronger identification prop — check Prairie Moon for bare-root or potted spring availability. If rhizomes are unavailable, a BHL botanical illustration (Sanguinaria canadensis appears in multiple 19th-century herbal plates) substitutes cleanly. Cost: $8–20 if rhizomes available; $0 if BHL illustration fallback.

**Ramps / Wild Leek** (*Allium tricoccum*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–10  
EFN ramps seeds are currently sold out. Check EFN daily (store.experimentalfarmnetwork.org). If restocked, 2–3 day shipping applies ($4.25/packet). Prairie Moon Nursery bulblets are the fallback — confirm spring availability. Etsy "ramps bulbs" or "wild leek bulbs" from small Appalachian native plant sellers is the tertiary path (3–7 day ship). Fresh ramp leaves are available at specialty grocery stores and farmers markets in May — purchase a small bunch for leaf close-up photography ($3–8). This is the highest-value ramps prop because readers recognize leaves immediately. Cost: $4–12.

**Wild Bergamot** (*Monarda fistulosa*)  
Order deadline: May 8 | Expected delivery: May 9–15  
Widely available from multiple nurseries year-round. Strictly Medicinal Seeds, Prairie Moon Nursery, Johnny's Selected Seeds, and Fedco all carry it. Seeds: $3–6. No spring availability risk. A potted flowering Monarda is typically available at local garden centers and hardware stores in May (Home Depot, Lowe's, independent nurseries) — this gives a live plant prop for the shoot at minimal cost ($4–8). Cost: $3–8.

## Section 2: Photography Logistics

### Location Strategy

No external location booking is required. The May 10–11 shoot is a studio-style prop shoot, not a field shoot. Primary locations:

- **Cluster A (seeds and plant props)**: East- or south-facing window, 9am–2pm natural light. Any home or apartment with 3+ hours of unobstructed morning light qualifies. An overcast day is preferable for flat-lay shots — diffuse light eliminates harsh shadows without additional equipment.
- **Cluster B (Appalachian context shots)**: Kitchen counter or wooden table surface. Afternoon light acceptable.
- **Cluster C (heritage illustration compositions)**: Any well-lit flat surface for botanical illustration printouts alongside prop materials.

If an outdoor garden or porch is available for Cluster A, use it on an overcast day. Direct outdoor sunlight creates unflattering specular highlights on dried seeds and roots.

### Shoot Dates and Hard Constraints

**Primary window**: May 10 (Saturday) and May 11 (Sunday).

May 10 is the earliest viable shoot date because:
1. Prop seed orders placed May 8 may not deliver until May 9–10.
2. Botanical illustration printouts need a print shop run by May 8, pickup May 9.
3. May 10–11 still allows 19 days of processing, guide production, and platform upload before May 30.

**Backup window**: May 17–18 (one-week slip). This pushes photo delivery to May 22 and guide production lock to May 26, compressing Canva work by 6 days but keeping May 30 viable with focused sessions. See Section 5, Contingency Path A.

**Photography must complete before**: Canva guide production begins in earnest. Etsy listing hero images come from the shoot; these cannot be uploaded until editing is complete.

### Shot List — Six Species, 30-Shot Minimum

Total target: 30 final edited compositions, producing both guide interior images and Etsy listing photos.

**Per species (5 shots each x 6 species = 30 shots)**:
1. Full plant / habitat view (sourced from iNaturalist CC-BY — cannot be created in-studio)
2. Leaf detail (iNaturalist CC-BY or in-studio prop if live plant available)
3. Flower or fruit (iNaturalist CC-BY — most species not in bloom in May; iNat archive is the source)
4. Root or rhizome close-up (in-studio prop: dried ginseng root, goldenseal root, bloodroot rhizome, Monarda root division)
5. Seed or seed pod close-up (in-studio prop: seeds on dark wood surface, loose or in small kraft envelope)

**Shoot-day additions (5–10 supplemental shots for Etsy listings and bundle cover)**:
- 3 hero flat-lay shots: "all-species" arrangement of prop materials on dark wood surface
- 2–3 "hands-on" contextual shots: hand holding a dried root, hand spreading seeds, page of botanical illustration with pencil annotation

Total RAW captures: 50–65. Cull to 30 selects by end of May 12.

## Section 3: Guide Production Critical Path — Canva

### When Must Photos Be Ready for Canva?

Photos must be available for Canva guide production by **May 15**. This is the zero-float date: Canva work requires 10 working days (May 15–28) to complete all six guide PDFs, conduct internal review, incorporate any corrections, and export final files by May 29 for Etsy upload on May 30.

If photos are not available until May 22 (backup shoot scenario), Canva work is compressed to 6 working days (May 22–28). This is achievable if guide structure is templated in advance (Zones 5–6 master template built May 15–17 without final photos, then photos inserted May 22–23). See Contingency Path A.

### Template Sequence: Zone Master First

The Canva production sequence follows the existing zone-card workflow from `phase-2-execution-timeline.md` but applies the same logic to endangered species guides. Build order:

**Week 1 (May 15–21)**: Build master guide template on American Ginseng guide (the anchor species and highest-research-depth guide). This guide establishes: typography, color palette, section header style, photo placement grid, conservation status badge treatment, attribution footer format, and bundle-compatible cover design. Time budget: 5–7 hours for the master.

**Week 2 (May 22–26)**: Duplicate master to Goldenseal, Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, and Ramps — substituting species-specific content, photos, and conservation status data. Time per guide: 2–3 hours after the master pattern is established.

**May 26–27**: Wild Bergamot guide (simplest conservation story, lowest photo-dependency risk). Time: 1.5–2 hours.

**May 27**: Bundle listing design — cover image for the 6-guide Appalachian Medicinals bundle Etsy listing. This is one composite image, not a guide document. Time: 45 minutes.

**May 27–28**: Full-set review pass. Check: all species name spellings correct, all iNaturalist photo attributions present, all BHL illustration credits present (or note public domain status), no placeholder text remaining, all footer URLs point to live Etsy listings and Kit landing page. Time: 1.5 hours.

**May 29**: PDF export (print quality) for all 6 guides + bundle cover. Upload to Etsy draft listings. This is the production lock date.

### Review Cycle and Lock Dates

Three review cycles are built into the schedule:

1. **Internal review** — May 20: Review Ginseng guide draft with fresh eyes after 48 hours. Check factual accuracy against `endangered-species-candidate-list.md` sources. Check that conservation status language is current (CITES Appendix II citations, UpS At-Risk roster, state-level data). Time: 45 minutes.

2. **Plant expert spot-check** — May 22: Email the draft Ginseng and Goldenseal guides to one trusted botanist, herbalist, or native plant society contact for a factual accuracy read. This is not a full peer review — ask specifically: "Does the conservation status section contain any errors? Any cultivation guidance that would raise concerns?" Response window: 48 hours (May 24). This gates Canva production of the remaining four guides. If no response by May 24, proceed anyway — the source citations in `endangered-species-candidate-list.md` are reliable.

3. **Legal review** — embedded, not a separate cycle: The guides do not give medical advice (they describe traditional use and ethnobotanical history, not therapeutic recommendations), do not instruct collection from regulated wild populations, and do not make commercial claims about plant compounds. A standard disclaimer ("This guide is for educational purposes. Consult a qualified practitioner before using any plant medicinally.") is added to every guide. No external legal review is required.

**Final design lock**: **May 28**. No content changes after this date. Any corrections surface during the May 27–28 review must be logged for a post-launch update (June 7 earliest), not addressed in-line.

**Etsy upload date**: May 29. All 6 guide PDFs + bundle cover uploaded to staged draft listings by end of day May 29.

### Critical Path Issues — "Uh Oh" Flags

**Flag 1 — Ginseng prop seeds (HIGH risk)**: Ginseng is a fall crop. If no spring seed source is found by May 7 EOD, this line item drops from the shoot. The guide can be produced without live seed props — dried root is a superior photo prop anyway. This is a degraded-props risk, not a launch risk.

**Flag 2 — Ramps EFN sold out (MEDIUM risk)**: If neither EFN nor Prairie Moon has ramp bulblets available by May 8, and local grocery ramp leaves are also unavailable (they are seasonal, typically available April–early May), the ramps guide relies entirely on iNaturalist CC-BY archive photos for leaf imagery and BHL illustrations for botanical plates. This is fully adequate for guide production. Prop photography for the Etsy listing hero image is the only gap — substitute with a ramps close-up from iNaturalist CC-BY printed at 11x17 as a flat-lay backdrop element.

**Flag 3 — Photo shoot slips to May 17–18 (MEDIUM risk)**: Compresses Canva production from 13 working days to 7 working days. Recoverable but requires focused execution — see Contingency Path A.

**Flag 4 — Canva guide production runs over time estimate (LOW risk)**: The ginseng master template is the only non-duplicable document. If the master takes 10 hours instead of 6, the remaining 5 guides can still be completed on a compressed schedule (2–3 hours each). Float: 2 days before design lock.

**Flag 5 — Expert spot-check reveals factual error (LOW risk)**: If a significant error is found after May 24, correct it in Ginseng and Goldenseal only; apply the same correction to remaining guides as they are produced. This does not affect the launch date unless the error requires a complete section rewrite.

**Flag 6 — Etsy upload on May 29 shows listing format issue (LOW risk)**: Etsy PDF delivery via a third-party platform (Payhip, Etsy's own digital delivery, or SendOwl) needs to be configured before upload. If this platform is not yet set up, digital delivery fails at purchase. Confirm the digital download mechanism is live and tested by May 25. See `PHASE_2_LAUNCH_PREP.md` for current setup status.

## Section 5: Contingency Paths

### Contingency Path A: Photo Location Scouting Slips 1 Week (Shoot Moves May 17–18)

**Trigger**: May 10–11 shoot is unavailable due to weather, equipment issue, or prop delivery failure.

**Recovery sequence**:
- May 15–16: Build Canva Ginseng master template structure (typography, layout, section headers) using placeholder images from BHL and iNaturalist CC-BY archive. This is fully produceable without May 10–11 shoot photos because the master template does not require the shoot's in-studio prop shots — it uses digital photo sources.
- May 17–18: Shoot executes as originally planned.
- May 19–20: Image processing (culling and retouching on accelerated timeline — 2 days, not 3).
- May 21: Photos available. Insert into Ginseng master template. Trigger full Canva production sequence.
- May 21–27: Canva production compressed to 7 days (was 13). Execute with 3-hour daily sessions.
- May 28: Design lock (same date as primary path).
- May 29: Export and upload (same date as primary path).
- **May 30: Launch date holds.**

This path holds the May 30 date because the Canva master template can be built without the in-studio shoot photos, and the remaining 5 guides are all template duplications. The only guides that genuinely require in-studio prop photos are the Etsy listing hero images — and those can be produced and uploaded May 21–22 if needed, before the May 30 launch.

**What does not slip**: Kit email automation, social content scheduling, Etsy platform configuration, and coupon activation are all independent of photography and can complete on schedule regardless.

### Contingency Path B: Plant Orders Arrive Late — Substitute Commercial Plant Material

**Trigger**: Prop seeds or specimens do not arrive by May 10, or key species (ginseng, bloodroot) have no spring availability from any vendor.

**Recovery**: All six species have commercially available dried material through retail herbal suppliers. The following substitutions work without lead-time risk:

| Species | Prop Seed Fallback | Purchase Point |
|---|---|---|
| Ginseng | Dried whole root (~$15) | Whole Foods, herb shop, or Mountain Rose Herbs 2-day ship |
| Goldenseal | Dried root (~$12) | Same retail channels |
| Black Cohosh | Dried root pieces (~$10) | Mountain Rose Herbs, Starwest Botanicals |
| Bloodroot | BHL botanical illustration printout | Free, print from home |
| Ramps | Fresh leaves from grocery or farmers market | Seasonal; check farmers markets week of May 5 |
| Wild Bergamot | Live potted plant from garden center (~$6) | Home Depot, Lowe's, local nursery |

**Schedule impact**: None. Retail herbals are same-day or 2-day purchases. No shoot delay.

### Contingency Path C: Canva Production Falls Behind — Soft Launch May 30, Final June 7

**Trigger**: Canva guide production is on track for 3 guides by May 30 but not all 6.

**Minimum viable launch (May 30)**: Publish 2 guides (Ginseng + Goldenseal) on May 30. These are the anchor species and highest-demand titles. The bundle listing goes live as "2 of 6 guides — additional guides releasing through June 7." The launch email references this explicitly: "Wave 1 begins today with two flagship guides. Four more release through June 7."

**June 7 completion**: Black Cohosh, Bloodroot, Ramps, and Wild Bergamot guides published. Bundle listing updated to full 6-guide description. A follow-up Kit email ("All six guides now live — grab the bundle") goes to the launch email list on June 8. This drives a second revenue event 8 days after launch, which is a positive conversion pattern.

**Revenue impact**: Minimal. Buyers who purchased Ginseng + Goldenseal at launch may upgrade to the bundle on June 7 (price the individual guides at $18 each and the bundle at $32 — the bundle represents a $4 discount, sufficient incentive to return). No refunds, no complaints, no trust damage if the staged release is communicated honestly in the listing copy.

**Trigger decision point**: If fewer than 3 guides are export-ready by May 26, activate Contingency Path C. Do not attempt to launch 1 guide only — the minimum viable launch is 2 (Ginseng + Goldenseal). If only 1 guide is ready by May 28, slip to June 7 full launch.
