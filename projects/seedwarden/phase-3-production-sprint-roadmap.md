## Executive Summary

This roadmap converts the existing Phase 3 medicinal herbs content outline into a week-by-week production calendar. The outline already specifies 18,300 words across five bundles (Women's Health, Respiratory, Sleep, Immunity, Digestive). Adjusted for shared-species efficiency (seven species appear in two bundles, requiring roughly 40% of the first-occurrence writing time at second occurrence), actual production effort is 56–66 hours of writing plus 12.5 hours of Canva design plus approximately 10 hours of upload administration: 78–88 hours total.

The binding constraint is writing. Design and photography run in parallel and do not gate writing milestones. The critical path runs sequentially through Women's Health (most complex, 14–16 hours, zero float in Week 1) → Respiratory → Sleep → Immunity → Digestive.

The recommended writer model for a June 22 start and August 3 full launch is the **hybrid path**: user writes the narrative and clinical-judgment sections (approximately 30 hours), contractor drafts the technical constituent-chemistry and cultivation-specification sections (approximately 30 hours), producing a 4–5 week timeline at roughly $750 in contractor cost. This is developed in detail in `phase-3-writer-load-model.md`.

If the June 19 checkpoint yields a NO-GO, the entire roadmap slides by the slip duration. Every section below is written with the June 22 start assumption; adjust calendar dates by the slip delta.

## Section 3: Critical Path Analysis

### Primary Bottleneck: Women's Health Writing (Days 1–3)

Women's Health is the bottleneck for three reasons:

1. It has the earliest upload deadline (June 29) — any slip cascades immediately to Respiratory's July 6–7 target.
2. It contains the two most research-intensive species in Phase 3 (Black Cohosh at 700 words with conservation sidebar, Vitex at 600 words with invasive-watch note).
3. Day 3 (June 24) is the last point at which scope reduction (Option C: 3 bundles) can be activated without sacrificing the June 29 Women's Health upload.

If Women's Health clears Day 3 with 2,200+ words, the rest of the sprint has substantially more cushion. Respiratory (Days 4–7) is complex but has no conservation sidebars to slow the research phase. Immunity and Digestive accumulate efficiency from shared species.

### Can Sleep Run in Parallel?

Sleep can be started in parallel with Immunity (Days 12–14) only if a second writer handles one of them. For a solo writer, the sequence Women's Health → Respiratory → Immunity → Sleep → Digestive is strictly optimal because:
- Immunity benefits from Respiratory's Echinacea/Elderberry research (saves 3–4 hours)
- Sleep's Lavender section benefits from Women's Health Lavender (saves 1.5 hours)
- Digestive benefits from both Women's Health Calendula and Sleep Lemon Balm (saves 3 hours)

Writing Sleep before Immunity would save nothing on the Sleep side and would cost 3–4 hours on Immunity. Writing Digestive second would cost 4.5 hours by losing all shared-species savings. The recommended sequence is not arbitrary — it maximizes efficiency.

**If using a contractor**: The contractor can write Immunity in parallel while the user writes Sleep (Days 12–14), cutting the sprint to approximately 18 days from 22. This is the hybrid model's primary advantage over solo.

### Should Digestive Be Deferred to Phase 3b?

Digestive has the longest float (7–14 days) and the August 3 upload target. It is the most tempting bundle to defer. However, deferring it has a business cost: the Digestive bundle contains the forager cross-sell hook (dandelion root, the bridge from Phase 2 wild-edibles buyers to Phase 3 medicinal buyers). That cross-sell is time-sensitive — the forager cohort's engagement with Phase 2 content is highest in the first 30–60 days post-Track B launch. Deferring Digestive to a "Phase 3b" risks losing the cross-sell window. August 3 keeps it within the 60-day engagement window from a June 5–7 Track B launch.

**Recommendation**: Do not defer Digestive unless writing pace falls below 12 hours per bundle through Week 2. The bundle's adjusted hour total (9–11 hours) means it is the fastest bundle to write once Women's Health, Respiratory, and Sleep are done.

## Appendix A: FTC Language Quick Reference

All five bundles must use qualifying language for every therapeutic claim. This is not optional — missing qualifiers create product liability exposure.

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Black Cohosh relieves menopause symptoms" | "Traditionally used in Cherokee and Appalachian folk medicine for women's hormonal transitions" |
| "Elderberry prevents colds" | "Studied in randomized controlled trials for cold duration and severity outcomes" |
| "Valerian cures insomnia" | "Studied in clinical trials for sleep-related outcomes, with mixed results across studies" |
| "Goldenseal is antimicrobial" | "Contains berberine, an alkaloid studied in vitro and in clinical settings for antimicrobial activity" |
| "Ashwagandha reduces cortisol" | "Contains withanolides, compounds studied in randomized controlled trials for stress-related physiological markers and sleep quality outcomes" |
| "Passionflower treats anxiety" | "Contains flavonoids studied in randomized controlled trials for anxiety-related outcomes" |
| "Ginger stops nausea" | "Gingerols and shogaols in ginger have been studied in multiple randomized controlled trials for nausea associated with pregnancy, chemotherapy, and postoperative recovery" |

**Mandatory in every bundle**: Copyright and disclaimer page (identical language across all five), contraindications section per species (with FTC disclaimer header), sources section at back of guide.

**Mandatory individual species warnings** (cannot be omitted regardless of word count constraints):
- Valerian: CNS depressant / benzodiazepine / anesthetic interaction, avoid before driving
- Passionflower: MAOI contraindication, sedative interaction, avoid driving
- Lemon Balm: TSH binding inhibition, thyroid medication caution
- Ashwagandha: Thyroid medication interaction, pregnancy contraindication
- Goldenseal: Pregnancy contraindication (berberine uterotonic), cytochrome P450 drug interaction, CITES Appendix II sidebar
- Mullein leaf tea: Must be filtered through fine cloth before consumption
- Elderberry: Raw/green berry toxicity (sambunigrin)
- Dandelion: Bile duct obstruction / gallstone caution, potassium-sparing diuretic interaction
