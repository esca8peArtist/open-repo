# Phase 3 Contractor Onboarding Workflow

**Activate**: Immediately on first contractor hire (expected June 24–28, 2026)
**Applies to all tracks equally**: Photographer, Writer, Habitat Specialist — same 6-phase sequence

---

## Phase 1 — Response Evaluation (Day 0–2 post-outreach reply)

Run this checklist on every candidate response before scheduling a call.

### Portfolio Fit
- [ ] Work samples include the correct medium (flat-lay product photos / published botanical writing / habitat restoration reports)
- [ ] Style matches Seedwarden brand: warm, naturalist, practitioner-grade — not clinical-cold or lifestyle-vague
- [ ] At least 1 sample directly comparable to Phase 3 deliverables (herb flat-lay / contraindication content / species habitat notes)
- [ ] No red flags: stock-only portfolio, AI-generated samples, generic wellness writing without species specificity

### Availability Confirmation
- [ ] Explicitly confirms July 1 – August 1 availability (not "probably" or "let me check")
- [ ] No competing projects during the July 1–13 sprint core weeks
- [ ] Confirms 3-day turnaround on edits (photographers) / 8–10 hr/week writing pace / scoped review timeline (specialists)

### Budget Fit
- [ ] Photographer: quoted rate within $150–$350/session ($600–$1,050 total for 3 sessions)
- [ ] Writer (natural history/clinical): flat-rate within $1,000–$1,350 for 11,000-word scope
- [ ] Habitat Specialist: scoped contribution within $400–$900 flat
- [ ] Upwork candidates: confirm true cost = quoted rate + 5% platform fee

### Communication Style
- [ ] Replied within 24–48 hours of outreach
- [ ] Message is specific (references the project brief, asks clarifying questions) — not a generic template reply
- [ ] Professional tone; no aggressive rate-pushing on first contact
- [ ] Demonstrates familiarity with the subject matter

**Go/No-Go**: Any single hard fail (unavailable July, portfolio completely mismatched, over-budget with no flexibility) = polite decline. Two soft fails = decline. All pass = advance to Phase 2.

## Phase 2 — Offer and Negotiation (Day 2–4 post-evaluation)

### Offer Email Structure
Use templates from `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_COMMUNICATION_TEMPLATES.md` (Offer Template).
Every offer includes:
1. Flat rate (not hourly) with milestone payment schedule
2. Exact deliverables list and delivery dates
3. Work-for-hire / commercial license clause
4. Deposit amount and payment method

### Payment Schedule (standard for all contractor types)
| Milestone | % | Photographer trigger | Writer trigger | Specialist trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Contract signing | 25% | Signed + deposit sent | Signed + deposit sent | Signed + deposit sent |
| First deliverable | 25% | Session 1 photos approved | Respiratory bundle delivered | First scoped section delivered |
| Second deliverable | 25% | Session 2 photos approved | Immunity bundle delivered | Second scoped section delivered |
| Final handoff | 25% | Session 3 photos + reshoots clear | Digestive final draft + revisions | Final section + revision round clear |

### Rate Negotiation Rules
- If candidate quotes above ceiling: offer the ceiling with scope-expansion incentive ("If Phase 3 performs, we plan a fourth bundle in August at the same rate")
- If candidate quotes below floor: probe why before accepting — under-scoped estimates create delivery risk
- Never agree to hourly billing; always convert to flat-rate milestones
- Upwork candidates: add 5% to budget ceiling for platform fee; cap negotiation at $1,286 (writers) or $333/session (photographers) to stay within project budgets

### Negotiation Script (if candidate pushes back on rate)
"Our budget for this scope is fixed at [RATE]. We are not able to go above that for this contract. Given your portfolio fit, we would like to work with you — and we are planning follow-on work in August at the same rate structure. Can we make the numbers work within [RATE]?"

## Phase 3 — Onboarding Kit (Week 1, Days 1–5 of engagement)

Deliver all four components within 24 hours of contract signing.

### Component 1: Project Brief (all contractor types)
Send `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_PREBID_MATERIALS.md` and add:
- Seedwarden brand voice summary: "Practitioner-grade accuracy, warm naturalist tone, no wellness hype, species-specific detail"
- Bundle scope and upload schedule (from `PHASE_3_LAUNCH_CALENDAR.md`)
- FTC compliance note: no therapeutic claims without evidence-tier framing
- CITES sidebar note (writers/specialists): Goldenseal and Black Cohosh sections have mandatory verbatim conservation language — do not summarize or abbreviate

### Component 2: Brand Guidelines (photographers)
- Color palette: Phase 3 Canva hex codes from `PHASE_3_CANVA_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`
- Surface and lighting standard: natural linen or light marble, diffuse window light or soft-box only
- Shot types: top-down flat-lay, 45° flat-lay, lifestyle-in-use, close-up detail, inside-spread mockup
- Technical specs: 2000×2000px minimum, sRGB, JPEG 80%+, 3-day edit turnaround
- Naming convention: `[bundle-slug]-[image-type]-[sequence].jpg` (e.g., `respiratory-flatlay-01.jpg`)

### Component 3: Tool Access
| Tool | Photographer | Writer | Specialist |
|---|---|---|---|
| Canva Phase 3 brand kit (view link) | Yes | No | No |
| Content outline Google Doc (full outline per bundle) | No | Yes | Yes |
| FTC Quick Reference (one-pager) | No | Yes | Yes |
| CITES sidebar verbatim text | No | If covering Immunity | If covering Immunity/WH |
| Attribution log template | Yes (CC images only) | Yes | Yes |
| Shot list per bundle | Yes | No | No |

### Component 4: Success Criteria (clear at kickoff)
- Photographer: 5 approved images per session, first set within 10 days of manuscript completion
- Writer: First draft 300–350 words/hour pace; self-edited before submission; zero disqualifying FTC claims
- Specialist: Habitat accuracy notes reviewed by user before any upload; primary sources cited per claim
- All contractors: Proactive status update if behind by 3+ days (not the day of a missed deadline)

## Phase 4 — Weekly Check-In (Weeks 3–4, every Monday)

Run a 15-minute async check-in every Monday for each active contractor. Use the template in `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_COMMUNICATION_TEMPLATES.md` (Weekly Check-in Template).

### Check-in Agenda
1. **Progress review** (2 min): What was delivered this past week vs. the plan?
2. **Blocker identification** (5 min): Anything blocking this week's work? (Prop delays, content outline gaps, source access issues)
3. **Q&A routing** (5 min): Questions about brand, accuracy, or scope — answer directly or route to correct reference document
4. **Next week's priority** (3 min): Confirm what deliverable is due and by which date

### Escalation Triggers (act same day, not next check-in)
- Contractor reports being more than 3 days behind on a zero-float deliverable
- Quality issue found in first-draft review (FTC claim, factual error, brand mismatch)
- Contractor goes silent for 48+ hours during active production week
- Scope creep request from contractor (wants to expand beyond contracted sections)

### Response to a blocker
1. Determine if the blocker is within your control (send missing content outline, clarify spec)
2. If blocker is external (prop delay, plant specimen shipping): activate the Wikimedia CC / Google Docs PDF fallback per `PHASE_3_CRITICAL_PATH_ANALYSIS_JUNE22_JULY13.md`
3. Log blocker and resolution in WORKLOG.md same day
4. Adjust delivery date by minimum necessary days only — do not give a week of float for a 1-day blocker

## Phase 5 — Final Handoff (Weeks 5–6)

Run this checklist before releasing final payment milestone (25%).

### Asset Verification Checklist
**Photographers**
- [ ] All 5 images delivered per session (15 total across 3 sessions)
- [ ] All images pass photo delivery checklist from `PHASE_3_PHOTOGRAPHER_AND_SOURCING_RUNBOOK.md` (resolution, sRGB, naming convention)
- [ ] Commercial license confirmed in writing (email or contract clause)
- [ ] Attribution log entry complete for any CC-licensed source images used as props
- [ ] No watermarks, RAW files only (if RAW delivery was specified), no white balance inconsistency within a set

**Writers**
- [ ] All 3 bundle manuscripts delivered (Respiratory 3,600w, Immunity 3,800w, Digestive 3,600w)
- [ ] FTC compliance review passed on all manuscripts (administered before final payment)
- [ ] CITES sidebar verbatim present and unedited in Immunity bundle (Goldenseal section)
- [ ] Latin binomials correct throughout; contraindication panels cite primary sources
- [ ] All revision rounds complete; no outstanding content placeholders

**Habitat Specialists**
- [ ] All scoped habitat notes delivered (per contracted sections)
- [ ] Each claim cites a primary source (USDA PLANTS, NatureServe, UpS, CITES Appendix documentation)
- [ ] Regional accuracy reviewed by user before upload clearance
- [ ] No overlapping or contradictory content with writer track deliverables

### Payment Clearance
- [ ] Asset verification checklist above: all items checked
- [ ] Invoice received from contractor (or platform payment request confirmed)
- [ ] Budget vs. actual comparison logged in `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_SUCCESS_DASHBOARD.md`
- [ ] Final payment sent within 3 business days of handoff approval

### Knowledge Transfer
- [ ] Ask contractor: "What was the hardest part of this project to research or execute?"
- [ ] Log any species-specific sourcing insights in WORKLOG.md (useful for Phase 4 bundles)
- [ ] Capture any FTC edge cases flagged during contractor review (log in FTC Quick Reference)
- [ ] Request reference: "Would you be willing to be our first-contact for Phase 4 scope in August?"

## Phase 6 — Post-Launch Appreciation Sequence (Week 7+)

Send within 7 days of public launch of the bundle they contributed to.

### Sequence
1. **Day 1 post-launch**: Send praise/results email (use template in `PHASE_3_CONTRACTOR_COMMUNICATION_TEMPLATES.md`)
   - Share launch metrics: listing live, first views, any early sales
   - Specific praise for one deliverable that made a measurable difference
   - Not required to share revenue numbers; "listing is live and performing" is sufficient

2. **Day 7 post-launch**: Testimonial and future collaboration email
   - Ask if they would be willing to provide a brief testimonial for your contractor roster notes (not public)
   - Open the door to Phase 4 scope (Sleep/Nervines bundle, Rare Natives series)
   - Offer to be a reference for their portfolio ("I am happy to respond to any future client who asks about working with you")

3. **If Phase 4 scope is confirmed**: Make contact before outreach window closes
   - Contracted Phase 3 performers get right-of-first-refusal on Phase 4
   - Phase 4 budget range: same per-unit rates, expanded scope
