## Demand Justification by Herb

### Elderberry
The global immune-support herbal market is dominated by elderberry. Sambucol RCT evidence (cold/flu duration reduction) is among the most cited in the mainstream herbal supplement space. On Etsy, "elderberry guide," "elderberry growing," and "elderberry syrup recipe" are top-tier search terms. The native species distinction — *S. canadensis* (American elder) vs. *S. nigra* (European elder) — is absent from all PLR content. Cross-listing to both the Respiratory and Immunity bundles doubles keyword surface for a single content investment.

### Echinacea
One of the most searched herbal supplement terms in North America. The two-species differentiation (*E. purpurea* common garden species vs. *E. angustifolia* UpS At-Risk prairie native) is the angle that separates Seedwarden from every PLR guide. Multiple meta-analyses on immune function make FTC-compliant evidence framing straightforward ("studied for immune function support in multiple clinical trials"). The prairie restoration content adds a second audience hook beyond the practitioner buyer.

### Lavender
Highest brand-recognition herb in the wellness market. Top-searched term in the Etsy bath-and-apothecary category. The cultivation gap is real: guides covering English vs. French vs. hybrid lavender, zone-specific overwintering, and distillation vs. fresh harvest are rare at any quality level on Etsy. Lifestyle guides and supplement guides dominate; cultivation guides are thin. The Women's Health secondary assignment (traditional use for relaxation in hormonal comfort protocols) provides the cross-sell path from the Sleep bundle.

### Lemon Balm
High Etsy search volume for anxiety and sleep support. Tea preparation is the gateway use; forager and homesteader cohorts already know it as an edible. The dual nervine-and-carminative profile is the differentiator: the guide works in both the Sleep and Digestive bundles with minor framing adjustments. The Phase 2 forager cohort cross-sell opportunity (lemon balm is edible and appears in wild-edibles context) is the most direct content bridge from Phase 2 to Phase 3 after Dandelion.

### Mullein
Consistently high Etsy search volume for cough and ear oil remedies. The biennial lifecycle (first-year rosette vs. second-year spike) is genuinely under-documented. The "roadside apothecary" framing — freely harvestable from roadsides in most states — has very high forager-cohort appeal. The ear oil preparation (olive oil maceration) is a culinary-category preparation rather than a drug manufacturing process, which keeps FTC compliance simple. Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA coverage is among the best of any plant in North America.

### Passionflower
The sleep and anxiety category is the fastest-growing herbal segment on Etsy. The passionflower thumbnail — the radial purple-and-white flower — drives exceptionally high click-through as a listing cover image. Native *Passiflora incarnata* cultivation content (trellis requirements, zone-specific hardiness, maypop fruit bridge to forager cohort) is absent from PLR competitors. PubMed anxiolytic research can be cited descriptively ("studied for GABA receptor interactions") without making an illegal health claim.

### Valerian
The most recognized sleep herb in the herbal supplement market. Most Etsy guides cover the supplement form only. Cultivation guides focusing on root division propagation, second-year harvest timing, and drying protocols are essentially absent from Etsy. The root is the medicinal part — guides showing how to grow and harvest rather than recommending a capsule meet the exact need practitioners describe to patients asking about whole-herb preparations.
