# Phase 2 Logistics Vendor Database

**Lead finding:** At ModRun's launch volume (under 200 orders/month), self-fulfillment + USPS via Pirateship dominates on cost. The 3PL crossover point is 300-350 orders/month. This database pre-stages 25 3PL providers, 12 packaging vendors, and 8 carriers so that Phase 2 vendor outreach can begin the same day the test print passes — no 2-3 week discovery delay. Priority tiers (A/B/C) indicate outreach sequence.

### 3PL Selection Notes for ModRun Specifically

1. **Pre-packaging requirement (non-negotiable):** All 3PLs above require units to arrive pre-packaged and individually labeled. Loose bulk prints will be charged receiving labor ($35-65/hr). ModRun must pre-bag each unit in a labeled poly pouch before inbound shipping to any 3PL. This does not change the economics but changes the workflow — QC happens at print time, not order time.

2. **Etsy sync timing:** ShipMonk, Simpl, and Cahoot all advertise real-time Etsy sync. In practice this means "within minutes of order placement." Below 50 orders/day, inventory collision risk (two buyers ordering the last unit simultaneously) is negligible.

3. **ShipMonk exit risk:** Multiple Trustpilot reviews document continued billing 6+ months after termination. Negotiate explicit exit terms: 30-day notice, inventory returned within 30 days, data exportable at no charge. Get in writing before signing.

4. **Q4 surcharges:** Most 3PLs apply 15-30% surcharges October-December. Budget accordingly if launching in Q4.

5. **Home inventory buffer:** Regardless of 3PL used, maintain a minimum 50-unit home buffer. Never route 100% of inventory through a 3PL.

### 2026 USPS Rate Changes (Critical Context)

- USPS implemented an 8% temporary increase effective April 26, 2026, valid through January 17, 2027
- Commercial rates still 10-20% below retail window rates even after this increase
- Ground Advantage remains cheapest domestic option for under-2-lb packages
- Pirateship provides automatic access to current commercial rates at no cost

---

### DHL eCommerce (Consolidator Model) — Relevant at 200+ Shipments/Month

DHL eCommerce operates as a last-mile consolidator, not a traditional carrier. How it works:
- Seller hands off bulk packages to DHL eCommerce at a drop-off point or scheduled pickup
- DHL injects packages into USPS last-mile delivery stream, bypassing USPS origin scans
- Rate advantage: ~$0.12/lb domestic (April 2026) vs. USPS retail
- Drawback: 2-8 day transit (slower than USPS Priority); tracking less granular
- Minimum volume for competitive rates: 200+ packages/month
- Peak surcharges: $0.30-6.50/package during Q4 2025-2026

**ModRun recommendation:** Do not use DHL eCommerce until 200+ shipments/month. The consolidator model's slower transit time conflicts with Etsy Star Seller delivery expectations. Use Pirateship + USPS Ground Advantage until volume justifies DHL.

## Part 4: Vendor Outreach Priority Queue

### Immediate Actions (Execute Within 7 Days of Test Print Pass)

**Priority A outreach sequence (estimated time: 2-3 hours total):**

1. **Request Fulfillrite quote** (fulfillrite.com/locations/baltimore-md) — 30 min call; mention ModRun is Maryland-based 3D print operation; ask about Etsy integration, inbound receiving for pre-packaged small products, per-order rate at 100-300 order volumes
2. **Request Simpl Fulfillment quote** (simplfulfillment.com/pricing) — online form; note expected volume ramp from 50 to 300 orders over 6 months; ask for flat-rate confirmation and Etsy sync details
3. **Order Uline poly mailers** (uline.com) — 200-pack of 9×12" white poly mailers, ~$18-25; no lead time (next-day shipping available); this removes any packaging bottleneck at launch
4. **Set up Pirateship account** (pirateship.com) — free; takes 15 minutes; immediately enables USPS commercial rates for current self-fulfillment

**Priority B outreach (Month 2-3, when order volume data exists):**
- Request ShipMonk quote with actual order history
- Evaluate Red Stag Fulfillment if heavy products (rail organizers) become primary SKU
- Order custom branded boxes from Arka (250-unit MOQ for good per-unit pricing) when brand investment is justified by revenue
