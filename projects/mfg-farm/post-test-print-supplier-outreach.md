# ModRun Supplier Outreach Automation

**Purpose**: Five pre-written supplier outreach emails ready to personalize and send in one batch on Day 1. All variable replacements are minimal — find-and-replace YOUR_NAME, YOUR_CITY, and volume figures. Total outreach time: 20 minutes.

**Success definition**: By Day 9, you have locked in confirmed pricing, lead times, and MOQs from at least 3 of these 5 suppliers. Every supplier either beats the win threshold or is replaced with their second-tier alternative.

---

## Outreach Timeline

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| T+0 (Day 1) | Test print confirmed → Send all 5 templates simultaneously (afternoon) |
| T+1–T+3 | Monitor inbox; no action required unless a supplier responds with a question |
| T+3–T+5 | Expected first responses arrive — begin negotiation window |
| T+5–T+7 | Follow-up cadence: one follow-up email per non-responsive supplier |
| T+7–T+9 | Negotiate pricing, lead time, MOQ — lock in agreements |
| T+9 | All supplier decisions finalized. Log confirmed pricing in response tracker |

---

## Win Thresholds — What Constitutes a Success

Before sending, internalize what you're actually trying to achieve. Do not accept worse than these:

| Supplier Category | Win Condition | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|
| Filament (eSUN primary) | ≤$12/kg at 10kg order, 2–5 day lead time, no MOQ | >$14/kg OR >7 day lead time |
| Filament (bulk alt — Anycubic) | ≤$10.49/kg, standard terms, Prime-equivalent lead time | >$12/kg |
| Packaging (poly mailer) | ≤$0.05/unit at 1,000-unit order, ≤5 day lead time | >$0.08/unit |
| Packaging (custom box, Phase 2) | ≤$1.10/unit at 500 units, ≤14 day lead time | >$1.50/unit OR >21 days |
| Thermal labels (BETCKEY) | ≤$0.008/label at 1,000-unit order | >$0.015/label |

## Negotiation Guidance — When They Respond Below Win Threshold

If a supplier responds with pricing above the win threshold, use this counter-offer framework (not a template — adjust the specific numbers):

**Filament counter-offer (if eSUN quotes above $12/kg):**
"I have a confirmed alternative at [$X]/kg from Anycubic. I prefer eSUN's AMS reliability track record — can you match [$X - $0.50]/kg at 10kg orders? If you can, I'll lock in a monthly order commitment now."

**Packaging counter-offer (if Shop4Mailers quotes above $0.06/unit):**
"Can you do [$0.05]/unit if I commit to a 2,000-unit initial order rather than 1,000? I'd rather place the larger order now if the economics work."

**Labeling counter-offer (if BETCKEY quotes above $0.012/label):**
"I'm currently evaluating three label suppliers. Your product is my preference based on quality reports. Can you sharpen the pencil to [$0.008–0.010]/label at 1,000 units? That's the threshold where I'll place the order today."

The principle in all cases: give them a specific number, give them a reason to move (competitive pressure or commitment upgrade), and make the ask easy to say yes to.
