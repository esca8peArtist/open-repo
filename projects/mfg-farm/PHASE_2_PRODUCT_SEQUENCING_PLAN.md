# Phase 2 Product Sequencing & Launch Plan

## Executive Summary

This document operationalizes the 15 SKU candidates from Q3_Q4_SKU_EXPANSION_MATRIX.md into a concrete launch calendar with **material sourcing schedule**, **inventory targets**, **fulfillment workflow**, and **go/no-go decision gates**. The goal is to execute 4 consecutive product waves (Wave 1-4, July-October 2026) with minimal production interruption and clear cash flow tracking.

**Key dependencies:**
- Wave 1 success (July 1): Validates supply chain and market response; unlocks Wave 2 capex
- Wave 1 demand validation (July 31): Drives 2nd printer investment decision (yes/no by Aug 15)
- Material sourcing discipline: Advance orders placed 2-3 weeks before production launch to avoid stock-outs

**Target outcome by December 31, 2026:**
- 15 products live on Etsy
- $21,200 gross revenue (6 months July-Dec)
- Single or dual printer operation (based on demand signals)
- $15,000+ net profit (after COGS + Etsy fees + overhead)

## Part 4: Fulfillment Workflow & Operational Procedures

### **Order-to-Ship Process (Standard)**

**Timeline: Order received → Ship within 1-2 business days**

| Step | Time | Action | Responsible | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Order received (Etsy notification) | T+0 | Receive order; note SKU, qty, customization (if applicable) | Etsy sync | Email notification + Etsy dashboard |
| 2. Pick inventory | T+0.25 hours | Retrieve from shelf; inspect for damage/defects | Fulfillment | Log which print batch unit came from (for quality tracking) |
| 3. Post-processing (if needed) | T+0.5 hours | Install rubber pads, magnets, or hardware; light packaging prep | Fulfillment | Varies by SKU (see below) |
| 4. Weight & measure | T+0.75 hours | Weigh final package; confirm dimensions for shipping cost accuracy | Fulfillment | Feed into Pirate Ship weight field |
| 5. Package | T+1 hour | Poly mailer (9×12" or 12×15.5" depending on SKU); include packing slip + care instructions | Fulfillment | Branded packing slip (if available by Phase 3) |
| 6. Generate shipping label | T+1.25 hours | Etsy → Pirate Ship; print label; weigh package on scale | Fulfillment | Use commercial USPS rates (Ground Advantage or Priority Mail Cubic) |
| 7. Ship | T+1.5 hours | Place label on package; drop at USPS mailbox or schedule pickup | Fulfillment | Strive for next-business-day cutoff (postmark by 5pm local) |
| 8. Notify customer | T+2 hours | Etsy auto-notification with tracking number | System | No manual action required |
| 9. Follow-up (post-delivery) | T+3-5 days | Monitor reviews; respond to feedback within 24 hours | Customer Service | Proactive follow-up email template (optional, Phase 3) |

### **SKU-Specific Post-Processing Checklist**

**Cable Tray:**
- [ ] Inspect all snap-fit joints (no cracks)
- [ ] Wipe dust from print
- [ ] No post-processing required
- **Packaging:** 9×12" poly mailer

**Surge Protector Holder:**
- [ ] Inspect adhesive tab pockets (no damage)
- [ ] Verify mounting hole alignment
- [ ] Include 2× 3M Command strips in package (customer applies)
- **Packaging:** 6×9" padded mailer or 9×12" poly mailer

**Under-Desk Cable Organizer:**
- [ ] Verify adhesive mounting pockets
- [ ] Light sanding if rough edges (1-2 min, fine grit)
- [ ] Include 3M Command strips
- **Packaging:** 9×12" poly mailer

**Magnetic Labels (per unit, before packaging):**
- [ ] Press N52 magnet into pocket (click feel = proper fit)
- [ ] Light tap with small mallet if tight (do not force)
- [ ] Verify magnet holds with ~500g pull test
- [ ] [If custom text] Inspect embossed text legibility
- **Packaging:** Small bubble mailer or 6×9" poly mailer; include instructions for magnet strength

**Monitor Riser:**
- [ ] Apply rubber pads to all 4 corners (adhesive bumpers; ensure flush)
- [ ] Inspect cable channel notch
- [ ] Verify stability on flat surface (no wobble)
- **Packaging:** 9×12" poly mailer; include installation guide + cable management tips

**Drawer Dividers (4-pack):**
- [ ] Verify snap-fit corners engage smoothly
- [ ] Test assembly/disassembly 2 cycles (durability spot check)
- [ ] Include assembly guide (one-page illustrated)
- **Packaging:** 9×12" poly mailer

**Pegboard Labels:**
- [ ] Install magnets (if magnetic variant)
- [ ] Verify text legibility
- [ ] Test magnet hold on steel pegboard sample
- **Packaging:** Bubble mailer or 6×9" poly mailer

**Shelf Dividers:**
- [ ] Verify friction-fit engagement (test on sample shelf edge)
- [ ] Inspect edge clips for damage

- **Packaging:** 6×9" poly mailer (small item)

- [ ] Verify mounting tabs or adhesive pockets
- [ ] Light sanding if text fill is rough
- **Packaging:** 6×9" poly mailer or small bubble mailer; include wall/door mounting instructions

**Miniature Storage Box:**

- [ ] Close magnetic lid; test operation (smooth, secure)
- [ ] Inspect interior for print imperfections
- **Packaging:** 9×12" poly mailer; include assembly instructions for internal dividers

**Dice Tower (resin):**

## Part 6: Quality Assurance & Returns Policy

1. Print 3-5 test units
2. Full post-processing (rubber pads, magnets, hardware installation)
3. Dimensional verification (calipers or ruler; confirm ±0.5mm tolerances)
4. Weight verification (confirm matches product description)

6. Photo quality check (clear, well-lit, representative of final product)

### **QA Protocol (Production)**

During ongoing production:
- Spot check: 1 unit per 10 printed (10% sampling rate)

## Part 8: Contingency Playbook

### **Contingency 1: Filament Stock-Out (Magnet Supply, Adhesive Strips, etc.)**

**Scenario:** Supplier runs out of stock; lead time suddenly extends to 3-4 weeks.

**Response:**
- **Immediate (same day):** Contact backup supplier (Amazon Prime or alternative brand)
- **Sourcing:** Accept higher unit cost ($0.50-1.00 premium) for emergency 2-3 day delivery
- **Communication:** Notify customers of potential shipping delay; offer discount code for patience
- **Prevention:** Maintain 3-4 week rolling safety stock; monitor supplier inventory online

---

### **Contingency 2: Printer Failure (Hardware Breakdown)**

**Scenario:** Bambu P1S requires repair/replacement; down for 3-7 days.

**Response:**
- **Days 1-3:** Use service bureau (Craftcloud, Slant 3D) at $6-15/part cost for emergency orders only (accept margin hit)
- **Day 3:** File Bambu warranty claim; arrange repair or replacement
- **Day 5+:** If 2nd printer available, shift Wave 1 production to 2nd unit; mitigate impact
- **Prevention:** Maintain 2-3 week inventory buffer; have service bureau contact info on file

---

### **Contingency 3: Demand Collapse (Wave 1 Traction Disappoints)**

**Scenario:** By Aug 15, only 10-15 units/week combined across Wave 1-2 SKUs; profitability at risk.

**Response:**
- **Immediate:** Pause Wave 3-4 launches; redirect resources to Wave 1 optimization
- **Price testing:** Reduce 1-2 SKUs by $2-3; measure elasticity
- **Advertising:** Allocate $100-150/month to Etsy Ads (target Cable Tray, Surge Protector high-margin SKUs)
- **Product photography:** Refresh Etsy listings with lifestyle shots, unboxing videos
- **Customer outreach:** Email past ModRun customers; offer bundle discount (ModRun + new SKU)
- **Decision:** If demand does not recover by Oct 1, discontinue lowest-performing SKU; focus on top 10 products

---

### **Contingency 4: Quality Issues (High Defect Rate, Returns)**

**Scenario:** By Sep 1, return rate reaches 5-8% (above 2-3% target); customers report design flaws.

**Response:**
- **Root cause analysis:** Examine failed units; identify design tolerance issue, material batch issue, or user error
- **Design revision:** If design flaw confirmed, release revised STL; offer free replacements to existing customers
- **Material investigation:** If magnet adhesion failing or print dimensional drift detected, swap suppliers/print settings
- **Communication:** Proactive email to customers with revision details + refund/replacement offer
- **Prevention:** Increase QA sampling to 15-20% until issue resolved
