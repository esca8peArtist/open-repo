# Phase 2 Market Expansion Channels

**Lead finding:** After Etsy (Phase 1) and Amazon FBA (Phase 2 planned), the highest-value next channels for ModRun's cable management + desk accessories product set are: (1) Facebook Shops/Instagram Shopping — zero additional fees, existing Meta audience, works with existing Shopify catalog; (2) Walmart Marketplace — 6-15% referral fee vs. Amazon's 15%, 75% off referral fees for new sellers in 2026, lower competition; (3) Faire wholesale — access to 900,000+ independent retailers, B2B revenue with larger per-order AOV. TikTok Shop is high-growth but requires content production investment. Google Shopping, Pinterest, and Squarespace/WooCommerce D2C are medium-priority supporting channels.

## Part 2: Fee Deep-Dive — Priority A Channels

### 2A: Facebook Shops (and Instagram Shopping)

**How it works:**
- Facebook Shops and Instagram Shopping are linked through Meta's Commerce Manager
- One catalog setup covers both Facebook and Instagram simultaneously
- Since 2023, US sellers using Facebook/Instagram Shops complete checkout on their own website (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.) — Meta no longer processes checkout natively in most cases
- When checkout occurs on your site, the only fee is your payment processor (e.g., 2.9% + $0.30 via Shopify Payments)
- For listings sold via Marketplace/Shops with Meta Checkout enabled (where available), Meta charges 5% of sale price (minimum $0.40)

**Per-$28.99 unit economics:**

| Scenario | Fee | Net Revenue | vs. Etsy ($25.79 net) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Checkout on own Shopify site (preferred) | 2.9% + $0.30 = $1.14 | $27.85 | +$2.06 better |
| Meta Checkout (if enabled) | 5% = $1.45 | $27.54 | +$1.75 better |
| Local pickup (no shipping) | $0 | $28.99 | +$3.20 better |

**Setup requirements:**
- Facebook Business Manager account
- Commerce Manager catalog (integrates with Shopify via native sync)
- Product photos meeting Meta commerce policy (white background or lifestyle; no text overlays)
- Processing: 1-3 business days for catalog review

**Audience fit:** Cable management products trend well in "work from home setup" and "desk organization" Facebook groups and Instagram desk-aesthetic accounts. The audience skews 25-45 male home office workers — exactly the ModRun buyer profile.

**Competitive intensity:** Medium. Facebook Shops product search is less developed than Amazon or Etsy. Discovery relies more on paid ads or organic social content than keyword search. This means less competition per keyword but also requires content creation investment.

**Priority: A — activate when Shopify Phase 3 launches. Zero incremental fee setup.**

### 2B: Walmart Marketplace

**How it works:**
- Walmart Marketplace is an invite-based (application required) third-party seller platform at walmart.com
- No monthly subscription fee (unlike Amazon's $39.99/month)
- Referral fees: 6-15% by category; most home goods/accessories fall at 8-15%
- For cable management accessories: typically 10-15% referral
- WFS (Walmart Fulfillment Services): optional; $3.45+/unit; reaches customers with 1-2 day delivery

**2026 New-Seller Savings Program:**
- New sellers going live after February 1, 2026 receive up to 75% off referral fees (capped at $75,000 in total savings)
- This effectively means 2.5-3.75% referral fee for first sales, dramatically improving early-stage economics
- Plus: 25% off WFS fees + 50% off storage fees for the first 90 days

**Per-$28.99 unit economics (new seller with 75% fee discount):**

| Scenario | Referral Fee | Net Revenue | vs. Etsy ($25.79 net) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-fulfill (new seller, 75% discount, ~10% rate) | 2.5% = $0.72 | $28.27 | +$2.48 better |
| Self-fulfill (standard, 10% rate) | 10% = $2.90 | $26.09 | +$0.30 better |
| WFS (self-fulfill + $3.45 WFS fee, standard) | 10% + $3.45 = $6.35 | $22.64 | -$3.15 worse |
| WFS (new seller discount, 75% off referral) | 2.5% + $3.45 = $4.17 | $24.82 | -$0.97 vs. Etsy |

**Application process:**
1. Apply at marketplace.walmart.com (2-4 week review)
2. US-based business required (EIN needed)
3. Vetted for product quality, shipping capability, customer service history
4. First listing approved separately after account approval

**Competitive intensity:** Lower than Amazon for niche home accessories. Walmart Marketplace has ~130 million monthly shoppers vs. Amazon's 310 million — meaningful volume with less seller saturation in cable management.

**WFS vs. self-fulfill decision:** At Phase 2 launch volumes (under 100 units/month on Walmart), WFS economics are poor. Self-fulfillment with Pirateship on Walmart gives Standard shipping, not 2-day. Use WFS only when Walmart becomes a meaningful revenue channel (50+ units/month) and 2-day delivery is needed for conversion.

**Priority: A — apply to Walmart Marketplace during Phase 2 Amazon FBA activation (similar logistics setup, additive audience).**

### 2C: Faire Wholesale

**How it works:**
- Faire is the dominant B2B wholesale marketplace (900,000+ independent retailers in North America + Europe)
- Brands list products at wholesale price (typically 50% of retail = ~$14.50 for $28.99 retail)
- Retailers order in bulk (MOQ set by brand; typically 3-12 units minimum)
- Faire charges commission on sales; brand controls wholesale price and MOQ

**Fee structure (2026):**
- 15% commission on marketplace orders from new customers (buyers Faire refers to you)
- $10 one-time new customer fee per retailer (first order only)
- 0% commission on Faire Direct orders (you invite existing stockists; they order through your Faire link)
- 0% commission on reorders from customers you brought via Faire Direct
- Payment processing: 1.9-3.5% + $0.30 (by payout speed)
- No monthly fee, no listing fee

**Per-order economics (wholesale order, example: 12 cable clips at $14.50/unit):**

| Cost Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Wholesale order value | 12 × $14.50 = $174.00 |
| Faire commission (15% marketplace) | -$26.10 |
| $10 new customer fee (one-time) | -$10.00 |
| Payment processing (2.5% est.) | -$4.35 |
| COGS (12 × $3.75) | -$45.00 |
| Packaging + shipping (ModRun ships to retailer) | -$15.00 |
| **Net on first wholesale order** | **$73.55 ($6.13/unit)** |

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Wholesale price per unit | $14.50 |
| Net per unit (first Faire order) | $6.13 |
| Net per unit (reorder, 0% Direct commission) | $9.97 |
| Comparable Etsy net per unit | $25.79 |
| AOV comparison | $174 (12-unit wholesale) vs. $28.99 (Etsy single) |

**Strategic logic for ModRun:**
- Wholesale orders are batched — one 12-unit order to a boutique office supply store = 12 prints in one batch run
- Retailer handles customer service and individual consumer sales
- Brand exposure: product sold through independent boutiques adds brand credibility
- Retailer reorder economics (0% commission via Faire Direct) become attractive at $9.97/unit net vs. Etsy's $25.79 — Faire wholesale reorders pay 38% of Etsy per-unit yield but in bulk

**When wholesale economics work:** Faire becomes genuinely attractive when you can fulfill wholesale orders without disrupting direct consumer production. At 2-printer operation (Phase 3+), a 24-unit wholesale batch can run overnight while the primary printer handles Etsy orders.

**Category fit:** Faire's strongest categories include homeware/home décor, gifts, stationery, and office accessories — all relevant to ModRun's cable management + desk organization products.

**Priority: A (apply during Phase 2 Month 3-4, once 50+ Etsy units/month is established as proof of production scale).**

## Part 3: Fee Deep-Dive — Priority B Channels

### 3A: TikTok Shop

**Fee structure (2026):**
- Commission: 5-8% depending on product category (home accessories: typically 6%)
- New seller promotional rate: 1.8% commission on first $100,000 in sales (90-day period)
- Payment processing: 2.3-2.9% + $0.20-0.30 per transaction
- Total fees at standard rates: 8-12% of gross sales
- Total fees at promo rate: ~4-5% of gross sales

**Per-$28.99 unit economics (promotional period):**

| Scenario | Fee | Net Revenue | vs. Etsy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Promo commission (1.8%) + processing (2.6% + $0.25) | $0.52 + $1.00 = $1.52 | $27.47 | +$1.68 better |
| Standard commission (6%) + processing | $1.74 + $1.00 = $2.74 | $26.25 | +$0.46 better |

**TikTok Shop strategic considerations:**
- Discovery model: products go viral via short-form video content (unboxing, desk-setup reveal, cable management transformation videos)
- Conversion rate: 4.7% (higher than Facebook Shops at 1.8% and Pinterest at 3.2%)
- Content requirement: TikTok Shop requires active content creation. Without regular posting, products receive minimal algorithmic exposure. This is a meaningful investment: 2-4 videos/week minimum for traction.
- Fulfillment by TikTok (FBT): TikTok's own fulfillment service; similar model to FBA; adds storage fees and per-unit handling; Q4 surcharges apply
- Risk: TikTok's US regulatory status remains uncertain (potential bans/restructuring); platform concentration risk is high

**Who this works for:** TikTok Shop is high-reward for products that have video content potential. Cable management transformations ("before and after desk cable chaos") are exactly the content format that goes viral. If ModRun can produce 2-4 short videos/week, TikTok Shop can drive significant volume.

**Priority: B — activate in Phase 3 when Shopify is live and content production bandwidth exists.**

### 3B: Google Shopping

**How it works:**
- Google Merchant Center: free to set up; free product listings in Google Shopping tab
- Products appear in Google Search results with images and prices
- No commission, no transaction fee — Google earns only through paid Shopping Ads (CPC)
- For free listings: set up Google Merchant Center, connect product feed (Shopify integrates natively)
- For paid Shopping Ads: CPC averages $0.46-1.20 for home goods

**Economics:**
- Free listings: zero cost; organic traffic only; lower volume than paid
- Paid Shopping Ads: average $0.66 CPC for home goods; at 3% conversion rate, $0.66 / 0.03 = $22 cost per sale on a $28.99 item (76% of ASP) — not viable without repeat purchase strategy
- Performance Max campaigns (Google's automated ads): average ROAS varies widely; 4-8x ROAS needed to justify spend at ModRun's margins

**Best use case for ModRun:** Set up free Google Merchant Center listing when Shopify launches (Phase 3). Do not run paid Google Shopping Ads until gross revenue exceeds $3,000/month and CAC data from Etsy/Amazon validates the customer profile for Google targeting.

**Priority: B — free listing setup when Shopify launches; paid ads later.**

### 3C: Pinterest Shopping (Product Pins)

**How it works:**
- Shoppable Product Pins are free to set up; no listing fee, no commission
- Checkout occurs on seller's website (Pinterest links out, not in-app checkout for most sellers)
- No transaction fee from Pinterest
- US-only for shoppable product features

**Per-$28.99 unit economics:**
- Pinterest fee: $0
- Only cost is payment processor on your checkout page (2.9% + $0.30 via Shopify Payments = $1.14)
- Net: $27.85 per unit (highest of all marketplaces)

**Pinterest audience:**
- 500M+ monthly users; 70% female skew; primary demographics: home décor, DIY, organization
- 3.2% conversion rate on Buyable Pins (higher than Facebook at 1.8%)
- "Desk organization," "cable management," "home office setup" are active search categories

**Content requirements:** Pinterest rewards high-quality vertical images (2:3 ratio). Lifestyle photography of organized desk setups with ModRun products is ideal content. Unlike TikTok, Pinterest is static image-based — less production burden.

**Priority: B — set up Pinterest business account and product catalog when Shopify launches. Zero cost; moderate upside for ModRun's aesthetic product category.**
