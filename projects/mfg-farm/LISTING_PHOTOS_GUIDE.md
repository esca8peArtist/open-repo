# Listing Photos Planning Guide — 3D Printed Products

**Lead finding**: Etsy listings with professional optimized photos receive up to 40% higher conversion rates and 90% more favorites than basic snapshots. For 3D printed parts, the single highest-ROI investment is a white-background hero shot that fills 80% of the frame — everything else builds trust on top of that foundation. For Amazon specifically, the main image must be pure white (RGB 255,255,255) or the listing will be suppressed. A complete 10-photo set on Etsy plus one 15-second video is the ceiling and should be used for every listing.

---

## Part 1: Equipment

### Camera Options — Ranked by Output Quality

**Option A: Dedicated mirrorless/DSLR (best quality)**

A Sony a6000-class mirrorless, Canon M50, or any entry-level DSLR with a kit lens (18-55mm) produces significantly better images than smartphones for two reasons: larger sensor handles low light without noise, and manual settings give full control over depth of field.

- Minimum resolution: 12MP (any modern camera qualifies)
- Ideal focal lengths: 50mm for flat-lay / overview shots; 100mm macro for snap-fit detail
- Cost: $300-600 used for body + lens

**Option B: iPhone 13 Pro or newer / Pixel 7 or newer (viable, lower cost)**

Modern flagship smartphones with computational photography now match entry-level DSLRs for well-lit product shots. Limitations emerge in macro detail (sub-10mm features) and in mixed artificial light (color casts). Pro mode (manual ISO and shutter) is available on most flagships.

- iPhone 14+ or Samsung S22+: use "1x" lens (not wide-angle), macro mode for close-ups
- Lock exposure and focus by tapping and holding subject in iOS camera before shooting
- Use a tripod adapter ($8-15 on Amazon) — hand-holding in a lightbox produces blur

**Option C: Ring light with phone mount (~$35 total)**

A 10-inch ring light with an integrated phone clamp provides both the mount and a diffused fill light source in one unit. Best for quick on-desk lifestyle shots. Not ideal for flat-lay (the ring creates a round catch-light in shiny surfaces).

### Lenses for Small Plastic Parts

**Standard kit lens (18-55mm f/3.5-5.6)**: Works for hero and lifestyle shots. Set to 35-50mm end to avoid distortion.

**Macro lens (50mm or 100mm f/2.8)**: Required for snap-fit detail shots. The snap-fit mechanism on the ModRun clip is approximately 3-5mm — a standard lens cannot focus close enough to fill the frame meaningfully. A used Canon 100mm f/2.8 macro runs $200-350 used; third-party options (7artisans 60mm macro) are $80-120.

**Phone macro clip lens ($10-20)**: Clip-on macro attachments for smartphones reduce minimum focus distance to 1-3 inches. Quality is inconsistent — test before relying on for final shots.

### Tripod

A tripod is non-negotiable for:
- Overhead flat-lay shots (no way to hold a camera directly overhead stably)
- Any shot in a lightbox where the camera must be positioned and locked
- Any detail/macro shot (minimum shutter speed of 1/125s is usually needed to avoid blur at macro distances; tripod enables this at lower ISOs)

A $25-40 aluminum tripod with a ball head is sufficient. A gorilla pod ($15-25) is more flexible for tight spaces like lightboxes.

### Lighting Hardware

**Natural light (free)**: North-facing window, no direct sun, shoot 10am-3pm. Diffuse with a sheer white curtain. White foam board (24"×36", $3 at a dollar store) on the opposite side acts as a reflector to fill shadows. This is the best starting point and produces warm, flattering light.

**Softbox kit ($40-80)**: A two-softbox kit (45cm × 45cm) with 45W daylight-balanced bulbs provides consistent, repeatable lighting independent of time of day. Essential for high-volume production photography where sessions happen at different times.

**Ring light 10" ($20-35)**: Good for phone-based product shots. Creates slightly even, flat lighting — fine for overview shots, less flattering for detail shots where side-lighting creates depth.

**LED lightbox panel kit ($25-40)**: A collapsible light tent (usually 12"×12" or 16"×16") with two built-in LED panels on the sides and a diffuse fabric top. Produces clean white backgrounds and even diffused light with minimal setup. The ModRun clip easily fits in a 12"×12" unit. These are purpose-built for exactly this use case and are the recommended starting point for minimal investment.

## Part 2: Camera Settings for Small Plastic Parts

### For Hero and Lifestyle Shots

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Aperture | f/5.6–f/8 | Keeps full clip in focus; slight background blur on lifestyle shots |
| Shutter speed | 1/125–1/250s | Prevents camera shake in natural light; tripod gives more flexibility |
| ISO | 100–400 | Minimizes digital noise; raise to 800 only if light is insufficient |
| White balance | 5500K daylight (or AWB in post) | Neutral whites; prevent orange or blue cast |
| Focus mode | Single-point AF, focused on snap-fit or product face | Do not use face detect or tracking |

### For Macro / Detail Shots

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Aperture | f/8–f/11 | At macro distances, depth of field is extremely shallow; f/8+ keeps snap arm and channel both sharp |
| Shutter speed | 1/200s minimum | Even on tripod, slight vibration causes blur at macro distances |
| ISO | 100–200 | Macro subjects need maximum sharpness; noise becomes visible at pixel level |
| Use manual focus | At the closest focus distance, manual focus gives more precise control | |

### For Flat-Lay Overhead Shots

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Aperture | f/8 | All clips need to be in focus across the frame plane |
| Camera angle | 90° directly overhead | Use tripod extended arm or mount camera on ladder above the surface |
| Lens | 35–50mm equivalent | Wider focal length causes barrel distortion of outer clips; 50mm minimizes this |
| Background | White poster board or lightbox floor | Must be pure white for Etsy/Amazon compliance |

## Part 3: Background Choices

### White Sweep / White Background

**Pros**: Required for Amazon main image (pure white, RGB 255,255,255 — any deviation causes listing suppression). Clean, professional, platform-standard. Makes the product color and geometry the focus. Works for both Etsy and Amazon.

**Cons**: Provides no lifestyle context. Can look sterile for Etsy where buyers expect handmade or craft warmth.

**Best uses**: Main image (photo slot 1 on Etsy, primary image on Amazon), flat-lay color variant shots, infographic overlays.

**DIY method**: White poster board ($1 at dollar store) placed on a flat surface and curving up into a wall creates a seamless white background. Avoid tape lines or creases — they photograph prominently under studio light.

### Light Gray / Off-White Background

**Pros**: Softer than pure white; more flattering for white PLA+ products that disappear against a bright white background. Shows fine surface texture and layer lines on light-colored parts.

**Cons**: Rejected by Amazon main image policy. Must be used in supplemental image slots only.

**Best uses**: Secondary product shots, detail shots where product is white or light-colored.

### Contextual / In-Use Background (Desk Surface)

**Pros**: Highest conversion driver for functional products — shows the buyer exactly where this goes and what it does. The "before/after" desk shot is consistently cited as the single highest-engagement photo type in cable management and desk accessories.

**Cons**: Requires staging. Harder to execute consistently. Does not work for Amazon main image slot.

**Best uses**: Photo slots 2-5 on Etsy. First secondary image on Amazon. Product lifestyle and UGC-style images.

**Desk surface options**:
- White/light gray laminate (most common desk color, universal appeal)
- Warm oak/walnut wood (home office aesthetic, premium positioning)
- Matte black (gaming/tech setup)

Shoot on the surface your target buyer is most likely to have. The ModRun buyer is primarily a home office worker or tech-focused user — white/light gray laminate is the primary target, wood secondary.

### Gradient Paper Background

**Pros**: Adds visual interest over pure white; differentiates from Amazon-required sterile white shots. Popular with photography accessory sellers and physical product shops that want a "studio" aesthetic without full lifestyle props.

**Cons**: Looks dated if overused; can clash with product color.

**Best uses**: Social media content (Instagram, Pinterest), not listing images.

## Part 4: Shot List — Cable Clip Product (ModRun Specific)

This is the minimum viable shot list for the ModRun cable clip launch. Etsy allows up to 10 photos; Amazon allows up to 9 (1 main + 8 supplemental). Shoot all 10 — unused slots are wasted conversion opportunity.

### Shot 1 — Hero (White Background, Main Image)

- Cable clip held by a hand or resting on a white surface
- Product fills 85% of the frame (Etsy and Amazon standard)
- Pure white background (RGB 255,255,255 for Amazon; near-white acceptable for Etsy)
- No props, no cables, product only
- Angle: 45° from front, slightly elevated — shows top face and one side
- **Purpose**: Search result thumbnail; first impression on listing page

### Shot 2 — Lifestyle Hero (In-Use, Desk Context)

- Single ModRun clip on a clean desk surface, cable inserted and routed through the channel
- Cable is a real cable (black USB-C or gray power cable — not props)
- Desk surface in soft focus behind the clip
- Clip is the sharp subject
- Angle: 45° from front, at desk level (eye-level if kneeling)
- **Purpose**: Converts interest to purchase — shows exactly where it goes and how it looks in use

### Shot 3 — Angle Shot (3/4 View, No Background)

- White or light gray background
- Product at a 3/4 front angle showing the channel opening, snap arm, and mounting base simultaneously
- Illuminates the three-dimensional geometry that the hero shot slightly obscures
- **Purpose**: Shows product form factor for buyers who want to understand the geometry

### Shot 4 — In-Use Shot (Cable in Clip, Mounted on Rail or Surface)

- Clip mounted to DIN rail or flat surface with cable routed through it
- Shows the full installation: mount point + cable entry + cable exit
- Wider shot than Shot 2 — shows context of where on the desk/surface the clip lives
- **Purpose**: Answers "how does this attach?" objection — critical trust signal

### Shot 5 — Detail Shot (Snap-Fit Feature Close-Up)

- Macro shot of the snap-fit arm in its engaged position with cable held in channel
- Focal point: the snap arm contact point and cable seat
- f/8–f/11 so both are in focus
- Side lighting to create depth and show the 3D geometry of the snap arm
- Optional: hand finger pressing the snap arm to show how it releases (sequential shot)
- **Purpose**: Demonstrates the quality of the mechanism; differentiates from injection-molded commodity clips

### Shot 6 — Scale Shot (Product with Coin or Ruler)

- Cable clip next to a US quarter coin or standard 30cm ruler
- White background
- Clearly shows actual product dimensions without requiring buyer to read the description
- **Purpose**: Eliminates returns from buyers who misjudge size; reduces "this was smaller than expected" review risk

### Shot 7 — Color Variants Flat-Lay

- All available colors (black, white, gray minimum) laid out in a row on white background
- Directly overhead (90° camera angle)
- Even spacing, all clips in sharp focus
- **Purpose**: Shows color options at a glance without requiring buyer to click through variants

### Shot 8 — 3-Pack / Bundle Contents Flat-Lay

- Full contents of the 3-pack SKU laid out: three clips (3mm, 6mm, 12mm variants) + any packaging
- Overhead angle
- Answers "what exactly do I get?" without the buyer reading the description
- **Purpose**: Reduces buyer confusion, supports the bundle price point

### Shot 9 — Before/After (Cable Management Transformation)

- Left panel: same section of desk with cables loose and tangled
- Right panel: same desk, same angle, same cables — now routed through 2-3 ModRun clips
- Divide with a vertical white line overlay in editing (Canva can do this in 2 minutes)
- Both halves must have the same crop, angle, and lighting — shoot with a fixed tripod
- **Purpose**: Highest-impact conversion image; answers "will this actually make a visible difference?" — the affirmative answer is visible

### Shot 10 — Lifestyle Context (Full Desk Setup)

- Wide shot of a clean, organized desk with 3-4 ModRun clips visible in natural positions
- Monitor, keyboard, maybe a plant visible in the background (soft focus)
- Shows how the clips integrate into a real desk system — not just one cable managed, but the whole desk improved
- Landscape orientation, 35mm equivalent
- **Purpose**: Aspirational lifestyle image; targets the r/battlestations buyer

### Optional Shot 11 — Video (15 seconds max on Etsy)

- 5 seconds: cable loose and messy on desk
- 5 seconds: snap cable into clip in real time (the "snap" moment is satisfying and shareable)
- 5 seconds: cable routed cleanly, pan back to show the full desk
- No music required; the snap sound is the audio
- **Purpose**: Etsy listings with videos receive higher search ranking; video dramatically increases session time on listing page

## Part 5: Free and Low-Cost DIY Setup Options

### Minimum Viable Setup (~$0–$15)

If you have a modern smartphone and access to any window with indirect natural light, you can shoot usable product images immediately. Requirements:

1. North-facing window (or any window not in direct sun) — position desk 1-2 feet from window
2. White poster board ($1 at dollar store) — fold in an L-shape for background sweep
3. Second piece of white foam board — lean on the shadow side to reflect light back and eliminate harsh shadows
4. Smartphone on a small tripod or propped against a book for stability
5. Shoot between 10am and 3pm for consistent natural light

Total cost: $1-5. This setup is sufficient for Etsy launch images. The result will not match a professional studio setup but is good enough to convert if the product is well-positioned.

### Lightbox DIY (~$5–$15)

A cardboard box (12-18 inch cube), white tissue paper or wax paper over the sides, and two desk lamps creates a lightbox that eliminates all directional shadows and produces an even, soft white field.

Instructions:
1. Cut three windows (left side, right side, top) in a cardboard box, leaving 2-inch borders
2. Tape white tissue paper or parchment paper over each window (diffuser)
3. Line the interior with white poster board (floor + back wall seamless sweep)
4. Position desk lamps outside each window — they shine through the tissue paper and diffuse
5. Camera shoots through the open front

Cost: $5-15 depending on whether you have desk lamps already.

### Purchased Lightbox Kit ($25-45)

A collapsible LED lightbox (typically 16"×16"×16" or 20"×20"×20") with built-in LED strips and a white fabric interior is the most efficient starting point. Available from Neewer, Raleno, or similar brands. Produces consistent images with minimal setup, folds flat for storage. Recommended for any seller shipping more than 10 units per week who needs consistent photography.

### Ring Light Setup ($20-40)

A 10-inch ring light with a clamp arm and phone mount costs $20-35 and handles lifestyle shots and in-use desk shots well. Not ideal for lightbox work (ring creates distracting circular catch-light on plastic surfaces). Good for the Shot 2 (lifestyle hero) and Shot 10 (full desk context) shots.

## Part 6: Etsy and Amazon Photo Limits and Conversion Best Practices

### Etsy

- Maximum photos per listing: **10 photos + 1 video (5-15 seconds, max 100MB)**
- Recommended minimum resolution: 2000 × 2000 pixels (1:1 square aspect ratio)
- Minimum accepted: 635 pixels on the long side (listings below this rank lower in search)
- No background requirement for Etsy — white is recommended but gray, wood, and contextual backgrounds are all accepted
- First photo is the thumbnail in search results — it is the single most important image for click-through rate
- Listings using all 10 photo slots consistently outperform listings using 5 or fewer — each slot answers a buyer objection

**Conversion-optimized photo order for cable management products**:
1. White background hero (search thumbnail — clean, clear, professional)
2. In-use lifestyle (first thing buyer sees on listing page — shows function)
3. Before/after (highest conversion driver — shows outcome)
4. Scale reference (eliminates size confusion)
5. Snap-fit detail close-up (demonstrates quality)
6. Color variants flat-lay (enables color decision without leaving page)
7. 3-pack contents flat-lay (answers "what do I get?")
8. Angle view
9. Full desk context
10. Video (or packaging/brand trust shot)

### Amazon

- Maximum images: **9 (1 main image + 8 supplemental)**
- Main image requirements: **Pure white background (RGB 255,255,255)** — off-white or light gray will suppress the listing
- Main image: product must fill ≥85% of the frame; no text, logos, watermarks, or accessories not included
- Supplemental images (slots 2-9): No background restriction — lifestyle, infographics, callouts permitted
- Minimum resolution for zoom function: **1,000 pixels** on longest side (2,000+ recommended for quality zoom)
- File formats: JPEG, PNG, TIFF (no animated GIFs)

**For Amazon cable clip listings, the 8 supplemental images should be**:
1. In-use lifestyle (cables routed on a desk)
2. Feature callout infographic (snap arm labeled, dimensions called out, materials noted)
3. Before/after transformation
4. Installation step-by-step (3-frame composite: mount → insert cable → snap closed)
5. Size variants / compatibility guide (which cable diameters fit)
6. Color options
7. Pack contents
8. Scale / dimension drawing overlay

## Part 7: Editing — Free Tools

### Canva (free tier)

Best for: Creating infographic images (slot 2 or 3 on Amazon with feature callouts), before/after composites, adding dimension text overlays, resizing photos to exact pixel specifications. The free tier is sufficient for all product photography editing. Use "Magic Background Remover" (Canva Pro required) or the free "Background Remover" in Remove.bg.

### GIMP (free, desktop)

Best for: Background removal with more precision than AI tools, batch resizing, color correction, sharpening. Steeper learning curve than Canva. Use for cases where automated background removal fails (e.g., white product against white background where AI cannot distinguish edges).

### Snapseed (free, mobile)

Best for: On-device editing immediately after shooting on smartphone. Adjust exposure, whites, shadows, sharpness, and perspective correction. The "Healing" tool removes minor imperfections (dust, scratches on the sweep). Good for getting images listing-ready without a desktop editing workflow.

### Lightroom Mobile (free tier)

Best for: Batch editing — apply a preset once and sync the same corrections across all 10 photos in a shoot for visual consistency. Free tier unlocks basic preset creation and selective adjustments. Recommended for anyone shooting more than one product.

**Suggested preset values for cable clip photography** (consistent with existing lifestyle photography brief):
- Exposure: +0.5
- Contrast: +15
- Highlights: -10
- Shadows: +5
- Whites: +5
- Saturation: +10
- Vibrance: +8
- Clarity: +10
- Temperature: +200K (toward warm)

### Remove.bg (free, limited)

Removes backgrounds with one click. Works well for product photos against a non-white background that needs to become white for Amazon compliance. Free tier: 1 preview per image (full-resolution download requires credits or subscription at ~$9/month for 40 images). Sufficient for a batch of 20-30 images once per quarter.

## Part 8: What Top-Performing 3D Printed Part Listings Look Like

Based on analysis of consistently high-review Etsy shops selling printed functional parts (cable management, desk accessories, Gridfinity, workshop tools):

**Photo composition patterns of top performers**:

1. **Photo 1 is always white-background or near-white, product centered at 45° angle**. The product is the only object in frame. No cables, no desk, no props. This communicates "serious seller" immediately.

2. **Photo 2 is in-use lifestyle, clean desk, cable in product**. High-review cable management sellers (100+ reviews) almost universally have this as the second image — the "working in context" shot that proves the product is real and functional.

3. **Before/after composites are present in listings with >200 reviews**. This is not coincidence — the before/after transformation is the most emotionally compelling image for organizational products and directly drives add-to-cart behavior.

4. **Multiple color options are shown in a single photo (flat-lay)**. Buyers who see all color options on one image are more likely to add to cart immediately rather than leaving to compare alternatives.

5. **Infographic images with labeled features and dimensions appear in listings that achieve "Star Seller" status**. The feature callout image addresses the "is this sturdy?" and "what size cable does it hold?" questions without requiring the buyer to read the description.

6. **Consistent photography style across all listings in a shop**. Shops with high conversion rates use the same background, same lighting, and same editing across their entire catalog. This signals professionalism and builds brand trust.

**What top-performing listings do not do**:
- Do not use cell phone photos with mixed lighting, orange color casts, or visible backgrounds
- Do not use only 2-3 photos
- Do not use images where the product is small in the frame
- Do not use photos where the product is sitting on carpet, a wooden cutting board, or other surfaces that suggest amateur photography
- Do not use stock photos or renders (buyers can detect these and trust them less than real photos)

## Part 9: Photography Priority Sequence for This Project

Given the existing lifestyle photography brief (post-test-print-doc-3-lifestyle-photography-brief.md) and the platform requirements above, execute in this order:

**Stage 1 (Launch blocker — do these before listing)**: Shot 1 (white background hero), Shot 2 (in-use lifestyle), Shot 7 (color variants flat-lay). These three images are the minimum required to list competitively.

**Stage 2 (Within first week of listing)**: Shot 5 (snap-fit detail), Shot 9 (before/after). Adding these within the first week lifts conversion as initial traffic arrives.

**Stage 3 (After first 10 sales)**: Shot 6 (scale), Shot 8 (bundle contents), Shot 10 (full desk context). These address buyer objections that emerge from actual customer questions.

**Stage 4 (After first 25 sales)**: Shot 11 (video), infographic overlay versions for Amazon. At this stage you have real customer feedback to inform what the infographic should highlight.
