# ModRun Lifestyle Photography Brief

**Purpose**: Provide a photographer with complete creative direction to capture professional lifestyle photos of ModRun cable clips for Etsy listings, social media, and marketing.

**Timeline**: 2-4 days (shoot + editing)
**Delivery**: 15-20 edited JPGs + RAW files
**Budget estimate**: $200-400 depending on photographer experience

---

## 1. Creative Direction & Mood

### Overall Aesthetic

ModRun is **precise, minimal, purposeful**. The photography should feel like the product itself: clean lines, intentional design, no excess, high-quality execution.

**Mood board references**:
- MKBHD desk setup (clean, organized, tech-focused)
- Das Keyboard product shots (minimal background, product forward)
- Autonomous desk marketing photos (lifestyle + functionality)
- Notebookers on YouTube desk tours (real desk, real cables, organized)

**What the photos should feel like**:
- Professional but approachable (not sterile studio photography)
- Functional and honest (real cable routing, real desk, not art direction that doesn't apply)
- Minimalist (negative space, clean backgrounds, nothing cluttered)
- Warm but controlled (natural light preferred, no harsh shadows, no overly warm color cast)

**What to avoid**:
- Over-saturated colors or overly warm/cool color grading
- Cluttered desks (even in "before" shots, the clutter should look organic, not staged chaos)
- Hands that look unnatural (only include if gesturing naturally)
- Branding or logos visible on clips (the clips should be plain)
- Anything that looks "produced for a tutorial" — these should feel like photos of real desks, not demonstrations

## 2. Color Palette & Environment

### Desk/Surface Colors (Primary)

- **Light gray or white laminate desk** (modern office aesthetic; most common desk color)
- **Warm wood surface** (if available; adds natural warmth, common in home offices)
- **Matte black desktop** (for gaming/tech setup photos)

**Minimize in background**:
- Avoid bright colors, posters, or branded materials
- Neutral wall (white, light gray, or soft beige)
- If shooting at your home: use an uncluttered room (home office, study, or living room corner)

### Cable & Clip Colors

**ModRun clips** (your products):
- Black, white, gray (matte finish PLA+)
- Should pop slightly against the desk but not aggressively

**Cables** (environment; use real cables for realism):
- Primary colors: Black USB, white USB-C, gray power cables
- Secondary: Blue/teal USB (often looks premium)
- Mix of cable gauges (thin USB, medium 3.5mm audio, thick power cable) to show versatility

**Lighting**:
- Soft natural light (north-facing window, or diffused studio light)
- Color temperature: Daylight (5500K) for neutral whites, or warm (3200K) for wood desks
- No harsh shadows; use bounce boards if needed to fill shadow areas

## 3. Shot List (12-15 Total Shots)

Deliver these specific shot types. Numbers indicate how many of each.

### Hero/Lifestyle Shots (3 total)

**Hero Shot #1 — Desk Setup with Clips**
- ModRun black or white clip holding USB cable on a clean light-gray desk
- Cable is threaded through the snap-fit channel, clearly visible
- Desk surface shows minimal other items (maybe a keyboard in soft focus, or empty)
- Angle: 45° from the desk, slightly elevated (eye-level if standing over the desk)
- Focus: The clip and cable entry point
- Background: Soft focus (blurred) desk surface and neutral wall
- Lighting: Soft natural light from the side, no harsh shadows
- **Use case**: Primary photo for Etsy listing (this will be your thumbnail)

**Hero Shot #2 — Angled Multi-Clip View**
- 2-3 ModRun clips on the same desk area, each holding a different cable
- Shows how a "system" looks (multiple clips in use)
- Different cable types visible (USB, audio, power)
- Angle: 30° angle, wider frame than Shot #1
- Focus: All clips visible and in focus
- **Use case**: Social media, lifestyle context, "see how it scales" narrative

**Hero Shot #3 — Minimal Context (Clip + Cable)**
- Single ModRun clip, single cable, minimal desk detail in frame
- Clean, almost "product photography" feel but still in-context
- Focus: The clip and how cable sits in the channel
- Background: Neutral (white or light gray), completely uncluttered
- **Use case**: Instagram post, Pinterest, versatile for marketing

**Detail Shot #3 — Cable-in-Channel Profile**

- Macro or mid-range focal length
- **Use case**: Demonstrates safety (cables aren't being pinched)

**Detail Shot #4 — Color Options Flat-Lay**
- All available colors (black, white, gray) shown side-by-side
- Overhead camera (90° angle), flat-lay style
- White or light gray background (poster board)
- Even lighting across all clips
- Depth of field: All clips in sharp focus
- **Use case**: Product variant showcase

## 4. Lighting Specifications

### Preferred Setup: Soft Natural Light

**Best location**: North-facing window (consistent, non-harsh light throughout the day)

**Setup**:
- Desk placed near window (but not in direct harsh sunlight)
- Soft diffusion between window and desk (sheer curtain, or white diffusion panel)
- Bounce boards on opposite side of desk to fill shadows (white foam board, 2'×3' minimum)
- No direct hard shadows on clips or cables

**Color temperature**: 5500K (daylight, neutral whites)

### Alternative: Studio Setup (If Natural Light Not Available)

**Equipment**:
- Key light: 3-4 ft softbox at 45° angle, 2-3 ft from desk (main light)
- Fill light: Bounced flash or second softbox at 90° opposite (optional, reduces shadows)
- Background: White or light gray poster board (3'×4' minimum), 3-4 ft behind desk

**Power settings**: 1/2-3/4 power on key light (not harsh, soft)

**Wattage**: 100-200W equivalent (softbox-mounted)

---

## 5. Camera & Lens Recommendations

### For Wide Shots (Desk Context, Before/After)

**Focal length**: 24-35mm equivalent
**Aperture**: f/4-f/5.6 (enough depth of field to keep desk in focus)
**Shutter speed**: 1/125-1/250 (fast enough for natural light, no camera shake)
**ISO**: Keep low (100-400) for clean image

---

### For Detail Shots (Snap-Fit, Close-Up)

**Focal length**: 50-100mm macro (or close-focusing lens)
**Aperture**: f/5.6-f/8 (keeps snap-fit sharp, slight background blur)
**Minimum focus distance**: Should be able to focus within 6-12 inches of subject
**Working distance**: At least 12 inches from clip to lens (don't crowd the subject)

---

### For Flat-Lay Shots (Color Options)

**Focal length**: 35-50mm (not too wide, not too narrow)
**Angle**: Directly overhead (90° perpendicular to surface)
**Aperture**: f/5.6-f/8 (all clips need to be sharp)
**Tripod**: Essential (overhead camera position is impossible without tripod)

## 6. Styling & Composition Notes

### Styling the Desk

**Before clips**:
- Cables should look realistically tangled (not aggressively messy, but loose and clearly disorganized)
- A few other desk items present (keyboard, mouse, monitor) for realism
- Natural clutter (not staged chaos)

**After clips**:
- Same items, same desk
- Cables routed through ModRun clips
- Cable routing looks natural (follows the desk edge, doesn't look awkward)
- Clips positioned where cables naturally run
- Clips secure cables firmly (cables shouldn't look loose in the channel)

### Prop Considerations

**Optional props that enhance (use sparingly)**:
- Hands (only if naturally gesturing, placing a clip, or showing scale)
- A coffee mug or small object for scale reference
- A plant in the background (adds life, stays soft-focused)

**Avoid**:
- Branded cups or items with logos
- User's workspace items (personal photos, personal items should be blurred or removed)
- Anything that dates the photo (calendar, specific branding)
- Over-decoration that distracts from the clips

### Hand Styling (If Including Hands)

**Hands work best when**:
- Naturally placing or adjusting a clip
- Showing the snap-fit mechanism (finger pressing the engagement tab)
- Showing cable scale (hand holding both clip and cable to show relative size)

**Hands styling**:
- Clean, well-lit hands
- Maybe light nail polish or natural nails (nothing distracting)
- Hands should be blurred slightly if not the focus (f/5.6 helps with this)

## 7. Post-Processing Guidance

### Lightroom Preset Values (Apply to All Shots for Consistency)

After shooting, batch-edit all photos in Lightroom using these settings:

```
Exposure: +0.5 (brightens image slightly)
Contrast: +15 (adds punch without looking over-processed)
Highlights: -10 (tames blown-out highlights)
Shadows: +5 (lifts dark areas slightly)
Whites: +5 (brightens pure whites in the image)
Blacks: -2 (deepens blacks slightly)
Saturation: +10 (colors pop slightly, but not overly vivid)
Vibrance: +8 (increases saturation of muted colors more than already-saturated ones)
Clarity: +10 (adds micro-contrast, makes details pop)
Temperature: +200 K (adds warmth if shooting under daylight; adjust if shooting with warm tungsten)
Tint: -5 (neutralizes any magenta cast, if present)
```

**Why these settings**:
- Slightly warm (not cold or clinical)
- Punchy but not over-processed
- Consistent across all photos
- Cables and clips look vibrant but realistic

### What NOT to Do

- ✗ No heavy filters or artistic presets
- ✗ No excessive vignetting (dark edges)
- ✗ No heavy watermarks (small logo only if necessary)
- ✗ No heavy noise reduction (makes images look plastic)
- ✗ No HDR processing (looks artificial)

### Color Grading

- Neutral color balance (daylight-balanced; 5500K)
- No strong color cast (no orange/purple tint)
- Blacks should be true black, not crushed
- Whites should be bright but not blown out

### Sharpening

- Standard sharpening: Amount 80-120, Radius 0.8-1.0, Detail 15-25 (in Lightroom)
- Apply sharpening equally to all photos (for consistency)

## 11. Where to Find a Photographer

### Options by Budget

**Option A: Fiverr/Upwork (Budget, $150-300)**
- Search: "product photography," "lifestyle photography," "desktop photography"
- Look for: Reviews 4.8+, portfolio shows cable/tech products
- Pros: Quick turnaround, affordable, easy process
- Cons: Variable quality, may need direction, revisions may be limited

**Option B: Local Freelancer (Moderate, $250-400)**
- Search: Instagram hashtags #[YourCity]photographer or local Facebook groups
- Look for: Portfolio showing desk/lifestyle work, testimonials mentioning quick turnaround
- Pros: Can meet in person, higher control over shoot, better quality usually
- Cons: May need to travel to them or wait for availability

**Option C: Photography Student (Budget, $150-250)**
- Contact local colleges' photography programs
- Often students need portfolio pieces and work for lower rates
- Pros: Eager to please, creative, affordable
- Cons: May have less experience, longer editing timeline

## Final Reminders for Photographer

- **Minimal aesthetic**: Every shot should feel clean and intentional, not cluttered
- **Functional narrative**: Show how cables actually fit in the clips, how they're held, what they look like in real use
- **Consistency**: All photos should feel like they're from the same shoot, same time, same aesthetic
- **No over-production**: This isn't a luxury watch commercial; it's a real-desk product photo
- **Cable realism**: Use real cables of various gauges and types; don't use placeholder materials
- **Lighting**: Soft and even; no harsh shadows or glare on plastic surfaces

Your job is to make these small cable clips look like a premium product through clean, intentional photography. You're building trust that this is a well-designed, thoughtfully made product worth buying.
