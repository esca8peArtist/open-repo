## Risk Scenarios

### Scenario A: Slow Algorithm Traction (most likely in Week 1–2)
**Signs:** < 25 impressions by Day 3, < 2% CTR by Day 5.  
**Action:** Revise tags using Etsy Autocomplete (type "cable" and note what auto-completes — those are high-volume terms). Do not change the title URL. If CTR is the issue, swap the primary photo.

### Scenario B: No Sales by Day 10
**Signs:** Views are accumulating but conversion is 0%.  
**Action:** Check these in order: (1) Is price too high vs. zero-review competitors? Drop $1.50 for 5-day test. (2) Is the description clear on value in the first 2 sentences? (3) Are photos professional? A $150 Fiverr photographer investment here can pay back in 10× within 30 days.

### Scenario C: Test Print Minor Fail (snap arm too tight/loose)
**Signs:** Snap arm requires excessive force to engage, or releases under light cable pull.  
**Action:** Adjust the `FDM_TOLERANCE` parameter in `cadquery/modrun_clip_b123d.py`. ±0.05mm per iteration. One reprint cycle = 1–2 hours. Maximum 2 iterations before launching. A "working but slightly stiff" clip is acceptable for launch — note it and plan a v1.1 revision after 20 sales.

### Scenario D: Etsy Listing Flagged for Originality Review
**Signs:** Listing removed or visibility suppressed with an "originality" flag.  
**Action:** The git commit history in this repo (cadquery/ directory) demonstrates original design provenance. Export a PDF of the commit history and upload as supporting evidence to Etsy's seller review process. The CadQuery parametric scripts are the key evidence — they prove this was designed from code, not traced from another product.
