# Etsy Listing & Launch Checklist

**Lead finding:** The single highest-leverage action before hitting "Publish" is getting the first photo right. Etsy's search algorithm ranks listings partly on click-through rate, and click-through rate is determined almost entirely by the thumbnail of Photo 1. A mediocre listing with a great hero photo will outperform a perfect listing with a mediocre photo. This checklist treats the photo workflow as the critical path, not the copy.

---

## Pre-Launch: What Needs to Be Ready Before You Touch Etsy

Do not open Etsy draft listings until all of the following are done:

- [ ] Test print passed all quality gates (see `post-test-print-stl-refinement.md`)
- [ ] At least 12 production-ready clips in the finished goods bin
- [ ] Hero photo taken (see Photo Workflow section below)
- [ ] Price confirmed against the cost model (see Pricing Confirmation below)
- [ ] 13 tags finalized (see Tag Finalization below)

Publishing before you have inventory and photos is the fastest way to accumulate the one metric that kills new Etsy shops: a high click-through rate with zero conversion. Buyers click, find no reviews and an average photo, and leave. The algorithm penalizes this.

## Section 1: Photo Upload Workflow

This is the most time-sensitive section. Do it before anything else because good photos require iteration — you will likely need to reshoot at least one.

### 1.1 What to Photograph

You need exactly 5 photos for the launch listing. More is better but diminishing returns; 5 is the minimum.

**Photo 1 — Hero (most critical):**
- The clip installed in a rail segment, holding a cable
- Background: clean wood desk surface or white/light grey card
- Lighting: natural window light (overcast day is ideal — soft, no harsh shadows)
- Framing: clip is the subject; the cable entering the snap-fit is visible and in focus
- What to avoid: hand in frame, messy background, competing objects

**Photo 2 — Detail shot:**
- Extreme close-up of the snap arm and cable bore
- Shows the engineering quality — layer lines are fine, geometry is crisp
- Same lighting as Photo 1
- Macro mode or close-focusing on your phone camera

**Photo 3 — Installation sequence:**
- A simple two-panel or before/after composition
- Left or top: cables loose on a desk
- Right or bottom: same cables routed and clipped
- This is the highest-conversion photo for buyers who are on the fence

**Photo 4 — Color flat-lay:**
- All available colors laid out on white/light grey background
- Overhead angle (phone directly above, perpendicular to surface)
- Even lighting — tape a piece of white card on the ceiling-facing side of your window to diffuse

**Photo 5 — Scale reference / in-context:**
- The clip next to a keyboard, or on a real desk setup
- Establishes scale and context for buyers who have never seen the product

### 1.2 From Test Print Photos to Etsy

Your test print photos (taken during the quality gate process) can serve as placeholder photos while you get better shots — but should not be your launch photos. The quality gate photos are diagnostic, not marketing.

**If you want to use test print photos temporarily:**
- The dimensional measurement shots with calipers in frame are useful as Photo 2 (detail) — they signal precision
- Crop out the measurement setup, keep the close-up of the snap feature
- Do not use as Photo 1 (hero) — calipers in frame read as "prototype" not "product"

**Etsy Mockup Generator (for listing preview before physical photos are ready):**
- Etsy's built-in mockup tool is under the listing editor's photo section
- Select "Create a mockup" and choose a desk/flat-lay template
- Upload your best test print photo
- The mockup adds a product-on-desk context without requiring a lifestyle photography session
- Use this as a temporary Photo 1 placeholder only — replace with a real photo within 48 hours of launch

### 1.3 Photo Specifications

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum resolution | 1000×1000px (Etsy minimum for zoom) |
| Aspect ratio | Square (1:1) — set this in camera or crop before upload |
| File format | JPEG preferred; PNG acceptable |
| File size | Under 10MB per photo |
| Naming | Use descriptive names: modrun-hero.jpg, modrun-detail-snap.jpg |
| Color grading | Neutral white balance; no aggressive filters |

**Phone camera settings (iPhone or Android):**
- Turn off Live Photo (creates a JPEG + video file; just upload the JPEG)
- Use portrait mode for Photos 1 and 2 (background blur)
- Use standard mode for Photos 4 and 5 (you want everything in focus)
- Shoot in natural light, not under fluorescent ceiling lights (color cast)

## Section 2: Title Tag Optimization

### 2.1 Confirmed Title Options

Reference: `post-test-print-doc-2-etsy-listing-design-templates.md` has three title options documented. Below is the launch decision with reasoning.

**Recommended launch title:**
`ModRun Cable Management Clip | 3D Printed | Snap-Fit Original Design`

Character count: 67 characters (well under the 92-character Etsy display limit)

**Why this specific phrasing:**
- "Cable Management Clip" is the highest-volume search term in this category (verified via Etsy autocomplete)
- "3D Printed" signals handmade/original to Etsy's algorithm and to buyers who filter for handmade
- "Snap-Fit Original Design" combines a functional keyword with an Etsy policy compliance signal
- The ModRun brand name is at the front — builds brand recall from first impression

**Alternative title for the rail listing (separate listing):**
`ModRun Cable Management Rail | Modular 3D Printed Desk Organizer`

**What NOT to include in titles:**
- Price ("$12 Cable Clip") — Etsy prohibits this in titles
- "Best" or "Premium" without evidence — reduces trust
- More than 3 pipe-delimited segments — becomes unreadable at thumbnail size
- Color names in the main title — put in listing variants, not the title

### 2.2 Tags — Final 13

These 13 tags are the result of balancing search volume (high-volume terms get more impressions) with competition (low-competition terms convert better from fewer impressions). Do not use the same phrase in the title and tags — Etsy already indexes title words; duplicating them in tags wastes tag slots.

| # | Tag | Search Volume Signal | Competition |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | cable management | High | High |
| 2 | cable organizer | High | High |
| 3 | desk accessories | High | Medium |
| 4 | desk cable clips | Medium | Low |
| 5 | 3d printed gift | Medium | Low-Medium |
| 6 | office gadgets | Medium | Low |
| 7 | cable holder | Medium | Medium |
| 8 | home office decor | High | High |
| 9 | workspace setup | Medium | Low |
| 10 | cable clips desk | Medium | Low |
| 11 | desk organizer set | Medium | Medium |
| 12 | wire management | Medium | Medium |
| 13 | gaming setup accessories | Medium | Low-Medium |

**Tag refinement protocol (Week 2 and monthly):**
1. In Etsy Ads dashboard, look at "impressions by tag" after 7 days of listing being live
2. Any tag with zero impressions in 7 days is either too specific or misspelled — replace with a variation
3. Any tag with high impressions and no clicks may be a mismatch between what searchers expect and what your listing shows — consider replacing
4. Keep the 3–4 top-performing tags forever; rotate the bottom 3–4 monthly

### 2.3 Category Selection

Navigate to: **Craft Supplies & Tools > Organization** or **Home & Living > Storage & Organization > Desktop Organizers**

Test both categories in the Etsy category selector and choose the one that shows more active 3D-printed competitors (indicates this is where the category traffic concentrates for this product type).

**Taxonomy note:** Etsy's June 2025 original-design policy update did not change category structure, but it did reduce the number of active listings in the 3D printing sub-categories. The remaining active listings in "Desktop Organizers" are more likely to be original-design sellers — meaning less competition from STL-marketplace prints.

## Section 3: Pricing Confirmation

### 3.1 Cost Model at Launch Volume (20 units/week)

Pull from `cost-model-spreadsheet.csv` row "20/week":

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Material (PLA+ at $12/kg, 75g blended weight) | $0.90 |
| Packaging (mailer $0.08, zip bag $0.03, card $0.05) | $0.16 |
| Shipping (USPS Ground Advantage via Pirate Ship, blended) | $4.10 |
| Equipment depreciation | $0.05 |
| Etsy fees (6.5% transaction + 3% payment on $24.99) | $2.37 |
| **Total COGS** | **$7.58** |
| **Revenue at $24.99** | **$24.99** |
| **Net margin** | **$17.41 (69.7%)** |

**Gross margin check:** Target is 72–73% at 20 units/week as documented. The 69.7% in this model reflects the 8% USPS surcharge in effect through January 2027 increasing shipping cost. If you set listings to "free shipping included" at $24.99 and ship via Pirate Ship at $4.10 vs. the $5.50 retail baseline, the margin is intact.

### 3.2 Launch Price Decision

**Recommended launch price structure:**

| SKU | Description | Price | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single clip, 6mm bore | 1× clip, black | $12.00 | ~69% |
| Single clip, 8mm bore | 1× clip, black | $12.00 | ~69% |
| 3-clip bundle | 3× clips, one bore size | $29.99 | ~72% |
| Rail (desk clamp) | 1× desk-clamp rail | $14.99 | ~68% |
| Starter kit | 1× rail + 3× clips | $38.99 | ~73% |

**Pricing rationale:**
- Single clips at $12 are at the bottom of the "standard" Etsy tier — easy first purchase, drives reviews
- The 3-clip bundle at $29.99 is the target conversion (72% margin, higher AOV)
- The starter kit at $38.99 is the highest-margin SKU — push this as the hero product in listings
- Do not launch the premium tier (PETG, $99+ kits) until you have 30+ reviews — premium buyers want social proof before committing

**First-30-days promotional pricing:**
Consider launching the starter kit at $34.99 (discounted from $38.99) for the first 30 days to accelerate the first 10–15 sales and reviews. Recover the margin once you have review velocity. At $34.99 the margin is approximately 70% — still healthy.

### 3.3 Etsy Free Shipping vs. Buyer-Paid Shipping

**Recommendation: include shipping in the price (list as "free shipping").**

Etsy's algorithm gives a marginal ranking boost to free-shipping listings. More importantly, buyers filter for free shipping. At your price points ($12–$39), buyers who see a $4.50 shipping fee on top feel nickel-and-dimed even though the total price is the same.

**How to bake shipping in:**
- At a $4.10–4.50 blended shipping cost, add $4.50 to every SKU price and list as free shipping
- Single clip: $16.50 → round to $16.99 or $17.00 (psychological pricing, just under $17)
- 3-clip bundle: $34.49 → $34.99
- Starter kit: $43.49 → $44.99 (or stay at $38.99 and absorb shipping into the existing margin — still above 68%)

**Simpler approach for launch:** Start with buyer-paid shipping. You can switch to free shipping in Month 2 once you've confirmed your actual blended shipping cost from real orders. Switching from "buyer pays" to "free shipping" mid-launch requires repricing all listings but takes only 30 minutes.

## Section 4: Initial Listing Review Checklist

Complete this checklist for every listing before hitting "Publish." Run through it twice — once when you first create the draft, and once immediately before publishing.

### Pre-Publish Checklist (per listing)

**Title:**
- [ ] Under 92 characters
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 40 characters
- [ ] No price, no "best," no "premium" without evidence
- [ ] Brand name (ModRun) present

**Photos:**
- [ ] Photo 1 is a lifestyle/hero shot, not a flat product shot
- [ ] Minimum 5 photos uploaded
- [ ] All photos are at least 1000×1000px
- [ ] No blur visible when zoomed on mobile (test this before publishing)
- [ ] Photos uploaded in logical sequence (hero → detail → context → colors → scale)

**Description:**
- [ ] First 150 words answer: what is it, what does it do, who is it for
- [ ] Bore diameter sizes listed explicitly
- [ ] Material (PLA+) and temperature rating mentioned
- [ ] Processing time accurate (3–5 business days)
- [ ] Custom color availability mentioned

**Pricing:**
- [ ] Price aligns with this document's pricing table
- [ ] Shipping profile configured correctly (or "free shipping" selected with price adjusted)
- [ ] All variants (bore sizes, colors) have prices set — no empty variant fields

**Tags:**
- [ ] All 13 tag slots filled
- [ ] No tag exceeds 20 characters (Etsy limit)
- [ ] No tag duplicates a word already in the title

**Category and attributes:**
- [ ] Category selected (Desktop Organizers or equivalent)
- [ ] Material attribute set to "Plastic"
- [ ] Made to order attribute confirmed
- [ ] Processing time set to "3–5 business days" (do not use 1–2 until you have a 12-clip inventory buffer)

**Policies:**
- [ ] Return policy: "I accept returns within 14 days for unused items. Contact me first."
- [ ] Custom order policy: "Custom orders welcome. Message me before purchasing."
- [ ] Processing time and shipping time estimates are realistic (do not promise 2-day if you can't deliver it)

## Section 5: Publish Sequence

**Optimal publish timing:** Thursday, 10 AM – 2 PM in the timezone where most of your target buyers live. For US cable management buyers, that is US Central or Eastern. If you are not in these timezones, convert accordingly.

**Why Thursday:** Etsy's algorithm appears to give a brief "freshness" boost to new listings, and search traffic peaks Thursday–Saturday for desk accessory categories (weekend browsing and home office purchases). Publishing Thursday morning positions you for the highest-traffic window of the week.

**Publish order:**
1. Publish the starter kit listing first (highest margin, best for AOV)
2. Publish the 3-clip bundle second
3. Publish single clips (6mm, 8mm, 12mm bores) last
4. Do not publish the rail listing until you have at least 2–3 reviews on the clip listings — first buyers are less likely to invest in the rail system before they've seen reviews

**Immediately after publishing:**
- [ ] Search "cable management clip" on Etsy — confirm your listing appears in the first 3–4 pages
- [ ] Enable Etsy Ads at $1/day budget (gives algorithm data even if organic traffic is low)
- [ ] Share the listing URL on at least one social channel (Reddit r/battlestations, Instagram, wherever your first customers are)

**If your listing does not appear in search within 24 hours:**
- This is normal — new listings sometimes take 24–48 hours to index
- Do not republish or delete and re-create — this resets any freshness signal
- Check that your listing is not in "Draft" status
