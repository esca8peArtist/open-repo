Before starting any step in this checklist, confirm all three conditions are true:

## Part 1: Account Setup (Week 1, Days 1–3)

### 1.1 Amazon Professional Seller Account

- [ ] Navigate to sell.amazon.com → "Start selling"
- [ ] Select "Professional" plan ($39.99/month; waived first month for new sellers; waived from Month 2 onward if Handmade approval granted)
- [ ] Provide:
  - Legal name (individual or LLC)
  - Business address
  - Bank account for disbursements (same bank account as Etsy or a dedicated business account)
  - Credit card for Amazon charges
  - Government-issued ID (SSN or EIN)
  - Phone number for two-step verification
- [ ] Complete identity verification (takes 1–3 business days; may require a video call)
- [ ] Confirm account email and enable two-step verification (mandatory; SMS or authenticator app)

**Time estimate**: 30–45 minutes to complete application; 1–3 days for verification approval.

### 1.2 Amazon Handmade Category Application

Amazon Handmade requires a separate application within your Professional Seller account. Approval is manual and typically takes 2–5 business days.

- [ ] In Seller Central: Navigate to "Sell on Amazon Handmade" landing page (search "Amazon Handmade" in Seller Central search)
- [ ] Click "Apply now"
- [ ] Complete the "About You" section:
  - Business type: Individual artisan / small production studio
  - Production method: Digital design (CadQuery CAD software) → FDM 3D printing (Bambu P1S)
  - Production location: [Your state], USA
  - Number of employees: 1 (sole proprietor)
- [ ] Complete the "Your Products and Process" section:
  - Materials used: PLA+ (polylactic acid) filament, premium grade; no ABS, no toxic materials
  - How items are made: "I design original cable management products using CadQuery open-source CAD software. I 3D print each item on a Bambu P1S FDM printer using PLA+ filament. All designs are original and created by me."
  - Upload 3–5 process photos:
    1. Photo of Bambu P1S printer with a clip on the print bed (print-in-progress shot)
    2. Photo of finished clips with calipers (quality inspection)
    3. Photo of packaged order ready for shipment
  - Product categories: Home (primary), Kitchen & Dining (secondary)

**Critical for approval**: Do not be vague. Amazon rejects applications that say "I make things with a printer." Specific material names (PLA+), specific equipment models (Bambu P1S), and original-design emphasis are the approval signal. ModRun's CadQuery design workflow is a legitimate artisan production process and should be described in detail.

**Professional plan fee waiver**: Upon Handmade approval, Amazon waives the $39.99/month Professional plan fee from Month 2 onward. First month is $39.99 (or free if within the New Seller Incentives 1-month free window). Budget for one month of plan fees only.

### 1.3 Brand Registry Application

Brand Registry is required to access Amazon Vine (critical for bypassing the cold-start review problem). Register during account setup, not after.

- [ ] Ensure you have or create a registered trademark for "ModRun"
  - **If no trademark yet**: Amazon now accepts pending trademark applications through IP Accelerator. File a trademark with TESS (USPTO) — the application itself (not just approval) can enable Brand Registry enrollment. USPTO trademark application: ~$250–$350.
  - **If trademark already filed/approved**: Proceed directly.
- [ ] In Seller Central: Navigate to Brand Registry → Enroll a brand
- [ ] Enter brand name, trademark registration number or application number, product categories, and list of ASIN prefixes
- [ ] Submit; Brand Registry review takes 3–7 business days
- [ ] Upon approval, New Seller Incentives unlock:
  - $200 Sponsored Products credits (90-day window from account creation)
  - $200 Vine program credits (6-month window from account creation)
  - $100 coupon credits
  - FBA New Selection benefits (free storage and removals for first 90 days per new ASIN)

**Cost note**: Brand Registry itself is free. The cost is the trademark application ($250–$350 USPTO filing fee). This is optional but high-value — the $200 Vine credit alone generates 15–30 early reviews that would otherwise cost $500–$1,500 in Sponsored Products spend to produce organically.

**Economy tier exclusion rationale**: At $8.99, Amazon FBA fees are $4.02/unit (44.7% of sale price). COGS $1.40 + fees $4.02 + packaging $0.45 = $5.87, leaving $3.12 net margin (34.7%). On Etsy, the same product yields $6.00+ net margin (66.7%). Never list the Economy tier on Amazon FBA.

**Recommended Phase 2 launch order**:
1. Starter Bundle ($28.99) — highest volume, best margin, prime search term target
2. Pro Expansion Pack ($38.99) — highest per-unit net revenue, appeals to established buyers
3. Standard 3-Pack Clips ($22.99) — entry SKU for Amazon buyers who won't buy a full bundle

Add Headphone Hook and Magnetic Bin Labels only after the cable clip SKUs have 20+ reviews and stable FBA velocity.

### 2.2 FBA Size Tier Classification

All ModRun SKUs fall in the **Small Standard-Size** tier (the cheapest FBA fulfillment tier).

Size tier requirements: ≤ 15 × 12 × 0.75 inches, ≤ 16 oz packaged weight.

| SKU | Packaged Dimensions | Packaged Weight | Size Tier | 2026 FBA Fee (incl. surcharge) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basic Clip Set (3–5 clips, PLA) | ~6 × 4 × 1 in | ~1.5–2.5 oz | Small Standard | $3.22 |
| Starter Bundle (rail + 3 clips) | ~8 × 4 × 2 in | ~4–6 oz | Small Standard | $3.41 |
| Pro Expansion Pack (5 clips + accessories) | ~7 × 5 × 2 in | ~3–4 oz | Small Standard | $3.31 |
| Headphone Hook | ~8 × 3 × 2 in | ~3–4 oz | Small Standard | $3.31 |
| Magnetic Labels 10-pack | ~6 × 4 × 1 in | ~4–6 oz | Small Standard | $3.41 |

**Packaging for FBA compliance** (differs from Etsy packaging):
- Each unit requires an individual poly bag (LDPE, transparent, with suffocation warning if bag opening > 5 inches)
- Each unit requires an FNSKU barcode label (printed from Seller Central) on the outside of the poly bag
- Each unit requires a scannable product barcode (FNSKU, not UPC) visible without opening packaging
- Kits/bundles require a "Sold as Set" sticker if multiple units are bagged together
- Batteries: no batteries in ModRun products → no additional hazmat compliance required
- No loose parts: all accessories (hardware, screws) must be in a sealed secondary bag inside the main poly bag

### 2.3 Inbound Shipment Requirements (2026)

Effective January 15, 2026, Amazon no longer offers prep or labeling services at FCs. All inventory must arrive fully prepped and labeled.

Pre-shipment checklist for each FBA batch:
- [ ] All units individually bagged in LDPE poly bags with suffocation warning
- [ ] FNSKU label on each poly bag (no scratches, no covering barcodes)
- [ ] Master carton labeled with Amazon shipment ID label (printed from Seller Central)
- [ ] Master carton labeled with FC address (generated in shipment plan)
- [ ] No packing peanuts or loose fill that could damage units during FC receiving automation
- [ ] Carton weight under 50 lbs (standard limit; no special handling required for ModRun batch sizes)

**Inbound placement fee (first batch — New Selection Program)**:
- First 100 units per new ASIN are exempt from inbound placement fees under the New Selection Program
- Phase 2 first batch of 50 units: $0 inbound placement fee
- After exemption expires: $0.21–$0.30/unit for minimal split (single FC); $0 for Amazon-optimized multi-FC split (operationally complex for small sellers; acceptable to pay the single-split fee)

## Part 4: Advertising Setup (Phase 2 Launch)

### 4.1 Vine Enrollment (Priority: Highest)

**Why this is the first advertising action, not Sponsored Products**:
The Amazon algorithm cold-start prevents organic ranking until a listing has 20–30 reviews with 4.3+ average. Vine generates 15–30 reviews from verified Vine Voice reviewers in 4–6 weeks. The $200 New Seller Incentives credit covers the first Vine enrollment (up to 30 units provided to Vine reviewers at no cost to the seller).

**Vine enrollment steps**:
1. Complete Brand Registry (prerequisite)
2. In Seller Central: Advertising → Vine → Enroll product
3. Select Starter Bundle (primary ASIN) — enroll first, before Pro Expansion Pack
4. Set review units: 20–30 units (covered by $200 NSI credit; beyond 30 units costs $200 flat)
5. Products must be in stock in FBA warehouse before Vine enrollment processes
6. Timeline: Vine reviews begin appearing 2–4 weeks after enrollment; bulk of reviews arrive at 4–6 weeks

**Expected outcome**: 15–25 verified Vine reviews within 6 weeks of inventory going live.

### 4.2 Sponsored Products Campaign

Launch Sponsored Products only after Vine enrollment is active (not before — running paid traffic to a listing with 0 reviews generates clicks that don't convert, wasting budget and suppressing the conversion rate signal).

**Campaign structure**:

| Campaign | Targeting | Bid | Daily Budget | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cable Management — Broad | "cable management," "desk cable organizer," "cable clips desk" | $0.60/click | $2.00 | Broad reach, keyword discovery |
| Cable Management — Exact | "3D printed cable clips," "modular cable management" | $0.80/click | $1.00 | Conversion-focused, lower competition |
| Competitor targeting | Robbosales ASIN, Infinaprint3d ASIN (product targeting) | $0.50/click | $1.00 | Capture competitor browser traffic |

**Total daily budget**: $4.00/day ($120/month)

**Optimization timeline**:
- Week 1–2: Let campaigns run without adjustment (data collection)
- Week 3: Pause keywords with 0 clicks after 100 impressions; increase bids on keywords with ACOS < 30%
- Week 4–6: Target ACOS 25–35% (sustainable at ModRun's ~70% gross margin on Starter Bundle)
- Month 2: Reduce bids on all campaigns by 10%; hold on converting keywords; use remaining NSI ad credit

**ACOS target explanation**: At a $28.99 price point with ~65% gross margin, the maximum viable ACOS is 65% (breakeven). Targeting 25–35% ACOS generates 30–40% gross margin on ad-attributed orders — acceptable for early phase when review velocity is the strategic goal, not short-term profit.

## Part 5: Performance Monitoring and Checkpoints

### 5.1 Launch Checkpoint (Day 60 After Inventory Live)

Evaluate at 60 days post-launch:

| Metric | Pass Threshold | Fail Action |
|---|---|---|
| FBA unit sales | ≥ 30 units sold | < 30: Audit listing quality; increase Sponsored Products budget |
| Amazon reviews | ≥ 15 reviews | < 15: Vine not yet delivering; check Vine enrollment status |
| Review average | ≥ 4.0 stars | < 4.0: Quality issue; stop FBA restocking; fix product first |
| Sponsored Products ACOS | ≤ 40% | > 40%: Pause broad campaign; focus spend on exact-match only |
| FBA inventory remaining | > 10 units | ≤ 10: Calculate restock timing (10–16 day lead time) |
| Amazon ODR (Order Defect Rate) | < 1% | ≥ 1%: Pause all FBA; investigate returns immediately |

**If all metrics pass at Day 60**: Proceed to Phase 2 expansion — print 75-unit restock batch, expand to all three primary SKUs, increase Sponsored Products to $6/day.

**If one or more metrics fail at Day 60**: Diagnose before restocking. Do not send new inventory to Amazon while listing quality or product quality is unresolved.

### 5.2 Inventory Replenishment Schedule

FBA inventory lead time is 10–16 days (print → prep → ship → FC receiving). At 30+ units/month FBA velocity:

- Reorder trigger: FBA inventory drops below 25 units (covers lead time + safety buffer)
- Batch size: 75 units at 30–50 units/month velocity (provides 45–60 day sell-through window)
- Never let FBA stock hit zero: losing the Prime badge requires re-receiving new inventory before Prime eligibility restores; organic ranking drops during stockout

**Low Inventory Level Fee (LILL) protection**:
- First 180 days after first inventory receipt: Exempt from LILL (new-ASIN protection)
- After Day 180: At 30 units/month (~1 unit/day), maintain minimum 35 units in FBA to stay above 28-day threshold and avoid the $0.32–$0.89/unit LILL fee
- Practical batch sizing after Day 180: Always send at least 35-unit batches; ideally 50–75 units

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon Professional plan (first month) | $0–$39.99 | Free first month with NSI; waived from Month 2 with Handmade approval |
| 50-unit Starter Bundle print batch (COGS $3.75/unit) | $187.50 | PLA+, packaged for FBA |
| FBA-compliant poly bags + FNSKU labels (50 units) | $25–$35 | LDPE bags, thermal label stock |
| Inbound shipping to Amazon FC (small carton, ~$15–25) | $20 | FedEx/UPS; first batch exempt from inbound placement fee |
| Sponsored Products campaign (60 days × $4/day) | $240 (offset by $200 NSI credit) | Net out-of-pocket: $40 after NSI credit |
| Brand Registry / trademark filing (USPTO) | $250–$350 | Optional but enables Vine; treated as deferred investment |
| **Total capital required (without trademark)** | **$272–$322** | With NSI credits applied |
| **Total capital required (with trademark filing)** | **$522–$672** | Full investment including trademark |

**Capital efficiency note**: The minimum viable FBA launch (without Brand Registry/trademark, using $200 NSI Sponsored Products credit for ads) costs $272–$322 — less than the $400 threshold in the activation trigger. The trademark/Vine path costs $522–$672 but generates 15–25 early reviews that meaningfully compress the cold-start timeline by 3–4 months.

**Recommendation**: At $400+ available capital, file the trademark and pursue Vine enrollment simultaneously. The compound ROI of 20+ early reviews ($0 per review via Vine vs. $50–$100 per review equivalent via Sponsored Products) justifies the trademark filing cost within 60 days.
