## Part 4: Decision Tree — Test Print Outcome Routing

**Use this tree to route to appropriate launch sequence based on test print results**:

### Outcome A: ✅ PASS — Clip functions, snap arm tolerance acceptable
→ **Proceed directly to Part 1 (May 25-29)**
→ Launch May 30 as planned (4-5 days prep)
→ Follow Part 2-3 execution

---

### Outcome B: ⚠️ PASS WITH MINOR ADJUSTMENTS — Snap arm 0.1mm too tight/loose, but functional
→ **Adjust CAD tolerance** (5 min)
→ **Reprint test sample** (1-2 hours)
→ **Re-test** (15 min)
→ If re-test passes: Proceed to Part 1 (May 26-29), **launch May 31 instead**
→ Note in first listing: "Redesigned for better snap fit based on prototype testing"

---

### Outcome C: 🔴 FAIL — Snap arm breaks, design non-functional
→ **Halt launch sequence**
→ **Redesign decision**:
  - Option 1: Reduce snap arm length by 10% (easier clip engagement, still functions)
  - Option 2: Increase snap arm thickness by 0.5mm (stronger arm, tighter snap)
  - Option 3: Pivot to different product (magnetic clips, adhesive-only, screw-mount variants)
→ **Timeline delay**: Minimum 1 week for redesign + re-test (launch target: June 6-13)
→ **Update Etsy**: Delay shop launch to June 6, publish announcement explaining "precision redesign in progress"

---

### Outcome D: 🟡 PARTIAL FAIL — Some sizes work, some don't
→ **Launch with functional sizes only**
→ **Part 1**: Prepare only passing-size variants for launch May 30
→ **Example**: If 0.5" works but 0.75" snaps: Launch 0.5" products May 30, test 0.75" variant separately June 2-5
→ **Staging**: List partial product range, note "Additional sizes coming soon"
→ **Recovery plan**: Re-test failed sizes June 1-3, add to catalog June 6

## Part 6: Contingency Plans & Risk Mitigation

**Risk 1: Zero orders in first 48h**
- **Root cause check**: Are listings visible in Etsy search? (Search test: "cable clip")
- **Quick fixes**: Adjust titles/keywords, add $1 discount, reorder photos
- **Fallback**: Pause listings, debug 48h, relaunch with adjusted keywords

**Risk 2: Shipping damage complaints** (clips arriving broken)
- **Prevention**: Triple-box packaging (clip → foam → box)
- **Response**: 100% refund + replacement shipped same day
- **Long-term**: Switch to padded envelope after first 10 shipments confirm safety

**Risk 3: Quality issues** (snap arm too tight in production batches)
- **Root cause**: Filament diameter or printer nozzle drift
- **Fix**: Calibrate printer before each print batch, adjust CAD tolerance -0.05mm per batch
- **Customer response**: Offer replacement or modified-design alternative

**Risk 4: Demand exceeds supply** (>5 orders/day)
- **Response**: Implement "Lead time: 5-10 business days" on listings
- **Decision gate**: If sustained >5/day for 1 week, consider printer upgrade or second-printer deployment
- **Timeline**: Pause May 30-June 2 rush if >10 orders, recover June 3+
