# Polymaker Wholesale Onboarding Guide — ModRun

**Lead finding:** Polymaker's US wholesale program is the strongest premium-tier filament program available to print farm operators in 2026. The $1,000 minimum order (approximately 67kg of PolyLite PLA at ~$14.99/kg), free shipping above $3,000, and net-30 availability for qualified accounts make it the optimal transition from retail/Amazon sourcing once ModRun reaches sustained 50kg/month consumption. The account registration is free, instant, and requires no approval process — you can establish your wholesale login today. The activation question is not eligibility but timing: the $1,000 MOQ is appropriate starting Month 3–4 when 50kg+ monthly production is confirmed and the order represents 2–3 weeks of supply, not 6 months.

---

## Section 1: Program Overview

Polymaker's North America wholesale program operates from a Houston, Texas warehouse. It serves print farms, resellers, and production operations. The program is separate from Polymaker's standard US retail site (shop.polymaker.com) and from their Amazon presence — wholesale pricing is not available through those channels.

**Program portal:** us-wholesale.polymaker.com

**Differentiators from retail purchasing:**
- Wholesale price vs. MSRP: Up to 45% off MSRP on qualifying products
- PolyLite PLA MSRP is approximately $24/kg retail; wholesale at $14.99/kg represents a ~38% discount
- Free shipping threshold: Orders exceeding $3,000 ship at no charge
- Net-30 payment terms available (not available on retail site or Amazon)
- Dedicated support contact: support.na@polymaker.com
- Custom order consideration: Non-catalog colors and alternative spool formats reviewed case-by-case
- Tax-exempt purchasing: Submit certificate to support.na@polymaker.com for approval

## Section 2: Account Application Process

Unlike some wholesale programs requiring manual review and approval periods, Polymaker's wholesale account setup is self-service:

**Step 1:** Navigate to us-wholesale.polymaker.com

**Step 2:** Click "Sign Up" in the top-right corner

**Step 3:** Create a business account with company name, address, and contact information

**Step 4:** Begin purchasing immediately — there is no waiting period or approval gate

**Step 5:** If requesting net-30 terms, contact support.na@polymaker.com after placing 2–3 prepaid orders to establish payment history. Net-30 is not automatic; it requires demonstrating reliability.

**Step 6:** If requesting tax-exempt status, email your state resale/exemption certificate to support.na@polymaker.com for review and application to future orders.

**Step 7:** For custom orders (unique colors, alternative spool sizes, bulk arrangements), contact support@polymaker.com with your request, business type, and monthly volume projection.

**Action for ModRun today:** Register the wholesale account now (zero cost, zero commitment). This establishes the account before it is needed, and enables sample requests, pricing confirmation, and relationship-building before the first real order.

## Section 3: Volume Pricing Tiers

Polymaker's wholesale pricing is less publicly tiered than commodity competitors (eSUN, Anycubic) but the disclosed structure is:

### Confirmed Pricing (May 2026, us-wholesale.polymaker.com)

| Product | Format | Wholesale Price | MSRP | Discount |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PolyLite PLA | Per kg (case quantity) | ~$14.99/kg | ~$24/kg | ~38% |
| Panchroma PLA | Per kg | $14.99/kg (listed) | ~$24/kg | ~38% |
| PLA Pro | Per kg | ~$17–19/kg (est.) | ~$28/kg | ~32–39% |
| PETG (various) | Per kg | From ~$15.99/kg | ~$26/kg | ~38% |

**Pricing confidence:** PolyLite PLA at $14.99/kg and Panchroma at $14.99/kg are confirmed from the public-facing wholesale pages. PLA Pro pricing requires a logged-in account and direct quote. The "up to 45% off MSRP" program description suggests deeper discounts are available at higher committed volumes — contact support.na@polymaker.com with a 3-month volume projection to test this lever.

### Volume Commitment Pricing Lever

Polymaker's wholesale program is structured for negotiation at sustained volume. The levers available to ModRun:

**Lever 1 — Monthly commitment statement:** Contact support.na@polymaker.com with a projection of "X kg per month across Y colors for the next 6 months." Reps have discretionary authority to negotiate 5–10% additional discount off wholesale base pricing for committed accounts.

**Lever 2 — $3,000+ single order:** Single orders above $3,000 (~200kg at $14.99/kg) trigger free shipping and typically invite a pricing conversation. At 50kg/month production, a 200kg quarterly order is a 4-month supply — feasible once production is stable.

**Lever 3 — Net-30 terms request:** After 3+ prepaid orders, request net-30 terms. This converts Polymaker from a cash-before-delivery supplier to a credit supplier, which is meaningful at $1,000–$3,000/month order values. At $2,000/month in Polymaker orders on net-30, ModRun effectively carries $2,000 in float that reduces the working capital requirement.

## Section 4: Minimum Order Requirements

| Threshold | What It Unlocks |
|---|---|
| $1,000 minimum per order | Wholesale pricing access; required for every order |
| $3,000 minimum per order | Free shipping (domestic US only) |
| $1,000 order × 3 payments | Qualifies to request net-30 terms |
| Sustained monthly volume (50kg+) | Opens negotiation for additional ~5–10% discount |

**At $14.99/kg for PolyLite PLA, the $1,000 MOQ equals approximately 66.7 kg.**

**At ModRun's Phase 2 production scale (50kg/month):** A single $1,000 MOQ order represents approximately 6.5 weeks of supply — a reasonable inventory build. As production scales to 100kg/month, a monthly $1,000–$1,500 order represents 2–3 weeks of supply, which is the ideal cadence for maintaining stock without excess inventory.

**Phase timing recommendation:**
- Month 1–2 (post-test print): Continue with eSUN via Amazon (no MOQ, Prime-shipped, proven AMS compatibility)
- Month 3 (50kg/month confirmed): Register Polymaker wholesale account; place $1,000 trial order (~67kg) in white and grey only — the two most quality-visible colors
- Month 4+: Expand Polymaker to primary supplier for white/grey; maintain eSUN for black (commodity pricing advantage for black is marginal, consistency is high)
- Month 6+: Target $3,000+ quarterly orders for free shipping + negotiate additional volume discount

## Section 6: Technical Support and Consultation

Polymaker differentiates on technical support quality compared to commodity suppliers (eSUN, Anycubic, SUNLU). The support resources relevant to a production print farm:

### Filament Consultation

**Contact:** support.na@polymaker.com

**Available support:**
- Print profile recommendations for Bambu P1S (temperature, speed, AMS settings)
- Material selection guidance for specific functional requirements (flex resistance, layer adhesion, heat tolerance)
- Batch-to-batch color consistency documentation (Polymaker publishes certificates of analysis on request)
- Moisture sensitivity guidance (PolyLite PLA vacuum-sealed foil bags with desiccant — superior to commodity brands' loose-wound packaging)

**Relevant for ModRun:** Request print profiles for PolyLite PLA on Bambu P1S at ModRun's standard print settings (layer height, infill, wall count). Polymaker's validated profiles eliminate the calibration iteration that commodity brands require. This is a genuine production advantage: consistent Polymaker profiles on 5–10 printers means batch QC is predictable.

### Failure Troubleshooting

If a production batch fails (layer delamination, color inconsistency, AMS feed errors), Polymaker's support team can diagnose from:
- Batch number on the spool label
- Description of failure mode
- Printer model and firmware version
- Print settings used

Polymaker will offer a replacement spool or credit for manufacturing defects confirmed by their QC team. Commodity brands (eSUN, Anycubic) have less systematic failure tracking. This is a tangible production continuity advantage.

### Custom Orders

For production operations with stable monthly volume, Polymaker considers custom arrangements:
- Custom spool sizes (500g spools for single-run jobs, 3kg jumbo spools for high-volume colors)
- Custom colors (Pantone matching for branded packaging inserts or product-matched filament)
- Dedicated production runs for colors not in the standard catalog

**Contact:** support@polymaker.com with your business type, monthly volume projection, and specific custom request. Turnaround on custom orders is typically 4–8 weeks for initial production run, then 2–4 weeks for reorders once your color is in their production queue.

## Section 7: Exclusive Dealer Territories and Co-Marketing

**Exclusive territories:** Polymaker's wholesale program does not operate on exclusive dealer territory model in North America. Multiple print farms and resellers can purchase the same wholesale pricing in the same geography. There is no protected territory grant.

**Co-marketing opportunities:** Polymaker has an active engagement with print farm operators and maker communities. Relevant opportunities for ModRun Phase 3+:

- Product tag/credit: Polymaker encourages sellers of printed products to note "Printed with Polymaker PolyLite PLA" in product descriptions. This is not a formal co-marketing program but generates goodwill and occasional social amplification from Polymaker's channels.
- Sample product submission: Polymaker occasionally showcases customer applications on their social media. High-quality ModRun cable management product photography tagged @polymaker3d can attract co-marketing attention without a formal arrangement.
- Maker-to-brand collaboration: Contact support.na@polymaker.com if monthly volumes reach 100kg+ to discuss whether a co-branded case study or feature is possible. At that volume, ModRun represents a meaningful reference customer for Polymaker's print farm program.

## Section 8: Quality Advantage vs. Commodity Suppliers

The case for Polymaker at $14.99/kg vs. eSUN at $11–13/kg:

| Quality Factor | eSUN | Polymaker |
|---|---|---|
| Diameter tolerance | ±0.03mm | ±0.02mm |
| Moisture packaging | Loose vacuum bag | Sealed foil bag with desiccant |
| Batch-to-batch color consistency | Moderate (visible shifts over months) | High (CoA available on request) |
| AMS reliability documentation | Community-sourced data | Published compatibility by printer model |
| Failure troubleshooting support | Email/ticket (slow) | Direct tech contact at support.na@polymaker.com |
| COGS delta at 75g/unit | $0.82/unit | $1.12/unit |
| COGS premium | — | +$0.30/unit |

**At 500 units/month, the Polymaker premium costs $150/month more than eSUN.** The business case: if Polymaker's consistency reduces production failures by 1.5% (from 3% to 1.5% on a 500-unit batch), that is 7.5 fewer reprints. At $1.40 COGS per clip, avoided reprints save $10.50/month. Premium cost minus savings = net extra cost of $139.50/month, which is a real cost — the primary ROI is quality consistency (better reviews, fewer defect-related returns) rather than direct material savings.

**Recommendation:** Use Polymaker for white and gray production runs specifically, where color accuracy and surface quality are most visible to the buyer. Continue with eSUN for black (where surface defects are less visible and commodity consistency is adequate). Blended cost impact is approximately $0.10–0.15/unit premium, manageable on a $25+ sale price.
