# ModRun Hybrid Launch Roadmap

**The core principle:** Earn your way onto Amazon. Etsy is the proving ground — lower capital, faster feedback, better audience fit for a new original-design maker product. Amazon is added only when Etsy has demonstrated real demand and produced a review base strong enough to survive Amazon's cold-start problem. Each phase has explicit go/no-go metrics. If metrics are not met, stay in the current phase and iterate rather than advancing prematurely.

---

## Phase 1: Etsy-Only Launch (Month 1–2)

**Trigger:** Test print complete. Product photographed. Etsy listing live.

**Objective:** Reach first 10 sales, collect first 5 reviews, validate pricing and product-market fit.

### What You Are Doing in Phase 1

Running the fulfillment workflow already documented in `fulfillment-workflow.md`: order → queue → print → QA → package → ship within 2 business days. The Bambu P1S is the engine. Made-to-order means zero forward inventory risk.

**Active listings strategy:**
- Start with 3–4 SKUs: Starter Bundle ($28.99), Standard Clips 3-pack ($12.99), Premium Clips PETG ($18.99), and one economy entry point ($8.99)
- All listings use the keyword-optimized copy from `etsy-listing-modrun.md`
- Tags filled completely (13 tags max), title front-loaded with highest-priority terms
- 5+ photos per listing (lifestyle + macro + in-use context)

**Etsy advertising strategy:**
- Turn off Etsy Ads for the first 14 days. Organic ranking for a new shop gets a temporary algorithm boost. Let organic run first to avoid paying for clicks during the boost window.
- After 14 days, if daily views are below 30 for any listing, start Etsy Ads at $1–3/day. Target 10–15% click conversion to sales.
- Do not exceed $50/month in Etsy Ads spend until the review base reaches 10+ reviews. Low reviews mean low conversion — you are paying for traffic that won't convert.

**Customer experience investment (review-building):**
- Include a printed card in every order: brief thank-you note + "If you love it, a review makes a huge difference for a small maker business." No incentive offered (Etsy prohibits incentivized reviews). Just a human ask.
- Respond to every buyer message within 4 hours during business hours. Star Seller requires 95%+ response rate within 24 hours — exceed it.
- Follow up on any "I received my order" messages with a genuine response. Early buyers often leave reviews after a human exchange.

**Capital deployed in Phase 1:** ~$40 (shop setup + initial listings). All materials are draw-as-needed. No forward inventory.

### Phase 1 Go/No-Go Metrics

Evaluate at the end of Month 2 (or when 20 sales are achieved, whichever comes first):

| Metric | Go Threshold | No-Go / Recalibrate |
|---|---|---|
| Sales velocity | 10+ units/month by end of Month 2 | < 5 units/month after 6 weeks |
| Average review rating | 4.7+ stars | Below 4.5 — investigate product quality or description mismatch |
| Review count | 5+ reviews | 0–2 reviews after 20+ sales — add review request card, improve follow-up |
| Margin health | Net margin > 55% after all fees | Below 50% — reprice or reduce packaging cost |
| Returns/disputes | < 2% of orders | > 5% — product quality or listing accuracy issue |

**If Go:** Proceed to Phase 2 at Month 3 planning cycle.

**If No-Go:** Diagnose the specific gap. Velocity under 5/month usually indicates a listing SEO problem (wrong keywords, poor photos, uncompetitive price point) — not a product quality problem. Review count zero usually indicates packaging/communication failure. Address root cause before advancing.

## Phase 2: Add Amazon Handmade + FBA Test Batch (Month 3–4)

**Trigger:** Phase 1 Go metrics met. Specifically: 20+ units/month on Etsy, 4.8+ review average, 15+ total reviews.

**Objective:** Establish Amazon presence with a 50-unit test batch, use New Seller Incentives for first reviews, validate whether Amazon channel produces margin-positive orders without unsustainable advertising spend.

### What You Are Doing in Phase 2

**Account setup (Week 1 of Month 3):**
- Open Amazon Seller Central account. Start with Professional plan ($39.99/month) — Individual plan's $0.99/item fee makes FBA math worse and Professional unlocks FBA, Vine, and advertising.
- Apply for Amazon Handmade storefront. Application requires demonstrating that products are artisan-made (photos of your process, description of your production method). 3D-printed original designs qualify. Review time is 3–7 business days.
- Register your first ASIN for the Starter Bundle. Use the existing Etsy photography (ensure images are white-background-first for Amazon requirements — Amazon requires primary image on pure white background).
- Enroll immediately in Vine via New Seller Incentives (free, expires 90 days post-account-opening). Request up to 30 Vine reviews. This is the single most important action in Phase 2 — Vine reviews break the cold-start problem without advertising cost.

**Inventory batch (Week 2 of Month 3):**
- Print 50 units of the Starter Bundle. Expected filament + time cost: $3.10/unit COGS at this volume = $155 total materials.
- Bag each unit in clear poly bag (FBA requirement for multi-component items). Apply FNSKU barcode sticker (Amazon-provided label printed from Seller Central, free). Box for shipment to fulfillment center.
- Create shipment plan in Seller Central. For 50 small units, Amazon will typically assign 1–2 fulfillment centers. Ship via UPS or FedEx (Amazon partnered rates). Estimated inbound shipping cost: $15–25 for a single small box.
- Accept the inbound placement fee waiver (New Selection Program, first 100 units per ASIN, first 90 days).
- **Total Phase 2 capital deployment:** ~$350–400 (Professional plan + inventory + packaging + shipping + $50 Sponsored Products seed budget).

**Advertising (Month 3, Day 15+ after Vine reviews start arriving):**
- Set Sponsored Products automatic campaign at $3/day. Let it run for 14 days on auto to discover which search terms are converting.
- After 14 days, review the search term report. Create a manual campaign targeting the top 3–5 converting terms at $0.50–1.00/bid.
- Total advertising budget cap: $50/month in Phase 2. If ACOS (advertising cost of sale) is above 30%, pause auto and focus on manual only.

**Fulfillment operations:**
- FBA handles all fulfillment for the 50-unit test batch. Your only task is monitoring inventory level and customer messages.
- Amazon customer messages must be answered within 24 hours (their metric). Set email notifications.
- Etsy orders continue under the existing made-to-order workflow. The two channels operate independently.

### Phase 2 Go/No-Go Metrics

Evaluate at the end of Month 4 (60 days after FBA batch goes live):

| Metric | Go Threshold | No-Go / Recalibrate |
|---|---|---|
| FBA units sold | 20+ of 50 batch (40%) | < 10 sold — investigate listing quality, pricing, or category fit |
| Amazon conversion rate | > 5% (unit sessions to orders) | Below 3% — listing content problem (photos, copy, price) |
| Amazon review score | 4.3+ (minimum viable for organic visibility) | Below 4.0 — product or expectation mismatch |
| FBA net margin | > 50% after all fees | Below 45% — fee structure unsustainable at this price point |
| Combined channel velocity | 40+ units/month across both channels | Below 30 — Phase 2 not adding incremental volume |
| Aged inventory pace | < 50% of batch unsold at Day 90 | > 50% unsold at Day 90 — at risk of aged inventory surcharge at Day 181 |

**If Go:** Proceed to Phase 3 planning. Restock FBA at 100 units and expand SKU count.

**If No-Go:** Do not restock FBA. Let remaining inventory sell through. Focus on Etsy. Diagnose: Is it a listing problem (fixable) or a category fit problem (Amazon buyers don't value original 3D prints the way Etsy buyers do)? If listing problem — fix and retry. If category problem — Amazon Handmade is not the right channel for this product and Etsy remains the primary forever.

## Phase 3: Full Product Line Expansion (Month 6+)

**Trigger:** Phase 2 Go metrics met. Additionally: Etsy repeat buyer rate above 15% (buyers returning for a second purchase or bundle upgrade). This signals product-market fit strong enough to justify broader investment.

**Objective:** Expand Amazon to full ModRun product line (all three tiers + bundles). Build toward 50%+ of total revenue from Amazon channel. Invest in A+ content and brand registry.

### What You Are Doing in Phase 3

**Amazon Brand Registry:**
If you have a registered trademark (or have applied), enroll in Amazon Brand Registry. Benefits include A+ Content (enhanced product description with images and comparison tables), brand protection against hijacking, and sponsored brand ads. Trademark registration takes 8–12 months; file early if Phase 2 succeeds.

Without Brand Registry, you can still use basic product descriptions and Sponsored Products. Brand Registry unlocks measurably better listing presentation (A+ Content increases conversion by an estimated 3–10% according to Amazon's own data).

**SKU expansion:**
- Add Standard Clips (both economy and premium PETG variants) as separate FBA ASINs
- Add Pro Expansion Pack
- Build a virtual bundle of top two sellers (Amazon allows virtual bundles without separate FBA shipment)
- Keep print-on-demand (FBM) available for custom color requests — list as a separate "customizable" variant

**Inventory management discipline:**
At this volume, inventory rotation matters. Target 60-day sell-through on each FBA batch (order enough to last 60 days, reorder when 30 days of stock remain). This keeps inventory fresh, avoids the 181-day aged inventory surcharge cliff, and maintains inventory depth signals for the algorithm.

**Pricing strategy at Phase 3:**
Etsy and Amazon prices can diverge slightly. Amazon buyers are less price-sensitive to the premium positioning (they are searching by function, not maker identity) but are more sensitive to Prime eligibility and review count. Consider pricing Amazon 5–10% higher than Etsy to offset the fee differential and maintain comparable net margins.

### Phase 3 Go/No-Go Metrics

Evaluate at Month 9 (3 months into Phase 3):

| Metric | Continue / Scale | Recalibrate |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon revenue share | 30–50% of total revenue | Below 20% — Amazon is not pulling weight, rebalance investment |
| Combined monthly revenue | $3,000+ | Below $2,000 — reassess SKU mix and pricing |
| Blended net margin (both channels) | > 55% | Below 50% — fee structure or COGS problem |
| Operational capacity | Fulfillment sustainable with current printer setup | Bottlenecking — expand printer farm (multi-printer-architecture.md) |
| Amazon ACOS (ad spend / revenue) | < 20% | Above 30% — advertising is not converting, investigate |

## Key Go/No-Go Decision Framework Summary

The roadmap is built around three gates. Each gate has a hard threshold and a default action if the threshold is not met.

**Gate 1 — Phase 1 → Phase 2 (Month 2):**
- PASS: 20+ units/month Etsy, 4.8+ stars, 15+ reviews
- FAIL: Remain in Phase 1, diagnose and iterate. Do not deploy $400 into FBA before demand is validated.

**Gate 2 — Phase 2 → Phase 3 (Month 4):**
- PASS: 40% FBA batch sell-through, 4.3+ Amazon stars, combined 40+ units/month
- FAIL: Let FBA batch sell through, return to Etsy-only. Amazon Handmade may not be the right channel for this product at this margin structure.

**Gate 3 — Phase 3 Continuation (Month 9):**
- PASS: Amazon at 30%+ revenue share, combined $3K+/month, ACOS < 20%
- FAIL: Rebalance. Amazon may be a secondary support channel (not co-equal) — that is fine. The business can be highly profitable on Etsy alone.

## Capital Deployment Summary

| Phase | Capital Required | When Deployed | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | $40 | At launch | Existing funds |
| Phase 2 | $350–400 | Month 3 (post Gate 1) | Etsy revenue (1–2 weeks of 20+ units/month at $28.99 = $580–870) |
| Phase 3 initial | $500–700 | Month 6 (post Gate 2) | Combined channel revenue |
| Phase 3 full | $1,000–2,000 | Month 6–9 | Operating cash flow |

The roadmap is self-funding by design. Phase 1 requires a trivial upfront spend. Phase 2 is funded by Phase 1 Etsy revenue — at 20+ units/month, the first two weeks of sales cover the full FBA setup cost. Phase 3 is funded by Phase 2 revenue. No external capital is needed at any phase if gates are met sequentially.
