# Capital Raise Strategy & Contingency — Phase 2 Traction Gates

## Executive Summary

ModRun faces a **binary capital decision in Q3 2026** based on Phase 1 traction:

- **High traction** (≥$3,000 cumulative by June 30): Pursue **friends+family pre-raise** ($10K–$25K) to fund aggressive Printer 3 + laser acquisition and accelerate to $15K+/month revenue
- **Moderate traction** ($1,000–$3,000): **Defer capital raise** — use organic cash flow to fund Standard scenario at controlled pace
- **Low traction** (<$1,000): **No raise** — remain bootstrapped, focus on product-market fit

This document defines the contingency logic, pitch frameworks, and investor contact strategies for each path.

## Part 2: Friends+Family Pre-Raise Strategy (High Traction Path)

### Scenario Trigger

**Pursue friends+family raise IF AND ONLY IF**:
1. Phase 1 cumulative revenue ≥ $3,000 by June 30
2. Etsy shop rating ≥ 4.7 stars (15+ reviews minimum)
3. Conversion rate ≥ 2.5% (demonstrates product-market fit)
4. Owner commits to 40+ hours/week through Q4 2026

**Raising friends+family without these signals is speculative and damages credibility.**

### Raising Structure & Terms

**Form**: SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) or convertible note (defer equity discussion; focus on capital + revenue share)

**Preferred structure**: Revenue-share agreement (no equity)
- 8–12% of gross revenue for 24–36 months
- Minimum payout: $X per month regardless of revenue (protects investor from stalls)
- Maximum payout: Capped at 1.5–2.0× initial investment (investor interest)
- Trigger: Begins when monthly gross revenue exceeds $3,000/month sustained

**Example terms** (for $15,000 raise):
- Investor receives 10% of gross revenue
- Minimum payout: $300/month (starting month 1)
- Maximum payout: $300/month × 36 months = $10,800 (returns capital at 1.73× ROI)
- If revenue hits $5,000+/month, investor earns $500/month (returns capital in 30 months, 2.0× ROI)
- Sunset: After investor receives 1.8× capital return, agreement terminates and investor retains no ongoing interest

**Why revenue-share > equity**:
- Equity dilutes future fundraising and creates governance complexity
- Revenue-share aligns investor incentive with **revenue growth** (the right metric), not just business valuation
- 10% revenue share at $15K/month = $1,500/month = 18% annual return (competitive with early-stage equity)
- Simpler legal structure (1–2 page agreement vs. full equity paperwork)

## Part 3: Organic Growth Strategy (Moderate Traction Path)

### Scenario Trigger

**Pursue organic (no external capital) if**:
- Phase 1 cumulative revenue $1,000–$3,000
- Etsy shop rating 4.5–4.8 stars (8–14 reviews)
- Conversion rate 1.5–2.5%

**Rationale**: Demand is real but not explosive. Self-funding allows time to validate product-market fit before larger capex commitment.

### Organic Funding Plan

**Self-fund Phase 2 using monthly profits**:

| Month | Opening Cash | Monthly Profit | Cumulative Investment Available | Constraint |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| June | $0 | $378 | $378 | None yet |
| July | $378 | $620 | $998 | Purchase Printer 2 now ($399) |

**Advantage**: No investor relations overhead; complete control; higher profitability (no revenue share)

**Disadvantage**: Slower scaling; misses Q3 growth window if demand accelerates; competitor entry risk

### Organic Scaling Roadmap (Standard Scenario)

**Phase 2A (July–August)**: 2-printer operation via organic cash
- Deploy Printer 2 ($399) in early July
- Accumulate capital for electrical upgrade + SimplyPrint by mid-August

**Phase 2B (August–September)**: Laser validation via bureau (low upfront cost)
- Bureau engraving: $50–150/batch × 2–3 batches = $150–300 total
- Risk-free validation before $1,959 xTool S1 purchase

**Phase 2C (September–October)**: Laser acquisition decision
- If bureau demand exceeds 100 cumulative units, purchase xTool S1 using accumulated profit
- If bureau demand <50 units, defer laser indefinitely

**Phase 2D (November–December)**: Printer 3 decision
- Q4 demand validates whether 2-printer cluster is capacity-constrained
- Order Printer 3 if monthly revenue sustains >$5K; otherwise hold

**Expected outcome (organic path, 12 months)**: $14K–$17K gross, $8K–$10K net (slower than raise scenario, same ultimate destination)

## Part 4: No Raise / Bootstrap Path (Low Traction)

### Scenario Trigger

**Pursue bootstrap-only (indefinite no raise) if**:
- Phase 1 cumulative revenue <$1,000
- Etsy shop <8 reviews or rating <4.5 stars
- Conversion rate <1.5%

**Rationale**: Product-market fit unvalidated. Raising capital before PMF is wasteful.

### Focus Areas (Q3–Q4 2026)

**Do NOT add printers or major capex.** Instead, optimize single-printer operation and test hypotheses:

1. **Conversion rate optimization** ($0 investment)
   - A/B test title keywords (4–6 variants)
   - Reorder product photos (test which image converts best)
   - Adjust pricing (-$1 temporary discount on slowest SKU)
   - Expected uplift: 1–2% baseline → 2–3% conversion

2. **SEO and organic visibility** ($0 investment)
   - Long-tail keyword research (Etsy search bar, Google Trends)
   - Create content: "Cable management desk setup" blog post (drive Etsy shop link)
   - Optimize listing descriptions for search intent
   - Expected uplift: Page 3–5 visibility → Page 1–2 in 2–3 long-tail keywords

3. **Product line expansion via CAD iteration** ($50–100 investment)
   - Headphone hooks (cadquery/headphone_hooks.py ready to slice)
   - Monitor stand with cable slot
   - USB hub organizer tray
   - These test different buyer segments without new manufacturing equipment

4. **Test bundle strategy** ($0 investment)
   - Create Etsy "bundle" listing: 3 clips + rail at $39.99 (vs. $24.99 + $34.99 individual = $59.98)
   - Bundles typically convert at 30–50% higher rate than individual SKUs
   - Expected impact: +20–30% AOV if bundle conversion succeeds

### If bootstrap path gains traction (PMF validates by October)

**Escalate to organic growth path**:
- October: Reassess traction vs. Moderate or High gates
- If October revenue >$2,000 cumulative, escalate to Standard scenario (2-printer + laser validation)
- Deploy Printer 2 + laser budget from Q4 profits + early Q1 2027 cash flow

**Timeline extension**: Bootstrap path adds 2–4 months to reaching $15K+/month run-rate vs. aggressive scenario, but with zero capital risk.

## Part 5: Pitch Angles & Investor Communication

### Pitch Angle 1: "The Alternative to Amazon FBA"

For investors interested in e-commerce efficiency:

"Most Etsy print-on-demand shops fail because they can't compete with Amazon's logistics. ModRun is different: we're making physical goods with 70% margin, not just reselling commodity 3D prints.

At 20 units/week (achievable Month 1–2), ModRun generates $1,200–$1,400 monthly profit with a single $399 printer. Scale to 3 printers: $15K/month revenue, $8K–$10K net profit. No Amazon FBA middleman, no fulfillment partner — we control the entire value chain.

Your capital accelerates us to 3 printers + laser engraving by Q3 2026, unlocking $15K+/month revenue 6 months earlier than organic scaling."

**Investor profile**: E-commerce entrepreneurs, Amazon FBA sellers looking for alternative (higher-margin) models

### Pitch Angle 2: "Hardware Gross Margins You Can Only Get from 3D Printing"

For manufacturing/supply-chain investors:

"70% gross margin on physical goods is exceptional. Compare:

- Traditional injection-molded clips: 40–45% margin (high tooling, low per-unit COGS, large MOQs)
- 3D-printed clips: 70% margin (low tooling, higher per-unit COGS, on-demand)
- FDM print farms scale linearly: each printer pays for itself in 3–4 weeks

Your $15K investment funds 3 printers + laser. At steady-state demand (80+ units/month), those machines generate $12K+/month in gross profit. Payback: 18–24 months (1.5–2.0× return)."

**Investor profile**: Manufacturing engineers, supply-chain consultants, operations experts

### Pitch Angle 3: "Vertical Integration Unlocks Margin"

For product/supply-chain optimization enthusiasts:

"Most cable clips on Etsy are re-sells of cheap AliExpress products (10–30% margin). ModRun designs and manufactures our own: CAD → 3D print → laser engrave → ship.

This vertical integration means:
- We control product quality (customer feedback loop direct)
- We can customize on demand (personalized engraving, color variants)
- We own the IP and customer relationships

Competitors can't easily copy because they'd need to reverse-engineer our CAD, invest in printers, and build Etsy review score. First-mover advantage in 3D-printed cable management is real."

**Investor profile**: Product designers, manufacturing consultants, vertical integration advocates

### Pitch Angle 4: "De-Risked by Pre-Revenue Validation"

For conservative investors:

"We've already proven product-market fit: test print passed, Etsy launch generated $3K in revenue in 4 weeks with <10 reviews. This isn't speculative.

Your capital doesn't fund a hypothesis — it funds scaling a validated model. We've proven:
- Unit economics (70% margin, $18.58 GP per unit)
- Demand signal ($3K in June)
- Customer willingness to pay ($24.99–$34.99 price point; strong conversion)

The only remaining risk is execution (can we manage 2–3 printers, fulfill orders on-time). Your capital reduces execution risk by enabling us to hire a part-time contractor for fulfillment."

**Investor profile**: Conservative angels, business coaches, people who prefer validation over speculation

## Final Note on Investor Relations

**Keep it simple and honest.** The best investor for a bootstrapped manufacturing business is someone who understands this is a multi-year value creation play, not a lottery ticket.

Revenue-share alignment (investor earns money when you earn money) is far superior to equity (which creates misaligned incentives and future friction). Simple terms + transparent reporting + consistent execution = long-term relationship.

If you execute the Standard or Aggressive scenario successfully through 2026, you'll have generated enough cash flow that you never need external capital again. The investor's 1.8× return in 18–24 months is a win, and you retain full control of the business going forward.
