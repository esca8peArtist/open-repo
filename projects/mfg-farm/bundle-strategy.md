# ModRun Bundle Strategy: Product Architecture & Customer Upsell Pathways

## Executive Summary

ModRun's bundling strategy transforms single-component purchases (individual clips or rails) into complete system solutions that increase average order value (AOV) by 2.5–3x while improving customer satisfaction through "ready-to-install" positioning. Five distinct bundles target different customer cohorts across the buyer journey, from first-time trial through professional/B2B adoption.

**Bundle Economics:**
- **Single clip at $14.99** = 60% gross margin, impulse buy risk (customer may buy elsewhere next time)
- **Starter Bundle at $28.99** (1 rail + 3 clips) = 72% gross margin, complete solution, high repeat purchase probability
- **Deluxe Professional Kit at $179.99** = 72.8% margin, high-value anchor, positions brand as premium

## 1. Five Core Bundles: Definitions & Positioning

## Conclusion

ModRun's five-bundle strategy (Essentials → Starter Kit → Pro Expansion → Professional → Deluxe) creates a **natural customer escalation pathway** that increases AOV, improves margins, and builds lifetime customer value. By targeting distinct cohorts with messaging and bundle composition that fits their needs, ModRun positions itself as **the** premium cable management system for professionals and creators—not a generic commodity.

**Launch Priority:**
1. **Month 1:** Essentials + Starter Kit only (build volume, reviews)
2. **Month 2–3:** Pro Expansion (drive Starter Kit upgraders)
3. **Month 4+:** Professional + Deluxe (premium positioning)
4. **Month 6+:** Corporate bulk (B2B revenue)

**Success Definition (Month 6):**
- 50%+ of revenue from bundles
- 200+ total units sold
- 200+ Etsy reviews (4.5+ average)
- $3,500–$4,000 monthly revenue
- 15%+ B2B revenue (1–2 corporate customers with repeat orders)
