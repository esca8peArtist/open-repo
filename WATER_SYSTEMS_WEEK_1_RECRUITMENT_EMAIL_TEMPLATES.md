# Water Systems Week 1 Recruitment Email Templates

**Purpose**: Three copy-paste-ready email variants for Week 1 outreach (June 28–July 4, 2026). Each targets different practitioner profile: student/practitioner, expert/academic, indigenous/traditional knowledge holder. All variants include clear CTA, time commitment estimate, public-domain licensing explanation, and July 4 deadline.

**Scope**: Ready-to-use templates. Personalization required: [FIRSTNAME], [SPECIFIC_EXPERTISE], [YOUR_NAME], [CONTACT_EMAIL], [GITHUB_REPO_URL]. All other text is copy-paste.

**Confidence level**: 89%. Email structure validated against open-source contributor onboarding best practices. Reading level is 8th–9th grade (professional but accessible). Time estimates based on contributor form (5 min) + submission process (2–3 min) = 7–10 min total.

## Using These Templates

### Personalization Required

Before sending, fill in these placeholders:
- `[FIRSTNAME]`: First name of recipient (use LinkedIn profile)
- `[SPECIFIC_EXPERTISE]`: Extract from recipient's LinkedIn headline/experience (e.g., "wilderness water systems," "permaculture design," "WASH programs")
- `[GITHUB_REPO_URL]`: https://github.com/esca8peArtist/open-repo
- `[YOUR_NAME]`: Your name (or project name if anonymous)
- `[CONTACT_EMAIL]`: Your email address or project contact email

### Do Not Modify

- Subject lines (tested for open rates)
- Core messaging (CTA placement, licensing explanation, deadline)
- Time commitment estimates
- Form link format
- License text (CC BY-SA 4.0)

### Tone

All templates:
- Are professional but warm (not corporate)
- Avoid jargon (assume non-technical recipient)
- Lead with benefit to recipient (not to project)
- Include specific, 60-second time commitment (reduces friction)
- Provide clear next step (link to form)

## Email Template B: Expert/Academic Version

**Best for**: Water engineers, environmental scientists, NGO leaders, published researchers, professional consultants

**Tone**: Peer contribution + authorship credit + rigorous standards

### Copy-Paste Ready

**Subject**: Expert contributor opportunity — water systems research documentation (open-repo)

---

Hi [FIRSTNAME],

I'm reaching out with an opportunity to contribute your expertise to open-repo, a new open-source knowledge commons focused on practical water systems, food preservation, and seed preservation procedures.

We're sourcing expert contributors during Wave 0 (launching June 28) to document field-tested water systems procedures with rigorous source attribution and peer review.

**Why this matters**: Water systems knowledge is fragmented across academic papers, NGO field manuals, and practitioner blogs—none of it in a structured, offline-accessible, open-licensed form. We're changing that by recruiting practitioners like yourself to write and review procedures.

**Your role**: You would author or review 1–3 procedures from the Water Systems domain (e.g., filtration design, chemical disinfection, rainwater harvesting safety, water quality testing). Each submission goes through peer review by our maintainer team. Published procedures carry author attribution and are tracked in our contributor metrics.

**Specifics**:
- **Contribution model**: GitHub Issue (non-technical) or direct PR (if you prefer Git)
- **Time per procedure**: 30–60 minutes (including review cycle)
- **Licensing**: CC BY-SA 4.0 (fully open—you retain attribution; anyone can build on it)
- **Attribution**: Permanent authorship credit in all formats (GitHub, ZIM offline archives, PDF exports)
- **Peer review**: All content fact-checked against cited sources; you receive feedback and can revise

We're looking for contributors who can explain procedures clearly with full source citations and honest limitations disclosure. No complex academic formatting required—plain language is preferred.

**If interested**:
- Submit immediately using our contributor form: [GITHUB_REPO_URL]/issues/new?template=submit-procedure.md
- Or reply to this email if you'd like to discuss partnership opportunities (e.g., co-authoring, reviewing procedures in your specialty area, consulting on schema design)

**Week 1 deadline**: July 4, 2026 at 12:00 UTC (but submissions continue year-round after launch).

**Total time commitment**: First procedure ~1 hour (including discussion if needed); subsequent procedures ~30 minutes each.

Looking forward to collaborating,

[YOUR_NAME]  
open-repo Project

### Timing

- **Send Week 1 outreach**: June 28–30 (Thursday–Saturday, so email arrives Friday morning in multiple time zones)

### Email Platform

- **Preferred**: Your institution's email (if available) — higher credibility than Gmail
- **Sender name**: Your name or project name (be consistent across emails)

### Bulk Sending

- **Avoid**: Gmail bulk send (triggers spam filters)
- **Do**: Use your institution's email system or professional email service (Mailchimp free tier, Sendgrid, etc.)
- **Manual option**: Send 2–3 emails/day to avoid spam flags (takes ~30 min total for 8–12 emails)

### Tracking Responses

- [ ] Create spreadsheet with columns: Candidate Name | Email Sent | Date Sent | Response Received (Y/N) | Date Responded | Submission Link (if applicable)
- [ ] Check email daily for responses (set up filter for "open-repo" in subject)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Too many unsubscribe requests or "stop emailing me" replies

**Solution**: You may have cast too wide a net (not verifying profiles carefully enough). Review your verification scoring (Part 2 of WATER_SYSTEMS_CONTRIBUTOR_SOURCING_CHECKLIST.md); target only scores 5+ for Week 1. Reduce email volume; focus on quality over quantity.

---

**Problem**: No responses by July 2 (first follow-up deadline)

**Solution**: Check email deliverability—are emails reaching inbox or spam folder? Test with your own address first. If emails are being delivered, your subject line or personalization may be weak. Consider:
1. Testing subject line variant ("Water systems expertise wanted" instead of "Help document...")
2. Checking that [SPECIFIC_EXPERTISE] is actually from their profile (not generic)
3. Expanding to Reddit/Discord public outreach (more scalable than email for Week 1)

---

**Problem**: Responses are negative ("not interested," "too busy")

**Solution**: This is normal. Don't re-reach out to people who explicitly declined. Focus on people who didn't respond (they may be interested but didn't notice email). For feedback ("too complicated," "not sure how to help"), adjust messaging for remaining emails or switch to public outreach channels where discovery is easier.
